# Cycle 7 Discovery: Forge Council Faction

## Implication Selected
**Forge Council faction file** - Political faction controlling Ashcrag governance

## Source
- Flagged in Cycle 5 build plan (lorekeeper identified gap)
- Flagged again in Cycle 6 lore check audit
- Referenced in 8+ existing files but no dedicated faction file

## Why This Implication
The Forge Council is a major political power structure in The Stone Heights:
- Governs Ashcrag (population ~350)
- Controls forge industry and land ownership
- Rival to Highland Merchant Cartel
- Target of Highland Workers Coalition strike (dual-target strategy)
- Led by Myra Cadros (master smith)
- Includes Aldric Harth (grain lord)

This is a **structural gap**: we cannot fully understand Ashcrag's politics, the coalition's strategy, or highland power dynamics without a dedicated faction file.

## What We Need to Build

A full faction file covering:
1. **Founding & History:** When/why the council formed, connection to highland independence
2. **Structure:** 5-member body (2 master smiths + 3 landowner reps), voting rules, term length
3. **Leadership:** Myra Cadros (current council head), Aldric Harth (landowner rep), Brennan (second master smith - **needs NPC file or at least detail**)
4. **Membership:** Who qualifies (guild masters, landowners), how representatives are chosen
5. **Territory:** Ashcrag jurisdiction, relationship with outlying settlements (Ironhollow is cartel-controlled, not council)
6. **Resources:** Control over forge output, land ownership, grain surplus, timber rights
7. **Goals:** Maintain highland independence from lowland influence, preserve guild control, resist cartel encroachment
8. **Opposition:** Highland Merchant Cartel (economic rivalry), Highland Workers Coalition (labor organizing), Crown Authority (nominal but distant), Saltmere Merchant Assembly (competing power)
9. **Internal Tensions:** Guild vs landowner interests, Myra's pragmatism vs conservative landowners, debt pressure (Aldric owes Corwin Marsh)
10. **Campaign Role:** Act as obstacle to labor reform, potential ally against cartel if approached correctly, source of political quests

## Gaps & Questions for Workshop
