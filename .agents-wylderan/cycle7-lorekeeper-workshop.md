**Current:** Aldric owes debt to Corwin Marsh, due in 2 months (Aldric Harth.md line 37)

### Timeline Questions for Council Founding

**No founding date is mentioned anywhere in the vault.**

**Analysis:** Myra took control "20 years ago during harsh winter" implies the council already existed. She didn't found it, she took leadership of it.

**Constraint 1:** Council must predate Myra's leadership (>20 years old)

**Constraint 2:** Halfway Burn Site supplied "Myra Cadros's ironworks and Brennan's Forge" 25 years ago (HBS.md line 40), implying both master forges existed, which implies the guild structure existed

**Constraint 3:** Aldric's family is "three generations" in Ashcrag (Orrin Pike.md line 37), suggesting landowner continuity predates recent council formation

**Inference Options:**

**Option A: Post-Theomachis (Ancient)**
- Council formed in aftermath of divine war as highland communities reorganized
- Would be 80-150 years old
- Explains independence from Crown (post-collapse autonomy)
- Explains guild-dominated structure (guilds preserved craft knowledge through chaos)

**Option B: Highland Independence (Mid-Period)**
- Council formed 50-80 years ago as highlands asserted independence from lowland/Crown control
- Coincides with development of river trade network
- Guild structure chosen deliberately to resist noble/Crown governance model

**Option C: Recent Reform (30-40 years ago)**
- Council formed within living memory, replacing earlier governance
- Myra's takeover 20 years ago would be early in council's existence
- Would explain why structure is still contested (farmers never accepted it)

**Lorekeeper Recommendation:** Option B (50-60 years ago) or Option A (ancient). Option C creates timeline compression issues and doesn't explain the established nature of the guild structure referenced 25+ years ago.

**Requires Workshop Decision:** Should council founding be:
- Ancient (post-Theomachis, 100+ years)?
- Mid-period (highland independence assertion, 50-80 years)?
- Recent (within living memory, 30-40 years)?

**Alignment Check:** Any chosen date must:
1. Predate Myra's leadership (>20 years ago)
2. Predate established guild forge operations (>25 years ago)
3. Not contradict Crown relationship (independent but nominal fealty?)
4. Explain why tenant farmers lack representation (designed that way? Erosion over time?)

## 4. Faction Relationship Web

### Highland Merchant Cartel

**Current Status:** Economic rivalry, professional transactions, growing tension

**Details:**
- Myra has borrowed from cartel for forge expansion, repaid on time (Myra Cadros.md line 47)
- Myra sees cartel as "necessary evil" providing capital highlands can't generate alone (Myra Cadros.md line 47)
- Myra dislikes cartel's growing influence over highland trade (Myra Cadros.md line 47)
- Tallow Breck (cartel factor) operates in Ashcrag, arranges contracts, collects debts (Ashcrag.md line 87)
- Orrin Pike views cartel and council as equivalent parasites (Orrin Pike.md line 46)

**Power Dynamic:** Cartel controls capital, council controls production/governance. **Rivalry, not war.** Both exploit highland workers, but compete for control of profits.

**Tension Points:**
- Cartel has displaced council in Ironhollow (company town, no council jurisdiction)
- Cartel finances lowland traders (Dace Torrn), undercutting council's barge priority control
- Cartel forecloses on farmers, removing them from council's tax/fee base
- Council needs cartel capital but resents dependence

**Future Trajectory:** If HWC strike succeeds, both factions lose. If strike fails, cartel gains leverage over weakened council (capital dependency increases).

### Highland Workers Coalition

**Current Status:** Direct opposition, escalating conflict

**Details:**
- Farmers strike targets council specifically (land reform, representation, reduced fees)
- Miners strike targets cartel primarily, council secondarily (coalition dual-target strategy, HWC.md)
- Council knows about organizing but hasn't responded yet (HWC.md line 110)
- Myra hasn't decided strategy: ignore, negotiate, or crush (HWC.md line 110)
- Orrin has 40 farmers, only 15 committed (Orrin Pike.md line 58)
- Council informant: Thom Harrow reports to Tallow Breck **[Missing NPC file, Cycle 6 audit finding]**

**Power Dynamic:** Coalition is fragile (15 committed of 40), council is stable but distracted by other pressures (Aldric's debt, charcoal shortage, winter road concerns).

**Tension Points:**
- Orrin demands council seat, Myra refuses (farmers have Aldric as rep)
- Aldric is compromised (debt to Corwin Marsh), can't represent farmers effectively
- Coalition threatens spring grain market boycott (would collapse highland trade)
- Council has leverage: eviction threats, market access denial, forge tool supply

**Future Trajectory:** Collision course. Orrin will force confrontation at next council meeting (Orrin Pike.md line 60). Council must choose: concessions (gives coalition legitimacy), refusal (coalition fractures or radicalizes), or arrests (makes martyrs).

### Saltmere Merchant Assembly

**Current Status:** Competing power, lowland leverage over council

**Details:**
- Corwin Marsh (Assembly member) holds Aldric Harth's debt, due in 2 months (Aldric Harth.md line 37, Ashcrag.md line 37)
- If Marsh forecloses, he controls half highland grain supply (Ashcrag.md line 37)
- Assembly uses debt to control highland exports (Ashcrag.md line 37)
- Assembly is coastal power, council is highland power (geographic/economic rivalry)

**Power Dynamic:** Assembly has financial leverage, council has production control. **Economic competition** for control of Millrun river trade network.

**Tension Points:**
- Aldric's debt gives Assembly direct influence inside Forge Council
- Aldric is smuggling grain outside official channels to pay debt (Aldric Harth.md line 45)
- If Assembly forecloses, highland grain flows to lowland merchants, not highland traders
- Council's independence threatened by lowland creditor influence

**Future Trajectory:** Aldric defaults in 2 months unless harvest is exceptional. Marsh seizes Harth Estate, Assembly gains highland foothold, council loses landowner member and political stability. **Campaign-scale power shift** (Ashcrag.md line 108).

### Crown Authority

**Current Status:** Nominal independence, unclear relationship

**Details:**
- "The Forge Council operates independently of Crown authority" (Ashcrag.md line 28)
- "Guild-dominated, not Crown authority" (CHANGELOG line 150)
- Lyman Corvish is Crown toll collector in Millford (NPCRegistry.md line 22)
- No Crown representative mentioned in Ashcrag

**Power Dynamic:** Unclear. "Independently" could mean:
- Nominal fealty, no active interference
- Complete independence (post-Theomachis autonomy)
- Active resistance (highlands reject Crown governance)

**Tension Points (Hypothetical):**
- Does council pay taxes to Crown? Or keep all revenue?
- If HWC topples council, does Crown intervene?
- Does Crown have legal claim to Ashcrag, just no enforcement capacity?

**Requires Clarification:** What is Crown's actual relationship to highlands? Is independence:
- **De facto** (Crown doesn't care about remote highlands)?
- **De jure** (Formal autonomy granted post-Theomachis)?
- **Contested** (Crown claims authority, highlands ignore it)?

**Lorekeeper Recommendation:** Clarify in faction file. Suggest **de facto independence with nominal fealty** (council claims Crown legitimacy when convenient, ignores Crown when inconvenient).

## Workshop Answers to Discovery Questions

### 1. Brennan detail level: Full NPC file or faction file detail only?

**Lorekeeper Recommendation: Faction file detail only, with option to expand later.**

**Reasoning:**
- Brennan is mentioned 3 times across vault (Ashcrag.md, Myra Cadros.md, CHANGELOG)
- Role: Council member (master smith seat), rival to Myra, rougher tool production
- Currently no direct plot involvement (unlike Myra/Aldric)
- Expanding to full NPC requires: personality, backstory, relationships, campaign role, roleplay notes (60-90 lines)

**What faction file needs:**
- Name, role, forge specialization (done)
- Voting pattern (conservative? Guild-first? Rivalry with Myra affects votes?)
- Position on HWC organizing (does he side with Myra's refusal or see opportunity?)

**Full NPC file becomes necessary if:**
- PCs negotiate with council (need to portray him in scene)
- Brennan breaks with Myra on key vote (becomes plot point)
- HWC tries to split council (Brennan as swing vote)

**Verdict:** Detail in faction file now, flag for potential expansion if gameplay requires.

### 2. Third landowner: Named NPC or generic "rotating seat"?

**Lorekeeper Recommendation: Generic rotating seat with 2-3 named examples.**

**Reasoning:**
- Aldric Harth is the major landowner (third-generation, largest estate, plot-critical debt)
- Third seat is "rotating" per Ashcrag.md line 26
- No plot hooks currently involve this character
- Giving them a name/personality creates expectations of plot relevance

**What faction file needs:**
- Explanation of rotation mechanism (how often? Who qualifies?)
- Current holder: Name and estate, but minimal detail ("Tomas Harren, second-generation grain farmer, typically votes with Aldric")
- 1-2 previous holders as examples of rotation

**Verdict:** Generic seat with current holder named but not developed. Allows flexibility for future plot needs.

### 3. Council founding: Recent (past 50 years) or older (post-Theomachis recovery)?

**Lorekeeper Recommendation: Mid-period, 60 years ago, highland independence assertion.**

**Reasoning:**
- Must predate Myra's takeover (20 years ago) and established forge operations (25+ years ago)
- "Post-Theomachis ancient" (100+ years) makes council seem too stable for current contestation
- "Recent" (30-40 years) compresses timeline too much given established references
- **60 years ago** allows:
  - Council is within grandparents' memory (living continuity)
  - Formed as highlands asserted independence from Crown/lowland influence
  - Guild structure was deliberate choice, not inherited tradition
  - Explains why farmers never accepted it (they remember earlier, less stratified system)

**Narrative Hook:** Council was founded to **resist lowland creditor control** (like current Corwin Marsh situation). Irony: council now faces same threat from Assembly it was created to prevent.

**Verdict:** 60 years ago, post-Crown autonomy assertion, guild-dominated by design.

### 4. Counter-insurgency: Does the council know about the coalition yet? If so, what's their response?

**Lorekeeper Recommendation: Council knows, hasn't acted, internally divided on response.**

**Existing Evidence:**
- HWC.md line 110: "Myra Cadros knows about the organizing but has not yet decided how to respond"
- Orrin's petition: 40 farmers signed publicly (Orrin Pike.md line 58, Ashcrag.md line 95)
- Council refused petition, claimed Aldric represents farmers (Ashcrag.md line 95)

**Assessment:** Council knows organizing exists, sees it as complaint not threat. **They are underestimating it.**

**Internal Positions (Inferred):**
- **Myra:** Sees it as destabilizing, wants to refuse and let it collapse on its own (doesn't want to make martyrs)
- **Aldric:** Trapped. Officially represents farmers, but can't support them without council backlash. Also can't suppress them without losing tenant cooperation on his estate.
- **Hal Brennan:** Unknown. Could side with Myra (guild solidarity) or see opportunity to undercut Myra politically.

**Current Strategy:** Refuse petition, maintain status quo, wait for coalition to fracture (15 committed of 40 will shrink under pressure).

**Weakness:** Council doesn't know about Ironhollow coordination (Senna-Orrin link). They think it's just Ashcrag farmers complaining, not a dual-target highland-wide movement.

**Verdict:** Council knows, is passive, assumes farmers will give up. This is a **strategic miscalculation** that could hand HWC a win if PCs intervene or external pressure (Aldric's default) distracts council.

### 5. Ironhollow claim: Does the council assert any jurisdiction over Ironhollow, or concede it to the cartel?

**Lorekeeper Recommendation: No current claim, historical claim abandoned, simmering resentment.**

**Existing Evidence:**
- Ironhollow is cartel company town (Ironhollow.md line 19)
- "No government" listed, cartel controls all aspects (Ironhollow.md line 19)
- Halfway Burn Site (between Ashcrag/Ironhollow) supplied both towns 25 years ago (HBS.md line 40), implying historical economic link

**Assessment:** Council once had economic influence (charcoal trade, tool supply). Cartel displaced council by controlling ore extraction and debt.

**Historical Timeline (Inferred):**
- 40+ years ago: Ironhollow was independent mining camp, traded with Ashcrag
- 25 years ago: Council forges supplied tools, charcoal from shared burn sites
- 15-20 years ago: Cartel consolidated control via debt/contracts, displaced council influence
- Current: Ironhollow is cartel company town, council has no jurisdiction

**Council's Position:**
- Publicly: "Ironhollow is outside Ashcrag's governance" (pragmatic acceptance)
- Privately: Resent cartel encroachment, see it as threat to highland independence
- Strategically: Can't contest cartel without capital/military force (don't have either)

**Myra's View:** Wants to break cartel control to secure independent ore supply (Myra Cadros.md line 40), but hasn't found way to do it.

**Verdict:** No current claim. Faction file should note **historical economic link, cartel displacement, council's desire to recover influence but no capacity to act**.

### 6. Crown relationship: Nominal fealty, active resistance, or complete independence?

**Lorekeeper Recommendation: Nominal fealty, de facto independence, mutual convenience.**

**Reasoning:**
- "Operates independently of Crown authority" (Ashcrag.md line 28) doesn't specify legal relationship
- Crown has toll collector in Millford (Lyman Corvish), suggesting Crown presence in region
- No Crown garrison, no Crown representative in Ashcrag (implies low priority for Crown)
- Council uses "guild-dominated" model, not noble governance (suggests rejection of Crown feudal structure)

**Proposed Relationship:**
- **Legal:** Council acknowledges Crown sovereignty, pays nominal annual tax (small enough to ignore)
- **Practical:** Crown doesn't interfere in highlands, council governs autonomously
- **Justification:** Post-Theomachis reconstruction, highlands were too remote/poor to administer. Crown granted autonomy, council filled power vacuum with guild structure.
- **Current:** Crown has bigger problems (lowland politics, other regions). Highlands are economically marginal, not worth enforcing control.

**What This Means:**
- Council can claim Crown legitimacy when convenient (resisting cartel, enforcing contracts)
- Council ignores Crown law when inconvenient (guild control vs. feudal obligations)
- If HWC topples council, Crown unlikely to intervene (too distant, too distracted)

**Tension Point:** If Saltmere Assembly seizes highland grain (via Aldric's default), does Crown notice? Assembly is lowland power, more connected to Crown. Could Assembly ask Crown to recognize their claim over highlands? (Makes highland independence contest a Crown legal issue.)

**Verdict:** Nominal fealty, de facto independence. Faction file should explain **historical autonomy grant, current mutual disinterest, potential for Crown intervention if highlands become economically/politically critical**.
