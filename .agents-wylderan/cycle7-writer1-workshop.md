# Cycle 7 Writer1 Workshop: Forge Council Faction

## 1. Founding & History

**Council age:** ~20 years old (founded ~20 years before present day)

**Founding context (from Myra Cadros.md):**
> "Twenty years ago, a harsh winter closed the southern road for five months. Ashcrag ran out of imported salt, and half the town's grain stores spoiled. Families starved. The Forge Council, newly formed and weak, could not coordinate a response. Myra took control, organizing rationing, negotiating emergency barge runs when the ice broke, and restructuring the council to prevent another disaster."

**What this tells us:**
- The council existed *before* the crisis, but was "newly formed and weak"
- The crisis was 20 years ago, so the council was likely founded 21-23 years ago
- Myra "restructured" the council after taking control, meaning its current form (guild-dominated, centralized) is Myra's design, not its original structure
- The council was formed in response to earlier instability, but we don't know what that was

**Connection to Theomachis:**
- No existing lore directly connects the council's founding to the Theomachis
- The Theomachis is ancient history in this world (divine war, dead gods)
- Highland independence from Crown authority is established, but timeline is unclear
- **Recommendation:** The council was founded ~22 years ago in response to trade route instability and lowland creditor pressure (post-Theomachis recovery period, not directly caused by divine war)
- The harsh winter 20 years ago tested the council and forced Myra's centralization

**Proposed founding narrative:**
25-30 years ago, highland settlements operated independently with no coordinated governance. Lowland creditors (early Merchant Assembly expansion) began buying up highland debts and foreclosing on farms. Ashcrag's guild masters and largest landowners formed the Forge Council as a defensive alliance to resist lowland economic control and coordinate trade. The council was decentralized and consensus-driven. Then the harsh winter hit. Families starved because the council could not coordinate a response quickly enough. Myra Cadros seized control, restructured the council to give guild masters permanent seats (ending rotation), and centralized decision-making. The current council is Myra's creation, not the founders' vision.

## 2. Governance Structure

**From existing lore (Ashcrag.md):**
- 5-member council: 2 master smiths (permanent) + 3 landowner representatives (rotating)
- Master smiths: Myra Cadros (Cadros Ironworks), Hal Brennan (Brennan's Forge)
- Landowner reps: Aldric Harth confirmed, two others unnamed
- Decisions by majority vote (3 of 5)
- Council meets monthly at the Stone Hearth tavern (second-floor meeting hall)

**Gaps to address:**

**How are landowner reps chosen?**
- Original design: Elected by landowners (those owning 50+ acres)
- Myra's restructure: Still elected, but eligibility restricted to guild-affiliated landowners (those who contract with the forges or sell through council-approved traders)
- This excludes independent farmers and tenant farmers (Orrin Pike's core complaint)

**Term length:**
- Landowner reps serve 3-year terms
- Elections held every year (staggered, one seat up each year, meaning two years have two reps elected, one year has one rep)
- Reps can be re-elected unlimited times (Aldric has served for 9 years, three consecutive terms)

**Voting rules:**
- Majority vote (3 of 5) for standard decisions
- Unanimous vote required for: changing council structure, expelling a member, levying new taxes/fees
- Myra, as council head, casts the tiebreaker if vote is 2-2 (never happens with 5 members, but legacy rule from earlier structure)

**Council head selection:**
- Originally rotated among members
- Myra has held the position for 20 years, re-confirmed annually by vote
- In practice, Myra controls the vote because the two master smiths (her + Brennan) plus one landowner ally = majority
- Brennan does not challenge her because competing with Myra politically would harm his forge contracts

**Quorum:**
- 4 of 5 members must be present for votes to be valid
- Designed to prevent Myra from ruling alone, but in practice, she ensures attendance

## 3. Territory & Jurisdiction

**From existing lore:**
- Ashcrag settlement proper (population ~350)
- Outlying farms within 5-10 miles (Harth Estate to the north, smaller holdings to the south and east)
- Timber yards south of town (where Orrin Pike operates)
- **Ironhollow is NOT council jurisdiction** (8 miles north, fully controlled by Highland Merchant Cartel)

**Jurisdiction claims:**
- The council governs Ashcrag and claims authority over "all settlements and holdings within the Ashcrag valley watershed" (defined as the Cold Run, Ashwater, and Piney Flow drainage basins)
- This includes farms, timber operations, and charcoal kilns that feed the Ashcrag economy
- It does NOT include Ironhollow, which is on the far side of the ridgeline and was never part of the council's founding territory

**Relationship with Ironhollow:**
- The council originally supplied tools to Ironhollow miners (via Myra's forge contracts)
- When the Highland Merchant Cartel established control over Ironhollow (~10 years ago), they began importing tools from lowland forges to undercut Ashcrag prices
- Myra sees this as cartel economic warfare and wants to reclaim the Ironhollow market
- The council has no legal claim to Ironhollow, but Myra frames cartel control as "highland resources feeding lowland profits"

**Outlying farms:**
- Farms within council jurisdiction pay the 10% market fee when selling grain through Grett Voss's tally house
- Farmers who bypass the market (selling directly to smugglers or lowland traders) are violating council ordinances
- The council has limited enforcement capacity (no standing militia, no jail, relies on social pressure and economic leverage)
- Aldric Harth's smuggling operation is a direct violation of council authority, but Myra does not know about it yet

**Geographic reach:**
- Northern boundary: The ridgeline separating Ashcrag valley from Ironhollow (Ironhollow is beyond council reach)
- Southern boundary: Where the three streams converge to form the Millrun River (the Confluence is the last council-controlled point before the river flows to Millford)
- Eastern/Western boundaries: Undefined, roughly where the forested hills become too remote for regular trade with Ashcrag

## 4. Resources & Assets

**What the council controls:**

**Market fees (10% of all transactions):**
- Primary revenue source
- Collected by Grett Voss at the tally house
- Estimated ~300-400 gold per harvest season (grain sales), ~100-150 gold per season (timber, tools, misc)
- Used to fund: council operations, road maintenance, emergency grain reserves, paying Grett Voss's salary

**Barge loading priority:**
- Council sets the schedule for which farmers load grain onto barges first
- Farmers who pay fees on time get priority
- Late payers or non-compliant farmers are pushed to the back of the queue (meaning their grain sits longer, risking spoilage)
- This is a weapon: the council can financially cripple a farmer by delaying their barge access

**Grain price influence:**
- The council does not set prices directly (market forces apply)
- But the council can manipulate supply by controlling barge loading, effectively creating artificial scarcity or gluts
- Myra uses this to stabilize prices during shortages (preventing panic buying) and to punish farmers who challenge council authority

**Timber rights:**
- The council does not own the forests, but it licenses timber operations
- Families like Orrin Pike's operate under council-approved contracts
- The council can revoke licenses for "unsafe practices" or "overextraction" (definitions are vague, enforcement is political)

**Emergency grain reserve:**
- Myra established this after the harsh winter 20 years ago
- Stored in a council-controlled warehouse near the market ground
- ~50 tons of grain (enough to feed the town for 2-3 months in a crisis)
- Myra has been quietly drawing from this reserve to keep prices stable during the current shortage (farmers do not know)

**What the council does NOT control:**

**Forge output:**
- Myra and Brennan own their forges privately
- The council does not dictate production, prices, or contracts
- But as council members, Myra and Brennan have insider knowledge of market conditions, giving them a competitive edge over smaller smithies

**Land ownership:**
- Landowners like Aldric Harth own their estates privately
- The council cannot seize land or redistribute it (Orrin Pike wants this changed)

**Charcoal supply:**
- Timber families like Orrin's control charcoal production
- The council can pressure them (via licensing), but cannot force output increases
- This is why Orrin's charcoal leverage is so powerful

## 5. Crown Relationship

**From existing lore:**
- "The Forge Council operates independently of Crown authority" (Ashcrag.md)
- "Independent of Crown" (CHANGELOG)

**What "independent" means:**

**Nominal fealty:**
- Ashcrag nominally acknowledges the Crown as the ultimate authority in the realm
- The council sends a representative to the regional Crown seat once per year to renew fealty (ceremonial, no substance)
- The Crown has not sent officials, tax collectors, or soldiers to Ashcrag in living memory

**Taxes:**
- The council does NOT pay taxes to the Crown
- Historical context: After the Theomachis, the Crown's reach collapsed. Highland regions like Ashcrag were too remote and poor to be worth taxing. The Crown focused on rebuilding core territories (lowlands, coastal cities). Highland settlements were left to govern themselves.
- Over time, this neglect became de facto independence

**Enforcement:**
- The Crown has no enforcement presence in the highlands
- If the Crown wanted to reassert authority, it would need to send soldiers, which it has not done
- Myra exploits this: she frames the council as "highland self-governance" (noble, traditional) vs. Crown interference (distant, illegitimate)

**Why the Crown tolerates this:**
- Ashcrag is small (~350 people), remote, and economically marginal from the Crown's perspective
- The highlands produce grain and tools, but lowland Merchant Assembly (Saltmere) controls the trade network, so Crown taxes lowland merchants instead
- The Crown benefits from highland-lowland trade without having to administer the highlands directly
- If Ashcrag becomes a problem (rebellion, banditry, trade disruption), the Crown *could* intervene, but it hasn't been worth the effort

**Tension:**
- If Corwin Marsh seizes Aldric Harth's estate, lowland economic control over highland grain supply deepens
- The Crown might see this as an opportunity to reassert authority (backing Marsh's claim with Crown legitimacy)
- Myra fears this: she sees lowland debt expansion as the precursor to Crown annexation

## 6. Goals & Motivations

**What the council wants (as an institution):**

**1. Maintain highland independence from lowland influence**
- Resist Merchant Assembly (Saltmere) control over highland trade
- Prevent lowland creditors (Corwin Marsh, Tallow Breck's cartel) from foreclosing on highland farms
- Keep the highland economy self-sufficient (grain, tools, timber produced and traded locally)

**2. Preserve guild control over Ashcrag's economy**
- Myra believes guild-run governance ensures stability and prosperity
- Tenant farmers and laborers are "represented" through landowner reps, no need for direct seats
- The guild knows what's best for the town (in Myra's view)

**3. Expand forge trade and secure long-term contracts**
- Myra wants to reclaim the Ironhollow market from cartel-imported tools
- Secure exclusive contracts with lowland merchants (Millford, Saltmere) for highland tool exports
- Position Ashcrag as the essential hub of the highland economy (grain + tools)

**4. Maintain the emergency grain reserve**
- Prevent another starvation crisis like the one 20 years ago
- Stockpile enough food to survive prolonged winter road closures
- (Secret: Myra has been drawing from the reserve to stabilize current prices, depleting the safety net)

**Internal tensions (council is not unified):**

**Myra's faction (guild interest):**
- Wants to break cartel control and resist lowland debt leverage
- Willing to negotiate with farmers *if* it strengthens the council's position against external threats
- But will not compromise guild dominance or give Pike a council seat

**Aldric's faction (landowner interest):**
- Wants to maximize grain exports to pay off lowland debts
- Torn between loyalty to the council and desperation to save his estate
- Secretly undermining the council (smuggling grain) to avoid default
- If forced to choose between the council and Corwin Marsh, Aldric will choose Marsh

**Brennan's position (rival smith):**
- Competes with Myra for forge contracts but supports guild dominance politically
- Would like to see Myra weakened but will not openly oppose her (too risky)
- Votes with Myra on most issues to maintain guild unity

**The other two landowner reps (unnamed):**
- Likely allies of Myra or Aldric, depending on debt exposure and forge contracts
- One is probably a Myra loyalist (giving her 3 votes: herself, Brennan, loyalist landowner)
- One is probably neutral or Aldric-aligned (meaning Aldric can block unanimous votes but cannot win majorities)

**What happens if these tensions break:**
- If Aldric defaults, Myra loses a landowner ally and Corwin Marsh controls highland grain supply (council's nightmare scenario)
- If Orrin Pike's coalition forces a crisis, Myra will either compromise (giving farmers representation) or crack down (arrests, blacklisting, violence)
- If the cartel offers to finance the council in exchange for influence, Myra faces a choice: accept cartel help (and control) or resist and risk economic collapse

**Q3: Council founding: Recent (past 50 years) or older (post-Theomachis recovery)?**

**Answer:** Recent (~22 years ago, within living memory).

**Reasoning:**
- Myra's backstory establishes the council as "newly formed and weak" 20 years ago
- A recent founding makes the council fragile and experimental, which fits the emerging political tensions
- An older founding would make the council an entrenched institution, harder to challenge
- Recent founding also means older residents remember life *before* the council, which validates Orrin Pike's argument that the council is not inevitable or natural

**Timeline:**
- ~22 years ago: Council founded by guild masters and landowners to resist lowland creditor expansion
- 20 years ago: Harsh winter crisis, Myra restructures the council and centralizes power
- Present day: Council has been stable for 20 years under Myra's control, but cracks are showing (farmer organizing, debt pressure, cartel encroachment)

---

**Q4: Counter-insurgency: Does the council know about the coalition yet? If so, what's their response?**

**Answer:** Myra knows. Brennan knows. Aldric knows. The council has not formally discussed it yet.

**Reasoning:**
- Highland Workers Coalition.md: "[[Myra Cadros]] knows about the organizing but has not yet decided how to respond."
- Grett Voss, the market factor, "knows every sale, every debt, every barge contract" and likely reports to the council
- Orrin Pike "has been organizing tenant farmers and timber workers to demand council representation" - this is not secret, it's semi-public

**Current council stance:**
- **Myra:** Watching, waiting, preparing. She believes the coalition will collapse on its own (internal tensions, lack of resources). If it doesn't, she will move to break it (economic pressure: deny barge access, revoke timber licenses, blacklist coalition members from forge contracts). She has not decided whether to arrest Orrin Pike yet (it would make him a martyr).
- **Brennan:** Quietly sympathetic to farmers but will not act. He sees the coalition as a threat to guild control, but he also sees it as a way to weaken Myra (if she mishandles the response, she loses credibility). He is waiting to see which way the wind blows.
- **Aldric:** Terrified. Coalition members work his land. If they strike, his grain harvest fails, he defaults to Corwin Marsh, he loses everything. He wants the council to crush the coalition immediately, but Myra has refused (she does not want to provoke a violent confrontation). Aldric is considering offering concessions to his own tenant farmers to peel them away from Pike.
- **Jorin Kelm (landowner rep):** Supports Myra's wait-and-see approach. No coalition members work his land, so he is insulated.
- **Marna Veld (landowner rep):** Worried. Her farm is near the timber yards where Pike operates. If the coalition boycotts the market, she loses buyers. She is privately exploring whether to negotiate with Pike or side with Myra.

**Council has not formally voted on a response yet.** Myra is keeping it off the agenda until she decides her strategy.

**Q5: Ironhollow claim: Does the council assert any jurisdiction over Ironhollow, or concede it to the cartel?**

**Answer:** The council does NOT claim jurisdiction, but Myra frames cartel control as illegitimate highland resource exploitation.

**Reasoning:**
- Ironhollow was never part of the Ashcrag valley watershed (it is on the far side of the ridgeline, 8 miles north)
- The cartel established control ~10 years ago when the Mining Factor (Caden Wroth) arrived and set up company town structures
- Myra has no legal claim, but she uses rhetoric: "Highland iron should serve highland forges, not lowland profits."
- This is propaganda, not policy. The council has no mechanism to challenge cartel control militarily or legally.

**Myra's strategy:**
- Economic competition: undercut cartel-imported tools by offering better prices or quality from Ashcrag forges
- Labor alliance: if the Highland Workers Coalition succeeds in Ironhollow, Myra could offer council support (grain shipments, tool supplies) in exchange for breaking cartel contracts
- Long-term goal: reclaim the Ironhollow market without fighting the cartel directly

**The council publicly concedes Ironhollow is outside its jurisdiction, but Myra privately sees it as lost highland territory to be reclaimed.**

**Q6: Crown relationship: Nominal fealty, active resistance, or complete independence?**

**Answer:** Nominal fealty with de facto independence.

**Reasoning:**
- The council sends a representative to renew fealty annually (ceremonial, no substance)
- The Crown has not collected taxes, sent officials, or enforced laws in Ashcrag in living memory
- The highlands are too remote and poor for the Crown to prioritize
- Myra frames this as "highland self-governance" (noble, traditional) but fears Crown intervention if lowland creditors push for it

**What could change this:**
- If Corwin Marsh seizes Aldric's estate and petitions the Crown to legitimize the claim, the Crown might reassert authority to protect lowland merchant interests
- If the Highland Workers Coalition escalates to violence, the Crown might intervene to restore order (but unlikely unless trade is disrupted)

**Current status: Nominal fealty, no enforcement, de facto independence, fragile and contingent on the Crown continuing to ignore the highlands.**
