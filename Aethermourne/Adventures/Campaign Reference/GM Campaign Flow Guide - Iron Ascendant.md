# GM Campaign Flow Guide , The Iron Ascendant

*Practical session-level guidance for running The Iron Ascendant.*

This is the companion to the [[Adventures/Campaigns/The Iron Ascendant/Campaign Overview|Campaign Overview]]. Where that document describes *what happens*, this one tells you *how to run it*: session pacing, transition hooks, the Iron Tithe's table impact, and the practical questions a GM faces between sessions.

---

## How the Campaign Moves

The Iron Ascendant has a geographic-then-vertical flow: surface politics compress into a single terrifying descent, then explode back outward for the finale. The players should feel the narrowing, like being funneled into a well.

```
Act I: The Iron Fields (Sessions 1-5)
  ↓ Fragment migration confirmed — trail leads to Ostivaar
Act II: The Contested Ground (Sessions 6-10)
  ↓ Heart of Iron discovered — the Descent is real
Act III: The Descent, Upper Levels (Sessions 11-15)
  ↓ The Mirror's offer refused — deeper or nothing
Act IV: The Descent, Lower Levels (Sessions 16-20)
  ↓ The Echo's deal — Mira arrives at the Heart
Act V: The Heart of Iron (Sessions 21-25)
```

**Each transition is a pull, not a push.** The players discover information that makes them *want* to go deeper. If they resist, Mira's army advances and the fragments converge without them.

## Act-by-Act Session Guidance

### Act I: The Iron Fields (Sessions 1-5)

**Region:** [[The Ironmarch]]
**Pacing:** Gritty and grounded. Mercenary politics, fragment strangeness, a warlord's shadow growing longer.

| Session | Focus | What the Players Should Feel |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Arrival in [[Ironhaven]]. Witness [[Mira the Red]]'s challenge declaration. The Ironmarch's mercenary ecosystem in action. | "This place breeds violence." |
| 2-3 | Fragment investigation in the Iron Fields. [[The Ember Guild]] patron provides structure. Discover migration patterns, amplified aggression near drifting shards, nightmares of iron corridors. | "The shards are moving. That should not be possible." |
| 4 | Encounter Mira's army directly. See her transformation: iron-grey eyes, inhuman voice, fragments pressed into her armor. She is terrifying and pitiable. | "She is not choosing this. Something is using her." |
| 5 | [[Yrsa Bloodhand]]'s secret revealed: the Morrhaelite shard, the Remnant transformation, the cost of clarity. Act I climax at [[The Shardfall]] as Mira seizes the fragment cache and demonstrates command over divine iron. | "The violence has a source and a direction." |

**Key GM Decisions:**
- Which hook did you use? Hook 1 (Ember Guild contract) provides the most structure. Hook 3 (Yrsa's request) provides the most intimacy. Adjust session 5's reveal accordingly: Ember Guild PCs *discover* Yrsa's secret; Yrsa's PCs have known from the start and see the *consequences* at the Shardfall.
- How human is Mira when the PCs first see her? She should be visibly transforming but still recognizable as a person. If the players see her as a monster from session 1, her tragedy loses its weight.
- The Iron Companies' Code is fracturing. Let the PCs pick sides or try to hold things together. Every alliance made here pays off (or costs) in Act V.

**Transition Hook:** [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]] reveals classified Ember Guild intelligence: the migration vector points directly at [[The Ashen Dominion#Ostivaar|Ostivaar]]. Something beneath the Dominion capital, something that dwarfs every fragment on the continent, is calling the iron home. Yrsa confirms: the Ironmarch is the staging ground, not the destination.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> If the players don't take the Ostivaar lead, accelerate Mira's advance. Her army pushes toward the Contested Corridor. The PCs either follow to stop her or the Ember Guild's intelligence network forces the issue: Ashworth's data shows the convergence will happen with or without them.

### Act II: The Contested Ground (Sessions 6-10)

**Region:** [[The Contested Corridor]], [[The Ashen Dominion]] western border, [[The Emberveil]] periphery
**Pacing:** Pressure builds. Active war zone, then political thriller, then the ground drops out.

| Session | Focus | What the Players Should Feel |
|---|---|---|
| 6 | Corridor crossing. Active skirmish zones, transactional alliances, Mira's army a day behind. | "We are in the middle of someone else's war." |
| 7-8 | Inside Dominion territory. Discover the iron shadow: Kaevroth's ambition layered beneath Solvaen's order. Every institution trends toward domination. The military's outsized role. The Hierarch's paranoia. | "Two dead gods built this place, and neither of them wanted peace." |
| 9 | The Heart of Iron revealed beneath Ostivaar. House-sized. War-essence that dwarfs all other fragments combined. The Hierarch does not know it exists. The iron corridors in the dreams are real. | "The ground dropped out." |
| 10 | Belara's prison discovered within the Heart. Forgeheart steel side quest via [[Brunhild Ironheart]] at the Emberveil periphery. Act II climax as Mira's army reaches the Corridor and she commands fragments from the earth itself. | "We cannot destroy the Heart without destroying mercy. We cannot leave it without unleashing war." |

**Key GM Decisions:**
- The Corridor crossing: how dangerous? Scale to party level and preparedness. Under-leveled parties should negotiate passage. Combat-ready parties can fight through but burn resources they will need later.
- The Heart reveal (session 9) is this campaign's "holy shit" moment. Do not rush it. Let the players sit with the scale. The Heart is the size of a house. It hums. The air tastes of metal. And it has been here for two thousand years, shaping a civilization without anyone knowing.
- Belara's imprisonment reframes the entire endgame. The party should leave Act II understanding that there is no clean solution: the Heart cannot be destroyed (Belara dies), cannot be cracked open (war and mercy flood out together), and cannot be ignored (Mira is coming).

**Side Quest:** Forgeheart steel from the [[The Emberveil|Emberveil]] periphery. The only material that safely contains Kaevroth fragments. Production is declining as Thalvor's fire cools. Securing a supply is strategic. Skipping it means improvising in the Descent.

**Transition Hook:** The Heart of Iron anchors the Iron Descent, the only intact surviving divine domain. The iron corridors are real. The Descent is a place, and the PCs can enter it. They may have to enter it. The path to Belara's prison runs through nine levels of concentrated ambition.

### Act III: The Descent, Upper Levels (Sessions 11-15)

**Region:** [[The Iron Descent]], Levels 1-5
**Pacing:** Each level is a full session. Do not compress. The psychological weight requires time.

| Session | Focus | What the Players Should Feel |
|---|---|---|
| 11 | Enter the Descent. Level 1, The Ashfield. [[Warden Erys Ironfall]] welcomes them with genuine warmth. Rustgate is beautiful. The first Iron Tithe takes something small: a childhood memory, a favorite taste, a loved one's voice. | "This place wants us to stay. That is what makes it dangerous." |
| 12 | Level 2, The Proving Grounds. [[Warlord Keth]] and the Crucible of Names. Combat determines identity. Names are won and lost through challenge. | "Violence without purpose, distilled to its essence." |
| 13 | Level 3, The Iron Market. [[Dealmaker Syra Voss]] and Greedhollow. Desire is currency. Every offer is fair. Every price is something you did not know you valued. | "The worst part is that the deal is genuinely good." |
| 14 | Level 4, The Forge Eternal. [[Forge-Warden Irontooth]] and the Foundry. Creation without rest. Masterworks built, melted, rebuilt. The ambition to create, and the trap of never being satisfied. | "Even beauty can be a prison." |
| 15 | Level 5, The Hall of Mirrors. [[Sovereign Kallis]] and Gloryholme. Every surface reflects not your face but the version of you that achieved everything you ever wanted. The visions are real. The offer is genuine. | "I could have everything I want. The cost is staying." |

**Key GM Decisions:**
- The Iron Tithe: track what it takes from each PC. Each loss should be specific and personal. Name the memories. Describe the moment when a PC reaches for something familiar and finds it gone. This is the Descent's true danger, not combat.
- Warlord Keth's secret (session 12): Keth is a construct, the Proving Grounds' immune response. Revealing this to Keth triggers psychological crisis and disables the level's violence-suppression. Helping Keth find new purpose creates a powerful ally. Let the players discover the truth through observation, not exposition.
- The Hall of Mirrors (session 15): this is the Descent's cruelest level because it does not lie. Each PC sees a real possible future. Give each player a private moment with their mirror. Make the offers *good*. If no player is tempted, the visions were not specific enough.
- Mira's vanguard catches up at Level 5. Her fragment-armed soldiers cut through the upper levels with the confidence of those who have already accepted the Descent's gift. This creates urgency for the descent into the lower levels.

**Transition Hook:** Descending means losing more to the Iron Tithe. Retreating means abandoning the Heart to Mira. Accepting the Mirror means becoming part of the Descent's architecture. There is only forward.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> If a PC accepts the Mirror's offer, they do not die. They become a permanent resident of Gloryholme, living their perfect life in a pocket of frozen ambition. The remaining party can rescue them later (at significant cost) or continue without them. This should feel like a genuine loss, not a punishment. The Mirror kept its promise. The PC got what they wanted.

### Act IV: The Descent, Lower Levels (Sessions 16-20)

**Region:** [[The Iron Descent]], Levels 6-9
**Pacing:** Escalating intensity. The upper levels seduced. The lower levels reveal.

| Session | Focus | What the Players Should Feel |
|---|---|---|
| 16 | Level 6, The Conquest. [[The Last General]] and the Thousand Thrones. Endless war, kingdoms built and conquered in cycles. The Hollowdeep crossover: iron-laced undead at the junction of two broken divine domains. | "Ambition for dominion is a wheel, not a ladder." |
| 17 | Level 7, The Iron Court. [[Lord Imperator Vaelgrim]] and Ironhold. Power without meaning. Politics as pure performance. The Court's Embassy offers passage out for a price that is always the same: something you are not willing to give. | "Power has been here so long it has forgotten what it is for." |
| 18 | Level 8, The Consuming. The Maw. [[The Hollow King]]. Identity erosion mechanics: hourly Wisdom saves, failed saves replace core character traits with Descent-flavored versions. Three failures means the PC cannot distinguish their desires from the Descent's hunger. | "I am forgetting who I am. I am forgetting that I am forgetting." |
| 19 | Level 9, The Heart. The Silence. [[The Echo of Kaevroth]]. Not a body, not a ghost, but a vast presence. **It does not attack. It talks.** The Echo's deal: absorb the fragments, contain the war-storm, in exchange for becoming more real. | "It makes sense. That is the horror." |
| 20 | The Mercy Gate. A hairline fracture in the Heart of Iron through which Belara's warmth bleeds. The Echo's deal on the table. Mira arrives, fragments embedded in flesh, more iron than person, still recognizably twenty-five years old. She reaches for the Heart because the voice told her to. | "We have to choose. Now. Here. With everything we have lost." |

**Key GM Decisions:**
- The Hollowdeep crossover (session 16): this is an emergency exit, violent and scarring but survivable. If the party is breaking, this is their escape route. Do not close it unless they have explicitly committed to deeper descent.
- The Consuming (session 18): this is mechanically the most dangerous level. Run the identity erosion with care. Lost traits should be replaced by specific Descent-flavored versions ("I fight for my friends" becomes "I fight because fighting is what I am"). Recovery requires reaching the Mercy Gate or being pulled back by companions who remember who the PC was. Lean on inter-party roleplay.
- The Echo's conversation (session 19): **do not rush this.** This is the campaign's philosophical heart. Give it a full scene, uninterrupted by combat. Let the Echo be charming, sorrowful, honest, and terrifying. Let the PCs ask questions. Let the Echo answer truthfully. The horror is not what the Echo says but that it makes sense. The Echo does not regret the Theomachis. It would do it again.
- Mira's arrival (session 20): she is the human face of everything the Echo is. If the GM has played her well across four acts, at least one player should want to save her even now, when she is the direct obstacle to their goals.

**Transition Hook:** The party must choose: accept the Echo's deal, reject it and find another way, save Mira or stop her, and decide what to do about the Mercy Gate. They carry these choices back to the surface.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> If the party accepts the Echo's deal, the campaign shifts. The war-storm is contained but the Echo becomes a semi-conscious divine presence in the Iron Descent. The Descent persists indefinitely. Mira may be freed (the fragments pull inward instead of through her), but the deal has consequences that unfold in Act V: the Echo's hunger grows, and in a generation it may break containment. The party has traded an immediate crisis for a future one.

### Act V: The Heart of Iron (Sessions 21-25)

**Region:** Beneath [[The Ashen Dominion#Ostivaar|Ostivaar]], the Heart of Iron, and the mortal-world aftermath
**Pacing:** Crescendo. The clock ran out while they were underground.

| Session | Focus | What the Players Should Feel |
|---|---|---|
| 21 | Return to the surface. Mira's army has pushed through the Corridor. The Ironmarch's ambient aggression has spiked to unprecedented levels. Yrsa is holding Ironhaven but may be a Remnant now. Ostivaar's militarism is spiking as fragments converge on the Heart beneath the city. | "The clock ran out while we were underground." |
| 22 | Ostivaar in crisis. Reach the Heart of Iron beneath the capital. The approach through ancient tunnels, the Descent's Level 9 connection, or Dominion infrastructure. Rally allies: Yrsa's clarity, the Ember Guild's expertise, whatever alliances were forged in Acts I-II. | "Everything we built, everyone we met, it all comes down to this." |
| 23-24 | The final confrontation. Mira at the Heart. The Four Paths: Destroy, Crack Open, Weaponize, or the Fourth Way. No right answer. Every choice has genuine appeal and genuine cost. Let the players debate. Let them invent solutions the overview did not anticipate. | "There is no right answer, only our answer." |
| 25 | Aftermath and epilogue. What happened to the Ironmarch? To Ostivaar? To Mira? To Yrsa? To the Iron Companies, the Ember Guild, the people who live on a dead war-god's bones? The consequences of the choice, played forward. | "We chose. Now we live with it." |

**Key GM Decisions:**
- Who comes with them? Every major NPC the players befriended should have a role. Yrsa's clarity, Torren's expertise, Ashworth's intelligence, Keth's strength (if they helped Keth), Ironheart's forgeheart steel. This is the payoff for four acts of relationship-building. Missing allies are missing options.
- Mira at the Heart: she is not a boss fight. She is a person reaching for the only thing that has ever made sense to her. The PCs can fight her. They can talk to her. They can try to sever her connection to the fragments. If she touches the Heart, the convergence accelerates and the choice becomes urgent.
- The Four Paths: do not decide in advance which one the players will choose. Review the Campaign Overview for full details on each option's appeal and cost. Reward creativity. The best outcomes require knowledge gathered across the campaign.
- The epilogue: take your time. The players have spent 20+ sessions building relationships with this world. Show them what their choice means for the people they met. Let them say goodbye.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **The hardest part of Act V** is letting the choice be hard. If the players reach the Heart and the decision is obvious, something went wrong earlier. Review: Did you show Mira's humanity alongside her threat? Did you make Belara's imprisonment feel personal, not abstract? Did you give each option genuine appeal and genuine cost? The difficulty of the choice is the campaign's measure of success.

## Iron Tithe Tracking

The [[Cosmology and Magic#Iron Tithe|Iron Tithe]] is the Descent's most important mechanic, both narratively and at the table. It is what makes time in the Descent *cost something*.

### What to Take

The Tithe takes one precious thing per day spent inside the Descent. "Precious" means personally meaningful to the character, not mechanically valuable.

**Early levels (1-3):** Small sensory memories. A childhood smell. The taste of a favorite meal. The sound of rain on a specific roof. The way a loved one laughed. These losses are subtle. The PC may not notice immediately.

**Mid levels (4-6):** Emotional connections. The feeling of trust toward a specific person. The desire for a specific goal. The warmth of a specific memory. The PC knows something is missing but cannot name it.

**Deep levels (7-8):** Core identity. A bond, a flaw, an ideal. The PC's sense of who they are begins to erode. Replaced not with nothing but with Descent-flavored versions: ambition without purpose, drive without direction, strength without cause.

**Level 9:** The Tithe stops. At the Heart, there is nothing left to take. The PC either has enough of themselves left to choose, or they do not.

### How to Play Losses at the Table

1. **Name the loss.** Do not say "you lose a memory." Say "you reach for the memory of your mother singing and find silence."
2. **Let other PCs notice.** "She used to laugh at that joke. She does not laugh anymore."
3. **Track it visibly.** Keep a shared list of what each PC has lost. Cross things off. The visible record makes the cost tangible.
4. **Never take agency.** The Tithe takes memories, feelings, and connections. It never takes the ability to choose. Even at Level 8, the PC chooses, they just choose from a diminished sense of self.
5. **The Tithe returns.** If the party exits the Descent, lost things come back slowly, over days or weeks. Except at Level 8+: identity erosion from The Consuming requires the Mercy Gate or companion intervention to reverse.

## Between-Session Checklist

After every session:

1. **Advance Mira's timeline** one step for any session the players did not directly address her army's movement
2. **Update Iron Tithe tracking** if the party is in the Descent: what did each PC lose today?
3. **Note NPC reactions** to what the players did. Who heard? Who is angry? Who is grateful? Who is dead?
4. **Prepare two rumors** for next session: one about a thread the players are pursuing, one about Mira's advance or fragment migration elsewhere
5. **Check fragment convergence:** how close are the fragments to critical mass at Ostivaar? If the party is in the Descent, the surface situation worsens each session.
6. **Update [[Sessions/Ongoing Threads]]** with current status of each thread

## Common GM Questions

**"What if the PCs refuse to enter the Descent?"**
The Descent is not mandatory. The party can try to stop the fragment convergence from the surface: rally armies, destroy individual fragments, confront Mira directly. But surface solutions cannot reach Belara or the Heart of Iron. The fragments will converge. Mira will reach the Heart. Without the PCs in the Descent, the only question is what Mira does when she gets there. Make the surface approach viable but incomplete: the PCs can slow the convergence but not stop it.

**"What if a PC accepts the Mirror's offer in the Hall of Mirrors?"**
They live their perfect life in Gloryholme. They are not dead. The remaining party can attempt a rescue (re-entering Level 5, confronting the Mirror, offering something of equal value to break the bargain) or continue without them. The PC's player can run a temporary character or play out the perfect-life scenes as interludes between Descent sessions. Make the loss matter. Make the rescue costly.

**"What if they try to save Mira early?"**
Mira can be saved, but only if the PCs act before the end of Act IV. The key is severing her connection to the fragments, which requires either the Mercy Gate's influence, Yrsa's Morrhaelite insight, or knowledge from the Echo about how fragment-bonding works. Early attempts (Acts I-II) fail because the PCs do not yet understand what is happening to her. Mid-campaign attempts (Act III) can partially succeed, slowing her transformation, buying time. The full rescue requires Act IV knowledge applied before the Act IV climax.

**"What if the party splits on the Four Paths?"**
Let them argue. The debate is the point. If they genuinely cannot agree, the choice defaults to whoever acts first, which creates its own drama. A PC who reaches for the Heart while others are still debating forces the issue. This is not a failure state. It is the campaign's themes made visceral: ambition acts while mercy deliberates.

**"My players went to the Emberveil instead of the Descent."**
Good. The Emberveil offers forgeheart steel, Ember Guild expertise, and [[Brunhild Ironheart]]'s knowledge. These are strategic assets for the Descent. Let them prepare. Advance Mira's timeline while they are away. When they return, the situation is worse but they are better equipped. Do not punish preparation.

**"How do I handle the Hollowdeep crossover?"**
Level 6 of the Descent presses against [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s broken domain. If the party is also running [[Adventures/Campaigns/The Second Silence/Campaign Overview|The Second Silence]], this junction can be a literal crossover point. If not, the Hollowdeep junction is an emergency exit: violent, scarring, but survivable. Iron-laced undead patrol the border. Morrhaelite counteracts the Iron Tithe temporarily. The crossover should feel like escaping one nightmare into a different one.
