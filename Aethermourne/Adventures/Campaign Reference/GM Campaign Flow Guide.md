# GM Campaign Flow Guide

*Practical session-level guidance for running The Second Silence.*

This is the companion to the [[Villain Action Timelines]] and the [[Campaign Overview]]. Where those documents describe *what happens*, this one tells you *how to run it*: session pacing, transition hooks, and the practical questions a GM faces between sessions.

---

## How the Campaign Moves

The Second Silence has a natural geographic flow, but the players should always feel like they're choosing their path, not being funneled.

```
Act I: The Hollowed Reach (Sessions 1-8)
  ↓ Cinder connection discovered
Act II: The Ashen Dominion (Sessions 9-16)
  ↓ Shadow-root corruption revealed
Act III: The Verdant Marches (Sessions 17-22)
  ↓ Dead army erupts
Act IV: The Pale Wastes (Sessions 23-28)
  ↓ Prison breaks
Act V: Return to the Hollowed Reach → Abyssal Trench (Sessions 29-34)
```

**Each transition is a pull, not a push.** The players discover information that makes them *want* to go to the next region. If they resist, the [[Villain Action Timelines]] advance and the consequences catch up.

## Act-by-Act Session Guidance

### Act I: Signs and Portents (Sessions 1-8)

**Region:** [[The Hollowed Reach]]
**Pacing:** Slow burn. Build atmosphere before revealing scope.

| Session | Focus | What the Players Should Feel |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Arrival. First Black Tide event. Save people, see the impossible. | "Something is very wrong here." |
| 2-3 | Investigate Sleepwalkers. Visit [[Mistholm]]. Meet [[Lira Ashvane]]. | "This is bigger than one town." |
| 4-5 | Discover the [[The Veil Unbound\|Veil Unbound]]. Infiltrate or confront a cell. | "Someone is *causing* this." |
| 6-7 | The Trench Dive. See the prison. Feel Serith's awareness. | "Oh no." |
| 8 | Act I climax: major Black Tide + Veil Unbound ritual attempt. | "We stopped it, but the trail leads south." |

**Key GM Decisions:**
- How much does [[Lira Ashvane]] trust the party? She's their primary patron. Calibrate based on player behavior.
- The Veil Unbound cell: do the players infiltrate (longer, more information) or confront (faster, more combat, less intel)?
- The Trench Dive: this is the campaign's first "holy shit" moment. Don't rush it. Let the players sit with the scale.

**Transition Hook:** Evidence linking the Veil Unbound to the [[The Cinders|Cinders]] in the [[The Ashen Dominion|Ashen Dominion]]. Ashvane can't follow (Lantern-Keepers have no jurisdiction). The players must go alone.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> If the players don't take the Dominion lead, have the Veil Unbound attack them directly. Captured agents carry Cinder documents. The trail becomes impossible to ignore.

### Act II: The Fractured Crown (Sessions 9-16)

**Region:** [[The Ashen Dominion]]
**Pacing:** Pressure cooker. The walls close in. Every ally is surveilled.

| Session | Focus | What the Players Should Feel |
|---|---|---|
| 9 | Arrive in the Dominion. Feel the weight of theocratic order. Cinders start watching. | "We are not welcome here." |
| 10-11 | Contact the [[Elowen Greaves\|Unbound Congregation]]. Begin intelligence gathering. | "The rot goes deep." |
| 12-13 | Navigate Ostivaar politics. Gather evidence against [[Delric Mourne]]. Survive his countermeasures. | "We're being hunted by the people hunting us." |
| 14-15 | Seek audience with [[Hierarch Valdren III]]. The Ashite revelation. | "The gods aren't as dead as we thought." |
| 16 | Confront Mourne. His argument. The Dominion fractures. | "Was he... right about any of that?" |

**Key GM Decisions:**
- How paranoid is the surveillance? Scale to player caution. Careful players get more freedom. Reckless ones get Cinder raids.
- [[Delric Mourne]]: confrontation or escape? If he escapes, he becomes a recurring antagonist. If captured, he's the campaign's most unsettling informant. Both work.
- The Hierarch: does Valdren rally or collapse? This depends on how the players approach him. Empathy works. Force doesn't.

**Side Quests:** The [[Quest Board - Ostivaar]] runs concurrently. The Hollow Chamber, the Unbound Congregation's plea, the Graffiti Campaign, and the Ashmarket Poisoning all feed into the main thread. Let players pick which ones they pursue. Each reveals a different facet of the Cinder conspiracy.

**Transition Hook:** Mourne's intelligence (or captured records) reveals shadow-root corruption in [[The Verdant Marches]]. Serith is using [[The Twelve#Thyrea\|Thyrea]]'s root network. If the players don't go west, the Marches fall.

### Act III: The Wild Heart (Sessions 17-22)

**Region:** [[The Verdant Marches]]
**Pacing:** Journey structure. The deeper they go, the stranger it gets.

| Session | Focus | What the Players Should Feel |
|---|---|---|
| 17 | Cross the Scarwall. Encounter the Marchwardens. | "This land doesn't want us here." |
| 18-19 | The Marchwardens' Moot. Meet [[Kaelith Thornborn]]. Political negotiation. | "We need these people. They don't need us." |
| 20 | Investigate Kaelith's connection. Is it Thyrea or Serith? | "The truth matters more than the answer we want." |
| 21-22 | Journey to the Cradle. Shadow Root encounter. Purification. | "We carried the bones of dead gods to fight a living one." |

**Key GM Decisions:**
- How did the party cross the Scarwall? Their method determines their reputation with the Marchwardens for the rest of the act.
- [[Kaelith Thornborn]]: she's scared, powerful, and unsure who to trust. She is not a quest giver. She is a person who needs help and is suspicious of outsiders offering it.
- The Shadow Root fight: this should feel like surgery, not warfare. Precision matters. The land fights back.

**Pacing Note:** Act III is shorter (6 sessions). The campaign accelerates here. Don't let the Marches become a sightseeing tour. The corruption is spreading. There's a clock.

**Transition Hook:** The moment the Shadow Root is severed, something screams in the north. Word arrives: [[The Pale Wastes#Hollowdeep\|Hollowdeep]] has erupted. Organized undead armies march south. The war has started.

### Act IV: The Dead March (Sessions 23-28)

**Region:** [[The Pale Wastes]]
**Pacing:** Wartime. Urgent decisions, limited resources, impossible choices.

| Session | Focus | What the Players Should Feel |
|---|---|---|
| 23 | Arrive to devastation. [[The Pale Wastes#Lastlight\|Lastlight]] has fallen. Refugee crisis. | "We're too late to prevent this. We can only survive it." |
| 24-25 | Rally the Holds. [[Bjorn Ashken]], [[Thessa Ironveil\|Commander Ironveil]], the Remnant alliance decision. | "Who do we trust when we can't afford to trust anyone?" |
| 26-27 | Descend into the Hollowdeep. Morrhael's domain. Speak with the dead god. | "The Death God is not a monster. He is tired." |
| 28 | Sever the conduit. Escape the collapsing domain. The dead army breaks. | "We won. But the cost..." |

**Key GM Decisions:**
- The Remnant alliance: this is a moral litmus test. [[Revenant Yael]] and the sentient undead are essential allies, but the living fear and hate them. How the players handle this defines their leadership.
- Morrhael's request: he asks the PCs to end his existence when this is over. Do they promise? Do they mean it? This comes back in Act V.
- The war: run it as a backdrop, not a wargame. The players are special forces, not generals. They hear reports. They see consequences. They make targeted strikes, not campaigns.

**What's Happening Elsewhere:** While the players are in the Wastes, the villain timelines advance in other regions. The Veil Unbound makes progress on coastal seals. The Dominion continues its political crisis (if Mourne escaped). The Oldborn make their decision. Drop these as news reports between sessions to maintain urgency.

**Transition Hook:** Stars go out. The sea turns permanently black. Serith's prison shatters. Everything converges on the Hollowed Reach.

---

### Act V: The Second Silence (Sessions 29-34)

**Region:** [[The Hollowed Reach]] → The Abyssal Trench
**Pacing:** Crescendo. No rest. The world is ending in real time.

| Session | Focus | What the Players Should Feel |
|---|---|---|
| 29 | Return to the Reach. The Second Silence falls. Divine magic fails. Stars dark. Sleepwalkers speak in unison. | "This is it." |
| 30-31 | Gather allies. Combine divine remnants. Prepare the vessel. Say goodbyes. | "Everything we've built, everyone we've met, it all matters now." |
| 32-33 | The Descent into the Abyssal Trench. The prison. Serith's guardians. | "We are very small and very far from home." |
| 34 | Serith. The argument. The choice. The end. | "What do we believe?" |

**Key GM Decisions:**
- Who comes with them? Every major NPC the players befriended across the campaign should have a role. Ashvane's intelligence. Greaves's courage. Kaelith's connection. Yael's understanding. Ironveil's discipline. This is the payoff for four acts of relationship-building.
- Serith's argument: she talks before she fights. Her case is compelling. Let the players debate. This is not a cutscene. If a player is genuinely persuaded, that should matter.
- The ending: the Campaign Overview outlines multiple possibilities. Don't decide in advance. Let the players' choices determine it.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **The hardest part of Act V** is letting go of your preferred ending. You have spent 30+ sessions building this world. The players may choose to let Serith unmake it. If their reasoning is sincere and their choice is informed, respect it. The campaign's moral weight comes from real consequences, even apocalyptic ones.

## Between-Session Checklist

After every session:

1. **Advance villain timelines** one step for any thread the players didn't address
2. **Note NPC reactions** to what the players did. Who heard? Who's angry? Who's grateful?
3. **Prepare two rumors** for next session: one about a thread the players are pursuing, one about a timeline advancing somewhere else
4. **Check the [[Villain Action Timelines]]**: is any thread about to reach a point of no return? If so, give the players a warning signal next session.
5. **Update [[Sessions/Ongoing Threads]]** with current status of each thread

## Common GM Questions

**"My players won't leave the Hollowed Reach."**
Advance the Mourne timeline. The Dominion's collapse affects trade in the Reach. Refugee waves from the Ashen Dominion arrive. The consequences come to them if they won't go to the consequences.

**"My players went to the Pale Wastes before the Marches."**
Fine. The acts can run in different orders. The Shadow Root in the Marches grows worse without intervention (accelerate that villain timeline), and the Hollowdeep eruption may happen while they're already there (making it more personal). Adjust session numbers accordingly.

**"My players sided with Serith."**
This is a valid campaign outcome. If they genuinely believe the world should be remade, play it out. What does it look like to help a goddess end the world? Who tries to stop them? The campaign becomes a tragedy. Tragedies can be the best stories.

**"My players killed Mourne in session 12."**
Serith compensates. The Veil Unbound accelerates coastal operations. The Dominion crisis resolves differently (maybe better, maybe worse, but the campaign's antagonist is Serith, not Mourne). Mourne's death removes a fascinating NPC but opens other doors: who takes over the Cinders? Do they reform or fragment?

**"The Oldborn subplot feels disconnected."**
It connects in Act V. The Oldborn hold the key to Primordial magic, the Loom's Edge, and alternative solutions to the divine decay. If the players earned their trust, the Oldborn contribute crucial knowledge or even military support in the finale. If they didn't, the players face the endgame without Primordial options. The Unwritten Lands arc is the campaign's wildcard.

**"How do I handle the divine materials?"**
They're MacGuffins with thematic weight. Mechanically, they're whatever you need them to be. Narratively, each one represents a dead god's lingering gift: Ashite is order, Thyrea's Amber is life, Morrhaelite is rest, Yvenne's essence is endurance. When combined, they represent what the dead gods sacrificed, and what the players are fighting to preserve.
