# The Prophecy Fragments

*The gods spoke their last words. Someone should have been listening.*

This document contains prophecy fragments that can be distributed to players across the campaign. Each fragment is a piece of a larger picture. Individually, they're cryptic. Together, they reveal the shape of the endgame.

---

## How to Use This

**Distribution:** Give fragments as rewards for investigation, discovery, or divine communion. Sources include:
- Star-Reader observatories (astronomical readings)
- Hollow Ones (speaking before they collapse into ash)
- Dream sequences (after contact with divine sites)
- Ancient inscriptions (ruins, temples, the Rootways)
- [[The Twelve#Orenthas\|Orenthas]]'s excised fate-threads (Loom's Edge, Act V)
- Dying NPCs with divine connections

**Pacing:** Roughly 1-2 fragments per act. By Act V, the players should have enough to assemble the full picture.

**Presentation:** Write each fragment on a physical card or separate handout. Let the players arrange them. The order matters, but players discovering the order is part of the puzzle.

---

## The Fragments

### Fragment 1: Orenthas's Final Prophecy (Known)

*Source: Public record. Every scholar knows this. It's the starting point, not the mystery.*

> [!quote] The Last Words of Orenthas, God of Time
> *"What was woven must unravel. What was silenced will speak again. The bones of my siblings will not hold the sky forever. When the second silence falls, the choice will not be mine to make."*

**When to give:** Session 1 or the Player Primer. This is common knowledge. Every NPC can quote it. The question is what it means.

**What it reveals:** The Second Silence was foreseen. Orenthas knew the gods' deaths were not the end but a transition. The "choice" belongs to someone else, mortals.

---

### Fragment 2: Aelindra's Tear

*Source: Star-Reader archives. Found in observatories, Celestine Vael's research, or the Archive House in Tidecross.*

> [!quote] The Millennial Observation, circa 1000 AS
> *"A second silence shall fall when the stars forget their names. The weaver is gone but the loom remembers. What was cut away was not destroyed. It waits where time has no dominion."*

**When to give:** Act I, during Star-Reader investigation or after examining celestial anomalies.

**What it reveals:** The Loom of Fate is still active. Orenthas's excised fate-threads survived. They exist in a place beyond time, the Loom's Edge in the Unwritten Lands.

---

### Fragment 3: The Drowned Prayer

*Source: Recovered from a Sleepwalker's murmuring, transcribed by Lantern-Keepers. Or found inscribed on the walls of a Veil Unbound ritual site.*

> [!quote] Transcription, Sleepwalker Subject 14
> *"The prison was not punishment. It was patience. She counts the cracks and calls them doors. The jailer is forgetting why she holds the key. When the jailer weeps, the prisoner walks free."*

**When to give:** Act I, during Sleepwalker investigation or after the Trench Dive.

**What it reveals:** Serith's imprisonment was strategic, not punitive. Yvenne (the "jailer") is losing her grip, not through attack but through emotional erosion. Serith's escape depends on Yvenne's breakdown.

---

### Fragment 4: The Ashfall Inscription

*Source: Carved into the deepest level of Solvaen's Spine in Ostivaar. Discovered during the Hollow Chamber quest or deep mining exploration.*

> [!quote] Pre-Theomachis Inscription, Author Unknown
> *"Twelve thrones. Twelve graves. The fire god knew before he burned. He chose the pyre. Ask not why the sun died willing. Ask what he saw that made death preferable to dawn."*

**When to give:** Act II, during Dominion investigation. Particularly powerful after the Hierarch reveals that Solvaen's body still bleeds.

**What it reveals:** Solvaen's death in the Theomachis was *voluntary*. He foresaw something that made self-sacrifice the better option. This reframes the entire divine war: if the gods chose to die, what were they choosing over?

---

### Fragment 5: The Root-Song

*Source: Sung by Thyrea's Children (plant-creatures) in the deep Cradle. Or found carved into a Greensinger's staff. Requires natural communion or Primordial pact interaction to translate.*

> [!quote] The Cradle Chorus
> *"The mother's bones became the forest. The mother's voice became the wind. But the mother's last thought was not of growing. It was of the shadow in the roots that would come after. She left a seed. Not for the trees. For the ones who would walk beneath them."*

**When to give:** Act III, during the journey to Thyrea's Cradle or communion with the forest.

**What it reveals:** Thyrea foresaw Serith's shadow-root corruption and left a countermeasure: a "seed" hidden somewhere in the deepest Cradle. This seed is Thyrea's Amber in its purest form, the divine essence the players need.

---

### Fragment 6: The Death God's Lament

*Source: Spoken by [[Revenant Yael]] in the Pale Wastes. Or found inscribed on the walls of Morrhael's domain inside the Hollowdeep.*

> [!quote] The Last Breath of Morrhael
> *"I kept the gate between the living and the gone. I did not close it. I did not lock it. I held it open so that passage was gentle. Now the gate is broken and no one passes. The dead accumulate. The living do not understand what 'forever' means when there is no exit. I am sorry. I tried to tell them dying was not the worst thing. Not dying is."*

**When to give:** Act IV, when the players reach Morrhael's domain or speak with the death god's lingering consciousness.

**What it reveals:** Morrhael's death broke the cycle of death itself. Souls can't pass on. The undead crisis isn't Serith's doing alone, it's a consequence of the Theomachis that Serith is *exploiting*. Morrhael's grief is genuine. His request to be ended is not defeat but mercy.

---

### Fragment 7: The Counter-Prophecy

*Source: Found in the Loom's Edge, the Unwritten Lands. This is the hardest fragment to obtain, requiring the Oldborn Accord quest chain or deep Unwritten Lands exploration.*

> [!quote] Excised from the Loom of Fate
> *"The weaver saw two threads. One: the silence falls, the shadow rises, creation ends and begins again, and the new world is built on the old world's bones, just as before. Endless. Repeating. The cycle as designed. Two: the silence falls, the shadow rises, and mortals, for the first and only time, refuse. They hold the bones of dead gods and say: this is ours now. The loom breaks. Fate becomes choice. The weaver could not decide which thread to cut. So he cut himself instead."*

**When to give:** Act V, or late Act IV. This is the campaign's keystone revelation.

**What it reveals:** Everything. Orenthas saw two possible futures and couldn't choose between them. He removed himself (his "silence") so that the choice would fall to mortals. Serith's plan (restart the cycle) is Thread One. The players' potential to refuse and claim the world is Thread Two. The entire campaign has been about reaching this choice.

---

### Fragment 8: The Final Line

*Source: Serith speaks this directly to the players during the Act V confrontation. Or it can be found inscribed on the prison walls inside the Abyssal Trench.*

> [!quote] Serith, the Goddess of Shadow
> *"He wove both futures into the loom and then he stopped weaving. Do you understand? Your god of time gave you the one thing no god has ever given mortals: the right to decide what happens next. And you are using it to preserve a graveyard. I am offering you a garden."*

**When to give:** Act V, Serith's confrontation. This is not a handout. This is dialogue. But if the players have collected the other fragments, they will understand exactly what she means, and exactly why she might be right.

---

## The Assembled Picture

When all fragments are laid out in order, they tell this story:

1. Orenthas foresaw the Second Silence and chose not to prevent it
2. The other gods knew too, some chose to die willingly (Solvaen), some left countermeasures (Thyrea), some tried to hold the line (Morrhael, Yvenne)
3. Serith's imprisonment was part of the plan, the pause before the choice
4. The excised fate-threads in the Loom's Edge are Orenthas's gift: proof that an alternative to the cycle exists
5. The choice is genuinely open: restart (Serith's way) or refuse (the players' way)
6. There is no objectively correct answer

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **The prophecy system exists to earn the endgame.** If the players reach Act V without understanding the philosophical stakes, Serith's argument hits like a cutscene. If they've been piecing together the fragments for 30 sessions, it hits like a revelation they built themselves.
>
> **Don't explain the fragments.** Let the players theorize. Let them be wrong. Let them argue about meaning. The uncertainty is the point. Prophecy in Aethermourne is unreliable by design, the Loom has no weaver. The fragments are true, but truth without context is just poetry.
>
> **The missing fragment:** There is no Fragment 0, the prophecy that Orenthas suppressed, the one Serith discovered that started the Theomachis. It does not exist in any recoverable form. If players ask, tell them: "Some things were not excised. They were unmade." Whether this is true or whether Orenthas hid it in the Loom's Edge is a question the campaign does not answer. Leave it open. The best mysteries have no solution.
