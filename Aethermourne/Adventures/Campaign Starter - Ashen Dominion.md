# Campaign Starter: The Ashen Dominion

*Order is not justice. But in Ostivaar, order is all there is.*

---

## Overview

A ready-to-run 3-session arc for a campaign beginning in [[Ostivaar]], capital of [[The Ashen Dominion]]. The PCs witness an Ashite refinement ceremony go catastrophically wrong and are drawn into the investigation of whether it was accident or sabotage, uncovering the rot at the heart of the Dominion's most powerful institutions.

**Themes:** Theocratic oppression, conspiracy, the cost of order
**Tone:** Cold geometry and hidden rot. A perfect city built on a corpse.
**Level:** 1-3

---

## Session 1: The Ceremony

### Opening Monologue

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Ostivaar wakes the way it always wakes: in unison. Half a million voices speak the Dawn Prayer at the same moment, the words rising through the ribs of a dead god that arch overhead like the vaulting of an impossible cathedral. Pale light filters through veins of Ashite crystal threaded through divine bone, casting geometric patterns across streets laid in perfect grids.
>
> The city is beautiful the way a crystal lattice is beautiful. Perfect. Cold. And if you look too closely at the precision of it, at the way every angle divides by sacred numbers and every shadow falls in ordained patterns, something in your chest tightens. Not fear. Recognition. This is what order looks like when it stops serving people and starts consuming them.
>
> You stand in the Ashmarket, the commercial heart of the Dominion, surrounded by merchants selling grain and Ashite tools on strict schedules at regulated prices. The crowd moves in patterns that feel almost choreographed. And overhead, the ribs of [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]] curve like the bars of a cage you are standing inside.
>
> Today is a Refinement Day. The Church is purifying a new vein of Ashite from the Spine of Order in a public ceremony. The whole city has turned out. It is meant to be a celebration.
>
> It will not be.

### Structure

**PC Focus:** All PCs equally. Establish the oppressive atmosphere.
**Goal:** Ground the PCs in Ostivaar, trigger the Ashite contamination event, create immediate stakes.
**Threads Advanced:** Ashite instability, Cinder conspiracy, caste tensions.

### Scene 1: The Ashmarket

The PCs are in Ostivaar for their own reasons (trade, pilgrimage, mercenary work, running from something). The Ashmarket is bustling but controlled: specific goods at specific hours, prices regulated, foreign merchants taxed and watched.

- **Atmosphere:** Precise. Beautiful. Unsettling. The Hum from the Spine of Order is a subsonic pressure that makes creative thought feel effortful.
- **Information (free):** Refinement Day is a public ceremony where the Church purifies raw Ashite ore into usable crystal. It is both religious ritual and economic event. The Anointed caste performs the rites. The Hewn caste watches.
- **Information (DC 12 Perception):** Cinder agents are in the crowd. Grey coats, watchful eyes, noting who attends and who doesn't.
- **NPC Introduction:** [[Torren Hearthforge]] is in the market, examining Ashite tools at a vendor's stall with obvious contempt. If approached, he grunts about declining craftsmanship. "Used to be an art. Now it's a factory."

### Scene 2: The Refinement Ceremony

The ceremony takes place at the base of the Ashen Cathedral. Thousands attend. Church officials in geometric white robes stand around a refinement crucible the size of a wagon, filled with raw Ashite ore glowing pale blue.

**The Rite:** Chanting in mathematical cadences. The ore begins to crystallize, transforming from rough stone to perfect geometric crystal. The crowd holds its breath. The light intensifies.

**The Failure:**
- The crucible cracks. Not an explosion, worse. The Ashite does not crystallize; it *disperses*. A cloud of pale dust billows outward across the crowd.
- DC 13 Dexterity save to avoid the cloud. Those who fail: immediate headache, thoughts sharpening painfully, a compulsive need to count, to measure, to order.
- Within minutes, three Church officials near the crucible have collapsed. Their eyes are open. They are breathing. They will not speak. Their fingers trace geometric patterns on the ground, over and over.
- **This is Order Sickness**, accelerated. What normally takes months of Ashite exposure has happened in seconds.

**Immediate Chaos:**
- The crowd panics. Cinder agents attempt to establish a cordon.
- [[Commander Ashara Voss]]'s soldiers arrive within minutes, sealing the area.
- Church officials are shouting contradictory orders.
- A Hewn-caste woman screams that her child was in the cloud.

### Scene 3: The Aftermath

The PCs are caught inside the cordon. A Church official, [[Prior Gault Ashworth]], approaches them quietly.

**Ashworth's Request:**
- He saw the PCs help people during the chaos (or at minimum, not panic).
- He needs outsiders. "Everyone inside this cordon has a superior, a faction, a loyalty that is not to the truth."
- The refinement ceremony has been performed thousands of times without failure. This was not an accident.
- He wants the PCs to investigate. Quietly. Before the Cinders control the narrative.
- Reward: 100 gold now, more if they find answers, and his personal protection within the Church.

**Information (DC 14 Medicine, examining victims):** The Order Sickness symptoms are wrong. Normal exposure causes gradual rigidity. This is acute, as if the Ashite was *concentrated* somehow. The ore was tainted before the ceremony.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The contamination was [[Delric Mourne]]'s work. The Cinders introduced a reagent (stolen from the Church's own restricted stores) into the raw ore before the ceremony. The reagent amplifies Ashite's order-enforcement properties to dangerous levels. Mourne's goal: demonstrate that Ashite is unstable, erode public trust in the Church, and create a more docile population through fear. He did not expect the acute reaction to be this severe. He is improvising.

## Session 2: The Supply Chain

### Structure

**PC Focus:** Investigation-focused PCs lead. Social PCs handle faction navigation.
**Goal:** Introduce the three major factions through investigation, uncover the contamination source, raise the stakes.
**Threads Advanced:** Cinder corruption, Unbound Congregation, Order of the Ashen Flame.

### Scene 1: The Ore Trail

Following the Ashite supply chain from the Ashmarket to the Spine.

**Investigation Steps:**
1. **The Ashmarket Vendor** (DC 12 Persuasion): The ore came from [[Foreman Edda Haelstrom]]'s mines in Hearthstone, delivered by Church-authorized convoy. Vendor noticed nothing unusual.
2. **The Convoy Records** (DC 14 Investigation at Church archives): The convoy stopped overnight at a Church waystation outside Ostivaar. Standard practice. But the waystation's log shows a "security inspection" by Cinder agents that night, not on the official schedule.
3. **The Waystation** (if visited): Empty now. DC 13 Perception: chemical residue on the floor of the ore storage chamber. DC 15 Alchemy/Arcana: the residue is a reagent that amplifies Ashite's crystalline properties. This was introduced to the ore *after* mining, *before* the ceremony.

### Scene 2: The Cinders Close In

[[Delric Mourne]] is aware outsiders are asking questions. He does not panic. He redirects.

**Mourne's Move:** A Cinder agent approaches the PCs with a "tip": the contamination was the work of the [[Unbound Congregation]], specifically a radical cell operating in the Lower Ribs. He provides a name: Cassia Lorn. He provides an address.

**The Trap:** The address is real. Cassia Lorn is real. But she is not responsible. The Cinders have planted fabricated evidence in her dwelling (false alchemical equipment, forged correspondence with "foreign agents").

> [!warning]- GM Only
> This is Mourne's standard playbook: redirect investigation toward the Congregation, eliminate a troublesome organizer, and reinforce the narrative that reform movements are dangerous. If the PCs fall for it, Cassia is arrested and disappears into the Cinder Halls. If they investigate further before acting, the truth starts to emerge.

### Scene 3: The Unbound Congregation

Whether following the Cinder tip or their own investigation, the PCs reach the Lower Ribs.

**The Lower Ribs:** Cramped, ash-tainted air, six families sharing spaces the upper castes give to one. The Ashite glow from above doesn't quite reach. People here are angry, frightened, and suspicious of outsiders.

**Meeting Cassia Lorn:**
- Cassia does not trust the PCs. She meets them on her terms, in a location she controls, with exits she's mapped.
- She knows about the contamination. She's been tracking Ashless disappearances for months, people taken from the Lower Ribs by Cinder agents for unknown purposes. No one investigates because Ashless don't matter.
- She has dates, names, patrol patterns. The pattern points to Cinder operations, not Congregation activity.
- She will share information only if the PCs prove they're not Cinder assets. "Don't tell me what you believe. Tell me what you've done."

**Information (Cassia's evidence, if earned):** Cross-referencing her disappearance data with the waystation inspection reveals overlap. The same Cinder unit that "inspected" the ore convoy has been active in every area where Ashless have vanished.

### Scene 4: Meeting Elowen Greaves

If the PCs earn Cassia's trust, she can arrange a meeting with [[Elowen Greaves]], leader of the Unbound Congregation.

**Greaves' Safe House:** A different location every night. She meets them in a candlelit cellar beneath a Hewn-caste bakery.

**Greaves' Perspective:**
- The Congregation did not contaminate the Ashite. They lack the resources and the knowledge.
- She suspects the Cinders, but she has no proof. What she has is context: the Cinders have been escalating operations for months. More arrests, more disappearances, more provocateurs in protest crowds.
- She offers alliance: the Congregation's network in exchange for the PCs' freedom of movement. "You can go places we cannot. We can tell you where to look."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Greaves is genuine. She is also a former junior Cinder herself (recruited at the Athenaeum, decades ago, left when she saw what the organization was becoming). This makes her invaluable, she knows Cinder methods, and a target. She has told no one.

## Session 3: The Order Is a Lie

### Structure

**PC Focus:** The whole party. Moral choices and consequences.
**Goal:** Confront the conspiracy's first layer, force faction commitments, end on a cliffhanger.
**Threads Advanced:** Mourne's Serith worship, Ashworth's crisis of faith, political fracture.

### Scene 1: The Graffiti Trail

The phrase *"The order is a lie"* has been appearing across Ostivaar, on the Hierarch's gate, the Cathedral steps, inside sealed rooms. [[Commander Ashara Voss]] is investigating and the PCs' names have reached her.

**Voss's Proposition:**
- She does not trust the Cinders. She does not respect the Hierarch's indecision. She trusts competence.
- If the PCs have been competent, she offers resources: military escort, access to restricted areas, her personal confidence.
- She wants the graffiti artist found. More importantly, she wants to know what "the order is a lie" *means*.

**The Trail:** Each graffiti location is a coordinate. DC 15 Investigation (or DC 12 with Cassia's help): the locations, taken together, spell directions to a specific place beneath Ostivaar.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The graffiti artist is Cassia Lorn herself (a fact she did not share with the PCs). She is a former Cinder who discovered Mourne's shrine during a routine security sweep. Unable to report through Cinder channels (Mourne controls them all), she chose public disruption. The coordinates lead to Mourne's private shrine to [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]].

### Scene 2: Beneath the City

Following the coordinates leads to the tunnels beneath Ostivaar, the Cinder Halls' outer edges. The PCs must navigate past Cinder patrols (DC 13 Stealth per section, or creative alternatives).

**The Shrine:**
- A small chamber, stone corroded by entropic energy into shapes that hurt the eye.
- A fragment of Serith's prison-stone, dark and warm, sitting on an altar.
- Divination tools. Coded correspondence. Evidence that the head of the Cinders has been receiving instructions from the imprisoned goddess of shadow.
- DC 12 Arcana: the entropic corrosion is Serith's signature. This is not worship from afar. This is *communication*.

**Encounter: Cinder Guards (2, Nimble)**
- Elite operatives: Lvl 3 | Armor: M | HP 22 | Speed 6 | Str +2 | Dex +4 | Will +3 | 1d8 slashing (shortsword)
- **Tactical:** Fight in darkness, use smoke grenades, retreat if outmatched to raise alarm
- **Complication:** Killing them is easy. Capturing them alive is harder and far more valuable.

### Scene 3: The Evidence

The PCs have proof that [[Delric Mourne]] worships [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]. The question is what to do with it.

**Option A: Take it to Ashworth.**
- Ashworth is shaken to his core. His faith in the Church cracks, not his faith in [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]], but in the institution built in Solvaen's name.
- He can get the evidence to [[Hierarch Valdren III]], but warns: Mourne controls access to the throne. The Hierarch is effectively imprisoned.
- This path leads to Session 2's campaign arc: political confrontation, Mourne's escalation.

**Option B: Take it to Voss.**
- Voss is furious. Controlled fury. She trusted the Cinders as fellow servants of order.
- She can mobilize the Order of the Ashen Flame, but warns: moving against the Cinders without the Hierarch's authorization risks civil war.
- This path leads to a military confrontation arc.

**Option C: Take it to Greaves.**
- Greaves sees the opportunity: proof that the Cinders are corrupted could turn public opinion, legitimize the Congregation, and force reform.
- She warns: if the evidence becomes public before the Cinders are contained, Mourne will activate his contingency (assassinate the Hierarch, blame the Congregation, trigger martial law).
- This path leads to a political reform arc.

### Cliffhanger Ending

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You stand in the tunnels beneath Ostivaar, holding evidence that could topple the most powerful institution in the Ashen Dominion. Above you, half a million people speak the Evening Prayer in perfect unison, their voices rising through the ribs of a dead god.
>
> Somewhere in those tunnels, Delric Mourne sits in a room that smells of cedar and ink, calm and patient, receiving whispers from a goddess who has waited two thousand years and is content to wait a little longer.
>
> Somewhere in the Lower Ribs, Elowen Greaves sleeps in a different safe house than last night, dreaming of a world where order serves the people instead of consuming them.
>
> Somewhere in the Ashen Cathedral, Prior Ashworth kneels before a geometric altar and prays to Solvaen for guidance, and for the first time in his life, the certainty does not come.
>
> The order is a lie. You know it now. The question is what truth you will build in its place.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Where This Goes:** The PCs have kicked the first domino. Mourne is aware his shrine has been discovered (Cinder guards who escaped or failed to report in). He begins his contingency timeline. The PCs have days, not weeks, to decide who to trust and how to act. The Ashite contamination was the opening move. The endgame is the Dominion's collapse or transformation, and the PCs are now at the center of that choice.
>
> **Campaign Connection:** The Serith shrine reveals that her influence extends far beyond the Abyssal Trench. The contamination reagent's formula, if analyzed by [[Star-Readers]], connects to techniques described in pre-Silence documents. The Second Silence is not coming. It is already here, and the Dominion's foundations are its first casualty.
