# Campaign Starter: The Hollowed Reach

*The sea remembers what the living forget. And tonight, the sea is angry.*

---

## Overview

A ready-to-run 3-session arc for a campaign beginning in [[Tidewall]], capital of [[The Hollowed Reach]]. The PCs arrive during a Black Tide warning and are drawn into the escalating crisis between [[Serith]]'s growing influence, the [[Concord of Tides]]' willful denial, and the [[The Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keepers]]' desperate struggle to hold the coast.

**Themes:** Coastal horror, political complicity, the price of ignorance
**Tone:** Salt air and dread. Beauty rotting from below.
**Level:** 1-3

---

## Session 1: The Tide Turns

### Opening Monologue

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The harbor of Tidewall stretches before you, a crescent of white stone docks and blue-green tile rooftops climbing the slopes above. The busiest port on the continent. Ships from every nation crowd the moorings, and the Tidemarket's noise carries across the water like a living thing.
>
> But the flags are wrong. Every mast in the harbor flies the black pennant, the Black Tide warning. The dockworkers move too fast, hauling cargo with the tight efficiency of people who know a storm is coming. Above the harbor, the Concord Hall's windows blaze with lantern light despite the hour. And on the wind, beneath the salt and tar and fish, there is something else. Something that smells like deep water and dreaming.
>
> You have arrived in Tidewall. The sea is not pleased.

### Structure

**PC Focus:** All PCs equally. Establish the party dynamic.
**Goal:** Ground the PCs in Tidewall, introduce the Black Tide, create immediate stakes.
**Threads Advanced:** Black Tide escalation, Lantern-Keeper recruitment, Bilges unrest.

### Scene 1: Dockside Arrival

The PCs disembark into controlled chaos. Dockmaster [[Aldren Vayne]]'s officers are redirecting ships, enforcing the Black Tide protocol: no vessels leave harbor until the tide passes.

- **Atmosphere:** Organized fear. Dock crews hauling cargo double-time. Street vendors packing stalls. A Tidecaller priest on a crate, leading a prayer to [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]] that nobody stops to join.
- **Information (free):** Black Tides are supernatural storms, sea turns dark, creatures surface from the Abyssal Trench. Used to happen every few months. Now every few weeks.
- **Information (DC 12 Persuasion, dock workers):** The Bilges flooded last time. Concord promised repairs. Nothing happened. People are angry.
- **Information (DC 14 Streetwise, market gossip):** A preacher called "Salt" has been drawing crowds in the Bilges, ranting about a voice beneath the waves. Lantern-Keepers tried to arrest him. He vanished.

### Scene 2: The Bilges

The PCs seek lodging in the lower districts (the Coral Quarter is far beyond their means). The Bilges are narrow, damp, perpetually shadowed. Waterline marks on walls show previous flood heights.

**The Drowned Anchor** (tavern, run by [[Harren Coyle]]):
- Coyle is a retired smuggler who trades in stories, not coin. He sizes up the PCs immediately.
- Offers rooms in exchange for "a tale worth telling, or a favor worth owing."
- If asked about work: "Lantern-Keepers are hiring. That should tell you how bad things are."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Coyle knows every tunnel and sea cave beneath Tidewall. He is testing the PCs. If they seem competent and honest, he will become a recurring contact. If they seem mercenary, he charges coin and forgets their names.

### Scene 3: The Tide Hits

That night, the Black Tide strikes. Not a storm but a wrongness. The sea goes ink-dark. The air pressure drops. Every lantern in the Bilges gutters simultaneously.

**Encounter: Trench Crawlers (Nimble)**
- 3-4 pale, eyeless crustacean-things the size of dogs, skittering up from the flooding Bilges streets
- Lvl 1 | Armor: None | HP 12 | Speed 6 | Str +1 | Dex +3 | Will -2 | 1d6 slashing (pincers)
- **Weakness:** Bright light (damage dice roll twice, take lower). Fire kills them quickly.
- **Behavior:** Drawn to sound. Will swarm the loudest target.

**Complication:** A family is trapped in a flooding ground-floor dwelling. The water is rising fast, the door is jammed, and two Trench Crawlers are between the PCs and the building.

**Resolution:** Saving the family earns the Bilges' attention. [[Sergeant Bryn Kellar]] of the Lantern-Keepers arrives after the fight, impressed. He offers the PCs temporary work: shore patrol, cave surveys, "anything that doesn't require a politician's approval."

## Session 2: Whispers and Walls

### Structure

**PC Focus:** Investigation-focused PCs get spotlight. Social PCs shine in faction introductions.
**Goal:** Introduce the three major factions, deepen the Black Tide mystery, give the PCs a choice of allegiance.
**Threads Advanced:** Veil Unbound presence, Concord corruption, Star-Reader investigation.

### Scene 1: The Lantern-Keepers

Kellar brings the PCs to [[Veil Harbor]] for a briefing with [[Lira Ashvane|Commander Ashvane]]. The underground harbor is lit by phosphorescent algae and alchemical lanterns. Patrol boats bob at moorings, hulls scarred by things that don't leave clean marks.

**Ashvane's Briefing:**
- Black Tides accelerating. Sleepwalker incidents spiking. Something is changing in the Abyssal Trench.
- She needs people to investigate a sea cave on the Shattered Coast where [[Bryn Kellar|Kellar]]'s patrol found a stone altar that was not there last month. Warm to the touch. Salt crystals in geometric patterns.
- Pay is poor. Danger is real. She is honest about both.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Ashvane is testing the PCs, but she is also desperate. She is sitting on transcripts from [[Dren Halwick]] in Whisper-Cell Seven, where an entity speaks through the former fisherman and knows things it shouldn't. She will not share this yet. She needs to trust them first.

### Scene 2: The Concord's Offer

[[First Tidekeeper Maren Selk]] summons the PCs to Concord Hall (word of their Bilges heroism reached the Coral Quarter). The circular chamber has a reinforced glass floor over a living tidal pool.

**Selk's Offer:**
- The Concord is "concerned" about unrest in the Bilges. She wants the PCs to find the preacher called Salt and bring him in, quietly.
- Reward: 50 gold each, plus Concord trade licenses (valuable for any merchant-minded PC).
- Selk is genuine in her concern but compromised by years of pragmatic corruption.

**Information (DC 14 Insight):** Selk is afraid, not of the PCs but of someone else in the room. [[Cordell Raith]], Master of Secrets, stands in the corner, watching.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Raith is a [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] sympathizer acting from fatalism, not faith. He wants Salt silenced because Salt's ravings contain real intelligence about the Abyssal Trench prison. If the PCs take this job, Raith will have them followed.

### Scene 3: The Star-Readers

At the [[Archive of Tides]], [[Arch-Reader Celestine Vael]] approaches the PCs with a different perspective. The Star-Readers have been tracking the Black Tide frequency and correlating it with stellar observations.

**Vael's Request:**
- The Star-Readers' sealed vault has begun emitting light and sound. Pre-Silence star maps inside are updating themselves, showing stars that went dark reigniting in impossible patterns.
- She needs someone to enter the vault and document what's happening. Her scholars are too frightened.
- Reward: Research access and "answers to questions you haven't thought to ask yet."

**Information (free):** The stars are going dark. Have been for decades. The Star-Readers call it the Star Fade. The Church denies it. The Concord ignores it. But the sky is emptier than it was.

### Scene 4: The Cave (Lantern-Keeper Mission)

If the PCs take Ashvane's mission, they investigate the Shattered Coast sea cave.

- **Environment:** Tidal cave, waist-deep water at entrance, narrowing passages. Bioluminescent algae. The smell of deep ocean.
- **The Altar:** Smooth dark stone, warm. Salt crystals in geometric spirals. DC 13 Arcana: the patterns are not random, they are a language. DC 16 Arcana: it is a prayer. To something beneath the Trench.
- **Encounter:** 2 [[Sleepwalkers]] (former fishermen, eyes open, expression peaceful, hostile if the altar is disturbed). Lvl 2 | Armor: None | HP 18 | Str +1 | Dex +1 | Will +4 | 1d8 cold (touch). They whisper in unison: *"She is tired. Let her rest. Let the cage open."*

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The altar is a Veil Unbound focus point, placed here to amplify [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s whispers along the coastline. Destroying it is possible but triggers a psychic backlash (DC 14 Wisdom save or hear Serith's voice for one terrifying moment: calm, rational, and certain that what she offers is mercy).

## Session 3: The Voice Beneath

### Structure

**PC Focus:** The whole party. This session is a crucible.
**Goal:** Escalate the crisis, force a faction choice, end on a cliffhanger.
**Threads Advanced:** Serith's prison weakening, Veil Unbound exposure, political crisis.

### Scene 1: Salt Speaks

Whether the PCs sought him or not, Salt finds them. He appears at the Drowned Anchor, wild-eyed but lucid.

**Salt's Truth:**
- He was a fisherman. He Sleepwalked. He woke up, but he remembers.
- He heard Serith. Not madness, not metaphor. A voice, feminine, patient, *reasonable*.
- She told him the prison is failing. Not because she is breaking it, but because [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]] is tired. The jailer is losing her mind after 2,000 years of listening to her prisoner.
- Salt is terrified because Serith made *sense*.

**DC 12 Insight:** Salt is telling the truth as he experienced it.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Salt is not Veil Unbound. He is not compromised. He is a man who heard a goddess speak and cannot un-hear it. His preaching is an attempt to warn people. The Veil Unbound wants to recruit him. The Concord wants him silenced. The Lantern-Keepers want to study him. None of them are asking what he needs.

### Scene 2: The Second Tide

A second Black Tide hits, stronger than the first. The sea turns black to the horizon. The Tidal Bridges flicker, their ancient stone losing solidity for a heartbeat before stabilizing.

**Crisis Points (simultaneous, party must split or choose):**
1. **The Bilges are flooding again**, worse this time. Families need evacuation. Kellar is already there.
2. **The Archive vault is screaming**, a keening sound audible across the district. Vael is trying to seal it.
3. **Trench creatures are surfacing in the harbor**, larger than before. Something is pushing them up.

> [!tip] GM Pacing
> Let the PCs choose where to go. Every choice means something else goes wrong. This is the moment that defines their priorities.

### Scene 3: What Surfaces

In the harbor, something rises. Not a creature. A fragment of divine bone, the size of a coffin, white as starlight, inscribed with silver runes that flicker and fail. It bobs in the black water like a beacon.

**The Prison Fragment:**
- Warm to the touch. The water around it steams.
- Anyone touching it hears Serith's voice, one sentence, perfectly clear: *"The cage breaks. Not because I am strong. Because she is tired."*
- DC 15 Arcana: This is a piece of [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]]'s divine bone, the same material used to construct Serith's prison. It should not be here. The prison is not supposed to shed pieces.

**Faction Reactions:**
- **Ashvane** wants it secured in Veil Harbor for study. The Lantern-Keepers can learn from it.
- **Selk** wants it hidden. If word spreads that the prison is breaking, trade collapses.
- **Vael** wants it examined by the Star-Readers. It may connect to the vault anomaly.
- **The Veil Unbound** wants it. [[Cordell Raith]] will attempt to have it stolen that night.

### Cliffhanger Ending

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Black Tide recedes. The sea returns to grey. The Tidal Bridges solidify. Tidewall breathes again.
>
> But the prison fragment sits in whatever hands now hold it, warm and whispering. And in the Archive vault, the star maps have changed. Where darkness spread across the charts for decades, a single point of light has appeared, new, bright, and impossible.
>
> It is located directly above the Abyssal Trench.
>
> The Star-Readers have no explanation. The Concord has no comment. Commander Ashvane stands at the harbor rail, staring at the water, and says nothing at all.
>
> Something is waking up. The question is whether it is the prisoner, the jailer, or something else entirely.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Where This Goes:** The new star is [[The Twelve#Aelindra|Aelindra]] responding. The Goddess of Knowledge has seen the prison fragment surface and, for the first time in centuries, is paying attention to the mortal world. This is the first thread connecting the Hollowed Reach to the larger Second Silence campaign. The PCs now hold evidence that the divine order is failing, and every faction in Tidewall wants to control what happens next.
