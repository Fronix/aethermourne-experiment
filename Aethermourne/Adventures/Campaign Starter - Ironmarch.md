# Campaign Starter: The Ironmarch

*Everything here was built for war. The question is whose war, and when it starts.*

---

## Overview

A ready-to-run 3-session arc for a campaign beginning in [[Ironhaven]], largest city in [[The Ironmarch]]. The PCs arrive as the region teeters between two crises: [[Mira the Red]]'s challenge to [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Iron Thane Yrsa Bloodhand]], and the Warspire's accelerating war-essence output. Both are symptoms of the same disease: [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s fragments are waking up.

**Themes:** Ambition as corruption, the cycle of violence, what holds a society together when every institution is built for conflict
**Tone:** Iron and blood. The hum of divine weapons in the walls.
**Level:** 1-3

---

## Session 1: The Iron Thane's City

### Opening Monologue

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Ironhaven sits on a basalt plateau overlooking the Iron Fields, and at its center, the Warspire rises. Sixty feet of black divine iron, the largest fragment of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s body in western Ironmarch, humming with a frequency you feel in your teeth before you hear it with your ears. The hum is constant. The locals do not notice it anymore. You will.
>
> The city is built for function, not beauty. Stone walls, iron-reinforced gates, streets wide enough for cavalry. The smithing quarter glows with forge-light at all hours. The fighting pits draw crowds every evening, governed by the Code of Iron: fight fair, yield when beaten, and never strike a blow you are not willing to receive.
>
> But the Code is fraying. You can see it in the way the pit-fighters eye each other after the bout, the way the smiths work with clenched jaws, the way arguments in the market escalate faster than they should. The Warspire hums, and the city vibrates with it, and the vibration feels like anger looking for a target.
>
> [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Iron Thane Yrsa Bloodhand]] rules from the war-hall at the plateau's edge. She has held the title for eleven years, won it in single combat, and defended it three times since. Her rule is iron: fair, heavy, and unyielding. But a new challenger is coming.
>
> [[Mira the Red]] has ten thousand soldiers camped three days to the east. She claims Kaevroth speaks to her. Her eyes have turned iron-grey. And the Warspire's hum has tripled since she began her march.

### Structure

**PC Focus:** All PCs equally. Establish the Ironmarch's culture of coded violence and ambition.
**Goal:** Ground the PCs in Ironhaven, introduce the dual crisis (Mira's challenge + Warspire acceleration), create immediate stakes.
**Threads Advanced:** Warlord challenge, war-essence contamination, fragment migration.

### Scene 1: The War-Hall

The PCs seek work or audience with the Iron Thane. [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] receives them in the war-hall, a stone chamber decorated with weapons taken from defeated challengers. She stands, never sits, and measures the PCs with a fighter's eye.

- **Atmosphere:** Controlled aggression. Warriors line the walls. The Warspire's hum is louder here, resonating through the stone floor.
- **Yrsa's Assessment:** Blunt, efficient. She needs people who are not from the Ironmarch, who are not soaked in Kaevroth's war-essence, who can think clearly when everyone else is thinking with their fists.
- **Work Available:** Escort [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]] to the Warspire's base to place monitoring equipment (the last two teams came back fighting each other), or scout Mira's army positions for intelligence on her timeline.
- **Information (free):** Mira's formal challenge has not been delivered yet. When it comes, Yrsa must respond within three days per the Code of Iron. She is buying time.

**Information (DC 14 Insight):** Yrsa is in pain. Constant, controlled, never acknowledged. Something she wears beneath her armor is causing it. (The Morrhaelite shard she uses to resist Kaevroth's influence.)

### Scene 2: The Factor's Warning

[[Factor Gellen Ashworth]] of the [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] finds the PCs at their lodging (The Anvil's Rest, run by a retired pit-fighter named Hroth). Ashworth is a thin, precise man who smells of ink and anxiety.

**Ashworth's Intelligence:**
- The Warspire's war-essence output has tripled in six weeks. This is not natural fluctuation. The fragment is reacting to something.
- His instruments show a directional component: the Warspire's energy is reaching toward the east, toward the Heart of Iron buried beneath [[Ostivaar]] in the Ashen Dominion.
- If the fragments converge, the result is a mindless divine storm of pure war-essence, a Theomachis weapon without a wielder. Everything in its path would be consumed by rage.
- He needs monitoring equipment placed at the Warspire's 60-foot mark. The last two teams turned on each other within minutes of reaching the upper levels. He needs people who can resist the aggression effect.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Ashworth is the most clear-eyed person in the region and the least equipped to act on what he knows. He has been drafting evacuation plans for Ember Guild personnel. He believes Ironhaven has months, not years. He is right.

### Scene 3: The Fighting Pits

That evening, the PCs witness (or participate in) a bout at the fighting pits. The Code of Iron governs: clean fights, fair yields, honor in defeat. The crowd is large and tense.

**The Incident:**

Mid-bout, one fighter's eyes flare with iron-orange light. He refuses to yield. He fights past the point where the Code demands he stop, breaking his opponent's arm, then turning on the crowd. The Warspire's hum spikes.

**Encounter: War-Essence Berserker**

- 1 Contaminated Fighter: Lvl 3 | Armor M | HP 40 | Speed 6 | Str +4 | Dex +1 | Will -1 | iron fists 1d8+4 bludgeoning
- **War-Essence Rage:** Advantage on Str-based attacks. Cannot be reasoned with or frightened. Fights until unconscious.
- **Aggression Aura:** All creatures within 2 spaces make Will save DC 13 at the start of each turn or must attack the nearest creature (friend or foe) on their next turn.

**Resolution:** Subduing (not killing) the fighter earns Yrsa's respect. Castellan Drenn arrives to contain the situation and admits privately that this is the third incident this month. The Warspire's influence is getting worse.

## Session 2: The Herald and the Spire

### Structure

**PC Focus:** Combat-oriented PCs tackle the Warspire. Social PCs handle the political crisis.
**Goal:** Place the monitoring equipment, confront Mira's herald, deepen the fragment migration revelation.
**Threads Advanced:** Warspire acceleration, Mira's challenge formalized, Yrsa's secret burden.

### Scene 1: The Warspire Ascent

Ashworth briefs the PCs on the monitoring equipment: three devices that must be placed at intervals along the Warspire's exterior, culminating at the 60-foot mark. The ascent is physical (climbing the black iron surface) and psychological (resisting escalating war-essence exposure).

**The Ascent (Escalating Hazard):**
- **20-foot mark:** Mild aggression. Will DC 12 or snap at companions. Place first device.
- **40-foot mark:** Moderate aggression. Will DC 14 or argue violently about the best route. Str DC 13 climb check (the surface vibrates). Place second device.
- **60-foot mark:** Severe aggression. Will DC 16 or attack the nearest creature. The Warspire's surface is hot, 1d4 fire damage per round of contact. Place third device.

**Discovery at the Peak:** At 60 feet, the PCs can feel the Warspire's directional pulse. It is reaching east, straining toward something, like iron filings drawn to a magnet. DC 14 Knowledge: the fragment is trying to move. If it shifts even slightly, the plateau collapses.

**Encounter: [[The Ambitious Dead]]**

An Ambitious Dead has been climbing the Warspire for what appears to be decades, driven by an implanted ambition to reach the top. It is at the 50-foot mark, rusted into partial immobility, and its eyes burn with iron-orange light. It attacks anyone who obstructs its path.

- 1 [[The Ambitious Dead|Ambitious Dead]]
- Lvl 4 | Armor M | HP 50 | Speed 5 | Str +3 | Dex +0 | Will +4 | iron fist 1d10+4 bludgeoning
- **Ambition Fury:** Advantage and +2 damage for 3 rounds if obstructed.
- **Weakness:** Fulfilling its ambition (helping it reach the top) causes it to collapse peacefully.

> [!tip] Running This Encounter
> The Ambitious Dead is a moral choice, not just a combat one. It has been climbing for decades. Killing it is easy. Helping it reach the top is harder but more interesting: what does it do when it arrives? (It touches the Warspire's peak, whispers "I am here," and collapses into rust. Its ambition was to deliver a message. The message was already irrelevant centuries ago.)

### Scene 2: Mira's Herald

While the PCs are on the Warspire (or immediately after), Mira's herald arrives at the city gates under flag of parley. The herald delivers a formal challenge: single combat between Mira and Yrsa, the Warspire as the prize.

**The Herald:**
- Professional, polished, and subtly wrong. Her eyes have the same iron-grey tint as Mira's. She speaks with conviction that borders on compulsion.
- She is also scouting the city's defenses while everyone focuses on the challenge's content.
- DC 14 Perception: she is mapping gate positions and garrison numbers.
- DC 16 Insight: she believes what she is saying. Mira genuinely believes Kaevroth speaks to her. The herald has heard it too.

**Yrsa's Response:** She has three days to answer per the Code. She uses the time to plan. In private, she asks the PCs for something she has never asked anyone: help.

### Scene 3: Yrsa's Secret

In her private quarters, Yrsa removes her armor's gorget to reveal a Morrhaelite shard pressed against her collarbone. The skin around it is grey and cold, spreading slowly. She has been using the shard to resist Kaevroth's war-essence influence for years. It works. It is also killing her.

**Yrsa's Truth:**
- The shard replaces war-essence with death-essence. It keeps her mind clear but is slowly converting her living tissue to something else. If she continues wearing it, she will eventually become undead, a Remnant, immune to Kaevroth but no longer alive.
- She cannot remove it. Without it, the Warspire's influence would consume her in days. She would become like Mira: ambitious, driven, iron-eyed, and unstoppable.
- She needs an alternative. Something that can shield a mind from divine influence without killing the body.
- She also needs the PCs to attend the challenge as her seconds. If she falls, they must ensure the city does not fall with her.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Yrsa's dilemma has no clean solution in Act I. The PCs cannot cure her or find an alternative in three sessions. What they can do is buy time: researching Ashworth's data to find leverage, negotiating with Mira's camp, or discovering that Mira's connection to Kaevroth can be severed if the shadow-influence directing the fragments is identified. The thread leads to the broader Second Silence: Serith's weakening of the Aetheric Web is what allows the fragments to migrate and Kaevroth's residual will to reach living minds.

## Session 3: The Challenge

### Structure

**PC Focus:** The whole party. Politics, combat, and revelation.
**Goal:** Resolve the immediate challenge, reveal the fragment migration's true cause, end on a cliffhanger.
**Threads Advanced:** Mira's challenge, fragment migration, campaign arc launched.

### Scene 1: The Eve of Combat

The night before the challenge. Ironhaven is tense. The Warspire hums louder than anyone remembers. The fighting pits are empty, everyone conserving their aggression for tomorrow.

**Intelligence Gathering:**
- **Ashworth's Data:** The monitoring equipment shows the Warspire's output is directly correlated with Mira's proximity. As her army moves closer, the fragment reaches harder. Mira is not just hearing Kaevroth. She is amplifying him.
- **Mira's Camp (if scouted):** Her soldiers are disciplined, well-supplied, and subtly wrong. They do not argue. They do not complain. They train with a focus that looks like devotion but feels like compulsion. DC 14 Insight: they are not free. The war-essence has them.
- **[[Sira Embersteel]]** (master weaponsmith, if consulted): She has been researching methods to forge Kaevroth's Iron without contamination. She has a prototype: a blade that channels war-essence outward rather than absorbing it. It is not ready. It might never be. But if Yrsa carried it into the challenge, it could redirect the Warspire's influence away from both combatants for the duration of the fight.

**The Prototype:**
- Sira needs a final material: a stabilizing agent that can anchor divine energy. Ashworth suggests Forgeheart Steel from the Emberveil, but there is none in Ironhaven. However, Yrsa's Morrhaelite shard could serve the same purpose, death-essence as a counterweight to war-essence.
- Using the shard means Yrsa loses her protection against Kaevroth's influence. She would have minutes of clarity before the war-essence floods her mind.
- The PCs must decide: give Yrsa the prototype (buying clarity for the fight but removing her long-term protection) or let her fight with the shard (protected but slowly dying).

### Scene 2: The Challenge

Dawn. The fighting pit is cleared. The entire city gathers. Mira arrives with an escort of iron-eyed soldiers.

**Mira the Red:**
- Young, charismatic, burning with purpose. Her eyes are solid iron-grey. When she speaks, the Warspire pulses in rhythm with her words.
- She is not evil. She is consumed. Kaevroth's residual will has found a channel, and Mira's ambition provided the opening. She genuinely believes she is the Ironmarch's savior.
- She fights with divine precision: war-essence guiding her strikes with inhuman efficiency.

**The Duel:**

This is Yrsa's fight, not the PCs'. Their role is seconds: ensuring fairness, watching for interference, and intervening only if the Code is broken.

- **If Yrsa carries the prototype:** She fights with clarity, matching Mira's divine precision with decades of experience. The fight is even. It comes down to the final exchange.
- **If Yrsa keeps the shard:** She fights well but is physically compromised. The death-essence makes her slower. Mira has the advantage.

**The Warspire Incident:**

Mid-fight, the Warspire pulses. A wave of raw war-essence washes over the arena. Every person present makes Will DC 14 or is overwhelmed by aggression. The crowd surges. The Code breaks.

**Encounter: Chaos**

The PCs must protect Yrsa and Mira from the crowd, prevent a riot, and contain the Warspire's influence.

- Crowd: Treat as environmental hazard. 1d6 bludgeoning per round to anyone in the press. DC 13 Str to push through.
- 4 War-Touched Citizens: Lvl 1 | Armor None | HP 15 | Speed 6 | Str +2 | Dex +1 | Will -1 | improvised weapon 1d6+2 bludgeoning. Fighting anything that moves.
- **Resolution Condition:** Knocking Mira unconscious or moving her away from the Warspire (6+ spaces) reduces the pulse. The crowd calms within 1 round of Mira's removal.

### Scene 3: The Aftermath

The challenge is unresolved. Yrsa is alive but wounded. Mira is unconscious (or restrained). The Warspire's hum has not returned to normal, it is louder than before, and the directional pulse toward the east is now visible as a faint shimmer in the air.

**Ashworth's Revelation:**

The monitoring data confirms what the PCs suspected:

- The fragments are migrating toward each other. Not physically (yet) but energetically, their war-essence fields reaching across hundreds of miles.
- Mira is not causing this. She is a symptom. Something has weakened the barriers that kept the fragments dormant and separate. Something is draining the Aetheric Web, the divine network that maintained balance between all god-remnants on the continent.
- If the fragments converge, the result is catastrophic: a divine storm of pure war-essence that would consume the Ironmarch and beyond.

### Cliffhanger Ending

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Yrsa sits in the war-hall, armor removed, the grey skin around her Morrhaelite shard spreading visibly. Ashworth's instruments tick and hum on the table. Mira is confined in the lower cells, sedated, her iron-grey eyes still open, still staring east.
>
> "The fragments are reaching for each other," Ashworth says, laying out his charts. "Something is letting them. The network that kept them apart is failing. And whatever is causing that failure is not here. It is not in the Ironmarch at all."
>
> He traces a line on his chart. East. Past the Ashen Dominion. Past the Hollowed Reach. Into the ocean.
>
> "Something at the bottom of the Abyssal Trench is draining the energy that keeps the divine remnants stable. If it succeeds, every fragment of every dead god on this continent wakes up. Not as gods. As weapons. Mindless, uncontrolled, and devastating."
>
> Yrsa stares at the chart. At the ocean. At the point where everything converges.
>
> "Then we need to talk to the people who live above it," she says. "We need the Hollowed Reach."
>
> Below, in the cells, Mira whispers in her sleep. The words are not hers. They are old, and hungry, and patient: *"Let them come together. Let the iron sing. The war that ended the gods was only the first movement."*
>
> The Warspire hums. The city vibrates. And somewhere east, something ancient smiles.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Where This Goes:** The fragment migration connects the Ironmarch directly to the continental crisis. [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s weakening of the Aetheric Web is allowing Kaevroth's fragments to reach for each other, a process that will culminate in a catastrophic convergence if not stopped. Mira is a channel for Kaevroth's residual will, amplified by Serith's interference. The thread leads east, toward the Abyssal Trench and the prison that is the source of everything.
