# Campaign Starter: The Pale Wastes

*The dead outnumber the living here. They always have. The difference is that now, the dead are organized.*

---

## Overview

A ready-to-run 3-session arc for a campaign beginning in [[Greyhold]], heart of [[The Pale Wastes]]. The PCs arrive to find a frontier community running on stubbornness and cremation fire, and are drawn into the truth that has haunted the Vigil for twenty years: the [[The Pale Wastes#Hollowdeep|Hollowdeep]] silence is not peace. It is preparation.

**Themes:** Survival horror, the politics of triage, what makes someone a person
**Tone:** Frozen grey and cremation orange. Grim people doing necessary things.
**Level:** 1-3

---

## Session 1: The Quiet Has Teeth

### Opening Monologue

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Greyhold squats on the tundra like something that refuses to die. Grey stone walls, low and thick, designed not for beauty but for survival. The Pyre Ground burns at the city's center, fire that has not been extinguished in two hundred years, fed by bone and duty. Every building faces inward, toward the fire. Every wall faces outward, toward the cold.
>
> You have traveled the South Road from Ember's Rest, or descended from the Ashen Dominion's northern passes, and the cold has been building for days. Not weather-cold. Something deeper. A chill that settles in the marrow and whispers that warmth was always temporary.
>
> The guards at Greyhold's gate are professional and exhausted. They check your gear for Morrhaelite contamination, ask if you have touched any divine bone in the last week, and wave you through with the same warning they give everyone: "Stay near the fires. Do not go north alone. If you see something burning with orange light in the dark, run."
>
> The Pyre Ground's flames cast long shadows across the stone. Somewhere in the Hall of Names, a mason is carving fresh letters into the limestone wall. There are always fresh letters.

### Structure

**PC Focus:** All PCs equally. Establish the Wastes' tone and rules.
**Goal:** Ground the PCs in Greyhold, introduce the Vigil and the Remnant question, create immediate survival stakes.
**Threads Advanced:** Hollowdeep army intelligence, Frostmere disappearance, Remnant politics.

### Scene 1: The Hall of Names

The PCs seek [[Bjorn Ashken]], Holdwarden of Greyhold. They find him in the Hall of Names, standing before a fresh section of wall, watching a mason carve. The wall stretches in every direction, thousands of names, the Wastes' memorial to every soul lost to the cold, the dead, and the bone-pull.

- **Atmosphere:** Sacred quiet. Cremation smoke. The sound of chisel on stone.
- **Bjorn's Assessment:** He is blunt. The Wastes need hands. He does not care where the PCs came from or why. He cares whether they can follow orders and survive the cold.
- **Work Available:** Shore up Bonecairn's Ward Ring (maintenance run, supposedly routine), escort a supply caravan to Lastlight, or investigate why Frostmere has missed its last two scheduled dispatches.

**Information (free):** The Hollowdeep has been "quiet" for twenty years. The Vigil watches. Nothing moves. Bjorn's tone when he says "quiet" makes it clear he does not believe his own word.

**Information (DC 12 Persuasion):** A bone-carved message arrived last month. Three words: *"Merren Vask lives."* Merren was one of fifty-four scouts sent into the Hollowdeep. None returned. Until now.

### Scene 2: The Remnant

On the way to their lodging (The Bone and Brand, Greyhold's only inn), the PCs encounter [[Revenant Yael]] outside the gate. She is not permitted inside Greyhold's walls between dusk and dawn, city ordinance. She stands in the frost, patient, wearing Vigil armor maintained with a soldier's discipline. Her skin is pale blue. Her eyes glow blue-white. She has been dead for thirty years.

- **Yael's Introduction:** Polite, formal, slightly amused by the PCs' discomfort. She was a Vigil captain in life. She died defending Lastlight. She woke up. She does not know why some dead rise with their minds intact and others rise hungry.
- **Yael's Request:** She needs an escort to the Holdwardens' Council at the next Great Burning. Two Holds have vowed to destroy her on sight. She has something to say that the living need to hear.
- **The Question:** "I am dead. I know this. But I remember everything. I love the people I loved in life. I fear the things I feared. If that is not a person, what is?"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Yael is genuine. Her cause is just. She is also, unknowingly, carrying a dormant connection to [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], who whispered *"You are useful. Go back"* during her three days between death and rising. If Serith activates that connection, Yael's autonomy could be stripped. This does not make her a villain. It makes her a tragedy waiting to happen.

### Scene 3: Frostmere's Silence

Bjorn assigns the PCs to investigate Frostmere (the other tasks go to Vigil regulars). The journey is two days across open tundra.

**The Journey:**
- Day 1: Uneventful but oppressive. The aurora pulses overhead. The tundra is flat and featureless. The cold is relentless.
- Night 1: A Hollowcall manifests at the edge of camp. A voice from the dark, familiar, saying the PC's name. Saying: *"You look tired."* (Will DC 15 to recognize the illusion. No combat. The voice leaves if challenged directly.)

**Frostmere:**
The Hold is intact. Hearths cold. Food on tables, untouched. Eight hundred people, gone. Tracks in the frost lead north, toward the Marrow Fields. Orderly rows. Not a stampede. A procession.

- **Investigation (DC 12 Perception):** The tracks show no struggle. Everyone walked in formation. Children's footprints beside adults'.
- **Investigation (DC 14 Knowledge):** The Ward Ring around Frostmere has been drained. Not broken. Drained. Something pulled the necromantic energy out of the divine bone wards like drinking through a straw.
- **Discovery:** [[Nara Frostmere]]'s personal effects, left on her desk. A half-written letter: *"The shapes under the ice are more active. I have asked Lastlight for reinforcement. No response. If you are reading this and I am not here..."* It ends there.

**Encounter: Frostbound Hungers**

As the PCs investigate, a pack of [[Waste Horrors#Frostbound Hunger|Frostbound Hungers]] closes in, drawn by the warmth of living bodies in a dead settlement.

- 3 Frostbound Hungers
- Lvl 4 | Armor M | HP 45 | Speed 8 | Str +3 | Dex +3 | Will +1 | frost-bite 1d8+3 cold + necrotic infection
- **Pack Coordination:** Advantage on attacks when 2+ are within 3 spaces.
- **Necrotic Infection:** Str DC 13 or infected (1d4 cold/day, death in 3 days, rises as Hunger).
- **Weakness:** Fire ignores armor. Radiant deals double and cures nearby infection.

**Resolution:** Surviving the pack and returning to Greyhold with evidence of Frostmere's fate establishes the threat as real and immediate. Bjorn's face when he reads Nara's letter is the most emotion the PCs will ever see from him.

## Session 2: The Living and the Dead

### Structure

**PC Focus:** Social and investigative PCs. Moral dilemma at the center.
**Goal:** Deepen the Remnant question, introduce the Vigil's intelligence problem, force the PCs to choose sides.
**Threads Advanced:** Yael's appeal, Vask's survival, Hollowdeep reconnaissance.

### Scene 1: The Vigil

[[Thessa Ironveil]] arrives in Greyhold from [[Lastlight]], gaunt and driven. She has seen the Frostmere report and needs to brief the PCs privately.

**Ironveil's Intelligence:**
- Merren Vask is alive. The bone-carved message is genuine. Vask was one of her scouts, the best, and she is trapped inside the Hollowdeep crater periphery.
- The Hollowdeep is not dormant. It is an army. Twelve battalions, roughly five hundred each, drilling in formation. Siege structures facing south. A bone gate nearly complete.
- Something near [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s skull is giving orders. Not Morrhael. Something else.
- Ironveil needs a team to reach the crater rim, make contact with Vask, and extract her. The intelligence Vask carries could determine whether the Holds survive the year.

**The Ask:** Will the PCs volunteer for the reconnaissance mission? Ironveil is honest: the survival rate for Hollowdeep operations is zero. She has lost fifty-four scouts. She is asking the PCs because she has no one left.

### Scene 2: Yael's Case

Yael requests the PCs escort her to a private meeting with Bjorn. The Holdwarden has agreed to hear her, but not publicly, not yet.

**Yael's Argument:**
- The Remnants number several hundred across the Wastes. They retain intelligence, memory, empathy. They are people.
- They can sense undead at range. They can operate in the cold without supplies. They could scout the Hollowdeep where the living cannot survive.
- She asks for legal recognition and integration into the Holds' defense. In exchange, the Remnants will fight.

**Bjorn's Dilemma:** He believes Yael. He knows the Remnants could save lives. But two Holdwardens (traditionalists) have threatened to withdraw from the Council if the dead are given standing. In a crisis that requires unity, he cannot afford to lose allies.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The PCs' position on the Remnant question shapes the campaign. Supporting Yael gains invaluable scouts but fractures the Council. Opposing her preserves unity but loses critical intelligence assets. There is no clean answer. That is the point.

### Scene 3: The Bone-Storm

A bone-storm rolls in from the north, unannounced and unseasonal. Shards of divine bone carried on winds that cut like razors. The Holds seal their gates and wait.

During the storm, the PCs hear something in the wind: whispers. Not the Hollowcall's personalized voice but something broader, tactical, like overhearing a commander's orders through a wall. Fragments: *"...southern approach ready... Ward Ring neutralized... the Pyre Ground... midwinter..."*

- **DC 14 Perception:** The whispers are coordinated, not random. They are communications.
- **DC 16 Knowledge:** These are military commands. Someone is using the bone-storm as a communication medium. The commands reference the Great Burning at midwinter, when all Holdwardens gather at the Pyre.

**Encounter: Bone-Wraith**

The storm delivers something. A [[Waste Horrors#Bone-Wraith|Bone-Wraith]] materializes on Greyhold's outskirts, walking through the storm toward the Hollowdeep with mechanical purpose.

- 1 Bone-Wraith
- Lvl 6 | Armor H | HP 70 | Speed 4 | Str +2 | Dex +3 | Will +4 | shard-lance 2d6+3 necrotic (4 space range)
- **Death-Pull Aura:** Will DC 15 or incapacitated (4 space radius). Silence Field kills verbal spells.
- **Inexorable March:** Cannot be stopped or slowed. Moves 4 spaces toward Hollowdeep every turn regardless.

> [!tip] Running This Encounter
> The Bone-Wraith is not meant to be defeated by level 1-3 PCs. It is meant to be survived. The PCs need to get the Vigil patrol out of its path, protect civilians, and observe. Destroying it is possible but requires radiant damage the party may not have. The encounter teaches the players what they are up against.

## Session 3: The Rim

### Structure

**PC Focus:** The whole party. Survival and revelation.
**Goal:** Reach the Hollowdeep rim, witness the army, recover critical intelligence, end on a cliffhanger.
**Threads Advanced:** Hollowdeep army confirmation, Serith's role revealed, campaign arc launched.

### Scene 1: The March to Lastlight

Ironveil leads the PCs and a small Vigil escort north to [[Lastlight]]. The journey takes a day. The tundra grows colder. The aurora overhead shifts from green-silver to grey-blue. The bone-pull is a constant pressure, a whisper at the back of the mind: *stop. rest. let go.*

**Lastlight:**
A ridge-settlement built on the reverse slope of the Hollowdeep crater rim. Watchtowers with Morrhaelite lenses. A garrison of forty Vigil soldiers who have been staring into the crater for months. They are professional, haunted, and deeply glad to see reinforcements.

The Watcher's Archive contains decades of observation logs. Recent entries document:
- Military formations drilling in shifts
- Siege structures facing south
- A figure on the crater rim using Vigil field signals (Scout-Captain Edda Voss, KIA fourteen months ago)
- The skull at the crater's center pulsing with rhythmic energy

### Scene 2: The Crater Rim

Ironveil takes the PCs to an observation post. They look into the Hollowdeep.

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The crater is vast. Miles across. The far rim is a grey line against a grey sky. Below, on the crater floor, the dead stand in formation. Thousands of them. Row after row, battalion after battalion, motionless and waiting. Not the shambling dead of nightmare. Soldiers. Standing at attention. Armor rusted but maintained. Weapons held at rest.
>
> At the crater's center, something enormous and pale: [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s skull, the size of a hill, eye sockets dark, jaw frozen mid-scream. It pulses. Not with light. With intention. Like a heartbeat.
>
> Near the southern rim, a structure: a gate. Iron-reinforced bone, thirty feet tall, nearly complete. It faces south. It faces you.
>
> And on the rim, two hundred yards away, a figure stands in Vigil armor. It raises a hand. Field signals. Vigil field signals.
>
> *Approach permitted. Observation sanctioned.*
>
> They are letting you watch. They want you to know.

### Scene 3: Vask

If the PCs respond to the field signals and approach carefully (Dex DC 13 stealth to avoid patrols), they find [[Merren Vask]] in a hidden crevice near the rim. She has been surviving for eighteen months on scavenged supplies and the fact that the dead seem to tolerate her presence.

**Vask's Intelligence:**
- The army numbers at least six thousand, possibly more. She has mapped patrol routes and timing gaps.
- The siege gate is nearly complete. It faces south. The army marches within the year.
- A shadow figure near the skull gives orders. Not Morrhael. Something external, projecting through the death-domain.
- Scout-Captain Edda Voss is dead but functional. She uses Vigil protocols. The dead are learning from their absorbed personnel.
- The Great Burning at midwinter is the target. When the Holdwardens gather at the Pyre, the army moves.

**Vask's State:** Exhausted, frost-bitten, running on duty. She died once already (killed in a skirmish, rose as a Remnant, kept going). She has not told anyone this. If the PCs notice (Perception DC 14: her breath does not frost, her skin is too pale, she does not shiver), she admits it quietly: *"I am dead. I did not stop being a scout."*

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Vask as a Remnant is the strongest argument for Yael's cause. A dead woman who continued her mission because duty mattered more than death. If the PCs bring her home and her nature is revealed, the Remnant debate shifts permanently.

### Cliffhanger Ending

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You descend from the rim with Vask and her intelligence. The crater watches you go. Edda Voss's figure on the far rim signs one last message in Vigil field-code:
>
> *See you at midwinter.*
>
> The march back to Lastlight is silent. Ironveil reads Vask's report, page after page, her face going from stone to something worse than stone: certainty. She looks at the PCs and says:
>
> "Six thousand dead. A siege gate. They are coming for the Pyre while every Holdwarden in the Wastes stands in one place. And something that is not Morrhael is directing them."
>
> She pauses. The aurora overhead shifts to grey-blue, the color of deep ocean, the same color the Greensingers would recognize, the same color the Star-Readers have seen in their failing telescopes.
>
> "We need to warn everyone. Not just the Holds. Everyone. Because whatever is controlling that army is not in the crater. It is somewhere else. Somewhere far away. And it has been planning this for a very long time."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Where This Goes:** The "something else" controlling the army is [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], projecting through [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s broken death-domain from her prison in the Abyssal Trench. Ironveil's warning connects the Pale Wastes directly to the continental crisis. The PCs now carry intelligence that every faction needs: the army's size, its target, its timeline, and the fact that its commander is external. The thread leads south, toward the ocean, toward the Hollowed Reach.
