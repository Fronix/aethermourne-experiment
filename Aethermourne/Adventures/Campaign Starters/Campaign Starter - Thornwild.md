# Campaign Starter: The Thornwild

*Everything grows. Everything decays. Everything changes. The only question is whether balance can hold.*

---

## Overview

A ready-to-run 3-session arc for a campaign beginning in [[Cyclethorn]], the central settlement of [[The Thornwild]]. The PCs arrive as the Cycle, the endless rhythm of life and death that defines the region, is accelerating beyond anything the [[The Cyclekeepers|Cyclekeepers]] have recorded. Harvesters are going missing, the [[The Threshold (Thornwild)|Threshold]] is narrowing, and something in the [[The Bonewood|Bonewood]] is pulling both forces toward itself.

**Themes:** Life-death tension, ecological horror, the cost of balance
**Tone:** Beauty and horror coexist. Growth that doesn't stop is as terrifying as death that doesn't stay. The question is whether balance can hold.
**Level:** 1-3

---

## Hooks

> [!info] Hook 1: Hired Escort
> [[Garret Boneharvest]] or Alchemist Drue hired the PCs to escort a cycle-material shipment into the Thornwild. The pay is good because the route is dangerous, and it gets worse when they arrive during a crisis. Garret meets them at [[Cyclethorn]] and immediately puts them to work.

> [!info] Hook 2: Exile's Refuge
> One or more PCs are exiles, from factions, from homelands, from lives that no longer fit. They've heard the Thornwild takes in people nobody else wants. They arrive at Cyclethorn seeking the [[The Cyclekeepers|Cyclekeepers]] and find a community balanced on a knife's edge.

> [!info] Hook 3: Scholarly Investigation
> A [[Star-Readers|Star-Reader]], [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] patron, or faction contact sent the PCs to investigate the Thornwild's accelerating Cycle. They arrive with questions and instruments, and discover that the answers are more urgent and more dangerous than anyone outside suspected.

> [!info] Hook 4: Remnant Connection
> A PC is a Remnant, or travels with one, seeking [[Ashbloom]] where the dead feel alive again. They pass through Cyclethorn on their way south and get drawn into the crisis before they can leave. The Cycle's acceleration threatens the very restoration they came to find.

## Session 1: Arrival in Cyclethorn

### Opening Monologue

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The trail breaks through the treeline and the world goes wrong. To your left, the forest is a cathedral of green: vines thicker than your arm, flowers the size of dinner plates, air so thick with pollen it glows golden in the slanting light. To your right, the same forest stands skeletal, bone-white trunks stripped bare, bark like parchment peeling in a wind that smells of cold stone and dry earth.
>
> Between them, a band of chaos. Trees half-alive and half-dead, leaves sprouting on one branch while another crumbles to dust. A bird takes flight from a living bough, crosses an invisible line, and falls, feathered and still, into the undergrowth. Something small and pale rises from where it landed, shakes itself, and scurries away on legs that should not work.
>
> Ahead, on a granite outcrop that juts above the madness like a fist, sits Cyclethorn. Stone buildings, vine-threaded walls, smoke rising from chimneys. A town that has learned to exist in the space between a heartbeat and a funeral. And today, that space is shrinking. You can see it from here: the green is pressing closer on one side, the white on the other, and the band of chaos between them is narrower than it should be.
>
> Welcome to the Thornwild. Everything here is alive. Everything here is dying. Usually both at the same time.

### Structure

**PC Focus:** All PCs equally. Establish the party dynamic and ground them in the Cycle.
**Goal:** Make the Cycle a visible, physical force. Introduce the three key NPCs. End with a mission.
**Threads Advanced:** Cycle acceleration, missing harvesters, faction introductions.

### Scene 1: The Cycle in Action

The PCs arrive during a visible Cycle event. Choose one based on tone:

- **Death Tide:** A wave of grey energy rolls through the Threshold, wilting everything it touches. Flowers close and crumble. A vine-covered wall goes skeletal in seconds. Cyclethorn's stone holds, but the Stray Quarter's wooden buildings groan and shed bark. Citizens move with practiced calm, pulling shutters closed, herding animals to stone shelters. It passes in ten minutes. Life surges back.
- **Bloom Surge:** Green pushes hard into the Stray Quarter. Vines crawl up walls fast enough to watch. A root cracks through flagstone. A tree sprouts in the middle of a side street, trunk thickening visibly, branches reaching for rooftops. Thornguard crews with axes and saws work to cut it back before it collapses the nearest building.

**Information (free):** The Cycle is what the Thornwild does. Life and death pulse through the region like a heartbeat. The Cyclekeepers read it, predict it, keep Cyclethorn in the narrow band where both forces are manageable. Except recently, the pulses are coming faster.

### Scene 2: The Shift Market

The PCs find [[Garret Boneharvest]] at his trading post in the Shift Market, the commercial heart of Cyclethorn where stalls on runners reposition as the Threshold moves.

**Garret's Welcome:**
- Cheerful, practical, evaluating. Offers food, drink, directions.
- If the PCs are his hires (Hook 1): pays them, then immediately asks if they want more work. "The shipment's done. But I've got harvesters who haven't come back, and I'd rather pay people I've already vetted."
- If not his hires: sizes them up. "You look like you can handle yourselves. Good. We're short on that lately."

**Information (DC 12 Persuasion, market vendors):** Three harvesting teams have gone missing in the last two weeks. All were working near the Bonewood border. Garret is worried but downplaying it publicly.

**Information (DC 14 Streetwise, Stray Quarter):** The Cycle shifted three hours early yesterday. That has never happened. The Cyclekeepers aren't saying why.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Garret suspects the [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] agent embedded in the Star-Reader expedition is probing for ways to commercialize amber harvesting. He doesn't know this for certain but the researcher's questions have been too pointed. He won't share this suspicion with strangers yet.

### Scene 3: The Perch

[[Kael Duskborne]] summons the PCs to the Perch, the Cyclekeepers' observation platform on the highest point of Cyclethorn. The wind carries the scent of flowers and bone-dust simultaneously.

**Kael's Briefing:**
- Wary, direct, military. Does not waste words.
- Three harvesting teams missing near the Bonewood. His Thornguard is stretched thin covering all four settlements.
- He needs outsiders. "I need people who aren't needed somewhere else. That's you."
- Pay is modest. The danger is real. He is honest about both.

**Information (free):** The Bonewood is the deep interior where the Cycle runs fastest. Trees sprout at dawn, reach full height by noon, wither by dusk, collapse by midnight. No permanent settlements. Even Cyclekeepers don't enter alone.

- **DC 13 Insight:** Kael is more worried than he's showing. The missing teams are not the only problem, they're the symptom he can point at.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Kael is receiving coded messages from a Vigil contact warning that [[Hollowdeep]]'s organized undead may be expanding westward toward the Thornwild. If that army reaches the Wilt, the death-phase would supercharge them. He hasn't told Yara yet. He wants confirmation before he starts a panic.

### Scene 4: The Binding Ground

[[Cyclekeeper Yara Thornbloom|Yara Thornbloom]] is performing the dual rites at the Binding Ground, a flat clearing stained green-black from years of offerings. One hand warm and glowing green (growth-blessing), the other cold and traced with frost (death-honoring). The sensation of watching is unsettling, like seeing someone breathe in and out at the same time.

**Yara's Test:**
- She does not greet the PCs. She finishes her rite, takes a long breath, and studies them.
- She asks each PC one question. Not their name, not their skills. Something personal: "What have you lost that you want back?" or "What are you running from?" or "What would you sacrifice if you knew it would matter?"
- She is reading their relationship to desire, to loss, to change. The Cycle amplifies both, and she needs to know if these people will survive it.

**Information (earned through honesty, not skill checks):** The Cycle shifted early. Her models cannot explain it. Something is changing in the Bonewood, and she cannot get close enough to find out. The forest turns her back. Nine times she has tried.

- **DC 15 Insight:** Yara is afraid. Not of the PCs, not of the Bonewood. Of something she has felt in the Cycle that she will not name.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Yara has felt a "third pulse" in the Cycle for the past six months, something that is neither growth nor death. She has told no one. The third pulse is [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s influence, seeping through both divine legacies simultaneously.

### Session 1 Ending

Kael formally asks the PCs to investigate the missing harvesters near the Bonewood border. Yara adds quietly: "If you see something that does not belong to life or death, do not touch it. Come back and tell me." Garret offers to outfit them with cycle-resistant gear from his stores.

## Session 2: Into the Threshold

### Structure

**PC Focus:** Exploration and problem-solving PCs get spotlight. Combat is secondary to understanding.
**Goal:** Show the Cycle as an environment, not just a backdrop. Find the harvesters. Return with evidence.
**Threads Advanced:** Cycle mechanics, the harvesters' fate, the Bonewood's nature.

### Scene 1: Threshold Travel

The PCs travel outward from Cyclethorn toward the Bonewood border, crossing the Threshold. The landscape shifts around them.

**Environmental Hazards (choose 2-3):**

- **Transition Zones:** Areas where the Cycle flips mid-step. A PC walking through a meadow steps into a patch where the grass is already dead, the air is cold, and the flowers are bone-white husks. DC 12 Constitution save or take 1d6 necrotic damage as the death-energy leeches warmth. Stepping back reverses it.
- **Wrong Amber:** The PCs find amber deposits along the path, beautiful golden resin trapped in transition-state. One piece contains what appears to be a face, frozen mid-expression, mouth open in either ecstasy or terror. DC 13 Arcana: this should not exist. Amber crystallizes at the transition point, but it should not capture living tissue. Something is wrong with the Cycle's timing.
- **Bloom-Lure Clearing:** A beautiful clearing, impossible flowers, sweet air. DC 14 Wisdom save or feel compelled to sit and rest. PCs who fail begin walking toward the center. On success, they notice the root network beneath the soil and the vine-tendrils at the clearing's edge. A [[Bloom-Lures|Bloom-Lure]], sessile predator relying on hallucinogenic pollen. Covering nose and mouth grants advantage on the save. The Lure does not chase, it does not need to.

**Encounter: Cycle-Stalkers (Nimble)**
- Pack of 4 gaunt, bone-ridged quadrupeds. Fur shifts grey-brown to pale white.
- Lvl 1 | Armor: Light (bone ridge) | HP 14 | Speed 8 | Str +2 | Dex +2 | Will -1 | 1d6 piercing (bite)
- **Tactic:** Probing attack. Two engage, two hang back. When one dies, it rises within minutes, retaining memory of the fight. After resurrection, the pack adjusts tactics.
- **Weakness:** They don't pursue fleeing prey. Retreating within the first round ends the encounter. They also avoid fire.

> [!tip] GM Pacing
> The Threshold travel should feel like a journey through a living thing. The Cycle is not weather, it is an ecosystem. Let the PCs ask questions. Let them observe. The horror is not the danger, it is the beauty. This place is magnificent and it is dying.

### Scene 2: The Harvesters

The PCs find the missing harvesters in a clearing near the Bonewood border. They are caught in a transition loop, cycling between states without progressing. One moment alive, gasping, reaching out. The next, dead, collapsed, grey-skinned and still. Then alive again. The same gasp. The same reach. The same death. Over and over.

**The Loop:**
- 4 harvesters trapped in a 30-foot circle. The Cycle within the circle runs on a 90-second loop, life-death-life, repeating endlessly.
- The harvesters are conscious during the life phase. They are terrified. They remember every death.
- Entering the circle pulls the PC into the loop. DC 15 Constitution save to resist. On failure, the PC begins cycling. On success, they can act within the circle for one life-phase (45 seconds) before being pushed out.

**Breaking the Loop (3 independent solutions, three-clue rule):**
1. **Disrupting the Cycle physically:** A strong source of pure life-energy (a Greensinger blessing, a healing spell, a living plant from outside the Threshold) or pure death-energy (a Morrhaelite relic, a Remnant's touch, a bone from the Wilt) can overload one side and break the oscillation. DC 14 Nature or Arcana to identify this approach.
2. **Grounding the loop:** The circle sits on exposed soil. Driving iron or stone stakes into the ground at the circle's edge disrupts the energy flow. DC 13 Survival or Investigation to notice the soil pattern. Requires 4 stakes placed simultaneously (whole party coordination).
3. **Emotional anchor:** Speaking to the harvesters during their life phase, calling them by name, giving them something to want, can pull them out of the loop. The Cycle feeds on desirelessness. Giving them a reason to live longer than 45 seconds creates asymmetry the loop cannot sustain. DC 14 Persuasion or Performance. This is the most elegant solution and the one Yara would approve of.

**The Harvesters:**

| Name | Role | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Seren Blackthorn | Lead harvester | Experienced, steady. Her daughter is in Cyclethorn. |
| Tomik Greaves | Apprentice alchemist | Young, terrified. Keeps trying to grab his satchel during life phase. |
| Wren | Thornguard escort | Kael's soldier. Calm even in the loop. Counts the cycles. She's on 347. |
| Old Hadden | Veteran harvester | Stopped reaching during life phase. Just stands. He may be losing himself. |

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The transition loop was not natural. The Cycle does not do this on its own. Something pulled the harvesters into it, the same force pulling the Cycle toward the Bonewood. The [[The Bloom-Wraith|Bloom-Wraith]] did not intend harm, it was trying to communicate, but its attempts to reach out create Cycle distortions around it. The harvesters stumbled into one.

### Scene 3: The Return

The journey back to Cyclethorn is different. The landscape has shifted. Paths that existed hours ago are gone, overgrown or withered. The Threshold has narrowed visibly. [[Threshold Weavers]] have built new webs across the old trail, beautiful load-bearing silk that hums with trapped energy.

**Evidence the PCs carry:**
- Harvester testimony (the loop, the direction of the pull, the wrongness)
- Wrong Amber samples (if collected)
- Firsthand experience of the Cycle's acceleration
- Whatever they observed in the Bonewood's direction: a flickering shape in the deep forest, half-alive and half-dead, that watched them and did not approach

## Session 3: The Threshold Breaks

### Structure

**PC Focus:** The whole party. This session is a crucible.
**Goal:** Escalate the crisis, reveal the deeper mystery, open the campaign outward.
**Threads Advanced:** Cycle acceleration, the Bloom-Wraith, Serith's hidden influence.

### Scene 1: The Crisis

A Bloom Surge or Death Tide hits Cyclethorn harder than anything in living memory. Choose based on session tone:

- **Bloom Surge:** The green comes fast. Roots crack through Cyclethorn's granite foundation, something that has never happened. Vines engulf the Stray Quarter in minutes. A tree erupts through the floor of the Shift Market, scattering stalls, its trunk swelling to fill the space. The air is thick with pollen, sweet and choking.
- **Death Tide:** The grey rolls in like fog. Stone cracks. Wooden structures collapse into splinters. A Thornguard soldier on patrol stumbles, goes grey-skinned and still, then rises confused and changed. For ten heartbeats, the Binding Ground's soil goes bone-white. Yara screams.

**Crisis Points (simultaneous, party must split or choose):**
1. **The Stray Quarter is collapsing.** Newcomers and travelers are trapped. Kael's Thornguard is already there but outnumbered by the destruction.
2. **The Binding Ground is destabilizing.** Yara is trying to hold the dual rites but the energy is overwhelming her. Without support, the Cycle's local balance fails and Cyclethorn loses its safe zone.
3. **Something is approaching from the Bonewood.** The Thornguard sentries on the eastern wall report movement in the deep forest, a shape that flickers between life and death, walking slowly toward Cyclethorn for the first time in recorded history.

> [!tip] GM Pacing
> Let the PCs choose where to go. Every choice means something else goes worse. This is the moment that defines their priorities. The Bloom-Wraith approaching is not a combat encounter, it is a mystery arriving at their doorstep.

### Scene 2: Defense and Aftermath

**The Stray Quarter:** Rescue operations. Collapsing buildings, panicked civilians, Cycle-hazards (rapid growth or death-energy pockets depending on the crisis type). Kael coordinates but needs the PCs' help with the dangerous extractions.

**The Binding Ground:** Yara is physically breaking. Her dual awareness is burning through her body faster than it can recover. A PC who helps channel (holding her hands, sharing the load, grounding her with physical contact) can stabilize her. DC 14 Constitution save for the PC, the sensation is like touching both a furnace and a glacier simultaneously.

**The Bonewood Edge:** The Bloom-Wraith stops at the treeline. It does not enter Cyclethorn. It stands in the space between living forest and dead forest, flickering, and it speaks. Two voices overlapping, one warm, one cold:

> *"...the turning is faster now... the song breaks... please..."*
> *"...she reaches through us both... the third note... not ours..."*
> *"...the roots remember... when they sang together... before the silence..."*

Then it turns and walks back into the Bonewood. The Cycle calms. The Surge or Tide recedes. Cyclethorn breathes.

### Scene 3: Yara's Revelation

In the aftermath, Yara gathers the PCs at the Perch. She is pale, her hands trembling, her heterochromatic eyes, one green, one grey, both ringed with exhaustion.

**What she tells them:**
- The Cycle is not just accelerating. It is being *pulled*. Something in the Bonewood, the Bloom-Wraith, is drawing both forces toward itself.
- She has felt a "third pulse" in the Cycle for months. Neither growth nor death. Something else. Something that should not be there.
- [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s influence is suspected but unproven. The pattern matches what the [[Star-Readers]] have documented in other regions: divine legacies destabilizing, slowly, deliberately, from within.
- The Bloom-Wraith is not attacking. It came to Cyclethorn's edge during the crisis. That has never happened. It was trying to communicate. She heard its words. She does not know what they mean, but she knows they matter.

**The campaign opens outward:**
- The Bonewood beckons. The Bloom-Wraith carries answers, but reaching it requires surviving the fastest Cycle zone on the continent.
- [[Rootpyre]]'s heretics may hold the key. [[Root-Speaker Fennara]]'s dual-channeling experiments are the only practice that bridges both forces. If anyone can stabilize the approach to the Bloom-Wraith, it is her.
- [[Ashbloom]]'s restoration is weakening, confirming the Cycle's imbalance is spreading.
- The wider world's dead-god ecology is connected. What is happening here mirrors crises in other regions: the Cooling in [[Emberveil]], the Trench expansion in [[The Hollowed Reach]], the fragment migration in [[Ironmarch]].

### Cliffhanger Ending

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Surge has passed. The Tide has receded. Cyclethorn stands, battered but unbroken, its granite foundation holding against forces that have never tested it this hard.
>
> But the Threshold is narrower than it was this morning. You can see it from the Perch, the band of chaos between the green and the white, compressed by another dozen yards. The Shift Market's runners have been repositioned three times today. The sentries on the eastern wall report the Bonewood's treeline is closer.
>
> Yara stands at the observation rail, one hand on her staff, the other pressed to the stone beneath her feet. She is listening to something none of you can hear.
>
> "It spoke," she says quietly. "For the first time in thirty years, it left the Bonewood and it spoke. Whatever is coming, whatever is pulling the Cycle apart, that creature knows what it is." She turns to face you. Her eyes, one green, one grey, are steady despite the exhaustion carved into every line of her face. "I have tried to reach it nine times. The Bonewood turns me back every time. I need people it has not learned to refuse."
>
> She looks at each of you in turn.
>
> "I need you to go into the Bonewood. I need you to find the Bloom-Wraith. And I need you to listen to what it has to say. Because I think it is trying to warn us, and I think we are running out of time to hear it."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Where This Goes:** The Cycle's acceleration is driven by [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s growing influence, destabilizing both [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s divine legacies simultaneously. The corruption spreading through the Verdant Marches' root system and the organization of [[Hollowdeep]]'s undead are symptoms of the same cause. The Thornwild, sitting at the intersection, is feeling the effects first.
>
> The Bloom-Wraith is not evil. It carries fused shards of both Thyrea's and Morrhael's divine consciousness, proof that balance is possible. It is the Cycle's attempt to communicate, to warn. Fennara's dual-channeling experiments in Rootpyre may be the only way to slow the Cycle around the Wraith long enough to hear its full message. That message would be campaign-altering: the gods were not always enemies, and the force pulling them apart now is the same force that started the Theomachis.
>
> The Thornwild's crisis mirrors what is happening across the continent. This is not a local problem. It is the Second Silence manifesting in the space between two dead gods.
