# Player Handouts

*In-world documents for GMs to print or display during sessions. Each is written in the voice of its fictional author. Use them as quest hooks, atmosphere pieces, or rewards for investigation.*

---

## 1. Wanted Poster: Veil Unbound Agent

*Found on public notice boards throughout [[Ostivaar]] and the greater [[The Ashen Dominion|Ashen Dominion]]. Printed on heavy cream paper bearing the Order's flame seal.*

> [!quote] BY ORDER OF THE FLAME
>
> **WANTED FOR HERESY, SEDITION, AND CRIMES AGAINST THE DIVINE ORDER**
>
> The [[Order of the Ashen Flame]] seeks information regarding agents of the proscribed cult known as **[[The Veil Unbound]]**.
>
> **Description of Known Agent:** Human or half-human, medium build, between twenty-five and forty years of age. Last seen wearing pilgrim's traveling clothes and a silver pendant in the shape of a closed eye. Speaks with a coastal accent consistent with the [[The Hollowed Reach|Hollowed Reach]]. Uses the alias "Brother Calm." Known to frequent dockside taverns and laborers' hostels, offering "answers about the silence" to those in spiritual distress.
>
> **Be advised:** This individual is trained in persuasion and deception. They will appear sympathetic, knowledgeable, and sincere. They will claim that [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], the Imprisoned Goddess, was wrongly condemned. They will speak of a "Second Silence" as though it were salvation rather than annihilation.
>
> **Do not engage in theological debate with the suspect.** Report all sightings to your nearest Flame garrison or [[The Cinders|Cinder]] field office.
>
> **Reward:** 200 silver marks for information leading to capture. 500 silver marks for the suspect delivered alive to Flame custody. The Order reminds all citizens that harboring agents of the Veil is punishable by imprisonment and spiritual audit.
>
> *By the authority of Commander [[Ashara Voss]], Third Flame Division*
> *Sealed under the Spine of Order, in the 847th year since the Silence*

## 2. Fragment of Solvaen's Last Prayer

*A stone tablet fragment recovered from the Hollow Chamber beneath [[Ostivaar]]. The text is carved in Pre-Silence Divine Script, translated here by Church scholars. Several lines are damaged or missing.*

> [!quote] The Architect's Final Words
>
> ... and I held the line for nine days, Orenthas. Nine days against the Ironborn, against [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] himself, while the cities I loved burned behind me and the mortals I built this order to protect screamed prayers I could no longer answer.
>
> I am dying. I know this now. The structure of me is failing, each rib a vault that held a thousand laws, each vertebra a pillar of governance, and I can feel them becoming stone. Becoming mountain. Becoming the thing they will build upon when I am gone.
>
> I do not regret the architecture. Every court of justice, every treaty of peace, every child who slept safely because a wall stood firm, that was me. That was my purpose. I was the foundation and the framework, and if the mortals remember nothing else, let them remember that once, a god loved them enough to become their bones.
>
> But I ask you, old friend, you who wove the [[The Loom|Loom]] and set the threads of all things in motion: was this always the design? Did you weave my death into the pattern before I drew my first breath of starfire? Was the Theomachis your architecture, not mine?
>
> I will not curse you for it. If this was fate, then fate is merely order on a scale I cannot see.
>
> [Several lines are illegible, the stone cracked and ash-stained.]
>
> ... let my bones hold. Let the Spine stand. Let them build, even in my absence. Let them remember that order is not a cage. It is a gift.
>
> And if they forget, if they make of my skeleton a prison rather than a home, then perhaps that too was in your design, Orenthas. And perhaps one day, they will learn the difference on their own.
>
> I am tired. The iron is in my chest. Let me sleep.

## 3. Tidecaller Blessing Inscription

*Carved into the walls of [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]]'s shrine in [[Tidecross]], on the Veilshore coast. The original text is in Old Reach liturgical script. The stone weeps seawater that tastes of salt and something else, something like grief.*

> [!quote] Blessing of the Depth-Singer
>
> O Yvenne, Depth-Singer, Mother of Tides,
> who taught the first sailors to read the stars
> and the first fish to know the current,
> hear us.
>
> We stand at the edge of your body.
> The waves are your breathing.
> The tide is your heartbeat.
> The salt on our lips is your kiss
> and your sorrow.
>
> Grant us passage through your waters.
> Let the deep things sleep beneath our hulls.
> Let the current carry us to harbor
> and the wind remember kindness.
>
> We do not ask to understand the trench.
> We do not ask to see what dwells below.
> We ask only that you hold the line
> between the world of air and the world of drowning,
> as you have held it since the gods first sang.
>
> And if your strength should falter,
> if the waters rise unbidden
> and the tides forget their names,
> then we will sing for you, Mother.
> We will stand at the shore and we will sing
> until the sea remembers what it was
> before the silence came.
>
> *Salt and breath, tide and bone.*
> *Carry us home.*
>
> [Below the inscription, in a different hand, recently scratched into the stone:]
>
> *She is trying to tell us something. The water screams at night. I can almost hear the words.*
> *Yvenne forgive me, I think she is afraid.*

## 4. Diplomatic Letter from Speaker Yavenne

*A sealed letter on pressed bark-paper, bearing the glyph of the Open Hand faction of the [[The Oldborn Council|Circle of Speakers]]. Found in a diplomatic pouch, intended for any outlander leader willing to receive it.*

> [!quote] From the Desk of Speaker Yavenne of Ur-Saethen
>
> To whomever reads this with open eyes and steady hands,
>
> I write to you in violation of the Circle's consensus, such as it is. The Circle has not reached consensus in three years. I have grown tired of waiting for permission to do what is necessary.
>
> My name is Yavenne. I speak for the Open Hand, those among the Oldborn who believe that four thousand years of isolation have made us not wiser but merely older. [[Keth'vaal]] is the first city in the world, and it is dying of stubbornness.
>
> The [[The Oldborn Council|Circle of Speakers]] is fractured. [[Speaker Kaelthen]] and the Sealed Gate wish to close our borders permanently, to seal the Rootways and withdraw from all outlander contact. [[Speaker Dravos]] and the Ash Reclaimers wish to march outward and reclaim territories we abandoned millennia ago. I stand between them, arguing for what should be obvious: that the world is changing, that the Great Anchor trembles, that the old pacts strain, and that we cannot face what is coming alone.
>
> Something is wrong with the deep places. The Rootways shift. The Primordial anchors flicker. Our Pact-Weavers report that the old agreements, the ones that have held since before your gods were born, are weakening. I do not know what this means. I know only that it frightens me, and I am not easily frightened.
>
> I offer this: diplomatic standing in Keth'vaal for any party willing to undertake the Rootway Trial, our test of worthiness. Succeed, and you will have the Open Hand's protection and access to the Deep Library's unrestricted collection. Fail, and the Sealed Gate will have the justification they need to close the borders forever.
>
> I will not pretend this is without risk. Speaker Kaelthen expects you to fail. Speaker Dravos expects to use you. I expect you to surprise us both.
>
> Come to Keth'vaal. The oldest city in the world has something to show you, if you have the courage to look.
>
> With cautious hope,
> *Yavenne of Ur-Saethen, Speaker of the Open Hand*
> *Beneath the shadow of the Great Anchor, in the 4,217th year of the City*

## 5. Page from Scout Vask's Journal

*A weathered field journal recovered from a dead drop near [[Lastlight]], in the [[The Pale Wastes|Pale Wastes]]. The handwriting deteriorates across entries. Several pages are missing or water-damaged.*

> [!quote] Field Journal, Scout Merren Vask, Ashen Vigil
>
> **Day 3.**
> Standard reconnaissance. Moved to Observation Post 7, eastern rim of [[Hollowdeep]]. Cold. Clear skies. No movement on the crater floor. Commander [[Thessa Ironveil\|Ironveil]] wants updated patrol maps. I can do that.
>
> **Day 5.**
> Movement. Not the shuffling, aimless drift we trained for. Formation. Twelve distinct groups on the crater floor, each roughly five hundred strong. They are drilling. Advance, halt, wheel left, reform. Military maneuvers executed with precision I have not seen from living soldiers, let alone dead ones.
>
> **Day 8.**
> They drill in shifts. Four hours active, eight hours standing motionless. Not dormant. Waiting. I can feel the difference. A sleeping army does not watch you. A waiting army does. They know I am here.
>
> The structures we assumed were collapsed architecture are siege works. A gate is being assembled at the southern approach. Massive. Iron-reinforced. Facing toward us.
>
> **Day 11.**
> I saw her. Edda Voss. Scout-Captain Edda Voss, Second Vigil Company, reported killed in action fourteen months ago. She was standing at the forward edge of the third battalion, eyes burning with that orange light they all have. She was signing. Field signals. Our field signals. The dead are using our communication protocols.
>
> She saw me watching. She signed: APPROACH PERMITTED. OBSERVATION SANCTIONED. They are letting me watch. They want us to know.
>
> **Day 14.**
> Three approach routes confirmed. All patrolled. Timing gaps exist but they are narrowing. Something near the center of the crater, near [[The Twelve#Morrhael\|Morrhael]]'s skull, is giving orders. A shadow with weight. Not Morrhael. Something else.
>
> The gate is nearly finished.
>
> **Day 16.**
> This army marches within the year. I am certain of it. I have counted six thousand bodies and I have not covered half the crater floor. The gate faces south. South is us. South is [[Lastlight]]. South is everything.
>
> I am leaving tomorrow. Ironveil needs to see this. Everyone needs to see this.
>
> [The journal ends here. Scout Vask's body was never recovered.]

## 6. Cinder Internal Memo: Operation Quiet

*A coded document recovered from a [[The Cinders|Cinder]] safe house in [[Ostivaar]]'s merchant quarter. Partially decoded by [[Unbound Congregation]] cipher-breakers. Some sections remain illegible.*

> [!quote] INTERNAL, EYES ONLY, ASH-HAND CLEARANCE
>
> **RE: OPERATION QUIET, PHASE TWO STATUS**
> **FROM:** Spark-Captain Tessam, Lower Ribs Station
> **TO:** [RANK REDACTED], Central Authority
>
> Phase One results exceed projections. Three master craftsmen in the Ashmarket have presented symptoms consistent with advanced Order Sickness: behavioral rigidity, loss of creative initiative, compulsive adherence to routine. All three have been attributed to "natural exposure" via proximity to the Spine. No suspicion directed toward our supply chain.
>
> The mechanism remains effective. Concentrated Ashite extract, refined from Church restricted stores and applied in trace quantities to tool handles, workbench surfaces, and material samples, produces a gradual amplification of Solvaen's residual ordering effect on the human nervous system. The subject's capacity for independent thought diminishes over weeks. Creativity and dissent are replaced by productive compliance.
>
> **Phase Two objectives:**
> - Expand delivery to textile workshops and food preparation facilities in the Lower Ribs
> - Maintain plausible attribution to [[Torren Hearthforge\|Guildmaster Hearthforge]]'s supply chain (his reputation absorbs the blame, weakening Ember Guild credibility)
> - Monitor [[Unbound Congregation]] activity for reduced recruitment success in treated districts
> - Prepare contingency narrative: "defective Ashite processing" if exposure is detected
>
> **Collateral assessment:** Acceptable. Treated subjects retain full productive capacity. They simply stop asking questions. This aligns with the Hierarch's stated priority of "civic harmony" and provides [SECTION ILLEGIBLE] with the added benefit of neutralizing labor resistance without visible enforcement action.
>
> Phase Three timeline: pending approval from the Thane's office. Recommend expansion to academic institutions and junior clergy training facilities.
>
> *For the Order. For the Quiet.*
> *Spark-Captain Tessam, Seventh Ember*
>
> [A note in different handwriting at the bottom, partially burned:]
> *The Thane approved. Move to Phase Three. Include the schools, D.M.*

## 7. Star-Reader Astronomical Observation Log

*Pages from the observatory journal at the [[The Star-Readers|Star-Reader]] chapter house in Tidewall's Archive District. The entries span several months. The handwriting is meticulous but grows increasingly hurried.*

> [!quote] Observation Log, Lens-Bearer Auren Dask, Tidewall Chapter
>
> **17th of Ashfall.**
> Routine observation cycle. Constellation mapping for the merchant fleet's seasonal charts. All positions nominal. [[The Twelve#Aelindra|Aelindra]]'s Lantern (the Pole Star) steady at magnitude 1.2. Prophecy calibration within expected parameters. Nothing to report.
>
> **3rd of Deepwater.**
> Anomaly. Aelindra's Lantern has dimmed to magnitude 1.4. Rechecked instruments twice. Calibration is correct. This is not an atmospheric effect. The star itself has faded. Reported to Chapter Arch-Reader. She asked me to re-observe next clear night.
>
> **9th of Deepwater.**
> Magnitude 1.5. The dimming is accelerating. Cross-referenced with the Ashveil Chapter's records via courier. They confirm identical readings. This is not local. This is not instrumental. The stars are fading.
>
> Three navigation constellations have shifted position by 0.3 degrees since last season's charts. This should not be possible on any timescale observable by mortals. I have recalculated six times. The math does not lie.
>
> **22nd of Deepwater.**
> Attempted divination calibration using standard celestial methodology. The results were, for the first time in my career, incoherent. Not wrong. Incoherent. The celestial framework that underlies all Star-Reader prophecy is returning noise instead of signal. It is as though something is standing between us and the stars, distorting what we can perceive.
>
> Arch-Reader [[Tessera Vane]] has ordered all chapters to halt publication of astronomical observations until further notice. She is afraid. I have never seen her afraid.
>
> **4th of Stormtide.**
> I attempted to contact [[The Twelve#Aelindra|Aelindra]] through the standard devotional channel. For ten years I have felt her presence in the ritual, a warmth, a clarity, a sense of being seen by something vast and kind. Tonight there was nothing. Not silence. Nothing. The absence of a thing that was always there.
>
> I told the Arch-Reader. She said: "I know. It has been months for me."
>
> **11th of Stormtide.**
> The Aetheric Web is destabilizing. I can see it in the data even if no one will say it aloud. The ley lines that carry divine influence across the continent are losing coherence. Prayer response times are increasing. Healing miracles are becoming less reliable. The infrastructure that connects what remains of the gods to the mortal world is degrading.
>
> I do not know what is causing this. I have three theories and I am afraid of all of them.
>
> **19th of Stormtide.**
> Aelindra's Lantern: magnitude 1.9. It has dimmed by nearly forty percent in three months. At this rate, in two years it will be invisible to the naked eye.
>
> The Arch-Reader has sent urgent dispatches to every faction that will listen. Most have not responded. The [[Concord of Tides]] acknowledged receipt. The [[Order of the Ashen Flame]] sent a formal letter suggesting we "avoid publishing alarmist speculation." The [[The Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keepers]] responded within the hour. [[Lira Ashvane\|Keeper Ashvane]] asked: "How long do we have?"
>
> I do not know how to answer that question. I do not know what "we" are losing, exactly. Only that the sky is going quiet, and no one with the power to act seems willing to listen.
>
> [The remaining pages are blank. The log was submitted to the Arch-Reader's office with a note reading: "I have nothing left to observe."]
