## Step 2: Occupation

| d10 | Occupation | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Merchant / Factor | Trades in regional goods. Knows supply routes and who buys what. |
| 2 | Soldier / Guard | Serves a local authority. Knows patrol routes and recent incidents. |
| 3 | Priest / Acolyte | Serves [[The Twelve]] or a regional faith tradition. Knows local spiritual concerns. |
| 4 | Craftsperson / Smith | Makes things. Knows material sources and who commissions unusual work. |
| 5 | Scholar / Archivist | Studies something. Knows obscure history and where records are kept. |
| 6 | Sailor / Dockworker | Works the water or the port. Knows ship schedules, smuggling routes, and harbor gossip. |
| 7 | Healer / Herbalist | Treats the sick. Knows who is ill, who is not healing right, and what diseases are spreading. |
| 8 | Spy / Informant | Works for a faction (roll on the faction table or choose). Knows secrets but lies about their source. |
| 9 | Criminal / Smuggler | Operates outside the law. Knows black markets, escape routes, and who pays for silence. |
| 10 | Farmer / Laborer | Works the land or the mines. Knows what the earth is doing, unusual growth, strange soil, missing workers. |

## Step 3: Motivation

*What do they want right now?*

| d8 | Motivation | Hook |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Coin.** Needs money urgently. Will take risks they normally would not. | Offers a bargain, asks for work, or tries to sell something valuable at a low price. |
| 2 | **Safety.** Something is threatening them or their family. | Asks for protection, information, or an escort out of the area. |
| 3 | **Revenge.** Someone wronged them and they want to settle the debt. | Asks the party to help "deal with" someone, or offers information in exchange for violence. |
| 4 | **Knowledge.** Wants to understand something, a phenomenon, a person, a place. | Asks questions, offers to trade information, or hires the party to investigate. |
| 5 | **Status.** Wants recognition, promotion, or to be seen as important. | Name-drops, exaggerates their connections, offers to "introduce" the party to powerful people. |
| 6 | **Faith.** Driven by religious conviction or spiritual crisis. | Proselytizes, asks theological questions, or seeks a divine relic/site. |
| 7 | **Freedom.** Trapped by a contract, debt, faction obligation, or literal imprisonment. | Asks for help escaping, offers future favors, or tries to recruit the party into their cause. |
| 8 | **Loyalty.** Acting on behalf of someone else. Will not reveal the principal easily. | Delivers messages, asks pointed questions, or follows the party at a distance. |

## Step 4: Secret

*What are they hiding?*

| d10 | Secret | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Works for [[The Cinders]] as a low-level informant. Reports unusual activity in the area. | Moderate |
| 2 | Has attended a [[The Veil Unbound\|Veil Unbound]] gathering. Not committed, but curious. Knows where the next meeting is. | Moderate |
| 3 | Carries a stolen divine material fragment (Ashite, Morrhaelite, or Thyrea's Amber). Does not fully understand what it is. | High |
| 4 | Witnessed something they should not have: a murder, a ritual, a faction betrayal. Is being hunted for it. | High |
| 5 | Is not who they claim to be. Real identity is from a different region or social class. Fled to start over. | Low |
| 6 | Owes a dangerous debt to a faction (roll or choose which one). The deadline is approaching. | Moderate |
| 7 | Has a mild case of Order Sickness (Ashen Dominion), Tidal Madness (Hollowed Reach), or regional supernatural affliction. Hiding the symptoms. | Moderate |
| 8 | Knows the location of something valuable: a ruin, a cache, a hidden passage. Will not share without trust. | Moderate |
| 9 | Is a Sleepwalker. Does not remember what happens at night. Wakes with salt on their skin and sand in their hair. | High |
| 10 | Has no secret. What you see is what you get. (This is its own kind of surprise in Aethermourne.) | None |

*What makes them memorable in conversation?*

| d10 | Quirk |
|---|---|
| 1 | Speaks in proverbs specific to their region. Half of them do not make sense to outsiders. |
| 2 | Touches iron compulsively (a superstition from the Ironmarch that has spread continent-wide). |
| 3 | Refuses to say any god's name aloud. Refers to [[The Twelve]] by title only ("the Forge-God," "the Tide-Mother"). |
| 4 | Counts everything. Steps, coins, words in a sentence. Claims it keeps the Order Sickness away. |
| 5 | Always eating. Carries dried meat, hard bread, salted fish. Offers food to everyone, including enemies. |
| 6 | Lowers their voice when discussing anything supernatural. Believes the dead gods can still hear. |
| 7 | Has a visible scar or mark from a supernatural encounter. Tells a different story about how they got it every time. |
| 8 | Maintains a small shrine in their pack to a dead god. Tends it each morning and evening without fail. |
| 9 | Laughs at inappropriate moments. Not cruelty, just a coping mechanism that makes people uncomfortable. |
| 10 | Carries an object they will not explain: a child's toy, a broken key, a sealed letter. Changes the subject if asked. |

---

> [!tip] Quick Generation
> For a fast NPC, roll once on each table in order: Name (region-appropriate), Occupation, Motivation, Secret, Quirk, Rumor. Total time: six rolls, one NPC ready to play.
>
> For a deeper NPC, roll twice on Motivation (conscious goal + hidden drive) and twice on Secret (what they are hiding + what they do not know about themselves).
>
> **Faction affiliation:** If the NPC needs a faction, roll d10: 1-2 dominant regional faction, 3-4 secondary regional faction, 5 [[The Star-Readers]], 6 [[The Ember Guild]], 7 [[The Cinders]] (secret), 8 [[The Veil Unbound]] (secret), 9-10 unaffiliated.
