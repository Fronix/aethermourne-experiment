# One-Shot: The Black Tide

*A night of rising water, failing light, and the voice beneath the trench.*

---

## What You Need

- **Players:** 3-5 characters, levels 3-5
- **Setting:** [[Tidewall]], [[The Hollowed Reach]]
- **Tone:** Investigative horror, maritime disaster, cosmic dread
- **Key NPCs:** [[Tidecaller Mirenne]] (or use the shrine Tidecaller), [[Keeper Lira Ashvane]], [[Sergeant Bryn Kellar]], [[Sable]] (information broker)
- **Key Factions:** [[The Lantern-Keepers]], [[Concord of Tides]], [[The Veil Unbound]]
- **Music:** Ambient ocean sounds, building to storm intensity. Low drones for underwater sequences.
- **Handouts:** The Black Tide Advisory notice from [[Rumors and Notices#The Hollowed Reach|Rumors and Notices]], the water-stained journal page

---

## Overview

A Black Tide event strikes Tidewall's Bilges district with unprecedented force. The PCs are caught in the evacuation, then drawn into investigating why this tide is different: it is not receding. Something is rising from the Abyssal Trench, carried upward by the tide, and [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne's]] control over her own waters is slipping.

The one-shot moves through three acts: evacuation, investigation, and confrontation. It ends with the PCs discovering a fragment of [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] prison that has broken free and surfaced, a physical manifestation of the failing seal that carries Serith's whisper inside it.

---

## Act 1: The Bilges Go Under (60-90 minutes)

### Scene 1: The Warning

**Setting:** A dockside tavern in the Bilges, late evening. The PCs are here for their own reasons (establish in character introductions).

**What happens:** A Tidecaller bursts through the door, robes soaked, eyes wild. "The water is coming. Not a storm. Not a tide. Something else. Everyone out. Now." The tavern empties. Outside, the harbor water is rising, not with the rhythm of tide but in a slow, inexorable push, like something enormous is displacing the ocean from below.

**DC 12 Perception:** The water is *warm*. Black Tide water is always cold. This is wrong.

**DC 14 Nature/Arcana:** The water carries a faint luminescence, not bioluminescence but something else, a light that seems to come from deep below the surface, pulsing at a rhythm that almost resembles breathing.

### Scene 2: The Evacuation

**Setting:** The Bilges district. Narrow streets. Water rising at roughly a foot per ten minutes.

**What happens:** Chaos. Residents are fleeing uphill toward the Coral Quarter. [[Sergeant Bryn Kellar]] is organizing the evacuation with a skeleton crew of City Watch. He spots the PCs and conscripts them: "You look capable. I need bodies. Three blocks south, the Tide Street tenements. Thirty people, at least. The water's already at their doorsteps. Get them out."

**Objectives:**
- Evacuate residents from Tide Street (social encounters: convincing the stubborn, carrying the injured, calming the panicked)
- Navigate flooded streets (DC 13 Athletics to move through waist-deep water, DC 15 if carrying someone)
- Deal with a collapsing building (DC 14 Dexterity save to avoid debris, DC 16 Strength to shore up a wall long enough for residents to escape)

**Complication:** A Veil Unbound cultist among the residents is *not* fleeing. She is walking *toward* the water, eyes glazed, whispering. If stopped, she says: "She's calling. Can't you hear her? She's so close. She's so close." Then she snaps out of it, terrified, with no memory of what she said.

> [!tip] GM Craft
> The evacuation should feel urgent but not impossible. Let the PCs be heroes. Save most of the residents. The point is to establish the Black Tide as a real, physical threat before the investigation begins.

### Scene 3: The Tide Holds

**Setting:** The Bilges waterline, now stabilized. The water has stopped rising but is not receding.

**What happens:** [[Keeper Lira Ashvane]] arrives with a Lantern-Keeper squad. She is calm, professional, and visibly shaken. "This isn't a Black Tide. Black Tides recede. This is something else." She needs volunteers to investigate the waterline. Specifically, she needs someone to go *into* the water and find out what is holding the tide in place.

**Transition:** The PCs agree (or are persuaded). Ashvane provides waterproofed lanterns and a rope line. The investigation begins.

---

## Act 2: Beneath the Surface (60-90 minutes)

### Scene 4: The Flooded Bilges

**Setting:** Underwater, in the flooded streets of the lower Bilges. Visibility: 20 feet with lantern, 5 feet without.

**What happens:** The PCs swim through submerged streets. The water is warm, unnaturally still, and faintly luminous. Furniture drifts past. Fish swim in confused circles. The silence is oppressive.

**DC 13 Perception:** Shapes in the water. Not fish. Not debris. Humanoid shapes, standing on the flooded street below, facing the same direction: toward the harbor's deepest point.

**These are Sleepwalkers.** Residents who were caught by the tide and did not drown. They stand, eyes open, breathing water as though it were air, whispering in unison. The whisper is faint but audible: a single word, repeated. *"Rise."*

**DC 15 Arcana:** The Sleepwalkers are channeling something. Their bodies are conduits for an energy flowing upward from the deep. They are not creating the tide. They are *maintaining* it, holding the water in place through sheer channeled will, directed by something far below.

**Interaction:** The Sleepwalkers do not attack unless physically disturbed. Breaking one from the trance (DC 16 Medicine or DC 14 with divine magic) causes them to gasp, panic, and need immediate rescue. They remember nothing. Disturbing multiple Sleepwalkers causes the water level to fluctuate, a warning that disrupting them carelessly could cause a catastrophic surge.

### Scene 5: The Harbor Deep

**Setting:** The harbor floor, forty feet down. The luminescence is stronger here, pulsing from a single source.

**What happens:** At the deepest point of the harbor, half-buried in silt, is a **fragment of Serith's prison**: a shard of divine bone the size of a coffin, inscribed with starlight-silver runes that are flickering, failing. The shard pulses with dark energy. The water around it is warm, almost hot. The whisper is loudest here, not the Sleepwalkers' chorus but a single voice, clear, feminine, and terrifyingly rational.

> [!quote] Serith's Whisper
> *"The cage breaks. Not because I am strong. Because she is tired. The jailer sleeps. The seal cracks. And the water, my water now, rises to greet what was buried. Do you hear me? You hear me. Good. Then hear this: I am not your enemy. I am the only one telling you the truth. The world is dying. I am offering to let it die gently."*

**DC 14 Wisdom save** to resist Serith's compulsion (those who fail feel a wave of calm agreement, a sense that the voice is right, that resistance is unnecessary). The effect fades after one minute but leaves a lingering doubt.

**The shard:** Physically, it is inert. It cannot be moved by mortal strength (it weighs tons and is partially phased into another plane). But touching it triggers a vision.

> [!warning]- GM Only: The Vision
> The PC who touches the shard sees the Abyssal Trench: a wound in the ocean floor, miles deep, sealed by walls of divine bone and starlight. The seal is cracked. Light leaks through the cracks, not light from outside but light from *within*, Serith's consciousness pressing against the walls. [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]] is there, vast, oceanic, her form wrapped around the prison like a mother protecting a child, or a jailer afraid to let go. Yvenne is *screaming*. Not in anger. In exhaustion. She has been holding this seal for two thousand years and she cannot hold it much longer.
>
> The vision ends with a single image: the seal breaking. Water rushing in. And something, vast, dark, brilliant, rising.

### Scene 6: The Veil Unbound

**Setting:** Returning to the surface. The Bilges waterline.

**What happens:** When the PCs surface, a group of [[The Veil Unbound]] cultists is waiting at the waterline. They are not hostile. Their leader, a woman in dark robes, speaks calmly: "You've heard her. We all have. You can fight the tide, or you can understand it. Come with us and we'll explain everything."

**Choice point:** The PCs can:
1. **Go with the cultists** (leads to a social encounter where the Veil Unbound explains their theology, Serith is not evil, the world is dying, she offers mercy. They are sincere. They are also wrong about the details.)
2. **Refuse and report to Ashvane** (leads directly to Act 3)
3. **Attack the cultists** (combat encounter: 5-6 cultists, moderate difficulty, but this forecloses the information they would have provided)

## Act 3: The Reckoning (45-60 minutes)

### Scene 7: The Tide Breaks

**Setting:** The harbor. Dawn is approaching.

**What happens:** Regardless of the PCs' choice in Scene 6, the tide begins to recede. Not naturally. Violently. The water pulls back with terrifying speed, draining from the Bilges in minutes. The Sleepwalkers collapse, unconscious. The harbor empties to an unprecedented low point, exposing the seabed.

And on the exposed seabed, visible to everyone on the waterfront, is the prison shard. Above water now. Pulsing. Whispering. A piece of a goddess's cage, sitting in the open air of Tidewall's harbor for anyone to see, to hear, to touch.

**The scene:** [[Keeper Lira Ashvane]] arrives with Lantern-Keepers. [[Sergeant Bryn Kellar]] arrives with City Watch. The [[The Veil Unbound]] cultists (if not defeated) arrive from the crowd. All three factions want the shard.

- **Ashvane** wants to ward it, contain it, study it. "This is evidence. This is the most important discovery in the Reach's history."
- **Kellar** wants to destroy it. "That thing is whispering to half the harbor. Smash it before it makes more Sleepwalkers."
- **The Veil Unbound** want to protect it. "She is speaking to us. You cannot silence her. She is the only one who knows how to save the world."

**The PCs decide.** Their choice determines the one-shot's outcome:

1. **Ward and contain** (Ashvane's plan): The shard is sealed in the Archive of Tides. The Lantern-Keepers begin studying it. Campaign consequence: the Star-Readers gain crucial intelligence about Serith's prison. The Veil Unbound marks the PCs as enemies.

2. **Destroy** (Kellar's plan): The shard is shattered. The whisper stops. Campaign consequence: a piece of the prison is gone. The seal is fractionally weaker. The immediate threat is resolved but the long-term crisis worsens.

3. **Protect** (Veil Unbound's plan): The shard is taken by the cult. Campaign consequence: the Veil Unbound gains a direct conduit to Serith. Their power in Tidewall increases dramatically. The PCs have made a dangerous ally.

4. **Something else** (the PCs' plan): Improvise. Good plans should be rewarded.

## Epilogue

**Read aloud or summarize:**

The Bilges are devastated. Hundreds displaced. Dozens of Sleepwalkers recovered, traumatized, unable to explain what happened to them. The harbor water has returned to normal, but the fishermen say it tastes different now. Saltier. Warmer. Like tears.

The Tidecallers have increased their rites to three times daily. [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]] has not calmed. If anything, she is louder. The prison shard was only one fragment. There will be more.

The Black Tide will come again. And next time, it will bring something larger.

> [!warning]- GM Only: Campaign Hooks
> This one-shot introduces several threads that can be picked up in campaign play:
> - The prison shard (wherever it ended up) is a recurring plot device
> - Sleepwalkers who were submerged retain fragments of Serith's communication
> - The Veil Unbound's presence in Tidewall is now overt
> - Yvenne's exhaustion is confirmed firsthand
> - Ashvane and Kellar become recurring allies depending on the PCs' choice
> - The vision of the Abyssal Trench is the first direct evidence of the prison's condition

*The tide rises. The tide falls. But what comes with it stays.*
