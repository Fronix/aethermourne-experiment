# One-Shot: The Drowning Bridge

*Before the gods shaped the world, something else built here. It has been waiting for the gods to leave.*

---

## Overview

A 3-4 hour dungeon crawl set beneath [[The Drowning Bridge]] in [[The Greymere Fenlands]]. The pre-divine ruins sealed beneath the bridge are accessible for the first time in centuries, exposed by a catastrophic ley surge that cracked the southern chambers open. The PCs explore flooded corridors of impossible architecture, face Gyre-Born remnants waking from millennia of dormancy, and discover evidence that rewrites the world's theological history: creation existed before the gods.

**Level:** 3-5
**Players:** 3-5
**Themes:** Ancient mystery, cosmic horror, the unknown before the known
**Tone:** Wet stone, green light, and the feeling of being watched by something that does not think the way you do.

---

## Background

The Drowning Bridge is a pre-Theomachis stone structure slowly sinking into the Greymere. Half-submerged, its upper levels support a small settlement (~2,000 pop). Beneath the waterline lies the Underbridge, a network of flooded chambers used by smugglers as cache sites. But below even the Underbridge are the sealed southern chambers, rooms no one has entered in living memory. Divers have reported sounds: stone grinding, water moving in patterns, a voice speaking a language no one recognizes.

Three days ago, a ley surge of unprecedented intensity cracked the seal on the southern chambers. The water inside drained into passages below, revealing dry corridors for the first time. [[Nettie Bridgeborn]], the settlement's most connected information broker, is offering good coin for someone to investigate before the Confluence Council sends their own people and seals it all up again.

## Act 1: The Descent (60 min)

### Scene 1: Nettie's Offer

**Location:** Nettie's tea house on the Drowning Bridge's upper level.

**Nettie Bridgeborn** is a sharp-eyed woman in her sixties who runs the settlement's de facto intelligence network from behind a teapot. She explains:

- The ley surge cracked the southern seal three days ago. Water drained. Dry air, for the first time.
- Two smugglers went down to look. One came back, mute, fingers bleeding from clawing at stone. The other hasn't come back.
- The Confluence Council will arrive within 48 hours to study and likely reseal the chambers.
- Nettie wants to know what's down there before the Council decides what everyone else gets to know.
- **Reward:** 200 gold, plus anything the PCs find that isn't "the kind of thing that ends the world."

**Information (free):** The bridge stone is laced with a material that matches no known divine signature. The crystal embedded in the bridge's central piling pulses in rhythm with ley surges, as if responding.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Nettie suspects the bridge predates the gods. She has spent decades collecting evidence: the stone composition, the crystal's behavior, the sealed chambers that no divine tradition claims. She is right, and she is terrified of what that means. If the PCs confirm pre-divine construction, she will sit very still for a long time before asking what the PCs intend to do about it.

### Scene 2: The Underbridge

**Environment:** Flooded stone corridors, waist-deep murky water, bioluminescent algae providing sickly green light. Smuggler cache markers scratched into walls (DC 12 Perception to notice, DC 14 to read the shorthand).

**Navigation:** Three routes to the southern chambers. Nettie provides directions for the safest.

- **Route A (Safe):** Longer, through mapped smuggler tunnels. 30 minutes. No encounters.
- **Route B (Direct):** Through unstable corridors. DC 13 Dex saves to avoid partial collapses. 15 minutes.
- **Route C (Submerged):** Fully flooded passage requiring breath-holding or water breathing. 10 minutes. DC 14 Str save against current.

**The Missing Smuggler:** Found in the tunnels on any route. Alive, curled in a corner, whispering numbers in a language she doesn't speak. She will not move. Her eyes track patterns in the water that aren't there. DC 13 Will save for PCs who listen closely, or hear the numbers too, feel them trying to order your thoughts into geometry.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The smuggler, Reva, touched a Gyre-Born inscription in the southern chambers. The inscription is a fragment of pre-Pattern thought, raw structural logic from before the Twelve imposed their order. Her mind is trying to process it and failing. She will recover in 1d4 days but will never be comfortable with irrational numbers again.

### Scene 3: The Breach

The southern seal is a massive stone door, cracked down the center by ley-surge force. Beyond: dry stone corridors in impossible geometry. The angles are wrong, not chaotic but following a logic that predates divine mathematics. Walls meet at intersections that should not work. The air tastes of ozone and deep time.

**DC 12 Arcana:** The geometry follows patterns similar to ley-line flow but older, more fundamental. This was built by something that understood the raw substrate of reality.

**DC 15 History:** Nothing in any known tradition matches this architecture. Not the Twelve's work. Not mortal. Something else.

## Act 2: The Ruins (90 min)

### Room 1: The Gallery

A long corridor lined with bas-relief carvings in the walls. The carvings depict:
- Shapes that are almost humanoid but shift when viewed from different angles
- Structures rising from formless ground, built without tools
- Something vast and undifferentiated being divided, shaped, organized
- Twelve figures arriving and imposing a new pattern over the old

**DC 14 Arcana:** The carvings tell a story. Something built civilization here before the Twelve. When the gods arrived and imposed their Pattern on reality, the builders were... overwritten. Dissolved. Their semi-structured forms couldn't survive fully structured reality.

**Hazard: Inscription Trap.** The final carving panel pulses with pre-Pattern logic. Anyone who touches it: DC 14 Will save or take 2d6 psychic damage as alien thought-structures force themselves into the mind. On failure by 5+, the PC gains a fragment of Gyre-Born knowledge (GM's choice: understanding of one pre-divine material property, ability to see ley-line flow for 24 hours, or a single phrase in the Gyre-Born language that they cannot forget).

### Room 2: The Cistern

A vast circular chamber, 60 feet across, with a drained pool in the center. The pool's basin is carved with concentric rings that spiral inward to a central point. At the center: a crystal formation, roughly humanoid in shape, three feet tall, glowing with pale light that has no color name.

**The Crystal Figure:**
- Not an artifact. Not a statue. A Gyre-Born, crystallized at the moment the Twelve imposed their Pattern, frozen mid-transition between coherence and dissolution.
- Warm to the touch. Vibrates at a frequency that makes teeth ache.
- DC 15 Arcana: This entity was alive when the gods arrived. It was caught in the restructuring and solidified. It has been here for longer than recorded history.

**Encounter: Threshold Crawlers (3)**
The Crawlers have been feeding on the thin point created by the crystal's presence. They emerge from the walls when the PCs approach the crystal.

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Threshold Crawler | 4 | M (translucent) / H (iron) | 28 | 30' | +3 | +4 | +2 | 1d6 cold (phase-touch) |
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Phase Shift.** Alternates between translucent (physical damage dice only) and iron form (magic damage halved). Shifts start of turn.
> - **Translocation Pull.** On hit, DC 14 Str save or pulled partially into another plane. Failure by 5+ = fully translocated.
> - **Disorientation Aura.** 10 ft, disadvantage on Perception.
> **Bloodied:** Phase shifting accelerates. Translocation DC increases to 16.

**Tactical Note:** The Crawlers avoid the crystal figure. If a PC positions themselves adjacent to the crystal, Crawlers will not approach within 10 feet. The crystal's pre-Pattern presence disrupts their boundary-dwelling nature.

### Room 3: The Archive

A small chamber lined with stone tablets set into the walls. The tablets are covered in geometric inscriptions that shift when not directly observed (DC 13 Will save to focus long enough to study one).

**What the Tablets Contain (cumulative, one revelation per successful study):**
1. The builders called themselves something that translates roughly as "the Shaped," entities that crystallized from raw potential into semi-stable forms before the gods existed.
2. They built structures across the world, using the raw substrate (the Gyre) as material. Their architecture stabilized reality locally, creating pockets of order in primordial chaos.
3. They knew the Twelve were coming. They had time to prepare. They chose not to resist. The inscriptions suggest they believed the Twelve's Pattern was necessary, even though it would destroy them.
4. They left something behind. A gift, or a warning, or a seed. The inscription is damaged. The word could mean "inheritance," "contingency," or "apology."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> This is campaign-critical lore. The Gyre-Born were proto-civilized entities who predated the gods. Their ruins are scattered across Aethermourne (the Drowning Bridge, deep mines in the Reach, structures beneath oldest ruins). The revelation that creation predates the gods challenges every theological assumption on the continent. The "gift/warning/seed" is connected to [[The Loom's Edge]] and Orenthas's excised fate-threads, a connection the PCs cannot make yet.

### Room 4: The Heart

The deepest chamber. A domed room with the Drowning Bridge's central crystal visible overhead, embedded in the ceiling, pulsing with ley-light. The floor is a single carved mandala of extraordinary complexity.

**The Gyre-Born Remnant:**

At the room's center, the air shimmers. A shape presses against reality from the other side, not breaking through, but leaning. It is the remnant consciousness of a Gyre-Born, not fully dissolved when the Pattern was imposed, sleeping in the space between structure and potential for millennia. The ley surge woke it.

It does not attack. It *communicates*, but not in words. PCs in the room experience:
- A sensation of vast patience
- An image: the world before the Pattern, beautiful and terrifying, all possibility and no permanence
- A question, felt rather than heard: *"Is it time?"*

**The Question refers to:** Whether the Pattern is failing (it is). Whether the Gyre-Born can return (unclear). Whether the PCs will help or hinder (their choice).

**PC Responses:**

The remnant cannot be fought (it is not fully present). It can be:
1. **Acknowledged.** The PCs accept its existence, answer honestly. The remnant shares a final image: a map of other Gyre-Born sites across the continent, burned into one PC's memory. Then it fades.
2. **Rejected.** The PCs refuse to engage. The remnant withdraws. The mandala cracks. The crystal overhead dims. The ruins begin flooding again. The PCs have 10 rounds to escape.
3. **Studied.** A PC with Arcana training attempts to understand the remnant's nature. DC 16 Arcana: gains a permanent ability to sense Gyre-Born structures within 100 feet (useful for campaign).

## Act 3: The Return (30 min)

### Complication: The Council Arrives Early

As the PCs ascend, they hear voices in the Underbridge. Two Confluence Council members and four guards have arrived ahead of schedule, sent by [[Old Fenris]] who sensed the ley surge's significance.

**The Council's Position:** They want to reseal the chambers immediately. Pre-divine ruins are dangerous, destabilizing, and (they admit quietly) theologically inconvenient.

**PC Choices:**
1. **Share everything.** The Council is shaken but grateful. They delay the reseal to study further. Nettie is furious (she wanted exclusive information). The PCs gain Council favor.
2. **Share selectively.** Tell the Council about the ruins but omit the Gyre-Born remnant. Nettie gets her exclusive. The Council reseals but incompletely. Both parties owe the PCs.
3. **Share nothing.** Claim the ruins were empty, already flooded again. The Council reseals. Nettie gets everything. The PCs are the only ones who know the truth about pre-divine civilization.

### Resolution

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You climb back into daylight, or what passes for it in the Greymere, a pale green glow filtered through perpetual mist. The Drowning Bridge groans beneath your feet, settling another fraction of an inch into the swamp. Somewhere below, in chambers that predate every god you have ever prayed to, a crystal figure stands frozen in the moment between existing and not existing, and a consciousness older than divinity itself has asked a question and is waiting, with infinite patience, for the world to answer.
>
> The tea is waiting at Nettie's. She will want to know everything. What you tell her, and what you keep, may matter more than the gold.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Hooks:**
> - The Gyre-Born site map (if received) marks locations across the continent, some beneath existing settlements. This is a campaign-long treasure map of pre-divine archaeology.
> - The crystal figure in the Cistern could be "thawed" if someone understood Gyre-Born crystallization. This is a long-term project with enormous implications.
> - The tablets' mention of a "gift/warning/seed" connects to the Loom's Edge and the excised fate-threads. The Gyre-Born knew about the Loom before Orenthas wove it.
> - If the Council learns the truth, the theological implications ripple outward: the Church of the Ashen Flame, the Tidecallers, every divine tradition must reckon with the fact that their gods were not first.
