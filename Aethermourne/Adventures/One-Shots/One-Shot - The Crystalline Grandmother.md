# The Crystalline Grandmother

*A one-shot adventure set in [[The Primordia]], [[The Unwritten Lands]]*

---

## What You Need

> [!info] Adventure Overview
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Party Size** | 3-5 players |
> | **Recommended Level** | 4-6 (adaptable) |
> | **Tone** | Surreal exploration, moral complexity, mythic horror |
> | **Estimated Duration** | 3-4 hours |
> | **Setting** | The eastern edge of [[The Primordia]], near [[Gyre's Maw]] |
> | **Themes** | Identity, transformation, what it means to be human when the world disagrees |

**Summary:** An [[The Oldborn Kingdoms|Oldborn]] guide named Vaelith asks a group of outlander adventurers to help save her grandmother, Orynn, who has been consumed by [[The Primordials#Ur-Ghael|Ur-Ghael's]] structuring force. Orynn made a Primordial pact decades ago. The pact has turned against her: her body is crystallizing, her mind is dissolving into pure pattern, and within days she will become a Structure-Broken, a living extension of the First Pattern with no memory of who she was. Vaelith believes outlanders, people untouched by Primordial pacts, might perceive Orynn differently enough to reach her.

**Key NPCs:** Vaelith (guide), Orynn (the grandmother), Syveth (Gyre-Touched advisor at Gyre's Maw), Warden Kethra (Silence-Watcher authority)

---

## Background

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Orynn was once one of the most respected Primordial pact-holders among the Oldborn, a woman who negotiated with [[The Primordials#Ur-Ghael|Ur-Ghael's]] structuring force for forty years. Her pact allowed her to stabilize unstable terrain, calm elemental surges, and reinforce Primordial Anchors. She was invaluable.
>
> Six months ago, the pact inverted. As the Second Silence weakens divine order across Aethermourne, Primordial forces are growing stronger and less predictable. Ur-Ghael's tendency toward structure, normally a stabilizing force, has become overwhelming. Instead of Orynn channeling structure, structure is channeling through Orynn. Her flesh is becoming crystal. Her thoughts are becoming equations. She is being absorbed into the pattern she once commanded.
>
> This is happening to pact-holders across the Oldborn Kingdoms. Orynn is one of dozens. The Oldborn call them the **Structure-Broken**: mortals whose pacts have consumed them. No Oldborn solution has worked because the Oldborn are too attuned to Primordial forces to resist them. Vaelith's theory, desperate and unproven, is that outlanders, people whose magic and biology carry divine residue rather than Primordial attunement, might be different enough to break through.

## Scene 1: The Hiring (Gyre's Maw)

**Location:** The Breathless Market, [[Gyre's Maw]]
**Mood:** Tense, desperate, alien

The players are in Gyre's Maw for their own reasons (establish during character introductions). Vaelith approaches them in the Breathless Market, an Oldborn woman in her mid-thirties with sharp features, calloused hands, and eyes that have not slept in days. She speaks accented Common, learned from outlander traders.

**Vaelith's pitch:** Her grandmother is dying, not from illness but from transformation. The Oldborn healers cannot help. The Silence-Watchers say it is irreversible. Vaelith disagrees. She believes that someone who is *not* attuned to Primordial forces, someone from the divine-touched continent, might be able to reach Orynn's mind before it dissolves entirely.

**What Vaelith offers:** Payment in stabilized Gyre-crystals (valuable anywhere). Passage through the Primordia with an experienced guide. The gratitude of an Oldborn family, which is worth more than outsiders realize.

**What Vaelith does not say:** She has already tried this with two other outlander groups. The first refused. The second agreed and came back changed, one of them Gyre-Touched, the others too shaken to speak. Vaelith does not mention this unless pressed (Insight DC 14 to sense she is withholding something, DC 18 to get the full truth).

> [!tip] Roleplay Notes: Vaelith
> Vaelith is proud, practical, and barely holding together. She does not like outlanders. She does not trust divine-touched magic. She is asking for help because she has exhausted every other option. Play her as someone swallowing her pride with every word. She will not beg. She will state facts and let the players decide.
>
> **Sample line:** *"My grandmother is becoming something that does not remember her own name. Your gods are dead and your magic smells like rot. But you are different from us in ways that might matter. Will you help, or will you haggle?"*

## Scene 2: The Journey (Eastern Primordia)

**Location:** A half-day trek from Gyre's Maw into the Primordia
**Mood:** Surreal, beautiful, threatening

Vaelith leads the players through the eastern Primordia toward her family's homestead. The journey takes roughly four hours and passes through increasingly unstable terrain.

**Environmental encounters (use 2-3):**

1. **The Singing Stones.** A field of floating boulders that hum in resonant frequencies. Navigation requires Perception DC 12 to find safe paths between them. Failure means a boulder drifts into a character (2d6 force damage, Dexterity save DC 13 for half).

2. **The Color Storm.** A wave of unnamed color rolls across the landscape. Characters must make Str saves (DC 14) or experience synesthesia for 1 hour (hearing colors, seeing sounds). No mechanical penalty, but deeply disorienting. Vaelith is unaffected and mildly contemptuous of the players' reaction.

3. **Elemental Coalescence.** A curiosity-driven air coalescence follows the party, mimicking their movements and speech in distorted echoes. It is not hostile but will not leave. If attacked, it disperses and reforms behind the party. If spoken to respectfully (Persuasion DC 10 or any Primordial language), it drifts away. Vaelith uses this as a teaching moment about how Primordial entities are not creatures.

4. **The Terrain Shift.** The ground beneath the party suddenly liquefies for thirty seconds, then resolidifies. Characters must make Dexterity saves (DC 15) or be trapped knee-deep in stone. Strength DC 16 to pull free, or wait for Vaelith to negotiate with the ground (she speaks to it in Oldborn ritual language, takes 10 minutes).

**During the journey, Vaelith explains:**
- Primordial pacts are negotiations, not worship. You ask the force for something. It asks something of you. The exchange must be balanced.
- Structure-Broken are pact-holders whose exchange has become unbalanced. The force takes more than it gives. The mortal dissolves into the force.
- Her grandmother's pact was with [[The Primordials#Ur-Ghael|Ur-Ghael]], the First Pattern. Ur-Ghael's nature is to impose structure. Orynn is being structured out of existence.

## Scene 3: The Homestead

**Location:** Vaelith's family compound, built around a small Primordial Anchor
**Mood:** Grief, quiet horror, intimacy

The homestead is a cluster of stone buildings around a ten-foot Primordial Anchor. The Anchor is smaller and dimmer than the one at Gyre's Maw, its stabilization field weaker. Within the field, the landscape is calm, recognizable. Beyond it, the Primordia churns.

**Orynn** sits in the central courtyard. She was once an elderly woman. Now she is something between human and geometry. Crystalline growths cover her arms, shoulders, and the left side of her face. Her right eye is still human, brown and frightened. Her left eye is a faceted prism that refracts light into patterns. She speaks, but her sentences alternate between lucid and algorithmic.

**Lucid Orynn:** Gentle, wry, frightened. She knows what is happening. She remembers who she is, but the memories are becoming abstract, patterns rather than feelings. She can tell the players about her pact, her life, her granddaughter. She is afraid, not of dying, but of becoming something that does not know it was ever alive.

**Patterned Orynn:** Speaks in geometric descriptions. "The angle of your grief is 47 degrees." "Your blood moves in spirals of decreasing radius." "Structure requires sacrifice. Sacrifice requires structure." This is [[The Primordials#Ur-Ghael|Ur-Ghael]] speaking through her, not with malice, but with the absolute indifference of gravity.

**Investigation options:**
- **Arcana/Religion DC 14:** The crystallization is not a disease. It is an integration. Orynn's body is becoming a Primordial Anchor, a living stabilization point. If completed, she will be immensely useful to the land and completely inhuman.
- **Medicine DC 16:** The process is reversible, but only if Orynn's consciousness can be separated from Ur-Ghael's influence before the crystallization reaches her brain stem. Estimated time remaining: 12-18 hours.
- **Insight DC 12:** Orynn is fighting. The lucid moments are not random. She is deliberately pushing back against the transformation. She needs help, not rescue. Someone to anchor her while she fights.

## Scene 4: The Resonance Dive

**Location:** Inside Orynn's consciousness (abstract/surreal)
**Mood:** Dreamlike, tense, deeply personal

The only way to help Orynn is to enter her consciousness through the Primordial Anchor's resonance field. Vaelith knows the ritual but cannot perform it herself, her Primordial attunement means she would be absorbed alongside her grandmother. The players must do it.

**The Ritual:** Characters touch the Anchor and Orynn simultaneously. Each makes a Wisdom save (DC 13). Failure means they enter the resonance dive with 1 level of exhaustion (the transition is jarring for divine-touched physiology). Success means clean entry.

**Inside Orynn's Mind:**

The players find themselves in a landscape that is half-memory, half-geometry. Orynn's homestead exists here, but it is being overwritten. The garden is becoming a crystal lattice. The kitchen table is becoming an equation. Orynn's cat is becoming a perfect geometric solid that purrs.

**Three challenges to reach Orynn's core self:**

1. **The Memory Garden.** A courtyard where Orynn's memories grow like flowers. Ur-Ghael's influence is converting them into patterns. The players must identify three genuine memories among the patterns (Investigation DC 13 each, or roleplay by asking Vaelith about Orynn's life via a connection maintained from outside). Each memory restored weakens Ur-Ghael's grip.

2. **The Pattern Guardian.** A crystalline construct that embodies Ur-Ghael's structuring force. It does not attack unless attacked. Instead, it offers each player a "gift": perfect clarity, the removal of doubt, the conversion of their messy emotions into clean, efficient structure. Refusing requires Wisdom save DC 15 or Charisma save DC 15 (player's choice, representing willpower or force of personality). Accepting the gift means the player begins crystallizing (1d4 temporary Constitution damage per hour until the dive ends).

3. **The Core.** Orynn's true self, a frightened old woman sitting in a chair that is half-crystal, half-wood. She is holding a clay cup, the last unstructured object in her mind. The cup is cracked. If it breaks, Orynn is lost. The players must convince Orynn to hold on, to choose her messy, imperfect humanity over Ur-Ghael's perfect structure.

**This is a roleplay challenge, not a combat one.** No single check resolves it. The players must talk to Orynn, remind her of who she is, give her reasons to fight. Use the memories they recovered in the garden. Reference details Vaelith shared during the journey. Make it personal.

**Suggested DCs for the final persuasion:**
- If all three memories were restored: DC 12
- If two memories: DC 15
- If one memory: DC 18
- If no memories: DC 22

## Scene 5: Outcomes

### Success: Orynn Stabilizes

The crystallization halts. Orynn will never be fully human again, the growths on her body remain, and she will occasionally speak in patterns. But she is *herself*. She knows her name. She remembers her granddaughter. She holds the cracked cup and does not let go.

Vaelith weeps. She does not thank the players with words. She presses her forehead to each of theirs, an Oldborn gesture of profound respect. She gives them the promised crystals and something more: a Primordial Anchor shard, a small stabilized crystal that grants rolling twice and taking higher on saves against environmental hazards in the Primordia (one use, then it crumbles).

**Campaign implication:** The method the players used to save Orynn could work on other Structure-Broken across the Oldborn Kingdoms. This makes the players valuable to the Oldborn, a diplomatic bridge that did not exist before.

### Failure: Orynn is Lost

The cup shatters. Orynn's remaining human eye goes faceted. She stands, perfectly symmetrical, perfectly calm, and walks out of the homestead into the Primordia. She is now a Primordial Anchor, a living stabilization point wandering the landscape. The land around her becomes calm, structured, orderly. She will never speak a human word again.

Vaelith does not weep. She goes very still, very quiet. She pays the players what she promised. She does not speak to them again.

**Campaign implication:** The failure demonstrates that Primordial forces are consuming the Oldborn's pact-holders, a slow-motion catastrophe that will eventually destroy their civilization. The outlanders' inability to help confirms the Oldborn's worst fears about the divine-touched.

### Partial Success: Orynn Chooses

If the players make a compelling argument but fail the final check by 3 or less, Orynn makes her own choice. She cannot reverse the transformation, but she can direct it. She chooses to become an Anchor, not because Ur-Ghael demands it, but because she decides her granddaughter's land needs protection more than it needs a grandmother.

This is a sacrifice, not a failure. Orynn walks into the Primordia with her human eye open and her clay cup balanced, cracked but unbroken, atop her crystalline palm. Vaelith watches her go with grief and pride in equal measure.

**Campaign implication:** This outcome is bittersweet but functional. The Oldborn see it as proof that their people can still negotiate with Primordial forces, even at the cost. The players earn respect.

> [!warning]- GM Notes
> **Tone guidance:** This adventure is about identity and transformation, not combat. The Pattern Guardian can be fought, but fighting it accomplishes nothing, it reforms. The real conflict is emotional. Lean into Orynn's humanity. Make the players care about a frightened old woman before asking them to save her.
>
> **Scaling:** For lower-level parties, reduce save DCs by 2 across the board. For higher-level parties, add a time pressure: the crystallization accelerates, giving the players fewer rounds in the resonance dive.
>
> **Connecting to campaign:** If running this as part of The Second Silence, the Structure-Broken crisis connects to the broader theme of Primordial resurgence. Saving Orynn (or failing to) foreshadows what happens when divine order collapses entirely: not destruction, but transformation into something that does not remember what it replaced.
