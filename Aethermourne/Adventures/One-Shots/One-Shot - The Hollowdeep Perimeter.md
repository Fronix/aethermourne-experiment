# One-Shot: The Hollowdeep Perimeter

*The dead have been quiet for twenty years. That is not the same as peaceful.*

---

## What You Need

- **Players:** 3-5 characters, levels 4-6
- **Setting:** [[Hollowdeep]] crater perimeter, [[The Pale Wastes]]
- **Tone:** Survival horror, military reconnaissance, dread
- **Key NPCs:** [[Thessa Ironveil|Commander Thessa Ironveil]], Scout Edda Voss (deceased), Scout Vask (deceased)
- **Key Factions:** [[The Ashen Vigil]], Serith's organized undead
- **Music:** Low wind ambience, distant percussion. Silence is more effective than score. Save any crescendo for the bone-storm.
- **Handouts:** Ironveil's mission briefing map, Vask's journal page, Morrhaelite lens sketch

---

## Overview

A 3-4 hour survival horror one-shot set on the edge of the [[Hollowdeep]] crater in [[The Pale Wastes]]. [[Thessa Ironveil|Commander Thessa Ironveil]] of the [[The Ashen Vigil|Ashen Vigil]] has hired the PCs to scout the perimeter of the crater and report what they observe. The mission is intelligence gathering, not combat. The goal is to get close, document what the organized undead are doing inside the crater, and get out alive.

The PCs will not clear the Hollowdeep. They will barely survive looking at it.

**Level:** 4-6
**Players:** 3-5
**Themes:** Survival horror, military reconnaissance, dread
**Tone:** Frozen silence, the weight of being watched, and the slow realization that the enemy has been preparing for this longer than you have been alive.

---

## Background

For twenty years, the Hollowdeep has been silent. No hordes spilling from the thirty-mile crater. No bone-storms erupting from its depths. The [[The Ashen Vigil|Ashen Vigil]]'s Watchers at [[Lastlight]] have observed through Morrhaelite lenses what the silence hides: undead forming military ranks, drilling in formations, constructing structures from bone and frozen earth. [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s skull, buried at the crater's center, pulses with rhythmic energy that suggests purpose rather than decay.

Commander Ironveil sent 54 scouts into the crater over the past year. None returned. She needs fresh eyes on the perimeter, people the Hollowdeep hasn't had time to study. The PCs.

---

## Act 1: The Briefing (30 min)

### Scene 1: Lastlight

**Location:** [[Lastlight]], the watchtower settlement built into a rocky ridge overlooking the Hollowdeep.

**Atmosphere:** Cold beyond weather. The supernatural chill radiating from [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s bones in the crater bleeds through every wall. Breath frosts indoors. The Watchers move with the tight efficiency of people who have looked at something terrible every day for years.

**Commander Ironveil's Briefing:**
- Tall, gaunt, late 40s, close-cropped grey hair, sharp blue eyes. Twin Morrhaelite short swords at her hips. Speaks rapidly and precisely.
- "The Hollowdeep has been quiet for twenty years. In my experience, silence means something is being built. I need to know what."
- The mission: approach the crater rim from the northeast (least observed approach per Watcher data), document undead activity, note any structures or formations, and return. Do not engage. Do not enter the crater. Do not be seen.
- Ironveil provides: Morrhaelite lens (allows one PC to see necromantic energy flows), cold-weather gear, signal flare (emergency extraction, one use), and a bone-cairn route map to the northeast approach.
- **Reward:** 300 gold per PC, plus Vigil recognition and Morrhaelite weapon access.

**DC 14 Insight:** Ironveil is not telling them everything. She is afraid, not of the undead, but of what she already suspects and needs confirmed.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Ironveil has seen through the Morrhaelite lenses herself. She knows the undead are organized. She suspects Serith's involvement but has no proof. She needs the PCs to confirm independently so she can take the intelligence to the mainland without being dismissed as paranoid. The 54 lost scouts haunt her. She will not send anyone she can't afford to lose, which is why she hired outsiders.

### Scene 2: The Ridge Posts

Before departing, the PCs can visit the Ridge Post observation stations.

**Through the Morrhaelite Lens:**
- The crater is thirty miles across, a bowl of frozen earth and bone descending into darkness.
- Necromantic energy flows are visible as dark currents, not random, but *patterned*, flowing in circuits like blood through veins.
- At the crater's center, a vast pulsing glow: Morrhael's skull, buried, rhythmic, alive with purpose.
- Movement on the crater floor: formations. Thousands of undead in disciplined ranks, marching in patterns that repeat with mechanical precision.

**DC 13 Perception (through the lens):** On the crater rim, opposite Lastlight's position, a single figure stands motionless. It wears Vigil armor.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The figure is Scout-Captain Edda Voss, one of the 54 lost scouts. She is dead, raised, and serving as [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s observation post. She retains enough skill to use Lastlight's sight-lines in reverse. Serith knows the settlement's defenses, garrison strength, and rotation schedule. The PCs will encounter evidence of her later.

## Act 2: The Approach (60-90 min)

### The Route

The bone-cairn path from Lastlight to the northeast crater rim. Approximately 8 miles through frozen tundra. Travel time: 4-6 hours in-world, condensed to three scenes.

### Scene 1: The Tundra

**Environment:** Ashborn Tundra. Grey-white frozen plains, frost-grass crunching underfoot, visibility 2 miles under clear sky. The Hum of the Hollowdeep is audible as a low vibration in the chest.

**Hazard: Bone-Pull**
As the PCs approach the Marrow Fields (divine bone fragments protruding from frozen earth along the route), they feel the pull:
- Stage 1 (1 mile out): Fatigue. Everything feels heavy. DC 11 Will save or gain 1 level of exhaustion.
- Stage 2 (half mile): Grey depression. Colors seem muted. Motivation drains. DC 13 Will save or lose the desire to continue (sit down for 1d10 minutes unless physically moved by allies).
- Stage 3 (at the bones): Peaceful acceptance. The cold feels warm. Sitting down feels right. DC 15 Will save or stop moving entirely, content to wait.

**Countermeasure:** Morrhaelite equipment grants rolling twice and taking higher on bone-pull saves. Strong emotional anchors (thinking of loved ones, reciting personal oaths) grant +2.

### Scene 2: The Patrol

Midway to the rim, the PCs encounter evidence of organized undead activity *outside* the crater.

**Discovery:** A line of footprints in the frost, perfectly spaced, perfectly parallel. Thirty sets, marching in formation. The prints lead from the crater toward Lastlight and then back. A patrol route. The undead are *scouting the scouts*.

**DC 14 Perception:** One set of prints is different. Boot treads consistent with Vigil-issue boots. A dead scout, walking with the formation, wearing their old equipment.

**Encounter (Avoidable): Undead Patrol**

If the PCs are careless (failed Stealth or loud movement), the patrol returns. Thirty skeletal soldiers in rusted armor, marching in perfect silence. They are not shambling. They are *disciplined*.

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Hollowdeep Soldier | 3 | M | 20 | 6 | +3 | +1 | +4 | 1d8 slashing (rusted blade) |
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Formation Discipline.** When adjacent to another Soldier, damage rolls gain +2. Cannot be frightened while in formation.
> - **Necromantic Resilience.** Non-Morrhaelite weapons deal dice only (no modifiers). Morrhaelite weapons deal full damage and prevent reformation.
> - **Silent March.** Makes no sound. Cannot be detected by hearing alone.
> **Bloodied:** Formation breaks. Soldier fights independently, loses Formation Discipline bonus but gains Frenzied Assault (attacks twice per turn, 1d6 each).

**Critical:** There are *thirty* of them. This is not a fight the PCs can win. Options:
1. **Hide.** DC 14 Dex (Stealth) per PC. Failure = spotted. The patrol does not chase immediately; it sends two runners back to the crater while the rest form a search pattern.
2. **Outrun.** The patrol marches at fixed speed. PCs can outpace them but must abandon stealth.
3. **Distract.** Throw equipment, create noise elsewhere. DC 13 Dex to place distraction convincingly. Patrol investigates for 1d4 rounds before resuming route.

### Scene 3: The Rim

**The Crater Edge.**

The ground drops away. Thirty miles of frozen devastation, descending in concentric rings toward a center the eye refuses to focus on. The air is colder here by twenty degrees. Breath crystallizes and falls. Sound behaves wrong, voices carry too far, echoing off walls that don't exist.

**What the PCs See (with Morrhaelite lens):**

1. **The Formations.** Thousands of undead in ranked columns, drilling in patterns. They move in absolute synchronization. No living army achieves this precision because living soldiers have will. These do not.

2. **The Structures.** Bone-and-earth constructions rising from the crater floor. Walls. Towers. Something that looks horribly like a *gate*, thirty feet tall, made from fused skeletal material. It faces south, toward the Holds.

3. **The Skull.** At the center, buried but visible through the lens as a pulsing mass of dark energy. [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s skull. The rhythm is steady, purposeful. Not decay. Command.

4. **The Figure.** On the opposite rim, watching them. Vigil armor. Standing perfectly still. DC 15 Perception: the figure raises one hand in the Vigil field-sign for "hold position."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The figure is Scout-Captain Edda Voss. She sees the PCs. She does not attack or alert the formations. She is waiting, because Serith wants the PCs to see this. Serith wants Ironveil to know what is coming. Fear is a weapon, and an enemy that retreats in terror spreads terror to everyone they meet. The PCs are being *allowed* to observe.

## Act 3: The Retreat (60 min)

### Scene 1: The Discovery

As the PCs begin withdrawing from the rim, they find something that changes everything.

**The Camp.**

In a sheltered depression 200 yards from the rim: a Vigil scout camp. Two weeks old. Bedrolls, cold rations, a Morrhaelite signal lantern (unlit). And a journal.

**The Journal (Scout Merren Vask):**

Selected entries (read aloud or paraphrase):

> *Day 3. Formations are real. Counted twelve distinct battalions, each ~500 strong. They drill in shifts. When not drilling, they stand motionless. Not dormant. Waiting.*
>
> *Day 7. The structures are siege works. The gate faces south. I've mapped approach routes from the crater to Lastlight. There are three. The dead have patrols on all of them.*
>
> *Day 9. Saw something near the skull. Not undead. Darker. It moved like smoke and gave orders the formations obeyed. Whatever is commanding this army, it is not Morrhael. He is dead. Something else is using his domain.*
>
> *Day 12. I think they know I'm here. A patrol walked within twenty feet of my camp last night and did not stop. They're letting me watch. That terrifies me more than if they'd attacked.*
>
> *Day 14. She came to the rim again. Edda. I know it's her. She's wearing her field signs. She signed "hold position" and "patience." I don't know if that's Edda or whatever is wearing her. I'm leaving tomorrow. If you find this and I didn't make it back, tell Ironveil: the army marches within the year. The gate is almost finished.*

**DC 12 Investigation:** The journal's final page has a map. Three approach routes from Hollowdeep to Lastlight, with patrol schedules and timing gaps. This intelligence is invaluable.

### Scene 2: The Bone-Storm

As the PCs retreat, the sky darkens. A bone-storm rolls in from the crater, dust and fragments of divine bone carried on necromantic wind. This is not natural. The storm is *aimed*.

**Bone-Storm Hazard (3 rounds to reach shelter or endure):**

**Round 1 - Death-Sight:** All PCs see the spirit world overlaid on reality. The tundra is not empty. Thousands of translucent figures stand in the frost, motionless, facing the crater. The dead who fell here over millennia, not yet risen, not yet at rest. Merely waiting. No save required, but deeply unsettling.

**Round 2 - Necrotic Wind:** DC 13 Str save or take 2d6 necrotic damage and suffer tissue numbness (rolling twice and taking lower on Dex saves for 1 hour). Exposed skin blackens at the edges. Healing restores the damage but the discoloration remains for 1d4 days.

**Round 3 - The Whisper:** DC 15 Will save. On failure, the PC hears a voice, feminine, calm, patient. It says one sentence, different for each PC, always true, always something they fear:

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Serith's whispers are personalized. She has been observing the PCs since they reached the rim. Tailor the whisper to each PC's deepest fear or guilt. Examples:
> - To a PC who lost someone: *"They are here. They are waiting for you. They are not angry."*
> - To a PC who fears death: *"It does not hurt. I promise. It is the waiting that hurts."*
> - To a PC who values control: *"You have never been in control. Not once. Isn't that a relief?"*
> - To a PC who is brave: *"Courage is just fear that hasn't understood the situation yet."*

### Scene 3: The Return

The PCs reach Lastlight. They are cold, shaken, possibly injured. The Watchers pull them inside without questions. Hot broth. Blankets. Silence.

**Debriefing with Ironveil:**

Ironveil reads the journal. Studies the map. Looks at the PCs. Her hands do not shake, but her jaw tightens.

"How long did the journal say?"

*Within the year.*

Ironveil is quiet for a long time. Then:

"I need to get this to the mainland. The Dominion, the Concord, all of them. They need to know. They won't believe me. A commander from a frozen outpost they've forgotten, raving about undead armies." She looks at the PCs. "They might believe you."

### Resolution

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You stand on Lastlight's ridge as the bone-storm passes, watching it dissipate over the tundra like a breath held too long. Below, the Hollowdeep is silent again. The formations have stopped drilling. The gate stands half-finished in the crater's depths, facing south.
>
> On the opposite rim, the figure in Vigil armor has not moved.
>
> Commander Ironveil stands beside you, Morrhaelite lens raised, watching the same nothing you're watching. She says, very quietly: "Twenty years of silence. We thought it meant the crisis was over. It meant the crisis was preparing."
>
> She lowers the lens. "Thank you for confirming what I already knew. Now help me convince the world."
>
> The wind carries the faintest sound from the crater. Not a whisper. Not a scream. A rhythm. Steady, patient, and getting louder.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Hooks:**
> - Vask's journal and map are the first concrete intelligence about Hollowdeep's military preparations. Getting this to the mainland is a campaign quest in itself (who believes it? who suppresses it? who uses it?).
> - The smoke-entity near the skull is [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s projected influence, commanding through Morrhael's broken death-domain. Confirming this requires deeper investigation (full Hollowdeep expedition).
> - Edda Voss on the crater rim is a personal hook. Is she a puppet, or is some part of her still fighting? Rescuing or releasing her is a mid-campaign objective.
> - Ironveil's request to "help me convince the world" is a direct campaign thread: the PCs become ambassadors of the Hollowdeep intelligence, traveling to the Dominion, the Reach, and potentially the Unwritten Lands to build a coalition.
> - [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s Black Lantern lies somewhere in the crater. If found and relit, it could release every trapped soul in the Wastes and destroy Serith's army overnight. Finding it is the endgame.
