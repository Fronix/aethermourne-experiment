# One-Shot: The Iron Thane's Challenge

*In the shadow of a dead war-god's shard, the strongest rise. But strength is not always what it seems.*

---

## What You Need

- **Players:** 3-5 characters, levels 4-6
- **Setting:** [[Ironhaven]], [[The Ironmarch]]
- **Tone:** Arena combat, political intrigue, creeping corruption
- **Key NPCs:** [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] (Iron Thane), [[Mira the Red]] (challenger), [[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal]], Castellan Drenn
- **Key Factions:** Iron Thane's court, [[The Iron Companies]], [[The Ember Guild]]
- **Music:** War drums, forge ambience. Escalate to dissonant strings during the corruption reveal.
- **Props:** Tournament bracket (draw on paper for players)

---

## Overview

Iron Thane [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] holds her annual tournament, the **Challenge of Iron**, in Ironhaven's Dueling Rings. The prize: a shard of [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] refined by [[The Ember Guild]], worth a fortune and rumored to grant its wielder supernatural combat prowess. Fighters from across the Ironmarch have gathered. The Warspire hums louder than usual.

The PCs enter the tournament, or are hired to investigate it, when it becomes clear that something is wrong. Competitors are becoming more violent than the Rings' rules permit. Fights to yield are becoming fights to the death. And the prize shard is not what the Ember Guild delivered. Someone has swapped it for a raw, unrefined fragment that is actively radiating war-essence, driving everyone in the arena toward blood-madness.

---

## Act 1: The Tournament (60-90 minutes)

### Scene 1: Arrival in Ironhaven

**Setting:** The Red Market, tournament day. The city is packed. Banners of competing companies hang from every wall. The Warspire looms overhead, its hum palpable.

**What happens:** The PCs arrive (either as competitors or as hired investigators). The atmosphere is festive but tense. Fights have already broken out in the crowds, more than usual for tournament day. Vendors are nervous. The City Watch is doubled.

**DC 12 Perception:** The air has a metallic taste, stronger than normal for Ironhaven. Characters who have been in the city before notice the difference. Those who haven't feel an unfamiliar tightness in their chest, a sharpening of focus, a quickening of temper.

**NPC introductions:**
- **Castellan Drenn** greets the PCs at the arena gates. He is efficient, professional, and subtly worried. "The Thane welcomes all challengers. Fights are to yield. Breaking the Code of Iron means disqualification and imprisonment. Are we clear?"
- **[[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal]]** is in the crowd, observing. If the PCs are investigators, he is their contact. "Something's off. The fighters are too aggressive. Watch the Round of Eight. If I'm right, we'll know by then."

### Scene 2: The Opening Rounds

**Setting:** The Dueling Rings. Seven stone circles, all active. Crowds roaring.

**What happens:** The PCs fight their first-round opponents (or observe if investigating). The fights are intense but controlled. Competitors are skilled. The Code of Iron is respected.

**Combat encounter (per PC):** One-on-one duels against tournament fighters. Moderate difficulty. Opponents yield when reduced to 25% health.

**DC 14 Insight (for observers):** Something changes between rounds. After each fight, the winner's behavior shifts: more aggressive posture, shorter temper, a faint iron-grey tinge in the eyes. The losers, oddly, seem relieved, as if a pressure has lifted.

**Clue:** The prize shard is displayed in a glass case at the arena's center, resting on a velvet cushion. DC 15 Arcana detects that the shard is radiating energy, far more than a refined Ember Guild product should. Refined shards are inert until activated. This one is *broadcasting*.

### Scene 3: The Round of Eight

**Setting:** The main Dueling Ring. The crowd has swelled.

**What happens:** The quarterfinals. The violence escalates sharply. The first fight goes to yield, barely, the winner has to be physically restrained from continuing. The second fight ends in death. The loser yields. The winner does not stop. The crowd gasps, then *cheers*.

Castellan Drenn intervenes, but the winner resists, eyes iron-grey, foam at the lips, screaming about glory and blood. Three guards are needed to subdue him. Drenn calls a recess.

**DC 12 Medicine on the berserk fighter:** His blood has a metallic sheen. His pupils are dilated and iron-colored. This is advanced [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] contamination, far beyond what ambient Warspire exposure causes. Something is accelerating the effect.

**Investigation opportunity:** During the recess, the PCs can:
- **Examine the prize shard** (DC 16 Arcana): It is not refined. It is raw, unprocessed Kaevroth's Iron, still carrying active war-essence. The Ember Guild seal on the case is forged.
- **Question the berserk fighter** (DC 14 Persuasion once sedated): "I couldn't stop. The closer I got to winning, the louder the voice. Not words. Just... *want*. I wanted to destroy him. Not defeat. Destroy."
- **Search the arena offices** (DC 13 Investigation): The delivery manifest shows the Ember Guild shard was accepted by "Factor Gellen Ashworth." But a second entry, partially erased, shows a *different* shard was delivered the same night, by an unnamed courier from [[Wrathspire]].

## Act 2: The Investigation (45-60 minutes)

### Scene 4: Following the Trail

**Setting:** Ironhaven's back streets. Evening.

**What happens:** The PCs investigate the shard swap. Three leads:

**Lead 1: Factor Gellen Ashworth.** The Ember Guild's local monitor. He is found at his office, nervous, sweating. He accepted the original shard and signed the manifest. But the shard in the arena is not the one he delivered. "Someone switched it. Between my delivery and the display. That's a raw fragment. An *active* raw fragment. If it stays in that arena during the semifinals, every fighter in there will go berserk. And the crowd might follow."

**DC 13 Insight:** Ashworth is telling the truth about the swap. He is lying about something else: he suspects who did it but is afraid to say.

**Lead 2: The Courier from Wrathspire.** Tracing the second delivery leads to a mercenary company called **The Forge Hammers**, one of [[Mira the Red]]'s allied companies. The courier is long gone, but a bribed stablehand (DC 12 Persuasion, DC 14 without coin) confirms: "Red-haired woman paid triple for a rush delivery. Said it was 'tournament supplies.' Came from the Wrathspire road."

**Lead 3: The Warspire's Response.** DC 15 Arcana near the Warspire: the massive fragment is resonating with the prize shard. They are feeding each other, the prize shard broadcasting war-essence, the Warspire amplifying it, creating a feedback loop that intensifies with every fight. By the finals, the contamination will be strong enough to affect the entire arena, two thousand spectators.

### Scene 5: Confronting the Truth

**Setting:** Varies based on PC approach.

**What happens:** The PCs have enough information to act. The shard swap was engineered by agents of [[Mira the Red]], who is not present in Ironhaven but whose plan is clear:

1. Replace the refined shard with a raw one
2. Let the tournament's violence feed the war-essence feedback loop
3. When the arena descends into blood-madness, Yrsa will be forced to intervene personally
4. In the chaos, Mira's agents will challenge Yrsa for the Iron Thane title, with the Warspire's amplified contamination making Yrsa's Morrhaelite defense insufficient

**The PCs must decide how to act before the semifinals begin.**

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Mira's plan is clever but imperfect. She does not know about Yrsa's Morrhaelite shard. Her agents expect Yrsa to succumb to war-madness like everyone else. If Yrsa fights contaminated, the Morrhaelite will protect her mind but not her body, the physical toll of resisting the amplified Warspire while fighting will be enormous. She could win, but it might kill her.

## Act 3: The Championship (45-60 minutes)

### Scene 6: The Arena

**Setting:** The main Dueling Ring. The semifinals and finals.

**What happens:** The PCs must resolve the crisis. Multiple approaches:

**Option A: Remove the shard.** Replace the raw fragment with the real Ember Guild shard (requires finding the original, which Ashworth has hidden in his office safe, DC 15 Thieves' Tools or DC 14 Persuasion to get the combination). Swapping the shards mid-tournament requires a distraction (social or physical) and DC 14 Sleight of Hand. Success: the contamination drops to normal levels. Mira's plan fails quietly.

**Option B: Expose the plot.** Present evidence to Castellan Drenn or directly to [[Yrsa Bloodhand]]. Drenn will halt the tournament immediately. Yrsa will *not* halt the tournament. She will demand the raw shard remain, use it to identify which fighters are Mira's agents (the ones who resist its influence, protected by Mira's own countermeasures), and deal with them publicly. This is Yrsa at her most tactically brilliant and most dangerous.

**Option C: Let it play out.** The PCs compete in the semifinals and finals with the contamination active. This is the hardest path. Combat encounters are against contaminated opponents who fight with reckless, berserk fury (increased damage, no regard for their own safety). The final opponent is Mira's champion, a fighter wearing a crude Kaevroth's Iron amulet that amplifies their strength at the cost of their sanity.

**Final combat (if Option C or if Yrsa handles it in Option B):**
- **Mira's Champion:** A veteran mercenary named **Harsk the Quiet** (a known NPC from the Ironmarch roster). He is wearing a raw iron amulet that has turned his eyes completely grey. He does not speak. He fights with terrifying precision and no mercy. Defeating him requires either overwhelming force or removing the amulet (DC 16 Athletics grapple, then DC 14 to remove it while he thrashes).
- When the amulet is removed, Harsk collapses, weeping. "I couldn't stop. She promised me it would just make me stronger. She promised." He means Mira.

> [!warning]- GM Only: Timeline Note
> This one-shot depicts a scenario where [[Mira the Red]] has already turned [[Harsk the Quiet]] using a Kaevroth's Iron amulet, magical coercion rather than the voluntary offer of army command described in Harsk's main arc. If running this as a standalone, no adjustment needed. If slotting into [[Adventures/Campaigns/The Iron Ascendant/Campaign Overview|The Iron Ascendant]] campaign, place this in Act I (Sessions 2-4), before Harsk has made his own choice about Mira's offer.

### Scene 7: The Thane's Judgment

**Setting:** The Iron Seat, after the tournament.

**What happens:** [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] summons the PCs privately. The public ceremony is over (the tournament was either completed, halted, or ended in chaos, depending on the PCs' choices). In private, Yrsa is different: exhausted, direct, and grateful.

**If the PCs solved it cleanly (Option A):** "You saved my city from a bloodbath without anyone knowing there was a threat. That is rare. I value discretion." She offers a favor: passage through Ironmarch territories, an introduction to the Ember Guild's inner circle, or a piece of refined Kaevroth's Iron.

**If the PCs exposed it (Option B):** "You gave me a weapon and I used it. Mira's agents are in my cells. Her plan is public. She will think twice before her next move." She offers the same rewards, plus: "I owe you a truth. Ask me one question. I will answer honestly." (This is how the PCs can learn about the Morrhaelite shard.)

**If the PCs fought through it (Option C):** "You walked into the Warspire's shadow and kept your minds. That impresses me more than the fighting." She offers the refined Kaevroth's Iron shard as a personal gift, plus a standing invitation to her war-council.

**Regardless of outcome, Yrsa adds:** "Mira is coming. Not today. Not this season. But she is coming. And when she does, the Ironmarch will burn. I need allies who can think as well as fight. Remember that."

---

## Epilogue

The tournament ends. Ironhaven returns to its tense, functional peace. The Warspire still hums. The contamination remains, ambient, persistent, the cost of living in a dead war-god's shadow.

[[Mira the Red]] receives word that her plan failed. She does not rage. She takes notes. The next plan will be better.

The PCs leave Ironhaven with scars, stories, and the knowledge that somewhere in the Ironmarch, a young woman with iron-grey eyes is building an army, and the dead god whose fragments litter the landscape is whispering to her through every piece.

> [!warning]- GM Only: Campaign Hooks
> - [[Yrsa Bloodhand]]'s Morrhaelite secret can be revealed or kept, either way she becomes a valuable recurring NPC
> - [[Mira the Red]]'s failed plot establishes her as a recurring antagonist whose plans grow more sophisticated
> - The Kaevroth's Iron contamination mechanics introduced here can recur whenever the PCs are near major fragments
> - [[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal]] becomes a reliable Ironmarch contact
> - The feedback loop between fragments (prize shard + Warspire) foreshadows the larger divine-material interactions that drive Act II-III

---

*Iron endures. So does ambition. The question is which one breaks first.*
