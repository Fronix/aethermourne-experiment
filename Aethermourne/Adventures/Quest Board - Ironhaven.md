# Quest Board: Ironhaven

*The iron hums. The dueling rings never cool. Every smile here is a negotiation.*

---

## 1. The Warspire Readings

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]], [[The Ember Guild]] monitor |
> | **Objective** | Assist Ashworth in placing new monitoring equipment on the Warspire's upper reaches, where war-essence output has spiked to unprecedented levels |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 250 gold, Ember Guild research access, Ashworth's data on fragment migration (campaign-significant intelligence) |
> | **Complication** | The Warspire's upper reaches are saturated with war-essence. Prolonged exposure causes aggression spikes, paranoia, and compulsive violence. The equipment must be placed precisely, which takes time the party's minds may not have. |

**Hook:** "Ashworth meets the party in his cramped office above the Red Market, surrounded by charts covered in numbers that trend in the wrong direction. His hands shake, though not from fear. 'The Warspire's output has increased twelve percent in six weeks. That is not fluctuation. That is acceleration. I requested an evacuation contingency meeting. The Iron Thane refused. I need monitoring equipment placed at the sixty-foot mark to confirm what I already know, but no one will climb it. The last team came down swinging at each other.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Ashworth's data is correct and terrifying. The Warspire is not malfunctioning; it is *reaching* toward the Heart of Iron buried beneath [[Ostivaar]] in the [[The Ashen Dominion|Ashen Dominion]]. Every Kaevroth fragment on the continent is slowly migrating toward the Heart as the [[Aetheric Web]] weakens under Serith's influence. If the Warspire physically moves, it will take half of Ironhaven's plateau with it. Placing the monitoring equipment confirms the acceleration curve and reveals something Ashworth did not expect: the Warspire is resonating at a frequency that matches fragments in the Shardfall, suggesting coordinated movement. His previous reports to the Ember Guild headquarters were intercepted by [[The Cinders]], meaning the Ashen Dominion knows about fragment migration and is not acting, because certain elements believe they can harness the reassembled Kaevroth as a weapon against Serith. They are catastrophically wrong.

## 2. The Silent Company's Silence

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal]], Head of the [[The Quartermasters Circle|Quartermasters Circle]] |
> | **Objective** | Determine why [[Harsk the Quiet]] and his Silent Company have not responded to three consecutive contract offers, and whether Harsk's condition is deteriorating |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | 300 gold, Quartermasters Circle favor, intelligence on mercenary company movements across the Ironmarch |
> | **Complication** | Harsk has not spoken in seven years. His company follows him with near-religious devotion. If Harsk is compromised, three hundred elite fighters become an uncontrolled variable in an already unstable region. |

**Hook:** "Voss adjusts his spectacles and slides three unopened contract scrolls across his desk. 'Harsk the Quiet has not acknowledged these. He has not acknowledged anything in two months. His company is camped outside the city. They drill. They eat. They do not take contracts. They do not leave. For a mercenary company, inaction is not rest. It is a symptom.' Voss folds his hands. 'I am a pacifist, which means I solve problems before they require swords. This problem is approaching the sword threshold rapidly.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Harsk is losing his battle with the fragment of Kaevroth's consciousness lodged in his mind. Seven years ago, he touched a Kaevroth fragment bare-handed and experienced the Theomachis from inside, the god's death, the rage, the scattering. That divine memory has been looping in his head ever since, which is why he stopped speaking: he is afraid that if he opens his mouth, what comes out will not be words. He is right to be afraid. The memory is intensifying as fragment migration accelerates, and Harsk is effectively a living Kaevroth fragment. Other fragments resonate when he enters a room. [[Mira the Red]]'s agents have noticed this and reported it. If the party can help Harsk release or contain the divine memory, he becomes a powerful ally with unique insight into Kaevroth's nature. If they cannot, the memory will eventually overwhelm him, and when he finally speaks, it will be Kaevroth's war-cry, driving everyone who hears it into berserker frenzy. Three hundred elite fighters becoming a weapon of mass destruction in the heart of Ironhaven.

## 3. The Red Market Contamination

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Sira Embersteel]], master smith |
> | **Objective** | Trace a shipment of unprocessed Kaevroth's Iron being sold as refined stock in the Red Market, and identify who is bypassing the Ember Guild's containment protocols |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 200 gold, a custom-forged blade from Sira (the finest weaponsmith on the continent), intelligence on black-market fragment trade |
> | **Complication** | The contaminated stock has already been sold to three buyers. The weapons forged from it will corrupt their wielders within months. Recalling them means admitting the Red Market's quality controls failed. |

**Hook:** "Sira does not look up from her forge when the party enters. 'I bought iron from the Market three days ago. Refined stock, Guild-certified seals. I put it on the anvil and it sang.' She pauses, hammer hovering. 'Refined iron does not sing. Raw iron sings. Someone is selling unprocessed Kaevroth's Iron with forged Guild seals, and every blade made from it will turn its wielder into a monster. I know because I made that mistake once. I will not describe what happened.' She sets the hammer down. 'Find who is doing this. I will pay from my own purse.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The contaminated stock is being funneled through a Red Market merchant named Cassen Drey, a middleman who genuinely does not know his supply is unprocessed. His supplier is one of [[Mira the Red]]'s agents, running raw Kaevroth's Iron from the Shardfall through Drey's legitimate operation. The goal is not profit but sabotage: spreading uncontained war-essence through Ironhaven's population to destabilize [[Yrsa Bloodhand]]'s control before Mira's challenge. The three buyers who already purchased contaminated stock are a Vigil armorer supplying the Contested Corridor garrison, a Hollowed Reach trade delegation equipping caravan guards, and a private collector who commissioned a ceremonial blade for [[Hierarch Valdren III]]. Each represents a different crisis if the contamination is not intercepted. The ceremonial blade heading to the Hierarch is the most dangerous: Kaevroth's Iron in the hands of the ruler sitting above the Heart of Iron.

## 4. Mira's Envoy

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Yrsa Bloodhand]], Iron Thane |
> | **Objective** | Attend the arrival of [[Mira the Red]]'s formal envoy demanding Yrsa's abdication, and identify the real purpose behind the diplomatic theater |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | 350 gold, Iron Thane's confidence, war-council intelligence, a Morrhaelite shard (minor, enough to dampen war-essence exposure for one person) |
> | **Complication** | The envoy is polite, professional, and reasonable. Mira's terms are generous. Several of Yrsa's own war-council members are quietly considering whether abdication might prevent a war Ironhaven cannot win. |

**Hook:** "Yrsa receives the party in the Iron Seat, directly beneath the Warspire. The air tastes of copper. She speaks without preamble: 'Mira the Red has sent an envoy. Formal challenge. She demands I abdicate or face her army in the field. Her terms are generous, which means she is confident, which means she has something I do not know about. I need people in that meeting room who are not on my war-council, not subject to my chain of command, and not susceptible to whatever she is actually doing with this envoy. Outsiders. You.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The envoy is a distraction. While the diplomatic meeting occupies Yrsa's attention, Mira's real agents are in Ironhaven for two purposes: first, to make contact with war-council members who might defect (three of eleven are considering it, swayed not by Mira's arguments but by the Warspire's increasing influence on their judgment). Second, to retrieve something from the Warspire itself. Mira has learned, through the voice of the iron, that fragments can be *called* if another fragment is brought close enough. She has sent an operative carrying a Kaevroth shard concealed in a diplomatic gift, intending to place it at the Warspire's base and accelerate the migration. If the shard is planted, the Warspire's output doubles within days, driving Ironhaven's population toward violence and making Yrsa's position untenable without a fight. The party must identify both the political manipulation and the physical sabotage. Yrsa's secret, the Morrhaelite shard she wears against her skin to dampen war-essence, is relevant: if she reveals it, her war-council may see it as weakness. If she does not, she has no way to explain why she alone resists the Warspire's influence.

## 5. The Duskfield Convoy

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal]], via the [[The Quartermasters Circle|Quartermasters Circle]] |
> | **Objective** | Escort a critical food convoy from [[Duskfield]] to Ironhaven through territory where three previous convoys have been raided |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 200 gold, Duskfield safe-passage token, Quartermasters Circle contract priority |
> | **Complication** | Duskfield is the only reliable food source in the Ironmarch. Both Yrsa and Mira have respected its neutrality. Someone is not respecting it anymore, and identifying who threatens the region's fragile balance. |

**Hook:** "Voss's office smells of ink and anxiety. 'Duskfield has declared neutrality. Both the Iron Thane and Mira the Red have honored it, because both need the food. Three convoys raided in two months. If Duskfield stops sending food, Ironhaven starves. If Ironhaven starves, Yrsa falls without Mira lifting a blade. I need this convoy to arrive. More importantly, I need to know who is raiding it, because the answer determines whether we are facing opportunistic bandits or the opening move of a war.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The raiders are not Mira's forces, though that is what they want everyone to believe. They are a Cinder operation from the [[The Ashen Dominion|Ashen Dominion]], wearing salvaged Ironmarch warlord armor and flying Mira's colors. The Dominion's goal is to provoke open war between Yrsa and Mira, weakening both sides so the Dominion can secure permanent control of the Contested Corridor during the chaos. The Cinder squad leader carries encrypted communications that, if captured, prove Dominion involvement. Exposing the operation gives Yrsa and Voss leverage against the Dominion, but also reveals the uncomfortable truth that the Ashen Dominion considers the Ironmarch a problem to be managed, not a neighbor to be respected. If the party kills all the raiders without capturing evidence, the blame falls on Mira, and war becomes inevitable.

## 6. The Smith's Impossible Commission

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Sira Embersteel]], master smith |
> | **Objective** | Acquire a stabilizing agent, specifically a fragment of Ashite or Thyrea's Amber, that Sira needs to forge a Kaevroth's Iron blade that does not corrupt its wielder |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | The blade itself (a weapon of extraordinary quality without war-essence contamination), Sira's lifelong alliance, proof that divine materials can be combined constructively |
> | **Complication** | Ashite is controlled by the Ashen Dominion's Church. Thyrea's Amber is sacred to the Verdant Marches. Neither will sell to an Ironmarch weaponsmith. The party must acquire the material through diplomacy, trade, theft, or discovering an unknown source. |

**Hook:** "Sira invites the party to her workshop after hours. The forge is cold, which is wrong, a smith's forge should never be cold. She shows them a sketch pinned to the wall: a greatsword, elegant, lethal. 'Three years I have been trying to forge a blade from Kaevroth's Iron that does not turn its wielder into a murderer. I came close once, using a sliver of Ashite to counteract the war-essence. It held for two weeks before shattering.' She taps the sketch. 'I need more. A proper quantity. Ashite, Thyrea's Amber, anything that carries a stabilizing divine essence. Get it for me, and I will forge something the world has never seen: a weapon that proves the gods' remains can heal as well as harm.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Sira's research is genuine and campaign-significant. A successfully forged hybrid blade would be the first proof that divine materials from different gods can be combined constructively, a discovery with implications far beyond a single weapon. The closest source of a stabilizing agent is a Thyrea fragment buried beneath [[Duskfield]], which Sira does not know about. [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]] has detected unusual readings from Duskfield's soil but has not identified the source. If the party connects Ashworth's data to Sira's need, they unlock both a powerful weapon and a new understanding of divine-material interaction. The [[The Ashen Dominion|Ashen Dominion]]'s diplomatic mission currently in Ironhaven has a small quantity of consecrated Ashite, technically sufficient for Sira's purposes, but acquiring it means negotiating with or stealing from a Dominion delegation that is already suspicious of Ironmarch intentions. [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] would be intensely interested in this research: a blade that resists Kaevroth's corruption is what she has needed for eleven years.
