# Quest Board: Roothold

> [!info]
> Quests available in [[Roothold]], de facto capital of [[The Verdant Marches]]. Mix of wilderness survival, clan politics, Thyrea's Cradle exploration, and Scarwall border tension.

---

## 1. The Silent Greensinger

**Hook:** A young Greensinger named Tessara Wildbloom entered deep communion with the root-network three weeks ago. Her body is still seated in the Greensinger Grove, breathing, eyes open, but her mind has not returned. The Greensingers have tried to reach her. Something in the roots is holding her, or she is holding onto something she found there.

**Quest Giver:** [[Elder Rosk]], Eldest Greensinger. He is frightened, which is itself frightening, because nothing has frightened Elder Rosk in eight hundred years.

**Objective:** Enter the deep root-network through guided communion (Rosk will facilitate) and find Tessara's consciousness. Determine what she encountered. Bring her back, if she can be brought back.

**Recommended Level:** 5-7

**Reward:** Greensinger gratitude, Elder Rosk's trust (access to the Grove and deep lore about Thyrea's awareness), and whatever Tessara discovered in the roots, which may be campaign-significant.

**Complication:** Tessara found the shadow corruption that Rosk suspects but cannot locate. Her consciousness is entangled with a dark presence in the deep root-network, something hungry, whispering, patient. Extracting her means confronting the corruption directly in a psychic landscape where combat is metaphorical and the enemy is doubt, despair, and the seductive promise that everything would be easier if the PCs just stopped fighting. The corruption is Serith's influence, but the PCs should not learn this yet. They should only know it feels *wrong*, alien, and old.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Tessara found Serith's shadow-root tendrils in the deep network. She tried to trace them to their source and was caught. Her consciousness is being slowly consumed, not killed but *read*, Serith is extracting everything Tessara knows about the Greensingers, the root-network, and Kaelith Thornborn's divine connection. If the PCs fail, Serith gains a complete map of the Marches' magical infrastructure. If they succeed, Tessara returns with fragmentary but critical intelligence: the corruption has a source, it is coming from the ocean floor, and it is connected to something imprisoned beneath the sea.

## 2. The Unification Vote

**Hook:** [[Kaelith Thornborn]] has called for a formal vote on Marches unification at the next Moot. She has four of twelve Marchwardens confirmed, needs seven. Three are undecided. [[Draven Ironbark]] leads the opposition with four. The vote is in two weeks. Both sides are courting the PCs.

**Quest Giver:** Kaelith Thornborn (asking for help persuading the undecided Marchwardens) or Draven Ironbark (asking the PCs to investigate Kaelith's "true ambitions" and protect clan sovereignty).

**Objective:** Navigate Marchwarden politics. Meet the three undecided Marchwardens, learn what each needs, and decide who to support. Each undecided Marchwarden has a specific concern that must be addressed:
- **Marchwarden Senna Deepmoss** (Mirewatch): Wants proof that unification will address the Marrow Bogs expansion, not just military threats.
- **Marchwarden Othren Ashbark** (Northern border): Wants guaranteed military protection for his territory against Dominion incursion before committing.
- **Marchwarden Lissa Fernwalker** (Eastern clans): Wants assurance that clan customs, laws, and land rights will be preserved under unified command.

**Recommended Level:** 4-6

**Reward:** Political influence in the Verdant Marches, alliance with whichever faction the PCs supported, and a seat at the table when the Marches' future is decided.

**Complication:** [[The Cinders]] have operatives feeding Draven fabricated intelligence about Kaelith's "true ambitions," making him more intransigent. If the PCs expose the Cinder manipulation, Draven's opposition softens dramatically. But the evidence is hidden in a dead drop in the northern forest, guarded by a Cinder agent who will destroy it rather than let it be captured.

## 3. The Marrow Bogs Investigation

**Hook:** The Marrow Bogs are expanding. New areas are becoming saturated with Marrow-Water. The substance is growing more potent. And it now carries a secondary effect that [[Vael Sundren]], Chief Researcher at Mirewatch, cannot explain: everyone who touches the new Marrow-Water feels despair, a bone-deep conviction that nothing matters and nothing will improve.

**Quest Giver:** [[Vael Sundren]], via a research request posted at the Traderoot market. She needs outside perspectives, someone not steeped in Marches tradition who can examine her data with fresh eyes.

**Objective:** Travel to Mirewatch. Review Vael's data. Investigate the Bogs' expansion by taking samples from the newest saturated areas. Determine why the Marrow-Water is changing.

**Recommended Level:** 5-7

**Reward:** Vael's expertise and alchemical knowledge (she can brew custom potions and identify unknown substances), proof of the corruption's source (campaign-significant evidence), and Marchwarden Senna Deepmoss's vote if the evidence is presented to her.

**Complication:** The investigation requires entering the newest Bog areas, where Marrow-Water concentration is dangerously high. Environmental hazards include quicksand-like Marrow pools, mutagenic spores, and creatures already transformed by the potent new substance. The deeper the PCs go, the stronger the despair effect becomes. At the source, they find a root-node where something dark has grafted itself onto Thyrea's root network, pumping corruption into the divine essence. Destroying the node is possible but triggers a violent reaction from the surrounding ecosystem, which interprets the intervention as an attack.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The corruption source is a shadow-root tendril from Serith's prison in the Abyssal Trench. It has traveled through the ocean floor, up through coastal root systems, and deep into the Marches' network. Destroying this particular node slows the contamination but does not stop it: there are more nodes, deeper, closer to Thyrea's Cradle Heart. This quest should give the PCs their first concrete evidence that the Second Silence is not natural entropy but deliberate sabotage originating from the ocean.

## 4. The Scarwall Awakening

**Hook:** The Scarwall, the living thorny fortification along the Marches' border with [[The Ashen Dominion]], is growing thicker and more aggressive than at any point in living memory. Thorns that once deterred travelers now actively lash out at anyone within fifty feet. Three border patrols have been injured. A Dominion trade caravan was destroyed, all passengers alive but cocooned in thorns, suspended twenty feet in the air.

**Quest Giver:** Marchwarden Othren Ashbark, via urgent message to Roothold. He needs help before the Dominion interprets the Scarwall's aggression as an act of war.

**Objective:** Travel to the northern border. Investigate why the Scarwall is agitating. Calm it if possible (Greensingers can communicate with it, but it is not responding to their usual methods). Free the Dominion caravan survivors before diplomatic tensions escalate.

**Recommended Level:** 4-6

**Reward:** Marchwarden Ashbark's trust and his vote in the unification debate, diplomatic goodwill with the Dominion caravan merchants, and Greensinger intelligence about what the Scarwall is responding to.

**Complication:** The Scarwall is responding to a genuine threat: a Cinder scout team has been probing the border, looking for paths through the fortification. The Scarwall detected them before anyone else did. Calming the Scarwall means removing the threat, which means finding and confronting the Cinder scouts in the dense border forest. The scouts are well-trained, well-equipped, and operating under orders to map Marches defenses for a potential Dominion military operation. Capturing them provides evidence of Dominion aggression. Killing them eliminates the evidence and leaves the question unanswered.

## 5. The Cradle Expedition

**Hook:** Elder Rosk has authorized a rare expedition into Thyrea's Cradle Heart, the deep primeval forest growing from the body of the dead goddess. Something is wrong at the Cradle's center. The land's awareness, the semi-sentient presence that the Greensingers commune with, is withdrawing inward, curling up, as if preparing for a wound that has not yet arrived.

**Quest Giver:** [[Elder Rosk]], with the formal backing of the Greensinger Circle.

**Objective:** Navigate the three layers of the Cradle (Floor, Mid-Canopy, Crown) to reach the Cradle Heart. Determine what is causing the land's withdrawal. Report back to the Greensingers.

**Recommended Level:** 8-10

**Reward:** Deep lore about Thyrea's nature and the divine ecology of the Marches, Greensinger initiation (PCs gain the ability to perform basic communion with the land), and a Thyrea's Amber artifact of significant power.

**Complication:** The Cradle is alive, and it is afraid. Environmental hazards escalate with each layer: the Floor has mutagenic fungi and territorial plant-creatures. The Mid-Canopy has **Thyrea's Children**, massive plant-beings that may be fragments of the goddess's consciousness, and they are panicked, attacking anything they perceive as a threat. The Crown is beautiful and serene, but reaching the Cradle Heart requires passing through a barrier of pure primal energy that tests the PCs' connection to the natural world.

At the Cradle Heart, the PCs find the source of the withdrawal: a shadow-root, identical to the ones in the Marrow Bogs, has reached the Cradle's deepest layer. Thyrea's distributed consciousness is pulling away from it, trying to isolate the corruption, but the effort is consuming her remaining coherence. If the shadow-root is not severed, Thyrea's awareness will fragment permanently, and the Marches will lose the semi-sentient land-connection that defines the region.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> This is the Verdant Marches' endgame crisis. If the PCs sever the shadow-root at the Cradle Heart, they buy time, but Serith will send more. If they trace the root to its origin (the Abyssal Trench), they discover the full scope of Serith's infiltration. The Cradle expedition should be one of the campaign's most emotionally resonant experiences: a journey into the body of a dead goddess who is still trying, with the last of her strength, to protect the world she grew.

## 6. The Dominion Spy

**Hook:** A Dominion merchant was caught in the Traderoot market selling diluted Ashite to Marches herbalists. When confronted, he claimed he was sent as a spy, but not by the Dominion. He says he was approached by a "woman with no shadow" who offered him gold to gather intelligence on Greensinger communion rituals. He is terrified. He is also lying about some things and telling the truth about others.

**Quest Giver:** The Traderoot market authorities, via public arrest and call for investigation. Or Draven Ironbark, who sees this as proof of outlander treachery.

**Objective:** Interrogate the merchant. Determine who sent him, what intelligence he gathered, and who the "woman with no shadow" is. Follow the trail to its source.

**Recommended Level:** 4-6

**Reward:** Intelligence about Veil Unbound operations in the Marches, evidence that can influence the unification debate (proving external threats are real), and the merchant's genuine trade contacts (useful for acquiring Dominion goods).

**Complication:** The "woman with no shadow" is a [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] operative. She is not interested in military intelligence. She wants to understand how Greensinger communion works because Serith's shadow-roots are attempting to co-opt the communion network. The Veil Unbound already knows about the corruption (they helped plant it) and they need to understand the Greensingers' defenses to circumvent them. The merchant is a pawn. The real target is the communion ritual itself. If the PCs follow the trail far enough, they encounter the operative, who offers them a deal: information about Serith's plan in exchange for a sample of the Greensinger communion protocol. The deal is genuine. The information is accurate. And accepting it would hand Serith's agents the keys to the Marches' defenses.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> This quest connects the Verdant Marches arc to the campaign's central Serith plot. The Veil Unbound operative knows that Serith is siphoning ley line energy to free herself, and she knows that the shadow-roots in the Marches are a secondary operation, not Serith's main plan. She will share this freely because she believes the PCs cannot stop what is coming. She may be wrong. But the information she provides should reframe the PCs' understanding of the Second Silence's true nature.
