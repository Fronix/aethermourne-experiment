# Quest Board: Tidecross

*The collision point of two continents. Everything is negotiable. Nothing is safe.*

---

## 1. The Vanishing Oldborn

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Captain Erys Vane]], de facto harbormaster |
> | **Objective** | Find [[Vosh]], the Oldborn observer, who has been missing for three days |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | 300 gold, Vane's political backing, potentially preventing a war |
> | **Complication** | The Oldborn have issued a one-week ultimatum: return Vosh or face retaliation. The clock is ticking. |

**Hook:** "Vane's hands are white-knuckled on the harbor rail. The Oldborn's message was clear: one week. No extensions. No negotiations. If Vosh is not returned unharmed, they will consider it an act of war. Three days are already gone."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Vosh was not kidnapped. They discovered a [[The Veil Unbound]] ritual site in the sea caves beneath the Oldborn Docks and went to investigate alone. They are alive but trapped, sealed inside a cave by a Veil Unbound ward that reacts to Primordial-attuned biology. An outlander can pass through the ward. An Oldborn cannot. Rescuing Vosh reveals the Veil Unbound's presence in Tidecross and earns the Oldborn's grudging respect.

## 2. The Archive Heist

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Arch-Reader Celestine Vael]] (via intermediary) |
> | **Objective** | Defend the Archive House from a [[The Veil Unbound]] raid, or recover the Loom trace if the raid succeeds |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | 400 gold, Star-Reader research access, information about the Loom's Edge |
> | **Complication** | The Veil Unbound has inside help: a Star-Reader initiate who believes the trace should be used, not studied. |

**Hook:** "A coded message arrives from Celestine Vael's network: the Veil Unbound is moving on the Archive within 48 hours. The Star-Readers cannot defend it alone. The Concord garrison is too small. She needs capable outsiders who are not politically compromised."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Archive contains a dormant excised fate-thread recovered from the Veilshore's Tidemark Cliffs. It is the first physical evidence of the [[The Loom's Edge]]'s influence extending beyond the Unwritten Lands' interior. The Veil Unbound wants it because Serith believes the excised patterns can be used to rewrite reality. The Star-Readers want to study it because it may reveal how to stabilize the Loom. Both sides are partially right.

## 3. The Fernwald Trial

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Prior Cael Drantine]], Ashen Chapel |
> | **Objective** | Retrieve an Ashen Dominion missionary who entered the [[The Veilshore#The Fernwald\|Fernwald]] without permission and was "changed" by the forest |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 150 gold, Dominion supply access, Drantine's earnest gratitude |
> | **Complication** | The Oldborn demand the missionary be surrendered for judgment under their law. The Dominion refuses. The missionary speaks only Oldborn and carries knowledge of both cultures. |

**Hook:** "Drantine is distraught. Brother Aldis walked into the Fernwald three days ago carrying a relic of [[The Twelve#Solvaen\|Solvaen]]. The forest rejected him. He was found alive but speaking a language he never learned. The Oldborn want him. The Dominion wants him back. Drantine just wants his friend safe."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Fernwald did not punish Brother Aldis. It *taught* him. The forest's Primordial intelligence detected the Ashite relic and responded by overwriting Aldis's linguistic framework with Oldborn comprehension, a forced education in Primordial-pact communication. Aldis now understands both divine and Primordial magic at a theoretical level no one else on the Veilshore possesses. He is the most valuable person in Tidecross, and he is terrified.

## 4. The Black Sand Rising

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Salice]], smuggler queen of the Wrack |
> | **Objective** | Investigate what is moving beneath the black sand of the Crossroads Market |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 200 gold, free passage through the Wrack, one favor from Salice's information network |
> | **Complication** | The market is Tidecross's economic lifeline. If it closes, the settlement collapses. But the sand is getting worse. |

**Hook:** "Salice leans against a crate in the Wrack tunnel, arms crossed. The market is dying. Merchants won't set up on the black sand anymore, not after last week when shapes pressed up from below. Salice doesn't care about supernatural nonsense. She cares about trade. Find out what's under the sand and make it stop."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Oldborn Docks beneath Tidecross are a Primordial listening post, and the black sand is its sensory apparatus. What is "rising" beneath the market is the listening post activating in response to the increasing concentration of divine residue from outlander presence. The shapes are not entities; they are the sand attempting to *analyze* the foreign energy. The Oldborn know this. They are waiting to see if the outlanders figure it out. If the party does, it is a major diplomatic breakthrough. If they respond with violence (trying to destroy whatever is beneath the sand), they damage the Oldborn Docks and guarantee war.

## 5. Yvenne's Shrine

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Tidecaller Mirenne]], senior priest |
> | **Objective** | Investigate why the Tidecaller Shrine is bleeding seawater and determine if [[The Twelve#Yvenne\|Yvenne]] is manifesting or if something else is pushing through |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | Tidecaller blessing (roll twice take higher on ocean travel checks), critical information about Yvenne's mental state |
> | **Complication** | The answer may require entering the ocean during a period of Yvenne's instability. |

**Hook:** "Mirenne's robes are soaked. The shrine walls bleed seawater that tastes of tears. The daily rites are holding, barely. She believes Yvenne is trying to communicate, but the message is garbled by pain, madness, or interference. She needs someone to listen more closely than prayer allows."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The shrine is a breach point. Not for Yvenne, for [[The Twelve#Serith\|Serith]]. Yvenne's weakening seal is creating micro-fractures in reality along coastlines where her influence is strong. The seawater is not Yvenne manifesting, it is the ocean itself becoming a conduit for Serith's whispers. Investigating the shrine reveals that Serith's voice can now reach the Unwritten Lands, a terrifying escalation that means her prison is failing faster than anyone realized.

## 6. The Smuggler's Dilemma

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Salice]], smuggler queen of the Wrack |
> | **Objective** | Escort a shipment of stolen Oldborn resonance crystals to a buyer on an incoming [[The Hollowed Reach\|Concord]] vessel |
> | **Difficulty** | Low |
> | **Reward** | 250 gold, a resonance crystal of the party's own, Wrack safe-passage token |
> | **Complication** | The Oldborn know the crystals were stolen. A patrol is searching the harbor. And the buyer may not be who Salice thinks they are. |

**Hook:** "Salice needs a simple job done. Move cargo from the Wrack to Pier 7. Don't get caught. Don't ask questions. The pay is excellent because the risk is real, but the risk is not what Salice thinks it is."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The buyer is a [[The Veil Unbound]] agent posing as a Concord merchant. The resonance crystals can be modified to amplify Serith's whispers, creating portable Sleepwalker-induction devices. Salice does not know this. Completing the delivery arms the Veil Unbound. Refusing it puts the party on Salice's bad side. Exposing the buyer earns Oldborn favor (the stolen crystals are returned) but burns the party's relationship with the Wrack's smuggling network.
