# Quest Board: Crucible

> [!info]
> Quests available in [[Crucible]], capital of [[The Emberveil]]. Mix of forge-craft challenges, Deep Warren exploration, political intrigue, and Thalvor-related mysteries.

---

## 1. The Dimming Forge

**Hook:** Rumors are spreading through Crucible's Ring Markets that the Grand Forge flickered three times this year. The [[The Forge Council|Forge Council]] denies everything. But the forge-keepers are whispering, and the temperature in the lower rings has dropped two degrees.

**Quest Giver:** [[Corrin Ashveil]], Factor and Keeper of the Ember Archive. He has found historical records showing the Grand Forge fluctuated before, always preceding major crises. He needs access to sealed records that [[Brunhild Ironheart|Forge-Matriarch Ironheart]] is withholding.

**Objective:** Investigate the Grand Forge's dimming. Gain access to the Ember Archive's sealed records (through persuasion, political leverage, or subterfuge). Determine whether the Cooling is natural entropy, the Second Silence reaching Thalvor's fire, or something else.

**Recommended Level:** 5-7

**Reward:** Corrin's historical research (campaign-significant information about Thalvor's sacrifice and the forge-chambers), Forge Council favor, and access to restricted areas of the Archive.

**Complication:** Forge-Matriarch Ironheart is suppressing the information because she believes panic will collapse the Emberveil's economy. She is not wrong. If the PCs obtain the records and go public, the consequences are economic devastation and political crisis. If they keep the secret, they become complicit in a cover-up that could cost lives if the Cooling accelerates.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Cooling is real. Thalvor's fire is weakening because the Second Silence is reaching even the deepest divine remnants. The sealed records contain a pre-Theomachis account of the forge-chambers that describes Thalvor's sacrifice in detail, including a passage suggesting the fire can be "rekindled" if someone descends to the core and offers a sacrifice of equivalent devotion. This is foreshadowing for a late-campaign choice.

## 2. The Apprentice's Trial

**Hook:** [[Apprentice Jyn]], the first non-dwarf accepted to the Grand Forge in a generation, faces their guild examination at the Proving Grounds. Traditionalists want them expelled. Progressives see them as proof that Thalvor's gift belongs to all who can receive it. Both sides are pressuring the PCs to intervene.

**Quest Giver:** Apprentice Jyn (directly, nervously, asking for help preparing) or [[Brunhild Ironheart]] (indirectly, wanting the PCs to ensure the examination is fair and no one gets hurt).

**Objective:** Help Jyn prepare for the guild examination, navigate the political minefield surrounding it, and ensure the Proving is conducted fairly. Investigate Jyn's true nature: they were born above a deep geothermal vent and are literally touched by Thalvor's fire.

**Recommended Level:** 3-5

**Reward:** Jyn's loyalty and friendship (a talented forge-mage ally), Ember Guild contacts, and political capital with whichever faction the PCs supported.

**Complication:** A saboteur is planning to rig the Proving to ensure Jyn fails spectacularly and publicly. The saboteur is not a simple bigot but a guild elder who genuinely believes a non-dwarf at the Grand Forge weakens the sacred tradition that gives dwarven culture its identity. Their motivations are sympathetic. Their methods are not.

## 3. The Deep Warren Expedition

**Hook:** A mining crew in the Deep Warrens broke through into a previously unknown chamber two weeks ago. The chamber is warm, too warm, and the walls are made of metal that vibrates when touched. Three miners entered. One returned, speaking in a voice that was not her own. She said: *"The smith remembers."* Then she collapsed and has not woken.

**Quest Giver:** [[The Forge Council]], via a discreet posting. They do not want this public. The miners were operating in a restricted zone near the forge-chambers.

**Objective:** Descend into the Deep Warrens, find the unknown chamber, determine what the miner encountered, and report back. Recover the two missing miners if possible.

**Recommended Level:** 7-9

**Reward:** Forgeheart steel (a small quantity, enough for one weapon or tool), Forge Council trust, and knowledge about the forge-chambers that the Ember Guild's inner circle has kept secret for centuries.

**Complication:** The chamber is a remnant of Thalvor's original workshop, a space where the god worked before his sacrifice. His tools are still here, massive, divine-scale implements that radiate creative energy. The missing miners are alive but have been transformed by exposure, their hands have become forge-tools, their skin has taken on the amber glow of forgeheart steel. They are not in pain. They are *making things*, compulsively, ceaselessly, producing objects of heartbreaking beauty that serve no purpose anyone can identify. Bringing them back means interrupting whatever Thalvor's residual memory is creating through them.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The chamber is Thalvor's Last Workshop, the place where he built the mechanism for his own sacrifice. The tools respond to master-level crafters, granting temporary divine-tier crafting ability at the cost of Tier 2 forgeheart exposure. The objects the transformed miners are creating are components, pieces of something Thalvor was working on when the Theomachis interrupted him. Assembled, they form a map to the world's core, to the place where Thalvor burns.

## 4. The Dominion Delegation

**Hook:** An official trade delegation from [[The Ashen Dominion]] has arrived in Crucible to renegotiate forgeheart steel quotas. They are polite, professional, and almost certainly not just here for trade. [[Tarn Smokeshield|Warden Smokeshield]] has warned the Forge Council that he captured a Dominion scout with encrypted Cinder communications three months ago. The Council is reluctant to provoke an ally openly.

**Quest Giver:** Warden Tarn Smokeshield, via a private meeting at Smokewall or a message passed through military contacts.

**Objective:** Identify the Cinder agent operating within the Dominion delegation. Determine what intelligence they are gathering (forge-chamber locations? Fragment Registry? military readiness?) and neutralize the operation without triggering a diplomatic incident.

**Recommended Level:** 5-7

**Reward:** Smokeshield's trust and military intelligence network, evidence of Dominion espionage that gives the Forge Council leverage in trade negotiations, and potential identification of [[The Cinders]] operational methods.

**Complication:** The Cinder agent is the delegation's trade attaché, a charming, competent diplomat who is also genuinely negotiating favorable trade terms. Exposing them destroys a trade deal the Emberveil needs. Letting them operate risks the Fragment Registry falling into Dominion hands. The PCs may need to turn the agent rather than expose them, or find a way to feed false intelligence while preserving the trade relationship.

## 5. The Rogue Engineer

**Hook:** [[Vessa Ironvow]], an expelled Forge Council engineer with burn scars and a zealot's conviction, is running illegal mining operations in the outer warrens. She believes Thalvor's forge can be directly accessed and reignited by mortal hands. She has already killed twelve miners in a previous attempt. She does not intend to stop.

**Quest Giver:** The Forge Council (officially, they want her stopped) or Vessa herself (she approaches the PCs, believing outlanders might understand what the dwarves will not).

**Objective:** Find Vessa's illegal mining operation. Determine how close she is to the forge-chambers. Either stop her (risking the loss of her expertise and knowledge) or redirect her reckless approach toward a controlled method (risking the consequences of accessing divine fire).

**Recommended Level:** 6-8

**Reward:** If stopped: Forge Council gratitude, Vessa's research notes (valuable information about deep warren geology). If redirected: a dangerous but potentially campaign-significant alliance with a woman who is closer to Thalvor's fire than anyone alive.

**Complication:** Vessa is closer than she knows. Her current dig is three days from breaking through into an active forge-chamber. If she reaches it without preparation, the resulting eruption could destabilize the volcanic systems beneath Crucible. But her research is genuine. She has mapped geological structures no one else has charted. And she may be right that the forge can be accessed, just not the way she is attempting it.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Vessa's guilt over the twelve dead miners is the engine of her obsession. She believes that if she can reignite Thalvor's forge, their deaths will have meant something. She is not evil. She is broken, driven, and in possession of knowledge that the campaign may need. The ideal outcome is redirection, pairing her expertise with the PCs' resources and the Forge Council's institutional support. But Vessa does not trust institutions, and earning her trust requires acknowledging that her methods, while reckless, come from genuine grief.

## 6. The Fragment Registry

**Hook:** [[Torren Hearthforge|Guildmaster Torren Hearthforge]], the 400-year-old leader of the [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]], maintains the Kaevroth Fragment Registry, the most comprehensive record of divine material deposits in Aethermourne. Someone has attempted to steal it. Twice. The Guildmaster suspects the second attempt came from inside the Guild.

**Quest Giver:** Guildmaster Torren Hearthforge, via a private audience at the Ember Guild headquarters.

**Objective:** Investigate the attempted thefts. Identify the internal traitor. Determine who is funding the operation and what they intend to do with the Registry's information.

**Recommended Level:** 6-8

**Reward:** Selective access to Fragment Registry information (the GM can use this to direct the party toward specific Kaevroth fragments relevant to the campaign), Guildmaster Hearthforge's personal trust (an extremely valuable asset), and an Ember Guild masterwork item.

**Complication:** The internal traitor is a respected Guild elder who believes the Registry should be shared with all factions to prevent any single group from monopolizing divine materials. Their motives are arguably noble. Their buyer, however, is not: the intelligence is being funneled to [[The Veil Unbound]], who want Kaevroth's Iron to forge weapons capable of breaking Serith's prison. The elder does not know this. The PCs must decide whether to expose a well-intentioned traitor, protect a dangerous secret, or find a middle path.
