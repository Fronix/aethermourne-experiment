# Session Toolkits: Social & Downtime Content

*Quick-reference tables and scenes for when the plot pauses and the world needs to breathe.*

Use these between major story beats. Roll or choose. Each entry is designed to be run in 2-5 minutes with no prep beyond reading it aloud.

# The Hollowed Reach

## Tavern Encounter Table (d6)

| d6 | Encounter |
|---|---|
| 1 | A sailor slams her drink down and announces she's seen lights beneath the Trench, moving in formation. The room goes quiet. Someone at the bar mutters, "Not again." |
| 2 | Two merchants argue over a trade contract. One accuses the other of selling fish that "tastes like coins." A DC 12 Perception check reveals the accuser's hands are faintly bioluminescent. |
| 3 | A stranger in a salt-stained coat offers to buy a round for anyone willing to deliver a sealed letter to [[Veil Harbor]]. He will not say who it's for. The letter smells faintly of deep water. |
| 4 | A [[The Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keeper]] sits alone, drinking steadily. She keeps glancing at the door. DC 13 Insight: she's waiting for someone who isn't coming back. She'll talk if someone sits down. |
| 5 | A bar fight breaks out over a dice game, but when one man draws a knife, the blade glows faintly blue. Everyone freezes. Someone whispers, "Trench-touched steel." The man with the knife looks terrified of his own weapon. |
| 6 | The tide shifts mid-evening and the tavern floor floods ankle-deep for thirty seconds. The locals don't react. A barmaid mops it up without comment. The water leaves a faint dark residue that wasn't there last month. |

## Downtime Activities

**Tide-Reading (DC 13 Will).** Spend a week studying tidal patterns with a local reader. Success: learn to predict the next Black Tide 1d4 days in advance. Failure: recurring dreams of drowning for 1d6 nights (no mechanical effect, deeply unsettling).

**Dock Work.** No check required. Earn 15 silver per week. Hear 1d3 rumors from sailors (GM rolls on Social Encounter Sparks). One in six chance of witnessing a Sleepwalker incident firsthand.

**Lantern-Keeper Training (DC 14 Dex).** A week of weapons drills with Keeper veterans. Success: gain proficiency with one martial weapon, or if already proficient, learn the Keeper technique of fighting in fog (no disadvantage on attacks in obscured conditions for 24 hours after a short meditation). Failure: bruises and respect. They invite you back.

**Black Market Research (DC 13 Will).** Spend a week in Tidewall's Bilges district asking careful questions. Success: learn one piece of GM-chosen intelligence about Trench activity, [[Concord of Tides]] secrets, or smuggling routes. Failure: someone notices. A Concord agent starts asking about you.

**Shrine Contemplation.** Visit a coastal shrine to [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]]. No check. The mist around the shrine is warm and smells of salt and flowers. Recover 1 spent hit die and lose one level of exhaustion. You dream of a woman weeping, but you wake feeling strangely comforted.

## Slice of Life Scenes

> [!quote] The Fish Market at Dawn
> The docks come alive before sunrise. Fishermen haul nets heavy with silver-scaled catch, shouting prices before the fish stop moving. A woman with brine-cracked hands sorts the haul by instinct, tossing anything that glows into a separate barrel without looking. Children run between the stalls stealing scraps. The fog is thick enough to taste, and every breath carries salt, smoke, and the faint copper tang that nobody talks about. A bell rings three times from the harbor, the tide-change warning, and the market pauses for exactly one breath before resuming.

> [!quote] The Sleepwalker Watch
> It happens at the same time every night. A man in a nightshirt walks past the tavern window, eyes open, expression serene, heading toward the water. Two [[The Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keepers]] follow at a careful distance, one carrying a weighted net, the other a lantern burning Trench-wax. They do not speak. They have done this before. The man reaches the dock's edge and stops, swaying, listening to something beneath the waves. Then the Keepers gently guide him back. He will not remember this tomorrow. Neither will the Keepers mention it.

> [!quote] Children's Game: Tide or Trench
> In the streets of [[Tidewall]], children play a guessing game. One child hides a stone in their fist. The others guess: "Tide or Trench?" If the stone is smooth (tide-polished), the hider gives it up. If rough (Trench-stone), everyone runs. The rules are simple. The laughter is genuine. None of the children notice that the ratio of rough stones to smooth has been shifting all year.

| 6 | A merchant offers the PCs a job: escort a shipment to [[Keel's Landing]]. Simple work, good pay. DC 14 Insight: the cargo crates are sealed with Lantern-Keeper wax. He does not want questions about the contents. |

## Regional Flavor Details (d6)

| d6 | Detail |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Smell:** Brine and copper. The air near the docks always carries a faint metallic tang, stronger at low tide. Locals call it "the Trench breathing." |
| 2 | **Sound:** The fog deadens everything. Conversations ten paces away sound like whispers. Ships' bells ring muffled and late. At night, the silence is broken only by the wet sound of waves that seem to come from the wrong direction. |
| 3 | **Weather:** The fog never fully lifts. On "clear" days, it thins to a grey haze that makes the sun look like a pale coin. Rain comes sideways off the sea, warm and tasting of salt. |
| 4 | **Food:** Grilled rock-eel on slate tiles, served with pickled kelp and dark bread. The ale is brewed with sea-barley and has a brackish finish. The expensive drink is Veil Harbor wine, sweet and faintly luminescent. |
| 5 | **Night:** Bioluminescence pulses along the waterline in slow waves, blue-green and rhythmic. It's beautiful. It's also new, only started appearing in the last two years. Nobody knows why. |
| 6 | **Architecture:** Salt-crusted stone buildings with blue-green patina on every metal surface. Doors face away from the sea. Windows on the harbor side are small and high, as if the buildings are trying not to look at the water. |

# The Ashen Dominion

## Tavern Encounter Table (d6)

| d6 | Encounter |
|---|---|
| 1 | A [[The Cinders|Cinder]] agent sits in the corner, sketching faces in a small book. DC 13 Perception: she's drawing the PCs. If confronted, she smiles and tears out the page. "Just a hobby." |
| 2 | A miner from [[Hearthstone]] is buying rounds with Ashite dust, technically illegal outside the guild. He's celebrating: his crew found a new vein. DC 12 Insight: he's also terrified. The vein was in a section of the Spine that's supposed to be sealed. |
| 3 | Two [[Order of the Ashen Flame|Flame-Bearers]] enter and the tavern goes quiet. They order nothing. They watch. After ten minutes, they leave. The barkeep exhales and pours himself a drink with shaking hands. |
| 4 | A scholar from [[Ashveil]] is arguing with a merchant about whether the Calcified Forest is growing. The scholar has measurements. The merchant has "common sense." Both are right, and both are missing the point. |
| 5 | Someone bumps a PC's table and spills their drink. The spilled liquid forms a perfect geometric circle on the wood before soaking in. Nobody else notices. DC 14 Arcana: that's an Ashite resonance pattern. Something in this room is saturated. |
| 6 | A traveling performer sings a ballad about [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]]'s last words. The official version ends with "Order endures." Her version ends differently. The room is very quiet when she finishes. A Flame-Bearer in the back stands up slowly. |

## Downtime Activities

**Ashite Study (DC 14 Will).** Spend a week with Ashveil scholars studying Ashite properties. Success: learn to identify Ashite purity by touch and sound, useful for detecting counterfeit or contaminated samples. Failure: mild headaches and an involuntary tendency to organize things symmetrically for 1d4 days.

**Confession and Correction (DC 12 Will).** Participate in the Church's weekly ritual. No deception possible, the rite uses Ashite resonance to detect falsehood. If you confess something genuine, gain advantage on one Will save in the next week (spiritual clarity). If you resist or lie, DC 15 Will or the presiding priest knows. Consequences vary.

**Street-Level Intelligence (DC 13 Will).** Spend a week in Ostivaar's lower castes, listening. Success: learn one rumor about Church politics, Cinder operations, or Unbound Congregation activity. Failure: a Cinder informant reports your questions. You receive a polite visit from a Church functionary.

**Physical Training (DC 12 Str).** Train with the garrison. Dominion soldiers drill with geometric precision, Ashite-enhanced conditioning. Success: gain +1 to initiative rolls for the next adventure (drilled reflexes). Failure: sore muscles and a new appreciation for Dominion military discipline.

**Craft Commissioning.** No check. Spend gold to commission Ashite-worked equipment from Dominion smiths. Ashite-inlaid weapons cost 3x standard price but resist corrosion and hold an edge indefinitely. Delivery takes 1d4+2 days.

## Slice of Life Scenes

> [!quote] Dawn Prayer
> The city wakes in unison. At first light, every citizen of [[Ostivaar]] speaks the Dawn Prayer simultaneously, a low murmur that rolls through the streets like a wave. The words are simple: "Order endures. Ash remembers. We serve." Spoken by half a million voices, the sound resonates through the stone and into the bones of [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]] beneath the city. For a moment, the Ashite veins in the walls pulse faintly brighter. Then it's over, and the city moves, every person to their appointed task, every task in its appointed time.

> [!quote] The Ashmarket
> Ostivaar's commercial district runs like clockwork. Stalls open at the same hour, close at the same hour, and prices are posted in exact figures, no haggling. A butcher arranges cuts of grey-tinged meat in precise rows. A tailor measures fabric to the thread. A child sweeps ash from a doorstep in methodical strokes, corner to corner, left to right. The only disorder is a fruit seller whose apples have grown in slightly irregular shapes. She is arranging them so the irregularity doesn't show. She has been doing this for twenty minutes.

> [!quote] The Calcified Deer
> On the road between settlements, the party passes a deer standing motionless at the treeline. Its antlers are bone-white stone. Its hide has the texture of ceramic. It watches with eyes that don't blink, then turns and walks into the Calcified Forest with movements that are smooth, precise, and somehow wrong, as if it is following a path drawn on a grid. It does not look back.

| 3 | A child approaches selling "lucky Ashite pebbles" for a copper each. The pebbles are genuine low-grade Ashite. DC 13 Investigation: she's collecting them from a crack in the street that shouldn't be there, a new fissure in the Spine above. |
| 4 | A Church scholar at a tea house wants to discuss theology with outsiders. She is genuinely curious and intellectually honest, a rarity. She also reports every conversation to her superiors. She does not see a contradiction. |
| 5 | A merchant hires the PCs to guard a routine cargo shipment to [[Cinderholm]]. The cargo is books, Unbound Congregation texts hidden inside Church-approved covers. He doesn't know what he's carrying. His employer does. |
| 6 | A garrison soldier asks a PC for directions, then realizes PCs are outsiders too. He laughs. "Everyone looks lost here. The streets are perfectly gridded and I still can't find the bakery." He's new, transferred from [[Pale Sentry]], and desperately lonely. |

## Regional Flavor Details (d6)

| d6 | Detail |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Smell:** Stone dust and faint sulfur. Near the Spine, the air carries a dry mineral scent like crushed chalk. After rain, the ash-grey soil releases an earthy, almost metallic odor. |
| 2 | **Sound:** The Hum. Near Solvaen's Spine, a subsonic vibration sits at the edge of hearing, like a note too low to identify. Locals don't notice it. Visitors feel it in their teeth and chest. It never stops. |
| 3 | **Weather:** Overcast more often than not. When the sun breaks through, it illuminates the Ashite veins in the mountain, and the Spine glows like a pale lantern. Rain is infrequent but heavy, turning ash-soil to grey mud. |
| 4 | **Food:** Dense grey bread, surprisingly good. Roasted root vegetables with a faintly sweet, mineral aftertaste. Pewter-skinned apples, crisp and tart. The local spirit is distilled from ash-grain and burns clean. |
| 5 | **Night:** The Spine glows faintly at dusk and dawn, Ashite phosphorescence. In full darkness, Ostivaar's streets are lit by Ashite lanterns that cast steady, unwavering light with no flicker. The shadows are precise. |
| 6 | **Architecture:** Geometric precision. Every building in Ostivaar aligns to a grid laid out centuries ago. Walls meet at exact right angles. Doorways are uniform in height. The effect is impressive and subtly oppressive, like living inside a diagram. |

# The Verdant Marches

## Tavern Encounter Table (d6)

| d6 | Encounter |
|---|---|
| 1 | A Marchwarden stumbles in covered in sap and pollen, orders the strongest drink available, and announces: "The western trail is gone. Grew over in an hour. Don't go west." She will not elaborate. |
| 2 | Two hunters argue about whether the game is moving differently. One says the herds are fleeing deeper into the canopy. The other says they're being herded. DC 13 Nature: the second hunter is closer to the truth. |
| 3 | A [[Sporeheim]] trader offers rare Bloom Peaks spores in tiny glass vials. "Prophetic visions, guaranteed." DC 12 Insight: the visions are real. DC 14 Nature: so is the addiction. One in four users can't stop. |
| 4 | A stranger sits in the corner eating raw mushrooms that glow faintly violet. Their eyes have a green-gold sheen. DC 14 Nature: early-stage Marrow-Water mutation. They don't seem to know. They feel "better than ever." |
| 5 | The floor of the tavern buckles as a root pushes through from below, an inch thick, pale, and warm. The barkeep sighs, grabs an axe, and hacks it back. "Third time this week." Root-sap smells like honey and copper. |
| 6 | A Greensinger begins a low chant in the corner and the candles dim. The wood of the walls groans softly. Leaves sprout from the ceiling beams. She stops abruptly, apologizes, and explains she was trying to fix a headache. The leaves remain. |

## Downtime Activities

**Greensinger Meditation (DC 14 Will).** Spend a week learning basic Primal Communion grounding techniques. Success: gain the ability to sense the general health of natural environments within 6 spaces (healthy, sick, corrupted, or "awake"). Failure: vivid dreams of being a tree for 1d6 nights. Disorienting but harmless.

**Herbalism Gathering (DC 12 Nature).** Forage the canopy layers for a week. Success: collect 2d6 doses of medicinal herbs (each heals 1d4 HP when applied during a short rest). In the Bloom Peaks, also collect 1d4 doses of prophetic spores (DC 14 Will or hallucinate for 1d4 hours; success grants one cryptic true vision, GM's choice).

**Canopy Navigation Training (DC 13 Dex).** Learn vine-bridge traversal and mid-canopy pathfinding from Marcher guides. Success: no longer require checks for standard canopy travel. Failure: a 3-space fall into a moss-net, bruised pride and 1d6 bludgeoning.

**Rootspeaking Observation.** No check. Attend a Marchwarden's Rootspeaking ceremony (outsiders may watch but not participate). Witness the land responding to the speaker: roots shifting, soil warming, ferns turning toward the speaker's voice. Gain understanding of Primal Communion's nature, advantage on Arcana checks related to primal magic for the next adventure.

**Marrow-Water Analysis (DC 15 Arcana).** Study Marrow-Water samples under controlled conditions. Success: learn to identify Marrow-Water contamination in water sources, food, and people. This knowledge is valuable and dangerous, [[Roothold]]'s healers will pay for it, and [[Thornhaven]]'s warlord will want to suppress it.

## Slice of Life Scenes

> [!quote] Market Day at Roothold
> The market isn't in a clearing. It *is* the clearing, a space the titan-tree's roots have shaped into a natural amphitheater, warm and dim, lit by phosphorescent fungi cultivated along the root-walls. Traders hang goods from living branches that bend accommodatingly. A woman sells Thyrea's Amber in rough chunks, each one warm to the touch and smelling of spring rain. A hunter barters a stag carcass for rope and salt. Children chase luminous beetles between the root-buttresses, laughing. The soil underfoot is soft and faintly warm, and if you stand still long enough, you can feel it pulse.

> [!quote] The Bloom Peaks at Dusk
> The flowers are dying. They bloomed at dawn, brilliant reds and golds carpeting the mountainside. By noon they'd gone to seed, clouds of pollen drifting downslope. Now, at dusk, frost crawls across petals that were tropical hours ago. A Spore-Seer sits cross-legged on a flat stone, breathing the last of the pollen, eyes closed, murmuring. The compressed seasons of this place mean beauty and death happen in the same breath. Tomorrow it starts again.

> [!quote] The Vine-Bridge Crossing
> The bridge is alive. Woven from vines as thick as a person's arm, it spans a gap between two canopy platforms two hundred feet above the forest floor. It sways. It creaks. Occasionally, it adjusts, a vine tightening here, a new tendril reaching there. A Marcher family crosses it without looking down, their youngest child riding on her father's shoulders, pointing at birds. They do this every day. The bridge has never dropped anyone it recognized as belonging here.

## Regional Flavor Details (d6)

| d6 | Detail |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Smell:** Loam, pollen, and the sweet-copper scent of Marrow-Water. After rain, the forest floor steams and the air is thick enough to chew. Deep in the canopy, the smell shifts to something older, like stone and moss and slow time. |
| 2 | **Sound:** Never quiet. Insects, birdsong, the creak of growing wood, the distant drip of canopy condensation. At night, the sounds deepen: something large moving through undergrowth, the low hum of Thyrea's roots, the occasional crack of a branch under weight that isn't wind. |
| 3 | **Weather:** Rain is daily and warm, falling in sheets through the canopy, arriving at the forest floor as a gentle mist. True storms are rare but violent, the titan-trees swaying in winds that sound like breathing. On calm days, pollen drifts in golden clouds. |
| 4 | **Food:** Roasted root-tubers with herb paste. Smoked game wrapped in broad leaves. Fermented fruit wine, sweet and potent, served in cups carved from living wood that slowly grows closed if you don't drink fast enough. Mushroom stew that glows faintly. |
| 5 | **Night:** Bioluminescence everywhere. Fungi along root-walls pulse in slow waves. Streams glow green-gold. The canopy above is dark, but the understory is a quiet light-show in blues, greens, and violets. It is beautiful and deeply alien. |
| 6 | **Architecture:** Nothing is built; everything is grown. Homes are shaped from living wood, walls of interwoven branches, roofs of dense leaf-thatch that repairs itself. Doorways are arched gaps in trunk-walls. Floors are smooth root-platforms, warm and faintly springy. Every structure is unique because every tree is unique. |

# The Ironmarch

## Tavern Encounter Table (d6)

| d6 | Encounter |
|---|---|
| 1 | Two mercenaries from rival companies sit at adjacent tables in hostile silence. The tension is physical. DC 12 Perception: both are gripping weapons under the table. The barkeep has placed a "Neutral Ground" marker on the bar. Nobody wants to test it tonight. |
| 2 | A smith from [[Wrathspire]] is drunk and talking too loudly about a weapon she forged last week. "It sang when I quenched it. Not cooling metal, *singing*. Kaevroth's Iron doesn't do that. It's never done that." She stops and looks at her hands. |
| 3 | A mercenary captain offers a PC arm-wrestling for 10 gold. Fair contest. DC 13 Str to win. If the PC wins, the captain laughs and pays. If the PC loses, the captain buys them a drink and offers work. Either way, the captain's company has been watching the PCs and wanted to assess them. |
| 4 | The tavern door opens and nobody enters. A cold wind carries a sound like distant drums. Everyone looks at each other. A veteran at the bar says, "That's the third time this month. The Shardfall's humming louder." Then he orders another drink. |
| 5 | A fight breaks out. It's fast, brutal, and over in thirty seconds. The loser is dragged outside. The winner sits back down and resumes eating. Nobody intervenes, comments, or seems surprised. The barkeep charges the winner for the broken chair without looking up. |
| 6 | A trader from [[Duskfield]] is selling "calming talismans," stones supposedly warded against war-essence. DC 14 Arcana: they're ordinary river stones with scratched runes. DC 12 Insight: the trader believes they work. He needs to believe they work. |

## Downtime Activities

**Mercenary Training (DC 13 Str or Dex).** Train with an Ironmarch company for a week. Success: learn one combat maneuver (disarm, shield bash, or dirty fighting, chosen at training start). Failure: 1d6 bludgeoning from training injuries and a standing invitation to try again.

**Fragment Prospecting (DC 14 Perception).** Spend a week searching for Kaevroth's Iron fragments in the field. Success: find 1d4 fragments worth 25 gold each to the right buyer. Also gain firsthand experience with war-essence exposure (DC 12 Will or feel a persistent urge toward aggression for 1d4 days, no mechanical effect but roleplayed). Failure: find nothing but rust-stained dirt and a growing headache.

**Reputation Building.** No check. Spend a week in [[The Red Garrison]] or [[Ironhaven]], buying rounds, telling stories, accepting minor contracts. Gain a contact in one mercenary company (GM's choice). Future hires and information requests in the region gain advantage on social checks.

**Talisman Crafting (DC 13 Arcana).** Attempt to create a genuine ward against war-essence influence. Requires 50 gold in materials. Success: create a talisman granting +2 to Will saves against war-essence for 30 days. Failure: materials wasted, and you learn why most Ironmarch talismans are fake, the essence resists being excluded.

**Rust Coast Salvage (DC 12 Dex).** Spend a week diving the shallow waters off [[Shieldbreak]] for submerged iron fragments and shipwreck salvage. Success: recover 2d6 x 5 gold worth of salvageable iron and trade goods. Failure: a close call with submerged fragment-fields, 1d6 slashing from underwater iron shards.

## Slice of Life Scenes

> [!quote] Morning at the Red Garrison
> The mercenary city wakes to the sound of steel. In every courtyard, every alley wide enough for two people, soldiers drill. The forms vary by company, some favor heavy blades, others polearms, a few train bare-handed, but the rhythm is universal: strike, guard, reset. A water-seller moves between the training groups with a cart, selling cups for a copper. She is the most popular person in the city before noon. The only building not echoing with combat is Duskfield's trade office, where a clerk tallies grain shipments with an expression of profound exhaustion.

> [!quote] The Fragment Field
> The road passes through an open plain where iron shards jut from the red earth like broken teeth. Some are the size of a fist. Some are taller than a person. All of them hum faintly, a vibration felt in the jaw and sternum more than heard. A farmer has built a fence around his field using fragment-posts, cheaper than wood and the livestock won't go near them. His crops grow in neat rows between the shards, dusted with rust. He waves as you pass, then returns to his work, jaw set, shoulders tight, the quiet endurance of a man who has lived his entire life next to something that wants him angry.

> [!quote] The Neutral Ground
> [[Duskfield]] at harvest is the closest the Ironmarch comes to peace. Mercenary companies that would kill each other anywhere else sit at shared tables eating bread and salt. The Duskfield Accord is older than any living company: no weapons drawn within the town walls during harvest, or every company starves. Children play between the tables. A grizzled sergeant is teaching a farmer's daughter to whittle. He is missing three fingers. She is missing none. He intends to keep it that way.

## Regional Flavor Details (d6)

| d6 | Detail |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Smell:** Iron and dust. The air tastes faintly of rust, stronger after rain when the red soil bleeds color into every puddle. Near the Shardfall, the smell sharpens to something almost electric, like the air before a storm that never breaks. |
| 2 | **Sound:** Distant drums. Or a heartbeat. Or neither. The hum of the fragments is constant, just below conscious hearing, and it makes every other sound feel slightly off-tempo. Veterans say you stop noticing after a year. Veterans also say a lot of things they don't mean. |
| 3 | **Weather:** Dry and windy. The wind carries iron dust that grits between teeth and stains clothing red-brown. Rain is rare but dramatic, turning the earth to rust-colored mud that stains everything it touches. Clear days are sharp and bright, the red landscape vivid under hard sun. |
| 4 | **Food:** Tough bread, salted meat, root vegetables roasted over iron-grate fires. Everything tastes faintly metallic, even food imported from other regions, as if the land seasons it by proximity. The local beer is dark and bitter, brewed with water that runs through iron-rich soil. |
| 5 | **Night:** Dark. The Ironmarch has few trees and no bioluminescence. Nights are vast and star-filled, but the fragments catch moonlight and glow dull red, so the landscape never fully darkens. Sentries report that the fragments are brighter on nights when violence has occurred nearby. |
| 6 | **Architecture:** Practical and fortified. Every building doubles as a defensible position. Walls are thick, windows narrow, doors reinforced. Even farmhouses have bolt-holes and weapon racks. Beauty is not a priority. Survival is. The only decorative element is ironwork, which is everywhere, because iron is the one thing this land has in abundance. |

# The Unwritten Lands

## Tavern Encounter Table (d6)

| d6 | Encounter |
|---|---|
| 1 | An outlander merchant and an Oldborn trader sit at the same table, communicating through a shared ledger of sketched trade goods. Neither speaks the other's language fluently. The negotiation has been going on for two hours and both look cautiously pleased. Then the merchant's Ashite-inlaid ring flares, and the Oldborn's expression goes cold. |
| 2 | A sailor from [[Tidecross]] is drunk and loudly describing what he saw past the Fernwald: "The trees moved. Not in the wind. They *moved*. Rearranged. The path we cut was gone in an hour." A local Veilshore guide nods without looking up. "Yes. They do that." |

## Downtime Activities

**Oldborn Language Study (DC 14 Will).** Spend a week with a willing Oldborn tutor (finding one is the hard part). Success: learn basic conversational Oldborn, enough for trade and simple questions. The language has no word for "prayer" or "worship," which tells you something. Failure: the grammar operates on principles that resist mainland logic. You gain scattered vocabulary but can't form sentences.

**Primordial Observation (DC 15 Arcana).** Spend a week at the Shimmer's edge, observing Primordial forces. Success: gain firsthand understanding of how Primordial pacts work. Future Arcana checks involving Primordial magic gain +2 for the next adventure. Failure: the Primordia is vast, indifferent, and incomprehensible. You feel very small for 1d4 days (no mechanical effect, but excellent roleplay fuel).

**Veilshore Trade (DC 12 Will).** Spend a week trading in [[Tidecross]]'s Crossroads Market. Success: earn 2d6 x 10 gold and gain one trade contact (Oldborn or outlander, player's choice). Failure: cultural misunderstanding costs you 1d6 x 5 gold and a contact refuses to deal with you again.

**Black Sand Study (DC 16 Arcana).** Collect and analyze Veilshore black sand samples. Success: discover the sand carries pre-Primordial resonance signatures, older than the Oldborn, older than the Primordials themselves. This knowledge has no immediate mechanical use but is deeply significant to scholars on either continent. Failure: the sand resists analysis. Your tools feel inadequate. You understand why the Oldborn say some things cannot be *studied*, only *negotiated with*.

**Quarantine Quarter Networking (DC 13 Will).** Spend a week in [[Hael'rune]]'s Quarantine Quarter, building relationships with other outlanders and the rare sympathetic Oldborn. Success: gain one ally in the Quarter (a scholar, trader, or stranded traveler) who can provide local intelligence and cultural translation. Failure: the seven-day limit expires. You must leave or find a sponsor.

## Slice of Life Scenes

> [!quote] The Crossroads Market
> [[Tidecross]]'s market is barely controlled chaos. Outlander merchants hawk Dominion steel and Reach fish alongside Oldborn traders offering volcanic glass tools and woven fern-cloth of impossible fineness. Languages collide. A Concord factor argues prices with an Oldborn weaver using an abacus and emphatic gestures. A child from the Reach stares at an Oldborn Pact-Weaver's crystalline arm, where flesh has been replaced by something that looks like smoky quartz, the cost of their pact. The Weaver notices the child staring, kneels, and makes the crystal sing a note that makes the child laugh. It is the most human moment in the most alien place.

> [!quote] Evening at the Shimmer's Edge
> The boundary between the settled Oldborn lands and the Primordia is visible as a faint distortion in the air, like heat-haze, except the air is cold. Beyond it, the world stops following rules. A tree grows, fruits, dies, and regrows in the time it takes to watch. A stream flows uphill, pauses, and flows sideways. The Oldborn Boundary Watchers stand at their posts with the calm of people who see this every day. One of them eats an apple. Behind her, a mountain rises from nothing over the course of an hour, reaches a peak, and begins to dissolve. She does not turn around.

> [!quote] The Threshold Gate at Dusk
> [[Hael'rune]]'s eastern gate glows faintly as the last outlander traders pass through for the day. Each one feels the pressure, like walking through warm water, the Gate reading their divine residue. An Oldborn guard marks a tally on a stone tablet: how many passed, how strong the readings. A young outlander woman pauses under the arch and looks up at the geometric wards carved into the stone. "It's beautiful," she says. The guard does not respond. The wards pulse brighter as she passes, registering contamination she doesn't know she carries from gods she's never consciously prayed to.

> [!quote] Night on the Veilshore
> The black sand beach is silver under moonlight. The waves arrive from an ocean that doesn't behave like any ocean on the known continent, the tides follow no lunar schedule, responding instead to Primordial rhythms the outlanders can't feel but the Oldborn navigate by instinct. A small camp of outlander traders huddles around a driftwood fire. An Oldborn fisherman anchors his boat nearby and offers them smoked eel without a word. They eat in shared silence, watching the water. The sand whispers. Not in words. In something older.

## Regional Flavor Details (d6)

| d6 | Detail |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Smell:** Salt and something unnameable, a scent like ozone and deep earth and the moment before a storm, but constant. Near the Oldborn cities, the air carries volcanic mineral notes. On the Veilshore, the black sand has no smell at all, which is itself unsettling. |
| 2 | **Sound:** The Primordia hums. Not loudly, but constantly, a vibration that sits beneath all other sound. Near the Shimmer, the hum becomes audible. In Oldborn cities, the Primordial Anchors pulse with a rhythm that replaces the hum with something structured, almost musical. At night on the coast, the waves arrive in patterns that don't repeat. |
| 3 | **Weather:** Unpredictable near the Primordia. Further from the boundary, the climate is temperate, sea-moderated, pleasant. But the sky sometimes displays colors that have no name, and clouds occasionally move against the wind, as if navigating by different rules. |
| 4 | **Food:** Oldborn cuisine uses volcanic-soil vegetables and ocean fish prepared with mineral salts and fern-herbs. The food is simple, well-made, and subtly unfamiliar, every flavor is recognizable but the combinations are different from anything on the known continent. Outlander taverns in Tidecross serve approximations of Reach and Dominion food, never quite right. |
| 5 | **Night:** No bioluminescence from divine sources. Instead, the Primordial Anchors cast faint blue-white light from city centers, and on clear nights, the stars are brighter here than anywhere on the known continent, as if the sky is closer. Oldborn navigate by stars the outlanders have never catalogued. |
| 6 | **Architecture:** Oldborn construction uses black volcanic stone shaped by Primordial craft into forms that appear grown rather than built. Curves where the mainland uses angles. No mortar, the stone fits together as if it was always one piece. Outlander settlements on the Veilshore are rough timber and canvas by comparison, temporary and knowing it. |

> [!tip] How to Use This File
> - **Between scenes:** Roll on the Tavern or Social Encounter table to fill a transitional moment.
> - **During downtime:** Let players choose from the Downtime Activities list. Run each in 5-10 minutes.
> - **Setting atmosphere:** Read a Slice of Life scene aloud when the PCs arrive in a new region or settlement.
> - **Adding texture:** Roll on Regional Flavor Details whenever you need a sensory grounding detail for narration.
> - **Improvising:** Combine entries across tables. The merchant from Social Spark #4 might be in the tavern during Encounter #2. The weather from Flavor #3 sets the scene for the Slice of Life moment.
