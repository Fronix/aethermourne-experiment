# The Ashfield Heresy

*A one-shot adventure set in [[Ashveil]], [[The Ashen Dominion]]*

---

## What You Need

> [!info] Adventure Overview
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Party Size** | 3-5 players |
> | **Recommended Level** | 3-6 (adaptable) |
> | **Tone** | Political intrigue, mystery, moral complexity |
> | **Estimated Duration** | 3-4 hours |
> | **Setting** | [[Ashveil]], the university city of [[The Ashen Dominion]] |
> | **Themes** | Truth vs. stability, faith vs. knowledge, who gets to control history |

**Summary:** A junior scholar at the Athenaeum named **Tessara Vynn** has uncovered a pre-Theomachis text that contradicts the Church of the Ashen Flame's official account of [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] death. The Church teaches that Solvaen died protecting mortals from [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]], a noble sacrifice. The text suggests Solvaen was already dying before Kaevroth arrived, poisoned by another god, and that his "last stand" was not a choice but a necessity: he was too weak to flee. The [[Major Factions#Order of the Ashen Flame|Order of the Ashen Flame]] has learned of the discovery and is sending Inquisitors. The PCs must decide what happens next.

**Key NPCs:** Tessara Vynn (scholar), Inquisitor Brael (Church enforcer), Keeper Maren Ashveil (librarian), Prior Gault Ashworth (moderate priest)

---

## Background

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **The text is real, and its claims are plausible.** The document, called the *Ash Testimony*, is a first-hand account written by a mortal priest who witnessed the final days of the Theomachis from within Solvaen's protective radius. It describes Solvaen arriving at the heartland already wounded, "bleeding light from a wound that was not Kaevroth's," and notes that the god's protective ward was faltering before the battle began.
>
> **Who poisoned Solvaen?** The text does not name the poisoner. It describes the wound as "a shadow that moved inside the light," which could implicate [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]. But it could also be interpreted as metaphor, exhaustion, or simple divine decay. The truth is deliberately ambiguous. The GM should decide for their campaign whether the Testimony is accurate, partially accurate, or a misinterpretation.
>
> **Why it matters:** If Solvaen was already dying, the entire theological foundation of the Dominion shifts. He was not a martyr who chose sacrifice. He was a casualty who happened to die in a useful place. The Spine of Order is not a monument to noble sacrifice but a gravestone for a murder victim. The Order of the Ashen Flame's authority rests on Solvaen's willing sacrifice. Remove the "willing" and the edifice cracks.
>
> **The Inquisitors' orders:** Suppress the text. Discredit Tessara. Ensure the Athenaeum understands that certain lines of inquiry are closed. They are not ordered to kill, but Inquisitor Brael interprets "ensure compliance" liberally.

## Scene 1: The Scholar's Plea

**Location:** The White Cloisters, Ashveil, late evening
**Mood:** Urgent, frightened, academic anxiety turned existential

The players are in Ashveil for their own reasons (travelers, hired guards, students, visiting scholars). Tessara Vynn finds them in a tavern near the Cloisters, breathless and barely holding together. She is a woman of thirty, ink-stained fingers, wild hair pinned hastily, and the wide eyes of someone who has been awake for three days.

**Tessara's story:**
- She is a junior archivist at the Athenaeum, specializing in pre-Theomachis texts
- Two weeks ago, she discovered a sealed chamber in the Athenaeum's sub-basement during renovation work
- Inside: the *Ash Testimony*, a 2,000-year-old account of Solvaen's final days
- She showed it to her supervisor, [[Keeper Maren Ashveil]], who went pale and told her to keep quiet
- Tessara did not keep quiet. She mentioned it to a colleague. The colleague told someone. Word reached the Order.
- Inquisitor Brael and three Church soldiers arrived in Ashveil this morning. They are at the Athenaeum now, demanding the text be surrendered.
- Tessara has the text. She took it from the archive before the Inquisitors could seize it. It is in her satchel.

**What Tessara wants:** Help. She does not know what kind. She wants the truth preserved. She does not want to die for it. She is not a rebel or a radical, she is a scholar who found something and does not know how to unfind it.

> [!tip] Roleplay Notes: Tessara
> Tessara is intelligent, principled, and completely out of her depth. She has never been in danger before. She alternates between fierce conviction ("The truth matters, it has to matter") and stark terror ("They will make me disappear"). She is not asking the players to fight the Church. She is asking them to help her figure out what to do.
>
> **Sample line:** *"I did not go looking for heresy. I was cataloguing water damage. But the text is real, the ink, the binding, the dialect, it is genuine pre-Theomachis. And what it says about Solvaen... if the Church buries this, we lose something we cannot get back."*

## Scene 2: The Investigation

The players have the night before the Inquisitors intensify their search. They can investigate, plan, and make alliances.

### The Ash Testimony (Reading the Text)

If the players examine the text (Arcana DC 12 to verify magical preservation, History DC 14 to authenticate the dialect):

The document is genuine. It describes Solvaen's arrival at the mortal heartland in fragmentary, urgent prose:

> *"He came to us already bleeding. Not from battle but from within. A shadow moved inside his light, eating it from the core. He raised his ward and held us safe but I saw his hands trembling. I saw the light in his eyes flickering like a candle in wind. When Kaevroth came nine days later, our god was already dying. He fought not because he chose to stand but because he could not run. He fell where he was because he had nowhere else to fall."*

The text does not identify who or what caused the "shadow." It is a witness account, not an investigation.

### The Athenaeum (Investigating the Discovery Site)

- **Keeper Maren Ashveil** is in her office, terrified. She has cooperated with the Inquisitors to protect the institution. She knows the text is genuine. She also knows that 80,000 people in Ashveil depend on the Athenaeum's continued operation, and antagonizing the Order threatens that. She will not help the players openly, but she will not stop them either.
- The sealed chamber where the text was found (Investigation DC 15) contains other artifacts: personal effects of the priest who wrote the Testimony, including a ring bearing the seal of Solvaen's personal clergy, a rank that no longer exists in the Church hierarchy. This corroborates the author's claimed proximity to the god.

### The Inquisitors (Gauging the Threat)

- **Inquisitor Brael** is staying at the Pilgrim's Rest inn with three Church soldiers. He is a lean, precise man with a face like a blade and the unshakeable certainty of someone who has never doubted. He is polite. He is patient. He is terrifying.
- Brael's position: the text is either a forgery or a misinterpretation. He does not need to read it to know this, because Solvaen's sacrifice is doctrinal truth, and doctrine does not yield to archaeology.
- If approached directly (Persuasion DC 18 or Deception DC 16), Brael may reveal his orders: recover the text, discredit the finder, ensure the Athenaeum understands the boundary between scholarship and sedition. He has authority to arrest Tessara. He would prefer not to, it creates sympathy for her cause, but he will if necessary.
- The soldiers are professional, not zealots. One of them, **Soldier Harath**, is privately uncomfortable with the assignment (Insight DC 14 to notice, Persuasion DC 12 to get him talking). Harath's mother was a scholar. He does not like burning books.

### Prior Gault Ashworth (A Moderate Voice)

- [[Prior Gault Ashworth]] is a senior priest in Ashveil, moderate and respected. He runs pilgrimages along the Spine of Order and is known for his measured temperament.
- If the players seek him out, Gault is sympathetic but cautious. He has heard rumors of the discovery. He believes truth and faith are not enemies, but he also believes that truth released carelessly can cause more harm than suppression.
- Gault's proposal: let him read the text. If it is genuine, he will argue for a controlled theological review rather than suppression. He has the standing to make that argument. But it will take months, and Tessara will need to remain silent in the meantime.
- This is a genuine compromise. It is also an indefinite delay that may result in the text being quietly buried.

## Scene 3: The Decision

The players must choose a path. Each has consequences.

### Path A: Help Tessara Flee

Get Tessara and the text out of Ashveil before the Inquisitors find her.

**Challenges:**
- The city gates are watched. Deception DC 15 or Stealth DC 16 to pass without drawing attention.
- Brael has informants. If the players were seen talking to Tessara, he knows. Pursuit begins 2 hours after they leave the city.
- The road out passes through Dominion territory for three days before reaching neutral ground.

**Chase complications (use 2-3):**
1. A patrol checkpoint. Bluff (Deception DC 14), bribe (50 gold), or detour (adds 4 hours, Survival DC 13).
2. Brael himself, mounted, with two soldiers. He offers one chance to surrender peacefully. If refused, combat. Brael fights with Ashite-enhanced equipment: +2 to hit, radiant damage on strikes, resistance to first spell cast against him.
3. A bridge over the Ashfall River, the last crossing before neutral territory. Brael's third soldier is already there, waiting.

**Outcome:** Tessara escapes with the text. She disappears into [[The Hollowed Reach]] or [[The Verdant Marches]]. The text survives but is discredited by the Church as a forgery. Tessara becomes a fugitive. The Athenaeum faces increased scrutiny. Seeds of doubt are planted among scholars who heard the rumor.

### Path B: Help the Church Suppress the Truth

Convince Tessara to surrender the text. Assist Brael in recovering it.

**Challenges:**
- Tessara must be persuaded (Persuasion DC 16) or deceived (Deception DC 14) or intimidated (Intimidation DC 12, but this haunts the player).
- If the players hand Tessara over willingly, Brael is satisfied. Tessara is "reassigned" to a remote monastery. She is not harmed, but she is silenced.

**Outcome:** Stability preserved. The Church rewards the players (200 gold, a letter of commendation useful in Dominion territory). Tessara is gone. The text is destroyed. But the sealed chamber still exists. The evidence of Solvaen's personal clergy still exists. The truth is buried, not erased.

### Path C: Investigate the Truth

The players decide the question matters and pursue it themselves.

**Challenges:**
- The sealed chamber (Investigation DC 15) yields the priest's personal effects, including a journal that corroborates the Testimony with additional detail.
- The Spine of Order itself (requires a day's travel) contains Solvaen's remains. A character with magical sensitivity (Arcana DC 18 at the Spine) can perceive the divine bone's resonance. There is something there, a faint, discordant note, a wound that is not consistent with Kaevroth's fire. Something cold. Something that hides.
- This does not prove the Testimony. But it supports it.

**Outcome:** The players have evidence but no proof. They must decide what to do with it: share it (Path A variant), suppress it (Path B variant), or hold it for leverage. The truth remains ambiguous. The implications are enormous.

### Path D: Gault's Compromise

Support Prior Gault's proposal for controlled theological review.

**Challenges:**
- Tessara must agree to silence (Persuasion DC 14, easier if the players frame it as "patience, not surrender").
- Brael must agree to stand down (Persuasion DC 16, Gault's presence gives advantage). Brael respects Gault but does not trust him. He agrees only if the text is placed in Gault's custody, not Tessara's.
- The review process is genuine but slow. The Church will attempt to control the outcome. Gault is honest but not powerful enough to guarantee the result.

**Outcome:** The most "responsible" path. The text survives in ecclesiastical custody. The truth has a chance, however slim, of being acknowledged through official channels. Tessara keeps her freedom but loses her discovery. The real question is whether the players trust institutions to handle uncomfortable truths.

## Inquisitor Brael (Combat Stats if Needed)

> [!example] Stat Block: Inquisitor Brael
> **Threat Level:** Serious
> **HP:** 65
> **Defense:** 16 (Ashite-reinforced chain)
> **Attack:** +7, 1d8+4 slashing + 1d6 radiant (Ashite longsword)
> **Special Abilities:**
> - *Ashite Ward:* Resistance to the first spell targeting him each round (the Ashite absorbs it)
> - *Commanding Presence:* Allies within 30 ft gain +2 to saves against fear and charm
> - *Solvaen's Judgment:* Once per encounter, force a target to make Wisdom save DC 15 or be paralyzed for 1 round (geometric light binds their limbs)
> **Weakness:** Brael will not attack unarmed or surrendering opponents. He follows the law absolutely, which can be exploited.
> **Recommended Party Size:** 4 players of level 4-5

**Church Soldiers (3)**

> [!example] Stat Block: Church Soldier
> **Threat Level:** Minor
> **HP:** 30
> **Defense:** 15 (chain mail)
> **Attack:** +5, 1d8+3 slashing (longsword)
> **Special Abilities:**
> - *Formation:* When adjacent to another soldier, +1 Defense
> - *Zealous Endurance:* First time reduced to 0 HP, drop to 1 HP instead (once per encounter)
> **Weakness:** Individually unremarkable. Separated from formation, they are standard soldiers.
> **Recommended Party Size:** 2-3 players of level 3-4

> [!warning]- GM Notes
> **The truth is deliberately ambiguous.** The Ash Testimony is genuine but its interpretation is debatable. "A shadow that moved inside the light" could be Serith's poison, Solvaen's own divine decay, metaphor for his grief at the Theomachis, or something else entirely. Do not resolve this definitively. The value is in the question, not the answer.
>
> **No clean victories.** Every path has costs. Escape means Tessara becomes a fugitive. Suppression means truth dies. Investigation yields ambiguity. Compromise means trusting a slow institution with an uncomfortable truth. Let the players sit with the discomfort.
>
> **Tone guidance.** This is not an action adventure. It is a moral puzzle wrapped in political danger. Combat should be a consequence of choices, not the default. The most interesting sessions will be the ones where the players argue with each other about what to do.
>
> **Connecting to campaign.** The "shadow in the light" connects to [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] broader manipulation. If Serith weakened Solvaen before Kaevroth's attack, it reframes the Theomachis as more orchestrated than anyone realized. This thread can be picked up in Act II or III of the Second Silence, especially if the players interact with the Veil Unbound, who would seize on this evidence to undermine divine authority.
>
> **Ashite influence.** Remind the players that the longer they spend in the Dominion, the more Ashite's ambient influence affects their thinking. Characters may find themselves *wanting* to support order, *wanting* to side with the Church, not from conviction but from environmental exposure. This is subtle and should be mentioned only once, as a creeping realization.
