# The Breathing Pass

*A one-shot adventure set in the Breathing Mountains, [[The Primordia]]*

---

## What You Need

> [!info] Adventure Overview
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Party Size** | 3-5 players |
> | **Recommended Level** | 4-6 (adaptable) |
> | **Tone** | Survival, wonder, existential danger, discovery |
> | **Estimated Duration** | 3-4 hours |
> | **Setting** | The Breathing Mountains, deep [[The Primordia]] |
> | **Themes** | Endurance, trust, the cost of ambition, what lies at the heart of the world |

**Summary:** A merchant caravan from [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]] needs escort through the Breathing Mountains, a range that literally rises and falls like breath. The mountains are mid-cycle: valleys becoming peaks, peaks becoming valleys, the earth inhaling. The caravan carries stabilized Gyre-crystals worth a fortune, bound for [[Gyre's Maw]]. The pass is the only viable route. The timing is terrible. But the crystals are needed to reinforce the Anchor at Gyre's Maw before the next surge, and delay means collapse.

**Key NPCs:** Caravan Master Duleth, Scout Arev, the Stonetalker, the thing in the mountain

---

## Background

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Breathing Mountains are not mountains. They are [[The Primordials#Thaelun|Thaelun]], the Primordial of simultaneous multiplicity, dreaming aloud through the stone. The rising and falling is Thaelun cycling through possibilities, the mountain existing in multiple states at once and flickering between them.
>
> The Oldborn know this. They navigate the mountains by reading Thaelun's dream-patterns, predicting which configuration will manifest next. But the Second Silence is disrupting Primordial rhythms across the continent. Thaelun's cycles have become erratic. The Oldborn navigators' predictions are failing. Two caravans have been lost in the past month.
>
> Duleth's caravan is the last attempt. If it fails, Gyre's Maw's Anchor weakens, and the Primordia's eastward expansion accelerates.

## Scene 1: The Caravan

**Location:** Base camp at the eastern approach to the Breathing Mountains
**Mood:** Grim preparation, professional anxiety

The players are hired at base camp, a cluster of Oldborn tents at the mountain range's edge. The mountains are visible from here: gray stone peaks that visibly shift position over hours, rising and falling in slow, rhythmic waves. It is beautiful and deeply wrong.

**Caravan Master Duleth** is an Oldborn trader in her fifties, built like a boulder, voice like gravel over glass. She has made this crossing eleven times. She is not confident about the twelfth. She needs the players as additional security, muscle, and expendable weight.

**The caravan consists of:**
- 3 crystal wagons (heavy, slow, valuable)
- 8 Oldborn teamsters
- 2 Oldborn scouts (including Arev)
- 1 Stonetalker (Primordial pact-holder who reads the mountain's rhythms)
- The players

**Duleth's briefing:**
- The pass takes two days on foot. One day in, one day out. No camping on the mountain itself, the ground shifts during sleep cycles.
- The Stonetalker reads the mountain's breath and calls out safe windows. When she says move, you move. When she says stop, you stop. No questions.
- The mountains are mid-cycle. Peaks are descending, valleys are rising. The pass that exists today will not exist tomorrow. Timing is everything.
- Threats: terrain shifts, elemental weather, and **Breath-beasts**, creatures that form from the mountain's exhalations, stone given temporary life, aggressive, territorial, and stupid.

**Scout Arev** is young, quiet, and carries a set of surveying instruments she checks compulsively. She maps the mountain's configurations in a leather journal filled with geometric diagrams. She is the backup navigator if the Stonetalker falls.

> [!tip] Roleplay Notes: Duleth
> Duleth treats the players as hired tools. She is not unkind, just efficient. She expects competence and punishes hesitation. She has lost people on this route before and carries each loss in the set of her jaw.
>
> **Sample line:** *"The mountain does not care about your bravery. It does not care about your fear. It breathes. You move between the breaths. Or you become part of the stone."*

## Scene 2: The Ascent (Day One)

**Location:** The Breathing Pass, ascending
**Mood:** Tension, survival, awe

The caravan enters the pass at dawn. The Stonetalker, a gaunt woman named **Tessavyn**, walks at the front with her palms pressed to the stone. She whispers constantly, listening to Thaelun's rhythms.

### Resource Tracking

Each player begins with **3 Stamina Points** (representing endurance, alertness, and physical reserves). Stamina is spent by environmental hazards. At 0 Stamina, a character gains a level of exhaustion. Stamina recovers during rest stops (1 point per short rest).

### Hazard Encounters (Day One)

Roll or choose from the following. Use 3-4 encounters on the ascent.

| d8 | Hazard | Details |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Terrain Shift** | The ground tilts 30 degrees without warning. Dexterity save DC 14 or slide 20 feet downslope (1d6 bludgeoning, costs 1 Stamina). Wagons require group Athletics DC 15 to anchor. |
| 2 | **Breath Surge** | The mountain exhales. A blast of mineral-laden wind fills the pass. Str save DC 13 or blinded for 1 minute (stone dust). Costs 1 Stamina regardless. |
| 3 | **Path Collapse** | The path ahead sinks into the mountain. The caravan has 2 rounds to find an alternate route (Survival DC 14 or Investigation DC 14) before the detour becomes a dead end. |
| 4 | **Breath-beast** | A creature of animate stone detaches from the cliff face. Use an Earth Elemental stat block (scaled to party level). It attacks the crystal wagons specifically, drawn to the resonance. Defeating it causes it to collapse into rubble. |
| 5 | **Temperature Spike** | A vein of thermal energy opens beneath the path. The ground becomes searingly hot for 100 feet. Str save DC 14 or 2d6 fire damage. Characters can jump between cool patches (Acrobatics DC 12). Costs 1 Stamina. |
| 6 | **The Stonetalker Falters** | Tessavyn loses Thaelun's rhythm. The caravan must halt for 30 minutes while she reconnects. During the halt, the mountain shifts unpredictably. Perception DC 14 to spot a safe area; failure means the caravan is caught in a minor terrain compression (2d6 bludgeoning to all, Dexterity save DC 14 for half). |
| 7 | **Echo Canyon** | The pass funnels through a canyon where sound behaves strangely. Whispered words become thunderclaps. Shouting produces silence. Communication is impossible for 10 minutes. Any ability requiring verbal components fails automatically. Stealth is effortless. |
| 8 | **Beauty** | No hazard. The caravan crests a ridge and sees the Primordia from above: a churning landscape of impossible color, shifting forests, floating stone, rivers of light. It is the most beautiful thing any of them have ever seen. Everyone stops. Tessavyn whispers: *"This is what the world remembers being."* No mechanical effect. Let it breathe. |

### Evening Camp

The caravan reaches a Primordial Anchor midway through the pass, a 15-foot crystal column that creates a stable zone large enough to camp. Duleth orders a halt. The Anchor's field means the ground will not shift here overnight.

**Camp events (use 1-2):**

- **Tessavyn confides:** She tells the players (if they have earned her trust) that Thaelun's rhythms are changing. The mountain is breathing faster. The cycles that once took days now take hours. She does not know why. She is afraid she will not be able to read them tomorrow.

- **Arev's maps:** Arev shows the players her journal. The mountain's configurations are not random, they follow a pattern, but the pattern is accelerating toward something. A convergence point. She does not know what happens when the mountain reaches the end of its cycle.

- **The crystal sings:** One of the Gyre-crystals in the wagons begins resonating with the Anchor. A low, harmonic tone fills the camp. It is beautiful and unsettling. The teamsters cover their ears. Duleth watches it with grim fascination.

## Scene 3: The Heart (Day Two)

**Location:** The deepest point of the pass
**Mood:** Revelation, existential weight, wonder

On the second day, the pass descends into the mountain's interior. The stone here is translucent, shot through with veins of crystal that pulse with slow, rhythmic light. The mountain's "breathing" is audible now, a deep, subsonic throb that the players feel in their chests.

### The Discovery

At the deepest point of the pass, Tessavyn stops. Her eyes go wide. She drops to her knees.

The path opens into a vast natural chamber, 200 feet across, with a ceiling that rises and falls like a diaphragm. In the center of the chamber is something that should not exist: **a Primordial Anchor the size of a building**, a crystal formation of impossible scale, pulsing with light that shifts through colors the players cannot name.

This is the **Heart of Thaelun**, the point where the Primordial's dream-patterns are strongest. The mountain breathes because this crystal breathes. The ground shifts because this crystal shifts. Everything the Breathing Mountains do originates here.

**Arcana DC 16:** This Anchor is not stabilizing the area. It *is* the area. The entire mountain range is an expression of this single crystal's resonance. Destroying it would collapse the mountains. Reinforcing it could stabilize them permanently.

**Perception DC 14:** Something is wrong with the crystal. A dark vein runs through its core, a thread of shadow that does not belong. It pulses at a different rhythm than the rest, faster, more insistent. It is pulling the mountain's cycles out of alignment.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The dark vein is not Primordial. It is a dormant thread of the [[Cosmology and Magic#The Loom of Fate|Loom of Fate]], one of the excised patterns [[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas]] buried in [[The Loom's Edge]]. This thread has migrated from the Loom's Edge into the Breathing Mountains, drawn by Thaelun's multiplicity. It is trying to weave itself into Thaelun's dream, to impose a specific future on the Primordial's infinite possibility.
>
> If it succeeds, the Breathing Mountains will stop breathing. They will lock into a single configuration, a fixed range that happens to form a perfect path from [[The Loom's Edge]] to the coast. A highway for whatever Orenthas buried, leading it straight to [[The Veilshore]] and the ships that could carry it to the known continent.

## Scene 4: The Choice

**Location:** The Heart of Thaelun
**Mood:** Urgency, wonder, consequence

As the players examine the crystal, the chamber begins shifting. The mountain is entering an accelerated cycle. The ceiling drops. The floor rises. Tessavyn screams that they have minutes before the chamber compresses.

The players must make a choice:

### Option A: Remove the Dark Thread

**How:** Arcana DC 18 or a creative magical solution to extract the dormant Loom thread from the crystal without shattering it. Physical extraction (reaching into the crystal) requires Str save DC 16 to resist Thaelun's overwhelming multiplicity, failure means the character experiences all possible versions of themselves simultaneously for 1 round (stunned, 2d8 psychic damage).

**Consequence:** The thread is removed. The mountain's cycles return to their natural rhythm. Tessavyn can read them again. The caravan completes its passage safely. But the players now possess an excised thread of the Loom of Fate. Every faction that knows about the Loom's Edge will want it. It hums in the carrier's pack, whispering futures that have not happened yet.

### Option B: Reinforce the Crystal Around the Thread

**How:** Using the caravan's Gyre-crystals to amplify the Anchor's resonance and overpower the thread. Duleth must sacrifice half the caravan's cargo (devastating financial loss). Requires group Arcana check, DC 14 average across the party.

**Consequence:** The mountains stabilize but lock into a configuration that includes the thread's influence. A path opens through the range, permanent and stable, leading toward [[The Loom's Edge]]. The mountains stop breathing. Thaelun's dream narrows to a single possibility. The Oldborn will mourn the loss of the living mountain. But the path is useful, and useful things are hard to close.

### Option C: Shatter the Crystal

**How:** Attack the crystal (Armor H, HP 50, immune to non-magical damage). Shattering it causes a catastrophic Primordial surge: everyone in the chamber makes Str save DC 18 or takes 6d6 force damage. The chamber begins collapsing immediately.

**Consequence:** The Breathing Mountains collapse into rubble. The pass is destroyed. Thaelun's dream ends, not the Primordial itself, but this expression of it. The dark thread is buried under a million tons of stone. The caravan's crystals are lost. Gyre's Maw does not get its resupply. The Primordia's eastward expansion accelerates.

### Option D: Do Nothing, Run

**How:** The players grab Tessavyn and sprint for the exit as the chamber compresses. Athletics DC 14 to make it out before the ceiling meets the floor. Failure means 4d6 bludgeoning damage and being trapped until the next breathing cycle (1d4 hours).

**Consequence:** The thread continues its work. Within months, the mountains will lock into the pathway configuration. The choice is merely delayed, not avoided.

---

## Scene 5: The Descent

Regardless of choice, the caravan must complete the descent. Use 1-2 more hazard encounters from the table, then narrate the arrival at the western base camp.

**If the thread was removed:** Tessavyn reads the mountain flawlessly. The descent is smooth. The teamsters cheer when they clear the pass. Duleth pays the players a bonus.

**If the crystal was reinforced:** The new stable path makes the descent trivial. Duleth is grim about the lost cargo but acknowledges the players' pragmatism. The Oldborn teamsters are silent, staring at the mountains that no longer breathe.

**If the crystal was shattered:** The descent is a desperate scramble through collapsing terrain. Use 3 hazard encounters in rapid succession. The caravan loses one wagon. Duleth does not speak to the players afterward.

**If they ran:** Normal descent, with the knowledge that they left something unfinished in the mountain's heart.

> [!warning]- GM Notes
> **Pacing:** The ascent should feel grueling. The Heart should feel revelatory. The descent should feel consequential. Distribute hazards accordingly: heavier on day one, lighter on day two until the Heart, then adjust based on the players' choice.
>
> **The Loom thread:** This is a significant campaign artifact if the players remove it. It contains a dormant pattern, a future Orenthas rejected. What that future is, you decide. Suggestions: a world where the Boundary never existed, a timeline where Serith succeeded in unmaking creation, or a path where mortals replaced the gods. The thread does not reveal its contents easily, it requires study, risk, and possibly a trip to [[The Loom's Edge]].
>
> **Thaelun's nature:** The mountain is not sentient. It does not make choices. It dreams. The players cannot negotiate with it any more than they can negotiate with gravity. Tessavyn's pact allows her to *read* the dream, not influence it. This distinction matters: the Primordia is not a puzzle to solve, it is a force to survive.
>
> **Connecting to campaign:** The excised Loom thread in the Breathing Mountains is the first tangible evidence the players encounter of Orenthas's buried secrets. It foreshadows [[The Loom's Edge]] and raises the question: if one thread migrated this far, how many others are loose? And where are they going?
