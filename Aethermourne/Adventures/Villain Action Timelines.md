# Villain Action Timelines

*What happens if the players do nothing.*

These timelines represent the antagonists' plans advancing unchecked. Each entry is keyed to an approximate session window. When the players disrupt a step, push subsequent steps back or alter them. When the players ignore a thread, advance it on schedule.

Use these to make the world feel alive between sessions. NPCs should reference events the players didn't witness. Rumors should foreshadow upcoming steps. The clock is always ticking.

---

## Serith, the Imprisoned Goddess

**Goal:** Escape the Abyssal Trench prison and restart the divine cycle (unmake the world, begin new creation).

**Method:** Weaken prison seals through multiple vectors simultaneously. No single vector is sufficient alone, but together they create cascading failure.

| Session Window | Event | Visible Signs |
|---|---|---|
| 1-3 | Black Tide frequency increases to weekly. Sleepwalker phenomenon spreads 10 miles inland from coast. | Tidewall overwhelmed. Mistholm sanitarium at capacity. Coastal trade disrupted. |
| 4-6 | Shadow-roots reach [[The Twelve#Thyrea\|Thyrea]]'s Cradle in the [[The Verdant Marches\|Verdant Marches]]. First crops wither in unnatural patterns. | Marches farmers report "black veins" in root vegetables. Elder Rosk issues warnings no one heeds. |
| 7-10 | [[The Twelve#Yvenne\|Yvenne]]'s sanity erodes further. Ocean becomes hostile, storms without weather patterns, rogue waves targeting coastal settlements. | Tidecallers report Yvenne "no longer answers prayers the same way." Shipping lanes become death traps. |
| 11-14 | Sleepwalkers begin *organizing*. Groups walk into the sea in coordinated formations. Some return changed, carrying Serith's will consciously. | Returned Sleepwalkers form cells. They are calm, articulate, and terrifyingly reasonable. They recruit. |
| 15-18 | Shadow-roots corrupt [[The Twelve#Morrhael\|Morrhael]]'s domain fully. The dead in [[The Pale Wastes]] stop being aimless and begin marching south with purpose. | Hollowdeep goes silent. Then scouts report organized columns moving toward the Ironmarch border. |
| 19-22 | Prison seals crack visibly. A permanent Black Tide zone forms around the Trench. Sea creatures mutate. Yvenne's barrier around the [[The Unwritten Lands]] fails. | The ocean between continents becomes navigable but nightmarish. Oldborn Kingdoms detect the failure and panic. |
| 23-26 | Serith's voice becomes audible to anyone within 50 miles of coastline. Not whispers. Words. She makes her case directly to the population. | Mass conversions. Riots. Some entire towns walk into the sea willingly. Others fortify and resist. |
| 27-30 | Prison fails. Serith rises. The divine cycle restarts unless the players have assembled the means to stop her. | The sky goes dark. The Aetheric Web shatters. Every divine material on the continent resonates simultaneously. |

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Serith's timeline is the master clock. Every other villain's timeline feeds into this one. If the players disrupt Mourne, Serith loses her Dominion vector but compensates through other channels (the shadow-roots accelerate, the Veil Unbound intensifies coastal operations). The timeline compresses or stretches, but the direction never changes unless the players address the root cause.
>
> **Critical:** Serith genuinely believes she is doing the right thing. Her recruitment pitch to the population (sessions 23-26) should be *compelling*. Some NPCs the players trust should be swayed. This is not a mind-control scenario. It is a philosophical argument backed by divine charisma.

## Delric Mourne, the Cinders

**Goal:** Neutralize the Ashen Dominion as a functional power so it cannot oppose Serith's release. Secondary goal: acquire divine materials for Serith's use.

**Method:** Institutional capture, intelligence manipulation, controlled destabilization.

| Session Window | Event | Visible Signs |
|---|---|---|
| 1-3 | Mourne tightens surveillance on the [[Elowen Greaves\|Unbound Congregation]]. Identifies three key leaders for elimination. | Congregation members disappear. Greaves goes deeper underground. Cinder patrols double in the Lower Ribs. |
| 4-6 | Operation Quiet expands. Tainted Ashite tools distributed to five more districts. Order Sickness rates climb. | Workers become docile, uncreative, compliant. Guild output drops. [[Torren Hearthforge]] raises alarm but is ignored. |
| 7-10 | Mourne engineers a provocation in the Lower Ribs. Cinder provocateur triggers violence at a protest. Martial law declared. | Ostivaar under military lockdown. Curfews. The [[Order of the Ashen Flame\|Ashen Flame]] deployed to streets. Commander Voss uncomfortable but obeys orders. |
| 11-14 | [[Hierarch Valdren III]] effectively imprisoned in the Cathedral. Mourne controls all information reaching him. Key advisors replaced with Cinder loyalists. | The Hierarch stops appearing in public. Official proclamations become paranoid and erratic. Prior Ashworth attempts to reach him and fails. |
| 15-18 | Ashite mining redirected. Rich veins sealed "for safety." Dominion's divine material reserves begin depleting. | Ashite prices triple. Church rituals weaken. The Cathedral's eternal flame dims noticeably. |
| 19-22 | Mourne reveals his Serith worship to select Cinder officers. Those who refuse are eliminated. The Cinders become a cult cell. | Cinder behavior shifts from authoritarian to fanatical. Executions increase. The remaining honest officers defect or die. |
| 23-26 | The Dominion fractures. Mourne attempts to assassinate the Hierarch and blame the Congregation. Civil war erupts regardless of success. | Ostivaar burns. The Ashen Flame splits between loyalists and Mourne's faction. The Church's authority collapses. |

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Mourne's timeline runs parallel to Act II. If the players expose him during sessions 9-16, his later steps don't happen, but Serith compensates by accelerating other vectors. If the players *don't* deal with Mourne in Act II, this timeline continues in the background and the Dominion collapses during Act III-IV.
>
> **Mourne's contingency:** If exposed, he detonates everything at once, triggering the session 23-26 events immediately. He would rather burn the Dominion to the ground than let it be used against Serith.
>
> **If captured alive:** Mourne becomes the campaign's most unsettling NPC. He is helpful, forthcoming, and genuinely believes the players will eventually agree with him.

## The Veil Unbound

**Goal:** Weaken Serith's prison seals. Prepare mortal infrastructure for her liberation. Neutralize the [[Major Factions#Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keepers]].

**Method:** Sabotage, recruitment, ritual magic at coastal seal-sites, assassination of key investigators.

| Session Window | Event | Visible Signs |
|---|---|---|
| 1-3 | Three simultaneous operations: assassinate a Lantern-Keeper investigator, recruit from Sleepwalker families, perform a weakening ritual at the Tidewall sea caves. | Keeper Ashvane loses an agent. Sleepwalker families become hostile to outside help. Strange lights in the sea caves at night. |
| 4-6 | The Whisperer ([[Orenna Deepwell]]) moves to consolidate coastal cells. New Tide-Callers appointed in [[Veil Harbor]] and [[Driftmere]]. | Cult activity spikes. More brazen. Graffiti appears: "She speaks. We listen." Veil Unbound pamphlets circulate in taverns. |
| 7-10 | First successful seal-weakening ritual. One of four coastal seals cracked. Black Tide surges in response. | A Black Tide strikes without the usual warning signs. Stronger than any in decades. Casualties in triple digits. |
| 11-14 | Veil Unbound agents in Tidecross steal Oldborn resonance crystals (the Smuggler's Dilemma quest). Modified crystals create portable Sleepwalker-induction devices. | New Sleepwalkers appear far inland, in locations with no ocean access. The devices work through any water source. |
| 15-18 | Second seal cracked. The Lantern-Keepers are stretched thin and losing the intelligence war. The Whisperer begins targeting the players directly. | Assassination attempts. Sabotaged equipment. NPCs the players trust are revealed as compromised. |
| 19-22 | Third seal cracked. Serith's influence floods the coastal regions. The Veil Unbound goes public, no longer a secret cult but an open movement. | "The Veil is Unbound" becomes a rallying cry. Public demonstrations. Some towns welcome them. Others burn them out. Civil conflict. |
| 23-26 | Fourth seal targeted. If cracked, prison failure becomes inevitable within days rather than months. | Everything converges. The Veil Unbound throws everything at the final seal. This is their endgame. |

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Veil Unbound timeline is the most directly interruptible. Every seal-weakening ritual can be stopped if the players know when and where. The Whisperer can be identified and captured (removing the single point of failure). The cult's cell structure means losing one cell doesn't stop others, but losing the Whisperer cripples coordination.
>
> **Orenna Deepwell** is the key. If the players discover her identity and confront her, they face a woman who is not insane, not evil, and not wrong about everything. She watched her fishing village die to a Black Tide and heard Serith's comfort in the aftermath. Her faith is genuine. Making her the villain requires the players to grapple with the fact that Serith's reach begins with real compassion.

## The Oldborn Factions

**Goal:** Respond to the outlander crisis and the Second Silence. Three competing visions, one of which will prevail.

**Method:** Political maneuvering within the Circle of Speakers. Unilateral action if consensus fails.

| Session Window | Event (if unchecked) | Visible Signs |
|---|---|---|
| 1-8 | Circle debates continue. No consensus. [[Speaker Kaelthen\|Kaelthen]] (Sealed Gate) gains support as outlander presence destabilizes a Primordial Anchor near the eastern border. | Outlander expeditions turned back with increasing force. Trade at [[Tidecross]] constricts. Oldborn patrols more aggressive. |
| 9-14 | Kaelthen proposes temporary majority rule (a Covenant violation). Two moderate Speakers waver. [[Speaker Dravos\|Dravos]] (Ash Reclaimers) begins covert military preparations. | Diplomatic missions fail. Hael'rune fortified. Pact-Weavers recalled from border duty. |
| 15-18 | A Veil Unbound operation in the Oldborn Kingdoms (undetected by the Oldborn) destabilizes a second Primordial Anchor. Kaelthen's faction reaches majority. | The Sealed Gate prevails. Borders close. Thornwood Coast sealed. Any outlander in Oldborn territory is arrested or expelled. |
| 19-22 | [[Speaker Yavenne\|Yavenne]] (Open Hand) attempts a last diplomatic initiative, smuggling a message to outlander leaders. Dravos interprets this as treason and moves to have her *unwritten*. | Political crisis within the Kingdoms. Yavenne goes into hiding. Dravos's military faction grows bolder. |
| 23-26 | If the Second Silence accelerates, Primordial Anchors begin failing regardless of outlander presence. The Oldborn realize too late that isolation cannot save them. Dravos launches an unauthorized military expedition to the known continent. | War. The worst possible outcome for everyone. |

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Oldborn timeline is the most player-influenced. If the players earn Oldborn trust (through the Tidecross quests, the Fernwald Trial, or diplomatic success at Keth'vaal), Yavenne's faction gains strength and the timeline shifts dramatically toward cooperation.
>
> **The best outcome:** Players broker an alliance between the Open Hand and the known continent's factions, gaining access to Primordial magic and the road to [[The Loom's Edge]].
>
> **The worst outcome:** The Sealed Gate prevails, Dravos goes rogue, and the players must fight through hostile Oldborn territory to reach the Loom's Edge in Act V.
>
> **The hidden variable:** The Veil Unbound is operating in the Kingdoms without Oldborn knowledge. If the players expose this, it changes everything. The Oldborn's internal debate becomes irrelevant when they realize an external enemy is already inside.

## Using These Timelines

### Between Sessions

After each session, advance each villain's timeline by one step if the players haven't disrupted it. If they have, note what changed and adjust future steps.

### Consequences the Players See

Drop hints of advancing timelines through:
- **Rumors:** Tavern talk, merchant gossip, NPC asides
- **News:** Messages from allies ("Things are getting worse in the Dominion")
- **Environmental changes:** Tides getting stronger, crops failing, stars dimming
- **NPC reactions:** "You're still investigating *that*? Things have moved on. Have you heard what happened in Ostivaar?"

### When Players Derail a Timeline

Good. That means they're engaged. When a timeline step is prevented:
1. The villain reacts (Mourne sends assassins, the Veil Unbound changes tactics, Kaelthen calls emergency session)
2. Other timelines may accelerate (Serith compensates for a lost vector)
3. New opportunities open (the disruption creates allies, information, or leverage)

### When Multiple Timelines Converge

The campaign is designed so that by sessions 20-25, multiple timelines converge into a single crisis point. The players cannot be everywhere at once. They must prioritize. This is intentional, it creates dramatic stakes and forces meaningful choices.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **The real endgame question** is not "can the players stop Serith?" but "what are they willing to sacrifice to do it?" Each timeline they let advance is a cost, a region devastated, an ally lost, a population suffering. The campaign's moral weight comes from the accumulation of these costs.
>
> Serith's argument, that the world is dying anyway and her restart is mercy, becomes harder to dismiss as the players watch the world crumble around them. The best campaigns end with the players genuinely debating whether she might be right.
