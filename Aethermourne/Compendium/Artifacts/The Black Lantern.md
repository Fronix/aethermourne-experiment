## Description

A lantern of black crystalline bone, hexagonal, roughly the size of a human head. Inside burns a pale blue-white flame that produces no heat. The flame does not flicker. It does not respond to wind. It responds to the dead. In the presence of restless spirits, the flame brightens and turns toward them like a compass needle. In the presence of the truly at rest, it dims to near-invisibility. In [[The Pale Wastes]], it never dims.

The Morrhaelite frame is cold to the touch, colder than the ambient temperature of the Wastes, which is saying something. The bone-crystal is inscribed with symbols in a language that predates mortal writing, Morrhael's own notation for the passage between states of being. Scholars who study the inscriptions report an overwhelming sense of peace followed by an equally overwhelming sense of loss.

## History

The Black Lantern was [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s personal instrument, the divine artifact through which he performed his sacred work. It was the beacon that guided souls through the transition between life and death, the light at the end. When Morrhael was struck down over the frozen north during the Theomachis and the death-cycle shattered, the Lantern was not destroyed. It survived his death, tumbling across the tundra until it was found by the first of the risen dead, the souls who would become the Remnants' Court.

[[Revenant Yael]] has held it for over a thousand years. It is the reason she retains her identity when other undead fragment. The Lantern's light preserves the dead who shelter near it, holding them in coherent form rather than letting them dissolve into mindless echoes.

The [[Major Factions#Ashen Vigil|Ashen Vigil]] has searched for the Lantern for centuries, believing it lost in the Hollowdeep. They do not know that Yael has it. She has kept it hidden because she does not trust the living to use it wisely, and because she is afraid of what happens to the Remnants if the light goes out.

## Properties

**Soul Sight.** The flame reveals the spiritual state of anyone nearby. The living appear haloed in warm light. The undead appear in cold blue. The corrupted show threads of darkness winding through their aura. [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s agents cannot hide from the Lantern, their souls carry her shadow like a stain.

**Passage.** The Lantern can grant true death to the willing dead. An undead who steps into its light and chooses rest is gently, painlessly unmade. This is the only known method of ending undeath peacefully. It does not work on the unwilling, and forcing an undead into the light produces nothing but screaming and a flame that dims in what feels like grief.

**Morrhael's Echo.** Once per week, the wielder can ask the flame a question about the dead: who died here, how they died, what they left unfinished. The flame answers in visions, not words, showing the final moments of the deceased. The visions are unflinching and carry the full emotional weight of the death witnessed. Wielders who use this ability frequently develop the quiet, sorrowful demeanor of Morrhaelite exposure, Tier 2 [[Divine Materials - Mechanics|Death-Touched]] after a month of regular use.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Critical in Act IV. The Lantern is the key to navigating the Hollowdeep and potentially repairing the death-cycle. [[Revenant Yael]] will lend it to the PCs only if they prove they will honor the dead, not use them as tools. This is a character test, not a skill check. She reads intent, not words. If relit at full power, it could restore passage for all souls in [[The Pale Wastes]], depopulating the region of undead almost overnight.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Lantern can detect [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s influence because shadow corrupts the soul, and the Lantern reads souls. If brought near the Abyssal Trench, it goes wild, its flame turning black as it detects the vast imprisoned consciousness below.
>
> **The Deeper Secret.** The flame inside is a fragment of Morrhael's purpose, his last act before dying. If the flame is extinguished, the death-cycle shatters completely. Every undead on the continent would simultaneously lose all coherence. Including Yael. Including every Remnant who has held onto themselves for centuries through sheer will. The flame is not just a tool. It is the last functioning piece of the afterlife.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** In Act V, if the PCs bring the Lantern to the final confrontation, it can be used to sever Serith's connection to the dead gods' remains, cutting the shadow-roots at their source. But doing so extinguishes the flame. Yael knows this. She has accepted it. The question is whether the PCs can accept it on her behalf.
