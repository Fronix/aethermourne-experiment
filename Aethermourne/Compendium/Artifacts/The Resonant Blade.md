## Description

The Resonant Blade is not a weapon that was forged. It is a weapon that *happened*, a paradox given edge, a moment of the [[Cosmology and Magic#The Loom of Fate|Loom's]] exposed process that froze into a shape mortal hands can hold.

It appears as a longsword of impossible silver, its surface rippling with thread-thin lines that shift and rearrange like living calligraphy. The blade has no weight until it is swung, at which point it becomes exactly as heavy as the consequence it delivers. Hold it still and it is lighter than air. Strike with intent to kill and it carries the weight of a death that has already occurred.

The hilt is warm. Not from heat, but from *presence*, the persistent sensation that something is watching through the blade, observing each moment the wielder experiences, cataloguing every choice they make. The Threadwalkers who recovered it from the Temporal Barrens reported that holding the Blade felt like being read, like a book being opened by a hand they could not see.

It hums. A single, sustained note that changes pitch depending on proximity to exposed fate-threads. Near the Skein, it screams.

## History

The Resonant Blade was found three months ago by a Threadwalker expedition mapping the southern edge of the Temporal Barrens. It was embedded in a cliff face, point-down, surrounded by a circle of stone that had aged ten thousand years relative to the rock around it. The Threadwalker who pulled it free, a woman named Thaevyn, experienced her next six hours in reverse before her anchor-discipline reasserted linear time.

No record of the Blade exists in Oldborn archives. The [[The Loom's Edge#The Weavers of the Scar|Weavers of the Scar]], the silver-thread entities deep in the Skein, reacted to its removal with unprecedented agitation, moving toward the Temporal Barrens for the first time in recorded history before retreating. They want it back. They have not explained why. They do not explain anything.

The leading theory among the Threadwalkers is that the Blade is a *scar artifact*, a physical manifestation of the wound Orenthas inflicted when he excised the counter-prophecy's threads. The Loom is a process, not a substance, but where a process is violently interrupted, the interruption itself can crystallize into something tangible. The Blade is not made of the Loom. It is made of the Loom's *pain*.

## Properties

**Temporal Severance.** The Blade cuts through causality. A wound inflicted by the Resonant Blade appears on the target *before the strike lands*. The target bleeds, staggers, or falls an instant before the blade makes contact. In combat, this is devastating: the opponent reacts to a wound they have not yet received, their body obeying a future that has already been decided. Parrying is nearly impossible. The wound is real before the cause exists.

**Fate-Sight.** The wielder perceives thin, silver lines connecting people, objects, and events, the visible threads of causality that bind cause to effect. These threads reveal relationships, dependencies, and vulnerabilities invisible to normal perception. A king's crown connected by a silver thread to a servant's hand reveals where power truly lies. A wall connected by a fraying thread to the ground reveals where it will collapse.

**Resonance.** The Blade vibrates in proximity to fate-threads, excised or active. The stronger the resonance, the closer the wielder is to a critical juncture, a moment where the Loom's pattern is being decided. The Blade does not distinguish between the active Loom and the counter-prophecy's excised threads. It resonates with both, which makes it both a compass and a liability.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Mechanical Reality.** The Resonant Blade is campaign-grade. It should be introduced in Act II at the earliest and should carry consequences proportional to its power.
>
> **Combat:** Temporal Severance means the wielder always strikes first, regardless of initiative. Wounds manifest before the strike, making conventional defense ineffective. However, each use frays the wielder's personal timeline. After three strikes in a single combat, the wielder begins experiencing temporal echoes: seeing their own actions replayed, hearing their words before they speak them, momentarily existing in two moments simultaneously. After six strikes, the wielder risks Fate Resonance (see [[The Loom's Edge#Fate Resonance]]).
>
> **Fate-Sight:** Useful for investigation, diplomacy, and navigation. The GM should reveal one hidden connection per scene when the wielder is actively looking. Overuse (more than three readings per day) causes the wielder's own fate-threads to become visible to others, including enemies, who can then perceive the wielder's vulnerabilities, dependencies, and destined moments of weakness.
>
> **The Real Danger:** The Blade is a piece of the wound Orenthas left in the Loom. Every time it is used, the wound opens slightly wider. The excised threads gain a fraction more purchase on reality. The counter-prophecy inches closer to completing itself. The wielder is not just using a weapon. They are *unraveling the containment* that Orenthas sacrificed his pantheon to build.
>
> **Faction Interest:**
> - [[The Star-Readers]] want to study it, believing it can reveal the Loom's architecture
> - [[The Veil Unbound]] want it as a tool to accelerate the Second Silence
> - The [[The Oldborn Kingdoms#The Sealed Gate|Sealed Gate]] want it destroyed before it destabilizes the Anchors
> - The [[The Loom's Edge#The Weavers of the Scar|Weavers of the Scar]] want it returned, possibly to reseal the wound
> - [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s agents want it because a blade that severs causality could sever the chains of her prison
>
> **What happens if destroyed:** Unknown. The Blade may be the only thing holding the wound partially closed. Destroying it could seal the wound permanently, or rip it wide open. This is a campaign-climax decision, not a casual choice.
