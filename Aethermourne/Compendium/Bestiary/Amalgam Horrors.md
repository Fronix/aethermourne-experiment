## Description

Amalgam Horrors are what happens when the Consuming goes wrong, or perhaps when it goes exactly right and the result is simply unbearable to witness. They are masses of fused flesh, iron, and overlapping faces, three or four or seven bodies merged into a single shambling form where boundaries between individuals have dissolved. Limbs protrude at wrong angles. Faces press outward from the mass like drowning swimmers breaking the surface, mouths open, eyes wild, each one animate and aware and separate from the others.

Each face can speak independently. Each one begs for help. Each one claims to be the original person, the one who entered the Descent alone, the one who does not belong in this body. Each one accuses the others of being the impostor, the parasite, the reason they are trapped. Some of them are lying. Some of them are telling the truth. There is no way to determine which, and the horror is not what the Amalgam does to you but what it makes you feel: the visceral, gut-level recognition that this could happen to you, that the boundary between your body and another's is only as solid as the Descent allows it to be.

## Behavior & Tactics

Amalgam Horrors are driven by conflicting impulses, each constituent soul pulling the shared body in a different direction. This makes them erratic and unpredictable. One face might plead for mercy while another directs the body to attack. One arm might reach out in supplication while the other swings a fist of fused iron and bone. They are not coordinated fighters, but their sheer mass, their resistance to pain, and their psychological impact make them dangerous opponents.

The true threat of an Amalgam Horror is not physical. It is the choice it forces on anyone who encounters it. The faces beg for separation, for rescue, for death. Some claim that if you destroy the body, their individual souls will be freed. Others insist that destruction will kill them all, that the merger is irreversible, that the only mercy is to help them learn to coexist. There is no established answer. The Descent does not provide one. Every Amalgam Horror is a moral problem with no clean solution, and the longer you stand debating, the closer the Consuming creeps toward you.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Use.** Amalgam Horrors are emotional encounters disguised as combat encounters. The faces should be specific: give them names, fragments of backstory, conflicting claims. One face says she was a merchant from Rustgate who entered Level 1 three days ago. Another says he has been in the Descent for centuries. A third face just screams. Let the PCs try to help, and let them discover that there may be no way to help. This is Level 8's lesson: the loss of self is not always dramatic. Sometimes it is simply permanent.
>
> **Moral Complexity.** If a PC attempts to separate the souls through magic or force, the result should be ambiguous. Perhaps one soul emerges intact while the others dissolve. Perhaps the separation kills them all. Perhaps the "rescued" soul is not who they claimed to be. The Descent punishes certainty.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> A shape blocks the corridor in the Maw, a mass of iron-laced flesh the size of a cart, pressing against the walls. Four faces are visible. One is weeping silently. One is speaking rapidly in a language no one recognizes. One looks directly at the party's healer and says, clearly and calmly, "Please. I know you can separate us. I can feel it. Just pull me free and let the others go." The fourth face looks at the same healer and says, "She is lying. If you pull her free, the rest of us die. She knows this." Both faces appear completely sincere.

## Stat Block

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble): Amalgam Horror (4-Soul Variant)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Amalgam Horror | 6 | M | 95 | 4 | +4 | -1 | +1 | flailing limbs 2d8+4 bludgeoning (reach 2 spaces) |
>
> **Resistances:** Resistant to slashing (iron-laced flesh).
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Conflicting Impulses:** At the start of each turn, roll 1d4: (1) Aggressive, attacks nearest creature; (2) Pleading, does not attack, begs for help; (3) Panicked, moves randomly, Dash; (4) Calculating, attacks most vulnerable target, rolling twice and taking higher on the attack.
> - **Grapple:** On hit, target is grappled (Str DC 15 to escape) as multiple arms seize them.
> - **Absorb:** If the Amalgam grapples a creature for 3 consecutive rounds, the creature begins merging. Str save DC 16 each round or take 2d6 necrotic damage. A creature reduced to 0 HP is absorbed permanently, adding +20 HP and a new face.
> - **Faces Speak:** Each face speaks independently with contradictory information. Insight DC 14 to determine truthfulness.
>
> **Bloodied (half HP):** The Amalgam's conflicting impulses intensify. Roll 1d4 twice and use both results simultaneously (the body tears at itself while attacking, taking 1d6 damage at the start of each turn).
>
> **Weakness:** Radiant damage stuns it for 1 round as souls briefly separate. Fire is effective but all faces scream, forcing Will save DC 12 on creatures within 6 spaces or frightened for 1 round.
