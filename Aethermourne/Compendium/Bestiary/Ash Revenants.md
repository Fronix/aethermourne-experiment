## Description

Ash Revenants are the residue of souls who surrendered to the [[Level 1 - The Ashfield|Ashfield]]. Their bodies are compressed iron ash shaped into crude humanoid forms, grey and brittle, held together by the last ember of whatever ambition they carried in life. They stand roughly human-height but weigh almost nothing, their frames hollow, their movements stuttering and uncertain, like someone trying to remember how walking works.

Each Revenant is locked to a single desire, the final ambition that defined them before the Ashfield consumed their identity. A merchant who craved wealth reaches endlessly for coins that are not there. A soldier who wanted glory swings a sword made of packed dust. Their faces, when they have them, are smooth and featureless except for the mouth, which moves constantly, whispering the same phrase over and over in a voice like wind through dead grass.

## Behavior & Tactics

Ash Revenants are not predators. They are remnants, and they behave like it. They drift across the grey plains in aimless patterns, drawn toward living creatures by instinct rather than hunger. When they encounter travelers, they follow at a distance, mimicking gestures and postures, a grotesque pantomime of the living. This behavior is unsettling but not dangerous.

The danger emerges when a Revenant encounters a situation that mirrors its defining ambition. A Revenant driven by greed becomes violent near valuable objects. One driven by conquest attacks anyone who appears to hold authority. The cruelest irony is that fulfilling their ambition destroys them, their ash-bodies collapse into powder the moment their desire is satisfied, so they instinctively avoid resolution. They circle their obsession endlessly, growing more agitated, more aggressive, but never completing the loop. A clever opponent can exploit this by offering the Revenant exactly what it wants.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Ash Revenants are useful as environmental storytelling. Each one is a cautionary tale about unchecked ambition, perfectly suited to [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s domain. When running them, give each Revenant a specific whispered phrase that hints at who they were. Players who listen carefully can piece together the Revenant's desire and use it as a weapon.
>
> **Campaign Connection.** If the Ashfield's planar thin points are widening (as suggested in the campaign arc), Ash Revenants may begin appearing near [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] fragments in the mortal world, formed from ambient war-essence and local ash or dust.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party crosses the Fade Line and finds a cluster of seven Ash Revenants standing in a circle, all reaching toward a central point where nothing visible exists. As the party approaches, the Revenants turn in unison. One of them is wearing the tattered remains of a uniform the party recognizes, a soldier from a mortal nation who went missing decades ago. The Revenants begin mimicking the party's movements. If anyone draws a weapon, the uniformed Revenant charges.
>
> **Twist:** The uniformed Revenant's whispered phrase is a name, someone still alive in the mortal world. Delivering the name to that person would destroy the Revenant, but the name belongs to a significant NPC.
