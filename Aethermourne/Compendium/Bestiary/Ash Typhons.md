# Ash Typhons

> | **Origin** | Accumulated conflict energy vented as meteorological phenomena by the domain |
> | **Threat Level** | Serious (environmental) |
> | **Associated God** | [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] |

## Description

Ash Typhons are living storms of iron ash that scour entire regions of the Descent's upper levels. They are not weather in any natural sense. They are rage given meteorological form, the domain's method of venting excess aggression that accumulates when too much conflict occurs in too small an area. They arise without warning: the air thickens, tastes of hot metal, and then the horizon disappears behind a wall of churning grey-black particulate that moves with the speed and malice of something that wants to find you.

Inside an Ash Typhon, visibility drops to nothing. The ash is composed of iron particulate fine enough to infiltrate clothing, armor, and lungs, and coarse enough to strip exposed skin to raw tissue within minutes. Breathing is impossible without filtration. Sound is swallowed by the roaring curtain of particulate, reducing communication to shouting at point-blank range. The storms can last minutes or hours, depending on how much conflict energy they are processing, and they move across the landscape with apparent intentionality, lingering over areas of recent violence and bypassing regions where the domain's equilibrium is undisturbed.

## Behavior & Tactics

Ash Typhons are not creatures in the traditional sense. They have no intelligence, no anatomy, no weak points. They are environmental events, expressions of the domain's internal pressure system. When conflict on a given level exceeds the ambient baseline, the excess energy manifests as an Ash Typhon, which scours the area until the energy is discharged. The more intense the preceding conflict, the larger and longer-lasting the resulting storm.

The storms cannot be fought, dispersed, or redirected through conventional means. Shelter is the only defense, and even shelter is temporary, as the iron ash infiltrates through any gap larger than a hair's width. The ash itself is mildly corrosive, etching metal and irritating skin on contact, and prolonged exposure causes iron deposits to form in the lungs, a condition the Descent's trapped mortals call "rust-lung" that slowly suffocates the afflicted over days or weeks. Ash Typhons are the Descent's primary method of enforcing a brutal ecological balance: fight too much and the domain itself punishes you, not out of malice, but out of the same mechanical necessity that causes a pressure valve to vent steam.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Use.** Ash Typhons serve as pacing tools and environmental tension. They limit how long a fight can last on the upper levels, force the party to seek shelter and interact with NPCs or environments they might otherwise bypass, and establish that the Descent is not a static dungeon but a living system with its own rhythms. A party that fights everything on Level 1 will learn quickly that the domain responds to violence with violence.
>
> **Foreshadowing.** The fact that Ash Typhons only occur on Levels 1-4 is significant. The deeper levels do not need pressure valves because the entities there have learned to channel their aggression constructively, or because the domain has already consumed enough of their identity that they no longer generate excess conflict energy. The absence of Ash Typhons on lower levels should feel wrong once the party realizes it.
>
> **Mechanical Note.** Treat Ash Typhons as hazard encounters with escalating severity. Round 1: reduced visibility, minor damage. Round 2: near-zero visibility, moderate damage, breathing becomes difficult. Round 3+: severe damage, suffocation risk, iron deposits begin forming. The party should be desperately seeking shelter by Round 2.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is crossing the Ashfield on Level 1 when the guide they hired in Rustgate stops mid-sentence and looks at the sky. "Storm," she says, and starts running. The horizon behind the party darkens, not with clouds but with a churning wall of grey-black particulate that stretches from ground to sky. The party has roughly two minutes to find shelter before the Typhon reaches them. The nearest structure is a half-collapsed iron bunker four hundred yards away. Inside, three other travelers are already huddled, their faces wrapped in cloth, their skin raw and bleeding from a previous exposure. The storm hits the bunker like a fist, and iron ash begins sifting through the ceiling seams.
