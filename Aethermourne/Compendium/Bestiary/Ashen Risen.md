# Ashen Risen

*She sat down at her old table. Picked up her old cup. Looked at her son with eyes made of cinder and said his name perfectly. He screamed. She did not understand why.*

## Description

The Ashen Risen are what happens when death stops being final and the Vigil's oldest safeguard fails. For centuries, [[The Ashen Vigil]] cremated their dead to prevent the bone-pull from raising them, reducing bodies to ash that could not be animated. It worked. Until it didn't.

An Ashen Risen assembles in silence, usually at night, usually near the cremation site where its ashes were scattered. Cinder and bone-ash draw together across the ground in slow spirals, compacting into a rough human shape that stands with the careful deliberation of someone remembering how legs work. The form is imperfect: features suggested rather than sculpted, fingers that end in blunt ash-points, eyes that are dark hollows where heat-glow pulses faintly like embers buried in snow. They are grey and fragile, trailing ash-dust with every movement, and they smell of cold hearth-fire.

They retain fragments of memory but no emotional context. A mother who was cremated six months ago walks back into her Hold, sits at her old table, picks up her old cup, and says her children's names in a voice like wind through dead coals. She remembers the names. She does not remember why they matter. A soldier returns to the barracks and stands at attention, waiting for orders that will never come, ash flaking from his shoulders with each hour of motionless patience. They are not hostile. They are not anything. They are echoes pressed into cinder, going through motions that have lost their meaning.

## Behavior & Tactics

Ashen Risen do not attack unless physically prevented from doing what their fragments of memory compel them to do. A Risen trying to enter its old home will push past obstacles with surprising strength, not violently but persistently, the way water pushes past a dam. If restrained, it struggles with confused, unfocused force. If struck, it does not fight back. It falls, reassembles over minutes, and tries again.

In combat they are fragile. A strong wind scatters their outer layers. Water dissolves the ash-bond that holds them together. A solid blow sends cinder and bone-dust spraying across the room. But they reform. Within days, the ashes draw back together, the shape reassembles, and the Risen walks again. The Vigil's Ash-Speakers have tried blessing the ashes, scattering them across running water, separating them into sealed containers. Nothing works for more than a week. The death-domain is broken, and broken things do not respect the rules that were supposed to govern them.

The real threat is not physical. It is what the Ashen Risen do to the living. Families who watch their cremated loved ones walk back through the door, stripped of everything that made them people, reduced to habit without heart. Children who see their father's face remade in grey ash and hear their name spoken without love. Soldiers who must destroy a comrade they burned with full honors last month. Morale in the Holds is crumbling, because the Vigil's promise, that cremation grants rest, that the dead stay dead, is visibly, walking-through-the-door false.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Ashen Risen are proof that Morrhael's domain has deteriorated beyond the Vigil's ability to compensate. Cremation was never a divine solution, it was a practical one, and the metaphysical conditions that made it effective have changed. [[The Ashen Vigil]] is running out of theological answers. Some Ash-Speakers have begun questioning whether the Vigil's entire approach to death needs to be reconsidered, a heresy that [[Thessa Ironveil]] cannot afford to acknowledge publicly.
>
> **Campaign connection:** The Ashen Risen are Serith's psychological weapon whether she directed them or not. Every Risen that walks back into a Hold erodes faith in the institutions that hold the Pale Wastes together. If PCs find a way to permanently lay an Ashen Risen to rest (perhaps through the Black Lantern, or through repairing a fragment of Morrhael's domain), it would be a politically and spiritually significant event, proof that the death-cycle can be mended.
>
> **Moral encounter:** The most effective use of an Ashen Risen is to make the party care about a specific one. Give it a name. Give it a family member who is begging the party not to destroy it. Make the party watch it do something heartbreaking, trying to cook a meal it can no longer taste, sitting by a cradle it remembers but cannot feel, and then ask them what they want to do.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party arrives at a small Hold to find the residents gathered around the central hearth, silent, staring at an Ashen Risen sitting in a chair by the fire. The Hold's elder explains: this was Marta. She died of fever two months ago. They cremated her properly, said the rites, scattered the ash. She came back three days ago. She sits in her chair. She says her husband's name every few hours. She does not eat, drink, or sleep. She is not hurting anyone. Her husband is in the back room and has not stopped crying since she returned. The elder asks the party: what do we do?
>
> **Complication:** Destroying the Risen in front of the husband may be necessary but will earn his permanent enmity. Finding a gentler solution (Ash-Speaker rites, carrying her ashes far from the Hold, discovering why she returned) earns the Hold's loyalty and provides intelligence about the death-domain's deterioration.

> **Resistances/Immunities:** Immune to poison, psychic, and necrotic (already dead, beyond death's reach). Vulnerable to bludgeoning, water, and wind effects.
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Fragile Form.** Bludgeoning damage, strong wind (natural or magical), and water-based attacks deal double damage. A critical hit scatters the Risen immediately.
> - **Reform.** A scattered Ashen Risen reassembles in 1d4 days at or near its cremation site. No known method permanently prevents reformation except repairing the death-domain itself.
> - **Memory Fragment.** The Risen retains 1d4 specific memories from its life, enacted without emotional context. These memories may contain useful information (a hidden cache, a secret the living person never shared, the identity of their killer if the death was suspicious).
> - **Non-Aggressive.** The Risen does not initiate attacks. It strikes only when physically restrained from completing its memory-driven behavior. Even then, its attacks are unfocused, confused rather than hostile.
>
> **Bloodied:** The Risen's form destabilizes, ash sloughing in sheets. It speaks one final memory fragment aloud, clear and precise, before collapsing. This memory is always the most personal, the most painful, and the most useful.
>
> **Weakness:** Wind and water destroy the form instantly. Fire re-cremation scatters the ashes but does not prevent reformation. The Risen can be guided away from populated areas by carrying objects associated with its memories (leading it with a lure of its old possessions).
