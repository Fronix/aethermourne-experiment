## Description

Ashite Geometers were people. Miners who worked too deep in the Spine for too long, breathing crystalline dust, handling raw Ashite with cracked gloves, feeling the Hum settle into their bones until it replaced their heartbeat. Their minds crystallized first, thoughts narrowing to pure pattern-recognition, then their bodies followed. Ashite threads through their tissues like frost through glass, white veins branching beneath skin that has taken on a faint mineral translucence. Their eyes glow with soft white light that brightens when they encounter disorder.

In [[Hearthstone]], miners whisper about the deep shaft crews that stopped coming up. They were found standing in a circle in a hollow chamber, unresponsive, smiling, *getting brighter*. They were still breathing. They were still, technically, alive. They just were not people anymore.

## Behavior & Tactics

Geometers do not attack. They organize. They rearrange their environment into perfect mathematical order: stones sorted by size and mineral composition, tools aligned on precise axes, debris cleared into geometric patterns. They work silently, methodically, and with a focus that is almost meditative.

The danger comes when they encounter living beings. Living bodies are asymmetric, chaotic, *wrong*. A Geometer that notices a person will attempt to "correct" them, breaking and resetting bones into symmetrical configurations, straightening the spine, aligning the ribs, making the body *orderly*. They do this with the gentle, patient attentiveness of a craftsman fixing a flaw. They do not understand that it hurts.

Geometers can be redirected. Presenting a mathematical problem complex enough to occupy their attention (DC 16 Intelligence to craft a sufficiently engaging proof or geometric puzzle) causes them to abandon their current task and focus entirely on solving it. They will work the problem for hours, sometimes days, buying time for escape or extraction.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Edda Haelstrom]]'s falsified mining reports conceal how many workers have become Geometers in the deep shafts of [[Hearthstone]]. The true number is at least thirty, possibly more. Finding one is meant to be a gut-punch: these were people, colleagues, someone's parent or child. The emotional weight matters more than the combat challenge.
>
> **Hidden Value.** The Geometers are inadvertently mapping the [[Cosmology and Magic#Ley Lines and the Aetheric Web|Aetheric Web]]'s structure through their compulsive patterning. The geometric arrangements they create on cave floors and walls are, if interpreted correctly (DC 18 Intelligence or Arcana), a three-dimensional model of local ley line configurations. The [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]] would kill for this information. The Church would kill to suppress it.
>
> **Campaign Connection.** Discovering the Geometers and tracing responsibility to Haelstrom's cover-up is a strong Act II subplot that exposes Dominion corruption and the human cost of Ashite dependency.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party descends into a disused mine shaft beneath [[Hearthstone]], following a missing miner's trail. The tunnel opens into a cathedral-sized cavern where everything has been arranged. Every loose stone sorted by size in concentric rings. Every tool laid out in perfect parallel lines. Mining carts aligned on invisible axes. And at the center, seven figures, standing in a circle, unblinking, skin faintly translucent, white light pulsing behind their eyes. One of them is the missing miner. She turns toward the party. She smiles. She reaches for the nearest PC's arm with gentle, crystalline fingers.
>
> **Complication:** One of the Geometers is [[Edda Haelstrom]]'s brother, reported as "transferred to another shaft" six months ago. If the party brings this information to the surface, Haelstrom will do anything to keep it quiet.
