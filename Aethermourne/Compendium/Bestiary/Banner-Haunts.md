## Description

Banner-Haunts are the war standards of empires that fell so completely that even their names have been consumed by the Descent. They appear as tattered banners, pennants, and gonfalons planted upright in the iron earth, their fabric scorched and stained but still bearing the heraldry of forgotten nations. The symbols shift when observed directly, cycling through the sigils of a hundred dead kingdoms in the time it takes to blink.

When a Banner-Haunt activates, it unfurls with a sound like a war horn heard from miles away. Phantom soldiers coalesce in its shadow, translucent figures in the armor of the empire whose sigil the banner currently displays. They form ranks, take up positions, and begin to march. The banner leads. The host follows. And anyone who falls into step, even by accident, even for a single stride, joins the phantom army.

## Behavior & Tactics

Banner-Haunts are patient traps. They stand motionless on battlefields, in corridors, at crossroads, waiting for the living to pass. The danger is movement. Walking in the same direction as a phantom host, matching pace with a spectral column, turning to follow the sound of phantom drums, any of these can bind a mortal to the banner's army. The binding is immediate and total. The victim's body continues marching while their mind watches from inside, unable to stop their legs from carrying them forward.

The phantom host marches in an endless loop: advance, engage an invisible enemy, fight, die, reform, advance again. Those bound to the banner experience every cycle. They feel the charge, the clash of weapons, the moment of death, a pain that is real despite the phantom nature of the combat. Then they reform, whole and screaming, and the banner calls the advance again. Freeing a bound companion requires destroying the banner itself, which releases the phantom host in a violent burst of war-essence, or pulling them physically out of the marching column, which requires overcoming the binding force while avoiding being caught in the same pattern.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Use.** Banner-Haunts illustrate the Conquest's central horror: war without end, victory without meaning. A PC trapped in the march experiences the complete arc of a soldier's life, the pride, the terror, the death, the resurrection into more of the same, compressed into minutes. This is what Kaevroth's war-essence does to ambition: it promises glory and delivers repetition.
>
> **Tactical Note.** Banner-Haunts are best used as environmental hazards rather than direct combat encounters. The party navigating Level 6 must constantly watch their footing, avoid matching pace with phantom columns, and resist the subtle pull of distant war drums. Multiple Banner-Haunts in proximity create overlapping phantom armies that turn entire regions of the Conquest into near-impassable marching grounds.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party approaches a crossroads on Level 6 where three banners stand planted in the iron ground, each bearing a different faded sigil. A phantom column of soldiers marches from east to west, their boots making no sound but their formation perfect. The party must cross the column's path. Any PC who steps into the column and takes a step in its direction feels iron gauntlets close around their arms as phantom soldiers pull them into formation. Initiative begins, with the trapped PC marching involuntarily while companions attempt a rescue before the next "death cycle" completes.
