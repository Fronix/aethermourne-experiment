## Description

Battle Echoes are not creatures in any conventional sense. They are recordings, vast psychic imprints of ancient battles seared into the fabric of the Proving Grounds by the intensity of the combat that occurred there. They manifest as ghostly replays: translucent armies clashing across iron plains, siege engines hurling phantom projectiles, war-constructs tearing through spectral ranks. The images shimmer with amber light, and the sound, the screaming, the clash of metal, the war-cries in languages dead for two millennia, builds from a whisper to a roar.

The danger is that Battle Echoes are not passive. They do not play out like a vision to be observed from a safe distance. A Battle Echo is a wound in reality, a moment of violence so intense that it refuses to stay in the past. When a living creature enters the Echo's radius, the boundary between observer and participant dissolves. The ghostly soldiers become solid. The phantom weapons draw real blood. The Echo does not care that you are not part of this battle. You are here, and here is the battle, and the battle does not end until it is finished or you are dead.

## Behavior & Tactics

A Battle Echo activates when living creatures cross into its boundary, which is invisible and can span anywhere from a small courtyard to an entire valley. The transition is instantaneous: one step you are watching translucent ghosts, the next you are standing in the middle of a Theomachis-era engagement with real swords swinging at your head. The Echo assigns you a role, always on the losing side, and the phantom soldiers treat you as one of their enemies.

The only way to survive a Battle Echo is to fight through it until the battle reaches its historical conclusion. Each Echo replays a specific engagement with a fixed duration and outcome. Some last minutes. Some last hours. The longest recorded Echo on the Proving Grounds replays a three-day siege. Within the Echo, injuries are real, exhaustion is real, and death is permanent. If you die inside the Echo, your body remains when the Echo fades, a fresh corpse on an ancient battlefield.

Escaping the boundary before the Echo concludes is theoretically possible but requires knowing where the boundary is while surrounded by chaos. Running in the wrong direction simply takes you deeper. Experienced travelers on the Proving Grounds learn to read the signs, a faint amber shimmer, a distant sound of horns, the taste of old blood, and give wide berth.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Battle Echoes are set-piece encounters. Prepare them like mini-scenarios: describe the historical battle, the forces involved, the terrain, and the fixed outcome. The party must survive until the Echo's losing side is destroyed (which happens regardless of what the party does, the outcome is predetermined). Their goal is to stay alive while history replays around them.
>
> **Narrative Tool.** Echoes can reveal lore about the Theomachis. The party might witness [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s war-constructs in action, see the tactics of armies long dead, or even catch a glimpse of the god himself in the Echo's background, a mountain of iron and stolen light raging across the horizon.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party hears distant horns and sees amber light bleeding across the horizon. Before they can change course, the light washes over them and they are standing in the middle of a Theomachis battlefield. To their left, a phalanx of divine soldiers loyal to [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]] is crumbling under a charge of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s war-constructs. The Echo has placed the party among the defenders. Iron constructs the size of horses bear down on them, and the phantom soldiers beside them are already dying.
>
> **Duration:** This Echo lasts approximately thirty minutes of real time. The defenders lose. The party must survive the rout and avoid being trampled during the collapse of the defensive line.
>
> **Reward:** After the Echo fades, a single real object remains on the battlefield, a weapon or piece of armor from the original engagement, preserved by the Echo's power.
