## Description

A Bone Choir is not a creature. It is an event. During Death Tides in the deep Wilt and along the Bonewood border, dozens of small cycling animals, birds, rodents, lizards, insects, die simultaneously and rise as a single coordinated organism. Their tiny skeletons shed fur and flesh in seconds, lifted by necrotic energy into a swirling cloud of bone fragments that moves with eerie synchronization. And it sings.

The sound is not music in any conventional sense. It is a resonant hum produced by hundreds of small bones vibrating at slightly different frequencies, creating a harmonic that bypasses the ears and settles directly into the listener's mind. The hum carries fragments of something older: syllables in a language no living person speaks, tonal patterns that evoke specific emotions (grief, acceptance, the peace of letting go), and, increasingly, coherent phrases that [[Yara Thornbloom]] has been transcribing with growing alarm. The Bone Choir is singing pieces of [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s death-song, the divine utterance that was supposed to guide the dead to passage. It was shattered when Morrhael fell. The Choir is trying to reassemble it from fragments, one Death Tide at a time.

During the life-phase, the swarm disperses. The animals return to their living forms, scattered and confused, with no memory of what they were part of. They resume eating, nesting, and fleeing from predators as if nothing happened. Then the next Death Tide comes, and they die, and they sing again.

## Behavior & Tactics

A Bone Choir does not hunt. It drifts through the Wilt like weather, following currents of necrotic energy that are invisible to most observers. It becomes dangerous when living creatures enter its resonance field, the area within roughly thirty feet where the death-song is audible. The hum disrupts concentration, imposes alien emotions, and, at close range, begins to convince listeners that they are already dead and should stop resisting.

The Choir does not attack physically. Its bones are fragile, its individual components negligible. The danger is entirely psychic. Prolonged exposure causes listeners to become passive, sitting down, lowering weapons, closing their eyes. Victims describe the experience afterward as "peaceful" and "correct," which is perhaps the most disturbing part. The death-song was designed to ease passage. It still works. It just has nowhere to send the people it convinces.

[[The Cyclekeepers]] have developed a counter-measure: Cycle-chanting, a rhythmic vocalization that harmonizes with the death-song and introduces life-phase frequencies that disrupt the Choir's coherence. It requires training and concentration. Untrained listeners should cover their ears and move perpendicular to the Choir's drift path. Running directly away is ineffective, the resonance field moves faster than a person walks.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The fragments [[Yara Thornbloom]] has transcribed are becoming more coherent with each passing month. Six months ago, the Choir produced isolated syllables. Three months ago, partial phrases. Last week, Yara heard a complete sentence: "The gate is open and no one stands at the threshold." She believes the Choir is a symptom of [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s death-domain trying to reassemble itself without its god, an automated system searching for its missing operator.
>
> **The Serith Connection.** The Cycle's acceleration is giving the Choir more Death Tides to work with, more repetitions, faster reassembly. [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s destabilization of both divine legacies is inadvertently helping Morrhael's death-song reconstitute. If the song completes, the consequences are unknown, it could restore proper death passage in the region (a miracle), or it could activate without a god to direct it and pull every living thing in the Thornwild into passage simultaneously (a catastrophe).
>
> **Campaign Hook.** The Bone Choir's fragments are a puzzle the PCs can piece together across multiple encounters. Each time they hear a Choir, they catch different phrases. Assembled, the phrases form instructions, a ritual that either stabilizes the Cycle or breaks it permanently, depending on how the PCs interpret the song and which parts they trust. [[Yara Thornbloom]] will share her transcriptions if the PCs earn her trust.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Bone Choir (swarm) | 3 | L | 30 | 3 (drift) | -2 | +2 | +4 | Death-Song (psychic aura) |
>
> **Resistances/Immunities:** Immune to poison, necrotic, cold. Resistant to non-magical physical damage (swarm). Vulnerable to thunder damage (shatters bones, disperses swarm).
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Death-Song Aura (6 spaces).** Creatures starting their turn in the aura make a DC 13 Will save. Failure: dazed (can move or act, not both) as alien peace floods the mind. Failure by 5+: passive (drops weapons, sits down, takes no actions) for 1 round. Success: immune for 1 round. [[The Cyclekeepers]] and trained individuals have advantage on this save.
> - **Fragmentary Prophecy.** A creature that fails the Will save hears a fragment of Morrhael's death-song. The GM delivers one cryptic phrase per failed save (these are campaign clues, not random flavor).
> - **Swarm Dispersal.** At 0 HP, the Choir scatters. Individual bone-animals are harmless. The Choir reforms at the next Death Tide (typically 4-8 hours).
> - **Life-Phase Dissolution.** If the Bloom's influence surges (Bloom Surge event, or a character channels growth/life energy into the swarm), the Choir disperses immediately as the animals' life-phase overrides the death-phase.
>
> **Weakness:** Thunder damage deals double damage and forces the Choir to move 3 spaces away. Radiant damage disrupts the death-song (aura suppressed for 1 round). Life-channeling (Greensinger, druid, or similar) forces immediate dispersal.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 2-4 players of level 2-4

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The trail into the deep Wilt goes quiet. Not forest-quiet. Wrong-quiet. The wind stops. The insects stop. Then the sound begins, not from a direction but from everywhere, a hum that vibrates in the party's teeth and settles behind their eyes. A cloud of tiny bones rises from the deadwood ahead, sparrow skulls and mouse ribs and beetle carapaces, spinning in a slow vortex that catches the grey light. The hum becomes a voice. The voice says something in a language no one speaks. The cleric's eyes fill with tears. The barbarian sits down. The rogue realizes she has already sheathed her daggers and cannot remember doing it.
>
> **Complication:** A Thornguard scout is sitting cross-legged in the Choir's path, eyes closed, smiling. She has been here for hours. She does not want to leave. She will resist rescue. The Choir is drifting toward [[Bone Garden]], and if it reaches the settlement during a shift change, the harvesters will be exposed without warning.
