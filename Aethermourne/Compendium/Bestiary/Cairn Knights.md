## Description

Cairn Knights are what happens when a goddess of secrets decides she needs an army and the only raw material available is the dead. Inside [[The Pale Wastes#Hollowdeep|Hollowdeep]], where the necromantic saturation is strongest, the risen dead have been organized into something that should not exist: a military. They wear salvaged armor, some of it centuries old, patched and maintained with a precision that suggests hands remembering a craft the mind has forgotten. Their weapons are Morrhaelite crystal and frozen bone, crude but effective, and they carry shields made from skulls fused together with necrotic ice.

They patrol Hollowdeep's perimeter in disciplined squads of six to twelve, build fortifications from crystallized bone, and maintain supply lines for resources the dead do not need. They have developed their own hierarchy based on fragmentary memories of living armies: rank badges scratched into armor, challenge-and-response protocols performed in voices like wind through hollow stone. Most Cairn Knights believe they serve a living commander they cannot remember. The name is gone. The face is gone. The duty remains, and the duty is enough.

A single Cairn Knight separated from its squad becomes something else entirely. Confused, docile, sometimes pathetic, sitting in the snow trying to remember its orders, touching its rank badge and whispering numbers that might have been a unit designation. These are soldiers who cannot stop serving because stopping means confronting the void where identity used to be.

## Behavior & Tactics

This is a military encounter, not a monster fight. Cairn Knights deploy shield walls, execute flanking maneuvers, and retreat-and-regroup when losing ground. They are individually weaker than [[Waste Horrors#Bone-Wraith|Bone-Wraiths]] but devastating in formation, where their overlapping defenses and coordinated strikes make them far more dangerous than the sum of their parts.

Breaking formation is more effective than raw damage. Disrupting the command structure (killing the squad leader, identifiable by additional rank scratches), isolating individuals from the group, or creating loud, chaotic noise that drowns out their whispered orders all cause the formation to collapse. A shattered formation reverts to individual Knights, and individual Knights are confused, slow, and heartbreakingly lost.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Cairn Knight numbers are growing. The Hollowdeep is industrializing death. [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s influence has transformed the crater from a graveyard into a garrison, and the garrison is expanding. [[The Ashen Vigil]] scouts report new fortification rings appearing monthly. The Knights are not preparing to defend Hollowdeep. They are preparing to march out of it.
>
> **Moral Weight.** Every Cairn Knight was a person. Some wear Hold insignia. Some carry personal effects, a locket, a child's toy, a letter never delivered. Finding these items mid-combat should be devastating. The PCs are not fighting monsters. They are destroying people who were already destroyed once.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Cairn Knight | 2 | M | 25 | 4 | +2 | +1 | -1 | bone-blade 1d6+2 cold |
>
> **Resistances/Immunities:** Immune to cold and poison. Resistant to necrotic.
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Shield Wall.** +2 Defense when adjacent to another Cairn Knight. Stacks with multiple adjacent allies (max +4).
> - **Coordinated Strike.** When a Cairn Knight hits a target that another Cairn Knight hit this round, deal +1d6 bonus cold damage.
> - **Formation Discipline.** Cannot be frightened while in formation (2+ Knights within 3 spaces). When last Knight in formation, immediately makes Will save DC 10 or becomes confused (attacks random target, moves randomly) for 1d4 rounds.
>
> **Bloodied:** The Knight's whispered orders become audible. Nearby PCs hear fragmentary commands in a language they almost understand. Will save DC 12 or be shaken (disadvantage on attacks) for 1 round from the horror of recognition.
>
> **Weakness:** Radiant damage bypasses Shield Wall. Thunder damage forces all Knights within 3 spaces to make Will save DC 13 or break formation (losing Shield Wall and Coordinated Strike for 1 round). Removing a squad leader causes all Knights in the squad to make Will save DC 12 or become confused.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 3-4 players of level 2-4 (per squad of 6)

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party crests a ridge overlooking a Hollowdeep approach and sees them: twelve figures in battered armor, moving in two columns of six with mechanical precision. Their shields interlock. Their bone-blades are held at identical angles. They are patrolling a perimeter that no living authority ordered them to patrol. As the party watches, the lead Knight raises a fist. The column halts. Twelve dead heads turn in unison toward the ridge. The lead Knight gives a hand signal. The columns split into flanking formation. They have been seen, and the dead remember how to respond.
>
> **Complication:** One Knight in the rear column is wearing a locket around its neck. It belongs to a Hold widow the party met two sessions ago.
