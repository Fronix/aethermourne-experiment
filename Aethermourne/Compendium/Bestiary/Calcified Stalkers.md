## Description

Calcified Stalkers are what happens when predators live too close to a dead god's bones for too many generations. Wolves, mountain cats, and bears from the high passes of [[The Ashen Dominion#Solvaen's Spine|Solvaen's Spine]] have been reshaped by ambient [[Divine Materials - Mechanics#Ashite|Ashite]] saturation into something beautiful and no longer animal. Their hides have hardened to white ceramic, smooth and faintly luminous at dusk. Their eyes are multifaceted crystal, refracting light into geometric patterns. They make no sound except a faint crystalline chime when their joints flex, like wind through glass.

They move in perfect mathematical formations, hunting in tessellation patterns that shift and interlock with mechanical precision. A pack of Stalkers approaching across the Ashfields looks less like predators closing on prey and more like a geometric proof unfolding across the landscape. They are eerily synchronized, turning, stopping, and striking at precisely the same instant, as though guided by a single will.

## Behavior & Tactics

Stalker coordination is inhuman, driven by mathematical compulsion rather than pack instinct. They attack in repeating tessellation patterns: approach vectors, flanking angles, and strike timing all follow geometric rules as rigid as the creatures themselves. This makes them tactically devastating on first contact but predictable once the pattern is recognized. A DC 14 Intelligence check mid-combat reveals the tessellation, granting advantage on subsequent saves and defensive rolls as the pattern becomes legible.

They do not vocalize, do not bluff, and do not retreat. A Stalker formation commits to its pattern until the pattern completes or every member is destroyed. Individual Stalkers separated from the formation become erratic, lashing out wildly, their crystal eyes flickering as though searching for a rhythm they can no longer hear.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The [[Order of the Ashen Flame|Order of the Ashen Flame]] considers Calcified Stalkers sacred guardians of the Spine. Killing one is technically blasphemy under Dominion law. This makes every encounter political as well as tactical. PCs who defend themselves may face legal consequences in [[Hearthstone]] or [[Crown's Reach]] unless they can prove the Stalkers attacked first, and Flame-Bearers are not inclined to believe outsiders over divine creatures.
>
> **The Real Problem.** The Stalkers are not guarding the Spine on purpose. They are responding to [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]]'s residual will the way iron filings align to a magnet. As the [[Cosmology and Magic#Ley Lines and the Aetheric Web|Aetheric Web]] destabilizes, their patterns are becoming erratic, breaking tessellation, circling without purpose, attacking each other. This terrifies the Church because it suggests Solvaen's order itself is failing. The Church has suppressed all reports.
>
> **Campaign Connection.** Stalker behavior is a visible barometer of the Aetheric Web's health. Orderly Stalkers mean the Web holds. Erratic Stalkers mean it is fraying. PCs who notice and report this pattern provide intelligence that the [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]] would value enormously.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is ascending a Spine pass toward [[Hearthstone]] when the chiming starts, faint, rhythmic, coming from multiple directions at once. On the ridge above, six white shapes move in perfect formation: three wolves and three mountain cats, ceramic hides catching the last of the dusk light, crystal eyes refracting it into geometric patterns on the grey stone. They are descending in a tessellation spiral, closing the distance with mathematical certainty. They are beautiful. They are also between the party and the only path forward.
>
> **Complication:** An [[Order of the Ashen Flame|Ashen Flame]] patrol is watching from a nearby overlook. If the PCs kill the Stalkers, the patrol will arrest them for blasphemy. If the PCs flee, the Stalkers will pursue in pattern until the party leaves the Spine's proximity.
