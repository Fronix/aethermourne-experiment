# Chance-Eaters

*"Three caravans lost cargo on the same stretch of road. A merchant's scale broke mid-weigh. Two horses threw shoes within an hour. I told the [[Edgewatch]] it was bad luck. They told me to look up. I did. I still can't describe what I saw, but my luck's been fine since they burned it out."*

## Description

Chance-Eaters are translucent knots in the air, visible only by the way light bends wrong around them, a subtle warping like heat-haze that never dissipates. They range from fist-sized to roughly the span of an outstretched hand, drifting lazily a few feet above ground level. Up close, they look like tangled skeins of glass thread, folding through themselves in patterns that hurt to follow. They produce no sound. They cast no shadow. They smell faintly of ozone and regret.

[[The Accord of Echoes|Edgewalkers]] can perceive them clearly: probability threads converge on a Chance-Eater like water spiraling into a drain. To Edgewalker sight, they are dark spots in the probability field, holes where possible outcomes should exist but don't. Regular people cannot see them at all. They only experience the consequences.

In the presence of a Chance-Eater, unlikely outcomes become certain. Swords slip from well-oiled grips. Footholds crumble under the lightest step. Healing poultices turn rancid. Bowstrings snap. Rain finds the gap in your cloak. Every minor misfortune that could happen, does, because the Chance-Eater is feeding, collapsing favorable probabilities and consuming the differential like a tick feeding on blood. A single Chance-Eater is an annoyance, the kind of day where nothing goes right. A cluster is a catastrophe. Drift-harvesters call them jinx-knots, and experienced crews carry shimmerglass lanterns to spot them before they settle in.

## Behavior & Tactics

Chance-Eaters are not malicious. They are parasitic. They gravitate toward areas of high activity, markets, caravans, expeditions, construction sites, anywhere that generates a high density of actions and decisions, because more actions mean more probability to collapse. They feed passively, drifting into a productive area and slowly draining the favorable outcomes until everything goes wrong. Then, sated, they drift away, leaving behind a trail of broken deals, dropped cargo, and bewildered people wondering what happened.

In combat, Chance-Eaters do not attack. They have no physical form capable of dealing damage. Instead, their aura imposes disadvantage on everything: attacks miss, spells fizzle, potions shatter in the hand. The fight is not against the Chance-Eater but against your own collapsing luck while trying to locate and destroy something you can barely perceive. Weapons pass through them without effect unless the strike is guided by probability-sight (Edgewalker perception) or disruptive energy (fire, radiant, or Vorrhyn's Dust-infused weapons). When destroyed, the consumed probability unravels in a sudden burst, and for a brief, giddy moment, everything goes improbably right.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Chance-Eaters are increasing in number as the Maelstrom widens. Surge-waves deposit them further into the Stable Ring and Drift with each expansion cycle. Their population density is a reliable indicator of Maelstrom activity, a canary in the coal mine that [[Lysara Shimmerborn]] tracks in her research logs.
>
> **Campaign connection:** [[Oren Blackfinger]] has noticed that certain trading days at the [[Riftmarket#The Dust Exchange|Dust Exchange]] are inexplicably bad, every deal sours, every measurement drifts, every scale tips wrong. He doesn't realize a Chance-Eater has nested in the Exchange's rafters. A party that clears it earns Oren's gratitude and a useful contact in Riftmarket's trade network. A party that investigates further discovers the jinx-knot population in the Stable Ring has tripled in two years, confirming the Maelstrom's accelerating expansion before anyone else has the data.
>
> **Escalation:** In areas of extreme Maelstrom saturation, Chance-Eater clusters can collapse probability so thoroughly that cause and effect begin to decouple. Effects occur before their causes. Injuries appear before the blow lands. Words are heard before the speaker opens their mouth. These "probability dead zones" are terrifying to experience and should be played as environmental horror, not combat.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter: Bad Day at the Market
> The party arrives in [[Riftmarket]] on a trading day. Every transaction goes wrong: a merchant's scale breaks, a crate lid jams, a potion seller's entire stock clouds over simultaneously. Tempers flare. Accusations of sabotage fly. A brawl is minutes away.
>
> A DC 15 Perception check (advantage for Edgewalkers or characters with magical sight) spots the distortion in the air above the market stalls, a fist-sized knot of bent light drifting lazily between awnings. A DC 13 Arcana check identifies it as a probability parasite. Attacking it requires overcoming its near-invisibility: attacks without magical guidance suffer disadvantage, and even guided strikes must hit AC 12. Fire, radiant damage, or weapons dusted with Vorrhyn's Dust are effective. All other damage passes through harmlessly.
>
> When destroyed, the collapsed probability unravels. Roll on the Correction Burst table:
>
> | d6 | Effect |
> |---|---|
> | 1 | Every coin purse in the market gains 1d10 extra coins (from nowhere) |
> | 2 | A merchant's broken goods spontaneously repair themselves |
> | 3 | The next attack each PC makes automatically hits |
> | 4 | A lost item the party has been searching for appears in someone's pocket |
> | 5 | The weather clears perfectly for exactly one hour |
> | 6 | A nearby NPC blurts out a secret they had no intention of sharing |

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Chance-Eater | 3 | None | 15 | fly 4 (drift) | -4 | +3 | +2 | none (aura only) |
>
> **Resistances/Immunities:** Immune to physical damage, poison, psychic, and cold. Vulnerable to fire and radiant. Vorrhyn's Dust-infused weapons bypass immunity.
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Probability Drain (passive aura).** All creatures within 4 spaces suffer disadvantage on attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws. Multiple Chance-Eaters stack: 2 creatures increase the radius to 6 spaces, 3+ creatures cause automatic failure on rolls of natural 5 or below.
> - **Near-Invisible.** DC 15 Perception to detect. Attacks without magical guidance suffer disadvantage. AC 12 if the attacker can perceive it.
> - **Correction Burst (on death).** When destroyed, consumed probability unravels. All creatures within 4 spaces gain advantage on their next roll. GM may additionally roll on the Correction Burst table above for a narrative bonus.
>
> **Weakness:** Fire and radiant damage (double damage). Vorrhyn's Dust disrupts their feeding cycle (1d6 damage per round if scattered within 2 spaces). Edgewalker probability-sight negates their invisibility.
>
> **Cluster Rules:** 3+ Chance-Eaters in the same area create a probability dead zone. All d20 rolls within the zone are made twice, taking the lower result. Destroying any single Chance-Eater in the cluster causes a chain reaction: each remaining Chance-Eater makes a Will save DC 12 or is destroyed by the cascading probability correction.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 3-5 players of level 2-5
