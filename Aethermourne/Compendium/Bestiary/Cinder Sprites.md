## Description

Thumb-sized and luminous, Cinder Sprites look like embers that grew wings. They drift in slow swirling clouds above geothermal vents, hot springs, and active forges, their bodies pulsing orange-gold with absorbed thermal energy. Each sprite is a translucent shell of mineral glass surrounding a core of concentrated heat, warm to the touch, faintly humming at a frequency that [[The Ember Guild]] smiths say matches the resonance of cooling metal. In healthy numbers they fill the air with a gentle amber glow and a warmth that makes the surrounding stone comfortable to work beside.

They are [[The Emberveil]]'s pollinators. Cinder Sprites process raw geothermal energy into a stable, ambient form that sustains the region's food web: the heat-loving root vegetables in the Warm Valleys, the bioluminescent fungi in the Deep Warrens, the mineral-rich hot springs at [[Cinderfall]]. Without them, [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]]'s fire reaches the surface as raw volcanic heat, too intense for life and too unstable for craft. The sprites are the intermediary, the gentle hands that make divine fire livable.

## Behavior & Tactics

Cinder Sprites are not combatants. They cluster, drift, and feed. A healthy swarm numbers in the hundreds and provides enough ambient warmth to heat a forge-chamber without fuel. Disturbed sprites scatter in a burst of sparks that can singe exposed skin (1 fire damage, no attack roll, just proximity). Cold damage disperses a swarm instantly, killing the sprites and dimming nearby forges until new sprites repopulate, which can take weeks.

The moral dilemma: sprite cores can be harvested as forge-fuel, each one burning hotter and longer than charcoal. [[The Ember Guild]] banned the practice two centuries ago, but black-market sprite-cores fetch high prices in [[Smokewall]]'s border trade. Every core harvested is a sprite killed, and every sprite killed accelerates the Cooling's effects in that area.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Brunhild Ironheart]] is tracking sprite populations across the region. The data is alarming: a 40% decline near [[Hearthdeep]], 25% near [[Crucible]], and near-total collapse in the outer Deep Warrens where the Cooling is most advanced. The sprites are the canary in the forge. When they vanish from an area, the geothermal system follows within months.
>
> **Campaign Connection.** The sprite die-off is the Second Silence reaching [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]]'s fire. As the [[Cosmology and Magic#Ley Lines and the Aetheric Web|Aetheric Web]] weakens, the divine energy sustaining the world's core diminishes, and the sprites, which feed on that energy, starve. This is not fixable by protecting the sprites. The source is failing.
>
> **Cinderfall's Healers** use living sprite swarms in their therapeutic baths, the ambient warmth and resonance easing chronic pain and accelerating bone-mending. As sprite numbers drop, healing appointments are being rationed. The wealthy are hoarding private swarms. The poor are losing access to the only effective treatment for mining injuries.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Environmental Feature
> | Name | Trigger | Effect |
> |---|---|---|
> | Healthy Swarm | Ambient (no trigger) | Provides warmth, light, and +1 to forge-magic checks within 3 spaces |
> | Disturbed Swarm | Loud noise, sudden movement, cold damage | Scatter burst: 1 fire damage to all within 2 spaces, swarm disperses |
> | Dying Swarm | Area affected by the Cooling | Dim light, no warmth bonus, DC 12 Perception to notice the absence before the cold sets in |
>
> **Detection:** DC 10 Perception (they glow). DC 14 Nature to understand their ecological role.

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party descends into a Deep Warren mining tunnel and rounds a corner into warmth. The air shifts from cold stone to forge-heat in a single step, and the tunnel ahead glows amber. Hundreds of Cinder Sprites drift in a slow spiral around a geothermal vent, their tiny wings beating in unison, their collective warmth turning the tunnel into a sanctuary. A dwarven miner sits on a boulder at the edge of the swarm, eating her lunch in the comfortable glow. She nods at the party. "Don't touch them," she says. "Don't even breathe hard. This is the last healthy swarm below Level Eight."
>
> **Complication:** A [[Smokewall]] trader has hired a crew to harvest sprite-cores from this swarm. They will arrive within the hour, and they have a cold-iron device designed to stun and collect. The miner knows, and she has sent word to [[Brunhild Ironheart]], but Crucible is hours away.
