## Description

A Contract Wraith looks exactly like the person who broke their word. It wears their face, their clothes, their posture, their habits. It speaks in their voice, uses their mannerisms, and knows their memories up to the moment the deal was broken. The resemblance is perfect in every detail save one: something about the expression is wrong. The smile comes a fraction too late. The eyes focus a fraction too sharply. It wears the shape of a person the way a puppet wears a costume, accurately but without the breath of genuine life behind it.

Contract Wraiths are not summoned or created. They manifest spontaneously when a bargain struck in the [[Level 3 - The Iron Market|Iron Market]] is violated. The Market's architecture remembers every deal made within its boundaries, iron remembers, and when a party reneges, the Market produces a consequence. That consequence takes the shape of the oathbreaker because the punishment for betrayal should come wearing a familiar face. There is a cruel logic to it: you broke your word, so now your word walks around without you, keeping the promises you abandoned.

## Behavior & Tactics

A Contract Wraith has a single purpose: enforce the broken deal. Its methods vary depending on the nature of the contract. A Wraith born from a broken trade agreement might hunt down the offending party and forcibly collect what was owed, ripping the debt from their possessions, their body, or their soul. A Wraith born from a betrayed alliance might systematically destroy every relationship the oathbreaker has, poisoning friendships, revealing secrets, turning allies into enemies, wearing the oathbreaker's face while doing it.

In combat, Contract Wraiths possess the skills and abilities of the person they resemble, making them as dangerous as their template. A Wraith modeled on a warrior fights like that warrior. A Wraith modeled on a mage casts their spells. They are not superior to the original, merely equal, which is enough. Fighting your own reflection, with your own techniques, knowing it knows every trick you know, is a uniquely demoralizing experience.

Contract Wraiths cannot be destroyed by conventional means while the broken contract persists. They reform within hours of being killed, reassembling from iron filings and Market dust. The only permanent solutions are to fulfill the original contract, to negotiate a new deal that supersedes the old one, or to find the iron tablet on which the contract is recorded in the Market's archives and destroy it.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Contract Wraiths are excellent recurring antagonists. If a PC makes a deal in the Iron Market and later breaks it, the Wraith becomes a persistent threat that follows them out of the Descent (through planar thin points) and into the mortal world. It looks exactly like the PC, which creates social chaos as the Wraith commits acts in the PC's name.
>
> **Plot Hook.** A Contract Wraith wearing the face of a known NPC appears in the mortal world. The NPC insists they never made a deal in the Iron Market. Either they are lying, or someone made a deal using their identity. Both possibilities are interesting.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party encounters someone they know, an NPC ally or a party member who is currently elsewhere, walking through the Iron Market. The figure greets them warmly, knows details only the real person would know, and behaves almost perfectly. Almost. One PC notices the wrongness: a gesture that is slightly off, a laugh that does not reach the eyes. The figure is a Contract Wraith hunting the oathbreaker who wore this face. It is friendly to the party only because the party is not its target. If they interfere, it becomes hostile, and it fights exactly like the person it resembles.
>
> **Stakes:** The Wraith is heading toward someone the party cares about. If they want to stop it, they need to find and resolve the original contract, not just fight the messenger.
