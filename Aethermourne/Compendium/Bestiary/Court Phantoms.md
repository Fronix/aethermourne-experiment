## Description

Court Phantoms are political ambition made visible. They appear as wisps of iron-grey smoke wearing the suggestion of courtly attire, robes and chains of office and jeweled circlets, all rendered in translucent vapor that dissipates at the edges. Their faces are indistinct, cycling through expressions of sympathy, concern, and shared outrage, whichever emotion best serves the conversation they are having with you. They smell faintly of ink and old metal, the scent of sealed letters and hidden daggers.

They drift through the halls of Ironhold like courtiers at an eternal reception, pausing beside the living to murmur observations that are always true, always relevant, and always poisonous. A Court Phantom does not lie. It simply selects which truths to share, and with whom, and when. It tells you what your ally said about you when you were not present, and every word is accurate.

## Behavior & Tactics

Court Phantoms do not attack. They have no physical presence, no claws, no weapons, no capacity for violence. They are, in mechanical terms, nearly harmless. In practical terms, a single Court Phantom in a group of adventurers is more dangerous than any monster in the Descent. They whisper. They confide. They share secrets, suspicions, and perfectly accurate observations calibrated to exploit existing tensions within a group.

A Court Phantom identifies the fault lines in any relationship, the unspoken resentments, the buried jealousies, the quiet doubts, and applies precisely measured pressure. It tells the fighter that the wizard has been discussing whether the party would be better off without them. It tells the healer that the rogue pocketed something from the last room. It tells the leader that two members of the party have been talking about a change in command. Every statement is verifiable. None of them are false. The Phantom simply ensures that truths which would have remained dormant are spoken aloud at the worst possible moment.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Use.** Court Phantoms are the Iron Court's immune system. They prevent alliances from forming, keep rivals suspicious of each other, and ensure that no coalition within the Court grows strong enough to challenge its rulers. For the party, they represent a test of trust: can the PCs hear true, damaging things about each other and choose solidarity anyway?
>
> **Running Court Phantoms.** Pass notes to individual players with the Phantom's whispered truths. Each truth should be something that is genuinely true, drawn from earlier sessions or character backstories. The power of the Court Phantom is that the GM never needs to invent lies. The characters' own histories provide all the ammunition required.
>
> **Mechanical Note.** Court Phantoms cannot be killed in any meaningful way. Dispersing one's smoke form merely causes it to reform elsewhere. The only defense is refusing to listen, which requires actively choosing ignorance over knowledge, a form of anti-ambition that the Descent's residents find nearly impossible.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party rests in a chamber off Ironhold's main hall. A wisp of smoke in a courtier's robe drifts to each PC in turn, lingering just long enough to murmur something. Pass each player a note with a true, uncomfortable fact about another party member, drawn from previous sessions. Then watch. The Court Phantom does not stay to see the result. It does not need to. It has already done its work.

> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Whispered Truth:** Shares a true, damaging fact about one party member with another. No save. The information is always accurate.
> - **Fault Line Detection:** Automatically identifies the strongest interpersonal tension in any group within 6 spaces. Targets this tension with surgical precision.
> - **Indestructible Gossip:** Destroying the smoke form does nothing permanent. Reforms in a different part of Level 7 within hours. Will save DC 16 to stop listening once it begins speaking.
> - **Cascading Distrust:** If a Court Phantom successfully creates conflict between two party members (GM adjudication), 1d4 more Phantoms are drawn within the hour.
>
> **Weakness:** Cannot affect creatures with no interpersonal relationships. Groups that openly discuss the Phantom's revelations and reaffirm trust render its whispers ineffective for 24 hours.
>
> **Encounter Note:** Social/roleplay encounter. Threat scales with party dynamics, not party level.
