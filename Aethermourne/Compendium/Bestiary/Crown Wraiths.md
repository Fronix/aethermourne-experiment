## Description

Crown Wraiths are the spectral remnants of kingdoms that no longer exist, wearing the crowns, diadems, and circlets of rulers whose names have been forgotten by every living soul. They appear as tall, translucent figures draped in the regalia of dead empires, their features blurred beneath crowns that burn with cold iron light. Each wraith carries the authority of a collapsed throne, the concentrated essence of command stripped from its context and left to wander the Conquest's endless battlefields.

They do not speak. They do not need to. Their presence radiates sovereignty so absolute that the body responds before the mind can intervene: knees bend, heads bow, hands release weapons. Standing in the presence of a Crown Wraith feels like standing before every authority figure you have ever feared and admired, compressed into a single silent figure that expects your obedience as naturally as gravity expects your weight.

## Behavior & Tactics

Crown Wraiths do not attack in any conventional sense. They rule. Their command aura extends outward in a radius that intensifies with proximity, compelling those within range to kneel, to serve, to defend the wraith with their own bodies. The compulsion is not mind control. It is worse. It speaks to the part of every mortal that wants to follow, that craves the certainty of hierarchy, that finds comfort in submission. Resisting requires not strength of will but a genuine rejection of the desire to be led, an act of philosophical independence that most people have never attempted.

A Crown Wraith with thralls becomes exponentially more dangerous. It commands its kneeling servants to fight on its behalf, turning adventuring parties against each other with nothing more than its presence. The wraith itself never strikes. It simply stands, crowned and silent, while those around it tear each other apart for the privilege of being its champion. Breaking free requires a PC to consciously reject not just the wraith's authority but their own desire for leadership, their own hunger to command or be commanded.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Use.** Crown Wraiths test the party's relationship with power. PCs with leadership ambitions, those who want to rule, to command, to be in charge, are the most vulnerable. The wraith's aura specifically amplifies the desire to serve because it promises the inverse: prove yourself worthy, and the crown could be yours.
>
> **Interaction with Level Theme.** The Conquest's theme is the futility of empire. Crown Wraiths embody this: they are the endpoint of every kingdom, a crown with no country, authority with no purpose, command with nothing left to command. Defeating one does not destroy its crown. The crown falls to the ground and waits for someone to pick it up.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party crosses a field of broken banners on Level 6 when silence falls. A figure stands at the center of the field, seven feet tall, wearing a crown of black iron that burns with pale light. No words are spoken. Each PC feels a pull in their chest, a sudden, overwhelming certainty that this figure deserves their loyalty. The first PC to fail their resistance drops to one knee. The wraith turns its blurred face toward them and extends a hand, not in greeting, but in command. The kneeling PC feels compelled to defend the wraith against their companions.
