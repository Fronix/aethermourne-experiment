## Description

Cycle-Stalkers were wolves once, or something like wolves. The Wilt's Death Tides have killed and raised them so many times that the boundary between states has become a tool rather than a trauma. They are gaunt, bone-ridged quadrupeds with fur that shifts between matted grey-brown and pale, papery white depending on where they sit in the Cycle. Their eyes hold a dull amber glow during the life-phase and go hollow and lightless when the death-phase takes them. Both versions hunt. Both versions are smart enough to make you wish they weren't.

What makes Cycle-Stalkers terrifying is not their individual strength but their use of death as tactics. A Stalker killed in combat rises within minutes, retaining full memory of the fight, including the position, weapons, and abilities of whatever killed it. It rises behind the line, flanking. The pack learns from every death. A prolonged engagement against Cycle-Stalkers is a losing proposition because every round of combat teaches them something and costs you resources they will never run out of.

## Behavior & Tactics

Cycle-Stalkers hunt in packs of four to eight. They open with probing attacks, sending one or two members to engage while the rest circle. The probes are expendable. When a probe is killed, the pack watches how it died, then the risen probe repositions behind the enemy while the rest adjust formation. After two or three cycles of death-and-repositioning, the pack has mapped the target's defenses completely and attacks from every angle simultaneously.

They do not pursue prey that disengages quickly. The pack's advantage requires sustained combat to build tactical intelligence. Fleeing within the first round of contact is the safest response, and [[Kael Duskborne]]'s Thornguard drills this into every new recruit. The problem is that Cycle-Stalkers have learned to ambush in terrain where retreat is difficult: narrow ravines, dense deadwood tangles, the edges of Death Tide zones where the footing is treacherous.

During the life-phase, Stalkers behave almost like natural wolves: territorial, cautious, avoidant of large groups. During the death-phase, they lose that caution entirely. Dead Stalkers feel no pain, no fear, and no hesitation. The transition between phases mid-combat is the most dangerous moment, the pack gains members who cannot be intimidated and do not flinch.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Cycle-Stalker packs are growing larger and more coordinated as the Cycle accelerates. Before the acceleration, packs rarely exceeded four. Now eight is common, and [[Kael Duskborne]]'s scouts have reported a mega-pack of sixteen operating near the Bonewood border. The faster Cycle means faster resurrection, which means the tactical-death strategy works in shorter windows.
>
> [[Kael Duskborne]] deserted the [[The Ashen Vigil|Vigil]] because he saw purposeful undead behavior in the Wastes. The Cycle-Stalkers are proof he was right, and proof that it is worse than he imagined. These are not undead obeying a hidden commander. These are undead that have independently developed military tactics through iterative death. The implications for the [[The Pale Wastes#Hollowdeep|Hollowdeep]] situation are staggering.
>
> **Campaign Hook.** If the Cycle breaks toward [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]], Cycle-Stalkers will lose their life-phase entirely and become permanent undead hunters, pouring out of the Wilt in packs of dozens. [[Yara Thornbloom]] knows this and considers them the clearest warning sign of collapse.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Cycle-Stalker (living) | 3 | L | 18 | 5 | +2 | +3 | +0 | bite 1d6+2 |
> | Cycle-Stalker (risen) | 3 | L | 12 | 5 | +2 | +2 | -2 | bite 1d6+2 cold |
>
> **Resistances/Immunities (risen):** Immune to poison. Resistant to cold.
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Tactical Resurrection.** When a Cycle-Stalker dies, it rises at the start of the next round with the risen stat line. It appears in any unoccupied space within 3 spaces of where it fell (flanking preferred). Each Stalker can rise once per encounter.
> - **Pack Intelligence.** After a Stalker is killed and rises, all Stalkers in the pack gain +1 to attack rolls against the creature that killed it (cumulative, max +3).
> - **Death-Phase Fearless (risen).** Cannot be frightened, intimidated, or forced to retreat.
>
> **Bloodied (living):** The Stalker's eyes flicker between amber and hollow. It fights recklessly, seeking death to trigger Tactical Resurrection.
>
> **Weakness:** Fire damage prevents Tactical Resurrection (the body is consumed). Radiant damage delays resurrection by 1 round. Disengaging within 2 rounds avoids Pack Intelligence buildup.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 3-4 players of level 3-5 (per pack of 4-6)

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The Thornguard patrol found the bodies an hour ago: three deer, torn apart, arranged in a rough triangle. [[Kael Duskborne]] recognizes the pattern. It is bait. The Cycle-Stalkers killed the deer knowing they would rise, knowing the risen deer would attract scavengers, knowing the scavengers would attract the party's attention. As the first deer twitches back to life, four pairs of eyes, two amber, two hollow, watch from the deadwood.
>
> **Complication:** The patrol includes a wounded Thornguard who cannot run. Disengagement, the standard doctrine, means leaving her behind. The pack has positioned itself between the party and the nearest safe route. They have done this before.
