# Depth Leviathans

*You do not see it. You see where it isn't. A shadow the length of a galley, moving against the current, blotting out the bioluminescence below. Then the water goes still, and you realize the shadow has stopped. It is looking up.*

## Description

Depth Leviathans are the oldest living things in the waters around the Hollowed Reach, massive eel-like organisms that inhabit the deepest reaches of the Abyssal Trench where pressure would crush any surface creature and light has never existed. They are between sixty and a hundred feet long, their bodies muscular and sinuous, covered in skin so dark it absorbs light rather than reflecting it. In open water they are visible primarily as absences, ship-length shadows that blot out the faint bioluminescence of the deep, voids that move with terrible, deliberate grace.

They have no eyes. Their heads are blunt, wedge-shaped, studded with clusters of sensory organs that detect magical energy rather than light or sound. These organs glow faintly, the only part of the Leviathan that emits light, a deep violet luminescence that pulses in time with the Trench's ambient magical currents. A Leviathan hunting is a constellation of violet points drifting through absolute darkness, beautiful until you understand what they mean.

Their mouths are underslung, lined with rows of teeth designed not for tearing but for gripping, for holding things in place against pressure that would crush stone. The teeth are composed of crystallized divine bone, fragments of [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s prison cage that the Leviathans have consumed and incorporated into their bodies over millennia.

## Behavior & Tactics

Depth Leviathans are drawn to magical activity. In their natural habitat, the deepest Trench, the only significant magical signature is the prison itself, and they circle it endlessly, patrolling. When Black Tides send surges of magical energy toward the surface, the Leviathans follow the energy upward, ascending from pressures that should kill them into waters they were never meant to inhabit. They surface because they are agitated, not hunting, responding to disturbances in the magical field they have patrolled since before mortal memory.

On the surface, they are catastrophic. A Depth Leviathan surfacing beneath a ship capsizes it through displacement alone. Their tails can shatter dock pilings. Their passage creates currents strong enough to drag swimmers under. They do not attack people deliberately, humans are too small to register as prey, but their presence in a harbor during a Black Tide turns a crisis into a disaster.

The [[Major Factions#Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keepers]] call them "the deep problem." Commander [[Lira Ashvane]] maintains a standing order: if a Leviathan surfaces, evacuate the harbor. Do not engage. Do not attempt to drive it off. Wait for it to descend. The order is not cowardice. It is experience.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Depth Leviathans are not just animals. They are descended from creatures [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]] shaped as prison guardians during the Theomachis, living wardens bred to patrol the cage's perimeter and respond to escape attempts. Two thousand years of reproduction in the Trench's hostile environment have stripped away the divine intelligence their ancestors possessed, but the instinct remains. They patrol because their ancestors were made to patrol. They surface because they sense something is wrong with the prison.
>
> **Campaign significance.** The Leviathans' increasing surface activity is one of the strongest indicators that the prison is failing. A Leviathan that surfaces and does not descend, instead circling the harbor as if confused, has lost track of its patrol route because the prison's magical signature has shifted. This is the underwater equivalent of a guard dog that cannot find its way home.
>
> **The teeth.** The divine bone in their teeth means that a Leviathan's bite can damage divine structures, including the prison itself. Serith knows this. One of her long-term strategies involves agitating the Leviathans enough that they attack the prison in confusion, using Yvenne's own guardians against her cage. If a PC examines a Leviathan tooth (obtainable from shed teeth that wash ashore), a DC 16 Arcana check reveals the divine bone composition, connecting the creatures to the prison's construction.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is aboard a ship crossing the Trench's edge when the water beneath the keel darkens. Not the gradual deepening of ocean color but a sudden, absolute absence of light below the hull, as if the sea floor has risen to just beneath them. The ship's mage feels a lurch in the ambient magical field, a pull, like something vast is drawing the energy downward. Then violet lights appear in the darkness below, arranged in a line, pulsing, each one the size of a fist. The line is seventy feet long. It is rising.
>
> **Complication:** The ship's captain has seen this before. She orders full stop and all magical activity ceased immediately, every enchantment suppressed, every spell-light extinguished. The Leviathan is drawn to magic. If the party complies, it circles twice and descends. If anyone casts a spell, it surfaces.

> **Resistances/Immunities:** Immune to cold, pressure, and psychic. Resistant to all non-magical physical damage.
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Magic Sense (passive).** Detects all magical activity within a quarter mile. Moves toward the strongest source at full speed.
> - **Displacement.** Any ship or structure within 3 spaces of the Leviathan when it surfaces or moves must make a DC 18 check or capsize/collapse from water displacement alone.
> - **Current Wake.** Movement creates powerful currents. Creatures within 4 spaces of the Leviathan's path make Str save DC 16 or are pulled 2 spaces toward it and knocked prone.
> - **Divine Bone Teeth.** Bite attacks deal full damage to divine constructs, wards, and enchantments. Can sever magical barriers.
> - **Deep Pressure Adaptation.** Takes 1d10 damage at the start of each turn spent on the surface (the low pressure is killing it). This is why surfacing events are brief.
>
> **Bloodied:** The Leviathan is dying and desperate. It thrashes, dealing 2d10 bludgeoning to all creatures and structures within 4 spaces each round. It will attempt to dive and return to the Trench.
>
> **Weakness:** Suppressing all magical activity within range causes the Leviathan to lose interest and descend within 1d4 rounds. [[The Tidemother's Bell]] calms it instantly (the Bell's resonance mimics Yvenne's command frequency). The Leviathan takes damage from surface pressure, so outlasting it is viable if the party can survive the collateral damage.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** This is not a fight. This is a disaster to survive. Level 8+ parties may attempt to drive one off. Killing one would require a fleet.
