## Description

Echo Pools are not creatures. They are memories with teeth.

Scattered throughout the Fenlands, these pools of dark fen-water have absorbed enough of [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]]'s fragmented consciousness to develop rudimentary awareness. They look like ordinary standing water, still, black, reflecting the sky. The only tell is a faint iridescence on the surface, a shimmer of prismatic color that moves against the light, and a smell like heated copper and ozone that intensifies when magic is used nearby.

Each pool has recorded every magical event that occurred within its range, sometimes centuries of accumulated spell-work. Some replay the same spell on loop: a pool near a ruined tower endlessly projects a partly-real fireball every six minutes, scorching the same patch of bog-grass into the same blackened circle. Others have learned to combine spell-fragments into new, impossible effects, illusions that carry physical weight, healing spells that burn, wards that attract rather than repel. These combinations are Nethys's memories trying to reassemble themselves, reflexive and incomplete but functional.

## Behavior & Tactics

An Echo Pool activates when a living creature steps within its trigger radius (typically 3 spaces). The pool replays its stored spells at the intruder, not with malice but with the reflexive response of a nervous system jolted by contact. Partly-real fireballs deal reduced but genuine damage. Charm effects produce genuine compulsion. Illusions of pre-Silence mages appear so real they can be conversed with, though their answers are fragments of conversations held centuries ago.

Echo Pools can be harvested. A trained [[The Confluence Council|Fencraft]] practitioner can extract a replayed spell as a one-shot scroll-equivalent, bottling the pool's memory into a usable form. This requires a DC 15 Arcana check and ten minutes of careful work. Success produces one scroll-equivalent per pool. Failure destabilizes the pool, which detonates with every spell it has ever absorbed simultaneously. The blast radius depends on the pool's age: a young pool might release a handful of cantrips. An ancient pool near a major confluence could level a quarter-mile of swamp.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Some pools have begun replaying spells that were never cast near them, memories from Nethys herself. These pools shimmer more intensely and smell of ozone even when no magic is present. Their replayed spells are fragments of divine-scale magic: gravity inversions, time stutters, reality folds. They are too powerful for any mortal to harvest safely, and too valuable for the [[The Confluence Council]] to leave alone.
>
> **Brother Lumen Connection.** Brother Lumen's fragment-reassembly project would involve collecting pool contents, effectively destroying the pools to reconstruct Nethys's scattered memories. The Fencrafters who depend on pool harvesting as income would resist. The pools themselves, semi-sentient, may resist. And the question of whether reassembling a shattered goddess is wisdom or hubris is the Fenlands' version of the campaign's central moral question.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Hazard Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Trigger | Effect | Harvest DC | Destabilization |
> |---|---|---|---|---|
> | Echo Pool (Minor) | Creature within 3 spaces | Replays 1 spell per round (1d6 damage, type varies) | DC 15 Arcana | 2d6 damage, 3-space radius |
> | Echo Pool (Major) | Creature within 5 spaces | Replays 1d3 spells per round (2d6 damage each) | DC 18 Arcana | 4d6 damage, 6-space radius |
> | Echo Pool (Ancient) | Creature within 8 spaces | Replays divine-scale fragments (see GM notes) | DC 22 Arcana (dangerous) | 8d6 damage, 12-space radius |
>
> **Detection:** DC 13 Perception to notice the iridescent shimmer. DC 15 Arcana to identify as an Echo Pool before triggering.
>
> **Avoidance:** Pools can be walked around if detected. Trigger radius is fixed and does not pursue.

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is following a Fencrafter guide through a stretch of deep swamp when the guide stops, raises a hand, and points at a perfectly still pool of black water twenty feet ahead. The surface shimmers faintly. "Memory pool," she whispers. "Old one. See how the color moves against the light? It has been here longer than the trees." As they watch, the pool projects an illusion: a woman in robes that have not been worn in eight hundred years, hands raised, lips moving in a spell that has no sound. Then the fireball appears, small, flickering, but genuinely hot, and strikes the same blackened patch of ground it has been striking for centuries.
>
> **Complication:** The guide wants to harvest it. The pool contains a spell-fragment worth a fortune. But the pool is old, and old pools are unstable, and the last Fencrafter who tried to harvest one this size is the reason there is a quarter-mile clearing in the southern fen with nothing growing in it.
