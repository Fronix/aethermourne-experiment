## Description

Fragment Hounds are eyeless quadrupeds built from fused iron shards, their bodies a jigsaw of rusted metal plates locked together at angles that should not support locomotion but do. They range from wolf-sized to the mass of a large warhorse, with the bigger specimens trailing sparks from joints that grind with every stride. They have no eyes, no ears, no visible sensory organs. Where a skull should be, there is only a wedge of overlapping iron plates that opens along a seam to reveal a mouth lined with needle-thin splinters.

They are born from the Shard Garden and the scattered remnants of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s body. Each Hound carries a core of war-essence in its chest, a fragment too small to manifest as anything more than hunger. That hunger is precise: Fragment Hounds sense desire. Not emotion broadly, not thought, but specifically the ache of wanting. The stronger the want, the louder the signal. A contented traveler might pass within yards of a pack without notice. A desperate one draws them from miles away.

## Behavior & Tactics

Fragment Hounds hunt in packs of three to eight, moving in absolute silence. They communicate through vibrations in the iron-ash ground, coordinating with a precision that suggests something more than animal intelligence. They flank, they herd, they cut off retreat routes. Their preferred tactic is to isolate the most desirous member of a group and drive them away from their companions.

Their bite carries war-essence that inflames aggression in the wounded. A bitten traveler does not feel pain so much as fury, a sudden, burning conviction that someone nearby is responsible for everything wrong in their life. In groups, this effect is devastating. One bite can turn a party against itself as the infected member lashes out at allies. The Hounds wait for this, circling at a distance while their prey tears itself apart, then moving in to feed on whatever remains.

The most effective defense is suppressing desire. Travelers who meditate, who release their attachments, who genuinely stop wanting, become invisible to the pack. This is, of course, nearly impossible when a pack of iron predators is circling you in the dark.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Fragment Hounds are an excellent way to externalize internal party conflict. The bite's aggression effect forces players to roleplay being irrationally angry at each other, which can surface real tensions in a controlled, diegetic way. Set a clear mechanical framework: the bitten PC makes a check each round to resist lashing out at the nearest ally.
>
> **Scaling.** Packs near the Shard Garden are smaller (3-4) and weaker. Packs near the Descent Gate are larger (6-8) and led by an alpha whose bite inflicts a longer-lasting aggression effect.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is crossing the open Ashfield at night (or what passes for night, a deepening of the grey). One PC with a strong personal goal, retrieving a lost item, saving someone, gaining power, notices the ash around them has gone still. No wind. No drift. Then the grinding begins, metal on metal, from every direction. Fragment Hounds emerge from the ash in a wide circle, already in formation. They ignore the party members with mild temperaments and fixate on whoever wants something most.
>
> **Complication:** A lone Ash Revenant stumbles into the encounter, drawn by the noise. The Hounds ignore it (it has no desire left). The Revenant begins mimicking the party's combat movements, getting in the way.
