## Description

Gyre-Wraiths are what happens when unstructured potential achieves awareness without divine order to give it shape. They are not undead, not elemental, not summoned. They are *unfinished*, crude approximations of existence that coalesce from [[The Gyre]]'s raw potential where the membrane between the substrate and structured reality grows thin. They are the question "what if something tried to exist without a template?"

The answer is unsettling.

A Gyre-Wraith appears as a shifting, translucent shape that refuses to resolve into anything recognizable. It is humanoid one moment, angular the next, then liquid, then geometric, cycling through possible forms the way a dreamer cycles through faces. Colors ripple across its surface that have no names in any mortal language, hues that exist only in the Gyre's unfiltered spectrum. Looking directly at a Gyre-Wraith causes the eyes to water and the mind to stutter, not from horror, but from the brain's inability to categorize what it is seeing.

They are silent. They produce no sound, no breath, no footfall. But their presence is felt as a pressure behind the eyes, a wrongness in the inner ear, a persistent sensation that the air is *too full*, as if reality is being asked to hold more than it was built for. Animals flee from Gyre-Wraiths. Plants near them grow in spirals or stop growing entirely. Water boils or freezes without apparent cause.

They smell of nothing. They smell of everything. Witnesses disagree.

## Behavior & Tactics

Gyre-Wraiths are not hostile. They are not anything. They have no will, no hunger, no malice, and no mercy. They are raw potential that has stumbled into a crude parody of awareness, and they behave the way a newborn behaves: reaching, grasping, reacting to stimuli without understanding what stimuli are.

This makes them extraordinarily dangerous.

A Gyre-Wraith moves toward structured things, living creatures, buildings, enchantments, anything with pattern and definition, because structure is what it lacks and what it craves on a level deeper than instinct. When it reaches something structured, it *interferes*. Not deliberately. It simply cannot help it. Its unresolved potential destabilizes whatever it touches. Wood warps. Metal flows. Flesh begins to lose definition, skin rippling, bones softening, the body forgetting what shape it is supposed to be.

Prolonged contact causes **Dissolution**, the physical equivalent of a word losing its meaning through repetition. The victim's body begins cycling through possible forms the way the Wraith cycles through shapes, trying to become everything and succeeding at nothing. The Oldborn call this "being unwritten."

In combat, Gyre-Wraiths are difficult to engage because they exist only partially in structured reality. Physical weapons pass through them unless the weapon carries Primordial resonance (forged near a [[The Primordia#Primordial Anchors|Primordial Anchor]] or anointed by an Achren pact-holder). Aetheric magic, drawing from the divine-residue traditions of the known continent, produces unpredictable results: spells aimed at a Gyre-Wraith may amplify it, feeding structured energy into its unstructured mass and making it temporarily more coherent, more powerful, and more dangerous.

Primordial pact magic is effective, particularly pacts of Structure ([[Ur-Ghael]]) and Boundaries ([[Achren]]). Imposing pattern on a Gyre-Wraith forces it to resolve, to become one thing instead of all things. This either destroys it (the potential cannot sustain a fixed form) or, rarely, *completes* it, giving birth to something new and strange that the Oldborn call a Resolved.

Gyre-Wraiths are solitary by default, but near [[The Primordia#The Shimmer|the Shimmer]]'s expanding edge, they swarm. A swarm of Gyre-Wraiths does not fight. It *dissolves*, unmaking structured reality in a spreading wave as dozens of unresolved potentials press against the world's pattern simultaneously. Swarms are the reason the [[The Primordia#The Silence-Watchers|Silence-Watchers]] maintain their wards. A swarm reaching [[The Veilshore]] would unmake everything it touched.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Gyre-Wraiths are increasing in number. As the Second Silence weakens divine order across Aethermourne and the membrane between structured reality and [[The Gyre]] thins, more raw potential bleeds through, and more of it achieves this crude awareness. The Silence-Watchers have documented a 40% increase in Gyre-Wraith manifestations over the past three years.
>
> **The deeper truth:** Gyre-Wraiths are not aberrations. They are what *all* existence looked like before [[The Twelve]] imposed the Pattern. They are the default state of reality, and the Pattern is the anomaly. If the Second Silence progresses far enough, Gyre-Wraiths will not be rare threats. They will be the environment. The Primordia is a preview of the entire world's future if divine order collapses completely.
>
> **Resolved:** On extremely rare occasions, a Gyre-Wraith that is forced to resolve by an Ur-Ghael pact-holder does not disintegrate but *becomes*. It locks into a fixed form, gains stable awareness, and exists as a new kind of entity, something that was born from pure potential and shaped by mortal will. The Oldborn have a word for these beings: *Aethyn*, "the decided." Three are known to exist. They are not human. They are not anything that has existed before. They remember being everything and nothing, and they are profoundly, quietly grateful to be *something*. Their role in the campaign is left to the GM.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter: The Silent Approach
> The party is camped near the eastern edge of [[The Primordia]], a day's march from the Silence-Watchers' outpost. Watches are set. At the darkest hour, the fire begins burning in colors that should not exist. A PC on watch notices that the stars overhead have rearranged themselves, constellations that make no sense, that shift when observed directly. The air thickens. The pressure builds.
>
> A Gyre-Wraith drifts into the camp's perimeter, a shape that is almost human, almost crystalline, almost liquid, almost nothing. It moves toward the sleeping PCs, not with intent, but with the blind tropism of a plant growing toward light. If it reaches a sleeping character, their dreams fragment into impossible geometries, and their body begins to soften at the edges.
>
> **Combat Notes:** Physical weapons are ineffective unless Primordially attuned. Aetheric magic risks amplifying the Wraith. The most effective response is to *impose structure*: bright light, loud sound, mathematical recitation, anything that asserts pattern and order. An Ur-Ghael pact-holder can force resolution. A calculist's structured magic might work, but risks resonance (the Wraith absorbs the order and becomes temporarily solid, coherent, and much larger).
>
> **Stakes:** If the Wraith contacts a PC for more than a few seconds, that PC suffers partial Dissolution: one hand becomes fluid for an hour, or their face shifts between possible versions of itself, or they temporarily forget their own name. These effects fade, but they are a visceral preview of what the Shimmer does permanently.

> [!example] Sample Encounter: The Shimmer Swarm
> The party is traveling through [[The Primordia]] toward a Primordial Anchor when they notice the landscape ahead has stopped making sense. Trees grow in all directions simultaneously. The ground is both stone and water. The air tastes of sound. They have reached the Shimmer's edge, and a swarm of Gyre-Wraiths churns within it, dozens of unresolved shapes pressing against the boundary between chaos and structure.
>
> This is not a fight. This is a navigation challenge. The party must find a way through or around without being caught in the dissolution wave. An Achren pact-holder can create a temporary corridor. A Thaelun pact-holder can perceive the safe paths through the multiplicity. Brute force will only feed the swarm.
>
> **Stakes:** A PC caught in a swarm suffers full Dissolution unless rescued within minutes. Full Dissolution is not death, it is worse. The PC's body and mind scatter into raw potential. They still exist, in a sense, but they exist as *everything*, which means they exist as nothing. Recovery requires a Resolved or an Ur-Ghael pact-holder of extraordinary skill, and even then, the PC who returns may not be exactly the PC who was lost.

## Stat Blocks

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble): Gyre-Wraith (Individual)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Gyre-Wraith | 5 | M | 55 | fly 6 | +0 | +3 | +1 | dissolution touch 2d8 force |
>
> **Resistances/Immunities:** Immune to poison and psychic. Resistant to all physical damage unless the weapon carries Primordial resonance. Absorbs Aetheric magic (regains HP equal to spell damage).
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Unresolved Form:** Physical weapons without Primordial resonance deal no damage.
> - **Aetheric Feedback:** When targeted by Aetheric (divine-residue) magic, the Wraith absorbs the energy, regains HP equal to the damage, and grows one size category for 1 minute. Primordial pact magic bypasses this.
> - **Structure Vulnerability:** Imposing strong pattern (bright light, rhythmic sound, mathematical recitation, Ur-Ghael pact magic) forces a Will save DC 14. Failure: stunned for 1 round. Three consecutive failures destroy it.
> - **Dissolution Aura:** At the start of each of its turns, each creature within 2 spaces makes Str save DC 13 or takes 1d6 force damage as their physical form destabilizes. On hit with dissolution touch, target makes Str save DC 14 or one limb becomes fluid for 1 hour.
>
> **Bloodied (half HP):** The Wraith's form begins resolving involuntarily, flickering between shapes. Its Dissolution Aura radius shrinks to 1 space, but the damage increases to 2d6.
>
> **Weakness:** Structure imposition. Cannot enter areas stabilized by Primordial Anchors.

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble): Gyre-Wraith Swarm
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Gyre-Wraith Swarm | 10 | H | 150 | 4 | +4 | +2 | +3 | dissolution wave 4d8 force (6 space radius, Str save DC 16 for half) |
>
> **Resistances/Immunities:** Immune to all conventional damage (physical and Aetheric). Only Primordial pact magic, Achren boundary-wards, or Primordial Anchor resonance can affect it.
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Swarm Dissolution:** Advances 4 spaces per round, unmaking everything in its path. Creatures hit by the dissolution wave suffer partial Dissolution (one random physical feature becomes fluid for 1d4 hours).
> - **Redirect (Achren pact):** DC 18 pact check diverts the swarm's path for 10 minutes.
> - **Navigate (Thaelun pact):** DC 16 pact check to perceive safe corridors through the swarm.
>
> **Last Stand:** When reduced to 0 HP, the swarm detonates in a final dissolution burst. All creatures within 12 spaces make Str save DC 18 or suffer full Dissolution (body scatters into raw potential, requires extraordinary intervention to recover).
>
> **Weakness:** Cannot cross Primordial Anchor stabilization fields. Disperses naturally after 1d4 hours if not near the Shimmer's edge.
>
> **Encounter Note:** Not intended as direct combat. Treat as environmental hazard for any level.
