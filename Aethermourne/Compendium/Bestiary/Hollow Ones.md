## Description

Hollow Ones are what remains after the Consuming takes everything that made a person a person. They look almost normal. That is what makes them horrifying. A Hollow One walks upright, wears clothing, navigates the iron streets of the Descent's cities with apparent purpose. Their faces are intact, their bodies functional, their movements smooth and deliberate. But something is missing, something so fundamental that the eye registers the absence before the mind can name it.

There is no personality behind their eyes. No memory in their movements. No will directing their steps. They are bodies animated by raw, undirected ambition, the last residue of what they wanted stripped of any context for why they wanted it. A Hollow One might walk the same route through Ironhold every day, performing the motions of a life that no longer exists: opening a door, sitting at a table, raising a cup to lips that taste nothing. They pass you in the street and you feel it, a cold vacancy where a person should be, an absence shaped like a human being.

## Behavior & Tactics

Hollow Ones are not aggressive by nature. They exist in a state of purposeful emptiness, driven to act but lacking any framework for action. Most are harmless, performing repetitive loops of behavior that echo their former lives. The danger emerges when a Hollow One encounters strong ambition in a living mortal. The vacancy in them responds to the fullness in others, drawn to it the way cold air rushes toward warmth.

A Hollow One near an ambitious mortal becomes a mirror of that ambition, adopting the mortal's goals, mannerisms, and behavioral patterns with uncanny precision. They do not copy consciously. They fill themselves with whatever is nearest, becoming a distorted echo of the living person beside them. In groups, this effect compounds: multiple Hollow Ones near a single ambitious mortal will all begin mimicking that person, creating a crowd of almost-identical figures moving in near-synchronization. The effect is deeply unsettling and, if the mortal does not withdraw, the Hollow Ones will eventually begin draining the source, pulling ambition and identity out of the living to fill their own emptiness.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Use.** Hollow Ones are walking consequences. Every one of them was a person who entered the Descent with hopes and plans and a sense of self. The Consuming took all of that and left the shell. They serve as a warning to PCs: this is what happens if you stay too long, if you let the [[The Iron Descent#The Descent's Hunger|Iron Tithe]] take too much. They also create a moral dilemma, as some Hollow Ones retain fragments of identity deep within, enough to occasionally speak a name or reach for a hand. Are they gone, or merely buried?
>
> **Mechanical Note.** Hollow Ones near a PC should trigger periodic checks against identity erosion. The PC feels their own sense of self flickering as the Hollow One unconsciously pulls at the edges of their personality. Extended proximity accelerates the Iron Tithe's effects.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party passes through a residential district on Level 7. The streets are populated, dozens of figures going about routines that look almost normal. A woman opens a shop that has no goods. A man sweeps a floor that is already clean. None of them look at the party, until the most ambitious PC draws too close. One Hollow One turns. Then another. Then six more, all facing the same PC, all tilting their heads at the same angle. They begin walking toward the PC, not threatening, just drawn, like moths to a lantern. The PC feels a faint tug at the edges of their thoughts.

## Stat Block

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Hollow One | 4 | None | 35 | 6 | +1 | +2 | -1 | grasping hands 1d6+3 bludgeoning |
>
> **Resistances/Immunities:** Immune to psychic damage, charm, and fear. There is nothing inside to affect.
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Ambition Mirror:** When within 6 spaces of a living mortal, copies posture, gait, and expressions. Within 2 spaces for more than 1 minute, begins speaking in the mortal's voice.
> - **Identity Drain:** A creature starting its turn within 1 space makes Will save DC 13. Failure: 1d4 psychic damage and the Hollow One gains temporary HP equal to damage dealt. A creature fully drained becomes a Hollow One.
> - **Synchronization:** When 3+ Hollow Ones are within 2 spaces, they share initiative and gain +1 to damage per additional Hollow One (max +3).
>
> **Bloodied (half HP):** The Hollow One's mimicry breaks down, its movements becoming jerky and its copied voice distorting into a hollow echo. Will save DC for Identity Drain drops to 11.
>
> **Weakness:** A creature that spends an action asserting its identity (stating its name, recounting a memory) forces all Hollow Ones within 6 spaces to make Will save DC 12 or be stunned until the end of the creature's next turn.
