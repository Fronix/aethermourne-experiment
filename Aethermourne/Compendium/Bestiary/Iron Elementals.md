## Description

Iron Elementals are not creatures. They are events. They manifest during moments of extreme conflict near the Heart of Iron, coalescing from the ambient war-essence that saturates Level 9 like heat haze over a forge. One moment the air is still. The next, iron is screaming into shape, a humanoid form assembling itself from fragments of the environment, floor plates tearing upward, wall sections peeling free, rust and metal and divine will compressing into something that exists for no reason other than violence.

They are brief and devastating. An Iron Elemental holds its shape for seconds or minutes, never longer, burning through the concentrated Kaevroth essence that formed it with the intensity of a star consuming its fuel. They are featureless iron silhouettes, vaguely humanoid but wrong in proportion, arms too long, joints too many, every surface edged and weaponized. They radiate heat that smells of blood and old battlefields, and the sound they make is not a roar but a chord, dozens of metal surfaces vibrating in sympathetic resonance, producing a sound that the body interprets as rage.

## Behavior & Tactics

Iron Elementals fight with impossible ferocity and no strategy whatsoever. They do not dodge, block, flank, or retreat. They close distance and destroy whatever is in front of them with the single-minded intensity of a blade mid-swing. Every part of their body is a weapon: arms that shear through iron walls, fingers that punch through armor, a torso that detonates shrapnel when struck hard enough. They do not feel pain, acknowledge injury, or respond to any stimulus other than proximity.

They cannot be reasoned with. They cannot be bargained with. They cannot be intimidated, distracted, or redirected. They are war distilled to its most elemental state, conflict without cause, violence without context, destruction for its own sake. The only viable response is to survive until they burn out, which means enduring seconds of combat against something that fights like a collapsing building. When an Iron Elemental exhausts its essence, it falls apart mid-motion, scattering into cooling iron fragments that slowly sink back into the floor and become part of Level 9's architecture once more.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Use.** Iron Elementals are the Heart's defense mechanism and its exhaust system. When conflict energy near the Heart exceeds the domain's capacity to contain it, an Elemental manifests to discharge the excess. They are not guards. They are pressure valves. This means they can appear at any moment during a conflict on Level 9, turning a manageable fight into a catastrophe without warning.
>
> **Mechanical Note.** Iron Elementals should be treated as environmental hazards rather than standard combat encounters. They hit harder than anything the party can face directly, but they last only 3-5 rounds before burning out. The tactical question is survival, not victory. Shelter, distance, and letting the Elemental exhaust itself are the only real options.
>
> **The Terrible Implication.** Iron Elementals are fragments of Kaevroth's own divine essence, momentarily shaped by the echoes of his will. When they manifest, PCs near the Heart may catch fragments of sensation: the memory of swinging a weapon the size of a mountain, the feeling of a body made entirely of iron, the last nine days of a god's life compressed into seconds of borrowed rage. This is as close as any mortal can come to experiencing what Kaevroth was.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is navigating the Silence on Level 9 when two members of the group argue, raising their voices in frustration. The floor vibrates. Iron plates buckle upward. In the space of a heartbeat, a seven-foot shape assembles itself from the corridor walls, edges gleaming, radiating heat that tastes like a battlefield. It does not pause. It does not assess. It strikes the nearest source of conflict with an arm that was part of the wall two seconds ago. The party has approximately four rounds to survive before the Elemental burns through its essence and collapses into smoking fragments.
