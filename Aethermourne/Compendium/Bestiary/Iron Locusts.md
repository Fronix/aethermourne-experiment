## Description

Iron Locusts are fist-sized insects with metallic chitin that shifts between rust-red and dark bronze depending on what they have recently consumed. Their mandibles are reinforced with crystallized war-essence, harder than any mortal steel, capable of shearing through refined [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] in seconds. A single locust is a curiosity, clicking and buzzing around exposed metal with the persistence of a horsefly. A swarm of several hundred is an economic disaster on wings.

They nest in the crevices of large [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] fragments, where the ambient war-essence keeps their eggs viable. A mature nest can produce a swarm every three weeks during warm months. The swarms drift across the Iron Fields in rust-colored clouds, following the scent of refined metal the way carrion birds follow death. Raw divine iron in the soil attracts them, but worked metal, swords, armor, tools, wagon fittings, drives them into a feeding frenzy. The more skillfully forged the metal, the stronger the attraction. Forgeheart steel and master-worked Kaevroth's Iron are irresistible.

They are the Ironmarch's most hated pest and its most essential organism. Without Iron Locusts, divine iron contamination would accumulate in the soil indefinitely, intensifying the war-essence until the land became uninhabitable. The locusts process and redistribute the iron through their digestive cycle, breaking divine metal into inert oxide that enriches rather than poisons the earth. [[Duskfield]]'s rare pocket of farmable soil exists because locust swarms have been stripping the area's iron for centuries.

## Behavior & Tactics

A swarm does not attack in any deliberate sense. It feeds. Metal-bearing creatures caught in the cloud experience rapid equipment degradation as hundreds of mandibles work simultaneously, shearing rivets, chewing through chain links, stripping plating. The sensation is not painful but deeply unsettling: a vibrating pressure across every metal surface on the body, accompanied by the high-pitched whine of chitin on iron. Victims who panic and swing weapons at the swarm lose those weapons first.

The tactical response is simple: drop metal and walk away. A swarm that has located a significant metal source (an armored caravan, a fragment outcropping, a cache of refined iron) will feed until sated and then disperse. Fighting in metal armor inside a swarm is suicide. Fighting with wooden or stone weapons is possible but futile, the swarm is not hostile, merely hungry, and it will not stop feeding until the metal is gone.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]] of [[The Ember Guild]] has noticed that Iron Locust swarms are following the fragment migration, moving in the same direction as [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] drifting shards. The swarms are eating more than usual, stripping fragments faster, as if the locusts are trying to consume the iron before it reaches its destination. If the locusts are the land's immune response to divine contamination, then the immune system is working overtime, and it is losing.
>
> **Campaign connection:** A party that needs to cross the Iron Fields can use a captured locust swarm as a fragment-detector, the insects always fly toward the densest iron concentration. Alternatively, a party that needs to weaken a Kaevroth fragment (perhaps to prevent it from migrating) could bait a swarm to it. [[Wrathspire]]'s smiths would be horrified, the fragments are their livelihood, but [[Duskfield]]'s farmers would help.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is camped on the Iron Fields when a distant clicking fills the air like rain on a tin roof. A rust-colored cloud crests the nearest ridge, moving against the wind, straight toward the party's equipment pile. They have about two minutes before the swarm arrives. Metal armor, weapons, cookware, coin, all of it is on the menu. Any PC wearing Kaevroth's Iron equipment is the primary target. The non-combat solution is to pile all metal in one location and retreat. The combat complication: the swarm is between the party and a fragment outcropping where a wounded traveler is sheltering. Reaching her means walking through the swarm in whatever non-metal protection they can improvise.

>
> **Resistances/Immunities:** Immune to slashing, piercing, and psychic. Resistant to bludgeoning. Vulnerable to fire and thunder (area effects scatter the swarm).
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Metal Consumption.** Any creature carrying metal items that starts its turn in the swarm loses one metal item (GM chooses or player sacrifices, smallest items first). Kaevroth's Iron items are targeted preferentially. No attack roll needed, the swarm feeds automatically.
> - **Iron Scent.** The swarm detects refined metal within 12 spaces. Kaevroth's Iron detected within 30 spaces. The swarm always moves toward the densest metal concentration.
> - **Dispersed Form.** The swarm occupies a 3-space-by-3-space area. Creatures can move through it but suffer Metal Consumption. Single-target attacks deal half damage.
> - **Satiation.** After consuming 10+ metal items, the swarm disperses to digest. It reforms near the nearest Kaevroth fragment in 1d4 days.
>
> **Bloodied (half HP):** The swarm contracts to a 2-space-by-2-space area and feeds more aggressively. Metal Consumption now affects two items per turn.
>
> **Weakness:** Fire disperses the swarm instantly (individuals burn). Thunder damage (3 spaces radius) scatters it for 1d4 rounds. Complete absence of metal within 12 spaces causes the swarm to drift away seeking other sources.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 3-5 players of level 1-3
