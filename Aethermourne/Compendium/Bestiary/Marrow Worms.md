## Description

Marrow Worms are enormous, blind, and ancient. They swim through the waterlogged soil of the Marrow Bogs the way eels swim through water, their pale bodies, each twelve to twenty feet long, visible only as a ripple in the mud, a raised line of earth that moves against the wind, a sudden sinkhole where solid ground was a moment ago. Their skin is translucent and faintly luminescent, the green-gold glow of [[Cosmology and Magic#Divine Materials|Marrow-Water]] visible through the tissue, pulsing in slow rhythm with the root-network's heartbeat. They have no eyes. They navigate by vibration and by chemical gradient, following the concentration of divine fluid through the soil the way a river follows gravity.

They are [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s circulatory system. Her body collapsed across the Verdant Marches, and her blood, the Marrow-Water, saturated the earth. The worms evolved within that saturation, their biology adapted to process and redistribute the divine fluid through the ecosystem. They swallow soil, filter the Marrow-Water through their bodies, and excrete concentrated divine material in the tunnels they leave behind. The tunnels fill with liquid that glows faintly in the dark, warm to the touch, and capable of rewriting any living tissue that contacts it.

The worms themselves are not hostile. They are oblivious. They surface to feed on decomposing organic matter, their massive bodies breaching the mud like something from the deep ocean, jaws ringed with grinding plates designed for soil, not flesh. But they are enormous, they are saturated with a substance that transforms anything it touches, and the tunnels they leave behind turn the landscape into a minefield of biological mutation.

## Behavior & Tactics

A Marrow Worm's threat is incidental. It does not hunt. It feeds, surfaces, burrows, and moves on, leaving a network of Marrow-Water-filled tunnels in its wake. Combat with a worm is a brute-force encounter: the creature is massive, blind, coated in a slick of divine fluid that burns and transforms on contact, and capable of swallowing a human-sized creature whole. Inside the worm, the Marrow-Water concentration is lethal, tissue-rewriting beginning within rounds rather than hours.

But the worm itself is less dangerous than its aftermath. A Marrow Worm tunnel is a trench of concentrated divine fluid, two to three feet wide, filled to the brim with glowing liquid. The surface appears solid, covered in a thin crust of transformed soil, until weight is applied. Dex save DC 13 or fall through into the Marrow-Water below. Contact triggers the transformation process described in [[Cosmology and Magic]]: incidental contact causes localized mutation (fingers fusing, skin hardening to bark, bones developing crystalline lattice), while full immersion is grotesque and potentially fatal without immediate intervention.

The [[Marchwardens Council|Marchwardens]] track Marrow Worm migration routes because wherever the worms go, the Bogs follow. Killing them slows the expansion. But the worms are also how Thyrea's root-network receives the divine fluid it needs to maintain the land's semi-sentient defenses. Every worm killed weakens the forest's ability to protect itself. [[Senna Deepmoss]] has commissioned studies on this exact dilemma, and the data says what everyone fears: the Marches cannot survive with the worms, and cannot survive without them.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Marrow Worm populations are exploding. The root-network is demanding more Marrow-Water as [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s corruption spreads, and the worms are reproducing to meet the demand. New tunnel networks are appearing in areas that were previously dry and safe, including the southern approaches to [[Roothold]]. [[Senna Deepmoss]]'s scouts have mapped the expansion rate: the Bogs are growing half a mile per season, twice the rate of five years ago.
>
> **Tactical note:** A party that discovers a fresh Marrow Worm tunnel can follow it to trace the Bogs' expansion direction. The tunnel always leads toward the nearest corruption node, because the corrupted root-network is pulling the worms toward zones where Serith's shadow-roots need to be drowned in divine fluid to prevent detection. The worms are being manipulated, used as unwitting supply lines for a war they cannot perceive.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is crossing a Marrow Bog at dawn when the ground ahead erupts. A pale shape, twenty feet of translucent flesh glowing green-gold, breaches the mud like a whale surfacing, showering the area with droplets of Marrow-Water. The worm crashes back into the earth, leaving a fresh tunnel in its wake, a trench of luminous liquid that bisects the party's path. The crust over the tunnel looks solid. It is not. A PC who steps on it falls through (Dex save DC 13) and is waist-deep in divine fluid that is already rewriting the leather of their boots into something that pulses.

>
> **Resistances/Immunities:** Immune to poison and acid (saturated with Marrow-Water). Resistant to bludgeoning (massive body absorbs impacts).
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Blind Sense.** Perceives through vibration within 6 spaces. Immune to visual effects. Loud impacts (thunder damage, stomping, drumming) disorient it (disadvantage on attacks for 1 round).
> - **Marrow-Water Coating.** Creatures that hit the worm with melee attacks or are hit by its bite make Str save DC 12 or suffer localized mutation (GM chooses cosmetic effect: fingers fuse, skin hardens, hair becomes moss). Effects reverse with Greensinger treatment or after a long rest.
> - **Swallow (on bite critical or against prone target).** Target is swallowed. 2d6 acid damage at start of each turn inside. Str save DC 14 to cut free (slashing weapons only). A swallowed creature that fails 3 saves begins full Marrow-Water transformation.
> - **Tunnel Aftermath.** When the worm moves through earth, it leaves a 1-space-wide trench filled with Marrow-Water. Creatures entering the trench: Dex save DC 13 or fall in, taking 1d6 acid and triggering Marrow-Water Coating.
>
> **Bloodied (half HP):** The worm thrashes wildly, spraying Marrow-Water in a 3 space radius. All creatures in range: Dex save DC 13 or take 1d6 acid and trigger Marrow-Water Coating.
>
> **Weakness:** Fire cauterizes Marrow-Water on contact (deals normal damage, and burning a Marrow-Water trench renders it inert for 1 hour). Cold damage slows the worm (speed halved for 2 rounds). Sound-based attacks disorient (see Blind Sense).
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 3-4 players of level 2-4
