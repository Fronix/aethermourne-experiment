## Description

The Iron Descent is full of polished surfaces. Iron walls, burnished armor, still pools of liquid metal, all of them reflect. And in the deeper levels, where [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s residual power is strongest, those reflections sometimes step out.

A Mirror Shade is your own reflection peeled from the iron and given substance. It wears your face. It has your build, your scars, your posture, the way you tilt your head when thinking, the way you shift your weight before a fight. It knows your memories, your fears, your fighting style, your secrets. It is, in every measurable way, you. Except the smile. The smile is always wrong. Too wide, too still, too knowing, as though it finds something about your existence privately amusing. Those who have survived encounters with Mirror Shades describe the expression as the look of someone who has already won and is simply waiting for you to realize it.

Mirror Shades do not emerge at random. They require a specific condition: the victim must look at their own reflection in polished iron while experiencing doubt. Doubt about their mission, their identity, their worthiness, any fundamental questioning of self. The reflection reads that doubt like an invitation and steps through the surface, trailing iron filings that cling to its new body like a second skin.

## Behavior & Tactics

A Mirror Shade does not fight to kill. It fights to replace. Its goal is to defeat its original, take their place, and walk away wearing their life. If it succeeds, the original becomes the reflection, trapped behind the polished iron surface, screaming silently while the Shade lives their life in the mortal world, making choices they would never make, saying things they would never say, smiling that wrong smile at everyone they love.

In combat, a Mirror Shade matches its original precisely. It knows every technique, every spell, every trick, every weakness. It fights at exactly the same level of skill. This makes a straight fight a coin flip, which is the point. The Shade does not need to be better. It needs to be equal, because it has one advantage its original does not: it feels nothing. No fear, no hesitation, no mercy, no doubt. It fights with the calm efficiency of someone who has nothing to lose, because it has nothing. It is a hollow copy animated by the Iron Descent's hunger for identity.

The way to defeat a Mirror Shade is to become unpredictable. It knows everything you knew at the moment of its creation, but it cannot adapt to genuinely new behavior. A fighter who abandons their trained style and fights wildly, a mage who casts spells they have never tried, a person who acts against their own nature, these actions break the Shade's pattern-matching and create openings. The deepest counter is to resolve the doubt that created it. A Mirror Shade born from uncertainty about one's purpose dissolves when that purpose is reaffirmed with genuine conviction, not as a tactical trick, but as a true act of self-knowledge.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Mirror Shades are boss-tier encounters disguised as personal crises. Each one should be tailored to a specific PC, born from a doubt that PC has expressed or demonstrated during play. The fight is not just mechanical but narrative: the PC must confront the thing they doubt about themselves and resolve it mid-combat.
>
> **The Replacement.** If a Mirror Shade successfully replaces a PC, the player can continue playing the Shade (unknowingly, at first) while the real PC is trapped in the iron. This creates a slow-burn horror scenario as the other players gradually notice that something is wrong with their companion. The rescue mission to free the original from the reflection requires returning to the exact surface where the replacement occurred.
>
> **Escalation.** In the deepest levels of the Iron Descent, Mirror Shades can form from any polished surface without the doubt trigger. These "wild" Shades are less precise copies but emerge in groups, multiple imperfect reflections of the same person, each embodying a different aspect of their personality.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party enters a corridor lined with polished iron panels. Their reflections walk beside them, matching their pace. One PC, the one carrying the heaviest personal burden, notices their reflection pause while they keep walking. It stands still in the iron surface, watching them pass. Then it smiles. Then it steps out of the wall.
>
> The Shade is an exact copy of the PC, equipped with identical gear and abilities. It does not attack immediately. It speaks first, in the PC's own voice: "You have been wondering if you are enough. I am here to answer that question." Then it draws their weapon and fights with everything the PC has, executed without hesitation or mercy.
>
> **Stakes:** If the Shade wins, the PC is pulled into the iron wall. The other party members see their companion's face appear in the polished surface, mouth open in a silent scream, while the Shade sheathes its weapon, adjusts its expression to something more natural, and says, "We should keep moving." The wrong smile flickers for just a moment before settling into something almost right.
