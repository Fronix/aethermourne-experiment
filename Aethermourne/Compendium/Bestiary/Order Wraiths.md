## Description

Order Wraiths are not undead. They are recordings. When Solvaen died, his final thoughts, fragments of a mind that understood the universe as pure mathematics, imprinted on the mineralized wood of the [[The Ashen Dominion#The Calcified Forest|Calcified Forest]] like sound pressed into crystal. The deeper one walks into the Forest, the denser the imprints become, until they begin to manifest.

They appear as translucent white figures, humanoid but featureless, their forms composed of fine geometric lines that shimmer like heat haze above stone. They move through the petrified trees in repetitive geometric patterns, tracing the same routes over and over, orbiting invisible points of mathematical significance. In the outer Forest they are faint, barely visible. At the center they are bright, solid enough to cast shadows, and there are dozens of them, moving in silent, interlocking processions through cathedrals of white stone.

They are harmless until disturbed. Step into their pattern, break the symmetry of their procession, and they notice you. And you are wrong. You are asymmetric, organic, chaotic. You need to be corrected.

## Behavior & Tactics

When an Order Wraith identifies an intruder, it attempts to "restore order" through contact calcification, rapid petrification spreading from the point of touch. The Wraith presses its translucent hand against the target and holds. White mineral growth begins at the contact point and spreads outward, turning flesh to pale stone.

Petrification is slow enough to resist (Constitution save each round, DC starting at 12 and increasing by 1 per failed save) but fast enough to terrify. A limb can calcify in minutes. Breaking free requires disrupting the geometric pattern the Wraith follows: sudden loud noise, fire, or physically breaking the symmetry of the surrounding environment (toppling a petrified tree, scattering arranged stones). The Wraith recoils from disorder, retreating to reestablish its pattern before attempting correction again.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Order Wraiths are not malicious. They are Solvaen's last coherent thoughts, fragments of a dying god trying to maintain order even in death. They "correct" intruders because the intruders are asymmetric, wrong, chaotic. They cannot conceive of a reason not to fix what is broken.
>
> **The Lost Student.** The expelled [[Order of the Ashen Flame|Ashveil]] student who vanished in the deep Forest was not killed. She was "corrected." She is in there, calcified from the waist down, still alive, still conscious, perfectly preserved by Solvaen's order. Finding her is a rescue mission and a horror. She has been alone, immobilized, and aware for months. Extracting her requires either dispelling the calcification (extremely difficult) or physically breaking the stone away from her body (painful, risky, but possible with careful work).
>
> **Campaign Connection.** The Order Wraiths' increasing density and agitation in the deep Forest correlates with the [[Cosmology and Magic#Ley Lines and the Aetheric Web|Aetheric Web]]'s instability. If the Web fails, the Wraiths may either dissipate entirely or spill out of the Forest into settled lands, "correcting" everything they encounter.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party enters the deep Calcified Forest, seeking the missing student or a route through to [[The Ashen Dominion#The Scar|the Scar]]. The trees here are pure white stone, silent as a crypt. Then the light shifts. Translucent figures appear between the petrified trunks, dozens of them, processing in interlocking geometric orbits. They are beautiful, mesmerizing, moving with the precision of a clockwork orrery. The party's path cuts directly through their procession. The moment someone steps between two figures and breaks the pattern, every Wraith in the procession stops. Every featureless face turns toward the intruder. They begin walking, calmly, purposefully, toward the source of the asymmetry.
>
> **Complication:** The student is visible deeper in, a figure half-merged with the stone floor, waving one free arm. Reaching her means pushing deeper into the Wraiths' territory, breaking more patterns, drawing more attention.
