# Recursion Stalkers

*"We found our own campfire, still warm, a mile ahead of where we'd stopped the night before. The ashes were arranged exactly the same. The boot prints around it were ours. Edgewalker Tyrell told us to run. Not away from something. Away from when."*

## Description

Recursion Stalkers are lean, angular predators with bodies that blur at the edges, as if reality cannot quite commit to their shape. They stand roughly human-height but wrong, limbs slightly too long, joints bending at angles that suggest a skeleton designed by someone who had a skeleton described to them but never saw one. Their skin is the grey-violet of a bruise, matte and light-absorbing. Their eyes reflect light that hasn't arrived yet, glinting with illumination from a future moment. Their footprints appear in soft ground seconds before their feet touch it.

They produce a faint sound, not from their bodies but from the air around them: a clicking, stuttering rhythm, like a clock trying to tick but catching on the same second over and over. Animals will not go near that sound. Experienced Drift-harvesters recognize it immediately and change course without discussion.

Recursion Stalkers exist in broken time. They do not experience moments sequentially. They loop, circling the same stretch of time until they have extracted everything useful from it. To a Stalker, an encounter with prey is not a single event but an iterative process: observe, retreat, loop, return with knowledge, repeat until the outcome is certain.

## Behavior & Tactics

A Recursion Stalker hunts in iterations.

**First encounter:** The Stalker observes. It tests a single attack, probing defenses, then retreats into a temporal fold. The party may not even realize they fought anything, just a blur at the edge of vision, a single claw-swipe, then nothing. The signs begin: a campfire ahead that is still warm, footprints that match the party's boots pressed into mud they haven't walked through yet, a slain animal bearing wounds from a weapon no one in the group carries.

**Second encounter:** Hours or a day later. The Stalker knows the party's weapons, their formation, their instinctive reactions. It targets the weakest member with tactical precision, exploiting gaps it observed in the first loop. It fights longer, pressing harder, then retreats again.

**Third encounter:** The Stalker has adapted completely. It anticipates spells before they are cast. It sidesteps attacks that haven't been swung. It counters formations the party hasn't shifted into yet. At this stage, conventional tactics fail, because the Stalker has already defeated them in a timeline that hasn't happened.

The party must break the loop. Dramatically changing location disrupts the Stalker's temporal anchor. Using abilities it has never observed (new spells, improvised tactics, equipment swaps) bypasses its predictive model. The most reliable method is finding the anchor itself, a specific point in space where time visibly stutters, the same thirty seconds replaying in a shimmering pocket of looped air, and destroying it. Collapsing the anchor forces the Stalker into a single, unprepared encounter where it has no iterative advantage.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Recursion Stalkers are born at the intersection of [[The Twelve#Vorrhyn, God of Chaos and Change , *"The Unbound"*|Vorrhyn]]'s chaos and the [[The Loom|Loom's]] temporal bleed. They are not natural predators. They are time-wounds given predatory instinct, fractures in causality that developed hunting behavior the way a river develops a current: not by choice, but by the shape of the terrain.
>
> **Campaign connection:** Their increasing appearance near the Threshold correlates directly with the Maelstrom's widening. [[Lysara Shimmerborn]] has documented three confirmed Stalker loops in the past year, up from one every few years historically. She suspects they are a symptom of the [[The Loom|Loom's]] threads fraying into the material world, temporal logic leaking through the wound and crystallizing into predatory patterns. A Recursion Stalker encounter is excellent foreshadowing for the Loom of Fate reveal: players experience broken causality firsthand and begin asking why time is coming apart.
>
> **Narrative tool:** The iterative encounter structure makes Recursion Stalkers ideal for multi-session tension. Seed the first signs (warm campfire, doubled footprints) at the end of one session. Run the second encounter mid-session the following week. Build the third encounter as a climactic set piece where the party must think creatively or find the anchor. The escalation mirrors the campaign's larger theme: threats that learn, adapt, and grow worse with time.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter: The Loop
> **Session 1 (seed).** The party is traveling near the Threshold border. At camp, a PC on watch notices a campfire pit fifty yards away, ashes still warm, arranged identically to their own. Boot prints around it match the party's footwear. A dead rabbit nearby bears a clean sword wound, but no one in the party killed it. Perception DC 14 spots a blur at the tree line, too fast to identify. The clicking-stuttering sound fades.
>
> **Session 2 (escalation).** The Stalker ambushes during travel. It targets the party member with the lowest AC, using knowledge from the first observation. It fights for 3 rounds, landing precise strikes that exploit the target's specific weaknesses, then disengages and vanishes into a temporal fold. Arcana DC 14 identifies the fold's residue. Investigation DC 16 reveals a pattern: the Stalker's retreat vector points toward a consistent location.
>
> **Session 3 (climax).** Following the vector, the party finds the temporal anchor: a shimmer in the air where the same thirty seconds replay on loop (a bird takes flight, a leaf falls, the wind shifts, then resets). The Stalker guards it. Arcana DC 16 to identify the anchor's nature. DC 14 to disrupt it (any attack dealing 20+ damage, or a targeted dispel effect). Destroying the anchor strips the Stalker of its iterative bonuses and forces a final, fair fight.

> **Resistances/Immunities:** Resistant to psychic. Immune to effects that slow or stop time (already temporally displaced).
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Iterative Learning.** The Stalker gains cumulative bonuses per encounter in a loop chain. First encounter: no bonus. Second encounter: +2 to attack and AC against creatures it has observed. Third encounter: +4 to attack and AC, advantage on all attacks against observed creatures, and it acts first in initiative regardless of rolls.
> - **Temporal Fold (recharge 5-6).** The Stalker steps into a time-fold, vanishing from the encounter. It reappears 1d4 hours to 1 day later with full HP and knowledge gained.
> - **Predictive Counter.** On the third encounter, the Stalker can use a reaction to negate one attack per round from a creature it has observed, provided the attacker uses a weapon or spell the Stalker has seen before. Attacks with new or improvised methods bypass this.
> - **Anchor Dependent.** If the temporal anchor is destroyed, the Stalker loses all Iterative Learning bonuses, Predictive Counter, and Temporal Fold. It fights at base stats with no special defenses.
>
> **Bloodied (half HP).** The Stalker's temporal coherence fractures. Its form stutters, occupying two positions simultaneously. Attacks against it roll twice and take the higher result, but it also attacks twice per round (once from each temporal position).
>
> **Weakness:** Novel tactics (abilities the Stalker has never observed) bypass Predictive Counter and ignore Iterative Learning AC bonuses. Destroying the temporal anchor removes all iterative advantages. Radiant damage disrupts temporal displacement (prevents Temporal Fold for 1 round).
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 3-5 players of level 5-8
