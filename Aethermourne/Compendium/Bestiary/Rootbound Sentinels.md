# Rootbound Sentinels

*The forest does not need walls. It grows its own.*

## Description

Rootbound Sentinels do not appear. They *assemble*. The ground trembles. Bark plates slide upward through the soil. Root-tendrils braid themselves into limbs as thick as tree trunks. Moss and lichen coat the surface in seconds, and within a minute, something roughly humanoid stands where a moment ago there was only forest floor, eight feet tall, silent, patient, and watching with eyes that are knotholes filled with faint green luminescence.

They are Thyrea's antibodies, the dead goddess's distributed consciousness deploying physical force to defend zones the root-network identifies as threatened. A Sentinel's body is composed of whatever vegetation is available, hardwood and ironbark near Roothold, bog-willow and reed-mat in the Marrow Bogs, thorn and fungal shelf in the deep Cradle. Their forms reflect their terrain, but their purpose is uniform: guard this place. Let nothing through.

Sentinels are territorial, not aggressive. They do not chase. They do not pursue beyond the boundary they were raised to defend. Step back across the line and the Sentinel watches, motionless, until you are out of sight. Step across it again and it is already moving. They are patient in the way that trees are patient, and when they strike, it is with the weight of root systems that extend a hundred yards in every direction.

## Behavior & Tactics

A Sentinel's first response to intrusion is presence. It rises, positions itself between the intruder and whatever it is guarding, and waits. If the intruder retreats, the encounter is over. If the intruder advances, the Sentinel strikes, root-slam attacks that carry the momentum of the entire root-network, each blow backed by tonnes of underground biomass channelled through a single limb. Between strikes, the Sentinel roots its feet back into the earth for seconds at a time, drawing regenerative energy from the network below. This makes it effectively unkillable through attrition. You cannot out-damage a creature that heals from an entire forest.

The counter is diplomacy. [[The Greensingers]] can calm a Sentinel with Primal Communion, speaking to the root-network in the harmonic language of Thyrea's domain. The Sentinel recognizes a Greensinger as an authorized presence and subsides, sinking back into the earth. [[Marchwardens Council|Marchwardens]] have learned to bring Greensinger escorts when traveling through active defense zones.

But shadow-touched Sentinels do not respond. Where [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s corruption has reached the root-network, the Sentinels rise wrong: bark blackened, luminescent eyes replaced by dark hollows that weep sap the color of brine, and the patience replaced by blind aggression. Shadow-touched Sentinels attack everything, Greensingers included. When a Greensinger attempts Primal Communion with a corrupted Sentinel and receives only static and salt-taste in return, it confirms that the immune system itself has been compromised. The forest is no longer defending itself. It is defending the infection.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Shadow-touched Sentinels are appearing with increasing frequency each season. The corruption rate tracks the expansion of the Marrow Bogs and the deepening of Serith's shadow-root network. [[The Greensingers]] maintain a map of which defense zones still respond to Communion and which have gone dark. The map is shrinking.
>
> **Campaign connection:** If the PCs clear a corruption node in the deep Cradle, the Sentinels in that zone revert to their uncorrupted behavior. This provides tangible, visible evidence that the corruption can be fought, which is politically significant for [[Kaelith Thornborn]]'s unification argument. A Sentinel that kneels to a Greensinger after being shadow-touched is a powerful symbol.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is traveling through the mid-Cradle when the ground shakes. A Sentinel rises from the earth ahead, blocking the path. Its bark is healthy, its knothole eyes glow green, and it shows no aggression, simply standing, waiting. The party can retreat, attempt Primal Communion (DC 14), or try to get past it (the Sentinel's reach is long and its patience is longer). A Greensinger NPC in the party can bypass it automatically, but if the path leads deeper into the Cradle, the next Sentinel may not be so accommodating.

>
> **Resistances/Immunities:** Immune to poison and psychic. Resistant to piercing and slashing (bark-plate absorbs cuts).
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Territorial.** Will not pursue targets beyond its guarded zone (approximately 6 space radius from its emergence point). Retreating ends combat.
> - **Deep Root Regeneration.** At the start of its turn, roots into the earth and regains 10 HP. Cannot regenerate if uprooted (knocked prone or lifted from the ground). Fire damage prevents regeneration for 1 round.
> - **Root Network Strike.** Once per round, a root erupts from the ground beneath a target within 4 spaces. Dex save DC 14 or knocked prone and dragged 2 spaces toward the Sentinel.
> - **Primal Communion Susceptible.** A successful DC 14 Primal Communion check calms the Sentinel for 10 minutes. Shadow-touched Sentinels are immune to this ability.
>
> **Bloodied (half HP):** The Sentinel partially uproots, gaining Speed 6 but losing Deep Root Regeneration. Attacks become frenzied, dealing an additional 1d4 damage.
>
> **Weakness:** Fire damage deals normal damage and prevents regeneration. Axes and slashing weapons that deal 10+ damage in a single hit crack bark-plates, reducing armor to M for 2 rounds. Uprooting (grapple check Str DC 16 from large creature or combined effort) prevents all regeneration.
>
> **Shadow-Touched Variant:** Replace knothole glow with dark hollows. Immune to Primal Communion. Add Brine Sap: melee attacks deal an additional 1d4 necrotic damage, and wounds smell of salt water. Will +4 instead of +2.
