# Shard-Singers

*I heard the song near the Monolith. I saw a battle I was not alive for. I felt a sword go through my chest and I died on a field I have never visited. When it stopped, I was on my knees in the grass. Sira Embersteel was standing over me. She said: "Now you know what the iron remembers."*

## Description

Shard-Singers coalesce near large [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] deposits when the ambient war-essence reaches sufficient concentration, usually at dusk or during storms when the iron resonates with atmospheric charge. They appear as translucent humanoid figures in rusted armor, carrying weapons that are suggestions of weapons, shapes without edges, silhouettes of swords and spears rendered in amber light. They flicker at the margins, never quite solid, never quite absent. Their faces are indistinct, but their mouths move.

They sing. Not words, not music, but a low resonant hum that vibrates through iron, through bone, through the war-essence saturating the soil. The song is the fragment's memory, centuries of warfare compressed into harmonic frequency, every battle fought near the deposit preserved in divine iron and replayed through the Singer's form. The hum builds slowly, almost pleasant at first, a warmth in the chest, a sharpening of the senses, and then it takes hold.

A creature caught in the song does not hear a battle. It *is* in the battle. The hallucination is total: sight, sound, pain, the weight of armor, the smell of blood, the screaming of the dying. The victim fights, runs, kills, and dies in a war that ended centuries ago, their body acting out the movements in the present while their mind is trapped in the past. To observers, they see a companion swinging at nothing, screaming at enemies who are not there, taking wounds from weapons that do not exist but bleed anyway.

## Behavior & Tactics

Shard-Singers are not hostile in any intentional sense. They are phenomena, war-memories given enough essence to manifest. They do not pursue, do not strategize, do not choose targets. They sing, and anything within range that carries enough iron in its blood or on its body becomes part of the performance. The song radiates outward from the Singer's anchor fragment, growing stronger with proximity.

The non-combat approach is the most valuable one. A creature that resists the song (or is shielded from it) can observe the replayed battle as a spectator, gaining genuine historical intelligence: troop movements, fortification layouts, tactical decisions, betrayals. The fragments remember everything. Near [[Wrathspire]], a Singer recently replayed a battle from the Theomachis itself, the first direct evidence of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] final fight visible to mortal eyes. [[Sira Embersteel]] stopped working for three days after witnessing it. She will not say what she saw.

A Singer can be dispersed by destroying its anchor fragment, but large fragments require significant force, and shattering a concentrated Kaevroth's Iron deposit releases a burst of raw war-essence that overwhelms anyone nearby. The safer solution is to wait: Singers dissipate on their own when the atmospheric conditions change, usually at dawn.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]] has documented that Shard-Singer appearances are increasing as the fragments migrate. The songs are getting older, reaching back to battles fought centuries ago, as if the migrating fragments are compressing their stored memories, replaying everything before they merge. If the fragments reach the Heart of Iron beneath [[Ostivaar]], the accumulated war-memories of twenty-two centuries would converge into a single, continuous psychic storm. Every battle ever fought in the Ironmarch, replayed simultaneously, forever.
>
> **Campaign connection:** A party that can endure a Singer's song (or find a way to "conduct" it selectively) gains access to the Ironmarch's true history, including battles the warlords have suppressed, treaties that were broken, and, if they find the right fragment, the final moments of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] death during the Theomachis. This last vision contains information about the nature of divine death that the [[The Star-Readers]] would trade almost anything for.
>
> **Encounter escalation:** A Singer anchored to a fragment that has absorbed other, smaller fragments replays multiple battles simultaneously. Victims experience overlapping wars from different centuries, a disorienting cascade of conflicting hallucinations that is far more dangerous than a single battle-vision.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is travelling near the [[The Ironmarch#The Shardfall|Shardfall]] at dusk when the air begins to hum. A translucent figure in rusted armor materializes beside a waist-high iron monolith, its mouth moving, its weapon-shapes flickering. The hum builds. PCs carrying metal feel it vibrate in their equipment first, then in their teeth, then behind their eyes. Will save DC 14 or be pulled into the vision: a battle from three hundred years ago, [[The Iron Companies|Iron Company]] against Iron Company, fought over a contract dispute that killed four hundred people. Inside the vision, the PC fights as one of the soldiers. Outside the vision, they swing at their companions.
>
> **Complication:** The battle-vision contains a detail that matters now. One of the soldiers in the three-hundred-year-old fight is carrying a map showing the location of a fragment cache that was buried during the battle and never recovered. [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]] would pay dearly for this information. But extracting the detail means enduring the full vision without succumbing.

> **Resistances/Immunities:** Immune to physical damage (non-corporeal), poison, and necrotic. Vulnerable to radiant damage and effects that dispel magical phenomena.
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **War-Song (passive aura).** All creatures within 6 spaces of the Singer at the start of their turn make Will save DC 14. Failure: trapped in battle-hallucination for 1d4 rounds (1d8 psychic damage per round, must attack the nearest creature each turn as if fighting in the replayed battle). Success: the creature observes the battle as a spectator and gains one piece of GM-chosen historical intelligence.
> - **Anchored.** The Singer cannot move. It manifests at its anchor fragment and remains until dispersed or conditions change. Destroying the anchor fragment (AC 16, HP 30, immune to fire and psychic, vulnerable to bludgeoning) disperses the Singer permanently but triggers War-Essence Burst.
> - **War-Essence Burst (on anchor destruction).** All creatures within 4 spaces make Str save DC 15. Failure: Enraged for 1 minute (must attack the nearest creature each turn, advantage on melee attacks, attacks against you have advantage).
> - **Layered Memory.** If the anchor fragment has absorbed smaller fragments (GM discretion), victims experience overlapping hallucinations. Will save DC increases to 16 and duration increases to 2d4 rounds.
>
> **Weakness:** Radiant damage disrupts the Singer's form (double damage). Dawn light disperses Singers without destroying the anchor. Ear protection (wax, magical silence) grants advantage on Will saves. A Pact-Weaver or fragment-trained character (DC 16 Primal/Arcane check) can "conduct" the song, choosing which battle to replay and allowing all observers to experience it without the combat compulsion.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 3-5 players of level 3-5
