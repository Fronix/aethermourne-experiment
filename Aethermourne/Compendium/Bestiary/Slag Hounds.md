## Description

Where [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]]'s creative essence seeps into the Deep Warrens, it does not distinguish between raw material and refuse. Slag Hounds are forge-waste given predatory form: quadrupedal shapes of cooled volcanic glass, iron slag, and crystallized mineral runoff, held together by the faint divine impulse to *make something*. Their eyes are molten orange, the only part that still holds heat, and they move with a grinding, scraping gait that echoes through mine tunnels like distant hammering.

Each Hound is unique, shaped by whatever waste it coalesced from. Obsidian variants are sleek and fast, their bodies glassy and sharp-edged but brittle under heavy blows. Iron-heavy variants lumber with thick plating, slow but armored, their slag-encrusted hides turning all but the hardest strikes. Crystalline variants are fragile and translucent, riddled with mineral deposits that channel residual forge-magic into their attacks. All share one trait: an overwhelming attraction to worked metal. The more refined the metalwork, the stronger the pull. [[Cosmology and Magic#Forgeheart Steel|Forgeheart steel]] drives them into a frenzy.

## Behavior & Tactics

Slag Hounds hunt by resonance. They sense worked metal the way a predator senses blood, and they pursue it with single-minded focus. Their bite corrodes metal on contact, a reverse-forging that breaks worked material back into raw components. Armor struck by a Slag Hound's jaws loses integrity as the metal softens and flakes. The Hound absorbs the corroded material, growing slightly larger and denser with each feeding.

They den in abandoned mine shafts and cooling chambers, building nests from accumulated slag and stolen metal. These dens contain refined slag-metal, a byproduct of the Hound's digestive process that [[The Ember Guild]] smiths value for alloy work. Clearing a Slag Hound den is dangerous but profitable, which is why the problem persists: the dens are worth enough that some miners tolerate the Hounds rather than exterminate them.

A new variant has appeared since the Cooling began. Cold-slag Hounds form from waste in cooling zones where [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]]'s fire has receded. These are fire-resistant, a disturbing inversion, and their bite carries a numbing cold that no forge-creature should possess.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Slag Hound populations are exploding. The Cooling is producing more forge-waste as geothermal systems destabilize, and [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]]'s creative essence, directionless without consciousness, animates it reflexively. [[Crucible]]'s Outer Warrens are infested. [[Vessa Ironvow]] has domesticated a pack of iron-slag variants as guard animals for her illegal mining operations, feeding them confiscated tools from rivals.
>
> **Migration.** [[Smokewall]] reports Slag Hounds crossing the border into [[The Ashen Dominion]], drawn by the Dominion's refined Ashite metalwork. If the migration continues, the Dominion will blame the Emberveil for the incursion, adding military pressure to existing trade tensions.
>
> **The Cold-Slag Variant** is the Cooling made predatory. These Hounds form only where Thalvor's fire has retreated, and their existence proves the retreat is not temporary. They are symptoms that fight back.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Slag Hound | 3 | M | 22 | 4 | +2 | +1 | -2 | bite 1d8+2 fire + Corrode |
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Corrode.** On hit, target's armor loses 1 AC (cumulative, repaired by a smith during rest). Shields targeted first.
> - **Metal Sense.** Detects worked metal within 10 spaces. Forgeheart steel: 20 spaces.
> - **Forge Frenzy.** When forgeheart steel is within 5 spaces, gains +2 to attack rolls and cannot retreat.
>
> **Bloodied:** Molten core exposed. Melee attackers take 1d4 fire damage on hit. Hound's Corrode now affects weapons as well as armor.
>
> **Weakness:** Cold damage (except Cold-Slag variant). Thunder damage shatters crystalline variants instantly.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 2-4 players of level 2-4 (per pack of 3-4)

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The mining tunnel ahead is silent except for a rhythmic scraping, something dragging metal across stone. The party rounds the corner and sees three shapes crouched over the remains of a mining cart, their molten eyes casting orange light across the tunnel walls. One is sleek black glass. One is massive, plated in rusted iron. One flickers with crystalline veins that pulse like a forge-bellows. The glass one lifts its head. Its jaws are full of half-dissolved iron. It looks at the fighter's breastplate. It begins to salivate molten slag.
>
> **Complication:** The mining cart belonged to [[Orin Deepstone]]'s survey team. The team is missing. Their trail leads deeper into the warren, past the Hound den, where the refined slag-metal in the nest is worth a month's wages, if anyone survives collecting it.
