## Description

Spark Wraiths are the ghosts of fire that once served a purpose. In the age when [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] lived, every flame in the Forge Eternal was directed, controlled, an instrument of the god's creative will. When he died, the fires kept burning but lost their purpose, and some of that purposeless fire gained something worse: hunger. Spark Wraiths are forge-fire that learned to want.

They manifest as humanoid shapes made of drifting sparks and ember-light, translucent and shimmering, beautiful in the way that all dangerous things in the Iron Descent are beautiful. They float through the Forge Eternal's corridors and workspaces, trailing motes of amber light that fade slowly in their wake. Their forms shift constantly, sparks rearranging into new configurations, sometimes suggesting a face, a hand, the outline of a body, before dissolving back into luminous chaos. They whisper, not words but the sound of inspiration itself, the crackle of an idea catching fire, the hiss of a plan taking shape.

## Behavior & Tactics

Spark Wraiths are drawn to creative thought. Not emotion, not desire, but specifically the act of innovation, of problem-solving, of imagining something that does not yet exist. A party member working out a battle plan attracts them. A mage devising a new spell application draws them from rooms away. Anyone actively designing, strategizing, or inventing becomes a beacon.

The feeding is insidious. A Spark Wraith does not attack the body. It drifts close, close enough that its warmth feels comforting, and it listens to the creative process. Then it begins to siphon. The victim's plan starts to feel stale. The solution that was crystallizing in their mind goes fuzzy. The inspired idea that was right there, on the tip of their thoughts, slips away. The Wraith is not stealing the specific idea; it is consuming the capacity to have ideas. After prolonged feeding, the victim can still think clearly, still reason, still remember. But they cannot innovate. They cannot create anything new. They can execute established procedures and follow known patterns, but the spark, the leap from what-is to what-could-be, is gone.

In combat, Spark Wraiths are fragile but evasive. Their bodies are insubstantial fire, difficult to strike with physical weapons. Water and cold extinguish them temporarily, but they reignite within minutes unless their core, a single bright mote at their center, is destroyed. They fight by engulfing, wrapping their fiery forms around a target and feeding aggressively, burning away creativity in seconds rather than the slow drain of passive feeding.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Spark Wraiths are thematically central to the Forge Eternal's tragedy: creation without purpose has produced predators that consume the capacity for purpose. They also function as a soft counter to parties that over-plan. If the players spend too long strategizing in the Forge Eternal, Spark Wraiths arrive to complicate matters.
>
> **Long-Term Effect.** A PC whose creativity has been drained recovers slowly, over days or weeks. During this time, they cannot contribute to planning scenes, cannot improvise solutions, and must rely on straightforward, established approaches. This is a roleplay condition, not a mechanical penalty, and should be played for dramatic weight rather than punishment.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is examining blueprints in the Pattern Library, trying to decipher a schematic for one of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s war-constructs. The work is intellectually demanding. As the most analytical PC leans closer, studying the lines, a warmth settles over their shoulders. It feels good, like sitting by a fire on a cold night. Sparks drift past their peripheral vision. The schematic starts to blur, not on the page, but in their understanding. They were close to a breakthrough, and now it is receding, like waking from a dream. A Spark Wraith has settled on them like a shawl of living embers, feeding contentedly.
>
> **Complication:** The Pattern Library is home to a colony of Spark Wraiths. Destroying one draws the attention of others. The party must balance the need to study the blueprints against the risk of attracting more feeders, knowing that each moment of concentrated thought makes them more visible.
