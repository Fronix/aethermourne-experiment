## Description

The Greymere Fenlands are saturated with magical residue, the ambient noise of a shattered goddess whose fragments still hum in the ley lines beneath the swamp. Most creatures adapt. Some absorb. Spell-Scarred Lurkers are the result: crocodilians, giant frogs, serpents, and bog-crawlers that have soaked in arcane radiation for so many generations that their bodies have become permanent hosts for specific magical effects they cannot control.

Each Lurker carries a single mutation, a spell-fragment from [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]]'s shattered consciousness that has embedded itself in the creature's biology. A bullfrog the size of a dog that projects a perpetual silence field. A serpent that phases in and out of visibility every few seconds. A crocodilian whose bite displaces the victim ten feet in a random direction. The mutations are not chosen, not intentional, and not malicious. They are magical static, the noise of a broken divine source leaking into the ecosystem.

## Behavior & Tactics

Every Lurker encounter is a unique puzzle. The mutation defines both the creature's danger and its weakness. The silence frog shuts down verbal spellcasting within its field but is vulnerable to thunder damage, which overwhelms and ruptures the silence effect. The phase serpent has a 50% miss chance on its own attacks because it phases mid-strike. The displacement croc cannot control where it sends bitten targets, sometimes depositing them in water, in trees, or directly behind the croc itself.

Lurkers are not aggressive by nature. Most attack only when startled, cornered, or defending territory. The danger is stumbling into a mutation's effect unprepared: walking into a silence field mid-conversation, reaching for a vine that is actually a phase serpent, stepping on what seems like solid ground only to be displaced into deep water. [[The Confluence Council]] Fencrafters have learned to read the signs, discolored water, unusual flora patterns, dead insects in geometric arrangements, but outsiders walk blind.

### Mutation Table

Roll d6 for each encounter:

| d6 | Mutation | Effect | Weakness |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Silence Field | 3-space radius silence, no verbal casting | Thunder damage ruptures field for 1d4 rounds |
| 2 | Phase Shift | Phases in/out, 50% miss chance vs. it, 50% miss on its own attacks | Radiant damage anchors it in reality for 1 round |
| 3 | Displacement Bite | Bite teleports target 2d6 spaces in random direction | Lurker takes 1d6 force damage per displacement |
| 4 | Gravity Warp | 2-space radius half/double gravity (alternating rounds) | Fire damage neutralizes field for 1 round |
| 5 | Arcane Leech | Touch drains 1 spell slot or magical charge from items | Nonmagical weapons deal double damage |
| 6 | Mirror Skin | Reflects first spell cast at it back at caster | Physical attacks ignore reflection |

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Mutations are increasing in complexity. Some Lurkers near major ley confluences now exhibit two effects simultaneously, a silence frog that also phases, a gravity-warp serpent with mirror skin. This escalation tracks with the [[Cosmology and Magic#Ley Lines and the Aetheric Web|Aetheric Web]]'s degradation: as Nethys's fragments wear down, the arcane noise gets louder and more chaotic.
>
> **Campaign Connection.** [[The Confluence Council]]'s Fencrafters harvest Lurker mutations as spell components (a silence frog's vocal sac can power a silence ward). This is ethically dubious and practically dangerous. If the Web's degradation accelerates, every Lurker in the Fenlands could become a walking magical catastrophe.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Spell-Scarred Lurker | 2 | L-M | 20 | 4 (swim 6) | +1 | +2 | -1 | bite 1d6+1 + mutation effect |
>
> **Special:** Roll on Mutation Table for each encounter. The mutation is always active (passive field or triggered on attack).
>
> **Bloodied:** Mutation destabilizes. Effect radius doubles or intensity increases. The Lurker panics and attempts to flee, trailing its amplified effect behind it.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 2-4 players of level 1-3 (single Lurker), level 3-5 (dual-mutation)

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is wading through knee-deep fen-water when conversation stops. Not because anyone chose to stop talking, but because sound has ceased. A silence field, three spaces wide, centered on a bloated grey frog sitting on a half-submerged log. It watches them with bulging eyes that flicker with faint arcane light. It is not hostile. It is in their path. And the spellcaster in the party just realized they cannot cast anything with a verbal component until they move past it, or through it.
>
> **Complication:** A Fencrafter from [[The Confluence Council]] is tracking this specific Lurker and will be upset if the party kills it. She needs the vocal sac intact.
