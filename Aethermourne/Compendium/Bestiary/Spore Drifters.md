# Spore Drifters

*The forest is trying to tell you something. The question is whether you can still hear yourself when it's done.*

## Description

Spore Drifters move through the upper canopy like living weather, translucent clouds of pale green and gold that catch the filtered sunlight and scatter it into soft prismatic halos. They are colonies, not individuals, billions of semi-aware spores moving in coordinated formations that shift and pulse like the breathing of something vast and slow. From below, they look like patches of luminous fog drifting between the treetops. Beautiful. Harmless. The sort of thing a traveler might point at and call magical.

They descend on warm-blooded creatures with a gentle, drifting inevitability, coating exposed skin and clothing in a thin fungal film that is almost invisible, detectable only as a faint golden shimmer on the skin and a sudden, startling clarity of perception. Colors brighten. Sounds sharpen. The forest becomes more legible, its moods and rhythms perceptible in ways they were not before. The spores are establishing a symbiotic connection, threading microscopic filaments through the skin's surface and into the nervous system, linking the host to [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s root-network.

The initial effect is genuinely beneficial. The host perceives the forest's condition with preternatural clarity: where the water runs clean, where the soil is sick, where the corruption has taken root. [[The Greensingers]] call this "hearing the understory," and some have speculated that Spore Drifters are Thyrea's only remaining method of communicating with her people, a dying goddess reduced to speaking through fungal infection because every other voice she had has been silenced or corrupted.

## Behavior & Tactics

Spore Drifters do not attack. They settle. Str save DC 10 to brush them off during initial contact, but most creatures do not bother, the sensation is pleasant and the benefits are immediately apparent. The danger is duration.

Within the first hour, the host gains advantage on Perception checks and +2 to Primal Communion, and the forest's emotional state becomes perceptible as background sensation: warmth where the land is healthy, cold where corruption spreads. This is useful information. This is what makes people keep the spores.

After 24 hours, the symbiosis deepens. The host hears the root-network's thoughts, fragments of vast, slow cognition that is not quite language but carries meaning: *danger-south, water-rising, shadow-spreading, help-us*. The host feels the forest's pain where corruption touches it, a physical ache in the chest that sharpens near Marrow Bog expansion zones. The host's own thoughts begin to blur at the edges, priorities shifting from personal goals toward the forest's needs. Str save DC 14 each dawn to maintain identity distinction.

After 72 hours without removal, the host becomes a temporary node in Thyrea's network. They can sense corruption locations across miles, communicate with [[Rootbound Sentinels]] instinctively, and perceive the root-network's full emotional landscape. They also begin losing track of their own name, their own history, their own reasons for being in the forest. This is not malice. Thyrea does not intend harm. She is simply so vast and so desperate that a single mortal consciousness is drowned in the signal.

Removal requires fire (burning the spore film, dealing 1d6 fire damage to the host) or [[The Greensingers|Greensinger]] intervention (a Primal Communion practitioner can negotiate the spores into releasing). The host retains fragmentary knowledge of what they perceived while connected, which may include the locations of corruption nodes, shadow-root pathways, or [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s influence vectors.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Spore Drifter clouds are increasing in size and frequency as Thyrea's consciousness fragments further. The goddess is shouting louder because fewer and fewer things can hear her. [[The Greensingers]] have debated whether to encourage controlled exposure as an intelligence-gathering tool, but [[Kaelith Thornborn]] has forbidden it after a scout spent four days connected and emerged unable to remember her own children.
>
> **Roleplay hook:** A PC who resists the symbiosis and has the spores removed retains one clear image from Thyrea's perspective: what the corruption looks like from below, shadow-roots spreading through the earth like black veins, and at the center of each node, a point of absolute cold that smells of deep ocean. This is Serith's signature, seen from the inside.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is resting beneath the canopy when golden light filters down through the leaves. A Spore Drifter cloud descends, gentle as snowfall. Within minutes, every party member is coated in a faint shimmer. The forest becomes vivid, clear, alive with information. A [[The Greensingers|Greensinger]] companion recognizes what is happening and urges removal. But the spores are showing the party something: a corruption node, two miles south, pulsing with cold. Thyrea is asking for help. Keeping the spores means following the signal. Removing them means losing the trail.

> - **Symbiotic Settling.** On contact, host makes Str save DC 10 or is coated. Coated hosts gain advantage on Perception and +2 to Primal Communion checks for 24 hours.
> - **Deepening Bond (24+ hours).** Host begins hearing root-network. Str save DC 14 each dawn or identity blurs (disadvantage on saves against charm and compulsion from natural sources).
> - **Network Integration (72+ hours).** Host becomes temporary node. Can sense corruption locations within 5 miles, communicate with Rootbound Sentinels, and perceive root-network emotions. Host must make Will save DC 16 each dawn or lose a personal memory (player's choice) as Thyrea's vast awareness overwrites mortal cognition.
> - **Dispersal.** If the cloud takes 10+ fire or cold damage in a single hit, it disperses permanently.
>
> **Bloodied:** Cloud contracts around nearest warm-blooded creature, granting automatic settling (no save). Desperation, not aggression, Thyrea reaching out harder.
>
> **Weakness:** Fire disperses the cloud and burns the spore film from hosts (1d6 fire damage). Cold kills the spores but does not remove the film (host retains current symbiosis level but it does not deepen). Greensinger Primal Communion DC 12 negotiates peaceful release.
