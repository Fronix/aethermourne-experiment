# The Ambitious Dead

> | | |

## Description

The Ambitious Dead are undead, but they are not Morrhael's undead. Where [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s broken domain produces revenants driven by hunger, instinct, and the fragments of a shattered afterlife, the Ambitious Dead are created by the Iron Descent's war-essence saturating a corpse until it stands again, not because death compels it, but because it has something left to do. Their eye sockets burn with iron-orange light, a cold fire that leaves faint trails when they turn their heads. Their bodies are partially iron, the war-essence replacing decayed flesh with dark metal, but the replacement is purposeful rather than random, reinforcing joints and bones to serve the single task that drives them.

Each Ambitious Dead carries an implanted ambition, a singular drive placed in them by the domain at the moment of their reanimation. The ambition is always specific: guard this corridor, claim that weapon, deliver this message, reach the next level down. They do not eat, rest, communicate, or respond to any stimulus unrelated to their ambition. They pursue it with a focus so complete that the world around them becomes irrelevant. An Ambitious Dead tasked with guarding a door will stand before that door for centuries, motionless, until something attempts to open it.

## Behavior & Tactics

The Ambitious Dead are single-minded to a degree that makes them both predictable and terrifying. They pursue their implanted ambition with absolute dedication, ignoring anything that does not interfere with it. A party that does not obstruct an Ambitious Dead's task can walk past it unmolested, because the Dead does not care about them. It cares about one thing, and one thing only.

When something does interfere, the response is immediate and extreme. The Ambitious Dead fights with the ferocity of someone who has waited centuries for this moment, channeling the Descent's war-essence through iron-reinforced limbs. They do not feel pain or fear. They do not retreat or surrender. They fight until their ambition is fulfilled or their body is destroyed, whichever comes first. If their body is destroyed, the iron-orange light lingers for a moment before fading, and the domain will eventually reanimate another corpse with the same ambition. The task endures even if the body does not.

The distinction from Morrhael's undead is critical. The iron-laced undead that emerge from the Hollowdeep crossover at Level 6 are hybrids, death-animated but war-driven, and represent a blending of two divine domains. The Ambitious Dead found elsewhere in the Descent are purely Kaevroth's creation, born from war-essence alone, and their iron-orange light marks them as servants of ambition rather than death.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Use.** The Ambitious Dead raise the question of what ambition means when stripped of everything else. Their implanted drives are often pathetically small, guard this empty room, carry this message to a person who died centuries ago, and the tragedy is that they pursue these meaningless tasks with the same intensity a living person might pursue a kingdom. They are ambition without context, desire without reason, purpose without meaning.
>
> **Distinguishing from Morrhael's Undead.** If the party has encountered Hollowdeep undead, the differences should be obvious. Morrhael's undead hunger. The Ambitious Dead do not. Morrhael's undead are erratic. The Ambitious Dead are focused. The iron-orange light is the clearest visual marker: Morrhael's domain produces cold, grey undeath, while the Descent produces something that burns.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party enters a chamber on Level 4 to find an iron-laced corpse standing motionless before an anvil, holding a hammer raised mid-strike. Its eye sockets burn with iron-orange light. It has been standing like this for what appears to be a very long time, based on the dust and Iron Mite tracks around its feet. As long as no one touches the anvil, the Dead is harmless. If anyone touches it, the Dead completes its strike, then turns toward the interloper with centuries of frustrated purpose behind its burning eyes.
