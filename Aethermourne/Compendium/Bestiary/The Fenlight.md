## Description

On certain nights, when the fog thins and the ley lines beneath the Fenlands pulse with unusual strength, a light appears in the deep swamp. Not a lantern. Not a will-o'-wisp. Something vast, cathedral-scale, drifting between the ancient trees with the slow grace of something that does not know it is lost. It shifts through every color the eye can perceive and several it cannot, prismatic light that makes the fog glow and the water shimmer and the air taste of copper and starlight.

The Fenlight is [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]]'s largest surviving consciousness fragment, a piece of a shattered goddess that retained enough coherence to remember being divine. It is not hostile. It is confused. It remembers being a goddess of magic, perceiving the universe as a single continuous flow of arcane potential, and it cannot understand why it is now a light drifting through a swamp. It approaches magic-users with desperate fascination, drawn to spellcasting the way a drowning person is drawn to air. It does not mean harm. It cannot help causing it.

## Behavior & Tactics

The Fenlight does not attack. It approaches. And proximity to a fragment of divine consciousness has consequences.

Within its influence radius (roughly 10 spaces), all magic becomes unpredictable. Healing might deal damage. Fireballs might heal. Divination shows the past instead of the future. Enchantments affect the caster instead of the target. The effect is not malicious but reflexive, the magical equivalent of static interference from a source broadcasting on every frequency simultaneously. Spellcasters within range feel their magic pulled toward the Fenlight, as though their spells want to join it, to become part of the divine consciousness again.

The Fenlight cannot be fought conventionally. It is not aggressive, and attempting to destroy a Nethys fragment has campaign consequences: the [[Cosmology and Magic#Ley Lines and the Aetheric Web|Aetheric Web]] destabilizes in the surrounding region, magic fails for miles, and anyone who knew what the Fenlight was, Brother Lumen, the [[The Confluence Council]], the [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]], will be furious. Killing the Fenlight is killing a goddess a second time.

The true encounter is communication. The divine intelligence inside the Fenlight remembers things no living being knows: the architecture of the [[Cosmology and Magic#Ley Lines and the Aetheric Web|Aetheric Web]], the mathematics of ley line construction, the original design of the magical infrastructure that sustains the world. Reaching that intelligence requires patience, magical sensitivity, and a willingness to endure the chaos of proximity.

### Proximity Effect Table

Roll d8 when a spell is cast within the Fenlight's influence:

| d8 | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 | Spell functions normally (rare) |
| 2 | Spell inverts: healing deals damage, damage heals, buffs become debuffs |
| 3 | Spell targets caster instead of intended target |
| 4 | Spell manifests visually but has no mechanical effect (pure illusion) |
| 5 | Spell succeeds but also casts a random cantrip centered on the Fenlight |
| 6 | Spell is absorbed by the Fenlight, which briefly brightens and whispers a fragment of Nethys's memories |
| 7 | Spell triggers twice at double intensity (beneficial or harmful) |
| 8 | Spell opens a 1-round window of clarity, the Fenlight stabilizes, and a coherent sentence from Nethys can be heard |

### Communication Progression

Reaching Nethys requires cumulative successes:
- **DC 14 Arcana:** Establish awareness (the Fenlight acknowledges you exist)
- **DC 16 Arcana:** Establish intent (the Fenlight understands you want to communicate)
- **DC 18 Arcana:** Ask one question and receive a fragmentary answer
- **DC 20 Arcana:** Ask one question and receive a coherent, detailed answer about the Aetheric Web

Each check takes 10 minutes and requires remaining within the wild magic zone. Failure does not end the attempt but triggers a d8 Proximity Effect centered on the communicator.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **The Fenlight IS Nethys.** Not a shard of her, not an echo, not a fragment behaving like her. This is the largest piece of a living divine consciousness, aware, remembering, and desperately trying to communicate what it knows about the Web's collapse. Interacting respectfully and successfully grants campaign-critical information: where the Web is weakest, which ley lines are failing, and what would happen if Serith's plan succeeds (the Web collapses entirely, magic dies, and the world follows within a generation).
>
> **Destroying the Fenlight** removes the stabilizing influence of Nethys's largest fragment from the Fenlands' ley network. Magic in the region becomes permanently unreliable. Echo Pools detonate in chain reactions. Spell-Scarred Lurkers mutate catastrophically. The Fenlands become uninhabitable for spellcasters. Brother Lumen's fragment-reassembly becomes impossible.
>
> **Serith's Interest.** Serith is aware of the Fenlight. She has not acted against it because a chaotic Fenlands serves her purposes, keeping the region too dangerous for organized resistance. But if the PCs begin communicating with the Fenlight and learning Web architecture, Serith will move to prevent further contact.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is camped in the deep fen when the spellcaster's focus crystal begins to vibrate. Then the fog starts to glow. Color bleeds through the grey, prismatic and shifting, and between the ancient trees, something vast and luminous drifts into view. It is beautiful beyond description, light given form, moving with a grace that suggests intelligence rather than phenomenon. It approaches the spellcaster. Their prepared spells begin to hum in their minds, pulling toward the light like compass needles toward north. The Fenlight stops ten paces away. The fog shimmers. And a voice, fragmentary, confused, heartbreakingly lucid for just a moment, says: "I can see the threads. They are... they are breaking. Can you see them breaking?"
>
> **Complication:** A [[The Confluence Council|Confluence Council]] Fencrafter has been tracking the Fenlight for months, planning to harvest its energy for a massive ward-stone. She does not know, or does not care, what the Fenlight actually is. She will arrive at dawn.
