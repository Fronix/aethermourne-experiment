## Description

The Forgeborn are the children of a dead god's industry. They roll off the [[Level 4 - The Forge Eternal|Forge Eternal]]'s assembly lines in an endless stream, each one a unique product of forges operating without design or direction. They range from blade-drones, small, fast, darting constructs no larger than a hawk, all cutting edges and articulated iron wings, to siege-scale walking engines that shake the ground with every step, bristling with weapons that serve no coherent tactical doctrine.

No two Forgeborn are identical. The forges produce them with divine-quality craftsmanship and zero coherent intent, so each construct is a masterwork of engineering that solves a problem nobody posed. A blade-drone might have sixteen articulated cutting surfaces and no way to see. A war-engine might carry enough firepower to level a fortress but lack the ability to turn left. They are beautiful, precise, and fundamentally purposeless, weapons built by a factory that forgot what war was for.

Their bodies are polished iron and dark steel, still warm from the forges, etched with war-glyphs that glow faintly amber. Fresh Forgeborn gleam. Older ones, the constructs that have been patrolling for centuries, show their age in patina and rust, their war-glyphs dimmed to a dull orange pulse. The oldest Forgeborn, the ones built while [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] still lived, are different: they move with purpose, their glyphs burn bright, and they remember what they were built to do.

## Behavior & Tactics

Without orders, the Forgeborn default to three behaviors: patrol, build, and disassemble. They patrol routes that may have had strategic significance two thousand years ago, guarding positions, securing perimeters, and executing maneuvers against threats that no longer exist. They build, using salvaged materials to construct fortifications, barriers, and structures of no discernible purpose. And they disassemble each other, the most unsettling behavior, methodically dismantling a fellow construct for parts and incorporating those parts into their own chassis, growing larger and more complex over time.

Blade-drones are fast and numerous, attacking in swarms with coordinated slashing passes. They are individually fragile but difficult to track, and a swarm of twenty can shred an armored target in seconds. Mid-scale Forgeborn, roughly humanoid in size and shape, fight like soldiers, using weapons integrated into their bodies with competent if uninspired tactics. The siege-scale engines are walking disasters, slow and ponderous but capable of destroying anything in their path through sheer mass and firepower.

The true danger is the ancient Forgeborn, the pre-Theomachis constructs that still carry fragments of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s command authority. These behave with genuine intelligence: they set ambushes, exploit weaknesses, and coordinate lesser Forgeborn into effective fighting units. An ancient Forgeborn leading a formation of newer models is a military force capable of threatening anything short of divine intervention.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Forgeborn are the Iron Descent's most scalable threat. A single blade-drone is a nuisance. A swarm is dangerous. An ancient Forgeborn commanding a battalion is a campaign-ending threat. Scale them to the party's level and the narrative stakes.
>
> **Campaign Connection.** The Forge Eternal's continued production is relevant to the mortal world. If a planar thin point near the Forge widens, Forgeborn could begin emerging into Aethermourne. Even a handful of blade-drones loose in a mortal city would be catastrophic. An ancient Forgeborn crossing over would be an extinction-level event for whatever region it appeared in.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is moving through the Forge Eternal when they hear the rhythmic clang of metal on metal ahead. Around a bend, they find a mid-scale Forgeborn methodically disassembling a larger construct that has fallen inactive. It has already incorporated one of the larger construct's weapon-arms into its own chassis and is reaching for a second. It has not noticed the party. Nearby, a cluster of blade-drones circles overhead in a holding pattern, waiting. If the party attacks or makes noise, the mid-scale Forgeborn designates them as a threat and the blade-drones descend. If the party waits, the Forgeborn finishes its work, stands, and reveals that it has grown half again its original size.
>
> **Escalation:** The noise of combat attracts more Forgeborn from deeper in the Forge. The party has three rounds before reinforcements arrive, and the reinforcements include something much larger.
