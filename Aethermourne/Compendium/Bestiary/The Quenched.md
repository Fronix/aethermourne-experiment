## Description

In the deepest forge-chambers, where the stone pulses with [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]]'s heartbeat rhythm and the heat blisters skin in minutes, the god's creative essence still tries to work. It has no consciousness to guide it, no design to follow, no hands to shape with. But the impulse to *make* persists, divine muscle memory firing without a mind behind it. The result is the Quenched: humanoid constructs of half-forged metal and volcanic glass, pulled together from raw material by an intent that cannot complete what it starts.

They are heartbreaking to look at. A torso without a face, smooth metal where features should be. Legs that end in formless lumps of cooling slag. Arms that reach for tools they will never hold, fingers fused into blunt shapes that almost resemble hands. They glow faintly from within, the residual heat of their creation bleeding through seams in their incomplete shells. They move with desperate, jerking purpose, lurching through the deep chambers seeking raw material to add to themselves, driven by the formless conviction that if they can just find the right piece, they will be *finished*.

They never are.

## Behavior & Tactics

The Quenched are not hostile by default. They approach creatures carrying worked metal with a reaching, grasping need, hands extended, bodies leaning forward, the posture of someone asking for help in a language they do not possess. The danger is the taking. A Quenched that gets hold of a metal object absorbs it, dissolving the item into its body and incorporating the material into its unfinished form. Each absorbed item makes it larger, denser, and more capable. A Quenched that has taken nothing is clumsy and fragile. One that has absorbed four or more items is a formidable construct, its body now reinforced with worked steel, its movements smoother, its half-formed hands now capable of striking with real force.

The true encounter is not combat but craft. A DC 16 Crafting check allows a character to *finish* a Quenched, working with the construct's incomplete form rather than fighting it, shaping the raw material with tools and intent until the divine essence recognizes completion. A finished Quenched goes still, its internal glow fading to a warm amber, its body settling into a final shape. It becomes inert, a statue of whatever the crafter's intent shaped it into. The process takes ten minutes and requires smithing tools.

### Absorption Progression

| Items Absorbed | Effect |
|---|---|
| 0 | Clumsy, fragile. Speed 3, Armor L, HP 15. Grapple only (no damage) |
| 1-2 | Functional. Speed 4, Armor M, HP 25. Slam 1d6+2 |
| 3 | Reinforced. Speed 4, Armor M, HP 35. Slam 1d8+3 |
| 4+ | Formidable. Speed 5, Armor H, HP 45. Slam 1d10+4, gains Multiattack (2 slams) |

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Quenched are increasing in number as the Cooling destabilizes [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]]'s forge-chambers. The divine creative essence, weakening alongside everything else, fires more erratically, producing more constructs that are less complete. [[Orin Deepstone]]'s deep mining teams have sealed off Level Fourteen beneath [[Hearthdeep]] after encountering a dozen Quenched in a single chamber, all reaching toward each other, trying to complete themselves by absorbing one another.
>
> **[[Apprentice Jyn]]'s Instinct.** During their first descent into the deep levels, Jyn encountered a Quenched and finished it without instruction. They shaped it into a small bird, smooth-winged and still warm. The senior smiths present said nothing for a long time. What Jyn did should require years of forge-magic training. It required compassion instead.
>
> **[[Brunhild Ironheart]]'s Secret.** The Forge-Matriarch keeps a finished Quenched in her private chambers. It is shaped like a dwarf, short and broad-shouldered, one hand raised as if about to speak. She finished it herself, thirty years ago, in the deep forge where her husband died in a collapse. She will not say whose likeness it bears. She does not need to.
>
> **Campaign Connection.** The Quenched are Thalvor trying to build without being alive. They are the forge equivalent of the Fenlands' [[Echo Pools]]: divine function persisting after divine consciousness has ended. If the Cooling continues, the creative essence will fail entirely, and no more Quenched will form. The last generation of Quenched, the ones being born now, are the final echoes of a god who gave everything to keep the world warm.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | The Quenched (base) | 3 | L-M | 15-45 | 3-5 | +2 | -1 | -2 | grapple or slam (see Absorption) |
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Absorb.** On successful grapple, target must make Str save DC 14 or lose one held/worn metal item. Shield first, then weapon, then armor.
> - **Incomplete.** Immune to charm, fear, and psychic damage. Cannot be reasoned with. Responds only to craft.
> - **Finish.** DC 16 Crafting check (10 minutes, requires smithing tools). On success, the Quenched becomes inert. On failure, the Quenched absorbs one of the crafter's tools.
>
> **Bloodied:** Internal glow intensifies. The Quenched's reaching becomes frantic. It gains advantage on grapple checks and moves toward the nearest source of worked metal regardless of danger.
>
> **Weakness:** Cold damage slows (half speed for 1 round). Thunder damage causes absorbed items to partially eject, reducing absorption level by 1.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 2-4 players of level 3-5 (per 2-3 Quenched)

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The forge-chamber is hot enough to make breathing difficult. The stone walls pulse with a deep, slow rhythm. In the center of the chamber, lit by its own fading glow, a figure stands. It is roughly humanoid, built from volcanic glass and half-forged iron. It has no face. Its legs end in shapeless lumps. Its arms are extended, fingers fused into blunt approximations of hands, reaching toward the party. It takes one lurching step forward. Then another. It is not threatening. It is not angry. It is reaching for the fighter's sword with the desperate, wordless need of something that knows it is incomplete and believes, with all the conviction a mindless construct can hold, that the missing piece is right there, in your hands, if you would only let it take what it needs to become whole.
>
> **Complication:** There are three more behind it, deeper in the chamber. One has already absorbed a mining pick and a lantern-hook. Its movements are smoother. Its half-formed hand is almost a fist. And behind all of them, slumped against the far wall, is a dwarven miner's pack, still warm, its owner nowhere to be seen.
