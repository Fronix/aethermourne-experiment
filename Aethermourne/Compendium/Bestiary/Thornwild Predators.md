## Sorrow-Rooted

### Description

Sorrow-Rooted were animals once, wolves, boars, jungle cats, things with teeth and purpose. Then the Marrow-Water found them, and behind the Marrow-Water came the shadow. Now they are neither animal nor plant, locked in agonized transformation, bodies constantly growing and reabsorbing tissue in a cycle that never completes. Bark-flesh splits to reveal wet muscle that immediately sprouts fungal threads. Limbs elongate, then curl inward, consumed by creeping vine. Flowers bloom from exposed bone, beautiful and wrong, petals the color of bruises.

They move with stuttering, uneven gaits, dragging root-tendrils that trail behind them like umbilical cords still attached to the corrupted earth. Their eyes, when they have them, weep amber sap. They howl, but the sound comes out wrong, half animal cry, half the groan of wood splitting in a storm.

### Behavior & Tactics

Sorrow-Rooted hunt by scent, drawn to warm blood because the predator instinct survives beneath the botanical corruption. They are solitary or found in pairs, never packs, because proximity to another Sorrow-Rooted accelerates the transformation in both, and something in them still fears that. They attack with vine-lash tendrils and thorn-studded limbs, and their touch spreads a localized growth effect: wounds do not bleed but sprout, tiny rootlets threading through torn flesh.

The most dangerous thing about them is the aura. Within 3 spaces, living creatures feel a wash of sourceless grief, a quiet whisper that nothing grows without something else dying, that all life is built on rot. This is [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s shadow bleeding through [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s corrupted root-network, and it is the clearest sign that these creatures are symptoms of something far worse.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Sorrow-Rooted appear near the densest concentrations of shadow-root corruption. Their presence is a map. If the party tracks where Sorrow-Rooted have been sighted, the pattern points directly toward the corrupted nodes in the deep Cradle. [[Elder Rosk]] will recognize the significance if told.
>
> **Scaling:** Near Roothold or the Cradle's edge, Sorrow-Rooted are recent transformations (smaller, weaker, still partly recognizable as the original animal). Deep in the Cradle, they are ancient, massive, barely recognizable as anything that was ever alive.

### Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is following a game trail through the mid-Cradle when they notice the undergrowth has gone wrong. Plants lean inward instead of reaching for light. Flowers bloom facing the earth. The air smells of sap and salt water, a combination that should not exist this far from the coast. Then they hear the howl, half wolf, half splitting timber, and a Sorrow-Rooted emerges from the treeline, a thing that was once a great stag, its antlers now a crown of flowering thorns, its body a battlefield between flesh and forest.

## Bloom-Revenant

### Description

Bloom-Revenants are what happens when [[The Verdant Marches#The Marrow Bogs|Marrow-Water]] saturates a corpse in a region where [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s shadow has touched the root-network. The body does not rot. It blooms. A flowering colony subsumes the remains, threading roots through bone, replacing muscle with vine, and animating the whole structure with a parody of life that is neither undead nor alive but something between.

They walk upright, dragging root-trails, their bodies barely visible beneath cascading flowers and fungal shelves. Three to five flower-heads crown each Revenant, each bloom a different species, each producing a different toxin. The largest bloom, always at the center, opens and closes in rhythm with the creature's movement, releasing clouds of iridescent spores that catch the light like scattered gems. They are beautiful. Travelers who see them at a distance sometimes mistake them for forest spirits. Travelers who see them up close do not make that mistake twice.

The spores are hallucinogenic. Mild exposure causes euphoria and vivid sensory distortion, colors brightening, sounds becoming music, the forest seeming to welcome you with open arms. Heavy exposure causes the victim to perceive the Bloom-Revenant as a loved one, a lost friend, a childhood home, anything that makes them stop running and stand still while the roots reach their ankles.

### Behavior & Tactics

Bloom-Revenants are territorial rather than predatory. They claim clearings and groves, spreading their spore-field outward until the entire area is a hallucinogenic garden. Creatures that wander in are not attacked immediately but dosed, slowly, until they sit down and stop resisting. Then the roots come, threading through clothing and skin, anchoring the victim to the earth. Given days, a victim becomes a new Bloom-Revenant.

They do not pursue prey that flees. They do not need to. The spores linger in the air for hours after the Revenant has moved on, and victims who inhaled even a small amount find themselves dreaming of the garden, wanting to return, certain that the peace they felt was real.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Bloom-Revenants are Serith's corruption wearing Thyrea's face. The hallucinogenic peace they offer is a microcosm of Serith's philosophy: dissolution is mercy, surrender is freedom, individuality is pain. A [[The Greensingers|Greensinger]] who analyzes the spore-patterns can detect Serith's signature, a faint taste of salt water and the whisper that nothing matters. This is one of the earliest clues that the Marches' corruption originates from the ocean.
>
> **Roleplay Note.** The hallucinations are personalized. Ask each player what their character sees when exposed. The answer reveals what the character most wants, which Serith's influence then weaponizes.

### Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party enters a clearing that is impossibly beautiful. Flowers cover every surface, bioluminescent fungi pulse with warm light, and the air smells of honey and rain. A figure stands at the center, humanoid, draped in cascading blooms. It turns toward the party, and for a moment, each PC sees someone different: a parent, a lover, a friend they lost. Then the spores hit, and the beauty becomes a question: why would you ever leave?

## Hunger-Crawler

### Description

Hunger-Crawlers are not creatures in any conventional sense. They are root-networks that have achieved mobility, vast webs of [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]-corrupted vegetation that move beneath the bog surface like predatory fishing nets. From above, they are visible only as patterns of bioluminescence, green-gold threads pulsing beneath mud and still water, geometric and deliberate, shifting in ways that suggest intelligence or at least hunger.

The network can span an area the size of a village square. It moves slowly, no faster than a walking pace, but it is patient in the way that all things rooted in the earth are patient. It herds prey by raising root-walls to cut off escape routes, channeling victims toward saturation zones where the Marrow-Water concentration is highest and resistance is lowest. When prey stops moving, the roots rise. They thread through boot-leather, through cloth, through skin if given time. They pull downward. The bog accepts what is offered.

Hunger-Crawlers are most common at the expanding edge of the [[The Verdant Marches#The Marrow Bogs|Marrow Bogs]], where contamination is fresh and the root-network is still establishing dominance. Deep in the Bogs, they are the terrain itself.

### Behavior & Tactics

A Hunger-Crawler does not charge or strike. It surrounds. It waits. It herds. Victims often do not realize they are being hunted until they notice the bioluminescent threads have formed a closed circle around them and the ground has become soft. The root-network communicates with itself through vibrational patterns in the mud, coordinating segments across its entire span to close escape routes simultaneously.

In combat, the Crawler fights by grappling from below, root-tendrils erupting from the ground to seize ankles and drag victims into the mud. Each round a creature remains grappled, the roots thread deeper, draining vitality and replacing it with a cold, green numbness. Victims describe a sensation of becoming part of the earth, of their thoughts slowing and spreading like water soaking into soil.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Hunger-Crawlers may be Thyrea's immune response to Serith's corruption, an attempt by the dying goddess's distributed consciousness to contain the shadow by consuming everything in the corrupted zone, friend and foe alike. [[The Greensingers]] debate this. If true, the Crawlers are not enemies but a desperate, mindless defense mechanism, and destroying them weakens the Marches' ability to fight the corruption.
>
> **Environmental Encounter.** Hunger-Crawlers work best as terrain hazards layered over another encounter. The party is navigating the Bogs when they realize the ground is alive, and then the Sorrow-Rooted arrives, and now they are fighting on a surface that is actively trying to swallow them.

### Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is crossing the Marrow Bogs at twilight. The water glows. Green-gold threads pulse beneath the surface in spiraling patterns, beautiful and hypnotic. A local guide warns them to stay on the raised path. Then the path sinks. The mud softens. Root-threads rise from the water on either side, forming a closing fence of bioluminescent tendrils. The Hunger-Crawler has been herding them for the last half-mile, and now the circle is complete.

> - **Segmentation.** When reduced to half HP, the Crawler splits into two independent segments (each with 25 HP, same abilities but 3 space radius). When a segment is destroyed, the other absorbs its remaining energy and regains 10 HP.
>
> **Bloodied:** Bioluminescence intensifies to blinding levels. All creatures within the Crawler's area make Dex save DC 13 or are blinded for 1 round. The Crawler splits (see Segmentation).
>
> **Weakness:** Fire damage deals double and cauterizes root-growth (grappled creatures are automatically freed if fire damage is dealt to the Crawler). Cold damage freezes root-threads (grapple DC drops to 11 for 2 rounds). Defoliant alchemical compounds (if available) deal 3d6 damage per application.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 4-5 players of level 4-6
