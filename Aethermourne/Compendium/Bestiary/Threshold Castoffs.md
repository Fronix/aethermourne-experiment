# Threshold Castoffs

## Description

Threshold Castoffs are what the Maelstrom throws away. During surge-waves, the wound in reality convulses and expels matter, energy, and occasionally things that are neither, fully formed creatures assembled from materials that should not exist and following behavioral patterns that obey no known logic.

A wolf-shaped thing built from floating stone and metal that breathes. A bird whose wings are frozen fire that casts cold shadows. A centipede of liquid glass that flows uphill, leaving trails of prismatic residue. A serpent woven from crystallized sound that hums a note no instrument can produce. Each one is unique because the Maelstrom never repeats itself. They are beautiful in the way a lightning strike is beautiful: brief, alien, and dangerous to stand near.

Castoffs have no evolution, no ecology, no lifecycle. They did not grow. They did not hatch. They were ejected into existence with bodies, behaviors, and abilities fully intact, as if reality sneezed and something impossible came out. Their forms are stable but wrong, composed of materials that contradict each other (stone that floats, fire that freezes, glass that flows) held together by residual Maelstrom energy. When that energy fades, which can take days, weeks, or never, the Castoff simply stops, its impossible materials collapsing into inert Vorrhyn's Dust.

## Behavior & Tactics

Castoffs follow behavioral patterns that make no natural sense. One circles a specific spot endlessly, as if guarding something that is not there. Another attacks only things of a particular color. A third flees from silence but charges toward loud noise. These behaviors are not random; they are consistent within each individual Castoff, but they follow a logic that belongs to whatever impossible place the Maelstrom connects to, not to the material world.

They are not inherently aggressive. Some are territorial, defending a patch of ground for reasons only they understand. Some are curious, approaching travelers with the cautious intensity of an animal encountering something entirely new, which, from their perspective, everything is. Some seem to be searching, moving in widening spirals, investigating objects and creatures with senses that may not correspond to sight, sound, or touch.

Combat with a Castoff is unpredictable because their impossible bodies produce impossible effects. A stone-wolf's bite might reverse gravity in a small radius. A glass-centipede's venom might render flesh transparent. A sound-serpent's strike might replace your voice with a frequency that shatters nearby objects. No two Castoffs fight the same way, and no preparation fully accounts for what they can do. [[Edgewatch]] patrols learn to identify new Castoffs at range and observe their behavioral patterns before engaging, cataloguing quirks and effects before committing to a fight.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Threshold Castoffs are increasing in both number and strangeness as the Maelstrom widens. Recent Castoffs have begun showing coordinated behavior, not intelligence, but synchronization, as if the Maelstrom is expelling them with purpose. Groups of three or four Castoffs have been observed moving in formation, their behavioral patterns interlocking like components of a system none of them understand individually.
>
> **Campaign connection:** Some Castoffs carry trace amounts of Loom-resonance, vibrating at the same frequency as [[Cosmology and Magic|Empyrean Shards]]. [[The Echoists]] believe Castoffs are [[The Twelve#Vorrhyn, God of Chaos and Change , *"The Unbound"*|Vorrhyn]]'s final gifts, fragments of his creative power still expressing itself through the wound. The truth is more complex: they are stress-fractures given form. When [[The Twelve#Serith, Goddess of Shadow and Secrets , *"The Veiled One"*|Serith]] pulls at the Maelstrom from the other side, reality snaps back like an overstretched cord, and the Castoffs are what shakes loose. Their increasing coordination suggests the pulling is becoming rhythmic, deliberate, systematic.
>
> **Narrative tool:** Castoffs are excellent for establishing the Shattered Edge's alien atmosphere. Every encounter is unique. The randomized generation table below ensures GMs never run the same Castoff twice, reinforcing the region's core theme: here, nothing repeats, nothing is predictable, and beauty and danger are the same thing.

> **Resistances/Immunities:** Each Castoff is immune to the damage type matching its body composition (fire Castoff immune to fire, etc.) and vulnerable to its opposite (fire Castoff vulnerable to cold, etc.). GM adjudicates based on the generated composition.
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Impossible Physiology.** The Castoff's body follows non-standard physics. Its special combat effect (from the table above) triggers on each successful hit or as a passive aura (GM's choice based on the rolled effect).
> - **Behavioral Override.** The Castoff's behavioral quirk takes precedence over tactical logic. A Castoff that freezes when observed will stop mid-attack if a new creature makes eye contact. A Castoff that flees silence will retreat if the battlefield goes quiet. Clever parties can exploit these patterns.
> - **Dust Collapse.** When reduced to 0 HP, the Castoff's body decomposes into Vorrhyn's Dust (1d4 doses for minor, 2d4 for standard, 3d4 for major). A Castoff carrying Loom-resonance (10% chance) also yields one Empyrean Shard fragment.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** Varies by tier. Minor: level 1-3. Standard: level 4-6. Major: level 7-9.
