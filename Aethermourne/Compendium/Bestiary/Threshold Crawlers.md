## Description

Threshold Crawlers inhabit the membrane between the mortal world and [[The Iron Descent]], living in the boundary itself rather than on either side of it. They are half-real, half-iron, flickering between states like a candle in a draft. One moment they appear as translucent, many-limbed shapes, pale and glistening, with too many joints bending in directions that make the eye ache. The next moment they solidify into rusted iron constructs, angular and sharp, before dissolving back into translucence.

They cluster near planar thin points, the cracks where [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s domain bleeds into the mortal plane. Near a buried iron fragment, a Threshold Crawler might be visible only as a shimmer in peripheral vision, a wrongness in the air that disappears when you look directly at it. In the Ashfield itself, they are more substantial, scuttling along the underside of reality like insects beneath wallpaper, occasionally pressing through to snatch at anything that moves.

## Behavior & Tactics

Threshold Crawlers are scavengers with ambitions above their station. They feed on disorientation, on the psychic static generated by a mind caught between two planes. New arrivals to the Ashfield are their preferred prey, souls stumbling through a thin point who do not yet understand where they are. The Crawler latches onto this confusion and amplifies it, keeping the victim suspended in the threshold state, neither fully in the mortal world nor fully in the Descent, while it feeds.

Their most dangerous ability is translocation. A Threshold Crawler in contact with a creature near a planar thin point can pull that creature into the Iron Descent. The process is not gentle: the victim feels their body become iron for a fraction of a second, every nerve replaced by cold metal, before they tumble through and land on grey ash under a colorless sky. The Crawler does not follow. It stays in the boundary, waiting for the next traveler.

In combat, they flicker between their translucent and iron states. In translucent form, they are difficult to hit with physical attacks but vulnerable to magic. In iron form, they are solid, heavy, and resistant to magic but can be struck normally. They shift states unpredictably, making them frustrating opponents. Groups of Crawlers stagger their shifts so that at least one is always in the state that counters whatever the attacker is using.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Threshold Crawlers are the primary mechanism by which mortals accidentally enter the Iron Descent. Any adventure involving a [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] fragment in the mortal world should include Crawlers as a warning sign: their presence means the boundary is thin enough to fall through.
>
> **The Pull.** Being dragged into the Descent by a Crawler is a one-way trip unless the victim can find another thin point or negotiate passage with something deeper. This makes Crawlers excellent plot devices for stranding a party in the Iron Descent.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is investigating a [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] fragment in the mortal world. The air near the fragment tastes of iron. Reflections in nearby water show a grey, ash-covered landscape instead of the surroundings. Then the shimmering begins at the edges of vision, Threshold Crawlers gathering at the thin point, drawn by the party's proximity. One PC feels iron creeping up their boots, cold and heavy. A Crawler has latched on and is pulling. The party must fight creatures that phase between states while preventing their companion from being dragged through to the Ashfield.
>
> **Stakes:** If the PC is pulled through, the rest of the party sees them vanish, replaced by a brief afterimage in rusted iron that crumbles to dust. The pulled PC wakes alone on the Ashfield with no obvious way back.

> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Phase Shift.** Alternates between translucent form (physical damage reduced to dice only, magic deals full damage) and iron form (magic damage halved, physical deals full damage). Shifts at the start of its turn.
> - **Translocation Pull.** On a hit against a target near a planar thin point, the target must pass DC 14 Str save or be pulled partially into the Iron Descent. On failure by 5+, the target is fully translocated.
> - **Disorientation Aura.** Creatures within 2 spaces roll twice and take lower on Perception checks and cannot determine true north.
> - **Boundary Lurk.** Can hide within planar boundaries, appearing as a shimmer in peripheral vision. DC 15 Perception to spot before it attacks.
> **Bloodied:** Phase shifting accelerates, switching forms at the start and end of its turn. Translocation Pull DC increases to 16.
> **Weakness:** Attacks that affect both planes simultaneously (radiant damage, divine magic) bypass the phase shift entirely, dealing full damage in either form.
> **Recommended Party Size:** 3-4 players of level 3-5 (per 2-3 Crawlers)
