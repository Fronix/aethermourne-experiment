## Description

Where the Bloom meets the Wilt, in the narrow band where [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s growth-essence and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s death-chill exist in temporary equilibrium, [[Cosmology and Magic#Thornwild Amber|Thornwild amber]] crystallizes. It does not form by accident. Threshold Weavers make it.

They are arachnid in shape but built from both sides of the Cycle: legs of pale bone wrapped in living vine, bodies of fused amber and grey chitin, spinnerets that extrude silk composed of equal parts sap and necrotic filament. Each Weaver is roughly the size of a large dog, though specimens near the Bonewood border grow larger. Their webs stretch between the last living trees and the first dead ones, shimmering curtains of amber-silk that catch the light and harden over hours into raw Thornwild amber. The webs are beautiful. They are also load-bearing traps designed to immobilize anything that touches them.

Weavers are not aggressive. They are territorial. Their webs mark crystallization points where transition energy is strongest, and they defend those points the way any predator defends a food source. The problem is that these crystallization points are exactly where [[Garret Boneharvest]]'s harvesters need to go.

## Behavior & Tactics

A Threshold Weaver's first response to intrusion is vibration. It plucks its web, sending a thrumming signal that warns other Weavers in the area. If the intruder does not withdraw, the Weaver drops from concealment and attacks with its bite, which delivers a paralytic venom that combines Thyrea's growth-acceleration with Morrhael's numbing cold. The venom does not kill. It immobilizes the target while accelerating cellular growth and decay simultaneously, wrapping the victim in a cocoon of their own rapidly cycling tissue. Left untreated, the victim becomes part of the web within hours, another node in the amber-producing network.

Weavers fight defensively. They retreat to their webs, where their movement is unimpeded while intruders are slowed by adhesive silk. They target isolated individuals and avoid engaging groups directly, preferring to snare stragglers. Multiple Weavers coordinate through web-vibration, creating kill-zones where overlapping webs funnel prey into ambush points.

Experienced harvesters from [[Bone Garden]] have learned to read the web patterns. Fresh silk (translucent, slightly warm) means a Weaver is nearby and active. Hardened amber-silk (golden, brittle) means the Weaver has moved on and the amber is safe to harvest. The margin between "safe" and "active" is about two hours. Many harvester injuries come from miscalculating the timing.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Threshold Weavers are the primary producers of [[Cosmology and Magic#Thornwild Amber|Thornwild amber]], though few outside [[Bone Garden]] understand this. [[The Ember Guild]] wants to exterminate Weavers to harvest amber freely. [[The Cyclekeepers]] argue that without Weavers, amber production would stop entirely, the creatures are not guarding the amber, they are creating it. This is a genuine ecological question with economic consequences.
>
> **The Cycle acceleration** is shrinking the Threshold. As the balanced zone narrows, Weavers are being compressed into a smaller territory. Web density is increasing. Harvester casualties are rising. Some Weavers have begun building webs outside the Threshold, in areas that were previously pure Bloom or pure Wilt. [[Yara Thornbloom]] interprets this as the Weavers searching for new equilibrium points, a sign that the old balance is failing.
>
> **Campaign Hook.** A Weaver cocoon contains a living person in suspended animation, cycling between life and death but aware. Rescuing them requires understanding the Cycle well enough to interrupt it without killing the victim. [[Garret Boneharvest]] lost a harvester to a cocoon three weeks ago and has not told the family.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Threshold Weaver | 2 | M | 20 | 4 (6 on web) | +1 | +3 | +0 | bite 1d6+1 + Cycle Venom |
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Cycle Venom.** On hit, target makes DC 13 Constitution save or is slowed (half movement, no reactions) for 2 rounds. On a failed save by 5+, target is paralyzed for 1 round as cellular growth and decay accelerate simultaneously.
> - **Web-Walker.** Moves at full speed on webs. Ignores difficult terrain in webbed areas.
> - **Amber Snare.** (Recharge 4-6) Launches a glob of amber-silk at a target within 6 spaces. Target makes DC 12 Dexterity save or is restrained. Escaping requires DC 14 Strength check or dealing 8+ damage to the silk.
> - **Web Sense.** Detects exact location of any creature touching its web.
>
> **Weakness:** Fire destroys webs instantly (but also destroys harvestable amber, making this a costly tactic). Cold damage slows the Weaver's life-phase, reducing speed by 2 for 1 round.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 2-4 players of level 2-3 (per nest of 2-3)

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The harvesting trail ends at a curtain of silk strung between a flowering oak and a bone-white birch. The silk catches the afternoon light and throws amber patterns across the forest floor. Beautiful. The trail of boot prints leading into the web and not coming back out is less so. Somewhere above, a faint vibration thrums through the canopy, one long pulse, two short. A harvester from [[Bone Garden]] goes pale. "That's the gathering call. We have about thirty seconds before the rest of them know we're here."
>
> **Complication:** The missing harvester is visible inside a cocoon at the web's center, still breathing, skin flickering between flushed and grey. The cocoon is hardening. In another hour it will be solid amber, and the person inside will be preserved forever in the transition moment between life and death.
