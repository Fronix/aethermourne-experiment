# Tideborn Horrors

*The sea gives back what it takes. It just gives it back wrong.*

> [!info]
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Region** | [[The Hollowed Reach]], [[The Hollowed Reach#The Shattered Coast|Shattered Coast]], coastal settlements after [[The Hollowed Reach#Black Tides|Black Tides]] |
> | **Origin** | Marine life mutated by Black Tide corruption |
> | **Threat Level** | Moderate |
> | **Associated God** | [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] (through corrupted tidal magic) |

---

## Description

Tideborn Horrors are what happens when Black Tide water saturates living things. Fish, seals, dolphins, crabs, anything caught in the ink-dark surge comes back changed. Bodies fuse together, multiple species merged into single organisms that should not be able to live but do. A seal's torso grafted to a shark's tail, mouths opening in places mouths should not be, limbs from the wrong animal attached at angles that defy skeletal logic. They trail dark water that does not evaporate, leaving slick black paths on stone and sand.

They beach themselves on the Shattered Coast after Black Tides recede, flopping and dragging across tidal pools with the desperate, uncoordinated movements of creatures that do not understand their own bodies. Some are dead on arrival, their fused biology unable to sustain itself. Others survive in visible agony, vocalizing in sounds that combine the calls of every species merged within them, a chorus of distress from a single throat. The smell is unmistakable: brine, rot, and something chemical, the sharp ozonic tang of corrupted magic.

The [[The Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keepers]] track their frequency. Three years ago, a Tideborn washing ashore was notable enough to file a report. Now it happens after every Black Tide. The creatures are getting larger, the fusions more complex, and the survival rate is climbing.

## Behavior & Tactics

Tideborn Horrors are disoriented and in pain. They do not hunt with purpose. They lash out at anything that approaches, not from aggression but from panic, multiple nervous systems firing contradictory signals through a body that was never meant to hold them. Their attacks are erratic, a crab-claw snapping while a seal's jaws bite while a fish-tail whips, each component operating on its own instinct.

The real danger is the residual Black Tide corruption they carry. Their blood and the dark water they trail are mutagenic on contact. Wounds inflicted by a Tideborn sting and discolor, the flesh around the injury briefly showing the translucent quality of deep-sea tissue before the effect fades. Prolonged contact, immersion in the dark water they leave behind, risks more permanent changes: gills appearing on the neck, skin developing bioluminescent patches, an unsettling comfort in deep water.

They are drawn to tidal magic. A [[Cosmology and Magic#Tidal Sorcery|tidal sorcerer]] casting nearby will attract every Tideborn within a quarter mile, the corrupted creatures homing on the magical signature like moths to flame.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The fusion is not random. A scholar who studies enough Tideborn specimens (requires access to 5+ bodies and a DC 16 Arcana check) discovers that the species combinations follow mathematical patterns identical to the [[Trench Crawlers|Trench Crawler]] bioluminescence sequences. Serith's corruption has a grammar, a syntax of transformation that operates on consistent rules. This discovery is the first evidence that the Black Tides are not chaotic events but structured operations, deliberate acts of reshaping rather than random surges of corrupted energy.
>
> **What this means.** If the corruption follows rules, it can be predicted. And if it can be predicted, it can potentially be countered. This information is invaluable to [[Celestine Vael]] and [[The Star-Readers]], who are trying to develop Black Tide countermeasures.
>
> **The deeper horror.** The Tideborn are not accidents. They are drafts. Serith is experimenting with biological fusion, learning how to merge separate things into unified wholes. She is practicing on fish and seals before she practices on something else.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> Dawn after a Black Tide. The party is on the Shattered Coast, helping Lantern-Keepers survey the damage. A fisherman's boat, overturned in the surge, lies on the rocks. Beneath it, something is moving. Wet clicking. A sound like three animals breathing at once. When the boat is shifted, the Tideborn Horror underneath lunges, a mass of fused fish and crab and something mammalian, trailing dark water, snapping with mouths that were never meant to share a body. Behind it, in the tidal pool, two more are dragging themselves toward shore.
>
> **Complication:** A Lantern-Keeper researcher begs the party to capture one alive. She needs a fresh specimen to continue her study of the fusion patterns. Capturing a Tideborn without killing it is significantly harder than putting it down, and the dark water it trails makes the footing treacherous.

> **Resistances/Immunities:** Immune to cold. Resistant to psychic (too many minds, none coherent).
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Mutagenic Contact.** A creature hit by a Tideborn's natural attack makes Str save DC 12 (small) or DC 14 (large). Failure: the wound discolors and the creature gains disadvantage on Dexterity checks for 1 hour as the flesh around the wound briefly loses solidity.
> - **Dark Water Trail.** The ground within 1 space of the Tideborn is coated in corrupted water (difficult terrain). Creatures that start their turn in the water make Str save DC 11 or take 1d4 cold damage.
> - **Erratic Attack.** On each turn, the Tideborn makes 2 attacks with different body parts. Each attack uses a different damage type (slashing, piercing, or bludgeoning, rolled randomly).
> - **Magic Drawn.** If a creature within 6 spaces casts a spell using tidal magic, the Tideborn immediately moves its full speed toward that creature (no opportunity attacks).
>
> **Bloodied:** The fusion destabilizes. The Tideborn splits partially, two halves still connected but attacking independently. It gains an additional attack per turn but its movement is reduced to 2.
>
> **Weakness:** Fire damage cauterizes the mutagenic tissue (removes Mutagenic Contact for 1 round). Radiant damage disrupts the corrupted magic holding the fusion together (deals double damage).
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 3-4 players of level 2-4 (per 2-3 Tideborn)
