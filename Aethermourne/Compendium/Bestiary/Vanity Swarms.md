## Description

Vanity Swarms are clouds of thumb-sized insects whose bodies are living mirror fragments, each shard reflecting not the world as it is but the viewer as they wish to be. The swarm descends in glittering curtains that catch the light of the Hall's endless reflections, throwing prismatic patterns across iron walls. Each insect shows a slightly different perfection: you as a king, you as beloved, you as powerful beyond reckoning, you as the person you secretly believe you deserve to be.

The swarm is beautiful. That is the point. Victims stand transfixed, watching a thousand tiny portraits of their ideal self dance in the air around them. While they stare, the insects land. Their mirror-edged wings are razors. They flay skin in paper-thin strips, so precisely that the pain arrives seconds after the cut. By the time a victim realizes they are bleeding, they are already covered.

## Behavior & Tactics

Vanity Swarms hunt by fascination. They drift through the Hall of Mirrors in loose formations, drawn to strong self-image and personal vanity. The more a creature values its own appearance, status, or identity, the more vivid the reflections become, and the harder it is to look away. The swarm does not pursue fleeing prey. It does not need to. Breaking eye contact with your own perfection requires a conscious act of self-rejection that most mortals find nearly impossible.

In combat, the swarm splits to surround its target, creating a sphere of mirrors that blocks sight of the real world entirely. Victims see only themselves, perfect and radiant, while the insects strip them layer by layer. The swarm avoids targets who refuse to look or who have already lost significant identity to the [[The Iron Descent#The Descent's Hunger|Iron Tithe]], as their reflections are too dim to sustain the fascination effect.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Use.** Vanity Swarms are an early warning system for the Hall of Mirrors. They demonstrate the level's core mechanic: self-deception as weapon. A PC who resists a Vanity Swarm has demonstrated the psychological resilience needed to survive Level 5. A PC who succumbs reveals which version of themselves the Descent will use against them later.
>
> **Mechanical Note.** Resisting the fascination is not a simple willpower check. The PC must actively reject the vision of their perfect self, which means articulating what they see and choosing to turn away from it. This creates a roleplaying moment: "I see myself as High King of the Reach, and I choose not to look."

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party enters a corridor in the Hall of Mirrors where every surface reflects slightly wrong versions of themselves, taller, stronger, more commanding. A glittering curtain descends from the ceiling, thousands of tiny mirror-winged insects catching the light. Each PC sees something different in the swarm's reflections: their deepest aspiration made visible. Ask each player what their character sees. Those who look for more than a heartbeat must resist or stand transfixed while the swarm begins its slow, precise work.

## Stat Block

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Vanity Swarm | 4 | None | 45 | fly 4 | -1 | +3 | +1 | mirror wings 2d6 slashing (all creatures in 2 space area) |
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Fascination Aura:** Any creature that can see the swarm within 6 spaces makes Will save DC 15 at start of each turn. Failure: fascinated, cannot willingly look away or move away. Repeat save at end of turn, but rolling twice and taking lower if the creature has not yet articulated (in character) what vision it sees and chosen to reject it.
> - **Selective Reflection:** Creatures with strong self-image or vanity roll twice and take lower on the Fascination save. Creatures who have lost identity to the Iron Tithe or genuinely lack vanity are immune.
> - **Mirror Sphere:** When the swarm surrounds a fascinated target (1 round after fascination), the target is blinded to the real world. All attacks against the target roll twice and take higher.
> - **Razor Landing:** A fascinated creature in the swarm's space takes 2d6 slashing damage automatically at the start of each turn. Pain is delayed by 1 round (the creature does not realize it is bleeding until it has taken damage twice).
>
> **Bloodied (half HP):** The swarm thins enough that reflections become fragmented and distorted. Fascination DC drops to 13 for the remainder of the encounter.
>
> **Weakness:** Closing eyes grants immunity to Fascination but imposes rolling twice and taking lower on attacks. Area-of-effect damage (fire, lightning, thunder) disperses the swarm for 1d4 rounds. A creature that openly declares self-rejection automatically breaks Fascination.
