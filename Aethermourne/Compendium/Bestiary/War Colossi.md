## Description

War Colossi are massive iron titans standing between forty and eighty feet tall, their bodies forged from the same divine iron that composed Kaevroth's own form. They stand in the deeper levels of the Descent like monuments, dormant and silent, their surfaces oxidized to a deep rust-red that makes them look like natural formations until the eye traces an arm, a shoulder, the suggestion of a helm. Dozens of them line the processional avenues of Levels 7 through 9, some standing sentinel at gates, others collapsed against walls where they fell during the Theomachis and never received the command to rise again.

Each Colossus bears the marks of its original purpose. Some carry siege hammers the size of towers. Others have hands shaped into battering rams or shields wide enough to shelter an army. Their faces, where faces exist, are blank iron plates with no features, no eyes, no mouth. They were not built to think or to feel. They were built to conquer, and the potential for that conquest still hums in their iron bones like a held breath.

## Behavior & Tactics

War Colossi respond to command authority. The Descent's hierarchy of power determines who can wake them: the more dominion a being holds within the Iron Descent, the more likely a Colossus will recognize their voice. The Iron Court at Level 7 commands a handful, using them as enforcers and territorial markers. The Hollow General at Level 8 controls two more. The rest, perhaps thirty or forty across the deeper levels, stand unclaimed, waiting for a voice with sufficient authority to speak the old activation phrases in Kaevroth's divine tongue.

A Colossus in motion is not a combat encounter. It is a catastrophe. They move with terrible slowness but absolute inevitability, each step cracking the iron ground, each swing of a siege weapon demolishing structures that have stood for millennia. They do not dodge. They do not defend. They advance, and what is in front of them ceases to exist. Fighting a War Colossus directly is suicide for any mortal party. The only viable strategies are evasion, redirection, or, for those who understand the Descent's power structure, seizing enough authority to countermand the Colossus's orders.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Use.** War Colossi serve two narrative functions. First, they establish scale: the Descent is not a dungeon, it is the remains of a war god's arsenal, and the Colossi are proof that Kaevroth fought battles on a scale mortals cannot comprehend. Second, they are temptation. An ambitious PC who accumulates enough power within the Descent might be able to command a Colossus. The question is what that power costs.
>
> **The Unclaimed.** The majority of War Colossi have stood dormant since the Theomachis. They remember Kaevroth's voice. If the Heart of Iron at Level 9 were ever fully activated, or if someone spoke with sufficient divine authority, every Colossus in the Descent could wake simultaneously. This is one of the Descent's most terrifying contingencies: an army of divine siege weapons with no war to fight, activated by accident or ambition.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party navigates a vast processional hall on Level 7, its ceiling lost in shadow. Iron statues line both sides, each one towering above the party, rust-covered and still. Midway through the hall, the party hears a grinding sound. One of the Colossi is moving, its head turning slowly to track a member of the Iron Court who walks below, issuing commands in a guttural language. The Colossus steps forward, shaking the ground, and begins following the courtier. The party must decide: continue through the hall while a Colossus is active, attempt to avoid its path, or follow and learn how the courtier commands it.
