## Bone-Wraith

### Description

Bone-Wraiths are the bone-pull given form, death-compulsion crystallized into something that walks. They manifest in the [[The Pale Wastes#The Marrow Fields|Marrow Fields]] where necromantic saturation is highest, assembling themselves from Morrhaelite shards and divine bone fragments held together not by joints or sinew but by raw necromantic force. They are skeletons made of black crystal, each fragment floating in precise position, held apart by gaps of visible darkness. Black frost coats their surfaces and spreads outward from their feet with each step, leaving geometric patterns on the tundra that take hours to thaw.

They make no sound. No footfall, no breath, no grinding of bone on bone. The silence around them is active, aggressive, a pocket of absolute quiet that swallows ambient noise. Wind dies within 4 spaces of a Bone-Wraith. Conversation becomes whisper. Heartbeats become the loudest thing in the world, and victims become acutely aware of each one, and of how easily they could stop.

### Behavior & Tactics

Bone-Wraiths move with slow, inexorable purpose toward a destination only they can perceive, usually the [[The Pale Wastes#Hollowdeep|Hollowdeep]]. They do not hunt. They walk. Everything in their path suffers the consequences.

Their aura projects concentrated bone-pull, the necromantic compulsion that permeates the Marrow Fields but distilled to lethal intensity. Living creatures within range feel the overwhelming urge to stand still, to stop struggling, to let the cold take them. Not despair exactly, but a terrible peace, a certainty that resistance is unnecessary because everything ends and ending is not so bad. Victims who succumb simply sit down in the frost and close their eyes. The Bone-Wraith walks past them without acknowledgment. They freeze to death within the hour.

When directly engaged, Bone-Wraiths fight with efficient, mechanical strikes, Morrhaelite shards launching from their body to impale targets before snapping back into formation. Their touch carries the cold of Morrhael's grave, necrotic damage that numbs rather than burns, that makes the wound feel like it happened to someone else.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Bone-Wraiths are Serith's scouts and shepherds. Their paths trace strategic routes between Hollowdeep and locations of military significance: supply depots, settlement approaches, defensible positions. A party that maps multiple Bone-Wraith paths will discover they form a marching route for the Hollowdeep army's eventual advance. Presenting this intelligence to [[Thessa Ironveil]] or [[Bjorn Ashken]] is campaign-significant.
>
> **Scaling.** Lone Bone-Wraiths are dangerous but manageable. Occasionally two or three walk in formation, and the overlapping bone-pull auras create a dead zone where nothing living can remain conscious for more than a minute without extraordinary willpower.

### Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is crossing a Marrow Field at dusk when the wind dies. Not gradually. Instantly. One moment the tundra wind is cutting through their cloaks; the next, silence. Absolute silence. Then they see it: a figure on the horizon, walking toward the Hollowdeep crater with the measured pace of something that has been walking for centuries and expects to walk for centuries more. Geometric frost patterns spread from its feet in spirals that are almost, but not quite, writing. It has not noticed them. But if they are in its path, it will not go around.
>
> **Complication:** A Vigil patrol is camped two hundred yards ahead, directly in the Bone-Wraith's path. They are sleeping. They do not know what is coming.

## Frostbound Hunger

### Description

Frostbound Hungers are the apex predators of the tundra, creatures that have adapted to Morrhael's broken domain by becoming something between alive and dead. They were wolves, once, or the great ice-bears of the [[The Pale Wastes#The Bitter Sea|Bitter Sea]] coast. Generations of exposure to necromantic radiation have transformed them into something new: massive, frost-sheathed predators with too many joints, eyes that burn with pale blue-white fire, and breath that crystallizes into razor-sharp particles.

They are covered in blue-white ice that grows from within rather than forming on the surface, their muscles threaded with frost-crystal that makes them stronger than any living animal their size. They trail mist wherever they walk, the cold radiating from their bodies intense enough to freeze standing water within 2 spaces. Their howls carry for miles across the tundra, and those who hear them report not fear but longing, an ache to lie down in the snow and let the cold cradle them to sleep.

### Behavior & Tactics

Frostbound Hungers hunt in coordinated packs of three to six, using the tundra's flatness and their natural mist-trail to approach from multiple directions simultaneously. They are patient predators who use their bone-pull aura, a weaker version of the Bone-Wraith's death-compulsion, to sap their prey's will to flee before closing for the kill.

Their bite carries necromantic infection: a cold that does not warm, that spreads from the wound site through the victim's body over hours. Untreated, the infection kills in three days. The victim does not decay. They freeze from within, ice replacing blood, frost threading through tissue. On the fourth day, they stand up and join the pack. The Holds cremate all Frostbound victims immediately for this reason.

The packs have grown bolder in recent months, pressing closer to Hold walls, hunting in daylight, and displaying coordination that suggests external direction. [[The Ashen Vigil]] believes Serith's influence is reaching even the wildlife near Hollowdeep, shaping them into advance scouts for the coming army.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Frostbound Hungers are the Pale Wastes' most common combat encounter and serve as an early indicator of Serith's growing influence. Their increasing boldness, coordination, and numbers track directly with the Hollowdeep army's development. A pack that attacks a Hold settlement is not random wildlife, it is a probe, testing defenses and response times.
>
> **Infection Mechanic.** The necromantic infection is a ticking clock. Bitten PCs have 3 days to find treatment (Vigil Ash-Speakers can cure it, or any radiant healing magic). Playing the infection for dread rather than punishment creates excellent tension: the PC functions normally but is slowly, visibly freezing from within.

### Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is traveling between Holds at night. The aurora pulses overhead, green and silver. Then the mist comes, rolling in from three directions simultaneously, unnatural, purposeful. Blue-white eyes appear at the mist's edge, low to the ground, circling. The howl comes, and it sounds like a lullaby, and for one terrible moment every PC wants to sit down and close their eyes. Then the first Frostbound Hunger charges from the mist, trailing ice crystals, jaws already open.
>
> **Complication:** One member of the party's escort (a Vigil scout or Hold messenger) was bitten by a Frostbound three days ago and has been hiding the infection. The frost is in their veins. They have hours. If the party does not notice and act, the escort will die mid-fight and rise as a new Hunger.

## Hollowcall

### Description

A Hollowcall is not a creature. It is an event wearing a creature's shape. It is [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s voice, filtered through [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s shattered death-domain, refined into a tool of recruitment and terror. It manifests as a distortion in the air, aurora-light where no aurora should be, frost patterns forming on flat ground in shapes that are almost words, a whispered voice that seems to come from the direction of [[The Pale Wastes#Hollowdeep|Hollowdeep]] regardless of where the listener is standing.

Sometimes it coalesces into a rough humanoid shape, ice crystals and bone-dust swirling into a figure that is almost recognizable, a dead friend, a lost commander, a family member who walked into the Marrow Fields and never came back. The shape is never quite right. The proportions shift. The face is assembled from fragments of memory rather than reality, a composite that triggers recognition without earning it.

It speaks. Not in Serith's voice (the listener would not recognize that) but in the voice of whoever the listener most needs to hear from. It says things that are true, things the listener has been afraid to admit, things about the futility of resistance, the peace of surrender, the beauty of the Hollowdeep's terrible order. It makes a compelling case. That is the horror: it is not lying.

### Behavior & Tactics

Hollowcalls appear at the edge of settlements during bone-storms, in the quiet hours before dawn, on the tundra when travelers are alone with their thoughts. They do not attack. They converse. They ask questions the listener cannot answer without confronting uncomfortable truths. They offer peace, genuinely, but the peace they offer is the peace of the grave.

Prolonged exposure, more than a few minutes of conversation, creates compulsion. The listener begins walking toward Hollowdeep, certain that something important is waiting for them there, something that will make everything make sense. This compulsion can be broken by physical restraint, loud noise, or the intervention of another person. Left unchecked, the victim walks into the Marrow Fields and does not return.

Hollowcalls cannot be killed in any conventional sense. They can be disrupted, broken apart by radiant energy, fire, or concentrated will, but they reform within hours, sometimes minutes, drawn back together by the sheer weight of Serith's attention on this region.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Hollowcalls are Serith's most sophisticated tool in the Pale Wastes, and they serve multiple functions. They recruit, drawing isolated individuals into the Hollowdeep to bolster the army. They terrify, eroding morale among the Holds' defenders. And they gather intelligence: every conversation with a Hollowcall is heard by Serith, who learns the victim's fears, loyalties, and knowledge.
>
> **Running a Hollowcall.** This is a roleplay encounter, not a combat one. The Hollowcall should feel like a conversation with someone the PC trusts. It tells truths. It asks good questions. It does not threaten. It simply, gently, makes the case that resistance is exhaustion and surrender is rest. The mechanic is secondary to the performance. If a player finds themselves genuinely persuaded, the encounter has succeeded.
>
> **Campaign Connection.** A PC who successfully resists a Hollowcall and then reports the conversation's content to [[Thessa Ironveil]] or [[Bjorn Ashken]] provides valuable intelligence: what questions did it ask? What did it seem to already know? This reveals Serith's intelligence gaps and priorities.

### Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party's camp, 3 hours past midnight. The watch-keeper sees frost forming on the ground in spiraling patterns. Then they hear a voice, quiet, familiar, coming from the dark. It sounds like someone they lost. It says their name. It says: "You look tired. Everyone here looks tired. How long can you keep this up? A year? Two? The cold does not get tired. The dead do not get tired. You are fighting time itself, and time does not lose." The figure stands just outside the firelight, almost visible, almost recognizable. It does not come closer. It does not need to.
>
> **Complication:** A Hold messenger sharing the party's camp has been having these visitations for weeks. She has not told anyone. She is close to walking. If the party notices her behavior (Perception DC 14: she sleepwalks toward Hollowdeep, she mouths words in her sleep, her breath frosts even by the fire), they can intervene. If they do not, she leaves camp the next night.

> - **Intelligence Harvest.** Everything said to a Hollowcall is heard by [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]. The Hollowcall asks probing questions (military dispositions, morale, supply levels) disguised as personal conversation.

Community (multiple creatures actively supporting each other, singing, sharing stories, holding hands) grants advantage on all Will saves against the Hollowcall. A creature that names the Hollowcall as what it truly is ("You are Serith's voice, not my friend") automatically breaks Familiar Voice for itself and all creatures within 3 spaces.
