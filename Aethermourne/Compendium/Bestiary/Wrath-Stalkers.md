## Description

Wrath-Stalkers are lean, hairless canids standing three feet at the shoulder, their rust-colored hide stretched tight over corded muscle. Their eyes glow dull orange near [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] deposits, a faint smolder that brightens when they sense prey. They move with a predator's economy, low to the ground, utterly silent on the rust-red earth of the Iron Fields. Their claws leave marks in iron-saturated stone.

They hunt exclusively in pairs. War-essence prevents larger groups: three or more stalkers in proximity turn on each other within hours, the ambient aggression overriding pack instinct. A mated pair maintains cohesion through a bond that predates the war-contamination, an older loyalty that [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] influence cannot fully erode. If one of a pair is killed, the survivor hunts alone until it starves or is killed in turn. They do not seek new mates.

Their defining trait is supernatural sensitivity to hostile intent. A Wrath-Stalker can sense aggression, fear, and combat-readiness the way a shark senses blood in water, detecting the emotional signature from hundreds of yards away. A creature feeling anger, drawing a weapon, or preparing to fight becomes visible to them across the Iron Fields like a torch in darkness. A creature that is genuinely calm, not suppressing anger but actually at peace, is effectively invisible.

## Behavior & Tactics

A hunting pair coordinates through body language so subtle that observers rarely notice the communication. One stalker provokes: a low, resonant howl that vibrates through the war-essence in the soil and triggers a surge of irrational anger in anyone who hears it. The howl exploits the ambient contamination, amplifying what [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] iron has already planted in the listener's nervous system. When the provocation succeeds and the target's emotional state spikes, the second stalker attacks from the blind side.

The only reliable defense is disciplined calm. [[The Iron Companies]] know this, and stalker territory is the final test for recruits seeking full membership. The candidate crosses a known stalker range without weapons, without armor, without fighting. Those who can maintain emotional control in the presence of a circling predator have proven they can resist [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] influence, the only qualification that truly matters in the Ironmarch.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] keeps two muzzled Wrath-Stalkers in [[Ironhaven]]'s dueling rings. They serve as crowd control: if any spectator's emotions spike too high, the stalkers lunge toward them, muzzled but terrifying, a reminder that the war-essence does not respect the boundary between the ring and the audience.
>
> [[Mira the Red]] reportedly walks through stalker territory unharmed. Her followers cite divine favor. The truth is more unsettling: Mira's connection to [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] residual will has replaced her personal emotions with something colder and more focused. She does not register as aggressive because she is no longer angry. She is purposeful. The stalkers cannot sense purpose, only rage, and Mira has moved beyond rage into something the stalkers' senses were never built to detect.
>
> **Campaign connection:** A party that can cross stalker territory without fighting has proven something about themselves that [[The Iron Companies]] and [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] will respect. It is one of the few non-monetary currencies accepted in the Ironmarch.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is crossing the Iron Fields at dusk when two shapes detach from the shadow of a fragment outcropping. Rust-colored, low to the ground, eyes glowing. They circle. One begins to howl, a sound that crawls into the chest and tightens every muscle. Will save DC 12 or feel sudden, burning anger at nothing in particular. The other stalker watches. Waits. The moment anyone draws a weapon or throws a punch, it attacks. The safe response is to stand still, breathe, let the howl wash through and fade. But the war-essence in the soil is whispering too, and every round that passes makes the calm harder to hold.
>
> **Complication:** A merchant caravan is visible half a mile ahead, under attack by a different stalker pair. The merchants are fighting, screaming, feeding the stalkers exactly what they want. The party can help, but running toward a fight means arriving angry, which means arriving as prey.

> **Resistances/Immunities:** Resistant to psychic (saturated with war-essence). Immune to fear and charm.
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Aggression Sense.** The stalker has advantage on attacks against any creature that attacked or was attacked since the start of its last turn. It has disadvantage on attacks against creatures that have not engaged in combat this encounter.
> - **Provoking Howl (1/encounter per stalker).** All creatures within 6 spaces make Will save DC 12. Failure: the target feels irrational rage and must attack the nearest creature on its next turn (friend or foe). Success: the target resists but has disadvantage on the next Will save against a howl this encounter.
> - **Paired Hunting.** When both stalkers in a pair are alive and within 4 spaces of each other, they coordinate: if one hits, the other gains advantage on its next attack against the same target.
> - **Calm Blindness.** A creature that has not attacked, been attacked, or failed a Howl save this encounter is effectively invisible to the stalker. The stalker cannot target it with attacks or abilities.
>
> **Bloodied (half HP):** The surviving stalker's howl intensifies. Provoking Howl DC increases to 14 and recharges. If its partner is dead, the stalker fights recklessly (advantage on all attacks, attacks against it also have advantage).
>
> **Weakness:** Calm negates them entirely (see Calm Blindness). Radiant damage disrupts the war-essence bond, dealing double damage. If both stalkers are reduced to half HP simultaneously, they disengage and flee.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 3-4 players of level 2-4 (encounter is always 2 stalkers)
