# Faction Relationship Matrix

*How every major faction in Aethermourne views every other. Use this to quickly determine NPC reactions, political complications, and alliance opportunities during play.*

---

## Reading the Matrix

**Relationship Key:**

| Symbol | Meaning | In Practice |
|---|---|---|
| **Allied** | Active cooperation, shared goals | Will share resources, fight together, cover for each other |
| **Friendly** | Positive relations, occasional cooperation | Will trade favors, share non-sensitive information |
| **Neutral** | No strong opinion, transactional | Will deal fairly but offer nothing extra |
| **Tense** | Friction, competing interests | Will cooperate reluctantly under pressure, otherwise obstruct |
| **Hostile** | Active opposition, open conflict | Will sabotage, arrest, or attack on sight |
| **Infiltrated** | One faction has agents inside the other | Appears friendly/neutral on the surface, secretly undermined |

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **The Serith Axis:** [[The Cinders]] and [[The Veil Unbound]] are secretly aligned through [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s influence. They coordinate indirectly through intermediaries. Both target the [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]]'s Kaevroth fragment registry. Neither faction's rank-and-file knows about the other's true allegiance.

## Detailed Faction Relationships

### [[Order of the Ashen Flame]]

| Faction | Relationship | Details |
|---|---|---|
| [[The Cinders]] | Allied (surface) | The Order treats the Cinders as loyal enforcers. They do not know [[Delric Mourne]] serves [[The Twelve#Serith\|Serith]]. |
| [[Unbound Congregation]] | Hostile | Considers the Congregation heretical and seditious. Commander [[Ashara Voss]] is pushing for a formal purge. |
| [[Concord of Tides]] | Neutral | Trade relationship only. The Order considers the Reach decadent but profitable. |
| [[The Lantern-Keepers]] | Neutral | Distant respect. The Order sees monster-hunting as beneath them but acknowledges its necessity. |
| [[The Veil Unbound]] | Hostile | Official enemy. The Order hunts Serith worshippers, though their intelligence on the Veil is poor. |
| [[The Ashen Vigil]] | Friendly | Nominal allies guarding the northern frontier. The Order sends supplies, though never enough. |
| [[The Star-Readers]] | Tense | Suspicious of the Star-Readers' neutrality and their insistence on "uncomfortable truths." |

| [[Marchwardens Council]] | Hostile | Voss is planning preemptive invasion. The Order views Marches unification as an existential threat to Dominion borders. |

### [[The Cinders]]

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Cinders are the most dangerous faction in the matrix because they appear loyal while actively sabotaging the Dominion's institutions. [[Delric Mourne]]'s inner circle (~12 members) serves [[The Twelve#Serith\|Serith]]. Rank-and-file Cinders believe they serve the Hierarch.
>
> **Secret objectives:** Secure Kaevroth fragments, weaken Yvenne's faithful, destabilize regional governments, prepare the way for Serith's return.

## Campaign Trajectory

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Act I:** Factions operate independently. The party encounters individual faction agendas. Serith's influence is background noise.
>
> **Act II:** Faction tensions escalate. The Cinders' infiltration becomes apparent. The Veil Unbound's operations intensify. Factions begin choosing sides without knowing what the sides really are.
>
> **Act III:** The Serith axis is exposed (or succeeds). Factions must forge alliances or fall. The party's relationships with individual factions determine who joins the coalition against the Second Silence.
>
> **Potential hero coalition:** Lantern-Keepers + Star-Readers + Unbound Congregation + Ashen Vigil + Remnants' Court. This alliance requires the party to bridge faction distrust.
>
> **Compromised factions that can be saved:** Concord of Tides (expose the two Tidekeepers), Marchwardens Council (stop the Cinders' rift-deepening), The Echoists (reveal Veil Unbound infiltration), Oldborn Council (resolve the Speaker crisis).
