# The Marchwardens' Council

|||
|---|---|
| **Region** | [[The Verdant Marches]] |
| **Type** | Loose Governing Body |
| **Leader** | No single leader, rotating chair. Currently a battleground between [[Kaelith Thornborn]]'s unification coalition and [[Draven Ironbark]]'s independence bloc. |
| **Alignment** | Diverse, ranges from principled to self-interested |
| **Founded** | ~300 AS (formalized; clan moots predate the Silence) |

## Description

Governing the Verdant Marches is like herding cats through a thunderstorm, and the Marchwardens' Council is the institution that tries anyway. Twelve major clan leaders gather in the ancient Moot-Hall at [[Roothold]] to debate, negotiate, threaten, posture, drink, and occasionally make decisions that affect the entire region.

In theory, the Council coordinates defense, manages trade routes, and resolves inter-clan disputes. In practice, each Marchwarden rules their own territory with near-total autonomy, and the Council is a forum for airing grievances and forming temporary alliances. Unanimous decisions are rare. Majority decisions are ignored by the losing side half the time.

But now, for the first time in centuries, the Council faces a question that cannot be tabled or deferred: **unification.** [[Kaelith Thornborn]]'s divine mandate, [[The Ashen Dominion]]'s growing aggression, and the corruption spreading through the deep Cradle have forced every Marchwarden to choose a side.

## Structure

- **Twelve Major Marchwardens**, Each represents a major clan and holds a vote on the Council. Hereditary or elected depending on clan tradition.
- **Minor Clan Observers**, Dozens of smaller clans send representatives who can speak but not vote.
- **The Moot-Chair**, A rotating position that manages Council sessions. Currently held by Marchwarden Seren Ashwood, a careful neutral trying to prevent open conflict. Ashwood is in her sixties, silver-haired, with a voice that carries without rising, and a reputation for cutting debates short with a single well-placed question.

### The Moot-Hall

The Moot-Hall is a natural amphitheater at [[Roothold]] where five of the Grandfather's root systems converge, forming a vaulted space the size of a cathedral, open to the sky through gaps in the canopy. Twelve stone seats are carved into the roots in a half-circle, each marked with a clan sigil that the roots have absorbed into their bark over centuries. The acoustics are uncanny: a speaker at the center can be heard clearly by every seat without raising their voice.

Before a Marchwarden can be seated on the Council, the land must acknowledge them through the **Rootspeaking ritual**. The candidate places both hands on the root behind their clan's seat and speaks their name. If [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s residual will accepts them, the root warms and the bark shifts to accommodate their handprint. If the root remains cold, the candidate is rejected. This has happened three times in recorded history.

Council procedure is formal but volatile. The Moot-Chair opens each session by striking a living branch against the central root-arch, and speaking continues until the branch wilts. Marchwardens speak in order of seniority. Minor clan observers may speak only when invited by the Chair. Outsiders may address the Council only if a seated Marchwarden sponsors their petition. Votes require a simple majority of seven, but losing Marchwardens are not bound to comply, a structural flaw that has paralyzed the Council for centuries.

**The Lakehouse**, a floating platform on Stillwater Lake maintained by [[Brannoc Stillwater]], is where the real decisions happen. Marchwardens meet there informally, away from Moot protocol and minor clan observers. Deals struck at the Lakehouse are never recorded. Brannoc takes a cut of every transaction.

### Current Factions within the Council

**The Thornborn Coalition (~5 Marchwardens)**
Led by [[Kaelith Thornborn]]. These Marchwardens believe unification is necessary for survival. They point to the Dominion's military buildup, the Cradle's corruption, and the Marches' inability to coordinate against large-scale threats. Kaelith's communion with the land gives their argument spiritual weight.

**The Ironbark Opposition (~4 Marchwardens)**
Led by [[Draven Ironbark]], the most powerful warden after Kaelith. Ironbark is proud, traditionalist, and deeply suspicious of anyone who claims divine authority. He argues that unification under one leader is just the Dominion's model with a different name. His supporters value clan independence above all.

**The Undecided (~3 Marchwardens)**
Their allegiance will tip the balance. Each has a price:

- **[[Senna Deepmoss]]**, Marchwarden of Clan Deepmoss in the Southern Cradle. Analytical, evidence-focused, distrusts divine mandates. She has quietly commissioned [[Vael Sundren]] to investigate the [[The Verdant Marches#The Marrow Bogs|Marrow Bog]] expansion. Her scouts have found dark roots spreading through the southern soil in spiraling patterns that do not match any known growth. If the evidence confirms an external cause, she sides with Kaelith. If not, she blocks unification. Senna does not trade in faith. She trades in data.
- **[[Torvin Ashwalker]]**, Marchwarden of Clan Ashwalker at [[Ashwalker's Post]]. A border pragmatist who cares only about defense and supply. He has been running a secret two-year trade with Captain [[Daren Kolst]] at [[Thornwall]], exchanging iron and tools for grain and [[Cosmology and Magic#Divine Materials|Ashite]] fragments through gaps in the Scarwall. Both men are committing treason for mutual survival. Torvin supports neither coalition and votes based on border needs alone.
- **[[Brannoc Stillwater]]**, Marchwarden of Clan Stillwater. A commercial pragmatist who controls the Lakehouse and takes a cut of every back-channel deal. He has been secretly selling [[Cosmology and Magic#Divine Materials|Marrow-Water]] samples to [[The Hollowed Reach]] merchants without [[The Greensingers|Greensinger]] approval. Some of those samples have passed through intermediaries into [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] hands for Serith-aligned rituals. Brannoc does not know.
- **Marchwarden Vell Roothollow**, who simply wants to see who is going to win before committing.

## Goals

- **Council (official):** Coordinate defense, manage trade, resolve disputes, maintain the fragile peace between clans.
- **Thornborn Coalition:** Pass a formal Act of Unification. Establish a unified military command. Prepare the Marches to face external threats as one nation.
- **Ironbark Opposition:** Block unification. Maintain clan sovereignty. Expose what Ironbark sees as Kaelith's dangerous ambition.
- **Undecided:** Survive. Profit. Avoid backing the wrong side.

## Strengths

- **Combined military strength.** If unified, the Marches' warriors, combined with the land's semi-sentient defenses, would be a formidable force.
- **The land itself.** The Verdant Marches' ecosystem responds to the clans' need. Forests grow to block invaders. Rivers shift to drown enemies.
- **Cultural resilience.** Marcher identity is strong. They've resisted Dominion expansion for centuries.
- **The Rootspeaking.** The land's direct participation in governance gives the Council a legitimacy no other faction can claim. A Marchwarden rejected by the roots has no seat, period.

## Weaknesses

- **Disunified.** The clans cannot coordinate effectively. In a crisis, each Marchwarden looks to their own territory first.
- **Slow to act.** Council decisions require debate, votes, and ratification by individual clans. Emergencies move faster than politics.
- **Internal rivalries.** Old blood-feuds, territorial disputes, and personal grudges make consensus nearly impossible.
- **Non-binding votes.** Losing Marchwardens are not obligated to comply with majority decisions. Seven votes pass a measure. Enforcing it against the other five is a separate problem entirely.

## Inter-Faction Relationships

- **[[The Greensingers]]**, politically neutral but spiritually authoritative. They recognized [[Kaelith Thornborn]]'s communion mark as genuine, which gave the unification argument its spiritual foundation. Several Marchwardens resent the Greensingers' influence on a question they consider political, not religious.
- **[[The Cinders]]**, tense and covert. Cinder operatives are deepening the Thornborn-Ironbark rift through a minor clan observer named **Hallen Redthorn**, who distributes fabricated intelligence about Ironbark's alleged secret negotiations with the Dominion to Thornborn's coalition, and vice versa.
- **[[The Star-Readers]]**, friendly. [[Vael Sundren]]'s research at [[Mirewatch]] has drawn Star-Reader interest in the Marrow Bog expansion. Both groups share concerns about corruption spreading through the root system.
- **[[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]]**, neutral on the surface. [[Brannoc Stillwater]]'s unauthorized Marrow-Water trade inadvertently feeds Veil operations. The Veil Unbound considers Stillwater a useful supply line and has no interest in disrupting it.

## Hooks for Player Interaction

- **Political Navigation.** PCs can work for Kaelith, Ironbark, or as neutral mediators trying to forge compromise. Swaying the undecided Marchwardens is a compelling quest line.
- **Espionage.** Both sides want intelligence on the other. The Dominion wants intelligence on both. Hallen Redthorn's fabricated intelligence is poisoning both factions, and exposing the forgeries could shift the balance.
- **The Undecided.** Each undecided Marchwarden has a problem that, if solved, would earn their gratitude and their vote: Senna needs evidence, Torvin needs border security, Brannoc needs profit, Vell needs a winner.
- **The Moot.** A formal Council session is a social encounter with the stakes of a battle. Speeches, backroom deals, the Lakehouse, and last-minute betrayals.
- **The Secret Trade.** [[Torvin Ashwalker]] and [[Captain Daren Kolst]]'s cross-border trade is technically treason on both sides. PCs who discover it face a choice: expose it (destroying the only diplomatic bridge) or protect it (earning trust from both nations).

> [!warning]- GM Notes
> Serith has no direct agents on the Council, but doesn't need them. [[The Cinders]] work through Hallen Redthorn, a minor clan observer from the Redthorn sept, who believes he is working for [[The Ashen Dominion]]'s intelligence service. He has no idea his handlers answer to [[Delric Mourne]] and, through Mourne, to Serith. The fabricated intelligence he distributes is carefully designed to confirm each side's worst assumptions about the other.
>
> Ironbark is not a villain. He has legitimate concerns about concentrated power. The best outcome for the Marches requires PCs to find a compromise that addresses both Kaelith's and Ironbark's positions, perhaps a unified military command with guaranteed clan autonomy, brokered at the Moot.
>
> **The Corruption Reaches Roothold.** The [[Roothold]] clearing edges are showing the first signs of corruption: shadow-growth among the Grandfather's outermost roots, spiraling patterns matching what [[Senna Deepmoss]]'s scouts found in the southern soil. Delegates from deep-forest settlements have begun arriving at the Moot changed, frightened, reporting phenomena they cannot explain, or in two cases, not arriving at all. When the corruption reaches the Moot-Hall itself, the Rootspeaking ritual may begin rejecting seated Marchwardens whose territories have been compromised.
>
> **The Ashwalker-Kolst Bridge.** [[Torvin Ashwalker]] and [[Captain Daren Kolst]]'s secret trade is the only reliable cross-border communication channel. If the Scarwall closes entirely or war breaks out, this relationship becomes strategically irreplaceable. PCs who discover and protect it gain leverage with both nations. PCs who expose it destroy the most important diplomatic asset in the setting.
