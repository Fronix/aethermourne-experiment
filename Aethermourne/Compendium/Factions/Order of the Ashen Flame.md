# The Order of the Ashen Flame

|||
|---|---|
| **Region** | [[The Ashen Dominion]] |
| **Type** | Military-Religious Order |
| **Leader** | [[Ashara Voss|Commander Ashara Voss]] |
| **Alignment** | Lawful, authoritarian, dogmatic, but genuinely believe they serve the greater good |
| **Founded** | ~50 AS (formalized from the Dominion's earliest temple guards) |

## Description

The Order of the Ashen Flame is the beating heart of the Ashen Dominion's power, its army, its priesthood, and its law enforcement fused into a single iron-disciplined institution. When the Hierarch speaks, the Order acts. When borders are threatened, the Order marches. When heresy festers, the Order burns it out.

They are not evil. That is what makes them dangerous. The men and women of the Order believe, fervently, sincerely, that [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]]'s divine mandate demands rigid hierarchy, unwavering obedience, and the suppression of chaos in all its forms. The caste system isn't cruelty to them; it is sacred geometry. The Hierarch's authority isn't tyranny; it is Solvaen's will made manifest.

[[Ashara Voss|Commander Ashara Voss]] is a career soldier in her late fifties, iron-haired and iron-willed, who rose through the ranks on merit despite the caste system's constraints. She is the most competent military mind in the Dominion, and also its most dangerous hawk. Voss sees [[The Verdant Marches]]' unification movement as an existential threat and has been quietly building the case for a preemptive invasion. She doesn't trust [[The Cinders|the Cinders]], doesn't respect the Hierarch's indecision, and doesn't suffer fools.

## Structure

The Order operates on a strict military hierarchy:

- **Commander**, Supreme authority ([[Ashara Voss]]). Reports only to the Hierarch.
- **Flame Captains**, Regional commanders, each overseeing a major garrison or campaign.
- **Flame Sergeants**, Unit leaders in the field.
- **Embers**, Rank-and-file soldiers. The term is both a title and a reminder: they are the sparks of Solvaen's flame.

Parallel to the military chain, the Order also includes **Ashen Speakers**, priests who perform religious rites, administer sacraments, bless troops, and oversee doctrinal compliance within the Order's ranks. Speakers answer to both their Flame Captain and the Church hierarchy, a dual-reporting structure that creates occasional friction.

### Military Infrastructure

The Order's power is concentrated in three key installations:

- **The Command Citadel** at [[Cinderholm]], the innermost ring of the fortress-city and [[Ashara Voss]]'s headquarters. The Citadel houses the war council chambers, the Ashite armory, and the Order's strategic archives.
- **Ashguard Barracks**, also at [[Cinderholm]]. Home to 3,000 elite heavy infantry equipped with [[Cosmology and Magic#Divine Materials|Ashite]]-forged weapons and armor, the Order's most devastating fighting force.
- **Scarwall Watch Towers**, a chain of fortified posts along the [[The Ashen Dominion#The Scar|Scarwall]] border, manned by rotating Order garrisons under [[Captain Daren Kolst]]'s operational command at [[Thornwall]].

### Notable Officers

- **Flame Captain Varen Ashfeld**, commander of the Ashguard. A hawk who shares Voss's appetite for a preemptive strike against the Marches. He trains the Ashguard for offensive operations and considers defensive garrison work a waste of the Order's best soldiers.
- **Flame Captain Sera Ironholt**, commander of the northern garrisons facing [[The Pale Wastes]]. A moderate who has fought undead incursions for twenty years and counsels diplomacy with the Marches so the Order can focus on the real threat from the north.
- **Flame Captain Aldren Voss** (no relation to the Commander), garrison commander at [[Greyfield]]. Sympathetic to the [[Unbound Congregation]]'s caste reform arguments. He has quietly declined to enforce three arrest warrants for Congregation preachers in his territory, citing insufficient evidence.
- **Captain Maren Dulwick**, Voss's adjutant and off-books intelligence handler at [[Cinderholm]]. Loyal to Voss personally, not the Church. Runs the Commander's quiet intelligence operation parallel to [[The Cinders]].
- **Quartermaster Borin Thell**, manages [[Cinderholm]]'s supply lines. Has noticed [[Cosmology and Magic#Divine Materials|Ashite]] shipment irregularities, consignments diverted or delayed by [[The Cinders|Cinder]] intermediaries, and keeps meticulous records of every discrepancy.

## Religious Practices

Every Order garrison begins and ends the day with the Flame Affirmation, spoken in unison at dawn and dusk: *"The bones hold. The flame endures. The order stands."* The ritual involves an Ashen Speaker lighting a brazier of Ashite-dusted kindling, the flame burning white-gold with Solvaen's residual divine heat. Soldiers kneel, press their fists to the ground, and speak the words while the flame burns. The practice is both devotion and discipline, a reminder that the divine architecture they serve is literal, not metaphorical.

Before battle or patrol, Ashen Speakers perform the Flame Blessing: tracing geometric patterns on soldiers' foreheads with Ashite-ink, speaking Solvaen's name, and pressing a heated coin to the soldier's breastplate. The coin-mark is considered sacred. Veterans' armor bears dozens of overlapping burn circles.

In the lower-caste districts of [[Cinderholm]] and [[Greyfield]], counter-graffiti has begun appearing on walls and barracks doors: *"The bones are cracking. The flame is guttering. The order is a lie."* The Cinders attribute this to the [[Unbound Congregation]]. The Order's leadership has ordered the graffiti scrubbed, but it reappears faster than it can be removed.

## Goals

- **Maintain Solvaen's order** across the Dominion, enforce the law, uphold the caste system, protect the faithful
- **Guard the borders** against external threats: the Verdant Marches to the west, [[The Pale Wastes]]' undead incursions to the north
- **Preserve the theocratic system** and the Hierarch's absolute authority
- **Commander Voss specifically:** Launch a preemptive war against the Verdant Marches before [[Kaelith Thornborn]] unifies the clans into a genuine rival power

## Strengths

- **Size and discipline.** The largest standing army on the continent. Well-equipped, well-trained, and fanatically loyal.
- **Ashite-enhanced equipment.** The Order's elite units carry weapons and armor forged with Ashite, remnants of Solvaen's divine fire. These burn hotter, cut deeper, and resist corruption.
- **Infrastructure.** The Command Citadel at [[Cinderholm]], the Ashguard Barracks, the Scarwall Watch Towers, and a network of garrison forts connected by the Dominion's military roads.
- **Legitimacy.** The common people of the Dominion view the Order as protectors, not oppressors (mostly).

## Weaknesses

- **Rigid doctrine.** The Order cannot adapt quickly. Unconventional threats, infiltration, corruption, guerrilla warfare, frustrate their conventional tactics.
- **Dependent on a weakening Hierarch.** The current Hierarch is old, paranoid, and increasingly isolated. If he falls, the Order's chain of command fractures.
- **Blind to the Cinders' betrayal.** The Order trusts [[The Cinders]] as fellow servants of the Dominion. They have no idea [[Delric Mourne|Mourne]] answers to Serith.
- **Strained morale.** Internal tensions between hawks (Ashfeld, Voss) and moderates (Ironholt), combined with the [[Unbound Congregation]]'s growing influence among lower-caste Embers, are eroding unit cohesion. Flame Captain Aldren Voss's quiet refusal to enforce Congregation arrest warrants has not gone unnoticed by the Cinders.
- **Caste fracture.** The tension is structural, not just ideological. Lower-caste soldiers do the hardest fighting and receive the fewest promotions. The counter-graffiti appearing in garrison districts reflects a disillusionment that the Flame Affirmation can no longer paper over.

## Inter-Faction Relationships

- **[[The Ashen Vigil]]**, nominal allies. The Order sends supply shipments to the Pale Wastes, never enough, and Flame Captain Ironholt maintains a professional correspondence with Commander [[Thessa Ironveil]].
- **[[The Star-Readers]]**, tense. The Order dislikes uncomfortable truths about the Aetheric Web's condition. [[The Cinders]] actively discredit Star-Reader findings that contradict Church doctrine, and the Order does not question these dismissals.
- **[[The Ember Guild]]**, neutral and commercial. The Order purchases Ashite-forged equipment through Guild channels. [[Quartermaster Borin Thell]]'s supply irregularities suggest someone is diverting Ashite through these same channels.

## Hooks for Player Interaction

- **Employers:** The Order hires mercenaries and specialists for tasks its rigid structure can't handle, border scouting, monster extermination, diplomatic missions.
- **Obstacles:** PCs working with the [[Unbound Congregation]] or the Verdant Marches will find the Order blocking their path at every turn.
- **Enemies:** If PCs openly defy the Hierarch, the Order will hunt them.
- **Uneasy Alliance:** Voss is a pragmatist beneath her ideology. If PCs can prove a greater threat (Serith, the Second Silence), she might cooperate, grudgingly, temporarily, and on her terms.
- **The Ashite Question:** [[Quartermaster Borin Thell]]'s records document missing shipments. Investigating the supply chain leads to the [[The Cinders|Cinders]]' diversion network and potentially to [[Delric Mourne]] himself.

> [!warning]- GM Notes
> Voss is not compromised by Serith. She is exactly what she appears to be: a loyal, competent, dangerous soldier with bad politics and good intentions. Her preemptive war plan is genuinely her own idea, and Serith's agents in [[The Cinders]] are quietly encouraging it, because a Dominion at war is a Dominion too distracted to notice the rot within.
>
> If the PCs can redirect Voss's aggression toward the real enemy, she becomes one of the most powerful allies in the campaign. But convincing her requires proof, not words.
>
> **The Ashite Warning System.** [[Prior Gault Ashworth]]'s private journal at [[Crown's Reach]] documents anomalous Ashite behavior: the Hum's pitch shifting, pilgrims returning with harder-edged certainties, Ashite veins pulsing in unfamiliar patterns. These are early symptoms of [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s prison weakening that the Order cannot contextualize. If Ashite weapons begin behaving erratically near [[Cosmology and Magic#Ley Lines and the Aetheric Web|Aetheric Web]] destabilization points, the Order's equipment becomes both their greatest strength and an early warning system they don't know they have.
>
> **Pale Sentry Connection.** Undead emerging from the northern border have been seen wearing remnants of Ashen Flame armor, soldiers lost to [[The Pale Wastes]] incursions who have risen wearing the Order's own colors. This suggests Serith's influence is reaching into the Order's material legacy, turning their honored dead into weapons against them.
>
> **The Flame Captain Split.** Ashfeld wants war. Ironholt wants diplomacy. Aldren Voss wants reform. This three-way tension within the officer corps mirrors the Dominion's larger fracture and gives PCs multiple entry points for influencing the Order's direction.
