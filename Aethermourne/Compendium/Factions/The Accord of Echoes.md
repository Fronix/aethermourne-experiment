# The Accord of Echoes

|||
|---|---|
| **Region** | [[The Shattered Edge]] |
| **Type** | Governing Body |
| **Leader** | [[Tyrell Vane]], Senior Edgewalker |
| **Alignment** | Pragmatic, consensus-oriented, survival-focused |
| **Founded** | ~150 years ago, when the Shattered Edge's scattered survivors first organized |

## Description

The Accord of Echoes is the governing tradition of [[The Shattered Edge]], and it is unlike any other system of governance in Aethermourne. It does not rely on bloodlines, elections, wealth, or military power. It relies on probability.

The Accord is led by Edgewalkers, individuals who can perceive the faint probability threads that bleed through from the Maelstrom, the ghostly outlines of possible futures overlaying the present. When a decision must be made, whether it concerns trade policy, surge response, territorial disputes, or resource allocation, the Edgewalkers convene and read the threads. They examine the likely consequences of each option across multiple potential timelines, weighing not what *will* happen, but what *could* happen, and how likely each outcome is.

This is not prophecy. Edgewalkers do not see *the* future. They see many futures, contradictory and shifting, and they assess their relative weight. The skill is interpretive, not visionary. Two Edgewalkers reading the same threads may reach different conclusions, and debate between them is not only accepted but essential. The Accord's decisions emerge from the consensus of multiple readers, each offering their interpretation, each challenged by the others.

## Structure

- **Senior Edgewalker**: The individual whose probability-reading is considered most reliable and whose judgment has been most consistently validated. Currently [[Tyrell Vane]]. The title is not hereditary or elected; it is earned through demonstrated accuracy over years of service.
- **The Circle**: A council of five to eight active Edgewalkers who convene for major decisions. Membership is fluid, based on availability, health, and current perceptual clarity (proximity to the Maelstrom can temporarily sharpen or blur probability-sight).
- **Settlement Representatives**: Non-Edgewalker leaders from each settlement who present concerns, propose questions, and ensure that probability-reading is applied to real problems rather than abstract speculation. [[Kessa Thornwall]] represents [[Edgewatch]]. [[Oren Blackfinger]] represents [[Riftmarket]]'s merchant interests, though he would deny being political.
- **The Echoing**: An annual gathering where all Edgewalkers, active and retired, convene at [[Riftmarket]] to read the broadest possible set of probability threads. The results shape the Accord's long-term priorities. The last Echoing produced unsettling readings that [[Tyrell Vane]] has not fully shared.

## Goals

- **Survival of the Edge's people** through Maelstrom surge management, resource allocation, and inter-settlement coordination
- **Protection of the Changed** from external prejudice, particularly from [[The Ashen Dominion]]'s proposals to quarantine or "cure" them
- **Understanding the Maelstrom** through support of [[The Last Certainty]]'s research and collaboration with [[The Star-Readers]]
- **Maintaining independence** from both the Dominion and [[The Verdant Marches]], neither of which considers the Edge a legitimate polity

## Strengths

- **Probability-informed decisions** give the Accord a significant advantage in crisis management. Surge responses, trade negotiations, and resource planning benefit from glimpses of likely outcomes.
- **Legitimacy through results.** The Accord has kept the Edge's people alive for a century and a half. That record earns trust.
- **Flexibility.** The system adapts naturally, since probability-reading responds to changing conditions in real time.

## Weaknesses

- **Edgewalkers are rare.** True probability-sight is uncommon even among the Changed, and it degrades with age or overuse.
- **The threads are unreliable.** Probability is not certainty. Bad readings lead to bad decisions, and the Accord has made costly mistakes.
- **Outsiders distrust it.** To the [[The Ashen Dominion|Dominion]], governance by chaos-touched visionaries is barely distinguishable from governance by madness.
- **The readings are worsening.** As the Maelstrom widens, the probability threads become denser, more contradictory, harder to interpret. Several Edgewalkers have reported threads that seem to *resist* being read, as if something is deliberately obscuring certain futures.

## Hooks for Player Interaction

- **Consultation.** The PCs may be brought before the Circle to have their intentions read, a deeply unsettling experience in which Edgewalkers describe possible futures the PCs have not yet chosen.
- **The Unseen Threads.** [[Tyrell Vane]] may recruit PCs to investigate futures that the probability threads refuse to show. Something is blocking the Edgewalkers' sight, and that absence is itself information.
- **The Last Echoing.** The most recent Echoing's results included a thread that all Edgewalkers saw identically, an extreme rarity. They refuse to describe it publicly. Vane may share it with PCs he trusts.

> [!warning]- GM Notes
> The thread that all Edgewalkers saw at the last Echoing was a convergence: every probability line, regardless of starting conditions, collapsed into the same outcome. The Maelstrom opening fully. Not expanding, *opening*, like a door being thrown wide. What waits on the other side was not visible, but the emotional resonance of the thread was overwhelming, a sense of something vast, patient, and intentional.
>
> The threads that resist being read are those connected to [[The Twelve#Serith, Goddess of Shadow and Secrets , *"The Veiled One"*|Serith]]'s plans. Her domain of shadow and secrets extends to concealing futures from probability-readers. The Edgewalkers are effectively blind to the most dangerous thread in the world, and [[Tyrell Vane]] knows it.
