# The Confluence Council

|||
|---|---|
| **Region** | [[The Greymere Fenlands]] |
| **Type** | Advisory Body / Ley-Line Monitoring Network |
| **Leader** | No formal leader. [[Old Fenris]] is the eldest and most respected member. |
| **Alignment** | Neutral, practical survivalists |
| **Founded** | ~2172 AS (approximately 30 years ago) |

## Description

The Confluence Council is not a government. Its members will tell you this repeatedly, with varying degrees of irritation, because outsiders keep treating it like one. The Council is a network of ley-line readers, individuals with the sensitivity to detect fluctuations in the Aetheric Web, who share their readings with [[The Greymere Fenlands|fenland]] communities so that people can prepare for surges, avoid dangerous zones, and harvest ley-energy safely. They advise. They warn. They do not command.

In practice, the Council is the closest thing the fenlands have to a governing institution, because when someone says "a ley surge will destroy your settlement in three hours," people tend to listen. The Council's authority is entirely practical: they have information that keeps people alive, and that information gives them influence whether they want it or not. Most of them do not want it. [[Old Fenris]], the Council's founder, set the precedent by refusing any title, any salary, and any formal power. The Council follows his example, advising freely and accepting whatever food, shelter, or goodwill communities choose to offer in return.

## Structure

The Council has no hierarchy, no charter, and no membership requirements beyond demonstrated ability to read ley-line fluctuations. In practice, this means:

- **Readers**, the full members. Individuals who can sense and interpret the Aetheric Web's activity. Most are self-taught Fencrafters. Some have formal magical training from other traditions. There are currently eleven Readers spread across the fenlands.
- **Watchers**, volunteers without ley-sensitivity who maintain observation posts at known surge points and report mundane signs of ley-activity (water colour changes, wildlife behaviour, air pressure shifts) to the nearest Reader.
- **Runners**, messengers who carry surge warnings between settlements. The fastest communication network in the fenlands, faster than any barge on the Sluice.

The Council convenes formally on the Council Platform in [[Fenholm]] four times a year for seasonal readings, comprehensive surveys of the Aetheric Web's state across the region. Emergency sessions are called when a Reader detects anomalous activity. Between sessions, Readers operate independently in their home communities.

## Goals

- **Monitor the Aetheric Web.** Track ley-line activity, predict surges, and provide early warnings to fenland communities.
- **Preserve fen independence.** The Council's practical authority is the fenlands' only alternative to Dominion governance. If the Council fails, the argument for outside intervention becomes much harder to resist.
- **Understand Nethys's fragments.** Several Readers, [[Old Fenris]] foremost among them, believe the ley-line surges carry information from [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]]'s shattered consciousness. Understanding that information could be the key to stabilizing the region.
- **Train new Readers.** Ley-sensitivity appears in roughly one in a hundred fenland residents. The Council identifies and trains these individuals, both for practical necessity and to build the network's capacity.

## Strengths

- **Essential service.** The Council provides ley-line warnings that save lives. This gives them goodwill and influence that no other faction in the fenlands can match.
- **Regional coverage.** Eleven Readers spread across the fenlands, backed by Watchers and Runners, create a monitoring network that covers most of the inhabited region.
- **Nethys connection.** The Council's Readers are the people most attuned to Nethys's fragments. If anyone can interpret the goddess's broken messages, it is them.

## Weaknesses

- **No enforcement power.** The Council advises. If a community ignores their warnings, there is no mechanism to compel compliance. This has gotten people killed.
- **Overreliance on [[Old Fenris]].** The founder is the most experienced Reader by decades. His readings are the benchmark against which all others are measured. His deteriorating health and lucidity threaten the Council's credibility.
- **Internal disagreement.** The Readers disagree about Nethys's fragments: are they inert residue, a failing consciousness, or something actively trying to communicate? This dispute paralyzes the Council on questions that go beyond simple surge prediction.
- **No external recognition.** Neither [[The Ashen Dominion]] nor [[The Hollowed Reach]] acknowledges the Council as a legitimate governing body. To outside powers, the fenlands remain lawless territory.
- **The Second Silence.** As ley-line activity intensifies, the Council's ability to predict surges is failing. Surges are arriving faster, stronger, and in patterns that don't match historical data. The Readers are stretched thin and increasingly frightened.

## Relationships with Other Factions

- **[[Maren Duskwater]]** , the Fenmother works closely with the Council, attending seasonal readings and incorporating their warnings into settlement planning. She is the Council's most important political ally.
- **[[The Star-Readers]]** , the scholarly order has a small presence in [[Fenholm]] and shares information with the Council when it suits them. The relationship is cordial but asymmetric: the Star-Readers see the Council as useful amateurs, the Council sees the Star-Readers as academics who don't understand the fen.
- **[[The Ember Guild]]** , the Guild's [[Fenholm]] outpost trades with Council Watchers and provides metalwork for observation post maintenance. The relationship is purely transactional.
- **[[The Ashen Dominion]]** , no formal relationship. The Dominion does not acknowledge the Council's existence. [[Captain Hale Ashmark]] has privately consulted Council Readers about ley-activity near [[Ashcreek]], which he has not reported to his superiors.
- **[[The Veil Unbound|The Veil Unbound]]** , no known contact, but the cult's presence in the fenlands concerns the Council. Ley-line readers have detected anomalous activity near [[The Drowning Bridge]] that doesn't match natural surge patterns.

## Hooks for Player Interaction

- **Surge Warnings.** PCs travelling the fenlands need the Council's information to survive. A Reader who agrees to guide them is an invaluable (and opinionated) companion.
- **The Fenris Question.** As Fenris deteriorates, the Council faces a succession crisis. Jessa Thornhollow is competent but lacks Fenris's connection to Nethys's fragments. PCs may be drawn into the question of who leads the Council, or whether it can survive without a leader.
- **Anomalous Readings.** The Council has detected ley patterns it can't explain, activity near [[Sporeling]] that suggests someone is manipulating the Web intentionally. They need investigators.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Council's biggest vulnerability is what they don't know: that [[Brother Lumen]]'s experiment in [[Sporeling]] is actively manipulating the ley lines they monitor. The anomalous readings the Council has detected are the side effects of Lumen channelling Nethys fragments through the fungal network. If the Council realized what was happening, they would be forced to act, but they have no enforcement mechanism and no one willing to confront a man doing what half of them secretly wish they could.
>
> Gareth Mirewalker, the former Calculist, is the Reader most likely to identify Lumen's work. He has the structured analytical training to distinguish intentional manipulation from natural surge patterns. He hasn't connected the dots yet because he trusts the Star-Readers and assumes Lumen is conducting sanctioned research. If the PCs point him in the right direction, he will figure it out quickly.
