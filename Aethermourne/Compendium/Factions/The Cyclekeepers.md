# The Cyclekeepers

|||
|---|---|
| **Region** | [[The Thornwild]] |
| **Type** | Druidic-Priestly Order |
| **Leader** | [[Yara Thornbloom]], Senior Cyclekeeper |
| **Alignment** | Neutral, serve the Cycle's balance and the communities that depend on it |
| **Founded** | ~2140 AS, when the first Marcher and Wastes exiles began cooperating to survive |

## Description

The Thornwild should kill everyone who enters it. The Cycle, that ceaseless churn of death and growth driven by the warring legacies of [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]] and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]], does not pause for human convenience. Death Tides raise the fallen and send undead ecology surging through the forest. Bloom Surges drown the world in aggressive, suffocating growth. The transitions between them are violent, unpredictable, and often lethal.

The Cyclekeepers are the reason anyone survives here at all.

Founded by the first generation of exiles who refused to die quietly, the order fuses two traditions that have no business coexisting. [[The Greensingers|Greensinger]] primal communion reads [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s growth-pulse in the soil. [[The Ashen Vigil|Vigil]] Ash-Speaker funeral rites sense [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s death-pull in the bones. A trained Cyclekeeper feels both simultaneously, reading the Cycle's rhythms the way a sailor reads weather. They call warnings before Death Tides hit. They guide communities to shelter during Bloom Surges. They perform the dual rites, growth-blessing and death-honoring, that keep the Thornwild's people sane in a landscape that defies sanity.

### The Dual Rites

A **growth-blessing** begins with the Cyclekeeper pressing bare hands into the soil, drawing up Thyrea's pulse. The air warms. Green light threads through the Keeper's fingers and spreads outward in branching patterns like veins. Onlookers feel a flush of vitality, a sudden awareness of their own heartbeat, the smell of rain on living wood. Seeds germinate. Wounds close. For a few moments, the Thornwild feels like the Verdant Marches must have before the world broke.

A **death-honoring** is its mirror. The Keeper kneels, places a hand on the deceased (or on bare stone if there is no body), and opens themselves to Morrhael's pull. The temperature drops. Frost traces geometric patterns outward from the Keeper's hand. The air tastes of cold iron. Those present feel a quiet certainty settle over them, not grief, but acceptance, a bone-deep understanding that endings are real and necessary.

Performed simultaneously, these rites are the Cyclekeepers' defining practice. The Keeper channels both pulses at once, one hand warm and green, the other cold and grey, and holds them in tension. The sensation for onlookers is indescribable and unforgettable: a feeling of being fully alive and fully mortal at the same time, aware of every breath as both a gift and a countdown. The dual rite is performed at births, deaths, harvests, and transitions between Cycle phases. It is what keeps the Thornwild's people grounded when the world around them cannot decide whether to grow or die.

[[Yara Thornbloom]], the Senior Cyclekeeper, is the most gifted Cycle-reader alive. She was born a Marcher, trained as a [[The Greensingers|Greensinger]], and walked into the Thornwild thirty years ago because she could feel something in the northwest that no one else could hear. She never went back. Her dual awareness, the ability to feel growth and decay at the same time, makes her indispensable. It is also destroying her body, aging and de-aging her with the Cycle's phases until she is sometimes ancient, sometimes young, and always exhausted.

## Structure

The Cyclekeepers have no formal hierarchy beyond seniority of skill. Rank is determined by **Cycle-depth**, how accurately and how far in advance a Keeper can read the Cycle's shifts.

- **Senior Cyclekeeper**, the deepest reader. Currently [[Yara Thornbloom]].
- **Full Keepers**, can read the Cycle hours in advance and perform dual rites. Roughly a dozen active.
  - [[Kael Duskborne]]: Former [[Major Factions#Ashen Vigil|Ashen Vigil]], left after witnessing purposeful undead. Commands [[The Thornwild#Cyclethorn|Cyclethorn]]'s Thornguard militia. Receiving coded messages from a Vigil contact about Hollowdeep organized undead expanding westward.
  - [[Root-Speaker Fennara]]: Leads [[The Thornwild#Rootpyre|Rootpyre]]'s heretical druidic order. Declared anathema by the [[Major Factions#Greensingers|Greensingers]]. Has entered transition state three times and experienced visions of Thyrea and Morrhael as pre-Theomachis collaborators.
  - Watcher Fell: Lone Cyclekeeper posted at [[The Thornwild#Bone Garden|Bone Garden]]. Responsible for monitoring the Wilt border.
- **Apprentices**, learning to feel both divine pulses. Most wash out. The dual awareness is psychologically punishing.

## Goals

- **Maintain the balance.** If either [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s or [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s influence overwhelms the other, the Thornwild becomes lethal for everyone in it and everyone near it.
- **Protect the communities.** The four Thornwild settlements depend on Cyclekeeper warnings and guidance:
  - [[The Thornwild#Cyclethorn|Cyclethorn]] (~2,000): Largest settlement. Contains The Perch (Cyclekeeper HQ). Pre-Theomachis stone foundation detected beneath the granite outcrop.
  - [[The Thornwild#Bone Garden|Bone Garden]] (~1,200): Cycle-material harvesting at Wilt border. Skeletal trees are Theomachis flash-kill remains holding trace divine memories.
  - [[The Thornwild#Ashbloom|Ashbloom]] (~800, half Remnant): Only place Remnants experience restoration during the life-phase. Effect weakening.
  - [[The Thornwild#Rootpyre|Rootpyre]] (~600): Marcher exile settlement. Dual-channeling practices combining [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]] and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]] attunement, a rediscovery of pre-Theomachis complementary theology.
- **Understand the acceleration.** The Cycle is speeding up. Transitions are getting shorter and more violent. The Cyclekeepers need to know why before it kills everyone.
- **Study [[The Bloom-Wraith]].** The entity in the Bonewood exists in permanent transition. If the Cyclekeepers could understand how, they might learn to stabilize the Cycle itself.

## Strengths

- **Dual awareness.** No other tradition in Aethermourne can read both growth and death energies. In the Thornwild, this is the difference between life and a very complicated form of death.
- **Community trust.** The settlements depend on the Cyclekeepers and know it. Their authority is earned daily.
- **Unique knowledge.** Decades of observation have given the Keepers an understanding of divine energy interaction that [[The Star-Readers]] would pay dearly for.

## Weaknesses

- **Tiny.** Fewer than twenty members total. The dual awareness required is rare, the training brutal, the attrition high.
- **Isolated.** Both [[The Greensingers]] and [[The Ashen Vigil]] view them with suspicion. The Greensingers call their death-rites a perversion. The Vigil calls their growth-communion naive.
- **[[Yara Thornbloom]]'s deterioration.** The Senior Cyclekeeper is the order's lynchpin. Her body is failing under the strain of dual awareness. There is no clear successor.
- **The Cycle itself.** If the acceleration continues, even the Cyclekeepers' readings will become too slow to save anyone.

## Inter-Faction Relationships

- **[[Major Factions#Greensingers|Greensingers]]:** Complicated. [[Elder Rosk]] mentored [[Root-Speaker Fennara]] as a Greensinger, then declared her anathema for dual-channeling. Envoy Lael at [[The Thornwild#Rootpyre|Rootpyre]] is conflicted about whether dual-channeling is truly heresy or a necessary evolution.
- **[[Major Factions#Ashen Vigil|Ashen Vigil]]:** [[Kael Duskborne]] deserted from the Vigil. [[Thessa Ironveil]] listed him as a deserter but has not enforced his return. Vigil intelligence about Hollowdeep flows to the Cyclekeepers through Kael's coded correspondence.
- **[[Major Factions#Star-Readers|Star-Readers]]:** Research interest at [[The Thornwild#Cyclethorn|Cyclethorn]]. Would pay dearly for Cyclekeeper knowledge of divine energy interaction, knowledge the Cyclekeepers are reluctant to share with outsiders.
- **[[Major Factions#Ember Guild|Ember Guild]]:** Smell profit in Thornwild amber. An Ember Guild agent has infiltrated a Star-Reader expedition to the region. If the Guild gains access, they could strip-mine the Threshold and destroy the Cycle's balance.
- **[[Remnants' Court]]:** [[Revenant Yael]] and [[Revenant Moss]] share complicated solidarity. Both are Remnant leaders who care about their people, but they disagree sharply on method.
- **Political isolation:** No relationships with any major continental factions. Completely alone. The Thornwild is too remote, too strange, and too dangerous for anyone to care about until it is too late.

## Hooks for Player Interaction

- **Guides and warnings.** Cyclekeepers are essential allies for any party entering the Thornwild. Without their guidance, survival is measured in hours.
- **The acceleration mystery.** Why is the Cycle speeding up? The Cyclekeepers will pay, beg, or bargain for anyone willing to investigate.
- **Dual-rite training.** A PC with connections to either primal or death magic might attempt Cyclekeeper training, a harrowing experience that forces confrontation with both life and death simultaneously.
- **[[The Bloom-Wraith]] expedition.** The Cyclekeepers need someone brave or foolish enough to enter the Bonewood and make contact with the Bloom-Wraith. They cannot spare their own, and they are running out of time.

> [!warning]- GM Notes
> The Cyclekeepers' dual awareness makes them uniquely sensitive to [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s interference, but they don't know that is what they're sensing. [[Yara Thornbloom]] feels "a third pulse" in the Cycle, something that is neither growth nor death, and it terrifies her. She hasn't told anyone because she doesn't know how to explain it, and because admitting a third force exists would mean the Cycle is more complex than anyone imagined.
>
> The third pulse is Serith's shadow-influence, seeping through both divine legacies simultaneously. If the Cyclekeepers could identify it, they would become one of the most important intelligence assets in the war against the Second Silence. Serith knows this. The Thornwild's isolation is the only reason she hasn't acted against them yet.
>
> **[[The Bloom-Wraith]]'s Secret.** The Bloom-Wraith carries fused shards of both [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s divine consciousness. It knows what Serith is doing. It is trying to warn the Cyclekeepers through fragmentary communication, visions during transition states, patterns in the Cycle's rhythm that spell out warnings if anyone can read them. Contact with the Bloom-Wraith is the fastest path to identifying the third pulse.
>
> **Rootpyre's Threat to Serith.** Rootpyre's dual-channeling harmonizes life and death energies rather than letting them war. Harmonized energy resists Serith's manipulation, which depends on exploiting the tension between the two divine legacies. Serith considers Rootpyre a threat. Expect interference: subtle at first (accelerating the Cycle near Rootpyre, increasing Wilt encroachment), direct later (Veil Unbound agents, organized undead probes from the Wastes).
>
> **If the Cycle Breaks.** Two catastrophic outcomes. If [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]] overwhelms: a Bloom Zone, uncontrolled growth spreading outward from the Thornwild into neighboring regions, consuming everything. If [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]] overwhelms: a Death Zone, a second Hollowdeep, undead ecology expanding unchecked. Both are continental-scale disasters. The Cyclekeepers are the only thing preventing either.
