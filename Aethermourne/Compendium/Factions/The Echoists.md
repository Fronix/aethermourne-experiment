# The Echoists

|||
|---|---|
| **Region** | [[The Shattered Edge]] (primary), with adherents across Aethermourne |
| **Type** | Philosophical-Religious Movement |
| **Leader** | [[Prior Echoes]] |
| **Alignment** | Peaceful, transformative, bordering on radical acceptance |
| **Founded** | ~80 years ago, formalized from older traditions of Vorrhyn-remembrance |

## Description

The Echoists are what remains of [[The Twelve#Vorrhyn, God of Chaos and Change , *"The Unbound"*|Vorrhyn]]'s faith, not a church, not a cult, but a way of living that honors the god of change by embracing transformation in all its forms. They teach that the Maelstrom is not a wound but a gift, the last trace of the god who kept the world from stagnation. That the Changed are not damaged but *continued*, carrying forward Vorrhyn's legacy in their altered bodies and shifted perceptions. That change itself is sacred, and that resisting it is the true catastrophe.

It is a comforting philosophy for people the world has broken. It is also, in the wrong hands, a justification for terrible things.

The movement occupies a complex space in Edge society. Most residents respect it. Many draw comfort from it. The [[The Accord of Echoes|Accord of Echoes]] tolerates it, recognizing its value in maintaining community cohesion among the Changed. But the Echoists' practice of conducting rituals at the Maelstrom's edge, deliberately inviting transformation, troubles even sympathetic observers. [[Prior Echoes]] teaches that these rituals are acts of communion, conversations with the echo of a dead god. Critics call them suicide dressed in theology.

The truth, as always on the Shattered Edge, is somewhere in between.

## Beliefs

- **Change is sacred.** Stasis is death. The world's decline is a direct consequence of Vorrhyn's absence, and every act of transformation, chosen or unchosen, is a small rebellion against that decline.
- **The Changed are blessed.** Not cursed, not damaged, not pitiable. They have been touched by the last remnant of divine change and survived. Their alterations are marks of communion.
- **The Maelstrom remembers.** The wound in reality is not empty. It contains the echo of Vorrhyn's final moment, his last act of transformation applied to reality itself. To approach it is to approach the divine.
- **Acceptance over resistance.** Fighting change causes suffering. Embracing it, even when it is painful, even when it is frightening, is the path to peace. This does not mean seeking change recklessly. It means not fleeing when change finds you.

## Structure

The Echoists have no hierarchy beyond [[Prior Echoes]] themselves, who serves as teacher, philosopher, and spiritual guide. The movement is organized around:

- **Gatherings**: Regular meetings in [[Riftmarket]], [[Shimmer]], and [[Edgewatch]] where Echoes or a senior adherent leads discussion, meditation, and shared testimony from the Changed. At Shimmer, Gatherings take place at **the Threshold View**, a promontory overlooking the Maelstrom at sunset. The ritual follows a fixed order: shared silence, testimony from a Changed adherent, guided meditation led by Prior Echoes, and a closing where each participant names one change they have accepted since the last Gathering. PCs attending feel the Maelstrom's hum in their teeth and a strange, warm loosening of certainty, as though the world's rules are suggestions rather than laws.
- **The Threshold Walk**: The most controversial practice. Adherents journey to the edge of the Threshold in small groups, entering the zone of active distortion and allowing the Maelstrom's influence to touch them. Preparation takes days: fasting, meditation, and a private conversation with Prior Echoes about what the adherent fears most about change. The Walk itself lasts minutes. The Maelstrom's edge is a wall of shimmering distortion where colors bleed, sound warps, and the air tastes of copper and ozone. Adherents step in and stand still. Minor changes (shifted eye color, altered voice pitch, a limb that bends differently) are common. Major changes (full physical restructuring, sensory rewiring, personality shifts) are rare but permanent. Cumulative exposure increases risk: adherents who Walk repeatedly develop a sleepwalking compulsion toward the Threshold border. Two Echoists who listened to whispering Shimmer-glass at [[Shimmer]] have begun sleepwalking toward the Threshold nightly.
- **The Remembrance**: An annual ceremony at [[Shimmer#The Threshold View|the Threshold View]] honoring Vorrhyn's memory. Echoists gather, share stories of transformation, and release small objects into the Shimmer Zone, offerings to the god who is not there anymore.

### Named Adherents

- **[[Prior Echoes]]**: Founder and spiritual guide. Formerly a Dominion town teacher before being Changed during a Threshold surge. Spent three days in the Shimmer Zone and emerged altered, perceptive, gentle, and no longer entirely certain which moment they are standing in. Carries a pale wooden staff carved from Maelstrom ejecta that hums near the Shimmer Zone. Recently, Prior Echoes has been hearing "wrong notes" in the Maelstrom's rhythm, undertones that feel like shadow rather than change, something foreign wearing Vorrhyn's resonance.
- **[[Lysara Shimmerborn]]**: Head Researcher at [[The Shattered Edge#The Last Certainty|The Last Certainty]]. Perceives three timelines simultaneously, a gift and a curse that makes conversation with her disorienting for everyone else. Discovered that the Maelstrom is being pulled open from the other side, not just expanding on its own. Has a Veil Unbound mole named Aldis among her junior researchers, undetected so far.
- **Kaelen Riftwalker**: A radical adherent who has completed the Threshold Walk eleven times, more than anyone else alive. Each Walk has changed him further: his left arm phases in and out of visibility, his voice arrives a half-second before his lips move, and he occasionally answers questions that have not been asked yet. He pushes for deeper, longer Walks and dismisses the sleepwalking as "the Threshold calling us home." Prior Echoes considers him a cautionary tale. His followers consider him a prophet.

### The Foretouched

A new type of Changed has been emerging in the last year, and they are unlike anything the Edge has seen before. Instead of physical alteration, the Foretouched carry fragments of knowledge: names of people they have never met, locations they have never visited, events that have not yet occurred. They speak in certainties about things they should not know, and they are right. Every time. [[Prior Echoes]] calls them sacred, proof that Vorrhyn's echo carries meaning, not just transformation.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Foretouched are leaks from [[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas]]'s [[Cosmology and Magic#The Loom of Fate|Loom of Fate]], bleeding through the weakening Maelstrom barrier. The knowledge they carry is real, accurate, and dangerous, because each fragment that passes through weakens the barrier further. They are not Vorrhyn's blessing. They are symptoms of the Maelstrom thinning into something it was never meant to be: a window into fate itself.

## Goals

- **Preserve Vorrhyn's memory** and the understanding that change is necessary for a living world
- **Support the Changed**, offering community, meaning, and dignity to those altered by the Maelstrom
- **Resist the Dominion's containment efforts**, which the Echoists view as an attempt to cage the last remnant of divine change
- **Understand the Maelstrom's deepening**, which Prior Echoes interprets as Vorrhyn's echo growing louder, a sign that the world's need for change is becoming critical

## Strengths

- **Moral authority among the Changed.** The Echoists provide meaning and community to people who have lost everything else. That loyalty is deep.
- **Widespread sympathy.** Even non-adherents on the Edge respect the Echoists' work with the Changed.
- **Prior Echoes' charisma.** Their teaching is compelling, humane, and difficult to argue against without sounding cruel.

## Weaknesses

- **The Threshold Walk kills people.** However voluntary, however prepared, some adherents die or are Changed beyond recovery. This gives critics ammunition and causes genuine harm.
- **Vulnerability to radicalization.** The philosophy of "embracing change" can be twisted into recklessness, fatalism, or worse. Fringe adherents sometimes seek out dangerous transformations deliberately.
- **No political power.** The Echoists are a philosophical movement, not a faction with resources or military strength. They influence culture, not policy.
- **The [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] finds them useful.** Serith's agents exploit Echoist theology, reframing Serith's desire to open the Maelstrom as compatible with Vorrhyn's legacy. Some Echoists have been unknowingly recruited.

## Inter-Faction Relationships

- **[[Edgewalker Tyrell Vane]] / [[The Accord of Echoes|Accord of Echoes]]:** Vane governs by probability. The Echoists teach by faith. "They disagree frequently and value each other enormously." Vane privately worries about the Threshold Walk's escalation but will not interfere with a voluntary spiritual practice, not yet.
- **[[The Ashen Dominion|Dominion]]:** The Echoists resist Dominion "containment" and "cure" efforts. The Dominion views the Accord's governance as "chaos-touched madness" and the Echoists as its most dangerous expression.
- **[[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]]:** Maintain a research presence at [[The Shattered Edge#The Last Certainty|The Last Certainty]]. Share intellectual interest in the Maelstrom. [[Lysara Shimmerborn]] collaborates with Star-Reader scholars, though she withholds her most alarming findings.

## Hooks for Player Interaction

- **Philosophical Challenge.** [[Prior Echoes]] may engage PCs in genuine debate about the nature of change, the Maelstrom, and whether the world is better or worse for Vorrhyn's loss. Their arguments are good. Agreeing or disagreeing both have consequences.
- **The Threshold Walk.** PCs may be invited to participate. The experience is genuinely transformative, both narratively and potentially mechanically.
- **Echoist Infiltration.** The [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] has planted an agent among the Echoists, someone who is subtly guiding the movement's theology toward conclusions that serve Serith's interests. [[Prior Echoes]] suspects something is wrong but cannot identify the source.

> [!warning]- GM Notes
> [[Prior Echoes]] is sincere, and their theology is not wrong, exactly. Vorrhyn *is* echoed in the Maelstrom. Change *is* necessary for a living world. The problem is that the echo is being *used*. [[The Twelve#Serith, Goddess of Shadow and Secrets , *"The Veiled One"*|Serith]]'s influence is threading through the Maelstrom's resonance, piggybacking on Vorrhyn's remnant to reach the people who approach the wound. The Threshold Walk does not connect adherents to Vorrhyn. It exposes them to Serith's whisper, disguised as the god of change's blessing.
>
> Prior Echoes does not know this. If they learned the truth, it would shatter them, but it would also make them the most motivated ally against Serith's plans that the PCs could find.
>
> **Veil Unbound Infiltration.** The mole is reframing Serith's goals in Echoist terms: "The Veiled One seeks to change the world, just as Vorrhyn did. To resist her is to resist change itself." This theological argument is genuinely compelling, and it is spreading among adherents who have never heard of Serith by name. The infiltrator (Aldis, embedded in [[Lysara Shimmerborn]]'s research team) feeds intelligence about the Maelstrom's structure back to the Veil Unbound while nudging the Echoists toward deeper, more frequent Walks.
>
> **Prior Echoes' Sensitivity.** The "wrong notes" Prior Echoes hears in the Maelstrom's rhythm are the boundary between Vorrhyn's genuine echo and Serith's parasitic overlay. With guidance (from the PCs, from the [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]], or from the Foretouched), Prior Echoes could learn to distinguish the two, making them a critical asset for detecting Serith's influence across the continent.
>
> **Edgewalker's Blind Spot.** [[Edgewalker Tyrell Vane]] cannot see Serith's probability threads. Her shadow domain conceals futures from probability-readers. The Shattered Edge is probability-blind to Serith, which means the most analytically governed region on the continent has a massive intelligence gap that only faith-based perception (the Echoists) can fill.
>
> **The Maelstrom's True Nature.** The Maelstrom is not just a wound. It is a passage. Serith wants to widen it to access [[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas]]'s [[Cosmology and Magic#The Loom of Fate|Loom of Fate]], the mechanism she needs to restart the divine cycle. Every Threshold Walk that weakens the barrier brings her closer to that goal.
