# The Forge Council

|||
|---|---|
| **Region** | [[The Emberveil]] |
| **Type** | Governing Body |
| **Leader** | [[Brunhild Ironheart|Forge-Matriarch Brunhild Ironheart]] |
| **Alignment** | Lawful Neutral, meritocratic governance through demonstrated craft |
| **Founded** | ~600 AS |

## Description

The Forge Council is the governing body of [[The Emberveil]], a guild-based assembly where seats are held not by birthright or election but by assessed contribution. Each guild in the region, smiths, miners, jewelers, builders, healers, engineers, holds seats proportional to its measurable output, goods produced, infrastructure maintained, services rendered, and expertise provided. The [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] holds the largest bloc by a significant margin, but it does not rule alone. The Jewelers' Conclave, the Miners' Union, the Builders' Circle, and the Healers' Lodge all carry weight, and the smaller guilds can form voting blocs that check the Ember Guild's dominance.

The Council meets in the Council Foundry in [[Crucible]], a hall whose architecture reflects its philosophy: semicircular, tiered, with the Forge-Matriarch's seat at the centre rather than at the head. Decisions are made by weighted vote, each seat carrying its guild's assessed value. The Forge-Matriarch holds a casting vote in deadlocks and the authority to call emergency sessions, but cannot override a supermajority. The system is dwarven in origin, rooted in the meritocratic traditions that predate the Emberveil's formal founding, and it works because the culture it governs genuinely values competence over politics.

## Structure

- **Forge-Matriarch/Forge-Patriarch**, Presiding officer. Elected by the full Council for life (or until resignation). Holds casting vote and emergency powers. Currently [[Brunhild Ironheart]].
- **Guild Representatives**, Seat-holders, one per assessed contribution unit. The [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] holds approximately forty percent of seats, the Jewelers' Conclave fifteen percent, the Miners' Union fifteen percent, the Builders' Circle ten percent, the Healers' Lodge ten percent, and smaller guilds share the remaining ten percent.
- **The Assessors**, An independent body that evaluates guild contributions annually and adjusts seat allocation. The Assessors are the most hated and most necessary institution in the Emberveil, respected for their impartiality and resented for their conclusions.
- **The Warden-Commander**, A military appointment made by the Council, currently [[Tarn Smokeshield]], commanding the Emberveil's border defences. The Warden-Commander sits on the Council in an advisory capacity without a vote.

## Goals

- **Maintain the Emberveil's independence** from [[The Ashen Dominion]] and any other external power seeking to control the region's resources.
- **Steward [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] legacy** by ensuring that the deep forges, the geothermal systems, and the forge-magic traditions are preserved and responsibly managed.
- **Regulate deep mining** to prevent the reckless exploitation that could destabilize the volcanic systems or expose the forge-chambers to unworthy hands.
- **Promote the craft** by maintaining guild standards, funding apprenticeships, and ensuring that the Emberveil's goods remain the finest in Aethermourne.

## Strengths

- **Meritocratic legitimacy.** The Council governs because it demonstrably earns the right to govern. This makes it more stable and more trusted than most political institutions in Aethermourne.
- **Economic leverage.** The Emberveil controls forgeheart steel, the most valuable crafting material in the world. This gives the Council bargaining power disproportionate to the region's size.
- **Cultural cohesion.** The Emberveil's shared devotion to craft creates a population that supports its governing institutions out of genuine belief, not fear or habit.

## Weaknesses

- **Slow to act.** Consensus-based governance means the Council deliberates when it should decide. The Cooling, the Dominion threat, and [[Vessa Ironvow]]'s rogue operation have all been discussed extensively and addressed insufficiently.
- **Ember Guild dominance.** Despite the balancing mechanisms, the Ember Guild's forty-percent seat share means it can effectively veto any proposal it opposes. Smaller guilds resent this and occasionally form obstructionist blocs in response.
- **Military weakness.** The Emberveil has never needed a large military, its geography and trade leverage have been sufficient defence. If the Dominion's posture shifts from commercial to military, the Council has no adequate response.
- **Secrecy around the Deep Forge.** The Council's policy of restricting information about the deepest forge-chambers, while understandable, has created a culture of institutional silence that now prevents it from responding transparently to the Cooling.

## Hooks for Player Interaction

- **Council Commissions.** The Council hires specialists for tasks its guilds cannot handle, investigating the Deep Warrens, diplomatic missions to the Dominion, tracking down [[Vessa Ironvow]]. These commissions are well-funded and come with Council authority.
- **The Assessor Controversy.** The next annual assessment may strip seats from the Miners' Union due to reduced forgeheart steel output, a reduction caused by the Cooling, not by the miners' performance. This injustice could be a point of intervention.
- **The Succession Question.** [[Brunhild Ironheart]] is one hundred and eighty. She is vigorous but mortal. The question of who succeeds her, and whether her successor will maintain her policies, is a quiet but growing concern.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Forge Council's greatest vulnerability is that its institutional secrecy, designed to protect [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] forge, now prevents it from responding to the Cooling effectively. [[Brunhild Ironheart|Ironheart]] knows this but cannot reform the culture she has spent forty years enforcing without undermining her own authority. The PCs can serve as the outside force that breaks this deadlock, bringing information the Council cannot generate internally and perspectives its insular culture cannot produce.
>
> If [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] agents reach the Emberveil, the Council's guild-based structure is both a defence and a vulnerability. The meritocratic culture is resistant to corruption by ideology, but the economic pressures of the Cooling create openings. A guild facing seat-loss might be susceptible to a Dominion trade offer that, several layers removed, serves Serith's interests. The Council could be turned not through infiltration but through the ordinary mechanics of desperate people making compromises they would not make in better times.
