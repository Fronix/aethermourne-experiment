# The Oldborn Council

|||
|---|---|
| **Region** | [[The Unwritten Lands]], centered in [[Keth'vaal]] |
| **Type** | Federated Deliberative Body |
| **Leader** | No permanent leader, consensus-based, currently fractured into three camps |
| **Founded** | ~3,400 years ago (with the Covenant of Ash, over 1,000 years before the Theomachis) |
| **Membership** | One Speaker per major city, currently eleven |

## Description

The Circle of Speakers is the governing body of [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]], a federation of city-states bound not by conquest or heredity but by the **Covenant of Ash**, a civilizational-scale Primordial pact struck with [[Ur-Ghael]] itself. The Circle has maintained Oldborn unity for over three millennia without a single god, king, or emperor. It is the oldest continuous political institution in either continent, and it has never faced a crisis it could not resolve through debate.

Until now.

The arrival of outlander ships on [[The Veilshore]] has fractured the Circle along ideological lines for the first time in living memory. Three factions have emerged, each led by a Speaker whose personal history shapes their position. Emergency session has been continuous for three years. No consensus has been reached. The Oldborn, who pride themselves on civilized disagreement, are discovering what happens when disagreement becomes irreconcilable.

## The Covenant of Ash

The Circle's authority derives entirely from the Covenant of Ash, the foundational legal framework of Oldborn civilization. Understanding the Council requires understanding the Covenant.

The Covenant is not a constitution. It is a Primordial pact, a negotiated contract between the first Pact-Weavers and [[Ur-Ghael]], the Primordial of Structure. In exchange for Ur-Ghael's cooperation (stable geography, reliable Primordial Anchors, the substrate's passive consent to ordered civilization), the Oldborn agreed to four binding principles:

1. **No mortal may claim dominion over a Primordial force.** Negotiation only. Never command.
2. **No city may expand beyond its Anchor's stabilization field** without securing a new Anchor.
3. **Knowledge is communal property.** Hoarding knowledge is the gravest crime in Oldborn law.
4. **The dead return to the substrate.** Burial is dissolution, returning the body to [[The Gyre]]. Preserving corpses is forbidden.

These are not guidelines. They are contractual obligations. Violation weakens the Covenant itself, and the Covenant is the only thing keeping Primordial forces cooperative across the continent. A broken Covenant means broken pacts, means unstable Anchors, means cities swallowed by [[The Primordia]]'s chaos.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **The Covenant's hidden cost:** The Covenant was struck at civilizational scale. The cost was proportional: the Oldborn's collective memory of what came before. Their pre-Covenant history is genuinely erased, not forgotten but *untethered from reality*. The Oldborn do not know what they were before the Covenant. They have chosen not to care.
>
> But the truth is buried in the Rootways beneath the Obsidian Plateau. What the Oldborn were before the Covenant, what they did that made Ur-Ghael's intervention necessary, is not comforting. This is a late-campaign revelation that should reframe the Oldborn's moral authority.

## Structure

### How Speakers Are Chosen

Each major Oldborn city selects its Speaker through its own method, there is no uniform process, reflecting the federation's commitment to local autonomy:

- **[[Keth'vaal]]** selects by elder consensus, the city's oldest practitioners deliberate in private
- **[[Vehlkaar]]** holds competitive debate, candidates argue before the full citizenry
- **Ur-Saethen** appoints its most published scholar, measured by peer-reviewed contributions to the communal archives
- **Draveth** selects through trial of craft, candidates demonstrate mastery of the skills that keep the mining city functional
- **Hael'rune** rotates through its founding families on a fixed schedule

All Speakers serve seven-year terms and cannot serve consecutively. This prevents entrenchment. It also means the Circle's composition shifts regularly, and long-term policy is difficult to maintain, which is by design. The Oldborn distrust permanence in governance.

### How Decisions Are Made

The Circle requires **consensus**, not majority. Every Speaker must agree, or the matter is unresolved. Deadlocks are broken through **ritual debate**, a formalized process that can last weeks. In ritual debate, each Speaker presents their position without interruption, then submits to cross-examination by every other Speaker. The process continues until either consensus emerges or all parties agree to table the issue.

In practice, tabling is rare. The Oldborn view unresolved questions as civic failure. Social pressure to reach consensus is enormous. This system has worked for three millennia because Oldborn disagreements have historically been narrow, differences of method, not philosophy.

The outlander crisis is a difference of philosophy. The system is breaking.

### The Pact-Weavers' Role

[[The Oldborn Kingdoms#The Pact-Weavers|Pact-Weavers]] are not members of the Circle, but they serve as essential advisors. Every major decision is reviewed by a Pact-Weaver conclave that assesses whether the proposed action is compatible with the Covenant of Ash. A Pact-Weaver veto, rarely exercised, kills any proposal outright. This is not a check on power. It is a check on *contract compliance*. The Covenant is a Primordial pact, and violating it has consequences beyond politics.

## The Three Factions

### The Sealed Gate (Isolationist)

**Leader:** Speaker Kaelthen of Hael'rune

**Position:** Close the borders. Expel the outlanders. Destroy any ship that approaches. Seal the Thornwood Coast permanently. The Oldborn survived three millennia without the known continent. They will survive three more.

**Argument:** The outlanders carry divine contamination in their blood, their tools, their breath. This contamination destabilizes Primordial pacts. It has already caused an Anchor to flicker near the eastern border. Every day the outlanders remain, the damage compounds. The Oldborn owe these people nothing. The known continent built its civilization on the corpses of gods and called it progress. Now that civilization is collapsing, and its refugees are washing ashore carrying the disease that killed it. The cure is quarantine.

**Strength:** Fear. Kaelthen's argument is grounded in measurable threat. Divine contamination is real. The Anchor destabilization is documented. Every Pact-Weaver in the federation has felt the outlanders' presence as a disturbance in the substrate. The Sealed Gate has the most Speakers' support, currently five of eleven.

**Weakness:** Impracticality. The ocean is opening regardless of Oldborn wishes. [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]]'s seal is failing because of the Second Silence, not because of anything the Oldborn can control. Sealing the coast treats the symptom, not the disease. And the outlanders are already here. Expelling them risks war, and the known continent has more soldiers.

**Speaker Kaelthen:** A veteran Pact-Weaver from Hael'rune, the eastern border city closest to [[The Veilshore]]. She has personally witnessed divine contamination disrupt a pact renewal, an event that killed two of her students when their Primordial bond destabilized. She is not xenophobic. She is traumatized, and her trauma aligns with genuine threat. Her voice shakes with controlled fury when she speaks of the outlanders. She would prefer peace. She has concluded peace is not possible.

### The Open Hand (Diplomatic)

**Leader:** Speaker Yavenne of Ur-Saethen

**Position:** Engage with the outlanders. Learn what they know. Share what is necessary to prevent catastrophe. The Second Silence threatens everyone, and the Oldborn cannot solve it alone. Isolation is a slower death than engagement.

**Argument:** The Oldborn have knowledge the known continent needs (Primordial magic, pre-Pattern history, the path to [[The Loom's Edge]]). The known continent has knowledge the Oldborn need (the divine ecology, the mechanics of the Second Silence, the nature of [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s threat). Trade is not capitulation. Negotiation is what the Oldborn do best. The Covenant itself is a pact, and pacts require two parties. Refusing to negotiate is a betrayal of Oldborn principles, not a defense of them.

**Strength:** Logic. Yavenne's argument is the most intellectually coherent, and in a culture that values debate, this matters. She has the support of the scholarly cities, Ur-Saethen and two others, currently three of eleven Speakers.

**Weakness:** Optimism. Yavenne has never met an outlander. She has never smelled the divine residue on their skin or felt an Anchor flicker in their presence. Her position is built on principle, not experience. The Sealed Gate faction asks, correctly, whether she would hold her position after watching a student die because an outlander stood too close to a pact renewal.

**Speaker Yavenne:** The youngest Speaker in the Circle's history, selected by Ur-Saethen's scholarly meritocracy at the age of thirty-two. Brilliant, idealistic, and dangerously persuasive. She reads outlander texts intercepted from Veilshore shipments, has taught herself three mainland languages, and has begun constructing a theoretical framework for Primordial-Aetheric magical compatibility. She believes the crisis is solvable. She may be right. She may also be the most dangerous person in the room, because her intelligence can make bad ideas sound inevitable.

### The Ash Reclaimers (Radical Expansionist)

**Leader:** Speaker Dravos of Draveth

**Position:** The outlanders' arrival is an opportunity, not a threat. The known continent's divine infrastructure is collapsing. When it falls, the Oldborn should be ready to expand, to bring their superior magical framework to the ruins of the gods' civilization. The age of divine order is ending. The age of Primordial governance can begin.

**Argument:** The Oldborn have the only magical tradition that is not dependent on dead gods. When the Second Silence completes, when the ley lines fail and the divine materials lose potency, only Primordial pact magic will still function. The Oldborn will be the only civilization with working infrastructure. This is not conquest. This is *survival of competence*. The known continent will need new foundations. The Oldborn can provide them, on Oldborn terms.

**Strength:** Ambition. In a culture paralyzed by fear and debate, Dravos offers a vision of the future, not just survival, but expansion. His faction is the smallest, currently three of eleven Speakers, but it is the most energized. Younger Oldborn, frustrated by the Circle's paralysis, are drawn to his clarity.

**Weakness:** Arrogance. Dravos underestimates the known continent's resilience, its military capacity, and the complexity of its political landscape. He also underestimates the Second Silence itself. His plan assumes the crisis will end neatly, that divine order will collapse and leave a vacuum for the Oldborn to fill. The reality, that the Second Silence may unmake structured reality entirely, including Primordial structures, is something he has not fully reckoned with.

**Speaker Dravos:** A former military commander from Draveth, the mining city built into a volcanic caldera. Dravos is charismatic, physically imposing, and impatient with deliberation. He was selected as Speaker after personally leading the defense of Draveth during a Primordial surge that nearly consumed the city. He thinks in terms of action, risk, and reward. The Circle's consensus model frustrates him. He has begun holding meetings with sympathetic Speakers outside the formal Circle, which is not technically a violation of the Covenant but is an erosion of its spirit.

## Relationships with Mainland Factions

The Oldborn have had no contact with mainland factions until three years ago. Impressions are forming rapidly and unevenly:

| Mainland Faction | Oldborn Perception | Reality |
|---|---|---|
| [[The Star-Readers]] | Scholars who might be worth talking to. The most tolerable outlanders. | The Star-Readers are genuinely interested in exchange. Their Lens-Bearers are respectful. Yavenne's primary argument for engagement is built on Star-Reader interactions. |
| [[The Ashen Dominion]] | Religious imperialists who worship a dead god's bones. Deeply offensive. | The Ashmark missionaries' insistence on bringing Solvaen's word to a people who have never heard it confirms the Sealed Gate's worst fears about outlander arrogance. |
| [[Concord of Tides]] | Merchants who treat everything as property. Predictable, manageable. | The Concord traders at [[Tidecross]] are the most pragmatic outlanders. They want profit, not conversion. The Oldborn understand transactional relationships. |
| [[The Veil Unbound]] | Unknown. The Oldborn have not identified the Veil Unbound as a distinct faction. | The Veil Unbound's agents on the Veilshore are operating covertly. Their interest in [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s reduced influence in the Unwritten Lands is something the Oldborn would find deeply alarming if they knew about it. |
| [[The Ashen Vigil]] | Unknown. No Vigil presence on the Veilshore yet. | If the Vigil arrives, the Oldborn's prohibition on corpse preservation will create immediate conflict with an organization that exists to manage the undead. |

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **The faction the Oldborn should fear most:** The Veil Unbound. Serith's agents are in the Unwritten Lands for a reason. Serith whispered Yvenne into sealing the ocean in the first place. Now that the seal is breaking, Serith's interest in what lies beyond is reawakened. The Veil Unbound are not seeking treasure or knowledge. They are seeking something Serith hid here before the sealing, something even the Oldborn do not know about. The Oldborn's ignorance of the Veil Unbound is their most dangerous blind spot.

## Current Internal Tensions

### The Consensus Crisis

Three years of emergency session without consensus is unprecedented. The Circle's legitimacy depends on its ability to resolve disagreements. The longer the deadlock persists, the more Speakers question whether the consensus model can survive a genuine existential threat.

Kaelthen has privately suggested a *temporary* shift to majority rule. This would be a Covenant violation, the Covenant mandates consensus. The Pact-Weavers have warned that altering the governance model could weaken the Ur-Ghael pact that underlies the entire Covenant. Kaelthen is weighing whether the risk is worth it.

### The Generational Divide

Younger Oldborn are more curious about outlanders than their elders. Some have traveled to [[The Veilshore]] independently. A few have traded with [[Tidecross]] merchants. One, a young Pact-Weaver apprentice from Hael'rune, attempted to study a ley line and suffered mild divine contamination, confirming the Sealed Gate's fears while simultaneously demonstrating the Open Hand's point that understanding the threat requires engagement.

The generational divide maps imperfectly onto the factional split. Young idealists lean toward the Open Hand. Young nationalists lean toward the Ash Reclaimers. Young traditionalists lean toward the Sealed Gate. The old guard, who remember a world without outlanders, overwhelmingly favors the Sealed Gate, but they are aging out.

### The Dravos Problem

Speaker Dravos's informal meetings with sympathetic Speakers outside the Circle are an open secret. He is building a coalition for action rather than debate. He has not violated the Covenant, yet. But the Pact-Weavers are watching. If Dravos attempts to act unilaterally, to send military forces to the Veilshore without Circle consensus, the Pact-Weavers will face a choice: enforce the Covenant and risk civil conflict, or look away and watch the Covenant erode.

This is the Oldborn's internal Second Silence: the slow failure of the institutions that hold their civilization together.

## Hooks for Player Interaction

- **Diplomatic Access.** PCs who earn the Circle's trust gain access to Primordial knowledge, pre-Pattern history, and the path to [[The Loom's Edge]]. PCs who alienate the Circle lose all of this.
- **Faction Balancing.** PCs can influence which faction gains ascendancy. Supporting the Open Hand opens dialogue. Supporting the Sealed Gate may protect the Oldborn but cuts off access. Supporting the Ash Reclaimers creates a dangerous ally.
- **The Consensus Breaker.** If PCs can provide information that changes the calculus (proof that the Second Silence threatens Primordial structures too, evidence of Serith's involvement, a demonstration of Primordial-Aetheric compatibility), they may break the deadlock.
- **Kaelthen's Trust.** Earning Speaker Kaelthen's trust is the hardest and most valuable diplomatic achievement in the Unwritten Lands arc. She is the key to Sealed Gate cooperation, and she will not be convinced by words. She needs to see proof that outlanders can exist near Primordial structures without causing harm.
- **Yavenne's Risk.** Speaker Yavenne may ask the PCs to serve as test subjects for her Primordial-Aetheric compatibility research. The experiment is dangerous. The results could change everything.
- **Dravos's Offer.** Speaker Dravos will approach the PCs privately and offer a deal: help him build a military alliance, and he will give them unrestricted access to the Loom's Edge. He is offering what the PCs want most, for a price the Oldborn may not survive.

> [!warning]- GM Notes
> **The Circle is a mirror.** The Oldborn's political crisis mirrors the known continent's: institutions built for stability failing under unprecedented pressure. The Circle of Speakers is to the Oldborn what the Empyrean Accord was to The Twelve, a structure of cooperation that works until the stakes become existential.
>
> **No correct faction.** The Sealed Gate is right that divine contamination is a real threat. The Open Hand is right that isolation will not survive the Second Silence. The Ash Reclaimers are right that the Oldborn's magic will outlast the known continent's. All three are also wrong. The campaign's resolution should emerge from what the PCs do, not from which faction "wins."
>
> **The Covenant as campaign stakes.** If the Covenant of Ash breaks, the Ur-Ghael pact that underlies it weakens, Primordial Anchors destabilize, and the Oldborn Kingdoms begin to dissolve into the Primordia. The Oldborn's greatest strength, their civilizational pact, is also their greatest vulnerability. Protecting it, or deciding whether it should be protected, is a campaign-scale decision.
