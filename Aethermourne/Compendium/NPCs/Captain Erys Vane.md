# Captain Erys Vane

|||
|---|---|
| **Title** | Concord Diplomat & De Facto Harbormaster of [[Tidecross]] |
| **Location** | [[Tidecross]], [[The Veilshore]], [[The Unwritten Lands]] |
| **Faction** | [[Concord of Tides]] |
| **Status** | Alive |

---

## Appearance

Erys Vane is a woman in her late forties, weathered by salt and sun and the weight of impossible responsibility. Her skin is deep brown, lined at the eyes and mouth in a way that suggests equal parts laughter and stress. She keeps her hair, black shot through with gray, cropped short and practical. She wears a Concord officer's coat, blue wool with silver trim, though the trim is tarnished and the wool is patched in three places. She stopped caring about appearances six months ago.

Her hands are never still. She taps fingers against her thigh, adjusts her coat, touches the small stone anchor charm at her throat. She moves with the efficiency of someone who has spent half her life on ships, weight shifting unconsciously to compensate for waves that are not there. When she speaks, her voice is hoarse, smoke and strain, and she chooses her words with a diplomat's precision even when exhausted.

She smells faintly of tar and pipe smoke. She quit smoking two years ago. She started again the day [[Tidecross]] began bleeding seawater from the docks.

---

## Personality

Erys is a pragmatist in a situation that defies pragmatism. She is sharp, quick-thinking, and ruthlessly practical. She does not have time for idealism, posturing, or anyone who wastes her attention. She solves problems by cutting through complexity until she finds the actionable core. This has made her an excellent diplomat and a terrible politician.

She is exhausted. Bone-deep, soul-tired, running on momentum and responsibility. She has not slept a full night in months. She forgets to eat. She snaps at people who do not deserve it, then apologizes immediately. Beneath the exhaustion is fear, the growing certainty that [[Tidecross]] is unsustainable, that violence is inevitable, and that when it comes, it will be her fault for not preventing it.

Despite this, she does not quit. Erys Vane does not know how to quit. She will hold [[Tidecross]] together with sheer willpower until the moment it tears itself apart, and then she will try to save whoever she can from the wreckage.

---

## Backstory

Erys was born in [[The Hollowed Reach]], daughter of a merchant captain and a Concord clerk. She grew up straddling two worlds: the rough pragmatism of the docks and the bureaucratic sophistication of the Concord's administrative class. She joined the Concord Navy at sixteen, served for twenty years, and earned a captaincy through competence, not connections.

She was good at her job. Too good. When the Concord needed someone to establish the first permanent presence on [[The Veilshore]], they sent Erys. She protested. She was a ship captain, not a diplomat, not an administrator. The Concord did not care. She was capable, trustworthy, and expendable if things went wrong.

Three years ago, she made landfall on the black sand beaches with forty sailors, three months of supplies, and orders to "establish trade relations and report findings." She built [[Tidecross]] from nothing. She negotiated with [[The Oldborn Kingdoms|Oldborn]] scouts who wanted her gone. She mediated between [[The Ashen Dominion]] missionaries who insisted on preaching and merchants who insisted on profit. She kept the peace when ships arrived carrying refugees, cultists, and people fleeing the Second Silence.

She succeeded. [[Tidecross]] grew. The Concord sent more people, more supplies, more demands. And Erys realized, too late, that success had trapped her. She could not leave. If she left, [[Tidecross]] would collapse. She was the only person all three factions trusted, or at least tolerated. She was the keystone. Remove her, and the structure falls.

She has written seventeen letters to the Concord requesting replacement. None have been answered. She no longer expects them to be.

## Relationships

- **[[Vosh]]** (Oldborn Observer), Complicated respect. Vosh is the closest thing Erys has to an ally among the Oldborn. They communicate in terse, practical exchanges. Erys suspects Vosh respects her competence. She knows Vosh is reporting everything she does. The relationship works because neither of them pretends otherwise.

- **[[Prior Cael Drantine]]** (Ashen Chapel Lead), Frustrated affection. Erys likes Cael. He is kind, earnest, and trying to help. He is also naive, culturally insensitive, and actively making her job harder. She has pulled him out of trouble four times. She will do it again. She wishes he would stop needing rescue.

- **[[Salice]]** (Smuggler Queen), Uneasy alliance. Salice runs the black market that keeps [[Tidecross]] supplied with things the Concord will not send. Erys knows this. She allows it. They have an unspoken agreement: Salice keeps violence out of the market, and Erys does not ask where the supplies come from.

- **[[Tidecaller Mirenne]]** (Shrine Priest), Shared dread. Mirenne and Erys are the only two people in [[Tidecross]] who fully understand how precarious the situation is. They meet weekly, share information the Concord and the Tidecallers would prefer kept separate, and quietly prepare contingency plans neither believes will work.

- **Concord Tidekeepers**, Bitter disappointment. Erys served the Concord for two decades. She believed in its mission. Watching the Concord prioritize profit over stability, ignore her warnings, and leave her stranded with inadequate support has eroded that faith. She still serves, but her loyalty is to [[Tidecross]], not the institution that abandoned it.

---

## What They Want

**Surface Goal:** Keep [[Tidecross]] functional long enough for the Concord to send proper support. Prevent the flashpoint violence she knows is coming.

**Deeper Need:** Erys wants to be released from this responsibility. She wants to go home, sleep for a week, and never make another diplomatic decision. But she will not leave until someone competent replaces her, and she does not believe anyone will.

What she will not admit, even to herself: she is terrified that if she leaves, everyone in [[Tidecross]] will die, and it will be her fault for abandoning them.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role:** Erys appears in Act II when the players first reach [[The Unwritten Lands]]. She serves as their primary point of contact in [[Tidecross]], the person who explains the situation, brokers introductions, and warns them about the dangers they do not yet understand.
>
> **Key Secret:** Erys is quietly evacuating people. She has arranged for ships to take refugees, non-combatants, and anyone smart enough to leave back to [[The Hollowed Reach]]. She does this without Concord authorization because she knows the Concord will not approve spending resources on evacuation when trade is still profitable. She funds this with money skimmed from tariffs and bribes. If discovered, it will end her career. She does not care.
>
> **Arc Trajectory:** If the players succeed in stabilizing [[Tidecross]], Erys becomes a key ally, providing resources, intelligence, and political cover. If they fail, she leads the evacuation, ensuring as many people survive as possible before the settlement tears itself apart. Either way, she does not leave until the last ship departs.
>
> **If the PCs Do Nothing:** [[Tidecross]] burns. The Oldborn lose patience, launch a coordinated strike, and destroy the settlement. Erys dies holding the docks, buying time for the last evacuation ship to escape. Her death radicalizes both sides. Diplomatic contact between the continents becomes impossible.
>
> **Roleplay Notes:** Erys speaks in clipped, efficient sentences. She does not waste words. She is patient with genuine questions but has no tolerance for posturing, grandstanding, or anyone who treats [[Tidecross]] as a political opportunity rather than a humanitarian crisis. When angry, she goes cold and quiet, not loud. Her tells: touching her throat charm, running her hand over her hair, looking toward the ocean as if calculating escape routes. Sample line: *"I don't need your promises. I need twenty tons of grain, forty barrels of clean water, and someone who knows how to defuse a mob. Can you provide any of those, or are you here to tell me the Concord sends its regards?"*
