## Appearance

An ancient elf, over eight hundred years old, withered and bent like a wind-worn branch. His skin has taken on the texture and color of aged bark, cracked and grey-brown, and small fungi and moss grow in the white tangles of his hair. His eyes are deep amber, startlingly clear for a face so weathered. He moves with deliberate slowness, each step considered, as though the ground might speak to him through his feet. He smells of damp earth and old wood, and his hands are stained green to the wrists from centuries of contact with living bark.

## Personality

Patient, cryptic, and occasionally maddening. Rosk perceives reality through his communion with the land, and translating that perception into words mortals can use is genuinely difficult for him, not an affectation. He answers questions with questions, lets silences stretch until they become uncomfortable, and hums tunelessly when thinking. Beneath the inscrutable manner lies a mind sharper than anyone expects, and a growing fear he rarely shows. When Rosk says something matters, every clan in the Marches listens.

## Backstory

Rosk's exact age and origins are unknown, even to himself. He has served the Marches as a Greensinger for longer than any other living practitioner, his communion with [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s lingering awareness deeper and more intimate than any other mortal's. He has watched generations of Marchwardens rise and fall, mediated wars, healed plagues, and guided the clans through crises both natural and supernatural.

He conducts his communion rituals in the Greensinger Grove, deep within the Grandfather titan-tree's root cluster in [[Roothold]], pressing his hands against the living bark and listening to the root-network that connects the Grandfather to every tree in the Cradle. For decades he has been the Marches' steadiest voice, the one who counseled patience when others demanded action.

In 2202 AS, Rosk broke decades of public silence to deliver a warning to the [[Marchwardens Council|Marchwardens' Council]]: "Shadow grows in the roots. The Mother's dream has turned. What wakes in the Cradle is not what sleeps there." He declared that [[The Verdant Marches#Thyrea's Cradle|Thyrea's Cradle]] was being corrupted from within, that something dark had taken hold in the deep root-networks. The warning shook the clans. For a being as patient as Rosk to speak with urgency meant the threat was real.

## Relationships

- **[[Kaelith Thornborn]]**, recognizes her mark as genuine and her communion as real, but remains politically neutral, which frustrates her
- **[[Tessara Wildbloom]]**, a young Greensinger he sent to investigate the deep Cradle corruption, who returned with critical intelligence
- **[[Root-Speaker Fennara]]**, a senior Greensinger and trusted colleague within the order
- **[[Draven Ironbark]]**, respects Ironbark's devotion to clan independence, even as he sees the man's stubbornness as dangerous in the current crisis
- **[[Senna Deepmoss]]**, provides counsel when asked, appreciates her careful analytical approach

## What They Want

Rosk wants to understand and stop the corruption spreading through the deep Cradle before it reaches the root-segments that sustain life across the entire Marches. Beneath that, he carries the weight of knowing his death would be a catastrophic loss of knowledge, and he fears he will die before the crisis is resolved, leaving no one with communion deep enough to guide the land through what is coming.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Guide and information source in Act III. Rosk directs the PCs toward the corruption's heart and provides essential knowledge about Thyrea's lingering consciousness, the root-network, and the nature of divine communion. His guidance is critical for the journey into the deep Cradle.
>
> **Key Secret.** Rosk suspects shadow corruption but does not know its source is [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]. He can feel something hungry in the roots, something that whispers and waits. His communion has been picking up faint wrongness for months. He assumed a natural die-back cycle. He is wrong, and the corruption is approaching the Grandfather's outermost root connections at [[Roothold]].
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs act on his guidance and sever the Shadow Root, Rosk's communion confirms the purification and he becomes the spiritual anchor for a unified Marches. If they delay, the corruption reaches the Greensinger Grove, and Rosk's communion, the deepest in the world, becomes a vector for Serith's whispers. He would sense the change, but fighting it would consume him entirely.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in metaphor and long silences. Answers questions with questions. Hums tunelessly when thinking. His voice is dry and quiet, like leaves settling. Despite the cryptic manner, he is not withholding information, he genuinely perceives reality in ways that are difficult to translate. When truly alarmed, his voice drops lower rather than rising.
> *"You ask what I hear in the roots. I hear breathing. But the thing that breathes... I do not think it is the Mother."*
