# Keeper Maren Ashveil

## Appearance

A woman in her middle years, though time in the Ashfield makes such estimates unreliable. She wears robes of layered grey cloth, patched and re-patched until they resemble the ash plains themselves, but kept clean with a discipline that borders on devotion. Her hands are rough from daily work, stained at the fingertips with the faint rust-orange of iron oxide, the only color she permits herself. Her face is calm and angular, with deep-set eyes that have lost most of their original color but retain a focused intensity. She moves with the practiced quiet of someone who has learned to navigate spaces where noise attracts attention.

## Personality

Steady, compassionate, and immovably present. Maren does not flinch from grief, panic, or rage, she has ministered to newly arrived souls in every state of distress imaginable and has learned that presence outlasts words. She performs rituals of comfort drawn from no theology, small ceremonies involving ash-drawing, whispered acknowledgments, and the lighting of iron-spark candles that serve no divine function but provide structure for the terrified. She is not naive. She understands the Ashfield perfectly and has made her peace with its nature. What she has not made peace with is the reason she came.

## Backstory

Maren was a village healer in [[The Ashen Dominion]], tending to a community that lived in the shadow of a [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] fragment. When the fragment's influence surged during a border conflict, it pulled several of her patients through a thin point into the Ashfield. Among them was her daughter, Lia, who was seven years old and running a fever that night.

Maren followed deliberately. She gathered supplies, spoke the transit prayers she had overheard from traveling scholars, and walked into the thin point with her eyes open. She arrived in the Ashfield and found Rustgate, but not Lia. She has searched every reachable corner of Level 1. She has questioned every soul who might have seen a child. She has stood at the Descent Gate and considered the possibility that Lia went deeper. She has not gone through it. Not yet.

In the years since, Maren has channeled her search into service. The Unnamed Shrine began as a place where she could sit and not think about descending. It became a place where others came to sit with her. It became Rustgate's only sanctuary, a space where the whisper seems slightly quieter, where the grey seems slightly warmer, where souls can grieve what they have lost without the domain trying to convert that grief into ambition.

## Relationships

- [[Erys Ironfall]], the Warden, who she regards as Rustgate's spine. They share a mutual respect built on the recognition that they are both staying for reasons beyond survival. Erys does not ask about Lia. Maren is grateful for this.
- [[Old Rust]], who watches her shrine from a distance. Maren has never spoken to Old Rust directly, but she leaves offerings of ash-tea at the base of the Warden's Nail, and they are always taken.
- [[The Ashen Crier]], whom she feeds and waters daily. The Crier has tried repeatedly to tell her something specific, and the desperation in those attempts frightens her more than anything else in the Ashfield.
- [[Scavenger Polk]], who brings her mortal-world artifacts that he cannot identify. She recognizes most of them, children's toys, prayer beads, pressed flowers turned to ash, and keeps them in the shrine as memorials.

## What They Want

Maren wants to find her daughter. That need has not diminished with time, it has simply been buried beneath layers of duty and purpose. What she tells herself she wants is to provide comfort to the souls of Rustgate, to be the quiet center that keeps people from breaking. Both are true. The tension between them is what keeps her awake in the hours when the Ashfield is at its most silent.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Maren is a source of emotional grounding and moral complexity on Level 1. She provides healing, shelter, and context for the human cost of the Iron Descent. She is also a potential quest-giver if the party learns about Lia.
>
> **Key Secret.** Lia is on Level 2. The Ashen Crier has been trying to tell Maren this for years but cannot force the information into coherent speech. Lia did not arrive as a child. Time moves differently in the Descent, and Lia has been in the Proving Grounds long enough to earn a name, a different name. She does not remember her mother. She does not remember being seven. Finding Lia means finding a warrior who does not know she is lost.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party discovers Lia's location and tells Maren, she will face a choice: descend to Level 2 and risk everything, or accept that her daughter is no longer someone who can be saved in the way she imagined. If she descends, the Unnamed Shrine will lose its keeper, and Rustgate will lose the only space where the whisper does not reach.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Soft-spoken but firm. Never raises her voice, but her words carry weight because she means every one. Pauses before answering questions, not from uncertainty but from care. Has a habit of touching her wrist where a bracelet used to be. Sample line: *"I do not worship anything in this place. I do not think anything here deserves worship. But these people deserve someone who will sit with them in the dark, and I can do that."*
