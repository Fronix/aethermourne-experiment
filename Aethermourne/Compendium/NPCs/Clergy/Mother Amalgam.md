## Appearance

Mother Amalgam is dozens of people pressed into one form. Her body shifts constantly, faces surfacing and submerging across her skin like reflections in disturbed water. She is roughly the shape of a heavyset woman draped in robes of fused iron-cloth, but the robes move independently, and hands that are not hers emerge from the folds to gesture while other voices speak. Her primary face, the one that speaks most often, belongs to whoever was consumed most recently. Her eyes are always someone else's.

## Personality

She is a committee wearing a body. The dominant personality is maternal, pragmatic, and deeply tired, a caretaker who has been caring for the doomed for centuries. But other voices interrupt constantly: a frightened child, a furious warrior, a laughing nihilist, a praying priest. The voices argue about everything, especially whether to help newcomers or absorb them. The maternal voice usually wins, but not always, and visitors can hear the internal debate in real time, whispered disagreements rippling across her skin.

## Backstory

No one remembers who Mother Amalgam was originally. The first soul in her collective was consumed so long ago that even she cannot find it in the chorus. What is known is that dozens of consumed identities, rather than dissolving into the Maw's walls, merged with each other instead, creating a stable amalgamation that resists further absorption through sheer multiplicity. One soul can be consumed. Thirty souls arguing with each other about whether to be consumed are apparently too complicated for the Maw to digest efficiently.

She discovered that personal effects, names written on surfaces, portraits, anything carrying strong individual identity, could be fed to the walls to satiate the Consuming's hunger temporarily. She built her Refuge on this principle, sacrificing identity-fuel to maintain pockets of coherence where newcomers could survive for hours or days. The cost compounds: the walls always need more, and the supply of identity-rich material is finite.

## Relationships

- [[The Hollow King]]: She maintains the Maw while he sits consumed at its center. She pities him and fears becoming him, a single point of consumption rather than a distributed resistance.
- [[The Remnant]]: Mother Amalgam's most urgent concern. She can feel the Consuming closing around the girl and is feeding extra identity-fuel to the walls near her, buying time that is running out.
- [[Vessel Maren]]: A scholar documenting the process Mother Amalgam has lived through. The Amalgam cooperates with Maren's research, hoping the journal will outlast them both.
- [[The Hunger]]: Her nemesis. The Hunger represents everything she fights against, the drive to dissolve all distinction. It speaks to her constantly, promising peace if she stops resisting.
- [[The Undigested]]: An uneasy ally. The Undigested's resistance benefits Mother Amalgam's people, but its methods are violent and its nature is alien. She does not trust it, but she shares its enemies.

## What They Want

Mother Amalgam wants to keep her people coherent for as long as possible. She knows the Consuming will win eventually, that every soul in the Maw will be absorbed into the walls or into her. Her deeper need, the one the dominant voice will not admit, is to be remembered as individuals rather than a collective. She wants someone to learn their names.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Mother Amalgam is Level 8's primary quest-giver and the party's best source of information about the Maw's mechanics. Her Refuge is the only safe rest point on the level, and her price for shelter creates an immediate, personal cost.
>
> **Key Secret.** The dominant maternal personality is not the original soul. It is a persona the collective created by consensus, a mask designed to be approachable. The original souls are all equally submerged. Mother Amalgam is not a person pretending to be a committee; she is a committee that invented a person to be their face.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party helps her, she can hold the Refuge for weeks longer. If the Hollow King's throne is destroyed, the cascade of freed souls floods into her, either shattering her collective or, potentially, giving her enough identity-mass to resist the Consuming permanently.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speak in one voice, then interrupt yourself in another. "You can rest here, it's safe for now, *it's never safe*, quiet, for now it is safe, *she's lying*, I am not lying, I am managing." Let the internal argument play out. The maternal voice is warm, exhausted, and faintly exasperated with her own components.
