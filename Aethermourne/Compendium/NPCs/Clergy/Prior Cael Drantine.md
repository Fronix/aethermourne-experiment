# Prior Cael Drantine

## Appearance

Cael Drantine is a man in his early thirties with the kind of face that makes you believe everything he says, open, earnest, utterly without guile. His skin is pale from years indoors, his hair light brown and perpetually disheveled. He has the build of someone who has never done manual labor, soft around the edges, hands unmarked by calluses.

He wears the white robes of [[The Ashen Dominion]]'s missionary corps, always freshly cleaned despite the dust, sweat, and chaos of [[Tidecross]]. The geometric mandala of [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]] is embroidered in silver thread over his heart. He treats the robe like armor, like as long as he wears it, he is protected by something greater than himself. He is wrong, but he does not know it yet.

His eyes are gray-blue, warm, and they light up when he talks about his faith. He smiles easily, too easily for [[Tidecross]], where smiles are currency and trust is expensive. He moves with the slightly clumsy grace of someone who is trying very hard to be helpful and is not quite succeeding.

He smells faintly of incense and old paper. He carries a small leather-bound copy of the **Ashen Canticles** everywhere, its pages dog-eared from constant reference.

---

## Personality

Cael is kind. Genuinely, unreservedly kind. He believes people are fundamentally good, that misunderstandings can be resolved through dialogue, and that [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] teachings can bring order and peace to a chaotic world. He is wrong about all of this, but his wrongness comes from a place of such sincere compassion that it is hard to dismiss him entirely.

He is naive. Not stupid, naive. He understands theology, history, scripture. He does not understand people. He does not see the way [[The Oldborn Kingdoms|Oldborn]] observers watch him with barely-concealed contempt. He does not register the danger in preaching divine order to people who have spent millennia rejecting divine authority. He thinks resistance to his message is ignorance, not philosophy. He is trying to save people who do not want saving, and he cannot comprehend why they do not want it.

Cael is optimistic in a way that is both admirable and infuriating. He has been beaten, threatened, and vandalized. The [[Tidecross#The Ashen Chapel|Ashen Chapel]] has been burned down three times. Each time, he rebuilds, more determined than before, convinced that persistence will eventually win hearts. He interprets opposition as a test of faith rather than a clear message that he is not welcome.

Beneath the optimism is loneliness. Cael came to [[Tidecross]] believing he would build a community. Instead, he has built a target. The Dominion missionaries who accompanied him have left, one by one, unable to bear the isolation and hostility. Cael stays because leaving would mean admitting failure, and failure would mean his faith was insufficient. He would rather die than admit that.

## Backstory

Cael was born in [[The Ashen Dominion]]'s capital, [[Ostivaar]], to a family of mid-tier clergy. He grew up inside the literal ribcage of a dead god, surrounded by the certainty that [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] sacrifice was the foundation of all civilization. He never questioned it. Why would he? The Dominion worked. Order prevailed. The alternative was chaos.

He entered the Church at fifteen, studied theology and scripture, and was ordained at twenty-three. He was good at his job. Compassionate, articulate, deeply faithful. He could have risen through the Dominion's ecclesiastical ranks, become a Bishop, lived comfortably. Instead, he volunteered for the missionary corps.

When the first ships reached [[The Unwritten Lands]], the Church saw an opportunity: a people who had never known divine grace, who had built their civilization without the gods' guidance. Cael volunteered immediately. He believed he could bring [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] light to the Oldborn, that they would see the wisdom of divine order and embrace it. He was twenty-eight and had never left Ostivaar.

He arrived in [[Tidecross]] three years ago with forty missionaries, a wagonload of supplies, and absolute faith. Within six months, thirty-five missionaries had returned to the Dominion. Within a year, the Ashen Chapel had been vandalized twice. Within two years, Cael was alone, rebuilding a church no one wanted, preaching to empty pews, convinced that if he just explained Solvaen's teachings clearly enough, people would understand.

He has been explaining for three years. No one is listening. He has not noticed.

## Relationships

- **[[Captain Erys Vane]]** (Tidecross Harbormaster), Frustrated affection. Erys likes Cael. She genuinely does. He is kind, well-meaning, and trying to help. He is also making her job exponentially harder. She has pulled him out of trouble four times. She will do it again. She wishes he would stop needing rescue.

- **[[Vosh]]** (Oldborn Observer), Observed threat. Vosh considers Cael the most dangerous person in [[Tidecross]], not because he is violent but because he spreads ideological corruption. The worship of dead gods is a cognitive plague. Cael is a willing vector. Vosh has recommended his removal. The council has not yet acted. Cael has no idea he is being evaluated for execution.

- **[[Salice]]** (Smuggler Queen), Frustrated pity. Salice has warned Cael twice that his preaching will get him killed. He thanked her and kept preaching. She thinks he is an idiot. She also thinks he is genuinely good, which is rare enough in [[Tidecross]] that she keeps pulling him out of danger despite herself.

- **The Dominion Church**, Abandoned. Cael writes monthly reports to the Church hierarchy. They have not responded in eighteen months. He tells himself they are simply busy, that the Church trusts his judgment. The truth is simpler: the Church has written off [[Tidecross]] as a failed mission and Cael as an acceptable loss. He does not know this. If he did, it would break him.

- **The Oldborn**, Unreciprocated compassion. Cael sees the Oldborn as people in need of salvation. The Oldborn see Cael as a representative of a dead god's delusions. He prays for them. They pray he leaves. The gap between these positions is unbridgeable, but Cael keeps trying to build a bridge anyway.

## What They Want

**Surface Goal:** Bring [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] teachings to the Oldborn. Build a functioning congregation in [[Tidecross]]. Prove that divine order can coexist with Primordial traditions.

**Deeper Need:** Cael wants to matter. He wants his faith to mean something. He grew up surrounded by centuries of accumulated religious infrastructure, and he has never contributed anything original to it. The missionary work in [[Tidecross]] is his chance to build something new, to be remembered, to prove that his faith is not just inherited certainty but genuine conviction. He will sacrifice anything to achieve this, including, increasingly, his own safety.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role:** Cael is a moral dilemma on legs. He is kind, compassionate, and fundamentally misguided. He represents the Dominion at its most well-meaning and its most blind. The players must decide whether to protect him, redirect him, or let him face the consequences of his actions.
>
> **Key Secret:** Cael knows the Dominion has abandoned him. He found the internal Church memo six months ago, a casualty report listing him as "unrecoverable." He has not told anyone. He stays because leaving would confirm that the Church was right to abandon him, and he cannot bear that. His optimism is not naivety. It is denial, the only thing keeping him functional.
>
> **Arc Trajectory:** Cael's story ends one of three ways. First: the players protect him long enough that he learns to respect Oldborn traditions, abandons his missionary work, and becomes a genuine bridge between cultures. Second: he is killed by Oldborn extremists, becoming a martyr whose death radicalizes both sides. Third: he witnesses something so horrific (a Pact-Broken, the Shimmer, the true cost of Primordial magic) that his faith shatters, and he becomes Unbound, rejecting Solvaen's teachings entirely.
>
> **If the PCs Do Nothing:** Cael enters [[The Fernwald]] without permission, carrying a relic of Solvaen. The forest rejects him violently. He is found three days later, alive but changed, speaking in a language no one recognizes, his body marked with root patterns that were not there before. The Oldborn demand he be handed over for judgment. The Dominion delegation refuses. [[Tidecross]] erupts into violence. Cael becomes the spark that ignites the war he was trying to prevent.
>
> **Roleplay Notes:** Cael speaks with earnest enthusiasm, often leaning forward, making direct eye contact, trying to connect. He quotes scripture naturally, not as argument but as comfort. He is gentle, patient, and utterly unaware of how his presence is perceived. His tells: clutching his prayer book when nervous, adjusting his robes (a self-soothing gesture), and pausing mid-sentence to pray silently when uncertain. Sample line: *"I know you've heard this before, but please, just listen. Solvaen taught that order is not oppression, it is the framework that allows freedom to flourish. Your people live in constant negotiation with forces that could consume you. What if there was another way? What if structure could coexist with your traditions, not replace them?"*
