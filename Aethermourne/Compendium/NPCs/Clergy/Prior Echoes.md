## Appearance

Ageless in the way that the significantly Changed sometimes are, appearing anywhere between thirty and sixty depending on the light, the angle, and perhaps which timeline the observer is standing in. Lean, with warm brown skin, high cheekbones, and a shaved head marked with faint, shifting patterns, not tattoos, but traces of chaotic energy visible just beneath the surface, moving like slow currents under ice. Their eyes are dark, calm, and deeply kind.

Prior Echoes dresses simply: undyed linen, sandals in warm weather, wrapped cloth in cold. No jewelry, no ornament, no insignia. They carry a walking staff of pale wood that is faintly warm to the touch and hums when brought near the Shimmer Zone, a piece of Maelstrom ejecta that Echoes found on their first Threshold Walk and has carried since.

---

## Personality

Prior Echoes is a philosopher first and a priest second. They speak with a quiet, resonant authority that comes not from volume or force but from the evident depth of thought behind every word. They ask questions more than they provide answers. They listen with a completeness that makes the speaker feel genuinely heard, possibly for the first time.

They are compassionate, patient, and unshakeable in their core belief: that change is sacred, that the Maelstrom is the echo of a god who loved transformation, and that the world's slow decline into stasis is the true catastrophe, not the wound that might heal it. This conviction is not blind. Echoes has buried adherents who did not return from the Threshold Walk. They grieve each one. They do not stop teaching.

There is a stubbornness beneath the gentleness that can surprise those who mistake kindness for softness. Prior Echoes will not be moved by threats, ridicule, or authority. They will engage with disagreement endlessly and respectfully, but they will not pretend to believe what they do not believe.

## Backstory

The person who became Prior Echoes was a teacher in a small [[The Ashen Dominion|Dominion]] town before the Maelstrom changed them. They do not share the details of their former life, not out of shame, but because they consider it irrelevant. That person died when the Change came. Prior Echoes was born.

Their Changing was dramatic: caught in a surge during a journey near the Edge's border, they spent three days in the Shimmer Zone before being found. When they emerged, their appearance had been altered, their memories rearranged, and something fundamental in their perception had shifted. They could feel the Maelstrom's rhythm, a deep, slow pulse that they describe as breathing. Not mechanical. Not random. *Alive*, in a way that speaks of intention even in the absence of a mind.

They came to the Shattered Edge seeking understanding and found a community of people who had survived what they had survived. The existing traditions of Vorrhyn-remembrance, scattered and informal, gave them a framework. Over the next decades, Echoes formalized these traditions into the Echoist movement, providing structure, ritual, and philosophy to a people who needed all three.

They chose the name "Prior Echoes" to indicate that what they teach is not new. It is what Vorrhyn always meant. The echo was there before anyone listened.

## Relationships

- **[[Tyrell Vane]]**: Mutual respect across a philosophical divide. Vane governs by probability. Echoes teaches by faith. They disagree frequently and value each other enormously.
- **[[Kessa Thornwall]]**: Echoes visits [[Edgewatch]] regularly to counsel watchers struggling with their Changes. Kessa is not an Echoist, but she is grateful for anyone who helps her people cope.
- **The Changed community**: Prior Echoes is the most trusted figure among the Edge's Changed population. When someone emerges from a surge transformed and frightened, Echoes is often the first person they are brought to.
- **The [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]]**: Echoes has noticed subtle shifts in some adherents' theology, language and ideas that do not originate from Echoist teaching. They suspect outside influence but have not identified the source.

---

## What They Want

- **To preserve Vorrhyn's memory** as a force of renewal, not destruction. The wider world remembers only the Maelstrom's horror. Echoes remembers the god who loved surprises.
- **To protect the Changed** from a world that fears them and from a Dominion that would "cure" them.
- **To understand the Maelstrom's deepening.** Echoes interprets the widening as Vorrhyn's echo growing louder, a signal that the world's need for change has become desperate. They may be more right than they know.
- **To find the source of the wrong notes.** Something in the Maelstrom's rhythm has changed. It still sounds like Vorrhyn, mostly. But there are harmonics that do not belong, undertones that feel like shadow rather than change. Echoes is troubled.

> [!warning]- GM Notes
> **Campaign Role.** Prior Echoes is a source of philosophical depth, emotional grounding, and eventual devastating revelation. Their theology is nearly correct, Vorrhyn *is* echoed in the Maelstrom, and change *is* sacred, but the echo has been infiltrated. [[The Twelve#Serith, Goddess of Shadow and Secrets , *"The Veiled One"*|Serith]]'s influence is riding Vorrhyn's resonance like a parasite on a heartbeat, using the Echoists' good faith to expose people to her whisper during the Threshold Walk.
>
> When Echoes learns this truth, it will be the defining crisis of their life. Everything they have taught is true, and everything they have practiced has been poisoned. The Threshold Walk does connect to Vorrhyn's echo. It also connects to Serith's shadow. Disentangling the two, if it is even possible, would require a journey to the Maelstrom's center and an understanding of divine metaphysics that no living person possesses.
>
> **The Wrong Notes.** Prior Echoes is right to be troubled. The harmonics they hear are Serith's influence, and the fact that they can hear the difference at all is significant. Their sensitivity to the Maelstrom's rhythm is rare, possibly unique. With guidance, Echoes could learn to distinguish Vorrhyn's echo from Serith's shadow, making them an invaluable tool for identifying where Serith's influence has spread. They would need to survive the knowledge first.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks slowly, with long pauses that are not hesitation but thought. Uses questions to guide conversation rather than assertions. Touches their staff when thinking deeply. When moved, their voice drops rather than rises. They do not preach; they invite. The emotional weight of their conviction makes their words heavier than their volume.
