# Prior Gault Ashworth

## Appearance

Sixties, tall and gaunt, with the geometric [[Cosmology and Magic#Ashite|Ashite]]-ink tattoos of the Anointed caste on his forehead. He wears white robes kept immaculate despite Crown's Reach's mountain dust, a discipline that speaks to the precision of his habits. His eyes are pale grey and unnervingly steady, the look of a man who has climbed The Crown twice and come down certain. His hands are long-fingered and still, folded before him when he speaks, as though every gesture has been considered and found unnecessary.

## Personality

A true believer, sincere, disciplined, and quietly tormented. Gault has dedicated his life to the Church and genuinely believes [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] order is divine truth. He is not corrupt, not cruel, not a hypocrite. He is a good man inside a bad system, which makes him more dangerous than a villain, because he legitimizes the system's worst features with his sincerity. He manages pilgrimages with meticulous care and genuine concern for the pilgrims' wellbeing. He weighs every word before speaking it, and the words he does not speak weigh more.

## Backstory

Gault was born to a minor Anointed family in [[Ostivaar]], the kind of clergy household where faith was as natural as breathing. He entered the Church's service at fourteen and rose through the ranks on competence and conviction, never ambition. His assignment to [[Crown's Reach]] twenty years ago was considered a quiet posting, a reward for faithful service, a place to grow old in proximity to the divine.

His first climb of The Crown changed him in the way the Church intended: he descended with absolute certainty that [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] order was righteous and the Church was its legitimate instrument. His second climb, ten years later, added a second certainty that sits alongside the first like a crack in a bell. He came down certain that something beneath the Spine of Order is wrong. Not the mines, not the politics, something deeper, something structural, as though the divine architecture itself were under stress.

He reported the change in the Hum's pitch to his superiors. They told him to stop asking. He has not stopped noticing. He keeps a private journal of unusual observations: the Hum's shifting frequency, pilgrims returning with harder-edged certainties, the way the Ashite veins in The Crown's summit have begun to pulse in patterns he cannot decode. The journal grows thicker every season.

## Relationships

- [[Order of the Ashen Flame]], devoted member. His faith in [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]] is unshakeable; his faith in the Church's leadership is eroding
- Sister Maren Veyl, Head of the Keepers of the Way. A professional respect built on shared mountain silence. She has never stayed at the summit long enough for the Hum to take hold, and Gault envies her that freedom
- Old Tam, the Doubt Quarter tavern keeper. Gault would never admit it, but he finds Tam's blunt pragmatism clarifying. They share a drink once a month in the guise of Gault "checking on the Quarter"
- [[Delric Mourne]], distant threat. Cinder agents stationed at Crown's Reach suppress inconvenient pilgrim revelations under Mourne's standing orders, and Gault has begun to notice the pattern
- The pilgrims, his responsibility. Every life that climbs the mountain is in his care, and he takes no death lightly

## What They Want

Gault's conscious goal is faithful service, to fulfil his duty to the Church and to [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]] with integrity. His deeper need is understanding. The Crown's Hum has changed, the pilgrims are returning different, and the Church told him to stop asking questions. A man whose entire life is built on certainty has discovered that certainty and truth are not the same thing, and the realization is consuming him.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Gault is a moral fulcrum. His sincerity gives the Church legitimacy; if he publicly questions the institution, it would shake the faith of thousands. He appears in Act I as a source of information and in Act II as a potential ally whose conversion from loyal servant to conscientious dissenter could shift the political landscape.
>
> **Key Secret.** His private journal contains years of observations that, taken together, document the early symptoms of [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] prison weakening: the Hum's pitch shift, anomalous pilgrim certainties, Ashite pulsing patterns. He does not understand what the data means, but someone with the right framework (a Star-Reader, a Calculist, or a PC with knowledge of the cosmological crisis) could read it like a seismograph.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs approach him with honesty and evidence, his faith in Solvaen's truth compels him to act, even against the Church. If left alone, his journal grows, his doubts deepen, and eventually the Cinders will notice his questions and silence him. A Gault who breaks with the Church becomes one of the most powerful moral voices in the Dominion. A Gault who is silenced becomes a martyr whose death proves the reformers right.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks slowly, choosing each word with visible deliberation. Long pauses mid-sentence, not hesitation, but precision. Folds his hands when listening. Unfolds them only when he has decided to trust. Never raises his voice. His distress shows in his stillness, the more troubled he is, the less he moves.
> *"I climbed The Crown and Solvaen gave me certainty. I did not ask for two kinds."*
