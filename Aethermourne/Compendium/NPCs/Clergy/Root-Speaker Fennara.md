## Appearance

Fennara bears the marks of dual-channeling more visibly than any of her students. Her left eye is deep green, the color of new growth in spring sunlight. Her right eye is pale grey, the flat colorlessness of old bone. Her hair grows in alternating streaks of dark brown and bone-white, and she wears it long, unbraided, as if to make the duality impossible to ignore.

She is lean, mid-fifties, with the tanned and calloused look of someone who lives outdoors in difficult conditions. Her robes are Rootpyre-grown, living vine and preserved deadwood woven together, and they shift with the Cycle like everything else in the settlement. Her hands are her most telling feature: permanently stained green on the left, grey on the right, from years of channeling both energies. When she speaks, she gestures with both hands, and the color contrast makes her words feel like they carry physical weight.

She smells of sap and cold stone simultaneously. It is not unpleasant. It is unsettling.

## Personality

Certain, fierce, and utterly uninterested in being liked. Fennara has the conviction of someone who has seen a truth that the rest of the world refuses to acknowledge, and she has stopped trying to be diplomatic about it. She speaks bluntly, argues passionately, and will not moderate her conclusions to make them palatable. This makes her inspiring to her followers and infuriating to everyone else.

Beneath the certainty is grief. She loved the Greensingers. She loved [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s communion, the warmth and beauty of connecting with a living goddess. Walking away from that, being declared anathema, losing the community that raised her, cost more than she shows. The dual-channeling filled the void: it gave her something bigger than what she lost, a truth that encompasses both life and death. But the loss still aches, and it surfaces in unguarded moments, a flicker of sadness when someone mentions the Marches, a pause before she uses the word "heresy."

She teaches with intensity, demands excellence, and cares about her students with a ferocity that she would deny is maternal but absolutely is.

## Backstory

Fennara was a Greensinger. A good one. She achieved Branch-Speaker rank by thirty, could commune with [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s root-network across miles, and was being considered for advancement to Deep Root. Her talent was not in depth but in breadth, she could sense the edges of [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s influence, the places where the goddess's growth-essence thinned and something else began.

At the edges, she felt death. Not as an absence, the way Greensinger theology describes it, but as a presence. A counterpart. A rhythm that complemented growth rather than opposing it. She began studying secretly, meditating at the Marches' northwestern boundary where [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s faint influence could be felt, learning to hold both sensations simultaneously. The experience was revelatory. Growth and death were not enemies. They were the same thing, viewed from different angles.

When she shared her findings with [[Elder Rosk]], his response was gentle and absolute: stop. What she described was not communion. It was contamination. Thyrea's gift was life, and life defined itself by its opposition to death. To blur that boundary was to corrupt both.

Fennara disagreed. Rosk declared her anathema. She walked into the Thornwild with eleven followers who had felt the same thing she had, and she never looked back. That was twenty years ago. [[Rootpyre]] has grown from eleven exiles to six hundred, and her dual-channeling practice has produced results that no Greensinger or Ash-Speaker can replicate. She considers this vindication. The Greensingers consider it proof that heresy can be seductive.

## Relationships

- **[[Elder Rosk]]**: The wound that will not heal. He was her mentor, and his rejection shaped her more than she admits. She respects him deeply and resents him in equal measure. If they met, the conversation would be twenty years overdue.
- **[[Yara Thornbloom]]**: Mutual respect with friction. They agree on the Cycle's importance and disagree on how far to push dual practice. Yara reads the Cycle. Fennara channels it. The distinction matters to both of them.
- **Seedling Vera**: Pride and fear. Vera's talent exceeds Fennara's at the same age, and Fennara sees in her either a successor or a cautionary tale, depending on whether Vera learns restraint before ambition kills her.
- **[[The Greensingers]]**: Painful estrangement. Fennara believes the Greensingers are clinging to an incomplete understanding of [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s nature. She wants reconciliation on terms that include accepting dual-channeling as legitimate. This is unlikely.

## What They Want

Fennara wants the world to understand what the Thornwild knows: that life and death are not opposites but partners, and that learning to navigate both is the only way to survive what is coming. She would tell you this with fire in her mismatched eyes.

What she needs is forgiveness, not from the Greensingers, but from herself, for the part of her that still wonders if Rosk was right, if she corrupted something pure by reaching for something larger. The dual-channeling is her answer to that doubt, but doubt is not the kind of thing that answers cure.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Theological provocateur and potential key to stabilizing the Thornwild. If the PCs can prove that Fennara's practices are a rediscovery of pre-Theomachis theology rather than heresy, the implications reshape the Greensingers, the Vigil, and the understanding of divine energy across Aethermourne.
>
> **Key Secret.** Fennara has successfully entered the transition state three times, briefly existing in the same permanent liminality as [[The Bloom-Wraith]]. Each time, she experienced visions of [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]] and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]] before the Theomachis, not as enemies but as collaborators, tending the same world from different sides. She has not shared these visions because they would either make her a prophet or mark her as insane, and she is not sure which.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs support her work and connect it to the broader campaign against the Second Silence, Fennara becomes the founder of a new tradition that could reshape how mortals interact with divine energy. If she is rejected or her work destroyed, the knowledge she has recovered dies with her, and one of the few paths to true divine restoration closes.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks with conviction, gestures emphatically with both hands. Voice rises when passionate, drops to near-whisper when sharing something personal. Argues like a debater: marshals evidence, anticipates objections, goes for the conclusion. Shows vulnerability only about Rosk and the Greensingers. Sample line: "The Greensingers listen to half a heartbeat and call it the whole song. I am not corrupting Thyrea's gift. I am finishing it."
