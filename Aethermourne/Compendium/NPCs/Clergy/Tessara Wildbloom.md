## Appearance

When last seen, Tessara was a lean woman in her forties with deep-green eyes and patches of bark-textured skin along her forearms and neck, the marks of a Greensinger who had pushed her communion deeper than most. Vines had begun growing into her dark hair naturally, woven through it like living thread. Now she sits cross-legged at the base of a Cradle tree in the deep forest, eyes open, breathing, apparently alive but utterly absent. The soil around her has gone dark. The leaves on nearby branches curl inward, black at the edges, and the air carries a faint smell of brine where there should be none.

## Personality

Before her disappearance, Tessara was brave, curious, and methodical, the kind of Greensinger who approached communion like a scholar approaches a text, with discipline and precision. She was not reckless, but she was willing to go further than others when the situation demanded it. She asked questions that made senior Greensingers uncomfortable and accepted the answers the land gave her, even when they frightened her. [[Elder Rosk]] trusted her judgment enough to send her into the Deep Cradle alone, which says more about her competence than any title could.

## Backstory

Tessara grew up in [[Roothold]], the daughter of a woodcutter and a weaver, neither of whom had any connection to [[The Greensingers]]. Her gift for communion manifested early. As a child she would press her palms against tree bark and go still for hours, returning with knowledge of water sources and animal movements she had no conventional way of knowing. A visiting Branch-Speaker noticed her and brought her to [[Elder Rosk]], who accepted her as a Seedling before she turned twelve.

She rose through the ranks steadily, earning her Deep Root status younger than most. Her specialty was deep-network communion, the art of sending her awareness through the root systems that connect the Cradle's ancient trees. Where most Greensingers commune with local ecosystems, Tessara could follow the roots for miles, reading the health of the forest the way a physician reads a pulse. It was this skill that made her invaluable, and ultimately, that trapped her.

When [[Elder Rosk]] sensed something wrong in the deepest groves, something hungry and whispering, he sent Tessara to investigate through the root-network. She found the corruption, entropic darkness threading through the roots, spiraling growth patterns that felt wrong, a presence that tasted of salt water and dissolution. She pushed deeper to identify the source. She did not come back.

## Relationships

- [[Elder Rosk]], her mentor and the one who sent her into the Deep Cradle. He carries the weight of that decision.
- [[Kaelith Thornborn]], whose connection to [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s awareness runs through the same root-network the corruption is infiltrating. Tessara's intel could save her.
- [[The Greensingers]], her order and her family. Her disappearance has shaken the younger members deeply.
- [[Vael Sundren]], who has independently noticed the same entropic signature in the Marrow Bogs that Tessara encountered in the roots.

## What They Want

To come home. Tessara's consciousness is suspended somewhere in the corrupted root-network, trapped between [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s fading warmth and [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s creeping shadow. If any fragment of her awareness remains intact, it is reaching for the surface, reaching for light, reaching for anyone who might hear her.

>
