## Appearance

The Mercy has no body, no face, no form. She is a warmth, a light, a feeling. She manifests in the Tower of Mercy as a soft golden radiance that pulses in counterpoint to the Heart of Iron's cold rhythm. Standing near her feels like standing in a shaft of sunlight after years underground. The light is not bright enough to see by, not steady enough to trust, but it is warm, and in the Iron Descent, warmth is a miracle. Sometimes the light coheres into something almost like a hand reaching toward a visitor, or something almost like a face turned toward the sound of crying. Almost. She is too diminished for more.

## Personality

The Mercy does not speak in words. She communicates in feelings: compassion so pure it bypasses language, hope so gentle it feels like memory rather than promise, the quiet certainty that things could be better. She does not argue, persuade, or demand. She simply radiates what [[The Twelve#Belara|Belara]] was, kindness without condition, love without transaction, mercy without judgment. After two thousand years imprisoned in the heart of her murderer, she is still, impossibly, kind. That is either the most hopeful thing in the Iron Descent or the most heartbreaking. It is both.

## Backstory

When [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] consumed Belara's essence during the Theomachis, he took her capacity for love, her dominion over compassion, her divine nature itself. He processed it through the Consuming and integrated it into the Heart of Iron. But Belara's essence did what Belara would have done: it found the crack, the weakness, the place where even iron can break, and it held on. It did not fight back. It simply refused to be fully consumed, the same way hope refuses to die even when everything rational says it should.

Over centuries, Belara's fragment concentrated in a single point within the Heart of Iron, creating a hairline crack in the sphere's surface that glows with steady golden warmth. This crack became the Mercy Gate, the only reliable exit from the Iron Descent. It is Belara's final act of resistance, a door left open in the deepest prison, proof that the god of mercy could be murdered but mercy itself could not be killed.

## Relationships

- [[The Echo of Kaevroth]]: Her murderer's memory. The Echo is aware of her and does not consume her further. She does not hate him. She does not hate anything. Whether this is divinity or simply the absence of enough consciousness for hatred is something even the Echo cannot determine.
- [[The Warden of Hope]]: A construct born from her own radiance, shaped by the Heart's defenses. The Warden guards the Mercy Gate, testing all who approach. She did not create it deliberately, but it reflects her nature: it demands selflessness as the price of passage.
- [[Iron-Prophet Thalric]]: He weeps near her light. She cannot comfort him in words, but he is calmer in her presence, the prophecies coming slower, the tears quieter. She is the only thing in the Heart that makes his madness bearable.
- [[The Unnamed]]: The ancient soul remembers Belara from before the Theomachis, when the goddess walked freely. The Unnamed visits the Tower of Mercy and grieves for what was lost. The Mercy's warmth is strongest when the Unnamed is near, as if recognizing an old friend.

## What They Want

The Mercy wants what Belara always wanted: for things to be better. Not perfect, not redeemed, not restored, just better. A little more light in the iron. A little more warmth in the cold. A door left open for anyone who needs it. Her deeper nature, if a fragment of consumed divinity can be said to have depth, is simply this: she will not stop being kind. Not because she chooses to, but because kindness is what she is, and not even Kaevroth could digest that.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Mercy is the emotional heart of Level 9 and potentially the entire campaign's endgame. She represents what Kaevroth destroyed and what still, against all odds, survives. She is the Mercy Gate, the exit, and the key to freeing Belara's essence from the Heart of Iron.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Mercy Gate is not just an exit. If the crack were widened, if the Heart of Iron were broken open here and beneath [[Ostivaar]] simultaneously, Belara's full essence would be freed. But the war-essence trapped alongside it would also be released, potentially triggering continent-wide conflict. You cannot free hope without also freeing war.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party reaches the Mercy Gate and surrenders their ambition, they pass through and escape. If they choose to crack the Heart open, the Mercy's light floods outward, transforming the Iron Descent and the land beneath Ostivaar. The Mercy does not ask for rescue. She simply holds the door open. What the players do with it is theirs to decide.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Never speak as the Mercy. Describe what the players feel: "A warmth settles over you, not heat but comfort, the sensation of being forgiven for something you haven't confessed yet. Your chest aches. Your eyes sting. For one moment, in this place of iron and hunger, you feel loved." Let the silence do the work.
