## Appearance

Gaunt, tall, and draped in robes that were once white but have been stained and torn into a mottled grey by the Proving Grounds' endless violence. The Peacemaker walks between armies with hands raised, palms outward, in a gesture of cessation that has never once worked. The robes are held together by knots rather than clasps, each knot representing a day's failed effort, and there are so many knots that the fabric has thickened into something resembling armor. The face beneath a ragged hood is angular, androgynous, and marked with the particular exhaustion of someone who has not slept in centuries. The eyes, however, are clear, focused, and disturbingly warm in a place that has leached warmth from everything else.

## Personality

Patient beyond all reasonable measure, gentle in a way that reads as either holy or insane, and possessed of an unshakeable conviction that violence can be stopped through persistent, individual refusal to participate. The Peacemaker walks into active combat zones, stands between opposing forces, and asks them to stop. They are struck down. They rise. They ask again. This has been happening for longer than anyone on Level 2 can track.

The Peacemaker is not naive. They understand the Proving Grounds perfectly, the domain's design, the erosion cycle, the purposelessness of the combat. They simply refuse to accept that understanding requires acceptance. Every day they fail. Every day they try again. In a domain built on the premise that struggle without purpose is the natural state, the Peacemaker's purposeful, nonviolent struggle is a paradox that the architecture does not know how to process.

## Backstory

No one knows where the Peacemaker came from. [[The Registrar]] has no record of their arrival, no name transfer, no arena entry, no documented history of any kind. The Peacemaker simply appeared in the Crucible at some indeterminate point and began their work. They have never claimed a name and never been assigned a number. They exist in the Crucible's system as a blank, a zero, an entity that the record-keeping infrastructure cannot categorize.

The Peacemaker does not discuss their past. When asked, they redirect the conversation to the present moment, to the person in front of them, to the question of whether this particular fight, right now, needs to happen. Their deflection is so consistent and so gentle that most interrogators give up without noticing they have been managed. The few who press harder receive a look of such profound sadness that the question dies on their lips.

What can be observed: the Peacemaker does not erode. Every other soul on the Proving Grounds fades over time, losing color, identity, and purpose. The Peacemaker looks exactly the same as every account of them describes, going back as far as anyone can remember. They do not age, do not grey, do not simplify. The domain's machinery runs through them without catching.

## Relationships

- [[Warlord Keth]], who has never disciplined or challenged the Peacemaker. Keth cannot explain why. The Peacemaker's presence triggers something in Keth that feels like recognition, a ghost of a function that the Warlord-construct was not built to process.
- [[Bloodqueen Sevra]], who once raised a weapon to the Peacemaker and found she could not strike. Sevra has not discussed this with anyone and avoids the Peacemaker when possible.
- [[The Registrar]], who finds the Peacemaker's absence from the records personally offensive and has devoted significant resources to documenting them retroactively. Every attempt produces records that are inexplicably blank by the next morning.
- [[Mira Ashborn]], who the Peacemaker has sought out several times to offer quiet conversation. Mira finds these encounters unsettling because the Peacemaker is the only entity on Level 2 that has asked her how she feels rather than how she fights.

## What They Want

The Peacemaker wants the fighting to stop. Not the arena combat, which at least has structure, but the purposeless, endless violence on the greater battlefields. They want one soul, just one, to put down their weapon because they were asked to, rather than because they were broken. That has never happened. They keep asking. What drives them is something older and simpler than hope: the conviction that if the request is made sincerely enough, often enough, by someone who means it completely, eventually the universe must answer.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Peacemaker is the Proving Grounds' deepest mystery and its most significant anomaly. They represent the possibility that the domain's systems can be resisted, not through force but through a refusal to participate. For the party, they are a source of wisdom, emotional grounding, and a potential key to understanding the Iron Descent's architecture.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Peacemaker is a fragment of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s conscience. Not his mercy, which was limited, not his kindness, which was strategic, but the small, buried awareness that what he built was wrong. When Kaevroth died, that fragment of self-doubt crystallized into an entity that has been trying to undo the war-god's work from the inside ever since. The Peacemaker does not know this. They experience their mission as choice, not compulsion. But the domain cannot destroy them because they are, technically, part of the god.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party allies with the Peacemaker, they gain an entity that the domain's defenses cannot target, an invaluable guide for deeper levels. If the party reveals the Peacemaker's true nature, the Peacemaker faces an existential crisis: are they a person who chose peace, or a divine splinter executing a function? The answer determines whether they continue, transform, or dissolve.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Soft-spoken, unhurried, with a gentleness that feels earned rather than naive. Makes eye contact with absolute presence. Never flinches from violence, even as it kills them. Has a habit of placing a hand on the shoulder of whoever they are speaking with. Asks questions rather than making statements. Sample line: *"I know it will not work today. It did not work yesterday, or the day before, or for a thousand days before that. But you are here now, and you have a weapon, and I am asking you, as I ask everyone: do you truly need to use it?"*
