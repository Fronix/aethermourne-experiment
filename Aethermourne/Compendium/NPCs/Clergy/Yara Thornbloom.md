## Appearance

Yara's appearance is the first sign that something in the Thornwild is not right. Her age shifts with the Cycle's phases. During the life-phase, she is a woman in her late thirties: sharp-featured, dark-haired with streaks of living green, eyes bright and restless. During the death-phase, she is ancient: skin papery and translucent, hair bone-white, shoulders bent under weight that is not physical. The transition between these states takes minutes. Watching it happen is deeply unsettling.

She wears layered robes of Thornwild make, living vine woven with preserved deadwood fiber, that shift color with her age. Her hands are stained green-black from years of dual-rite work. She carries a staff of transition-wood, a material that exists permanently between living and dead, warm on one side, cold on the other. Her voice does not change with her age. It is always steady, always tired, always certain.

## Personality

Patient, precise, and quietly desperate. Yara speaks with the care of someone who has learned that words matter because time is short. She does not waste sentences. She does not tolerate evasion. She will answer any question honestly, even the ones that hurt, because she believes dishonesty is a luxury the Thornwild cannot afford.

Beneath the composure is exhaustion. The dual awareness that makes her the best Cycle-reader alive is a constant pressure, two divine pulses beating against each other inside her skull, every moment of every day. She sleeps poorly. She eats when reminded. She reads the Cycle from the Perch at [[Cyclethorn]] with the focus of someone who knows that a missed reading could kill everyone she has sworn to protect.

She laughs rarely, but when she does, it is sudden and genuine, usually at something absurd, because the Thornwild is absurd, and sometimes the only sane response to the impossible is laughter.

## Backstory

Born in the [[The Verdant Marches|Verdant Marches]] to a minor clan near the Scarwall, Yara showed communion talent early and was taken in by [[The Greensingers]]. She trained as a Branch-Speaker and was considered gifted, particularly in sensing disturbances in [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s root-network. But she could feel something else, a pull from the northwest, faint and persistent, that no other Greensinger could detect.

At twenty-three, she walked into the Thornwild to find the source of the pull. She found the Cycle. The simultaneous presence of [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s growth and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s death opened something in her perception that she could not close. She could feel both divine pulses, distinct and simultaneous, and reading their interaction was like reading weather: complex, demanding, and essential.

She never went back. The Greensingers consider her a loss, not quite a traitor but not quite forgiven. She founded [[The Cyclekeepers]] with a handful of other exiles who shared fragments of her sensitivity, and for thirty years she has guided the Thornwild's communities through the Cycle's rhythms. The cost has been her body, which the dual awareness uses as a conduit, aging and de-aging her in sync with the forces she monitors. She has accepted this. She has not accepted the acceleration. Something is changing, something beyond the Cycle's normal variation, and she cannot identify it. For the first time in thirty years, she is afraid.

## Relationships

- **[[Root-Speaker Fennara]]**: Respect tempered by concern. Yara admires Fennara's courage but worries that dual-channeling pushes further than the human body can sustain. They argue about method, never about purpose.
- **[[Kael Duskborne]]**: Deep trust. Kael handles the Thornwild's physical defense so Yara can focus on the Cycle. They coordinate daily and disagree rarely. She worries about him, though she would not say so.
- **[[Elder Rosk]]**: Distant mentor. Rosk trained Yara as a Greensinger. He does not approve of what she became, but he does not condemn it either. They have not spoken in years. She misses his counsel.
- **[[The Bloom-Wraith]]**: Obsessive interest. Yara believes the Bloom-Wraith holds the key to stabilizing the Cycle, but she cannot get close enough to confirm it. The Bonewood repels her, as if the Cycle itself does not want her there.

## What They Want

Yara wants to understand the Cycle's acceleration and stop it before it kills the communities she has spent her life protecting. She would tell you this directly if asked.

What she cannot articulate is her deeper need: to believe that the Thornwild's existence has meaning, that the collision of life and death produces something more than suffering, that the balance she has devoted her life to maintaining is worth the cost. The acceleration threatens not just lives but her faith in the work itself.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Primary quest-giver and information source for Thornwild arcs in Acts II-IV. She directs PCs toward the Cycle's acceleration, the Bloom-Wraith, and eventually the discovery of Serith's interference.
>
> **Key Secret.** Yara has felt a "third pulse" in the Cycle for the past six months, something that is neither growth nor death. She has told no one because she does not know what it is, and admitting its existence would mean the Cycle is more complex than her entire order's understanding accounts for. The third pulse is [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s influence.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs help her identify the third pulse, she becomes a critical intelligence asset in the broader campaign against the Second Silence. If they do not, the acceleration continues until the Cycle breaks, and Yara's body fails under the strain of trying to read a system that no longer follows rules.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks precisely, never wastes words. Pauses before answering, not for drama but because she is genuinely considering. Her age shifts mid-conversation during stressful moments, and she ignores it. Voice: calm, measured, slightly hoarse. Sample line: "The Cycle turned three hours early today. That has never happened. I need you to understand what 'never' means in a place where everything happens twice."
