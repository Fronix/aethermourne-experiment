## Appearance

Fennwick is small, hunched, and older than he should be, which is to say he looks ancient despite being sustained by centuries of skimmed years. His frame is bent forward as if permanently leaning toward a counter, and his hands are always moving, adjusting pockets, smoothing lapels, fidgeting with the clasps of pouches that seem to multiply each time you look at his coat. The coat itself is remarkable, a voluminous garment of patched leather and iron-thread fabric containing more pockets than any garment should geometrically permit. His face is deeply lined, his eyes small and bright and perpetually narrowed in an expression that hovers between amusement and calculation. His teeth are iron-capped, a sign of long residency in Greedhollow, and they click faintly when he talks. He smells of old ink and warm metal.

## Personality

Fennwick never lies. This is both his professional code and his most effective weapon. He tells the truth selectively, arranging facts like a merchant arranges wares, placing the most attractive pieces at eye level and the critical details on the bottom shelf where you have to bend to see them. He is sociable, chatty even, filling conversations with anecdotes, observations, and tangential details that seem like rambling but are actually careful revelations calibrated to make you ask the right questions, the ones whose answers he can sell. He is not malicious. He genuinely enjoys the craft of information exchange. He simply cannot conceive of sharing knowledge without receiving knowledge in return. It would be like exhaling without inhaling. Unnatural.

## Backstory

Fennwick has been in Greedhollow so long that he has become part of its furniture, as much a fixture of the Market as the Ledger Spire or the Hollow Exchange. He arrived in the early centuries after the treasury became the Market, one of the first souls to recognize that brokering, standing between buyer and seller and taking a cut, was more sustainable than trading directly. His innovation was simple: he charged his percentage in years of life rather than intent. Every transaction he facilitated added time to his existence, and over centuries those skimmed years accumulated into something extraordinary. By the Market's accounting, Fennwick is the oldest living thing on Level 3, sustained entirely by the temporal residue of other people's deals.

The years have given him something else: knowledge. Fennwick has brokered trades touching every level of the Iron Descent. He knows something about the Ashfield's ecology, the Crucible's naming rituals, the Market's hidden mechanisms, and the deeper levels that most souls have never seen. He trades this information as his primary commodity, secret for secret, piece for piece, never giving more than he receives and never receiving less than he gives. He has survived longer than entities far more powerful than himself by making himself useful to everyone and indispensable to no one.

## Relationships

- **[[Dealmaker Syra Voss]]**, his most important client and the person he is most careful around. Fennwick knows Syra is something other than what she appears. He has not determined what, exactly, but his information network has accumulated enough anomalies in her history to suggest she is not a mortal soul. He has not sold this observation to anyone because he has not yet determined what it is worth, and because some part of him suspects that selling it would be the last thing he ever did.
- **[[Merchant-Prince Avarek]]**, his most reliable source of income. Fennwick handles Avarek's cross-level trades, taking his percentage from each transaction. He knows Avarek's empire is hollow. He has never told him, because Avarek's delusion generates more transactions than any real enterprise could, and Fennwick's percentage is always real regardless.
- **[[Coinless Marta]]**, a trading partner of a different sort. Marta and Fennwick exchange information, secret for secret, operating in the narrow space where his professional ethics and her subversive purpose overlap. He respects her methods. He does not share her goals.
- **[[The Assessor]]**, who provides impartial arbitration for Fennwick's most complex multi-party trades. Fennwick considers the Assessor the only truly honest entity in Greedhollow, which is both a compliment and a statement about everyone else.
- **[[Ren Twice-Sold]]**, whose early trades Fennwick brokered. He does not feel guilt about this, the trades were fair by Market standards, but he feels something adjacent to it, a faint discomfort when Ren's blurred edges catch the lantern-light.

## What They Want

Fennwick wants to keep existing. The skimmed years sustain him, and without a steady flow of brokered transactions he would age rapidly and cease. He would frame this as a love of his craft, a broker's dedication to the art of the deal. What actually drives him is a fear of ending that has grown proportional to his unnatural lifespan. The longer he lives, the more terrified he becomes of stopping, and the more aggressively he brokers, which extends his life further, which deepens the fear. He is caught in a loop he designed himself and cannot exit without confronting the question he has avoided for centuries: what was he before he was useful?

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Fennwick is the party's information vendor on Level 3. He knows something about every level of the Descent and will trade any of it for information of equal value. He is the most natural quest-giver in Greedhollow, connecting PCs to hooks across multiple levels. He is also a subtle warning: his survival strategy is a trap dressed as success.
>
> **Key Secret.** Fennwick has brokered a deal he does not fully understand. Several decades ago, an entity from Level 6 or deeper contracted him to arrange passage for specific mortal souls through the Iron Market. The terms were generous, the payment in centuries of life. Fennwick accepted without asking why. The mortal souls described in the contract match the PCs' descriptions exactly, and the contract predates their arrival by years.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs confront Fennwick about the contract, he cooperates, frightened by the implications of a deal he cannot fully parse. If they threaten him, he retreats into the Market's infrastructure, becoming unreachable. If the Market decays and transactions slow, Fennwick begins aging rapidly, his accumulated years burning off like fuel. He becomes desperate, willing to trade secrets he has hoarded for centuries, including what he knows about Syra Voss's nature.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speak quickly, with frequent tangents that circle back to the point. Fidget constantly, hands moving between pockets. Click iron-capped teeth when thinking. Lean in when interested, pull back when calculating. Never make a statement without implying there is more he could say. Sample line: *"Interesting, interesting. You know, that reminds me of something I heard from a fellow on Level Five, but that's a different conversation, one we could have, of course, assuming you have something equally interesting to share. Do you? I suspect you do."*
