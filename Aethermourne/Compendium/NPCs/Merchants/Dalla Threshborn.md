# Dalla Threshborn

## Appearance

A compact woman with clever hands and a face built for bargaining, all sharp angles and quick expressions. Her clothing is the Ashfield's universal grey, but she wears it like she chose it, layered and belted in a way that suggests organized pockets rather than desperation. Her hair is pulled back with a clasp made from a polished iron shard, the only accessory in Rustgate that could be called decorative. Her eyes have not yet fully lost their color, a faint hazel that she considers proof she has not been here long. She has been here much longer than she thinks.

## Personality

Relentlessly optimistic in a way that borders on pathological. Dalla approaches the Ashfield as a problem to be solved, a puzzle with a solution she has not yet found rather than a trap with no exit. This attitude is infectious and dangerous in equal measure. She is not cruel, she genuinely believes her exit schemes could work, and she genuinely believes the next one will be the breakthrough. The fact that she charges desperate souls everything they have for plans that fail is not malice. It is the Ashfield's ambient ambition filtered through an entrepreneurial mind. She is the whisper's most successful convert, not because she descends, but because she embodies the promise that effort and cleverness can overcome the domain's design.

## Backstory

Dalla arrived in the Ashfield as a scholar, having traced planar theory through mortal-world libraries to a ritual that, she believed, would allow controlled passage into [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s domain and back. The passage in worked perfectly. The passage out did not. She spent her first weeks convinced the return ritual had a minor flaw she could correct. Weeks became months. Months became something she stopped counting. Each failure refined her approach, and each refinement attracted clients, souls who would pay anything for the hope of escape.

She now runs Rustgate's only organized trade operation, exchanging mortal-world artifacts, salvaged iron, information, and exit plans in a network that keeps the settlement's economy functional. The exit plans are her primary product. She has devised forty-seven distinct escape methods. All forty-seven have failed, some spectacularly. She is currently developing number forty-eight, which involves mapping the resonance patterns of the Warden's Nail and using them to locate a thin point's mortal-side anchor. It will not work. She does not know that.

## Relationships

- [[Erys Ironfall]], who allows her operation because Rustgate needs a merchant and because hope, even false hope, keeps people from descending. They have an unspoken agreement: he does not expose her failures, and she does not undermine his authority.
- [[Scavenger Polk]], her primary supplier. Polk brings her salvage from the outer Ashfield and she converts it into trade goods. Their relationship is purely transactional, which suits them both.
- [[The Ashen Crier]], an occasional source of information. Dalla has learned to filter the Crier's babble for useful fragments, a skill she considers her most valuable asset.
- [[Old Rust]], who she visits when her optimism wavers. Old Rust has seen everything fail and is still here. Dalla finds this either comforting or terrifying depending on the day.

## What They Want

Dalla wants to escape the Ashfield and return to the mortal world with proof that controlled planar travel is possible. That is what she tells herself every morning. What actually drives her is the need to be right, to prove that her original theory was sound and that the failure was a solvable error rather than a fundamental misunderstanding of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s domain.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Dalla is a source of information, goods, and false leads. She can equip the party with Ashfield survival knowledge and point them toward locations and contacts on Level 1. Her exit schemes, while ultimately futile, contain fragments of genuine planar theory that could be useful to a party with their own means of travel.
>
> **Key Secret.** Exit plan number thirty-one actually worked. She opened a passage back to the mortal world, stood at its threshold, and could not step through. The Ashfield's ambition had already taken root in her. Leaving would mean admitting she had wasted years, that her theory was flawed, that she was wrong. She closed the passage, told herself it was unstable, and moved on to plan thirty-two. She has never told anyone.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party confronts her with the truth about plan thirty-one, Dalla will either break down and ask for help leaving, or double down and insist the party is wrong. If helped to leave, she emerges into the mortal world decades older than she expected, a stranger in a world that moved on without her.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Fast-talking, enthusiastic, gestures constantly. Speaks about escape plans the way an inventor talks about prototypes, with genuine excitement about the engineering even when the product does not work. Never admits failure, only "data collection." Sample line: *"Forty-seven is not a number of failures, it is a number of things I now know do not work. Forty-eight, however, the resonance pattern is completely different this time."*
