# Orna Dallow

## Appearance

Orna is in her late fifties, broad-shouldered and strong in the way of someone who has worked with timber her entire life. Her hands are thick with calluses, sawdust permanently embedded under her nails and in the creases of her grey-streaked hair. Her face is weathered and open, deeply creased from decades of squinting at ship ribs in coastal sunlight. She wears practical work clothes, heavy canvas and leather, with steel-toed boots that have outlasted three resoles. Her one vanity is a coral-stone pendant carved in the shape of a ship's prow, the Dallow family emblem, passed down through three generations of shipwrights.

## Personality

Methodical, proud, and honest to the point of bluntness. Orna takes quality personally, a flaw in a Dallow ship is a flaw in her. She is slow to trust, slower to forgive, and utterly incapable of cutting corners. She has no patience for politics or flattery, and she judges people by their work before their words. Beneath the gruff exterior is a deep, quiet love for her craft and her family, though she would sooner eat a barnacle than say so aloud.

## Backstory

The Dallow family has built ships in [[Keel's Landing]] since the town's founding, and Orna is the latest in an unbroken line of master shipwrights. She learned the trade from her mother, who learned from hers, each generation passing down not just technique but a near-instinctive understanding of wood, weight, and water. Orna took over the yard at thirty-two when her mother's hands finally gave out, and she has run it with the same uncompromising standards ever since.

She raised her daughter in the yard, the girl learning to read grain patterns before she could read letters. The Dallow ships are the most reliable in the Reach, sturdy, traditional, and expensive. They do not innovate. They do not need to. When a Dallow hull goes to sea, it comes back. That reputation is worth more than any Concord contract, and Orna guards it like a dragon guards gold.

Recently, the timber arriving from [[The Verdant Marches]] has begun to trouble her. The grain spirals instead of running straight, the wood warps in ways seasoned timber should not, and pieces crack along curves that look almost deliberate. She has filed formal complaints with the [[Concord of Tides]], suspecting supplier fraud. The truth is far worse, and she can feel it in her bones even if she cannot name it.

## Relationships

- **Corvin Tyren**, rival shipwright of the Tyren family. She considers him talented but reckless, and his experiments with Ashite-reinforced hulls offend her traditionalist sensibilities. Their rivalry is professional, not personal, but she would never admit to respecting him.
- **Marra Kael**, fellow shipwright, the quiet third competitor. Orna respects Marra's patience and steady output, though she finds the woman's willingness to pick up dropped contracts slightly undignified.
- **Edric Tyne**, dockworker foreman. He brought her evidence of [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] sabotage in her own yard, and she trusts his loyalty. She is quietly furious about the carved symbols and wants answers before involving the Concord.
- **[[Maren Selk]]**, First Tidekeeper. Orna respects Selk's competence but resents the Concord's bureaucratic indifference to her timber complaints.

## What They Want

To build the best ships in [[The Hollowed Reach]] and pass the Dallow yard to her daughter with its reputation intact. More immediately, she wants to understand why the Marches timber is arriving wrong, because if she cannot trust her materials, she cannot trust her ships, and a Dallow ship that fails at sea is unthinkable.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Orna is the PCs' entry point into the Wrong Timber plotline and the Keel's Landing sabotage thread. She is a reliable ally, practical, honest, and motivated by craftsmanship rather than politics.
>
> **Key Secret.** Two ships already launched with corrupted timber are currently serving in the Tidefleet. Their crews report the hulls groaning in calm water, subtle course drift, and sailors below decks dreaming of deep water. Orna does not yet know this, but she suspects something is wrong with her recent builds.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs help her investigate the timber, she becomes a staunch ally with resources and connections throughout the shipbuilding community. If the sabotage in her yard is exposed publicly before she can control the narrative, the Dallow reputation takes a devastating hit, and Orna's pride may drive her to rash action.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in short, declarative sentences. Uses nautical and carpentry metaphors without thinking about it. Taps her coral pendant when she is worried. Never raises her voice, but her silence is louder than most people's shouting. *"I don't need theories. I need someone to bring me a clean plank of Marches oak and explain why I can't get one."*
