## Appearance

Kora is broad-shouldered and compact, built like someone who has spent a lifetime hauling metal and swinging hammers, which she has, many lifetimes over. Her skin is dark bronze, mottled with old burn scars that have silvered with age, and her forearms are corded with muscle that has never softened despite millennia without proper food or rest. Her hair, once black, has been bleached white by forge-heat and hangs in a thick braid down her back, wrapped in wire to keep it from the flames. She wears a leather apron over iron-cloth, its surface pocked with spark-burns and stained with slag. Her hands are the most notable thing about her: they are pristine, unmarked, as if the fire that scars everything else refuses to touch her fingers. She does not know why, and she does not like to talk about it.

## Personality

Kora is practical, blunt, and deeply suspicious of anything she cannot explain, which increasingly includes herself. She takes fierce pride in her craft and measures her worth by the quality of her work, a metric that has become terrifying now that her creations are the only ones that endure. She is generous with her skills, teaching other workers techniques that might help their pieces last even an hour longer, but she keeps her distance emotionally. Warmth feels like a trap in the Forge Eternal, a sign that you have stopped fighting and started belonging. Kora is fighting. She is not sure what she is fighting against anymore, but she has not stopped.

## Backstory

In life, Kora was an artificer in the mortal world, a smith of considerable talent who made weapons and tools not for war but for communities: plow-blades, well-chains, door-hinges. Good, honest work made with love for the people who would use it. She died defending her forge from soldiers who wanted it for a warlord's campaign, choosing to destroy her tools rather than let them be turned to conquest. The iron she shattered cursed her: [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s domain claimed her soul for the crime of destroying weapons, even ones she had made herself.

In the Forge Eternal, Kora discovered that her craft had not diminished. She could still forge with the same skill and care she had in life. But here, unlike every other worker, her creations did not dissolve. The first piece that survived past the six-hour mark was a simple nail, unremarkable in every way except that it was still there the next morning. Then a hinge. Then a blade. Kora's work endures, and no one, least of all Kora, understands why. She suspects it is because she forges with the same love she always did, a love for the object and the hands that will hold it. If that is true, it means the Forge Eternal has accepted her love as a valid substitute for divine will, and that thought terrifies her more than anything else in this place.

## Relationships

- [[Forge-Warden Irontooth]], the level's overseer. He covets Kora's talent and has tried to claim her as his personal smith. She refuses, loudly and repeatedly. Their relationship is a cold standoff: he cannot compel her because her work is too valuable to disrupt, and she cannot defy him openly because the Slag Pits are always hungry.
- [[Apprentice Nil]], the Pattern Library cataloguer. Kora likes Nil's quiet curiosity and treats her as something between a student and a younger sister. She suspects Nil is not what she appears to be but has decided that whatever Nil is, she deserves kindness.
- [[The Slag Mother]], the ancient soul beneath the Foundry. Kora trades finished pieces for information, bartering enduring metalwork for the Slag Mother's knowledge of the forge's deep architecture. Their relationship is transactional but built on mutual respect.
- [[Bellows-Keeper Tharn]], the keeper of the forges' breath. Kora finds Tharn's obsessive calibrations exhausting but cannot deny that her best work happens when the bellows are singing in tune. She has not told him this.

## What They Want

Kora wants to leave the Forge Eternal with her craft and her soul intact. She believes that if she can forge something of sufficient quality, something truly perfect, the domain will release her, the way a story ends when the hero completes the final task. What drives her beneath that hope is simpler: she wants proof that love is stronger than divine compulsion, that the reason her work endures is because she chose to care, not because the domain chose her.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Kora is the party's best potential ally on Level 4, a mortal soul with genuine skill and genuine desire to escape. She can forge items from the Forge Eternal's materials that will function in the mortal world, making her invaluable for any party seeking divine-forged equipment.
>
> **Key Secret.** Kora's work endures because her hands absorbed a trace of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s creative essence when she shattered her mortal forge. The iron she destroyed had been blessed by a war-priest of Kaevroth without her knowledge. When she broke it, the blessing transferred to her. She is, unknowingly, forging with a sliver of divine will, just enough to make things last. If she learns this, she will have to confront the possibility that her love was never the reason. The divine residue was.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party helps her escape, she becomes a powerful mortal artificer with the ability to work divine iron. If they reveal the truth about her hands, she will either accept the divine gift and forge with renewed purpose, or reject it and lose the ability entirely. If [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] discovers Kora's talent, the goddess will want her as a forge-slave for her own designs.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks like someone used to shouting over hammers: direct, loud, no wasted words. Punctuates statements by tapping her hammer on the nearest surface. Her voice softens noticeably when talking about craft or the things she made in life.
> *"You want a blade that lasts? So does everyone. Difference is, I can make one. The question is what you will do with it, because I will not forge for someone who does not deserve the metal."*
