# Torben Greaves

## Appearance

A broad-shouldered man in his early forties with ruddy skin, a thick red-brown beard going grey at the edges, and shrewd blue eyes set deep in a weathered face. He dresses well for a smuggler, clean linen shirts, a good leather coat, boots that cost more than most fenfolk earn in a season, but there's mud under his fingernails and calluses on his palms. He carries a knife on his belt and a small crossbow in his coat, both well-maintained. A thin scar runs from his left ear to his jaw, a memento from his first year on the Sluice.

## Personality

Affable, calculating, and more sentimental than he wants anyone to know. Torben plays the charming rogue because it's good for business, but underneath the performance is a man who thinks three moves ahead and sleeps poorly. He is generous with his crew, fair with his clients, and utterly ruthless with his competitors. He tells himself he chose profit over principle. He is less certain of this than he was ten years ago.

## Backstory

Torben and [[Elowen Greaves]] grew up in the Hewn caste in [[The Ashen Dominion#Ostivaar|Ostivaar]], children of a stone-cutter and a seamstress. They were close. Elowen was the idealist, always reading forbidden texts, always asking why the castes existed. Torben was the pragmatist, always figuring out how to get things, food, coin, favours, that the caste system said they couldn't have.

When Elowen began organizing what would become the [[Unbound Congregation]], she asked Torben to join. He refused. Not because he didn't believe in the cause, but because he'd seen what the Dominion did to dissenters and calculated that he'd be more useful alive and free than dead or imprisoned. They argued. It was the last real conversation they had.

Torben left Ostivaar with a stolen barge and a manifest of goods that didn't belong to him. He followed the trade routes south, discovered the Smuggler's Sluice, and found his calling. Within five years he was the Sluice's dominant operator, running goods between [[The Ashen Dominion]] and [[The Hollowed Reach]] with an efficiency that made him wealthy and an ethical flexibility that made him essential.

He settled at [[The Drowning Bridge]] because it was the perfect base: central, defensible, and on nobody's territory. He built a network of barge captains, warehouse operators, and bribed officials that spans both borders. He tells himself it's just business. But he smuggles Ashite to [[Maren Duskwater]] at cost, he's never turned away a refugee who couldn't pay passage, and he keeps a letter from Elowen, unopened, in the bottom of his desk drawer. It arrived two years ago. He hasn't been able to bring himself to read it.

## Relationships

- **[[Elowen Greaves]]** , his sister, the leader of the [[Unbound Congregation]] in [[The Ashen Dominion]]. They haven't spoken in over a decade. He follows her work through informants and is privately terrified that her activism will get her killed.
- **[[Maren Duskwater]]** , his primary client for Ashite smuggling. Their relationship is professional, built on contracts and careful boundaries. He respects her intelligence. She doesn't entirely trust his motives.
- **[[Nettie Bridgeborn]]** , his most valuable intelligence asset. Nettie knows everything that happens on the Bridge, and Torben pays well for the privilege of hearing it. He suspects she tells him only what she wants him to know.
- **[[Sera Gloomhollow]]** , his enemy. Her bandits are bleeding his operation. He has tried bribery, negotiation, and threats. Nothing has worked. He is considering more permanent solutions.
- **[[Captain Hale Ashmark]]** , his most dangerous problem. Ashmark's agents are watching the Bridge, and Torben knows it. He has maintained deniability so far, but the Dominion officer is not stupid.

## What They Want

Security. Torben wants his operation to run smoothly, his people to be safe, and his position to be unassailable. Beneath that, he wants to stop feeling guilty about leaving his sister behind, and he is beginning to suspect that the only way to do that is to open her letter.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Information broker, quest-giver, and morally grey ally. Torben can provide PCs with resources, smuggling routes, and intelligence about both the Dominion and the Reach. He's also a source of interpersonal drama: the Elowen connection ties him to the Dominion plotline, and his Ashite smuggling ties him to Maren's research.
>
> **Key Secret.** Elowen's unopened letter contains a plea for help. The [[Unbound Congregation]] is being infiltrated by [[The Cinders]], and Elowen needs someone outside the Dominion to carry evidence of Cinder atrocities to neutral parties. She chose Torben because he's the only person she trusts who isn't being watched. He doesn't know any of this because he hasn't read the letter.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** Torben begins as a self-interested smuggler. The PCs can push him toward opening the letter and reconnecting with Elowen, which transforms him from a local operator into a key link between the fenlands and the Dominion resistance. Alternatively, if pressured, he retreats further into cynicism and becomes an obstacle.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks with easy charm, keeps his hands visible, makes self-deprecating jokes. His smile doesn't always reach his eyes. He deflects personal questions with business offers. When caught off guard emotionally, he goes very quiet and changes the subject. Never mentions his sister first.
>
> *"I move things from where they are to where people want them. That's all. What, you expected a philosophy? I'm a barge man with good timing."*
