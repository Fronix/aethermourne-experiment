## Appearance

An ancient dwarf, over four hundred years old, with a white beard that reaches his belt and hands permanently stained the grey-black of forge carbon. Thick spectacles sit on a nose that has been broken at least twice. He is broad even by dwarven standards, built like a furnace, and moves with the slow deliberation of someone who has learned that rushing ruins metalwork and conversation alike. His clothing is practical, heavy leather apron over sturdy wool, and he smells of coal smoke and hot iron. His eyes, behind those spectacles, are sharp and assessing. He looks at your gear before he looks at your face.

## Personality

Gruff, practical, and philosophically stubborn in the way only a dwarf who has outlived entire human dynasties can be. Torren treats the craft as sacred and politics as noise. He is blunt to the point of rudeness, dismissive of flattery, and openly contemptuous of anyone who treats divine materials as mere commodities. Beneath the curmudgeon exterior, he is deeply sentimental, moved to tears by exceptional craftsmanship, quietly fond of young people who show genuine dedication. He does not volunteer this warmth. It must be earned.

## Backstory

Torren was already old when he was elected Guildmaster by the Master Craftsmen of [[The Ember Guild]], a position he has held for longer than most Guild members have been alive. He is, by wide consensus, the finest living smith in Aethermourne, a master of [[Cosmology and Magic#Ashite|Ashite]] work and one of the few crafters who has successfully shaped raw [[Cosmology and Magic|Kaevroth]] iron without it detonating. His election was unanimous. No one else was even nominated.

Under his leadership, the Guild has maintained its ancient neutrality through civil wars, religious purges, and the slow territorial expansion of [[The Ashen Dominion]]. He travels constantly between Guild halls across the continent, insisting that "the headquarters is where I am." He is currently based in [[The Ashen Dominion#Ostivaar|Ostivaar]], working from the Ashmarket, where he is recognized as one of the Dominion's finest Ashite-workers.

His personal obsession is [[Cosmology and Magic|Kaevroth's]] iron fragments. He maintains a private registry of every known fragment's location, a document he considers both his greatest responsibility and his heaviest burden. He knows this registry makes him a target. He has taken extraordinary precautions, but he is also realistic enough to know that one old dwarf, however stubborn, cannot hold out forever against the forces that want what he knows.

## Relationships

- [[Brunhild Ironheart|Thane Brunhild Ironheart]], Old friend and frequent adversary. They share a devotion to [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] legacy but have been arguing for eighty years about whether the Guild has political obligations. Their disputes are legendary and, by all accounts, productive.
- [[Corrin Ashveil|Factor Corrin Ashveil]], The Guild's historian, personally appointed by Torren to maintain the Ember Archive. Torren trusts Corrin's loyalty absolutely but is beginning to sense that the archivist has doubts about the Guild's secrecy.
- [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]], The Guild's fragment monitor in [[The Ironmarch]]. Gellen reveres Torren's craftsmanship but is increasingly frustrated by the Guildmaster's silence on his field reports. Torren may not even be receiving them.
- [[Polara Facet]], One of the few people whose standards Torren considers comparable to his own. They share a mutual, unspoken respect that neither would dream of acknowledging aloud.
- [[Elowen Greaves]], Leader of the [[Unbound Congregation|Unbound Congregation]]. Torren is quietly sympathetic to her cause, though he would never say so publicly. The caste system offends his conviction that skill, not birth, determines worth.

## What They Want

Torren wants to protect the craft and keep [[Cosmology and Magic|Kaevroth's]] fragments out of any faction's hands, including those who claim good intentions. His deeper need is simpler and sadder: he wants to believe that neutrality is still enough, that the Guild can remain above the coming storm without becoming complicit in it. He is beginning to suspect it cannot.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Craftsman, information source, and moral test. Torren can forge divine-material equipment for the PCs and holds the Kaevroth fragment registry, one of the campaign's most important MacGuffins. Earning his trust is a significant milestone. He appears across multiple acts as a recurring anchor.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Kaevroth fragment registry is being hunted by both [[Delric Mourne]] (through [[The Cinders]]) and [[The Whisperer]] (through [[The Veil Unbound]]). Torren knows he is a target but does not know how close both networks have already gotten. He is not compromised by [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], his neutrality is genuine.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** As [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] threat becomes undeniable, the Guild's neutrality becomes untenable. The central question of Torren's arc is whether he will break tradition and take a side. If the PCs earn his trust, he shares registry information selectively. Full access requires proving they understand the stakes, a very high bar. If the PCs fail to reach him, the registry eventually falls to one of the two intelligence networks hunting it.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Mumbles into his beard. Evaluates people by examining their gear first, their face second. Calls everyone "lad" or "lass" regardless of age or species. Will talk for hours about metallurgy if allowed. Cries openly at truly exceptional craftsmanship. Voice is a low, gravelly rumble, like stones grinding together.
> *"Show me your blade. No, not the fancy one. The one you actually use. ...Aye, that tells me everything I need to know about you."*
