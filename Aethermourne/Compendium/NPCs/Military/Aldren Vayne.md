# Aldren Vayne

## Appearance

Late sixties, still broad-shouldered but going soft around the middle. White hair cropped short, a deeply tanned face seamed with decades of wind and salt. He walks with a slight limp from an old injury, a broken leg badly set after a storm twenty years ago that he refused to treat properly because he would not leave the quarterdeck. He wears the Tidefleet's blue-grey uniform at all times, even in Concord Hall, even at formal dinners. The uniform is always clean, always pressed, and always slightly out of place among the silks and embroidery of his fellow Tidekeepers.

## Personality

Blunt, dutiful, and deeply uncomfortable with politics. Vayne is a career sailor who rose through the ranks by being reliable rather than brilliant, and he has never pretended to be anything else. He is good at patrol, escort, logistics, and the routine work of keeping sea lanes open. He is less good at the extraordinary challenges the Reach now faces. Trench creatures do not fight like pirates. Black Tides do not respond to naval tactics. He knows his fleet is being asked to do things it was never designed for, and the knowledge sits in him like ballast water, heavy and impossible to pump out.

## Backstory

Vayne was born in [[Tidewall]]'s dockside quarter, the son of a shipwright. He enlisted in the [[Tidefleet]] at sixteen and spent the next four decades working his way from deck hand to admiral through steady competence, unbending honesty, and a refusal to play political games. He commanded escort convoys, anti-piracy patrols, and the fleet's training program. He was never flashy, never controversial, never the subject of tavern stories. He was simply always there, doing the work.

He was appointed Master of Ships twelve years ago, a position that made him a Tidekeeper on the [[Concord of Tides]]. The promotion was meant as a reward for long service, a comfortable final posting for a reliable old sailor. No one expected the Black Tides to accelerate, the Trench to stir, or the fleet to face threats that no amount of seamanship could answer.

Now Vayne sits in Concord Hall among politicians and merchants, arguing for funding he will not receive, filing reports that are quietly shelved, and watching his sailors face horrors that belong in deep-sea legends rather than patrol logs. He has served the Reach his entire life. He does not know how to stop, even as the institution he serves fails around him.

## Relationships

- **[[Maren Selk]]**, the First Tidekeeper. Vayne respects Selk's intelligence but wishes she would act more decisively. She, in turn, values his honesty but finds his political naivety exhausting.
- **[[Jessamine Kole]]**, the Master of Coin. Vayne despises her willingness to sacrifice lives for revenue. She considers him a sentimental relic. Their votes on the Concord are almost always opposed.
- **[[Lira Ashvane]]**, the Lantern-Keeper Commander. Vayne quietly admires Ashvane's directness and shares her frustration with Concord inaction. They have exchanged letters, careful and unofficial, about the worsening conditions at sea.
- **[[Captain Rook Tessavar]]**, the pirate captain. An unspoken arrangement: the Tidefleet stays out of [[Driftmere]]'s waters and Rook keeps the outer lanes clear of unaffiliated raiders. Vayne considers this pragmatic. He does not consider it honorable.
- **His sailors**, the people he cares about most. He knows every ship captain by name, attends every memorial service, and takes every death personally. This is his greatest strength and his heaviest burden.

## What They Want

Vayne wants to protect his sailors and do his duty, in that order. He would never admit the conflict between these goals, but it is eating him alive. His deeper, unspoken wish is to retire before things get worse, because he knows they will. He has served long enough to recognize the shape of a disaster before it arrives, and everything he sees tells him the Reach is heading toward something the Tidefleet cannot survive.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Vayne is an honest man inside a failing system. He will not suppress reports, but he will not amplify them either. His greatest weakness is institutional loyalty, trusting the system even as it crumbles. His greatest strength is genuine care for his people, and he can be motivated by threats to his sailors. He is a potential ally if the PCs can prove the scale of the crisis, but he will insist on working through official channels unless those channels are demonstrably compromised.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Tidefleet's desertion rate is climbing sharply, and Vayne is hiding it from the Concord. Sailors are abandoning their posts after encounters with Trench creatures, Black Tides, and the worsening conditions at sea. Two ships launched from [[Keel's Landing]] with corrupted timber are already in the fleet, their crews reporting anomalies: hulls groaning in calm water, ships drifting off course, sailors dreaming of deep water below decks.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs present hard evidence of the prison's failure, Vayne will face a crisis of loyalty: report to a Concord he knows is compromised, or break with the institution he has served his entire life. Without intervention, he will eventually be forced into retirement by the political Tidekeepers, silenced and sidelined at the worst possible moment. If the PCs earn his trust, he becomes a powerful ally with the full weight of the Tidefleet behind him.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in blunt, seamanlike language, short sentences, naval terminology, no embellishment. Stands with his hands clasped behind his back. Uncomfortable sitting in chairs that do not move with the waves. Clears his throat before saying something he considers important.
> *"I've lost eleven sailors this quarter to things that don't have names in any manual I was trained on. You want my help? Tell me something I can act on. Give me an enemy I can point a bow at."*
