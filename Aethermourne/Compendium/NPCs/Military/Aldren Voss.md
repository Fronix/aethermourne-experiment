# Flame Captain Aldren Voss

## Appearance

A stocky man in his mid-thirties with brown hair, warm hazel eyes, and callused hands from farm work before he enlisted. His face is unremarkable in the best way, the kind of face that blends into crowds and makes people trust him without thinking about why. He wears his [[Order of the Ashen Flame|Order]] armor with meticulous care, but there is no swagger to him, no parade-ground polish. He moves like a man who knows every street in his town and stops to talk to everyone. He smells of honest sweat, bread from the garrison ovens, and faintly of ink from the reports he writes late at night. His voice is steady, quiet, the voice of a man who has learned that shouting rarely solves problems.

## Personality

First impression: solid, approachable, thoughtful. Aldren is the kind of officer soldiers want to follow because he listens to them, knows their names, and treats garrison duty as service rather than punishment. He believes in the [[Order of the Ashen Flame|Order's]] mission to protect the Dominion but has grown increasingly uncomfortable with how that mission is being defined. Beneath the quiet competence is a man wrestling with a crisis of conscience, torn between his oath to the Order and his growing conviction that the caste system is a betrayal of [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] true vision of order serving the people, not grinding them down.

## Backstory

Aldren grew up in [[Greyfield]], the son of a Hewn-caste farmer who worked the fields around [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] Finger. He joined the [[Order of the Ashen Flame|Order]] at twenty, driven not by divine calling but by the simple need to support his family. The military salary allowed his younger brother to attend the Athenaeum at [[Ashveil]].

He rose slowly through the ranks, earned his Flame Captain commission at thirty, and requested assignment back to [[Greyfield]]. His superiors considered it a waste of his abilities, but Aldren saw it as a homecoming. He knows every family in the garrison town, attends their weddings, mourns their dead, and enforces the law with the lightest hand he can justify.

Three years ago, the [[Unbound Congregation]] began organizing in [[Greyfield]]. [[Elowen Greaves|Speaker Greaves]] herself visited to address a gathering of Hewn-caste workers at a barn outside town. Aldren was ordered to arrest the organizers. He read Greaves' pamphlets first. What he found there, arguments for reforming the caste system based on [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] original teachings, shook him. He declined to enforce the arrest warrants, citing insufficient evidence and the risk of inciting unrest. The [[The Cinders|Cinders]] noticed.

## Relationships

- **[[Varen Ashfeld]]**, growing problem. Ashfeld has recommended Aldren's reassignment to a remote garrison, and Aldren knows the Ashguard commander considers his Congregation sympathy a betrayal.
- **[[Sera Ironholt]]**, quiet support. Ironholt advised him to be more careful, and Aldren suspects she shares some of his doubts about the Order's direction.
- **[[Elowen Greaves]]**, dangerous respect. He has not met her personally since the barn gathering, but he reads everything she publishes and has quietly passed Congregation pamphlets to soldiers he trusts.
- **[[Petra Venn]]**, local ally. Petra organizes [[Unbound Congregation|Congregation]] study circles in [[Greyfield]], and Aldren has looked the other way more than once when patrols report suspicious gatherings.
- **[[Delric Mourne]]**, watching. Mourne has sent [[The Cinders|Cinder]] agents to [[Greyfield]] to monitor Aldren's loyalties. Aldren does not know he is under active surveillance.

## What They Want

Aldren wants to serve the Dominion and protect his people, but he wants to do it in a way that honors what he believes [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]] actually stood for, order as a framework for flourishing, not as an excuse for oppression. His deeper need is absolution, proof that declining the arrest warrants was the right choice, that his conscience is not just weakness, and that the Order can be reformed from within. If forced to choose between his oath and his principles, he does not know which he will choose, and the uncertainty terrifies him.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Internal Order dissenter, potential ally for PCs working with the [[Unbound Congregation]]. He represents the possibility that not all Order officers are ideological zealots.
>
> **Key Secret.** [[Delric Mourne|Mourne]] has compiled a file on Aldren's "unreliability" and is waiting for the right moment to arrest him on charges of treason. Mourne sees Aldren as a useful test case, arresting a popular officer will gauge whether the Congregation has enough support to resist publicly, or whether they will abandon one of their quiet allies to protect themselves.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If PCs earn his trust and provide evidence of the [[The Cinders|Cinders']] corruption, Aldren can become a crucial inside source on Order movements. If arrested, he faces a choice: recant publicly and betray the Congregation, or hold to his principles and become a martyr. Either outcome radicalizes the lower-caste soldiers under his command.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks plainly, no military jargon unless necessary. Knows everyone's name and uses it. Asks questions more than he gives orders. Rubs the back of his neck when uncomfortable, a tell his soldiers recognize. Sample line: *"I've read the arrest warrants. I've also read what these people are actually saying. Tell me which one you want me to enforce, the law or the fear."*
