## Appearance

A tall, powerfully built woman in her late fifties with short-cropped iron-grey hair and a burn scar running from her left temple to her jaw, earned in a Scarwall incursion fifteen years ago. She wears the Order's grey-and-white plate armor at nearly all times, maintained to a mirror shine that contrasts with the weathered face above it. Her eyes are dark brown, steady, and uncomfortably direct. She moves with the controlled economy of someone who has spent a lifetime training her body to be a weapon. Her hands are calloused from swordwork, not ceremony.

## Personality

Voss is discipline made flesh. She is blunt, decisive, and impatient with anything she considers evasion, which includes most forms of diplomacy. She genuinely believes [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] legacy demands order, and she has built her life around being the instrument of that order. She is brutal when necessary and principled in her own rigid way: she does not punish without cause, does not tolerate cruelty for its own sake among her troops, and holds herself to the same standards she imposes on others. She respects strength and conviction. She despises cowardice, indecision, and politics.

## Backstory

Voss rose through the Order's ranks on merit despite being born to the Hewn caste, a rarity that required her to be twice as good as every Flame-Bearer-born officer around her. She earned her commission at twenty-two, her first command at thirty, and the Commandership at forty-five after her predecessor died in a border skirmish with [[The Verdant Marches]]. She has spent the last decade stationed at [[Cinderholm]], watching the Scarwall grow ten feet per year toward her fortress and receiving nothing from [[Ostivaar]] but orders to wait.

She has fought border engagements, suppressed Hewn riots she privately sympathized with, and buried soldiers whose deaths she considers preventable. She has watched the Hierarch's decline from a distance with growing frustration. She has built the case for a preemptive strike against the Marches, believing [[Kaelith Thornborn]]'s unification of the clans represents an existential threat. The Hierarch's refusal to act is, in her view, not caution but cowardice.

What she has not done is question the Order's fundamental assumptions. Voss is exactly what she appears to be: a loyal, competent soldier with bad politics and good intentions. That is both her strength and her limitation.

## Relationships

- [[Hierarch Valdren III]], her nominal commander. She respects the office but has lost faith in the man. His refusal to authorize action against the Scarwall is a wound that festers.
- [[Delric Mourne]], head of [[The Cinders]]. She distrusts him instinctively and has begun running her own quiet intelligence operation using trusted veterans rather than Mourne's agents.
- [[Captain Daren Kolst]], garrison commander at [[Thornwall]]. A subordinate she trusts, one of the few officers who shares her frustration with Ostivaar's paralysis.
- [[Petra Venn]], an [[Unbound Congregation]] sympathizer in [[Ashveil]]. Voss has quietly arranged for a trusted veteran to warn Petra before any arrest. She does not support the Congregation, but she opposes the Cinders on principle.
- [[Kaelith Thornborn]], the Marchwarden she considers the Dominion's greatest external threat. Voss has never met her. She respects Thornborn's ability while believing she must be stopped.

## What They Want

Voss wants to defend the Dominion. That is the conscious goal she would state without hesitation. Beneath it, she wants the war she has been preparing for, not out of bloodlust, but because a clear enemy with a clear solution is infinitely preferable to the slow rot she cannot fight with a sword. She needs a threat she can march against. If shown proof that the real threat is internal, she will redirect her fury with terrifying efficiency.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Potential antagonist or powerful ally in Act II. If the PCs can prove the Cinders' corruption, Voss becomes one of the strongest allies in the campaign, bringing the Order's military might against the real enemy. If they cannot convince her, she remains an obstacle, potentially starting a war with the Marches that serves Serith's interests by keeping the Dominion distracted.
>
> **Key Secret.** Voss is not compromised by Serith. She is exactly what she appears to be. The Cinders have been quietly encouraging her war plans, because a Dominion at war is a Dominion too distracted to notice the rot within. Voss does not know she is being manipulated.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If turned, Voss is the military arm of the Dominion's salvation, leading the Order's purge of corrupted Cinder elements and potentially becoming the power behind a reformed government. If left unchecked, she launches her invasion, weakening the Dominion at the worst possible moment.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Clipped, military diction. Never sits, always stands or paces. Makes eye contact that borders on intimidation. Does not ask questions she does not want answered honestly. Speaks louder when she is angry, quieter when she is dangerous.
>
> *"I have watched that wall of thorns grow for fifteen years. I have buried forty-seven soldiers to border skirmishes. I have sent nine formal requests for authorization to act. Do not speak to me of patience. Patience is what the dead have."*
