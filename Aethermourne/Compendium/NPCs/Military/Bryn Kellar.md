# Sergeant Bryn Kellar

## Appearance

Early thirties, stocky, with ruddy skin and a perpetual sunburn that never quite fades. Red-brown hair kept short under his Keeper's cap. A wide, honest face that makes him look younger than he is, young for his rank. His hands are scarred from rope burns and Trench-creature encounters, the knuckles thick and calloused. He moves with the steady, unhurried gait of a man used to walking wet stone in the dark, though there is a nervous energy to him, always adjusting his gear. He wears a Lantern-Keeper badge on his chest that he polishes compulsively. It was his father's.

## Personality

Earnest, brave, and slightly too idealistic for his line of work. Bryn volunteered for [[The Lantern-Keepers]] because he believed in the mission, and three years in, he still believes, which makes him rare among the rank and file. He is good at his job, solid in a fight, steady in the fog, and absolutely terrible at politics. He says what he thinks, which endears him to his squad and infuriates his superiors. There is no guile in him, and he considers this a strength rather than a flaw.

## Backstory

Bryn grew up in a fishing village on the Shattered Coast, the eldest of four children. His father, Aldric, was a Lantern-Keeper who died on patrol in the Bilges when Bryn was twelve. The official report said contaminated air in a sealed cistern. Bryn has his father's patrol journal and knows the air was tested before entry. No body was ever recovered.

He joined [[The Lantern-Keepers]] at twenty-eight, older than most recruits, driven by the simple conviction that someone ought to stand between the coast and whatever took his father. This is why he patrols the same routes his father walked. He trained hard, earned his rank through competence rather than ambition, and was assigned to shore patrols along the Shattered Coast under [[Lira Ashvane]]'s command.

Three years of patrols have taught him the coast's rhythms: the way the fog moves before a Black Tide, the sounds Trench creatures make when they surface, the particular quality of silence that means something is watching from the water. His latest discovery, a stone altar in a tidal cave that appeared overnight, covered in geometric salt-crystal patterns and warm to the touch, has shaken his steady composure for the first time. He reported it to Ashvane in clinical detail and recommended sealing the cave. She has not slept since reading his report.

## Relationships

- **[[Lira Ashvane]]**, his commander and mentor. He would follow her into the Trench without question. Worries she is being sidelined by political pressure from the Tidekeepers.
- **[[Dren Halwick]]**, Kellar has visited the Whisper-Cells once. He found the experience deeply unsettling, particularly the hours when the entity speaks through Halwick. He prays for the man but keeps his distance.
- **His squad**, a team of six Keepers who patrol the Shattered Coast. They trust Kellar completely, and he carries that trust like a weight. Halford is the veteran, cautious and methodical. Denna is the new recruit, eager and still learning. Marsh is quiet, the best tracker in the unit.
- **"The Ghost"**, he does not know her by name, but he has seen signs of organized smuggling that suggest a single coordinator. He calls them "the Ghost" in his reports. (This is [[Sable]], though he does not know it.)
- **[[The Veil Unbound]]**, he does not know the cult exists by name, but the altar he found is their work. They know he found it, and they are watching him.
- **Venna Creel**, the Black Market fence in [[Veil Harbor]]. Kellar disapproves of the market's existence but has grudgingly acknowledged that Creel's information network has saved Keeper lives.

## What They Want

Bryn wants to do the right thing, to protect the coast and the people who live on it. His deeper need is to understand what is happening to the Reach, because the altar in the cave made him feel something he has never felt on patrol before: that someone, or something, is watching him even when he is alone. He wants that feeling to stop, and he is afraid it never will.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Kellar is the ideal ground-level ally for PCs in [[Veil Harbor]]. He is competent, trustworthy, and connected to events through his patrol route. He can guide PCs along the Shattered Coast, introduce them to Ashvane, and provide the honest perspective that higher-ranking NPCs are too political to offer.
>
> **Key Secret.** The altar he found is part of the [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound's]] ritual network, a chain of sites along the coast designed to weaken [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] prison from the surface. If the PCs follow his lead, they will find a trail of ritual sites stretching south toward [[Mistholm]].
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** The Veil Unbound knows Kellar found the altar and is deciding what to do about him. Killing a Lantern-Keeper draws attention, but leaving him alive risks exposure. If the PCs do not intervene, Kellar will be targeted, either through an "accident" on patrol or through a Sleepwalker episode engineered to discredit him. If protected, he becomes a steadfast ally throughout Act I.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks plainly, without flourish. Scratches the back of his neck when uncomfortable. Calls everyone "sir" or "ma'am" regardless of rank. Laughs easily but goes very quiet when something is wrong. Speaks too fast when nervous, which is often. Formal with superiors, casual with his squad. Sample lines: *"I wrote it up clean, just what I saw. Stone altar, salt crystals, warm to the touch. I don't know what it means, sir, but I know it wasn't there last month, and I know I don't like it."* *"Something's wrong down here, Sarge, I know, I always say that, but this time, look at the walls."*
