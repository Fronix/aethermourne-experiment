## Appearance

Forties, lean and weathered, with close-cropped brown hair going grey at the temples. He wears standard Order armor but keeps it practical rather than ceremonial, the breastplate scratched from thorn-vine contact, the cloak cut short so it won't snag on Scar vegetation. His face has the permanent squint of someone who spends long hours watching the Scarwall, and his hands are never far from the short sword at his hip. A quiet man who watches more than he speaks, and whose stillness reads as competence rather than passivity.

## Personality

Competent, patient, privately frustrated. Kolst is a career officer posted to the Scar because someone needed to be, and he turned out to be good at the unglamorous work of watching a wall of thorns grow. He follows orders, but he also thinks, and thinking has become dangerous. He is loyal to the Dominion in the way a soldier is loyal: not to its politics or its Church, but to the people behind him who depend on the wall holding. He has a dry, understated humor that surfaces in his reports and vanishes entirely when superiors are present.

## Backstory

Kolst grew up in [[Cinderholm]], the son of a Flame-Bearer quartermaster who taught him that the Order's strength was logistics, not glory. He enlisted at eighteen, served garrison duty at three postings without distinction, and was assigned to [[Thornwall]] eight years ago as a mid-career posting that no one with ambition wanted. He accepted it without complaint, and in doing so found the work that suited him: border watch in a place where the border itself was alive and hostile.

His first year at Thornwall taught him that the Scar obeyed no doctrine he had been trained to enforce. The Scarwall grew on its own schedule. Patrols reported phenomena that fit no category in the Order's manuals. Requests for guidance from [[Ostivaar]] returned slowly, vaguely, or not at all. Kolst adapted. He began making decisions on his own authority, small ones at first, then larger ones as the gap between his orders and his reality widened.

Two years ago, a Marcher trader appeared at the Scarwall's edge carrying iron tools and asking for grain. Kolst's standing orders were to arrest any Marcher who approached the wall. Instead, he traded. The man was [[Torvin Ashwalker]], a Marchwarden whose pragmatism matched Kolst's own. Their relationship has grown into a regular exchange, iron and tools for grain and Ashite fragments, conducted through gaps in the Scarwall that both men pretend they discovered by accident. Both understand they are technically committing treason. Both consider the trade essential.

## Relationships

- [[Ashara Voss]], respected superior. Kolst admires Voss's competence and shares her frustration with the Hierarch's inaction, though he lacks her appetite for war
- [[Torvin Ashwalker]], secret trading partner across the Scarwall. Two years of mutual treason have built a trust neither man expected
- Researcher Mila Ashgrove, the [[Ashveil]] scholar assigned to study the Scar. Kolst protects her work because her data confirms what his instincts tell him: the Scar is changing in ways that matter
- Sergeant Lira Vane, his senior scout. The best eyes in Thornwall and the person he trusts most with what she sees in the Scar
- [[Delric Mourne]], distant obstruction. Cinder interference in Thornwall's supply lines has made Kolst's job harder and his suspicions sharper

## What They Want

Kolst wants clarity: clear orders and adequate resources for a garrison facing a threat that his chain of command refuses to acknowledge honestly. His deeper need is to act on what he knows. A Marcher emissary has appeared at his gate claiming the Marches share a common enemy, and the intelligence from [[Torvin Ashwalker]] aligns with his own observations. He is beginning to believe that the real threat is not across the Scarwall but somewhere behind him, in the institutions he serves.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Kolst is a linchpin NPC at the Dominion's western border. His secret trade with [[Torvin Ashwalker]] is the only reliable communication channel between the Dominion and the Marches. If the Scarwall closes entirely or war breaks out, this relationship becomes strategically irreplaceable. He appears in Act I as a contact at the border and in Act II as a potential bridge between nations.
>
> **Key Secret.** He has allowed a Marcher emissary to stay at Thornwall in violation of his orders and has been conducting unauthorized conversations. He has also intercepted a copy of an encrypted Cinder message sent from Thornwall the same day his patrols reported deliberate movement inside the Scarwall. He cannot break the cipher, but he knows someone at Thornwall is reporting to the Cinders about things they should not know.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** Kolst is moving toward a decision point: report directly to [[Ashara Voss]] and bypass the Cinders' intelligence chain, or continue to follow a command structure he no longer trusts. If the PCs give him reason to act, he becomes an active ally with garrison resources and border intelligence. If the Cinders discover his trade with Ashwalker first, he faces execution for treason, and the only cross-border channel dies with him.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in short, measured sentences, a soldier's economy with words. Pauses to look at whoever is speaking as though memorizing them. Refers to the Scarwall as "the Wall" and the Scar as "the line." Never sits when he can stand. Dark humor surfaces in his official reports but never in conversation with strangers.
> *"The thorns grow ten feet a year. My reports grow ten pages a year. Neither one gets read in Ostivaar."*
