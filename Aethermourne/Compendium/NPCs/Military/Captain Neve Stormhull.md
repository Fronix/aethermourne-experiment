## Appearance

A tall, lean woman in her early forties with sun-darkened skin, salt-bleached hair pulled back in a tight braid, and the permanently squinting expression of someone who has spent years staring at horizons. She wears a naval officer's coat from [[The Hollowed Reach]], faded and patched but still recognizable, over practical sailor's clothing. Her left hand bears a burn scar in the shape of no identifiable pattern, a souvenir of the Black Tide that destroyed her ship. She stands with the balanced posture of a lifelong sailor, adjusting unconsciously to ground that does not roll, and her voice carries the precise diction of Hollowed Reach naval training overlaid with the blunt vowels she has absorbed from the Ironmarch.

## Personality

Neve is disciplined, competent, and running from something she has never told anyone about. She applies naval order to a civilian port because it is the only way she knows to function: schedules, hierarchies, clear chains of command. Beneath the discipline is a woman who is deeply afraid of the sea she cannot leave behind. She chose [[Shieldbreak]] because it let her stay near the water without going back on it, a compromise between the pull of the ocean and the terror of what she saw in it. She is protective of her town, her people, and her refugees with a fierceness that surprises even her, because caring about things on land is new, and she is not sure she is doing it correctly.

## Backstory

Neve served fifteen years in the [[Concord of Tides|Hollowed Reach navy]], rising to the rank of captain by thirty-two. She commanded the patrol vessel *Ashenmere's Promise* on the southern trade routes, a routine posting that changed her life when a Black Tide, a surge of abyssal corruption from the deep ocean, swallowed her ship off the coast of the Ironmarch. Every member of her crew died. The ship broke apart. Neve survived by clinging to wreckage for two days before washing ashore near [[Shieldbreak]].

In the water, she heard a voice. It spoke with the cadence of something old and patient and imprisoned, and it told her that the sea was not finished, that the deep places were waking, and that the tide would come for the land when the silence fell. She does not know if it was [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], madness, or both. She has never spoken about it. She reported herself dead, adopted the name she already had, and took over [[Shieldbreak]]'s port because it needed someone competent and she needed something to do that was not thinking about what she heard.

## Relationships

- **Pilot-Elder Hask Reefwalker**, head of [[Shieldbreak]]'s Pilot Guild. They have a professional relationship built on mutual grudging respect. Reefwalker resents her authority. Neve respects his knowledge. Neither will admit they need each other.
- [[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal]], who has offered Neve a consulting contract through [[The Quartermasters Circle]] to organize logistics for [[The Red Garrison]]. She has not accepted, because it would require traveling inland and leaving the port.
- **Maren Driftwood**, the town's information broker. Neve tolerates her because Driftwood's intelligence network provides early warning of threats. She distrusts her because Driftwood sells to everyone.
- The refugees. Neve has no single relationship here, but the growing refugee population in [[Shieldbreak]] is becoming her primary concern. She was a refugee herself, once, clinging to wreckage and hoping for shore.

## What They Want

Neve wants to keep [[Shieldbreak]] functioning. She wants the port open, the ships moving, and the people fed. She wants to never go back on the open ocean. She wants to forget what she heard in the water. None of these goals are fully compatible with each other, and the tension is slowly tearing her apart. Her deepest need is to face the thing she is running from, but she is not ready, and she may never be.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Supporting NPC in Acts II-III, potential bridge to [[The Hollowed Reach]] storyline. Her knowledge of the Black Tide connects the Ironmarch arc to [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] broader plans.
>
> **Key Secret.** What Neve heard in the water was genuine. [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] influence is extending through the deep ocean, and the Black Tides are growing more frequent and reaching further. The voice told Neve specifically that the iron would call to the iron, that the war-god's pieces would try to become whole, and that when they did, the sea would swallow what remained. This is a prophecy, a warning, or a taunt, and Neve cannot tell which.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs earn her trust, Neve will share what she heard, providing a crucial connection between the Ironmarch's fragment migration and [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] campaign. If they can help her face her fear of the sea, she becomes a powerful naval asset, one of the few people alive who has survived a Black Tide and understands how [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] ocean-based forces operate. Without PC intervention, Neve will eventually be found by [[Concord of Tides]] agents and face a choice between arrest and flight.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks with naval precision, giving orders disguised as suggestions. Stands near windows or open doors, always able to see the water. Taps her burned hand against her thigh when nervous, a repetitive gesture she is not aware of. Becomes terse and pale when the conversation turns to the deep ocean.
> *"I run a port. I keep the ships moving and the cargo flowing and the people from killing each other over dock space. That's the job. The sea is out there. I'm in here. We have an arrangement."*
