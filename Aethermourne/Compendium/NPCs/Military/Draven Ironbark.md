## Appearance

A massive, scarred man in his fifties with a braided red beard going grey at the edges and arms like tree trunks. His face is weathered and hard, marked by a long pale scar running from his left temple to his jaw, earned in a border skirmish with [[The Ashen Dominion|Dominion]] scouts decades ago. He wears ironwood armor, dark and dense as metal, and carries a massive axe whose haft is carved with his clan's history. His hands are calloused to the texture of bark. He moves with the deliberate weight of a man who knows exactly how much damage he can do.

## Personality

Loud, blunt, confrontational, and more perceptive than he lets on. Draven tests people by provoking them, gauging their character by how they respond to pressure. He respects those who push back without losing control and dismisses those who fold or flatter. He drinks heavily but never seems impaired. Beneath the combative exterior, he is fiercely protective of his people and carries a genuine, principled conviction that concentrated power corrupts, no matter whose hands hold it. He is surprisingly gentle with children and animals.

## Backstory

Clan Ironbark has held the northern reaches of the Cradle for generations, defending their territory against everything the wild throws at them without asking for help. Draven earned his Marchwardenship the traditional way, through the Rootspeaking and a series of trials that left two other candidates dead. He has held his seat for over twenty years and defended his borders against Dominion incursions, territorial predators, and the slow encroachment of the deep Cradle's more dangerous flora.

He watched [[Kaelith Thornborn]]'s rise with growing alarm. When she founded [[Thornhaven]] and began organizing formal military training, he saw the first stones of a cage being laid. "She'll have us marching in lines next," he told the [[Marchwardens Council|Council]]. "And after that, she'll have us kneeling." He leads four Marchwardens in opposition to unification, not because he denies the threats facing the Marches, but because he believes the cure is worse than the disease. The Marches survived two thousand years without a king. They do not need one now, even one who calls herself something else.

He has called for a formal challenge to Kaelith's authority at the next Moot, a political gambit that could force the undecided Marchwardens to choose sides before they are ready.

## Relationships

- **[[Kaelith Thornborn]]**, his principal opponent, a woman he privately admits has courage and conviction, but whose ambition he considers existentially dangerous to the Marches' way of life
- **[[Elder Rosk]]**, respects the Eldest Greensinger's spiritual authority, one of few people whose words give Draven pause
- **[[Senna Deepmoss]]**, an undecided Marchwarden he is actively courting, appealing to her analytical nature with arguments about the dangers of centralized power
- **[[Torvin Ashwalker]]**, the border warden at [[Ashwalker's Post]], who supports neither side fully, a man Draven considers pragmatic but frustratingly noncommittal
- **[[The Greensingers]]**, he respects their neutrality and spiritual role, even as he wishes they would speak against Kaelith's militarization

## What They Want

Draven wants to preserve clan sovereignty and the freedom that defines Marcher identity. He wants to prove that the Marches can face their threats through alliance and cooperation rather than unification under a single leader. Beneath that, he fears being remembered as the man who let the Marches become another [[The Ashen Dominion|Dominion]], trading wildness for safety and losing everything that matters in the exchange.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Potential obstacle or crucial ally in Act III. If the PCs earn his respect through strength and deeds rather than words, he becomes a grudging but powerful supporter of a compromise solution. If not, he actively undermines Kaelith and may fracture the Marches at the worst possible moment. The best outcome requires the PCs to broker a deal that addresses both positions.
>
> **Key Secret.** Draven is not wrong about the dangers of concentrated power, and his concerns are legitimate. But his stubbornness is being exploited. [[The Cinders]] have operatives in the Marches working to deepen the Thornborn-Ironbark rift, feeding Draven intelligence (some real, some fabricated) about Kaelith's "true ambitions." He does not know he is being manipulated. The information feels too useful to question.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs find a compromise, perhaps a unified military command with guaranteed clan autonomy, Draven becomes the Marches' fiercest defender in the fight against the Shadow Root. If the rift deepens, the Marches splinter, and the clans face Serith's corruption divided and vulnerable.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in a booming voice, often interrupting. Gestures with his axe when making a point, which is more intimidating than he intends. Laughs loudly and rarely, but genuinely. When he is truly angry, he goes quiet, which is far more dangerous than his shouting. Calls everyone younger than him "boy" or "girl" regardless of rank.
> *"You want me to kneel to a woman because the trees told her she's special? My grandfather knelt to no one. My father knelt to no one. And the forest didn't swallow them for it."*
