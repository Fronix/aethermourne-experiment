## Appearance

Sixteen years old but looks older, mid-twenties at a glance, the premature aging a visible cost of her probability-sight. Her dark hair is streaked with grey at the temples, and fine lines mark the corners of her eyes. She is small and wiry, all nervous energy, with the restless hands of someone whose mind moves faster than her body can follow. Her eyes are the most striking feature: pale silver-blue, the irises faintly luminous in low light, a side effect of being born Changed. She wears the standard Edgewatch uniform, leather and padded wool in muted greys, though hers always looks slightly too large, borrowed from the stores of an outfit that was not designed for a teenager. She moves with a quick, darting attention, her head turning toward things no one else can see.

## Personality

Rin is brilliant, impatient, and terrified of what she knows. She was born Changed, the probability-sight wired into her from her first breath, and she has never known a world without the threads. To her, reality looks like a web of glowing possibilities layered over everything, futures branching and collapsing with every decision. Other Edgewalkers learn to read the threads. Rin has to learn to stop reading them. She is the most sensitive probability-reader at Edgewatch, capable of detecting surges earlier than any adult Edgewalker, but the strain is aging her body and wearing at her patience. She respects [[Kessa Thornwall]] deeply but cannot make the Warden understand what she feels from the direction of [[The Loom Scar]], a resonance that is not a surge, not a threat, but something structured, something that sounds like it *means* something. She is disobedient not out of rebellion but out of compulsion. The signal is too important to ignore.

## Backstory

Rin was born on the Shattered Edge to a drift-harvester mother who was caught in a surge during pregnancy. The change passed to the child. Rin opened her eyes and saw the threads. Her mother, who had gained the ability to feel temperature through stone, raised her in [[Riftmarket]] until Rin's sight became too acute to function in a crowded settlement. Every person trailed branching futures. Every decision split the air with light. She could not sleep without seeing the threads of everyone in the building shifting through the night.

[[Tyrell Vane]] identified her gift at age nine and arranged her transfer to [[Edgewatch]], where the Threshold's proximity dampened the noise from the settled world and gave her something clear to focus on: the Maelstrom's movements. She has served at the Watch for seven years. Her surge-detection record is flawless. Twelve warnings, twelve evacuations, zero casualties from any surge she called.

Three months ago, she began feeling something new from the direction of the deep Threshold. Not the chaotic wash of the Maelstrom's surges but a structured pulse, regular, repeating, like a signal. She reported it to [[Kessa Thornwall]]. Kessa filed it as an anomalous reading. Rin filed a classified addendum through the [[The Accord of Echoes|Accord]] channels, describing what she heard more precisely: a pattern in the Threshold static that sounded like a voice, speaking in a language made of probability rather than sound. The addendum reached [[Tyrell Vane]], who read it and went very quiet.

Since then, Rin has made three unauthorized trips to [[The Loom Scar]], drawn by the signal's clarity at the Scar's edge. Each trip has sharpened her perception. She can now distinguish layers within the signal: a deep, old current that feels like architecture, and a thinner, newer thread woven through it that feels like *intention*. She does not know whose intention. She needs to go back.

## Relationships

- [[Kessa Thornwall]], her commanding officer and closest thing to a parental figure at the Watch. Kessa cares about Rin fiercely and is terrified of losing her to the Threshold. Their relationship is strained by Rin's unauthorized trips
- [[Tyrell Vane]], who discovered her gift and arranged her posting. Rin trusts Vane more than anyone, but his silence after reading her classified report has shaken that trust. She does not know he is frightened
- [[Kaelen Riftwalker]], who noticed her visits to the Scar and has been quietly allowing access. Rin is drawn to his certainty but disturbed by his recent silence. She wonders if what happened to him is what will happen to her
- [[Prior Echoes]], who has tried to recruit her into the Echoists. Rin respects the philosophy but considers herself a soldier, not a pilgrim. The distinction matters to her
- The Watch garrison, who treat her as a younger sister. Her premature aging unsettles them, but her perfect surge-detection record keeps her respected

## What They Want

Rin wants to understand the signal. She is a soldier and a pragmatist, and she believes the structured pattern she is detecting at the Loom Scar is intelligence, something that can be decoded, reported, and acted upon. Her deeper need is simpler: she wants to stop feeling like her gift is consuming her. The aging, the sleeplessness, the inability to unsee the threads. If the signal means something, if her perception has a purpose beyond early warning, then maybe the cost is worth paying.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Rin is the Shattered Edge's sensory instrument. Her probability-sight is sensitive enough to detect [[The Twelve#Serith, Goddess of Shadow and Secrets , *"The Veiled One"*|Serith]]'s broadcast signal threading through the Loom Scar's fate-bleed, something even [[Tyrell Vane]] cannot perceive because Serith's shadow domain conceals futures from standard probability-readers. Rin's Born-Changed nature gives her a different frequency of perception, one Serith's concealment does not fully cover. She is the Shattered Edge's answer to its intelligence blind spot.
>
> **Key Secret.** The two layers Rin has distinguished in the signal are real. The deep, architectural current is [[The Twelve#Orenthas, God of Fate and Knowledge , *"The Loom-Keeper"*|Orenthas]]'s Loom of Fate, still operating, still weaving, still generating fate-threads for a world that has mostly forgotten it exists. The thinner, intentional thread woven through it is Serith, using the Loom's own infrastructure to broadcast her influence. Rin can perceive both. With guidance, she could learn to separate them, making her the only person on the continent who can distinguish Serith's signal from the Loom's natural output.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs recruit Rin and connect her perception to [[Kaelen Riftwalker]]'s Loom-thread awareness and [[Prior Echoes]]' wrong-note sensitivity, the three form a triangulated detection system capable of mapping Serith's broadcast network across the Edge. If left alone, Rin's unauthorized trips will eventually take her too deep. The signal will become the only thing she can hear. Kessa will find her at the Scar, silver-eyed and still, listening to something that is not speaking to her benefit.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks fast, clipped, military-cadence with academic vocabulary that slips in when she forgets to simplify. Fidgets constantly, tapping fingers, shifting weight, turning her head toward things only she can see. When she focuses on the threads, her eyes go distant and her speech slows to a murmur. She is painfully aware of her premature aging and deflects questions about it with dark humor.
> *"I can see every way this conversation might go. Three of them end with you telling me to stay away from the Scar. I am already bored of those three."*
