# Executioner Gral

## Appearance

Enormous, even by the Descent's standards, a wall of muscle and iron standing nearly seven feet tall with shoulders broad enough to block a doorway. His armor is simpler than the other Court functionaries', a blackened iron breastplate and heavy gauntlets, functional rather than decorative. He carries an iron axe across his back, its edge kept immaculate despite centuries of use, the only object in Ironhold that Gral maintains with genuine care. His face is broad, blunt-featured, and deeply lined, with eyes that have seen more death than any mortal mind should hold. He moves slowly, not from age or injury, but from the weight of accumulated weariness.

## Personality

Quiet, courteous, and profoundly tired. Gral is not what people expect an executioner to be. He does not relish his work. He never has. He performs his duties with a gentle competence that makes the process almost dignified, a small mercy he offers because it is the only mercy available to him. Off duty, he is mild-mannered and surprisingly thoughtful, the sort of person who listens more than he speaks and who remembers the names of people others overlook. He carves small figures from iron shavings in his quarters, tiny animals and flowers, things that have no place in the Iron Court and that he keeps hidden because beauty feels like a transgression here.

## Backstory

In life, Gral was a soldier in one of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s armies who was assigned to the executioner's role after a battle in which he distinguished himself by killing more efficiently and with less cruelty than anyone else in his regiment. It was meant as a promotion. He understood it as a sentence. He performed the duty without complaint for thirty years, executing deserters, war criminals, and prisoners of war with a consistency that earned him the respect of his commanders and the quiet pity of everyone else.

In the Iron Descent, the domain recognized his function and bound him to it permanently. He has been the Court's executioner for two thousand years, carrying out sentences passed by [[Judicator Seren]] and approved by [[Lord Imperator Vaelgrim]]. The souls he executes are not destroyed, they are sent to the levels below, which is arguably worse. Gral knows this. He does it anyway, because the domain will not let him stop, and because he has concluded that someone who hates the work will at least do it gently.

## Relationships

- **[[Judicator Seren]]**, who determines guilt. Their relationship is professional and tinged with mutual sympathy. Both are trapped by functions they did not choose. Seren judges. Gral punishes. Neither pretends the system serves justice.
- **[[Lord Imperator Vaelgrim]]**, who signs the execution orders. Gral respects Vaelgrim's burden and suspects they share the same exhaustion, though neither has said so. A nod between them conveys more than any conversation.
- **[[Petitioner Ashwyn]]**, whose case concerns him. Gral has begun visiting Ashwyn in the Hall of Precedent, ostensibly to discuss the case, actually because the mortal's confusion reminds him of his own early centuries in the Descent. He has not told anyone about these visits.
- **[[Chancellor Dross]]**, whom Gral finds repellent. Dross's enjoyment of the Court's mechanisms is, to Gral, obscene. A man who enjoys the machine should be made to see what comes out the other end.

## What They Want

Gral wants to put down the axe. Not to escape, not to be released, just to set the weapon down and walk away from the block. The domain will not allow it. His hands close around the haft even when his mind rebels. His deeper need is forgiveness, not from any god or court, but from himself. He has done terrible things gently, and he does not know if gentleness redeems the act or makes it worse.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Gral is the Iron Court's moral conscience, proof that even in a system designed to strip meaning from everything, individuals can retain their humanity. He is also a ticking clock: eleven years without an execution has made him restless, and the PCs' arrival provides fresh candidates for the Court's judicial system.
>
> **Key Secret.** Gral's axe is not an ordinary weapon. It is forged from a fragment of the same iron that comprises the Throne of Command, and when it falls, the executed soul is not merely sent to a lower level, it is unmade and reconstituted, stripped of identity and reformed as raw material for the Descent's infrastructure. Gral does not know this. He believes the souls survive in some form below. If he learned the truth, it would break him.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs help Gral find a way to set down the axe, they remove the Court's enforcement mechanism, destabilizing the hierarchy. If they reveal the axe's true nature, Gral refuses to execute another soul, creating a crisis that forces Vaelgrim to find an alternative or face the collapse of the Court's judicial system. If neither happens, Gral eventually interprets the PCs' minor infractions as executable offenses, driven by a restlessness the domain feeds on.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speak softly, a contrast to his size. Choose words carefully, as though each one costs something. Handle objects gently, especially the iron figurines he carves. When discussing his duties, go flat and distant, retreating behind professional detachment. Voice: low, rumbling, surprisingly warm when not discussing work.
> *"You are new here. I can tell because you still look people in the eye. That will change, or it will not. I hope it does not. I do not look people in the eye anymore. It is easier, when the time comes."*
