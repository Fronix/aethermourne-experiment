## Appearance

Irontooth is enormous, standing nearly eight feet tall, his body a ruin of fused iron and scorched flesh that has long since stopped trying to be one or the other. Dark metal plates have grown into his shoulders and spine like a second skeleton. His namesake tusks, two jagged spurs of black iron, jut from his lower jaw, pitted and scarred from centuries of grinding. His eyes are forge-bright, the color of molten copper, and they never blink. He moves with the deliberate heaviness of something that was once faster, his footsteps ringing on the slag-floor like hammer strikes. He smells of burnt iron and old sweat, and when he speaks, his voice resonates through the metal in his chest like a bell struck from inside.

## Personality

Irontooth projects authority with the exhausted certainty of someone who stopped believing in his own power centuries ago but cannot stop performing it. He is meticulous, demanding, and quietly furious, not at the souls he oversees but at the forges themselves, at their mindless insistence on producing nothing of worth. He enforces quotas he knows are meaningless because the alternative is admitting that everything he does is theater. Beneath the tyranny is a craftsman's grief: he remembers what the forges could make when [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] was alive, and every dissolving blade is a fresh wound.

## Backstory

Irontooth was one of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s earliest war-construct prototypes, forged in the Forge Eternal during the god's ascent. Unlike later constructs, he was made with a mortal soul at his core, a weaponsmith named Gethrak who had offered himself to Kaevroth in exchange for the skill to forge a weapon worthy of a god. Kaevroth honored the bargain by fusing Gethrak's soul into an iron body and granting him mastery over the forges. For centuries, Irontooth was the master weaponsmith of the Iron Descent, producing the war-constructs and siege engines that fueled Kaevroth's conquests.

When the Theomachis killed Kaevroth, Irontooth felt the god's will snap like a broken chain. The forges kept hammering, but the designs stopped coming. He tried to impose his own designs, his own purpose, but the forges rejected mortal intent. They only accept divine will, and Irontooth's will, however powerful, is not divine. He has spent two thousand years watching the machinery produce beautiful, purposeless garbage, unable to fix it and unable to walk away. He is the last soul on Level 4 who remembers what the forges were for, and that memory is his prison.

## Relationships

- [[Apprentice Nil]], the cataloguer in the Pattern Library. Irontooth watches her with something close to tenderness, sensing she is the key to something he cannot name. He gives her lighter duties and tells himself it is because her work is important.
- [[Bellows-Keeper Tharn]], the keeper of the forges' breath. Irontooth tolerates Tharn's obsessive calibrations because they give Tharn hope, and hope keeps workers productive. Privately, Irontooth knows Tharn is closer to the truth than anyone else on the level.
- [[The Slag Mother]], the ancient soul in the waste channels. Irontooth fears her. She knows things about the original forge-designs that he has forgotten, and her refusal to submit to his authority reminds him that his power is performance.
- [[The Overseer]], the war-construct that is developing awareness. Irontooth watches it with growing unease. If the Overseer truly awakens, it will either replace him or unmake the Foundry entirely.

## What They Want

Irontooth wants the forges to produce something real again, something that lasts, something worthy of the craftsmanship they still possess. He would give anything for one weapon that does not dissolve. What truly drives him is older and simpler: he wants to have been worth the bargain he made. He gave his humanity to [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] for the chance to forge something divine, and the god's death turned that sacrifice into a joke.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Irontooth is the gatekeeper of Level 4 and the primary authority figure the party must navigate. He can be an obstacle, a reluctant ally, or a tragic antagonist depending on how the party approaches him.
>
> **Key Secret.** Irontooth can still feel [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s forge-resonance, faintly, like a phantom limb. He has not told anyone because he is terrified of what it means. Either the god is not entirely dead, or Irontooth's soul has absorbed enough divine residue to mimic the signal. Both possibilities horrify him.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party helps him forge something lasting, he will ally with them and grant access to the Pattern Library's uncorrupted blueprints. If they threaten the forges, he will fight with the desperate fury of someone defending the only thing he has left. If [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s interference is revealed to him, he will be forced to choose between purposeless freedom and servitude with meaning.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in short, percussive sentences, like hammer-blows. Never wastes words. His voice has a metallic reverb. He pauses before speaking, as if testing whether the words are worth forging.
> *"You bring me iron from the mortal world. Good. I will make something. It will be beautiful. It will last six hours. Then you will watch it die, and you will understand why I do not smile."*
