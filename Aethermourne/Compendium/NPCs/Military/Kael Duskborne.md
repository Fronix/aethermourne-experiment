## Appearance

Kael looks like what he is: a Vigil warrior who walked away from the Vigil but never stopped being a soldier. Tall, broad-shouldered, mid-forties, with the weathered dark skin and close-cropped grey hair of a man who has spent decades in harsh conditions. A Morrhaelite scar runs from his left temple to his jaw, a pale line where a blade of divine bone grazed him during a Hollowdeep patrol years ago. The scar never healed properly, and it pulses faintly grey during Death Tides.

He wears modified Vigil armor, the black leather and bone-plate of an Ashen Vigil veteran, repaired with Thornwild materials where the original has worn through. Living vine stitches hold deadwood patches in place. The effect is distinctive: a warrior dressed in the Thornwild itself, half-living, half-dead, entirely functional. He carries a Morrhaelite short sword, the one piece of Vigil equipment he will never give up, and a Thornwild-grown shield of ironwood that regenerates minor damage during the Cycle's life-phase.

## Personality

Pragmatic to the bone. Kael does not deal in ideology, theology, or philosophy. He deals in threats, defenses, and the number of people still alive at the end of the day. He speaks plainly, acts decisively, and has no patience for arguments that do not end in actionable conclusions. He is not unkind, but kindness is not his primary mode. Competence is.

Beneath the soldier's discipline is a man who made a choice that cost him everything he knew and has spent fifteen years proving it was the right one. He left the Vigil because he saw undead in the Thornwild behaving with purpose rather than hunger, and he could not reconcile that with the Vigil's doctrine of destruction. He stayed because the Thornwild needed someone who could fight, and because the people here, living and dead alike, deserved protection as much as anyone in the Holds.

He does not talk about this. He does not need to. His presence is the argument.

## Backstory

Kael served with [[The Ashen Vigil]] for twenty years, rising to the rank of Captain. He was competent, reliable, and unquestioning, the ideal Vigil officer. His posting was the northwestern frontier, where the Wastes' edge bled into what would become the Thornwild, and his duties included eliminating undead that wandered out of the transitional zone.

Fifteen years ago, he followed a group of risen into the Thornwild and watched them stop wandering. The Cycle caught them, and instead of the mindless hunger he expected, they fell into the rhythm: hunting, dying, rising, hunting again. Not mindless. Purposeful. Part of something. He watched for three days before returning to his post, and he could not bring himself to report them as threats.

He went back. Then again. Then he stopped going back to the Vigil entirely. Commander [[Thessa Ironveil]] listed him as a deserter, and there is technically a standing order for his return. Ironveil has not enforced it. She has larger concerns, and some quiet part of her suspects Kael found something in the Thornwild that the Vigil's doctrine does not account for. She will never say this aloud.

In the Thornwild, Kael built the Thornguard from volunteers: Marcher hunters, Wastes exiles, and a handful of Remnants who could still fight. They defend the settlements against threats from outside, from the dangerous extremes of the Cycle, and from each other when tempers flare. It is not the Vigil. It is not meant to be.

## Relationships

- **[[Yara Thornbloom]]**: His closest ally and the person he trusts most. They coordinate the Thornwild's defense and survival daily. He respects her mind and worries about her deterioration.
- **[[Thessa Ironveil]]**: Unresolved. She was his commander. He was her best captain. He left without explanation, and neither of them has processed it. If they met, the conversation would be difficult and necessary.
- **[[Revenant Moss]]**: Mutual respect. Moss was the first Remnant Kael worked with willingly, and the experience redefined his understanding of what the dead can be. He visits Ashbloom when duties allow.
- **[[Root-Speaker Fennara]]**: Wary respect. He admires her conviction but thinks her druids take unnecessary risks. He has pulled Rootpyre survivors out of Bloom Surges more than once and would prefer not to keep doing it.

## What They Want

Kael wants the Thornwild's people to be safe. He would tell you this in exactly those words, and he would mean it completely.

What he needs, and would not admit, is absolution. Not from the Vigil, but from himself. He left his brothers and sisters in the hardest fight in Aethermourne because he saw something that contradicted their purpose, and he has never fully resolved whether that makes him a visionary or a coward.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Military contact and quest-giver for Thornwild defense arcs. Potential bridge character between the Thornwild and the Ashen Vigil if the PCs facilitate a reunion with [[Thessa Ironveil]].
>
> **Key Secret.** Kael has been receiving coded messages from a Vigil contact warning that the Hollowdeep's organized undead may be expanding westward toward the Thornwild. He has not shared this with Yara because he wants to confirm it first. If the Hollowdeep army reaches the Thornwild, the Cycle's death-phase would supercharge them, creating a force that could overwhelm every settlement in days.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs bring Kael and Ironveil together, the Vigil may recognize the Thornguard as a legitimate allied force, providing the Thornwild with resources and intelligence it desperately needs. If they do not, Kael fights the coming threats alone, and the Thornwild's military capacity remains fatally thin.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Clipped sentences. Military directness. Never uses two words where one will do. When uncomfortable, he maintains eye contact and says nothing rather than fill the silence. Voice: low, steady, with the faint rasp of someone who has given too many orders in cold weather. Sample line: "I didn't leave the Vigil because I stopped believing in the fight. I left because the fight was bigger than they thought."
