## Appearance

A weathered woman in her late thirties with short auburn hair streaked with grey, sea-green eyes, and weathered hands calloused from rope and weapon alike. She wears the Lantern-Keepers' salt-stiffened grey coat with a single lantern pin at the collar, the only ornamentation she permits herself. She moves with the deliberate economy of someone who has learned that wasted motion can kill you underwater, though she walks with a slight list from an old leg wound. Her office in [[Veil Harbor]] smells of black tea, alchemical brine, and old paper. She always looks like she slept two hours less than she needed. Her voice is low and measured, though it speeds up when angry.

## Personality

First impression: competent, direct, slightly intimidating. She weighs her words before speaking and says exactly what she means, no more. Warmth is not her language; trust is, and she shows it through action, sending you somewhere dangerous and being honest about the risk. Beneath the command presence is a woman carrying more than she lets anyone see, grief for fallen Keepers, fear that she is running out of time, and the quiet certainty that something unprecedented is building in the Trench. She does not ask for pity. She asks for capable people.

## Backstory

Lira joined the [[The Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keepers]] as a Wick-Bearer at seventeen and rose through the ranks as a Diver, one of the order's underwater operatives who investigate submerged anomalies in the [[The Hollowed Reach#The Abyssal Trench|Abyssal Trench]]. Diving is the most dangerous work in the order, and the casualty rate is brutal. Lira survived where others didn't, not because she was fearless, but because she was methodical. She mapped currents, catalogued Trench creatures, and built the operational procedures that still keep Divers alive today.

She took command after her predecessor drowned in a Black Tide incident that should have been survivable. The official report called it an accident. Lira has never been satisfied with that conclusion, and her instinct is correct: the [[The Veil Unbound]] arranged the death, though she doesn't know this yet. Discovering the truth will give her the lead she needs to start unraveling the cult's network.

Since taking command, she has watched the Black Tides accelerate, Sleepwalker incidents multiply, and the [[Concord of Tides]] cut her funding while the threats grow worse. She is brilliant, charismatic, exhausted, and increasingly desperate. She has begun hiring freelancers and outsiders because she simply cannot afford to be selective anymore.

## Relationships

- **[[Dren Halwick]]**, a Sleepwalker in her care whose lucid episodes alternate with something else speaking through him. Lira has been conducting secret interviews with the entity and keeping the transcripts locked in her personal safe.
- **[[Maren Selk]]**, First Tidekeeper of the [[Concord of Tides]]. Selk has been quietly reaching out to Lira for help investigating her own council, a politically dangerous alliance that both women maintain at arm's length.
- **[[The Whisperer]]**, an unseen enemy. Lira knows someone is sabotaging her order but hasn't identified the cult or its leader. This ignorance costs her people's lives.
- **Her Divers**, the specialists who would follow her into the Trench itself. She inspires fierce loyalty and repays it by never sending them anywhere she wouldn't go herself.
- **[[Bryn Kellar]]**, her most trusted sergeant. She promoted him personally after seeing his instincts in the Bilges. Worries he is too reckless but values his loyalty.
- **[[Celestine Vael]]**, cautious intelligence-sharing arrangement. Ashvane feeds the Star-Readers patrol reports; Vael shares astronomical anomaly data. Neither fully trusts the other.
- **[[Elowen Greaves]]**, complicated. Greaves is the Hierarch's envoy in Tidewall. Ashvane finds her rigid but respects her conviction. They clash over jurisdiction.

## What They Want

Lira wants to find out what is happening in the Trench before it is too late. She has a gut feeling, sharpened by twenty years on the coast, that the escalating Black Tides are building toward something catastrophic. Her deeper need is simpler and harder: she wants to stop losing people. Every Keeper who dies under her command is a weight she carries, and the weights are accumulating faster than she can bear.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The campaign's primary patron in Act I and a trusted ally throughout. She gives the PCs their first missions, provides resources and intelligence, and becomes the anchor that grounds the Reach storyline. Returns in Act V with grim determination.
>
> **Key Secret.** She is sitting on intelligence she has not shared with the [[Concord of Tides]]. The entity speaking through [[Dren Halwick]] knows things it should not, names of dead Keepers, locations of sealed Trench sites, dates that have not yet arrived. Lira suspects the entity is [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] herself, but has not voiced it.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** Starts desperate but composed. As the PCs bring her evidence, her composure cracks into action. If her predecessor's murder is uncovered, she gains the fury and focus to take the fight to the [[The Veil Unbound]]. Without PCs, she burns out within the year.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** No-nonsense delivery. Pauses before speaking. Never raises her voice, the quiet is the authority. Shows trust through action, not warmth. If she sends the PCs somewhere dangerous, she'll say so plainly. Drinks black tea constantly. Speaks in short declarative sentences. Rarely asks questions, makes statements and waits. Sample lines: *"I'm not going to lie to you. Three Keepers went down that shaft last month. Two came back. I need people who hear that and still volunteer."* *"The tide tells you when it's coming. People don't. That's why I watch people."*
