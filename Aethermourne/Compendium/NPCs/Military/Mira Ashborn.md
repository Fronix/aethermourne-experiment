## Appearance

Mira still looks like a soldier, which makes her conspicuous on a level where military identity has been ground into something more primal. She wears her Ashen Vigil insignia pinned to a battered iron pauldron, the metal tarnished but polished daily with an obsessiveness that speaks to what it represents. Her build is compact and athletic, marked with fresh scars that layer over older ones in a record of the Proving Grounds' relentless combat cycle. Her hair is cropped close, practical, already threading with iron-grey at the temples. Most distinctively, her eyes still hold color, a deep brown that has resisted the Proving Grounds' leeching effect. On Level 2, where everything fades to bruise-light and rust, those brown eyes mark her as something dangerously out of place.

## Personality

Disciplined, alert, and holding herself together through the habits of military training. Mira organizes her days by watch rotations that no longer correspond to anything. She maintains her equipment according to Vigil standards. She addresses other combatants by rank designations she has invented for them. All of this is armor against the Proving Grounds' erosion, and she knows it. She is terrified that if she stops performing the rituals of a mortal soldier, she will begin performing the rituals of the Crucible instead, and then she will forget why she wants to leave.

She is aware that the wanting itself is a trap. The Ashfield's whisper told her ambition leads deeper. The Proving Grounds' cycle tells her that struggle without purpose is the domain's default state. She wants to escape, but she has learned that raw desire feeds the machinery. So she has refined her wanting into something colder: a plan, a route, a calculated assessment of risk. Whether this makes her escape more likely or simply makes her a more interesting specimen for the domain to consume is a question she does not allow herself to ask.

## Backstory

Three years ago by mortal reckoning, Sergeant Mira Ashborn of the [[Ashen Vigil]] was part of an expedition into the Hollowdeep, investigating unusual fragment activity near a thin point in [[The Ashen Dominion]]. The thin point activated during the expedition, and Mira was pulled through along with two members of her squad. She arrived in the Ashfield alone. She passed through Rustgate in less than a day, driven by training and urgency, seeking her squad members. The whisper led her to the Descent Gate, and she walked through it.

The Proving Grounds took her name at the Crucible's threshold, as they take everyone's. But Mira held onto her real name, the one the domain stripped, by a method she does not fully understand. She thinks it is because she did not come here through ambition. She came through duty, looking for her soldiers. The domain's mechanisms were designed for the ambitious. Duty is a gear that does not fit.

She has been fighting in the arena for what feels like decades, earning names and losing them, climbing the Crucible's hierarchy to gain access to information and resources that might help her escape. She has identified a route through the outer battlefields to a point where the domain's architecture thins. She needs combat-capable allies to survive the journey.

## Relationships

- [[Warlord Keth]], who watches Mira with an interest that Mira finds unsettling. She suspects Keth recognizes something in her that Keth cannot name. She is right, but the recognition is the domain's awareness of an incompatible element, not Keth's personal insight.
- [[The Registrar]], who has recorded Mira's arena matches with unusual annotation. The Registrar has noted that Mira's combat style changes between matches, unlike the long-term combatants whose techniques calcify. The Registrar finds this statistically significant.
- [[Bloodqueen Sevra]], who has offered to mentor Mira. The offer is genuine but the price is implicit: Sevra's mentorship would mean committing to the Crucible's system rather than escaping it.
- [[Pit-Master Grenn]], who schedules Mira's matches with a fairness she appreciates. Grenn is the closest thing she has to a neutral contact in the Crucible, someone who treats her as a professional rather than a curiosity or a threat.

## What They Want

Mira wants to escape the Proving Grounds, return to the mortal world, and report back to the Ashen Vigil. That is the mission she has maintained through years of subjective time. What she is beginning to suspect, and cannot afford to examine too closely, is that the mortal world may have moved on without her, that her squad is dead, her post reassigned, and her report irrelevant. The mission may be the only thing keeping her human, and the mission may already be over.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Mira is the most relatable NPC on Level 2, a mortal soldier trying to get home. She can serve as an ally, guide, and emotional anchor for a party navigating the Proving Grounds. Her escape plan provides a concrete objective that the party can assist with.
>
> **Key Secret.** Mira's two squad members did not die in transit. One dissolved in the Ashfield within weeks. The other descended to the Proving Grounds before Mira and has been here much longer. That soldier is now an Iron Wraith, stripped of identity, fighting with empty precision on the outer battlefields. Mira has seen the Wraith and recognized the fighting style. She has not acknowledged this to anyone because doing so would mean admitting she failed to save them.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party helps Mira escape, she arrives in the mortal world years after her departure, a temporal refugee carrying firsthand intelligence about [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s domain that the [[Ember Guild]] and the [[Ashen Vigil]] would kill for. If she stays too long, the Proving Grounds will eventually erode her mortal identity, and the brown-eyed soldier will become just another grey combatant.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Clipped military diction, efficient with words. Reports observations rather than expressing feelings. Stands at attention by default. Calls people "soldier" regardless of whether they are one. Has a habit of checking sight lines when entering a room. Sample line: *"I have been here for three years, or thirty, or three hundred. The Proving Grounds do not track time. I track it by how many names I have earned and lost. The count is fourteen. That is how I know I am still me."*
