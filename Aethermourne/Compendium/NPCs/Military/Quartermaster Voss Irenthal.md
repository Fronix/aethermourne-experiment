# Quartermaster Voss Irenthal

## Appearance

A lean, precise man in his early fifties, with receding brown hair, wire-framed spectacles, and the kind of face that is immediately forgettable, which is useful in his line of work. He dresses in clean but unadorned clothing, favoring dark blues and greys that stand out against the Ironmarch's ubiquitous reds and blacks. He does not carry a weapon visibly, though he keeps a thin knife in his boot that he has never had to use. His hands are stained with ink. His posture is perfect. He moves through the chaos of [[The Red Garrison]] like a man walking through a rainstorm without getting wet.

## Personality

Voss is calm in the way that deep water is calm: still on the surface, with powerful currents underneath. He is the only self-described pacifist in the Ironmarch, a position that is either absurd or heroic depending on who you ask. He does not fight. He has never fought. He considers violence a failure of negotiation, and he has spent his career ensuring that the Ironmarch's violence is at least organized, contractual, and bounded by rules. He is meticulously fair, maddeningly patient, and possessed of a dry wit that surfaces at inappropriate moments. People underestimate him constantly. This is his greatest advantage.

## Backstory

Born in [[The Ashen Dominion]] to a minor bureaucratic family, Voss came to the Ironmarch as a young clerk attached to a trade delegation. The delegation was massacred by bandits in the Contested Corridor. Voss survived because he was trailing behind, taking notes on the terrain. He walked into [[The Red Garrison]] with nothing but his ledger and his spectacles, and he never left.

He discovered that his talent for administration, contract law, and interpersonal mediation was exactly what the Garrison needed. He rose through the [[The Quartermasters Circle|Quartermasters' Circle]] on competence alone, becoming its youngest head at thirty-eight. He has held the position for fourteen years, navigating the Ironmarch's politics with the same meticulous attention to detail he applies to contract language. He has brokered more peace than any warrior in the region, and he has done it with paperwork.

## Relationships

- [[Yrsa Bloodhand]], the Iron Thane. They communicate through coded letters and share a conviction that the Ironmarch's cycle of violence can be disrupted. Voss is the closest thing Yrsa has to a political ally outside her own forces.
- [[Mira the Red]], the rising challenger. Voss has tried to open negotiations with her three times. Each time, her envoys have been polite and noncommittal. He suspects she is humoring him until she is strong enough to ignore him.
- [[Harsk the Quiet]], captain of the Silent Company. Voss is one of the few people Harsk will sit in a room with. Their relationship is wordless, mutual respect expressed through contracts honored and obligations met.
- **Marshals-Captain Torva**, his enforcement arm in the Garrison. Voss makes the rules. Torva enforces them. They disagree on methods frequently and trust each other completely.

## What They Want

Voss wants the Garrison's neutrality to survive. He wants the mercenary codes to hold, the contracts to be honored, and the structured violence of the Ironmarch to continue because the alternative, unstructured violence, is incalculably worse. Beneath this, he wants to prove that civilization is possible here, that rules and fairness can function even in a land saturated with divine aggression. He is terrified that [[Mira the Red|Mira's]] war will destroy everything he has built, and he is preparing contingency plans that he hopes he will never need.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Key ally and information source in Acts II-III. Voss knows the Ironmarch's political landscape better than anyone alive, and his network of contacts extends across the continent through mercenary contracts. He is an excellent quest-giver for politically sensitive missions.
>
> **Key Secret.** Voss has been secretly funding a network of informants inside [[Mira the Red|Mira's]] camp. He knows the scope of her ambitions and the extent of her [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] contamination. He has not shared this intelligence with [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Yrsa]] because he fears it would provoke a preemptive strike that would destroy the Garrison's neutrality. He is sitting on information that could save lives, paralyzed by the fear that sharing it would cost more.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs help preserve the Garrison's neutrality, Voss becomes a crucial stabilizing force during the region's crisis. If the neutrality breaks, Voss will try to evacuate the Garrison's archives, the centuries of contracts and precedents that represent the Ironmarch's only body of law, and carry them to safety. He will die protecting paperwork, and he will consider it a worthwhile trade.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in complete, grammatically precise sentences. Never interrupts. Adjusts his spectacles when thinking. Has a habit of quoting contract language in casual conversation. Smiles rarely but genuinely.
> *"Violence is expensive, inefficient, and, in my experience, almost never solves the problem it was deployed to address. But I have learned not to say that too loudly in the Ironmarch."*
