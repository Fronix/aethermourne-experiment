## Appearance

A woman who died at thirty and has been undead for decades. Her appearance is preserved but unmistakably wrong: grey-blue skin, frost-white hair, eyes that glow faintly with blue-white light like a frozen lake catching the sun. A black handprint over her heart, the mark of the bone-wraith that killed her, has never healed and never will. She wears the [[The Ashen Vigil|Ashen Vigil]] armor she died in, maintained with the discipline of a soldier who no longer needs to eat but still needs purpose. She does not breathe, does not blink unless she remembers to, and moves with a stillness that the living find difficult to watch.

## Personality

Fierce, articulate, and deeply empathetic, ironic for someone technically dead. Yael knows her appearance unsettles people and compensates with deliberate warmth, careful eye contact, measured gestures designed to seem alive. She is patient, but beneath the patience is a profound anger she controls with visible effort, anger at a world that calls her people monsters for the crime of not staying dead. She advocates for the [[Remnants Court|Remnants]] with the articulate precision of someone who has been making the same argument for years and refuses to stop.

## Backstory

In life, Yael was a [[The Ashen Vigil|Vigil]] captain, one of the best by all accounts, who died defending [[Lastlight]] during an undead incursion. Her body was recovered, prepared for burning, and placed on the Pyre Ground. Three days later she sat up, looked at the fire-keeper, and said: *"Not yet."*

The decades since have been spent proving, through action and argument, that Remnants are people. She helped negotiate the expansion of the Remnant Accords, founded the [[Remnants Court|Remnants' Court]] as a governing body for the aware undead of [[Ashenmere]], and made herself impossible to dismiss. Her record as a Vigil captain in life gives her credibility that most Remnants lack. She has fought alongside the living, counseled Holdwardens, and buried friends who aged and died while she remained unchanged.

She remembers what it was like to breathe, to feel warmth, to sleep. She does not mourn these things. She mourns the assumption that losing them made her less.

## Relationships

- **[[Bjorn Ashken]]**, her most important living ally. Bjorn judges her by what she does, not what she is, and that pragmatic acceptance means more to Yael than any formal accord.
- **[[Thessa Ironveil]]**, complicated respect. Ironveil was raised in the Vigil tradition that all undead are enemies. She works with Yael because she is too pragmatic to refuse, but the discomfort has not fully faded.
- **[[Merren Vask]]**, a Vigil scout who became a Remnant. Yael feels responsible for every new Remnant, especially those who did not choose it. Merren's survival in the [[The Pale Wastes#The Hollowdeep|Hollowdeep]] worries her deeply.
- **Revenant Moss**, a Remnant in [[Ashenmere]] who is deteriorating, losing memories, becoming less coherent. Yael watches Moss's decline with private terror, wondering if the same fate awaits her.

## What They Want

To prove that death did not end her personhood, and to build a world where the [[Remnants Court|Remnants]] are recognized as people rather than useful anomalies. Deeper than that, she wants to understand why some dead retain consciousness and others do not, because the answer to that question determines whether her existence is a miracle or an accident.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Morally complex ally in Act IV. Working with Yael means accepting that the line between life and death is blurry. She is ancient and powerful, and if the PCs earn her trust, she will guide them into the [[The Pale Wastes#The Hollowdeep|Hollowdeep]] and share what she knows of [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael's]] domain within. She remembers the god as he was before the Theomachis: not a figure of horror but a steward of transition. Her grief for what Morrhael has become, a skull full of enslaved dead, is the emotional anchor of Act IV.
>
> **Key Secret.** During the three days between her death and her rising, Yael was somewhere: a cold, vast place filled with frozen figures standing in rows that stretched to a horizon she could not see. She heard a voice, not [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael's]], something smaller, sharper, more deliberate. It said: *"You are useful. Go back."* The voice was [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]]. Yael is genuinely autonomous, but the connection exists, dormant. If Serith chooses to activate it, Yael may hear whispers she cannot refuse. This should be a late-campaign revelation handled with care.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** Begins as a political advocate demanding recognition. As Act IV escalates, she becomes a war leader and spiritual guide, the only person alive (or otherwise) who can navigate [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael's]] death-domain. If the PCs protect her from Serith's influence, she emerges as one of the campaign's most powerful allies. If they fail, Serith may co-opt the dormant connection and turn Yael into an unwitting conduit.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks with careful warmth, deliberate and measured. Occasionally pauses mid-sentence as if remembering what breathing felt like. Fiercely protective of the Remnants under her care. Humor is dry and self-aware. Does not fidget, does not shift weight, stands with the absolute stillness of someone whose body no longer demands movement. Sample line: *"I have been dead for longer than most of your soldiers have been alive. I can wait. But the Hollowdeep cannot."*
