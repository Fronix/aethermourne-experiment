## Appearance

A weathered dwarven soldier of one hundred and forty years, Tarn Smokeshield looks like the volcanic pass he has guarded for three decades. His skin is rough and darkened by years of exposure to sulphurous haze, his beard kept regulation-short and streaked with grey. His left eye is clouded by a chemical burn sustained in a gas vent accident twenty years ago, but he sees well enough with the right. He wears practical half-plate armour of forgeheart steel over mail, the metal bearing the distinctive deep-orange lustre of Emberveil craft. A heavy warhammer hangs at his belt, more symbol of office than weapon, though he is more than capable with it. He moves with the deliberate economy of a professional soldier who has never wasted a step in his life.

## Personality

Tarn is a pragmatist wrapped in a soldier's discipline. He does not deal in ideology, philosophy, or grand strategy. He deals in defensible positions, supply lines, and the observable intentions of potential enemies. He has spent thirty years reading the Dominion through the lens of their trade delegations, their scout movements, and the questions their merchants ask, and he trusts that reading more than any diplomatic assurance. He is deeply loyal to the Emberveil and deeply sceptical of the Forge Council's belief that trade leverage alone will keep the Dominion at bay. He is gruff, taciturn, and surprisingly humorous in private, a bone-dry wit that emerges when he trusts his company.

## Backstory

Tarn served in the Emberveil's volunteer militia before the Forge Council formalized a military structure at Smokewall. He was one of the original garrison, a young soldier assigned to a post that most considered a backwater, watching traders and checking customs manifests. Over three decades, he watched the post grow from a customs checkpoint to a fortified border town, shaped it into a defensible position through persistent lobbying, creative engineering, and the kind of stubborn refusal to accept "no" that the Forge Council eventually learned to call leadership.

He has never left the Emberveil. He has never visited [[The Ashen Dominion]]. He knows the Dominion entirely through thirty years of observing its representatives, its traders, its spies, and its soldiers from the other side of the haze. This focused perspective has made him one of the most perceptive analysts of Dominion intentions in the region, if not the most diplomatic one.

Five years ago, he submitted a formal assessment to [[The Forge Council]] predicting that Dominion trade pressure would escalate into strategic interest within a decade. The assessment was filed and forgotten. He has watched every prediction in it come true on schedule.

## Relationships

- [[Brunhild Ironheart|Forge-Matriarch Brunhild Ironheart]], He respects her but believes she underestimates the military dimension of the Dominion threat. He has sent three formal warnings in the past year. The responses have been polite and insufficient.
- **Customs-Chief Brenna Scalehand**, His most trusted civilian counterpart. Between them, they control what enters and leaves the Emberveil. Their working relationship is seamless and has quietly become the region's first line of intelligence.
- **Rook Ashendale**, The cross-border trader is useful but not trusted. Tarn feeds him carefully selected information to see where it surfaces on the Dominion side. So far, Ashendale has been honest. Tarn is waiting for the day he is not.
- [[Vessa Ironvow]], He considers her a security risk. Her illegal mining operations in the outer warrens create tunnels that could theoretically provide a Dominion force with a route past Smokewall's defences. He has asked the Council for permission to shut her down. The request is pending.

## What They Want

Tarn wants the Forge Council to take the Dominion threat seriously before it becomes a crisis. He wants the garrison reinforced, the pass fortifications upgraded, and a formal contingency plan for the possibility of Dominion military action. What drives him deeper is the soldier's fear of being right too late, of submitting the correct assessment and watching his leaders ignore it until the smoke clears and the pass is already lost.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Military contact and border intelligence source. He can provide the PCs with detailed information about Dominion troop movements, trade patterns, and spy activity. He is also the most likely NPC to detect Cinder agents entering the Emberveil, if the PCs share what they know about [[Delric Mourne]] and the Cinders, Tarn becomes an invaluable ally.
>
> **Key Secret.** He has captured and interrogated a Dominion scout whose story did not hold up under questioning. The scout was carrying a coded message that Tarn's cipher-breaker could not crack. He suspects it is Cinder encryption but has no way to confirm this without Dominion intelligence contacts he does not have.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs bring him evidence of the Cinders' role in engineering the Dominion's trade pressure, he goes from cautious defensive commander to active intelligence operator, running counter-espionage operations from Smokewall. If ignored, he eventually closes the pass unilaterally, triggering an economic crisis that forces the Forge Council's hand.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in clipped military cadence. Never uses three words where two will do. Looks at people's boots and hands, assessing whether they have marched and whether they have fought. Drinks a bitter volcanic-mineral tea that he offers to everyone and no one enjoys.
>
> *Sample dialogue:* "I've watched the Dominion for thirty years through two hundred feet of sulphur haze. I can't see their faces. But I can count their boots, and the count is going up."
