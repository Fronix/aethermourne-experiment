## Appearance

A towering figure in a general's uniform from a civilization that no longer exists, its cut and insignia belonging to no culture any living scholar could identify. The fabric is immaculate, pressed and buttoned as though for a parade, yet the medals pinned across the chest are tarnished beyond recognition. Their face is sharp, angular, and ageless, with eyes that burn a dull amber, the color of cooling iron. They carry no weapon. They carry themselves as though they do not need one.

## Personality

Precise, commanding, and utterly sincere. The Last General issues orders with the calm authority of someone who expects absolute obedience, and the fact that no one is there to obey does not diminish the conviction. They are brilliant, genuinely so, capable of analyzing tactical situations with a depth that would humble mortal strategists. They are also kind in a distant, military way, the sort of commander who memorizes every soldier's name and writes letters to families that no longer exist. Their tragedy is not madness but clarity misapplied.

## Backstory

The Last General was [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s mortal champion in the final battle of the Theomachis, the last soldier to fight under the god's banner before he fell. In life, they commanded armies that shaped the fate of nations, and their tactical genius was legendary even before Kaevroth's influence magnified it. When the god died, the General's soul was drawn into the Iron Descent, not as punishment but as preservation. Kaevroth's fading will needed someone to maintain the Conquest, and the General was the only soul he trusted.

For two thousand years, the Last General has moved between the cycling kingdoms of the Thousand Thrones, presiding over each empire's single perfect moment before its inevitable fall. They understand the cycle completely. They know every kingdom will collapse. They rule anyway, because command is all they have left, and because somewhere in the iron splinter buried in their chest, Kaevroth's ambition still whispers that the next campaign will be the one that holds.

## Relationships

- **[[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]**, the god they served. The General remembers him as he was before the end, brilliant and driven, not yet consumed by his own nature. They carry a splinter of his iron body in their chest and consider it both a gift and a chain.
- **[[Lord Imperator Vaelgrim]]**, the ruler of Level 7. A complicated respect exists between them. Both were Kaevroth's commanders. Vaelgrim governs the Court; the General governs the memory of war. They disagree on whether the Descent should be maintained or allowed to collapse.
- **[[Crown-Keeper Ash]]**, the archivist of the Field of Banners. The General values Ash's meticulous record-keeping but finds her acceptance of futility unsettling. She catalogues failure. The General refuses to.
- **[[Herald Unbroken]]**, the guardian of the Bone Gate. The General gave Herald their standing order two thousand years ago and has never rescinded it. They trust Herald absolutely, which may be a mistake, since Herald's strength is fading.

## What They Want

The Last General wants to win. Not a specific battle, not a specific war, just to experience victory one more time, genuine and lasting. They know it is impossible within the Conquest's cycling nature. Their deeper need is release from the compulsion to command, but they cannot distinguish between who they are and what Kaevroth made them.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Last General is a potential ally or obstacle on Level 6. They control access to the Bone Gate and can provide intelligence about the Descent's deeper levels. Their challenge (lead a kingdom through a full cycle without falling) is the primary encounter hook for this level.
>
> **Key Secret.** The iron splinter in the General's chest is the only fragment of Kaevroth's body that exists within the Iron Descent. It is the mechanism that synchronizes the Conquest's kingdom cycles. Removing it would plunge Level 6 into permanent chaos, every kingdom rising and falling at random.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs earn the General's respect, they become a reluctant guide deeper into the Descent. If the PCs remove the splinter, the General collapses, their identity inseparable from Kaevroth's will. If the PCs convince the General that the war is truly over, they may be the first soul to voluntarily dissolve from the Descent, but the consequences for Level 6 would be catastrophic.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speak in clipped, military cadence. Never waste a word. Address everyone by rank, assigning ranks to the PCs based on first impression. Pause before answering questions, as though consulting a battle plan. Voice: low, steady, certain.
> *"You have the bearing of a lieutenant. Unpolished, but serviceable. Report."*
