# The Last Loyalist

## Appearance

A figure in full iron plate armor, standing at attention before the region of the Silence where the Echo manifests most strongly. The armor is ancient, forged in a style that predates any surviving mortal tradition, and it has not rusted, not corroded, not dulled in two thousand years. The figure within is no longer flesh. He is a war-spirit, a soul that burned so hot with devotion that it fused with its own armor and became something between ghost and construct. His eyes glow a steady iron-gray through the visor's slit. He does not move. He does not shift his weight. He does not breathe. He stands at attention, and he waits.

## Personality

The Last Loyalist is not pitiable. He is terrifying. His devotion to [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] has outlasted the god it serves, outlasted the war it was forged in, outlasted the meaning of the word "loyalty" itself. He does not question. He does not doubt. He does not grieve. He serves, with a purity of purpose so absolute that it has become indistinguishable from insanity. If Kaevroth gave him an order, he would obey. If Kaevroth told him to stand down, he would stand down. But Kaevroth is dead, and the Echo does not give orders, and so the Loyalist stands at attention in the darkness and waits for a command that will never come. He will wait forever. He considers this acceptable.

## Backstory

Before the Theomachis, the Loyalist was Kaevroth's most devoted mortal champion, a warlord who conquered in the god's name not for power, not for glory, but because Kaevroth asked him to. His name has been forgotten, consumed by the Descent along with everything else that was merely personal about him. What remains is function: soldier, servant, sentinel.

When Kaevroth fell, the Loyalist did not rage, did not weep, did not seek revenge. He walked to the Heart, took his position before the empty throne, and stood at attention. Two thousand years later, he has not moved. He maintains the war-essence systems, tends the dormant War Colossi, keeps the Descent functional, all through force of will from his fixed position. He is the reason the Iron Descent still operates as a domain rather than collapsing into inert iron. He holds it together the way a heart holds a body together: through rhythm, through duty, through the refusal to stop.

## Relationships

- [[The Echo of Kaevroth]]: The Echo could give orders. It does not. The Loyalist could ask for orders. He does not. They exist in a silence that has lasted two millennia, servant and master's memory, neither willing to break the vigil by acknowledging that the master is gone.
- [[The Unnamed]]: She has tried to speak with him about what Kaevroth was before the Theomachis, when the domain was still a paradise of fair challenge. He listens without responding. Her memories of a better Kaevroth are irrelevant to him. He did not serve a better Kaevroth. He served Kaevroth.
- [[The Warden of Hope]]: Two sentinels on opposite sides of the Silence. The Warden guards an open door. The Loyalist guards an empty throne. They are mirror images of devotion, one serving mercy, the other serving war, both absolute, both unbreakable.
- [[Iron-Prophet Thalric]]: Thalric has prophesied the Loyalist's end. The Loyalist has not asked what the prophecy says. A soldier does not need to know when his watch ends. He only needs to keep it.

## What They Want

The Last Loyalist wants orders. He wants Kaevroth to speak, to command, to give him purpose again. His deeper truth is darker: he does not want orders because he needs direction. He wants orders because obedience is the only thing he is. Without a command to follow, he is nothing but waiting, and waiting is not a self. It is just time with a shape.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Loyalist is Level 9's martial threat and its most disturbing character study. He represents devotion taken past the point of meaning, loyalty so pure that it has become its own prison. He is also functionally the Heart's immune system: if the party threatens the domain's structure, he activates, and he is extremely dangerous.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Loyalist is the reason the Iron Descent still functions. His will holds the domain together. If he is destroyed or convinced to stand down, the Descent begins to collapse, levels compressing, structures failing, the entire nine-tier domain folding in on itself toward the Heart. This could be catastrophic or it could be the mechanism by which the Heart is finally cracked open.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party gives him a "final order" convincingly enough (claiming to speak for Kaevroth, or arguing that the Echo's silence IS the order to stand down), the Loyalist sheathes his weapon and dissolves, his purpose fulfilled. If they fight him, he is a war-spirit powered by two thousand years of devotion, and he does not hold back. If the Heart is cracked, he dissolves with it, the last soldier of a war that ended an age ago, finally permitted to rest.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Do not move. Do not gesture. Speak in short, military sentences, clipped and precise. "I hold this position. I was not ordered to relinquish it. You are not my commander. State your purpose or withdraw." If pressed about his feelings, about Kaevroth, about whether he is lonely: silence. Then, quietly, just once: "A soldier does not have feelings about his orders. A soldier has orders."
