# Marchwarden Torvin Ashwalker

## Appearance

In his sixties, lean and deeply scarred, with dark skin and grey-streaked hair worn in tight warrior-braids. His face carries the permanently suspicious squint of a man who has spent decades watching the Scarwall and the horizon beyond it for trouble. He moves with the careful economy of someone who has learned that wasted motion gets people killed in the Scar. He carries a Scarwall-thorn spear, a weapon made from harvested thorns of the living wall, effective and distinctive, its shaft wrapped in leather stained green from the sap. His clothes are practical and patched, a mix of Marcher leathers and, if one looks closely, Dominion-made buckles and clasps.

## Personality

Suspicious of everyone, fiercely territorial, and surprisingly practical. Torvin has no patience for ideology. He has watched the Scarwall grow, listened to the Greensingers at the Listening Post report the wall's increasing agitation, and traded quietly with the enemy because his people need iron and tools the forest cannot provide. He does not see a contradiction between defending the border and trading across it. He sees survival.

He supports neither [[Kaelith Thornborn]] nor [[Draven Ironbark]] fully, believing both are right about different things and wrong about the things that matter most. Unification means nothing if the border falls. Independence means nothing if the clans starve for lack of iron. He judges people by what they do, not what they believe.

## Backstory

Torvin inherited the Ashwalker wardenship from his mother, Kera Ashwalker, who held the border for thirty years before a Scar-quake collapsed a section of the wall and killed her during the repair effort. He was twenty-five, unprepared, and terrified. He learned quickly because the border does not wait for readiness.

The Scar, the ecological collision zone where [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]]'s and [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s divine influences war, shaped him into something harder than the other Marchwardens. Living where two dead gods grind against each other teaches a person that principles are luxuries and pragmatism is survival. He watched calcified trees split by living vines, saw Ashite crystal formations wrapped in pulsing moss, and learned that the world does not care about borders drawn by mortals.

Two years ago, he established a secret trading relationship with [[Captain Daren Kolst]], garrison commander at [[Thornwall]] on the Dominion side. Iron, worked metal, and stone tools flow into the Marches. Medicinal herbs, alchemical compounds, and information flow out. Both men understand they are technically committing treason against their respective nations. Both consider the trade essential. The relationship has grown into something resembling mutual respect, though neither man would use that word.

## Relationships

- [[Captain Daren Kolst]], his secret trading partner across the Scarwall. The closest thing Torvin has to a friend, though they meet only at the Gate Market and speak in careful, practical terms.
- [[Kaelith Thornborn]], respected but not trusted. Torvin believes her divine mandate is genuine but worries that unification will bring politics to his border.
- [[Draven Ironbark]], understood but not supported. Torvin shares Ironbark's distrust of concentrated power but thinks pure independence is a death sentence.
- [[The Greensingers]] at the Listening Post, whose reports of the Scarwall's increasing agitation keep him awake at night.
- [[Marchwardens Council]], where he votes based on border needs and ignores everything else.

## What They Want

A quiet border, iron for his people, and answers. Something has been moving inside the Scarwall, and a Dominion patrol recently approached under a white flag asking to discuss a shared threat from below. Torvin agreed to listen, and what they described matches what the Greensingers have been sensing in the deep Cradle. He wants to know what is happening beneath the earth, and he wants it to stop before it reaches his people.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Torvin is a strategically critical NPC whose secret trade with Kolst may become the only communication channel between the Marches and the Dominion if the Scarwall closes entirely. He is also a potential ally for PCs navigating the border, providing passage, information, and connections on both sides.
>
> **Key Secret.** His trading relationship with [[Captain Daren Kolst]] has been active for two years. Both men are committing treason. Both consider it necessary. The trade includes not just goods but intelligence, and both have begun sharing observations about the corruption spreading beneath the Scar.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs discover the trade, they face a choice: expose it (destroying a vital cross-border link) or protect it (earning trust from both sides). If the Scarwall closes, Torvin and Kolst become the most important diplomatic channel in the setting. If either man is killed or compromised, the other nation loses its best source of ground-level intelligence.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in short, blunt sentences. Never wastes words. Watches hands and eyes, not faces. Pauses before answering questions, not because he is slow, but because he is deciding how much truth to share. Carries a faint smell of thorn-sap and iron filings. *"I don't care what your politics are. I care whether you can hold a line and keep your mouth shut. Can you?"*
