# Captain Rook Tessavar

## Appearance

In his forties, tall and lean, with sun-darkened skin and a shaved head. A gold earring in his left ear catches the lamplight. On his right hand he wears a ring of [[Cosmology and Magic#Divine Materials|Kaevroth's Iron]], a trophy from a raid he refuses to discuss, though those who know what the metal does wonder how he wears it without consequence. He dresses well for a pirate: clean linen, good boots, a long coat that was clearly stolen from a [[Concord of Tides]] official. His smile comes easily. His eyes do not.

## Personality

Pragmatic, dangerous, and considerably more intelligent than he lets on. Rook plays the charming rogue because it opens doors and closes suspicions, but beneath the persona is a cold, calculating mind that has kept him alive and prosperous in the most treacherous waters in [[The Hollowed Reach]]. He fights dirty, negotiates dirtier, and operates by a code that is entirely his own: he does not hurt children, he never breaks a sealed deal, and he kills anyone who threatens [[Driftmere]]. This last rule is not negotiable.

## Backstory

Rook was born on Driftmere to a dockhand mother and a father he never knew. He grew up swimming between the lashed hulls, learning the city's unwritten laws before he could read written ones. By fifteen he was crewing on smuggling runs through the outer archipelago. By twenty he had his own ship.

He earned his Council seat the hard way, by surviving when others did not. During a Black Tide eight years ago, three Council captains were lost along with their fleets. Rook kept the *Blacktide* and her crew alive by navigating through the Trench fog using dead reckoning and sheer nerve. The survivors rallied around him, and the Council seat followed. He commands the *Blacktide* and a fleet of six smaller vessels, making him the largest single force in Driftmere's defense.

He has never lived anywhere else. Driftmere is the only home he has ever known, and his loyalty to the floating city is absolute, even if his methods are ruthless.

## Relationships

- **[[Sable]]**, he knows her as the quiet chandler who runs the Pale Margin. He suspects she is a smuggler, possibly connected to something larger, but has never investigated. He is wrong about how deep it goes.
- **[[Maren Selk]]**, has never met her personally, but benefits from the Concord's unspoken tolerance of Driftmere. He considers the arrangement fragile and plans accordingly.
- **Mags Underhill**, the Driftwood's owner and Driftmere's unofficial intelligence broker. They understand each other perfectly, which means they trust each other exactly as far as necessary.
- **[[Lira Ashvane]]**, respects the Lantern-Keepers from a distance. He would deal with Ashvane if the price were right, but has no interest in her crusade unless it threatens Driftmere directly.
- **[[Aldren Vayne]]**, the Tidefleet stays out of Driftmere's waters and Rook stays out of the inner shipping lanes. The arrangement works because both men prefer pragmatism to pride.

## What They Want

Rook wants Driftmere's survival and his own power within it, in that order. He would sacrifice his profit, his fleet, and his life for the floating city, though he would never say so aloud. His deeper fear is that the Black Tides will eventually destroy the thing he has spent his life building, and that when the moment comes, all his cleverness will not be enough.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Rook is the most accessible Pirate Council contact for PCs. He can be negotiated with, hired, or allied with if the stakes are framed in terms of Driftmere's safety. He appears in Act I as a potential ally or obstacle depending on the party's approach.
>
> **Key Secret.** He captured a map of the [[Abyssal Trench]] from a [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] agent and is running the auction at the Driftwood. The map's annotations are changing overnight in inhuman handwriting, and Rook is more disturbed by this than he shows. He can be persuaded to sell to [[The Lantern-Keepers]] if given a good reason, or a good price.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs ally with him, Rook becomes a reliable, if self-interested, asset throughout Act I. If the Black Tides worsen dramatically, his pragmatism may crack into genuine desperation, making him either a fiercer ally or a dangerous wildcard willing to make deals with anyone who promises to protect Driftmere.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks with an easy, unhurried drawl that makes everything sound like a joke, even threats. Leans back in his chair, plays with his Kaevroth-iron ring when thinking. Never raises his voice. The less he smiles, the more dangerous the situation.
> *"I don't care who wants the map, friend. I care who can afford it, and what happens to my city if the wrong buyer walks away with it."*
