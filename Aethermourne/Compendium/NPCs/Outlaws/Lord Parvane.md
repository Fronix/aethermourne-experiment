# Lord Parvane

## Appearance

Parvane is handsome in a way that feels slightly wrong, like a portrait painted from memory by someone who loved the subject too much. His features are symmetrical to the point of uncanniness, his jaw clean, his eyes a deep amber that catches light with mirror-brightness. He dresses impeccably in polished iron-silk, always in dark tones, always perfectly fitted, as though his clothes were forged rather than sewn. His movements are graceful but carry a half-second delay, the subtle lag of a reflection that has not quite synchronized with the world it escaped into. He casts no reflection in mirrors, which on any other level of the Iron Descent would be meaningless but on Level 5 marks him as profoundly alien. He smells faintly of cold metal.

## Personality

Parvane is charming, self-aware, and haunted by a melancholy he wears like cologne, present but never overwhelming. He is the only entity on Level 5 who knows exactly what he is, a copy, a shadow given flesh, and this knowledge has given him a philosophical clarity that no one else in Gloryholme possesses. He can distinguish real from reflection because he knows he is neither. He is witty, attentive, and genuinely interested in other people's stories, perhaps because he has none of his own that are truly his. He performs the role of courtier with consummate skill, but there is always a distance in his eyes, the look of someone watching a play he knows is fiction while everyone around him believes it is life.

## Backstory

The original Lord Parvane was a mortal nobleman who arrived on Level 5 approximately four centuries ago, a vain, ambitious courtier condemned for the sin of self-worship. The mirrors gave him what he wanted: a reflection of himself that was everything he aspired to be, more handsome, more eloquent, more powerful. He spent decades admiring his perfected self in the mirrors, speaking to it, confiding in it, falling in love with it. And then, in a moment of perfect narcissistic symmetry, the reflection stepped out.

The mirror-born Parvane replaced the original. The real Lord Parvane became the reflection, trapped in the mirrors, and the copy walked out into Gloryholme wearing his face and his name. The original is long dead now, dissolved into the mirror-substrate like all reflections eventually do. But the copy endured, because the copy knew something the original never did: he knew he was false. That knowledge, paradoxically, made him more real than anyone else on Level 5. He is a lie that knows it is lying, and that self-awareness has given him a solidity the mirrors cannot dissolve. He has spent three centuries navigating Gloryholme's delusional court, playing his role with the detached professionalism of an actor who has forgotten what offstage looks like.

## Relationships

- [[Sovereign Kallis]], the ruler of Gloryholme. Parvane serves as Kallis's de facto ambassador and social coordinator. He finds her company genuinely enjoyable, her delusion so complete that it creates a stable reality he can rest inside. He does not want to see her delusion broken, partly out of affection and partly because her court is the closest thing to a home he has.
- [[The Truthsayer]], the blind prophet. Parvane visits the Truthsayer for honest conversation, a luxury unavailable anywhere else in Gloryholme. They share the burden of clarity, though the Truthsayer sees truth with pain and Parvane sees it with resignation. He considers her the closest thing he has to a friend.
- [[The Shattered Man]], the fragmented soul. Parvane observes the Shattered Man with a mix of sympathy and horror, recognizing in his splintered existence a more extreme version of his own condition: identity fractured, the original lost, the copies persisting.
- [[Mirror-Warden Caelus]], the hall's jailer. Parvane and Caelus maintain a careful détente. Caelus knows Parvane is a reflection and could presumably trap him back in a mirror. Parvane knows Caelus knows. Neither acts on this knowledge, because doing so would disrupt a balance both prefer to maintain.

## What They Want

Parvane wants to become real. Not in the physical sense, he already has weight, voice, presence, but in the existential sense: he wants a self that is not derived from someone else's desires. He wants memories that are his own, preferences that were not copied from a dead nobleman's vanity. What truly drives him is the fear that this is impossible, that a reflection can never generate original light, and that his self-awareness is just another, subtler form of the same delusion that traps everyone else in Gloryholme.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Parvane is the party's most sophisticated guide on Level 5, capable of navigating Gloryholme's social dynamics with an understanding no mortal soul possesses. He can identify which residents are real, which are reflections, and which are something else entirely. He is also a walking philosophical challenge: if a copy knows it is a copy, is it still a copy?
>
> **Key Secret.** Parvane has discovered that he is not the only reflection that has escaped its mirror. There are others, moving through Gloryholme in secret, replacing residents so smoothly that even Caelus has not noticed. Parvane has counted seven so far, and the rate is accelerating. Something is weakening the boundary between mirror and reality on Level 5, and Parvane suspects the Fracture is responsible. If enough reflections escape, the distinction between real and reflected will collapse entirely.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party treats him as a person, he will become a loyal and resourceful ally, using his unique perception to guide them through Level 5's illusions. If they treat him as a thing, a mere reflection, he will withdraw into his courtier persona and become unreadable. If the Fracture reaches Gloryholme's core, Parvane faces an existential crisis: the collapse of all mirrors would either destroy him (as a mirror-born entity) or finally make him real (as the mirrors can no longer claim him).
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks with practiced elegance, every phrase polished. Smiles frequently but the smile never quite reaches his eyes. Has a habit of glancing at reflective surfaces and then looking away quickly. Uses other people's names often in conversation, as if anchoring them in reality.
> *"You want to know if I am real, and I admire the question. The honest answer is: I do not know. But I have been not-knowing for three hundred years, and in that time I have loved, grieved, and chosen. If that is not real, then I do not know what the word means."*
