# Mira the Red

## Appearance

A young woman, barely twenty-five, with the lean, hard build of someone who has been fighting since childhood. Her hair is dark red, the color that gives her name, and she wears it loose, a vanity that no practical soldier would indulge but that has become her banner. Her most unsettling feature is her eyes: iron-grey from rim to rim, with no visible white, the most advanced case of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] contamination anyone in the Ironmarch has seen in a living person. She wears mismatched armor, pieces taken from defeated opponents, and carries a sword of [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] that she forged herself in a field-smithy, crude but functional. She does not sleep. She has not slept in months. She does not appear to need to.

## Personality

Mira is brilliant, charismatic, and no longer entirely herself. She has the natural magnetism of a born leader, the tactical instincts of a gifted commander, and the bottomless ambition that [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] essence amplifies into something beyond human scale. She believes she is destined to unite the Ironmarch, then the continent, then everything, and she believes this because a voice in the iron tells her so. She is not delusional in the conventional sense. She is correct that [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] residual will is communicating with her. She is incorrect in believing that it has her best interests at heart.

What makes Mira tragic rather than simply monstrous is that part of her knows. In rare moments, usually at dusk when the fragments are quietest, she catches herself and sees the pattern: the escalating ambition, the inability to stop, the way her followers look at her with devotion that feels manufactured. She cannot hold onto these moments. The iron fills them in, smooths over the doubt, replaces clarity with certainty. She is drowning in someone else's purpose, and the water is warm enough that she has mostly stopped struggling.

## Backstory

Born in a frontier hold in the Iron Fields, Mira lost her family to a territorial raid at seven and was raised by a mercenary company, the Red Dust, that found her scavenging in the aftermath. She grew up on campaign, learned to fight before she learned to read, and killed her first opponent at twelve. By twenty, she had command of the Red Dust. By twenty-two, she had absorbed three other companies into her force. She was talented, ambitious, and hard, the Ironmarch's standard model of a rising warlord.

The change came when she made camp in the Shardfall and spent a night sleeping on bare earth saturated with [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] iron. She woke with grey eyes and a voice in her head that spoke in the cadence of a war-drum. It told her she was chosen, that she would unite what was broken, that the iron would be whole again. She has been listening ever since. Her army has grown from hundreds to thousands. Her ambitions have grown from regional to continental. And the voice has never stopped.

## Relationships

- [[Yrsa Bloodhand]], the Iron Thane. Mira sees Yrsa as an obstacle, a tired old woman clinging to a title she lacks the vision to use. She respects Yrsa's strength but believes her time has passed. She does not understand that Yrsa is the only person in the Ironmarch with the clarity to see what Mira is becoming.
- [[Harsk the Quiet]], whose Silent Company she wants. Mira has offered Harsk command of her entire army. His silence frustrates her. She cannot read him, and [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] voice cannot reach him (he is already full).
- [[Sira Embersteel]], whose weapons she wants. Mira has sent agents to [[Wrathspire]] to secure Sira's services. Sira's refusal is a problem she has not yet decided how to solve violently.
- The voice. Mira's most important relationship is with [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] residual will. She calls it "the Iron" and speaks of it the way a devout person speaks of their god: with reverence, obedience, and a flicker of fear she will not acknowledge.

## What They Want

Mira wants to unite the Ironmarch under a single banner, hers. She wants to end the petty squabbling of warlords and forge the region into something powerful enough to matter on the continental stage. This is her conscious goal, and it is [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] goal wearing her face. What Mira, the person beneath the contamination, wants is simpler: she wants the family she lost, the safety she never had, and the ability to stop fighting. She will never get these things, because the iron will not let her stop.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Primary antagonist for the Ironmarch arc in Acts II-III. She is the human face of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] fragmented will, tragic rather than evil, a weapon being wielded by a dead god's reflexes.
>
> **Key Secret.** Mira is not just influenced by [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]], she is becoming a vessel for his reassembly. The fragments are migrating toward each other, and they are also migrating toward her. She has been unconsciously collecting shards, embedding them in her armor, sleeping with them, pressing them against her skin. Her body is slowly incorporating divine iron. In six months, she will be more fragment than person. In a year, she will be something entirely new: not [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] resurrected, but a mortal-divine hybrid with a god's hunger and a human's capacity for cruelty.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** The PCs can save Mira, but only if they act before the transformation is irreversible. Separating her from the fragments, exposing her to a counter-essence (Morrhaelite, Ashite, or Thyrea's nature-essence), and giving her a moment of genuine clarity could break [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] hold. If they kill her, the fragments she carries will scatter and the migration will pause, but not stop. If they do nothing, she will march on [[Ironhaven]], destroy [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Yrsa]], and then march west through the Contested Corridor toward Ostivaar and the Heart of Iron. The convergence will be catastrophic.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks with confidence that borders on serenity, the absolute certainty of someone who does not doubt. Her voice occasionally drops into a lower register that does not sound entirely human, the iron speaking through her. In rare moments of clarity, she becomes younger, confused, frightened, a twenty-five-year-old who does not understand what is happening to her. These moments are brief and heartbreaking.
> *"They call me a warlord. A conqueror. They're thinking too small. The Ironmarch has been broken for two thousand years. I'm going to make it whole. The iron promised me. The iron always keeps its promises."*
