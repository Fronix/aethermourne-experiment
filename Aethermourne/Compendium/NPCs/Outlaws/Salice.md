## Appearance

Salice is a woman in her mid-thirties, sharp-featured and perpetually amused by something no one else finds funny. Her skin is pale olive, her hair black and cut asymmetrically, one side shaved, the other falling past her shoulder. She wears it this way deliberately, it makes her memorable, and being memorable is good for business.

She dresses like someone who has money but does not care about appearances: well-made leather coat, sturdy boots, practical trousers, all in shades of gray and black. No jewelry except a single silver ring on her left hand, engraved with a pattern that shifts when you look at it too long. Primordial artifact. She will not say where she got it.

She moves with the confidence of someone who has never been outmatched and the caution of someone who knows that streak will not last forever. Her smile is sharp, her eyes sharper. She watches everyone, cataloging weaknesses, calculating leverage. When she speaks, her voice is warm, conversational, the tone of someone telling you a secret. You feel like you are in on the joke. You are not. You are the joke.

---

## Personality

Salice is a pragmatist with a sense of humor. She sees [[Tidecross]] for what it is: a chaotic mess held together by desperation and the fiction that any of the three factions have control. She finds this hilarious. She also finds it profitable.

She is brilliant, not in the academic sense, but in the way that matters on the ground. She reads people instantly, knows what they want, what they fear, and what they will pay to get or avoid either. She is charming when it serves her, ruthless when charm fails, and utterly without loyalty except to herself and the handful of people she considers worth protecting.

Salice does not lie. She does not need to. She tells you exactly what she is doing, frames it as mutual benefit, and makes you believe it is your idea. She is the best negotiator in [[Tidecross]] because she treats every interaction as a transaction and every person as a potential asset or obstacle. Morality is a luxury. She cannot afford it.

Beneath the calculation is exhaustion, the same bone-deep tiredness that affects everyone in [[Tidecross]]. Salice is tired of being the only person who sees how fragile this all is. Tired of profiting from desperation. Tired of knowing that when [[Tidecross]] collapses, she will survive because survival is what she does, and she will hate herself for it.

## Backstory

Salice was born in [[The Hollowed Reach]], daughter of a banker and a ship captain, a pedigree that opened doors in the [[Concord of Tides]]. She was smart, ambitious, and idealistic. She believed the Concord's mission to connect the world through trade was noble. She joined the diplomatic corps at twenty-two, served with distinction, and was promoted rapidly.

Then she was sent to negotiate a contract dispute in the outer archipelago and discovered the Concord's merchants were trafficking Primordial artifacts looted from sacred sites. She reported it. The Concord thanked her, quietly shelved the report, and reassigned her to a desk job. She learned the lesson: the Concord cared about profit, not principle.

When the first ships reached [[The Veilshore]], Salice volunteered for the expedition. She told herself she would do better, build something cleaner. She lasted six months before she realized the Concord's operation on [[The Veilshore]] was the same corrupt machinery transplanted to a new shore. The merchants exploited the Oldborn. The diplomats lied. The money flowed upward while the people on the ground starved.

Salice quit. She did not return to [[The Hollowed Reach]]. She stayed, went underground, and built something the Concord could not control: the Wrack, a smuggling network that moved goods the Concord would not supply and information the Concord did not want shared. She made a fortune. She also saved lives. The two are not mutually exclusive.

## Relationships

- **[[Captain Erys Vane]]** (Tidecross Harbormaster), Uneasy alliance. Erys and Salice understand each other. Both are pragmatists trying to keep [[Tidecross]] functional. Erys allows Salice's smuggling because it keeps people fed. Salice respects Erys's refusal to profit from suffering. They meet monthly, exchange information, and pretend they do not rely on each other. They do.

- **[[Vosh]]** (Oldborn Observer), Mutual respect. Salice is the only outlander who treats Vosh as an equal rather than a curiosity or a threat. She speaks plainly, does not waste their time, and has never tried to manipulate them. Vosh finds this refreshing. Salice finds Vosh's bluntness useful. They have traded information three times. Both came out ahead.

- **[[Prior Cael Drantine]]** (Ashen Chapel Lead), Frustrated pity. Salice likes Cael. He is kind, earnest, and hopelessly naive. She has warned him twice that his preaching will get him killed. He thanked her and kept preaching. She will pull him out of trouble when it happens because she cannot help herself.

- **The Wrack Crew**, Loyalty. Salice's smuggling network is small, twenty people, chosen for competence and discretion. They are not friends. They are professionals. But Salice pays well, never lies to them, and has never abandoned one of them when things went wrong. That loyalty goes both ways.

- **Concord Tidekeepers**, Bitter contempt. Salice knows the Concord officials in [[The Hollowed Reach]] personally. She knows their corruption, their hypocrisies, their willingness to sacrifice lives for profit margins. She left because she could not stomach it. She has not forgiven them for making her care enough to leave.

---

## What They Want

**Surface Goal:** Profit. Keep the Wrack operational, move goods, gather intelligence, stay ahead of the Concord's attempts to shut her down.

**Deeper Need:** Salice wants [[Tidecross]] to work. Not because she is idealistic, she burned that out of herself years ago, but because she cannot stand waste. All these people, Concord merchants, Oldborn observers, desperate refugees, could coexist if they stopped being idiots. She knows it is possible. She also knows it will not happen.

What she will not admit: she stayed in [[Tidecross]] because leaving would mean admitting she failed. She left the Concord to build something better. The Wrack is not better. It is necessary, and necessity is not the same as success.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role:** Salice is the players' black-market contact in [[Tidecross]]. Need Primordial artifacts? Salice can get them. Need passage through the Fernwald without Oldborn interference? Salice knows the smugglers' routes. Need information the Concord is suppressing? Salice has it. Everything has a price.
>
> **Key Secret:** Salice is funding [[Captain Erys Vane]]'s secret evacuation operation. Erys skims tariffs. Salice launders the money and arranges ships. Neither has told the other they know. Both pretend the arrangement is purely transactional. It is not. Salice respects Erys's refusal to let people die for bureaucratic convenience. If the Concord discovers this, both will be executed for embezzlement and treason. Salice has an escape plan. Erys does not. Salice's plan includes Erys. Erys does not know this.
>
> **Arc Trajectory:** Salice becomes the players' most reliable ally in [[Tidecross]] if they treat her fairly. She does not require loyalty, just honesty and payment. If they betray her, she vanishes, taking her network with her, and [[Tidecross]]'s black market collapses. If they earn her trust, she begins sharing intelligence the Concord does not have: Oldborn movements, Veil Unbound activity, Yvenne's erratic patterns. She is the player's eyes and ears in [[The Veilshore]].
>
> **If the PCs Do Nothing:** Salice evacuates the night before the Oldborn strike. She takes her crew, as many refugees as her ships can carry, and vanishes. She does not warn the broader population, doing so would compromise the evacuation, but she leaves a sealed letter for [[Captain Erys Vane]] with the departure time of the last ship. Erys finds it six hours too late. Salice survives. She does not forgive herself.
>
> **Roleplay Notes:** Salice speaks in a conversational, almost conspiratorial tone, as if every sentence is an inside joke. She leans in when talking, making eye contact, making you feel like you are the only person in the room. She is charming, disarming, and always three steps ahead. Her tells: she taps her Primordial ring when calculating, tilts her head when skeptical, and smiles wider when lying (which is rarely, she prefers truth framed cleverly). Sample line: *"Here's what I love about this situation: everyone thinks they are in control. The Concord thinks they own the docks. The Oldborn think they own the land. The Dominion thinks they own the moral high ground. And me? I own the space in between. That's where the real work happens. So, what do you need, and what are you willing to trade for it?"*

---

*She is the queen of the cracks, the spaces where official power does not reach. In [[Tidecross]], that makes her the only one who matters.*
