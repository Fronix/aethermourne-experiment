## Appearance

A wiry, restless man built for crawling through collapsed structures and running from things he should not have disturbed. His clothes are a patchwork of salvaged materials, leather strips, corroded chain links, and scraps of fabric tied in an arrangement that only he understands, with dozens of pockets, hooks, and loops for carrying salvage. His face is lean and angular, permanently creased in an expression that hovers between amusement and appraisal. His skin has gone full Ashfield grey, and his eyes are pale iron-colored. He moves with the quick, darting energy of a rat in a familiar maze, and he smells faintly of ozone and old metal.

## Personality

Cheerful, pragmatic, and entirely without moral framework. Polk is not malicious. He simply does not have a category for cruelty versus kindness anymore. He strips the newly arrived of anything valuable with the same pleasant efficiency that he trades those goods in Rustgate's market. He asks after people's health while rifling through their belongings. He genuinely does not understand why some newcomers find him offensive. The Ashfield has worn away his ethical sense so gradually that he experienced it not as a loss but as a simplification. Things have value. People need things. He moves things from people who have them to people who need them. What could be more honest?

## Backstory

Polk does not remember how he got here. He does not remember where he came from. He does not remember his first name, if Polk is even his surname. These gaps do not bother him, which is itself a symptom of how long he has been in the Ashfield. He has been scavenging the outer plains and rusted towers for long enough that he has developed an instinctive sense for the terrain, knowing which structures are stable, which ash drifts conceal salvage, and which patches of ground sound hollow because something is moving underneath.

His collection of mortal-world artifacts is the closest thing he has to a personal history, though none of the items are his. He has gathered coins from kingdoms he cannot name, books in languages he cannot read, children's dolls with painted faces, weapons of unfamiliar design, and a music box that still plays when wound, though the tune means nothing to him. He keeps these items in a hollowed-out section of a rusted tower he calls "the museum." He does not know why he keeps them. He does not know why he cannot bring himself to trade them.

## Relationships

- [[Dalla Threshborn]], his primary trading partner. Their relationship is purely commercial and perfectly functional. Polk brings raw salvage, Dalla converts it into trade goods, and they split the proceeds. Neither has ever asked the other a personal question.
- [[Erys Ironfall]], who considers Polk a useful nuisance. Polk respects the Warden's authority in the way a scavenging animal respects a territorial boundary, by observing it when convenient.
- [[Keeper Maren Ashveil]], who he brings unidentifiable artifacts. He does not understand why she keeps them in her shrine, but he likes that someone wants what he finds.
- [[Old Rust]], who Polk avoids. He cannot explain why. Something about the ancient revenant makes the hair on his arms stand up, which is notable because Polk is not afraid of anything else on Level 1.

## What They Want

Polk wants to keep moving, keep scavenging, keep trading. Stillness makes him uncomfortable in a way he cannot articulate. If pressed, he might admit that he is afraid stopping would force him to notice how much of himself is already gone. What drives him deeper is the collection, the museum of mortal artifacts he cannot trade. Something in those objects connects to a self he has forgotten, and the act of finding them is the last thread tying him to the idea that he was, once, a person with a life.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Polk is a source of supplies, local knowledge, and morally uncomplicated assistance. He can guide the party through the outer Ashfield, identify hazards, and trade for equipment. He also serves as a mirror for the Ashfield's long-term effects on identity.
>
> **Key Secret.** The music box in Polk's collection plays a lullaby from [[The Hollowed Reach]]. If a PC from that region hears it, they would recognize it immediately. The music box belonged to Polk's daughter, who died in the mortal world before Polk was pulled into the Ashfield. He does not remember her. He does not remember having a family. But he winds the music box every night before sleeping, and he does not know why his eyes sting when it plays.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party helps Polk recover a memory, even a fragment, it will destabilize his cheerful equilibrium. He will either cling to the recovered self and become desperate to escape, or reject it and descend to the Proving Grounds, seeking the violence that will finish erasing what remains. Either outcome destroys the Polk that Rustgate knows.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Fast, chatty, always moving. Talks about gruesome things in the same tone he discusses weather. Pats people's pockets while making eye contact. Refers to the dead as "former customers." Never sits when he can crouch, never stands when he can lean. Sample line: *"Oh, you just got here? Welcome, welcome. Nice coat. You will not need it. No one needs coats here, no weather, you see. I will take it off your hands, save you the carrying. Fair trade, I will tell you which direction not to walk. Very valuable information, that."*
