# Sera Gloomhollow

|---|---|
| **Title** | Bandit Queen of the Deep Fen |

## Appearance

A wiry woman in her mid-thirties with dark skin weathered by years of marsh living, close-cropped black hair, and sharp, watchful eyes. She moves with the fluid economy of someone who has spent her life in difficult terrain, never a wasted step, never a careless sound. She wears a patchwork of scavenged armour over fenland leathers, carries a short recurve bow and a marsh-iron hatchet, and paints her face with Deep Fen mud before raids, a practice her band has adopted as tradition. The mud has a faint ley-glow that makes her look spectral in the dark.

## Personality

Fierce, pragmatic, and burdened by a cause she's not sure she believes in anymore. Sera presents herself as the fenlands' protector, the one person willing to fight for independence when everyone else just talks. She is charismatic, inspiring loyalty in her band through a combination of competence, shared danger, and genuine care for her people. But the violence is taking its toll. She started out robbing Dominion tax collectors and Reach trade agents. Now she's robbing fenland smugglers, and the distinction between liberation and predation has grown uncomfortably thin.

## Backstory

Sera was born in the Glimmer Marshes to a family of fen-guides, people who knew the safe paths and sold their knowledge to travellers. When she was twelve, a Dominion survey expedition forced her family to guide them deep into the fenlands. The expedition's Calculists attempted to map and catalog the ley lines for potential exploitation. The ley lines did not cooperate. A surge killed three of the Dominion soldiers. The expedition's officer blamed her father for leading them into danger. He was executed on the spot. Her mother died of grief three months later.

Sera grew up in the Deep Fen, raised by the land and her own fury. She learned its paths better than anyone alive, discovered its hidden places, its pockets of stable ground in the shifting marsh, its corridors through the densest ley-fields. By twenty, she was guiding smugglers. By twenty-five, she was robbing them. She justified the transition as a tax, the fen's toll for passage through its territory. She gave a portion of every haul to remote settlements that couldn't feed themselves.

Her band grew as the fenlands attracted more outcasts. Former soldiers, escaped serfs, failed mages, broken people who needed a purpose and found one in Sera's anger. She calls them the Hollowguard, and they are fiercely loyal. But the targets have shifted. The Dominion patrols she used to hit are rare now, the Sluice traffic is easier, and [[Torben Greaves]]'s caravans carry goods that the fenlands need. Sera knows she's hurting the people she claims to protect. She hasn't figured out how to stop.

## Relationships

- **[[Torben Greaves]]** , her primary target and, in another life, someone she might respect. They've never met face to face. He's tried to buy her off. She's refused on principle. The principle is getting expensive.
- **[[Maren Duskwater]]** , the Fenmother has sent emissaries asking Sera to stop raiding Sluice traffic. Sera's response was polite, detailed, and unprintable. She sees Duskwater as a politician, someone who talks about independence while making deals with smugglers.
- **[[Captain Hale Ashmark]]** , the Dominion officer at [[Ashcreek]]. Sera considers him the enemy by default, but she's heard he's different from the last garrison commander. She hasn't decided whether that makes him more dangerous or less.
- **Her Hollowguard** , forty fighters who depend on her for leadership, purpose, and survival. She feels the weight of their trust daily. Several of them are growing restless, wanting to escalate beyond raiding into open warfare against the Dominion.
- **The Deep Fen itself** , Sera knows the interior better than anyone. She has seen things in the Deep Fen that she doesn't discuss, structures that shouldn't exist, lights that move with intention, and once, a sound like a woman weeping that came from everywhere and nowhere.

## What They Want

To protect the fenlands from outside exploitation. And, beneath the ideology, to feel something other than the anger that has driven her since she was twelve. She is exhausted, but she doesn't know how to be anything else.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Wildcard antagonist and potential ally. Sera is an obstacle for anyone trying to use the Sluice corridor, but she can be redirected toward legitimate threats if given a target worthy of her anger. The Dominion's eventual push into the fens would give her exactly that.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Deep Fen has been changing Sera too. Years of exposure to the region's intense ley-field have given her a rudimentary sensitivity to the Aetheric Web, nowhere near [[Wick]]'s level, but enough to sense surges before they hit and navigate ley-hazards that would kill others. She has also begun hearing the whispers from the Nethys Pools, faint, fragmentary, but persistent. She has told no one because she is terrified of becoming like [[Old Fenris]].
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** Sera begins as a local threat disrupting trade. If the PCs engage with her, they can channel her anger toward the real enemies: the Dominion's coming offensive, [[The Veil Unbound]]'s operations, or the Second Silence itself. If ignored or antagonized, she escalates into a genuine insurgency that destabilizes the entire region. Her best arc ends with her finding a fight worth her fury.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks bluntly, economy of words. Doesn't explain herself or justify her choices. Watches people's hands, not their faces. Uncomfortable with praise or gratitude. Softens noticeably around children or around people who remind her of her father. Never asks for help, but doesn't refuse it when offered respectfully.
>
> *"The Dominion killed my father for the crime of knowing the fen better than they did. Every tax collector I rob, every supply barge I sink, that's interest on a debt they'll never finish paying."*
