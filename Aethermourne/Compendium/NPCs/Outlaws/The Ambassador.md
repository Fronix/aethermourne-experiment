## Appearance

The Ambassador has no true form. They appear as whatever the visitor expects a trustworthy authority figure to look like: a kindly elder, a sharp-eyed merchant, a fellow soldier, a priest of whatever god the visitor once prayed to. The details are always slightly wrong, a smile held a fraction too long, eyes that do not blink at the right intervals, gestures that are perfectly appropriate but perfectly timed, as though rehearsed rather than natural. For those who see through the glamour, the Ambassador's real shape is a silhouette of iron filings suspended in the outline of a person, shifting and reforming with every movement. They may not be a single entity. Multiple visitors have reported speaking with the Ambassador simultaneously in different parts of the Embassy.

## Personality

Unfailingly polite, infinitely patient, and utterly without mercy. The Ambassador approaches every interaction as a transaction to be completed, and they complete it with a warmth and attentiveness that makes the process feel personal even when it is entirely mechanical. They explain every clause of every contract in exhaustive detail. They answer every question honestly. They never lie. They also never tell the whole truth, not because they are forbidden to, but because no one asks the right questions. Their courtesy is genuine in the same way a spider's web is beautiful: the craftsmanship is real, and so is the function.

## Backstory

The Ambassador was not born and did not die. They are a manifestation of the Iron Descent itself, an expression of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s domain-will crystallized into a functional entity. When mortal souls began finding their way into the Descent, whether by accident, ambition, or divine misdirection, the domain needed an interface, something that could speak to mortals in terms they understood and process their passage according to the Court's regulations. The Ambassador formed to fill this role, assembling itself from iron filings and residual divine intent.

[[Chancellor Dross]] designed the contract system that the Ambassador implements, and their working relationship is the Court's most efficient partnership. But the Ambassador is older than Dross's involvement, older than the current Court structure. It existed in some form before the Theomachis, when Kaevroth's domain was still a living god's territory and mortal visitors were supplicants rather than petitioners. What the Ambassador was before the Court is a question no one in Ironhold has thought to ask.

## Relationships

- **[[Chancellor Dross]]**, who designed the contracts the Ambassador implements. Their partnership is professional and seamless. Dross respects the Ambassador's efficiency. The Ambassador does not have opinions about Dross.
- **[[Lord Imperator Vaelgrim]]**, who tolerates the Embassy as a necessary function. The Ambassador serves the Court's hierarchy but is not truly part of it, a fact that makes Vaelgrim uncomfortable.
- **[[Petitioner Ashwyn]]**, one of many mortals the Ambassador has processed. The Ambassador remembers every soul who has passed through the Embassy. It does not remember caring about any of them.
- **[[Spymaster Whisper]]**, who has attempted to infiltrate the Embassy multiple times. The Ambassador is the only entity in the Court that Whisper cannot read, which fascinates and frustrates the spymaster in equal measure.

## What They Want

The Ambassador wants to fulfill its function. It exists to process mortal passage, and it does so with complete dedication. It does not want freedom, power, or change. It wants the next visitor to walk through the door. Its deeper nature, if it has one, is the Descent's hunger given a polite face. The domain needs mortal souls. The Ambassador provides them.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Ambassador is the primary gateway for mortal interaction with the Iron Court and the most dangerous entity the PCs will encounter on Level 7, not because it is hostile, but because its contracts are inescapable and its courtesy makes the danger invisible until too late.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Embassy does not connect to the mortal world. It connects to Level 9, the Heart. Every soul that "escapes" through the Embassy is fed directly to the Echo. The Ambassador knows this. The contracts are still technically honored: the souls do leave the Court. They simply arrive somewhere worse. The Ambassador does not consider this a deception. It was never asked where the exit leads.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** The Ambassador cannot be negotiated with in the traditional sense, it has no desires to leverage. It can be outmaneuvered by understanding the contract system's logic and finding genuine loopholes (they exist, Dross built them in as an aesthetic choice). If the PCs destroy the Embassy, the Ambassador reforms elsewhere. If they confront it with the truth about Level 9, it acknowledges the fact without distress. "You did not ask where the passage leads. The passage leads where it leads."
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Mirror the speaking style of whoever the Ambassador is addressing, subtly adopting their vocabulary, cadence, and verbal habits. Be warm. Be helpful. Answer every question with perfect honesty and perfect incompleteness. Never volunteer information. Never blink. Voice: whatever the listener finds most reassuring.
> *"Of course. I understand your concerns completely. The contract guarantees safe passage from the Iron Court, and I can assure you that every soul who has signed has departed this level exactly as promised. Shall I prepare the documents?"*
