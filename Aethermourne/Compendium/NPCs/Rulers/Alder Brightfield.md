## Appearance

A thin, sun-browned man in his late fifties with calloused farmer's hands, a receding hairline, and kind eyes that are constantly calculating. He dresses in practical work clothes, linen and leather, and looks exactly like what he is: a man who has spent his life growing things. His face is deeply lined, more from worry than age, and he walks with a slight limp from a plowing accident years ago. He carries no weapons, which in the Ironmarch is either a statement of principle or an act of extraordinary confidence. In Brightfield's case, it is both.

## Personality

Alder Brightfield is warm, unpretentious, and far more intelligent than anyone gives him credit for. He has the farmer's patience, the ability to plant seeds and wait months for results, and he applies this temperament to diplomacy with devastating effectiveness. He listens more than he talks. He asks questions that seem simple and are not. He remembers every favor, every slight, and every promise, and he tracks these debts with the same precision he applies to grain yields. Beneath the warmth is fear: he is terrified for his town, his people, and the fragile neutrality that is the only thing keeping them alive. He does not sleep well.

## Backstory

Brightfield was born in [[Duskfield]], the son of farmers, the grandson of farmers, part of a lineage that has worked the Green Belt's soil for generations. He never left. He never wanted to. While the Ironmarch tore itself apart around him, he grew wheat, raised cattle, and married a woman named Lessa who died of fever eight years ago. He became mayor because no one else wanted the job, and he has kept the position because no one else could do it.

His genius, if it can be called that, is understanding that in the Ironmarch, food is power. Every warlord needs grain. Every mercenary company needs provisions. And [[Duskfield]] is the only place that reliably produces both. Brightfield has leveraged this into a neutrality that would be impossible for any settlement that did not control the food supply. He sells to everyone at fair prices, allies with no one, and trusts that mutual need will keep the swords away from his gates. He has also, quietly, built a network of informants, bribed scouts, and hidden weapon caches across the town that suggest he is prepared for the day diplomacy fails.

## Relationships

- [[Yrsa Bloodhand]], the Iron Thane. Brightfield supplies her forces with grain and receives tacit protection in return. He respects her but does not rely on her, because Iron Thanes do not last.
- [[Mira the Red]], whose forces have been seen near [[Duskfield]]'s borders. Brightfield has sent envoys offering the same trade terms he gives [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Yrsa]]. [[Mira the Red|Mira]] has not responded.
- **Tam Redsoil**, his head farmer and closest advisor. She is the practical counterweight to his strategic thinking, and she is the one person he cannot hide his fear from.
- **Healer Maren**, Duskfield's physician, who Brightfield quietly recruited from [[The Ashen Dominion]] five years ago by offering her the one thing she could not find elsewhere: patients who did not try to kill her.

## What They Want

Brightfield wants [[Duskfield]] to survive. Not thrive, not expand, not become important, simply survive. He wants his people to keep growing food, raising families, and living without the constant threat of violence that defines every other settlement in the Ironmarch. His deeper need is control: the world is chaotic and violent, and maintaining order in his small corner of it is the only way he can manage the fear that keeps him awake at night.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Supporting NPC in Acts II-III. Brightfield is a potential ally for any faction that values stability, and [[Duskfield]] itself is a strategic asset that every major player wants to control. He is also sitting on top of the [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]] fragment without knowing it.
>
> **Key Secret.** Brightfield has noticed the changes near the Quiet Chapel, the sweet water, the accelerating root growth, and he suspects that something beneath [[Duskfield]] is responsible for the calm zone. He has not investigated because he is afraid that understanding it will attract the attention of people who would take it. He is right to be afraid.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs protect [[Duskfield]] and help him understand the [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]] fragment, Brightfield becomes a key player in containing the fragment migration, his settlement becomes a sanctuary and a base of operations. If [[Duskfield]] falls, Brightfield will fight, with the hidden weapons, with the informant network, with everything he has. He will die defending his fields, and the Ironmarch will lose its only source of food and its only proof that peace is possible here.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in the unhurried cadence of a man accustomed to long seasons. Uses agricultural metaphors constantly. Makes eye contact that is uncomfortable not because it is intense, like [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Yrsa's]], but because it is genuinely kind, and kindness in the Ironmarch is disarming.
> *"I grow wheat. That's what I do. And I've found that if you grow enough wheat, the people with swords decide you're more useful alive than dead. It's not a complicated strategy. But it's mine."*
