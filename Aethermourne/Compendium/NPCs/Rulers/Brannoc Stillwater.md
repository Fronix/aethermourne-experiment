# Marchwarden Brannoc Stillwater

## Appearance

In his fifties, heavyset and broad-faced, with the weathered skin of a man who has spent his life on the water. His easy smile makes people underestimate him, which is exactly how he likes it. He dresses better than most Marchwardens, wearing traded goods from [[The Hollowed Reach]], dyed fabrics and worked-leather vests that would look out of place on anyone else in the Marches. His hands are soft for a Marcher. He is a negotiator, not a warrior, and his body reflects it. A silver ring on his left hand bears the Stillwater clan mark, a calm lake under a crescent moon.

## Personality

Commercially minded, pragmatic, and genial in the way that successful merchants are genial, warmth as a tool, generosity as investment. Brannoc is the Marches' best trader and he knows it. He reads people the way [[The Greensingers]] read the forest, instinctively, constantly, and with an eye toward what they need and what they will pay for it. He sees opportunity everywhere and treats ethics as flexible guidelines rather than fixed principles. He is not cruel. He simply does not allow moral considerations to interfere with commerce when commerce feeds his people.

Beneath the affability, he is sharper than he appears. He cultivates the image of a cheerful merchant-warden precisely because it makes people careless around him. He remembers every deal, every favor, every slight, and he collects debts the way other men collect weapons.

## Backstory

Brannoc was not born to the wardenship. His predecessor, Warden Arla Stillwater, died without an heir, and the clan's Rootspeaking selected Brannoc, then a successful lake trader with no political ambitions, as the land's choice. He accepted with the pragmatism that defines him: if the land wanted a merchant-warden, the land would get one.

He transformed [[Stillwater]] from a quiet fishing village into the Marches' premier neutral trading ground. The Lakehouse, the floating platform where Marchwardens meet informally and merchants negotiate without oversight, was his creation. He positioned Stillwater as the place where deals happen that cannot happen at [[Roothold]]'s Moot Hall, and he takes a cut of every transaction.

His success attracted attention from outside the Marches. [[The Hollowed Reach]] merchants discovered that Brannoc could supply Marrow-Water samples, and Brannoc discovered that Reach gold could buy things the forest could not provide. The arrangement grew. He began selling samples without [[The Greensingers|Greensinger]] approval, a serious cultural violation he justifies as economic necessity. What he does not know is that some of his samples have passed through Reach intermediaries into [[Veil Unbound]] hands, used in rituals that would horrify him if he understood their purpose.

## Relationships

- [[Marchwardens Council]], where he positions himself as a reliable neutral, valuable to both [[Kaelith Thornborn]]'s coalition and [[Draven Ironbark]]'s opposition precisely because he refuses to commit to either.
- [[The Ember Guild]], his primary trade partners for worked goods and materials the Marches cannot produce.
- [[Vael Sundren]], whose Mutagenists at [[Mirewatch]] are both competitors and suppliers in the Marrow-Water trade. They maintain a cautious professional respect.
- [[The Greensingers]], who would revoke his wardenship if they knew the full extent of his unauthorized Marrow-Water sales.
- [[The Hollowed Reach]] merchants, his most profitable contacts and, unknowingly, his most dangerous liability.

## What They Want

Prosperity for [[Stillwater]] and Clan Stillwater, a larger share of the Marrow-Water trade, and distance from the unification debate for as long as possible. Brannoc sees the Thornborn-Ironbark conflict as bad for business and wants it resolved quietly, preferably in a way that preserves Stillwater's neutrality and his ability to trade without oversight. He does not want to choose a side. Sides are for people who cannot afford to do business with everyone.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Brannoc is an unwitting link in the chain connecting Marrow-Water to the [[Veil Unbound]]'s experiments. His greed fuels rituals that accelerate the very corruption threatening the Marches. He is also a swing vote on the Marchwardens Council, one of the undecided wardens whose allegiance could tip the balance.
>
> **Key Secret.** He is selling Marrow-Water samples to Reach merchants without Greensinger approval. Some of those samples have ended up in Veil Unbound hands, used in rituals involving [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s influence. Brannoc does not know this. He would be genuinely horrified to learn it, but horror would not stop him unless the alternative was worse for business.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs expose his trade, it could shame him into compliance, shatter Stillwater's neutrality, or be used as leverage. If the PCs trace Veil Unbound resources backward, they will eventually reach Stillwater and Brannoc. Confronted with the consequences of his trade, he faces a genuine moral crisis, the first in his life. Whether he rises to it or retreats into denial depends on how the PCs handle him.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Warm, expansive tone that fills a room. Laughs easily, often at his own expense. Uses people's first names immediately, as if they are old friends. Offers food and drink before discussing business, never after. His smile tightens almost imperceptibly when he is cornered. *"Everyone eats, friend. The forest feeds us, the lake feeds us, and trade feeds us. I just make sure the feeding happens in the right order."*
