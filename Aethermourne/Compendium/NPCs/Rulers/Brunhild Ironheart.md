## Appearance

A broad-shouldered dwarven woman of one hundred and eighty years, Brunhild Ironheart carries herself with the settled gravity of a mountain. Her iron-grey hair is pulled back in a tight braid threaded with forge-scale, the metallic flakes that accumulate on anyone who works near extreme heat for decades. Her hands are scarred, callused, and enormous for her frame, hands that have shaped steel for over a century and still grip a hammer every morning before dawn. Her face is deeply lined, not from worry but from a lifetime of squinting into furnace-light. She wears no jewelry, no insignia of office, only the leather apron of a working smith over practical clothing. When she speaks, her voice is low and unhurried, accustomed to being heard without raising it.

## Personality

Brunhild is direct to the point of bluntness, patient to the point of stubbornness, and honest to the point of pain. She does not flatter, does not hedge, and does not make promises she cannot keep. Her authority comes entirely from demonstrated competence, she has held the Forge-Matriarch's seat for forty years not because of political skill but because no one in the Emberveil can argue with the quality of her work or the clarity of her judgment. She is deeply compassionate but expresses it through action rather than words, solving problems rather than offering comfort. In private, she carries an immense burden of worry that she reveals to no one, believing that her people need certainty, not her fears.

## Backstory

Brunhild was born in [[Crucible]] to a family of common smiths, unremarkable in a city of smiths. She showed early promise but no extraordinary talent, earning her Master's mark at the standard age after a standard apprenticeship. What set her apart was endurance. While other masters specialized, Brunhild mastered one technique after another, structural ironwork, fine blade-smithing, geothermal engineering, forge-magic inscription. By her hundredth year, she had earned Master's marks in six disciplines, a feat unmatched in three generations.

The Forge Council elected her Forge-Matriarch after her predecessor died in a Deep Warrens collapse, a disaster Brunhild had predicted and been ignored about. Her first act was to overhaul mining safety protocols. Her second was to visit every settlement in the Emberveil personally, walking the mines, inspecting the forges, meeting the workers. She has repeated this circuit every five years since.

She has known [[Torren Hearthforge|Guildmaster Torren Hearthforge]] for over a century. They respect each other deeply and disagree about almost everything regarding the [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild's]] role in Emberveil governance. Their arguments are legendary and, by all accounts, productive.

## Relationships

- [[Torren Hearthforge|Guildmaster Torren Hearthforge]], Old friend and frequent adversary. They share a devotion to [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] legacy but disagree about the Guild's political obligations. She believes he hides behind neutrality. He believes she overreaches with governance. They have been having this argument for eighty years.
- [[Orin Deepstone|Delver-Captain Orin Deepstone]], She appointed him and trusts his judgment implicitly. His recent silence worries her, but she has not pressed him, respecting the unspoken code that what the deep miners find below stays below until they are ready to share it.
- [[Corrin Ashveil|Factor Corrin Ashveil]], She values his scholarship but has refused his request to access the sealed records, not because she distrusts him, but because she fears what the records might confirm.
- [[Vessa Ironvow]], She expelled Vessa from the Council personally after twelve miners died. The decision was correct. It still costs her sleep.
- [[Polara Facet|Gemwright Polara Facet]], An occasionally prickly relationship between the Emberveil's two most powerful guild leaders. Brunhild respects Polara's skill but finds the Conclave's autonomy inconvenient.

## What They Want

Brunhild wants to protect the Emberveil and its people from whatever is coming. She can feel it, the Cooling, the Dominion's growing aggression, the unease in the deep mines, and she wants to face it with clear eyes and steady hands. What drives her deeper is a fear she will not name: that [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] fire is dying, and that the civilization built on his sacrifice will die with it, and that she will be the one holding the hammer when the forge goes dark.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Major NPC in any Emberveil arc. She is the region's leader and the PCs' most likely point of authority contact. She can commission quests, provide resources, and grant access to restricted areas. Her trust is hard-won but extremely valuable.
>
> **Key Secret.** She has seen the Grand Forge dim three times this year and has suppressed the information. She knows the Cooling is real, and she knows it may be terminal. She is waiting for certainty before acting because she believes premature disclosure would cause panic, but the waiting is destroying her.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs help her face the truth, she becomes a powerful ally who mobilizes the entire Emberveil in response to the Second Silence. If left alone, her suppression of the Cooling data eventually leaks, causing a crisis of confidence that fractures the Forge Council and leaves the region vulnerable to Dominion pressure.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks slowly, chooses words like she chooses tools, the right one for the job, no more. Evaluates people by watching them work, not by listening to them talk. Calls young people "apprentice" regardless of their actual rank. When deeply troubled, she goes to the Grand Forge and hammers steel until her arms ache, because the only thing she trusts completely is the work.
>
> *Sample dialogue:* "I don't need your opinion. I need your assessment. Opinions are free. Assessments cost something."
