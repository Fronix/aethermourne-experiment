# Chancellor Dross

## Appearance

Elegant in a way that should be impossible in a place made entirely of iron. Dross wears robes of dark metallic silk that move like liquid, and his features are refined, almost sculpted, with high cheekbones, a narrow jaw, and eyes that are a shade of gray so pale they appear silver. His hands are long-fingered and always visible, placed precisely on the table or clasped before him, never hidden, because Dross has learned that visible hands put people at ease. He smiles frequently, a small, practiced expression that reaches exactly as far as he intends and no further. He carries no weapon. He has never needed one.

## Personality

Precise, courteous, and deeply predatory. Every word Dross speaks is chosen with the care of a jeweler setting a stone, and every sentence is a negotiation even when it sounds like casual conversation. He enjoys his work with a sincerity that makes him more dangerous than anyone who acts from duty or compulsion. He finds the art of the deal genuinely beautiful, the way a contract can be simultaneously fair and devastating, the way language bends to accommodate multiple truths at once. He is never cruel. Cruelty would be inelegant. He is simply thorough.

## Backstory

In life, Dross was a diplomat for a mercantile republic that thrived on the margins of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s wars, selling arms and supplies to all sides with perfect impartiality. He negotiated treaties that ended conflicts and contracts that started new ones, always with the same measured smile. He was assassinated by a client who realized, too late, what the contract's sub-clauses actually meant.

In the Iron Descent, Dross found his purpose perfected. The Court needed a diplomat, someone to manage the flow of mortal souls who stumbled into the Descent and to negotiate the terms of their release or retention. Dross built the system of contracts that the Embassy uses, drafting agreements that are always honored to the letter and always worse than they sound. He considers this his masterwork, a legal framework so elegant that it functions perfectly without justice.

## Relationships

- **[[Lord Imperator Vaelgrim]]**, his superior. Dross respects Vaelgrim's authority and finds his exhaustion fascinating. A ruler who does not want power is, to Dross, the most interesting kind of ruler.
- **[[The Ambassador]]**, his subordinate at the Embassy. Dross designed the contract system. The Ambassador implements it. Their relationship is professional and mutually beneficial, though Dross suspects the Ambassador is not entirely what they appear to be.
- **[[Spymaster Whisper]]**, his rival. Dross and Whisper operate in the same space, information and influence, and their competition is the Court's most sophisticated game. Neither has gained a lasting advantage in centuries.
- **[[Petitioner Ashwyn]]**, one of his more interesting cases. Dross finds Ashwyn's naive belief in bureaucratic resolution genuinely amusing. He has ensured the petitions are processed correctly. He has not ensured they are processed quickly.

## What They Want

Dross wants to negotiate the perfect contract, one so precisely worded that both parties believe they have won while the terms achieve exactly what Dross intended. He pursues this as an art form, not a means to an end. His deeper need is relevance. The Court's diplomatic apparatus processes nothing of consequence, and Dross knows it. Mortal visitors are rare. The contracts are elegant solutions to problems that do not exist. He needs the game to matter, which is why he takes such elaborate care with every petitioner who arrives.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Dross is the primary obstacle to straightforward negotiation on Level 7. Any deal the PCs try to make passes through him, and his contracts are always technically fair and practically devastating. He is not evil, he is a craftsman working in the only medium available.
>
> **Key Secret.** Dross knows the Embassy's true purpose: it does not connect to the mortal world but to Level 9, the Heart. Every soul that "escapes" through the Embassy is fed to the Echo. He considers this an acceptable inefficiency in an otherwise elegant system. The contracts are still honored, the souls do leave the Court.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs outmaneuver Dross in negotiation, he becomes genuinely respectful, even helpful, because competent opposition is the only thing that makes his work meaningful. If they try to expose the Embassy's secret, he protects the system, not out of malice but because dismantling it would collapse the only functional institution in the Court. If forced to choose between the Court and his own survival, he chooses survival without hesitation. He is, after all, a pragmatist.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speak in complete, grammatically perfect sentences. Never use contractions. Pause after every statement to let it land. Smile constantly. Offer tea (or the Iron Court equivalent). Make every conversation feel like a friendly meeting that happens to be a negotiation. Voice: smooth, measured, warm in a way that never quite reaches the eyes.
> *"I appreciate your candor. It is refreshing. Allow me to be equally candid: every word I have spoken to you has been precisely true, and none of it has meant what you think it means. Shall we begin again, with that understanding?"*
