## Appearance

A man designed to be overlooked. Late fifties, thin, with silver hair combed back from a narrow face and deep-set eyes that give nothing away. He dresses conservatively in dark blues, well-made but never memorable, the kind of clothes that blend into any room without drawing attention. His hands are still, his posture unremarkable, and he moves quietly through crowds as if he has practiced the art of being forgettable. This is entirely deliberate.

## Personality

Calculating, patient, and outwardly competent. Raith processes information the way a mill processes grain: everything goes in, only useful output comes out. He is respected by his peers on the Concord for his thoroughness and feared by his subordinates for his precision. He never volunteers information, only responds to direct questions, and he answers exactly what was asked, nothing more. Beneath the professional mask is a man consumed by a cold, quiet terror, someone who has looked at the intelligence reports and concluded that the Reach is already lost.

## Backstory

Cordell Raith built his career in the Concord's intelligence apparatus over three decades, starting as a cipher clerk and rising through competence, discretion, and a talent for making himself indispensable. He earned the title of Master of Secrets fifteen years ago, and for the first decade he served the position honestly, protecting the Reach from pirate networks, foreign espionage, and internal corruption.

Everything changed when he began interrogating Sleepwalkers. Standard intelligence work, trying to understand the phenomenon, looking for patterns. But the patterns he found disturbed him. He read Whisper-Cell reports, cross-referenced Trench activity logs, and interviewed [[Lantern-Keepers]] who had been stationed at [[Mistholm]]. Slowly, methodically, he assembled a picture that no one else in the Concord had the clearance or the patience to see: [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] was waking, and nothing the Reach could muster would stop it.

The conclusion broke something in him. Not his mind, his courage. Raith is not insane. He is a coward making pragmatic survival calculations, and his cowardice is killing people. He began suppressing reports that might galvanize the Concord into action, ensuring the [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] was never investigated too closely, feeding information selectively to amplify distractions and bury real threats. He tells himself he is being realistic. He is being complicit.

## Relationships

- **[[Maren Selk]]**, First Tidekeeper. Selk trusts Raith's intelligence assessments, which makes his betrayal particularly devastating. He respects her, which makes his guilt manageable only because he has convinced himself she would fail regardless.
- **[[Jessamine Kole]]**, Master of Coin. A professional alliance built on mutual competence. Kole feeds him financial intelligence; he feeds her threat assessments. He is careful to keep his suppressed reports away from her analytical mind.
- **[[Delric Mourne]]**, Veil Unbound leader. Raith is not a member of the cult, but he has encountered Mourne's network through his intelligence work and chosen not to dismantle it. The distinction between sympathizer and collaborator grows thinner each month.
- **[[Sera Greywater]]**, Toll-Warden of Bridgemere. Raith does not yet know about her investigation into diverted bridge funds, but her trail leads to his suppression of infrastructure reports. When he discovers her, she becomes a problem he must solve.
- **[[The Whisperer]]**. Raith has intercepted fragments of communication that suggest the Whisperer's existence, but he has buried the intelligence. He does not want to know more, because knowing would force him to act or to consciously choose not to.

## What They Want

To survive what is coming. Raith has no illusions about the Reach's chances and no faith in the Concord's ability to respond. He wants to position himself for the world that comes after [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s release, whatever that world looks like. Beneath the pragmatism is a man who desperately wants someone to prove him wrong, someone to show him a path forward that does not require courage he no longer has.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Raith is the Concord's internal rot personified. He is not evil like [[Delric Mourne]], he is a competent man paralyzed by justified fear who has chosen complicity over resistance. He is one of the most dangerous NPCs in the Reach because his betrayal is invisible and systemic.
>
> **Key Secret.** He has been suppressing intelligence reports about the [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]], Sleepwalker escalation, and Trench activity for years. He also diverts bridge maintenance funds and infrastructure budgets to create the appearance of resource scarcity, making inaction seem like the only option. The Concord's paralysis is not accidental. Raith is engineering it.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs expose him, the Concord is thrown into crisis but gains the intelligence it needs to act. If they confront him privately, there is a narrow chance he could be turned, his fear is real, but so is his guilt, and a credible plan to fight Serith might rekindle the courage he lost. If left unchecked, his suppression ensures the Reach is blindsided when the crisis peaks.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in measured, precise sentences, never a word wasted. Watches exits and entrances constantly, an old habit he does not bother hiding. His hands stay perfectly still when he is lying, a tell he has trained out of every other part of his body but never noticed in his hands. Pauses before answering any question, even simple ones. *"I deal in what is known, Tidekeeper. What is suspected belongs in a different ledger."*
