## Appearance

A lean, elegant man in his early fifties with dark hair going silver at the temples and calm, warm brown eyes that reveal nothing. His face is pleasant, unremarkable by design, the kind of face that slides out of memory the moment he leaves a room. He dresses impeccably in dark grey wool and polished boots, never ostentatious, never shabby. His hands are soft, unmarked by labor or combat, though he is competent at both. He smells faintly of cedar and ink. Nothing about him suggests danger. That is the point.

## Personality

Mourne is the most dangerous kind of antagonist: a reasonable one. He is soft-spoken, warm, and genuinely attentive. He remembers names, asks after people's families, and listens with real interest. He never threatens; he simply states facts and lets the implications settle. He is patient to a degree that borders on geological, having spent thirty years hollowing out the Dominion from within without once losing his composure. He does not consider himself evil. He considers himself correct.

## Backstory

Mourne was appointed head of the Cinders around 2201 AS, but his appointment was not a decision made by [[Hierarch Valdren III]]. It was a decision made for Valdren, by a Cinders apparatus that has served [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] for three centuries. Each successive Thane was recruited or replaced by one slightly more sympathetic to the voice from the deep. Mourne is the culmination of that long corruption: not a puppet, but a true believer.

He came to Serith's service through philosophy, not compulsion. Decades ago, as a young intelligence officer, he began studying the patterns beneath the Dominion's decay: the Ashite depletion, the fraying Aetheric Web, the slow failure of divine systems built on dead gods' bones. He concluded that the current world is a temporary arrangement built on a rotting foundation. When Serith's whispers found him, they did not seduce him. They confirmed what he had already reasoned.

Since then, he has been methodical. He feeds the Hierarch's paranoia, sidelines competent officials, and accelerates Ashite depletion by directing mines away from rich veins. He suppresses the [[Unbound Congregation]]'s most effective leaders, not destroying the movement (that would create martyrs) but decapitating it whenever it grows too organized. He maintains the Cinders' cell structure with flawless operational security. Fewer than a dozen people know the true agenda.

## Relationships

- [[Hierarch Valdren III]], his primary instrument. Mourne has cultivated Valdren's trust and dependence over decades, shaping the intelligence reports the old man sees, replacing his personal guard with Cinder agents, and isolating him from anyone who might restore his clarity.
- [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], the imprisoned goddess he serves. Mourne receives instructions through dreams and divination in a hidden shrine beneath the Cinder Halls. His loyalty is philosophical, not fanatical.
- [[Elowen Greaves]], the reform leader he considers a manageable threat. He has standing orders to suppress her most effective operations but not to kill her, martyrs are more dangerous than dissidents.
- [[Ashara Voss]], the Commander whose war plans he quietly encourages, because a Dominion at war is a Dominion too distracted to notice the rot within.
- [[Cassia Lorn]], an Ashless activist in [[Ostivaar]] who is tracking the disappearance of Ashless citizens. She is getting dangerously close to discovering his entropic experiments.

## What They Want

Mourne wants the divine cycle to restart. He believes, with calm certainty, that the world is dying and that [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] plan to unmake it and begin a new creation is not destruction but mercy. His conscious goal is the Dominion's complete collapse at the moment of Serith's liberation. His deeper need is to be proven right, to know that his thirty years of patient betrayal served a purpose greater than any empire.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Primary antagonist of Act II, and a recurring threat through Act IV if he survives. The PCs must uncover his conspiracy and either defeat, expose, or turn him. He is the campaign's most dangerous mortal enemy: intelligent, patient, well-resourced, and philosophically compelling.
>
> **Key Secret.** Mourne carries a fragment of Serith's shadow within him, a gift from the goddess. If killed, the shadow escapes and flees toward the nearest divine remnant. If captured alive, he becomes a valuable but deeply unreliable source of intelligence about Serith's prison, her servants, and her theology. His private chambers contain a shrine corroded by entropic energy, warded against Ashite-based detection.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If exposed, Mourne's contingency plan triggers: he attempts to assassinate the Hierarch and blame the [[Unbound Congregation]], sparking a civil war that serves Serith's interests regardless. If cornered, he delivers Serith's case so compellingly that the PCs may not be certain he is wrong. He can be defeated, can escape to recur, or can be argued with, but he cannot be easily dismissed.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Soft-spoken, warm, attentive. Never raises his voice. Asks questions instead of making accusations. The kindest person in the room, and the most lethal. When confronted with evidence, he does not deny, he reframes. His calm is not an act; he genuinely believes he has already won the argument.
>
> *"You think I'm a traitor. I understand. But consider: a physician who tells a dying man the truth, is that betrayal? The world is built on corpses, friend. I am simply the one honest enough to say what the corpses are becoming."*
