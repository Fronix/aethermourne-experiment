## Appearance

A woman in her late thirties with dark skin, warm brown eyes, and close-cropped hair. She is slight of build but carries herself with the posture of someone accustomed to standing before hostile crowds. She wears simple homespun rather than the caste markers she is entitled to, a deliberate rejection of the system she fights. Her hands are ink-stained from writing pamphlets and speeches. A thin silver chain around her neck holds a pendant shaped like an open flame, Solvaen's symbol, reclaimed and reinterpreted.

## Personality

Elowen is idealistic, eloquent, and tireless. She speaks with passion, leans forward when making a point, and gestures broadly. She listens earnestly, even to people who despise her, because she believes that understanding precedes change. She quotes [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] early texts to counter the Church's orthodoxy, turning the state religion's own scripture against it. She is uncomfortable with violence but not a coward; she has stood her ground before Cinder agents and Order patrols when running would have been safer. She is possibly naive, but her naivety is the kind that moves mountains or gets crushed by them.

## Backstory

Elowen grew up Hewn-caste in [[Ostivaar]], the daughter of a stone-cutter and a seamstress. Her brother [[Torben Greaves]] was the pragmatist; she was the one reading forbidden texts and asking why the castes existed. She earned a scholarship to the Athenaeum in [[Ashveil]] as a Keeper-caste scholar, one of the rare exceptions the system permits to demonstrate its own fairness.

At the Athenaeum, she studied Solvaen's earliest writings, the texts the Church cites but rarely reads in full. What she found changed her: Solvaen's vision was ordered justice, systems built to serve people, civilization as collective endeavor. The caste system, the Hierarch's absolute authority, the Confession and Correction apparatus, none of it appeared in the source texts. The Church had built a prison and called it a temple. She renounced her Keeper standing and returned to the Lower Ribs to live among the Hewn.

She asked Torben to join her movement. He refused, calculating that he would be more useful alive and free than dead or imprisoned. They argued. It was the last real conversation they had. He left Ostivaar with a stolen barge. She stayed, and built the [[Unbound Congregation]] into the most significant reform movement the Dominion has seen in centuries. She sleeps in a different safe house every night. She has been doing this for over a decade.

## Relationships

- [[Torben Greaves]], her brother, now a smuggler operating from [[The Drowning Bridge]] in [[The Greymere Fenlands]]. They have not spoken in over a decade. She sent him a letter two years ago asking for help. He has not opened it.
- [[Delric Mourne]], head of [[The Cinders]], who considers her a manageable threat. She suspects the Cinders of deep corruption but lacks proof.
- [[Cassia Lorn]], an Ashless activist in the Lower Ribs. Cassia respects Elowen but considers reform insufficient; she wants revolution. Their alliance is real but strained.
- [[Petra Venn]], a young Congregation sympathizer in [[Ashveil]] who worships Elowen from afar. They have never met.
- [[Maren Duskwater]], a Fenlands leader who follows the Congregation's work with intense interest and considers Elowen a potential ally on the inside.

## What They Want

Elowen wants the Dominion to become what Solvaen actually intended: a civilization that serves its people rather than consuming them. She wants the caste system abolished, the Hierarch's power checked by a representative council, and the Church reformed to reflect its own founding texts. Beneath that, she wants to believe that change is possible without blood, that the system can be redeemed from within. She is less certain of this than she was five years ago.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Natural ally in Act II, returning in Act V. She provides the PCs with information, safe houses, political support, and a connection to Ostivaar's underground. She coordinates intelligence for the Congregation and may become a political leader if the Dominion reforms after the Cinders' exposure.
>
> **Key Secret.** Elowen once served as a junior Cinder herself, decades ago, recruited during her time at the Athenaeum. She left when she saw what the organization was becoming. This makes her both invaluable (she knows Cinder methods and protocols) and a target (if the Cinders learn she is alive and active, they will come for her with everything). She has told no one.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** Her faith in nonviolent reform is tested by the depth of the Cinders' corruption. If the PCs support her and the conspiracy is exposed, she emerges as the voice of a reformed Dominion. If the radical wing of the Congregation seizes control, or if Mourne's contingency plan triggers a civil war, Elowen faces an impossible choice between her principles and her people's survival.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Passionate, earnest, gestures broadly when speaking. Quotes Solvaen's early texts the way a lawyer quotes precedent. Her voice carries naturally; she does not need to shout to fill a room. When frightened, she becomes very still and very precise. She calls everyone "friend" until they prove otherwise.
>
> *"Solvaen wrote, 'Order exists to shelter the weak, not to crown the strong.' That is scripture. That is their own god's word. And they are terrified of it, because it means everything they have built is a betrayal of everything they claim to believe."*
