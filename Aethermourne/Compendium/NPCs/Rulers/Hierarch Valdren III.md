## Appearance

An aging man of seventy-three, gaunt and sharp-featured, with grey-white hair that falls in thin, uncombed strands past his collar. His pale eyes were once piercing but are now clouded, darting toward doorways and shadows mid-conversation. He wears geometric white-and-grey robes that hang loosely on a frame that has shrunk with years of poor sleep and worse appetite. He clutches an Ashite scepter at all times, its faint warmth the only comfort he trusts. His hands tremble when he thinks no one is watching.

## Personality

Valdren was once sharp, curious, and genuinely reformist, a Hierarch who believed [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] legacy could evolve without breaking. That man is buried under decades of paranoia and isolation. He is suspicious of everyone except [[Delric Mourne]], which is the worst possible instinct. He speaks slowly and deliberately, pausing mid-sentence as if listening for eavesdroppers. Occasionally the old lucidity surfaces, a razor observation, a flash of real authority, which makes the decline more painful to witness. He refers to himself in the plural and treats disagreement as evidence of conspiracy.

## Backstory

Valdren ascended to the Hierarch's throne around 2150 AS, the latest in a dynasty stretching back to Valdren the Founder. He was young for a Hierarch and genuinely idealistic, making early gestures toward easing caste restrictions and modernizing the Dominion's institutions. The Hewn dared to hope. The Anointed caste watched with guarded displeasure.

The crises came in waves. Ashite shortages threatened the economy. The [[Unbound Congregation]] grew bolder underground. The [[The Cinders|Cinders]] delivered intelligence reports painting threats on every border, each report carefully calibrated to deepen his fear. Valdren retreated into conservatism, then rigidity, then outright paranoia. He purged competent officials, issued contradictory orders, and drew his circle of trust tighter until it contained only the one man engineering his destruction.

He now rarely leaves the Throne Room in [[Ostivaar]]. He sleeps on a cot behind the throne, eats at his desk, and receives visitors at all hours. His personal guard has been gradually replaced with Cinder agents over the past three years. He is, in every meaningful sense, a prisoner who does not know he is imprisoned.

## Relationships

- [[Delric Mourne]], his most trusted advisor and the head of [[The Cinders]]. Valdren considers Mourne the only honest man in the Dominion. This trust is the architecture of his ruin.
- [[Ashara Voss]], Commander of the [[Order of the Ashen Flame]]. He respects her competence but fears her ambition. He has denied her request to strike the Scarwall repeatedly.
- [[Elowen Greaves]], leader of the [[Unbound Congregation]]. He views her as a dangerous radical, though he has never met her. The Cinders' reports have shaped his image of her entirely.
- [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]], the dead god whose bones hold up his nation. Valdren's faith is genuine, desperate, and increasingly the only thing holding him together.

## What They Want

Valdren wants the world to stop changing. He wants the order he was raised to believe in to be real, the castes to hold, the borders to stand, the Flame to burn steady. Beneath that, he wants someone to tell him the truth, someone he can trust without reservation. He has already chosen that person, and chosen wrong.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Tragic authority figure in Act II. His response to the Cinder conspiracy shapes the Dominion's entire trajectory. If the PCs present evidence of Mourne's betrayal, Valdren's reaction depends on how they handle him: with empathy, he may rally and aid them; with force or contempt, he collapses into paralysis or denial.
>
> **Key Secret.** Every Hierarch since the Founding has guarded a truth: Solvaen's body, buried beneath the Ashen Cathedral, still bleeds. The Ashite mined from the Spine is not inert residue but divine blood, still flowing from a god who should have been dead for two millennia. Valdren does not understand why. The players eventually will.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs reach him and handle the revelation with care, Valdren can become a broken but genuine ally, ordering the Cinders dissolved and legitimizing the party's mission. If they fail to reach him, or if Mourne's contingency plan triggers first, Valdren dies, and the Dominion fractures into civil war.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in the royal "we," pauses often, glances at doors. Alternates between trembling uncertainty and sudden flashes of real authority. His voice drops to a whisper when he says something true. Touch his faith in Solvaen and he becomes fierce, briefly, before the fear returns.
>
> *"We have... considered your petition. We have considered many petitions. The difficulty, you understand, is knowing which voices are loyal, and which merely sound that way."*
