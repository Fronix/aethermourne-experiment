# Jessamine Kole

## Appearance

Early forties, striking rather than pretty, with sharp cheekbones and cool grey eyes that evaluate everything they rest upon. Dark hair worn in a tight braid that never loosens, never shifts. She dresses expensively but plainly, the wealth visible in the fabric quality rather than the ornamentation, silk-lined wool in deep navy, a single gold clasp at her collar. Her fingers are perpetually stained with ink, and she carries the faint scent of sealing wax and parchment. She moves with the deliberate economy of someone who considers wasted motion as offensive as wasted coin.

## Personality

Jessamine sees [[The Hollowed Reach]] as a system of inputs and outputs. People are variables. Trade is the equation. Her job is to keep the numbers favorable, and she does it better than anyone in the Concord's history. She is not cruel. She simply does not factor compassion into her calculations unless it serves a measurable purpose. She would sacrifice the Bilges and every soul in them to protect the Coral Quarter without blinking, because the Coral Quarter generates revenue. This is not callousness, in her mind. It is clarity.

## Backstory

Jessamine was born into one of [[Tidewall]]'s mid-tier merchant families, wealthy enough to educate her but not wealthy enough to buy her a seat at the table. She earned that seat herself. She apprenticed at the Concord's treasury at fifteen, demonstrated a preternatural talent for pattern recognition in trade ledgers, and was managing the Reach's tariff schedules by twenty-two. Her rise was not political, it was mathematical. She simply produced better results than anyone else, and the Concord, whatever its faults, respects results.

She took the Master of Coin seat at thirty-six, the youngest Tidekeeper in living memory. In six years she has restructured the Reach's taxation system, renegotiated trade agreements with the [[Ashen Dominion]] and [[The Verdant Marches]], and tripled the treasury's emergency reserves. She is, by any objective measure, the most competent member of the Concord. She is also the most dangerous, because she has contingency plans for every crisis, and several of those plans involve acceptable losses measured in lives.

The Black Tides concern her, not as a supernatural horror but as an economic disruption. Shipping losses are up. Fishing yields are down. Insurance costs for trade vessels have doubled. She has been quietly building financial models for various crisis scenarios, and the projections are grim. She knows the Reach cannot sustain the current trajectory, but she also knows that panic would accelerate the collapse faster than the tides themselves.

## Relationships

- **[[Maren Selk]]**, First Tidekeeper. Jessamine respects Selk's intelligence but considers her caution a liability. She provides Selk with accurate financial assessments, which is both loyalty and leverage, because Selk cannot make policy without Kole's numbers.
- **[[Cordell Raith]]**, Master of Secrets. A professional alliance of mutual usefulness. Raith provides threat assessments; Jessamine provides budget allocations. She has not detected his suppression of reports, but discrepancies in the defense budget have begun to nag at her accountant's instincts.
- **Orin Blackwater**, Master of Trade. Her primary rival on the Concord. Blackwater wants Selk's seat and considers Kole's fiscal conservatism an obstacle to his expansionist trade ambitions. Their arguments in council sessions are legendary for their precision and venom.
- **[[Elara Duskmantle]]**, coral-trading matriarch. Jessamine monitors Duskmantle's aggressive property acquisitions in the Bilges with professional interest. She suspects Duskmantle knows something about the sea caves beneath those properties, something that would affect her investment calculations.
- **[[Sera Greywater]]**, Toll-Warden of Bridgemere. Jessamine is aware of the Toll-Warden only as a line item, an appointee who generates slightly more revenue than projected. She does not yet realize that Greywater's investigation into diverted bridge funds could expose cracks in Kole's own financial architecture.

## What They Want

To keep the Reach's economy functional through whatever crisis is coming, because if the money stops flowing, everything else collapses. Beneath the numbers, she wants to be proven right, to demonstrate that rational analysis and fiscal discipline can solve any problem. She has never faced a crisis that defied calculation, and she is not prepared for the possibility that one exists.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Jessamine is the Tidekeeper most likely to become an ally if the PCs present the threat in economic terms. She responds to data, not emotion. Show her the cost projections of continued inaction, lost ships, depopulated coastal towns, collapsing trade routes, and she will move mountains. She is also the most dangerous Tidekeeper to have as an enemy, because she controls the money, and crossing her means losing access to [[Tidewall]]'s entire financial infrastructure.
>
> **Key Secret.** She has already drafted contingency plans for abandoning the outer islands and concentrating resources on [[Tidewall]] and the inner archipelago. The plans include cutting off [[Mistholm]], [[Saltmere]], and the Shattered Coast settlements entirely. She has not shared these plans with the full Concord, only with Selk, who rejected them. Kole filed them away rather than destroying them.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs win her over with hard evidence, she becomes the campaign's most powerful institutional ally, unlocking Concord funding, fleet resources, and political cover. If they appeal only to morality, she dismisses them. If Raith's betrayal is exposed and the financial discrepancies surface, Kole's faith in her own systems is shaken for the first time, and she becomes either more dangerous or more human depending on what the PCs offer her.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Always has ledgers or correspondence nearby, referencing them mid-conversation. Frames every decision in terms of cost and return. Speaks in complete, polished sentences that sound rehearsed because she thinks before she talks. Taps her ink-stained fingers on whatever surface is nearest when someone is wasting her time. Never raises her voice, lowers it instead, forcing people to lean in and listen. *"Compassion is a luxury funded by revenue. Show me the revenue, and I will show you compassion at scale."*
