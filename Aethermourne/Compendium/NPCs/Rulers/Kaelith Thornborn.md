## Appearance

A striking woman in her mid-thirties with sun-darkened skin and hair the color of wet bark, threaded with small leaves that grow there naturally since her awakening. Traceries of green-gold light follow her veins from her left hand up to her shoulder and the side of her neck, pulsing faintly in the presence of heavy vegetation. Her eyes shift between green and gold depending on her emotional state. She moves with a hunter's economy, lean and quick, and when she raises her voice flowers have been known to bloom in response. She dresses in layered hide and woven vine, practical but unmistakably a leader's garb.

## Personality

Charismatic, fierce, impatient, and deeply uncertain beneath her confidence. Kaelith trusts her instincts with a certainty that worries [[The Greensingers]], yet in private moments the doubt surfaces, raw and honest. She is passionate about the Marches' future, quick to anger when she perceives injustice, and quicker to forgive when she sees genuine effort. She does not suffer fools, but she is surprisingly gentle with those who are afraid, perhaps because she understands fear better than she lets on.

## Backstory

Born around 2050 AS to a minor clan in the Verdant Marches, Kaelith's childhood was unremarkable. Nothing in the histories suggested she would become anything special. She grew up learning to hunt, to navigate the Cradle's lower reaches, and to respect the land the way all Marchers do, with wariness and gratitude in equal measure.

Around 2170 AS, she entered [[The Verdant Marches#Thyrea's Cradle|Thyrea's Cradle]] alone, venturing deeper than any mortal had gone in centuries. She emerged three weeks later, changed. The mark on her hand pulsed with green-gold light. Her eyes had shifted. She claimed to have heard [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s voice, the Verdant Mother speaking from beyond death through the living forest that was her body. The [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] recognized her mark. The [[The Verdant Marches#The Scarwall|Scarwall]] bent to let her pass.

Her rise was meteoric. She founded [[Thornhaven]] in a clearing the Cradle itself provided, and clan after clan rallied to her banner. Now four of twelve major clans have pledged, three more lean her way, and the rest watch or oppose. She has built the most militarily organized settlement in the Marches, complete with formal training regimens and supply logistics, a fact that drives [[Draven Ironbark]] to open fury.

## Relationships

- **[[Elder Rosk]]**, respects his wisdom deeply but finds his cryptic manner frustrating when urgency demands clarity
- **[[Draven Ironbark]]**, her principal opponent on the [[Marchwardens Council]], a man whose concerns she privately admits have merit but whose stubbornness she cannot afford
- **[[Senna Deepmoss]]**, an undecided Marchwarden whose support could tip the balance, Kaelith courts her carefully
- **[[Tessara Wildbloom]]**, a young Greensinger who carries critical intelligence about the corruption heading toward Kaelith's channel
- **[[The Greensingers]]**, they confirmed her mark is genuine but remain politically neutral, which Kaelith finds both admirable and maddening

## What They Want

Kaelith wants to unite the Marches against the threats she can feel gathering, corruption in the deep Cradle, [[The Ashen Dominion]]'s aggression, and something darker she cannot yet name. Beneath that conscious goal, she needs to know whether the voice she hears is truly [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s, because if it is not, everything she has built is founded on a lie.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Key ally in Act III. The PCs must determine whether her divine connection is genuine, help or hinder her unification, navigate the Marchwardens' Moot, and journey with her into the deep Cradle. Their credibility from Acts I and II lends weight to her warnings.
>
> **Key Secret.** Kaelith's connection to Thyrea IS genuine, but [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s shadow-root corruption is creeping toward that connection through the root network. If it reaches her channel, Serith will co-opt the link entirely, turning Kaelith into an unwitting conduit for false warnings engineered to provoke conflict rather than unity. She would not know. The guidance would feel the same. Only the intent would be different.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs help sever the Shadow Root in time, Kaelith unifies the Marches and becomes a powerful regional ally. If they fail or delay, her visions become corrupted, and she begins making decisions that serve Serith's agenda while believing she serves Thyrea's. In the worst case, she leads the Marches into self-destructive conflict.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks quickly when excited, goes quiet when afraid. Touches the mark on her hand unconsciously, especially when discussing her visions. Alternates between inspiring conviction and private doubt, the PCs may see both sides if they earn her trust. Voice is warm and slightly rough, like wind through dry leaves.
> *"The forest opened for us. Not because I asked, because it needed us here. I have to believe that. If I stop believing that, I stop."*
