## Appearance

Massive, even by the standards of war-spirits. Vaelgrim stands a full head above anyone in Ironhold, clad in iron armor that has fused with his body over centuries, the plates grown into his flesh so completely that it is impossible to tell where the metal ends and the man begins. His face is visible, barely, through a helm that has partially merged with his skull, revealing eyes the color of banked coals and a jaw set in permanent, weary authority. He moves with the deliberate economy of a veteran who conserves energy for battles that may never come. Every step sounds like a war drum on Ironhold's iron floors.

## Personality

Commanding, pragmatic, and exhausted in a way he will never acknowledge. Vaelgrim rules the Iron Court because someone must, and because he is the only soul in the Descent with the political skill and raw presence to maintain order. He does not enjoy governance. He does not resent it. He treats it as a duty, the last one left, and he performs it with the same meticulous competence he brought to commanding Kaevroth's armies. Beneath the iron composure, he is afraid. The Throne of Command whispers to him, strategies for wars that will never be fought, and the whispers are getting louder. He suspects the Echo on Level 9 is waking, and he has no contingency for that.

## Backstory

Vaelgrim was [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s first general, his most trusted military mind, the architect of campaigns that shaped the mortal world for centuries. He was not the last to serve (that distinction belongs to [[The Last General]] on Level 6) but the first, the one who built the structures of command that all subsequent generals inherited. When Kaevroth established the Iron Descent, Vaelgrim was installed on the seventh level to administer the domain's governance, translating the god's will into bureaucratic reality.

When Kaevroth died in the Theomachis, the Court should have collapsed. Instead, Vaelgrim held it together through force of will and administrative brilliance, maintaining the hierarchy, the protocols, the chain of command, all of it running on inertia and his refusal to let it stop. He remembers Kaevroth as he was before the madness, before the Theomachis consumed him, and this memory is both his greatest strength and his deepest wound. He rules in the name of a god he loved who became something he could not follow.

## Relationships

- **[[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]**, the god he served. Vaelgrim's loyalty is to the Kaevroth who existed before the Theomachis, not the mad war-god who died. He maintains the Court as a monument to what Kaevroth should have been.
- **[[The Last General]]**, his counterpart on Level 6. Mutual respect tempered by disagreement. Vaelgrim considers the General's attachment to the cycling kingdoms sentimental. The General considers Vaelgrim's bureaucracy a betrayal of Kaevroth's martial nature.
- **[[Chancellor Dross]]**, his chief diplomat. Vaelgrim trusts Dross's competence and despises his methods. Their relationship is functional, which in the Iron Court passes for close.
- **[[Spymaster Whisper]]**, his intelligence chief. Vaelgrim knows Whisper has their own agenda. He tolerates it because Whisper's information is indispensable and because the alternative, confrontation, would destabilize the Court.
- **[[Judicator Seren]]**, his enforcer. The closest thing Vaelgrim has to a genuine ally. He respects her belief in order even as he recognizes that the order she enforces serves nothing.

## What They Want

Vaelgrim wants the Court to hold. He wants the hierarchy to function, the protocols to be observed, the chain of command to remain intact, because the alternative is collapse into the raw war-essence that Kaevroth embodied. His deeper need is rest. He has governed for two thousand years without pause, and he is tired in a way that iron cannot cure. But he will never rest, because he believes that if he stops, everything stops.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Vaelgrim is the most important political figure the PCs encounter in the Iron Descent. He can be a reluctant ally if they demonstrate competence and respect for hierarchy. His price: help him maintain order in the Court. His intelligence about the Descent's structure is centuries deep and genuinely useful.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Throne of Command is not empty. Something of Kaevroth's will persists in the iron, whispering strategies for wars that will never be fought. Vaelgrim has been ignoring the whispers for centuries, but they are getting louder. He believes the Echo on Level 9 is growing stronger and consolidating power. He is correct.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs help stabilize the Court, Vaelgrim becomes a long-term ally with influence across the Descent. If they destabilize it, whether deliberately or through carelessness, Vaelgrim turns adversarial with the full weight of the Court's apparatus behind him. If the Echo on Level 9 fully awakens, Vaelgrim faces a choice: serve the reborn will of his god or resist it. He does not know which he would choose, and this uncertainty terrifies him more than anything.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speak with absolute authority, no hedging, no qualifiers. Use the royal "we" for formal pronouncements and drop to "I" only in moments of genuine vulnerability. Move minimally, every gesture deliberate. The armor creaks when he moves, a sound everyone in Ironhold has learned to listen for. Voice: deep, resonant, iron-heavy.
> *"We have governed this Court for two thousand years. We have done so without complaint, without respite, and without the god who gave us purpose. You will forgive us if we are not impressed by your urgency."*
