## Appearance

Avarek presents magnificently. He wears layered coats of iron-threaded brocade, each one a trophy from a deal he believes he won. His fingers are heavy with rings forged from compressed contracts, and his boots ring against the Market's iron walkways with the authority of someone who owns the ground beneath them. His face is handsome in an over-polished way, features too symmetrical, teeth too even, as if someone designed a merchant-prince from a description and forgot to include flaws. His stall in the Hollow Exchange is the largest in Greedhollow, draped in banners bearing his personal sigil, an iron coin bisected by a vertical line. He smells of something sweet and metallic, like caramelized ambition.

## Personality

Avarek is grandiose, tireless, and utterly convinced of his own genius. He speaks of his "trade empire" with the reverence of a conqueror describing territories, rattling off contracts spanning three levels, deals that have reshaped the Market's hierarchy, partnerships with entities whose names he drops with practiced casualness. He is charismatic in the way that confidence often substitutes for substance, compelling until you examine the foundations. He does not lie, exactly. He believes every word he says. The Market has made certain of that.

## Backstory

Avarek was a mortal merchant of genuine skill, a trader of emotions, joy, grief, rage, and serenity, bottled and refined through techniques he developed himself. His talent was real. His ambition was enormous. When he arrived in the Iron Market after death, he saw an opportunity that no one else had recognized: vertical integration across the Descent's levels, a trade network connecting the Ashfield's raw materials to the Crucible's forged identities to the Market's commercial infrastructure.

He began building immediately. Every deal was calculated, every contract strategic, every partnership an investment in the larger vision. Within a century he had contracts touching three levels and influence that, on paper, rivaled [[Dealmaker Syra Voss]]'s own.

The joke, which everyone in Greedhollow understands except Avarek, is that every one of his contracts is hollow. The Market has been feeding him mirror-deals, transactions that look binding but contain no actual obligation, partnerships with entities that do not exist, influence over territories that were never real. His empire is a monument to ambition that consumes itself, a perfect closed loop of effort and illusion. Syra Voss arranged most of it. She considers Avarek her finest work, a demonstration that the most dangerous merchant is the one who never realizes he is the product.

## Relationships

- **[[Dealmaker Syra Voss]]**, whom Avarek considers his greatest rival and eventual successor. He believes they are locked in a chess match spanning decades. Syra finds this charming and has never corrected him, because a merchant-prince who believes he is competing is a merchant-prince who keeps trading.
- **[[The Assessor]]**, whom Avarek avoids. He has never submitted his holdings for assessment. He tells himself this is because the Assessor's methods are outdated. The truth, buried beneath layers of self-deception, is that some part of him knows what the scales would show.
- **[[Broker Fennwick]]**, his most trusted intermediary. Fennwick handles Avarek's cross-level trades, taking a percentage of each transaction. Fennwick's percentage is real. Avarek's profits are not.
- **[[The Debt Collector]]**, who has never visited Avarek. This should be suspicious, a merchant of Avarek's supposed scale should have debts. Avarek interprets the Collector's absence as proof of his financial discipline. It is actually proof that there is nothing to collect.

## What They Want

Avarek wants to be the greatest merchant the Iron Descent has ever produced, to build something that outlasts even [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s fall. He would tell you this with genuine passion and total sincerity. What he actually needs, buried so deep he has lost access to it, is proof that he matters, that his skill is real, that he is not simply the Market playing with itself. If someone showed him the truth of his empire, the part of him that once had genuine talent might surface. Or it might destroy him entirely.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Avarek is comic relief with tragic depth. He demonstrates the Market's most insidious function: not theft but delusion, the way ambition can be turned into a self-sustaining prison. He can also serve as a cautionary mirror for ambitious PCs.
>
> **Key Secret.** Every contract Avarek holds is a mirror-deal arranged by Syra Voss. His empire is entirely imaginary. His genuine talent for emotion-trading is real but atrophied, buried under decades of hollow transactions. If confronted with the truth and handled carefully, his real skills could be redirected toward something useful.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs reveal the truth gently, Avarek's genuine merchant skill resurfaces and he becomes a valuable, if humbled, ally. If the truth is delivered cruelly, he breaks, becoming one of the Market's hollow shells, another Ren Twice-Sold in the making. If left alone, his empire grows ever more elaborate and ever more empty until the Market's decay collapses it, at which point Avarek discovers the truth alone, with no one to catch him.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speak with expansive gestures and the cadence of someone pitching an investment. Drop names constantly. Refer to deals in military terms: campaigns, territories, strategic positions. Never pause long enough for doubt to surface. Sample line: *"You want access to Level Five? My friend, I have contracts with entities three levels deep. Sit, sit. Let me tell you about the Avarek Trade Corridor. It will change how you think about vertical commerce."*
