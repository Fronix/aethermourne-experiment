# Holdwarden Nara Frostmere

## Appearance

A broad-shouldered woman in her early fifties with a wind-burned face and iron-grey hair worn in the tight braids common to Bitter Sea communities. Her hands were scarred from decades of ice-fishing and Hold defense, calloused in ways that told her entire life in texture. She wore her Holdwarden's bone-mark pendant always, carved from divine bone by her predecessor, and it hung at her throat like a second heartbeat. In death, she stands among rows of the [[Frostmere]] dead near a major bone formation in the Marrow Fields, the pendant still visible against her frost-stiffened clothes, an ash-urn placed carefully at her feet.

## Personality

Stern, practical, and deeply protective in the way of someone who understood that warmth was a resource to be rationed. Nara was not soft. She did not comfort with words. She comforted by ensuring the hearths stayed lit, the nets came in full, and the Watch Ring held through the night. Every person in [[Frostmere]] knew that Nara would die before she let harm come to them. She kept eight hundred people alive for fifteen years through harsh winters, ice-storms, and the slow attrition of the shapes beneath the frozen sea. She was respected, not loved, because [[Frostmere]] did not have the luxury of loving its leaders. It needed them competent.

## Backstory

Nara took her surname from the Hold itself, a tradition among [[Frostmere]]'s wardens, a statement that leader and settlement were one thing. She inherited the role from old Holdwarden Ivar, who carved the bone-mark pendant himself and pressed it into her hands on his deathbed with the words: *"Keep them warm."*

She did. For fifteen years, through three severe winters that killed the frost-elk herds, two bone-storms that breached the Watch Ring, and the slow, grinding isolation of being the northernmost permanent settlement in [[The Pale Wastes]]. She traded surplus fish to [[Greyhold]] and [[Ember's Rest]], maintained the ice docks, kept the Warm Hall's three hearths burning without interruption. Her last communication with [[Greyhold]] was a routine supply request. She mentioned the shapes under the ice had been more active. She mentioned it the way one mentions weather.

Then [[Frostmere]] went silent. Failed to send a delegation to the Great Burning at [[The Pyre]]. When [[The Ashen Vigil|Vigil]] scouts reached the Hold, they found it intact: doors closed, hearths cold, urns of ash arranged neatly on shelves, food stored and untouched. Eight hundred people gone. Tracks in the frost leading south toward the nearest Marrow Field, a single set of eight hundred footprints walking in orderly rows.

## Relationships

- **[[Bjorn Ashken]]**, they exchanged routine supply messages for years. Bjorn carries her silence as a personal failure, convinced he should have sent help sooner.
- **Holdwarden Ivar** (deceased), her predecessor, who carved her bone-mark pendant and entrusted her with [[Frostmere]]. She kept his ash-urn on the highest shelf in the Warm Hall.
- **The people of [[Frostmere]]**, all eight hundred of them. She knew every name. In the end, she walked with them.

## What They Want

To keep her people safe. She failed.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Nara is not an NPC the players will meet. She is an NPC the players will learn about, from survivors, from records, from the bone-message that never arrived from her Hold. She is the human cost given a name.
>
> **Key Secret.** During a severe bone-storm amplified by [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], all 800 residents of [[Frostmere]] walked into the Marrow Fields simultaneously. The bone-pull was weaponized, boosted through the death-domain into an irresistible compulsion. Every man, woman, and child walked calmly into the frost and waited to die. They are now undead, integrated into the Hollowdeep army. Nara walked out last. She carried her own ash-urn, the one containing her mother's remains. It now sits at her feet among the rows of the [[Frostmere]] dead, neatly placed, which suggests something of her survived long enough to perform that one last act of tenderness.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** Nara does not change. She is presence, not dialogue. If the party reaches the Marrow Fields, the GM should describe a broad-shouldered woman with iron-grey braids, still wearing a bone-mark pendant, with an ash-urn placed carefully at her feet. Let the players connect the details themselves. Whether any fragment of Nara's will persists inside the undead shell is unknown. The ash-urn suggests the answer, and the answer is devastating.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Nara never speaks. She enters play through the words of others. Survivors describe her in short, reverent sentences: *"She kept us alive." "She walked out last." "She carried her mother's ashes."* Use her to make the Hollowdeep personal.
