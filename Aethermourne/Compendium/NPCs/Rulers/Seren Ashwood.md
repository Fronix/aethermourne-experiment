# Seren Ashwood

## Appearance

A silver-haired woman in her mid-sixties with weathered brown skin, sharp green eyes, and the erect posture of someone who has spent a lifetime commanding attention without effort. Her hair is worn in a single thick braid wrapped with living vines that bloom small white flowers each morning. She dresses in Marcher formal attire, woven bark-cloth in deep earth tones, with a wooden chain of office carved from one of the Grandfather's fallen branches. Her hands are strong and scarred from decades of climbing the Cradle's canopy. She smells of cedar smoke and rain-soaked earth. Her voice carries without rising, a gift from years of speaking in the Moot-Hall's uncanny acoustics, and when she walks through [[Roothold]], people step aside not from fear but from respect.

## Personality

First impression: composed, impartial, impossibly patient. Seren has served as Moot-Chair for nine years, a rotating position she has been re-elected to seven times because no one else can manage the Council's chaos as effectively. She treats every Marchwarden's argument with equal seriousness, cuts through rhetoric with a single well-placed question, and has a reputation for fairness that both the [[Kaelith Thornborn|Thornborn Coalition]] and [[Draven Ironbark|Ironbark Opposition]] grudgingly acknowledge. Beneath the neutrality is a woman deeply worried that the Council's traditional gridlock will get them all killed, but who believes the only way to prevent that is to force the Marchwardens to reach consensus themselves, not to pick sides.

## Backstory

Seren was born to Clan Ashwood, a mid-sized clan in the eastern Cradle known for producing diplomats and mediators. She served as her clan's representative to minor disputes for decades before being elected Marchwarden at fifty-three when her predecessor died of swamp-fever. Her first Council session was a disaster, three Marchwardens left early, two nearly came to blows, and nothing was decided. She watched the chaos and understood that the Council's dysfunction was not accidental, it was structural.

She campaigned for Moot-Chair on a simple platform: enforce the rules. When she took the position, she did exactly that. Marchwardens speak in order of seniority, no exceptions. Votes require a majority of seven, no negotiating that number. The living branch that times each session wilts when it wilts, no extensions. The rules had always existed, she simply made them inescapable. The Council has hated and needed her ever since.

The current crisis, [[Kaelith Thornborn|Thornborn's]] unification push versus [[Draven Ironbark|Ironbark's]] opposition, is testing her neutrality to its limits. She has privately concluded that unification is probably necessary, but she will not say so publicly because the moment she takes a side, the Council loses its only neutral arbiter and collapses into open factionalism. She is holding the center while it tears itself apart, and she does not know how much longer she can do it.

## Relationships

- **[[Kaelith Thornborn]]**, careful respect. Seren acknowledged Kaelith's communion as genuine but has not endorsed the unification effort. Their relationship is formal and professional.
- **[[Draven Ironbark]]**, wary mutual respect. Ironbark respects Seren's enforcement of Council procedure even when it frustrates his efforts to block votes. She respects his genuine commitment to clan sovereignty even when she thinks he is strategically wrong.
- **[[Elder Rosk]]**, old friendship. Rosk and Seren have known each other for forty years. They meet privately to discuss the Council's direction, and Rosk is one of the few people Seren has admitted her doubts to.
- **[[Senna Deepmoss]]**, professional ally. Seren values Deepmoss's analytical approach and has quietly encouraged her evidence-gathering efforts.
- **Hallen Redthorn**, growing concern. Seren has noticed that Redthorn's reports on Ironbark's activities seem designed to inflame tensions rather than inform, and she has begun investigating his sources.

## What They Want

Seren wants the Council to reach a decision, any decision, through legitimate democratic process rather than collapsing into violence or external conquest. Her deeper need is to prove that the Marcher way of governance, messy, argumentative, democratic, can survive a genuine crisis. If the Council fails, it will validate every authoritarian who has ever argued that people cannot govern themselves, and Seren has spent her life proving them wrong.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Neutral arbiter and procedural guardian of the [[Marchwardens Council]]. She can facilitate PC access to the Council, provide impartial intelligence on factional positions, and serve as the voice of Marcher democratic tradition.
>
> **Key Secret.** Seren has discovered irregularities in [[Hallen Redthorn]]'s intelligence reports, inconsistencies that suggest fabrication. She is conducting a quiet investigation but has not confronted him directly because she does not yet know who is directing him. If the PCs expose [[Delric Mourne|Mourne's]] manipulation, Seren will use her authority as Moot-Chair to publicly discredit the [[The Cinders|Cinders']] interference.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** Begins as a neutral facilitator. If the PCs help expose external manipulation (Cinders' operation through Redthorn), she becomes an active ally who can rally the undecided Marchwardens. If the Council collapses into violence, she will attempt to hold the Moot-Hall as neutral ground, a last stand for democratic process.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in complete, measured sentences. Never raises her voice. Asks clarifying questions rather than making accusations. Taps the wooden chain of office when thinking. Sample line: *"I have a question for both coalitions. If the Dominion marches tomorrow, who coordinates our defense? The Moot-Hall? Seven separate war councils? Or do we simply hope [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea's]] roots do the work for us?"*
