# Speaker Yavenne

|---|---|

| **Faction** | [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]] (Open Hand diplomatic faction) |
| **Status** | Alive |

---

## Appearance

Yavenne is young for a Speaker, thirty-eight, and carries that youth like a weapon. She is shorter than most Oldborn, compact and energetic, with skin the color of warm bronze and eyes so dark they are nearly black. Her hair is black, worn loose to her shoulders in deliberate contrast to the traditional braided styles of senior Pact-Weavers. She is making a statement: the old ways are not the only ways.

Her Pact-Weaver patterns are subtle, faint silver tracings along her hands and forearms, visible only in certain light. She bonded with [[The Primordials#Achren|Achren]], the Primordial of boundaries, a choice that surprised the Circle. Achren pacts are rare, dangerous, and philosophically complex. Yavenne chose Achren because she understands that boundaries are not walls. They are thresholds. They define where one thing ends and another begins, and thresholds can be crossed.

She dresses in deep blue and gray, practical but well-made, and wears a single piece of jewelry: a silver ring engraved with interlocking circles, the symbol of the Open Hand. She moves quickly, speaks quickly, thinks quickly. She is the opposite of [[Speaker Kaelthen]]'s deliberate stillness, all motion and energy and barely-contained urgency.

---

## Personality

Yavenne is idealistic, brilliant, and terrified that she is wrong. She believes engagement with the outlanders is the only path that does not end in mutual destruction. The Second Silence is not the Oldborn's problem alone. The stars are going dark for everyone. If the divine infrastructure collapses completely, the known continent will fall into chaos, and that chaos will spread. The Oldborn cannot survive alone on an island of stability while the world drowns around them.

She is a diplomat by training and instinct. She listens more than she speaks, asks questions before making declarations, and has a gift for finding common ground where others see only conflict. She believes in the power of negotiation, not because she is naive, because she has seen it work. She brokered the peace accord that ended the Hael'rune border dispute, a conflict [[Speaker Kaelthen]] said was unsolvable. Yavenne proved her wrong.

Beneath the confidence is doubt. Yavenne knows the outlanders are dangerous. She has read [[Vosh]]'s reports. She has seen the divine corruption in their artifacts. She knows Kaelthen's threat assessment is not paranoia, it is data. But she also believes isolation is a slower death than engagement. The Oldborn can build walls, and those walls will hold for a generation, maybe two. Then the Shimmer will reach [[The Veilshore]], or the Primordial forces will destabilize, or something worse will emerge, and the Oldborn will face it alone because they chose purity over allies.

Yavenne is gambling that she can teach the outlanders to respect the land before they destroy it. She knows the odds. She plays anyway.

## Backstory

Yavenne was born in [[Ur-Saethen]], the Oldborn's scholarly capital, to a family of historians. She was raised on stories of the Age Before Pattern, the time when the Unwritten Lands were the testing ground for creation itself. She learned early that the Oldborn were not inherently superior to the outlanders. They simply avoided the gods' mistakes by never trusting divine authority in the first place.

She trained as a Pact-Weaver at sixteen, bonded with [[The Primordials#Achren|Achren]] at twenty-two (a shockingly young age for such a complex pact), and entered the diplomatic corps immediately. She spent a decade negotiating disputes between Oldborn city-states, resource conflicts, pact territory boundaries, and philosophical disagreements that threatened to fracture into violence. She was good at it. Extraordinarily good.

When the outlanders arrived, Yavenne saw an opportunity. The Circle was paralyzed by fear, and fear breeds bad decisions. She argued for engagement, for sending observers, for learning what the outlanders knew. She was outvoted. Kaelthen's Sealed Gate faction dominated the Circle, and isolation was the consensus.

Yavenne spent two years building a coalition. She spoke to every Speaker individually, asked what they feared, what they needed, what they hoped for. She found common ground. She made promises. She compromised on things she believed in to secure votes on things that mattered more. At thirty-six, she became the youngest Speaker in the Circle's history and immediately founded the **Open Hand** faction.

She has been fighting Kaelthen ever since.

## Relationships

- **[[Speaker Kaelthen]]** (Sealed Gate Leader), Respectful opposition. Yavenne disagrees with Kaelthen on policy but respects her competence. They meet privately once a month, a ritual neither enjoys but both consider necessary. These meetings are the only thing keeping the Circle from open schism. Yavenne believes Kaelthen is wrong. She also knows Kaelthen has never been wrong about a threat before.

- **[[Speaker Dravos]]** (Ash Reclaimers Leader), Tactical alliance, personal distrust. Yavenne and Dravos vote together on engagement policy but for entirely different reasons. Yavenne wants to help the outlanders. Dravos wants to exploit them. She knows this. She allies with him anyway because the alternative is Kaelthen's isolationism. The alliance makes her uncomfortable. It is also necessary.

- **[[Captain Erys Vane]]** (Tidecross Harbormaster), Cautious hope. Yavenne has never met Erys in person, Kaelthen blocks all formal contact, but she receives reports from [[Vosh]] that Erys is competent, pragmatic, and genuinely trying to prevent violence. Yavenne believes Erys is proof that coexistence is possible. She has sent three covert messages to Erys through intermediaries, offering support if Erys needs it. Erys has not responded. Yavenne hopes that means the offer is being considered, not rejected.

- **The Open Hand Faction**, Loyal but fragile. Yavenne's faction is the Circle's smallest, one-third of the Speakers. They follow her because they believe engagement is necessary, but their support is conditional. If Yavenne cannot demonstrate progress, if the outlanders continue to exploit the land, the Open Hand will fracture. She knows this. She has six months, maybe a year, to prove her position works.

- **Her Mentor, [[Maeve Tal'shen]]** (retired Pact-Weaver), Guidance and doubt. Maeve trained Yavenne, supported her political rise, and believes Yavenne is the Oldborn's best hope. She also believes Yavenne is too optimistic. They argue constantly. Maeve attends every Circle session, watching from the gallery, a silent reminder that Yavenne's decisions will outlive her.

---

## What They Want

**Surface Goal:** Establish formal diplomatic relations with the outlanders. Share Primordial knowledge to help stabilize the Second Silence. Prevent the Sealed Gate from closing the borders.

**Deeper Need:** Yavenne wants to prove that cooperation is possible, that the Oldborn do not have to choose between purity and survival. She believes the world can be better than it is, that people can change, that catastrophe can be averted through dialogue and mutual effort. She needs this to be true because if it is not, Kaelthen was right all along, and isolation was the only rational choice.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role:** Yavenne is the players' primary ally in [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]] if they pursue a diplomatic path. She provides political cover, shares intelligence, and argues for their access to restricted areas. She is also their warning system: if Yavenne tells them to leave, they should leave immediately.
>
> **Key Secret:** Yavenne's Achren pact is degrading. Achren is the Primordial of boundaries, and Yavenne has been using her pact to hold boundaries that should not exist: the boundary between Oldborn and outlander, between engagement and isolation, between hope and despair. The cost is cumulative. Her pact patterns are spreading. In three years, she will become Boundary-Broken, a living edge that divides everything around her. She knows this. She has not told the Circle. If they knew, they would remove her from leadership, and the Open Hand would collapse.
>
> **Arc Trajectory:** If the players prove themselves, Yavenne becomes their most valuable ally. She provides access to Primordial Anchors, introduces them to friendly Pact-Weavers, and argues for their inclusion in the expedition to [[The Loom's Edge]]. If the players fail, if they exploit the land or spread divine corruption, Yavenne loses her political battle. The Open Hand faction fractures, Kaelthen wins the vote to close the borders, and Yavenne resigns from the Circle in disgrace.
>
> **If the PCs Do Nothing:** Yavenne exhausts her political capital arguing for engagement while the players make no effort to demonstrate they are worth the risk. The Open Hand faction fractures. Kaelthen's Sealed Gate wins the vote to expel the outlanders. Yavenne does not fight the decision. She is too tired, too broken by her pact's cost, too aware that she failed. She leaves [[Keth'vaal]], returns to [[Ur-Saethen]], and becomes Boundary-Broken in isolation, a living monument to the cost of failed idealism.
>
> **Roleplay Notes:** Yavenne speaks quickly, energetically, often leaning forward as if sharing a confidence. She asks questions before making statements, genuinely curious about perspectives she does not hold. Her tells: touching her Achren pact patterns when uncertain (the silver lines grow slightly warmer under stress), pacing when thinking, and a habit of rephrasing others' arguments to confirm she understands before responding. Sample line: *"Kaelthen sees you as contamination. I see you as a variable. Variables can be accounted for, worked with, integrated into a solution. Tell me: what do you want from us? Not what you think we want to hear. What do you actually need?"*
