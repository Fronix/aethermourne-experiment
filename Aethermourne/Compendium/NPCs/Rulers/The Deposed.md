# The Deposed

## Appearance

An unremarkable man in simple clothes, the only soul in the Thousand Thrones not wearing the trappings of authority. He has the build of someone who was once imposing but has settled into a comfortable softness. His face is calm, lined with age rather than suffering, and his hands are folded in his lap more often than not. He has no crown, no sigil, no badge of office. He sits in the Hollow on a stone bench he dragged there himself, and he looks, against all probability, content. In a place defined by the wreckage of ambition, his contentment is the most unsettling thing on the level.

## Personality

Gentle, patient, and quietly amused by the absurdity of his situation. The Deposed gave up his throne voluntarily, the only soul in the Conquest's history to do so, and the Descent does not know what to do with him. He should have been released. A soul that abandons ambition has no place in Kaevroth's domain. But he was not released, and after some initial confusion, he stopped caring about why. He stays in the Hollow by choice, keeping company with the overthrown rulers who cycle through between reigns. He listens to their raging, their weeping, their bewildered silence, and he offers no advice. He simply sits with them. Sometimes that is enough.

## Backstory

In life, the Deposed was a king of a small, prosperous kingdom that owed its founding to one of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s ancient wars. He inherited the throne young, ruled competently for thirty years, and then, on the morning of his fiftieth birthday, walked into his throne room, removed his crown, placed it on the seat, and walked out. No one stopped him. No one understood. He spent his remaining years as a gardener in a village on the border of his former kingdom. He died peacefully, which should have excluded him from the Descent entirely.

But Kaevroth's domain claimed him anyway, perhaps as a curiosity, perhaps as a test case, perhaps as an error. His kingdom cycles in the Thousand Thrones like all the others, but he never sits its throne. His echo-self was meant to rule, but the real soul's abdication overwrote the pattern. Now the kingdom cycles without a ruler, its throne room empty at its peak, and the Cartographer has noted it as the only anomaly in the Conquest's otherwise perfect repetition.

## Relationships

- **[[Queen Vassera]]**, who envies him. Vassera sees his peace as an accusation, proof that freedom was possible all along if she had been willing to let go. He sees her clarity as a kind of courage he never needed to test.
- **[[The Last General]]**, who considers him a failure. The General cannot comprehend willingly surrendering command and treats the Deposed with a distant contempt that masks genuine bewilderment.
- **The overthrown rulers of the Hollow**, his companions between cycles. He has listened to thousands of them rage and grieve. He does not try to fix them. He sits with them. Some find this infuriating. Others find it the only comfort available.
- **[[Crown-Keeper Ash]]**, who catalogued his empty throne as the Conquest's single anomaly. She visits occasionally to ask questions he answers with a shrug and a smile.

## What They Want

The Deposed wants nothing, and this is precisely why he is still here. He has no ambition to fuel, no desire to exploit, no drive to redirect. He stays because the souls in the Hollow need someone who is not suffering, a reminder that peace exists even in the Iron Descent. His deeper truth is that he is not selfless. He simply found, in life and death alike, that wanting nothing is the only form of freedom the world offers.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Deposed is the Descent's philosophical counterpoint, proof that Kaevroth's system has a flaw. A soul that truly abandons ambition cannot be held, yet the Descent holds him anyway. This suggests either that the domain's mechanisms are imperfect or that the Deposed is not as free of desire as he believes.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Deposed's kingdom cycling without a ruler is not an anomaly. It is a wound in the Conquest's structure. The empty throne creates a vacuum that the level's cycling mechanism cannot resolve, and it is slowly destabilizing the kingdoms adjacent to it. The Cartographer has not yet realized that his anomaly is spreading. If left unchecked, the empty-throne pattern will propagate until the entire Conquest collapses into arrhythmic chaos, achieving by accident what removing the Last General's iron splinter would achieve by force.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs understand the Deposed's true impact, they face a choice: convince him to resume his throne (stabilizing the Conquest but imprisoning him in the cycle) or accelerate the anomaly (freeing the cycling souls but destroying the level's structure). The Deposed himself would accept either outcome with the same mild shrug, which is either enlightenment or the deepest form of the Descent's madness.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speak slowly, with long pauses. Smile more than anyone in the Iron Descent should. Sit while others stand. Offer no opinions unless directly asked, and even then, frame answers as questions. Voice: warm, unhurried, faintly amused.
> *"You seem troubled. Would you like to sit? The bench is stone, but you get used to it. I have had quite a long time to get used to it."*
