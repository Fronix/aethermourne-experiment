## Appearance

A throne-shaped mass of fused iron and flesh, roughly humanoid from the torso up but merged seamlessly with the seat below the waist. His upper body retains the outline of a broad-shouldered man, though the skin has hardened to a dull metallic sheen, cracked along the joints like old armor. His face is frozen in an expression of triumph that has curdled into something else over two thousand years. His mouth moves constantly, cycling through shapes that belong to other people, forming words in voices that are not his. His eyes are open, iron-gray, and aware.

## Personality

The Hollow King has no single personality. He is a chorus of consumed identities speaking through a single ruined body, and the original conqueror who chose this fate surfaces only in brief flashes of arrogance and bewilderment. In those moments he is commanding, imperious, a man who genuinely believed he could eat a god's power and keep his own shape. Between those flashes, he is everyone else: pleading, weeping, raging, whispering in languages no living person speaks. He is terrifying not because he is powerful but because he is proof that power without limit devours the one who wields it.

## Backstory

Before the Theomachis, the Hollow King was a mortal conqueror whose devotion to [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] bordered on worship. He waged wars in the god's name, fed battlefields to the Descent, and dreamed of ascending beyond mortality. Kaevroth, impressed or amused, elevated him to rule the Consuming, the god's own digestive level. It was a reward. It was also a test.

When Kaevroth fell, the Consuming lost its purpose but not its hunger. The King, now its ruler, became its first meal. His absorption was incomplete, his ambition strong enough to resist full dissolution but not strong enough to stop it. He became the template: the first soul the Consuming processed without direction, the pattern all subsequent consumptions followed. He is both ruler and foundation, the throne that holds everything together and the man being crushed beneath it.

## Relationships

- [[The Echo of Kaevroth]]: His god, his idol, the source of his ruin. He still worships the Echo in the moments when his own voice surfaces, calling out to a dead god who left him to be digested.
- [[Mother Amalgam]]: She maintains the Maw's safe spaces while he sits consumed at its center. They are opposing forces, he is the proof that consumption is inevitable, she is the argument that it can be delayed.
- [[The Hunger]]: The manifestation of the drive that consumed him. It circles his court like a predator that has already eaten but cannot stop salivating.
- [[The Remnant]]: He was what she is becoming. He watches her fight to keep her name and says nothing, because the voices inside him are already calling hers.

## What They Want

In his rare moments of clarity, the Hollow King wants to be destroyed. He begs visitors to shatter his throne, to end the anchor that keeps him alive and consumed simultaneously. But destruction would unravel the entire level, releasing every consumed soul in an uncontrolled cascade. His deeper need is simpler and impossible: he wants to have been wrong. He wants proof that what he chose was not the only possible outcome of ambition.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Hollow King is Level 8's central set piece and moral lesson. He is what happens when a mortal tries to become divine through consumption rather than creation. His throne is also a structural keystone: destroying it disrupts the flow of consumed essence to Level 9, potentially weakening the Echo.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Hollow King's incomplete absorption created the template for every soul consumed after him. If his throne is destroyed, every consumed soul in Level 8 is released simultaneously, an event that could be catastrophic or redemptive depending on how the players manage the cascade.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the players destroy the throne, the Hollow King dies truly and the Consuming destabilizes. If they leave him, he endures forever, a cautionary monument no one learns from.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Cycle between voices mid-sentence. One moment a child's whisper, then a soldier's bark, then an old woman's prayer, then, briefly, a deep commanding tone that is *his*: "I chose this. I chose this. I would choose it again." Then the voices swallow him once more.
