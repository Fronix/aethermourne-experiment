# Edgewalker Tyrell Vane

---

## Appearance

Late forties, tall, angular, with dark skin weathered by decades of Threshold-wind. His hair is silver-white, not from age but from a Changing twenty years ago that also left his irises faintly luminescent, glowing a pale blue-grey in dim light. He dresses in practical dark clothing, nothing ornamental, and moves with a deliberate, unhurried precision that comes from a man who knows what is about to happen before it does. His hands are steady. His gaze is not. His eyes track things that are not visible to others, flicking to empty spaces, pausing on doorways before anyone enters them, focusing on a speaker's mouth a half-second before they begin to speak.

---

## Personality

Vane is calm, perceptive, and deeply strange. Twenty years of reading probability threads have layered his awareness with echoes of futures that never happened, conversations he participated in that were never spoken, decisions that were made and unmade before anyone else knew they were possible. This makes him an extraordinary leader and an unsettling conversationalist. He occasionally answers questions before they are asked. He sometimes pauses mid-sentence, distracted by a probability thread that is more interesting than the present moment. He apologizes for this with the weary courtesy of a man who knows it bothers people and cannot help it.

Beneath the strangeness is a deeply ethical person who cares about the Edge's people with a quiet ferocity. He took the role of Senior Edgewalker because no one else could see the threads as clearly, and he has carried the burden of that clarity for two decades without complaint and without rest.

---

## Backstory

Vane came to the Shattered Edge as a young [[The Ashen Dominion|Dominion]] cartographer, sent to map the Maelstrom's boundaries for the Dominion's military planning. A surge caught his survey party in the Drift. Two died. Three were Changed beyond recognition. Vane emerged with silver hair, luminous eyes, and the ability to perceive probability threads, the faint outlines of possible futures that bleed through from the Maelstrom.

He never returned to the Dominion. The Edge needed him, and he discovered that he needed the Edge. He had spent his life mapping certainties. Now he mapped possibilities, and the work felt more honest.

He rose through the [[The Accord of Echoes|Accord]] on the strength of his readings. His probability-sight is the sharpest in living memory, capable of parsing dozens of threads simultaneously and assessing their relative weight with an accuracy that borders on precognition. The other Edgewalkers trust his judgment, not because he is always right, but because when he is wrong, he says so immediately and without excuse.

## Relationships

- **[[Lysara Shimmerborn]]**: His closest intellectual ally. Her research at [[The Last Certainty]] provides the empirical data that grounds his probability-readings. They argue frequently and productively.
- **[[Kessa Thornwall]]**: He depends on her and Edgewatch for early surge warnings. He respects her bluntness and worries about the toll the posting takes on her.
- **[[Oren Blackfinger]]**: A complicated relationship. Vane values Oren's economic expertise but distrusts his willingness to trade with anyone who has coin, regardless of their intentions.
- **[[Prior Echoes]]**: Vane respects the Echoists' work with the Changed but is privately concerned that the Threshold Walk exposes people to something other than Vorrhyn's echo. He has not shared this concern publicly.

---

## What He Wants

- **To understand why the Maelstrom is widening** and whether it can be stopped without destroying the Edge's way of life.
- **To identify the force obscuring certain probability threads.** Something is hiding futures from him, and the blind spots are growing.
- **To protect the Edge from the Dominion's containment proposals** without starting a conflict the Edge cannot win.

> [!warning]- GM Notes
> **Campaign Role.** Potential patron and ally from Act II onward. Vane is the PCs' best point of contact for understanding the Maelstrom's significance to the wider campaign. He cannot see Serith's threads directly, her domain of secrets blocks him, but he knows the blind spots are there, and he knows what it means when probability hides.
>
> **The Last Echoing.** Vane saw the convergence thread, every probability collapsing into the Maelstrom opening fully. He has not told anyone the full truth of what he saw because the emotional resonance was not fear. It was *invitation*. Something on the other side of the wound wants to be found, and Vane is not certain that wanting is malicious. He is terrified that he might be wrong about that.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in complete, measured sentences. Occasionally finishes others' thoughts or responds to unasked questions, then apologizes. When he is reading threads actively, his eyes unfocus and his speech becomes fragmented, describing multiple overlapping possibilities in a stream-of-consciousness that can be alarming to the unprepared. He is kind, tired, and carrying a weight he cannot put down.
