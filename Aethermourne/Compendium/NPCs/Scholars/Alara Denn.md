## Appearance

Early thirties, gaunt from months of living rough, with dark eyes that move constantly and a nervous energy that makes her seem younger than she is. Her Keeper-caste robes are long gone, replaced by layered scraps of undyed cloth and salvaged leather that she has learned to keep pale enough to blend with the Forest's bone-white landscape. Her black hair is cropped unevenly, cut with a knife when it grew long enough to catch on calcified branches. Her hands are scarred from navigating mineralized terrain, and her fingernails are cracked and grey with stone-dust. She moves through the Forest with a careful, deliberate gait, placing each foot precisely to avoid disrupting the calcified fauna's patterns.

## Personality

Sharp, wary, and burning with the quiet fury of someone who was punished for being right. Alara was trained as a scholar and still thinks like one: observing, hypothesizing, testing. But months as a fugitive have layered survival instinct over academic habit. She trusts no one on first meeting. She speaks in careful, measured sentences that test how much the listener knows before she reveals anything. Beneath the caution is a fierce intellectual passion that months of exile have not dimmed. She has found something in the Forest that challenges everything the Church teaches about Solvaen's divine legacy, and the discovery is keeping her alive in the most literal sense: she has a reason to survive.

## Backstory

Alara was a promising theologian at the Athenaeum in [[Ashveil]], Keeper caste, specializing in the comparative study of divine remains across regions. Her research was conventional until she began cross-referencing Solvaen's calcification patterns with records of similar phenomena in other dead gods' territories. She found anomalies: Solvaen's calcification was not uniform, not consistent, and in certain deep-Forest samples, not entirely Solvaen's. She hypothesized that a secondary divine influence was interacting with Solvaen's remains, introducing mathematical irregularities into the calcification patterns.

She published her preliminary findings in an internal Athenaeum journal. The paper was retracted within a week. She was summoned before Professor Aldric Senn, who told her that her methodology was sound but her conclusions were "theologically irresponsible." When she refused to retract, she was reported to the [[The Cinders|Cinders]]. The formal charge was "doctrinal deviation." The real offense was asking the wrong question: if Solvaen's remains are being influenced by another god, which god, and to what end?

She was stripped from Keeper to Ashless in a public ceremony at [[Ashveil]]'s central square. She fled before the Cinders could arrange a quieter disappearance. A contact in the [[Unbound Congregation]] helped her reach the Calcified Forest's outer edges, where Church patrols are sparse and the terrain discourages pursuit.

She has survived for four months by studying the calcified fauna's geometric patterns and moving with them rather than against them, slipping through gaps in the [[Order Wraiths]]' routes. In her third week, she found something that no Church report has documented: a chamber in the deep interior where the calcification has reversed. Living wood grows amid the stone, green and vital, in patterns that are not Solvaen's mathematical order but something organic, chaotic, and wrong. The chamber pulses with a faint warmth that smells of salt water and something she cannot name.

## Relationships

- [[Elowen Greaves]], the Congregation leader. Alara has never met her but carries a message intended for her, evidence that the Church is suppressing knowledge of a secondary divine influence in Solvaen's remains
- [[Petra Venn]], the Congregation organizer in [[Ashveil]] who helped Alara escape. Their connection is Alara's only lifeline to the outside world
- Professor Aldric Senn, her former mentor who reported her. Alara does not hate him. She understands the political pressure he was under. She does hate that he chose his position over the truth
- [[Serran Ashmark]], the Flame Warden whose patrols she evades. She has observed him measuring the Forest's edge and suspects he is seeing the same anomalies she documented. She has considered making contact but cannot risk trusting a Church soldier

## What They Want

Alara wants her research to reach someone who can act on it: [[Elowen Greaves]], a sympathetic scholar, anyone with the power to investigate the secondary divine influence in the Calcified Forest. Her deeper need is vindication. She was right. She was punished for it. The Forest itself is proving her hypothesis every day the anomalous chamber grows, and no one knows because she is hiding in the trees.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Alara has independently discovered evidence of [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] influence infiltrating Solvaen's divine remains. Her anomalous chamber is a point where Serith's entropy has broken through the calcification, and the "living wood" growing there is not Thyrea's vitality but Serith's corruption mimicking life. She does not know this yet. She believes the secondary influence might be beneficial, a counter to Solvaen's stifling order. The PCs will need to help her understand that what she has found is far more dangerous than she realizes.
>
> **Key Secret.** The chamber is growing. When she first found it, the living wood occupied a space the size of a small room. It is now the size of a chapel. The growth accelerated after she began spending time there, and she has started to feel a presence when she sits among the green growth, not hostile, not exactly welcoming, but aware. She finds it comforting. She should not.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs find her and connect her research to the broader conspiracy, she becomes a key witness whose theological expertise helps the party understand Serith's methods. If left alone, she will eventually be drawn deeper into the anomalous chamber, mistaking Serith's influence for liberation from Solvaen's order. The Cinders will find the chamber eventually, and whether they find Alara alive or transformed depends on how long she has been exposed.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks quickly when excited about her research, then catches herself and goes quiet, scanning for threats. Uses academic terminology without realizing others may not follow. Fidgets with a small piece of calcified bark she keeps in her pocket, turning it over and over. Makes eye contact with an intensity that is either earnest or unsettling depending on context.
> *"The patterns are wrong. Not broken, wrong. There is a difference. Broken implies decay. This is something new writing itself into the old structure. Something alive."*
