## Appearance

A wiry man in his mid-forties with thinning red hair, freckled skin, and the haunted expression of someone who has run the numbers and does not like where they point. He wears the [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild's]] standard working coat, grey canvas with iron-thread reinforcement, though his is ink-stained and patched at the elbows. He carries a leather satchel stuffed with notebooks, measurement instruments, and fragment samples at all times, and his fingers are permanently stained with the red-black residue of [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] particulate. He has the look of a man who has not slept well in months. He has not.

## Personality

Gellen is brilliant, anxious, and deeply frustrated. He is a scientist in a land of warriors, a man who deals in data and evidence surrounded by people who deal in swords and ambition. He has discovered something catastrophic, the fragment migration, and he cannot get anyone in a position of authority to listen. [[The Ember Guild]]'s Guildmaster, [[Torren Hearthforge]], has not responded to his last three reports. [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] will not see him. The local smiths think he is trying to shut down their livelihood. Gellen is not paranoid, but the mounting evidence of a world-ending event combined with universal indifference is pushing him toward something that looks very much like it.

## Backstory

Born in [[The Hollowed Reach]] to a merchant family, Gellen showed an early aptitude for artifice and was apprenticed to [[The Ember Guild]] at twelve. He rose through the ranks on the strength of his analytical mind, eventually specializing in divine material resonance, the study of how fragments of dead gods interact with each other and their environment. He was assigned to the Ironmarch posting eight years ago, replacing a predecessor who had succumbed to [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] contamination and been quietly retired.

For the first five years, the posting was routine: measure the Monolith's output, track fragment positions, maintain containment wards, file reports. Then Gellen noticed that his position markers were off. Not by much, a few inches per year, but consistently and in the same direction. Every fragment he was tracking was moving toward the Kaevroth Monolith. He rechecked his data. He rechecked it again. He redesigned his instruments and measured from scratch. The migration was real, it was accelerating, and nobody would listen.

## Relationships

- [[Torren Hearthforge]], his Guildmaster. Gellen reveres Hearthforge's craftsmanship and is increasingly infuriated by his silence. He does not know if his reports are being ignored, intercepted, or simply lost in the Guild's bureaucracy.
- [[Sira Embersteel]], the master smith. Gellen respects her skill and fears her proximity to the Monolith. He has urged her to relocate. She has declined. They share a mutual concern about the iron's behavior that neither has fully articulated.
- [[Yrsa Bloodhand]], who has refused his requests for a meeting. Gellen suspects she already knows about the migration (she does, through intercepted reports) and is choosing not to act (she is not, she is simply overwhelmed).
- [[Mira the Red]], whom Gellen has never met but whose rise correlates with a measurable increase in fragment activity. He suspects she is a symptom, not a cause.

## What They Want

Gellen wants someone to listen. He wants his data taken seriously, his warnings acted upon, and the migration contained before it reaches a critical threshold. He has calculated that threshold: if enough fragments converge within a five-mile radius, the combined war-essence output will create a permanent zone of divine rage that will make the Ironmarch uninhabitable and eventually expand. His deeper need is validation, proof that his work matters, that the years of careful measurement and analysis were not wasted, that science can save people even in a world that prefers swords.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Critical information source in Acts II-III. Gellen has the data the PCs need to understand the fragment migration and the catastrophe it portends. He is the expert who can explain the mechanics and point the party toward solutions.
>
> **Key Secret.** Gellen's missing reports were not lost. They were intercepted by [[The Cinders]], the intelligence arm of the Dominion's theocracy. The Dominion knows about the migration and is not acting to stop it because the Heart of Iron beneath Ostivaar is the convergence point, and certain elements within the Dominion believe they can harness a reassembled [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] as a weapon against [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]. They are catastrophically wrong, but Gellen does not know this yet.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs help Gellen get his warnings to the right people ([[Torren Hearthforge]], [[Yrsa Bloodhand]], or the broader Guild network), the migration can potentially be slowed or redirected. If they bring him to the Kaevroth Monolith at dusk, he will discover the fragment map in the fracture lines and gain a complete picture of the crisis. Without PC intervention, Gellen will eventually attempt to sabotage the Monolith's base himself, a desperate act that will either slow the migration or trigger an immediate war-essence detonation.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Talks fast, uses technical jargon, and forgets that other people do not have his background. Catches himself, apologizes, then does it again. Pulls out notebooks mid-conversation to reference data points. His hands shake slightly, a combination of caffeine, fear, and [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] contamination he is managing with Guild-issue containment talismans.
> *"The fragments have moved fourteen inches toward the Monolith in the last six months. Fourteen inches. That doesn't sound like much, does it? It took them two thousand years to move the previous fourteen inches. Do you see the acceleration curve? Do you see what that means?"*
