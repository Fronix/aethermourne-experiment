## Appearance

A tall man, once strong, now wasted to sinew and sharp angles. His skin has taken on the metallic sheen common to long-term residents of the Descent, but his eyes burn with something that is not iron: knowledge, too much of it, pressing against the inside of his skull. He wears the tattered remnants of star-reader's robes, the astronomical symbols still visible beneath layers of rust and grime. Iron filings collect in the creases of his face like metallic tears. His hands shake constantly. His mouth moves even when he is not speaking, rehearsing prophecies that have not yet forced their way out.

## Personality

Broken and brilliant. Thalric's mind was never designed to hold the kind of knowledge the Echo poured into it, divine understanding spanning multiple consumed gods' worth of insight. The knowledge did not destroy his intelligence; it destroyed his ability to stop knowing. He sees connections, patterns, futures, truths, and they will not stop coming. He delivers prophecy in torrents, weeping as he speaks, not because the prophecies are sorrowful (though many are) but because he cannot close the door the Echo opened. He is lucid enough to know he is mad. He is mad enough to know he is right.

## Backstory

Thalric was a Star-Reader, one of the astronomers who study the movements of celestial bodies for signs of divine activity. He was brilliant, ambitious, and convinced that the Iron Descent held answers that the surface world could not provide. He was correct. He descended through all nine levels over the course of years, surviving through preparation, skill, and a stubbornness that the Descent's inhabitants mistook for strength.

When he reached the Heart, the Echo of Kaevroth spoke to him. Not in the vague, atmospheric way it addresses most visitors, but directly, intimately, as a teacher to a student. The Echo found in Thalric something it had not encountered in centuries: a mind capable of receiving divine knowledge. So it gave him everything. Fragments of Belara's understanding of love. Pieces of Thyrea's comprehension of growth. Shards of knowledge from gods whose names are lost. Thalric's mind, mortal and finite, cracked under the weight. He now speaks prophecy drawn from this stolen divine knowledge, and the prophecies are accurate, devastatingly so.

## Relationships

- [[The Echo of Kaevroth]]: His teacher, his destroyer, the closest thing he has to a god. The Echo speaks to him still, feeding more knowledge into a vessel that is already overflowing. Whether this is generosity or cruelty depends on whether you ask Thalric on a good day or a bad one.
- [[The Mercy]]: The only thing that soothes him. Near her light, the prophecies slow, the tears quiet, the shaking eases. He spends hours in the Tower of Mercy, sitting in her warmth, not speaking, just breathing.
- [[The Last Loyalist]]: Thalric has prophesied the Loyalist's end. He will not share what he saw. The Loyalist does not ask. Neither is sure whether this is mercy or cowardice.
- [[Vessel Maren]]: He has never met her, but his prophecies mention "a woman whose hand turns to iron, writing the truth the walls try to eat." He hopes she reaches Level 9 before she loses the ability to write.
- [[The Unnamed]]: She brings him food, water, basic sustenance that he forgets to seek on his own. She does it without speaking, and he is grateful in the way that drowning people are grateful for driftwood.

## What They Want

Thalric wants the prophecies to stop. He wants to close the door the Echo opened and be a person again, a man who can think a single thought without it branching into seventeen futures. His deeper need is meaning: he wants his suffering to have been worth something. He offers visitors a choice, escape through the Mercy Gate or help him crack the Heart of Iron open entirely, because if the Heart breaks, the knowledge disperses, and maybe then the knowing will finally stop.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Thalric is the party's interpreter and the source of the campaign's most significant choice: escape via the Mercy Gate or crack the Heart open. His prophecies can also serve as campaign foreshadowing, delivering accurate predictions about events on the surface that the party cannot yet know about.
>
> **Key Secret.** One of Thalric's prophecies concerns the Second Silence directly. He has seen what happens if the Aetheric Web fails completely, and the vision is so terrible that he has not spoken it aloud. He will share it only if the party earns his trust, and only if they promise to act on it. The prophecy is accurate.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the Heart is cracked open, Thalric's mind clears as the stolen knowledge disperses. He becomes himself again, older, scarred, but sane. If the party escapes without cracking the Heart, Thalric remains, prophesying to an audience of iron and silence, forever.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speak in bursts. Start a sentence about one thing, veer into prophecy mid-word, then wrench yourself back. "You need to reach the, the walls of Ostivaar will crack in thirteen months and the war-essence will, no, I'm sorry, you need to reach the Gate, you need to, the Web is fraying at the third anchor and when it breaks the silence will, STOP. Please. I'm sorry. What was I saying?"
