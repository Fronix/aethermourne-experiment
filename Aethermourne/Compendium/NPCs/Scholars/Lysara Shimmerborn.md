# Lysara Shimmerborn

---

## Appearance

Early fifties, slight build, with olive skin and dark hair shot through with streaks of silver that shift position from day to day, a minor but visible mark of her Changing. Her most striking feature is her eyes: they move independently of each other, tracking different focal points in a way that is immediately unsettling to anyone unfamiliar with her condition. This is because Lysara perceives three timelines simultaneously, and her eyes are each following a different version of the present. She wears layered scholar's robes, ink-stained at the cuffs, and keeps a set of three notebooks, one for each timeline, bound together with a leather strap at her belt.

---

## Personality

Brilliant, meticulous, and profoundly tired. Lysara's mind operates on three tracks at once, perceiving three versions of every conversation, every observation, every moment. This gives her an unparalleled ability to cross-reference and analyze, but it also means she has not experienced a single, coherent present in twenty years. She compensates with rigorous discipline, writing down what she observes in each timeline and comparing notes with herself. The result is research of extraordinary depth and reliability, produced at the cost of a cognitive load that would break most people.

She is patient with questions, generous with her knowledge, and quietly desperate. Her data tells her the Maelstrom is widening, and she has begun to suspect why. The implications frighten her more than anything the Maelstrom has done to her personally.

---

## Backstory

Lysara arrived at the Shattered Edge twenty-two years ago as a young researcher from the [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]]' academic network, drawn by the opportunity to study the most significant metaphysical phenomenon in the world. She was careful, methodical, and appropriately cautious. It did not matter. Two years into her research, a surge overran the observation post where she was working. Her colleagues were evacuated. Lysara was caught at the edge of the wave.

She woke three days later perceiving three versions of reality layered on top of each other. The experience nearly destroyed her. She spent six months unable to function, overwhelmed by contradictory sensory input. She learned to manage it, slowly, painfully, by developing a system of constant note-taking and cross-referencing that allowed her to identify which timeline was "primary," the one most consistent with the physical world others inhabited.

She stayed at the Edge because leaving would mean abandoning the only environment where her condition was understood and valued. At [[The Last Certainty]], her triple perception is not a disability. It is the most powerful research tool the institution has ever possessed.

## Relationships

- **[[Tyrell Vane]]**: Her closest ally. His probability-sight and her triple perception complement each other, his readings providing future-oriented data that she cross-references against her multi-timeline observations of the present.
- **Wardkeeper Sorin**: A professional relationship strained by shared anxiety. Sorin maintains the wards that keep the Certainty stable. Lysara's research explains why those wards are failing. Neither enjoys these conversations.
- **[[The Star-Readers]]**: She maintains correspondence with her former colleagues, sharing sanitized versions of her findings. The visiting Star-Reader who brought astronomical data is an old friend, and their combined work is producing conclusions that neither anticipated.
- **Aldis (junior researcher)**: Lysara mentors the quiet young researcher without suspecting that Aldis is a [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] mole. In at least one of Lysara's perceived timelines, Aldis does something that doesn't happen in the others. She has noted the discrepancy but not yet understood it.

---

## What She Wants

- **To prove her theory.** The Maelstrom is not expanding randomly. Something is pulling it open from the other side. She needs more data, specifically from inside the Threshold, to confirm.
- **To save the Certainty.** If the wards fail completely, the fortress will be consumed by the Shimmer Zone. A century of irreplaceable research will be lost.
- **To understand the alien text.** The fragment that appeared during the ward failure haunts her. In two of her three timelines, the text says different things. In the third, it is not text at all but a diagram.

> [!warning]- GM Notes
> **Campaign Role.** Lysara is the intellectual key to understanding the Maelstrom's true nature. Her research, combined with the Star-Readers' astronomical data, will eventually prove that the Maelstrom is not just a wound but a *passage*, and that something is actively working to open it. She can provide this conclusion to PCs who earn her trust, giving them crucial information about [[The Twelve#Serith, Goddess of Shadow and Secrets , *"The Veiled One"*|Serith]]'s plans.
>
> **The Timeline Discrepancy.** Lysara has noticed that Aldis behaves differently in one of her three perceived timelines. In the primary timeline and one alternate, Aldis is a diligent, quiet researcher. In the third, Aldis leaves the Certainty at night and returns before dawn. This is not a hallucination, it is Lysara's perception catching a thread that Aldis's handlers have not accounted for. If Lysara follows this thread, she will uncover the [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] mole, but doing so will put her in danger.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks carefully, sometimes pausing mid-sentence to consult a timeline the listener cannot see. Occasionally describes events that have not happened yet or happened slightly differently, correcting herself with practiced ease. Her three notebooks are color-coded (black, blue, red) and she writes in all three during conversations. She is warm, exhausted, and afraid, and she hides the fear behind data.
