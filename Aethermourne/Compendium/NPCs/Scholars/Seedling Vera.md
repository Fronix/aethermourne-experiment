# Seedling Vera

## Appearance

Young, early twenties, with the lean and slightly hollowed look of someone who forgets to eat because the work is more interesting than food. Her most striking feature is her hair, which shifts between dark brown and bone-white depending on which force she is channeling, sometimes mid-sentence. The transitions are faster than Fennara's, less controlled, and observers find them deeply unsettling. Her fingers are stained green on the left hand and grey on the right, like her mentor's, but the coloring extends further up her forearms, past the wrists, creeping toward the elbows. Fennara's staining stopped at the hands. Vera's has not stopped at all.

She wears practical Rootpyre clothing, vine-and-deadwood weave, but hers is singed and patched in places where dual-channeling has surged beyond her control. A leather satchel at her hip carries specimen jars, charcoal sticks for sketching transition-states, and a journal bound in Thornwild amber that she writes in constantly. Her eyes are brown, warm, and too bright, the alertness of someone running on fascination rather than rest. She smells of crushed flowers and old bone, the Thornwild's signature scent concentrated on a person who spends too much time in its deepest places.

## Personality

Brilliant, obsessive, and impatient in the specific way of someone who can see the answer just past the boundary of what everyone else considers safe. Vera does not lack caution because she is foolish. She lacks it because she has weighed the risk and decided the knowledge is worth more than the danger. This makes her more frightening than a fool, because she is making an informed choice, and she might be right.

She is warm in conversation, quick to laugh, generous with her observations, and genuinely excited to share what she has learned. People like her immediately. The concern starts later, when they realize that the enthusiasm does not dim when the subject turns dangerous, that her eyes light up the same way when describing a beautiful flower as when describing the moment a risen predator's ribcage bloomed with moss. She does not see the line between wonder and horror. In the Thornwild, she would argue, there is no line.

She loves Fennara with the fierce loyalty of a student who knows her teacher saved her from mediocrity, and the growing guilt of someone who has started keeping secrets from the person she trusts most.

## Backstory

Vera arrived in the Thornwild five years ago, a seventeen-year-old Marcher exile's daughter who had been caught communing with a dead sapling in a way that the local Greensingers found blasphemous. She was not channeling death. She was listening to what the death had to say. The distinction mattered to her. It did not matter to the Greensingers.

[[Root-Speaker Fennara]] took her in, recognized the talent immediately, and began training her in dual-channeling. Vera learned faster than any student Fennara had taught, faster than Fennara herself had learned. Within two years she could hold both forces simultaneously for minutes at a time. Within four, she could sustain the state long enough to perceive the Cycle's transition-moment directly, the instant where growth and death occupied the same space. She described it as the most beautiful thing she had ever experienced.

The Bonewood called to her after that. She began making solo trips to the edge of the deep interior, sensing the Cycle's acceleration firsthand, sketching the patterns she perceived, filling journal after journal. Six months ago, she went deeper than anyone except the Bloom-Wraith has gone and survived. She found it. The Wraith spoke to her, two voices overlapping, and showed her something in the transition-state that she has not been able to describe coherently. She came back shaking, silent for three days, and then asked Fennara for permission to go again. Fennara refused. Vera went back without telling her. She has been back four times since.

## Relationships

- **[[Root-Speaker Fennara]]**: Mentor and the closest thing to a mother Vera has known since leaving the Marches. Fennara's increasing worry is justified, and Vera knows it, which makes the guilt worse. She has not told Fennara about the return trips to the Bonewood.
- **[[The Bloom-Wraith]]**: Obsession. The Wraith showed her something during their first contact, a vision of two forces in harmony that predated the Theomachis, and Vera cannot stop returning to it. She believes she is close to understanding what the Wraith is trying to communicate. She may be right. She may also be losing herself.
- **[[Yara Thornbloom]]**: Disapproval and concern. Yara recognizes Vera's talent but considers her recklessness a threat to herself and to Rootpyre. Yara has tried to reach the Bloom-Wraith nine times and failed. That a student succeeded where she could not is both promising and alarming.
- **[[Garret Boneharvest]]**: Transactional warmth. Vera trades rare cycle-materials she collects on her Bonewood trips for supplies and equipment. Garret likes her, worries about her, and has started setting aside emergency supplies in case she comes back hurt.

## What They Want

Vera wants to understand the Bloom-Wraith completely, to master dual-channeling at the level the Wraith embodies, to prove that the transition-state is not a boundary but a destination. She would tell you this with shining eyes and absolute conviction.

What she needs is to learn that understanding does not require becoming. That knowledge can be received without being consumed by it. That patience is not weakness, and the Bonewood's call is not an invitation but a test. She has not learned this yet, and the window is narrowing.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Quest objective and potential ally. In the quest board's "rescue" thread, Vera has gone too deep into the Bonewood and has not returned. If the PCs retrieve her, she becomes a critical resource: the only person who has communicated with the Bloom-Wraith and survived. If they do not reach her in time, the Cycle catches her, and she becomes a second Bloom-Wraith, permanently locked in transition, a cautionary tale that devastates Fennara and hardens Yara's stance against Bonewood expeditions.
>
> **Key Secret.** Vera has already communicated with the Bloom-Wraith once successfully and returned four additional times without telling Fennara. During her first contact, the Wraith showed her a vision of [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]] and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]] working in concert before the Theomachis, the same vision Fennara received but clearer, longer, and with a warning attached: "the third voice is eating what we built." Vera does not know this refers to [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], but she knows the Wraith is afraid.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If rescued and supported, Vera becomes a bridge between the PCs and the Bloom-Wraith, the translator who can slow the Cycle long enough to receive the Wraith's full message. If lost, her transformation proves the Bonewood's danger is escalating, and the path to the Wraith's knowledge becomes significantly harder. Fennara's grief could drive her to attempt the same journey, compounding the crisis.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks quickly, jumps between ideas, finishes other people's sentences. Hands move constantly, sketching shapes in the air. Eyes widen when excited, which is often. Gets quiet and still when talking about the Bonewood, the only time the energy stops. Voice: bright, slightly breathless, with a Marcher lilt she has not lost. Sample line: "Fennara sees the pattern. I see it too. But she stops at the edge and writes it down. I need to know what it feels like from the inside. You can't map a river from the bank."
