## Appearance

A robed figure of indeterminate shape seated behind an enormous iron scale that rests on no visible support. The robes are layered and dark, the color of old rust, and they hang with a weight that suggests the fabric itself is metallic. The Assessor's face is hidden beneath a deep cowl, and where eyes should be there is only smooth, scarred skin, the remnants of a trade made centuries ago. Their hands are long, thin, and perpetually stained with the residue of iron dust. The scales before them are massive, ornate, and covered in inscriptions too small to read. When the Assessor speaks, their voice carries no inflection, no warmth, no judgment. It sounds like a measurement being recorded.

## Personality

The Assessor is precisely what they appear to be: impartial. They do not favor, do not resent, do not hope or fear. They assess. Every interaction is a valuation, not because they are calculating but because valuation is the only mode of engagement they possess. They answer questions with exact precision, never volunteering information beyond what is asked, never withholding what is requested. They perform their assessments as a public service and have never explained why, because no one has ever offered a price sufficient to purchase that explanation.

## Backstory

The Assessor was once one of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s treasury custodians, a soul bound to catalogue and appraise the spoils of divine conquest. When the treasury became the Market, the Assessor continued their function while every other custodian turned to commerce. They traded away their sight in the Market's earliest days, paying for the ability to perceive true value, the weight of a memory measured against the depth of a regret, the worth of an emotion calibrated to the fraction. The trade was, by the Assessor's own measure, fair. They gained infallibility. They lost only the ability to see surfaces, which they had already learned to distrust.

They have sat at the central crossroads of Greedhollow for longer than any current merchant has been present. The crossroads formed around them, not the other way around.

## Relationships

- **[[Dealmaker Syra Voss]]**, the only entity in Greedhollow whose worth the Assessor cannot fully calculate. This is not a limitation of their gift. It is the result: Syra's value fluctuates in ways that suggest she is not entirely a fixed quantity. The Assessor has not shared this observation.
- **[[Ren Twice-Sold]]**, a frequent visitor. The Assessor can see exactly what Ren has lost and what remains. They have, on three occasions, told Ren the precise value of what was taken. Ren keeps coming back, though the Assessor has never understood why.
- **[[The Debt Collector]]**, a peer of sorts. Both are functions of the Market given form. The Assessor values. The Collector enforces. They do not interact socially, but they acknowledge each other with the recognition of parallel mechanisms.
- **[[Broker Fennwick]]**, who brings the most complex valuations. Fennwick's multi-party brokered deals often require the Assessor's arbitration, and the Assessor finds Fennwick's transactions the closest thing to interesting that their nature permits.

## What They Want

The Assessor would tell you they want nothing, that wanting is incompatible with impartial assessment. This is almost true. The single exception: they recently learned that their sight is for sale in the Remnant Arcade. They want it back. They have not yet calculated whether the cost of wanting it is greater than the cost of reclaiming it, because they suspect that regaining their eyes will destroy the gift that defines them.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Assessor serves as an oracle figure for PCs navigating the Iron Market. They can appraise anything, including abstract qualities, giving the GM a mechanism to reveal information through valuation. Their quest to reclaim their sight is a self-contained encounter hook (see Iron Market encounters).
>
> **Key Secret.** The Assessor's blindness is the mechanism of their infallibility. Regaining sight will make them mortal-perceptive again, destroying their gift. They suspect this but have not confirmed it, because confirming it would make the wanting stop, and some part of them does not want it to stop.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs help the Assessor reclaim their sight, the Market loses its only impartial arbiter. Disputes escalate. The decay accelerates. If the PCs convince the Assessor to remain blind, the Assessor owes them a debt, the first debt they have ever carried, and it changes them in ways neither party anticipates.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speak in flat, precise statements. Never use metaphor. State values as exact quantities even when the units are abstract. Turn the head toward speakers as though listening to something behind the words. Sample line: *"Your happiest memory is worth fourteen transactions of standard weight. Your grief at losing it would be worth nineteen. I mention this only as context."*
