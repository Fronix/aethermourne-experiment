## Appearance

A gaunt man with ink-stained fingers and eyes that dart constantly, measuring distances, noting angles, cataloguing spatial relationships even mid-conversation. He wears a mapmaker's apron over clothes that were once fine but have been reduced to functional rags by centuries of neglect. His tower is papered floor to ceiling with overlapping charts, and he carries a set of brass instruments, calipers, compasses, and dividers, that he fidgets with compulsively. His hair is wild, his posture stooped from bending over drafting tables, and he smells of old paper and iron-gall ink.

## Personality

Obsessive, brilliant, and tragically hopeful. The Cartographer believes with absolute conviction that if he can map every kingdom in the Conquest at every stage of its cycle simultaneously, the act of completion will restore them. The map will become the territory. He has been working on this project for centuries and is nowhere close to finishing, because the Conquest is infinite and always shifting. He does not see this as discouraging. He sees it as a challenge worthy of his talents. He speaks almost exclusively in geographical coordinates and spatial metaphors, and he becomes agitated when people describe locations imprecisely.

## Backstory

In life, the Cartographer was a royal surveyor for an expansionist empire, one of the many civilizations that [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s ambition fueled. He mapped the territories his empire conquered, drawing borders that would define nations for generations. He was proud of this work. He believed that to map a place was to claim it, to make it permanent. When his empire fell, he died clutching his charts, convinced that as long as the maps survived, the kingdom survived.

The Conquest took him and gave him what he wanted: infinite kingdoms to map, forever. His tower stands apart from the palaces, the only structure on Level 6 that does not cycle. It is his domain within the domain, a fixed point in a landscape of perpetual change. He has mapped passages, hidden routes, and structural connections that no one else in the Conquest knows about, including paths to the Bone Gate that bypass [[The Last General]]'s control entirely.

## Relationships

- **[[The Last General]]**, who tolerates his eccentricity. The General occasionally consults his maps for tactical purposes, which the Cartographer considers the highest form of validation.
- **[[Crown-Keeper Ash]]**, a kindred spirit in archiving. The Cartographer respects her work but considers it incomplete, a record of endings without the spatial context that would make them meaningful.
- **[[Queen Vassera]]**, who stopped visiting. Her absence hurts him, though he would never say so. He does not understand that she stopped coming because his hope causes her pain.
- **[[Herald Unbroken]]**, whose post at the Bone Gate the Cartographer has mapped from every conceivable angle. He knows the Gate's dimensions better than Herald does.

## What They Want

The Cartographer wants to finish the map. He wants to chart every kingdom, every cycle, every permutation of the Conquest's infinite landscape and bind it all into a single, perfect atlas. He believes this will bring the kingdoms back. His deeper need is to believe that his life's work, in both life and death, has meaning, that the act of recording a place can preserve it against destruction.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Cartographer is the party's best resource for navigating Level 6. His maps are genuinely useful, showing paths and connections that are not otherwise visible. He can provide routes to the Bone Gate that avoid the Last General's patrols, which is critical if the party needs to reach Level 7 without the General's permission.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Cartographer's maps are more real than he realizes. The act of mapping the Conquest for centuries has given his charts a low-level planar resonance. If a kingdom is destroyed from his maps, erased completely, it stops cycling. He has done this accidentally once, smudging a chart, and a minor kingdom simply vanished. He does not know what happened. He thinks it was a drawing error. If he learned the truth, it would either give him terrible power or destroy him.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs reveal the truth about his maps, the Cartographer faces a choice: use this power to reshape the Conquest, or destroy his life's work to free the cycling souls. If left alone, he continues mapping forever, content in his delusion. He is, in his own way, the happiest soul on Level 6.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speak rapidly, jumping between topics. Reference locations by coordinates rather than names. Gesture constantly, sketching invisible maps in the air. Become visibly distressed when anyone gives imprecise directions. Voice: quick, eager, slightly breathless.
> *"The throne room is not 'over there.' It is fourteen degrees north-northwest, elevation minus three, cycling period forty-seven hours. Precision matters. If I am imprecise, the kingdom stays dead."*
