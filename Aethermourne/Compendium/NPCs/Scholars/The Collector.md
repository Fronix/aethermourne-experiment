# The Collector

## Appearance

Small, nondescript, and forgettable in a way that feels engineered rather than natural. The Collector appears as a figure of indeterminate age and build, wearing drab clothing that matches whatever level's aesthetic surrounds them, grey rags on the Ashfield, tarnished finery in Greedhollow, soot-stained work clothes in the Foundry. Their face is pleasant and entirely unmemorable. People who have spoken to them at length struggle to describe them afterward, not because the Iron Tithe has taken the memory, but because there was nothing distinctive to hold onto in the first place. They carry a small, leather-bound book that never seems to fill, no matter how much they write in it.

## Personality

Quiet, attentive, and genuinely interested, or performing interest so perfectly that the distinction is academic. The Collector asks questions with the patience of someone who has nowhere else to be and nothing they would rather do than hear your answer. They do not judge. They do not advise. They do not react with horror to horror or with pity to tragedy. They simply listen, nod, and write. Their manner puts people at ease in a way that feels uncanny in retrospect, as though the act of being heard was itself a form of magic. They are unfailingly polite, never pushy, and always willing to wait for a story to be told in its own time.

## Backstory

No one knows when the Collector appeared in the Descent, or how. They have no origin story, no entry narrative, no account of falling through a fragment or being lured by ambition. They are simply present, moving through all nine levels with an ease that should be impossible for a trapped soul, approaching individuals and asking, with gentle curiosity, to hear their tale.

They collect stories of transformation. The moment a general became a tyrant. The instant a scholar's curiosity curdled into obsession. The breath between a merchant's honest trade and their first act of exploitation. They are interested in the hinge points, the moments where someone became something else. They write these accounts in their book with a script no one in the Descent recognizes, and when they have finished recording a story, they thank the teller with a sincerity that feels, despite everything, genuine.

They are not affected by the Iron Tithe. They do not corrode, do not forget, do not display any sign of the Descent's influence. Like the Iron Shepherd, they move through the domain without resistance. Unlike the Shepherd, they have no visible connection to the domain's architecture. They simply do not belong here, in a way that is both obvious and impossible to articulate.

## Relationships

- **[[Kavothei]]**, the Fathom entity known as "The Collector" that gathers things drifted out of creation. If this figure is Kavothei's agent, it means something from outside the Boundary is operating inside a dead god's domain, a transgression with implications that extend far beyond the Descent.
- **[[The Iron Shepherd]]**, the two have been observed in the same space on rare occasions. Neither acknowledges the other, which, in the Descent, is itself a kind of statement.
- **[[Factor Ashlen Hale]]**, the Collector approached Hale once and asked to hear her story. Hale, characteristically, demanded to know who they were first. The Collector smiled, said nothing, and left. They have not approached her again.
- **[[Vex the Defiant]]**, Vex told the Collector her story once, early in her time in the Descent, before she learned to be suspicious. She regrets it, though she cannot explain why.
- **The trapped souls of the Descent**, many have spoken to the Collector without registering anything unusual. It is only in retrospect, when they try to recall the encounter, that the strangeness becomes apparent.

## What They Want

The Collector wants your story. Specifically, they want the moment you changed, the pivot, the transformation. They would tell you this directly if you asked, with no evasion. What they do with these stories, where they go, and what purpose the collection serves are questions they will not answer, not because they refuse, but because they seem genuinely puzzled by the concept of explaining themselves. They collect because collecting is what they are. The deeper question, whether they serve Kavothei, whether they are Kavothei's extension into creation, whether they represent an intrusion from outside the Boundary into the architecture of a dead god, is one they will not confirm or deny.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Collector is a slow-burn mystery that connects the Iron Descent to the wider cosmological threat of the Fathom entities. Their presence raises a question far larger than the Descent itself: if something from outside creation can operate inside a surviving divine domain, what does that mean for the Boundary? For the Fathom's relationship to the mortal world? For Serith's imprisonment at the Boundary's edge?
>
> **Key Secret.** The Collector is a tendril of [[Kavothei]], extending through one of the micro-fractures in the Boundary that the Primordial exploits to gather lost things. The Iron Descent, as a pocket plane within the Shattered Empyrean, exists close enough to the Boundary's edge that Kavothei can reach into it. The Collector gathers stories of transformation because these are the moments most likely to "drift out" of creation through fractures, the points where identity is unstable, where a person is neither who they were nor who they will become. These liminal moments are Kavothei's primary harvest, and the Descent is rich with them.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs realize what the Collector is, they face a choice with campaign-wide implications. They can ignore it (the Collector is harmless, arguably even benevolent, and their presence does not threaten the PCs directly). They can attempt to communicate with Kavothei through the Collector (dangerous, unprecedented, but potentially a route to recovering knowledge lost from creation, including knowledge needed to address the Second Silence). Or they can try to sever the Collector's connection to the Descent (which would alert Kavothei to mortal awareness and potentially provoke a response from an entity whose nature and capabilities are almost entirely unknown).
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Soft-spoken, unhurried, with a voice that seems to come from slightly farther away than the Collector's physical position suggests. They use "I" rarely, preferring constructions like "one wonders" or "the question presents itself." They never interrupt. They lean forward when listening, as though your words are the most important sound in the world. When they write, the pen makes no sound on the page. Sample line: "Every soul in this place has a moment when they became what they are. The moment before, and the moment after. It is the space between that interests me. Would you share yours? There is no cost. Only the telling."
