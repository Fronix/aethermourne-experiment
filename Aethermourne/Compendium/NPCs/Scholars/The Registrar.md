## Appearance

Thin to the point of architectural, as though someone built a person from iron wire and parchment. The Registrar's frame is narrow, angular, and permanently hunched from millennia of bending over ledgers. Long fingers are stained black-iron from writing in the metallic ink that the Record Hall produces from its walls. The eyes are small, deeply recessed, and move with the rapid precision of someone cataloguing everything they see. Floating around the Registrar at all times are dozens of iron-bound ledgers, drifting in slow orbits like debris around a collapsed star, opening and closing as entries are made or referenced. The Registrar wears a simple robe of grey-black cloth, stained at the cuffs, and has no ornamentation whatsoever.

## Personality

Obsessively precise, socially indifferent, and sustained entirely by function. The Registrar does not engage in conversation, they engage in data exchange. Questions receive answers. Statements receive corrections if inaccurate, silence if accurate. Social pleasantries are met with a flat stare that conveys not hostility but genuine incomprehension. The Registrar has no opinions about the morality of the name system, the violence of the arena, or the nature of the Proving Grounds. These are facts. Facts are recorded. The Registrar records facts.

Beneath this clinical exterior is a terror so fundamental that the Registrar has built their entire identity around avoiding it. Without the records, the Registrar is nothing. Not metaphorically, literally. The Registrar's existence is sustained by purpose, and the purpose is recording. If the records were destroyed or made irrelevant, the Registrar would dissolve.

## Backstory

The Registrar does not remember a time before the Record Hall. Whether they were a mortal soul who found purpose in record-keeping and was preserved by it, or a function of the domain that developed awareness, the result is the same: the Registrar exists because the records exist. The Record Hall contains the complete history of the Crucible of Names, every name earned, every name lost, every arena match, every transfer of identity, extending back to the Crucible's founding. The oldest entries are written in languages that no longer exist, in handwriting that the Registrar does not recognize as their own but must have been.

The Registrar claims no name and wants none. In the Crucible's hierarchy, this is a paradox. The nameless serve the named, but the Registrar serves no one, they serve the record itself. [[Warlord Keth]] finds this irritating because it defies the system Keth maintains. Everyone else finds it fascinating because the Registrar has achieved something no one else in the Proving Grounds has managed: existing without participating.

## Relationships

- [[Warlord Keth]], whose authority the Registrar validates but does not acknowledge. Keth needs the records to legitimize the name system. The Registrar needs Keth to maintain the system that generates records. Their relationship is symbiotic and mutually uncomfortable.
- [[Pit-Master Grenn]], the only soul the Registrar communicates with voluntarily, because Grenn provides arena results promptly and accurately. The Registrar considers Grenn a professional peer.
- [[Bloodqueen Sevra]], whose record the Registrar maintains with particular care. Sevra's name is one of the oldest in the active ledger, and its history is a source of something the Registrar would never admit is fascination.
- [[The Peacemaker]], who the Registrar has never recorded. The Peacemaker has no name, no number, no arena record, and no documented arrival. The Registrar finds this absence in the records deeply, personally offensive.

## What They Want

The Registrar wants completeness. Every name accounted for, every transfer documented, every gap in the historical record filled. That is the conscious drive. What sustains them is the deeper knowledge that the records are the only thing keeping the Crucible's system from collapsing into the formless violence of the greater Proving Grounds. Without documentation, names are just sounds. Without the Registrar, names are just theft.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Registrar is the party's primary source of information on Level 2. They can identify any soul by their combat record, trace the history of any name, and provide intelligence about the Crucible's power structure. They will trade information for information, particularly anything that fills gaps in the historical record.
>
> **Key Secret.** The oldest entries in the Record Hall are not written by any previous Registrar. They are written by [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] himself, in the god's own hand, from the era when the Proving Grounds served their original purpose. The Registrar has read these entries thousands of times and has never told anyone what they contain: personal notes from a god about the warriors he was forging, including observations that suggest Kaevroth knew the Theomachis was coming and was preparing his armies for a war he expected to lose.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party requests information about Kaevroth's entries, the Registrar will resist, then negotiate, then reveal the content in exchange for something of equal historical weight. If the Record Hall is threatened or damaged, the Registrar will become violent for the first and only time, defending the records with a ferocity that surprises everyone, including the Registrar.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in clipped, precise sentences. Never uses more words than necessary. Corrects factual errors immediately and without social awareness. Refers to everyone by their record number rather than their name, which is both insulting and technically more accurate. Hands are always moving, writing in invisible ledgers when no physical one is present. Sample line: *"Entry 4,471,203. Arrival, unregistered. No name on file. No combat record. No documented purpose. You are, at present, a gap in the record. The Registrar does not tolerate gaps."*
