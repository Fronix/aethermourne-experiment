## Appearance

The Truthsayer is a haggard woman of indeterminate age, gaunt from centuries of minimal sustenance, her cheekbones sharp enough to cast shadows in the dim light of her unmirrored cell. Her most striking feature is her eyes, or rather their absence: she scarred them shut herself, the lids sealed with burn-marks that have healed into smooth, shiny ridges of tissue. Her hair is white, unwashed, hanging in matted ropes past her shoulders. She wears the same tattered iron-cloth as every damned soul, but hers is turned inside-out, a deliberate choice, she says, because the outer surface is too reflective. Her hands shake constantly, a tremor that worsens when she is perceiving something she wishes she could not. She sits cross-legged in the center of her cell, surrounded by small iron tokens she has etched with symbols, a private notation system for recording the truths she sees.

## Personality

The Truthsayer is blunt, bitter, and exhausted in the way of someone who has been carrying a weight no one else can see. She does not soften her words because softness requires energy she cannot spare. She is not unkind, merely stripped of every social grace by the relentless clarity of her perception. She sees truth, all truth, at all times, and the truth of the Iron Descent is a thing of such concentrated horror that her composure is less a personality trait than a survival mechanism. She laughs sometimes, sharp and sudden, at things that are not funny, because the alternative is screaming. She is the sanest person on Level 5, and the residents of Gloryholme consider her dangerously insane.

## Backstory

In life, the Truthsayer was a mortal scholar named Veradha, a philosopher who devoted her career to the study of self-deception. She catalogued the ways mortals lie to themselves, the comfortable fictions that make existence bearable, and she published her findings with the cold precision of a surgeon. She was brilliant, respected, and universally disliked. She died alone, unmourned, and [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s domain claimed her for the sin of ambition turned inward, a mind so devoted to penetrating illusion that it became its own kind of conquest.

On Level 5, the mirrors tried to give Veradha her heart's desire: a world where everyone acknowledged her brilliance, where her work was celebrated, where she was loved for her uncompromising honesty. But Veradha saw through the mirrors instantly. She recognized the gift as a lie more sophisticated than any she had studied, and the recognition was agony. She could see the truth behind every mirror, every delusion, every false glory in Gloryholme, and she could not stop seeing. She gouged out her own eyes to escape the mirrors' visual assault, but the truth-seeing persisted, shifting to some other sense, a perception that operates through vibration, through the resonance of iron and light. She sees more clearly blind than she ever did with eyes.

## Relationships

- [[Sovereign Kallis]], the ruler of Gloryholme. Kallis visits the Truthsayer's cell with food and kind words, believing she is tending a troubled subject. The Truthsayer endures these visits in silence, because telling Kallis the truth would either shatter the Sovereign or accomplish nothing. Both outcomes are unbearable.
- [[The Shattered Man]], the fragmented soul. The Truthsayer is the only person on Level 5 who can see all seven of the Shattered Man's fragments simultaneously and knows which one holds the core of who he was. She has not told him, because the knowledge would destroy the fragments that are currently keeping the others stable.
- [[Lord Parvane]], the escaped reflection. Parvane visits the Truthsayer regularly, the only resident who comes not for prophecy but for honest conversation. She finds his company tolerable because he already knows he is not real, which gives them common ground: they are both living with truths that would break anyone else.
- [[Mirror-Warden Caelus]], the hall's jailer. Caelus leaves the Truthsayer alone, which she considers the closest thing to respect anyone on this level has shown her. She suspects he can see reality as clearly as she can and has simply chosen not to care.

## What They Want

The Truthsayer wants the mirrors to stop recording. She has perceived that they are not merely projecting illusions but cataloguing every soul's deepest desires, feeding that data somewhere beyond the Hall of Mirrors, and the purpose behind that collection terrifies her. She wants to destroy the recording mechanism, or at minimum, warn someone who can. What truly drives her is older: she wants to prove that truth has value, that all her mortal work, her catalogues of self-deception, her unflinching honesty, mattered. That seeing clearly is worth the cost of never being comfortable again.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Truthsayer is the party's most reliable source of unfiltered truth on Level 5, and possibly in the entire Iron Descent. She can see through every illusion, identify every lie, and perceive the true nature of anything on this level. She is also the party's early warning system for [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s interference.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Truthsayer has perceived that the mirrors are recording and that [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] is reading the data. She does not know Serith's name or identity, only that something external is harvesting the desires of every soul in Gloryholme. She has also perceived the Fracture's true nature: it is death-energy from [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s domain bleeding upward through Level 6, and it is growing. If the Fracture reaches Gloryholme's core, every delusion on the level will shatter at once, and the psychic shockwave will cascade through the Iron Descent.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party earns her trust, she will share everything she knows, including her perception of the Fracture and the recording mechanism. If they try to "cure" her or restore her eyes, she will refuse violently, knowing that physical sight would restore the mirrors' visual assault. If they help her destroy the recording mechanism, she will experience something she has not felt in centuries: peace.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in short, clipped sentences with no filler words. Her voice is hoarse from disuse. She tilts her head toward sounds with unsettling precision, tracking speakers by vibration. Occasionally interrupts someone mid-sentence to state something they were about to say, then apologizes flatly.
> *"You are going to ask if I can help you. Yes. You are going to ask what it will cost. Everything. Not because I am greedy, but because truth is expensive and you cannot afford to hear it cheaply."*
