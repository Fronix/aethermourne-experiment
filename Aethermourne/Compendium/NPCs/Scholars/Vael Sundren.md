## Appearance

In her thirties, wiry and sharp-featured, with restless eyes that never quite settle on one thing. Her left hand bears webbed fingers, a controlled mutation she undertook deliberately to better handle Marrow-Water samples. She wears stained leather work-clothes, practical and frequently spattered with reagents, and smells of peat, chemical preservatives, and the faintly sweet scent of diluted Marrow-Water. Her dark hair is cropped short and unevenly, cut with a knife whenever it gets in her way. She moves quickly, as though standing still wastes time she does not have.

## Personality

Brilliant, pragmatic, and anxious. Vael is a scientist in a world without the word. She observes, hypothesizes, tests, and draws conclusions with a rigor that would impress [[The Ashen Dominion|Dominion]] Calculists. She cares about data and evidence, not politics or tradition. She is terrified, not of the Bogs or mutation, but because her data is telling her something is wrong and she cannot determine what. She deals with fear by working harder, sleeping less, and talking too fast when explaining her findings.

## Backstory

Vael came to [[Mirewatch]] as a young researcher drawn by the Marrow-Water's impossible properties, a divine substance that rewrites living tissue according to patterns no alchemist can fully predict. She rose to Chief Researcher through competence and a willingness to take controlled risks that others would not, including mutating her own hand to prove that targeted exposure could be managed safely.

Under her leadership, the Mutagenists have refined their craft. Controlled preparations, vials that enhance night vision, salves that accelerate healing, tinctures granting temporary poison resistance, flow out of Mirewatch to buyers across the continent. The most controversial product, **Green Draught**, produces significant permanent physical enhancement at the cost of significant permanent alteration. Warriors drink it before major battles. The desperate drink it to survive.

Recently, everything has changed. The Bogs are expanding. Streams that were safe a year ago now carry trace amounts of Marrow-Water. New batches are more potent than they should be, and they carry a secondary emotional effect, a subtle pull toward despair, a quiet whisper that nothing matters, that dissolution is peace. Green Draught test subjects have begun sleepwalking toward the Bogs at night. Two had to be physically restrained. One did not want to be restrained. Vael's data screams that something external is destabilizing the Bogs' equilibrium, but she cannot identify the cause.

## Relationships

- **[[Senna Deepmoss]]**, the undecided Marchwarden who has quietly commissioned Vael to investigate the Bog expansion, her most important patron
- **[[The Greensingers]]**, an uneasy professional relationship, they disapprove of commodifying [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s divine marrow, and Vael considers their spiritual approach unscientific
- **[[Mirewatch]]**, her laboratory and home, a town that depends on her expertise and trusts her judgment
- **The Mutagenist team**, researchers and harvesters who follow her lead, several of whom are showing signs of the despair effect themselves

## What They Want

Vael wants answers. Why is the Marrow-Water becoming more potent? Why the secondary emotional effect? Why are the Bogs expanding beyond their historical boundaries? She needs more researchers, better equipment, and someone who will listen to her findings without dismissing them as alarmism. Beneath that practical drive, she needs someone from outside the Marches to examine her data with fresh eyes, because she is beginning to suspect the answer is something her framework cannot accommodate.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Critical evidence source in Act III. Vael's research notes are the clearest empirical evidence of [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s influence anywhere in the Marches. A PC with knowledge of entropic magic or divine corruption could help her connect the pieces, turning her data into proof that an external force is destabilizing the Bogs.
>
> **Key Secret.** The contamination is Serith's entropic influence bleeding through the deep root network into the divine essence saturating the Bogs. The Deepchange protocol (Green Draught) amplifies this, turning test subjects into potential vectors for Serith's influence, not monsters, but carriers spreading subtle despair through their communities. Vael does not know this, but her data points directly toward it.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs help Vael identify the cause, her findings become the scientific proof that convinces [[Senna Deepmoss]] to support unification, potentially swinging the entire political debate. If ignored, the Green Draught protocol continues, creating more carriers, and Vael's own prolonged exposure to the contaminated samples begins to erode her clarity. She may eventually become what she studies.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks rapidly, especially when explaining data, hands moving constantly to sketch diagrams in the air. Interrupts herself mid-sentence to correct or qualify. Taps her webbed fingers on surfaces when anxious, which is most of the time. Her voice is clipped and precise, but cracks when she admits she does not understand something.
> *"The concentrations are wrong. They should not be this high, not this season, not in these channels. Something is agitating the substrate, and I need you to understand that when I say 'something,' I mean I have eliminated every natural explanation I can think of."*
