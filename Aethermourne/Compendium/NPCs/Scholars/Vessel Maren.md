## Appearance

A gaunt woman in her middle years, though age has become approximate in the Consuming. Her left arm is iron to the elbow, the metal spreading in veins up her shoulder. Her right hand, her writing hand, is still flesh, though the fingertips have begun to gray. She wears scholar's robes that have fused partially with her skin along the back, and she moves with the careful deliberation of someone who knows that too sudden a motion might accelerate the merging. Her eyes are sharp, present, and furious with purpose. She writes constantly, in a journal whose pages are themselves beginning to dissolve.

## Personality

Methodical, driven, and unnervingly calm about her own dissolution. Maren entered the Iron Descent voluntarily, knowing what it would cost, and she treats her own consumption as data. She documents the process with clinical precision: the progressive loss of sensation, the blurring of memory, the way iron replaces flesh in patterns that follow the nervous system rather than the circulatory one. She is not brave so much as committed, a scholar who decided that this knowledge was worth dying for and has not reconsidered, even as the dying part becomes immediate.

## Backstory

Maren was a scholar of divine remains from the mortal world, specializing in the physical remnants of dead gods and their effects on surrounding environments. She studied the war-essence beneath [[Ostivaar]], the bone-shard formations in [[The Hollowed Reach]], and the tidal corruption in the [[Drowned Verge]]. When her research led her to theorize that divine domains retained functional consciousness after their god's death, she needed proof. The Iron Descent was her laboratory.

She descended willingly through a passage beneath [[Ostivaar]], navigating the upper levels with preparations and protections that carried her further than any mortal scholar had gone before. She reached Level 8 knowing she would not return the same way. Her plan was to document the Consuming's mechanics, leave her journal where it might be found, and accept absorption as the final price of knowledge. She has maybe days before her writing hand turns to iron.

## Relationships

- [[Mother Amalgam]]: Her primary informant and reluctant host. Maren interviews the Amalgam's component voices, cataloguing individual identities within the collective. Mother Amalgam tolerates this because she wants her people remembered.
- [[The Remnant]]: A subject Maren observes with growing emotional investment she did not plan for. Watching Sable fight to keep her name has made Maren question whether clinical detachment is courage or cowardice.
- [[The Hunger]]: She has documented seventeen conversations with the Hunger, transcribing its arguments verbatim. She notes in her journal that each conversation makes more sense than the last, and she is no longer certain that is because she understands the arguments better.
- [[Iron-Prophet Thalric]]: She has heard of Thalric on Level 9 and desperately wants to compare notes. Her journal contains questions she hopes to ask him if she reaches the Heart before the iron reaches her hand.

## What They Want

Maren wants to finish her journal. She wants to leave behind the most complete record of divine domain consciousness ever written, proof that the gods' domains are not inert aftereffects but living systems that process and consume and, in some terrible way, think. Her deeper need is legacy: she has no family, no students, no institution that will remember her name. The journal is her only monument.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Maren is the party's best source of mechanical and theoretical information about the Consuming. Her journal contains insights that could help the party navigate Level 8 and prepare for Level 9. She is also a ticking clock: her dissolution gives the party urgency and a potential rescue mission.
>
> **Key Secret.** Maren's journal contains a discovery she has not shared with anyone: the Consuming follows patterns that suggest intentional design, not residual function. Someone or something is directing the consumption, choosing what to absorb and what to leave. She suspects the Echo on Level 9 is not as unconscious as it appears.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** Without help, Maren's writing hand turns to iron within days, and her journal dissolves shortly after. If the party carries her journal to the surface, her work survives and becomes foundational scholarship. If they speak her name in the Heart of Iron, the Consuming slows around her, buying weeks. She does not ask to be saved. She asks for her journal to be saved.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speak precisely, like someone dictating notes. Pause mid-sentence to write something down. "The progression follows, wait, I need to record this, the progression follows neural pathways rather than vascular ones, which suggests, hold on, which suggests the Consuming targets consciousness specifically, not biology." Occasionally trail off, staring at her iron arm, then resume as if nothing happened.
