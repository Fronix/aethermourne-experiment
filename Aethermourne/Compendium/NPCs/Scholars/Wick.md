## Appearance

A slight, sharp-featured young person of indeterminate age, somewhere between sixteen and twenty. Dark hair cropped unevenly, light brown skin marked with faint ley-burn scars along both forearms, eyes that shift colour depending on the ambient ley-light, brown in still air, pale gold near the Glimpool. They dress in whatever is clean and close at hand, usually mismatched, and carry no tools or focuses. Fencrafters who work with Wick note that the air around them hums faintly, even when they're not casting.

## Personality

Restless, curious, and dangerously confident. Wick approaches magic the way some people approach music, with instinctive fluency and no patience for theory. They are charming in a scattered, unpolished way, quick to laugh, quicker to argue, and entirely without reverence for authority or tradition. Beneath the bravado, they are lonely. Their talent isolates them. The other students are afraid of what Wick can do, and the senior Fencrafters watch them with an attention that feels more like surveillance than mentorship.

## Backstory

Wick doesn't talk about where they came from, and the senior Fencrafters at [[Glimwater]] have learned not to ask. They arrived at the Bright House two years ago, filthy, malnourished, and already capable of Fencraft that should have taken a decade to learn. They claimed the fen-water taught them. Given what the fen-water contains, this may be literally true.

Their talent is unprecedented. Most Fencrafters spend years learning to sense ley-line surges and react to them. Wick doesn't sense the surges. They anticipate them, riding the Aetheric Web's chaos as if they can hear the pattern beneath it. During a surge last winter that killed two experienced Fencrafters, Wick stood in the epicentre, redirected the energy, and used it to light every lantern in Glimwater simultaneously. They were unconscious for three days afterward. When they woke, they said they'd been "talking to the water."

Elder Sarenne, the Bright House's director, has quietly assigned Wick a private workroom and forbidden them from training with other students after an incident where Wick's presence amplified a student's misfired spell to dangerous levels. Wick resents the isolation but doesn't understand the fear behind it.

## Relationships

- **Elder Sarenne** , their reluctant mentor. Sarenne recognizes Wick's talent and is terrified of it. She provides structure and caution that Wick does not appreciate and desperately needs.
- **[[Old Fenris]]** , the only person Wick genuinely respects. Fenris understands what Wick hears in the water because he hears it too. He has never told Wick what it is. He is waiting to see if they figure it out themselves.
- **[[Maren Duskwater]]** , the Fenmother has visited Glimwater twice to observe Wick. Wick found the attention uncomfortable but flattering. They don't know Maren is assessing whether they're a resource or a threat.
- **Voss Ember** , a failed Dominion Calculist at the Bright House who resents Wick's instinctive talent. Voss spent twenty years mastering structured magic only to watch a teenager surpass him by listening to a puddle. The resentment is mutual.
- **[[Brother Lumen]]** , they met once before Lumen left for [[Sporeling]]. Lumen asked Wick strange questions about what they hear during surges and wrote the answers down with visible excitement. Wick thought he was odd. They were right.

## What They Want

To understand what they are. The voices in the water are getting louder, and Wick is beginning to suspect that their talent isn't talent at all, that something is speaking through them, and they want to know what it is before they lose the ability to ask.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Living plot device and potential ally. Wick is the person most connected to Nethys's fragments, which makes them invaluable to anyone trying to understand, repair, or exploit the Aetheric Web. Multiple factions will try to claim them: [[The Star-Readers]] want to study them, [[The Ashen Dominion]]'s Calculist orders want to contain them, and [[Brother Lumen]] wants to use them as a conduit.
>
> **Key Secret.** Wick is not just hearing Nethys's fragments. They are being used as a junction point, a living node where multiple fragments can communicate through a single consciousness. This is not intentional on Nethys's part (she has no unified intention), but the fragments are drawn to Wick's mind because it is unusually receptive. If enough fragments converge through Wick simultaneously, the prodigy could become a temporary vessel for a partial reconstruction of the goddess's awareness. This would give Wick access to impossible knowledge, and it would almost certainly destroy their mind.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** Wick begins as a talented, reckless young mage. As the Second Silence deepens and the fragments grow louder, they must choose: embrace the connection and risk losing themselves, or sever it and lose the power that defines them. The PCs can influence this choice.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks fast, changes subjects mid-sentence, fidgets constantly. Makes eye contact only when they're being completely serious. Their humour is deflective, covering real fear about what's happening to them. When they're channelling ley energy, they go very still and their voice drops to a whisper.
>
> *"I don't cast spells. I just, listen? And then I move my hands, and things happen. I know that sounds mad. It sounds mad to me too. But the water's got a rhythm, and if you stop trying to control it, it carries you."*
