# Tidecaller Mirenne

| **Location** | [[Tidecross]], [[The Veilshore]], [[The Unwritten Lands]] |
| **Faction** | Tidecallers (clergy of [[The Twelve#Yvenne\|Yvenne]]) |

---

## Appearance

Mirenne is a woman in her mid-fifties who looks seventy. The ocean has not been kind to her. Her skin is weathered, lined deeply at the eyes and mouth from years of squinting into salt spray and sun. Her hair, once black, is now more silver than not, worn in a single thick braid down her back, practical and unadorned. Her eyes are the pale gray-blue of storm clouds, and they carry a weight that has nothing to do with age.

She wears the traditional robes of the Tidecallers: layered blue and gray fabric designed to shed water, wrapped and belted in a style that has not changed in three centuries. Around her neck hangs a silver pendant shaped like a cresting wave, the symbol of [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]]. The pendant is warm to the touch, always, a faint resonance of the goddess whose presence grows stronger every day.

Mirenne moves with the careful deliberation of someone who is exhausted but cannot afford to show it. She does not waste motion. Every gesture is precise, economical. She walks like someone bracing against wind that only she can feel. Her hands shake slightly when she is not actively using them. She has not slept more than three hours a night in six months.

She smells of salt, incense, and something else, something cold and deep, like the ocean at night.

---

## Personality

Mirenne is faithful, exhausted, and terrified. She has served [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]] for thirty years, tended her shrines, sung her hymns, read her moods in the tides. She knows the goddess better than anyone alive. That knowledge is killing her.

Yvenne is not stable. She has not been stable since the Theomachis, but her instability is accelerating. The goddess's presence in [[The Veilshore]] is stronger than it should be, stronger than it has been in centuries, and it is growing. Mirenne feels it every day, a pressure against her skull, a voice in the back of her mind that whispers in languages she does not know. She performs the rites to keep Yvenne calm, but the rites are working less effectively. The goddess is slipping.

Mirenne is practical. She does not speak in metaphors. When she says Yvenne is "trying to manifest," she means it literally. The goddess is pushing against the boundaries of her own domain, attempting to force her presence into the physical world in a way she has not done since before the Theomachis. If she succeeds, [[Tidecross]] will drown. Not metaphorically. The ocean will rise, and everything within a mile of the shore will be consumed.

Mirenne is also pragmatic. She knows she cannot stop this alone. She has sent messages to the Tidecaller hierarchy in [[The Hollowed Reach]], begging for support, for senior priests, for anyone who understands what is happening. The hierarchy has not responded. They do not believe her, or they do not care, or they are too paralyzed by their own crises to act. It does not matter. Mirenne is alone. She holds the line because no one else will.

## Backstory

Mirenne was born in [[The Hollowed Reach]], daughter of a fisherman and a Tidecaller priest. She grew up on the water, learned to read the ocean's moods before she learned to read words. She felt Yvenne's presence her entire childhood, a vast, distant awareness beneath the waves, more concept than being.

She joined the Tidecallers at sixteen, trained in the rites, and became a priest at twenty-three. She served in [[The Hollowed Reach]]'s temples for twenty years, tending shrines, blessing ships, interceding with Yvenne on behalf of sailors who needed calm seas. It was good work, meaningful work. Yvenne was erratic, but the rites worked. The goddess listened. The balance held.

Then the first ships reached [[The Unwritten Lands]]. The Tidecallers sent observers to monitor Yvenne's reaction. Mirenne volunteered. She was experienced, competent, and one of the few priests who had successfully calmed Yvenne during her worst episodes. She arrived in [[Tidecross]] two and a half years ago expecting to spend six months documenting the goddess's moods and return home.

She has not left. She cannot. Yvenne's presence here is too strong, too active, and too unstable. If Mirenne leaves, if the rites stop even for a day, the goddess will break through. Mirenne knows this with absolute certainty because Yvenne has told her. The goddess speaks to her now, not in words but in sensations, pressure, cold, the overwhelming awareness of something vast and hungry pressing against the boundary of the world.

Mirenne is trapped. Not by obligation, not by duty, by necessity. She is the only thing standing between [[Tidecross]] and annihilation, and she does not know how much longer she can hold.

## Relationships

- **[[Captain Erys Vane]]** (Tidecross Harbormaster), Shared dread. Mirenne and Erys are the only two people in [[Tidecross]] who fully understand how precarious the situation is. They meet weekly, share information the [[Concord of Tides]] and the Tidecallers would prefer kept separate, and quietly prepare contingency plans neither believes will work. They do not speak about the fact that they are both trapped. They do not need to.

- **[[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]]** (The Goddess), Intimate terror. Mirenne's relationship with Yvenne is not worship in the traditional sense. It is negotiation, appeasement, and increasingly, fear. Yvenne is not a distant divine presence. She is immediate, overwhelming, and demanding. Mirenne feels the goddess's moods like weather, knows when she is calm, agitated, or on the edge of breaking. The relationship is exhausting and inescapable.

- **The Tidecaller Hierarchy**, Bitter abandonment. Mirenne has sent seventeen messages to the Tidecaller leadership in [[The Hollowed Reach]]. She has received three responses, all variations on "continue your work, we trust your judgment." They do not believe her reports. They think she is exaggerating, panicking, or compromised. Mirenne knows she will receive no help. She has stopped asking.

- **The Sailors and Fishermen of Tidecross**, Protective responsibility. Mirenne blesses every ship that leaves [[Tidecross]], performs rites for safe passage, and intercedes with Yvenne on their behalf. The sailors trust her. They bring her gifts, fish, salt, small tokens of gratitude. They do not know how fragile Yvenne's calm is. Mirenne does not tell them. What would be the point?

- **[[Vosh]]** (Oldborn Observer), Wary respect. Mirenne and Vosh have spoken three times. Vosh understands, better than most outlanders, that the ocean is not merely water, that Yvenne's domain is a liminal space between the physical and the divine. Mirenne appreciates this. She has warned Vosh that if Yvenne manifests, the Oldborn observation posts on the coast will be consumed. Vosh took the warning seriously. It is the only time anyone has.

---

## What They Want

**Surface Goal:** Keep Yvenne calm. Perform the rites. Prevent the goddess from manifesting physically. Protect [[Tidecross]] from drowning.

**Deeper Need:** Mirenne wants to go home. She wants to sleep. She wants to stop feeling the ocean pressing against her skull every waking moment. She wants someone, anyone, to take this burden from her. But she will not leave. She cannot. She holds the line because letting go means death for everyone in [[Tidecross]], and she is not willing to carry that weight.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role:** Mirenne is the canary in the coal mine, the warning system that Yvenne's containment is failing. If the players ignore her warnings, [[Tidecross]] drowns. If they help her, they can delay the inevitable, but not prevent it. Yvenne's manifestation is tied to the Second Silence's progression.
>
> **Key Secret:** Mirenne is not merely feeling Yvenne's presence. She is becoming a **conduit**. The goddess is using her as an anchor point, bleeding divine essence through her to establish a foothold in the physical world. Mirenne's hands shake because they are not entirely her hands anymore. The cold she carries is Yvenne's touch. In six months, if nothing changes, Mirenne will dissolve into the ocean, and Yvenne will manifest fully through the space she occupied. Mirenne suspects this. She has not told anyone.
>
> **Arc Trajectory:** If the players help Mirenne, they can extend her time by reinforcing the shrine's wards, finding ancient Tidecaller texts, or negotiating with [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] to stabilize Yvenne (extremely dangerous). If they do nothing, Mirenne dissolves, Yvenne manifests, and [[Tidecross]] is consumed. If they kill Mirenne (a drastic solution some may suggest), Yvenne manifests immediately in rage. There is no clean answer.
>
> **If the PCs Do Nothing:** Mirenne performs the rites for six more months, growing weaker, more translucent, more obviously not-human. One night, during a storm, she walks into the ocean. She does not return. The next morning, the sea around [[Tidecross]] is glassy, flat, and eerily warm. Ships cannot move. The water does not obey wind or current. Three days later, Yvenne rises from the deep, not a goddess but a *presence*, vast, cold, and furious. [[Tidecross]] drowns. The survivors flee inland. The Veilshore becomes impassable. The barrier between continents re-seals, but this time it is not Yvenne's protection. It is her rage.
>
> **Roleplay Notes:** Mirenne speaks in short, exhausted sentences. She does not waste words. She is patient with genuine questions but has no tolerance for denial or dismissal. Her tells: touching her Yvenne pendant when the goddess's presence intensifies (it grows warmer), looking toward the ocean (a compulsive check, like watching a predator), and a slight tremor in her voice when discussing the shrine's wards (she knows they are failing). Sample line: *"The tides have stopped following the moon. The water is three degrees warmer than it should be. Ships report hearing singing beneath the waves. These are not metaphors. Yvenne is coming through. I can hold her for a time. Not forever. If you have solutions, now is the time."*
