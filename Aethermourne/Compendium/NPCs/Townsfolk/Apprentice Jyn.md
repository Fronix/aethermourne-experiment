## Appearance

A lanky human teenager of sixteen, all elbows and unfinished growth, with sun-brown skin, unruly dark hair kept out of their face with a leather cord, and eyes of startling amber that catch the forge-light in a way the other apprentices have noticed and muttered about. Their hands are already scarred with the small burns of forge work, collected faster than a dwarven apprentice would accumulate them because their skin is thinner and heals differently. They wear the standard apprentice's leathers, too large and constantly adjusted, and carry themselves with the particular combination of defiance and uncertainty that marks someone who knows they do not belong but refuses to leave.

## Personality

Jyn is stubborn, talented, and desperately lonely. They have the prodigious forge-magic gift that earned them acceptance to the Grand Forge, but not the cultural context that would make the acceptance feel like belonging. They are quick to anger when challenged about their right to be here and quick to doubt themselves when no one is watching. Beneath the defensive shell is a genuine love of the craft, a deep, almost spiritual connection to the act of shaping metal that even their dwarven masters have recognized as authentic. They do not know why they can do what they do. They only know that when they hold a hammer and the forge is hot, the world makes sense.

## Backstory

Jyn was born in a farming village on the Ashfall Slopes, the child of human settlers who worked the volcanic soil. They showed an affinity for metalwork from a young age, repairing farm tools with an intuitive understanding of how metal wanted to be shaped. A travelling [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] journeyman noticed the quality of their repairs and, after some debate, arranged an examination.

The examination was supposed to be a formality, a polite rejection to satisfy the journeyman's enthusiasm. Instead, Jyn demonstrated forge-magic sensitivity that the examiners had never seen in a human. They could feel the temperature of metal through stone walls. They could sense flaws in a blade by holding it. The Grand Forge's master of examinations, after three days of testing, declared the result "unprecedented, inconvenient, and undeniable."

Jyn's acceptance was controversial. The Grand Forge has not trained a non-dwarf in a generation, and the traditionalist faction on the Forge Council opposed the decision. [[Brunhild Ironheart|Forge-Matriarch Ironheart]] overruled them, citing the Guild's founding principle that craft honours all who honour it. Jyn arrived at [[Crucible]] six months ago and has been enduring a combination of grudging mentorship and social isolation ever since.

## Relationships

- [[Brunhild Ironheart|Forge-Matriarch Brunhild Ironheart]], The Matriarch approved their acceptance and checks on their progress personally, which is both a protection and a pressure. Jyn is terrified of disappointing her.
- [[Ashara Cinderbloom|Mender Ashara Cinderbloom]], She has treated Jyn's burns and noticed the unusual intensity of their forge-magic aura. She is one of the few adults who speaks to Jyn as a person rather than a political symbol.
- **Master Holdt**, Jyn's assigned mentor at the Grand Forge, a traditional dwarven smith who initially opposed their acceptance but has come to grudgingly respect their talent. He is teaching them properly but refuses to make it easy.
- The other apprentices, A spectrum from hostile to curious to cautiously friendly. Jyn has one tentative friend, a young dwarven woman named Essa, who was the first person to eat lunch with them.

## What They Want

Jyn wants to belong. They want to be judged by their work, not their species, and they want the Grand Forge to feel like home. More deeply, they want to understand their own gift, why a human from a farming village can feel [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] fire in their blood. They have not articulated this question yet, but it drives them toward the forge every morning with an intensity that their masters recognize as something more than ambition.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Social and thematic mirror. Jyn's presence at the Grand Forge raises the central question of who [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] legacy belongs to, all who create, or only the dwarves who built their civilization on his bones? They are also a potential PC companion for Emberveil arcs, young and brave enough to volunteer for dangerous missions and talented enough to be useful.
>
> **Key Secret.** Jyn's forge-magic sensitivity is not a random gift. They were born directly above one of [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] deeper geothermal vents, a place where divine energy seeps closest to the surface. Their mother experienced an unusually warm pregnancy, and Jyn was born with a body temperature two degrees above human normal. They are, in a very literal sense, touched by the Ember-Smith's fire, not chosen, but shaped by proximity to his ongoing sacrifice.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If supported, Jyn grows into a bridge between the Emberveil's dwarven traditions and the wider world, proving that Thalvor's gift is universal. If rejected or abandoned, they leave the Grand Forge and take their extraordinary talent somewhere it will be exploited, possibly by agents who serve less benign masters.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Fidgets constantly. Speaks too fast when nervous, which is most of the time. Goes very still and focused when working at the forge, the only time they seem fully at peace. Unconsciously warms their hands by pressing them flat against any nearby surface, a habit that comes from their unusual body temperature.
>
> *Sample dialogue:* "I know I'm not supposed to be here. Everyone keeps telling me. But when I hold the hammer and the metal is hot, I can feel it, something in the fire that knows me. And I'm not leaving until I understand what that is."
