# Cassia Lorn

## Appearance

Thirties, thin, with sharp dark eyes and the calloused hands of someone who has worked every menial job the Dominion offers. Her hair is cropped short, as Ashless cannot wear it long by law. A pale scar crosses her left forearm, a souvenir from a Cinder interrogation she survived by giving them nothing. She dresses in the undyed cloth that marks her caste, but keeps it clean, pressed, deliberate, a small act of defiance against the assumption that the Ashless are beneath dignity.

## Personality

Fierce, articulate, furious in a controlled way. Cassia does not shout. She speaks precisely, choosing words like tools, and she uses silence the way a soldier uses cover. She organizes survival networks in the Lower Ribs, food, shelter, hiding places for those fleeing Cinder attention, and she is very good at not being found. She mistrusts grand movements and ideological speeches. People who talk about systems tend to forget the individuals ground up inside them, and Cassia never forgets a name.

## Backstory

Cassia was born Ashless, the daughter of a Hewn-caste miner who lost his standing after a workplace injury left him unable to meet his labor quota. The caste system does not forgive debts. When her father was stripped of caste, his entire family fell with him. Cassia was twelve. She learned early that the Dominion's laws were not written for people like her, and that survival meant building the systems the state refused to provide.

By her twenties she had become the informal leader of the Lower Ribs' Ashless community, coordinating food distribution, sheltering families, and maintaining a network of safe routes through the city's underlevels. A Cinder sweep in her late twenties caught her, and the interrogation left the scar on her forearm and a permanent distrust of anyone who speaks for institutions. She gave them nothing because there was nothing they could threaten her with that the Dominion hadn't already taken.

She is aware of [[Elowen Greaves]] and the [[Unbound Congregation]], and respects Greaves as a person. But the Congregation wants reform, a better version of the system. Cassia wants recognition, legal personhood for the Ashless, an acknowledgment that they exist at all. The distinction matters to her. Reform assumes the system can be fixed. Cassia is not sure it deserves to be.

## Relationships

- [[Elowen Greaves]], respected but not allied with. Cassia admires Greaves' courage but considers the Congregation's goals insufficient for the Ashless
- [[Unbound Congregation]], ideological overlap on caste abolition, but different priorities and methods
- [[Delric Mourne]], unknowing adversary. Mourne's operations are disappearing her people, and she is closing in on the pattern
- [[Ostivaar]] Lower Ribs community, her people, the network she has built and will protect at any cost
- [[Petra Venn]], fellow Ashless and trusted ally in the survival networks

## What They Want

Cassia's spoken goal is legal personhood for the Ashless, recognition that they are citizens, not ghosts walking through a city that pretends they do not exist. Her deeper, more urgent need is answers. Ashless people are disappearing from the Lower Ribs without the usual Cinder processing, no arrests, no labor conscription, just empty beds and unanswered questions. She is tracking the pattern and growing more alarmed with each name added to her list.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Cassia is a key investigative thread in Act I. She is the only person in [[Ostivaar]] tracking the Ashless disappearances, which are [[Delric Mourne]]'s agents abducting subjects for entropic experiments beneath the Spine. No one of consequence misses the Ashless, which is precisely why they were chosen.
>
> **Key Secret.** Cassia's list of names, dates, and patrol patterns is enough to trace the abductions back to Cinder operations. She does not yet know the Cinders are responsible, only that the disappearances follow a pattern that rules out random violence.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs earn her trust, she becomes an invaluable intelligence asset, providing ground-level information the party cannot get anywhere else. If ignored, she will eventually confront the Cinders alone and either disappear or trigger a crisis in the Lower Ribs that forces the issue. Late in the campaign, if she survives, she becomes a political figure in her own right, the voice the Ashless have never had.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks quietly, never raises her voice. Pauses before answering questions, weighing whether the answer serves her people. Uses "we" when talking about the Ashless, never "they." Distrusts charm and grand promises. Responds to honesty and concrete action.
> *"You want my trust? Don't tell me what you believe. Tell me what you've done."*
