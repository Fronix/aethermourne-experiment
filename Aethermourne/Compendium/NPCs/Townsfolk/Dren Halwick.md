# Dren Halwick

## Appearance

Forties, gaunt, with hollow cheeks and dark circles that never fade. Once a big man, broad across the shoulders from years of hauling nets, now wasted by months of confinement and whatever is consuming him from within. His eyes are the tell: when he is himself they are brown and frightened, darting toward every sound. When the Other speaks, they go flat and dark, like deep water seen from above. His hands shake constantly during his lucid hours. During the Other's hours, they are perfectly still.

## Personality

When lucid, Dren is a terrified man doing his best to hold together. He is cooperative, desperate for news of his family, and prone to speaking in rushed fragments as though afraid the clarity will end before he finishes. He apologizes constantly, for the trouble, for the noise, for whatever the Other said while he was gone. Beneath the fear is a stubborn decency, the remnant of a man who was once well-liked in his village, known for mending his neighbors' nets without being asked.

## Backstory

Dren was a fisherman from a small coastal village south of [[Veil Harbor]], married with two children. He fished the same waters his father and grandfather had worked, a quiet life defined by tide charts and mending twine. He never wanted anything more.

During a Black Tide six months ago, Dren was pulled from his boat by something beneath the surface. He was underwater for three days. His crew searched for a day before giving up. His wife held a memorial. Then he walked out of the sea at dawn on the third morning, naked, shivering, unable to explain where he had been or what had happened.

Within a week, the episodes began. He would go still, his eyes would change, and something else would speak with his mouth, calmly, precisely, in languages he had never learned. The village elder contacted [[The Lantern-Keepers]], and Dren was brought to [[Veil Harbor]]'s Whisper-Cells. He has been in Cell Seven for four months. He is lucid approximately four hours per day. During those hours, he is the most frightened man in [[The Hollowed Reach]], because he knows what speaks through him the rest of the time, and he cannot stop it.

## Relationships

- **[[Lira Ashvane]]**, his captor and, in a strange way, his protector. Ashvane conducts documented interviews with the entity and has kept Dren alive and as comfortable as the cells allow. He trusts her more than anyone in Veil Harbor, though he does not know what she has learned from the Other's words.
- **His family**, a wife and two children in a village south of Veil Harbor. He begs for news of them during every lucid period. The Keepers have told him they are safe. This is true, but his wife has stopped asking when he will come home.
- **[[Bryn Kellar]]**, Kellar visited the Whisper-Cells once and found the experience deeply unsettling. Dren does not remember the visit; it happened during the Other's hours.
- **The Whisper-Cell wardens**, they work in pairs and rotate on short shifts. Most treat him with professional compassion. One or two are afraid of him. He can tell the difference.

## What They Want

Dren wants to be free, to go home, to hold his children, to stop hearing the voice. None of these things may be possible. What he needs, though he cannot articulate it, is for someone to tell him whether any part of him will survive this, whether the man who mended nets and loved his family still exists, or whether he drowned six months ago and the thing in Cell Seven is all that is left.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Dren is a living window into [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] prison. During the twenty hours when he is not lucid, the entity speaking through him is Serith herself, communicating through a mortal conduit. This means the prison's integrity is far worse than anyone has estimated. [[Lira Ashvane|Ashvane]] suspects this but has not voiced it to anyone.
>
> **Key Secret.** The entity knows things it should not: names of dead Keepers, locations of sealed Trench sites, dates that have not yet arrived. Ashvane's interview transcripts are locked in her personal safe, and the intelligence they contain could reshape the campaign. The entity has recently begun asking its own questions, which is a new and alarming development.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs investigate Cell Seven, they can attempt to communicate with both Dren and the entity. The entity is cooperative but manipulative, offering genuine information laced with half-truths designed to erode certainty. Dren's condition will worsen as the campaign progresses, his lucid hours shrinking. If nothing changes, he will eventually be consumed entirely, becoming a permanent vessel. Saving Dren may require confronting the prison itself.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Two distinct voices. Lucid Dren: rapid, fragmented speech, frequent pauses to check if he is still himself, hands trembling. The Other: measured, calm, faintly curious, speaks with a cadence that is almost musical. Transition between them is marked by a moment of absolute stillness.
> *Lucid: "Is it, is it still daytime? How long was I... please, has anyone heard from my wife? I need to, I just need to know they're all right."*
> *The Other: "You have questions. Good. I have been patient. Ask, and I will answer what I choose, as I always have."*
