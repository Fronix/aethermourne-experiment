# Foreman Edda Haelstrom

## Appearance

Fifties, broad and stocky, with grey-streaked hair always tied back in a practical knot. Her hands are scarred from decades of shaft work before she earned her promotion, and she moves with the deliberate steadiness of someone accustomed to narrow tunnels and loose footing. Her left eye has a faint grey film, early-stage Order Sickness she hides from Church inspectors. She wears the heavy work-leathers of the Spine miners, patched and re-patched, and carries a surveyor's hammer at her belt that she uses more often for pointing than for measuring.

## Personality

Tough, maternal, pragmatic. Edda has kept her crews alive for thirty years in the deep shafts, and she treats her miners the way a captain treats a crew at sea: with discipline, protectiveness, and the understanding that the mountain can kill any of them at any time. She knows the Spine's moods, the way the Hum changes before a collapse, the early signs of Order Sickness, the faint smell of bone-dust that precedes a vein giving way. She fakes inspection reports to keep sick miners on light duty instead of being "retired" to the Grey Ward, which everyone knows is a death sentence. She is beloved by her crews and tolerated by the Church because she delivers [[Cosmology and Magic#Ashite|Ashite]] on schedule.

## Backstory

Edda was born in [[Hearthstone]] to a mining family, third generation in the shafts. Her father died in a tunnel collapse when she was fourteen, and her mother worked herself into Order Sickness by forty. Edda swore she would do better, not by leaving the mines, but by making them survivable. She started as a shaft runner at sixteen, hauling ore carts through the mid-depth tunnels, and worked her way up through every role the mines offered.

Her promotion to Foreman came not from Church appointment but from necessity: during a deep-shaft emergency, she organized the evacuation that saved sixty-three miners when the senior foreman froze. The Church confirmed her afterward because refusing would have caused a revolt among the crews. She has held the position for twelve years, navigating the gap between the Church's production demands and the physical limits of the people who meet them.

The real Order Sickness rates in her shafts are three times what she reports. She falsifies the numbers because the alternative is the Church shutting down the deep shafts, which would collapse Hearthstone's economy and leave thousands without work. She carries this deception as a weight, not because she feels guilty, but because she knows that one day the numbers will become impossible to hide.

## Relationships

- [[Order of the Ashen Flame]], her nominal overseers. She respects the local clergy and despises the distant bureaucrats who set quotas without entering a shaft
- Granite, the old deep-shaft miner who claims to hear a voice in the tunnels. Edda protects him from Church scrutiny and is quietly terrified of what he describes
- [[Delric Mourne]], unknowing adversary. Cinder "advisors" have been redirecting her crews away from rich veins, and she has documented the discrepancies
- Her mining crews, fiercely loyal. They would walk into a collapsing shaft if she told them it was necessary, and she would never ask unless it was
- [[Hearthstone]] community, she is the town's most respected figure after the Shift Bell itself

## What They Want

Edda's conscious goal is survival, keeping her people alive and working, not discarded by a system that views them as expendable inputs. Her deeper need is answers. The Church keeps directing crews away from the richest veins, and when she pushed back, she received a visit from a man who spoke softly and smiled without warmth. She has copies of the mining discrepancies hidden in her quarters, and she is waiting for someone she trusts enough to share them with.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Edda holds documentary evidence of [[Delric Mourne]]'s sabotage of Ashite production. Her records, cross-referenced with the Cinder-maintained central ledgers in [[Ostivaar]], can prove that rich veins are being listed as "exhausted" while remaining untouched. This thread leads directly toward Mourne's conspiracy.
>
> **Key Secret.** Her own Order Sickness is progressing. The grey film in her left eye appeared six months ago. She has perhaps two years before it becomes impossible to conceal, and she knows what the Grey Ward means.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs gain her trust (she is protective, cautious, and will not risk her crews for strangers), she shares the mining discrepancies, but only if they can guarantee her people won't suffer for it. If the conspiracy is exposed, she becomes a key figure in Hearthstone's reconstruction. If ignored, the Cinders will eventually discover her hidden records and she will disappear into the same system that took the Ashless.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in short, practical sentences. Calls everyone "miner" regardless of whether they've ever held a pick. Stands with her arms crossed when she's deciding whether to trust someone. Taps her surveyor's hammer against her palm when she's anxious. Smells faintly of bone-dust and lantern oil.
> *"The mountain gives and the mountain takes. You just pray it gives longer than it takes."*
