## Appearance

A weathered man who looks seventy but moves like someone half that age. His skin has taken on the grey-iron pallor that the Ashfield presses onto everything, but his eyes still hold a faint, stubborn warmth, the last color left in him. He wears a patchwork coat of salvaged leather and corroded mail, cinched with a belt made from fused iron rings. His hands are scarred from decades of handling rusted metal. When he speaks, his voice carries the dry rasp of someone who has been breathing iron ash for forty years.

## Personality

Patient, deliberate, and quietly immovable. Erys governs Rustgate not through force or charisma but through the accumulated weight of simply refusing to leave or break. He listens more than he speaks, and when he does speak, people listen because he has outlasted every other voice in the settlement. Beneath the steadiness is a deep, private exhaustion. He has talked thousands of newcomers out of descending to the Proving Grounds. He has failed with most of them. He keeps count.

## Backstory

Erys fell through a planar thin point in [[The Hollowed Reach]] forty years ago, drawn by a [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] fragment embedded in a battlefield where he served as a conscript. He was twenty-three. He arrived in the Ashfield expecting to die within hours and instead found a place that would not let him. The domain aged him, slowly, cruelly, letting him feel every year, but it would not grant him death. He does not know if this is the Ashfield's nature or something specific to him.

In his first decade, he tried to escape. He mapped routes, tested boundaries, bartered with every soul who claimed to know a way out. Nothing worked. In his second decade, he stopped trying to leave and started trying to make Rustgate survivable. He organized scavenging parties, established trade protocols, and built the fragile social order that keeps the settlement from collapsing into despair. Somewhere in his third decade, he realized he could no longer remember his birth name. "Erys Ironfall" is a name he chose, or thinks he chose, or was given by someone he can no longer recall. The uncertainty gnaws at him in the quiet hours.

## Relationships

- [[The Ashen Crier]], the herald whose ravings occasionally contain truths Erys needs. He tolerates the Crier's presence at Rustgate's edge and secretly dreads the day the Crier says something about him.
- [[Dalla Threshborn]], the smuggler whose exit schemes give false hope to the desperate. Erys allows her to operate because the alternative, people with no hope at all, is worse.
- [[Old Rust]], the only entity in the Ashfield older than Erys's tenure. They share a quiet, wordless understanding. Old Rust knows things about the domain that Erys has never asked about, because he is afraid of the answers.
- [[Keeper Maren Ashveil]], the priest whose shrine provides the only spiritual comfort in Rustgate. Erys respects her steadiness and suspects she is the only person in the settlement who stays by genuine choice.
- [[Scavenger Polk]], a useful nuisance. Erys relies on Polk's salvage but distrusts his cheerfulness, which feels like a symptom of something surrendered.

## What They Want

Erys wants to protect the people of Rustgate from descending deeper into the Iron Descent. That is his conscious purpose, the thing that gets him out of his cot each morning. What actually drives him is the terror that if he stops, if he lets go of the role of Warden, there will be nothing left of him. No name, no purpose, no self. Just another layer of ash.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Erys is the party's primary contact and guide on Level 1. He provides exposition about the Ashfield's nature, warns against descent, and can be persuaded to share what he knows about the deeper levels if the party earns his trust.
>
> **Key Secret.** Erys has figured out the truth about the whisper, that it is Kaevroth's last standing order, "Send me soldiers," and that every soul that descends feeds the domain. He has been quietly sabotaging the whisper's pull for decades, which is why Rustgate exists at all. The domain has noticed. The ash-winds are getting stronger near the Warden's Nail.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party helps him, Erys may finally attempt to descend himself, not out of ambition but to find the source of the whisper and silence it. If they ignore Rustgate, the settlement will slowly collapse without his stewardship, and the flow of souls to the deeper levels will accelerate.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks slowly, with long pauses. Never raises his voice. Has a habit of looking past the person he's talking to, scanning the grey horizon. Calls everyone "newcomer" regardless of how long they've been here. Sample line: *"You'll want to go deeper. They all do. Sit down, have some ash-tea, and tell me why you think you're different."*
