# Hanna Tide

## Appearance

Small, wiry, and nearly eighty, with a face that looks carved from driftwood, all angles and weathering. White hair pulled back in a tight knot that has not changed style in forty years. Her eyes are pale blue, almost colorless, with the peculiar flatness of someone who has seen too much and stopped being surprised. She dresses in layers of grey wool and oilcloth against the perpetual damp. She walks with a cane, not because she needs it, but because it is useful for pointing at things and hitting people who argue too slowly.

## Personality

Fierce, dry-humored, and unbreakable. Hanna has earned the right to lead by the simple fact that she is still here and still fighting. She protects her people with a territorial ferocity that would make a Marchwarden nod in respect. She has no patience for outsiders who come to study the Sleepwalkers like curiosities, and infinite patience for anyone who comes to help. Her humor is bone-dry, delivered without a change in expression, and newcomers often cannot tell whether she is joking or threatening them. She is usually doing both.

## Backstory

Hanna was born in [[Mistholm]] and has never left. She married a fisherman named Torben who walked into the sea on a calm night twelve years into their marriage. She buried him in the town's memory, since the sea never returned his body. Two of her three children followed their father into the water over the next decade, each on a different night, each wearing that terrible expression of perfect peace. Her surviving daughter, Maren, fled to [[Tidewall]] at sixteen and has not returned.

The losses hardened Hanna into something beyond grief. She took on the role of elder not through election but through attrition, outlasting every other candidate by sheer refusal to die or leave. She organizes the night watches, maintains the lantern rotation, and personally checks the restraints at the Binding House each evening. She has pulled more people back from the waterline than she can count, and she remembers every one she was too late to save.

She has watched the Sleepwalker phenomenon worsen over the decades, spreading from occasional incidents to nightly horror. She has written to the [[Concord of Tides]], to the [[Lantern-Keepers]], to anyone who might listen. The responses, when they come at all, are promises and platitudes. Hanna has stopped expecting rescue. She is holding the line because no one else will.

## Relationships

- **[[Maren Selk]]**, First Tidekeeper. Selk recently wrote to Hanna asking about her sister Nereva, who was last seen near Mistholm. Hanna remembers the woman and has information that could unlock the [[The Whisperer|Whisperer's]] identity.
- **Garrison Captain Lukas Fenn**, the young [[Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keeper]] officer on his second tour. Hanna respects his stubbornness and worries about his journal full of names. She sees her younger self in his refusal to leave.
- **Ren Ashwick**, the sixteen-year-old night watcher who lost her brother to the sea. Hanna watches over the girl with a fierce, unspoken protectiveness, recognizing the same hollow determination that kept her own feet planted in Mistholm all these years.
- **[[Nessa Grieve]]**, elder of [[Saltmere]]. The two women correspond by letter, sharing what little knowledge they have about the spreading Sleepwalker crisis. They agree on one thing: it is getting worse, and no one with power is paying attention.

## What They Want

Help. Real, practical help, not the Concord's promises or the [[Lantern-Keepers]]' stretched-thin patrols. She wants someone to figure out why her town is cursed and fix it, or at least make the nights survivable. Beneath that, she wants her daughter to come home, though she would never ask.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Hanna is the key NPC in [[Mistholm]], the first person the PCs should talk to and the one they can trust. She knows every inch of the town, every resident, every pattern in the Sleepwalker movements. She is the PCs' anchor in a deeply unsettling location.
>
> **Key Secret.** She remembers a woman matching [[The Whisperer|Nereva Selk's]] description visiting Mistholm years ago, asking about the sea caves. The woman visited the Deep Cave at night and came back "like someone who'd swallowed the fog." This memory is a major clue to the Whisperer's identity and activities.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs earn her trust and investigate the Deep Cave, Hanna becomes a vital source of local knowledge and a moral compass. If Mistholm falls, if the Sleepwalkers breach the watch lines or the Concord enforces quarantine, Hanna will be the last person standing, and the PCs will have to decide whether to evacuate her by force.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in clipped, matter-of-fact sentences. Never wastes a word. Uses her cane to gesture, tap the floor for emphasis, or prod people who are standing too close to the waterline. Her humor is so dry it takes a moment to register. She does not cry. She ran out of tears decades ago. *"You want to help? Good. Grab a rope. We'll see if you mean it come midnight."*
