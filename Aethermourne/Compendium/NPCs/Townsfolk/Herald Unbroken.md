## Appearance

A broad-chested figure in ceremonial armor that was designed for proclamation, not combat, polished iron plate engraved with the insignias of a hundred legions, a tabard of faded crimson, and a helm crested with iron feathers that have never tarnished. He carries no weapon, only a herald's staff topped with Kaevroth's sigil, which he plants on the ground when he speaks. His voice is enormous, a battlefield voice trained to carry over the clash of ten thousand soldiers, and it has not weakened in two millennia. His face, beneath the helm, is weathered and earnest, the face of a man who believes absolutely in what he announces.

## Personality

Devoted, unwavering, and profoundly lonely. Herald Unbroken has two duties: to proclaim the victories of the Conquest each morning and to guard the Bone Gate against passage. He performs both with the same tireless conviction he brought to them two thousand years ago. He does not question his orders. He does not reinterpret them. He simply obeys, because obedience is all he has left of the god he served. Beneath the devotion, there is an isolation so deep it has become structural, a man who has stood at his post so long that the post has become his identity. He is grateful for any visitor, though he would never say so, and his proclamations grow slightly louder when someone is near to hear them.

## Backstory

In life, Herald was a battlefield crier for [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s armies, the voice that announced victories and rallied troops. He was not a soldier, not a general, not a tactician. He was the man who stood on the ridge and told the army it had won. He was good at it. His voice could turn a retreat into a charge, a defeat into a last stand. When he died, Kaevroth preserved him specifically for this purpose: someone had to announce the Conquest's endless triumphs.

[[The Last General]] assigned him to guard the Bone Gate as a secondary duty, trusting his loyalty absolutely. For two thousand years, Herald has stood at the fracture between Kaevroth's domain and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s, proclaiming victories to an empty battlefield and turning back anything that tries to cross. But the order is old, and the will behind it is dead. Herald's strength is fading, not his voice, never his voice, but the binding that keeps him at his post. The Ambitious Dead at the Bone Gate have noticed.

## Relationships

- **[[The Last General]]**, who gave him his orders. Herald considers the General the highest authority in his world and would obey any command without question. The General, in turn, trusts Herald more than any other soul in the Conquest.
- **[[Crown-Keeper Ash]]**, his only regular visitor. She brings news from the Field of Banners, and he looks forward to her visits with a quiet desperation he cannot articulate.
- **[[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]**, the god he served. Herald does not know Kaevroth is dead. He believes the god is merely absent, occupied elsewhere, and that his orders will be updated eventually. This belief is the only thing sustaining him.
- **The Ambitious Dead**, the war-spirits massing at the Bone Gate. Herald fears them, not because they are dangerous, but because defeating them is not part of his orders. He was told to guard the Gate, not to fight a war. The distinction matters to him enormously.

## What They Want

Herald wants to hear a new order. Any order. The one he follows is two thousand years old, and it has worn grooves in his mind so deep that he cannot think around them. His deeper need is to matter again, to feel that his proclamations reach ears that care, that his vigil at the Bone Gate serves a purpose beyond habit.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Herald is the gatekeeper to the Bone Gate, the most strategically important location on Level 6. The party must either earn his permission, bypass him (via the Cartographer's maps), or deal with the consequences of the Gate being breached.
>
> **Key Secret.** Herald's binding is failing. Within months (Iron Descent time), he will no longer be able to hold the Bone Gate against the Ambitious Dead. When they breach it, war-energy and death-energy will flood in both directions, destabilizing both the Iron Descent and whatever remains of [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s domain. [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] is aware of this and considers it an acceptable risk, because a controlled collapse serves her interests better than a stable border between two dead gods.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs reinforce Herald's binding (through divine magic, a new oath, or a fresh order from the Last General), the Bone Gate holds. If they convince him his god is dead, Herald collapses, his identity inseparable from his duty. If they give him a new purpose, a new order from a living authority, he transfers his loyalty with terrifying completeness and becomes one of the most reliable allies in the Descent.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Project. Always project. Speak as though addressing an army even in intimate conversation. Stand at attention, feet planted, staff grounded. When uncertain, default to proclamation: announce what is happening as though narrating a battle. Voice: booming, resonant, slightly hoarse at the edges from centuries of use.
> *"HEAR THIS. Visitors approach the Bone Gate. Their bearing suggests rank. Their purpose is unknown. THE GATE HOLDS. As it has held. As it will hold. You may state your business."*
