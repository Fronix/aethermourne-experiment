# Nettie Bridgeborn

## Appearance

A small, round woman in her late sixties with deep brown skin, bright dark eyes, and silver-white hair pinned under a faded kerchief. She wears layers of practical fenland clothing and an apron that smells of bog-herb tea and woodsmoke. Her hands are small, quick, and never still, always pouring, stirring, mending, or sorting. She looks exactly like someone's harmless grandmother, which is the most dangerous thing about her.

## Personality

Warm, observant, and utterly ruthless in the deployment of information. Nettie is the most genuinely liked person on [[The Drowning Bridge]], because she listens to everyone, judges no one, and makes excellent tea. She is also the most feared, because she remembers everything she hears and understands exactly how much each secret is worth. She trades in information the way other people trade in goods, with precision, discretion, and an eye for long-term value. She never lies. She simply chooses very carefully which truths to share.

## Backstory

Nettie was born on the Bridge, literally. Her mother went into labour on the Span during a trading run, and Nettie arrived between the Northern and Central Arches, the first and possibly only person native to the structure itself. She has never left. Not once, in sixty-seven years.

She grew up in the Bridge's bones, exploring the arches, the submerged chambers, the hidden passages that even experienced smugglers don't know about. By fifteen, she could navigate the Underbridge blindfolded. By twenty, she was running messages between smuggling crews, paid not for delivery but for discretion. By thirty, she had opened the Kettle, and discovered that a tea house on a smuggling bridge is the finest intelligence-gathering apparatus ever devised.

Over decades, Nettie has accumulated a mental archive of the southern fenlands' secrets: smuggling routes, hidden caches, old debts, new betrayals, family connections, political maneuvering, and things whispered in the dark by people who forgot she was listening. She has never written any of it down. She doesn't need to.

She has also explored the Bridge's deepest chambers, the sealed spaces beneath the waterline that predate the fenlands' flooding. She found something there, years ago, that she has never told anyone about. Whatever it was, it changed her. She was reckless before. She became careful after. She began watching the ley lines, tracking the surges, and quietly preparing for something she won't name.

## Relationships

- **[[Torben Greaves]]** , her primary client. She sells him intelligence about Sluice traffic, competing smugglers, and Dominion movements. He pays well. She tells him exactly what he needs to know and not a syllable more.
- **[[Maren Duskwater]]** , Nettie has met the Fenmother twice. They understood each other immediately, two women who protect their people through knowledge rather than force. Nettie provides Maren with intelligence about the Sluice corridor. The Fenmother has never asked what Nettie wants in return. She will.
- **[[Captain Hale Ashmark]]** , Nettie knows the Dominion officer has agents watching the Bridge. She knows who they are. She has not told [[Torben Greaves]] because she finds the situation more useful unresolved.
- **[[Sera Gloomhollow]]** , the bandit queen has never visited the Bridge, but her lieutenants have. Nettie knows the Hollowguard's supply routes, camp locations, and internal tensions. She has not sold this information to anyone. Yet.
- **Delk "the Diver" Marsh** , the Bridge's best Underbridge navigator. The only person Nettie trusts with even partial knowledge of the deep chambers. He dives for her occasionally, retrieving things from sealed spaces. He doesn't ask what they are.

## What They Want

To keep the Bridge standing and her community safe, by controlling the flow of information that determines who has power and who doesn't. And, beneath that, to understand what she found in the deep chambers before the rising ley-activity reaches it.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Information broker and mystery hook. Nettie is the NPC who knows more than anyone else in the southern fenlands. She is the quest-giver who points PCs toward trouble, the source who confirms suspicions, and the keeper of the Bridge's deepest secret.
>
> **Key Secret.** In the Bridge's deepest sealed chamber, Nettie found a crystalline structure that doesn't match any known divine material. It is not Ashite, not Thyrea's Amber, not Morrhaelite. It is older. It predates the Twelve. The Star-Readers who briefly examined the Bridge's construction suspected it was built before the gods' era, and Nettie's discovery confirms it. The crystal pulses with a rhythm that has been accelerating over the past year, synchronizing with the ley-line surges. Nettie believes something is waking up inside it. She does not know what it is, only that the Bridge was built to contain it.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** Nettie begins as a useful contact and local colour. As the PCs investigate the fenlands' mysteries, they discover that Nettie has been managing information flows to prevent anyone from looking too closely at the Bridge's foundations. When the ley surges intensify enough to threaten the sealed chambers, she must reveal what she knows or risk the consequences of silence. Her arc is about the cost of hoarding secrets when the world needs them shared.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks warmly, offers tea before conversation, uses "dear" and "love" as forms of address. Her eyes are always moving, cataloguing who entered, who left, who's listening. She deflects personal questions with cheerful non-answers. When discussing something genuinely important, she drops the grandmother act entirely, her voice flattens, her gaze sharpens, and she becomes someone quite different. Then she pours more tea and the mask goes back on.
>
> *"Tea, dear? Of course. Sit, sit. You look like you've been walking the deep paths. No, don't tell me, I can see it in your boots. The Sluice mud has that particular grey. Now then. What brings you to old Nettie's kettle, and what are you willing to trade for the answer?"*
