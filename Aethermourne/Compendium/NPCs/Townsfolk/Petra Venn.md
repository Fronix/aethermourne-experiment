# Petra Venn

## Appearance

Late twenties, slight build, with dark hair worn loose, a deliberate violation of Keeper-caste grooming standards that marks her as a woman who has stopped performing obedience. She still dresses as an Athenaeum student despite being expelled two years ago, wearing the pale grey scholar's coat with its bone-stone clasps. Her hands are perpetually ink-stained, her fingernails short and chipped from hand-setting type on a contraband printing press. She carries pamphlets in every pocket, folded into tight squares, and smells faintly of lamp oil and cheap paper.

## Personality

Passionate, idealistic, and reckless in equal measure. Petra is the Congregation's most effective organizer in [[Ashveil]] because she genuinely does not care about personal risk, a quality that inspires devotion in her followers and dread in anyone who cares about her survival. She speaks with the fervor of a convert, citing [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] teachings with a fluency that embarrasses Church theologians. She is impatient with caution, dismissive of compromise, and possesses the dangerous certainty of someone who has never been wrong about anything that mattered to her. Beneath the idealism runs a vein of loneliness; she has sacrificed every personal connection for the cause and would never admit the cost.

## Backstory

Petra was born Keeper-caste in [[Ashveil]], the daughter of a mid-ranking Athenaeum archivist who spent his life cataloguing texts he was forbidden to read. She watched her father's quiet compliance erode him year by year, and swore she would not inherit his silence. She enrolled at the Athenaeum at sixteen, excelled in theological history, and began asking questions her professors could not safely answer.

Her expulsion came at twenty-five, after she presented a thesis arguing that [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] original civic codes, preserved in fragments across the Athenaeum's restricted collection, contained no mention of hereditary caste. The thesis was suppressed. Petra was removed from the rolls. She walked out of the Athenaeum's gates and into the [[Unbound Congregation]]'s study circles the same week.

In the two years since, she has built a network of sympathetic scholars, students, and junior faculty across the [[Ashveil#The White Cloisters|White Cloisters]]. She organizes secret meetings in Athenaeum cellars, distributes banned pamphlets, and runs one of the Congregation's most active cells. She has never met [[Elowen Greaves]], who operates from [[Ostivaar]], but she worships the Speaker with the intensity of someone who needs a hero to believe in.

## Relationships

- **[[Elowen Greaves]]**, idol and distant leader. Petra models herself on Greaves and would die for her without hesitation. They have never met.
- **[[Ashara Voss]]**, unknowing protector. The Commander has quietly ensured Petra receives warnings before Cinder raids, not out of sympathy for the Congregation, but out of principled opposition to [[The Cinders]].
- **[[Delric Mourne]]**, nemesis by proxy. The Cinder Thane's agents in Ashveil have Petra's name on a list. She knows it and considers it a badge of honor.
- **Archscholar [[Ashveil#Key NPCs|Verity Dunne]]**, complicated ally. Dunne privately sympathizes but cannot protect Petra without exposing herself. They communicate through intermediaries.
- **Her father**, estranged. He begged her to recant after the expulsion. She refused. He stopped writing. She keeps his last letter folded inside her coat.

## What They Want

Petra wants to abolish the caste system and prove that [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] true legacy was justice, not hierarchy. More immediately, she is obsessed with a document she believes exists in the Athenaeum's restricted collection, a pre-Church interpretation of Solvaen's civic codes that would validate everything the Congregation argues. Finding it would give the movement theological ammunition the Church could not dismiss.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Petra is the PCs' most likely point of contact with the [[Unbound Congregation]] in [[Ashveil]]. She functions as a local quest-giver, information broker, and moral compass for Act I and Act II scenes set in the scholar's city.
>
> **Key Secret.** [[The Cinders]] are not merely watching Petra; they are planning to arrest her during the next purge and use her network to identify and eliminate Congregation sympathizers throughout Ashveil's academic community. Her entire cell is compromised, and she does not know it.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs warn her, she goes underground and becomes a more effective, more cautious leader. If they do not, she is arrested, and her network is dismantled. In the latter case, she survives imprisonment but emerges hardened, her idealism replaced by the pragmatic ruthlessness of someone who has learned what the system does to believers. Either path can lead her to eventually meet [[Elowen Greaves]], a moment that should feel earned.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks quickly, interrupts herself, quotes Solvaen's civic codes from memory mid-sentence. Her voice drops to a whisper when she is most serious, not from fear, but from intensity. She fidgets constantly, folding and unfolding pamphlets while she talks. Sample line: *"You want to know what Solvaen actually said about caste? Nothing. Not one word. The Church built a prison and carved his name above the door."*
