## Appearance

Ren looks like a young man drawn in charcoal and then partially erased. His features are present but indistinct, a face that the eye slides off of, a body that seems to flicker between states of solidity. His edges blur when he moves quickly and sharpen when he stands still. He wears clothes that do not match any particular era or culture, assembled from trades and castoffs, and they fit him the way borrowed things always do, almost but not quite right. His voice is clear despite everything else about him, surprisingly warm, with the cadence of someone who has learned to make the best of a terrible situation. He smells of nothing at all, which is the most unsettling thing about him in a place where everything else has a scent.

## Personality

Ren is cheerful. This is the most disturbing thing about him. A soul who has been traded so many times that he has no remaining claim on himself should be despairing, furious, or empty, and Ren is none of these. He is genuinely, persistently, almost aggressively pleasant. He jokes about his condition, offers advice to newcomers with the warmth of a concerned older brother, and navigates Greedhollow's shifting streets with the ease of someone who has nothing left to want and therefore cannot be redirected. Whether his cheer is genuine resilience, a coping mechanism, or simply the last personality trait that nobody wanted to buy is a question Ren himself cannot answer.

## Backstory

Ren arrived in the Iron Market as a young soul, freshly dead and full of the desperate wants that make new arrivals such easy marks. His first trade was small, a childhood memory for directions to the Descent Throat, because he believed he could descend further and find a way back to the living. His second trade was larger, the sound of his mother's voice for information about the deeper levels. The third, fourth, and fifth trades blurred together, each one reasonable, each one diminishing him by precisely the amount he agreed to.

By the time he realized what was happening, he had been traded himself, his remaining identity sold as collateral in a deal between two merchants who never told him the terms. He passed through six more owners before the chain of custody became so tangled that no one could prove a claim on him. He belongs to no one now, which in Greedhollow means he belongs to the Market itself, present but unclaimed, a coin that has been in circulation so long its face has worn smooth.

He knows every merchant's methods from the buyer's side. This makes him the Market's most effective guide and its most honest warning.

## Relationships

- **[[Dealmaker Syra Voss]]**, who watches him with something Ren cannot identify. He thinks it might be guilt, but he has traded away his ability to recognize that emotion in others, so he cannot be certain. She has never tried to trade with him, which in Greedhollow is the closest thing to kindness.
- **[[The Assessor]]**, who tells him exactly what he has lost each time he visits. Ren keeps returning because the Assessor is the only entity in Greedhollow who sees him completely, not as an outline but as a ledger of specific, quantifiable losses.
- **[[Coinless Marta]]**, a genuine friend, one of the few connections Ren maintains that is not transactional. Marta sometimes steals back fragments from the Remnant Arcade and slips them to Ren without asking for payment. He never keeps them long, the Market always reclaims, but the gesture matters.
- **[[Broker Fennwick]]**, who was involved in one of Ren's middle trades. Ren does not hold grudges, he has traded away most of his capacity for resentment, but he watches Fennwick with the careful attention of someone who remembers the shape of a wound even after forgetting the pain.

## What They Want

Ren would tell you he wants nothing, that wanting is what got him here and he has learned better. This is almost true. The one want he cannot trade away, because it is so fundamental that the Market cannot isolate it from his existence, is the desire to be seen. Not as a cautionary tale, not as a guide, not as an outline, but as a person. He does not articulate this. He may not even know it. But he gravitates toward anyone who looks at him without flinching, and he will risk what little remains of him for someone who treats him as whole.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Ren is the party's best source of practical guidance through the Iron Market and a living illustration of what the Market does to those who engage with it carelessly. His warning about someone buying combat-earned names from the Crucible above is a campaign-significant hook that connects Level 3 to the broader plot.
>
> **Key Secret.** Ren's condition is not as irreversible as the Market claims. The fragments of his traded identity still exist in Greedhollow, scattered across merchant inventories and the Remnant Arcade. Theoretically, they could be reassembled. The cost of purchasing them all would be staggering, but [[Coinless Marta]] has been quietly cataloguing their locations. She has not told Ren because she cannot yet afford the final piece: his capacity for hope, which [[Dealmaker Syra Voss]] holds personally.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs help restore Ren, the Market loses its most visible cautionary tale, which accelerates the decay as new arrivals trade more recklessly without the warning. If Ren remains as he is, he becomes increasingly transparent, eventually fading into the Market itself, becoming another function rather than a soul.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speak brightly, with the cadence of someone telling a joke they have told many times. Gesture with hands that blur at the edges. Make eye contact with an intensity that suggests he is memorizing the other person's face. Sample line: *"Oh, don't worry about me. I'm mostly here. Enough of me, anyway. Let me show you the Arcade, you'll want to see it before you start making deals. Trust me on that."*
