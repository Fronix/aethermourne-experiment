# Revenant Moss

## Appearance

Moss looks like what time does to the dead when the dead refuse to stop caring. Their body is preserved by the Cycle's rhythm, neither decaying further nor truly regenerating, locked in a state of dignified stillness. Pale skin, slightly translucent at the edges, with a faint grey-green tinge where the Thornwild's dual energies have left their mark. Their eyes are the most striking feature: during the death-phase, flat and colorless, the empty gaze common to all Remnants. During the life-phase, they flood with warmth, brown-gold and wet with tears that Moss never tries to hide.

They dress simply. Undyed wool and preserved deadwood-fiber, the same practical clothing every Ashbloom resident wears. No adornment, no marks of office. Moss does not need insignia. Everyone in Ashbloom knows who they are.

Their movements are deliberate, slightly too smooth, the way Remnants move when they have had decades to learn how to make a dead body express a living mind. When Moss touches someone's hand, the contact is cool and dry, except during the restoration, when their skin warms and they grip tight, as if trying to hold the sensation in place by force.

## Personality

Gentle, precise, and heartbroken in a way that has become structural. Moss speaks carefully, choosing words the way a jeweler selects stones, because after forty years of cycling between sensation and numbness, they have learned that language is the only thing that survives both states unchanged.

They are patient with newcomers, unflinching with the truth, and incapable of cruelty. This is not softness. It is the hard-won calm of someone who has experienced the worst thing, losing the feeling of being alive, and then experienced the second-worst thing, getting it back for a few minutes before it is taken away again, thousands of times. Moss has been refined by repetition into something that is either deeply wise or deeply damaged, and the distinction may not matter.

They weep during the restoration. Every time. Without shame, without restraint. Forty years has not dulled it. They say this is the point.

## Backstory

Moss died approximately sixty years ago in the [[The Pale Wastes|Pale Wastes]], killed in a bone-storm while traveling between Holds. They rose as a Remnant, aware, confused, and immediately unwelcome. The Hold they had called home burned their possessions and told them to leave. They wandered for twenty years, surviving on stubbornness and the peculiar resilience of the undead, before hearing rumors of a place in the northwest where the dead could feel alive again.

They arrived at Ashbloom when it was a camp of twelve people, living and Remnant, huddled in the Wilt and trying not to die. Or not to die again. Moss stayed because the first restoration, the first moment of warmth in twenty years of cold, broke something open inside them that had sealed shut. They wept for three hours. Then they stood up and started helping.

Over forty years, Moss became Ashbloom's heart. Not its leader in any formal sense, though the community votes them onto the council every year without opposition. Its heart. The person who sits with new Remnant pilgrims during their first restoration and holds their hand while they cry. The person who explains to living visitors why this place matters. The person who has given more of themselves to the idea that the dead deserve dignity than most living people give to anything at all.

They are tired. The restoration has been weakening. Moss has noticed and has told no one.

## Relationships

- **[[Kael Duskborne]]**: Mutual respect. Kael was the first Vigil soldier who saw Moss as a person rather than a target. Their friendship is quiet, practical, and important to both of them.
- **[[Revenant Yael]]**: Complicated solidarity. Yael leads the [[Remnants Court]] in [[Ashenmere]] and considers Moss a kindred spirit, but they disagree about method. Yael demands recognition through political force. Moss demonstrates it through daily living. Both approaches are needed.
- **[[Yara Thornbloom]]**: Quiet alliance. Yara consults Moss about the Cycle's effects on Remnant physiology, and Moss provides observations no one else can. They share the burden of knowing things they have not yet shared with their communities.
- **Pilgrim Thane**: Concern. The recently arrived Remnant from Ashenmere reminds Moss of themselves sixty years ago, desperate and hopeful. Moss wants to protect Thane from the disappointment that comes when hope is answered with impermanence.

## What They Want

Moss wants the restoration to continue. They want Ashbloom to endure as proof that the living and the dead can share a life. They would tell you this with quiet conviction and wet eyes.

What they need is to believe that what they feel during the restoration is real, not a mechanical side effect of divine energy interaction, but something meaningful. That the warmth is not just physics. That the tears matter. That being alive, even for a moment, is not just a chemical process but a statement about what it means to exist. The Thornwild's honesty, its refusal to pretend that life and death are separate things, is what gives Moss hope. If the Cycle breaks, that hope dies, and Moss is not certain they can survive losing it a second time.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Emotional anchor for Thornwild arcs. Moss provides the human (and post-human) stakes for the Cycle's destabilization. They are also the best source of information about what the Cycle does to Remnants, knowledge that becomes critical if the Second Silence's effects on the dead intensify.
>
> **Key Secret.** Moss has noticed the restoration weakening and has told no one. They are carrying this alone because they believe that if Ashbloom's Remnant population learns the truth, the despair will destroy the community before the Cycle does. This is a secret that the PCs can discover, and how they handle it will determine Ashbloom's future.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs stabilize the Cycle or find a way to preserve the restoration, Moss becomes a voice for Remnant rights across the continent, their example proving that the dead and the living can coexist meaningfully. If the restoration fails, Moss must find a reason to continue without the one thing that kept them going, and that struggle is one of the most emotionally demanding arcs in the campaign.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks slowly, softly, with deliberate pauses. Never raises their voice. Physical stillness broken by small, precise gestures. During the restoration, voice cracks and warms. During the death-phase, voice flattens. The contrast is the performance. Sample line: "You want to know what it feels like? Imagine you forgot what sunlight was. Now imagine remembering, for one minute, every day, and then forgetting again. Now imagine choosing to stay."
