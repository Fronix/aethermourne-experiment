# Sera Greywater

## Appearance

Late thirties, sharp-featured, with dark hair cut precisely at her jawline and watchful brown eyes that catalogue everything they see. She dresses in the Concord's official blue-grey but adds personal touches, a good leather belt, polished boots, a silver ring that belonged to her mother. She carries herself like someone who expects to be underestimated and enjoys proving people wrong. Her hands are always ink-stained from her ledgers, and she keeps a quill tucked behind her ear more often than not.

## Personality

Ambitious, clever, and restless in the way of someone who knows they are smarter than their station allows. Sera was given [[Bridgemere]] as a minor patronage position, a polite way of sidelining a junior official who asked too many questions. She turned it into a far more profitable chokepoint than the [[Concord of Tides]] expected, and she has been accumulating leverage ever since. She is not corrupt in the venal sense, she does not steal for personal enrichment. She is corrupt in the structural sense, using her position to gather influence and information that she should not have. She sees systems, not people, and she is very good at finding the pressure points.

## Backstory

Sera grew up in [[Tidewall]]'s middle districts, the daughter of a minor Concord clerk who died young, leaving her the silver ring and a deep understanding of how bureaucracy really works. She clawed her way into a junior administrative post through competence and persistence, but her talent for asking uncomfortable questions made her inconvenient. When the Toll-Warden position in [[Bridgemere]] opened, her superiors saw an opportunity to remove her from the capital without the appearance of punishment.

Sera saw the same opportunity from a different angle. [[Bridgemere]] sits at the junction of three Tidal Bridges, and every traveler, merchant, and message moving between the inner islands passes through her toll gates. She keeps meticulous official records and an even more meticulous set of private ledgers. Within two years, she knew the trade patterns of the Reach better than most Tidekeepers.

When cracks appeared in the northern bridge, Sera began investigating the Concord's bridge maintenance budget. She has been skimming small amounts from the tolls, not for herself, but to hire private investigators in [[Tidewall]]. She suspects the maintenance funds are being diverted. She is right, but the trail leads somewhere far more dangerous than simple embezzlement.

## Relationships

- **[[Cordell Raith]]**, Master of Secrets. Sera's investigation into diverted bridge funds will eventually lead to Raith's suppression of infrastructure reports. This makes her both a potential ally to the PCs and a target Raith cannot afford to ignore.
- **Dalla Tideborn**, innkeeper of the Three Arches in Bridgemere. The two women have an uneasy alliance built on exchanged information. Dalla provides rumors from travelers; Sera provides protection from Concord tax audits.
- **Osric Flint**, [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] metalworker studying the bridge cracks. Sera watches him closely, aware that his findings could either validate her suspicions or render them irrelevant. She has not yet approached him directly.
- **[[Jessamine Kole]]**, Master of Coin. Sera admires Kole's competence from a distance and models her own ambitions on the older woman's career. She does not yet realize that Kole would view her investigation as a threat to fiscal stability.

## What They Want

A seat in [[Tidewall]]'s political structure. [[Bridgemere]] is a stepping stone, and the bridge crisis is an opportunity to demonstrate her value, or to expose the rot in the Concord and ride the consequences upward. Beneath the ambition is a genuine anger at institutional incompetence, the belief that the people running the Reach are failing it, and that she could do better.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Sera is a potential ally who can provide the PCs with financial intelligence and a thread into the Concord's corruption. Her investigation into diverted bridge funds leads to [[Cordell Raith]]'s suppression activities, and through them, to the [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]]'s front operations.
>
> **Key Secret.** The bridge maintenance funds are being routed through a shell company connected to [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] front operations. The Concord officials involved do not know this, they believe they are simply redirecting money to military spending. Sera has not yet traced the full chain, but she is close.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs support her investigation, Sera becomes a valuable intelligence asset and potential political reformer. If Raith discovers her activities before the PCs intervene, she will be quietly reassigned, discredited, or worse. She is playing a dangerous game with insufficient protection.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks with careful precision, choosing words the way she chooses numbers, for maximum effect with minimum waste. Tilts her head slightly when she is evaluating someone. Taps her mother's silver ring against hard surfaces when thinking. Never asks a question she does not already suspect the answer to. *"The tolls are accurate to the last copper. The budget is not. I find that interesting, don't you?"*
