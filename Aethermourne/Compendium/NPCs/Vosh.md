## Appearance

Vosh is tall, six and a half feet, with the kind of stillness that makes you forget they are in the room until they move. Their skin is deep umber with faint, geometric patterns visible beneath the surface, not tattoos but something intrinsic, as if their flesh carries an echo of Primordial structure. Their eyes are black, sclera and all, reflecting light like polished obsidian. When they blink, it is slow, deliberate, the movement of someone conserving energy over lifetimes.

They wear simple, layered robes of dark gray and charcoal, cut in a style unfamiliar to the known continent. No ornamentation. No weapons visible. Their hair is long, black, and braided with small crystalline beads that hum faintly when they move, a sound just at the edge of hearing. The beads are Primordial resonators, tools of their trade.

Vosh does not gesture. They do not fidget. When they stand, they could be a statue. When they sit, they are a shadow in the corner. Their presence is *heavy*, not oppressive, but undeniable. You feel watched. You are.

---

## Personality

Vosh is patient. Not in the way mortals are patient, waiting for something to happen, but in the way stone is patient, existing without urgency. They observe. They record. They report. Emotion, if they feel it, is filtered through layers of discipline so thick that nothing shows.

They speak rarely and only when necessary. Their voice is low, resonant, and utterly without inflection. They choose words with surgical precision, as if each syllable has weight and cost. They never repeat themselves. If you did not understand the first time, that is your failure, not theirs.

Vosh is not cruel. They are not kind. They are *functional*. They have a role: monitor the outlanders, assess the threat, and determine whether [[Tidecross]] can be allowed to continue existing. Every interaction they have is data collection. Every question they ask is an evaluation. You are a problem to be solved, and Vosh is gathering the variables.

Beneath the stillness, there is something else. Not hostility, not warmth, curiosity. Vosh is genuinely interested in the outlanders, not as threats but as a phenomenon. They have never encountered a culture that builds on the corpses of its gods. They find it horrifying and fascinating in equal measure.

## Backstory

Vosh's true name is not Vosh. That is an outlander simplification of a longer designation in the Oldborn language, something closer to "The One Who Watches the Threshold Between Known and Feared." They accepted the shortened name because insisting on accuracy would waste time better spent observing.

They were born in [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]], trained from childhood in the discipline of the **Silence-Watchers**, the order that maintains [[The Primordia]]'s observation posts. Silence-Watchers are taught to perceive without interference, to witness without judgment, to endure environments that break lesser minds. Vosh excelled. They spent eight years stationed at the edge of the Shimmer, the expanding chaos-zone in [[The Primordia]], and returned sane. Barely.

When the outlanders began arriving on [[The Veilshore]], the Oldborn council needed observers who could tolerate prolonged exposure to divine corruption without succumbing. Vosh volunteered. They have been stationed in [[Tidecross]] for two years, attending negotiations, cataloging behaviors, and reporting everything back to the Kingdoms.

The Oldborn do not trust the outlanders. Vosh's reports determine how much longer the outlanders will be tolerated before action is taken.

## Relationships

- **[[Captain Erys Vane]]** (Tidecross Harbormaster), Professional respect. Vosh finds Erys competent, pragmatic, and less corrupted by divine taint than most outlanders. They communicate in brief, efficient exchanges. Erys is the only outlander Vosh has warned directly when danger approached. This is not kindness. It is recognition of a functional tool worth preserving.

- **[[Prior Cael Drantine]]** (Ashen Chapel Lead), Observed threat. Vosh considers Cael dangerous, not because he is violent but because he spreads ideological corruption. The worship of dead gods is a cognitive plague, and Cael is a willing vector. Vosh has recommended his removal. The council has not yet acted. Vosh is patient.

- **[[Salice]]** (Smuggler Queen), Wary neutrality. Salice treats the Oldborn with respect, and respect is rare among outlanders. Vosh has observed her long enough to conclude she operates outside outlander power structures, which makes her less predictable but also less dangerous to the Kingdoms. They have spoken three times. Each conversation was brief. Each was informative.

- **The Oldborn Council**, Absolute loyalty. Vosh's purpose is defined by the council. They serve without question, not out of blind obedience but because they understand the necessity. The outlanders are a threat. The threat must be quantified. Vosh is the instrument of that quantification.

- **The Land Itself**, Symbiotic connection. Vosh hears [[The Veilshore]]'s Primordial whispers. The black sand speaks to them. The Fernwald recognizes them. This is not magic in the outlander sense. It is attunement, the result of years spent in communion with [[The Gyre]]'s substrate. Vosh knows when the land is disturbed. They feel it like pressure against their skin.

---

## What They Want

**Surface Goal:** Observe the outlanders. Assess the threat they pose to [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]]. Report findings to the council.

**Deeper Need:** Vosh wants to understand why the outlanders built their civilization on divine corpses. Not to condemn them, to *understand*. The Oldborn and the outlanders are fundamentally incompatible worldviews occupying the same space. Vosh believes understanding may reveal a path to coexistence. They do not know if they believe this because it is logical or because two years of exposure to outlander culture has begun to erode their Silence-Watcher conditioning.

They will never admit the latter possibility. Not even to themselves.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role:** Vosh is [[Tidecross]]'s living timer. As long as they remain, the Oldborn are still observing, still gathering data, still open to the possibility that the outlanders are not an immediate existential threat. If Vosh leaves, disappears, or sends a final report, the Oldborn strike.
>
> **Key Secret:** Vosh is not one observer. "Vosh" is a role rotated among multiple Silence-Watchers. The person the players meet in Session 10 may not be the same person they met in Session 5. The Oldborn do this to prevent individual observers from becoming compromised by prolonged exposure to outlander culture. The current Vosh is the third. They have been in place for four months. They have six months remaining before rotation. If the players have not convinced them the outlanders are worth tolerating by then, the next Vosh will be less patient.
>
> **Arc Trajectory:** Vosh can become an ally if the players demonstrate respect for the land, understanding of Primordial forces, and willingness to act against outlander exploitation. If the players prove themselves, Vosh begins feeding them information, small things at first, then critical intelligence that saves lives. If the players fail, Vosh vanishes one night. Three days later, the Oldborn attack.
>
> **If the PCs Do Nothing:** Vosh completes their observation cycle, files a final report concluding that the outlanders are irredeemable threats, and departs [[Tidecross]]. The report triggers a coordinated Oldborn response: the Fernwald closes, cutting off inland access; the black sand rises, consuming outlander structures; and [[The Primordia]]'s Primordial forces are *allowed* to breach containment, flooding [[The Veilshore]] with elemental chaos. [[Tidecross]] does not burn. It dissolves. The barrier between continents re-seals, this time permanently.
>
> **Roleplay Notes:** Vosh speaks in flat, emotionless tones. No contractions. No filler words. Long pauses between sentences. They make eye contact without blinking. When listening, they are utterly still, absorbing every word. Their tells: the crystalline beads in their hair hum louder when they are agitated (though their expression never changes), and they touch the ground when uncertain, grounding themselves in the land's Primordial resonance. Sample line: *"You carry the stain of Solvaen's marrow in your bones. The land recoils from you. This is not accusation. It is observation. Whether you choose to address it determines whether you may remain."*
