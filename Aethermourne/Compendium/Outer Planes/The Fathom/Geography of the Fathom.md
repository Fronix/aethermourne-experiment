# Geography of the Fathom

*This article details the internal structure and regions of [[The Fathom]]. For an overview of the Fathom and its role in creation, see the main article.*

---

The Fathom is not featureless. It has structure, but structure that operates by rules incompatible with creation's physics. Mortal language fails here more completely than it does when describing [[The Gyre]], because the Gyre at least shares a common origin with structured reality. The Fathom does not. Its "geography" is described below in metaphors that are, at best, useful approximations of something that resists approximation.

## The Near Fathom

The region closest to the Boundary. Most influenced by creation's proximity. The Near Fathom is where [[The Primordials#Orvhal|Orvhal]]'s observation is densest, where The Unremembered press closest, where the alien reality is most affected by the structured reality next door. The Near Fathom is "almost understandable," alien but with echoes of familiar principles. Deep-sea divers and [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]] who perceive the Fathom are perceiving the Near Fathom, and even that is enough to drive many of them to silence or madness.

The Near Fathom is the transition zone. Its rules are contaminated by creation's proximity, creating a region where alien principles and structured physics overlap in unstable, unpredictable ways. The wrongness of deep-sea life, the impossible geometries in Trench creatures, the colors without names on contaminated shells, these originate in the Near Fathom, where alien nature has been filtered through the thinnest remaining membrane of the Boundary into something that mortal senses can almost, but not quite, process.

> [!info] Perceiving the Near Fathom
> Those who have perceived the Near Fathom describe it in terms that are contradictory but consistent across sources: "organized emptiness," "structure without pattern," "meaning without content." The Tidal Sorcerers of [[The Hollowed Reach]] who describe "touching the floor of the world" are sensing the Near Fathom through the thinning membrane at extreme ocean depth. The [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]] who perceive it through darkened patches in the sky describe an identical sensation approached from the opposite direction: a vast, structured presence that makes no sense, but makes no sense in a very specific, very consistent way.

## The Deep Fathom

Further from creation, where alien rules dominate completely. The Deep Fathom is genuinely unknowable by mortal minds. No perception from inside creation has ever reached it. It may contain entire realities operating on principles that have no equivalent in creation. It may contain nothing. It may contain something so vast that "contain" is the wrong word. The Deep Fathom is where the Fathom keeps its secrets.

[[The Twelve#Aelindra|Aelindra]], from her vantage on the Boundary, can perceive deeper into the Fathom than any other being associated with creation. What she has seen has contributed to her paralysis. She has described the Deep Fathom, in the rare moments she communicates at all, in a single phrase: "It continues." The phrase is not illuminating, but the despair with which she says it suggests that "continues" means something far more terrible than its mundane usage implies.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Deep Fathom should never be described in detail. Its narrative power comes from being the thing that terrifies a goddess. If you need to evoke it, use Aelindra's reactions rather than direct description: she looks away, she falls silent, she weeps without explanation. The Deep Fathom is the question that has no answer, the door you should not open, the thought you cannot finish.

## The Convergence Zone

Where [[The Primordials#The Convergence|The Convergence]] presses against the Boundary. A moving region of intensified pressure that erodes the Boundary faster than anywhere else. The Convergence is the most active and directed force in the Fathom, a phenomenon (or entity, or process) that moves along the Boundary's exterior, testing it, pressing against it, wearing it down.

[[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]] have begun mapping its path by tracking which stars go dark, though they do not yet understand what they are tracking. They see a pattern in the stellar disappearances, a progression that follows a discernible route across the celestial sphere, and they have logged it as an astronomical anomaly without grasping its cause. The Convergence moves, and where it passes, stars fail.

The Convergence Zone is currently pressing against the Boundary near the Abyssal Trench. This puts [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s prison at the intersection of two weakening forces: her own escape attempts from inside, and the Convergence's pressure from outside. Whether this convergence is coincidental, causal, or deliberately coordinated is unknown. The implications of deliberate coordination, that something in the Fathom is working in concert with Serith, or that Serith's activity is attracting the Convergence, are deeply alarming.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Convergence Zone is the Fathom's most direct threat to creation's integrity. Use it sparingly but deliberately:
>
> - **Act II-III**: Star-Readers note that the pattern of stellar disappearances is not random but follows a path. They argue about what this means. The path is heading toward the region above the Abyssal Trench.
> - **Act III-IV**: The Convergence Zone reaches the Trench. Stellar disappearances accelerate. The Boundary thins dramatically over Serith's prison. Black Tides intensify. The Lantern-Keepers' Divers report that the deep ocean has become significantly more alien, faster than anyone predicted.
> - **Act V**: The intersection of Serith's internal pressure and the Convergence's external pressure creates a crisis point. The Boundary may breach, not as a pinhole but as a collapse. This is the cosmological dimension of the campaign's climax.
