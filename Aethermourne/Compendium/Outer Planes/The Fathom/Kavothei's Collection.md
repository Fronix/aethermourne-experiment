# Kavothei's Collection

*This article details the archive of creation's lost things held by [[The Primordials#Kavothei|Kavothei]] within [[The Fathom]]. For an overview of the Fathom itself, see the main article.*

---

[[The Primordials#Kavothei|Kavothei]], the Fathom entity that absorbs and preserves what drifts through cracks in the Boundary, has been collecting for two thousand years. Every lost thing, every forgotten name, every destroyed text that leaked through micro-fractures in the Boundary's structure has been absorbed, preserved in alien amber, held in a state of perfect, incomprehensible archive.

Kavothei does not understand what it has. It does not sort, categorize, or value its collection. It simply absorbs, preserves, and continues. It is a hoarder of drift, a net that catches everything the Boundary lets slip. The collection is vast, spanning two millennia of lost things from across all of creation.

Scholars who learn of Kavothei's existence will be obsessed. Every lost text, every destroyed artifact, every forgotten name is out there, preserved by an alien entity that does not understand what it has and does not care. The temptation to reach Kavothei is immense. The cost is unknown, but given that reaching Kavothei requires crossing the Boundary into the Fathom, it is likely to be catastrophic.

## What Kavothei Has

The following items are known or theorized to exist within Kavothei's collection, based on fragmentary evidence and scholarly extrapolation:

1. **[[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas]]'s unfiltered prophecies**: The raw, unedited visions of the Loom of Fate, before Orenthas shaped them into comprehensible form. These would contain truths too vast and terrible for mortal minds, prophecies that the god of fate himself chose to redact.
2. **The Empyrean's blueprints**: The architectural principles by which [[The Twelve]] built the divine realm. These would contain the foundational rules of creation itself, the source code of structured reality.
3. **[[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]]'s memories, pre-shattering**: The goddess's complete consciousness from before she broke apart, including her full understanding of the [[Cosmology and Magic#Ley Lines and the Aetheric Web|Aetheric Web]], [[The Gyre]], and the Primordials. This alone would be worth any price.
4. **The true names of the Gyre-Born**: The identities of the proto-entities that existed [[Before the Pattern|before the pattern]], knowledge that could reveal what creation was before the gods refined it.
5. **An echo of [[The Twelve#Belara|Belara]]'s stolen essence**: When [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] consumed Belara's power, fragments of her nature leaked through the Boundary. Kavothei holds a copy, an impression, a ghost of the Wild goddess's essence in its original form.
6. **The original Empyrean Accord text**: The compact between all Twelve gods that established the rules of divine governance. The version known to mortal scholars is a summary. The original contains clauses that the gods never shared.
7. **Songs from the last night before the Theomachis**: Music, conversation, laughter from the final hours before the gods went to war. The emotional record of a divine family on the eve of its destruction.
8. **The names of every mortal killed in the Theomachis**: Every single one. Kavothei absorbed the death-echoes as they leaked through the Boundary in the cataclysm's aftermath.
9. **[[The Twelve#Vorrhyn|Vorrhyn]]'s final words**: What the god of sky and freedom said in the instant before annihilation. Lost to creation when he was unmade. Preserved, somewhere, in Kavothei's collection.
10. **Pre-Twelve star charts**: Maps of the celestial sphere from before [[The Twelve#Aelindra|Aelindra]] encoded her knowledge into the stars. These would show the Boundary's original architecture, a baseline against which its current degradation could be measured.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Kavothei's collection is a narrative treasure chest. Use it as motivation for NPCs, as a quest hook, or as the ultimate reward for players who find a way to reach the Fathom.
>
> Key considerations:
> - **Reaching Kavothei requires crossing the Boundary.** This is not a thing mortals can do safely, if at all. The cost should be enormous.
> - **Kavothei does not trade.** It absorbs. Anyone who reaches it must find a way to extract what they need from an entity that preserves everything and releases nothing.
> - **The knowledge within is dangerous.** Orenthas's unfiltered prophecies could drive a mortal mad. The Empyrean's blueprints could give someone the power to remake reality. Nethys's pre-shattering memories could restore the Aetheric Web, or could overwhelm and destroy whoever receives them.
> - **[[Lysara Shimmerborn]] would kill for this knowledge.** So would many others. The mere rumor of Kavothei's existence could trigger a crisis among Aethermourne's scholars and power-brokers.
>
> The collection is best introduced gradually: a hint in Act II, confirmation in Act III, a desperate plan to reach it in Act IV-V. Whether the players actually attempt contact with Kavothei depends on how desperate they become.
