# Ecology of the Gyre

*This article details the internal structure and phenomena of [[The Gyre]]. For an overview of the Gyre's nature and cosmological position, see the main article.*

---

The Gyre is not featureless chaos. It has patterns, regions, and phenomena, even if "region" is a crude metaphor for something that predates spatial structure. What follows is a model assembled from Maelstrom observations, ley-confluence visions, and the theoretical work of [[Lysara Shimmerborn]] and the [[The Confluence Council|Confluence Council]].

## The Shallows

The uppermost layer of the Gyre, closest to the membrane of structured reality. This is where the [[Cosmology and Magic#Ley Lines and the Aetheric Web|Aetheric Web]]'s filtration draws from. The Shallows are the most "structured" part of the Gyre, influenced by proximity to creation above. Raw potential here has a slight tendency toward coherence, like water that is almost about to freeze. Maelstrom-spawned creatures originate from the Shallows, pulled upward through the breach and violently crystallized by contact with structured reality's rules.

The Shallows are where [[The Primordials#Ur-Ghael|Ur-Ghael]]'s pattern-tendency is most visible, because the proximity of creation above reinforces the lean toward structure. Potential here almost-forms constantly, flickering in and out of recognizable shapes before dissolving back. It is a liminal space, neither raw chaos nor true creation, a twilight zone of becoming.

## The Currents

Deeper than the Shallows, where [[The Primordials#Ur-Ghael|Ur-Ghael]]'s pattern-tendency and [[The Primordials#Vorthaan|Vorthaan]]'s dissolution play out most visibly. Vast streams of almost-becoming and un-becoming flow through the Currents, potential that briefly crystallizes into something recognizable before dissolving back. Scholars who have glimpsed the Currents through the [[The Shattered Edge|Maelstrom]] describe seeing ghost-images of things that almost exist: cities that never were, creatures that were never born, geometries that lasted for an instant before Vorthaan's influence pulled them apart.

The Currents are where the Gyre's eternal rhythm is most apparent. [[The Primordials#Ur-Ghael|Ur-Ghael]] leans potential toward pattern. [[The Primordials#Vorthaan|Vorthaan]] dissolves it back. The result is a ceaseless flow of near-creation, a river of might-have-beens that churns through the substrate like weather through an atmosphere. The Currents do not move in a direction, because direction implies spatial structure. They move in a *tendency*, toward and away from coherence simultaneously.

> [!quote] From the research notes of [[Lysara Shimmerborn]]
> "I have seen the edge of the Currents. Twice. Both times through the Maelstrom's core, during a surge. I saw a city. It had towers, streets, a harbor. It was beautiful and it lasted for perhaps a heartbeat before it unmade itself. I do not know if I saw a memory of something that existed before the gods, or a preview of something that might exist after them. The distinction may not matter."

## The Deep Churn

The furthest depths of the Gyre, where even the Primordials' distinct natures blur together. This is where [[The Primordials#Yith|Yith]] dreams, where [[The Primordials#Thaelun|Thaelun]]'s multiplicity is densest, where the difference between one Primordial and another becomes meaningless. The Deep Churn is not a place. It is the condition of absolute undifferentiation, potential so raw that even the concepts of "pattern" and "dissolution" have not yet separated from one another.

No mortal perception has ever reached the Deep Churn. [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]] may have glimpsed it in life. What she saw is one of the pieces of knowledge lost when she shattered. Her fragments sometimes transmit impressions that scholars believe originate from memories of the Deep Churn, sensory data so alien that even a divine mind struggled to process it. These transmissions are the most disorienting of all Nethys-fragment visions, and have driven more than one mage permanently mad.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Deep Churn is deliberately unknowable. Do not define it further than this. Its power as a story element comes from its absolute mystery. If players ask what is at the bottom of the Gyre, the honest answer is: no one knows, and knowing might not be survivable.
>
> If you need the Deep Churn for a dramatic moment, describe it through sensation rather than image: a feeling of infinite density, of everything pressing against everything else, of meaning so concentrated it becomes meaningless. The Deep Churn is not dark. It is not light. It is the state before the distinction between dark and light existed.

## Gyre Fauna

The Gyre produces quasi-entities constantly, brief crystallizations of potential that resemble life without being life. They form, exist for unmeasurable spans, and dissolve. Some are tiny, motes of almost-light, fragments of nearly-sound. Some are vast, currents of near-consciousness that flow through the Shallows like whales through an ocean. None are permanent. None are aware. They are the Gyre dreaming of structure, and the dreams never quite resolve.

But occasionally, one of these quasi-entities drifts close to the [[The Shattered Edge|Maelstrom]] breach and is pulled through into structured reality, where it suddenly, violently solidifies into something that obeys creation's rules imperfectly. These are Maelstrom-spawn, the impossible creatures that emerge from the wound in the [[The Shattered Edge]]. They are not invaders. They are accidents, potential that stumbled into structure and was forced to become *something* without any guidance about what.

This explains the characteristic features of Maelstrom-spawn: their wrongness, their instability, their tendency to partially dissolve or shift form. They are structures that were never properly designed, raw material that was forced into shape by creation's ambient rules rather than by divine intent. Some stabilize over time, adapting to structured reality. Most do not, breaking down within hours or days as the imperfect structure fails to sustain itself.

> [!info] Maelstrom-Spawn Classification
> The [[The Last Certainty|Last Certainty]] has attempted to classify Maelstrom-spawn by their apparent origin layer:
>
> - **Shallow-spawn**: Most common. Partially coherent, recognizable as almost-creatures. Often resemble distorted versions of existing animals or plants. Relatively stable (days to weeks).
> - **Current-spawn**: Rare. More alien, less recognizable. May exhibit properties of multiple forms simultaneously. Unstable (hours to days).
> - **Deep-spawn**: Theoretical. No confirmed sighting. If a quasi-entity from the Deep Churn ever reached the breach, the result would likely be catastrophic, a solidification of such concentrated potential that it could warp structured reality around itself.
