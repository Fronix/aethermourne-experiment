# The Iron Descent

> [!quote] From the journal of Commander Aldric Solhane, recovered from the Hollowdeep perimeter, 847 AS
> "We did not descend. We were *invited*. The iron sang to us, a sound like ambition given voice, and we followed it down because we wanted to, because it promised us everything we had ever wanted. That is the cruelty of the place. It does not drag you in. It makes you walk."

---

## Overview

> [!info]
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Origin** | The surviving divine domain of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]], God of War and Ambition |
> | **Status** | Active, self-sustaining, expanding |
> | **Anchor Point** | The Heart of Iron beneath [[The Ashen Dominion#Ostivaar|Ostivaar]] |
> | **Access** | Kaevroth fragments, Hollowdeep crossover, Veil Unbound ritual |
> | **Levels** | 9 |
> | **Nature** | Predatory pocket plane within the [[The Gyre\|Shattered Empyrean]] |

When [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] was destroyed during the Theomachis, his iron body shattered and scattered across the continent. His fragments embedded themselves in the earth of every region, radiating war-essence and breeding conflict for two thousand years. But Kaevroth's body was not all that survived. His domain within the Empyrean, the divine seat from which he had ruled ambition and conquest, did not collapse the way the other gods' domains did. It folded inward.

The reason is simple and terrible: Kaevroth's domain was saturated with hunger. Every other divine domain was defined by what it *was*, order, death, knowledge, beauty. Kaevroth's domain was defined by what it *wanted*. That wanting did not require a god to sustain it. Ambition is self-perpetuating. The domain fed on itself, compressed, and survived, a pocket plane of pure aspiration twisted into nine descending layers of increasingly refined cruelty.

The Iron Descent is the Shattered Empyrean's most intact surviving structure, more coherent than [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s broken afterlife within his skull, more organized than the scattered remnants of any other divine domain. Where Morrhael's Domain is a wound that leaks, the Descent is a trap that *hunts*. It reaches through Kaevroth's scattered fragments like fingers through keyholes, whispering to the ambitious, the hungry, the driven. It does not need to be found. It finds you.

The nine levels of the Descent correspond to the nine days of battle between Kaevroth and [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]], the war-god's final and greatest conquest. Each level distills a different facet of ambition, from the seductive promise of greatness at the Ashfield to the annihilating truth at the Heart. Those who enter descend not through physical space but through the architecture of their own desires, each level stripping away another layer of self-deception until nothing remains but the raw, screaming core of what they truly want.

## Cosmological Position

> [!info] Planar Location
> The Iron Descent exists as a self-contained pocket within the Shattered Empyrean, anchored to the mortal world through Kaevroth's scattered iron fragments. It is not a place in the conventional sense. It is a *condition*, a state of reality that activates when mortal ambition resonates with divine war-essence at sufficient intensity.
>
> The primary anchor is the **Heart of Iron**, Kaevroth's largest surviving fragment, buried beneath [[The Ashen Dominion#Ostivaar|Ostivaar]]. This fragment serves as Level 9's foundation, the deepest and most potent connection between the Descent and the mortal world. The Heart's presence beneath the Dominion's capital is not coincidence. The Descent *chose* that location, drawn to the density of mortal ambition concentrated in a seat of empire.
>
> A crossover point exists with [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s Domain via the Hollowdeep at Level 6, where the Iron Descent's architecture of conquest intersects with the broken afterlife's architecture of death. This junction is unstable, violent, and the source of the strange iron-laced undead that occasionally emerge from the Hollowdeep's deepest tunnels.
>
> Lesser anchor points exist wherever Kaevroth fragments are found: in the [[The Ironmarch|Ironmarch]], within [[The Ember Guild]]'s sealed vaults, and in locations yet undiscovered. Each fragment is a potential doorway, though most are too small to permit full passage. They leak influence instead, bleeding war-essence and ambition into their surroundings.

## The Nine Levels

The Descent is not a physical structure with floors and staircases. Each level is a *thesis* about ambition, a complete environment that embodies one facet of the drive to become more. Descending does not mean going deeper. It means understanding more, and being understood in return.

| Level | Name | Theme | City | Danger |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | The Ashfield | Seduction of ambition | Rustgate | Low |
| 2 | The Proving Grounds | Violence without purpose | The Crucible of Names | Moderate |
| 3 | The Iron Market | Ambition as transaction | Greedhollow | Moderate |
| 4 | The Forge Eternal | Creation without rest | The Foundry | High |
| 5 | The Hall of Mirrors | Self-deception | Gloryholme | High |
| 6 | The Conquest | Futility of empire | The Thousand Thrones | Severe |
| 7 | The Iron Court | Power without meaning | Ironhold | Severe |
| 8 | The Consuming | Loss of self | The Maw | Extreme |
| 9 | The Heart | The truth of ambition | The Silence | Absolute |

Each level has a settlement, a concentration point where the Descent's trapped souls congregate. These are not true cities. They are *performances* of civilization, populated by the ambitious dead and the still-living who wandered too deep. The residents of each level's city believe they are thriving. They are not.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Nine Levels are deliberately structured to mirror a character arc of corruption. A PC entering the Descent will be tempted at each level by something that speaks to their specific ambitions. The Descent is perceptive. It reads desire the way a predator reads weakness.
>
> Level 1-3 are survivable for a prepared party. Levels 4-6 require significant willpower and planning. Levels 7-9 should be treated as endgame content, with each session spent there carrying permanent mechanical consequences.
>
> The level structure also functions as a narrative countdown: the deeper the PCs go, the more they learn about Kaevroth's nature, and the less certain they become about their own motivations for being there.

## Entry

The Iron Descent does not have a front door. It has *invitations*.

The most common method of entry is proximity to a Kaevroth fragment during a moment of intense personal ambition. A general planning a campaign, a merchant scheming to ruin a rival, a scholar desperate for a breakthrough, any mortal whose desire blazes hot enough can cause a nearby fragment to resonate. The world around them shifts. Iron bleeds through stone. The air tastes of metal and old smoke. They find themselves on the Ashfield, and the fragment that brought them is nowhere to be seen. These entries are catastrophic and typically one-way. The fragment does not follow the traveler in. It merely opens the door.

The Heart of Iron beneath [[The Ashen Dominion#Ostivaar|Ostivaar]] is the primary stable access point, though no living mortal has knowingly used it. The Heart sits at Level 9, meaning anyone who enters through it arrives at the Descent's deepest and most dangerous layer. The Hollowdeep connection at Level 6 is an accidental junction, a place where Kaevroth's domain and Morrhael's domain press against each other through the fractured Empyrean. The [[The Ashen Vigil|Ashen Vigil]] has encountered this crossing without understanding what it was, and lost soldiers to it. Veil Unbound rituals, the dangerous practice of tearing temporary holes in planar boundaries, can theoretically target the Descent, but the plane's predatory nature means such openings tend to pull the ritualist in rather than allowing controlled observation.

## Exit

Leaving the Iron Descent is the cruelest part of its design.

The Descent feeds on ambition. The desire to escape *is* ambition, a goal, a hunger, a refusal to accept one's current state. The harder a soul strains toward freedom, the more the Descent drinks from them, and the deeper they sink. This is not a metaphor. The plane responds to intent. A prisoner who schemes and plots and burns with the need to leave will find themselves descending through levels they never meant to reach. The Descent does not punish escape attempts. It *encourages* them, because every failed attempt feeds it more.

There are four known methods of departure, each terrible in its own way. **The Surrender** requires the traveler to genuinely, completely abandon all desire, not merely suppress it, but release it entirely. This is functionally impossible for most mortals, as the will to survive is itself a form of ambition. Those rare souls who achieve it simply cease to be present in the Descent, reappearing in the mortal world with no memory of how they returned and a profound, lasting emptiness where their drive once lived. **The Mercy Gate** exists at Level 9, a hairline fracture in the Heart of Iron through which a faint warmth bleeds, the imprisoned fragment of [[The Twelve#Belara|Belara]]'s stolen essence, her capacity for hope. A traveler who reaches the Heart and finds the Gate can pass through it, but only by accepting that their ambition was never the point. The Gate does not reward the strong. It opens for the broken. **Morrhaelite severance**, the forcible cutting of a soul's connection to the Descent using death-magic, is theoretically possible at Level 6's crossover point. The reaction is violent: the Descent treats severance as an attack and responds with concentrated war-essence. Survivors of this method are rare and scarred. **The Iron Court's Embassy** at Level 7 offers passage out in exchange for a price determined by the Court's rulers, entities of ancient ambition who have been in the Descent so long they have become part of its architecture. The price is always the same in principle, though it varies in form: something you are not willing to give.

## The Descent's Hunger

The Iron Descent is alive in the way a forest fire is alive, not conscious, not deliberate, but relentlessly consuming. It feeds on ambition the way flame feeds on air, drawing in desire and converting it into the substance of itself. Every soul that enters the Descent makes it marginally larger, marginally more real, marginally more capable of reaching through its fragments into the mortal world. It has been feeding for two thousand years. It is patient, and it is growing.

The mechanism of feeding is called the **Iron Tithe**, though no one in the Descent named it that. For every day spent within the Descent's levels, the plane takes something from the visitor. The first things to go are memories, small ones at first: the name of a childhood friend, the taste of a favorite meal, the sound of a loved one's voice. Then desires begin to erode, not the central ambition that brought the visitor in, never that, but the smaller wants that make a person human. The desire for comfort. The desire for companionship. The desire for rest. Finally, identity itself begins to dissolve, the boundaries between self and Descent growing thin until the visitor cannot remember whether they entered the plane or were always part of it. The Descent does not want to kill. Death releases a soul, sends it tumbling toward Morrhael's broken domain. The Descent wants to *keep* you, to add your ambition to its own, to make you another voice in the iron chorus that sings beneath [[The Ashen Dominion#Ostivaar|Ostivaar]].

## History

The Iron Descent formed in the immediate aftermath of the Theomachis, during Year 2 of the Age of Silence. When Kaevroth fell on the ninth day of his battle with [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]], his iron body shattered and his divine domain began to collapse, as every other god's domain was collapsing. But where other domains dispersed into the fractured Empyrean, Kaevroth's folded inward, compressed by the weight of its own hunger. The nine days of the Solvaen-Kaevroth battle encoded themselves into the domain's structure, each day of combat becoming a level, each level preserving the specific quality of ambition that Kaevroth embodied on that day of fighting. The Ashfield is the first day's confidence. The Heart is the ninth day's truth.

For two millennia, the Descent has been quietly shaping the mortal world through its fragment-anchors. Wars that should have ended dragged on. Rivalries that should have faded intensified. The [[The Ironmarch|Ironmarch]]'s culture of endless conflict, the [[The Ashen Dominion|Ashen Dominion]]'s relentless expansionism, the political ruthlessness that defines [[The Ashen Dominion#Ostivaar|Ostivaar]], all of these carry the Descent's fingerprints. The plane does not cause these things directly. It amplifies what is already there, turning natural ambition into something consuming.

The most significant mortal contact with the Descent occurred in 1203 AS, when a [[The Star-Readers|Star-Reader]] expedition investigating fragment resonance patterns inadvertently breached Level 1. Of the twelve scholars who entered, three returned. Their accounts, fragmentary and contradictory, form the basis of most mortal knowledge about the Descent's existence. The full account is preserved in [[Origins of the Iron Descent]].

## Mortal Knowledge

Almost no one in Aethermourne knows the Iron Descent exists. The concept of a surviving divine domain, intact, predatory, and growing, contradicts the established theological understanding that the gods' domains collapsed with the Empyrean. Those few who suspect the truth guard their knowledge carefully, aware that speaking too loudly about a plane that feeds on ambition tends to attract its attention.

The [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] suspects the most. Their work tracking Kaevroth fragments has revealed patterns that cannot be explained by simple war-essence radiation: fragments that resonate in synchrony across vast distances, ambient aggression spikes that correspond to no local cause, and the disturbing tendency of fragment-keepers to develop identical recurring dreams of iron corridors and descending stairways. Guildmaster [[Torren Hearthforge]] has compiled a classified dossier on these anomalies, though even he has not connected them to a coherent picture. The [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]] possess the 1203 AS expedition accounts, sealed in their deepest archives and accessible only to senior readers. The [[The Ashen Vigil|Ashen Vigil]] has encountered the Level 6 crossover through the Hollowdeep without recognizing it for what it was, recording only that certain deep tunnels contain "iron-veined passages inconsistent with known geology" and produce undead with metallic growths fused to their bones.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Implications.** The Iron Descent is a slow-burn revelation. Early sessions should include fragment anomalies as atmospheric detail, dreams of iron, unexplained aggression, Ember Guild concerns. The Descent itself should not become directly relevant until Act II at the earliest, when the Heart of Iron beneath Ostivaar enters the narrative.
>
> The key question for the campaign: is the Descent a symptom or a cause? Is it merely Kaevroth's echo, or has two thousand years of feeding made it something new, something that has outgrown its creator? The answer should depend on what the players discover and what they choose to believe.

> [!warning]- GM Only: Using the Iron Descent in Campaign
> **Pacing.** The Iron Descent is an optional deep-dive location. It can serve as a full multi-session arc (a descent through all nine levels), a single terrifying session (an accidental breach into Level 1 or 2), or a background threat that never requires direct engagement. The Descent is patient. It has been waiting for two thousand years. It does not need to be used immediately.
>
> **Act II Connection.** The Heart of Iron beneath [[The Ashen Dominion#Ostivaar|Ostivaar]] becomes narratively relevant when the PCs investigate the Dominion's power structures. The Hierarch does not know what lies beneath the capital, but the city's history of militarism, expansion, and crushing ambition makes terrible sense once the Heart is discovered. If the PCs learn about the Heart, they may choose to investigate, which could lead to an accidental or intentional entry into Level 9, the most dangerous possible starting point.
>
> **Act IV Connection.** The Hollowdeep crossover at Level 6 becomes relevant if the PCs explore the deep tunnels beneath [[The Pale Wastes]]. The junction between Kaevroth's domain and Morrhael's domain is unstable and produces hybrid horrors: undead animated by death-essence but driven by war-ambition, far more dangerous and purposeful than standard Hollowdeep revenants. This crossover also represents a potential route *into* the Descent for PCs who have been exploring the Hollowdeep.
>
> **Tone.** The Descent should never feel like a dungeon crawl. It is a psychological horror environment. Combat exists, but the true danger is the plane's influence on the PCs' minds and motivations. Every session spent in the Descent should include at least one moment where a PC is offered exactly what they want, and the offer is genuine. The Descent does not lie. It simply makes the truth unbearable.
>
> **The Belara Thread.** The Mercy Gate at Level 9, the crack where [[The Twelve#Belara|Belara]]'s stolen hope bleeds through, is potentially the most important discovery in the entire Descent. If the PCs reach it and understand what it is, they face a profound choice: crack the Heart of Iron to free Belara's essence (which would destabilize the Descent but flood the Dominion with hope), leave it sealed (preserving stability but condemning the essence to eternal imprisonment), or attempt to weaponize it (which is exactly the kind of ambition the Descent rewards and punishes simultaneously).
