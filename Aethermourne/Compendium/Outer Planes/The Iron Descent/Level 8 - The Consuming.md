## Description

The Consuming is [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s hunger made manifest, an entire level shaped by the act that defined his fall: the consumption of other gods' essence. Here, the boundaries between entities break down. Walls absorb shadows. Floors drink footprints. The air eats sound. Everything merges into everything else, slowly, inevitably, with a patience that makes it worse than violence.

Travelers feel it within minutes of arrival: a softening at the edges of themselves. Memories blur. Names become difficult to recall. The distinction between "I" and "the world around me" grows thin, like a wall of ice melting from both sides. Veterans of the Consuming describe it as the opposite of drowning, instead of water rushing in, you rush out, leaking into the iron and the silence and the hungry architecture until there is not enough of you left to remember what you were.

The landscape itself is in constant flux. Iron walls breathe, expanding and contracting like the ribs of some vast organism. Corridors merge and split without warning. A room that existed an hour ago may have been absorbed into the floor, its contents digested, its purpose forgotten. The ground is warm and faintly pulsing, and those who press their ears to it swear they can hear voices, thousands of them, speaking in unison, saying nothing.

Color does not survive here. Everything trends toward iron-gray. Bright clothing fades within hours. Skin loses its warmth, taking on a metallic sheen. Even fire burns gray in the Consuming, casting light without warmth, illumination without comfort. The level is not dark, but it is colorless in a way that feels like a wound.

## The City: The Maw

The Maw is a city that devours its own inhabitants. Its architecture is organic, breathing, swallowing, a labyrinth of iron passages that open and close like throats. Buildings merge and split on cycles that no one has successfully mapped. A tavern becomes a temple becomes a wall becomes a face frozen mid-scream in the iron. Streets consume themselves and reform in new configurations, carrying their residents with them like food through intestines.

The Maw's population exists in various stages of being consumed. New arrivals are still distinct, still recognizably individual, still capable of speech and independent thought. Those who have been here longer are halfway merged with the walls, their bodies partially absorbed into the iron, still alive, still conscious, but no longer entirely separate from the city. The longest residents are just faces in the metal, mouths that open and close, eyes that track movement, expressions that shift between resignation and something that might once have been terror but has been diluted by merger into something quieter.

Mother Amalgam, the closest thing the Maw has to a leader besides the Hollow King, maintains a network of "safe spaces," rooms that resist absorption longer than others. These spaces cost dearly to maintain, requiring constant sacrifice of identity-rich materials: names written on paper, portraits, personal effects, anything that carries a strong sense of individual selfhood. Mother Amalgam feeds these to the walls to keep them sated, buying her people hours or days of coherence.

## Notable Locations

**The Throat.** The primary passage from Level 7 into the Consuming, a long, narrowing corridor of iron that contracts rhythmically around travelers as they descend. The walls are warm and slick. The passage takes approximately one hour to traverse, and by the time travelers emerge into the Maw, they have already lost something, usually a minor memory, a childhood detail, the sound of a loved one's voice.

**The Hollow King's Court.** A vast chamber at the Maw's center where the Hollow King holds an audience with no one. The King sits on a throne that has partially absorbed him, his lower body merged with the iron, his torso still distinct, his face a mask of frozen ambition. He speaks in the voices of everyone he has consumed, cycling through accents, languages, and emotional registers mid-sentence. Approaching the throne is possible but not advised, the floor around it is hungry.

**Mother Amalgam's Refuge.** A cluster of rooms that Mother Amalgam has kept free of absorption through constant sacrifice. The walls here are covered in names, written in blood, charcoal, and rust, each one serving as a anchor against the Consuming. Residents surrender their names willingly, trading personal identity for the communal identity of "one who has not yet been consumed." It works, but the cost compounds.

**The Gallery of the Merged.** A long corridor where the walls are dense with absorbed faces, some ancient, some recent. The faces occasionally speak, offering fragments of memory, warnings, or pleas. Scholars believe the Gallery contains the absorbed remnants of every soul the Consuming has taken since the Theomachis. The oldest faces have been merged so long that they no longer remember being separate and speak only in the Consuming's own voice, a low hum that sounds like satisfaction.

**The Undigested's Cache.** A hidden pocket of resistance maintained by The Undigested, a small group of souls who have found ways to resist absorption through sheer force of identity. They hoard personal effects, repeat their own names like mantras, and scar their skin with identifying marks that the iron cannot easily absorb. Their hideout shifts location constantly, staying one step ahead of the Maw's digestion.

## Native Creatures

**Hollow Ones.** Souls who have been almost entirely consumed, retaining only the barest spark of individual consciousness within shells of living iron. They wander the Maw in humanoid shapes that are slightly wrong, limbs too long, faces too smooth, movements too fluid. They are drawn to strong identities the way moths are drawn to flame, pressing close, touching, trying to absorb what they have lost. Contact with a Hollow One accelerates the Consuming's effect on living creatures.

**Amalgam Horrors.** The result of multiple souls being consumed simultaneously and merged into a single entity. They are masses of fused iron and flesh, bearing multiple faces, limbs, and voices. Each component soul is still partially conscious, creating creatures that argue with themselves, fight their own limbs, and weep from one face while laughing from another. They are not deliberately aggressive, but their size and instability make them dangerous. When they collapse, they scatter into smaller fragments that begin consuming independently.

**Rust Walkers.** Creatures born from the Consuming itself, manifestations of the level's digestive process given ambulatory form. They appear as vaguely humanoid shapes of corroded iron, leaving trails of rust wherever they walk. Their touch accelerates decay and absorption in everything they contact: metal rusts, wood rots, flesh softens and begins to merge with the nearest iron surface. They do not hunt. They simply walk, and everything in their path is consumed.

## History

The Consuming was not always a level of dissolution. In the earliest days of the Descent, before the Theomachis, it was Kaevroth's inner sanctum, a place where the god processed the power he had accumulated, digesting conquered domains and integrating stolen strength into his own nature. It functioned as a divine stomach, transforming what Kaevroth consumed into fuel for his ambition. When he tore fragments from [[The Twelve#Belara|Belara]], [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]], and others during the war, this is where their essence was broken down and absorbed.

After Kaevroth's death, the Consuming did not stop. It simply lost its purpose, continuing to digest without direction, consuming everything within reach out of residual hunger. The souls trapped here are being processed by a system that no longer has a destination for what it produces. They are being consumed by nothing, for nothing, forever.

## Encounters & Hooks

1. **The Remnant's Warning.** The Remnant, a being who claims to be what remains of a god Kaevroth partially consumed during the Theomachis, approaches the party with fragmented intelligence about Level 9. Its information is reliable but delivered in a voice that shifts between divine authority and whimpering confusion, as the consumed deity's personality surfaces and submerges.
2. **Mother Amalgam's Price.** Mother Amalgam offers the party shelter in her Refuge, but the safe rooms are running low on identity-fuel. She needs fresh names, memories, or personal effects to feed the walls. Each party member must sacrifice something that defines them, or the Refuge will begin absorbing everyone inside within hours.
3. **Vessel Maren's Offer.** Vessel Maren was once a mortal scholar who entered the Descent willingly to study Kaevroth's nature. She has survived the Consuming by allowing it to partially absorb her, becoming a vessel through which the level's hunger speaks. She offers to guide the party to Level 9 in exchange for a promise: if they reach the Heart, they will speak her true name aloud, which she has forgotten. She believes hearing it will restore her.
4. **The Hunger Wakes.** The Hunger, a vast, formless presence that represents the Consuming's collective appetite, occasionally focuses its attention on specific individuals. When it focuses on a party member, every surface in the Maw begins leaning toward them, walls bending, floors tilting, faces in the iron turning to stare. The party has minutes to distract the Hunger before the focused character begins merging with the level.
5. **The Hollow King Remembers.** During the party's passage through the Maw, the Hollow King briefly regains full consciousness, speaking in his own voice for the first time in centuries. He begs the party to destroy his throne, which is the anchor keeping him alive and consumed simultaneously. Destroying it would free him into true death but would also destabilize the entire level, potentially accelerating the Consuming across all remaining souls.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Consuming is a horror level. Its danger is not combat but erosion, the slow loss of self that makes players question whether their characters are still their characters. Use mechanical reinforcement: have players make saves to retain memories, ask them to cross off personality traits or background details as the Consuming progresses, let them feel the dissolution mechanically as well as narratively.
>
> **The Hollow King's Identity.** The Hollow King was once a mortal conqueror who worshipped Kaevroth with such devotion that the god elevated him to rule this level. He was the first mortal soul the Consuming processed after Kaevroth's death, and his incomplete absorption created the template for every soul consumed after him. Destroying his throne would not just free him, it would unravel the entire level's structure, potentially releasing every consumed soul simultaneously. The resulting cascade of freed identity-fragments could be catastrophic or redemptive, depending on how the players manage it.
>
> **Connection to Level 9.** The Consuming feeds directly into the Heart. Everything absorbed here eventually filters down to Level 9, where it becomes fuel for the Echo of Kaevroth. The Consuming is, in a very real sense, the Echo's digestive system. If the players can disrupt the Consuming, the Echo weakens. If they destroy the Hollow King's throne, the flow of consumed essence stops entirely, which may be the only way to approach the Heart safely.
>
> **Belara's Thread.** Faint traces of [[The Twelve#Belara|Belara]]'s consumed essence sometimes surface in the Consuming, manifesting as moments of impossible compassion: a Hollow One that suddenly weeps and asks if you are alright, a wall that releases a trapped soul instead of absorbing a new one, a voice singing a lullaby in the iron. These fragments are being pulled toward Level 9, toward the Heart of Iron where the bulk of Belara's stolen essence resides. Players who follow these moments of mercy will find themselves guided, however faintly, toward the Mercy Gate.
