# Ithael, The Threshold Singer

> [!info]
> **Nature:** The boundary between creation and The Fathom, given voice, its song IS the separation
> **Manifestation:** A harmonic vibration perceived by entities of extreme magical sensitivity, the stars as crystallized notes
> **Cross-Cultural Names:** "The Song at the Edge" (Star-Readers), "The Hum" (Lantern-Keeper Divers), "The Boundary's Voice" (Aelindra's private notation)

**What It Is**

Ithael is not a being that sings at the boundary. Ithael *is* the boundary, singing. Its song is the separation between creation and [[The Fathom]], a harmonic vibration that maintains the membrane keeping inside and outside apart. It is phenomenon as much as entity, a process given awareness in the same way that [[Ur-Ghael]] is a tendency given weight. Whether there is a "singer" behind the song, or whether the song is all there is, is an open question that may not have an answer.

The song has qualities. Those who perceive it, and only a handful of beings in creation's history ever have, describe it with tears. It is beautiful. It is the most beautiful thing they have ever perceived. And it is thinning.

**The Stars**

The stars are crystallized notes of Ithael's song. Each one is a resonance point, an anchor in the harmonic, a place where the song is strong enough to solidify into light. The celestial sphere is not merely a visual phenomenon. It is a musical one: a vast, incomprehensibly complex composition that holds creation together the way a glass holds water.

When a star goes dark, a note has been lost. The harmonic weakens. The composition loses a voice. And the boundary at that point thins, because the song is the boundary, and a song with missing notes is a wall with missing stones.

**Relationship to The Twelve**

[[The Twelve#Aelindra|Aelindra]] can hear the song. She has listened to it for two thousand years from her place on the celestial sphere. She has heard it weaken, note by note, star by star, for eighteen months now. She is the only entity inside creation who truly understands how little time the Boundary has, because she can hear the composition losing coherence in real time. Her despair is not abstract. It is the despair of a musician listening to a masterwork being erased, note by note, knowing she cannot stop it and cannot replace the missing voices.

**History**

Whether Ithael began singing when [[The Twelve]] built the Boundary, or whether it had always been singing and the Twelve built the Boundary by tuning creation to resonate with Ithael's existing song, is a question of immense cosmological significance. The first interpretation means the Twelve created the Boundary from scratch. The second means they discovered a pre-existing separation and amplified it. The implications for creation's nature, and for the possibility of repairing the Boundary without the Twelve's power, are profound.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Ithael offers one of the most evocative campaign hooks in the entire cosmology: a musician who accidentally composes a melody matching a fragment of Ithael's song. The composition has unexpected properties: it reinforces local reality, making magic more stable, the air clearer, the colors brighter. Within a small radius around the musician, the Boundary *strengthens*.
>
> This could seed a new magical tradition, a form of "harmonic magic" that reinforces reality through sound. Or it could be a weapon against the Second Silence, a mortal-scale method of patching the holes in the Boundary without requiring divine intervention.
>
> The musician would not understand what they have done. They would only know that the melody came to them in a dream, that it felt external rather than invented, and that something in the world responds to it.
