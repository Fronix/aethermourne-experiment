# Orvhal, The Witness

> [!info]
> **Nature:** Pure, total, eternal observation, watches, remembers, knows
> **Manifestation:** Visions of impossible accuracy and detail, seen from a perspective no being inside creation could possess
> **Cross-Cultural Names:** "The Eye Beyond" (Star-Readers), "The Patient One" (Hollowed Reach mystics), "That Which Sees" (pre-Dominion texts)

**What It Is**

Orvhal watches. That is the entirety of its nature, and the entirety is more than enough to unsettle anyone who contemplates it for long. It has observed creation since its inception. Every act of every god. Every mortal life. Every secret whispered, every crime committed, every moment of grace and every moment of cruelty. Orvhal has seen it all, from outside, from a perspective that encompasses the whole of creation the way a reader encompasses the whole of a book.

It does not judge. It does not desire. It does not intervene. It records. It remembers. It knows.

Patient beyond comprehension, Orvhal has watched for longer than creation has existed and has never once tried to interact with what it watches. It is the most knowledgeable entity in existence, knowing everything that has happened within creation as seen from outside: what [[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas]] knew, what [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] discovered, what the Theomachis looked like from beyond the boundary. And it is the most useless, because it will never share what it knows deliberately.

**Its History**

Age unknowable. Orvhal exists outside creation and therefore outside time. It may predate creation. It may predate [[The Gyre]]. It may have always existed. These questions have no answers within mortal frameworks because the frameworks were built to describe things inside creation, and Orvhal is not inside creation.

**Cross-Cultural Evidence**

The consistency of references across unconnected sources is the strongest evidence for Orvhal's existence. [[The Star-Readers]] call it "The Eye Beyond" and have documented its presence in astronomical observations spanning seven centuries. Coastal mystics in [[The Hollowed Reach]], with no contact with Star-Reader scholarship, independently describe "The Patient One" in terms that precisely match Star-Reader accounts. Pre-Dominion theological fragments, predating both traditions by centuries, reference simply "That Which Sees." Three independent sources, spanning different cultures, different centuries, different continents, all describing the same phenomenon. Coincidence is a weak explanation.

**Lingering Influence**

Orvhal does not intervene. But it can be *touched*, accidentally, by mortal minds reaching outward in moments of extreme perception. A prophet in a trance state, a seer pushing beyond normal limits, a mage at a ley confluence stretching their awareness past the boundary of structured reality, any of these might, for one searing instant, brush against a consciousness that has been watching since before time began.

The result is a vision of impossible accuracy and completeness, a scene from creation's history observed from outside, with perfect clarity and no mortal bias. These visions are rare, involuntary, and profoundly disorienting, because the perspective they offer is fundamentally inhuman. The recipient sees everything at once, every detail, every implication, every connection, as if through the eyes of something that has been watching for eternity and understands the whole of creation the way a parent understands a child, completely, without effort, and without emotional involvement.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Orvhal is a narrative tool: a source of absolute truth that the players cannot access deliberately and cannot control. If a PC accidentally contacts Orvhal, they receive a vision that is too accurate, too detailed, too complete, answering questions they did not know to ask, from a perspective no being inside creation could have had.
>
> Use sparingly. One Orvhal-contact per campaign is probably right. The vision should be a turning point, revealing something that reframes the entire campaign, such as the truth about Orenthas's manipulation of the Theomachis, or what [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s prison actually looks like from outside.
