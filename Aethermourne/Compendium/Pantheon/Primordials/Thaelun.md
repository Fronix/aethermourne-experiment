# Thaelun, The Skein of Many

> [!info]
> **Nature:** Simultaneous multiplicity, genuinely being all possibilities at once
> **Manifestation:** Reality stuttering, objects existing in multiple states, probability distortion, impossible simultaneity
> **Mortal Names:** "The Unresolved" (Last Certainty), "The Many-At-Once" (Star-Readers), "What The Maelstrom Remembers" (Shattered Edge locals)

**What It Is**

Thaelun is the most alien of the Gyre Primordials, and the most difficult to describe, because description requires choosing words, and choosing is precisely what Thaelun does not do. It is simultaneously many things at once, not shapeshifting, which implies a base form that changes between states, but genuinely *being* all forms at the same time. It is the living embodiment of [[The Gyre]]'s fundamental nature: undifferentiated potential that has not yet chosen what to become.

Where [[Ur-Ghael]] creates the lean toward pattern and [[Vorthaan]] dissolves pattern back, Thaelun exists in the space between: the moment of maximum possibility, the instant before a coin lands, extended to infinity. It is potential itself, aware and unresolved, every answer simultaneously true, every shape simultaneously real.

**Its History**

Thaelun is the youngest of the identified Gyre Primordials, in the sense that multiplicity requires the prior existence of distinct possibilities. You cannot be "many things at once" until there are distinct things to be. Thaelun is downstream of both [[Ur-Ghael]] (which creates distinct patterns) and [[Vorthaan]] (which dissolves them, generating new potential for new patterns). Thaelun is the state between formation and dissolution, the trembling superposition of everything the Gyre could become.

**Relationship to The Twelve**

[[The Twelve]] chose to be specific things. In crystallizing from the Gyre into defined gods with defined domains, they rejected all other possibilities. They chose order over chaos, fire over water, death over life, each god a specific answer to the question of what to become. Thaelun is the embodiment of what they gave up, the living potential they left behind when they narrowed themselves into defined beings.

[[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]] perceived reality as a web of interconnected potentials, a shimmering field where everything was magic and magic was everything. She was the god closest to perceiving Thaelun's nature. This is why her fragments sometimes transmit Thaelun-adjacent visions, flashes of the universe as a trembling superposition of infinite states. The "Foretouched" from [[The Shattered Edge]], people who have emerged from Maelstrom exposure carrying fragments of impossible knowledge, may be individuals who have brushed against Thaelun's outermost edge through the Maelstrom breach, receiving slivers of its infinite simultaneity that their minds have partially, imperfectly translated into something resembling prophecy.

**Lingering Influence**

Reality stutters near Thaelun bleed-through. Things exist in multiple states simultaneously. A door that is open and closed. A person who appears young and old depending on the angle of observation. An animal that is alive and dead at once, moving and still, predator and prey. These phenomena are brief, usually lasting only seconds, but deeply disturbing to witness. The mind recognizes that what it is seeing is impossible, but the eyes insist.

In severe cases, entire locations can enter a state of superposition, a house that is both standing and collapsed, a river that flows in two directions simultaneously, a forest clearing where it is day and night at the same time. These events are documented at the edges of [[The Maelstrom]] and nowhere else, which suggests the Maelstrom breach is the primary channel for Thaelun's influence.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Maelstrom-Changed people at [[The Shattered Edge]], particularly those with probability-perception or precognitive flashes, may be Thaelun-adjacent. Their abilities are not true prophecy (which channels [[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas]]'s Loom) but something stranger: a partial perception of multiple simultaneous realities, an echo of Thaelun's nature grafted onto a mortal mind.
>
> This is mechanically useful: if a PC becomes Maelstrom-touched, their new perception could be flavored as Thaelun-bleed rather than standard precognition. They don't see the future; they see *all* futures at once and must sort through them. The disorientation, the headaches, the nosebleeds, all of it is the cost of a mortal mind trying to process simultaneous multiplicity through hardware designed for one reality at a time.
