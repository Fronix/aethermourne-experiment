# The Convergence, The Pressing Dark

> [!info]
> **Nature:** Not a being but a pressure, a region of The Fathom where alien reality presses most strongly against the Boundary
> **Manifestation:** Accelerated star-darkening, deepening oceans, local reality becoming slightly less reliable
> **Cross-Cultural Names:** "The Weight Outside" (Star-Readers), "The Pressing Dark" (Lantern-Keeper sealed reports), "The Storm That Is Not A Storm" (Hollowed Reach coastal communities)

**What It Is**

The Convergence is not an entity in any recognizable sense. It is a pressure, a region, a phenomenon. [[The Fathom]] is not uniform. Some regions of the alien beyond are further from creation, some closer. The Convergence is the closest point, the place where The Fathom presses most strongly against the Boundary, testing its integrity with a pressure that is constant, patient, and slowly increasing.

Think of it as a storm system in an alien ocean, a moving region of maximum pressure that drifts along the Boundary's exterior the way a weather front drifts across a landscape. Where the Convergence presses, the Boundary bows inward. Stars go dark faster. The ocean deepens. Reality becomes slightly less reliable, not broken, not distorted, just subtly *loosened*, as if the rules of physics have developed a slight give.

**Movement**

The Convergence is not static. It moves slowly, drifting along the exterior of the Boundary over decades and centuries. Its path is not random, but its pattern, if it has one, has not been identified by any mortal scholar. Where it presses against the Boundary, the effects are measurable: increased stellar disappearances, more frequent Black Tides, a deepening of the ocean floor, and a general sense of unease among magically sensitive individuals.

**History**

The Convergence has been pressing against the Boundary since the Boundary was created. Its pressure is constant and has slowly been eroding the membrane over two millennia, the way water erodes stone, not through force but through patience. The [[Cosmology and Magic#The Second Silence|Second Silence]] may be accelerating this erosion, or the erosion may be accelerating the Second Silence. The causal relationship is unclear and may be circular.

**The Abyssal Trench**

[[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s prison is located at a point where the Convergence most frequently presses against the Boundary. The surviving gods may have placed her where the Boundary was already thinnest, reasoning that the prison's divine construction would reinforce that weakness. The prison was designed to serve double duty: containing Serith and patching a cosmological vulnerability.

Two millennia later, both prison and Boundary are weakening. The prison was never designed to serve as permanent infrastructure, and the Boundary was never designed to be reinforced by a prison. Both systems are degrading under stresses their builders did not anticipate.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Convergence's movement is trackable. If the [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]] realize what they are seeing, that the pattern of stellar disappearances follows a path rather than occurring randomly, they can predict WHERE the Boundary will thin next. This allows preparation, evacuation, or, most ambitiously, preemptive reinforcement.
>
> A Star-Reader who charts the dark patches and plots their progression over time would discover that they form a spiral, a slow, tightening path that is converging on a specific point in the celestial sphere. Where that point corresponds to on the surface of Aethermourne is left to the GM, but the Abyssal Trench is the obvious and most dramatic answer.
>
> The Convergence is not an antagonist. It is a deadline. It tells the players that the Boundary is not merely weakening passively; it is being pressed, tested, eroded by something vast and patient and utterly indifferent to what lives inside the walls it is slowly crushing.
