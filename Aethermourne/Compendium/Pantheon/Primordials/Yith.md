# Yith, The Dreaming Deep

> [!info]
> **Nature:** Perpetual reverie, the dream from which creation's inspirations leak upward
> **Manifestation:** Strange dreams, external-feeling inspiration, visions too specific to be imagination, oracle-trance states
> **Mortal Names:** "The Sleeper" (Greymere folk tradition), "The Wellspring" (Greensingers), "The Dream Beneath" (coastal mystics of the Hollowed Reach)

**What It Is**

Yith does not churn, pattern, dissolve, or separate. Yith *dreams*. It exists in perpetual reverie, a state of consciousness that has no waking counterpart, no beginning, and no end. Its dreams leak upward through the membrane between [[The Gyre]] and structured reality as visions, inspirations, creative impulses, and, occasionally, madness.

Of all the Gyre Primordials, Yith is the most approachable, not because it is conscious of mortals, not because it is friendly, but because its influence manifests as something mortals recognize: dreams. Every other Primordial bleeds through as a distortion of reality, pattern-compulsion, dissolution, superposition, hardened boundaries. Yith bleeds through as images, narratives, emotions, the stuff of sleep. It is still alien. It is still vast. But it arrives wearing a mask that mortal minds can almost comprehend.

Some scholars at [[The Last Certainty]] have proposed a theory so audacious it borders on heresy: that the Gyre itself is Yith's dream. That the churning potential, the undifferentiated fullness of the substrate, is the content of a mind too vast to comprehend, dreaming all possibilities simultaneously. If this is true, then everything, [[The Twelve]], creation, mortals, history, is a dream within a dream, a structure built from the imagery of a sleeping colossus.

This is probably metaphor. But [[The Gyre]] is a place where "probably" is a weak word.

**Its History**

Yith may be the oldest Primordial, or the most alien, or both. Its relationship to the others is unclear. Does it contain them, dreaming [[Ur-Ghael]] and [[Vorthaan]] and [[Thaelun]] and [[Achren]] into existence? Or does it sit alongside them, one more force among many? The question may have no answer, because the framework in which such questions make sense, categories, distinctions, hierarchies, is itself a product of the Gyre's nature, and the Gyre does not explain itself.

**Relationship to The Twelve**

[[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas]], God of Time and Fate, who saw all possible futures simultaneously, may have been Yith's closest divine echo. The [[Cosmology and Magic#The Loom of Fate|Loom of Fate]] may be connected to Yith's dreaming: if the Loom weaves possibilities into destiny, and Yith *is* all-possibility-at-once in its endless reverie, the Loom may have been Orenthas's method of giving structure to Yith's dreams, translating infinite dreaming into finite destiny.

This would mean the Loom is not merely a divine mechanism but a bridge between the substrate and structured reality, a way of drawing coherent threads from the dream-stuff of a sleeping Primordial. If so, the Loom's degradation since the Theomachis means those threads are becoming unfiltered. Raw dream-stuff, unprocessed by the Loom's structuring function, is seeping into reality. This could explain why prophecy has become unreliable, why oracles receive visions they cannot interpret, why seers go mad.

**Lingering Influence**

Strange dreams. Artistic inspiration that feels external, as if the idea came from somewhere else and was merely received rather than created. Visions too specific and too detailed to be imagination, arriving during sleep or deep meditation with the force of lived experience. Oracle traditions across multiple cultures and centuries may draw, unknowingly, on Yith's dream-bleed, receiving fragments of its endless reverie and interpreting them through local frameworks.

The Sleepwalkers on the [[The Hollowed Reach]] coast, people who rise at night and walk to the water's edge in perfect unison, may be responding not only to [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s whispers from her prison but to Yith's dreams filtering up through the ocean floor, two sources of influence layered atop each other, indistinguishable from the mortal perspective.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Yith is the most player-accessible Primordial because its influence comes in the form of dreams, which are a natural storytelling tool. Use Yith-bleed to deliver visions, atmosphere, and cryptic information:
>
> - A character who enters deep meditation at a ley confluence and touches Yith's dreaming, receiving a vision of what the world looked like before [[The Twelve]] imposed structure: raw, beautiful, terrifying, and utterly alien. Not chaos, not void, but a *fullness* so complete it has no room for individual things.
> - An artist in a border town who has begun painting images of places that do not exist, cities built according to principles that violate architecture, skies with colors that have no names. She paints compulsively and cannot stop. The paintings are beautiful. They are also wrong.
> - The Greensingers in [[The Verdant Marches]] who commune with the land's awareness may be drawing on Yith's dream-bleed without knowing it. The "voice" of the forest may be partly Yith's dreaming, filtered through Thyrea's distributed consciousness, arriving in mortal minds as nature-communion rather than Primordial contact.
