# The Primordials

Before the gods, there was something else. Not nothing, never nothing, but a fullness so vast and so formless that the word "something" barely applies. The Primordials are the presences within and beyond that fullness, forces older than time, older than structure, older than the distinction between "one thing" and "another."

They are not gods. Gods have domains, personalities, wills, histories. The Primordials are closer to natural laws given a kind of awareness, processes so vast and so fundamental that calling them "beings" is like calling the ocean a puddle. [[The Twelve]] shaped the world. The Primordials are what the world was shaped *from*, and what still churns beneath it, and what presses against it from outside.

This document catalogs the ten Primordial presences that mortal scholarship has tentatively identified: five within [[The Gyre]], the raw substrate beneath creation, and five within or upon [[The Fathom]], the alien expanse beyond creation's boundary. There are certainly more. These ten are simply the shapes that human perception has, briefly and imperfectly, managed to name.

> [!quote]
> *"The gods were not the first. They were merely the first to choose what they would be. Before them, there was only the choosing itself, and the things that did not choose, and the things that could not."*
> , Fragment attributed to [[The Last Certainty]], date unknown

---

## The Nature of Primordials

Primordials are not beings in the mortal sense. They do not think, plan, desire, or act, at least not in ways that map onto those words. They are better understood as vast processes: tendencies, pressures, rhythms that operate at a scale so far beyond mortal experience that calling them "alive" or "conscious" is a category error. And yet they are not merely mechanical. They respond. They shift. They have, for lack of a better word, *character*.

The distinction between Primordials and [[The Twelve]] is fundamental. The Twelve *chose* what they would be. They crystallized from [[The Gyre]]'s raw potential into defined gods with defined domains. That act of self-definition, of becoming *this* rather than *everything*, is what separates a god from a Primordial. The Primordials never chose. They never narrowed. They remain what they have always been: vast, unresolved, and absolute.

There are two categories of Primordial presence, corresponding to the two great unknowns of Aethermourne's cosmology:

- **The Gyre Primordials** exist within and below creation, in the raw substrate from which all structured reality was drawn. They are foundational. They predate the gods. They are what the gods emerged from.
- **The Fathom Entities** exist outside creation, in the alien expanse beyond the boundary of the celestial sphere. They are foreign. They follow rules that have nothing to do with creation's architecture. They are what presses in from beyond the walls.

Both categories are poorly understood, fragmentarily documented, and deeply unsettling to contemplate. What follows is the most comprehensive synthesis of available scholarship, drawn from [[The Star-Readers]], [[The Last Certainty]], the sealed archives of the Lantern-Keepers, and pre-Theomachis texts that most theologians would prefer did not exist.

A critical distinction: the Gyre Primordials are not hostile to creation. They are the raw material from which creation was built. Fearing them is like fearing the earth beneath your house. The Fathom entities are not hostile either, but they are *foreign*, operating under rules that are incompatible with creation's architecture. The difference between "foundational" and "foreign" is the difference between the ground shifting beneath you and a flood pouring through the walls. Both are dangerous. Neither is malicious. And neither cares whether you survive.

## The Gyre Primordials

[[The Gyre]] is the primordial substrate of all creation, the churning ocean of undifferentiated potential from which [[The Twelve]] drew the raw material to build the world. Within that substrate, five vast presences have been identified, not creatures or minds, but tendencies given a kind of awareness.

## The Fathom Entities

[[The Fathom]] is the alien expanse outside the boundary of creation, operating according to rules that have nothing to do with the reality [[The Twelve]] built. The entities that inhabit or constitute The Fathom are more recognizably *beings* than the Gyre Primordials, possessing will, intelligence, and purpose, but their nature is fundamentally alien. Five have been tentatively identified. There are certainly more.

|---|---|---|---|---|

## The Primordial Ecology

The Primordials do not exist in isolation. They interact, overlap, and define each other in ways that mirror the divine ecology of [[The Twelve]] but operate at a far deeper level.

**Within [[The Gyre]]:**

[[Ur-Ghael]] and [[Vorthaan]] are complementary forces, pattern and dissolution, structure and entropy. Their interaction *is* the Gyre's churning, the eternal cycle of almost-becoming and un-becoming that constitutes the substrate's existence. Neither can exist without the other. Neither would mean anything without the other.

[[Thaelun]] is downstream of both, embodying the multiplicity that their interaction generates. Where Ur-Ghael creates distinct patterns and Vorthaan dissolves them, Thaelun exists as the simultaneous superposition of all patterns, all dissolutions, all states at once.

[[Achren]] emerged from the first distinctions Ur-Ghael created and became the principle that allows ALL distinctions to exist. It is the meta-pattern: not a specific structure but the capacity for structures to be separate from one another. This includes the Boundary between creation and [[The Fathom]], which is Achren's principle applied at the cosmological scale.

[[Yith]] may underlie everything, its dreaming being the medium in which the other Primordials operate. Or it may be one more force among many. The ambiguity is irreducible.

**Within [[The Fathom]]:**

The Fathom entities are fundamentally different from the Gyre Primordials, alien to each other as well as to creation. [[Orvhal]] watches. [[Ithael]] sings. [[The Unremembered]] yearn. [[Kavothei]] collects. [[The Convergence]] presses. They do not appear to interact with each other in any systematic way, though mortal scholarship on the subject is essentially nonexistent.

**Between the Two:**

The Gyre and The Fathom relate to each other through creation's "floor" and "ceiling." Ithael's song separates The Fathom from creation, but it does not separate The Fathom from the Gyre. The two are connected through the deep structures of the Loom of Fate, which extends from the Gyre's depths through creation and into the Boundary itself.

The Convergence presses against Ithael's song. Where the Convergence is strongest, Ithael's notes fail, and stars go dark. This is not a conflict, Ithael and the Convergence are not adversaries, but a dynamic: pressure and resistance, weight and wall, the slow contest between what pushes in and what holds it back.

One interaction deserves special note: [[Achren]] and [[Ithael]]. Achren is the principle of separation within creation. Ithael's song is the separation between creation and The Fathom. They are echoes of each other, operating at different scales, one internal and one external. If someone could harmonize them, could align Achren's internal boundaries with Ithael's external song, the Boundary would strengthen dramatically. This is not a theory anyone in Aethermourne has articulated. It is, however, the logical conclusion of combining Star-Reader cosmological models with [[The Last Certainty]]'s research on Primordial influence. The pieces exist. No one has assembled them.

Another: [[Kavothei]] and [[Vorthaan]]. Everything Vorthaan dissolves from creation eventually drifts outward through micro-fractures in the Boundary. Kavothei collects it. In a sense, Kavothei is Vorthaan's archivist, preserving everything that dissolution erases. The entropy of creation becomes the inventory of an alien collector. There is something almost poetic in this, if you can stomach the implications.

## Theological Implications

Most mortal scholars do not know about the Primordials. Those who do are working from fragmentary evidence gathered across centuries, and their conclusions are tentative, contested, and deeply unsettling to established theological frameworks.

The implications of Primordial existence are staggering. [[The Twelve]] were not the first. The Twelve were not the only. The material of creation predates the creators. The gods emerged from the Gyre's raw potential, which means the Gyre, and everything within it, is older than the gods. Older than time. Older than the distinction between "old" and "new."

This reframes the entire theological edifice of Aethermourne.

**The [[Order of the Ashen Flame|Order of the Ashen Flame]]** would consider Primordial cosmology heresy of the highest order. Their theology places [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]] at the apex of creation, the supreme architect whose order underlies all things. The suggestion that Solvaen was *downstream* of [[Ur-Ghael]], a divine echo of a principle that predated him by unguessable spans, would be politically and theologically devastating. The Hierarch of [[The Ashen Dominion]] has, on at least two occasions, suppressed academic publications that approached this conclusion.

**[[The Star-Readers]]** would find Primordial cosmology validating. Their astronomical observations have always implied a deeper architecture beneath the divine layer, structures too vast and too old to be the gods' work. Primordial cosmology explains anomalies in their star-charts that have resisted interpretation for centuries.

**The Greensingers** of [[The Verdant Marches]] might recognize [[Yith]]'s dreaming in their communion with the land. The "voice" they perceive in Thyrea's distributed consciousness, vague, somnolent, vast, may be partly Primordial in nature, Yith's dream-bleed filtered through a dead goddess's remains. This would not disturb the Greensingers, who have always been more comfortable with ambiguity than certainty, but it would change their understanding of what they commune with.

**The [[The Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keepers]]** of [[The Hollowed Reach]] have, in their sealed archives, Diver reports that describe phenomena consistent with Fathom entity contact. These reports are classified, available only to senior Keepers, and deeply troubling to those who have read them. The Lantern-Keepers suspect that what lives in the deep Trench is not merely Serith's influence. They are right.

**The Fatalists** would find in Primordial cosmology the ultimate validation of their philosophy. If even the gods were products of forces they did not control, then fate truly does govern all things, not through the Loom, but through the Gyre's own nature. The tendency toward pattern, the inevitability of dissolution, the multiplicity of possibility, these are forces that no god, no mortal, no act of will can countermand. The Fatalists would argue that fighting the Second Silence is as futile as fighting [[Vorthaan]] itself.

**The Veil Unbound** would seize on Primordial cosmology as proof that [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s plan is not merely justified but cosmologically *appropriate*. If creation was drawn from the Gyre, then returning it to the Gyre is not destruction but *completion*, the exhale after the inhale, the dissolution that follows every pattern. Serith's restart is not an ending. It is Vorthaan's nature, applied to the grandest pattern of all.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The theological implications of Primordial cosmology are a tool for creating factional conflict and philosophical tension. A PC who discovers the truth about the Primordials possesses information that could destabilize the Ashen Dominion's religious authority, validate the Star-Readers' cosmological models, and reframe every faction's understanding of the world's origins.
>
> Whether to share that information, and with whom, is a meaningful campaign decision. The truth is not dangerous because it is wrong. It is dangerous because it is right, and because the world's power structures are built on a simpler, more comforting story.

## A Note for the Game Master

The Primordials are not villains. They are not allies. They are not quest objectives. They are *context*, the vast, deep, terrifying context that makes creation feel small and precious and worth fighting for.

Use them the way you would use weather, or gravity, or the slow turning of the seasons. They are forces too large to confront, too fundamental to circumvent, too alien to comprehend. A PC cannot fight [[Ur-Ghael]] any more than they can fight the concept of pattern. A PC cannot negotiate with [[Vorthaan]] any more than they can negotiate with entropy. These are the conditions of existence, not the enemies within it.

What PCs *can* do is deal with the consequences of Primordial influence bleeding through weakened membranes. A village where compulsive order has replaced free will. A coastline where memories dissolve like morning fog. A forest clearing where reality has not decided what it is. These are encounter-scale manifestations of cosmological forces, and they are where the Primordials become playable.

The deeper campaign use is philosophical. The Primordials raise questions that have no answers, questions about the nature of creation, about what the gods really were, about whether the world can survive without its original architects or whether something older and stranger must be called upon. These questions do not need resolution. They need to be *felt*, to sit in the background of every decision, every conflict, every moment of hope and every moment of despair, like the hum of a song that is slowly, impossibly, fading.

The deeper campaign use is also structural. The Primordials provide alternative solutions to the campaign's central crises, solutions that do not depend on the gods:

- **[[Achren]]'s principle** could reinforce the Boundary without divine intervention
- **[[Ithael]]'s song** could be learned and replicated through mortal harmonic magic
- **[[Kavothei]]'s collection** contains lost knowledge needed to reassemble [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]], relight [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s Lantern, or understand [[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas]]'s true intentions
- **[[Ur-Ghael]]'s influence** could stabilize [[The Maelstrom]] if properly channeled
- **[[Yith]]'s dreaming** could provide visions of creation's original architecture, knowledge that might allow mortals to repair divine mechanisms without divine help

These are not easy solutions. They are *possible* solutions, which is more than the gods alone can offer. The Primordials do not care about mortals. But mortals, if they are clever enough, brave enough, and desperate enough, might find ways to work with forces that have no interest in working with them.

The gods are dead. The Primordials were here before the gods, and they will be here after. Make your players feel that weight, and then watch what they choose to do with it.
