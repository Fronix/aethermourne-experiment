# Rumors and Notices

*In-world handouts for tavern conversations, notice boards, and found documents. Read aloud or hand to players. Truths, half-truths, and lies are mixed deliberately.*

---

## The Ashen Dominion

### Tavern Rumors

> [!quote] Overheard at a Lower Ribs alehouse
> "The Hierarch hasn't been seen outside the Throne in three months. His advisors say he's 'in contemplation.' I say the Cinders have him locked in there. Who's actually running this city?"

*(Half-true. [[Hierarch Valdren III]] is not physically imprisoned but is increasingly isolated by [[Delric Mourne]]'s control of access.)*

> [!quote] A merchant at the Ashmarket, whispering
> "Someone's been tainting the Ashite tools coming out of the Forgemaster's quarter. Three smiths down with Order Sickness in a month. Hearthforge swears his stock is clean, but coin says he's covering for a bad supplier."

*(False about Hearthforge. True about the tainting. The source is a [[The Cinders|Cinder]] operation.)*

> [!quote] A drunk soldier at a garrison tavern
> "They found a chamber inside the Spine. Hollow, full of these crystal spheres. The miners who went in sat down and wouldn't get up. Just... staring. Church sealed the whole level. Nobody's talking about what was in there."

*(True. The chamber contains memory-stones from [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] dying consciousness.)*

> [!quote] A Hewn-caste laborer, barely audible
> "The graffiti? 'The order is a lie'? I heard it's not paint. It's Ashite dust, mixed with something else. Appears on its own. Even showed up inside the Cathedral once. You tell me how paint gets past wards."

*(Half-true. The graffiti is placed by [[Cassia Lorn]], a former Cinder, using knowledge of the ward system. It does not appear on its own.)*

### Public Notices

> [!info] Notice: Nailed to a board in the Ashmarket
> **BY ORDER OF THE ASHEN FLAME**
>
> The individual responsible for defacement of public and sacred property with seditious slogans is **WANTED** for questioning. Reward of **50 gold crowns** for information leading to identification. All citizens are reminded that possession of unauthorized literature is punishable under the Third Statute of Order.
>
> Report to your nearest Cinder office. Anonymity guaranteed.

> [!info] Notice: Pinned to a shrine in the Lower Ribs
> **THE UNBOUND CONGREGATION**
>
> Prayer meeting, seventh night after the half-moon. The old granary on Ashwell Street. All are welcome. All castes. Bring bread to share. We ask only that you come with an open heart.
>
> *"Order serves the people. The people do not serve order."*

### Found Document

> [!quote] A letter found tucked inside a book in the Ashmarket
> *Petra,*
>
> *Do not come back to the Halls. I know what you saw in the lower chamber. So does he. Your name is already on the list. Take the south gate road before dawn and do not look back. If you make it to the Fenlands, find the woman called Nettie at the Bridgeborn crossing. She will know what to do.*
>
> *Burn this.*
>
> *C.L.*

*(From [[Cassia Lorn]] to [[Petra Venn]], warning her about [[Delric Mourne]]'s awareness of her discovery.)*

## The Hollowed Reach

### Tavern Rumors

> [!quote] A sailor at Harren Coyle's tavern
> "The *Silver Current* didn't sink. I know a spotter who saw her through a glass, three leagues out, drifting. Lights on. Crew on deck. Nobody at the helm. She's just... out there. And nobody's willing to go get her."

*(True. The ship is crewed by Sleepwalkers guarding a Star-Reader cargo.)*

> [!quote] A Tidecaller at a dockside shrine
> "She screams at night now. Yvenne. Not the storms, that's different. This is a voice, under the water, wailing like she's trying to warn us about something. The old hymns barely calm her anymore. Something's wrong. Something's changed."

*(True. [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne's]] coherence is fracturing as [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] erodes her sanity.)*

> [!quote] A merchant counting coin at the Tidemarket
> "Fenn Dalloway's been buying property in the Outer Isles. Nobody buys Outer Isle property unless they know something. Either the fishing's about to get very good or very bad. I'm betting bad."

*(Half-true. [[Fenn Dalloway]] is laundering money, not investing. The properties are fronts.)*

### Public Notices

> [!info] Notice: Posted at every harbor gate
> **CONCORD OF TIDES, HARBOR AUTHORITY**
>
> **BLACK TIDE ADVISORY.** All vessels are advised to avoid the Outer Reach between the hours of dusk and dawn until further notice. Black Tide activity has been reported at levels exceeding seasonal norms. Vessels encountering Black Tide phenomena should extinguish all divine light sources and make for the nearest safe harbor.
>
> By order of [[Admiral Aldren Vayne|Master of Ships Vayne]].

> [!info] Notice: Nailed to the Lantern-Keepers' post
> **WANTED: INFORMATION**
>
> Regarding the individual known as **"Salt,"** a street preacher operating on the East Docks. Description: thin, male, early fifties, salt-crusted clothing, speaks with unusual cadence. Not wanted for any crime. The [[The Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keepers]] wish only to speak with this individual regarding their experiences. Reward: **25 gold** for confirmed sighting leading to contact.

### Found Document

> [!quote] A water-stained journal page found in a beached dinghy
> *Day 14. The water is wrong. Not the waves, the substance. It is thicker today. Warmer. The compass has been useless since Day 3. I can see the far shore, I think, but it never gets closer.*
>
> *Day 15. Something is beneath us. Not a whale. Not a current. Something aware. It rose to within a fathom of the hull last night and stayed there, just below the surface, for six hours. I could see light coming from it. Not reflected light. Its own.*
>
> *Day 16. It spoke. Not words. Meaning. Directly into*
>
> *(The entry ends mid-sentence. The remaining pages are blank.)*

## The Verdant Marches

### Tavern Rumors

> [!quote] A Marchwarden at a clan gathering-hall
> "Tessara Wildbloom's been missing for three months. The Council says she's on 'extended communion.' I've done communion. It doesn't take three months. Something in the deep wood took her, or she found something she wasn't supposed to."

*(True. [[Tessara Wildbloom]] is missing. The cause is unknown.)*

> [!quote] A Greensinger acolyte, nervous
> "The communion is getting harder. Not just for me. Elder Rosk won't say it publicly, but the land is pulling back. Like it doesn't trust us anymore. The rituals still work, but they take longer. More effort. More pain."

*(True. The Second Silence is weakening all divine-adjacent magic, including Primal Communion.)*

> [!quote] A traveling herbalist
> "There's a pool in the deep Marches, Marrow-Water, pure as anything. The Greensingers guard it, but a merchant out of the Fenlands claims to have a sample. Selling vials at five hundred gold each. Says it cures any wound. Doesn't mention the mutations."

*(Half-true. [[Cosmology and Magic#Divine Materials|Marrow-Water]] heals but causes physical transformation.)*

### Public Notices

> [!info] Notice: Carved into a waymarker at a clan border
> **BY DECREE OF THE MARCHWARDENS COUNCIL**
>
> The deep wood south of the Thornbranch River is **closed** to all non-Greensinger traffic until the current growth anomaly is resolved. Root-Speaker Fennara reports accelerated and unpredictable growth cycles. Travelers who ignore this advisory do so at their own peril.

### Found Document

> [!quote] A bark-scroll found in an abandoned Greensinger camp
> *The communion showed me something I was not meant to see. The land beneath the Thornwild, in the layers below the roots, there is movement. Not growth. Not the cycles we maintain. Something older, pushing upward. The land is afraid of it. I have never felt the land be afraid.*
>
> *I am going deeper. If this note is found and I am not with it, tell Elder Rosk that the cycle is not breaking. Something is trying to break through it from below.*

## The Pale Wastes

### Tavern Rumors

> [!quote] An Ashen Vigil soldier at an outpost tavern
> "The Hollowdeep breached again last week. Third time this season. Commander Ironveil's not telling the mainland, says it'll cause a panic. She's not wrong. But we're down to half strength and the dead keep coming."

*(True. The [[The Ashen Vigil|Vigil]] is concealing the severity of the undead incursions.)*

> [!quote] A traveling merchant, incredulous
> "The dead are *organizing*. Not the ferals, the smart ones. [[Revenant Yael]]'s 'Court' is sending actual diplomats to the Vigil outposts. Dressed nice. Speaking polite. Asking for territorial rights. The soldiers don't know what to do. You can't stab a diplomat."

*(True. The [[Remnants' Court]] is pursuing diplomatic recognition.)*

> [!quote] A trapper warming hands by a fire
> "Don't go near the Hollowdeep rim. Not even close. My partner got within a hundred yards and his shadow started moving on its own. Reached toward the rim like it wanted to go in. He hasn't been right since. Says the cold got inside him and won't leave."

*(True. Necrotic energy near the Hollowdeep causes these effects.)*

### Public Notices

> [!info] Notice: Carved into stone at a Vigil checkpoint
> **ASHEN VIGIL, NORTHERN COMMAND**
>
> **BOUNTY: Hollow Ones.** 5 gold per confirmed neutralization. Bring the skull to any Vigil outpost for verification. Do NOT attempt communication with Hollow Ones displaying speech capability. Report these immediately.

### Found Document

> [!quote] A frozen letter found on a dead courier outside a Vigil outpost
> *Commander Ironveil,*
>

>
> *I am requesting reinforcements from the mainland. I know your orders. I am ignoring them. The Vigil cannot hold another season at current strength.*
>
> *Holdwarden Ashken*

## The Primordia

### Tavern Rumors

*(No taverns exist in the Primordia. These are campfire conversations at [[Anchorseat]].)*

> [!quote] A Silence-Watcher during evening meal
> "The Shimmer surged again last night. Two hundred meters in a single push. That's more than the annual average. And the Anchor flickered. Just for a second. But it flickered."

*(True. The Shimmer's expansion is accelerating.)*

> [!quote] A Gyre-Touched hermit at the stabilization field's edge
> "The Anchors aren't just stabilizers. They talk to each other. I can hear them when the wind is right, a vibration beneath hearing. They're worried. I know that sounds mad. But the crystal is *worried*."

*(Half-true. The Anchors do communicate via Primordial resonance. Whether they experience emotion is debatable.)*

> [!quote] A returning explorer, shaking
> "There's something in the Fluid Wastes. Not a coalescence. Something permanent. A gate, stone, covered in symbols older than anything. It wasn't there six months ago. It hums. It wants to be opened."

*(True. The Fluid Gate is a new manifestation.)*

### Found Document

> [!quote] A Silence-Watcher's log, left on a desk in Anchorseat
> *Observation Log, Day 247.*
>
> *The Listening Wall transmitted something new today. Not the usual substrate noise. A pattern. Rhythmic. Almost linguistic. Watcher Solenne insists it is communication from the Anchor itself. I am not prepared to confirm this. But I have transcribed the pattern and will submit it to Ur-Saethen for analysis.*
>
> *Note: Watcher Ghael heard his name in the Wall's output. He is rattled. I am reassigning him to interior duties for the remainder of his rotation.*

## The Veilshore

### Tavern Rumors

> [!quote] A sailor at a Tidecross dockside bar
> "The Oldborn docks under this town? They're not just stone. They're alive. I dropped a rope over the side at low tide and something grabbed it. Not pulled, *grabbed*. Like a hand. I cut the rope and haven't gone back."

*(Half-true. The docks are a Primordial listening post. They react to contact but do not have "hands.")*

> [!quote] A Dominion missionary at Ashmark
> "Our fields are producing three harvests a season. Three! The soil here is blessed by Solvaen's grace. The Oldborn refuse to see it. They call our work 'contamination.' Can you imagine? Calling abundance contamination?"

*(False framing. The harvests are caused by Ashite restructuring the Primordial substrate, which is destroying local ecology.)*

> [!quote] A nervous merchant in the Crossroads Market
> "Something's moving under the sand. At night, after the market closes, you can see shapes pressing up from below. Like faces. Like hands. The Oldborn say the sand is 'listening.' I don't care what it's doing. I want it to stop."

*(True. The black sand is a Primordial sensory apparatus activating in response to divine residue.)*

### Public Notices

> [!info] Notice: Posted at Tidecross harbor
> **CONCORD TRADE AUTHORITY, VEILSHORE OFFICE**
>
> All trade in **Primordial artifacts** must be registered with the Archive House. Unregistered sale or transport of crystalline resonators, Anchor fragments, or materials of unknown origin is subject to seizure and fine. Violators will be reported to [[Captain Erys Vane|Harbormaster Vane]].

### Found Document

> [!quote] A note found pinned to a tree at the Fernwald's edge, written in Oldborn (translation provided)

## The Oldborn Kingdoms

### Tavern Rumors

*(Oldborn "taverns" are communal debate halls where drink accompanies argument.)*

> [!quote] A young Oldborn at a Keth'vaal debate hall
> "Speaker Yavenne has been corresponding with outlanders. I know someone who saw the letters. If the Sealed Gate finds out, she's finished. But I've read what the outlanders say about their 'Second Silence,' and if even half of it is true, isolation is a death sentence."

*(True. [[Speaker Yavenne]] is secretly corresponding with scholars in [[Tidecross]].)*

> [!quote] An elderly Pact-Weaver, speaking carefully
> "The Great Anchor fractured during the last renewal. Not visibly. But those of us who listen, we heard it. A note in the resonance that was not there before. Something is pressing against the Anchor from outside. Not Primordial. Something else."

*(True. The fractures are caused by temporal resonance from [[The Loom's Edge]].)*

> [!quote] A Rootway traveler, uneasy
> "The deep tunnels are wrong. Sounds coming from below, from sections we sealed centuries ago. Three travelers vanished on the Keth'vaal-to-Vehlkaar route last month. The Pact-Weavers say it's substrate instability. The travelers' families say the substrate doesn't drag people through walls."

*(True. Something from the deep Gyre has entered the Rootways.)*

## The Loom's Edge

### Tavern Rumors

*(No taverns. Fireside talk at [[Skeinhold]].)*

> [!quote] A Threadwalker after an expedition
> "I saw myself in the Mirror Valley. Not a reflection. A version of me, older, scarred, carrying a weapon I've never seen. She looked at me and said, 'It's too late.' Then she wasn't there. I checked my anchor-moment. It held. But I don't know whose future I saw."

*(True. Echoes of possible futures are increasing in frequency and specificity.)*

> [!quote] Threadwalker Issek, speaking to no one in particular
> "The threads aren't random. They're language. A sentence being written across the mountains, one word at a time, over centuries. I can almost read it. The first word is 'choose.' The second word is 'before.' I don't know the third yet."

*(Half-true. [[Threadwalker Issek]] perceives patterns others cannot. Whether they constitute "language" is debated.)*

> [!quote] Archivist Dael, during evening study
> "Seventeen fragments in the vault. There were twelve when I started keeping count. The new ones didn't come from outside. They grew. Silver thread, spinning itself out of nothing, inside sealed lead cases. Tell me that's dormant. Tell me that's contained."

*(True. The thread-fragments in the vault are generating new material at an accelerating rate.)*

### Found Document

> [!quote] A message carved into the Anchor Line, in handwriting matching no living Threadwalker
> *To those who come after:*
>
> *The Scar is not a wound. It is a door. What is behind it is not an enemy. It is an answer. The question is whether you want the answer badly enough to accept what it costs.*
>
> *You will know when the time comes. The threads will show you. Trust nothing else.*
>
> *Do not trust the threads either.*

*(Origin unknown. The carving appeared overnight. The Threadwalkers have no explanation.)*
