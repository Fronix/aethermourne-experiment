# Shops and Services

*Quick reference for when players go shopping, need supplies, or ask "Is there a tavern?" Prices in gold crowns (gc). Adjust for local economy.*

---

## Tidewall (The Hollowed Reach)

*Salt-stained commerce. Everything's for sale if you know who to ask.*

### Dallow's Outfitters (General Store)

**Proprietor:** Orna Dallow, shipwright's wife, practical and no-nonsense.
**Location:** Tidemarket, east wing.

| Item | Price |
|---|---|
| Rope, 50 ft (hemp) | 1 gc |
| Rope, 50 ft (silk, sea-treated) | 5 gc |
| Lantern, hooded | 3 gc |
| Lantern oil (1 flask) | 0.5 gc |
| Rations, 1 week (salted fish, hardtack) | 2 gc |
| Waterskin | 0.5 gc |
| Healer's kit (10 uses) | 5 gc |
| Compass (unreliable near divine materials) | 10 gc |
| Grappling hook | 2 gc |
| Explorer's pack (backpack, bedroll, mess kit, tinderbox, 10 torches, 10 days rations, waterskin) | 10 gc |
| Spyglass | 50 gc |
| Anti-Tide salve (1 dose, grants advantage vs. Black Tide effects for 1 hour) | 25 gc |

**Flavor:** Shelves stacked floor to ceiling. Smells of tar and dried fish. Orna remembers every customer and what they bought. She extends credit to Lantern-Keepers. Everyone else pays upfront.

### The Brine Witch (Alchemist / Tidal Apothecary)

**Proprietor:** "Mags," an elderly Tidecaller who left the priesthood. Real name unknown.
**Location:** The Coral Quarter, tucked behind the Archive of Tides.

| Item | Price |
|---|---|
| Healing potion (restores 2d4+2 HP) | 50 gc |
| Antitoxin (advantage vs. poison for 1 hour) | 30 gc |
| Tidal Draught (breathe underwater for 1 hour) | 75 gc |
| Clarity Tonic (advantage vs. Serith's whisper/charm effects for 4 hours) | 100 gc |
| Bioluminescent ink (glows in salt water, 1 vial) | 15 gc |
| Sleepwalker's Rest (sedative, induces dreamless sleep, 1 dose) | 40 gc |
| Black Tide Neutralizer (cleanses 5 sq ft of Black Tide residue) | 60 gc |

**Flavor:** A cramped shop that smells of brine and something herbal. Bottles line shelves in no discernible order. Mags knows exactly where everything is. She talks to her potions. They do not talk back. Probably.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Mags left the Tidecallers because she began hearing [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne's]] voice directly, without ritual mediation. It terrified her. She still hears it. Some of her potions are more effective than they should be because Yvenne's influence seeps into the brewing. The Clarity Tonic is her best seller and her personal coping mechanism.

### The Barnacle (Tavern)

**Proprietor:** [[Harren Coyle]], information broker disguised as a tavern owner.
**Location:** The Bilges, dockside.

| Drink | Price |
|---|---|
| Ale (house brew, "Tide's End") | 0.2 gc |
| Wine (imported Dominion red) | 1 gc |
| Grog (Coyle's special, very strong) | 0.5 gc |
| Meal (fish stew, bread, pickled seaweed) | 0.5 gc |
| Room, per night (shared bunks) | 1 gc |
| Room, per night (private, lockable) | 3 gc |

**Atmosphere:** Low ceilings, sticky tables, the constant sound of water against the pilings below. Crowded at all hours. Sailors, dockworkers, Lantern-Keeper off-duty, and the occasional Veil Unbound recruiter in the corner. Coyle works the bar personally, trading stories for stories.

**Rumor hook:** "Buy Coyle a drink (5 gc worth of good wine) and he'll share a rumor. His information is always accurate. His price is always a story in return, something true that he hasn't heard before."

## Ostivaar (The Ashen Dominion)

*Regulated, taxed, and monitored. But the black market thrives in the Lower Ribs.*

### The Ordered Supply (General Store)

**Proprietor:** Hestal Grenn, a Hewn-caste merchant with a Church license.
**Location:** The Ashmarket, third row.

| Item | Price |
|---|---|
| Rope, 50 ft | 1 gc |
| Torch, 10 (standard) | 0.5 gc |
| Ashite lantern (never needs oil, glows by Ashite resonance) | 25 gc |
| Rations, 1 week (grain bread, dried meat, salt) | 2 gc |
| Holy symbol of [[The Twelve#Solvaen\|Solvaen]] (standard issue) | 1 gc |
| Healer's kit | 5 gc |
| Ink and parchment (10 sheets) | 2 gc |
| Manacles (Church-standard, Ashite-reinforced) | 15 gc |
| Travel papers (forged, ask quietly) | 50 gc |
| Cinder-badge (forged, very illegal, ask even more quietly) | 200 gc |

**Flavor:** Neat, orderly, every item in its labeled place. Hestal is polite, deferential to anyone wearing Church insignia, and terrified of the Cinders. The forged documents are available only if the party gains his trust (DC 14 Persuasion after establishing they are not Cinder agents).

### Hearthforge Metalworks (Smith / Enchanter)

**Proprietor:** [[Torren Hearthforge]], master smith.
**Location:** Forgemaster's Quarter, near the Ashmarket.

| Item | Price |
|---|---|
| Weapon, standard (any type) | 15-50 gc |
| Armor, standard (any type) | 25-200 gc |
| Weapon, Ashite-edged (+1 damage, enforces precision) | 150 gc |
| Armor, Ashite-reinforced (+1 Defense) | 300 gc |
| Shield, Ashite-inlaid (advantage vs. chaos-type magic) | 200 gc |
| Repair service (standard) | 5 gc |
| Repair service (divine material equipment) | 50 gc |
| Custom commission (2-week wait) | Price varies, starting 100 gc |

**Flavor:** The sound of hammers never stops. The forge runs on Ashite-heated coals that burn perfectly uniform. Hearthforge is gruff, honest, and quietly sympathetic to the Unbound Congregation. He gives discounts to anyone who treats his Hewn-caste apprentices with respect.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Hearthforge's Ashite-enhanced items are the best in Ostivaar but are being undercut by sabotaged tools from the Cinder-tainted supply chain. He suspects something but cannot prove it. Helping him investigate is a quest hook (see [[Quest Board - Ostivaar#4. The Ashmarket Poisoning]]).

### The Ashen Hearth (Tavern)

**Proprietor:** Calla Firren, a former Cinder clerk who quit and opened a bar.
**Location:** Border of the Ashmarket and Lower Ribs.

| Drink | Price |
|---|---|
| Ale (Dominion pale, unremarkable) | 0.2 gc |
| Ashwine (local specialty, warm, spiced, slightly numbing) | 0.8 gc |
| Mead (imported from the Verdant Marches, expensive) | 2 gc |
| Meal (stew, bread, roasted root vegetables) | 0.5 gc |
| Room, per night (Lower Ribs standard) | 1 gc |
| Room, per night (Ashmarket-facing, quieter) | 3 gc |

**Atmosphere:** Warm, dim, smells of spiced wine and woodsmoke. The Ashen Hearth is neutral ground where Hewn-caste workers, mid-rank Church officials, and the occasional Unbound sympathizer drink in cautious proximity. Calla knows things from her Cinder days and will share them, carefully, with people she trusts.

**Rumor hook:** "Calla notices if you're not Dominion locals. She'll approach after midnight with a quiet offer: 'I can tell you who's watching you in this city. It'll cost you a favor, not coin. I'll collect when I need to.'"

## Crucible (The Emberveil)

*Forge-smoke and divine metal. Everything here was made by someone proud of making it.*

### The Ember Stockhouse (General Store)

**Proprietor:** Delver-Captain Orin Deepstone, retired miner turned merchant.
**Location:** Central market, near the Grand Forge.

| Item | Price |
|---|---|
| Rope, 50 ft (iron-fiber, heat resistant) | 5 gc |
| Miner's pick | 3 gc |
| Heat-resistant gloves (4 hours near forge temperatures) | 10 gc |
| Lantern, forge-grade (withstands extreme heat) | 8 gc |
| Rations, 1 week (smoked meat, forge-bread, mineral water) | 3 gc |
| Climbing gear (pitons, hammer, harness) | 15 gc |
| Fireseed (creates a controlled flame for 1 hour, no fuel) | 20 gc |
| Slag sample (Slag Crawler art, decorative, occasionally magical) | 5-500 gc |

**Flavor:** Deepstone runs the shop like a mining operation: inventory tracked, every transaction recorded, no haggling. His prices are fair. His opinions on forge-craft are not solicited but delivered freely. He has a display case of Slag Crawler sculptures that he insists are "the purest art in Aethermourne."

### The Grand Forge Annex (Smith / Divine Material Workshop)

**Proprietor:** Managed by [[The Ember Guild]] under [[Forge-Matriarch Brunhild Ironheart]]'s oversight.
**Location:** Adjacent to the Grand Forge.

| Item | Price |
|---|---|
| Weapon, forge-quality (any type, +1 to hit) | 100 gc |
| Armor, forge-quality (any type, +1 Defense) | 250 gc |
| Kaevroth's Iron weapon (+2 damage, causes aggression in wielder, DC 12 Wisdom save after each combat) | 500 gc |
| Thalvor-tempered blade (fire damage on critical hits) | 400 gc |
| Divine material identification (per item) | 25 gc |
| Equipment reinforcement (resist 1 Slag Crawler dissolution) | 50 gc |
| Custom divine-material commission (4-week wait, guild approval required) | 1,000+ gc |

**Flavor:** The heat hits you at the door. The Annex is staffed by guild smiths who take their work personally. Insult a blade and you insult its maker. The Kaevroth's Iron weapons are kept in a locked case with a warning sign. The guild sells them reluctantly but acknowledges the market demand.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Kaevroth's Iron weapons are tracked by the Ember Guild. Every sale is recorded. If a weapon causes an incident (violence, contamination), the guild investigates. PCs who buy one may find guild agents checking in on them later.

### The Cooling Yard (Tavern)

**Proprietor:** Vessa Ironvow, a former smith whose hands were burned in a forge accident.
**Location:** Forge district, overlooking the cooling channels.

| Drink | Price |
|---|---|
| Forge Ale (brewed with mineral water, strong, metallic aftertaste) | 0.3 gc |
| Emberveil whiskey (smooth, warm, a local point of pride) | 1.5 gc |
| Imported wine (anything from outside the Emberveil, rare) | 3 gc |
| Meal (grilled meat, root mash, fire-roasted vegetables) | 0.8 gc |
| Room, per night (basic, warm from forge heat) | 2 gc |

**Atmosphere:** The Cooling Yard is where smiths go after shift. The air is warm. The conversation is loud. Arguments about forging technique are common and occasionally settled with arm-wrestling. Vessa tends bar with prosthetic hands of her own design, articulated bronze fingers that she controls through a minor Ember Guild enchantment. She is proud of them.

**Rumor hook:** "Vessa overhears everything and forgets nothing. She'll trade information for stories about divine materials from other regions. She's compiling a private reference guide and is always hungry for new data."

## Roothold (The Verdant Marches)

*The forest provides. If you know how to ask.*

### The Canopy Exchange (General Store)

**Proprietor:** A Greensinger acolyte named Fern (possibly not their real name).
**Location:** Central platform, Roothold's main walkway level.

| Item | Price |
|---|---|
| Rope, 50 ft (vine-woven, living, regrows if cut) | 3 gc |
| Torch, 10 (resin-coated, waterproof) | 1 gc |
| Rations, 1 week (foraged fruits, smoked game, root bread) | 1.5 gc |
| Healer's kit (herbal, 10 uses, Marches-sourced ingredients) | 4 gc |
| Antitoxin (herbal, brewed from Thornwild extracts) | 20 gc |
| Climbing gear (living-vine harness, adjusts to wearer) | 12 gc |
| Bug repellent (1 week supply, essential in the Marches) | 2 gc |
| Marrow-Water detector (resonance crystal, glows near mutagenic water) | 30 gc |
| Root-Map (living bark that shows paths through the local forest, valid for ~1 month before the forest changes) | 15 gc |

**Flavor:** The shop is a platform woven into a massive tree. Inventory hangs from branches or sits in living-wood shelves that grow new compartments as needed. Fern is cheerful, distracted, and talks to the tree. The tree may or may not respond. Root-Maps are the most popular item; without one, navigating the Marches is nearly impossible.

### The Grove Apothecary (Herbalist / Primal Healer)

**Proprietor:** [[Ivara Mistwalker]], Greensinger herbalist.
**Location:** Lower canopy, accessible by vine-lift.

| Item | Price |
|---|---|
| Healing potion (herbal, restores 2d4+2 HP, tastes of moss) | 40 gc |
| Antivenom (specific to Marches fauna) | 25 gc |
| Mutation suppressant (delays Marrow-Water effects for 24 hours) | 80 gc |
| Communion incense (aids Primal Communion, +2 to nature checks for 1 hour) | 30 gc |
| Wound salve (stabilizes a dying creature, 1 use) | 15 gc |
| Thornwild extract (poison, DC 14, 2d6 damage per round for 3 rounds) | 60 gc |
| Spore-sight powder (inhale to see invisible fungal networks for 10 minutes) | 20 gc |

**Flavor:** The apothecary smells of earth, green things, and something faintly medicinal. Plants grow directly from the walls, floor, and ceiling. Ivara harvests ingredients during the conversation, snipping leaves and crushing berries without looking. She is kind but blunt about the dangers of the Marches. "The forest heals. The forest also kills. Same forest."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Ivara knows [[Tessara Wildbloom]] personally and is deeply worried about her disappearance. If the PCs mention Tessara or show interest in the deep Marches, Ivara will offer a significant discount on supplies in exchange for any information about her friend.

### The Hollow Trunk (Tavern)

**Proprietor:** Brannoc, a retired Marchwarden scout.
**Location:** Ground level, in the hollowed base of Roothold's central tree.

| Drink | Price |
|---|---|
| Sap-ale (fermented tree sap, sweet, mildly intoxicating) | 0.2 gc |
| Wildbloom mead (floral, strong, brewed from forest honey) | 1 gc |
| Spring water (Roothold's springs, genuinely refreshing) | free |
| Meal (roasted game, root stew, forest mushrooms) | 0.5 gc |
| Room, per night (hammock in the upper branches) | 1 gc |
| Room, per night (private bower, woven walls, very comfortable) | 4 gc |

**Atmosphere:** Warm, woody, lit by bioluminescent moss. The ceiling is the inside of a living tree, and the walls breathe. Literally. The trunk expands and contracts slightly with the tree's respiration. Brannoc is quiet, watchful, and will not serve anyone who disrespects the forest. His sap-ale is an acquired taste. His wildbloom mead is excellent.

**Rumor hook:** "Brannoc doesn't gossip. But he'll share what his scouts have reported if the party buys a round for the house (10 gc). Recent reports: unusual growth patterns in the south, Marchwarden patrols finding areas where the forest has *retreated*, and a Greensinger expedition that went silent three weeks ago."

## Tidecross (The Veilshore, Unwritten Lands)

*Chaotic, improvised, overpriced. The only market between two continents.*

### The Crossroads General (General Store)

**Proprietor:** A [[Concord of Tides]] merchant named Vael Ashford, opportunistic but fair.
**Location:** Crossroads Market, center stall.

| Item | Price |
|---|---|
| Rope, 50 ft (hemp, mainland import) | 2 gc |
| Torch, 10 | 1 gc |
| Rations, 1 week (mixed mainland/local, quality varies) | 3 gc |
| Waterskin | 1 gc |
| Healer's kit | 8 gc |
| Compass (unreliable on the Veilshore) | 5 gc |
| Translation phrase card (basic Oldborn greetings, hand-copied) | 10 gc |
| Primordial ward charm (minor, reduces Primordial Taint symptoms for 8 hours, probably) | 50 gc |
| Black sand collection jar (sealed, for "research") | 3 gc |
| Passage letter (Concord-issued, allows harbor access) | 15 gc |

**Flavor:** A ramshackle stall under a canvas awning. Prices are inflated because everything is imported across the ocean. Vael apologizes for the markup but does not lower it. The Primordial ward charms are of dubious efficacy, purchased from an Oldborn vendor who may or may not be genuine. The translation cards are popular but full of errors that occasionally cause diplomatic incidents.

### The Archive Trade Desk (Scholarly Goods / Artifacts)

**Proprietor:** A Star-Reader initiate named Jorel, operating under [[Arch-Reader Celestine Vael]]'s authority.
**Location:** Archive House, ground floor.

| Item | Price |
|---|---|
| Map of the Veilshore (current, updated weekly) | 20 gc |
| Oldborn language primer (Star-Reader compiled, incomplete) | 30 gc |
| Primordial Anchor resonance reading (identifies Anchor proximity) | 15 gc |
| Artifact identification (per item, 24-hour turnaround) | 50 gc |
| Research consultation (1 hour with a Star-Reader scholar) | 25 gc |
| Loom-thread detection kit (experimental, detects temporal anomalies) | 100 gc |

**Flavor:** The Trade Desk is the most organized space in Tidecross, which is not saying much. Jorel is young, eager, and slightly overwhelmed by demand. The maps sell out constantly. The Loom-thread detection kit is new, untested, and may or may not work. Jorel sells it with full disclosure of its experimental nature.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Archive Trade Desk is also where the Star-Readers quietly recruit capable individuals for expeditions into the interior. Any party that buys a Loom-thread detection kit will be approached within 24 hours with an offer to field-test it, with generous compensation, on a trip toward [[The Loom's Edge]].

### The Salt and Sand (Tavern)

**Proprietor:** A one-armed sailor named Hark, who was among the first to cross the ocean and lost his arm to something in the water.
**Location:** Waterfront, built on the ancient Oldborn dock pilings.

| Drink | Price |
|---|---|
| Ale (mainland import, stale by the time it arrives) | 0.5 gc |
| Veilshore grog (local distillation, rough, effective) | 0.3 gc |
| Oldborn herb tea (purchased from Fernwald-edge traders, calming) | 1 gc |
| Meal (grilled local fish, black sand bread, unfamiliar but edible vegetables) | 1 gc |
| Room, per night (hammock in the loft, shared) | 2 gc |
| Room, per night (private, such as it is) | 5 gc |

**Atmosphere:** The tavern sits directly on the ancient docks, which means the floor is warm and faintly vibrating. Hark does not explain why. The clientele is a mix of every faction in Tidecross: Concord merchants, Dominion missionaries, independent explorers, and the occasional Oldborn observer who sits alone and watches everything. Arguments are common. Violence is surprisingly rare, Hark keeps a Primordial-charged stun rod behind the bar and is not shy about using it.

**Rumor hook:** "Hark lost his arm to something in the ocean during the first crossing. He doesn't talk about it. But if you buy him Oldborn herb tea (he's the only outlander who drinks it), he'll tell you what he saw beneath the water on the way over. It's not what anyone expects."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> What Hark saw: [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]] herself, vast and luminous, staring at his ship from below the waves. She did not attack. She *looked* at him, and in that look he understood that she was holding the ocean closed through sheer will, and the will was failing. His arm was lost to a tentacle of concentrated tidal energy, Yvenne reaching out, whether to help or harm he could not tell.
