## Description

Mount Thalvor is the largest peak in the Caldera Ring and the spiritual heart of the [[The Emberveil|Emberveil]]. Its summit stands higher than any other point in the region, perpetually wreathed in smoke that glows amber at sunrise and deep red at dusk. On clear days, the caldera rim is visible from [[Crucible]], a jagged crown of black rock venting columns of heat-shimmer into the sky. On overcast days, the mountain vanishes into its own haze, and the people below navigate by the warmth radiating from its flanks.

This is where [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]] descended. During the Theomachis, the Ember-Smith chose annihilation over surrender, burning his consciousness completely to reignite the world's cooling core. He walked into the caldera and did not walk out. The heat that warms the valleys, feeds the hot springs, and makes [[Cosmology and Magic#Forgeheart Steel|forgeheart steel]] possible, all of it flows upward from the place where a god chose to die working. The dwarves do not worship at Mount Thalvor. They work there. The distinction matters to them.

The volcano has been unusually quiet. [[Cinderfall]], built on its lower slopes, has documented three months without tremors or minor venting, the longest silence in recorded history. The summit steam has thinned. The caldera's glow, once visible at night from miles away, has dimmed to a sullen flicker. To outsiders, a quiet volcano is reassuring. To the people who live in its shadow and depend on its heat for everything, the silence is terrifying. A volcano that grumbles is a volcano that breathes. This one has stopped breathing.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The quieting is the Cooling made visible. [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] fire is weakening as the Second Silence drains the divine essence sustaining the world's core. The volcano's activity was never geological in the conventional sense, it was the exhaust of a divine forge still running on its creator's sacrifice. As that fuel thins, the forge's compensatory mechanism is working harder, the Deep Sound heard at [[Hearthdeep]], pulling energy from the volcanic system to maintain core output. The mountain is quieting because its heat is being redirected downward.
>
> If the compensatory mechanism overloads, the stored energy will release through the volcanic system in a single catastrophic pulse. The eruption would not follow normal volcanic patterns. It would be a surge of divine thermal energy through the entire Caldera Ring, triggering sympathetic eruptions at the Anvil, the Bellows, the Tongs, and the Quench in sequence. [[Brunhild Ironheart]] has seen the Builders' Circle's structural models. She has not shared them with the public.

## Encounters & Hazards

**The Ember Stairs.** A pilgrim route carved into the mountain's western face, rising from [[Cinderfall]] to the caldera rim. Forge-mages have traditionally climbed the Stairs to present their masterworks to the mountain, leaving offerings on stone platforms at each of the seven landings. The climb takes a full day in good conditions. Recently, offerings left on the upper landings have been found pushed back down the Stairs, deposited neatly on lower platforms as if returned by careful hands. The thermal vents along the route have reversed direction, drawing air downward instead of pushing it up, and climbers report a sensation of being gently but firmly discouraged from ascending. The mountain, it seems, is refusing gifts.

> [!example] Ember Stairs Ascent (Nimble)
> Lower Stairs (landings 1-4): Constitution DC 12 (heat, thin air). Upper Stairs (landings 5-7): Constitution DC 15, and reversed thermal vents create unpredictable downdrafts, Dex DC 14 or be pushed back one landing. Caldera rim: Will DC 16 to resist an overwhelming urge to turn back, not magical compulsion but a deep, bone-level certainty that you should not be here. PCs who reach the rim see the caldera floor far below, dimmer than any living person has seen it, pulsing in a slow, irregular rhythm like a faltering heartbeat.

**Thermal Instability.** The mountain's cooling is not uniform. Cold pockets alternate with sudden heat surges as the geothermal system redistributes unevenly. A vent that was lukewarm an hour ago can blast scalding steam without warning. The stone itself is unreliable, surfaces that were warm and stable can turn brittle as heat withdraws from them, crumbling underfoot.

**The Summit Silence.** At the caldera rim, sound behaves strangely. Voices carry too far or not at all. Echoes return with wrong timing. Dwarves who have climbed in previous decades describe the summit as "loud with Thalvor's breath," a constant roar of venting heat. Now it is quiet enough to hear your own heartbeat, and the silence presses against the ears like depth underwater.

## Hooks

1. **The Returned Offerings.** Three master forge-mages climbed the Ember Stairs last month to present their masterworks. All three found their offerings returned to the base of the Stairs the following morning, undamaged but cold. The forge-mages are shaken. In centuries of tradition, the mountain has never refused a gift. [[Ashara Cinderbloom]] at [[Cinderfall]] believes the reversed vents are connected to the springs' declining efficacy and wants someone to reach the caldera rim and measure what is happening.
2. **The Missing Expedition.** [[The Forge Council]] sent a three-person survey team to the summit two weeks ago to take thermal readings. They have not returned. Their last signal-fire was lit at the fifth landing. [[Brunhild Ironheart]] wants them found but cannot send a large party without causing public alarm.
3. **The Pilgrim Crisis.** Word of the returned offerings is spreading. Pilgrims who planned the traditional summit climb are cancelling or arriving at [[Cinderfall]] and refusing to ascend. The town's economy depends on pilgrimage traffic. Pilgrim-Warden Dask is urging honesty about the mountain's condition. The Spring Council fears that honesty will cause a panic that destroys Cinderfall faster than the Cooling will.
