## Description

The Abyssal Trench is a massive undersea rift running parallel to the Shattered Coast, visible from the surface only as a darkening of the water, a line where the ocean's blue deepens to black and does not lighten again. Fishermen who sail above it report their compasses spinning, their nets returning empty or full of things they cannot name, and a low vibration felt through the hull that some describe as breathing. The Trench's depth is unknown. Every survey attempt has failed, the instruments crushed, the chains snapped, the divers lost. The [[The Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keepers]] maintain a perimeter of marker buoys along its edge. Several have been dragged deeper in recent months, their anchor chains taut and descending into darkness at angles that suggest something below is collecting them.

This is where [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], Goddess of Shadow and Secrets, was imprisoned during the Silence. The surviving gods judged her role in the Triumvirate of Ruin and debated her destruction. [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]] argued for imprisonment instead. The cage was forged from [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]]'s divine bone, order made manifest in latticed architecture, and sealed with Yvenne's own essence, a lock keyed to the Sea-Goddess herself. For two thousand years, the prison held. It is no longer holding well. The Black Tides that batter [[The Hollowed Reach|the Reach's]] coast are the seal cracking. The [[Sleepwalkers]] who walk into the sea are Serith's whispers given direction. The [[Trench Crawlers]] and [[Depth Leviathans]] surfacing with increasing boldness are the Trench's ecology responding to a change in pressure that has nothing to do with water.

No mortal has reached the bottom of the Trench and returned. What is known about its structure comes from three sources: Lantern-Keeper survey data (reliable but shallow), [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] doctrine (detailed but filtered through Serith's intentions), and the possessed utterances of [[Dren Halwick]] in Whisper-Cell Seven at Veil Harbor (fragmented, disturbing, and increasingly specific).

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The prison is failing. Each Black Tide weakens the seal. The acceleration is not random: the intervals between Black Tides have compressed from months to weeks, following a logarithmic curve that, if projected, reaches zero within the campaign's timeframe.
>
> Serith does not want to escape violently. She wants to be *invited* out. The [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]]'s rituals are her preferred method, a willing mortal opening the seal from outside, which would circumvent the divine lock without triggering whatever failsafe the surviving gods may have built in. Brute force would work eventually, but Serith is patient, and patience is cheaper than war.
>
> An expedition to the Trench would be a late-campaign event (Act IV-V). Throughout Acts I-III, the Trench should be feared, referenced, and never directly entered. Its presence is felt through consequences: the tides, the whispers, the creatures, the slow erosion of everything [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]] built to keep the coast safe.

## Depth Zones

The Trench descends in four distinct zones, each a threshold that demands more to cross.

**The Shelf.** Where the continental shelf fractures and the descent begins. Coral formations grow here in geometries that no natural process produces, spirals that tighten into angles, branches that curve back to meet themselves. [[Trench Crawlers]] surface at the Shelf's edge, pale and pressurized, their bodies splitting in the shallower water. The Lantern-Keeper buoys mark this boundary. Below the Shelf, the water temperature drops sharply and the light begins to fail. A ship can reach the Shelf. Returning from it requires knowing when to turn around.

**The Fog Line.** A thermocline where warm water rising from the Trench's depths meets the cold ocean above. This is where the perpetual coastal fog originates, not weather but exhalation, the Trench breathing out. Visibility drops to nothing. Sound distorts: echoes arrive before the noise that caused them, voices carry from directions that contain no speakers, and the hull of a ship groans in harmonics that its construction cannot produce. [[Fog Singers]] congregate at the Fog Line, their calls amplified by the acoustic distortion. Ships that cross the Fog Line rarely return. The Driftmere survivor is the only known exception in living memory, and the geometric burns on her arms suggest she was sent back deliberately.

**The Watcher's Depth.** Below the Fog Line, the Trench narrows and the pressure becomes lethal to unprotected mortals. Here dwells the entity the Lantern-Keepers call the Watcher Below, not a creature but a living ward, a fragment of divine will left behind by the gods who built the prison. It is vast, slow, and growing agitated. Its movements displace water in volumes sufficient to cause the tidal surges that wreck coastal ships. It is not hostile to mortals. It does not notice mortals. It senses the prison weakening and cannot repair what it was made to guard, and its distress is measured in waves that kill fishermen who have never heard its name.

**The Cage.** The prison itself, at a depth no mortal has survived. Solvaen's divine bone forms a lattice of absolute geometric precision, each bar a line of pure order, each intersection a law that says *this far and no further*. Yvenne's essence fills the spaces between, a seal of living ocean that recognizes only the Sea-Goddess's will. No one has seen this. The descriptions come from Serith herself, whispered through the Veil Unbound's communion rituals and through Dren Halwick's episodes of possession. The [[Sleepwalkers]] who reach the Cage press their hands against the bone and dissolve the wards, fraction by fraction, each one a prayer Serith taught them in their sleep. She is patient. The fractions accumulate.

## Encounters & Hazards

Each depth zone escalates the danger. The Shelf presents natural hazards: Trench Crawlers, unstable coral, and currents that pull toward the deep. The Fog Line adds sensory distortion, navigational failure, and Fog Singer ambushes. The Watcher's Depth introduces crushing pressure, total darkness, and the Watcher's unintentional violence, its wake alone can shatter a ship's hull. At every depth, Serith's psychic presence intensifies. On the Shelf, it manifests as unease and bad dreams. At the Fog Line, thoughts begin to blur, memories surface unbidden, and the distinction between your own inner voice and something else's suggestion becomes difficult to maintain. At the Watcher's Depth, the whispers are no longer subtle. She speaks clearly, calmly, and what she says is reasonable, and that is the most dangerous thing about it.

[[Tideborn Horrors]] can be encountered at any depth, creatures born from the Black Tides that carry the Trench's corruption in their bodies. They are more common near the Fog Line, where the conditions for their generation are strongest.

The Trench's environmental progression strips away mortal certainty in layers. Temperature drops from cold to killing. Light fails from dim to absolute. Sound warps from unreliable to actively deceptive. And beneath all of it, Serith's presence grows, not louder but *clearer*, as if the noise of the surface world was what obscured her, and the deep is simply where she has always been speaking at her natural volume.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Watcher Below is not hostile to mortals, it simply does not perceive them as relevant. Its agitation is focused entirely on the prison's integrity. However, its wake generates pressure waves capable of capsizing ships and its body displaces water volumes that create the tidal surges devastating the coast. If PCs somehow communicated with it, the Watcher would convey a single concept: *failing*. Not anger. Not warning. A status report from a ward that has been alone for two thousand years and cannot do the one thing it was made to do.

## Hooks

1. **The Descent.** [[Corvin Tyren]] at [[Keel's Landing]] is building a ship designed to reach the Trench floor, an Ashite-plated vessel called the *Descent*. The Concord has not approved the expedition. Corvin is testing anyway and offering a fortune for crew willing to sail into the dark.

2. **The Dragged Buoys.** Lantern-Keeper survey buoys along the Shelf are being pulled deeper, their anchor chains taut and descending at impossible angles. Something is collecting them. Commander [[Lira Ashvane]] wants a team to investigate before the perimeter fails entirely.

3. **The Light Below.** A Black Tide survivor, recovered from driftwood three miles off the Shattered Coast, claims she saw light at the bottom of the Trench. Structured light, geometric, like a city. She does not know what a divine bone lattice looks like. She described one perfectly.
