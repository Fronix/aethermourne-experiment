## Description

The Ashborn Tundra is what most of the Pale Wastes actually is: hundreds of miles of frozen plains rendered in shades of grey and bone-white, where hardy lichens and frost-grass cling to permafrost that has not thawed in living memory. It is featureless in the way that open ocean is featureless, a vastness that erases the eye's ability to judge distance or direction. The sky is low and grey. The wind is constant. The cold is not weather. It is the ambient necromantic chill of [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s remains radiating through the earth, a temperature that bites deeper than windchill alone can explain.

Bone-cairn route markers are the tundra's circulatory system. Built from stacked divine bone fragments by generations of Vigil engineers, each cairn glows with a faint necromantic luminescence visible at roughly one mile under clear conditions. The routes connect every Hold in the Wastes, cairn to cairn, a network of faintly glowing waypoints across the grey. Under clear skies with roughly two miles of visibility, a traveler can see two cairns ahead, enough to maintain direction. During bone-storms, visibility drops to yards, and the cairns vanish. Getting lost between cairns in a storm is a death sentence measured in hours.

Frost-elk are the Wastes' answer to the tundra's hostility. Standing seven feet at the shoulder, with pale fur and antlers branching like frozen lightning, they are domesticated mounts, draft animals, and an early warning system. Frost-elk sense undead at a distance no one has precisely measured, and their behavior is the most reliable indicator of danger on the tundra. A herd that refuses to move forward has detected something ahead. A herd that bolts has detected something close. Experienced drovers trust the elk's judgment over their own eyes, a habit that has saved more lives than any weapon.

The aurora overhead shifts color based on proximity to [[The Hollowdeep]]. In the southern Wastes near [[Ember's Rest]], it runs green-silver, almost beautiful. As you travel north toward the crater, the light bleaches to grey-blue, and a low vibration settles into the chest, not a sound but a pressure, a hum that the body feels before the ears register. Locals call it the Hum. It means you are close enough to the Hollowdeep that the dead god's presence has weight.

Bone-storms have increased roughly forty percent in frequency over the past decade, and recent storms carry whispers. Not the ambient murmur of necromantic energy that bone-dust always produces, but structured sounds, syllables arranged with intent. The whispers do not belong to [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]. They belong to something that is using the storms as a channel.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The whispers in the bone-storms are [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s doing. She has learned to encode information into the necromantic particulate, using the storms as a communication channel to transmit tactical intelligence about Hold defenses, patrol schedules, and population counts outward from the [[The Hollowdeep|Hollowdeep]]. The storms carry this data to [[Cairn Knights|Cairn Knight]] relay posts positioned across the tundra, where the dead receive their orders through the bone-dust itself.
>
> Organized undead patrols of thirty or more soldiers have been spotted on the tundra in silent march, moving between positions with military discipline. These are advance elements of the Hollowdeep army, mapping routes and testing response times. They avoid Holds and known patrol routes, moving at night across terrain the living consider impassable.
>
> **Campaign Role.** The Ashborn Tundra is the corridor the army will march south through when the assault begins. PCs who discover the advance patrols, decode the bone-storm whispers, or notice the cairn-route disruptions gain early warning of the invasion timeline. The tundra's vast emptiness makes conventional scouting nearly impossible, which is exactly what Serith is counting on.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Bone-Storms.** The tundra's deadliest hazard. A bone-storm lasts 1d4+1 hours and reduces visibility to 10 feet. Characters caught in a storm face three effects: death-sight (DC 13 Will or perceive spirits for 1d4 hours), tissue necrosis (DC 13 Constitution every hour or suffer 1d4 necrotic damage and blackened extremities), and spell contamination (DC 15 Will for spellcasters or suffer unpredictable magical distortion). See [[Travel Rules and Hazards]] for full mechanics. Recent storms additionally carry structured whispers, DC 16 Perception to distinguish words, DC 18 Arcana to recognize the whispers as encoded communication rather than ambient necromantic noise.

**[[Cairn Knights]] on Patrol.** Organized formations of six to twelve undead soldiers moving across the tundra in disciplined silence. They follow set routes and respond to intruders with military tactics: shield walls, flanking, coordinated withdrawal. Encountering a patrol far from the Hollowdeep is alarming, it means the army's reach has extended well beyond the crater.

**Frost-Elk Warnings.** Frost-elk behavior serves as a passive detection system. If the party travels with elk, the GM can telegraph danger through animal behavior: ears forward and feet planted (undead within half a mile, stationary), circling and stamping (undead within a quarter mile, moving), full bolt (undead within 200 feet, hostile). A DC 12 Nature check interprets the elk's behavior correctly.

**Cairn-Route Darkness.** Something is consuming the necromantic energy that powers the bone-cairns along a forty-mile stretch of the northern route between [[Greyhold]] and [[Lastlight]]. The cairns have gone dark, their glow extinguished. Travelers on this stretch must navigate by dead reckoning (DC 14 Survival every 4 hours, failure means 1d4 hours lost in a random direction). What is feeding on the cairns' energy is unknown and has not been seen directly.

**The Hum.** Within fifty miles of [[The Hollowdeep]], characters feel a low vibration in the chest that grows stronger with proximity. The Hum imposes no mechanical penalty but makes rest difficult (DC 12 Will to achieve a full rest within the Hum's range). Characters who have previously experienced bone-pull feel the Hum sooner and more intensely.

## Hooks

1. **The Dark Route.** Bone-cairns along a forty-mile stretch of the Greyhold-Lastlight route have gone dark, stranding a trade caravan somewhere in the unlit corridor. [[Greyhold]]'s Holdwarden needs a rescue party that can navigate without cairns and, if possible, determine what is draining them. The caravan carries a season's worth of [[Cosmology and Magic#Morrhaelite|Morrhaelite]] bound for southern markets, making it a high-value target for both the living and the dead.

2. **The Herds Know.** Frost-elk herds across the central tundra have abandoned their traditional grazing routes and are migrating south in numbers not seen in decades. Drovers report the animals are terrified, refusing to face north regardless of incentive. Something along the northern routes has spooked every elk in the region simultaneously. Following the path the herds are fleeing from leads toward [[Cairn Knights|Cairn Knight]] patrol routes that should not exist this far from the Hollowdeep.

3. **Storm Whispers.** A bone-storm deposited something besides dust and bone-fragments over [[Bonecairn]]: whispers containing recognizable names and locations. A harvester swears she heard her own Hold's name, the number of its defenders, and the word "south" repeated in a cadence that sounded like a march count. She is not the first to report this, but she is the first to write it down. The list she recorded matches no known intelligence report, because it is not Vigil intelligence. It is the enemy's.
