## Description

The Ashfall Slopes are the outer flanks of [[The Caldera Ring]], descending from the peaks toward [[The Ashen Dominion]] to the east and [[The Hollowed Reach]] to the south. Centuries of eruptions have deposited layers of extraordinarily rich volcanic soil across these slopes, and the dwarven and human communities who live here have terraced them into orchards, vineyards, and grain fields that produce distinctive wines, fruits, and spices found nowhere else. The eastern slopes are steeper and drier, facing the Ashfields. The southern slopes are gentler, catching moisture from the coastal weather systems of the Reach. [[Smokewall]] sits at the point where the eastern slopes meet the Tongs pass, the primary crossing into Dominion territory.

The people of the Ashfall Slopes are harder and more independent than their valley cousins. They are the first to feel ashfall when the peaks vent, the first to see Dominion scouts on the ridgelines, and the last to receive [[The Forge Council|Forge Council]] aid when either becomes a problem. Villages here are smaller and more exposed, clusters of stone-and-slag houses with reinforced roofs built to shed hot ash. Evacuation routes are taught to children alongside their letters. The slope-dwellers rebuild without complaint and resent without forgetting. [[Apprentice Jyn]], the first non-dwarf accepted to the Grand Forge in a generation, was born in one of these villages, a human child whose talent for forge-magic emerged in a community where dwarves and humans have worked side by side for centuries without the valley's obsession with guild hierarchy.

This is the Emberveil's first line of defense and its most exposed flank. [[The Ashen Dominion]] has always coveted the region's resources, and the slopes are where that covetousness becomes physical. Dominion scouts have been detained this season carrying detailed topographical maps of water sources, defensible positions, and road conditions. Not soldiers, but the kind of people soldiers send ahead of them. [[Tarn Smokeshield]] at [[Smokewall]] considers the eastern slopes a theater of slow-motion invasion and is not entirely wrong.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Dominion's direct trade offers to individual slope villages are [[Delric Mourne]]'s initiative, bypassing normal diplomacy to establish economic leverage on the Emberveil's periphery. If enough villages sign independent agreements, the [[The Forge Council|Forge Council's]] trade monopoly weakens, creating political fractures that [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] eventual agents can exploit. The strategy is patient and deniable, each agreement looks reasonable in isolation, and only the pattern reveals the intent.
>
> The glowing fruit appearing on the western slopes is a symptom of the compensatory mechanism pushing thermal energy into new, uncharted pathways. The trees' roots have reached a previously unknown geothermal deposit activated by the thermal redistribution. The fruit is not dangerous, its minor restorative properties are genuine, but its existence proves that the underground thermal map of the Emberveil is changing in ways no one predicted. What else might the new pathways be feeding?

## Encounters & Hazards

**Ashfall Events.** Active vents on the Caldera Ring periodically shower the slopes with hot volcanic ash. Ashfall imposes a -2 penalty to Perception checks, and prolonged exposure (four or more hours) requires a DC 12 Constitution save or gain one level of exhaustion from inhaling particulate. A cloth mask or wet cloth over the mouth negates the breathing hazard but not the visibility penalty.

**Unstable Terrain.** Eruption deposits create layers of compacted ash that look solid but conceal hollow spaces beneath. DC 12 Perception to notice the subtle give before stepping onto false ground. Collapse: fall into a pocket 5-15 feet deep, DC 13 Dexterity save or 1d6 bludgeoning damage and difficult extraction.

**Dominion Scouts.** Patrols from [[The Ashen Dominion]] operating on the eastern slopes, ranging from legitimate trade envoys to covert surveyors mapping the region's vulnerabilities. Encounters may be diplomatic, tense, or hostile depending on what the scouts are carrying and whether they have been caught before.

**[[The Quenched]].** The upper slopes, where the Cooling has created thermal confusion between warm volcanic stone and cooling air, attract these half-forged constructs. They wander the treeline where orchards give way to bare rock, drawn to the transitional zone between heat and cold.

## Hooks

1. **The Cut-Off Village.** A slope village on the southern face has been isolated by a fresh lava flow from a minor vent that should have been dormant for decades. The flow is narrow, no more than twenty feet across, but molten and impassable. The village has supplies for two weeks. [[The Forge Council]] considers a rescue operation across active volcanic terrain too risky. The village elder has sent a runner to [[Smokewall]] begging for help, and [[Tarn Smokeshield]] is looking for anyone willing to attempt the crossing or find an alternate route through the upper slopes.

2. **The Generous Terms.** Dominion trade envoys have been visiting individual slope villages, offering direct trade agreements that bypass [[The Forge Council]] and [[Smokewall]]'s customs enforcement. The terms are generous, above-market prices for produce, guaranteed purchase volumes, no tariffs. Three villages have signed already. [[Tarn Smokeshield]] suspects economic warfare but needs proof before he can act. The villages that signed see a lifeline from a Council that has never prioritized their needs. Both perspectives have merit.

3. **The Glowing Harvest.** An orchard on the western slopes is producing fruit that glows faintly at night, warm to the touch and tasting of forge-smoke and honey. The trees' roots have reached a previously unknown geothermal deposit, and the fruit has minor restorative properties, easing fatigue and warming the body for hours after eating. Word is spreading. Merchants from [[Anvilrest]] and [[Crucible]] are already making offers. The orchard's owner, a human farmer named Pell, wants to understand what is happening to his trees before he sells anything. He is wise to be cautious.
