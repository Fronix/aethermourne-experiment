## Description

The Ashfields are vast plains of pale grey soil surrounding the Spine of Order, formed from [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] death-exhalation compacted over two millennia into something surprisingly fertile. They stretch hundreds of miles in every direction from the Spine, flattening into gentle rolling country that would be unremarkable if not for the color. Everything is grey. The soil, the grass, the sky on overcast days. Silver-green wheat ripples in fields that shimmer like pewter under sunlight. Orchards produce apples the color of old iron, sweet but faintly metallic on the tongue.

The Dominion's breadbasket feeds millions. The food is safe, generations have eaten nothing else, but those generations trend toward orderliness and obedience in ways that scholars outside the Dominion find unsettling. Whether this is Solvaen's lingering influence in the soil or simply the culture that grows in a theocracy is a question no one inside the Dominion is permitted to ask.

The road from [[Ostivaar]] to [[Pale Sentry]] crosses the Ashfields and deteriorates steadily as it leaves settled territory, a 10-12 day journey through increasingly empty country where the only landmarks are the Fingers and the occasional farming village clustered around them.

## Solvaen's Fingers

Scattered across the Ashfields are protruding fragments of divine skeleton, pale shards of bone rising from the grey earth like broken teeth. The smallest are waist-high. The largest, like the one at [[Greyfield]], stand thirty feet tall, smooth and faintly warm to the touch. They radiate ambient magical energy, a gentle version of the Spine's structured power, enough to improve crop yields, stabilize weather patterns, and provide a weak but reliable source for [[Cosmology and Magic#Calculism|Calculist]] focuses.

Communities cluster around good Fingers. A village with a strong, undepleted Finger is prosperous: crops grow thick, livestock are healthy, and the local Calculist can maintain wards against ash storms and [[Calcified Stalkers]]. A village whose Finger has depleted, its energy spent or drained, is desperate. Crops wither. The ash storms hit harder. People leave, or they petition the Church for relocation, which means abandoning everything.

The Fingers are depleting. Not all of them, not yet, but the pattern is accelerating. Church officials insist this is natural, that Fingers cycle through periods of activity and dormancy. The farmers who depend on them are less certain.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Finger depletion is not natural. [[Delric Mourne]]'s Cinders have been quietly redirecting mining operations away from rich Ashite veins and toward locations that destabilize Finger energy networks. The effect is subtle, almost impossible to trace without understanding the underground Ashite vein topology, but it is systematic. Every depleted Finger weakens the Dominion's agricultural base and increases dependence on Church-controlled supply chains. Mourne is starving the countryside to consolidate power.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Ash Storms.** Seasonal near the Ashfields, more frequent in late autumn and early spring. Visibility drops to 30 feet. Fine particles of divine ash scour exposed skin.

> [!example] Ash Storm (Nimble)
> Visibility 6 spaces. 1d4 slashing damage per round (unprotected). Str DC 12 to maintain footing in gusts. Duration: 1d4 hours. Locals wrap cloth over nose and mouth and shelter behind Fingers, which break the wind. Ashite focuses malfunction during storms (20% chance per use).

**[[Calcified Stalkers]]** hunt across the Ashfields in tessellation patterns, geometric patrol routes that are predictable if you know the mathematics and lethal if you don't. They are drawn to movement near depleted Fingers, where the ambient energy no longer masks living signatures.

## Hooks

1. **The Dying Finger.** [[Greyfield]]'s Finger, the thirty-foot shard that sustains the village, has begun to dim. Elder Seren Holloway has sent word to [[Ostivaar]] requesting Church assessment, but the response has been delayed for months. A Hewn-caste crop warden named Jorik Ashsoil has been tracking the depletion with his own crude measurements and believes the drain is coming from underground, not from the Finger's natural cycle. He needs someone to investigate the root network beneath the fields.
2. **The Grey Harvest.** A caravan of Ashfield grain bound for [[Cinderholm]] has gone missing on the plains. The Church blames bandits. The farmers blame [[Calcified Stalkers]]. The Cinder patrol that was supposed to escort the caravan never showed up.
3. **The Walking Finger.** Farmers near the eastern Ashfields swear that a Finger moved overnight, leaving a furrow in the soil where it dragged itself three hundred yards south. The Church has declared this impossible. The furrow is still there.
