## Description

The Bloom Peaks are the western mountains of [[The Verdant Marches]], where [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s growth-essence saturates the stone itself and drives vegetation through seasonal cycles compressed into impossibly short spans. At the base of the range, cycles run daily: flowers at dawn, seeds at noon, frost at dusk, bare stone at midnight, and new shoots before the next sunrise. Higher up, the cycles were once slower, measured in weeks. Now they are faster than the lowlands, hourly oscillations that turn mountainsides into strobing displays of color, then white, then bare grey, then color again. The mountains breathe seasons the way lungs breathe air.

The atmosphere is the primary hazard. Pollen and spores from a thousand simultaneous bloom-cycles saturate the air in concentrations that would be impossible in a normal ecosystem. The effect is cumulative: mild euphoria at first, then disorientation, then hallucinations that range from vividly beautiful to lethally distracting. A traveler watching a non-existent sunrise can walk off a cliff feeling nothing but wonder. The [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] who maintain shrines in the peaks wrap their faces in treated moss-cloth and limit their exposure to hours. [[Sporeheim]], the settlement built into the mountain caves above the worst of the pollen layer, survives by elevation and by the hard-won knowledge of which spores to breathe and which to flee.

What makes the Bloom Peaks more than a hazard zone is the prophecy. Some of the hallucinations are true. The spores of certain high-altitude flora, species that grow and die in minutes, carry chemical compounds that interact with the human mind in ways the [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] call Sight and the Mutagenists of [[Mirewatch]] call "neurochemical interference with predictive modeling." The distinction matters less than the result: people who breathe the right spores at the right time see things that have not happened yet, and those things come true. [[Ivara Mistwalker]], [[Sporeheim]]'s Spore-Seer, has built her life around distinguishing prophetic visions from lethal ones. Her success rate is not perfect. The graves outside Sporeheim belong to the people who guessed wrong.

Rare alchemical ingredients grow in the Bloom Peaks and nowhere else, organisms that complete their entire lifecycle in hours and produce compounds that slower-growing plants never develop. Alchemists from across the continent pay extraordinary prices for Bloom-harvested reagents. The harvesting is itself dangerous: the plants that produce the rarest compounds grow in the highest-cycle zones, where exposure times are measured in minutes before the pollen overwhelms even protected harvesters.

## The Acceleration

The cycles are speeding up. What was daily is now hourly. What was hourly is approaching minute-by-minute. The atmosphere at mid-elevation has become dangerous even for adapted inhabitants. [[Sporeheim]]'s Seeing Chamber, where Spore-Seers commune with prophetic spores under controlled conditions, now requires shorter sessions because the visions arrive faster, overlap, and blur into incoherence. [[Ivara Mistwalker]] describes recent sessions as "trying to read a book while someone flips the pages."

The practical consequences are mounting. Harvest windows have shrunk. Travel routes that were passable during slow-cycle windows now require precise timing. The pollen concentration at lower elevations is increasing as faster cycles produce more spores per day. Animals adapted to daily rhythms cannot adjust to hourly ones and are dying or fleeing downslope into [[The Verdant Marches#Thyrea's Cradle|the Cradle]], adding to the displacement crisis already caused by the deep-forest corruption.

### The Frozen Peak

One summit in the central range has stopped cycling entirely. No growth, no frost, no pollen. Bare grey stone in a ring of mountains that pulse with seasonal color. The peak is visible from [[Sporeheim]] on clear days, a dead tooth in a living jaw. The [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] sent a scouting party. They found the vegetation dessicated, the soil cold, and a smell of brine where there should be none. They did not stay long. The silence, they said, was wrong.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The acceleration is a symptom of [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s shadow-root corruption reaching the Bloom Peaks' root systems. [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s growth-essence, pushed into overdrive by the corruption's destabilizing influence, is cycling faster because the underlying divine signal is being jammed, the roots carrying Thyrea's seasonal instructions are receiving interference that distorts the timing.
>
> The Frozen Peak is where a shadow-root has killed the local root-network entirely. The vegetation did not wither naturally, it was cut off from Thyrea's essence and died in a single moment, mid-cycle. The brine smell is Serith's signature. The silence is the absence of a divine signal that has hummed beneath these mountains for two thousand years. If the corruption spreads, more peaks will go silent. If enough of the network dies, the Bloom Peaks' entire ecosystem collapses, and the pollen-dependent species (including the prophetic flora) go extinct.
>
> **Campaign Hook.** The Frozen Peak is a preview of what happens when Serith's corruption wins. Investigating it reveals the shadow-root mechanism and provides evidence that [[Senna Deepmoss]] would accept as proof of external interference. [[Ivara Mistwalker]] suspects the truth but needs someone to confirm it, someone willing to climb to the dead peak and bring back what they find.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Pollen Intoxication.** DC 12 Will save per hour of exposure at mid-elevation (lower elevations during acceleration surges). Failure: mild euphoria and disadvantage on Perception for 1 hour. Three or more cumulative failures: sleep 1d4 hours in place, vulnerable to environmental hazards and predators. Treated moss-cloth (available at [[Sporeheim]]) grants advantage on the save. Full face covering with sealed eyewear grants immunity but imposes disadvantage on Perception from restricted senses.

**Prophetic Spores.** DC 14 Will save when encountering high-altitude bloom zones. Failure: hallucinate for 1d4 hours (GM determines content, mix of beautiful imagery and disorienting sensory overload). Success: one cryptic but true vision of a future event (campaign clue). Critical success (beat DC by 5+): clear, specific vision. The [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] consider this a gift. The Mutagenists consider it a side effect. Both are correct.

**Cycle Hazards.** Rapid seasonal shifts create environmental traps: frost forming on handholds mid-climb, flowering vines sealing cave entrances in minutes, sudden pollen bursts during bloom-surges. Navigating the peaks during acceleration requires DC 13 Dexterity saves to avoid environmental damage (1d6 cold from frost-snaps, 1d4 poison from concentrated pollen bursts).

**Predators.** Bloom-adapted raptors hunt during transition moments when prey is disoriented by cycle shifts. They strike from above during frost-to-bloom transitions when the sudden explosion of color and scent overwhelms other senses. A nest of cycle-hawks near the Frozen Peak has grown aggressive, their hunting patterns disrupted by the dead zone.

## Hooks

1. **The Accelerating Crisis.** [[Sporeheim]]'s elders report the cycles are now faster than anyone alive has seen. Harvesters cannot work safely. The Seeing Chamber's visions are becoming unreliable. [[Ivara Mistwalker]] is requesting outside help from anyone willing to investigate the cause.

2. **The Frozen Peak.** A dead summit surrounded by living mountains. The [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]]' scout report was inconclusive. Someone needs to climb it, document what killed it, and bring back evidence. [[Senna Deepmoss]] would pay well for proof that the corruption is external and not a natural cycle.

3. **The Last Harvest.** A rare alchemical bloom, one that produces a compound capable of neutralizing Marrow-Water mutation, grows on a peak that has just entered rapid cycling. The harvest window is shrinking by the day. [[Vael Sundren]] at [[Mirewatch]] needs the compound for her research and cannot spare her own people for the climb.
