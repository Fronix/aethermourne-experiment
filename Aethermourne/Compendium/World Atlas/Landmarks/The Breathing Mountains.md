## Description

The Breathing Mountains are a mountain range in the central Primordia that rises and falls over the course of days, as though the earth itself is inhaling and exhaling. Valleys become peaks. Peaks become valleys. Ridgelines shift like the ribs of a sleeping giant. The [[The Oldborn Kingdoms|Oldborn]] call this [[The Primordials#Thaelun|Thaelun]], the Primordial of Simultaneous Multiplicity, dreaming aloud through the stone, the mountains expressing every possible configuration of themselves in slow, grinding rotation.

The range stretches roughly sixty miles east to west, a barrier between the Primordia's deeper chaos and the more stable lands near [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]]. At any given moment, the mountains present a different face: jagged alpine peaks one week, rolling foothills the next, sheer cliffs the week after. The cycle takes approximately nine days from peak height to valley depth and back again, though the timing has grown irregular in recent years.

The Breathing Mountains are extraordinarily beautiful. During the rising phase, stone flowers bloom from fresh cliff faces, mineral formations that catch the light in prismatic patterns. During the falling phase, rivers appear from nowhere, cascading down newly formed slopes in waterfalls that will not exist tomorrow. The air smells of mineral dust and ozone, and the sound of the mountains' movement, a deep, subsonic grinding felt more than heard, is strangely hypnotic.

They are also extraordinarily dangerous. Being caught in a valley when it rises means being crushed beneath thousands of tons of ascending stone. Being caught on a peak when it falls means plunging into a chasm that opens beneath your feet. The Oldborn navigate the range by reading the stone's vibration patterns, predicting the cycle's progression, and timing their crossings to the brief windows between movements. Outlanders who attempt passage without this knowledge rarely survive.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Breathing Mountains' cycle is destabilizing. The nine-day rhythm has shortened to seven, then to six. The Silence-Watchers at [[Anchorseat]] believe the acceleration is connected to the Shimmer's expansion, as [[The Gyre]]'s influence strengthens, Primordial expressions like the Mountains become more volatile.
>
> There is a deeper concern. The Oldborn's Pact-Weavers maintain a centuries-old agreement with Thaelun's local expression that prevents the Mountains from rising above a certain height or falling below a certain depth. This pact is straining. If it breaks, the Mountains' cycle will become unlimited, potentially rising miles high or collapsing into bottomless chasms. The surrounding regions would be devastated.
>
> The Breathing Mountains also contain something the Oldborn do not discuss: a network of caverns that only exist during the falling phase, chambers that open for hours before being sealed again by the rising stone. Within these transient caverns, explorers have reported finding carvings that predate the Covenant of Ash, records from the Oldborn's erased pre-Covenant history. The Circle of Speakers has forbidden investigation. The carvings are too dangerous to be known.

## Encounters & Hazards

**The Cycle.** The primary hazard is the mountains themselves. During the rising phase (approximately 4.5 days), all low ground ascends. Camps must be struck and moved to already-high positions. During the falling phase (approximately 4.5 days), all high ground descends. Travelers must descend ahead of the collapse or risk being swallowed. The transition between phases, roughly two hours of violent geological upheaval, is the most dangerous window.

**Mineral Predators.** The Breathing Mountains host unique fauna: creatures of living stone that ride the cycle, hibernating during calm phases and hunting during transitions. They resemble angular, crystalline wolves, pack hunters that use the geological chaos as cover. Their bite carries trace Primordial energy that causes localized petrification, skin hardening to stone around the wound.

**Transient Geography.** Paths, passes, and landmarks change with each cycle. A route that worked last week may not exist this week. Navigation requires either Oldborn guidance or the ability to read the stone's vibration patterns, a skill that takes years to develop.

**The Hypnotic Grind.** The subsonic vibration of the mountains' movement has a measurable effect on mortal cognition. Extended exposure induces a trance-like state, a sense of deep peace and belonging, as though the listener is becoming part of the cycle. This is Thaelun's influence, the Primordial's dreaming touching mortal minds. Resisting requires active effort. Succumbing means sitting down, closing your eyes, and waiting for the stone to rise around you.

## Hooks

1. **The Timed Crossing.** The PCs need to cross the Breathing Mountains to reach [[The Loom's Edge]] from Oldborn territory. An Oldborn guide offers to lead them, but the window is narrow: three days to traverse a range that is actively rearranging itself. Timing, trust, and endurance are tested. Failure means waiting nine days for the next window, or attempting the crossing mid-cycle.

2. **The Transient Caverns.** An Oldborn dissident approaches the PCs with a proposition: during the next falling phase, a cavern network will open for approximately six hours. Inside are carvings from before the Covenant of Ash, the Oldborn's erased history. The dissident wants the PCs to document the carvings before the stone seals them again. The Circle of Speakers has forbidden this. Discovery means severe consequences.

3. **The Broken Pact.** The Pact-Weavers' agreement with Thaelun's local expression has failed during the PCs' crossing. The mountains begin rising beyond their normal limits, reaching heights that block the sun. The Oldborn guide panics. The PCs must either help renegotiate the pact (requiring a form of magic they may not understand) or find a way through mountains that no longer obey any rules.
