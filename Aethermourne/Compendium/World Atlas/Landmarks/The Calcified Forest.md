## Description

Old-growth woodland transformed by [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] lingering essence into white stone. The transformation is not uniform. At the outer edges, the trees merely look wrong: bark bleached to bone-white, leaves stiff as parchment, branches that do not sway in the wind. Walk deeper and the stiffness becomes rigidity, trunks of pale porcelain, branches fused into geometric lattices, the forest utterly silent. No birds. No insects. No wind. Sound carries strangely here, footsteps echo off surfaces that should absorb them, and whispers travel farther than they should.

At the center, the trees are no longer recognizable as trees. They are fully mineralized stone pillars, lifeless cathedrals of petrified wood arranged in patterns too precise to be natural. The grain of the original bark has been replaced by fine geometric lines, Solvaen's mathematical signature preserved in crystal. The air is dry, still, and carries a faint mineral scent like old chalk. Light enters the canopy in columns that do not move with the sun.

Strange calcified fauna inhabit the deeper zones: deer with bone-stone antlers that click as they turn their heads, wolves with ceramic hides that move in coordinated geometric patterns, birds that glide on rigid wings in perfect circles. They are alive, warm-blooded, but their behavior has been ordered, stripped of randomness, reduced to pure mathematical function. They do not startle. They do not flee. They simply adjust their patterns to incorporate new variables, including visitors.

The [[Order of the Ashen Flame|Church]] considers the Forest sacred ground. Entering without a Church-issued permit is a crime punishable by reduction to Ashless caste. In practice, this prohibition serves two purposes: it preserves the Forest as a spiritual site, and it ensures that no one investigates too closely what Solvaen's lingering mind is still doing to the landscape.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The calcification is spreading. Slowly, a few inches per year at the outer boundary, but measurably. The Church's own surveyors have documented the expansion and buried the reports. More troubling: the geometric patterns in the deep interior are becoming erratic. Where the calcified fauna once moved in clean, orderly tessellations, they now trace patterns that contain anomalies, stutters, repetitions, as if the underlying mathematical order is developing errors. This mirrors the discordant note detected in the Spine's Hum. [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] influence is introducing entropy into Solvaen's remaining order, and the Forest is where the corruption shows first because the Forest is where Solvaen's essence is least diluted by human activity.
>
> An expelled [[Ashveil]] student who fled into the deep Forest reported finding structures that predate the Dominion, non-calcified stone buildings beneath the petrified canopy. The Church sealed the report. The student has not been seen since.

## Encounters & Hazards

The Forest suppresses spontaneous thought. Visitors report difficulty making decisions, generating creative solutions, or remembering why they entered. The effect intensifies with depth.

> [!example] Calcified Forest (Nimble)
> Off-trail: Perception DCs +2 (sound distortion). Will DC 14 or lose 1d4 hours (temporal disorientation, wandering without awareness). Deep interior: Will DC increases to 16, lost time increases to 1d8 hours.

**[[Order Wraiths]]** inhabit the deep interior. They are not undead but recordings, Solvaen's dying thoughts imprinted on mineralized wood and given form. They appear as translucent white figures tracing repetitive geometric routes through the stone pillars. In the outer forest they are faint, barely visible. At the center they are bright enough to cast shadows, moving in silent, interlocking processions. They are not hostile unless a visitor disrupts their patterns, stepping into a Wraith's path, breaking a calcified branch, or introducing disorder into the Forest's structure. A disrupted Wraith attempts to *correct* the disruption, which for a living creature means forcing it into a geometric pattern, pinning it in place, straightening its posture, or in extreme cases, beginning the calcification process on living tissue.

## Hooks

1. **The Expanding Edge.** Farmers on the Forest's western boundary report that their fences are turning white. Posts planted in living soil are calcifying from the base up. A Flame Warden named Serran Ashmark commands the perimeter guard and has documented the expansion, but his reports to [[Ostivaar]] have been acknowledged and ignored. He is loyal to the Church but increasingly frightened by what his measurements show.
2. **The Fugitive Scholar.** An [[Unbound Congregation]] member named Alara Denn, a former Keeper-caste scholar stripped to Ashless for questioning doctrine, has been sheltering in the outer Forest for months. She has learned to navigate the calcified fauna's patterns and avoid the [[Order Wraiths]]. She has also found something in the deep interior: a chamber where the calcification has reversed, where living wood grows amid the stone, green and vital and wrong. She needs someone to bring this information to [[Elowen Greaves]] without the Cinders intercepting it.
3. **The Quarry Dispute.** [[Ashveil]]'s bone-stone quarries in the Forest's mineralized core are producing material with visible flaws, hairline fractures in geometric patterns that were not there a year ago. The quarry master suspects the stone is degrading. The Church insists the material is sacred and unchanged. Buildings in Ashveil are developing cracks along the same geometric lines.
