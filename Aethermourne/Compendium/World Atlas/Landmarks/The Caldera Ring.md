## Description

The Caldera Ring is a chain of five volcanic peaks encircling the heart of [[The Emberveil]], their summits crowned with ash and their flanks scored by ancient lava flows. The largest and most sacred is [[Mount Thalvor]], the active volcano at the center where [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]] descended during the Theomachis. The four outer peaks are named for aspects of the Ember-Smith's craft: **the Anvil**, **the Bellows**, **the Tongs**, and **the Quench**. Smoke rises from a dozen vents at any given time, giving the skyline a perpetual industrial grandeur that the dwarves consider beautiful and outsiders find oppressive.

The Anvil is flat-topped, its summit plateau used for open-air forging rituals during festivals and guild examinations. The Bellows is a twin-peaked formation whose paired vents produce powerful wind-tunnel effects, howling updrafts that carry heat and ash into the upper atmosphere. The Tongs are two peaks flanking the main eastern pass, the primary overland route between [[The Emberveil]] and [[The Ashen Dominion]], where [[Smokewall]] guards the narrows. The Quench holds a crater lake of mineral-rich water cooled by altitude, traditionally used in the tempering of ceremonial blades and considered sacred by the Jewelers' Conclave of [[Anvilrest]].

The passes between these peaks are the only overland routes into the Emberveil's interior, narrow, defensible, and thick with volcanic haze that can reduce visibility to arm's length on bad days. [[Crucible]] sits inside the ring. [[Hearthdeep]]'s surface entrance opens at the Anvil's inner roots. [[Anvilrest]] occupies a sheltered position between the Tongs and the Quench. Every settlement in the Emberveil exists because the Caldera Ring protects it.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Caldera Ring is slowly collapsing inward. [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] fire is contracting as the Second Silence drains the divine essence sustaining the world's core, and the compensatory mechanism in the deep forge is pulling thermal mass downward and inward. The peaks are shifting by inches per month, imperceptible to casual observation but measurable by the Builders' Circle engineers monitoring [[Smokewall]]'s cracking walls. In decades, the passes will narrow to the point of impassability. In centuries, the ring would compress enough to threaten [[Crucible]] itself. [[Brunhild Ironheart]] has seen the Builders' Circle projections. She has shared them with no one.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Volcanic Haze.** The passes fill with sulfurous fog that stings the eyes and burns the throat. Visibility drops to thirty feet on moderate days, ten feet during venting surges. Navigation without a guide or established markers requires DC 14 Survival checks per hour or the party drifts off-route.

**Geothermal Vents.** Unpredictable eruptions of scalding steam and superheated gas. 1-in-8 chance per hour of travel through the passes. DC 13 Dexterity save or take 2d6 fire damage. Heat exhaustion sets in during summer months, DC 12 Constitution save per four hours or gain one level of exhaustion.

**[[Slag Hounds]].** Packs den in abandoned mine adits on the peak flanks, drawn to the residual heat and worked metal left behind by defunct mining operations. They are bolder in the passes, where the haze gives them cover.

**[[The Quenched]].** Half-forged constructs wander the higher slopes where the Cooling has created pockets of thermal confusion, zones where hot and cold air alternate unpredictably. The Quenched are drawn to these transitional spaces, seemingly caught between states just as the temperature is.

**Vent Wyrms.** Serpentine creatures that ride the thermal updrafts between the peaks, gliding on superheated air currents rising from the caldera vents. They are most active around the Bellows, where the twin-vent wind tunnels provide the strongest lift. Travelers crossing exposed ridgelines between the peaks are most vulnerable, the wyrms strike from above, diving out of the volcanic haze with little warning.

## Hooks

1. **The Narrowing.** The Tongs pass is closing. [[Smokewall]]'s defensive walls have developed cracks that the Builders' Circle cannot explain through normal settling. [[Tarn Smokeshield]] has requested a geological survey, but the Forge Council is slow to respond. Meanwhile, the pass is measurably narrower than it was six months ago, and trade caravans from [[The Ashen Dominion]] are reporting that the route feels tighter. If the pass closes, the Emberveil's primary trade route dies with it.

2. **The Pilgrimage Circuit.** Forge-mage tradition holds that visiting all five peaks in sequence, leaving an offering at each, completes a sacred circuit that deepens one's connection to [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] craft. No one has completed the circuit in two years. Thermal instability, haze surges, and the unsettling quiet of [[Mount Thalvor]]'s summit have turned every attempt back. A party that completed the full circuit would earn enormous prestige, and might learn something about the Cooling that the individual peaks cannot reveal alone.

3. **The Nesting.** Something has taken up residence in the Bellows. The twin vents have always produced a distinctive howling, but miners on the Anvil's eastern face report new sounds carried on the updrafts, rhythmic, resonant, and nothing like wind. The sounds are loudest at night. Ash samples collected downwind of the Bellows contain unfamiliar mineral traces, crystalline and warm to the touch, that the Jewelers' Conclave in [[Anvilrest]] cannot identify.
