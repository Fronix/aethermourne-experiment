## Description

The Contested Corridor is a narrow lowland pass, barely six miles wide at its broadest, cutting between ridgelines of shattered basalt where the Ironmarch's rust-red earth meets the Dominion's pale, calcified stone. It is the only overland route between the two regions that can sustain wagon traffic, and it has changed hands more times than anyone has bothered to count. The number usually cited is seventy-three, but that only covers the documented sieges. The raids, skirmishes, ambushes, and quiet occupations that fill the gaps between formal conflicts would push the count into the hundreds.

The ruins tell the story. Fortifications are layered here like geological strata, each generation building atop the bones of the last. Dominion watchtowers of cut Ashite stone stand half-collapsed atop Ironmarch earthworks, which sit on older Dominion walls, which rest on foundations so ancient that no one can identify the builders. Dig anywhere in the Corridor and you find weapons, bones, collapsed tunnels, sealed granaries. The soil is rust-red and chalite-white in alternating bands, two dead gods' territories bleeding into each other. The air tastes of iron and old chalk, and the wind through the broken walls makes sounds that locals have long stopped pretending are just wind.

Control of the Corridor means control of [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] flowing west into the Dominion and Dominion goods flowing east. The current arrangement is an armed stalemate: Ironmarch warlords hold the eastern approach, Dominion garrisons hold the western, and a contested no-man's-land of ruins and fragment-strewn rubble separates them. Trade passes through under armed escort, taxed by both sides, reliable only three days in seven. [[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal]] brokered this arrangement fourteen years ago. It is the longest period of functional commerce the Corridor has seen in a century, and it is unraveling.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Dominion's push to "stabilize" the Corridor is [[Delric Mourne]]'s work. He needs the trade route secured not for commerce but for troop movement. When [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] plans advance, forces will need to cross the border in strength, and the Corridor is the only viable passage. Mourne's agents among the Dominion garrison are mapping fortification gaps and fragment positions, preparing for the day the stalemate breaks.
>
> More critically, the Corridor sits directly on the migration path of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] fragments. The shards drifting westward from the Iron Fields pass through here on their slow convergence toward the Heart of Iron beneath [[Ostivaar]]. Dominion surveyors have documented this movement for decades, reports sealed and buried in Church archives. If Ironmarch's warlords learned the Dominion has known about fragment migration since before [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] was born, the diplomatic consequences would be catastrophic.
>
> [[Mira the Red]]'s trajectory, if she claims the Iron Thane title, leads directly here. Her army would pour through the Corridor and into the Dominion, driven by [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] will to reunite with its buried heart. She would believe she is conquering. She would be delivering.

## Encounters & Hazards

**War-Ghosts.** The Corridor's saturation with both war-essence and death makes it one of the few places in Aethermourne where the dead do not rest quietly. They are not true undead but impressions, soldiers from a dozen eras replaying their final moments in translucent pantomime. Most are harmless. Some are not. A war-ghost that died in rage can lash out at living creatures who stumble into its loop, striking with phantom weapons that leave real bruises. The ghosts are densest at dawn and dusk, when the light catches the Ashite dust in the air and gives them form.

> [!example] War-Ghost Encounter (Nimble)
> Perception DC 14 to notice a war-ghost's patrol loop before entering it. If triggered: Dex DC 13 to avoid the phantom strike (1d6 psychic damage). The ghost replays its loop every 10 minutes. Destroying a war-ghost requires disrupting the fragment it is anchored to, typically a shard buried in the nearest ruin wall.

**Unstable Ruins.** The layered fortifications are structurally compromised. Walls that have supported three centuries of additional construction collapse without warning. Tunnels between layers flood with iron-oxide groundwater. Sealed granaries, when breached, release air that has been contaminated by [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] dust for generations, a single breath induces hours of paranoid aggression.

**Fragment Drift.** Shards in the Corridor are visibly moving, a few inches per week, grinding through soil and stone. The sound is a low, constant scraping that sets teeth on edge. Travelers who camp near a drifting shard wake irritable, suspicious, and convinced their companions are plotting against them.

## Hooks

1. **The Sealed Survey.** A Dominion patrol was ambushed in the Corridor, and among the dead, an Ironmarch scout found a sealed document case. The documents inside are surveyor's reports cataloguing fragment positions over fifty years, proving the Dominion has known about the migration and said nothing. [[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal]] wants the documents authenticated before they become a weapon in someone else's hands. [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] wants them immediately.
2. **The Garrison Buildup.** Dominion troops at the western approach have doubled in the past month, officially to "protect trade convoys." The new soldiers are better equipped and more disciplined than the usual border garrison. [[Voss Irenthal]] suspects [[Delric Mourne]]'s influence and needs someone to infiltrate the Dominion camp and confirm.
3. **The Deep Ruin.** A tunnel collapse in the no-man's-land has exposed a fortification layer that predates the Ironmarch's settlement, stonework that matches neither Dominion nor Ironmarch construction. The architecture is angular, precise, and the walls are inscribed with symbols that predate the Theomachis. Both sides want to claim it. Neither side knows what it is.
