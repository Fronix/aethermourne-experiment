## Description

A sea cave beneath [[Mistholm]]'s eastern cliffs, accessible only at low tide through a narrow passage slick with algae. The townspeople avoid it. Fishermen say the water inside is warmer than it should be and that sounds carry strangely, as if the cave is breathing. They are not wrong. The cave sits directly above a fissure connecting to the [[The Hollowed Reach#The Abyssal Trench|Abyssal Trench]], and what rises through that gap is not air but something older, warmer, and patient.

The environmental motif is warmth where cold should be. The Shattered Coast is frigid, salt-spray and cutting wind, but the Deep Cave radiates a gentle heat that intensifies as you descend. The water pooling on the cave floor is blood-warm. The stone is smooth to the touch and faintly damp, not with seawater but with condensation from below, as though the cave is exhaling. Everything here belongs to [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], not to Mistholm.

[[The Whisperer]] has been visiting for years, descending through passages unknown to the townspeople to commune with the imprisoned goddess. During these visits, Sleepwalker activity in Mistholm spikes dramatically. Garrison Captain Lukas Fenn's records confirm the correlation, but no one has connected it to the cave.

### The Tidal Throat

The entrance passage, accessible only at low tide, narrow enough that two people cannot walk abreast. The walls are slick with algae that bioluminesces when touched, but the light does not respond to contact. It pulses in its own rhythm, slow and steady, synchronized with something deep below. The sound of breathing grows louder as you descend, a long, patient inhalation that takes thirty seconds to complete, followed by a silence that feels like listening. The passage descends for roughly two hundred feet before opening into the Communion Chamber.

### The Communion Chamber

A natural cavern large enough for a small congregation, perhaps twenty people standing. The floor is worn smooth by centuries of tidal action, the walls dark and glistening. Spiral markings cover the stone, not carved but grown into it, raised patterns that match the routes [[Sleepwalkers]] trace on the beach and the dead-fish spiral from [[Mistholm#The Spiral|the Spiral]]. Residue of ritual activity is visible to anyone who knows what to look for: salt circles on the floor, puddles of hardened candle wax, the deep brine scent of offerings left and taken by the tide. This is where the PCs discover evidence of organized [[Veil Unbound]] worship of Serith.

### The Fissure

Where the chamber floor cracks open. Not a passage anyone could physically descend, a gap perhaps two feet wide and twenty long, jagged-edged, with water visible below that is darker than it should be, warmer than it should be, and moves against the tide. Standing near the edge, you hear Serith's whisper directly, not through dreams or Sleepwalker proxy, but as a clear voice rising from impossible depth. The voice is feminine, measured, and reasonable. It knows your name. It asks questions that are too perceptive. It offers truths you did not want to hear.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The fissure is one of only three known surface connections to the Abyssal Trench. [[The Whisperer]] has been building a congregation from coastal communities for years, using the cave as the site of communion rites that bind worshippers to Serith's will. The spiral markings are a fragment of Serith's dreaming mind expressed through stone, the same pattern appearing in the Sleepwalker routes, the dead-fish spiral, and the glyphs found on [[Veil Unbound]] ritual sites across the Reach.
>
> **First Divine Contact.** If the PCs reach the fissure, Serith will attempt to speak to them directly. This is the campaign's first direct divine contact and should be played as both terrifying and seductive. Serith is not hostile. She is curious, intelligent, and genuinely interested in the PCs. She will offer truthful answers to questions, each answer laced with perspective that makes her cause seem reasonable. She will not threaten. She will not demand. She will simply, gently, make her case, and she is very good at it.
>
> **Connection to Nereva Selk.** [[Maren Selk]]'s sister Nereva was last seen near the cave (see [[Mistholm#The Letter|The Letter]]). Nereva descended to the fissure and heard Serith's voice. What happened next is for the GM to decide: she may have joined the [[Veil Unbound]], she may be imprisoned below, or she may have learned something that Serith needed silenced. Her fate is a thread connecting Mistholm to Tidewall and giving [[Maren Selk]] a personal stake in the Act I climax.

## Encounters & Hazards

- **Tidal timing.** The cave floods at high tide. PCs who misjudge the window have roughly four hours before the water rises. Trapped PCs must survive hours in rising water in total darkness, the bioluminescence extinguishes when submerged.
- **Bioluminescent exposure.** The algae's glow reveals your position to whatever watches from below. Moving through the Tidal Throat without triggering it requires DC 16 Dexterity (avoid wall contact in a narrow passage).
- **Serith's presence.** Cumulative Will saves near the fissure (DC 13, increasing by 1 per round) against Serith's direct psychic contact. Failure does not cause harm, it causes understanding, which is worse. Failed saves grant fragments of Serith's perspective that make her cause seem increasingly reasonable.
- **The Whisperer.** If the PCs arrive during a communion, they face [[The Whisperer]] and 4-8 [[Veil Unbound]] cultists in a confined space with one exit that may be flooding.

## Hooks

1. **The Pointing.** The [[Sleepwalkers]] now stop before reaching the water and point at the cave entrance, holding the pose until dawn. The garrison is terrified because this is new behavior. (Source: [[Mistholm#The Pointing]])
2. **Fenn's Records.** Garrison Captain Lukas Fenn's journals show a precise correlation between Sleepwalker spikes and dates that match no known tidal or lunar pattern. A DC 14 Investigation check cross-referencing the dates with [[Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keeper]] coastal reports reveals the spikes coincide with new-moon low tides, the only times the cave is fully accessible.
3. **The Letter.** [[Maren Selk]] in [[Tidewall]] is searching for her sister Nereva, who was last seen near Mistholm asking questions about the tides and the cave. [[Hanna Tide]] remembers her. Nereva visited the Deep Cave at night and was never seen again. (Source: [[Mistholm#The Letter]])
