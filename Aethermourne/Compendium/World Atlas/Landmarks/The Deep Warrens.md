## Description

The Deep Warrens are the underground world beneath [[The Emberveil]], a vast network of natural caverns, ancient lava tubes, and two millennia of dwarven excavation that stretches from the foundations of [[Crucible]] to the volcanic roots of the [[The Emberveil#The Caldera Ring|Caldera Ring]]. The network is not a single system but hundreds of interconnected ones, tunnels that branch, merge, dead-end, and reconnect across miles of subterranean terrain. Some passages are wide enough for loaded carts. Others require crawling. Some are mapped. Many are not.

The upper levels are the Emberveil's economic engine. Conventional mines extract iron, copper, and gemstones from volcanic deposits enriched by two thousand years of geothermal activity. The tunnels here are well-maintained, lit by [[Cinder Sprites|Cinder Sprite]] swarms and forge-lanterns, ventilated by shaft systems that channel heat upward to warm the Warm Valleys above. Miners work in shifts, their picks ringing through stone that is always warm to the touch. The air smells of mineral dust and heated metal.

The middle levels are where the warmth becomes heat and the mining becomes a vocation. Past the first thermal barrier, the stone glows faintly where geothermal veins run close to the tunnel walls. Rare mineral deposits shimmer with residual divine energy, compounds that form only under pressures and temperatures no natural geology produces. The air is dry enough to crack skin and hot enough to make breathing an effort. Workers rotate in shortened shifts, their exposed surfaces coated in heat-resistant salve brewed at [[Cinderfall]]. The second thermal barrier marks the boundary where casual mining ends and Delver operations begin (see [[Hearthdeep]] for the vertical city built around the deepest accessible shaft and its thermal barrier system).

Below the third thermal barrier, the Deep Warrens become something other than mines. The stone pulses with a slow rhythm that older Delvers swear is a heartbeat. The walls are warm the way living flesh is warm. Seams of [[Cosmology and Magic#Forgeheart Steel|forgeheart steel]] thread through the rock like veins, glowing with a light that is not reflection but emission, the residual divine energy of a god who burned himself to keep the world's core alive. Harvesting forgeheart steel at this depth requires tools made of forgeheart steel, anything lesser softens on contact. Only the most experienced Delvers from [[Hearthdeep]] descend past the third barrier, and what they bring back funds the Emberveil's economy.

## The Outer Warrens

Beyond the mapped tunnels maintained by [[The Forge Council]], the Deep Warrens extend into unmapped territory: collapsed lava tubes, flooded chambers, passages sealed by ancient eruptions and reopened by geological shift. The Outer Warrens are officially off-limits, designated as structurally unsound and thermally unpredictable. In practice, they are where the Emberveil's illegal mining operations thrive.

[[Vessa Ironvow]]'s operation is the largest. The expelled Forge Council member has established a network of tunnels in the Outer Warrens northeast of [[Hearthdeep]], accessed through a separate shaft that her miners have widened over months of covert work. Her crews extract mineral deposits from areas the Council deemed too dangerous to mine, selling the output through [[Smokewall]]'s border trade. She has domesticated a pack of iron-slag [[Slag Hounds]] as guards, feeding them confiscated tools from rivals who stumble into her territory. Her tunnels have breached close enough to Hearthdeep's outer shafts that both operations can hear each other's picks through the stone.

Vessa believes the Cooling can be reversed by reaching [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] forge-chambers directly and intervening in whatever mechanism is failing. She has correctly identified the problem. Her methods, blasting through thermally unstable rock with improvised charges, are the wrong solution applied with the right urgency. If she breaches the wrong chamber, she risks disrupting the forge's compensatory mechanism, triggering either a cooling cascade that kills the geothermal system or a pressure release that becomes a volcanic eruption.

The Outer Warrens are also where [[Cinder Sprites]] populations have collapsed most dramatically. Tunnels that once glowed with ambient sprite-light are dark and cooling, the thermal energy that sustained them withdrawing deeper as the Cooling progresses. [[Slag Hounds]] are filling the ecological vacuum, their populations exploding in the abandoned tunnels, packs growing bolder as prey grows scarce.

## The Deep Sound

Since the Cooling began, miners throughout the Deep Warrens have reported rhythmic sounds from below: a steady percussion that carries through the stone, felt in the bones before it reaches the ears. The sound is loudest at [[Hearthdeep]]'s Resonance Chamber (see [[Hearthdeep]]) but audible throughout the middle and lower levels of the entire network. It sounds like hammer-strikes. It sounds like a heartbeat. It sounds like the earth straining.

The rhythm is not constant. It accelerates during cooling episodes, when hot springs above run tepid and forge-vents dim, then slows when temperatures stabilize. Miners who have worked the deep levels for decades say the sound has always been there, a faint background pulse they attributed to geological settling. It has grown louder in the past year, loud enough to vibrate loose stone, loud enough to keep light sleepers awake on the middle levels, loud enough that [[Orin Deepstone]] has ordered continuous monitoring and restricted access to anything below Level Eight without Council authorization.

The sound divides the Emberveil. Some miners believe it is [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]] trying to communicate, a dead god's forge still working, still creating, still burning. Others believe it is a warning, the deep earth telling them to leave before something breaks. The [[The Forge Council|Forge Council]] has commissioned three separate investigations. None have reached the source. The heat beyond the third barrier makes sustained exploration impossible with current equipment, and the sound originates from somewhere deeper still.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Deep Sound is the forge's compensatory mechanism. [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]] burned his consciousness completely when he descended to reignite the world's core, but his forge was built to outlast him, a divine machine that runs on the fuel he became. The Second Silence is draining that fuel through the weakening [[Cosmology and Magic#Ley Lines and the Aetheric Web|Aetheric Web]], and the forge is responding automatically, increasing output to compensate for the energy loss, like a heart beating harder when the body is failing.
>
> If the compensatory mechanism succeeds, the forge stabilizes at a lower output, the Cooling slows, and the Emberveil survives diminished but functional. If the mechanism is overwhelmed, it fails in one of two ways: collapse (the forge goes cold, geothermal systems die, the Emberveil freezes) or overcompensation (the forge pushes past safe limits, volcanic eruptions follow, the Caldera Ring becomes uninhabitable). The current trajectory is toward overcompensation, the sound is getting louder, not quieter.
>
> [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] wants access to the Deep Forge. A tool shaped in divine fire, [[Cosmology and Magic#Forgeheart Steel|forgeheart steel]] worked at the source by hands guided by her will, could break her prison from the inside. She has no agents in the Emberveil yet, but [[Delric Mourne]]'s trade pressure on the Emberveil is creating the leverage she needs. If the Dominion gains influence over Forge Council politics, resources can be steered toward Serith's goals without anyone in the Emberveil knowing who they truly serve.
>
> **Campaign Role.** The Deep Warrens are the Act III-IV approach to [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] forge, the only known path to the divine mechanism keeping the world's core alive. Investigating the Deep Sound reveals the forge's compensatory strain (evidence the Second Silence is a world-scale crisis, not a regional one). Reaching the deepest levels requires either Delver expertise from [[Hearthdeep]] (earning [[Orin Deepstone]]'s trust) or following [[Vessa Ironvow]]'s reckless tunnels (faster but structurally dangerous). The forge itself is the ultimate crafting site, a place where divine-grade artifacts could be made or unmade.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Thermal Barriers.** The three marked boundaries represent sharp temperature increases. Past the first barrier: heat-resistant clothing or salve required, 1d4 fire damage per hour without protection. Past the second: DC 13 Constitution save per hour or gain one level of exhaustion from heat exposure. Past the third: unprotected flesh blisters within minutes (1d6 fire damage per minute), and only forgeheart steel tools function. See [[Hearthdeep]] for the specific levels at which these barriers occur within the vertical shaft.

**Structural Instability.** The Outer Warrens are riddled with unstable passages. DC 12 Perception to notice weakened tunnel sections before entering. Collapse: DC 14 Dexterity save or 2d6 bludgeoning and trapped (DC 14 Strength to free). Vessa's tunnels are worse, blasted rather than carved, with a 1-in-6 chance per hour of minor collapse in any section.

**[[Slag Hounds]].** Packs of 3-4 hunt throughout the middle and outer levels, drawn to worked metal. Their bite corrodes armor and weapons. Den sites contain refined slag-metal worth salvaging, if the pack can be cleared.

**[[Cinder Sprites]].** Healthy swarms in the upper levels provide warmth and light. Their absence in the outer levels is itself a hazard, tunnels without sprites cool rapidly, and the transition from warm to cold stone marks the Cooling's advancing edge.

**The Deep Sound.** Below the second barrier, the rhythmic vibrations impose DC 12 Will save per hour. Failure: compulsive need to descend further, toward the source. The effect intensifies with proximity, DC 14 below the third barrier. Dwarves with forge-magic training have advantage on these saves. The compulsion is not malicious, it is the forge's work-rhythm, and those caught in it feel not fear but purpose, as if they have somewhere important to be.

## Hooks

1. **The Deep Sound Investigation.** [[Brunhild Ironheart|Forge-Matriarch Ironheart]] needs a party capable of descending past the third thermal barrier to locate the source of the hammer-strikes. [[Hearthdeep]]'s Delver teams are overstretched, and [[Orin Deepstone]] has refused to send anyone deeper until he understands what they would find. The Matriarch suspects he already knows and is not saying. She needs outsiders who owe nothing to Deepstone's chain of command.

2. **Vessa's Tunnels.** [[Vessa Ironvow]]'s illegal mining operation has breached dangerously close to [[Hearthdeep]]'s outer shafts. The [[The Forge Council|Forge Council]] wants her shut down before she triggers a structural collapse. Vessa wants allies who understand that the Cooling is real and that the Council's caution is killing the Emberveil slowly instead of saving it quickly. Both sides have a point.

3. **The Dying Light.** The last healthy [[Cinder Sprites|Cinder Sprite]] swarm below the second thermal barrier is fading. Without it, the forgeheart steel deposits in that section become inaccessible, the ambient heat without sprite modulation is lethal even with protection. [[Brunhild Ironheart]] is offering guild-credit to anyone who can determine why the sprites are dying and whether the process can be reversed. The answer is not one she wants to hear.
