## Description

The Ejecta Fields are what the Maelstrom leaves behind. Stretching across the outermost Drift zone of [[The Shattered Edge]], they are a surreal landscape of impossible objects half-buried in otherwise normal terrain: stones that float a hand's breadth above the earth, anchored only by the roots of plants that grew around them over decades; slabs of metal embedded in hillsides that expand and contract in slow, rhythmic pulses, as if breathing; frozen lightning standing upright like pale sculpture, crackling faintly when the wind shifts; puddles of water that flow uphill along invisible channels, pooling in depressions that do not exist to the naked eye.

After every Maelstrom surge, new debris appears. The pattern is never the same. A surge might deposit a field of crystalline dust across a quarter-mile of grassland, or drop a single object, a chair, a stone, a piece of something that has no name, into a harvester's camp with surgical precision. The debris follows no physical law. Some of it is useful. Some of it is dangerous. Most of it is both.

Drift-harvesters, the Edge's most practical and least romantic profession, comb the Fields after every surge. They work in small teams, moving quickly, cataloguing and collecting [[Cosmology and Magic#Vorrhyn's Dust|Vorrhyn's Dust]], [[Cosmology and Magic#Empyrean Shards|Empyrean Shards]], and salvageable materials before the next surge reshuffles the landscape. The work is unglamorous, exhausting, and essential. The Edge's economy starts here, with people in heavy gloves picking through impossible debris and hoping nothing picks back.

The Fields are also where the Changed most commonly occur. Travellers passing through the Drift, unaware of the Maelstrom's reach, are caught in surge-waves that roll outward from the wound. The transformation is not instant. A surge-wave passes through a person like a shudder, and the changes manifest over hours or days: altered features, shifted memories, new perceptions that did not exist before. Some stagger to [[Riftmarket]] or [[Shimmer]] for help. Others are found by harvesters, disoriented and altered, sitting among the ejecta and not understanding what has happened to them. The harvesters know the routine. Water, a blanket, and directions to the nearest settlement. It happens often enough that they carry spare supplies.

The Fields have been expanding. Each surge pushes the debris zone outward, and the surges have been growing more frequent and more powerful. Terrain that was stable farmland a year ago is now ejecta-littered wasteland. Harvesters report finding objects deeper in the Drift than ever before, and the objects themselves are stranger, heavier with chaotic potential, harder to handle safely.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The surges are intensifying because [[The Twelve#Serith, Goddess of Shadow and Secrets , *"The Veiled One"*|Serith]]'s rhythmic pulling at [[The Maelstrom]] is increasing in frequency and force. Each cycle expels more ejecta, pushes the Drift boundary further outward, and deposits debris with higher concentrations of chaotic energy. The Fields are the visible symptom of a wound being deliberately reopened.
>
> [[Oren Blackfinger]]'s supply chain starts here. His drift-harvester contacts bring raw materials to collection points in the Fields before transporting them to the Dust Exchange in [[Riftmarket]]. The unusual buying patterns he has noticed, [[Cosmology and Magic#Empyrean Shards|Empyrean Shards]] being purchased aggressively at above-market prices, originate from intermediaries operating in the outer Fields. One of these intermediaries is a [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] agent posing as an independent harvester, funneling Shards toward the hidden cache near [[The Loom Scar]] for the widening ritual.
>
> The Fields also serve as an early warning system that no one is reading correctly. The ejecta's composition is changing. Recent surges have deposited objects that carry faint traces of structured information, not chaotic noise but fragments of pattern and order that should not exist in Maelstrom debris. These are Loom-fragments, scraps of fate-thread caught in the surge-wave and expelled alongside the usual chaos. A Star-Reader or Fencrafter who examined them would recognize the anomaly immediately. No one has thought to look.
>
> **Campaign Role.** The Ejecta Fields are the accessible entry point to the Shattered Edge's mysteries. PCs exploring here encounter the Edge's economy, its people, and its dangers at a manageable scale before confronting the Threshold's lethal distortions. The Shard-purchasing trail, the expanding surge pattern, and the Loom-fragments in recent ejecta all provide investigative threads that lead deeper into the region's arc.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Surge-Wave Arrival.** Maelstrom surges roll outward through the Fields as visible distortion waves, the air rippling like heat-haze, the ground shuddering, ejecta vibrating in resonance. DC 15 Perception to notice an incoming wave with enough time to take cover (1d4 rounds of warning). Characters caught in a surge-wave must make a DC 14 Will save. Failure: Changed transformation begins, manifesting over the following 1d4 days. Success: minor, temporary disorientation (disadvantage on Perception for 1 hour).

**Unstable Ejecta.** The debris is unpredictable. A floating stone might suddenly obey gravity (1d8 crushing damage, DC 12 Dexterity to dodge). Breathing metal exhales toxic fumes when disturbed (DC 13 Constitution or 1d4 poison damage and nausea for 1 round). Frozen lightning discharges when approached within 2 spaces (2d6 lightning damage, DC 14 Dexterity for half). Pools of uphill-flowing water are deceptively deep, as the water occupies space that does not visually correspond to the depression it fills (DC 12 Perception to notice before stepping in, failure means waist-deep immersion in water that resists movement, DC 13 Strength to pull free).

**Disoriented Changed.** Newly Changed travellers are found periodically in the Fields, confused and frightened. Most are harmless and grateful for help. Some react with fear or aggression, not understanding their altered bodies or perceptions. A Changed individual in distress may lash out with abilities they do not yet know they have (GM's discretion on the nature of the change and its combat implications, if any).

**Chaotic Creatures.** Surge-waves occasionally birth creatures from raw chaotic potential. [[Threshold Castoffs]], malformed things assembled from random possibility that dissolve within hours, are common after major surges. [[Chance-Eaters]], predatory entities that feed on probability and leave their victims unable to succeed at anything for days afterward, hunt in the deeper Fields where surges are strongest. Both are more frequent as the surges intensify.

## Hooks

1. **The Overdue Team.** A harvester crew of five went deep into the outer Fields three days ago, following a major surge that deposited something *large*. Their signal marker has not moved since the first day. [[Riftmarket]]'s harvester guild is offering pay for a search party, but the area where the team was working has seen two additional surges since they entered. Whatever they found, it was big enough to keep experienced harvesters in place despite the risk.

2. **The Foretouched Traveller.** A newly Changed woman has arrived at the Fields' eastern edge, disoriented and carrying knowledge she should not have. She is not merely Changed in the usual sense. She speaks with certainty about events in [[The Ashen Dominion]] that have not yet occurred, names people she has never met, and describes the interior of buildings she has never entered. She was caught in a surge-wave that penetrated unusually deep into the Drift, a wave strong enough to carry fate-thread fragments from [[The Loom Scar]]. She does not understand what has happened to her. The [[The Echoists|Echoists]] want to shelter her. [[The Star-Readers]] want to study her. Someone needs to get to her before the [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] does.

3. **The Shard Trail.** [[Oren Blackfinger]] hires the party to investigate why Empyrean Shard prices have spiked. His contacts in the Fields report that an unknown buyer has been paying above market rate for high-quality Shards, outbidding established traders and purchasing quantities that suggest a specific, large-scale project. The trail leads through three intermediaries operating collection points in the outer Fields, and from there to the Veil Unbound's hidden cache near [[The Loom Scar]].
