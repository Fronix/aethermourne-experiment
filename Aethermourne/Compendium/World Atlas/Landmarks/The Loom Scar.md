## Description

At the deepest point of the Threshold that a mortal can reach and return from, the ground changes. The chaotic distortions of the Maelstrom's periphery, the shifting gravity, the broken physics, the reality that cannot decide what it wants to be, all of it goes quiet. Not calm. *Quiet*, the way a held breath is quiet, the way the moment before a verdict is quiet. The air stops tasting of copper and begins tasting of certainty.

The Loom Scar is a crystallized fracture zone roughly a quarter-mile across, where the barrier between the mortal world and [[The Twelve#Orenthas, God of Fate and Knowledge , *"The Loom-Keeper"*|Orenthas]]'s [[Cosmology and Magic#The Loom of Fate|Loom of Fate]] is thinnest. Here, time does not stutter or skip as it does in the surrounding Threshold. Instead, it *layers*. Standing at the Scar's edge, a visitor can see the landscape as it is now, as it was decades or centuries ago, and as it might be in futures that have not yet committed to existing. The images overlap, translucent and flickering, and the eye cannot choose which to focus on. A ruin that has not yet fallen stands beside the ruin it will become. A tree that grew here once shares space with the stump it is now and the sapling it might be again.

The air carries whispers. Not the chaotic noise of the Maelstrom, but structured fragments: mathematical certainties, names spoken in unfamiliar voices, descriptions of events that have not occurred. The whispers are quiet near the edges, growing clearer and more coherent toward the center. Those who linger too long begin to understand them, and understanding is where the danger lies.

This is where the Foretouched are created. People exposed to the Scar's resonance sometimes emerge carrying fragments of Loom-knowledge, information about places they have never been, events that have not happened, names of people they will not meet for years. The [[The Echoists|Echoists]] consider the Loom Scar the holiest site on the Edge, the place where [[The Twelve#Vorrhyn, God of Chaos and Change , *"The Unbound"*|Vorrhyn]]'s wound opens onto something even deeper than chaos: the architecture of fate itself.

Kaelen Riftwalker has made this his territory. He has completed the Threshold Walk to the Scar eleven times, more than any other person alive, and each walk has changed him further. His left arm phases in and out of visibility. His voice arrives a half-second before his lips move. He sometimes answers questions that have not been asked yet. He guides the most daring [[The Echoists|Echoist]] pilgrims to the Scar's edge, and he keeps watch over what bleeds through.

[[Edgewatch]] does not officially sanction expeditions to the Loom Scar. [[Kessa Thornwall]] considers the site too dangerous and the walks too unpredictable. Edgewalker Rin, the most sensitive probability-reader at the Watch, has been sneaking to the Scar alone against Kessa's direct orders, drawn by a resonance she can feel from miles away.

Serith's influence is piggybacking on the walks, each one brings a mortal close enough for her to whisper through the fate-threads, planting suggestions and nudging behavior without the walker ever knowing the voice is not Vorrhyn's echo but Serith's.

> **Campaign Role.** The Loom Scar is the critical intelligence node for the Shattered Edge arc. Disrupting the tap point here cuts off one of Serith's major transmission routes but also severs the Foretouched phenomenon, which the Echoists will resist violently. The site connects directly to the endgame: the question of whether the Loom of Fate can be accessed, repaired, or must be destroyed.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Fate-Thread Exposure.** Characters who spend more than 10 minutes at the Scar must make a DC 15 Will save. Failure: the character gains a fragment of impossible knowledge (roll 1d6: 1, a name they will hear spoken within the month; 2, the layout of a place they have never visited; 3, the final words of someone still living; 4, a language they have never studied, fluent for 1d4 days; 5, the solution to a problem they have not yet encountered; 6, a memory belonging to someone else, vivid and emotionally devastating). Each fragment weakens the barrier, though PCs will not know this immediately.

**Temporal Layering.** The overlapping time-states make coordinated action difficult. DC 14 Perception to act coherently in combat, as targets appear simultaneously in their past, present, and possible-future positions. Failure means attacks target the wrong temporal image (miss automatically). Spellcasting requires the same DC or the spell targets the wrong time-state, producing delayed or premature effects.

**Empyrean Shard Resonance.** Shards carried into the Scar vibrate and heat as the Loom's proximity activates their crystallized divine essence. Characters carrying shards take 1d4 fire damage per round unless the shards are shielded. Unshielded shards shatter explosively after 3 rounds (2d6 force damage in a 2-space radius, DC 13 Dexterity for half).

**Kaelen Riftwalker.** Not hostile, but deeply Changed and unsettling to interact with. He answers questions before they are asked, refers to events that have not happened, and occasionally speaks in a voice that is not his own. He will not allow harm to the Scar and considers any attempt to disrupt it an act of desecration. If threatened, he fights with a probability-sight advantage (cannot be surprised, reacts to attacks before they are made, effectively has advantage on all defensive checks).

## Hooks

1. **Kaelen's Silence.** Kaelen Riftwalker returned from his eleventh walk three days ago and has not spoken since, except to say, in a voice that was not entirely his: *"It is reaching back."* [[Prior Echoes]] is concerned. Kaelen's followers are ecstatic. [[Kessa Thornwall]] wants someone who can get a coherent answer out of him before the Echoists turn his silence into scripture.

2. **Rin's Disobedience.** Edgewalker Rin has been making unauthorized trips to the Scar, drawn by a resonance only she can feel. [[Kessa Thornwall]] needs someone to follow her, find out what she is doing there, and bring her back before the Threshold changes her permanently. The complication: Rin's sensitivity means she may be detecting Serith's broadcast signal without understanding what it is.

3. **The Cache.** A [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] operative has been spotted near the Scar's perimeter, moving with the confidence of someone who knows the terrain. [[Oren Blackfinger]]'s investigation into unusual Empyrean Shard purchases has traced one intermediary to this area. Finding the hidden cache reveals the Veil Unbound's ritual preparations and the scale of Serith's ambitions.
