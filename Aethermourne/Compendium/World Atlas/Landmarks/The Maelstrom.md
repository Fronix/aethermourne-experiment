## Description

The Maelstrom is the wound left when [[The Twelve#Vorrhyn, God of Chaos and Change , *"The Unbound"*|Vorrhyn]] was annihilated during the [[Timeline|Theomachis]] in Year 2 AS. It is not a crater. It is not a ruin. It is a hole in what-is, a visible, churning absence where the God of Change was unmade so completely that reality itself could not close over the gap. From a distance, it looks like a bruise in the sky, a smear of wrong colors rotating slowly above a landscape that refuses to hold still. From the Threshold's inner edge, where [[Edgewatch]] maintains its crescent of watchtowers, it looks like the end of the world.

The wound has no fixed boundary. It breathes. It contracts and expands in rhythms that defy prediction, pushing the Threshold outward during surges and retreating during lulls. The air near it tastes of copper and static and something that has no name, a sensation that registers in the sinuses and the hindbrain simultaneously. Sound behaves strangely: voices echo before they are spoken, footsteps arrive out of sequence, and occasionally the wind carries fragments of noise that do not belong to any source, whispers in languages no one has studied, laughter from empty sky, the low grinding of something vast turning slowly in a space that should not have room for it.

The Maelstrom expels material during surges. Objects, substances, and occasionally creatures are thrown outward from the wound's periphery, deposited across [[The Shattered Edge#The Drift|the Drift]] in patterns that change with every cycle. This ejecta follows no physical law. Stones float. Water burns cold. Metal breathes. Lightning freezes mid-strike. The people of the Edge call this debris "Vorrhyn's gift," and their economy depends on harvesting it, but the gift is not given freely. Everything the Maelstrom touches, it changes. [[Cosmology and Magic#Vorrhyn's Dust|Vorrhyn's Dust]], the fine residue scraped from the wound's edge, is the most valuable and most dangerous magical catalyst on the continent.

No one has reached the center. No one knows if "center" is a meaningful concept for a wound in reality. What the Edgewalkers of [[Edgewatch]] can perceive through their probability-sight is that the wound deepens as well as widens, that the layers of reality thin as one approaches until the barrier between the mortal world and what lies beyond becomes gossamer, then suggestion, then nothing at all. One watcher, a scout named Tarla, walked willingly toward the Maelstrom during a patrol and did not return. The three survivors who watched her go reported that she moved as if listening to something, her face calm, her steps certain.

[[Kessa Thornwall]] has not declared Tarla dead.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Maelstrom is not merely a wound. It is a *door*. When Vorrhyn was annihilated, the force of his unmaking punched through the floor of existence, tearing open a passage to the space between worlds. At the wound's deepest point, the barrier between the mortal plane and [[The Gyre]]'s raw substrate is nonexistent, and the space beyond is close enough to touch [[The Twelve#Orenthas, God of Fate and Knowledge , *"The Loom-Keeper"*|Orenthas]]'s [[Cosmology and Magic#The Loom of Fate|Loom of Fate]] directly.
>
> [[The Twelve#Serith, Goddess of Shadow and Secrets , *"The Veiled One"*|Serith]], imprisoned beneath the [[The Hollowed Reach#The Abyssal Trench|Abyssal Trench]], has been rhythmically pulling at the Maelstrom from the other side, stretching and releasing like a muscle being exercised. Each cycle widens the wound fractionally. The surges that threaten [[Edgewatch]] and the settlements of the Edge are symptoms of this manipulation, each expansion a test of how far the wound can be pushed before it snaps back. Serith does not want the Maelstrom to collapse. She wants it to open, fully and permanently, into a passage she can use to reach the Loom. With the Loom, she could rewrite the threads of fate so that her prison was never sealed.
>
> The [[The Veil Unbound]] is preparing a ritual at the wound's edge, using stockpiled [[Cosmology and Magic#Empyrean Shards|Empyrean Shards]], to accelerate the widening. Their unusual purchasing activity through [[Oren Blackfinger]]'s supply chain is the ritual's logistics trail.
>
> Tarla is alive. She reached the wound's deepest accessible point and touched the Loom's resonance, a sound so fundamental it bypasses conscious thought and speaks directly to the part of the mind that understands pattern and purpose. What that contact has done to her is an open question. She may be the first mortal to have directly perceived the Loom of Fate since [[The Twelve#Orenthas, God of Fate and Knowledge , *"The Loom-Keeper"*|Orenthas]] fell silent.
>
> The convergence vision: recently, every Edgewalker on the Edge perceived the same event simultaneously. Every probability thread they could read collapsed into a single outcome, the Maelstrom opening fully, like a door thrown wide. [[Tyrell Vane]] has not spoken publicly about the vision. Privately, he is terrified.
>
> **Campaign Role.** The Maelstrom is an existential threat, not a dungeon. It connects to the Second Silence through Serith's manipulation, the Veil Unbound's ritual, and the Loom of Fate's accessibility. PCs who investigate the surges, trace the Shard purchases, or follow Tarla's trail will discover the wound's true nature. Sealing, stabilizing, or choosing to open the Maelstrom is a major campaign decision with continent-wide consequences.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Maelstrom Surge.** The wound expands unpredictably. DC 15 Dexterity to flee the expanding edge. Characters caught in a surge must make a DC 14 Will save or gain one mutation as a Changed transformation begins. Surges last 1d6 rounds. [[Edgewatch]]'s signal-fires provide 1d4 minutes of warning. Frequency is increasing: currently 1-in-4 chance per day within the Threshold zone.

**Reality Distortion.** Within the Threshold and deeper, physics is unreliable. Ranged attacks have a 1-in-4 chance of trajectory shift (reroll direction randomly). Gravity shifts require a DC 13 Dexterity save per hour or reverse locally for 1 round, causing a 2-space "upward" fall and 1d6 damage. Time stutters: 1-in-6 chance per hour of losing or gaining 1d4 hours, with party members potentially experiencing different durations.

**Ejecta Hazards.** Objects expelled by the Maelstrom follow no known law. A floating stone might suddenly obey gravity again (1d8 crushing damage, DC 12 Dexterity to dodge). Breathing metal exhales toxic fumes (DC 13 Constitution or 1d4 poison damage and nausea for 1 round). Frozen lightning discharges without warning (2d6 lightning damage in a 3-space radius, DC 14 Dexterity for half).

**Transformation Risk.** Prolonged exposure to the Maelstrom changes everything it touches. For each hour spent within the Threshold, characters must make a DC 12 Will save. Failure accumulates: first failure causes minor cosmetic change (eye color, hair texture, shadow direction). Second failure causes functional change (altered sense, new phobia, shifted handedness). Third failure causes significant Change (as the region's Changed, permanent and identity-altering). The [[The Echoists|Echoists]] consider this sacred. Most others consider it terrifying.

## Hooks

1. **Tarla's Trail.** [[Kessa Thornwall]] will pay almost any price for information about her missing scout. Tarla's last known position was deeper in the Threshold than any patrol has gone since. The three survivors of her patrol can describe the route, but none of them will go back. Kessa needs someone willing to follow the trail, and she needs them to understand that whatever they find may not be what they expect.

2. **The Convergence Vision.** Every Edgewalker on the Shattered Edge saw the same thing: every probability thread collapsing into one outcome, the Maelstrom opening fully. [[Tyrell Vane]] needs someone to investigate what triggered the vision and whether the outcome it showed can be prevented. The investigation leads to [[The Loom Scar]], where the Loom of Fate bleeds closest to the mortal world.

3. **The Shard Trail.** [[Oren Blackfinger]] has noticed an aggressive, coordinated effort to purchase [[Cosmology and Magic#Empyrean Shards|Empyrean Shards]] in bulk through intermediaries operating in [[The Ejecta Fields]]. The buyers are careful, well-funded, and clearly working toward a specific objective. Tracing the trail leads to [[The Veil Unbound]]'s ritual preparations at the wound's edge.
