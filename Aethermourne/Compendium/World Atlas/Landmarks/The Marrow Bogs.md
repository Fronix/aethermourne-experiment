## Description

South of Thyrea's Cradle, the goddess's divine marrow seeped into the water table and never stopped. The Marrow Bogs are a labyrinth of channels, pools, and floating islands where the boundary between land and water is a suggestion that changes with the season, the tide of the root-network, and the mood of something vast and half-aware beneath the surface. Bioluminescent at night, the water pulses green, gold, and violet like breath, slow rhythmic waves of light that ripple outward from unseen sources deep below. The beauty is a lure.

The air is warm and heavy, thick with the smell of deep earth and something sweeter, almost floral, that settles in the lungs and makes the body feel lighter. Visitors describe a pleasant drowsiness, a desire to sit, to rest, to let the warmth seep in. The experienced know this is the first stage of exposure. [[Mirewatch]], the floating research settlement built on stilted platforms and buoyant root-wood pontoons, sits at the Bogs' northern edge, where contamination is manageable. Deeper in, the water glows brighter, the channels narrow, and the things that move beneath the surface grow larger.

[[Cosmology and Magic#Divine Materials|Marrow-Water]] rewrites living tissue on contact. The effects are well-documented and tiered by exposure:

- **Incidental contact:** Heightened senses, accelerated healing, pleasant warmth. The body feels better than it should. This stage is addictive.
- **Moderate exposure:** Permanent physical changes. Bark-skin, green-gold irises, animal-sharp senses. The changes are functional but irreversible.
- **Heavy exposure:** Grotesque transformation. Limbs become root-like, thoughts fragment, minds go feral. The fully transformed are the **Rootbound**, shambling things that were once people, wandering the deep Bogs in patterns that mimic the root-network's pulse.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Marrow Bogs are a primary shadow-root node for [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] whisper network. Her influence has threaded through [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea's]] decaying root-network, creating nodes that transmit entropic energy across the continent. The despair effect in the new Marrow-Water is Serith's influence bleeding through, a bone-deep conviction that nothing matters and nothing will improve. [[Vael Sundren]] has documented the secondary effect but lacks the theological framework to identify its source.
>
> A [[The Veil Unbound]] operative guards a deep-bog node where the Marrow-Water glows wrong colors, pale silver instead of green-gold, cold instead of warm. This node is Serith stress-testing how far her influence can reach through Thyrea's remains. The expanding Bogs are not Thyrea's growth. They are Serith's expansion, wearing Thyrea's body like a mask.

## Encounters & Hazards

**[[Marrow Worms]]** are the Bogs' largest inhabitants, twelve-to-twenty-foot blind organisms that swim through waterlogged soil, redistributing Marrow-Water through tunnel networks. The tunnels they leave behind are trenches of concentrated divine fluid covered by a thin crust of transformed soil. Dex DC 13 or fall through into the liquid below. The worms are not hostile, merely enormous, saturated with transformative fluid, and utterly oblivious to anything smaller than themselves.

**Rootbound** appear with increasing frequency at the Bogs' expanding edges. They were once people, travelers, researchers, curious locals who stayed too long. Their bodies are twisted composites of flesh and root, their movements synchronized with the root-network's pulse. They are not aggressive by default, but they defend the deep-bog zones with animal ferocity.

**The Deep-Bog Node.** Accessible via Marrow Worm tunnels that seem to converge on a single point in the Bogs' southern interior. The water here glows pale silver, cold to the touch, and contact induces immediate, overwhelming despair rather than the usual warmth. The node pulses in a rhythm distinct from the surrounding root-network, slower, colder, as if something beneath is breathing on a different schedule.

## Hooks

1. **The Despair.** New areas of Marrow-Water carry a secondary effect that [[Vael Sundren]], Chief Researcher at [[Mirewatch]], cannot explain: everyone who touches the new water feels despair, a conviction that nothing matters. The old Marrow-Water never did this. Something has changed in the source. Sundren needs someone willing to follow the contamination upstream.
2. **The Convergent Tunnels.** [[Marrow Worms|Marrow Worm]] tunnels in the deep Bogs are converging on a single point, as if the worms are being drawn there. The tunnel network forms a pattern that Sundren's assistants have mapped from above: it resembles a root-system, but inverted, reaching down rather than spreading out. Something at the convergence point is pulling them.
3. **The Rootbound Pilgrim.** A Rootbound was found at the Bogs' edge carrying a satchel of intact documents, letters from a [[The Veil Unbound]] cell describing "the Lady's gift" and referencing a location in the deep Bogs where "the water speaks." [[Brannoc Stillwater]], Marchwarden of [[Stillwater]], wants the location investigated before the Greensingers hear about it and panic.
