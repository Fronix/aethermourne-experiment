## Description

The Marrow Fields are not one place. They are dozens of sites scattered across the Pale Wastes where [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s black divine bones protrude from the frozen earth, ranging from finger-length fragments jutting from snowdrifts to ribs the size of city walls arcing across the tundra like the architecture of something that was never meant to be a building. The bones are black, not the black of stone or metal but the black of closed eyes, of the moment before sleep, of the absence that follows a stopped heartbeat. They are warm to the touch despite the frost, and standing near them causes a progressive emotional erosion that the [[The Ashen Vigil|Vigil]] calls bone-pull.

Bone-pull begins as exhaustion, a heaviness in the limbs that feels like the end of a long day. Then grey depression, a flattening of emotion where nothing seems worth the effort. Then, if you stay long enough, a peaceful acceptance that is the trap. People stop wanting to leave. They sit in the frost near the bones and wait, smiling faintly, for the cold to finish them. Bone-pull is one of the leading causes of death in the Wastes. It is not violent. It is gentle. That is what makes it terrible.

[[Cosmology and Magic#Morrhaelite|Morrhaelite]] grows along fracture lines in the divine bone, a black glass-like crystal harder than steel that holds an edge indefinitely and resonates with necromantic energy. The Vigil's signature black-glass blades are forged from it. Bone-harvesting teams work in threes: one cuts, one carries, one watches for bone-pull symptoms and undead. Shifts are limited to four hours. The watcher's job is the most important and the least glamorous, standing at a distance with a rope tied to the cutter's waist, watching for the moment their partner's shoulders relax and their cutting slows. That relaxation is the first sign. The watcher pulls them out. It is dangerous, lucrative, and grim work, and every Hold in the Wastes depends on it.

The bones are not static. New protrusions surface every few years, divine bone pushing through frozen earth with slow, grinding inevitability. A rib that wasn't there last season cracks through permafrost and stands twenty feet tall by spring. Tremors accompany the surfacing, shallow but sharp, fracturing ice and opening crevasses that swallow the unwary. The Fields are expanding. The largest formation, a cluster of vertebrae near [[Bonecairn]] that locals call the Spine, has grown measurably in the past decade. Something beneath the earth is still settling, still shifting, still not finished dying.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The 800 residents of [[Frostmere]] did not simply vanish. During a weaponized bone-storm engineered by [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], the entire population walked to a major bone formation in the northern Fields and succumbed to amplified bone-pull. Their bodies remain there, frozen in attitudes of peace, arranged in concentric circles around the bone. [[Nara Frostmere]]'s body is among them, her Holdwarden's pendant visible at her throat, her ash-urn cradled in her arms. They were not killed. They were called.
>
> Geometric scratches have been found carved into bone fragments recovered from several sites, scratches made from below, from inside the bone. These are Serith's command-glyphs, a language she has developed to direct undead forces through the death-domain's infrastructure. The glyphs are carved into the divine bone itself, using Morrhael's remains as a communication network. A scholar who studies them will recognize structure, grammar, intent, but the language corresponds to nothing in any known archive.
>
> **Campaign Role.** The Marrow Fields are where Serith's control infrastructure is literally carved into divine bone. PCs who investigate the geometric scratches gain evidence that someone is commanding the dead through Morrhael's remains. Combined with intelligence from [[The Hollowdeep]], this reveals the scope of Serith's operation.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Bone-Pull.** The primary hazard. Characters within 100 feet of a major bone protrusion must make Will saves every hour. First hour: DC 11, failure causes fatigue and emotional numbness. Second hour: DC 13, failure causes grey depression (disadvantage on all Charisma checks and initiative rolls, loss of motivation to act decisively). Third hour: DC 15, failure causes peaceful acceptance (character sits down, refuses to move, smiles, and waits). A character in peaceful acceptance can be physically dragged away but will resist passively. Recovery takes 1d4 hours away from the bones. Bone-pull effects stack with proximity, characters within 30 feet of a protrusion make saves at +2 DC.

**Bone-Storms.** Winds from the Fields carry divine bone-dust saturated with necromantic energy. A bone-storm lasts 1d4 hours and imposes three possible effects: death-sight (DC 13 Will or perceive spirits and echoes of the dead for 1d4 hours, disorienting but not directly harmful), tissue necrosis (DC 13 Constitution or suffer blackened extremities and 1d4 necrotic damage per hour of exposure), and spell contamination (DC 15 Will for any spellcaster, failure means healing spells may drain instead of restore, necromancy amplifies wildly and unpredictably).

**[[Memory Eaters]].** These creatures drift through the Fields at night, attracted to [[Cosmology and Magic#Morrhaelite|Morrhaelite]] deposits and the emotional residue of bone-pull victims. The Vigil has learned to use Memory Eater sightings as a prospecting tool, their presence often indicates rich Morrhaelite veins nearby. They carry fragments of the dead's final experiences, and encountering one near the Fields means hearing whispered last words, seeing ghostly faces, feeling the cold peace of someone who stopped fighting.

**[[Ashen Risen]].** Undead that form near harvesting camps, assembled from bone fragments and frozen earth by ambient necromantic energy. They are drawn to the living, not by hunger but by a fragmentary memory of warmth. They reach for campfires, stand at the edges of light, and sometimes try to sit at empty places around a fire circle. They become hostile only when the living react with violence, at which point the fragmentary warmth-seeking becomes confused aggression.

**Tremors.** When new bone surfaces, the ground shakes. DC 13 Dexterity to keep footing. Failure: prone and 1d6 bludgeoning from fracturing ice. Crevasses open without warning near surfacing sites, 10-30 feet deep, requiring DC 14 Dexterity to avoid if within 20 feet of the epicenter.

## Hooks

1. **The Lost Harvesters.** A three-person bone-harvesting team from [[Bonecairn]] has been overdue for six days. When found, they are alive, sitting in a circle around a massive bone protrusion, smiling, refusing to leave. They are not undead. They are not restrained. They simply do not want to go. Rescuing them requires reaching the protrusion (bone-pull saves every round at close range) and physically dragging them out before the rescuers succumb.

2. **The New Spine.** A massive new bone protrusion has surfaced near the border between the Fields and the [[The Hollowdeep|Hollowdeep]] perimeter, the largest single emergence in recorded history. The Morrhaelite deposits along its fracture lines are extraordinarily rich. [[The Ashen Vigil]] wants to claim it for weapons. Merchants from [[Ember's Rest]] want to sell it south. [[Bonecairn]]'s harvesters want to work it before anyone else arrives. And something about this particular bone is different: it hums, a low vibration felt in the chest rather than heard, audible only to those who have experienced bone-pull before.

3. **The Carved Bones.** A scholar visiting from [[The Star-Readers]] has examined bone fragments purchased at [[Greyhold]]'s market and noticed something harvesters have overlooked for years: geometric scratches on the interior surfaces of certain fragments, carved from below, from inside the bone. The scratches show consistent structure. They are not natural. Someone, or something, is writing inside the dead god's bones.
