## Description

The Nethys Pools are the middle ring of [[The Greymere Fenlands]], the transitional zone between the relatively stable Glimmer Marshes and the reality-thin Deep Fen interior. Here, the continent's densest convergence of ley lines breaks the surface through standing water, creating hundreds of luminous pools scattered across miles of bog, flooded forest, and reed-choked channels. Some pools are no wider than a footprint. Others span acres. All of them glow.

The water in the Pools does not behave like water. It flows uphill along ley-line channels, following paths of arcane potential rather than gravity. It freezes on midsummer nights when the Web's current shifts, forming delicate lattices of ice that shatter at dawn and reform the next night in different patterns. It boils without heat when surges pass through, sending columns of luminous steam into the canopy. And it whispers, fragments of words in a language that sounds like mathematics spoken aloud, the residual voice of a goddess whose consciousness is embedded in every drop.

Each pool retains a distinct quality of [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]]'s shattered mind. Some replay spells cast centuries ago, projecting partly-real fireballs or illusions of long-dead mages (see [[Echo Pools]]). Others radiate fields of inverted magic where healing burns and fire soothes. A few, the oldest and deepest, seem to think, responding to the presence of magic-users with surges of light and cascading whispers that grow louder and more coherent the longer the visitor stays. The [[The Confluence Council|Confluence Council]] maintains a network of marker-posts through the Pools, carved with surge-pattern warnings and pool classifications. The markers need replacing every few months as the pools shift position, merge, split, or simply vanish and reappear elsewhere.

[[Glimwater]] sits at the eastern edge of the largest pool, the Glimpool, where Fencraft training takes place on warded surge platforms. But the Glimpool is only one node in a network that extends for miles in every direction, growing denser and more dangerous the deeper one travels toward the interior. The Pools are the only navigable route to [[Sporeling]] and the Deep Fen beyond. Every traveller heading inward must pass through them.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Nethys Pools are overcompensating. As the [[Cosmology and Magic#Ley Lines and the Aetheric Web|Aetheric Web]] weakens across the continent, the ley lines here are rushing energy toward the wound, like blood pooling around an injury. The surges are growing stronger and more frequent not because the Pools are failing but because they are working too hard, pulling more of [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]]'s fragmented consciousness into the local network to compensate for the drain elsewhere.
>
> This cannot last. The overcompensation is accelerating, each surge drawing more energy, each recovery period shorter than the last. The [[The Confluence Council|Confluence Council]] tracks the intervals and the numbers are clear: within a year, the surges will reach a frequency and intensity that makes the Pools uninhabitable. Within two years, the overcompensation will exhaust the local fragments entirely and reverse catastrophically, turning the most magically saturated zone on the continent into the largest dead zone.
>
> [[Brother Lumen]]'s fragment-reassembly project depends on these pools as a communication medium. His experiment at [[Sporeling]] uses the mycelial network to relay signals between fragments, and the Pools are the relay stations. If the Pools fail, his work fails. If his work succeeds before the Pools fail, the resulting reconstitution would either stabilize the entire Web or detonate the accumulated overcompensation in a single catastrophic release. [[Wick]]'s ability to hear multiple fragments simultaneously runs through this same network, making them both the most valuable asset and the most vulnerable point in the Nethys plotline.
>
> **Campaign Role.** The Pools are the navigational and informational gateway to the Greymere's deeper mysteries. PCs passing through gain firsthand evidence of the Web's instability (surges, wild magic, Echo Pool encounters). Fencrafters and [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]] can extract fragmentary intelligence from the pools' whispers, providing clues about the Second Silence's mechanics. The Pools' expansion threatens [[Glimwater]] directly, creating an urgent local crisis that connects to the campaign's cosmic stakes.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Ley Surges.** The Pools surge unpredictably, flooding the surrounding terrain with arcane energy. During a surge (1-in-4 chance per hour of travel), all magic within 10 spaces becomes unstable: roll on [[The Fenlight#Proximity Effect Table|the Fenlight's Proximity Effect Table]] for any spell cast. Surges last 1d6 rounds. The warning signs are a rising hum in the water, a brightening of the pool-light from blue to white, and a metallic taste in the air.

**[[Echo Pools]].** The densest concentration of Echo Pools in the Fenlands is here. Travellers who stray from the marker-post routes risk triggering spell-replay hazards. Minor Echo Pools trigger within 3 spaces, replaying a single spell per round. Major Echo Pools near ley-line confluences trigger at 5 spaces and replay multiple spells simultaneously. Detection requires DC 13 Perception (iridescent shimmer on still water) or DC 15 Arcana (identifying the pool before triggering it).

**Soporific Mists.** Where warm ley-water meets cooler air, luminous fog banks settle into the channels between pools. The mist carries dissolved arcane particulate that induces drowsiness: DC 12 Will save per 10 minutes of exposure. Failure imposes disadvantage on Perception. Three cumulative failures cause sleep for 1d4 hours. Sleepers in the Pools dream of [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]]'s memories, vivid and disorienting, and wake unable to distinguish their own thoughts from the goddess's for several minutes.

**[[Spell-Scarred Lurkers]].** The Pools' ley-saturation produces the highest concentration of mutated fauna in the region. Lurkers here tend toward more complex mutations, dual-effect creatures are common near the strongest confluences.

**Shifting Geography.** The pools move. Channels that were navigable yesterday are flooded today. Marker-posts stand in dry ground one week and waist-deep water the next. Navigation without a fen-guide or [[The Confluence Council|Confluence Council]] chart requires DC 14 Survival checks per hour. Failure adds 1d4 hours of travel time and increases the chance of encountering hazards.

## Hooks

1. **The Expanding Pools.** The Glimpool's expansion is threatening [[Glimwater]]'s inner buildings, but the broader pool network is also growing, swallowing trails, drowning marker-posts, and cutting off routes to [[Sporeling]]. [[Maren Duskwater]] needs a team to chart the new pool boundaries before the next supply run to the Deep Fen. The charting expedition will pass through areas that haven't been navigable in living memory, revealing pools that have been sealed beneath peat for centuries, now rising with new and unknown properties.

2. **The Singing Pool.** A Fencrafter from [[Glimwater]] reports discovering a pool near the Deep Fen border that produces coherent speech rather than fragments, complete sentences in [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]]'s voice, repeating what sounds like instructions for repairing a ley-line junction. [[Old Fenris]] wants it investigated immediately. The [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]] want it documented. [[Brother Lumen]], if he learns of it, will want it harvested. The pool is deep in surge-active territory, and reaching it requires navigating a gauntlet of Echo Pools and Lurker nesting grounds.

3. **The Fencraft Accident.** A student from the Bright House at [[Glimwater]] attempted an unsanctioned deep-pool communion and hasn't returned. Her training partner says she walked into a major pool during a surge and the water closed over her. She has not surfaced. The pool glows brighter than before, and the whispers from its surface have changed, they now include a voice that sounds like the missing student, speaking words she never knew.
