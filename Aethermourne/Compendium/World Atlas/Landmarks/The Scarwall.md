## Description

The Scarwall is a living fortification of twisted thorn-vines stretching hundreds of miles along the border between [[The Ashen Dominion]] and [[The Verdant Marches]]. It was not built. It was not planted. It emerged on its own during the Age of Bones as [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea's]] wild vitality and [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] structured order collided in the border zone called the Scar. The result is neither forest nor wall but something with characteristics of both, and an agenda that belongs to neither dead god.

Some thorn-vines are thick as castle towers, their surfaces ridged with bark harder than iron, their thorns longer than swords. The wall rises sixty feet at its tallest, though its height varies along its length, lower at the natural gaps where trade passes through, higher where the collision of divine essences is most intense. The interior is a labyrinth of hollow passages, some wide enough for a horse, others barely passable, all of them shifting as the wall grows and reorganizes.

The Scarwall is notably asymmetric. On the Dominion side, it is hostile. Thorns extend toward approaching figures. Sap burns exposed skin. Vines reach for metal, for Ashite, for anything that carries Solvaen's structured energy, and they strangle it. On the Marches side, the wall is almost gentle. It parts for recognized travelers, offers edible fruit from branches that lean toward the path, and closes behind those it has allowed through. The wall knows the difference between the two nations. It has opinions.

## The Scar

The wider border zone surrounding the Scarwall is a landscape of violent ecological collision. Calcified trees from the Dominion side stand half-shattered, their hollow stone trunks threaded with living vines. Ashfield soil erupts with growth so aggressive that plants visibly move. [[Cosmology and Magic#Ashite|Ashite]] crystal formations jut from the earth wrapped in moss that pulses like a heartbeat. The air tastes of both ash and chlorophyll. The ground lurches as root systems and mineral deposits struggle for dominance beneath the surface. [[Scar Chimeras]], creatures warped by the collision of two divine ecologies, hunt here.

[[Thornwall]] (~8,000) guards the Dominion side under [[Captain Daren Kolst]]. [[Ashwalker's Post]] operates on the Marches side under Marchwarden [[Torvin Ashwalker]]. The Gate Market, a neutral trade point at one of the Scarwall's natural gaps, permits limited commerce between the two nations three days a week.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Scarwall's growth is accelerating, and the cause is not the divine collision itself. Researcher [[Mila Ashgrove|Ashgrove]] at [[Thornwall]] has documented a correlation: growth spikes coincide with Black Tides in [[The Hollowed Reach]], hundreds of miles away. Both phenomena, along with the Star Fade, are symptoms of [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] prison weakening. The Scarwall is not just a border. It is a barometer. When divine boundaries destabilize, the collision zone between Thyrea and Solvaen intensifies, and the wall grows faster, thicker, angrier.
>
> A [[The Cinders|Cinder]] agent, one of [[Delric Mourne]]'s operatives enhanced with entropic energy and carrying corrupted Ashite, is inside the Scarwall mapping passage routes. The agent has left an ash handprint on the inner surface of a thorn-passage, and the wood around it has died, calcified in the pattern of Dominion petrification. Mourne is planning for the day when forces will need to cross the border in strength. The wall, which has its own form of awareness, has noticed the intruder. It is not pleased.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Thorn Response.** The Scarwall reacts to perceived threats on the Dominion side. Travelers carrying Ashite, wearing Church insignia, or displaying structured magical signatures provoke aggressive growth: thorns extend, passages narrow, sap drips from overhead. On the Marches side, [[The Greensingers|Greensinger]]-trained travelers can request passage through Primal Communion, though the wall's cooperation has become less reliable in recent months.

**Interior Navigation.** The thorn-labyrinth shifts constantly. Passages that existed yesterday may be sealed today. Mapping is futile without a guide who can read the wall's growth patterns.

> [!example] Scarwall Passage (Nimble)
> Dominion approach: Dex DC 14 to avoid thorn damage (1d6 piercing per round). Ashite carriers: thorns actively target, advantage on "attacks." Marches approach: Primal Communion DC 12 to request passage (DC 16 during growth surges). Interior: Navigation DC 16 per hour. Failure: passage closes, 2d6 piercing from constricting vines, rerouted 1d4 hours off course.

**[[Scar Chimeras]]** hunt in the Scar zone, creatures warped by the collision of Thyrea's mutagenic vitality and Solvaen's calcifying order. They are unpredictable, their forms unstable, and they are drawn to the boundary between the two divine influences.

## Hooks

1. **The Narrowing Gap.** The Gate Market's natural passage through the Scarwall is closing. Thorns creep inward, the gap narrowing by inches each week. If it seals entirely, the last reliable trade route between the Dominion and the Marches is gone. The [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] at the Listening Post cannot explain the acceleration. [[Torvin Ashwalker]] is preparing for the economic fallout. [[Captain Daren Kolst]] is preparing for the military one.
2. **The Ash Handprint.** Something from the Dominion side has entered the Scarwall and left a mark: an ash handprint on the inner surface of a thorn-passage, surrounded by calcified wood. The wall is reacting, growing thicker around the contamination site, sealing passages. The Greensingers want to send a team inside to investigate. Torvin wants to send soldiers. Both sides need someone expendable enough to risk the interior.
3. **The Shared Threat.** A Dominion patrol approached the Gate Market under a white flag and asked to speak with "whoever leads here" about a threat from below. What they described matches the corruption the Greensingers have been sensing in the deep Cradle. For the first time in living memory, both sides of the border are afraid of the same thing.
