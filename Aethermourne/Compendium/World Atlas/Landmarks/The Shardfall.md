## Description

The Shardfall is the southeastern interior of [[The Ironmarch]], where the densest concentration of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] divine iron struck the earth during the Theomachis. This is not a scattering of fragments but a continental impact zone, miles of rust-red earth studded with iron monoliths driven into the bedrock like the nails of a god's coffin. The smallest shards stand waist-high. The largest rise dozens of feet, black-red pillars tapering to jagged points, their surfaces etched with fracture patterns that shift in the light. The Kaevroth Monolith near [[Wrathspire]], the tallest at two hundred feet, anchors the southeastern end of the field (see [[Wrathspire]] for details on the Monolith itself and the settlement built in its shadow).

The ground between the monoliths is bare. Nothing grows here except scrub grass that comes in rust-colored and bitter. The soil is so saturated with oxidized god-metal that rain puddles turn red within minutes, and the air carries a permanent metallic taste that coats the back of the throat. The landscape has a terrible beauty to it at dusk, when the monoliths catch the last light and glow like embers in a dying fire, their fracture lines luminous with trapped war-essence.

The defining feature of the Shardfall is the Hum. Every fragment vibrates at a subsonic frequency that human ears cannot quite hear but human bodies feel, a pressure in the chest, a tightening behind the eyes, a low throb in the teeth that never stops. Near the field's edges, the Hum is merely uncomfortable, an irritation that fades to background after a few hours. Deeper in, near the larger monoliths, it becomes physiological. Heart rates increase. Muscles tense. The mind begins to interpret neutral stimuli as threats, a companion's glance becomes a challenge, a bird's shadow becomes an ambush. The Ironmarch's ambient war-essence contamination is everywhere, but in the Shardfall it is concentrated enough to reshape behavior within hours rather than weeks.

The ecosystem has adapted. [[Iron Locusts]] nest in the crevices of the larger fragments, their swarms drifting across the field in rust-colored clouds, processing divine iron into inert oxide through their digestive cycle. [[Wrath-Stalkers]] hunt in pairs among the monoliths, sensing the aggression the Hum provokes in travelers and using it to flush prey. [[Shard-Singers]] coalesce at dusk near the densest deposits, their war-memory hallucinations replaying centuries of battles fought on this ground. The Shardfall is not uninhabited. It is inhabited by things that thrive on what drives everything else away.

## The Migration

The fragments are moving. [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]] of [[The Ember Guild]] has documented it with a surveyor's precision: smaller shards across the Shardfall are drifting toward the larger monoliths at a rate of inches per year, creeping through the soil like roots seeking water. Monoliths that stood fifty yards apart a century ago now stand thirty. Fragment outcroppings that were mapped at fixed positions have shifted measurably between annual surveys. The divine iron is converging.

The migration has accelerated since the Second Silence began. What was inches per year is now inches per month. The Hum is louder. The war-essence contamination gradient is steepening, safe-distance estimates from a decade ago are no longer reliable. [[Wrathspire]]'s smiths report that the iron closest to converging fragments is easier to work but harder to control, blades that hold impossible edges but shatter unpredictably, armor that turns strikes but whispers to its wearer in dreams of violence.

The practical consequences are mounting. [[Iron Locusts]] swarms are following the migration, consuming fragments at accelerating rates, the land's immune response working overtime and losing. [[Shard-Singers]] are appearing more frequently, their war-memory replays reaching further back in time, compressing centuries of stored violence into overlapping hallucinations. Travel routes through the Shardfall that were passable with precautions are narrowing as the contamination zones expand.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The fragments are responding to the weakening of the [[Cosmology and Magic#Ley Lines and the Aetheric Web|Aetheric Web]]. The Web's structure once held [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] scattered essence in place, each fragment pinned by the same force that keeps the world's divine architecture stable. As [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] influence erodes the Web, the pins loosen, and the iron's collective will reasserts itself: a hunger to reunite.
>
> The surface fragments are not the destination. They are migrating toward the Heart of Iron, [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] largest fragment, buried beneath [[Ostivaar]] in [[The Ashen Dominion]]. The Shardfall is a staging ground. If enough divine iron converges on the Heart, the result would not be resurrection but something worse: a mindless divine storm of pure war-essence, a Theomachis weapon without a wielder.
>
> The Monolith's fracture patterns are a map. The lines etched into its surface, visible only at dusk when the iron's luminescence reveals them, show the positions of every major Kaevroth fragment on the continent. [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]] has not yet discovered this (the lines are only visible from specific angles at specific times), but a PC with cartography knowledge, divine material expertise, or fragments of Kaevroth's memories could read it. The map is the single most valuable intelligence asset in the Ironmarch.
>
> **Campaign Role.** The Shardfall is the key to understanding the fragment migration, which is itself a consequence of the Second Silence. Investigating here provides evidence that the Aetheric Web is failing (useful for convincing skeptics in other regions) and reveals the Heart of Iron's location (critical for Act III-IV). [[Mira the Red]]'s connection to Kaevroth runs through the Shardfall's concentrated war-essence, and confronting her here, where the iron amplifies everything, is both the most dangerous and most informative option.

## Encounters & Hazards

**The Hum.** DC 12 Will save per hour spent in the outer Shardfall. Failure: disadvantage on Insight and Persuasion checks as neutral interactions feel hostile. Three cumulative failures: compulsive aggression for 1d4 hours (must respond to any perceived slight with violence, Persuasion attempts against the character automatically fail). Near major monoliths, DC increases to 14 and saves are required every 30 minutes. Ear protection provides no benefit, the Hum travels through bone and soil. [[Cosmology and Magic#Morrhaelite|Morrhaelite]] contact suppresses the effect (as [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] discovered), but carrying death-essence in war-god territory has its own consequences.

**War-Essence Contamination.** Long-term habitation (1+ weeks) without leaving the Shardfall requires a DC 14 Will save per day. Failure: paranoia, insomnia, and iron-grey discoloration of the eyes. Extended failure (5+ days): the contamination becomes self-sustaining, persisting for 1d6 weeks after leaving the Shardfall. Chronic sufferers hear the Hum even in silence.

**Fragment Hazards.** Converging fragments create crush zones where the ground shifts as buried iron moves through soil. DC 13 Dexterity save to avoid being caught between closing shards (2d6 bludgeoning, restrained until freed). Newly exposed fragments emit war-essence bursts as they surface (DC 12 Will save or 1d4 rounds of blind rage).

**Ecology.** [[Iron Locusts]] strip metal from travelers. [[Wrath-Stalkers]] hunt those the Hum has provoked into aggression. [[Shard-Singers]] trap the unwary in battle-hallucinations. The Shardfall's hazards compound: a traveler who loses equipment to locusts, grows angry from the Hum, and draws a weapon becomes a Wrath-Stalker target, and a Shard-Singer's song ensures they are fighting ghosts when the real predators arrive.

## Hooks

1. **The Surveyor's Report.** [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]] needs someone to carry his fragment migration data to [[The Ember Guild]] headquarters. His previous couriers turned back, turned on each other, or disappeared in the Shardfall. The report contains evidence that the fragments are converging on a single point outside the Ironmarch, information that multiple factions would kill to suppress or obtain.

2. **Mira's Pilgrimage.** [[Mira the Red]] has been seen entering the deep Shardfall alone, walking among the monoliths without apparent distress. Her followers say she communes with [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]. [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] wants to know what she is doing there, and whether she is accelerating the migration or being shaped by it.

3. **The Singing Map.** A [[Shard-Singers|Shard-Singer]] near the Kaevroth Monolith has been replaying the same battle for three consecutive dusks, a fight from the Theomachis itself. [[Sira Embersteel]] witnessed part of it and stopped working for three days. She believes the vision contains information about how [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] died and what his last act was. She needs someone brave enough, or foolish enough, to endure the full replay and report what they see.
