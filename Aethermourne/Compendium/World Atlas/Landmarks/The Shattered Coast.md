## Description

The fractured western coastline of [[The Hollowed Reach]], a labyrinth of jagged cliffs, sea caves, tidal pools, and perpetual fog. The coast was shattered during the current crisis as [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]]'s protective infrastructure began to fail: the Tidal Bridges that once calmed the surf are crumbling, the fog barriers that kept the deep cold offshore have thinned, and the wards carved into the cliffside are going dark one by one. What remains is a coastline that feels actively hostile, stone that crumbles underfoot, currents that shift without warning, and fog that swallows sound and direction alike.

The fog carries what locals call "the breath," a mineral chill rising from the [[The Hollowed Reach#The Abyssal Trench|Abyssal Trench]] that coats the tongue with the taste of salt and deep iron. The sensory palette is grey and silver: grey stone, grey water, grey sky, broken only by occasional flashes of wrong color, bioluminescent algae pulsing in alien rhythms, Glass Pool reflections showing things that are not there, the faint green-black glow of Trench-touched wreckage on the shore. The Shattered Coast is not a destination. It is what you pass through to reach [[Mistholm]], [[Weeping Shore]], and the sea caves beyond, and it should feel dangerous every time.

## Sub-Locations

### The Broken Teeth

Jagged rock spires jutting from the surf where the coastline fractured, some reaching forty feet above the waterline, others barely breaking the surface at high tide. Navigating between them by boat requires a DC 14 Dexterity check in calm conditions, DC 18 during fog or rough seas. The spires create natural acoustics that amplify sound, which is why [[Fog Singers]] congregate here, their melody bouncing between the stones until it comes from everywhere at once. The gaps between spires are natural ambush points: narrow, fog-choked, and impossible to see through until you are already inside.

### The Tidal Caves

A network of sea caves exposed at low tide and submerged at high, honeycombing the cliffs for miles along the coast. Some are shallow, ending in dripping stone after twenty feet. Others connect to underground systems that extend inland, beneath [[Mistholm]], beneath the cliffs, and in at least two cases, downward toward the Trench. One particular cave, **Saltmother's Mouth**, bears [[Veil Unbound]] markings scratched into the stone above the high-water line: a spiral glyph and a system of notches that function as a dead-drop for cult communications between coastal cells. Messages are left in waterproof bone tubes wedged into cracks. [[The Whisperer]] has used at least two coastal caves for communion with [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], and Saltmother's Mouth connects to the same cave network.

### The Glass Pools

Tidal pools where the water is supernaturally still and clear, like liquid glass, even when the surf crashes around them. Looking into a Glass Pool does not show your reflection. It shows something beneath the surface looking back, a pale shape, indistinct, patient. These are natural thin spots where Serith's perception reaches the surface, scrying points that function whether anyone intends them to or not. [[The Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keepers]] mark known Glass Pools with warning stakes driven into the rock nearby. A DC 13 Wisdom save is required to look away once you have looked in. Failure means you stand transfixed for 1d4 rounds, during which time Serith observes you and learns what you are carrying, where you are going, and what you are afraid of. New Glass Pools appear after every Black Tide.

### The Fog Banks

The perpetual coastal fog is not weather. It is [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]]'s failing barrier made visible, the last remnant of divine protection dissolving into grey mist. Navigation through the Fog Banks requires a DC 13 Wisdom check every hour of travel. Failure means lost direction and 1d4 hours of wasted movement. Prolonged exposure (more than four hours) causes vivid waking dreams, scenes of the ocean floor, the Trench's darkness, a voice that sounds like someone you trust. After six hours, a DC 14 Will save is required to resist the compulsion to walk toward the water, a lighter version of the [[Sleepwalkers|Sleepwalker]] effect. Everyone on the Coast is mildly under Serith's influence. They just do not stay long enough for it to take hold.

### The Wreck Shore

A half-mile stretch of gravel beach where currents deposit debris from ships that sailed too close to the Trench. Splintered hulls, tangled rigging, cargo crates split by pressure, and occasionally bodies, waterlogged and eerily preserved by whatever the Trench water does to organic matter. Scavengers work the shore despite risks, and a small economy of salvaged goods flows inland to [[Mistholm]] and beyond. One regular scavenger, a woman called **Ossa**, has started finding objects that are clearly made, not shipwreck debris but artifacts of unknown origin pushed up from the Trench floor. She cannot identify the material. She cannot damage it. And she has noticed that she feels calmer, more organized, more certain when she holds them.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Coast is where Yvenne's protective infrastructure is failing. Each failure, a crumbled Tidal Bridge, a darkened ward, a thinned fog barrier, opens a new vector for Serith's influence. The decline is accelerating.
>
> **Glass Pool Proliferation.** Before the current crisis there were perhaps a dozen Glass Pools. Now there are scores. Each one is a point where Serith can observe the surface. The [[The Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keepers]] cannot mark them fast enough. Some Keepers have stopped trying and simply avoid the coast entirely.
>
> **Saltmother's Mouth.** The cave connects to the same network [[The Whisperer]] uses. It is part of the [[Veil Unbound]]'s communication infrastructure linking coastal cells from [[Weeping Shore]] to [[Mistholm]] and beyond. Intercepting the dead-drop messages reveals cell locations, communion schedules, and the names of converted [[Sleepwalkers]] who now serve willingly.
>
> **The Wreck Shore Artifacts.** The objects Ossa is finding are fragments of [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]]'s divine bone, pieces of the prison lattice that are breaking free as the seal weakens. They are functionally indestructible and radiate a cold, ordering influence (a connection to Solvaen's domain of structure). Serith's prison was forged from this material. The fact that pieces are surfacing means the prison is physically degrading, not just weakening. This is physical evidence of the campaign's central threat, and Ossa has been pocketing it.

## Encounters & Hazards

- **Environmental:** Crumbling rock (DC 12 Dexterity to traverse), tidal surges (Constitution save DC 14 or swept into the surf), hypothermic fog exposure (1d4 cold damage per hour without protection)
- **[[Fog Singers]]:** Melody at the Broken Teeth, cumulative Will saves (DC 12, +1 per round of exposure) against compulsion to follow the sound toward the water
- **Glass Pool Visions:** Wisdom save DC 13 to look away, failure grants Serith intelligence on the viewer
- **Trench-Touched Objects:** Contact with Wreck Shore artifacts requires a DC 12 Will save or gain a persistent sense of calm order that makes chaotic or emotional decisions difficult for 1d4 hours
- **Creature Encounters:** [[Trench Crawlers]] in the Tidal Caves, [[Fog Singers]] at the Broken Teeth, [[Tideborn Horrors]] on the shore after Black Tides

## Hooks

1. **The New Pool.** A [[The Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keeper]] patrol reports a Glass Pool showing clear images of the Trench floor, not the usual indistinct shapes but detailed architecture, a lattice of pale bone holding something vast and dark. The Keepers need someone to verify what they saw.
2. **The Dead-Drop.** Saltmother's Mouth contains a [[Veil Unbound]] message in a bone tube, written in a cipher the PCs can crack (DC 15 Intelligence). The message names the next communion date and location, and references "the offering the Depth-Singer's bell will silence."
3. **The Scavenger's Find.** Ossa on the Wreck Shore has an artifact she cannot destroy or put down. She is calmer than she has ever been, more focused, and increasingly unable to tolerate disorder. She wants help. She also does not want to give the artifact up.
