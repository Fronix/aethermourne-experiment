## Description

The Skein is the heart of [[The Loom's Edge]], a high plateau where [[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas's]] excised fate-threads hang exposed in the open air. They are visible to the naked eye: silver and gold filaments suspended between stone spires, connecting to nothing, leading everywhere, humming with the resonance of futures that were never allowed to happen. The threads are not metaphorical. They are not symbolic. They are the literal substance of excised destiny, patterns torn from the [[Cosmology and Magic#The Loom of Fate|Loom of Fate]] and buried here where the Loom's influence was weakest.

The Skein is not survivable by conventional means. The density of exposed fate-threads creates an information field that overwhelms mortal cognition. Standing on the plateau means perceiving every possible future simultaneously, not as visions or prophecies, but as *experienced reality*. Every branching path, every decision, every consequence, all at once, all equally real. The mind collapses under the weight. Most who enter the Skein do not leave. Those who do are rarely coherent enough to describe what they saw.

The plateau itself is roughly two miles across, ringed by the Mirror Valleys and accessible only through the Threadstone Passes. The stone is pale grey, veined with silver, warm to the touch. The air is perfectly still, no wind reaches the Skein, as though the atmosphere itself has been suspended, caught between moments. Sound behaves strangely: words spoken aloud arrive at the listener's ears before the speaker opens their mouth. Footsteps echo in rhythms that do not match the walker's pace.

At the center of the Skein stands the **Loom Scar**, a vertical tear in reality roughly sixty feet tall and ten feet wide. Through the Scar, something vast and silver is partially visible, not the Loom itself (which exists partially outside reality), but the wound Orenthas left when he cut the threads free. The Scar pulses with light that is not light, a radiance that illuminates nothing but is perceived nonetheless, felt in the bones and behind the eyes.

The Scar has been stable for two thousand years. It is no longer stable. The edges are widening. The light is brightening. Whatever Orenthas sealed within the excised patterns is pressing against its containment, and the containment is failing.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **What the Loom Scar contains:** The counter-prophecy. Not a document or a message, but a *living pattern*, a self-executing fate-thread that describes a future where the Loom of Fate becomes absolute authority. In this future, free will is an illusion. Every mortal life is predetermined. Suffering ends because choice ends. Peace reigns because no one can choose war.
>
> Orenthas found this future within his own creation and was horrified. He tore it out. He hid it in the one place the Loom's reach was weakest. He then engineered the Theomachis to shatter the divine infrastructure that could have sustained the counter-prophecy's execution.
>
> But the pattern is patient. It does not need the gods. It does not need the Aetheric Web. It needs only to be *completed*, to find enough living souls to weave into its structure to reach critical mass. The Second Silence, by weakening every other system of order, is making the counter-prophecy the strongest pattern remaining. It is winning by default.
>
> **The campaign decision:** The PCs must reach the Loom Scar and choose. They can:
> 1. **Destroy the counter-prophecy.** Permanently end the possibility of guaranteed survival. The world remains free but continues dying.
> 2. **Accept the counter-prophecy.** Surrender free will in exchange for a guaranteed future. The world survives but becomes a cage.
> 3. **Reweave the counter-prophecy.** Attempt to modify the pattern, keeping its stabilizing effects while preserving choice. This is the hardest path, requiring Primordial knowledge, divine understanding, and mortal will in concert. Success is not guaranteed. Failure means the worst of both options.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Cognitive Annihilation.** Entering the Skein without preparation means experiencing every possible future at once. Mechanically, this is an immediate and catastrophic mental assault. The [[The Loom's Edge#The Threadwalkers|Threadwalker]] anchoring discipline provides partial protection, reducing the assault to manageable (but still dangerous) levels. Divine magic provides different protection: filtering the threads through a single god's domain, seeing only the futures relevant to that god. Both protections degrade over time.

**Fate Resonance (Acute).** The Skein's concentrated threads cause immediate and severe Fate Resonance. Within minutes, individuals begin losing the ability to distinguish between possible and actual futures. Within hours, they begin *living* multiple timelines simultaneously, their body flickering between states as the threads try to weave them into the pattern.

**The Weavers.** Silver-thread humanoid shapes that move through the Skein with apparent purpose, connecting threads, severing others, rearranging patterns. They do not acknowledge mortals. They do not communicate. Whether they are fragments of Orenthas's consciousness, autonomous pattern-maintenance systems, or something else entirely is unknown. They are not hostile, but they will defend the Loom Scar if anyone attempts to interfere with it.

**Temporal Paradox.** Within the Skein, time has no direction. PCs may encounter future versions of themselves, past versions of their enemies, or echoes of events that cannot have occurred. These encounters are real within the Skein's context but may not persist outside it. The GM should use paradoxes to reveal information, create dramatic tension, and force philosophical choices.

## Hooks

1. **The Final Expedition.** [[The Loom's Edge#The Threadwalkers|Threadmaster Kaeven]] has determined that the Loom Scar will open fully within months. She needs the PCs to reach it before it opens on its own terms. The expedition requires Threadwalker guides, Primordial protections from [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]], and divine knowledge from the known continent. Assembling these resources is a quest in itself.

2. **The Scar Speaks.** The Loom Scar has begun emitting sound, a voice that carries across the entire Loom's Edge. It speaks in Orenthas's cadence, reciting the Final Prophecy in reverse. The Threadwalkers at [[Skeinhold]] believe this is a countdown. When the reversed prophecy completes, the Scar opens. They have been tracking the recitation. It is accelerating.

3. **The Thread That Leads Home.** One of the Skein's exposed threads connects to the known continent, a silver filament stretching eastward across the ocean, visible from the plateau's edge. Following it (metaphysically, not physically) reveals visions of the Second Silence's progression: the Aetheric Web's final collapse, Serith's release, the Boundary's breach. The thread shows what happens if no one intervenes. It is not hopeful.
