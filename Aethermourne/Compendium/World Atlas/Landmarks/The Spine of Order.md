## Description

The Spine of Order is [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] actual vertebral column, a mountain range of divine bone running north-to-south through the Dominion's heart. The smallest vertebrae are the size of hillforts. The largest dwarf any natural mountain on the continent. Where natural stone has grown around the bones over two millennia, the boundary between god and geology is often invisible, until a mine shaft breaks through grey granite into the smooth, pale surface of something that was once alive.

Veins of crystallized [[Cosmology and Magic#Ashite|Ashite]], Solvaen's blood turned to stone, thread through the vertebrae. They glow faintly at dawn and dusk, a cold blue-white light that pulses with a regularity too precise to be natural. The glow is brightest near The Crown, the topmost vertebra where Solvaen's skull would have connected to his body. The skull itself was driven into the earth on impact and has never been found. [[Crown's Reach]] sits at The Crown's base, a pilgrimage town built on the faith that the skull rests in a sacred hollow beneath the world.

The Spine is the foundation of the Dominion's economy, its faith, and its identity. [[Hearthstone]] is wedged into a cleft between two lesser vertebrae, its mines pushing deeper each decade as surface deposits thin. [[Ostivaar]], the capital, sits within Solvaen's ribcage to the south. The entire civilization is built on, in, and around these bones, and the bones are running out of what made them valuable.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Spine creates the densest point in the Membrane on the continent, the barrier between the mortal world and the Outer Planes. This means planar incursions are almost impossible near the Spine, but it also means the Iron Descent's slow expansion is being funneled around it, creating pressure points at the Spine's northern and southern tips. If the Ashite is ever fully depleted, the Membrane thins with it.
>
> The Ashfield Heresy adventure notes that an Arcana DC 18 check at the Spine detects a discordant note in the Hum, a wound inconsistent with [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] fire during the Theomachis. Something cold. Something that hides. This is [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] influence, a crack in the divine remains that the imprisoned god's entropy is slowly widening. The Cinders know about the discordant note. They are ensuring no one investigates it.

## Encounters & Hazards

**The Hum.** A subsonic resonance that pervades the Spine's entire length. Visitors feel it in their teeth and chest before they hear it. Locals claim they no longer notice it. The Hum makes spontaneous thought feel effortful, like pushing against a current. Creative solutions come slower. Ordered, methodical thinking feels natural, even pleasurable. At The Crown's summit, the Hum induces absolute certainty in whatever the listener is thinking, a phenomenon the Church calls divine revelation and the miners call "getting tuned."

> [!example] The Hum (Nimble)
> Within 1 mile of the Spine: disadvantage on creative/improvisational checks. Will DC 12 to resist compulsive ordering behavior (straightening objects, organizing belongings, correcting others' grammar). At the summit of The Crown, Will DC 16 or gain unshakeable certainty in the current dominant thought, true or not, lasting 1d6 days.

**Order Sickness.** Prolonged exposure to raw Ashite causes progressive mental rigidity. The Church calls it divine communion. The miners call it a death sentence.

> [!example] Order Sickness (Nimble)
> 8+ hours near raw Ashite: DC 13 Will save per day. Failure: -1 to all non-Calculism checks (cumulative, max -3). Advanced cases lose the ability to improvise, deviate from routine, or tolerate disorder. Resets after 3 days away from Ashite. Chronic sufferers (years of exposure) do not fully reset.

**Ashite Mine Collapse.** The deeper shafts are structurally unsound. Divine bone does not behave like natural rock, it resonates, shifts, and occasionally *reorganizes* when disturbed.

The Spine is also home to [[Calcified Stalkers]], which hunt in tessellation patterns across the surrounding Ashfields and along the lower slopes.

## Hooks

1. **The Thinning Veins.** [[Hearthstone]]'s deep shaft miners report that the newest tunnels are producing less Ashite than projected. A Calculist surveyor named Verity Ashgrove has been mapping the depletion using geometric divination, and her numbers show the veins are emptying faster than natural extraction accounts for. Someone, or something, is draining Ashite from below. Her reports keep being "lost" by Church administrators.
2. **The Discordant Note.** A Star-Reader visiting the Spine detected something wrong with the Hum, a cold frequency beneath the order that should not be there. She wants to investigate further but needs access to the deep shafts, which requires Church approval that will never come. She is looking for someone willing to go without permission.
3. **The Sealed Document.** A miner near [[Crown's Reach]] broke through into a previously unknown chamber and found a sealed document dated 847 AS. The Church confiscated it immediately. The miner remembers one line: *"The bones do not sleep. They listen."*
