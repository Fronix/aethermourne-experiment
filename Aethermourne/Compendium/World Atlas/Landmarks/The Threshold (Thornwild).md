# The Threshold (Thornwild)

*Not to be confused with the Threshold zone of [[The Shattered Edge]]. This is the Thornwild's central balance band where [[The Bloom]] and [[The Wilt]] meet.*

## Description

The Threshold is the narrow band where [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s growth and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s decay achieve something close to balance. It runs through [[The Thornwild]]'s center like a seam, sometimes a mile wide, sometimes narrowing to a few hundred yards, shifting with the Cycle's rhythm. Here the air carries both the sweetness of the [[The Bloom|Bloom]] and the dry parchment scent of the [[The Wilt|Wilt]], layered together into something that smells like neither and both. Trees along the Threshold grow at a measured pace, their bark half-green and half-white, leaves that are alive on one side and skeletal on the other. The light filters through in patterns that shift as the Cycle turns, warm gold yielding to pale silver and back.

This is where Thornwild amber forms. Not through geological pressure or slow accretion, but through the [[Threshold Weavers]], arachnid creatures whose amber-silk webs crystallize during transition moments when both divine forces are precisely equal. The Weavers are the amber's producers, not its incidental inhabitants, and the Thornwild's entire amber economy depends on their territorial instincts driving them to spin at crystallization points along the Threshold's length. Timing the harvest means working in the narrow window after crystallization and before the Weavers return to claim their territory. [[Garret Boneharvest]] has built [[Bone Garden]]'s economy around this rhythm.

[[Cyclethorn]] (~2,000), the Thornwild's largest settlement, sits on a granite outcrop near the Threshold's center that resists both divine essences. [[Yara Thornbloom]] reads the Cycle's pulse from The Perch, [[Cyclethorn]]'s observation platform. [[Kael Duskborne]] and the [[Thornguard]] defend the Threshold settlements. [[The Ember Guild]] and [[The Star-Readers]] have both sent representatives, drawn by the amber trade and the Cycle's academic interest respectively. The Thornwild's isolation is ending, and not everyone in [[Cyclethorn]] is glad.

The Threshold has narrowed by a third in the past year. Where it once gave [[Cyclethorn]] comfortable breathing room on both sides, the Bloom and the Wilt now press closer, their boundary effects visible from the settlement walls. Recent amber harvests have produced inclusions that should not be there: tiny preserved organisms from no known species, fragments of text in no known language, and occasionally what appear to be faces frozen mid-expression in the golden resin.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Yara Thornbloom]] has detected a "third pulse" in the Cycle for the past six months, a rhythm that belongs to neither Thyrea nor Morrhael. She has not told anyone. The pulse is [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s influence, subtle and systematic, destabilizing the equilibrium that keeps the Threshold open.
>
> The granite beneath [[Cyclethorn]] contains pre-Theomachis stonework: carved symbols in an ancient language and a faint resonance of something older than the god-war. The [[Cyclekeepers]] know the stone resists divine energy but have never investigated why. Whatever built the foundation predates both Thyrea and Morrhael's deaths.
>
> An [[Cosmology and Magic#The Aetheric Web|Aetheric Web]] anchor (Cross-Region Chain 5, Waypoint 3) sits at risk of failure as the Threshold narrows. If the Threshold closes entirely, the Bloom and Wilt collide directly, triggering a catastrophic Cycle break that would consume the region. Stabilizing the Threshold is a regional objective in Act III-IV of the campaign.

## Encounters & Hazards

**[[Threshold Weavers]]** are territorial and aggressive near crystallization points. Their webs of amber-silk function as both trap and construction material, load-bearing strands that can support a creature's weight before snapping shut around prey. Their bite delivers a paralytic venom combining growth-acceleration and numbing cold, and victims are cocooned in cycling tissue that slowly becomes part of the amber-producing network.

**[[Sap-Wraiths]]** form frequently at transition points along the Threshold, their composition flickering between green-gold and ashen grey as both divine forces compete for dominance within their translucent bodies.

**Threshold Shift** is the most disorienting hazard. The balance band moves. A party camping in equilibrium territory may wake to find the Bloom encroaching from one side, vines pushing through the undergrowth, or the Wilt advancing from the other, trees bleaching white and leaves curling to parchment. Mid-encounter, the terrain itself may change: what was balanced ground becomes Bloom or Wilt territory, altering the behavior of creatures, the properties of the air, and the nature of the footing.

**Amber Crystallization** can trap the unwary. During intense transition moments, the amber-forming process accelerates and any organic material in contact with Weaver silk or active crystallization points risks partial encasement. The inclusions in recent harvests, organisms, text fragments, faces, suggest the crystallization is capturing things it shouldn't, recording echoes of the Cycle's increasing instability.

## Hooks

1. **The Narrowing.** The Threshold is closing faster than [[Yara Thornbloom]]'s models predicted. The [[Cyclekeepers]] need help mapping which sections remain stable and which are collapsing, a journey along the Threshold's full length through Weaver territory, shifting boundaries, and increasingly volatile transition events.
2. **Wrong Amber.** [[Garret Boneharvest]]'s traders are refusing to buy recent harvests. The inclusions, faces, text, unknown organisms, are unsettling buyers and threatening to collapse the amber economy that sustains [[Bone Garden]] and [[Cyclethorn]]. Garret needs someone to investigate what the [[Threshold Weavers]] are capturing and why.
3. **Under the Stone.** The granite beneath [[Cyclethorn]] has always resisted both divine essences, but no one has ever asked why. Pre-Theomachis stonework lies beneath the settlement's foundation, carved with symbols that predate the god-war. What built it, and what does it mean that it still holds when everything else in the Thornwild is losing balance?
