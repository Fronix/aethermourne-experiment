## Description

The Warm Valleys are the fertile lowlands nestled between the peaks of [[The Caldera Ring]], heated from below by geothermal vents that keep the soil warm even in the deepest winter. They radiate outward from [[Crucible]] like the spaces between fingers, each valley shaped by the peaks that frame it. The largest runs northeast from the capital toward [[Hearthdeep]]'s surface entrance at the inner roots of the Anvil. [[Anvilrest]] occupies a smaller eastern valley between the Tongs and the Quench. The western valleys are the most agricultural, their broad floors given over to terraced fields of hardy root vegetables and heat-loving grains that thrive nowhere else on the continent.

Hot springs dot the valley floors, some large enough to bathe in, others scalding and dangerous, marked by rings of mineral crust and the sharp smell of sulfur. Steam rises from the ground on cold mornings, filling the valleys with luminous fog that glows amber at dawn and dusk. The air smells of mineral water and warm earth. Roughly 140,000 of the Emberveil's 200,000 people live here, in towns and villages clustered around the most reliable thermal vents.

The vents themselves are called **Thalvor's Breath**, and each is tended by a guild-appointed keeper who monitors its temperature, flow, and mineral output. Vent-keepers are minor civic officials, respected but unglamorous, the kind of people who wake before dawn to check gauges and go to bed worrying about pressure readings. They maintain the ventilation shafts that channel heat upward from the [[The Deep Warrens]] below, and they are the first to notice when something changes. Lately, something has been changing.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The western vent failures are the Cooling's most visible surface symptom. The compensatory mechanism in [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] deep forge is redirecting thermal energy away from peripheral vents to maintain core output, sacrificing the margins to preserve the center. The Forge Council knows the western valleys are failing but has no solution. Publicly acknowledging the failures would cause panic that could fracture the guild-based social order.
>
> The buried structure exposed by the reversed spring is an auxiliary forge-vent, part of [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] original divine infrastructure, sealed by the upheaval of the Theomachis and untouched for two millennia. If excavated, it would provide a second access point to the deep forge, bypassing [[The Deep Warrens]] entirely. [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] would very much like someone to find it. The structure's exposure is not accidental, the compensatory mechanism's thermal redistribution is uncovering things that were meant to stay buried.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Scalding Springs.** Not all hot springs are safe. Unmarked springs can reach temperatures that blister exposed skin on contact. DC 12 Perception to notice the telltale mineral crusting and heat shimmer before stepping too close. Contact: 1d6 fire damage, or 2d6 for full immersion.

**Luminous Fog.** On cold mornings, the steam rising from the valley floors creates dense, disorienting fog. Navigation through unfamiliar valleys requires DC 12 Survival checks. The fog is not dangerous in itself, but it conceals terrain hazards and makes ambush easy.

**[[Cinder Sprites]].** Abundant in healthy valleys, their bioluminescent swarms provide ambient warmth and light. Their absence in the cooling western zones is itself a warning, a canary in a geothermal mine. Zones without sprites are measurably colder and growing more so each season.

**Vent Collapse.** Failed vents leave unstable ground where the ventilation shafts below have partially collapsed. DC 13 Perception to notice the subsidence before crossing. Collapse: DC 14 Dexterity save or fall 10-20 feet into a cooling shaft, 2d6 bludgeoning damage.

## Hooks

1. **The Cold Breath.** Three Thalvor's Breath vents in the western valleys have gone cold this season, the first recorded failures in the Emberveil's history. Crops in the affected zones are already dying, the soil cooling to temperatures that kill the heat-loving grains within weeks. The vent-keepers reported the failures immediately. [[The Forge Council]] has ordered silence while they investigate. The keepers are frightened, the farmers are angry, and the silence is not holding.

2. **The Reversed Spring.** A hot spring in the northeast valley has reversed its flow, pulling water downward instead of pushing it up. A sinkhole is forming around the spring's basin, widening by a foot each day. At the bottom, partially exposed by the subsidence, something is visible: a structure of dark metal laced with veins of faintly glowing mineral. It is warm. It hums. [[Ashara Cinderbloom]] at [[Cinderfall]] has heard rumors and wants to examine it before the Forge Council seals the site.

3. **The Blue Fog.** In one of the central valleys, the luminous morning fog has changed color, shifting from its normal amber to a deep blue-green that no one has seen before. The [[Cinder Sprites]] in that valley have stopped their normal dispersal patterns and begun clustering in dense, stationary swarms above the affected vents, pulsing in unison. Travelers passing through report feeling watched, not threatened, but observed with patient, focused attention. The vent-keeper for that section has resigned, citing "a feeling I cannot explain and do not wish to."
