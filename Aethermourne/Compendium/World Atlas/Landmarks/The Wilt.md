## Description

The Wilt is where death organizes itself. The northeastern reaches of [[The Thornwild]] are a forest of skeletal trees with bark like parchment, leafless and bone-white, standing in geometric rows that no natural growth pattern produces. [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s necromantic essence imposes a terrible order on decay here, and the result is a landscape that looks curated rather than wild: deadwood carpeting the ground in neat radial patterns, fallen trunks arranged like ribs, clearings opening at regular intervals as if the forest were breathing and left spaces for its lungs.

[[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s underlying pulse still reaches the Wilt, and during the Cycle's life-phase the skeletal trees flush briefly with impossible color: leaves unfurling from bare branches, sap running warm through dead wood, the air shifting from cold parchment to something almost like spring. The Remnants in [[Ashbloom]] live for these moments. The settlement of roughly 800, half living, half [[Remnants Court|Remnant]], sits deep in the Wilt where the life-phase pulse is strongest. For a few hours each Cycle, the Remnants feel warmth, hunger, the phantom sensation of breath filling lungs that no longer need it. It never lasts.

[[Bone Garden]] (~1,200) sits on the border between the Wilt and [[The Threshold (Thornwild)|the Threshold]], its harvesters working the edge of Morrhael's influence where cycle-materials are richest. [[Revenant Moss]] leads Ashbloom's community with quiet authority. And in a house at the settlement's edge, Tomas Breck sits in his chair, a harvester who died in the Wilt and walked home in a state that is neither living nor Remnant. He responds to questions in single words. He does not eat, sleep, or decay. No one knows what he is.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Revenant Moss]] has felt the life-phase restoration weakening over the past year. The warmth is less warm, the breath shallower, the sensations more distant. Moss has not told anyone, fearing what it means for Ashbloom's growing Remnant community and the pilgrimages arriving from [[Ashenmere]].
>
> The [[Cycle-Stalkers|Cycle-Stalker]] mega-packs (16+) appearing near the [[The Bonewood|Bonewood]] border are a symptom, not a cause. The Bonewood's accelerating Cycle is pushing predators outward into the Wilt. Tomas Breck's "third state" mirrors [[The Bloom-Wraith]] on a smaller, human scale. Understanding one may be the key to understanding the other.
>
> Remnant pilgrimages from [[Ashenmere]] are increasing, and pilgrims who die along the route through the Wilt rise during Death Tides as confused, directionless undead, creating a trail of lost Remnants between the Pale Wastes border and Ashbloom.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Death Tides** are the Wilt's defining danger. When the Cycle's death-phase peaks, everything that has died since the last Tide rises. Not as mindless undead, but as participants in the Wilt's necrotic ecology: risen predators hunt risen prey, risen prey flees along the same paths it used in life. The risen retain no intelligence, only pattern. A Death Tide transforms the silent forest into a hunting ground where the dead replay their final hours on loop.

**[[Cycle-Stalkers]]** are wolves reshaped by the death-rebirth rhythm into tactical pack hunters. Packs of four to eight move through the Wilt's skeletal corridors, and when killed, they rise within minutes retaining full memory of the fight. Every death teaches the pack. Every resurrection repositions a member to counter what killed it. Near the [[The Bonewood|Bonewood]] border, mega-packs of sixteen have been observed, driven outward by the accelerating interior Cycle.

**[[Bone Choir]]** manifests during Death Tides along the Wilt's border with the Bonewood, a synchronized swarm of small cycling animal remains producing a harmonic hum that resonates in the listener's mind. Prolonged exposure induces passivity, a desire to sit down, close eyes, rest. The Choir drifts through the Wilt like weather, following necrotic currents. Recently, it was heard singing what sounded like a complete sentence for the first time: *"The gate is open and no one stands at the threshold."*

**The Transition Moment** is not a hazard but a roleplay encounter. When the life-phase arrives, the skeletal trees flush with color, the air warms, and risen dead throughout the Wilt gasp and touch their own faces with something like recognition. For a few minutes the Wilt is beautiful, green and gold threading through the bone-white, and then it reverses. Watching a Remnant experience this, the hope and the loss, is the Wilt's truest danger: not to the body, but to the heart.

## Hooks

1. **The Lost Pilgrim.** A Remnant pilgrim from [[Ashenmere]] is overdue. They were last seen heading deeper into the Wilt, seeking a spot where the life-phase pulse was stronger, closer to how it used to feel. The route passes through [[Cycle-Stalkers|Cycle-Stalker]] territory, and the next Death Tide is hours away.
2. **The Third State.** Tomas Breck needs investigation. The [[Cyclekeepers]] want to understand what he is, the [[Remnants Court]] wants to claim him as one of their own, and the [[The Ashen Vigil|Ashen Vigil]] wants him burned. His condition may hold answers about the Cycle's acceleration, but studying him means choosing whose authority to recognize.
3. **The Choir's Sentence.** The [[Bone Choir]] was heard singing a complete, intelligible sentence for the first time in recorded history. [[Yara Thornbloom]] needs witnesses who can get close enough to hear it again and report exactly what it says, without succumbing to the Choir's passivity effect. The [[Cyclekeepers]] believe the Choir is assembling fragments of [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s shattered death-song, and a complete sentence may be the first coherent message from a dead god in centuries.
