## Overview

Draveth clings to the interior walls of a volcanic caldera in the Ashfall Range, its terraced levels descending from the rim to the geothermal vents at the basin floor. The city is black stone and heat, every surface warm to the touch, every shadow lit from below by the orange glow of magma channels that the Pact-Weavers have negotiated into providing both warmth and power. The air smells of sulfur and hot iron, and the constant low rumble of the mountain's breath is so pervasive that Draveth's citizens no longer hear it. Visitors do.

This is a frontier city in every sense. The Ashfall Range marks the western edge of settled Oldborn territory, and beyond the caldera rim, [[The Primordia]] begins. The land there does not obey. Mountains rise and fall like breathing. Forests grow and burn in hours. Gyre-Wraiths drift at the Shimmer's edge. Draveth exists because the Oldborn need what the Primordia produces: raw Primordial materials that can be extracted, refined, and used to maintain the Anchors that hold Oldborn civilization together.

Life in Draveth is hard, dangerous, and direct. There is no patience here for Ur-Saethen's scholarly debates or Keth'vaal's political maneuvering. The caldera teaches a simple lesson: the mountain gives nothing freely, and everything extracted comes with a cost negotiated in real time. This philosophy has produced a population of pragmatists, risk-takers, and soldiers, and a political faction, the [[The Oldborn Council#The Ash Reclaimers|Ash Reclaimers]], that believes the Oldborn should stop waiting and start expanding.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Draveth's volcano is not dormant. It is *negotiated*. A Primordial pact struck four centuries ago keeps the magma channels stable and the eruption cycle suppressed. The pact costs the city one significant sacrifice per century, traditionally a masterwork of Draveth craftsmanship destroyed in the caldera's heart. The next sacrifice is due within the year, and the Pact-Weavers report the volcano's demands have changed. It no longer wants objects. It wants *knowledge*, specifically, knowledge of the outlanders. The Primordia is curious.

## Layout & Landmarks

**The Rim**
The caldera's upper edge, where the city meets the sky. Watchtowers ring the rim, manned by Boundary Commanders who scan the Primordia's edge for incursions. The Rim is also where Draveth's military trains, conducting drills on black stone platforms that overlook the volcanic interior on one side and the chaotic wilderness on the other. The view is a daily reminder of what the city defends against.

**The Terrace Mines**
Cut into the caldera walls, the mines descend in spiraling terraces toward the basin floor. Miners extract volcanic glass, obsidian, raw Primordial resonance crystals, and occasionally materials that have no name, substances pushed up from the Primordia's depths that defy conventional analysis. The Mining Guilds control access, set quotas, and fund the city's politics through [[Speaker Dravos]]'s Ash Reclaimers faction.

**The Forge Basin**
The caldera floor, where geothermal vents provide natural furnace heat. Draveth's smiths work here, crafting weapons, tools, and Anchor components using Primordial materials and techniques that combine physical craft with negotiated pact-magic. The Basin is brutally hot and constantly dangerous; a vent can shift without warning if the volcanic pact fluctuates. Smiths carry resonance chimes that warn of instability.

**The Speaker's Redoubt**
[[Speaker Dravos]]'s headquarters, a fortified compound on the caldera's eastern rim. Part military command post, part political headquarters, part armory. Dravos conducts strategy sessions here, meeting with Mining Guild leaders, Boundary Commanders, and, increasingly, covert contacts from the known continent's merchant houses.

## People & Culture

Draveth's citizens are defined by proximity to danger. Every resident has basic combat training and emergency evacuation protocols. Children learn to read resonance chimes before they learn to read text. The city's governance reflects this: the Trial-of-Craft that selects Draveth's Speaker tests practical mastery, not theory. Candidates must demonstrate mining technique, volcanic negotiation, combat readiness, and crisis management. The most capable leads. Ambition is not merely tolerated here; it is expected.

The city's relationship with the Primordia is complex. Draveth's miners venture further into raw chaos than any other Oldborn, extracting materials from the boundary zone where reality thins. This breeds a particular kind of courage and a particular kind of arrogance. Draveth's people believe they understand the Primordia better than anyone, and they may be right, but understanding is not the same as control.

### Notable Residents

- [[Speaker Dravos]], the city's representative on the Circle. A former Boundary Commander whose ambition has expanded from defending the caldera to reshaping the continent's power structure.
- **Forge-Master Kethra**, the Basin's senior smith, a quiet woman whose Primordial-forged tools are considered the finest in the Kingdoms. She distrusts Dravos's expansionist politics but depends on his Mining Guild funding.
- **Boundary Commander Sael**, Dravos's military successor at the Rim. Loyal to the city, not to Dravos personally, a distinction that may matter when loyalties are tested.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The Volcanic Demand.** The century-pact sacrifice is due, and the volcano wants knowledge of the outlanders instead of a masterwork. The Pact-Weavers are debating whether to comply (sharing intelligence with a Primordial force) or attempt renegotiation (risking the caldera's stability).

2. **The Boundary Breach.** Something crossed the Primordia's edge last week, not a Gyre-Wraith but something structured, deliberate, and old. It left footprints in volcanic glass that match no known species. Boundary Commander Sael wants it found before Dravos uses the incursion to justify military expansion.

3. **Dravos's Network.** The Speaker has been sending covert messages to merchant houses in [[The Hollowed Reach]]. If exposed, it proves the Ash Reclaimers are already building the infrastructure for continental intervention. The Circle would strip his Speakership. He has planned for that, too.

4. **The Ashfall Prophecy.** The volcanoes of the Ashfall Range are erupting out of cycle. The pacts are failing. The Pact-Weavers cannot determine why. Whispered rumors suggest something in the deep volcanic chambers, something older than the Primordials, is waking.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Dravos has stockpiled Primordial resonance crystals sufficient to outfit a small expeditionary force with Anchor-calibrated weapons. He is preparing for a scenario where Oldborn intervention on the known continent becomes necessary, or advantageous. The crystals are hidden in the deep Terrace Mines, guarded by Ash Reclaimer loyalists. If the PCs discover this cache, it confirms Dravos's coup is not theoretical but logistical.
