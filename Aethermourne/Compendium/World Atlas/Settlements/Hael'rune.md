## Overview

Hael'rune sits on the eastern edge of [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]], where the highland plateau descends through terraced foothills toward [[The Veilshore]]'s coastal lowlands. It is the first Oldborn city that outlanders would reach if they traveled inland from [[Tidecross]], and the last line of Oldborn civilization before the cultural contact zone begins. This position has defined the city for three years, ever since the first outlander ships pierced [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]]'s weakened barrier.

The city is built from the same black volcanic stone as all Oldborn settlements, but Hael'rune's architecture has a defensive quality absent from [[Keth'vaal]] or [[Ur-Saethen]]. Walls are thicker. Sight-lines are longer. The Primordial Anchor at the city's center is surrounded by a ring of observation towers rather than debate alcoves. Hael'rune has always been a border city, first defending against Primordial incursions from the east, now monitoring the cultural incursion of outlander influence. The two threats feel increasingly similar.

The air here carries the salt smell of the distant coast mingled with highland stone. On clear days, the fern-canopy of [[The Veilshore#The Fernwald|the Fernwald]] is visible as a dark green line on the eastern horizon. At night, if the wind is right, Hael'rune's residents can see the faint glow of Tidecross's lanterns, outlander light burning in a land that has never needed it.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Hael'rune is ground zero for divine contamination. Every outlander who passes through brings trace amounts of divine residue, the remnant energy of dead gods embedded in their tools, clothing, breath, and magical traditions. This residue destabilizes the city's Primordial Anchor, causing fluctuations that the Pact-Weavers must constantly compensate for. [[Speaker Kaelthen]] uses Hael'rune's data as evidence for the Sealed Gate position: the contamination is measurable, progressive, and will eventually compromise the Anchor permanently if outlander contact continues.

## Layout & Landmarks

**The Threshold Gate**
Hael'rune's eastern entrance, a massive stone archway carved with geometric wards that glow faintly when anyone carrying divine residue passes through. The Gate was originally designed to detect Primordial anomalies; it has been repurposed as a contamination scanner. Outlanders passing through feel a brief pressure, like walking through warm water. Oldborn passing through feel nothing. The difference is the point.

**The Quarantine Quarter**
A walled district on the city's eastern edge, built in haste three years ago when the first outlander traders arrived. The architecture is functional Oldborn stone, but the atmosphere is entirely outlander: hanging lanterns (the Oldborn do not use them), drying laundry strung between dormitory windows, the smell of salt-fish and foreign spices competing with the highland air. Dormitory rooms are small, stone-floored, furnished with a sleeping pallet and a washbasin. A family of four shares a room meant for two. The communal kitchen serves Oldborn grain-porridge that outlanders have learned to tolerate and supplement with whatever they brought from the ships.

Outlanders are permitted to stay for up to seven days. Longer stays require a sponsoring Oldborn citizen, and the sponsorship economy has become Hael'rune's most awkward institution. A sponsor pays one year's trade income as surety and assumes legal responsibility for their outlander's behavior, including any divine contamination attributed to them. Most sponsors are young merchants seeking trade advantage. A few are scholars seeking knowledge. All are taking a social risk: sponsoring an outlander marks you as sympathetic to the Open Hand position, and in Hael'rune, that is increasingly dangerous.

**The Anchor Plaza**
The city's center, dominated by the Primordial Anchor, a column of dark crystal rising thirty feet from a basin of still water. The Anchor's pulse is visible as a faint blue ripple that moves through the water at regular intervals. Since outlander contact began, the intervals have become irregular, sometimes fast, sometimes slow, occasionally stopping entirely before restarting. The Pact-Weavers assigned to maintain it work in shifts, and their exhaustion is visible.

**The Border Market**
A roofless corridor of stone stalls wedged between the Quarantine Quarter's inner wall and the city proper, where Oldborn and outlanders trade under the gaze of Anchor Plaza guards. The stalls are divided by a waist-high stone barrier: outlanders on one side, Oldborn on the other, goods passed across the top. Outlanders sell salt, rope, iron tools, and occasionally divine-residue curiosities (crystals, blessed tokens, enchanted trinkets) that the Pact-Weavers confiscate on sight. Oldborn sell volcanic glass, fern-weave textiles, and Primordial resonance trinkets of dubious quality. Prices are extortionate on both sides. The atmosphere is tense, transactional, and fascinating, the only place in the Oldborn Kingdoms where two civilizations haggle face to face.

**The Watcher's Walk**
A raised stone promenade along the city's eastern wall, where citizens gather at dusk to look toward the coast. It has become a place of quiet anxiety. Older residents remember when the eastern horizon was empty. Now there are lights, and smoke, and the occasional distant sound of outlander construction carrying on the wind.

**The Old Wardstone**
A weathered stone pillar standing outside the eastern wall, half-buried in highland grass and largely ignored. Its surface bears geometric carvings that predate the current Anchor system by millennia, wards from the pre-Covenant era when the Oldborn defended against threats now erased from memory. For centuries it was a curiosity, a relic too stubborn to crumble. Three months ago it started glowing, a faint amber pulse that activates whenever outlanders pass within fifty paces. The glow is warm, steady, and responds to no known Primordial frequency. The Pact-Weavers have filed reports. [[Ruling Elder Dael]] has ordered the area cordoned off. Nobody wants to investigate because nobody wants to know what the wards were originally built to detect.

## People & Culture

Hael'rune's rotating governance was designed for stability, not crisis. Each founding family holds authority for a fixed period (traditionally twelve years), ensuring no single lineage accumulates permanent power. The system worked well for millennia when the city's primary concern was Primordial border maintenance. It is struggling now, as the pace of change from outlander contact outstrips the governance cycle's ability to adapt.

[[Ruling Elder Dael]]'s term has been defined by caution. Under her governance, daily life in Hael'rune follows a careful rhythm: morning prayers at the Anchor Plaza (attendance voluntary but noted), work shifts organized around the Anchor's maintenance schedule, and evenings spent on the Watcher's Walk or in family courtyards where debate about the outlander question has replaced all other conversation. Dael governs by consensus, which means she governs slowly. In a crisis that moves faster every month, her deliberation looks like paralysis to the young and like wisdom to the old. The rotation system, designed for centuries of stability, has no mechanism for emergency leadership. Citizens are beginning to ask what happens when the next family takes power mid-crisis.

The city's population is divided between those who have interacted with outlanders and those who refuse to. The divide cuts across families, professions, and generations. Young Oldborn are disproportionately curious, older citizens disproportionately hostile. The Pact-Weavers, who see the Anchor data firsthand, tend toward [[Speaker Kaelthen]]'s position: the contamination is real, measurable, and dangerous.

### Notable Residents

- **Ruling Elder Dael**, current head of the rotation, an elderly woman from a founding family who governs with deliberate caution. She has authorized limited outlander contact but restricts it to the Quarantine Quarter and refuses to discuss relaxing the seven-day limit.
- [[Speaker Kaelthen]], who was born here and trained as a Pact-Weaver before serving at the Shimmer. Her Sealed Gate faction draws its strongest support from Hael'rune's population.
- **Pact-Weaver Osshen**, the youngest member of the Anchor maintenance team. He has begun corresponding with outlander scholars in the Quarantine Quarter, exchanging notes on comparative magical theory. If discovered, his career and possibly his freedom are forfeit.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The Flickering Anchor.** Hael'rune's Primordial Anchor stopped pulsing for eleven seconds last week, the longest interruption in recorded history. The Pact-Weavers are debating whether to request emergency support from [[Keth'vaal]] or attempt a local repair that risks accelerating the destabilization. Outlander presence is blamed, but the timing correlates with a ley surge in [[The Greymere Fenlands]] on the known continent, suggesting a deeper connection.

2. **The Quarantine Breach.** An outlander family overstayed their seven-day limit by hiding in the Quarantine Quarter's storage cellars. They are desperate, their ship damaged (unable to return through [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]]'s barrier), with nowhere else to go. [[Ruling Elder Dael]] must decide: enforce the law and expel them into the Fernwald (likely fatal), or bend the rules and face political consequences from the Sealed Gate faction.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The family is the Tarennes, a merchant household from [[The Hollowed Reach]]. Father, mother, two children, and an elderly grandmother who is a retired [[Major Factions#Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keeper]]. They crossed the barrier seeking trade opportunities and cannot return because Yvenne's barrier has strengthened behind them. The grandmother carries intelligence that could be invaluable: firsthand accounts of the Black Tides' escalation, Sleepwalker patterns, and rumors of something stirring in the [[The Hollowed Reach#The Abyssal Trench|Abyssal Trench]]. She does not know how significant this information is. The Oldborn do not know to ask.

3. **Pact-Weaver Osshen's Letters.** The young Pact-Weaver's correspondence with outlander scholars has produced a breakthrough: a theoretical framework for filtering divine residue from outlander presence, potentially solving the contamination problem. The framework involves calibrating the Anchor's resonance field to neutralize divine frequencies on contact, but testing requires an outlander physically present at the Anchor Plaza, standing in the basin water while Osshen adjusts the crystal's harmonics. The research cannot happen in the Quarantine Quarter. It requires the city's heart. [[Speaker Kaelthen]]'s faction would view this as capitulation, and Osshen knows it.

4. **The Contaminated Anchor.** The Anchor has begun resonating with a frequency that matches no known Primordial source. [[Ur-Saethen]]'s scholars believe it originates from [[The Loom's Edge]]. If correct, the destabilization is not caused by outlanders at all but by the Second Silence reaching pre-divine structures. This revelation would reshape the entire Sealed Gate argument.

5. **The Impossible Child.** An Oldborn child born in Hael'rune, six years old, daughter of a founding family, has started manifesting abilities that resemble divine magic. She heals scraped knees with a touch that leaves warmth, not the cold precision of Primordial pact-work. Her eyes glow faintly amber when she concentrates. This is impossible under the Primordial system. Her parents are terrified. The Pact-Weavers are fascinated. [[Speaker Kaelthen]] wants the child quarantined as proof that divine contamination has reached the Oldborn population itself.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Anchor's new frequency is the same one detected in [[Ur-Saethen]]'s Silence-Watcher report. The Loom's excised fate-threads are sending resonance pulses through the Primordial substrate, and every Anchor in the Oldborn Kingdoms is receiving them. The contamination from outlanders is real but secondary; the primary cause is the Loom itself. If the PCs can prove this, it shifts the political balance in the Circle of Speakers, undermining Kaelthen's core argument and giving Yavenne's Open Hand faction leverage. Kaelthen suspects this and is suppressing the data.
