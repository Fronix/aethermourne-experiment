## Overview

Tidecross is not a city. It is barely a town. It is an accident of geography and desperation, a collection of hasty structures clinging to the one natural harbor on [[The Veilshore]]'s 300-mile coastline. Three years ago, this was empty shore. Today, it is the gateway to [[The Unwritten Lands]], the single point of contact between two continents that share no history and increasingly little patience.

The settlement sprawls chaotically around a crescent bay of black sand and volcanic rock. Ships from [[The Hollowed Reach]], [[The Ashen Dominion]], and independent captains crowd the harbor, jockeying for dock space. Warehouses, taverns, shrines, and trade stalls line the waterfront in a patchwork of architectural styles, none of which belong here. The air smells of salt, tar, unfamiliar spices, and the faint ozone tang of Primordial energy bleeding up from the stone beneath.

Three factions claim authority over Tidecross. None have it. The [[The Hollowed Reach|Concord of Tides]] built the first docks and claims economic control. [[The Ashen Dominion]]'s missionaries claim spiritual jurisdiction. The [[The Oldborn Kingdoms|Oldborn]] observers claim the land was never the outlanders' to settle. In practice, the settlement governs itself through improvisation, bribery, and the threat of violence.

Tidecross is where fortunes are made and lost in a single tide. It is where scholars hunt for Primordial secrets, cultists gather in basements, and refugees discover that the Unwritten Lands are no safer than the world they fled. It is the collision point of two worlds, and the impact is accelerating.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Tidecross is a pressure cooker.** Every session spent here should escalate tensions. A brawl. A diplomatic incident. A ship that does not return. The Oldborn are watching, and they are running out of patience. When violence comes, it will be sudden, overwhelming, and catastrophic. The players cannot stop it. They can only choose which side they are on when it breaks.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Oldborn Docks

The harbor's foundation is a set of ancient docks built by the Oldborn centuries ago, massive stone pilings carved with glyphs that predate [[The Twelve]]. The pilings are indestructible. Outlander engineers have tried to drill into them, carve them, even blow them apart. Nothing works. The stone is warm to the touch, hums faintly, and resists all damage.

The Oldborn claim the docks were built as a ritual anchor, not for trade. The outlanders do not care. They have extended the docks with wooden platforms, rope bridges, and scavenged materials, building a chaotic superstructure atop the ancient foundation. The Oldborn watch this desecration in silence. For now.

### The Crossroads Market

A sprawling open-air market occupies the center of the settlement, built on the black sand despite the sand's unsettling properties. Merchants from [[The Hollowed Reach]] sell salt, rope, and tools. Independent traders offer Primordial artifacts of dubious provenance. Oldborn vendors, rare but present, trade rare herbs, crystalline resonators, and knowledge for goods they cannot produce themselves.

The market is neutral ground by unspoken agreement. Violence here disrupts trade, and trade is the only thing keeping Tidecross from collapsing into open war. Enforcers from all three factions patrol the market, glaring at each other but maintaining peace.

The market closes at sunset. After dark, the black sand is too dangerous, too many reports of whispers, vanishings, and things moving beneath the surface.

### The Tidecaller Shrine

A small stone building on the northern cliffs, overlooking the ocean. The shrine is dedicated to [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]], maintained by a handful of Tidecaller priests who made the crossing from [[The Hollowed Reach]]. The walls bleed seawater. The air inside tastes of salt and something older. The priests perform daily rites to keep Yvenne calm, singing the old hymns, offering libations to the sea.

The shrine has become a pilgrimage site for sailors, a place to pray before departing or to give thanks after surviving the crossing. The priests do not turn anyone away, but they are exhausted, terrified, and increasingly convinced that Yvenne is trying to manifest here. What that means, they do not know.

### The Archive House

A fortified building near the docks, claimed by [[The Star-Readers]] as a research station. The Archive catalogues everything learned about the Unwritten Lands: Oldborn language fragments, Primordial phenomena, maps that become obsolete within weeks. The building is guarded, warded, and off-limits to non-scholars.

Rumor says the Star-Readers have recovered a fragment of [[Cosmology and Magic#The Loom of Fate|the Loom]] and are keeping it in the Archive's vault. The rumor is true. [[The Veil Unbound]] knows this. They are planning to take it.

### The Wrack Tunnel

A hidden passage through the Tidemark Cliffs, leading to a smugglers' cove known as **The Wrack**. Goods that cannot pass through official channels, Primordial artifacts, outlawed texts, stolen Oldborn relics, move through the Wrack. The entrance in Tidecross is disguised as a collapsed warehouse. Only those who know the signs can find it.

The Wrack is run by a woman called **Salice**, a former [[The Hollowed Reach|Concord]] diplomat who saw more profit in smuggling than in politics. She is sharp, ruthless, and the only person in Tidecross who treats the Oldborn as equals. Her neutrality has made her indispensable. It has also made her a target.

### The Ashen Chapel

A small wooden chapel built by [[The Ashen Dominion]]'s missionaries, conspicuously out of place among the settlement's pragmatic architecture. The chapel is painted white, adorned with the geometric mandala of [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]], and staffed by a rotating group of priests who preach to anyone who will listen.

The Oldborn find the chapel offensive. The presence of a dead god's symbol on their land is an insult. The missionaries do not understand why. They believe they are bringing salvation to a people who have never known divine grace. The Oldborn believe the missionaries are spreading a plague.

The chapel has been vandalized three times. The missionaries rebuild every time, more determined than before.

## People & Culture

Tidecross has no unified culture. It is a collision of three: the mercantile pragmatism of [[The Hollowed Reach]], the rigid theocracy of [[The Ashen Dominion]], and the Primordial reverence of [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]]. No side understands the others. Misunderstandings are constant.

Daily life is transactional. Everything is negotiated: dock space, warehouse access, water rights, even the right to build a shelter on the black sand. Trust is rare. Contracts are sacred. Breaking a deal in Tidecross marks you as unreliable, and unreliable people do not survive long.

The population is transient. Sailors come and go with the tides. Merchants stay for weeks, sell their cargo, and leave. Refugees arrive hoping for safety and discover there is none. A few hardy souls have put down roots, built permanent structures, claimed this chaotic shore as home. They are the exceptions.

### Notable Residents

**[[Captain Erys Vane]]** - Concord diplomat and de facto harbormaster. Pragmatic, sharp-tongued, and exhausted. She is trying to keep Tidecross functional while three factions tear it apart. She knows violence is coming and is quietly preparing evacuation plans.

**[[Prior Cael Drantine]]** - Lead missionary of the Ashen Chapel. Earnest, kind, and dangerously naive. He genuinely believes he is helping. The Oldborn see him as a threat. His insistence on preaching in the Fernwald has nearly sparked violence twice.

**[[Vosh]]** - Oldborn observer stationed in Tidecross. Silent, watchful, and deeply unsettling. Vosh speaks fluent Common but prefers not to. They attend every major negotiation, take notes, and report back to [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]]. No one knows Vosh's real name or rank. No one dares ask.

**[[Salice]]** - Smuggler queen of the Wrack. Former Concord diplomat, current criminal mastermind. Salice knows everyone, hears everything, and sells information as readily as stolen goods. She is the closest thing Tidecross has to a neutral power broker.

**[[Tidecaller Mirenne]]** - Senior priest at the Tidecaller Shrine. Exhausted, faithful, and terrified. Mirenne feels [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne's]] presence growing stronger every day. She prays the goddess is protecting Tidecross. She fears the goddess is drowning it.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The Vanishing Oldborn.** Vosh has not been seen in three days. The Oldborn delegation sent a message: if Vosh is not returned unharmed within a week, they will consider it an act of war. No one knows where Vosh is. The players must find them before the deadline expires.

2. **The Archive Heist.** [[The Veil Unbound]] is planning to raid the Archive House and steal the Loom fragment. The Star-Readers know something is coming but not when or how. The players might be hired to defend the Archive, infiltrate the cult, or steal the fragment themselves before either faction gets it.

3. **The Black Sand Rising.** Something is moving beneath the black sand of the market. At night, shapes press upward against the surface, never breaking through. The Oldborn say the sand is rejecting the settlement, that the land is preparing to expel the outlanders. Merchants are refusing to work. Trade is collapsing. If the market dies, Tidecross dies with it.

4. **The Chapel Arson.** Someone burned the Ashen Chapel to the ground last night. Prior Drantine survived but was badly injured. The Dominion delegation demands justice. The Oldborn deny involvement. Evidence points in multiple directions. The players must investigate before retaliation spirals into massacre.

5. **Yvenne's Tide.** The ocean has stopped moving. For three days, the tide has not shifted. The water is glassy, flat, silent. The Tidecallers are performing emergency rites. Ships cannot leave the harbor. Something is coming. The players can feel it. Everyone can. The silence before the wave.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role:** Tidecross appears in Act II, when the players first learn about the Unwritten Lands. It serves as the base for all mainland expeditions into the continent.
>
> **Key Secret:** The ancient Oldborn docks beneath Tidecross are not just ritual anchors. They are a listening post, a Primordial interface that allows [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]] to monitor everything that happens in the harbor. Every word spoken near the docks is heard. Every deal made is recorded. The Oldborn know everything. They are waiting to see if the outlanders can prove themselves worthy of coexistence. So far, the answer is no.
>
> **Arc Trajectory:** Tidecross begins as a tense but functional trade hub. By mid-campaign, it is a warzone. If the players intervene successfully, it becomes a model for peaceful coexistence. If they fail, it burns, and the two continents sever contact permanently.
>
> **Roleplay Notes:** Tidecross NPCs are tired, desperate, and operating on the edge of collapse. Everyone is one bad day away from snapping. Lean into the exhaustion. Make the players feel the weight of trying to hold something together that was never meant to work.
