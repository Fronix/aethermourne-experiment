## Overview

Ur-Saethen rises from the central highlands of [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]] like a monument to careful thought. Built from pale volcanic stone veined with obsidian, the city is arranged in concentric rings around the Deep Archive, each ring representing a deeper tier of scholarly access. The outermost ring holds residential quarters and workshops. The innermost ring, accessible only to Readers and their guests, contains the Archive's oldest vaults, where pre-Covenant records are preserved in stone tablets and crystalline memory-matrices.

The city hums with a different frequency than [[Keth'vaal]]'s political urgency. Here, conversations happen slowly. Scholars walk the Archive corridors in contemplative silence, pausing to debate at intersection alcoves designed for exactly that purpose. The buildings flow in the Oldborn style, black and pale stone shaped by Primordial craft into forms that seem grown rather than built, but Ur-Saethen's architecture tends toward the angular and precise, reflecting the city's devotion to structured knowledge.

The air smells of mineral ink and old stone. At dusk, the obsidian veins in the city's walls catch the last light and hold it, so the streets glow faintly for an hour after sunset, long enough for scholars to walk home without lanterns. It is beautiful, quiet, and deeply strange to outlander eyes: a city that has spent four thousand years collecting knowledge and has never once gone to war.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Ur-Saethen's greatest vulnerability is its certainty. The scholars believe knowledge is inherently protective, that understanding prevents catastrophe. The [[The Loom's Edge]] and the excised fate-threads challenge this assumption: there may be knowledge that is itself dangerous, truths that destabilize reality simply by being understood. If PCs bring evidence of the Loom's nature to Ur-Saethen, it creates a philosophical crisis that divides the scholarly community.

## Layout & Landmarks

**The Deep Archive**
The heart of the city, a series of interconnected underground chambers descending seven levels beneath the central plaza. The upper levels contain contemporary scholarship: botanical surveys, Pact-Weaver methodology, Primordial interaction records. The deeper levels hold older material, pre-Covenant histories, accounts of the world before the Oldborn organized, and in the lowest vault, sealed behind wards maintained by three Pact-Weavers in rotation, records of what the Covenant of Ash erased. No one reads these without the Circle of Speakers' authorization.

**The Alcove Gardens**
Open-air debate spaces scattered throughout the city, circular stone platforms shaded by cultivated fern-trees. Each alcove has a specific topic tradition: the Westward Alcove discusses politics, the Anchor Alcove discusses Primordial theory, the Threshold Alcove discusses pre-Pattern history. Scholars congregate by interest, and heated arguments can last days.

**The Reader's Terrace**
The second-innermost ring, where senior scholars live and work. Apartments are modest (the city's meritocracy discourages ostentation) but filled with personal libraries that would be the envy of any mainland institution. The Terrace overlooks the Archive entrance and the city's central Primordial Anchor, which pulses with a steady rhythm that scholars use to time their work cycles.

**The Stranger's Quarter**
A small district on the outermost ring, recently expanded to accommodate outlander scholars who have begun arriving via [[Tidecross]]. The Quarter is functional but isolated, a polite quarantine that allows the city to engage with outlander knowledge without exposing the inner rings to divine contamination.

## People & Culture

Ur-Saethen's citizens value precision, patience, and intellectual honesty above all else. Debate is civic participation. Disagreement is expected and respected, provided it is grounded in evidence. The worst insult in the city is "unsourced," applied to anyone who makes claims without foundation.

Children begin scholarly training at eight. By fifteen, most have chosen a specialization. Those who show aptitude for Pact-Weaving are sent to [[Keth'vaal]] for formal bonding. Those who excel in pure scholarship remain, ascending through the Archive's seven levels over decades of work. The city's leader is selected purely by peer review: the scholar whose contributions are judged most significant by their colleagues holds authority until surpassed.

### Notable Residents

- [[Speaker Yavenne]], born here to a historian family, now represents the city on the Circle of Speakers. Her [[The Oldborn Council#The Open Hand|Open Hand]] philosophy reflects the city's belief that knowledge should flow, not be hoarded.
- **Archivist Therune**, the Deep Archive's custodian, an elderly Oldborn who has spent sixty years cataloguing the lower vaults and refuses to discuss what is in the seventh level.
- **Reader Vael-Shen**, a young scholar studying outlander divine traditions with genuine fascination, the first Ur-Saethen academic to request transfer to the Stranger's Quarter.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The Seventh Vault.** Archivist Therune has sealed the seventh level of the Deep Archive, claiming structural damage. DC 14 Insight: she is lying. Something in the vault has changed since the outlanders arrived, and she is afraid of what the pre-Covenant records now reveal.

2. **The Outlander Scholar.** Reader Vael-Shen wants to cross-reference Oldborn Primordial theory with outlander divine theology. The implications are staggering but the cultural resistance is fierce. She needs allies who can bridge both traditions.

3. **The Silence-Watcher Report.** A report from the Primordia's edge was submitted to Ur-Saethen for analysis. It contains a Primordial Anchor transmission that matches no known frequency. Archivist Therune believes it originates from [[The Loom's Edge]]. If correct, the Loom is attempting to communicate, and Ur-Saethen may be the only place with the knowledge to translate.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The seventh vault contains records of pre-Covenant Oldborn history, specifically, the entity worship that the Covenant of Ash erased as payment to [[Ur-Ghael]]. The records are waking up. The crystalline memory-matrices are replaying content that has been dormant for millennia, triggered by the same resonance causing Primordial Anchors to destabilize across the Kingdoms. What was erased is trying to be remembered.
