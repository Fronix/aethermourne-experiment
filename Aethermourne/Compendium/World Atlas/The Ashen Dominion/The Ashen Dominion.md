# The Ashen Dominion

> [!quote]
> "We are the architecture of a dead god's will."
> , Hierarch Valdren III, Convocation of the Flame

---

## Overview

The Ashen Dominion is the mightiest nation in Aethermourne, a vast theocratic empire built upon, within, and around the skeletal remains of [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]], God of Order. Two thousand years ago, Solvaen fell. His bones became mountains. His blood crystallized into [[Cosmology and Magic#Ashite|Ashite]]. His death-exhalation blanketed a thousand miles of earth in pale grey ash. The survivors built a civilization on the corpse, literally.

## Geography

### The Spine of Order

[[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] actual vertebral column, a mountain range of divine bone running north-to-south through the Dominion's heart. The smallest vertebrae are the size of hillforts. The largest dwarf any natural mountain on the continent. Veins of crystallized Ashite thread through them, glowing faintly at dawn and dusk. The Spine is riddled with mines, the foundation of the Dominion's economy. Prolonged proximity causes **Order Sickness**, a progressive mental rigidity that the Church calls divine communion and the miners call a death sentence. The air near the Spine carries **The Hum**, a subsonic resonance that makes spontaneous thought feel effortful.

### The Ashfields

Vast plains of pale grey soil surrounding the Spine, compacted divine ash turned surprisingly fertile over two millennia. The Dominion's breadbasket. Crops grow grey-tinged, silver-green wheat and pewter-colored apples. The food is safe, but generations raised on it trend toward orderliness and obedience. Scattered across the fields are **Solvaen's Fingers**, protruding fragments of divine skeleton that communities cluster around for ambient magical energy. A village with a good Finger is prosperous. A village whose Finger is depleted is desperate.

### The Calcified Forest

Eastern Dominion. Old-growth woodland transformed by Solvaen's lingering essence into white stone. The outer edges are merely bone-white bark and stiff leaves. Walk deeper and the trees become rigid porcelain, the forest utterly silent. At the center, fully mineralized stone pillars, lifeless cathedrals of petrified wood. Strange calcified fauna inhabit it, deer with bone-stone antlers, wolves with ceramic hides, all moving in geometric patterns. The Church considers it sacred ground. Entering without permission is a crime. In practice, this makes it an excellent hiding place for dissidents and [[Major Factions#Unbound Congregation|Unbound Congregation]] members.

### The Scar

Where the Dominion meets [[The Verdant Marches]], two dead gods' legacies collide. Solvaen's order grinds against [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea's]] wild vitality. Calcified trees cracked open by living vines. Grey ash erupting with luminous thorned growth. Ashite formations wrapped in pulsing moss. At the rough center grows **the Scarwall**, a living fortification of twisted thorn-vines, some thick as castle towers, reaching sixty feet. It answers to neither nation. It is growing, roughly ten feet per year on the Dominion side.

## Society

The Dominion operates a rigid **five-caste system**: the Anointed (highest clergy), Flame-Bearers (military and administrative elite), Keepers (scholars, mages, professionals), the Hewn (miners, farmers, laborers, the vast majority), and the Ashless (those without caste or rights). The [[Major Factions#Church of the Ashen Flame|Church of the Ashen Flame]] is the state. Laws derive from scripture. The distinction between sin and crime does not exist. Daily life is structured around Dawn Prayer spoken in unison, geometric meditation, and a Confession and Correction system that ensures the Church knows about dissent before it organizes. Magic is practiced as mathematics through [[Cosmology and Magic#Calculism|Calculism]], precise, reproducible, channeled through Ashite focuses.

## Current Tensions

1. **The Hierarch's Decline.** [[Hierarch Valdren III]] is consumed by paranoia, issuing contradictory orders, purging competent officials, trusting only [[Delric Mourne]] of the Cinders.
2. **Ashite Depletion.** The mines are thinning. The Dominion's economy, military, and magical infrastructure all depend on Ashite. Shortfalls are projected within a decade.
3. **The Unbound Congregation.** [[Elowen Greaves]] leads a swelling reform movement among the Hewn. The Church's crackdowns only produce martyrs.
4. **The Scarwall Advances.** The living thorn-wall creeps toward [[Cinderholm]]. [[Ashara Voss]] demands permission to strike. The Hierarch refuses.
5. **The Star Fade.** Stars are going dark. The Church refuses to acknowledge it. The population grows uneasy beneath a dimming sky.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **The Cinders' Betrayal:** [[Delric Mourne]], head of the Cinders, secretly serves [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], the imprisoned god of entropy. The Cinders are engineering the Dominion's collapse from within, amplifying the Hierarch's paranoia, sidelining competent officials, and accelerating Ashite depletion by directing mines away from rich veins. When the Dominion falls, Mourne intends it to fall completely.
