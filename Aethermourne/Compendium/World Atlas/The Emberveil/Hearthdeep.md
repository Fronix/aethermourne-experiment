## Overview

Hearthdeep is not a city so much as a wound in the earth. Built vertically into a vast volcanic shaft, the settlement descends in concentric platforms bolted to the shaft walls, connected by lifts, ladders, and spiralling ramps carved from the living rock. Looking up from the lower levels, the surface is a distant circle of grey sky. Looking down is worse.

The upper levels are comfortable enough, warm stone, well-lit workshops, communal kitchens that serve meals at all hours to accommodate the shift rotations. The middle levels are hotter, the air thick with mineral dust, the stone walls glowing faintly where geothermal veins run close to the surface. The deepest accessible levels are hostile to sustained life, so hot that workers rotate in shifts of minutes rather than hours, their skin slick with heat-resistant salve, their lungs burning through wet cloth masks. This is where forgeheart steel is found, metal seams that glow with residual divine energy, too hot to touch with bare hands, too precious to leave in the ground.

[[Orin Deepstone|Delver-Captain Orin Deepstone]] commands the mining operations and has gone deeper than any living person. He came back changed. The miners who work under him trust him absolutely and fear whatever he will not talk about.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The deepest level that [[Orin Deepstone|Deepstone]] reached, Level Fourteen, is not on any official map. He found a chamber where the walls were not stone but something between metal and flesh, warm, yielding slightly to the touch, and pulsing with slow rhythm. The chamber was shaped like the interior of a vast ribcage. He believes he was inside a fragment of [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] physical remains, a piece of the god's body preserved by his own heat. He sealed the entrance and has told no one. He does not know what to do with this knowledge.
>
> The rhythmic sounds the miners report, the hammer-strikes from below, originate from deeper than Level Fourteen. They are the forge's compensatory mechanism, the deep earth straining to maintain thermal output as the Second Silence drains divine energy. The sounds are getting louder, which means the strain is increasing.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Shaft Head

The surface entrance to Hearthdeep, a fortified compound of workshops, barracks, and supply depots surrounding the shaft opening. The compound includes the Shift House, where miners clock in and out, the Assay Office, where extracted materials are graded and catalogued, and the Salve Hall, where heat-resistant compounds are prepared and applied. A massive iron gantry supports the primary lift, a platform large enough to carry twenty miners and their equipment, lowered and raised by a geothermal-powered winch system designed by the [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]].

### The Thermal Barriers

Three marked boundaries within the shaft where temperature increases sharply. The First Barrier (Level Four) is where conventional mining equipment fails and must be replaced with heat-treated tools. The Second Barrier (Level Eight) is where shift times drop to two hours and heat-salve becomes mandatory. The Third Barrier (Level Eleven) is where only Delver teams, the most experienced deep-miners, are permitted to operate. Beyond the Third Barrier, shift times are measured in minutes.

### The Resonance Chamber

A natural cavern at Level Six, roughly spherical, where the shaft's acoustics create an amplification effect. Every sound from below is funnelled through this space, making it the best listening post for monitoring deep activity. Since the hammer-sounds began, the Resonance Chamber has been staffed around the clock by a team of listeners who log every change in rhythm, volume, and frequency. The data fills notebooks that [[Orin Deepstone|Delver-Captain Deepstone]] reviews personally each morning.

### The Cooling Pools

A series of geothermally heated pools on Levels Two and Three where miners rest between shifts. The water is mineral-rich and mildly therapeutic, easing the joint pain and skin damage that deep mining inflicts. Off-shift miners gather here to eat, sleep, and tell stories. The atmosphere is somewhere between a bathhouse and a barracks, intimate and communal in the way that shared danger creates.

## People & Culture

Hearthdeep's population is almost entirely dwarven, with a small human contingent who work the upper levels and surface operations. Life here is defined by the shaft. You are either going down, coming up, or resting before your next descent. Social status correlates directly with depth, the deeper you work, the more respect you command. Delver teams, the miners who operate beyond the Third Barrier, are treated as something between elite soldiers and holy pilgrims.

The culture is tight-knit to the point of insularity. Hearthdeep miners trust each other with their lives and trust outsiders with very little. The settlement has its own dialect, its own songs (rhythmic chants that mark time during shifts), and its own superstitions, never whistle in the shaft, always touch the wall before descending, leave a piece of your work at the Shaft Head so something of you stays above.

### Notable Residents

- [[Orin Deepstone|Delver-Captain Orin Deepstone]], Commander of all mining operations. Has been to Level Fourteen. Will not speak of what he found. His silence is louder than the hammer-strikes.
- **Shift-Boss Marga Coalvein**, A veteran of forty years in the shaft, responsible for scheduling and safety on Levels Five through Eight. Pragmatic, sharp-tongued, and quietly terrified that the temperature readings are wrong in the wrong direction.
- **Listener Fen**, A young dwarven woman who has been stationed in the Resonance Chamber for six months. She claims the hammer-sounds have started to form patterns, not random percussions but something approaching a rhythm that almost resolves into meaning.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The Hammer-Strikes.** The rhythmic sounds from below are getting louder. Some miners refuse to descend past Level Eight. Others are drawn deeper, convinced the sound is [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]] trying to communicate. [[Orin Deepstone|Delver-Captain Deepstone]] maintains discipline, but morale is fraying.
2. **The Temperature Anomaly.** Levels Ten and Eleven have experienced sudden temperature drops, places that should blister exposed skin have become merely warm. Forgeheart steel deposits in the affected areas have dulled, losing their characteristic glow. If the cooling reaches the active forgeheart veins on Level Twelve, the region's most valuable export dries up.
3. **The Sealed Level.** [[Orin Deepstone|Deepstone]] sealed Level Fourteen and posted guards. He says the tunnel collapsed. The guards say nothing at all. Rumours are spreading.
4. **[[Vessa Ironvow]]'s Tunnels.** The expelled Council member's illegal mining operation has broken into Hearthdeep's outer warrens from a separate shaft. Her miners and Hearthdeep's are now close enough to hear each other's picks. If she breaches the wrong chamber, both operations could be endangered.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> If the PCs can gain [[Orin Deepstone|Deepstone's]] trust, he will eventually share what he found on Level Fourteen. He needs someone he trusts to go back down with him, because he is not brave enough to go alone a second time, and he knows that what he found changes everything about the Emberveil's understanding of [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] sacrifice.
>
> The sealed level is also the most direct route to the forge-chambers. If [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] agents ever reach the Emberveil, this is the path they would need. [[Orin Deepstone|Deepstone]] sealed it not just to hide what he found, but because he instinctively understood that the route must be protected.
