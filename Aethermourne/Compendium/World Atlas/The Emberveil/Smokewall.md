## Overview

Smokewall sits in the only major pass between [[The Emberveil]] and [[The Ashen Dominion]], a narrow defile between two volcanic ridges where geothermal vents exhale a constant, low-hanging haze of warm sulphurous gas. The haze is not dangerous in the concentrations found at the pass, merely unpleasant, stinging the eyes and leaving a metallic taste in the mouth. But it is perpetual, reducing visibility to a few hundred feet and making the pass feel like a liminal space between two worlds. Which, in many ways, it is.

The town is functional rather than beautiful, a cluster of warehouses, customs houses, barracks, and trading posts arranged along the single road that threads the pass. Everything is built of dark volcanic stone and reinforced against both weather and the possibility of military action. Smokewall is a trade hub first and a fortress second, but [[Tarn Smokeshield|Warden Smokeshield]] has spent thirty years ensuring the second function is never far from anyone's mind.

Forgeheart steel, master-crafted goods, precision instruments, and geothermal exports flow east through Smokewall toward the Dominion. Ashite-alloyed currency, raw materials, grain surpluses, and manufactured goods flow west. The exchange rates are set by [[The Forge Council]] and enforced by Smokewall's customs agents with a rigidity that the Dominion finds infuriating. The Emberveil controls the supply of forgeheart steel. The Dominion needs forgeheart steel. This asymmetry is Smokewall's true fortification.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Dominion's trade pressure is intensifying because [[Delric Mourne]] is engineering it. By directing Ashite mines away from rich veins (as part of his broader sabotage), he is creating artificial scarcity that increases the Dominion's need for alternative resources, particularly forgeheart steel. Mourne does not care about the steel itself. He wants the Emberveil's political independence weakened so that, when the time comes, Dominion forces can move through the pass without meaningful resistance.
>
> [[Tarn Smokeshield|Warden Smokeshield]] has noticed the increased scout activity and the aggressive tone of recent trade delegations. He has quietly reinforced the garrison and sent warnings to [[The Forge Council]] that the Dominion's posture is shifting from commercial to strategic. The Council has acknowledged his concerns without acting on them, a decision Smokeshield considers dangerously naive.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Customs House

The largest building in Smokewall and the chokepoint through which all cross-border trade must pass. Every shipment is weighed, assessed, taxed, and recorded. Customs agents are recruited from the Miners' Union for their ability to spot quality and from the garrison for their inability to be bribed. The process is thorough and slow, and Dominion merchants complain about it constantly. [[Tarn Smokeshield|Warden Smokeshield]] considers their complaints a sign the system is working.

### The Haze Gate

A pair of stone pylons marking the eastern edge of the Emberveil's territorial claim, where the pass enters the permanent volcanic haze. Beyond the pylons, the road descends into Dominion territory through increasingly clear air. The pylons are carved with [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] hammer-and-anvil symbol and inscribed with trade regulations in both Emberveil dwarven and Dominion common. They are also load-bearing elements of the pass's defensive architecture, designed to be collapsed in an emergency, sealing the pass with rubble.

### The Warden's Bastion

[[Tarn Smokeshield|Smokeshield's]] headquarters, a squat fortress built into the northern ridge wall with commanding views of the pass in both directions. The Bastion houses the garrison, an armoury, and Smokeshield's intelligence operation, a network of informants on both sides of the border who report on Dominion military movements. The fortress is designed to hold the pass against a force ten times the garrison's size, which Smokeshield considers barely adequate.

### The Smoke Market

An open-air trading ground outside the Customs House where merchants from both sides mingle, negotiate, and spy on each other. The market has its own informal culture, a neutral ground where Dominion and Emberveil traders drink together, share gossip, and conduct the unofficial business that greases the wheels of cross-border commerce. Information flows here as freely as currency.

## People & Culture

Smokewall's permanent population is small but cosmopolitan by Emberveil standards, roughly equal parts dwarven garrison and mixed-race trading community. The town attracts people who are comfortable in grey areas, traders who understand both cultures, translators, smugglers-turned-merchants, and the occasional genuine spy. Social life revolves around the Smoke Market's taverns, where the haze outside provides a constant excuse to stay indoors and drink.

The garrison maintains a professional military culture that contrasts with the Emberveil's civilian guild-governance. [[Tarn Smokeshield|Warden Smokeshield]] runs his command as a meritocratic hierarchy within the broader meritocratic hierarchy of the Forge Council, but with a soldier's emphasis on discipline and readiness that the craftspeople of Crucible sometimes find excessive.

### Notable Residents

- [[Tarn Smokeshield|Warden Tarn Smokeshield]], Commander of the garrison. Thirty years of keeping the Dominion at arm's length through trade leverage and fortification. Increasingly worried that leverage alone will not be enough.
- **Customs-Chief Brenna Scalehand**, A dwarven woman who runs the Customs House with terrifying efficiency. She can assess the quality of a forgeheart steel ingot by weight alone and has personally caught three smuggling operations in the past year.
- **Rook Ashendale**, A human trader who operates legitimately on both sides of the border. He is the closest thing Smokewall has to a neutral party, trusted by neither side completely and useful to both. He has recently been approached by a Dominion "trade attaché" with questions about Emberveil mining output that strike him as uncomfortably specific.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The Trade War.** [[The Ashen Dominion]] is demanding a thirty percent reduction in forgeheart steel tariffs. [[The Forge Council]] has refused. The Dominion has responded by imposing retaliatory tariffs on grain exports, which the Emberveil needs. Smokewall is caught in the middle, its merchants watching their livelihoods become bargaining chips.
2. **The Scout Problem.** Dominion scouts have been spotted inside Emberveil territory, beyond the established patrol zones. [[Tarn Smokeshield|Warden Smokeshield]] has detained two, both carrying detailed topographical maps of the Ashfall Slopes. The Dominion claims they were lost merchants. No one believes this.
3. **The Forgery Trail.** The counterfeit guild-marks plaguing [[Anvilrest]] appear to originate from a workshop somewhere in Smokewall's trading district. Customs-Chief Scalehand is investigating, but the operation is well-hidden, and she suspects it has protection from someone with enough influence to keep the customs agents looking elsewhere.
4. **The Closed Pass.** [[Tarn Smokeshield|Warden Smokeshield]] has privately recommended to [[The Forge Council]] that the pass be closed entirely if Dominion military posture continues to escalate. The economic consequences would be severe, cutting the Emberveil off from its largest export market. The Council has not yet responded.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The forgery operation in Smokewall is run by a Dominion intelligence asset, not a Cinder, but a standard Dominion spy whose mission is to undermine confidence in Emberveil trade goods. The operation is small and ultimately a distraction, but investigating it could lead the PCs to discover the broader scope of Dominion intelligence activity in the region.
>
> The real threat at Smokewall is the gradual shift in Dominion posture from trade partner to potential aggressor. The Dominion does not intend military conquest of the Emberveil, not yet, but [[Delric Mourne]] is ensuring that the option remains open. If the Second Silence accelerates and the Dominion's Ashite reserves truly fail, the forgeheart steel in the Emberveil becomes a strategic necessity rather than a trade good, and strategic necessities get taken, not bought.
