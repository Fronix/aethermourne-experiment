# The Emberveil

> [!quote]
> "The god is dead. The forge still burns. We are the hands he left behind."
> , Inscription above the Grand Forge, [[Crucible]]

---

## Overview

The Emberveil is a volcanic highland rising between [[The Ashen Dominion]] and [[The Hollowed Reach]], a region of fire-veined stone, sulphurous geysers, and deep warmth that never fades. The ground here is always warm. In winter, when snow falls on the outer slopes, it melts within hours, wreathing the highlands in perpetual mist that glows amber at dawn and dusk. The locals call this light the Emberveil, the curtain of warm haze that gives the region its name.

Beneath the stone lies [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]], or what remains of him. The Ember-Smith descended into the earth during the Theomachis to reignite the world's dying core, and his divine fire still burns at the heart of everything. The Emberveil sits directly above the shallowest approach to those deep forge-chambers. Every hot spring, every volcanic vent, every seam of glowing mineral is a reminder that a dead god's sacrifice keeps the world alive, and that his people have built their entire civilization atop his grave.

This is the homeland of the [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]], the beating heart of dwarven culture on the continent, and the source of [[Cosmology and Magic#Forgeheart Steel|forgeheart steel]], the finest crafting material known. The Emberveil is prosperous, proud, and fiercely independent. Its people worship through the act of creation itself, every well-forged blade a prayer, every precisely cut gem an act of devotion. But the fire is flickering. The hot springs run cooler some mornings. The deepest mines report temperature drops where there should be none. Something is wrong with [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] fire, and the people who depend on it are beginning to notice.

## Geography

### The Caldera Ring

A chain of volcanic peaks encircling the region's heart, their summits crowned with ash and their flanks scored by ancient lava flows. The largest is **Mount Thalvor**, an active volcano whose deep caldera vents enough heat to warm the surrounding valleys year-round. The peaks are sacred to the dwarven clans who settled here, each mountain named for a different aspect of [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] craft: the Anvil, the Bellows, the Tongs, the Quench. Smoke rises from a dozen vents at any given time, giving the skyline a perpetual industrial grandeur. The passes between peaks are the only overland routes into the region's interior, narrow, defensible, and thick with volcanic haze.

### The Warm Valleys

Fertile lowlands nestled between the Caldera Ring's peaks, heated from below by geothermal vents that keep the soil warm even in the deepest winter. Crops grow year-round here, hardy root vegetables and heat-loving grains that thrive nowhere else. Hot springs dot the valley floors, some large enough to bathe in, others scalding and dangerous. The air smells of mineral water and warm earth. Steam rises from the ground on cold mornings, filling the valleys with luminous fog. Most of the region's population lives here, in towns and villages clustered around the most reliable thermal vents. The dwarves call these vents **Thalvor's Breath**, and each one is tended by a guild-appointed keeper who monitors its temperature and flow.

### The Deep Warrens

Below the Warm Valleys, a vast network of natural caverns, lava tubes, and dwarven-carved tunnels descends into the earth. The upper levels are well-mapped and well-trafficked, home to conventional mines extracting iron, copper, and gemstones. The middle levels are hotter, the stone warm to the touch, the air thick and dry. Here, rare mineral deposits glow faintly with residual divine energy. The deepest levels are the approach to [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] forge-chambers themselves, so hot that unprotected flesh blisters within minutes, so deep that the stone pulses with a rhythm that older miners swear is a heartbeat. Only the most experienced delvers descend past the third thermal barrier, and what they find there, forgeheart steel, is worth more than its weight in gold.

### The Ashfall Slopes

The outer volcanic flanks where centuries of eruptions have deposited layers of fertile volcanic soil. These slopes face outward toward [[The Ashen Dominion]] and [[The Hollowed Reach]], and they serve as the region's agricultural buffer and first line of defense. The soil is extraordinarily rich, producing orchards and vineyards that benefit from the volcanic minerals. Villages here are smaller and more exposed, subject to occasional ashfall from the active peaks. The people of the Ashfall Slopes are hardier and more independent than their valley cousins, accustomed to evacuating when the mountains grumble and rebuilding when the ash settles.

## Society

The Emberveil is the most culturally cohesive region in Aethermourne. Where the Dominion is held together by doctrine and the Marches by necessity, the Emberveil is bound by a shared devotion to the act of creation. Dwarven traditions dominate, not through exclusion of the human and gnomish minorities, but through the sheer gravitational pull of a culture built around [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] legacy.

**Governance** flows through the guilds. [[The Forge Council]] assigns seats proportional to each guild's assessed contribution to the region's welfare, measured in goods produced, infrastructure maintained, and services rendered. The [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] holds the most seats by a wide margin, but the Jewelers' Conclave, the Miners' Union, the Builders' Circle, and the Healers' Lodge all have voices. Decisions are made by weighted vote, with the Forge-Matriarch holding a casting vote in deadlocks. The system is meritocratic in principle and largely functional in practice, though the Ember Guild's dominance creates friction with smaller guilds who feel permanently outvoted.

**Daily life** revolves around craft. Children begin apprenticeships at twelve. A dwarf's standing is measured by the quality of their work, not their parentage or wealth. Master craftspeople are celebrities. Guild examinations, where journeymen present their masterwork for assessment, are public festivals. The greatest compliment in the Emberveil is "well-made," applied to everything from furniture to friendships.

**Forge-magic** is the Emberveil's native magical tradition. Not a separate school but magic woven into the act of creation itself, every hammer-stroke an opportunity to bind intention into material, every pour of molten metal a chance to shape reality alongside the physical form. A forge-mage does not cast spells in the traditional sense. They craft objects that carry enchantment as an intrinsic property, a blade that holds its edge because it was forged with the concept of sharpness, a shield that deflects because it was shaped with the principle of protection. The tradition is related to [[Cosmology and Magic#Calculism|Calculism]], both are precise and structured, but forge-magic is warmer and more intuitive, less mathematical formula and more master craftsperson's instinct.

**Religion** in the Emberveil is inseparable from work. [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]] is honoured not through prayer but through creation. Every forge is a shrine. Every finished piece is an offering. The phrase "Thalvor's fire still burns" is both a statement of geological fact and an article of faith. There is no organized clergy; the master craftspeople serve that function, teaching through example rather than sermon.

## Current Tensions

1. **The Cooling.** The hot springs are running cooler. The deepest mines report temperature drops. [[Brunhild Ironheart|Forge-Matriarch Ironheart]] has ordered discreet monitoring, but the data is alarming. If [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] fire is weakening, everything the Emberveil depends on, its economy, its agriculture, its magic, is at risk.
2. **The Dominion's Hunger.** [[The Ashen Dominion]] is pressuring for more favorable trade terms on forgeheart steel and master-crafted goods. With Ashite reserves depleting, the Dominion's need for alternative resources is growing desperate. Scouts have been spotted probing the Ashfall Slopes.
3. **The Deep Sound.** Miners at [[Hearthdeep]]'s lowest levels have reported rhythmic sounds from below, like hammer-strikes echoing through stone. Some believe it is [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] forge responding to something. Others believe something is trying to reach the surface.
4. **The Non-Dwarf Question.** [[Apprentice Jyn]], a human teenager, has been accepted at the Grand Forge, the first non-dwarf in a generation. Their prodigious talent for forge-magic has forced an uncomfortable conversation about whether [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] legacy truly belongs to the dwarves alone, or to anyone who can wield a hammer with purpose.
5. **The Rogue Miner.** [[Vessa Ironvow]], expelled from the Forge Council for reckless deep-mining that killed twelve people, is running illegal operations in the outer warrens. She claims she can reach [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] forge directly and reignite whatever is failing. If she succeeds, she could save the region. If she fails, she could trigger a catastrophic eruption.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **The Cooling and the Second Silence.** [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] fire is weakening because of the Second Silence. The same force that is dimming the stars and thinning the Aetheric Web is sapping the divine essence that fuels the world's core. Thalvor burned himself completely, consciousness and all, to keep the forge lit. But even divine fuel runs low when the mechanisms that sustain reality are breaking down. The cooling is not a local problem. It is a symptom of the world dying.
>
> **Serith's Interest.** [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] wants access to the Deep Forge. A tool shaped in divine fire, forgeheart steel worked at the source, could break her prison from the inside. She has no agents in the Emberveil yet, but she is working to place them. The most likely vector is through the Dominion's trade pressure: if the Dominion gains leverage over Emberveil politics, [[Delric Mourne]] can steer resources toward Serith's goals without anyone in the Emberveil knowing who they are truly serving.
>
> **The Deep Sound.** The rhythmic hammer-strikes heard at [[Hearthdeep]]'s lowest level are real. They are not [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]]. His consciousness is gone. They are the forge's mechanical memory responding to the weakening, an automatic process, like a heart beating harder when the body is failing. The forge is trying to compensate for the energy drain, and the sound is the deep earth straining under the effort. If the forge's compensatory mechanism fails, the geothermal system collapses. If it overcompensates, volcanic eruptions follow.
>
> **Vessa Ironvow's Gamble.** [[Vessa Ironvow]] is closer to the truth than anyone on the Forge Council. She has correctly identified that the forge needs intervention, not just monitoring. But her methods are reckless, and she does not understand the compensatory mechanism. If she breaches the wrong chamber, she could disrupt the forge's automatic balancing, triggering either a cooling cascade or a volcanic chain reaction. She is the right person with the wrong approach.
