# Ashcreek

> [!quote] "The Dominion says the line is here. The fen says otherwise."
> , [[Captain Hale Ashmark]], in a private letter to his commanding officer

> [!info] At a Glance
> - **Region:** [[The Greymere Fenlands]] (northern edge, Dominion border)
> - **Population:** ~1,500
> - **Government:** Dual authority. [[Captain Hale Ashmark]] commands a Dominion garrison. A local fen-boss named Vedra Coalhand runs the civilian side.
> - **Economy:** Ashite smuggling, border trade, Dominion supply depot
> - **Motto:** *"Pay the toll."*
> - **Notable Feature:** The only settlement in the fenlands with stone buildings, constructed by the Dominion as a border outpost and slowly being reclaimed by the marsh

---

## Overview

Ashcreek sits where the fenlands begin, or where the Dominion ends, depending on whom you ask. It occupies a stretch of relatively firm ground at the northern mouth of the Smuggler's Sluice, where a creek stained grey by Ashite runoff feeds into the fenland waterways. The [[The Ashen Dominion|Dominion]] built a garrison here decades ago to assert sovereignty over the region's northern reaches. The garrison still stands. The sovereignty is theoretical.

The settlement is split. The Dominion compound, stone walls and orderly barracks, occupies the northern half. The fen-town, wooden stilt-houses and muddy lanes, sprawls to the south. Between them is the Market Line, a strip of contested ground where Dominion merchants and fen traders do business with mutual suspicion. The real economy runs beneath the surface: Ashite, the crystallized divine blood of [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]], is smuggled south through Ashcreek in quantities that would horrify the Dominion's Church hierarchy. [[Captain Hale Ashmark]] knows. He has chosen, so far, not to see.

The town is a pressure point. The Dominion wants taxes, compliance, and a foothold. The fenfolk want to be left alone. Both sides are right to be nervous.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Ashcreek is the campaign's most likely flashpoint for Dominion-fenlands conflict. [[Captain Hale Ashmark]] has orders from Ostivaar to extend tax collection south to [[Fenholm]], which would require a military expedition through the fens. He has been delaying, filing reports about "logistical challenges," but his superiors are losing patience. He has perhaps two months before they send someone less sympathetic to replace him.
>
> The Ashite smuggling is more significant than it appears. The Ashite flowing south isn't just being sold, it's being studied. [[Maren Duskwater]] believes Ashite's order-enforcing properties might be used to stabilize ley-line surges, providing a way to protect fenland settlements without Dominion control. She's been quietly funding the research through [[Torben Greaves]]'s smuggling network. If this works, it would give the fenlands true independence. If the Dominion finds out, they will treat it as theft of a sacred material and respond with force.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Garrison
A walled compound of grey stone, Dominion-standard construction. Houses fifty soldiers, a small administrative office, and an Ashite-detecting wardstone that Captain Ashmark has conveniently never calibrated to full sensitivity. The walls are well-maintained. The morale is not.

### The Fen-Town
A loose sprawl of stilt-houses and mud-brick structures south of the garrison. Run by Vedra Coalhand, a hard-bitten fen-boss who handles civilian disputes, coordinates the fen-side economy, and maintains a careful detente with Captain Ashmark.

### The Market Line
The border strip where trade happens. Dominion-regulated stalls on the north side, unregulated fen stalls on the south. The Market Line is the only place in Ashcreek where both communities interact openly. It is also where most fights start.

### The Grey Creek
The Ashite-stained waterway that gives the town its name. The water has a metallic sheen and a faintly numbing effect on skin contact. Long-term residents develop a slight greying of the fingertips. The creek marks the informal boundary between Dominion and fen territory.

### The Sluice Gate
A wooden checkpoint at the southern edge of town where the Smuggler's Sluice begins in earnest. Officially controlled by the Dominion. Actually controlled by whoever Vedra Coalhand has posted there that day. Most barges pass through with a bribe and no inspection.

## People & Culture

Ashcreek is a town of divided loyalties. The garrison soldiers are mostly young Dominion conscripts who didn't ask to be stationed in a swamp, homesick, underpaid, and increasingly sympathetic to the fenfolk they're supposed to be policing. The fen-side residents are border people, tougher and more pragmatic than their counterparts deeper in the marshes, accustomed to dealing with authority even as they resent it.

The two communities coexist through a web of unofficial arrangements. Soldiers buy fen-brewed ale. Fenfolk use the garrison's healer. Everyone pretends the Ashite isn't moving south. The arrangement is stable only as long as nobody with real authority looks too closely.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **Orders from Ostivaar.** Captain Ashmark's superiors want him to push south. He's stalling, but the next dispatch could bring orders he can't ignore, or a replacement who won't try.
2. **The Ashite pipeline.** Vedra Coalhand's smuggling operation is growing bolder. A recent shipment was nearly intercepted by a Dominion patrol. If the pipeline is exposed, Ashcreek's detente collapses.
3. **A soldier's secret.** Pell Oakley is learning Fencraft from a local girl named Lissa. If the garrison discovers a Dominion soldier practicing heretical magic, the consequences will be severe for both of them.
4. **[[The Cinders]] are watching.** The Dominion's secret police have placed an informant in Ashcreek. Captain Ashmark doesn't know who it is. The informant's reports will determine whether the Dominion sends diplomats or soldiers.
5. **The creek is changing.** The Grey Creek's Ashite concentration is fluctuating wildly as ley-line surges interact with the divine material. Some days the water enforces unnatural calm. Other days it induces paranoia. Old Greta says the creek is "arguing with itself."
