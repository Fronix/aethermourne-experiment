# Fenholm

> [!quote] "Sink or build."
> , Fenholm founding proverb

> [!info] At a Glance
> - **Region:** [[The Greymere Fenlands]]
> - **Population:** ~6,000
> - **Government:** No formal authority. [[Maren Duskwater]] (the Fenmother) mediates disputes. The [[The Confluence Council|Confluence Council]] meets here.
> - **Economy:** Trade hub, ley-crystal exchange, provisioning, smuggling logistics
> - **Motto:** *"Sink or build."*
> - **Notable Feature:** The Lattice, a vast network of interconnected wooden platforms, bridges, and stilt-buildings spanning nearly a mile of marshland

## Overview

Fenholm is the largest settlement in [[The Greymere Fenlands]] and the closest thing the region has to a capital. It sprawls across the Glimmer Marshes on a lattice of wooden platforms, rope bridges, and stilted structures that have been built, rebuilt, extended, and repaired so many times that no two sections match. The oldest platforms are blackened ironwood driven into bedrock. The newest are lashed-together driftwood that sway alarmingly underfoot. Beneath it all, the marsh water glows faintly with ley-light, casting the underside of every structure in pale blue.

The settlement grew organically around the safest ley-line crossing in the region, a place where the Aetheric Web's fluctuations are strong enough to be useful but mild enough not to kill you. Traders, refugees, and opportunists gathered here because it was the only place in the fenlands where you could reliably meet other people. Over decades, that gathering became permanent. Fenholm has no walls, no gates, and no laws beyond the unspoken understanding that you don't steal from your neighbours and you help when the surges come.

[[Maren Duskwater]], the Fenmother, is the settlement's unofficial leader. She resolves disputes, allocates resources during emergencies, and represents Fenholm's interests to outside powers. She holds no title and claims no authority. People listen to her because she's usually right.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Fenholm is more fragile than it appears. The Lattice's oldest supports are failing, and the ley-light beneath the platforms is intensifying, a sign that the surges are reaching even the Glimmer Marshes' relatively stable zone. Maren Duskwater knows the settlement may need to relocate within the year, but she hasn't told anyone because there's nowhere to go.
>
> The [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]]' local chapter operates from a cramped observatory on the Lattice's eastern edge, and their Lens-Bearer, [[Brother Lumen]], was reassigned to [[Sporeling]] three months ago. He hasn't reported back. The chapter's Arch-Reader equivalent, a woman named Tessera Vane (cousin to Thessaly Vane in Ashveil), is growing concerned.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Lattice
The interconnected platform network that constitutes Fenholm proper. Divided informally into the Upper Lattice (newer construction, merchant stalls, the Council Platform) and the Lower Lattice (older, residential, closer to the water). The Lower Lattice floods during surges.

### The Council Platform
The largest single platform in Fenholm, reinforced with ironwood and Ember Guild metalwork. The [[The Confluence Council|Confluence Council]] convenes here during surge warnings and seasonal readings. Between sessions, it serves as a public gathering space and market square.

### The Fenmother's House
[[Maren Duskwater]]'s home and de facto seat of governance. A modest stilt-house distinguished only by the blue lantern that burns in its window at all hours, a signal that the Fenmother is available for petitions. The interior is cluttered with maps, ledgers, and correspondence from every corner of the fenlands.

### The Ember Forge
An [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] outpost on the western edge of the Lattice. The only proper forge in the fenlands, operated by a Guild factor named Corren Steelhand. He trades metalwork for ley-crystals and raw reagents, and he's the primary conduit for Guild intelligence about the region.

### The Glow Market
Fenholm's commercial heart. Open-air stalls on the Upper Lattice where ley-crystals, reagents, preserved food, smuggled goods, and Fencraft services are bought and sold. Operates on barter, with ley-crystals as the de facto currency. The most reliable place in the fenlands to buy anything.

## People & Culture

Fenholm's population is transient by nature. Roughly half are permanent residents, the rest are traders, travellers, smugglers, and seasonal workers passing through. The permanent residents are a mix of every culture and caste on the continent, united only by their refusal to live under anyone else's rules.

Social hierarchy is informal and merit-based. The people who matter are the ones who keep the settlement alive: the fen-guides who know the safe paths, the Fencrafters who contain surges, the builders who maintain the Lattice, and the Fenmother who holds it all together. Wealth matters less than competence. A skilled fen-guide outranks a rich merchant.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The Lattice is failing.** Ley-light intensification is warping the oldest ironwood supports. Engineers estimate months before sections of the Lower Lattice collapse. Maren Duskwater needs resources she doesn't have.
2. **The Star-Reader gone silent.** [[Brother Lumen]] was sent to [[Sporeling]] to study the Deep Fen's Nethys Pools three months ago. No word since. Tessera Vane wants someone to find him.
3. **Dominion tax envoys.** [[Captain Hale Ashmark]] has sent representatives from [[Ashcreek]] demanding Fenholm submit to Dominion taxation. The Fenmother is stalling, but the envoys are growing impatient.
4. **Ley-crystal shortage.** Surges are becoming too dangerous for standard harvesting runs. Ley-crystal supply is dropping while demand rises. Prices are spiking and tempers are following.
5. **The Second Silence.** Stars visible from the Lattice are dimming noticeably. The [[The Confluence Council|Confluence Council]]'s readings show the Aetheric Web destabilizing in patterns that match no historical record. [[Old Fenris]] says the Web is "screaming."
