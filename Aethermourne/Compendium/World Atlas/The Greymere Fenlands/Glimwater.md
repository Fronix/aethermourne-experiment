# Glimwater

> [!quote] "Don't fight the current. Become it."
> , First lesson of Fencraft, attributed to [[Old Fenris]]

> [!info] At a Glance
> - **Region:** [[The Greymere Fenlands]]
> - **Population:** ~3,000
> - **Government:** Informal council of senior Fencrafters
> - **Economy:** Fencraft training, ley-crystal harvesting, arcane research
> - **Motto:** *"The water teaches."*
> - **Notable Feature:** The Glimpool, the brightest ley-line surfacing point in Aethermourne, where the water glows bright enough to read by

---

## Overview

Glimwater exists because of the Glimpool, a vast body of ley-saturated water where the Aetheric Web's densest lines break the surface in visible, shimmering bands of light. The pool is beautiful and lethal. Its light shifts through colours that have no names in common speech, and its waters carry such concentrated arcane energy that unshielded contact can rewrite a person's nervous system. The settlement was built at the pool's eastern edge, where the concentration is potent but survivable, by the first Fencrafters who learned to draw power from the surges rather than be consumed by them.

Today, Glimwater is the centre of Fencraft training. Aspiring mages come from across [[The Greymere Fenlands]] and, increasingly, from [[The Ashen Dominion]] and [[The Hollowed Reach]], to study under practitioners who can teach them to ride the ley-line chaos. The training is dangerous. Roughly one in five students suffers permanent arcane injury. The ones who survive are among the most adaptable spellcasters on the continent.

The settlement's culture revolves entirely around magic. Social standing is determined by Fencraft ability. Conversations are peppered with jargon about ley-line currents and surge patterns. Non-mages live here too, running the services that keep the mages fed and housed, but they are a permanent underclass in everything but name.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Glimpool is not just a ley-line surfacing. It is the closest point on the material plane to where Nethys's core consciousness fragmented. The pool's light is the goddess's shattered awareness, trying to think, failing, and trying again. [[Wick]] can hear what it's trying to say. If they ever learn to translate it fully, they will possess knowledge of the Aetheric Web's architecture that no living mortal has held since the Silence.
>
> The pool is also growing. It has expanded by roughly thirty feet in every direction over the past year. This is not natural growth, it is the Web overcompensating for the Second Silence's drain. If the expansion continues, it will reach Glimwater's innermost buildings within months, making them uninhabitable.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Glimpool
The settlement's reason for being. A shimmering body of ley-saturated water roughly half a mile across, its surface traced with luminous veins of raw arcane energy. Fencrafters train at its edges. No one swims in its centre and returns unchanged.

### The Surge Platforms
Wooden platforms extending over the Glimpool's shallows where Fencrafters practice. Each platform is inscribed with grounding wards (the only structured magic that functions reliably here) to prevent lethal feedback. The wards need replacing every few weeks as the surges eat through them.

### The Bright House
The largest building in Glimwater, a three-storey stilt-structure where senior Fencrafters teach, debate, and conduct research. Part school, part laboratory, part philosophical salon. The walls are saturated with ambient ley-energy and glow faintly at night.

### The Still Shore
The eastern quarter of Glimwater, farthest from the pool, where non-mages live. Quieter, darker, and considerably less dangerous. The taverns here cater to students recovering from training injuries, which is most of them.

### The Nethys Stone
A standing stone at the pool's northern edge, carved with pre-Silence script that no one has fully translated. It predates the fenlands' settlement by millennia. [[Old Fenris]] believes it was placed by Nethys herself as a marker. It hums audibly during surges.

## People & Culture

Glimwater is a mage's town. The culture is competitive, experimental, and more than a little reckless. Fencrafters test new techniques on each other, argue about theory with the passion of theologians, and celebrate breakthroughs with drinking and controlled ley-fire displays. There is a camaraderie born of shared danger, but also an edge of ruthlessness: talent is everything here, and those without it are treated kindly but never equally.

The senior Fencrafters who run the Bright House operate as an informal governing council. They set training standards, adjudicate magical disputes, and maintain the grounding wards that keep the settlement from being consumed by the Glimpool's expansion. They answer to no one and answer for everything.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The Glimpool is expanding.** The ley-saturated water is creeping toward the settlement's inner buildings. The senior Fencrafters are divided: evacuate or attempt to contain it?
2. **[[Wick]]'s talent.** The prodigy's abilities are attracting attention from [[The Star-Readers]], [[The Ashen Dominion]]'s Calculist orders, and less savoury interests. Someone will try to recruit or capture them soon.
3. **The Nethys Stone is cracking.** The pre-Silence monument has developed hairline fractures that widen during surges. [[Old Fenris]] insists that if it breaks, something will be released. He won't say what.
4. **Desperate students.** Aspiring mages arrive weekly, some from as far as [[The Ashen Dominion]], fleeing persecution or seeking power. Not all of them survive training, and the bodies are becoming a problem.
5. **Magic growing unreliable.** Even experienced Fencrafters are struggling with surges that behave unlike anything in living memory. Spells are misfiring in new, alarming ways, as if the ley lines are carrying different instructions.
