# Sporeling

> [!quote] "The mushrooms remember what we forget."
> , Local saying in Sporeling

> [!info] At a Glance
> - **Region:** [[The Greymere Fenlands]] (Deep Fen)
> - **Population:** ~800
> - **Government:** Communal decision-making guided by the Mycelial Circle, a council of elders who commune with the fungal network
> - **Economy:** Empyrean Shard recovery, rare reagents, fungal cultivation
> - **Motto:** *"Grow where you're planted."*
> - **Notable Feature:** Built among towering luminescent fungi, some over forty feet tall, that respond to magic and may carry fragments of [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]]'s consciousness

---

## Overview

Sporeling is the strangest settlement in [[The Greymere Fenlands]], which is saying something. Located deep in the fen's interior where reality thins and the ley lines burn brightest, it exists within a grove of colossal luminescent fungi that glow in shifting hues of violet, blue, and pale gold. The mushrooms are not natural. They grow from soil saturated with Nethys's fragmented essence, and they have developed something that might be intelligence, or might be an echo of the goddess's shattered mind running on organic substrate.

The settlement's residents live in and among the fungi, hollowing out the largest caps for shelter, cultivating smaller varieties for food and reagents, and navigating the mycelial network that connects the grove underground. The network pulses with faint light when magic is performed nearby, and experienced residents claim they can feel emotions through it: curiosity, alarm, something that might be loneliness.

People who live in Sporeling change. It happens slowly. Their skin develops a faint luminescence. Their dreams become vivid and shared. They begin to finish each other's sentences, not out of familiarity but because the mycelial network is connecting their thoughts. Long-term residents report difficulty thinking independently, as if the boundary between self and colony is blurring. Most of them don't mind. Some of them can't remember how to mind.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The fungal network is a biological extension of the Aetheric Web, grown from Nethys's essence in the Deep Fen's saturated soil. It is, in a very real sense, Nethys's nervous system rebuilt in organic form. The "intelligence" the mushrooms display is a fragment of the goddess trying to reconstitute herself through a medium she never intended.
>
> [[Brother Lumen]] came here three months ago and hasn't left because he realized what the network is. He is now attempting to use it as a conduit to gather and reconnect other Nethys fragments, essentially using Sporeling as a relay station for divine reassembly. His research is working. The network is growing faster, the residents are changing more rapidly, and Nethys's whispers through the mycelium are becoming words.
>
> If Lumen succeeds, the consequences are unpredictable. A partially reconstituted goddess of magic, channelled through a fungal network in the most ley-saturated location on the continent, could reshape the Aetheric Web. It could also destroy every mind connected to the network, including Sporeling's entire population.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Cap Houses
Dwellings carved into the largest mushroom caps, accessible by ladders and rope bridges strung between stalks. The interiors are warm, dry, and faintly luminescent. Each cap houses one to three families. The walls pulse gently with the mycelial network's rhythm.

### The Rootmeet
A clearing at the grove's center where the mycelial network's density is greatest. The ground glows steadily here, and sounds carry strangely. The Mycelial Circle convenes at the Rootmeet for communal decisions, sitting in a ring on the luminous soil and, they claim, listening to what the network has to say.

### Brother Lumen's Workshop
A cap house on the grove's western edge that [[Brother Lumen]] has converted into a research station. Covered in star-charts, ley-line maps, and fungal growth diagrams. The walls pulse faster here than anywhere else in Sporeling. He does not welcome visitors.

### The Shard Bogs
The treacherous marshes beyond the grove where Empyrean Shards, fragments of the collapsed divine realm, surface from the deep mud. Shard recovery is the settlement's primary source of outside income and its most dangerous occupation. The bogs are unstable, ley-charged, and haunted by phenomena that defy explanation.

### The Dreaming Hollow
A natural depression in the grove where the mycelial network's influence is strongest. Residents come here to share dreams, a practice that started as recreation and has become something closer to communion. Non-residents who sleep here report vivid, disturbing visions of a woman made of light trying to speak through static.

## People & Culture

Sporeling's residents are reclusive, gentle, and deeply unsettling to outsiders. They speak softly, move in coordination without apparent communication, and display a serenity that borders on the uncanny. They are protective of their grove, suspicious of outsiders, and entirely unwilling to discuss the mycelial network's nature with anyone who hasn't experienced it firsthand.

The community operates on consensus, decisions are made at the Rootmeet, and no individual holds authority. In practice, the longest-term residents, those most deeply connected to the network, carry the most influence. Whether that influence is theirs or the network's is a question they find irrelevant.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **[[Brother Lumen]]'s experiment.** The Star-Reader's research is accelerating the network's growth and changing the residents faster. Some are losing themselves. Lumen doesn't see this as a problem.
2. **The Shards are surfacing faster.** Empyrean Shards are appearing in greater numbers and closer to the settlement, suggesting the boundary between the material plane and the divine realm's remnants is thinning.
3. **A missing shard-hunter.** Fen Holloway hasn't returned from the Shard Bogs in four days. The mycelial network is agitated, pulsing in patterns the elders can't interpret.
4. **Outsider interest.** [[The Star-Readers]] want to know what happened to [[Brother Lumen]]. The [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] wants access to the Shard Bogs. [[Sera Gloomhollow]]'s bandits want the settlement's reagent stockpile. Sporeling wants to be left alone.
5. **The network is dreaming of the Silence.** Shared dreams in the Dreaming Hollow have begun showing the same vision: a woman shattering into light, stars going dark, and a sound like the world forgetting how to breathe. The residents believe Nethys is remembering how she died.
