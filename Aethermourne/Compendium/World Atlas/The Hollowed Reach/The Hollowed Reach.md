# The Hollowed Reach

> [!quote]
> *"Every harbor has a bottom, and every bottom has a darkness, and every darkness has a voice. The trick isn't not hearing it. The trick is not answering."*
> , Keeper Lira Ashvane, Lantern-Keepers' Field Manual, Entry One

---

## Overview

The Hollowed Reach is the wealthiest region in [[World Overview|Aethermourne]] and the most haunted. It stretches along the continent's southern and southeastern coast, a dramatic seaboard of shattered cliffs and tidal caves opening onto a sprawling archipelago. A merchant-republic governed by the [[Concord of Tides|Concord of Tides]], it runs on coin, compromise, and willful ignorance. Its capital, [[Tidewall]], houses roughly 250,000 souls and serves as the busiest port on the continent. The local currency is the Drift, silver coins stamped with wave-patterns.

Everything about the Reach is defined by what lies beneath it, whether the people who live here know it or not. [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], Goddess of Shadow, is imprisoned at the bottom of the Abyssal Trench. [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]]'s tides shape daily life, and the [[The Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keepers]] fight the things that crawl up from the deep. The fog never quite lifts, the tides occasionally turn black, and people sometimes walk into the sea at night with expressions of perfect peace.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **The Prison Beneath:** Serith's prison sits at the unknowable bottom of the Abyssal Trench, a cage woven from [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]]'s divine bone and weighted by [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]]'s essence. It has held for two thousand years. It is failing, not because the materials are degrading, but because Serith has spent millennia whispering to Yvenne through the bars, slowly eroding the sea goddess's certainty. A sea goddess who doubts herself is a sea that becomes unpredictable. Everything wrong in the Hollowed Reach, the Black Tides, the Sleepwalkers, the fog, the Trench creatures, traces back to this: the lock is being picked from the inside, and the key is the locksmith's own uncertainty.
>
> The [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] cult accelerates the process from the surface. Their leader, the Whisperer, receives direct instructions from Serith through communion at sites where the prison's influence bleeds through, particularly at [[Mistholm]] and certain deep sea-caves along the Shattered Coast.

## Geography

### The Shattered Coast
The mainland coastline is a labyrinth of fractured cliffs, sea caves, and tidal pools. Perpetual fog rolls inland from the Trench, carrying a faint mineral chill that locals call "the breath." Fishing villages cling to sheltered coves, but the coast grows more dangerous each year as [[#Sleepwalkers|Sleepwalker]] incidents multiply and Trench creatures surface with increasing boldness.

### The Reach Archipelago
Dozens of islands connected by the ancient Tidal Bridges, stone causeways sustained by [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]]'s power that rise from the waves at low tide. The inner islands are developed and prosperous, home to [[Tidewall]] and the Reach's major settlements. The outer islands remain wild, dotted with ruins and haunted by things that avoid the light. The Bridges are slowly failing, a fact the [[Concord of Tides|Concord]] publicly denies.

### The Abyssal Trench
A massive undersea rift running parallel to the coast, depth unknown. The water above it is visibly darker, and sailors give it wide berth. Trench creatures, organisms adapted to crushing pressure and total darkness, have been surfacing more frequently. At its deepest point, something vast has begun rising higher than it has in centuries. The Lantern-Keepers call it the Watcher Below, a prison guardian whose movements suggest growing agitation.

### The Weeping Isles
A small cluster of mist-shrouded islands where [[The Twelve#Belara|Belara]], Goddess of Compassion, fell during the [[Timeline|Silence]]. Warm mist hangs permanently over the islands, said to be the goddess's lingering tears. The waters possess extraordinary healing properties, but prolonged exposure leaves visitors marked by a permanent, gentle melancholy. Tended by the Merciful, a contemplative order led by [[Abbot Cael]].

## Society and Culture

The Reach runs on a simple philosophy: profit is freedom. Pragmatism trumps ideology, privacy is sacred, and people mind their own business as long as the trade flows. Religion is transactional. [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]] is the most worshipped goddess, but it is a business arrangement, offerings for safe passage, not devotion.

The defining phenomenon of the region is the **Black Tides**, events where the sea turns ink-dark, magic surges unpredictably, Trench creatures surface, and Sleepwalker incidents spike. They have always occurred, but their frequency is accelerating. The Concord insists this is cyclical. The [[The Lantern-Keepers|Lantern-Keepers]] are not so sure.

**Magic** in the Reach follows the tides, waxing and waning with [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]]'s rhythms. For details on Tidal Sorcery, see [[Cosmology and Magic#Tidal Sorcery]].

## Current Tensions

1. **Black Tides accelerating**, intervals shortening from months to weeks
2. **Sleepwalkers spreading** beyond [[Mistholm]], now appearing in [[Saltmere]] and even [[Tidewall]]
3. **[[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] cult growing**, recruiting from the desperate and the grieving
4. **Concord corruption**, multiple Tidekeepers compromised by fear, greed, or outside influence
5. **[[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]] becoming unpredictable**, tides arriving at wrong times, Bridges failing without warning
6. **Something massive stirring in the Trench**, the Watcher Below rising higher than recorded in two millennia
