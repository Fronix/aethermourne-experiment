## Overview

Ironhaven is the largest settlement in the Ironmarch and the closest thing the region has to a capital. It sprawls across a low plateau in the western Iron Fields, a fortress-city of red stone quarried from iron-saturated bedrock and walls reinforced with salvaged divine iron. The streets are wide enough for cavalry, the buildings thick-walled and squat, and the whole city is oriented around the Warspire, a fragment of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] the size of a cathedral that erupts from the plateau's center like a broken sword thrust into the earth.

The Warspire hums. Everyone in Ironhaven can feel it, a vibration in the bones, a tightness behind the sternum that sharpens focus and shortens patience. The city has adapted. Disputes are settled quickly, through formalized dueling rings rather than street brawls. Public spaces are designed to minimize crowding. Taverns close at sundown. The city functions, but it functions the way a loaded crossbow functions: carefully, under tension, with the understanding that something could snap at any moment.

[[Yrsa Bloodhand]] rules from the Iron Seat, a fortress built into the Warspire's base. She has held the city and the title of Iron Thane for eleven years through a combination of tactical brilliance, political pragmatism, and a will strong enough to resist the shard's influence. Under her rule, Ironhaven has become less violent than it has been in generations. This worries people who know how the Ironmarch works, because peace here is always the prelude to a larger war.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Yrsa's]] resistance to the Warspire's influence comes from the shard of [[Cosmology and Magic#Morrhaelite|Morrhaelite]] she wears against her skin, hidden beneath her armor. The death-essence dampens [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] war-contamination, but the cost is physical pain and a creeping numbness that she fears will eventually consume her entirely. She has told no one about this. If her enemies learned her secret, they would either steal it or destroy it, and without it, she would become another conquest-driven warlord within weeks.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Warspire

The city's heart and curse. A pillar of divine iron sixty feet wide at the base and over a hundred feet tall, black-red and faintly warm to the touch. The air around it tastes of copper. Birds avoid it. Dogs brought near it become aggressive. The Warspire is the largest [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] fragment in the Ironmarch (though not the tallest, that distinction belongs to the Kaevroth Monolith near [[Wrathspire]]) and the focal point of war-essence contamination in the western region. [[The Ember Guild]] maintains monitoring equipment around its base, measuring the fragment's output. Recent readings have been climbing.

### The Iron Seat

The fortress-palace of the Iron Thane, built into and around the Warspire's base. Thick walls of red stone and salvaged iron, designed as much to contain the Warspire's influence as to defend against attackers. The throne room is directly beneath the fragment, the most contaminated room in the city. [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] holds court there deliberately, because anyone who cannot control themselves in the Warspire's shadow has no business advising the Iron Thane.

### The Dueling Rings

Seven stone circles spread throughout the city, each sixty feet across. All disputes in Ironhaven, legal, commercial, personal, can be settled in the Rings under the Code of Iron. Fights are to yield, not death, though deaths happen. The Rings serve a practical purpose: they channel the aggression the Warspire generates into structured, bounded violence. Without them, the city would tear itself apart.

### The Red Market

Ironhaven's sprawling commercial district, where arms, armor, mercenary contracts, and [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] raw stock are traded. The largest weapons market on the continent. Buyers come from as far as [[The Hollowed Reach]] and [[The Verdant Marches]]. [[The Ember Guild]] maintains a trade office here, regulating the sale of refined divine iron and taking a percentage of every transaction.

## People & Culture

Ironhaven's citizens are hard, practical people who have learned to live under the constant pressure of divine contamination. They value self-control above almost everything else, because losing control here is not a social failing but a survival threat. Children are taught breathing exercises before they learn to read. Adults practice what they call "iron discipline," the ability to recognize when the Warspire is influencing their emotions and step back before acting on them. It does not always work.

The city is cosmopolitan by Ironmarch standards. Traders from [[The Ashen Dominion]], mercenaries between contracts, [[The Ember Guild]] artisans, and refugees from smaller settlements all mix in Ironhaven's streets. The result is a culture that is rough but not provincial, capable of diplomacy and commerce even as it is saturated in violence.

### Notable Residents

- [[Yrsa Bloodhand]], Iron Thane. The woman who has held the Ironmarch together for eleven years through will alone. She wants to break the cycle of war, and she is running out of time to do it.
- **Castellan Drenn**, commander of Ironhaven's garrison. A loyal veteran who knows [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Yrsa]] is hiding something and trusts her enough not to ask.
- **Forge-Mistress Kellan**, head of Ironhaven's smithing quarter. Supplies [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Yrsa's]] forces with arms and resents the [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild's]] trade regulations.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. [[Mira the Red]] has sent an envoy to Ironhaven demanding that [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Yrsa]] abdicate the Iron Thane title. The envoy was polite. The army massing on the Iron Fields is not.
2. [[The Ember Guild]]'s monitoring equipment at the Warspire has recorded a sharp increase in war-essence output. [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]] has requested a meeting with the Iron Thane to discuss evacuation contingencies. [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Yrsa]] has refused.
3. A dueling ring fight escalated to a killing last week, despite the Code of Iron. The killer claims the Warspire "made him do it." He may be right, which is the problem.
4. [[The Ashen Dominion]] has sent a diplomatic mission offering military aid against [[Mira the Red]], in exchange for permanent trade concessions in the Contested Corridor. [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Yrsa]] suspects the real price is sovereignty.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Warspire is one of the fragments responding to the migration pull. It has not moved yet, but its output is increasing because it is "reaching" toward the Heart of Iron buried under Ostivaar. If it does move, physically, it will take half of Ironhaven's plateau with it. [[Factor Gellen Ashworth|Gellen Ashworth]] suspects this is possible but has no proof yet.
>
> The Dominion diplomatic mission is genuine in its offer and deceptive in its intent. [[Hierarch Valdren III]] wants the Ironmarch as a buffer state under Dominion influence, and [[Mira the Red|Mira's]] rebellion is a convenient excuse to insert Dominion forces. If [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Yrsa]] accepts, she saves her city but loses her region's independence. If she refuses, she faces [[Mira the Red|Mira]] alone.
