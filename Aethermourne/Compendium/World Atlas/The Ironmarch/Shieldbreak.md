## Overview

Shieldbreak clings to a natural harbor on the Rust Coast, a crescent of sheltered water between two headlands of iron-streaked rock. The town is named for the reef that protects the harbor, a submerged ridge of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] fragments that tears the hull off any ship whose pilot does not know the three safe channels. The fragments are the shield, and the passages through them are the break. Navigating them requires a local pilot, and the pilots charge what they like, because the alternative is the sea floor.

The town is rough, wind-scoured, and permanently damp. Salt-bleached timber buildings lean against each other on the hillside above the docks. The harbor is always busy: trade cogs loading [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] for export, mercenary transports embarking companies for contracts overseas, fishing boats dragging catches that taste of copper out of rust-stained waters. Shieldbreak smells of brine, fish, and iron. It sounds like rigging, hammers, and arguments.

[[Captain Neve Stormhull]] runs the port with the discipline of her former career in [[The Hollowed Reach]] navy. She deserted after a Black Tide event drove her ship into the Ironmarch coast, and she never went back. She is not the town's official leader, because Shieldbreak has never had one. She is simply the person who makes the harbor work, and in a town that lives and dies by its port, that makes her indispensable.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Captain Neve Stormhull|Stormhull]] did not simply desert. She was the sole survivor of a Black Tide encounter that destroyed her ship and killed her entire crew. She saw something in the Tide, something that spoke with [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] voice, and it told her that the sea would eventually come for the land. She has never spoken about this. She chose Shieldbreak because it is as far from [[The Hollowed Reach]] as she could get while still being near water. She cannot bring herself to leave the sea entirely.
>
> [[The Hollowed Reach]] considers her a deserter and has an outstanding warrant for her arrest. If agents of [[The Concord of Tides]] discover her location, she will face trial, and the intelligence she carries about the Black Tide would be invaluable to both the Concord and [[The Veil Unbound]].

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Iron Docks

Three stone piers extending into the harbor, reinforced with salvaged [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] fragments that make the pilings nearly indestructible. The docks can handle six large vessels simultaneously and twice that many smaller craft. Loading and unloading runs day and night during trade season. The dock workers are the toughest laborers in the Ironmarch, which is a competitive category.

### The Breaker's Yard

A stretch of rocky beach where damaged ships are stripped for salvage. The submerged fragment-reef claims several vessels a year, and the Yard's workers are expert at recovering usable timber, iron fittings, and cargo from wrecks. The Yard also serves as an informal memorial, figureheads from lost ships are mounted on poles along the beach, facing the sea.

### The Pilot's Hall

A stone building overlooking the harbor entrance, where Shieldbreak's pilots, the navigators who guide ships through the fragment-reef, are organized. Piloting is a hereditary trade, knowledge of the safe channels passed from parent to child. There are currently fourteen qualified pilots, and their guild is the wealthiest organization in town. [[Captain Neve Stormhull|Stormhull]] maintains an uneasy working relationship with the guild, which resents her authority but respects her seamanship.

### The Flotsam Market

Shieldbreak's market district, built along the waterfront. Named for the salvaged goods that make up a significant portion of its inventory. Anything that washes ashore, falls off a ship, or is stripped from a wreck ends up here. Alongside the salvage, the Market trades in [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] ingots packaged for export, preserved fish, imported goods from [[The Hollowed Reach]], and information, which is the most expensive commodity on the coast.

## People & Culture

Shieldbreak's people are a mix of Ironmarch natives and outsiders, refugees, deserters, adventurers, and merchants who found their way to the coast and stayed. The town is less contaminated than the interior, whether because of distance from the major fragments or the sea air, no one is certain. Tempers are shorter than in normal ports but longer than in Ironhaven. The culture is pragmatic, transactional, and surprisingly tolerant. Shieldbreak does not care where you came from. It cares what you can do.

Fishing is the town's secondary economy, and the fishers are a breed apart, tough, superstitious, and convinced that the eastern sea hides things worse than storms. They are probably right. Ships that venture too far from the coast sometimes return with crews that cannot explain the missing hours in their logs and cargo holds that smell of something no one can identify.

### Notable Residents

- [[Captain Neve Stormhull]], harbor master. She wants Shieldbreak to thrive, and she wants to never see the open ocean again. These goals are increasingly in conflict.
- **Pilot-Elder Hask Reefwalker**, head of the Pilot's Guild. An elderly man who has guided ships through the fragment-reef for forty years. He knows the channels are shifting as the fragments migrate, and he has not told anyone because admitting it would destroy the guild's authority.
- **Maren Driftwood**, a salvager and information broker who operates out of the Flotsam Market. She knows everything that arrives in and departs from Shieldbreak, and she sells that knowledge to whoever pays.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. A merchant vessel from [[The Hollowed Reach]] arrived carrying a sealed cargo consigned to [[The Veil Unbound]]. [[Captain Neve Stormhull|Stormhull]] impounded it. She has not opened the crate, and she is not sleeping well.
2. The fragment-reef is shifting. Two pilots have reported that the safe channels have narrowed, and one channel may have closed entirely. If the reef becomes impassable, Shieldbreak dies.
3. Refugees from a destroyed inland settlement have been arriving in increasing numbers, people with nothing, fleeing [[Mira the Red|Mira the Red's]] advance. Shieldbreak does not have the resources to absorb them, and [[Captain Neve Stormhull|Stormhull]] cannot bring herself to turn them away.
4. A fishing boat returned from the eastern sea with its crew unconscious and its nets full of iron shards, not from the reef but from the deep water. [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] fragments on the sea floor. No one knew they were there.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] cargo contains a ritual component, a piece of worked [[Cosmology and Magic#Ashite|Ashite]] inscribed with [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] sigils, intended for a cell operating in the Ironmarch. [[The Veil Unbound|Serith's]] cult is interested in the fragment migration because the reassembly of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] would create chaos that benefits their agenda. The Ashite component is designed to accelerate the migration.
>
> The deep-sea fragments are real and represent a previously unknown deposit of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] iron on the ocean floor. They are migrating upward, toward the surface, drawn by the same force pulling the land-based fragments together. If [[The Ember Guild]] learns about this, [[Factor Gellen Ashworth|Gellen Ashworth's]] already dire projections will need to be revised dramatically upward.
