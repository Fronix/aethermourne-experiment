# The Ironmarch

> [!quote]
> "Every man in the Ironmarch knows the shards are what make us fight. Every man knows it, and every man fights anyway. That's the joke. That's the whole bloody joke."
> , [[Yrsa Bloodhand]], Iron Thane, in a rare moment of candor

---

## Overview

The Ironmarch is the eastern lowland of Aethermourne, a broad sweep of rust-colored earth and battered coastline stretching from the foothills of [[The Ashen Dominion]] to the grey waters of the eastern sea. When [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]], God of War and Ambition, was broken apart during the Theomachis, the densest rain of iron fell here. His shattered body seeded the soil with war-essence so deeply that the land itself became a wound, a place that breeds conflict the way fertile ground breeds wheat.

The fragments of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] divine iron are everywhere. Fist-sized shards litter the fields. Monoliths the height of towers stand like ruined sentinels on the horizon. The soil is red with oxidized god-metal, and the rivers run the color of old blood after heavy rain. Anyone who stays in the Ironmarch long enough feels it: a tightening behind the eyes, a sharpening of temper, a growing conviction that enemies surround them. The land does not permit peace. It never has. Twenty-two centuries of unbroken conflict attest to that.

What the Ironmarch does produce is soldiers and steel. The mercenary companies born here are the finest in the world, forged in an environment where fighting is as natural as breathing. And [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]], worked by skilled smiths, produces weapons of unmatched quality, blades that hold an edge through anything, armor that turns strikes that should kill. The rest of the continent needs what the Ironmarch makes. The Ironmarch needs everything the rest of the continent has. It is a relationship built on blood and commerce, and no one pretends otherwise.

## Geography

### The Iron Fields

The interior heartland, a rolling plain of rust-red earth where [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] fragments are densest. Shards protrude from the ground like broken teeth, and the soil is so saturated with divine iron that crops grow stunted and bitter. Livestock raised here are aggressive and lean. The ambient war-essence is strongest in the Fields, and settlements cluster around the fewer, smaller fragments where the contamination is manageable. Travelers crossing the Fields report a constant low hum at the edge of hearing, like distant drums or a heartbeat not their own.

### The Rust Coast

The eastern shoreline, where red-stained rivers empty into the grey sea and the beaches are streaked with iron oxide. Fishing villages cling to the coast, their catches tasting faintly of metal. The Rust Coast is the Ironmarch's lifeline to the outside world, its only access to maritime trade. [[Shieldbreak]] is the primary port. The waters offshore are treacherous, riddled with submerged fragment-fields that have torn the hulls of a thousand ships. Local pilots who know the channels charge dearly for their knowledge.

### The Contested Corridor

A narrow lowland pass connecting the Ironmarch to [[The Ashen Dominion]], the most fought-over strip of land on the continent. Control of the Corridor means control of trade between the Dominion and the Ironmarch, and by extension, control of the [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] supply that flows west. The Corridor has changed hands dozens of times. Its fortifications are built on the ruins of older fortifications, which are built on the ruins of older fortifications still. [[The Ashen Dominion]] wants permanent control. The Ironmarch warlords will die before they allow it.

### The Shardfall

The southeastern interior, where the largest of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] monoliths stand. These are not fragments but continental impacts, towers of divine iron driven into the bedrock like nails. The largest, the Kaevroth Monolith near [[Wrathspire]], rises two hundred feet and hums with a sound that makes teeth ache and tempers flare. The Shardfall is sparsely populated because long-term habitation near the monoliths drives people to violence, paranoia, and eventually madness. The weapon-smiths of [[Wrathspire]] work here anyway, because the iron closest to the monoliths produces the finest blades.

## Society & Culture

**War is weather.** The people of the Ironmarch do not romanticize conflict. They endure it the way coastal folk endure storms. You prepare, you fight, you bury your dead, and you prepare again. Heroism exists, but it is the heroism of the practical, surviving one more season, keeping one more town standing.

**The mercenary codes.** Because the Ironmarch cannot sustain formal armies or lasting governments, the mercenary companies evolved their own laws. Contracts are sacred. A company that breaks contract is dissolved and its members branded. Neutral ground, particularly [[The Red Garrison]], is inviolable. These codes are the closest thing the region has to a constitution, and they work because everyone has seen what happens without them.

**The Iron Thane.** The strongest warlord in the region claims this title, which carries no legal authority but enormous practical influence. The current Thane, [[Yrsa Bloodhand]], has held the title for eleven years, longer than anyone in three generations. The title changes hands through challenge, assassination, or the slow erosion of support. No Iron Thane has ever died of old age.

**Kaevroth's shadow.** Everyone in the Ironmarch knows the shards cause the fighting. It is common knowledge, discussed openly, even joked about. It changes nothing. Knowing the sickness does not cure it. Some wear iron talismans meant to ward off the influence. Some practice breathing exercises or meditation. Some simply drink. The contamination is a fact of life, like gravity, and resisting it is a daily, private struggle that no one talks about because everyone is fighting it.

## Current Tensions

1. **Mira's challenge.** [[Mira the Red]], a young warlord from the Iron Fields, is gathering forces to challenge [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] for the title of Iron Thane. Her eyes have turned iron-grey and she claims [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] speaks to her. Her army grows daily.
2. **Dominion pressure.** [[The Ashen Dominion]] is pushing to secure permanent control of the Contested Corridor, framing it as "stabilization." The Ironmarch warlords see it as conquest by another name.
3. **Fragment migration.** [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]] of [[The Ember Guild]] has discovered that [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] fragments are moving, drifting slowly toward each other as if trying to reassemble. The implications are catastrophic.
4. **Duskfield's neutrality.** [[Duskfield]], the only reliable food source in the region, has declared itself neutral territory. Both [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Yrsa]] and [[Mira the Red|Mira]] have respected this so far. So far.
5. **The Second Silence.** War-essence across the Ironmarch has intensified noticeably in recent months. Smiths report that [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] is easier to work but harder to control. Tempers are shorter. Violence is spiking. Something is waking up.

## Key Factions

- [[The Iron Companies]], the mercenary guilds that form the backbone of the Ironmarch's economy and military
- [[The Quartermasters Circle]], the neutral arbitration body governing [[The Red Garrison]] and brokering contracts
- [[The Ember Guild]], the continent-spanning crafting guild, which maintains a major presence at [[Wrathspire]] to monitor fragment activity
- **The Holds**, the shifting territories of individual warlords, currently dominated by [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Yrsa Bloodhand's]] coalition and [[Mira the Red|Mira the Red's]] rising faction

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The fragment migration is real and accelerating. [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] divine iron is responding to the weakening of the Aetheric Web as [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] influence spreads. The god of war is not dead in the way the other gods are dead. His essence was scattered, not extinguished, and the fragments retain a collective will, a hunger to reunite. If enough fragments converge, the result would not be resurrection exactly, but something worse: a mindless divine storm of pure war-essence, a Theomachis weapon without a wielder.
>
> [[Mira the Red]] is genuinely hearing [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] residual will. She is not insane, or rather, she is insane in a way that happens to be accurate. The fragments are calling to each other through her, using her ambition as a conduit. If she claims the Iron Thane title and unites the Ironmarch, she will march on the Contested Corridor, then the Dominion, then everywhere else. She cannot stop. The iron will not let her.
>
> [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] is the only person in the region who has found a way to resist [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] influence: a shard of [[Cosmology and Magic#Morrhaelite|Morrhaelite]] pressed against her skin, death-essence counteracting war-essence. This is not a cure. It is agony. But it gives her clarity, and she is using that clarity to try to break the cycle of conquest before [[Mira the Red|Mira]] starts the next one. She is running out of time.
>
> The Heart of Iron, [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] largest fragment, is buried beneath Ostivaar in [[The Ashen Dominion]]. If the surface fragments are migrating, they are migrating toward it. The Ironmarch is not the destination. It is the staging ground.
