## Overview

The Red Garrison is the beating heart of the Ironmarch's mercenary economy, a walled city of barracks, training yards, contract halls, and taverns built on a defensible hill in the northern Iron Fields. It is neutral ground. This is not a suggestion, a preference, or a guideline. It is the oldest and most strictly enforced law in the Ironmarch, predating the Iron Thane tradition by centuries. No warlord, no company, no desperate fool with a grudge may shed blood within the Garrison's walls. Those who try are executed by the [[The Quartermasters Circle|Quartermasters' Circle]], immediately, publicly, and without appeal.

The neutrality works because everyone needs it to work. Without the Garrison, there is no centralized market for mercenary contracts. Without contracts, there is no structured employment for the thousands of professional soldiers the Ironmarch produces. Without structured employment, those soldiers become bandits, raiders, or the personal armies of would-be conquerers. The Garrison is the valve that keeps the Ironmarch's violence productive rather than purely destructive. It is, in its grim way, the most important institution in the region.

The city itself is functional rather than beautiful. Red stone walls, iron-reinforced gates, streets laid out in a military grid. Training yards occupy a quarter of the city's footprint. The air smells of sweat, leather, and the metallic tang of [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] from the weapons stalls. The Garrison sits in a relatively low-contamination zone, which is one reason it became the neutral site. Tempers are manageable here. Not calm, never calm in the Ironmarch, but manageable.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Garrison's neutrality is under more pressure than it has faced in living memory. Both [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] and [[Mira the Red]] are trying to hire every available company, driving up prices and depleting the available pool. [[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal]] is terrified that one side will try to seize the Garrison outright, breaking the neutrality to deny the other side access to mercenaries. If that happens, the entire Ironmarch economy collapses and every company becomes a free agent with no rules. It would be a bloodbath.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Contract Hall

A massive stone building at the Garrison's center, its walls lined with slate boards displaying active contracts, company rosters, and arbitration notices. This is where mercenary work is brokered. Clients post contracts, companies bid for them, and the [[The Quartermasters Circle|Quartermasters]] mediate disputes over terms, payment, and exclusivity. The Hall operates from dawn to dusk, and the noise inside is deafening, hundreds of negotiations happening simultaneously in a space designed for sound to carry.

### The Proving Grounds

Four large training yards where mercenary companies drill, spar, and demonstrate their capabilities to prospective clients. Watching the Grounds is the best way to assess a company's quality before hiring. Exhibition bouts between companies draw crowds and serve as informal recruiting tools. Blood is allowed here, death is not. The distinction is enforced by Garrison marshals with blunted-steel authority.

### The Arbiter's Tower

Headquarters of [[The Quartermasters Circle]], a square stone tower overlooking the Contract Hall. Disputes that cannot be resolved by individual Quartermasters are escalated here for final arbitration. The Tower also houses the Garrison's archives, centuries of contracts, precedents, and company records that constitute the Ironmarch's only body of consistent law.

### The Sheathed Blade

The Garrison's largest tavern, named for the custom of peace. Weapons are checked at the door and stored in iron racks along the walls. Inside, rival mercenaries drink side by side, because the alternative is drinking alone, and drinking alone in the Ironmarch is how people go mad. The Blade is where information moves: rumors, intelligence, warnings, and the quiet negotiations that happen before the formal ones in the Contract Hall.

## People & Culture

The Garrison's permanent population is small, mostly [[The Quartermasters Circle|Quartermasters]], support staff, and retired mercenaries who have nowhere else to go. The transient population doubles or triples the city's size during contract season (spring and autumn, when campaigns begin). The culture is professional. People here define themselves by their company, their contract record, and their reputation. A mercenary who breaks contract is finished. A company that fails to deliver is dissolved. The system is harsh because it has to be. Trust is the only currency that matters more than iron.

### Notable Residents

- [[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal]], head of [[The Quartermasters Circle]]. The closest thing the Ironmarch has to a peacekeeper. He wants the neutrality to hold and suspects it will not survive [[Mira the Red|Mira's]] war.
- **Marshals-Captain Torva**, chief of the Garrison's internal enforcement. A former mercenary who lost her sword arm and found a second career keeping other people from using theirs.
- **Old Garrett**, proprietor of The Sheathed Blade. Has run the tavern for thirty years, knows every company captain by name, and has never once expressed a political opinion. This is either wisdom or cowardice, and nobody can tell which.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. [[Mira the Red]] has offered triple rates for any company willing to sign exclusively with her campaign. Several companies are tempted. If they accept, [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] will be outgunned.
2. A company called the Rust Wolves returned from a contract in the Shardfall and their entire leadership has become erratic, paranoid, and aggressive. They touched something near the monoliths. [[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal|Voss]] has quarantined them in their barracks, but they are armed and getting worse.
3. An envoy from [[The Ashen Dominion]] is in the Contract Hall, quietly hiring companies for "border security." The contracts are suspiciously generous. Several Quartermasters suspect the Dominion is building a proxy army.
4. The Garrison's stores of [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] weapons have been growing, as if the metal is accumulating. Blades left in the armory overnight are found rearranged in the morning. No one has been caught entering.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Rust Wolves' contamination is a preview of what happens when [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] fragments intensify. Their captain touched the Kaevroth Monolith bare-handed and received a vision of an iron army marching west. He cannot stop talking about it. The vision is accurate, it is [[Mira the Red|Mira's]] army as [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] will intends it. If the Rust Wolves are not contained, they will break neutrality and join [[Mira the Red|Mira]], and the precedent will shatter the Garrison's authority.
