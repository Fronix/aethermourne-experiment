## Overview

Wrathspire exists because of the Monolith and despite it. The settlement clings to the southeastern Shardfall like a barnacle on a warship's hull, a cluster of forges, workshops, and heavily reinforced dwellings built in the shadow of the tallest [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] fragment ever recorded. Two hundred feet of divine iron, black-red and faintly luminous at dusk, rising from the fractured bedrock like a finger pointed at the sky in accusation. The Monolith hums at a frequency that makes fillings ache and conversations turn into arguments. The air around it smells permanently of heated metal and ozone.

No one should live here. The war-essence contamination is the worst in the Ironmarch outside the deep fragment-fields. Tempers run hot, fights break out over nothing, and the turnover rate for residents is measured in months rather than years. But the iron closest to the Monolith, the fragments embedded in the rock within a half-mile of its base, produces weapons of extraordinary quality. Blades forged from Monolith-proximate iron hold an edge that never dulls, strike with force beyond their weight, and resonate with a faint harmonic that some warriors find intoxicating. The best weapons in the world come from Wrathspire. So do the worst stories about what those weapons do to the people who wield them.

[[The Ember Guild]] maintains its regional stronghold here, ostensibly to monitor the Monolith's activity and regulate fragment harvesting. In practice, the Guild is the only institution with the expertise and authority to keep Wrathspire from tearing itself apart. [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]] leads the Guild's operation, and his increasingly alarming reports about fragment migration have made him few friends among the smiths who depend on the Monolith for their livelihood.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Kaevroth Monolith is the largest surface fragment of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] in the world, and it is the anchor point for the fragment migration. Smaller shards across the Ironmarch are drifting toward it, and the Monolith itself is resonating with the Heart of Iron buried beneath Ostivaar. [[Factor Gellen Ashworth|Gellen Ashworth]] has calculated that if the migration continues, within a decade enough divine iron will have converged on the Shardfall to create a catastrophic war-essence event, a permanent zone of divine rage that would make the entire eastern continent uninhabitable.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Kaevroth Monolith

The defining feature of the Shardfall and the reason Wrathspire exists. Two hundred feet of divine iron, roughly cylindrical, tapering slightly toward the top. Its surface is covered in fracture patterns that some scholars claim are writing, though no one has been able to translate them. The base is surrounded by a ring of smaller fragments that have visibly migrated closer over the past century, documented by [[The Ember Guild]]'s records. Touching the Monolith bare-handed is inadvisable. Those who have done so report visions of the Theomachis from [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] perspective, a sensation of consuming other gods, of infinite hunger and rage. Most do not recover fully.

### The Ember Bastion

[[The Ember Guild]]'s regional stronghold, a fortified compound near the Monolith's base bristling with monitoring equipment, containment wards, and divine material storage. The largest Guild facility outside of its mobile headquarters. [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]] and a team of twelve Guild artificers work here, measuring the Monolith's output, tracking fragment positions across the region, and researching containment methods. The Bastion's wards dampen war-essence within its walls, making it the calmest building in Wrathspire, which is not saying much.

### The Singing Forges

Wrathspire's famous smithies, a row of workshops built into the rock face a quarter-mile from the Monolith. The forges are called "singing" because [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] emits a high, clear tone when heated properly, a sound that experienced smiths use to judge the metal's readiness. [[Sira Embersteel]], the finest weaponsmith in Aethermourne, works here. Her blades are sought by kings and warlords across the continent, and each one leaves her forge with a note of warning that the buyer invariably ignores.

### The Cooler

A deep cellar complex carved beneath Wrathspire, lined with imported stone from outside the Ironmarch and warded against war-essence. Residents retreat here when the Monolith's output spikes, sometimes for days. The Cooler has its own water supply, food stores, and sleeping quarters. It is cramped, unpleasant, and necessary. Without it, Wrathspire would have been abandoned generations ago.

## People & Culture

Wrathspire's residents are craftspeople first and everything else second. They endure the contamination because the work matters to them, and because the iron calls to those with the skill to shape it. There is a grim camaraderie among the smiths, the fellowship of people who understand exactly how dangerous their profession is and choose it anyway. Newcomers are watched carefully. If they cannot handle the Monolith's influence, they are sent away, firmly, but without shame. Not everyone can live here, and the smiths respect that.

The settlement has no formal government. The Forge Council, composed of the senior master smiths and [[The Ember Guild]]'s representatives, mediates disputes and manages resources. [[Sira Embersteel]] sits on the Council but rarely attends meetings, preferring her forge to politics.

### Notable Residents

- [[Sira Embersteel]], master smith. The finest weaponsmith in Aethermourne. She wants to forge a blade that does not corrupt its wielder, and she is beginning to suspect it cannot be done.
- [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]], [[The Ember Guild]]'s chief monitor. He wants someone in authority to listen to his warnings about the fragment migration before it is too late.
- **Forge-Warden Hask**, a retired mercenary who serves as Wrathspire's security chief. Rotates residents into and out of the Cooler on a strict schedule and does not tolerate arguments about it.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. [[Factor Gellen Ashworth|Gellen Ashworth's]] latest report to [[The Ember Guild]] headquarters describes the fragment migration in urgent terms. The Guildmaster has not responded. Ashworth suspects the report was intercepted.
2. A smith named Corren forged a blade from iron harvested at the Monolith's base and immediately fell into a trance. He has been standing motionless in his forge for three days, eyes open, unresponsive, the blade in his hand glowing faintly. No one can get close enough to take it from him.
3. [[Mira the Red]] has sent agents to Wrathspire to secure exclusive access to the Singing Forges. [[Sira Embersteel]] refused. The agents have not left.
4. The fracture patterns on the Monolith have changed. New lines have appeared in the last month, and a [[The Star-Readers|Star-Reader]] passing through claims they are not random, they are a countdown.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Corren's trance is genuine contact with [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] residual will. The blade he forged is a fragment-anchor, a weapon that draws other shards toward it. If removed from Wrathspire, it will accelerate the fragment migration in whatever direction it is carried. If carried toward Ostivaar and the Heart of Iron, the consequences would be catastrophic. [[Mira the Red|Mira's]] agents are not just here for the forges. They want this blade.
>
> The Monolith's new fracture lines are indeed a pattern, not a countdown but a map. They show the positions of every major [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] fragment on the continent, including the Heart of Iron. [[Factor Gellen Ashworth|Gellen Ashworth]] has not yet noticed this because the lines are only visible at dusk, when the Monolith's luminescence reveals them. A PC with the right knowledge (cartography, divine materials, or fragments of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] own memories) could read the map.
