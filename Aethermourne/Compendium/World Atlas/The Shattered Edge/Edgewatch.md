# Edgewatch

> [!quote] Warden Kessa Thornwall
> "We watch because if we look away, we will not know the surge is coming until it has already arrived. And by then it is not a surge. It is a funeral."

---

## Overview

Edgewatch is the closest permanent settlement to the Maelstrom, perched on the boundary between [[The Shattered Edge#The Stable Ring|the Stable Ring]] and [[The Shattered Edge#The Threshold|the Threshold]]. It is a watchtower community of roughly eight hundred souls, Edgewalkers and militia and support staff, whose single purpose is to monitor the wound in reality and sound the warning when it moves.

The settlement is not comfortable. It is not beautiful. It is necessary.

Edgewatch exists because the Maelstrom surges. Periodically, without warning, the wound expands, pushing the Threshold outward, consuming stable ground, transforming everything it touches. These surges can last minutes or days. They can extend a few hundred yards or several miles. When a surge comes, the settlements of the Shattered Edge need time to prepare, to evacuate, to brace. Edgewatch gives them that time.

The cost is paid by the watchers themselves. Prolonged proximity to the Threshold changes people. Every member of Edgewatch's garrison has been Changed to some degree. It is the price of the posting, understood and accepted.

Recently, the surges have been getting worse.

**Population:** ~800

## Layout and Landmarks

### The Watchtowers

Seven stone towers arranged in a crescent facing the Threshold, each staffed by Edgewalkers who maintain constant observation of the Maelstrom's movements. The towers are connected by covered walkways and signal lines. When a surge is detected, signal-fires are lit in sequence, cascading warnings outward across the Shattered Edge. The towers are warded, but lightly, because heavy wards interfere with the Edgewalkers' probability-sight. The watchers need to *feel* the Maelstrom shifting. Comfort would blind them.

### The Garrison

A fortified compound behind the watchtowers housing the settlement's permanent population. Stone buildings, reinforced and warded, arranged for rapid evacuation. Every resident knows three exit routes by heart. Drills are weekly. Gear is kept packed. The architecture is practical to the point of austerity, because anything decorative might change during a surge, and no one wants to wake to find their furniture trying to become something else.

### The Surge Line

A marked boundary on the ground, updated after each major surge, showing the Maelstrom's current high-water mark. The line has moved closer to Edgewatch three times in the past year. The previous lines are still visible, a record of retreat etched into the earth.

### The Memorial Stones

A field of rough-carved stones behind the garrison, each bearing a name and a date. Not graves, because there are rarely bodies. These mark the watchers who were consumed by surges, caught in expansions they detected too late or chose to face so that others could flee. The field is growing. [[Kessa Thornwall]] visits it every morning.

## People and Culture

Edgewatch attracts a particular kind of person: those who need a purpose badly enough to accept the cost. Former soldiers, outcasts, the guilt-ridden, the devoted. People who cannot sit still while the wound in the world threatens to swallow everything they care about. They are not heroes in any romantic sense. They are exhausted, Changed, frightened, and profoundly stubborn.

The culture is military in structure but familial in practice. Everyone knows everyone. Meals are shared. Nightmares are common and discussed without shame. The Changed are not merely accepted, they are the majority. [[Kessa Thornwall]]'s shifting arm is a badge of service, not a mark of damage. A watcher whose eyes now see in spectra invisible to normal humans is valued for the advantage, not pitied for the strangeness.

Humor is dark, constant, and necessary. Edgewatch's people laugh because the alternative is silence, and silence at the Threshold's edge feels too much like the quiet before a surge.

## Notable Residents

- **[[Kessa Thornwall]]**: Warden and commander. A former [[The Ashen Dominion|Dominion]] soldier Changed during a surge that overran her patrol. She stayed because no one else would have her, and now she would not leave for anything.
- **Edgewalker Rin**: The most sensitive probability-reader at the Watch, a teenager who was born Changed and has never known a world without the threads. She can detect surges earlier than any adult Edgewalker, but the strain is aging her prematurely.
- **Quartermaster Dahl**: A former [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] logistics specialist who came to trade and stayed to keep the garrison supplied. Practical, unflappable, and the only person at Edgewatch who maintains a functioning sense of normalcy.

## Tensions and Hooks

> [!tip] Hooks for Edgewatch
> - Three surges in the last month. Each one pushed the Surge Line closer. [[Kessa Thornwall]] estimates that at the current rate, Edgewatch itself will be inside the Threshold within six months. She needs help, either reinforcements, better wards, or someone willing to go into the Threshold to find out *why* the surges are accelerating.
> - A patrol of four watchers entered the Threshold to retrieve a downed observation instrument. Three returned, two of them significantly Changed. The fourth did not return, and the three survivors report seeing her walk *toward* the Maelstrom willingly, as if she heard something calling.
> - An [[The Ashen Dominion|Ashen Dominion]] military delegation has arrived proposing a "containment initiative," essentially a plan to build a massive ward-wall around the Maelstrom. [[Kessa Thornwall]] knows it won't work, the Maelstrom cannot be walled, but the Dominion is offering resources Edgewatch desperately needs. The price is Dominion control of the region.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The surges are accelerating because [[The Twelve#Serith, Goddess of Shadow and Secrets , *"The Veiled One"*|Serith]]'s influence is rhythmically pulling at the Maelstrom from the other side, stretching and releasing it like a muscle being exercised. Each cycle widens the wound slightly more. The watcher who walked toward the Maelstrom heard a fragment of the Loom of Fate's resonance, a sound so fundamental that it bypasses conscious thought and speaks directly to the part of the mind that understands pattern and purpose. She is not dead. She is at the Maelstrom's center, and she has seen the Loom. What that has done to her is a question worth pursuing.
>
> The Dominion's "containment initiative" has a secondary purpose: several members of the delegation are aligned with factions that want to *weaponize* the Maelstrom, not seal it. They believe chaotic energy, properly harnessed, could restore the Dominion's fading military supremacy. They are catastrophically wrong, but they are well-funded.
