# Riftmarket

> [!quote] Common greeting in Riftmarket
> "What are you today?" "Same as yesterday, mostly."

---

## Overview

Riftmarket is the largest settlement on [[The Shattered Edge]], a sprawling trade post built on a patch of ground that has not shifted in over two hundred years. The locals call it "the Anchor," the one reliable piece of earth in a region defined by unreliability. Nobody knows why this particular stretch of land resists the Maelstrom's influence. Nobody questions the gift.

The settlement exists because of Vorrhyn's Dust. The residue from the Maelstrom's edge, a chaotic magical catalyst of extraordinary potency, is the most valuable substance extracted from the Shattered Edge, and Riftmarket is where it changes hands. Merchants from [[The Ashen Dominion]], scholars from across the continent, [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] traders, desperate alchemists, and those who deal in things better left undealt come here for one purpose: to buy what reality discards.

The result is a settlement that is colorful, chaotic, loud, and genuinely dangerous. Riftmarket has no walls because walls would give the false impression that anything here is contained.

**Population:** ~5,000

## Layout and Landmarks

### The Anchor Stone

A massive slab of dark rock at the settlement's center, smooth and faintly warm to the touch. It predates the settlement by centuries. Some believe it is a fragment of Vorrhyn's own essence, calcified into permanence, the last stable thing the god of change ever created. Whether this is true or sentimental nonsense, the ground within a half-mile of the Anchor Stone has not shifted, buckled, or distorted in recorded memory. The settlement grew around it like a city around a well.

### The Dust Exchange

The commercial heart of Riftmarket, a covered marketplace where Vorrhyn's Dust and Maelstrom-harvested materials are bought, sold, appraised, and occasionally detonated by careless handling. The Exchange operates on a barter-and-coin hybrid system, since currency values fluctuate with Maelstrom surges. A dedicated team of appraisers, most of them Changed with enhanced perceptual abilities, assess the quality and stability of goods. Fraud is dealt with harshly. The Edge has no prisons, but it has a long memory.

### The Wander-Lanes

The residential and commercial districts radiating outward from the Anchor Stone. Named not for their layout, which is grid-like and practical, but for the fact that the outermost lanes occasionally drift. A shop on the edge of the settlement might be three doors down from where it was last week. Residents mark their buildings with personal sigils rather than addresses, and giving directions in Riftmarket is an art form that involves landmarks, probabilities, and a degree of optimism.

### The Guide Hall

Where expeditions into the Shimmer Zone, the Drift, and the Threshold are organized. Licensed guides, most of them Changed or Edgewalkers, offer their services here. The Hall maintains a registry of known hazards, surge predictions, and the current state of each zone. A large board near the entrance lists active expeditions and their expected return dates. Names that stay on the board too long are eventually moved to a smaller board in the back. No one looks at the smaller board unless they have to.

## People and Culture

Riftmarket attracts everyone the wider world does not want. The Changed come because they are accepted here. Scholars come because the Maelstrom is the most significant metaphysical phenomenon in the world and Riftmarket is the closest safe base. Merchants come because Vorrhyn's Dust sells for more per ounce than gold. Criminals come because a town without walls and with a fluid sense of identity is excellent for disappearing.

The culture is pragmatic and warm in equal measure. Riftmarket runs on trust, because in a place where your neighbor might wake up looking different than they did yesterday, trust must be built on something deeper than appearance. People are known by their word, their work, and their willingness to help when the Maelstrom surges. Newcomers are welcomed with a meal and a warning: the Edge changes you. Not always physically. But always.

## Notable Residents

- **[[Tyrell Vane]]**: Senior Edgewalker of the Shattered Edge, based in Riftmarket. He governs with an unsettling prescience that comes from reading probability threads.
- **[[Oren Blackfinger]]**: The most successful Vorrhyn's Dust trader in the settlement. His fingers are permanently stained from the material, and small objects near him occasionally change shape.
- **[[The Mirror]]**: An information broker whose original face is unknown. They are tolerated because their intelligence is reliable, feared because their methods are not.
- **[[Prior Echoes]]**: The philosopher-priest of the [[The Echoists|Echoists]] preaches regularly in the market square, drawing crowds of the curious and the devout.

## Tensions and Hooks

> [!tip] Hooks for Riftmarket
> - A shipment of Empyrean Shards has gone missing between [[Edgewatch]] and the Dust Exchange. The guides who were transporting it returned with no memory of the journey, and two of them have been Changed, they now speak a language no one recognizes.
> - [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] agents are buying Empyrean Shards in large quantities through intermediaries. [[Oren Blackfinger]] has noticed the unusual demand and is quietly looking for someone to investigate.
> - A newcomer arrived claiming to have walked *out* of the Maelstrom, not from its edge, but from its center. She carries an object that should not exist: a perfectly ordinary wooden cup that is absolutely, impossibly stable. It does not change. It cannot be changed. Nothing in the Shattered Edge has ever been that certain.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Anchor Stone is not a natural formation. It is an excised component of [[The Twelve#Orenthas, God of Fate and Knowledge , *"The Loom-Keeper"*|Orenthas]]'s Loom, ejected during the Theomachis and embedded in the landscape. Its stability radiates outward, creating the patch of reliable ground on which Riftmarket was built. The [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] does not know this yet, but [[Lysara Shimmerborn]] is beginning to suspect. If the Stone were removed or destroyed, Riftmarket would be swallowed by the Shimmer Zone within days.
>
> The woman who walked out of the Maelstrom's center is genuine. She has touched the Loom's edge and been *stabilized* by it, the opposite of the usual Maelstrom effect. What she knows, and does not yet understand she knows, could change the course of the Second Silence.
