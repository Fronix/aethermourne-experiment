# The Shattered Edge

> [!quote] Edgewalker saying
> "Reality is a habit. Out here, we've broken the habit. What's left is honesty."

---

## Overview

The Shattered Edge is the scar tissue around a wound in the world. When [[The Twelve#Vorrhyn, God of Chaos and Change , *"The Unbound"*|Vorrhyn]] was annihilated during the Theomachis, the force of his unmaking tore a hole in reality itself, a churning, visible wound called the Maelstrom. Around that wound, reality frayed. Physics loosened its grip. Time became inconsistent. Space forgot its own geometry.

Most people fled. Some stayed.

Those who stayed became the people of the Shattered Edge, a loose confederation of the chaos-touched, the desperate, the curious, and the stubborn, living in the borderlands between [[The Ashen Dominion]] and [[The Verdant Marches]] where tomorrow might not work the same as today. They harvest [[Cosmology and Magic|Vorrhyn's Dust]] from the Maelstrom's periphery, guide expeditions into the distortion zones, and trade in materials that exist nowhere else. They have built a culture around the acceptance that reality is a suggestion, and they have found a strange, surreal beauty in that acceptance.

The Edge is not a nation. It is a shared understanding.

## Geography

The Shattered Edge occupies the ring of unstable territory surrounding the Maelstrom, stretching from the western marches of [[The Ashen Dominion]] to the eastern reaches of [[The Verdant Marches]]. The landscape is divided into four concentric zones radiating outward from the wound.

### The Threshold

The innermost ring, the closest anyone can approach the Maelstrom and return. Here the sky fractures into overlapping colors. Gravity shifts direction between footsteps. Sound arrives before its source. The ground is littered with Maelstrom ejecta, objects and materials expelled from the wound that follow no known physical law, stones that float, water that burns cold, metal that breathes.

No permanent settlement exists in the Threshold. [[Edgewatch]] maintains rotating observation posts along its outer boundary, staffed by Edgewalkers who can perceive the Maelstrom's movements before they manifest physically. Expeditions into the Threshold are short, dangerous, and extraordinarily profitable. The Empyrean Shards found here, crystallized fragments from the spaces where reality is thinnest, are among the most valuable materials in Aethermourne.

### The Shimmer Zone

Beyond the Threshold, reality's distortions become less lethal but no less strange. The air shimmers with visible probability, faint outlines of things that could exist overlapping with things that do. Buildings shift colors. The sky displays different times of day depending on which direction you face. Shadows fall the wrong way, or not at all, or in too many directions at once.

The distortions here are largely aesthetic rather than dangerous, though newcomers find them deeply disorienting. The settlement of [[Shimmer]] thrives in this zone, its people having adapted to the strangeness and turned it into art. Shimmer-glass, a material that captures moments of altered reality, is produced only here.

### The Stable Ring

The widest and most populated zone. Reality here is mostly reliable, with periodic "drift events" where local physics briefly become unpredictable. A pot might boil sideways. A door might open onto a room that wasn't there yesterday. These events are minor and temporary, and the people of the Stable Ring have learned to treat them the way coastal communities treat tides, a nuisance, a rhythm, a fact of life.

[[Riftmarket]] and [[The Last Certainty]] sit in the Stable Ring. Most agriculture, trade, and daily life happens here. The soil is fertile, enriched by trace amounts of Vorrhyn's essence, and crops grow with unusual vigor, though they occasionally grow into shapes the farmer did not plant.

### The Drift

The outermost zone, where the Shattered Edge meets the wider world. Maelstrom ejecta collects here, carried outward by periodic surges, strange objects, altered materials, and occasionally creatures born of chaotic potential. Drift-harvesters scour this zone for valuable materials, and the Edge's economy depends heavily on what the Maelstrom expels.

The Drift is also where the Changed most commonly occur. People passing through, unaware of the Maelstrom's reach, are caught in surge-waves and transformed. Some continue their journey, altered but alive. Others find their way to the Edge's settlements, unable or unwilling to return to a world that now looks at them differently.

## Society

### The Changed

The defining feature of Edge culture is the Changed, people who have been physically, mentally, or spiritually altered by Maelstrom exposure. In the wider world, the Changed are feared, pitied, or reviled. On the Edge, they are family.

Changes range from the subtle to the profound. A woman whose eyes now show two different colors. A man who remembers a childhood he never lived. A child born with fingers that phase through solid objects when she's frightened. A veteran whose left arm shifts between shapes, never settling on one form. These are not curses. They are what happens when reality forgets its rules, and they are survivable. Not comfortable, not painless, but survivable.

Edge culture treats transformation as sacred, an echo of [[The Twelve#Vorrhyn, God of Chaos and Change , *"The Unbound"*|Vorrhyn]]'s gift of change. The Changed are not broken. They are proof that the human spirit can endure what reality cannot. The [[The Echoists|Echoists]] teach that every transformation is a conversation with the god who is no longer there, and that the answer to "what am I now?" is always "something that survived."

### Edgewalker Governance

The [[The Accord of Echoes|Accord of Echoes]] governs through probability, not politics. Edgewalkers are rare individuals who can perceive the faint outlines of possible futures bleeding through from the Maelstrom. When a decision must be made, the Edgewalkers consult the probability threads, examining the likely consequences of each choice across multiple potential timelines.

This is not prophecy. The threads are possibilities, not certainties. They contradict each other. They shift. They lie. An Edgewalker's skill lies not in seeing the future but in reading the weight of probability, which outcomes are more likely, which are more dangerous, which lead to cascading consequences. It is governance by informed uncertainty, and it works better than it has any right to.

### Daily Life

Living on the Edge requires a flexibility of mind that most people never develop. Routines exist but are held loosely. Plans are made in multiples, with contingencies for reality behaving unexpectedly. Children learn to check whether gravity is working before jumping. Merchants price goods with "drift adjustments" accounting for the possibility that their inventory might change overnight.

The people are warm, darkly humorous, and profoundly practical. They help strangers because the Edge is too dangerous for selfishness. They tell stories because stories are the one thing the Maelstrom cannot change. They celebrate transformation because the alternative is despair.

## Current Tensions

1. **The Maelstrom is widening.** [[Lysara Shimmerborn]] at [[The Last Certainty]] has documented a sustained expansion over the past two years. The surges are stronger and more frequent. [[Edgewatch]] has sounded three emergency evacuations in the last season alone.
2. **Dominion containment proposals.** [[The Ashen Dominion]] has dispatched emissaries arguing that the Maelstrom must be contained or sealed. The Edge sees this as an existential threat, not to the Maelstrom, but to themselves. Containment would mean militarization of the region and displacement of the Changed.
3. **Veil Unbound infiltration.** Agents of the [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] have been spotted in [[Riftmarket]], posing as scholars and Dust traders. Their interest in the Maelstrom is unclear to most, but [[Tyrell Vane]] has seen their probability threads, and what he saw frightened him.
4. **The wards at The Last Certainty are failing.** The fortress-monastery's protections against reality distortion have weakened steadily. [[Lysara Shimmerborn]] believes this is connected to the widening, that the Maelstrom is not just expanding physically but intensifying, eroding the principles that make warding possible.
5. **A new kind of Changed.** In the last year, several people have emerged from Maelstrom exposure with changes that do not fit the established patterns. Instead of physical alteration, they carry fragments of *knowledge*, information about places they have never been, languages they have never studied, memories of events that have not happened yet. [[Prior Echoes]] calls them "the Foretouched" and considers them sacred. Others are less certain.

## Factions

- **[[The Accord of Echoes]]**: Governing body of the Shattered Edge, led by Edgewalkers who navigate by probability.
- **[[The Echoists]]**: Philosophical-religious movement honoring [[The Twelve#Vorrhyn, God of Chaos and Change , *"The Unbound"*|Vorrhyn]]'s memory and treating transformation as sacred.
- **[[The Star-Readers]]**: The cross-regional scholars' faction maintains a significant presence at [[The Last Certainty]], studying the Maelstrom's behavior and cataloguing its effects.
- **[[The Ember Guild]]**: Trade operations centered in [[Riftmarket]], dealing in Vorrhyn's Dust and Empyrean Shards. The Guild's interest is profit, and the Edge provides it in abundance.
- **[[The Veil Unbound]]**: [[The Twelve#Serith, Goddess of Shadow and Secrets , *"The Veiled One"*|Serith]]'s agents operate covertly, seeking access to the Maelstrom for purposes the Edge's people do not yet understand.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Maelstrom is widening because something is pulling at it from the other side. [[The Twelve#Serith, Goddess of Shadow and Secrets , *"The Veiled One"*|Serith]], imprisoned but not powerless, has been exerting influence on the wound's far edge. The Maelstrom is not just a scar in reality, it is a *door*, and at its center, the barrier between worlds is thin enough to touch [[The Twelve#Orenthas, God of Fate and Knowledge , *"The Loom-Keeper"*|Orenthas]]'s Loom of Fate directly.
>
> Serith wants access to the Loom. With it, she could rewrite the conditions of her imprisonment, not by breaking free through force, but by altering the threads of fate so that her prison was never sealed. The widening Maelstrom is the first stage: stretching the wound until it becomes a passage.
>
> The "Foretouched" Changed, those who emerge with impossible knowledge, are carrying fate-echoes that have bled through from the Loom. They are not prophets. They are leaks. The knowledge they carry is real, accurate, and extremely dangerous, because every fragment that bleeds through weakens the barrier further.
>
> The [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] agents in [[Riftmarket]] are preparing a ritual at the Maelstrom's edge, one designed to widen the wound enough for Serith's influence to reach the Loom directly. They need Empyrean Shards, large quantities of them, and they are buying aggressively through intermediaries. [[Oren Blackfinger]] has noticed the unusual demand but does not yet understand why.
