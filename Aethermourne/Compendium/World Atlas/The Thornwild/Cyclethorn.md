## Overview

Cyclethorn exists because of a geological accident. A massive granite outcrop, dense enough to resist the saturation of both [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s growth-essence and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s death-pull, rises from the forest floor in the Threshold like a clenched fist. The stone is old, older than the Theomachis, and whatever mineral composition makes it resistant to divine energy has made it the only truly stable ground in the Thornwild.

The settlement that has grown on and around the outcrop is part Marcher camp, part Wastes hold, and part something that belongs to neither tradition. Buildings are wood-and-stone hybrids designed to be abandoned quickly when the Threshold shifts: hinged walls, collapsible roofs, belongings kept in carry-ready packs. The streets smell of herbs and bone-ash, the twin preservatives that slow the Cycle's effects on harvested materials. Market stalls sell cycle-materials alongside dried meat and fungal bread. Children play games that teach them to recognize the signs of Death Tides and Bloom Surges the way children elsewhere learn nursery rhymes.

It is not a beautiful town. It is a functional one, built by people who understood that beauty is a luxury you earn after survival. But there are moments, when the Cycle's phases align and the Threshold widens, when the forest around Cyclethorn blooms in colors that have no names, and even the most hardened Cyclekeepers stop to watch.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The granite outcrop is not naturally resistant to divine energy. Deep beneath it lies a fragment of pre-Theomachis stonework, a foundation from a structure that predates the god-war. [[The Star-Readers]] would give almost anything to study it. [[Yara Thornbloom]] knows the outcrop is unusual but has never investigated its depth. If someone did, they would find carved symbols in a language that predates the current age, and a faint resonance that is neither growth nor death nor Serith's shadow, but something older.

## Layout & Landmarks

**The Perch**, the highest point of the granite outcrop, where [[The Cyclekeepers]]' headquarters occupies a squat stone building reinforced with ironwood beams. [[Yara Thornbloom]] reads the Cycle from the Perch's observation platform, a raised stone circle where the wind carries the scent of both the Bloom and the Wilt. The building's walls are carved with generations of Cycle observations, a record of rhythms and anomalies that constitutes the most detailed study of divine energy interaction in existence.

**The Shift Market**, the main commercial area built on the outcrop's lower slopes. Stalls are mounted on runners so they can be repositioned as the Threshold moves. Cycle-material traders, herbalists, alchemists, and provisioners do brisk business here. [[Garret Boneharvest]] maintains a permanent trading post, one of the few structures built to stay.

**The Binding Ground**, a flat clearing on the outcrop's eastern face where the dual rites are performed. Growth-blessings and death-honorings happen here daily, sometimes simultaneously. The ground is stained green-black from years of offerings. New arrivals to Cyclethorn undergo the Binding, a ceremony where they are ritually introduced to both the growth-pulse and the death-pull, so they can recognize the Cycle's signs.

**The Stray Quarter**, the lower town built off the outcrop's base, where the Cycle's effects are mild but present. This is where newcomers, transient traders, and people not yet committed to the Thornwild stay. Buildings here wilt and regrow slowly, gaining a coat of moss one day, shedding it the next. It is unsettling until you get used to it. Most people either get used to it or leave within a week.

## People & Culture

Cyclethorn's population is roughly split between Marcher-descended and Wastes-descended families, with a growing number of people who identify as neither, simply Thornwild. The cultural fusion is imperfect and sometimes fractious. Marcher traditions of clan loyalty and oral history sit alongside Wastes traditions of communal pragmatism and death-honoring. Arguments about funeral practices, child-rearing, and whether mushrooms should be eaten raw or cooked can escalate with startling speed.

What unites them is the Cycle. Everyone in Cyclethorn has watched something die and come back. Everyone has lost someone to a Death Tide or a Bloom Surge. The shared experience of living in the impossible has created a community that is blunt, practical, dark-humored, and fiercely protective of its own. Newcomers are tested not by their origins but by their willingness to learn the Cycle's rhythms and contribute to the settlement's survival.

### Notable Residents

- **[[Yara Thornbloom]]**, Senior Cyclekeeper. Reads the Cycle from the Perch. She wants to understand the acceleration before it kills everyone she's sworn to protect.
- **[[Kael Duskborne]]**, Ashen Warden of the Thornguard. Runs the settlement's defense. He wants to prove that the Thornwild's way of coexisting with the dead is not madness.
- **Elder Maren**, civilian council leader, a Marcher exile who came to the Thornwild forty years ago with nothing and built a community. She wants Cyclethorn recognized as a legitimate settlement by the wider world.
- **Alchemist Drue**, a former [[The Star-Readers|Star-Reader]] apprentice who abandoned academia for hands-on study. She wants to catalog every cycle-material and understand their properties before someone weaponizes them.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The Threshold is narrowing.** Cyclethorn's buffer zone has shrunk by a third in the past year. If it continues, the town will be caught between unfiltered Bloom and Wilt forces. [[Yara Thornbloom]] is searching for a way to stabilize it.
2. **Star-Reader expedition.** A team from [[The Star-Readers]] has arrived to study the Cycle, bringing instruments, funding, and an attitude that treats the Thornwild's people as research subjects. The civilian council is torn between the resources they offer and the disrespect they bring.
3. **Missing harvesters.** Three cycle-material harvesting teams have vanished in the Bonewood in the past month. The Bonewood is always dangerous, but this is unusual. Something in the deep forest is changing.
4. **Remnant caravan.** A group of [[Remnants Court|Remnants]] from [[Ashenmere]] is approaching, seeking passage to [[Ashbloom]]. Some of Cyclethorn's Wastes-descended residents welcome them. Others, carrying old Vigil prejudices, do not.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The missing harvesters were drawn deeper into the Bonewood by [[The Bloom-Wraith]], which has become more active as the Cycle accelerates. They are not dead, not exactly. They are caught in a transition loop, cycling between life and death too rapidly for their minds to process. If found quickly, they can be pulled out. If not, they will become permanent fixtures of the Bonewood, like the Bloom-Wraith itself.
>
> The Star-Reader expedition is legitimate, but one member is secretly reporting to [[The Ember Guild]], which wants to commercialize Thornwild amber production. If the Guild gets involved, the Thornwild's fragile independence will be threatened.
