## Overview

Rootpyre is alive. Not metaphorically, the way the [[The Verdant Marches|Verdant Marches]] are alive, but literally, visibly, disturbingly. The settlement's structures are grown, not built: walls of woven vine, roofs of interlocking branches, floors of compressed root-mat. When the Cycle's life-phase peaks, the buildings bloom, sprouting flowers, thickening, reaching upward with new growth. When the death-phase sweeps through, they wither, leaves curling brown and grey, wood going pale and brittle. By the next dawn, they are growing again.

The people of Rootpyre wither and bloom with their homes. Not physically, not yet, but spiritually. [[Root-Speaker Fennara]]'s druids practice a form of communion that channels both [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s growth-pulse and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s death-pull simultaneously, a dual attunement that both [[The Greensingers]] and [[The Ashen Vigil]] consider abominable. The Greensingers call it a perversion of [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s gift. The Vigil calls it necromantic dabbling dressed in leaves. Fennara calls it the future.

She may be right. Her druids can do things no Greensinger or Ash-Speaker can: accelerate healing by channeling life through death, calm risen undead by threading growth-energy through their animation, and, most disturbingly, step willingly into the transition state between life and death for brief periods, experiencing both simultaneously. They come back changed. Quieter. More certain. And with eyes that sometimes glow faintly green in one iris and grey in the other.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Fennara's dual-channeling is not heresy. It is the rediscovery of a practice that existed before the Theomachis, when [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]] and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]] were not opposed forces but complementary aspects of a single cycle. The gods' war shattered this understanding along with everything else. Fennara has stumbled onto a fragment of pre-war theology, and if she can develop it further, her druids may hold the key to stabilizing not just the Thornwild but the wider divine ecology of Aethermourne.
>
> The danger is that dual-channeling draws attention. [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] is aware of Rootpyre, and she considers Fennara's work a threat. A force that can harmonize life and death energies is a force that could resist Serith's manipulation of both. Expect interference, subtle at first, direct later.

## Layout & Landmarks

**The Heartroot**, the central tree around which Rootpyre was founded. A massive specimen that cycles faster than anything else in the Bloom, completing a full life-death-rebirth loop every six hours. Fennara's druids meditate at its base, using its accelerated rhythm as a training tool for dual-channeling. The Heartroot's roots extend beneath the entire settlement, and during communion, a druid can feel every structure, every plant, every person standing on them.

**The Circle of Thorns**, the training ground where new druids learn dual-channeling. A ring of thorn-vines that grow and die in synchronized patterns, creating a shifting maze that responds to the channeler's emotional state. Calm produces open paths. Fear produces walls. Anger produces thorns. The Circle teaches control before it teaches power, and the thorns leave scars that serve as reminders.

**The Wilt-Room**, a deliberately maintained pocket of death-energy within the settlement, a small stone chamber where [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s pull is allowed to dominate. Druids meditate here to practice attunement to death-energy without leaving the settlement's relative safety. The room is cold, still, and smells of clean bone. It is not comfortable. It is not meant to be.

**The Offering Pyre**, a fire pit where the dead are cremated in the Wastes tradition, an acknowledgment of the dual heritage Rootpyre's people carry. Cremation here is performed with green wood that blooms during the burning, wreathing the flames in flowers. It is, despite everything, beautiful.

## People & Culture

Rootpyre's population is almost entirely Marcher-descended, exiles and outcasts who followed Fennara into the Thornwild because her vision resonated with something they could not name. They retain Marcher clan customs, oral traditions, and the fierce independence that defines the [[The Verdant Marches|Marches]], but they have added something the Marches do not have: a willingness to look at death and call it partner rather than enemy.

Daily life follows the Cycle's rhythm in a way that goes beyond practicality into spiritual practice. Meals are prepared during the life-phase and consumed during the death-phase, a ritual acknowledgment that life sustains itself through death. Sleep is taken during transitions, the brief periods of balance that Fennara calls "the breath between." Children are born whenever they arrive, but their naming ceremonies wait for the moment of transition, when both forces are present, and the child is introduced to both.

The druids' dual-channeling has physical effects over time. Long-term practitioners develop heterochromatic eyes, one green, one grey. Their hair grows in streaks of living color and bone-white. They age oddly, sometimes looking older than their years, sometimes younger. These marks are worn with pride. In Rootpyre, they are proof of commitment.

### Notable Residents

- **[[Root-Speaker Fennara]]**, founder and spiritual leader. A former Greensinger who was declared anathema for her practices. She wants to prove that dual-channeling is not heresy but necessity, and that the Thornwild is a preview of the world's future.
- **Ashspeaker Torin**, Rootpyre's funeral officiant, a Wastes exile who joined Fennara's circle after witnessing his first dual-rite. He wants to honor the dead without fearing them, and he believes Rootpyre's way is closer to what [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]] intended than the Vigil's eternal war.
- **Seedling Vera**, Fennara's most promising student, a young woman whose dual-channeling ability exceeds her teacher's at the same age. She wants to enter the Bonewood and commune with [[The Bloom-Wraith]]. Fennara has forbidden it. Vera is considering going anyway.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **Greensinger emissary.** [[The Greensingers]] have sent an envoy to demand that Fennara cease dual-channeling and return to the Marches to face judgment. Fennara has refused. The envoy is still in Rootpyre, watching, reporting, and growing increasingly uncomfortable with how much sense Fennara's practices make.
2. **The Heartroot is destabilizing.** Its cycle has accelerated from six hours to four, and the transitions are growing violent, branches snapping, roots heaving. If it destabilizes completely, Rootpyre loses its training anchor and its structural foundation simultaneously.
3. **Vera's ambition.** Seedling Vera is planning an unauthorized expedition to the Bonewood to find [[The Bloom-Wraith]]. If she goes alone, she will likely die, or become something that is not quite death. If she succeeds, she could bring back knowledge that changes everything.
4. **A Bloom Surge is building.** The Cycle's life-phase has been intensifying in the Bloom, and Rootpyre's druids can feel a surge approaching that will be stronger than anything they have experienced. The settlement's living structures may grow out of control, trapping residents inside walls of thorn and vine.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Greensinger envoy, a Branch-Speaker named Lael, is genuinely conflicted. They came expecting to find corruption and instead found something that feels true. If the PCs interact with Lael, they can potentially broker a dialogue between Rootpyre and the Greensingers that could reshape both traditions. Alternatively, if Lael reports back that Fennara's practices work, the Greensingers' leadership will face an internal crisis about the nature of their communion with [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]].
>
> Vera's expedition to the Bonewood is a potential PC quest. If accompanied by capable allies, she might survive the Bonewood and reach the Bloom-Wraith. What she learns there could provide the key to understanding the Cycle's acceleration, but the Bloom-Wraith does not communicate in ways that mortal minds process easily. Expect fragmented visions, not clear answers.
