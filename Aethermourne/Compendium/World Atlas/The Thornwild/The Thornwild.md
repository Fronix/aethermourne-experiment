# The Thornwild

> [!quote]
> "I watched a stag die at noon. By sunset it stood again, antlers wrapped in flowering vine, and it walked into the forest to feed. I don't know what it feeds on now. I don't want to know."
> , Marcher scout's report, circa 2178 AS

---

## Overview

The Thornwild is the collision zone. Northwest of the continent, where the [[The Verdant Marches|Verdant Marches]]' western frontier bleeds into the [[The Pale Wastes|Pale Wastes]]' western boundary, two dead gods' legacies meet and refuse to yield. [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s growth-essence saturates the soil from the south and west. [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s necromantic chill seeps in from the north and east. The result is not a border. It is an ecosystem that has gone insane.

Everything here cycles. A tree sprouts at dawn, reaches full height by noon, withers by dusk, and collapses into bone-white deadwood by midnight, only to sprout again the following morning. Animals killed by predators rise within hours, hunt their former killers, die again, and fertilize the soil for new growth. Flowers bloom from ribcages. Roots crack through skulls and drink the marrow. The Thornwild is the most beautiful and the most terrifying wilderness on the continent, and it does not care which of those things you notice first.

Neither the Marcher clans nor the Wastes Holds claim this land. The people who live here are exiles, naturalists, heretics, and survivors who have learned to read the rhythms of the Cycle and build their lives in the narrow margins where death and life briefly balance. They are governed, loosely, by [[The Cyclekeepers]], a hybrid order of druids and death-priests who fuse [[The Greensingers|Greensinger]] practice with [[The Ashen Vigil|Vigil]] funeral rites. It is a strange place. The people who stay say it is the most honest place in Aethermourne.

## Geography

### The Bloom

The southwestern reaches of the Thornwild, where [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s influence dominates. Growth here is aggressive, relentless, and beautiful in the way a wildfire is beautiful. Vines grow fast enough to watch. Flowers open and close in hours. The canopy thickens and thins with the Cycle's rhythm, sometimes cathedral-tall and dripping with light, sometimes so dense overhead that the forest floor goes dark and suffocating.

The danger is not the death-phase here, it is the life-phase. Bloom Surges send growth spiraling out of control: roots that crack stone, pollen clouds thick enough to choke, vines that reach for body heat and wrap tight. The Marcher exile community of [[Rootpyre]] has learned to ride these surges rather than resist them, and their heretical druids channel the Bloom's fury with a skill that both impresses and horrifies the [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]].

### The Wilt

The northeastern reaches, where [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s necromantic essence holds sway. The forest here is skeletal: pale trees with bark like parchment, leafless branches reaching upward like fingers, bone-white deadwood carpeting the ground. But even here, the Cycle persists. Death Tides raise the fallen, and the undead ecology that results is hauntingly ordered, predators and prey locked in a hunt that neither side can permanently win.

The Pale Wastes exile community of [[Ashbloom]] has settled deep in the Wilt, where the cycling dead are a constant presence. [[Remnants Court|Remnants]] who make the journey here discover something extraordinary: the Cycle's life-phase briefly restores their living sensations. Warmth. Hunger. The feeling of breath in lungs that no longer need it. It never lasts. They come back anyway.

### The Threshold

The narrow band between Bloom and Wilt where both forces are roughly balanced. This is where the Thornwild's largest settlement, [[Cyclethorn]], stands on a rocky outcrop that neither god's essence fully penetrates. The Threshold shifts, sometimes widening to a mile across, sometimes narrowing to a few hundred yards. [[The Cyclekeepers]] monitor its movements obsessively because when it narrows, the settlements in the Bloom and the Wilt lose their buffer zone, and the Cycle accelerates dangerously.

The Threshold is also where the most valuable cycle-materials form: substances that exist only in the transition moment between life and death, crystallized in the narrow band where both forces are equal. Alchemists across the continent pay extraordinary prices for Thornwild amber, a fusion material that combines properties of [[Cosmology and Magic#Thyrea's Amber|Thyrea's Amber]] and [[Cosmology and Magic#Morrhaelite|Morrhaelite]].

### The Bonewood

The deep interior, where the Cycle is fastest and most violent. Trees sprout, grow, die, rise, and collapse within hours. Animals exist in a blur of hunting, dying, rising, and hunting again. The air smells of flowers and rot simultaneously, sweet and foul, intoxicating and nauseating. No permanent settlements exist here. Even [[The Cyclekeepers]] enter only when necessary, and never alone.

Something lives in the Bonewood's heart. [[The Bloom-Wraith]], a figure that exists permanently in the transition state, neither alive nor dead, neither plant nor animal. It wanders the deepest groves and sometimes speaks in fragments that reference both [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]] and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]. [[The Cyclekeepers]] consider it sacred. Everyone else considers it terrifying.

## Society

The Thornwild's people come from two traditions that despise each other everywhere else in the world. Marcher exiles bring clan bonds, oral tradition, and primal communion. Wastes exiles bring Hold pragmatism, funerary discipline, and the grim humor of people who have buried too many friends. In the Thornwild, these traditions have fused into something new.

**The Cycle shapes everything.** Meals are cooked and eaten quickly, before ingredients begin to wilt or twitch. Buildings are constructed to be abandoned and reoccupied as the Cycle's phases shift. Children learn to read the Cycle's signs before they learn to read words: the color of the sky, the sound of the wind, the smell that precedes a Death Tide or a Bloom Surge.

**[[The Cyclekeepers]]** guide the communities through the Cycle's rhythms, calling warnings, leading evacuations, performing the dual rites that honor both growth and passage. They are part [[The Greensingers|Greensinger]], part [[The Ashen Vigil|Vigil]] Ash-Speaker, and wholly something that exists nowhere else.

**Death is not feared here. It is expected.** The Thornwild's people have watched death feed life feed death so many times that the boundary between them has lost its horror. This is either profound wisdom or profound damage, depending on whom you ask.

## Current Tensions

1. **The Cycle is accelerating.** Transition periods are shortening. Death Tides and Bloom Surges are growing more frequent and more violent. [[Yara Thornbloom]] believes both divine legacies are destabilizing as the Second Silence deepens, but she cannot determine why.
2. **Remnant pilgrimages.** Word of [[Ashbloom]]'s Cycle-restoration has reached [[Ashenmere]]. [[Remnants Court|Remnants]] are making the dangerous journey north, and not all of them survive the trip. The [[Remnants Court]] is debating whether to officially endorse the pilgrimage or forbid it.
3. **Greensinger condemnation.** [[The Greensingers]] view [[Rootpyre]]'s dual-channeling as heresy, a corruption of [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s gift. [[Root-Speaker Fennara]] has been declared anathema. Tensions between Rootpyre and Marcher border communities are rising.
4. **Vigil desertion concerns.** [[The Ashen Vigil]] considers [[Kael Duskborne]] a deserter and his Thornguard an unauthorized militia. Commander [[Thessa Ironveil]] has more pressing concerns, but hawks within the Vigil want him dragged back in chains.
5. **Outside interest.** [[The Star-Readers]] have sent researchers to study the Cycle. [[The Ember Guild]] smells profit in Thornwild amber. The Thornwild's isolation, its greatest protection, is ending.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Cycle is accelerating because [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s growing influence is destabilizing both [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s divine legacies simultaneously. The corruption spreading through the Verdant Marches' root system and the organization of the Hollowdeep's undead are both symptoms of the same cause, and the Thornwild, sitting at their intersection, is feeling the effects first.
>
> If the Cycle breaks, one of two catastrophes occurs. If [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s influence overwhelms [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s, the Thornwild becomes a Bloom Zone: unstoppable, aggressive, predatory growth that will spread northwest into the Wastes and south into the Marches. If [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s influence overwhelms [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s, the Thornwild becomes a Death Zone: a second Hollowdeep, an engine of undead that pours into both neighboring regions.
>
> [[The Bloom-Wraith]] is the key. It exists in permanent transition, the living proof that balance is possible. If the PCs can understand what it is and how it stabilized, they may find a way to stabilize the Cycle itself. [[Yara Thornbloom]] suspects this but cannot get close enough to the Bloom-Wraith to confirm it.
>
> The Thornwild is, in miniature, what the entire world will become if the Second Silence continues: a place where divine legacies war with each other while mortals scramble to survive in the margins. [[Root-Speaker Fennara]] is right about this, even if her methods are heretical.

## Key Factions

- **[[The Cyclekeepers]]**, dual-tradition order guiding the Thornwild's communities through the Cycle
- **The Thornguard**, [[Kael Duskborne]]'s volunteer militia, drawn from both Marcher and Wastes exiles
- **Rootpyre's Circle**, [[Root-Speaker Fennara]]'s heretical druids who channel both life and death energy
- **The Harvesters**, cycle-material traders centered at [[Bone Garden]], led by [[Garret Boneharvest]]
