## Overview

Ashmark sits on a rocky headland on the Veilshore's southern coast, three days' walk from [[Tidecross]], isolated by design. This is [[The Ashen Dominion]]'s foothold on [[The Unwritten Lands]], a missionary settlement built to bring the word of [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]] to a continent that has never heard it and does not want to.

The settlement is conspicuously orderly. Where [[Tidecross]] sprawls in chaotic improvisation, Ashmark is planned: straight roads, uniform buildings, a central chapel aligned precisely east-west in accordance with Dominion architectural tradition. The white-painted walls and geometric mandala banners stand out against the Veilshore's dark stone and black sand like a declaration of intent. The Dominion has come here to stay.

To the missionaries, Ashmark is a beacon. A proof that [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen's]] order can take root even in soil untouched by divine authority. To the [[The Oldborn Kingdoms|Oldborn]], it is an insult, a dead god's symbol planted on land that predates gods. To the other outlander settlements, it is a liability, a provocation that risks escalating the already fragile peace between continents.

The settlement is growing. New missionaries arrive with each supply ship. The chapel is expanding. The fields, cultivated with Ashite-enhanced tools brought from the Dominion, produce yields that outstrip anything the other settlements can manage. The Order works. It always works. That is what makes it dangerous.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Ashmark is a theological time bomb. The Dominion missionaries genuinely believe they are helping, bringing order to chaos, structure to wildness, salvation to the unenlightened. They cannot conceive that their presence is destructive.
>
> But Ashite, Solvaen's crystallized blood, reacts violently with Primordial energy. The settlement's foundation is literally poisoning the local Primordial substrate. Plants within a mile of Ashmark are dying. The black sand has turned grey. The Oldborn's Primordial pacts in the area are failing. The missionaries do not notice because the effects look like "civilization improving the wilderness." They are wrong.
>
> This is a slow-motion disaster. If Ashmark continues to expand, it will create a dead zone in the Primordial substrate, a wound that will not heal. The Oldborn know this. They have tried to explain. The missionaries do not understand, or do not believe, that their god's remains could be harmful.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Chapel of Enduring Order

The settlement's crown. A stone chapel built from imported granite (the Dominion refused to build with local volcanic stone, which they consider "spiritually contaminated"). The interior is pristine: geometric mosaics on the floor, an altar of polished Ashite, windows of clear glass that fill the space with unfiltered light. Services are held at dawn and dusk.

The chapel radiates Ashite energy in a subtle field. Within its walls, thought is clearer. Doubt recedes. Conviction strengthens. The missionaries find this comforting, proof that Solvaen's presence extends even here. In truth, the Ashite is suppressing local Primordial resonance, creating a bubble of imposed order in a substrate that was never meant to hold it. The effect is widening.

### The Mission Fields

Terraced farmland on the headland's slopes, cultivated with Ashite-enhanced tools and seeds. The yields are extraordinary, three harvests where [[Tidecross]] manages one. The missionaries cite this as proof of divine blessing. The soil, saturated with trace Ashite from the tools, is actually undergoing forced restructuring, Solvaen's order overwriting the Primordial substrate's natural patterns.

The fields are beautiful. Orderly rows. Uniform crops. No weeds, no pests, no variation. The Oldborn observers who have seen the fields describe them as "horrifying."

### The Prior's Residence

A modest stone house adjacent to the chapel, home to [[Prior Maeven Ashcroft]], the settlement's leader. The residence doubles as Ashmark's administrative center, a meeting room where the Prior receives visitors, mediates disputes, and plans the settlement's expansion. A map on the wall shows proposed mission sites across the Veilshore. There are seven. The Oldborn have seen this map. They are not pleased.

### The Waystation

A fortified building at the settlement's edge, where supplies from [[The Ashen Dominion]] are stored and distributed. The Waystation also serves as Ashmark's only accommodation for outsiders, four bare rooms with cots and a shared hearth. Non-Dominion visitors are welcome but observed. The missionaries are friendly. They are also vigilant.

## People & Culture

Ashmark's population is almost entirely [[The Ashen Dominion|Ashen Dominion]] faithful: priests, lay workers, farmers, and a small contingent of Calculist mages who maintain the Ashite tools. Daily life follows the Dominion's liturgical calendar, prayers at dawn and dusk, communal meals, structured work shifts, and evening study of Solvaen's teachings.

The missionaries are earnest, kind, and profoundly certain. They do not doubt their mission. They do not question whether their presence is welcome. They have been sent by the Dominion's highest authorities to bring order to a chaotic land, and they will succeed because order always succeeds. This certainty makes them both admirable and infuriating.

Cultural exchange with the Oldborn is minimal and fraught. The missionaries offer food, medicine, and education. The Oldborn see these gifts as contamination vectors. Every interaction reinforces both sides' worst assumptions about the other.

### Notable Residents

**[[Prior Maeven Ashcroft]]**, leader of the Ashmark mission. A middle-aged woman with iron-grey hair and an unshakable calm. She is intelligent, compassionate, and utterly convinced that Solvaen's order is the answer to the Unwritten Lands' instability. She genuinely cares about the Oldborn and wants to help them. She cannot understand why they reject her help. Her blind spot is her faith's blind spot: the assumption that divine order is universally beneficial.

**[[Calculist Brenneth]]**, the mission's senior mage. Young, nervous, and increasingly troubled. Brenneth has noticed the environmental changes around Ashmark, the dying plants, the greying sand, the failing Primordial resonance. He has raised concerns with Prior Ashcroft. She has told him to pray harder. He is not satisfied with this answer.

**[[Sister Vael]]**, the mission's healer and the only Ashmark resident who speaks fluent Oldborn. She learned the language from an Oldborn trader in [[Tidecross]] and has developed genuine friendships across the cultural divide. She is the settlement's best hope for diplomacy, and the Oldborn's best source of information about the missionaries' plans.

**[[Warden Torr]]**, head of Ashmark's small security force. A former Dominion soldier who volunteered for the mission after losing his family to the Second Silence's effects. He is grief-driven, protective, and increasingly paranoid about Oldborn sabotage. He has fortified the settlement beyond what the Prior approves and is stockpiling weapons.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The Grey Spread.** The black sand around Ashmark is turning grey in an expanding circle. Plants are dying. The Primordial substrate is going silent. Calculist Brenneth believes Ashite is the cause but cannot prove it without admitting that divine materials can be harmful, which is heresy. The PCs must investigate and navigate the theological minefield.

2. **The Oldborn Ultimatum.** An Oldborn delegation arrives with a demand: dismantle the Chapel of Enduring Order within thirty days or face consequences. They present evidence of Primordial substrate damage. Prior Ashcroft refuses, calling it "pagan manipulation." The PCs must mediate before the situation escalates to violence.

3. **The Convert.** A young Oldborn has begun attending services at the chapel, fascinated by Solvaen's teachings. The Oldborn demand their return. The missionaries claim the right of sanctuary. The convert does not want to leave but does not fully understand what conversion means, what it costs to invite a dead god's order into a Primordial-attuned body.

4. **Warden Torr's War.** Torr has captured an Oldborn scout near the settlement's perimeter and is holding them in the Waystation. He claims the scout was conducting sabotage. The Oldborn claim the scout was conducting routine observation. The truth is more complicated: the scout was attempting to place a Primordial ward around the settlement to contain the Ashite contamination. Torr's paranoia may have doomed the containment effort.

5. **The Supply Ship.** The next Dominion supply ship carries more than food and tools. It carries a consecrated Ashite monolith, a massive crystalline structure intended to serve as Ashmark's permanent divine anchor. If planted, it will create an Ashite field strong enough to suppress Primordial activity within a mile. The Oldborn's Pact-Weavers will feel it the moment it activates. This is the spark that could ignite war.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role:** Ashmark is a microcosm of the known continent's relationship with divine power, well-intentioned, destructive, and incapable of seeing its own harm. It forces the PCs to confront whether the Dominion's worldview can survive contact with a reality that predates it.
>
> **Key Secret:** Prior Ashcroft has received private instructions from the Dominion's inner council. The mission is not purely evangelical. The Dominion wants to determine whether Ashite can be used to "tame" the Primordial substrate, to extend Solvaen's order across the Unwritten Lands as a bulwark against the Second Silence. Ashmark is a test site. If it works, a full colonization effort follows.
>
> **Arc Trajectory:** If the PCs help Brenneth prove the Ashite contamination, Ashcroft faces a crisis of faith that can lead to genuine reform or devastating denial. If they do not intervene, the Ashite monolith arrives, the Oldborn retaliate, and Ashmark burns.
