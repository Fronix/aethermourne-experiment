## Overview

Keth'vaal is the oldest city in the world. Not the oldest ruin, not the oldest foundation, the oldest *living* city, inhabited without interruption for over four thousand years, its traditions unbroken, its records complete (barring the pre-Covenant gap that no Oldborn discusses), its identity intact across more centuries than the known continent has recorded history.

The city rises from the center of the Obsidian Plateau, built in concentric terraces around a Primordial Anchor so massive it dwarfs every other known crystal. The **Great Anchor** stands two hundred feet tall, a faceted spire of deep violet crystal that pulses with light visible from twenty miles in every direction. It is the heart of the [[The Oldborn Kingdoms|Oldborn Kingdoms]]' magical infrastructure, the nexus from which Primordial energy flows outward through the Rootways to every Anchor in the federation. If the Great Anchor fails, every Oldborn city fails with it.

Keth'vaal is built from the same black volcanic stone as every Oldborn settlement, but here the stone has been worked with millennia of Primordial craftsmanship into something that transcends architecture. Buildings flow like liquid frozen mid-pour. Bridges arc between terraces in curves that defy engineering. Walls are thin as paper but harder than steel, reinforced by pacts that trade structural weakness for aesthetic perfection. The city is beautiful in a way that makes outlanders uneasy, beauty without a god's hand in it, beauty achieved through negotiation with forces that do not care about beauty, which makes the achievement more remarkable.

The city is also tense. The Circle of Speakers has been in emergency session for months, debating the outlander crisis. The three factions, Sealed Gate, Open Hand, and Ash Reclaimers, have turned Keth'vaal's normally civil streets into arenas of political maneuvering. Arguments spill from the Speaker's Hall into taverns, markets, and homes. Families are divided. Old friendships fracture along factional lines. Keth'vaal has survived four thousand years without a civil war. That record is in danger.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Keth'vaal is the political heart of the Unwritten Lands arc. Every major decision about the Oldborn's response to the outlander presence flows through this city. The PCs' actions here determine whether they have allies or enemies for the rest of the campaign.
>
> The Great Anchor is also the single most important magical structure on the continent. If it falls, Oldborn civilization collapses. The Second Silence is not directly threatening it yet, but the resonance from [[The Loom's Edge]]'s awakening threads is causing micro-fractures that the Pact-Weavers cannot explain. The clock is ticking.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Great Anchor

The city's foundation and reason for existence. The violet crystal dominates the skyline, visible from every district, casting a faint purple hue over the surrounding terraces at dusk. The Anchor's stabilization field extends half a mile in every direction, making Keth'vaal the most geologically stable location in [[The Unwritten Lands]].

The base of the Great Anchor is sacred ground, accessible only to Pact-Weavers during renewal ceremonies. The stone around it is worn smooth by four thousand years of ritual circuits. Offerings are left at the base: crystalline resonators, hand-written contracts, and small carvings representing requests made of the Primordial substrate. The offerings are always gone by morning.

### The Speaker's Hall

A massive circular amphitheater carved into the second terrace, open to the sky, with seating for three thousand observers. The floor is polished obsidian, mirror-smooth, reflecting the Great Anchor's light. This is where the Circle of Speakers convenes, where law is debated, where the Covenant of Ash is interpreted and enforced.

During the current crisis, the Hall is in near-continuous session. Speakers argue. Observers shout. Occasionally, formal challenges are issued, ritual debates that can last days, with the loser's position stricken from consideration. The atmosphere is electric, scholarly passion transformed by existential fear into something volatile.

### The Deep Library

Keth'vaal's greatest treasure: a library that extends seven levels underground, carved into the bedrock beneath the city. The collection spans four millennia, every text, treaty, pact-record, and scholarly work the Oldborn have produced. The library is maintained by a dedicated order of archivists who have traded their names for perfect memory, a Primordial pact that ensures nothing in the collection is ever lost or misfiled.

The Deep Library is open to all Oldborn citizens. It is closed to outlanders. This is the knowledge the PCs most desperately need, pre-Pattern history, Primordial theory, maps to [[The Loom's Edge]], and it is behind a door that will not open for them without the Circle's permission.

### The Rootway Gate

The primary entrance to the underground tunnel network that connects all Oldborn cities. The Gate is a massive stone archway inscribed with pact-glyphs, guarded at all times by Pact-Weavers who verify the identity and intent of every traveler. The Rootways are fast, efficient, and dangerous in their deeper sections. Most Oldborn prefer them to overland travel. Outlanders are forbidden from entering.

### The Ash Market

Keth'vaal's commercial district, occupying the fourth terrace. Named for the volcanic ash that once covered this level before the city expanded, the market trades in Primordial-crafted goods: resonance crystals, pact-forged tools, temporal textiles (cloth woven with minor time-dilation pacts that slows wear to near-zero), and food grown in Anchor-stabilized fields. Currency is contractual, debts recorded and enforced through minor pacts. Physical money does not exist.

The market has a section recently and reluctantly opened to outlander trade. The goods offered there are inferior to what Oldborn citizens can purchase, a deliberate choice. The Oldborn are willing to trade but not to share their best work with people they do not trust.

## People & Culture

Keth'vaal's culture is defined by debate. The Oldborn value argument, rigorous, structured, passionate argument, as the highest form of civic participation. Children are taught rhetoric before they are taught to read. Disagreement is not rude; it is *expected*. Silence in the face of a flawed idea is the true discourtesy.

This makes Keth'vaal exhausting for outsiders. Every interaction is a potential debate. Every statement is examined for logical coherence. The Oldborn do not make small talk. They make *positions*.

Daily life revolves around the Great Anchor's rhythms. The crystal's pulse quickens at dawn and slows at dusk, and the city's schedule follows suit. Work during the active hours. Study during the quiet. Meals are communal, eaten in long halls where conversation, inevitably, turns to politics.

### Notable Residents

**[[Speaker Kaelthen]]**, leader of the Sealed Gate faction. A veteran Pact-Weaver in his seventh decade, scarred by a pact-failure that cost him his left hand and two students. He is fierce, brilliant, and terrified. He has seen what divine contamination does to Primordial structures and will not allow it to reach Keth'vaal. He is not wrong to be afraid. He may be wrong about how to respond.

**[[Speaker Yavenne]]**, leader of the Open Hand faction. The youngest Speaker in the Circle's history, elected at twenty-eight on a platform of engagement over isolation. She is charismatic, idealistic, and more politically savvy than her opponents realize. She has been secretly corresponding with outlander scholars in [[Tidecross]], exchanging knowledge through intermediaries. If the Sealed Gate discovers this, her career, and possibly her life, ends.

**[[Speaker Dravos]]**, leader of the Ash Reclaimers. A former military commander whose ambition is a blunt instrument. He sees the known continent's collapse as opportunity, a chance for the Oldborn to expand their influence, to prove that mortal civilization without gods is superior. His rhetoric is popular among younger Oldborn. His plan is reckless.

**[[Archivist Vel]]**, keeper of the Deep Library's restricted collection. Vel has no name, having traded it decades ago for perfect recall. They are referred to by title only. They know every text in the Library, every secret in the archive, every piece of pre-Pattern history that survived the Covenant's erasure. They will share none of it without the Circle's authorization. But they can be persuaded, for the right price, and the right reason.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The Circle's Verdict.** The Speaker debate reaches its climax during the PCs' visit. All three factions present their final arguments. The PCs are invited to address the Circle, an unprecedented honor for outlanders. What they say, and how they say it, may determine the Oldborn's course: isolation, engagement, or expansion.

2. **The Library Heist.** The PCs need information from the Deep Library that the Circle will not share. Archivist Vel implies, without stating, that the restricted collection contains maps to [[The Loom's Edge]]'s deepest reaches and records of what Orenthas buried there. Getting access requires either Circle authorization (difficult), Vel's personal cooperation (costly), or theft (dangerous and relationship-destroying).

3. **The Rootway Incursion.** Something has entered the Rootways from below, from the deep tunnels where reality thins and the Gyre bleeds through. Three travelers have vanished. The Pact-Weavers cannot identify the threat. The PCs, as outsiders unbound by Primordial pacts, may be uniquely suited to investigate what the Oldborn's magic cannot perceive.

4. **The Great Anchor's Fracture.** During a pact-renewal ceremony, the Great Anchor cracks audibly. The stabilization field flickers. Panic spreads through the city. The Pact-Weavers are summoned. They cannot identify the cause. The PCs, if they have encountered [[The Loom's Edge]]'s temporal distortions, may recognize the resonance pattern. The Loom's awakening threads are reaching even here.

5. **Yavenne's Secret.** Speaker Yavenne's correspondence with outlander scholars is discovered. She faces charges of treason. She contacts the PCs in desperation, asking them to testify before the Circle that engagement is necessary, that the Second Silence threatens both continents, and that isolation means mutual destruction. Her life hangs on their testimony.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role:** Keth'vaal is where the PCs earn or lose access to Oldborn knowledge, Primordial magic, and the path to the Loom's Edge. It is the political hub that gates progression into the deeper Unwritten Lands content.
>
> **Key Secret:** The Covenant of Ash's pre-history erasure was not voluntary. The first Pact-Weavers traded the Oldborn's collective memory of their origins to [[The Primordials#Ur-Ghael|Ur-Ghael]] in exchange for the Great Anchor's creation. What was erased: the Oldborn were not always independent. Before the Covenant, they worshipped something, not the Twelve, but something older, something in the Gyre. The Covenant was an act of liberation, cutting ties with an entity that demanded worship. The entity is still there. It still remembers. And with the Second Silence weakening all constraints, it is beginning to reach out.
>
> **Arc Trajectory:** Keth'vaal begins as an obstacle (the Oldborn will not cooperate) and becomes an ally (the shared threat demands cooperation). The speed of this transition depends entirely on the PCs' diplomatic skill and willingness to engage with Oldborn values on Oldborn terms.
