## Overview

Skeinhold perches on a narrow mountain shelf at the southern border of [[The Loom's Edge]], the last place where time still flows in one direction. Beyond the settlement's boundary markers, carved obsidian pillars inscribed with temporal anchoring glyphs, the world becomes unreliable. Shadows move wrong. Echoes arrive before sounds. Snow falls upward. Skeinhold is the threshold, the line between the possible and the paradoxical, and its inhabitants have dedicated their lives to standing on that line without falling.

The settlement is small by necessity. The Threadwalker discipline that allows survival near the Loom's distortions requires intense mental focus, and too many minds in one place create "temporal noise," overlapping intentions that confuse the already unstable local causality. Ninety residents is the tested maximum. The one time the population exceeded 120, three Threadwalkers were displaced into the past and never recovered.

Skeinhold's architecture reflects its philosophy: sparse, functional, deliberately impermanent. Buildings are constructed from local stone but designed to be dismantled and rebuilt quickly. The Threadwalkers do not build to last. They build to adapt. When the Loom's distortions shift (and they always shift), the settlement reconfigures. Walls that faced the distortion zone are torn down. New walls rise. The settlement has never occupied the same footprint for more than two consecutive years.

The view from Skeinhold is extraordinary and terrible. To the north, the Threadstone Passes glow with silver thread-veins, pulsing like arteries. Beyond them, the Mirror Valleys shimmer with reflected time, their walls showing pasts and futures simultaneously. And far in the distance, visible on clear days as a vertical tear of silver light against the mountain sky, the Loom Scar marks the heart of the forbidden interior.

The Threadwalkers watch it constantly. They are waiting for it to move.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Skeinhold is the staging ground for any expedition into the Loom's Edge's deeper regions. The Threadwalkers are the only guides who can navigate temporal distortion, and they will not take anyone deeper than the Threadstone Passes without extraordinary cause.
>
> The PCs represent extraordinary cause. The Threadwalkers have been waiting for outlanders, people whose timelines are not entangled with the Oldborn's Primordial pacts, who might be able to approach the Loom Scar without being swallowed by its resonance. They will not admit this eagerness readily. They will test the PCs first.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Anchor Line

A row of carved obsidian pillars marking Skeinhold's northern boundary, the edge beyond which temporal distortion begins in earnest. Each pillar is inscribed with a personal anchor-glyph by the Threadwalker who maintains it, a fixed reference point in their personal timeline that they project outward as a stabilizing ward. The pillars hum faintly. The sound is different for every listener, each person hears their own most stable memory reflected back at them.

Walking past the Anchor Line without Threadwalker discipline is not immediately fatal, but it is disorienting. Visitors report seeing themselves from other angles, hearing conversations that have not happened yet, and feeling a pulling sensation toward the north, toward the Skein, toward the threads. The Threadwalkers call this the **Loom's Hunger**, the excised patterns reaching for new material to weave into their structure.

### The Hall of Returns

The settlement's central building, where Threadwalkers debrief after expeditions into the distortion zone. The Hall's interior walls are covered in dense notation, observations from every expedition conducted in Skeinhold's history. Temporal phenomena, Echo sightings, displacement events, thread-vein movements, all recorded in meticulous detail.

The Hall also serves as the settlement's only social space. Meals are taken here. Stories are shared. The Threadwalkers are not monks, despite appearances. They laugh, argue, and gossip. But the laughter has an edge. Everyone in this room has seen their own future reflected in a cliff face. Some have seen their own death. The humor here is the humor of people who know too much.

### The Meditation Terrace

An open stone platform on the settlement's eastern edge, overlooking a valley that still behaves normally. Threadwalkers use the Terrace for their anchoring practice, the mental discipline that keeps them coherent in the distortion zone. The practice involves fixing one's awareness on a single moment in personal history, the anchor-moment, and holding it with absolute clarity while the surrounding reality offers alternatives.

Visitors are invited to use the Terrace. The Threadwalkers teach the basics of anchoring to anyone willing to learn, not from generosity, but from pragmatism. An unanchored mind near the distortion zone is a liability. Better to give visitors the tools to resist the Loom's pull than to rescue them when they wander past the Anchor Line.

### The Echo Garden

A clearing south of the settlement where Echoes appear with unusual frequency. Not the deep Echoes of the Mirror Valleys, but minor ones: brief temporal impressions of people and events, flickering in and out like candle flames. The Threadwalkers study them, cataloguing patterns, trying to determine whether the Echoes represent actual futures or merely possibilities.

The Echo Garden is the safest place to encounter temporal phenomena. The impressions are faint, non-interactive, and dissolve within minutes. Threadwalker initiates begin their training here, learning to observe without engaging, to witness without being drawn in. The first lesson: an Echo cannot hurt you. The second lesson: what an Echo *shows* you can.

### The Thread Vault

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Beneath the Hall of Returns, accessible through a concealed passage, the Threadwalkers maintain a vault containing dormant patterns from the excised fate-threads that have manifested as tangible objects. Silver filaments, each no longer than a forearm, stored in lead-lined cases that dampen their temporal resonance.
>
> There are seventeen dormant patterns in the vault. Each shows a different vision when touched: a future that was excised, a path that Orenthas forbade. Some show beautiful worlds. Some show horrors. All show certainty, futures without doubt, without ambiguity, without choice.
>
> The Threadwalkers study these dormant patterns carefully, never touching them bare-handed, using specially prepared gloves that filter the visions into manageable impressions. Their research suggests the patterns are *growing*, generating new thread-material at an accelerating rate. The vault was built for twelve cases. It now holds seventeen. Within a decade, it will not be large enough.

## People & Culture

Skeinhold's culture is defined by discipline and acceptance. The Threadwalkers accept that they live beside the incomprehensible. They do not seek to control it, explain it, or worship it. They observe. They record. They hold their ground.

Daily life follows a simple rhythm: morning meditation, expedition or observation duty, afternoon study, evening communal meal, and night rest. The schedule is strict because structure is survival. In a place where time itself is unreliable, routine is the only anchor that does not require magic.

New Threadwalkers train for three years before their first expedition past the Anchor Line. The training is equal parts philosophy, meditation, and exposure therapy, gradually increasing proximity to temporal distortion while building the mental resilience to withstand it. Not everyone completes the training. Those who cannot are sent home without shame. The Loom's Edge does not punish weakness. It simply consumes it.

### Notable Residents

**[[Threadmaster Kaeven]]**, leader of Skeinhold's Council of Three. A weathered Oldborn woman in her sixties who has conducted more expeditions into the Mirror Valleys than any living Threadwalker. Her anchor-moment is her daughter's birth, thirty-two years ago. She holds it with a clarity that borders on obsession. She has seen her own death in the Mirror Valleys. She will not say when.

**[[Threadwalker Issek]]**, the settlement's most gifted young practitioner and its most troubled. Issek can navigate temporal distortion with an ease that unsettles even Kaeven. He claims the threads *speak* to him, that they are not random patterns but *language*, a communication from whatever remains of [[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas]] within the Loom. The Council does not know whether to promote him or exile him.

**[[Archivist Dael]]**, keeper of the Hall of Returns' records. An elderly Threadwalker who no longer conducts expeditions but whose knowledge of the Loom's Edge's history is unmatched. Dael has been cataloguing Echo appearances for forty years and believes he has identified a pattern: the Echoes are increasing in frequency and specificity, converging on a single future that all threads point toward. He will not describe this future to anyone who has not earned his trust.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The Thread Vault Breach.** One of the vault's lead cases has cracked. The dormant pattern inside is generating thread-material at visible speed, silver filaments crawling across the vault floor. The Threadwalkers cannot contain it with existing methods. They need help, someone with knowledge of divine magic (which interacts with the Loom differently than Primordial magic) to understand what is happening. The PCs, carrying traces of the known continent's divine traditions, may be exactly what is needed.

2. **Issek's Expedition.** Threadwalker Issek has proposed going deeper than any Threadwalker has gone, past the Mirror Valleys, into the Skein itself. He claims the threads are calling him, that they want to show him something. The Council has forbidden it. Issek intends to go anyway. The PCs must decide: stop him, join him, or let him go and deal with the consequences.

3. **The Echo of the PCs.** An Echo appears in the Echo Garden that matches one of the PCs perfectly, same face, same voice, same gear. The Echo speaks a single sentence: a warning, a plea, or a revelation, then vanishes. The Threadwalkers are shaken. Personal Echoes are extremely rare and always significant. The sentence the Echo speaks should be tailored to the PC's personal arc.

4. **Kaeven's Request.** Threadmaster Kaeven approaches the PCs privately. She has seen something in the Mirror Valleys that she cannot discuss with her own people: a future where the Loom Scar opens fully and the counter-prophecy rewrites reality. She needs the PCs to reach the Scar and determine whether it can be sealed. She cannot go herself. Her anchor-moment is failing. If she enters the Skein, she will not return.

5. **The Convergence.** All seventeen dormant patterns in the vault begin resonating simultaneously, producing a harmonic that can be heard throughout the settlement. The Anchor Line pillars crack. The distortion zone surges southward. For twelve terrifying minutes, Skeinhold exists in all times at once before the Threadwalkers' collective discipline pulls it back. Something in the Loom Scar just moved. The next convergence will be stronger.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role:** Skeinhold is the gateway to the campaign's deepest revelations. Here the PCs learn the Threadwalkers' discipline (giving them the tools to survive the Loom's Edge), encounter manifested fate-threads (their first direct contact with the counter-prophecy), and receive the guidance needed to reach the Loom Scar.
>
> **Key Secret:** Threadmaster Kaeven knows more than she reveals. In her deepest Mirror Valley expedition, she saw a future where a group of outlanders, matching the PCs' descriptions, reached the Loom Scar and made a choice that determined the fate of both continents. She has been waiting for them. Her acceptance of outlander visitors, unusual for a Threadwalker leader, is not hospitality. It is preparation.
>
> **Arc Trajectory:** Skeinhold begins as an obstacle (the Threadwalkers test and challenge the PCs), becomes a training ground (the PCs learn anchoring and temporal navigation), and culminates as a launching pad (the expedition to the Loom Scar departs from here). The settlement's survival depends on sealing or stabilizing the Scar. If the PCs fail, Skeinhold is consumed by the expanding distortion, and with it, the only mortals who understand the Loom's Edge.
