# The Loom's Edge

*Time has no authority here. The future remembers what the past has not yet done.*

---

## Overview

The Loom's Edge is the forbidden interior of [[The Unwritten Lands]], a mountainous expanse where the fabric of causality has been torn, restitched, and torn again. This is where [[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas]], the God of Time and Fate, hid the threads he could not allow to remain in the [[Cosmology and Magic#The Loom of Fate|Loom of Fate]], patterns so dangerous that even a god who saw all futures chose to excise them rather than let them play out.

The threads are not metaphorical. They are woven into the stone, into the air, into the passage of time itself. Walk through the Loom's Edge and you will see your own future reflected in a cliff face. Turn a corner and encounter a ruin that will not be built for a century. Speak a word and hear the echo arrive before you open your mouth. Cause and effect are suggestions here, not laws, and the suggestions are growing louder.

The Oldborn call this place **Kaelvoth**, the Wound in the Weave, and they do not go there. Their Pact-Weavers cannot negotiate with time. Their Primordial anchors do not stabilize temporal disruption. The Loom's Edge is the one place in [[The Unwritten Lands]] where Oldborn certainty fails, where their philosophy of mortal self-sufficiency collides with a force that does not negotiate, does not compromise, and does not care.

For two thousand years, the excised threads have been dormant. Now, as the Second Silence weakens divine power across Aethermourne, they are waking. The patterns Orenthas buried are trying to complete themselves, futures that were never allowed to happen are pushing against the present, demanding to be born.

Something is pulling the threads taut. Something is trying to reweave what was deliberately unwoven. And deep in the heart of the Loom's Edge, in a place no mortal has reached and returned from, something Orenthas hid within the excised patterns is beginning to stir.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **What Orenthas hid:** The excised threads contain a *counter-prophecy*, an alternative to the Final Prophecy. Where the Final Prophecy speaks of choice ("the hand that pulls it will choose for all"), the counter-prophecy describes a future where no choice is given, where the Loom itself decides, where fate becomes absolute and mortal agency is erased.
>
> Orenthas excised these threads because they represented a future where he *won*, where the Loom of Fate became the only authority, where free will was an illusion and every mortal life was a thread woven to his specification. He found this future monstrous. He tore it out. He buried it here, in a land where the Loom's reach was weakest.
>
> But the threads are patient. And the Second Silence, by weakening the systems Orenthas left in place to contain them, is giving them room to grow.
>
> **Campaign relevance:** The Loom's Edge is where the party discovers that Orenthas was not merely passive in the Theomachis. He engineered it. He saw that the only way to prevent absolute fate was to shatter the divine order that sustained his power. He killed his own pantheon to save free will. The counter-prophecy is the proof, and the temptation. Because the counter-prophecy offers something the dying world desperately needs: certainty. A guaranteed future. Survival. At the cost of choice.

## Geography

The Loom's Edge occupies a vast mountain interior northwest of [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]], bordered by the Ashfall Range to the south and the Primordia's chaos to the west. The terrain is alpine, steep valleys, jagged peaks, glacial rivers, but nothing here behaves as geology should.

### The Threadstone Passes

The only approach to the Loom's Edge from Oldborn territory. Narrow mountain passes where the stone is shot through with veins of luminous silver, visible threads of fate woven into the geological strata. The veins pulse with a rhythm that is not heartbeat, not breathing, but something between: the cadence of possibility considering itself.

Travelers through the Threadstone Passes experience minor temporal distortions. Conversations replay. Footsteps echo before they are taken. Shadows move independently of their sources. These effects are unsettling but not dangerous. They are the outermost ripple of the Loom's influence, the gentlest warning before the deeper disruptions begin.

### The Mirror Valleys

A series of valleys where the walls reflect not light but *time*. Look at the left wall and see the valley as it was a thousand years ago, pristine, untouched, no thread-veins in the stone. Look at the right wall and see the valley as it will be in a thousand years, consumed, the stone dissolved into pure temporal energy, nothing remaining but the naked threads of fate hanging in empty air.

The floor of the Mirror Valleys exists in the present, but the present here is thin. Step wrong and you fall into yesterday. Step wrong again and you stumble into next week. Navigation requires focus, intention, and an understanding of temporal topology that no mortal education provides.

The [[The Oldborn Kingdoms|Oldborn]] claim that the Mirror Valleys were once ordinary mountains, that Orenthas's excised threads have been *eating* the geography, replacing stone with time, slowly converting the physical into the metaphysical. The process is accelerating.

### The Skein

The heart of the Loom's Edge. A plateau where the excised threads of the Loom are visible to the naked eye, hanging in the air like cobwebs, silver and gold and colors that have no name. They connect to nothing. They lead everywhere. They hum with unrealized futures, paths that were never taken, lives that were never lived, choices that were never made.

The Skein is not survivable. Mortal minds cannot process the raw informational density of exposed fate-threads. Expeditions that reach the Skein report immediate cognitive collapse: visions of every possible future simultaneously, the crushing weight of infinite choice compressed into a single instant. Most who enter do not leave. Those who do are rarely coherent.

At the center of the Skein, visible from the edges but unreachable by any known path, stands a structure. The Oldborn call it the **Loom Scar**, a massive vertical tear in reality through which something vast and silver is partially visible. It is not the Loom itself, the true Loom exists partially outside reality, but it is a *wound* in the Loom, a place where Orenthas cut, and the cut never healed.

### The Temporal Barrens

The southern approach, where the Loom's influence bleeds into more stable territory. Here, the distortions are spatial rather than temporal. Distances change. A mountain that appears an hour's walk away takes three days to reach. A cliff that seems miles distant is one step ahead. Perception and reality negotiate, and neither wins.

Flora and fauna in the Temporal Barrens are adapted to temporal instability. Trees grow in spirals, their rings counting years that have not yet passed. Animals move in stuttering loops, reliving the same seconds repeatedly before jolting forward. Nothing here is quite alive in the conventional sense. Everything is *becoming*, perpetually, without arrival.

## Inhabitants

### The Threadwalkers

The only mortals who voluntarily enter the Loom's Edge and survive. The Threadwalkers are an Oldborn sect, unaffiliated with the Circle of Speakers, who have spent centuries developing techniques for navigating temporal distortion. Their methods are not magical. They are *philosophical*, a discipline of radical present-awareness that allows the mind to resist the Loom's pull toward past and future.

Threadwalkers shave their heads and tattoo their scalps with intricate geometric patterns that represent "temporal anchors," fixed moments in their personal timeline that they use as reference points to maintain coherence. When a distortion strikes, they focus on their anchor-moment and hold fast.

The discipline works. Mostly. Threadwalkers can navigate the Threadstone Passes and the Mirror Valleys with relative safety. The Skein remains beyond them. Those who have tried to enter the Skein have not returned, or have returned centuries before they left, appearing in historical records as unnamed strangers who spoke of futures no one understood.

The Threadwalkers maintain a settlement on the southern edge of the Loom's Edge: [[Skeinhold]], part monastery, part research station, part last outpost before the impossible.

### The Echoes

Not inhabitants in any meaningful sense, but impossible to ignore. The Loom's Edge is populated by **Echoes**, temporal impressions of people who will exist, did exist, or might have existed. They appear solid. They speak. They interact with their environment. But they are not real, not in the present tense.

An Echo might be a traveler who will pass through this valley next year, already visible because the Loom's Edge does not distinguish between "now" and "then." Another might be a long-dead Oldborn scholar, still conducting research in a loop of preserved time. A third might be a version of a PC, slightly different, from a future that the excised threads describe but that has not yet occurred.

Echoes cannot be harmed. They cannot harm. They are recordings, projections, possibilities given shape. But they can be spoken to, and they will answer, and what they say is always true, for the timeline they inhabit. Whether that timeline is *this* timeline is the question that drives every Threadwalker mad.

### The Weavers of the Scar

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Deep within the Skein, something is alive. Not the Echoes, which are impressions, but something with intent. The Threadwalkers call them the **Weavers**, and they believe they are fragments of Orenthas's consciousness, left behind when he excised the threads, still trying to complete the work he abandoned.
>
> The Weavers appear as humanoid shapes made of silver thread, moving through the Skein with purpose, connecting threads, severing others, rearranging the patterns. They do not acknowledge mortals. They do not communicate. They work, endlessly, tirelessly, on a pattern that may be the counter-prophecy trying to complete itself.
>
> Whether they are truly pieces of Orenthas or something else entirely, something that moved into the wound after he left, is unknown. The Threadwalkers believe the answer matters enormously. They are right.

## Dangers

### Temporal Displacement

The primary hazard. Travelers in the Loom's Edge can be displaced forward or backward in time without warning. Minor displacements (seconds, minutes) are disorienting but survivable. Major displacements (years, centuries) are effectively fatal: the displaced individual arrives in a time where they know no one, own nothing, and have no context.

Displacement is more likely near exposed thread-veins and in the Mirror Valleys. The Skein guarantees displacement. The direction and magnitude are unpredictable.

### Fate Resonance

Prolonged exposure to the Loom's Edge causes **Fate Resonance**, a condition where the individual's personal timeline begins to *vibrate* in sympathy with the excised threads. Early symptoms: precognitive flashes, deja vu that is actually accurate memory of unlived events, the sensation that every choice has already been made. Advanced symptoms: loss of the ability to make decisions (all options feel predetermined), physical flickering between possible versions of oneself, eventual dissolution into the thread-network.

Fate Resonance has no cure. It can be managed through the Threadwalkers' anchoring discipline, but once established, it never fully recedes. The resonance is Orenthas's threads reaching out, trying to weave the individual into the pattern, trying to make the excised future *real* by incorporating living souls into its structure.

### The Counter-Prophecy's Pull

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The closer one gets to the Loom Scar, the stronger the pull of the counter-prophecy becomes. It manifests as certainty, a growing, euphoric conviction that everything will be fine, that the world's problems are solvable, that a future of guaranteed safety and order is not only possible but *already happening*. This is the counter-prophecy selling itself, showing travelers the future it promises: a world without suffering, without uncertainty, without the terrifying burden of choice.
>
> It is seductive. It is honest, the future it shows is genuinely possible. But it is also a cage. The price of guaranteed survival is the death of free will, every mortal life reduced to a thread in a predetermined weave. Orenthas found this future monstrous. The PCs must decide for themselves.

## Hooks

1. **The Threadwalker's Plea.** A Threadwalker has returned from the Mirror Valleys carrying a message she did not write. It is addressed to the PCs, by name, in handwriting that matches one of their own. The message describes events that have not happened yet and begs them to come to [[Skeinhold]]. The date on the message is three years in the future.

2. **The Echo of Orenthas.** Deep in the Threadstone Passes, an Echo has appeared that matches no historical record and no predicted future. It is a tall figure in silver robes, faceless, silent, who stands at a crossroads and points toward the Skein. The Threadwalkers believe it is an Echo of [[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas]] himself, a temporal impression of the god at the moment he excised the threads. If they are right, following the Echo's direction may lead to whatever Orenthas hid at the center of the Loom Scar.

3. **The Vanishing Valley.** One of the Mirror Valleys has disappeared. Not collapsed, not destroyed, *disappeared*, removed from the present entirely, as though the excised threads swallowed it. The Threadwalkers' maps no longer include it. Their memories of it are fading. Only the PCs, as outlanders unburdened by Primordial pacts, retain clear recollection. Something in the Loom's Edge is editing the present, and it is getting bolder.

4. **The Resonant Blade.** A weapon has been found in the Temporal Barrens, a blade that appears to be made of solidified causality, silver threads wound impossibly tight, less a weapon and more a paradox given edge. It cuts through time, severing cause from effect. In combat, a wound from this blade appears before the strike that causes it. The weapon is campaign-grade powerful and campaign-grade dangerous. Every faction wants it. The Weavers of the Scar want it back.

5. **The Counter-Prophecy Fragment.** A Threadwalker returns from the Skein's edge carrying a single thread, physically manifested, a silver filament that shows anyone who touches it a vision of guaranteed peace, perfect order, absolute safety. It is the most beautiful and terrifying thing anyone has ever experienced. The thread is a piece of the counter-prophecy's pattern, and it is trying to grow. The PCs must decide: destroy it, study it, or let it complete itself.

> [!warning]- GM Notes: Running the Loom's Edge
> The Loom's Edge is the most metaphysically dangerous region in either continent. Use it sparingly, save it for key campaign moments where the revelations justify the risk.
>
> **Tone:** Surreal, philosophical, deeply unsettling in a way that is different from the Primordia's chaos. The Primordia is pre-order. The Loom's Edge is *post-order*, a place where order existed, was torn apart, and the pieces are trying to reassemble themselves into something new.
>
> **Mechanics:** Temporal distortions should be narrative tools, not combat mechanics. Use them to reveal information (showing PCs events from other timelines), create tension (displacement threats during critical moments), and force choices (do you accept the counter-prophecy's certainty or reject it?).
>
> **The Big Reveal:** The Loom's Edge is where the PCs learn that Orenthas engineered the Theomachis. He saw all futures. He chose the one where the gods died and mortals were free. The counter-prophecy is the road not taken, the future where the gods lived and mortals were slaves to fate. This reframes the entire campaign: the Second Silence is not just a crisis. It is the *consequence* of a god choosing freedom over safety. The PCs must decide if that choice was right.
>
> **Pacing:** The Loom's Edge should be visited multiple times across the campaign, each time going deeper, each time learning more. First visit: the Threadstone Passes, temporal tourism, unsettling but manageable. Second visit: the Mirror Valleys, serious distortions, the Echo of Orenthas. Third visit: the Skein, the Loom Scar, the counter-prophecy. The final visit should be the campaign's climactic decision point.
