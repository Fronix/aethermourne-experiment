# The Oldborn Kingdoms

*They built a civilization on nothing but their own will. And it has stood longer than any god's empire.*

---

## Overview

The Oldborn Kingdoms occupy the central and southern reaches of [[The Unwritten Lands]], a sprawling network of city-states, scholarly enclaves, and agricultural communes built without a single prayer to [[The Twelve]]. Their cities rise from black volcanic stone and polished obsidian, gleaming like dark mirrors against the jungle canopy. Their roads are wide, maintained by civic contract rather than royal decree. Their libraries predate the Theomachis by centuries.

This is what a civilization looks like when it owes nothing to the gods.

The Oldborn are not a single culture. They are a federation of peoples bound by a shared philosophical rejection of divine authority and a shared practical dependence on Primordial pacts. Their governments range from meritocratic republics to hereditary councils to rotating tribunals. Their disagreements are frequent, fierce, and conducted through debate, trade leverage, and occasionally ritual combat. What they share is a certainty, bone-deep and tested across millennia, that mortals do not need gods to build something worth preserving.

When ships from [[The Hollowed Reach]] began washing ashore on [[The Veilshore]], the Oldborn did not panic. They observed. They catalogued. They debated. And then they grew afraid, not of the outlanders themselves, but of what the outlanders carry: the residue of dead gods, the corruption of a dying divine order, and a worldview that treats the sacred as a resource to be mined.

The Oldborn Kingdoms are preparing for war. They hope it will not come. They are not optimistic.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Oldborn are not villains. They are a sophisticated civilization facing an existential threat they are correct to fear. The outlanders' divine corruption is real. It does destabilize Primordial pacts. The Oldborn's hostility is rational self-defense.
>
> The campaign tension here is that the outlanders need Oldborn knowledge (Primordial magic, pre-Pattern history, the path to [[The Loom's Edge]]) to solve the Second Silence, but the Oldborn have no reason to share it. Negotiation, not conquest, is the path forward.

## Geography

The Oldborn Kingdoms stretch across a vast highland plateau ringed by volcanic peaks. The climate is tropical at the lowland edges, temperate on the plateau, and alpine in the volcanic heights. The land is more stable than [[The Primordia]] but less predictable than the known continent. Earthquakes are common. Volcanic eruptions follow negotiated schedules (the Oldborn have pacts with the geological Primordial forces that allow them to redirect eruptions away from population centers, most of the time).

### The Obsidian Plateau

The heartland of Oldborn civilization. A massive elevated plain of volcanic stone, fertile where rivers of rich mineral water irrigate the fields, barren where the stone is too dense for roots. Cities cluster around Primordial Anchors, the massive crystalline structures that stabilize reality and provide the foundation for Oldborn magic.

The Plateau is crisscrossed by roads of polished black stone that never crack, never shift, and never grow over. They were laid using Primordial pacts and will endure as long as the pacts hold.

### The Ashfall Range

A chain of active volcanoes along the western edge of the Kingdoms, separating Oldborn territory from [[The Primordia]]. The volcanoes are not random. They erupt on negotiated cycles, releasing pressure in controlled bursts that fertilize the lowland farms. When pacts fail, when the Primordial forces grow restless, the eruptions become unpredictable. This is happening more frequently as the Second Silence destabilizes even pre-divine magical structures.

### The Rootways

An extensive underground network of tunnels and caverns that connects the major Oldborn cities. The Rootways are natural formations expanded and stabilized through Primordial pacts with [[The Primordials#Achren|Achren]], the Primordial of Boundaries. Travel through the Rootways is faster than overland, but not without risk. The deeper tunnels open into spaces where reality thins, where the Gyre bleeds through, and where things that have never seen sunlight make their home.

### The Thornwood Coast

The southern coastline, dense with forests of razor-leaved trees that grow in salt water. The Thornwood is the Kingdoms' natural barrier against ocean approach. Ships that attempt to land here are shredded by the forest's tidal reach, branches that extend underwater during high tide, retract during low.

The Oldborn maintain one port on the Thornwood Coast: [[Vehlkaar]], a city built within the forest itself, using the razor-trees as both defense and building material.

## Society & Governance

### The Covenant of Ash

The Oldborn Kingdoms are bound by the **Covenant of Ash**, a foundational legal framework established roughly 3,400 years ago, over a thousand years before the Theomachis. The Covenant is not a constitution in the mainland sense. It is a *negotiated contract with the land itself*, a Primordial pact writ large, binding Oldborn civilization to a set of principles in exchange for the Primordials' continued cooperation.

The Covenant's core principles:
- **No mortal may claim dominion over a Primordial force.** Negotiation only. Never command.
- **No city may expand beyond its Anchor's stabilization field** without securing a new Anchor.
- **Knowledge is communal property.** Hoarding knowledge is the gravest crime in Oldborn law.
- **The dead return to the substrate.** Burial is dissolution, returning the body to the Gyre. Preserving corpses is forbidden.

Violation of the Covenant carries severe consequences: exile, severing of Primordial pacts, or, in extreme cases, *unwriting*, a ritual that erases the offender from all records, all memory, all pacts. The unwritten cease to exist in any meaningful sense. Their name is unspeakable. Their works are unmade.

### The Circle of Speakers

Each major city sends a Speaker to the Circle, a deliberative body that meets at the neutral ground of [[Keth'vaal]], the Oldborn's oldest settlement. Speakers serve for seven years and cannot serve consecutive terms. Decisions require consensus, not majority. Deadlocks are resolved through ritual debate, a formalized process that can last weeks.

The Circle has been in emergency session since the first outlander ships arrived. The debate is simple: do we fight, negotiate, or retreat? No consensus has been reached. The Oldborn are fracturing along these lines.

### The Pact-Weavers

The closest thing the Oldborn have to a priestly class. Pact-Weavers are specialists in Primordial negotiation, trained from childhood in the languages, protocols, and dangers of treating with foundational forces. A Pact-Weaver speaks for a city when renewing its Anchor pact, mediates disputes between mortal communities and elemental coalescences, and maintains the wards that keep the Primordia's chaos at bay.

Pact-Weavers are revered, feared, and isolated. The work changes them. Long-term practitioners develop crystalline patterns on their skin, voices that resonate at frequencies mortal ears struggle with, and an emotional flatness that unnerves those around them. They are not Gyre-Touched, not in the way [[The Primordia]]'s victims are. They are *negotiated*, their humanity partially traded away in exchange for the ability to interface with forces that predate consciousness.

## Magic Without Gods

Oldborn magic operates on fundamentally different principles than any tradition on the known continent.

### The Principles

1. **All magic is negotiation.** You do not cast a spell. You propose a change to reality and offer compensation.
2. **The cost is always real.** Primordial forces accept payment in time, sensation, memory, potential, or physical substance. The Primordials do not trade in abstract currency.
3. **Scale requires consensus.** A single practitioner can negotiate minor effects. City-scale magic requires the collective will of the population, channeled through a Pact-Weaver.
4. **Nothing is permanent.** All pacts expire. All enchantments fade. The Oldborn do not build to last forever. They build to be *renewed*.

### Contrast with Divine Magic

| Aspect | Oldborn Magic | Known Continent Magic |
|---|---|---|
| **Source** | Primordial pacts with [[The Gyre]]'s forces | Divine remains, ley lines, [[Cosmology and Magic#The Aetheric Web\|Aetheric Web]] |
| **Stability** | High (no decay taint) | Declining (Second Silence) |
| **Power ceiling** | Limited by negotiation | Higher peaks, but unreliable |
| **Cost** | Personal (time, memory, sensation) | Material (divine materials, focus) |
| **Risk** | Pact failure, over-negotiation | Corruption, Order Sickness, Tide-Touched |

The Oldborn view mainland magic as *parasitic*, feeding on divine corpses like maggots in a wound. They are not entirely wrong.

## The Outlander Crisis

The arrival of ships from the known continent has thrown the Oldborn Kingdoms into their first existential crisis in centuries.

### What the Oldborn Fear

- **Divine contamination.** Outlanders carry trace amounts of divine residue in their blood, their tools, their very breath. This residue destabilizes Primordial pacts. An outlander standing near a Primordial Anchor causes it to flicker. A group of outlanders can disrupt a pact renewal entirely.
- **Cultural erosion.** The outlanders' assumptions, that gods are real, that land is property, that power is something you *take*, are corrosive to Oldborn values. The younger generation is curious. Curiosity is dangerous.
- **Military threat.** The known continent's population dwarfs the Oldborn's. If the outlanders decide to colonize, the Oldborn cannot win a conventional war. They know this. It terrifies them.

### The Three Factions

The Circle of Speakers has fractured into three camps:

**The Sealed Gate** (isolationists): Close the borders. Expel the outlanders. Destroy any ship that approaches. Seal the Thornwood Coast permanently. Led by **Speaker Kaelthen**, a veteran Pact-Weaver who lost students to divine contamination.

**The Open Hand** (diplomats): Engage with the outlanders. Learn what they know. Share what is necessary to prevent catastrophe. The Second Silence threatens everyone, and the Oldborn cannot solve it alone. Led by **Speaker Yavenne**, the youngest Speaker in the Circle's history, who believes isolation is a slower death than engagement.

**The Ash Reclaimers** (radicals): The outlanders' arrival is an opportunity, not a threat. The known continent's divine infrastructure is collapsing. When it falls, the Oldborn should be ready to expand, to bring their superior magical framework to the ruins of the gods' civilization. Led by **Speaker Dravos**, a former military commander whose ambition exceeds his wisdom.

## Settlements

- **[[Keth'vaal]]**, the oldest city, neutral meeting ground and seat of the Circle of Speakers
- **[[Vehlkaar]]**, southern port city built within the Thornwood, the only Oldborn city accessible by sea
- **[[Ur-Saethen]]**, the scholarly capital, home to the greatest library in either continent
- **[[Draveth]]**, a mining city in the Ashfall Range, built into the caldera of a dormant volcano
- **[[Hael'rune]]**, eastern border city, closest to [[The Veilshore]], first point of contact with outlander expeditions

## Hooks

1. **The Contaminated Anchor.** A Primordial Anchor near the eastern border has begun to flicker and destabilize. The Oldborn blame outlander presence. Investigation reveals the cause is more complex: the Anchor is resonating with something in [[The Loom's Edge]], a frequency it was never meant to receive. The Second Silence is reaching even pre-divine structures.

2. **The Unwritten.** A prominent Speaker has been *unwritten*, erased from all records and memory. But the players, as outlanders, were never bound by the Covenant. They still remember her. She appears to them in secret, begging for help. She claims she discovered something in the Rootways, something the Circle does not want known.

3. **The Pact-Weaver's Bargain.** A Pact-Weaver is offering to teach an outlander PC the basics of Primordial negotiation, in exchange for something deeply personal. The cost of Oldborn magic is always real. What is the PC willing to trade?

4. **The Ashfall Prophecy.** The volcanoes of the Ashfall Range are erupting out of cycle. The pacts are failing. The Pact-Weavers cannot determine why. Whispered rumors suggest that something in the deep volcanic chambers, something older than the Primordials, is waking up. The Oldborn have never encountered anything they could not negotiate with. They are about to.

5. **Diplomatic Crisis.** An outlander expedition blundered into a pact renewal ceremony and disrupted it. The consequences are severe: a city's Anchor has gone dormant. Thousands are at risk. The Circle demands the expedition be surrendered for judgment. The outlander factions refuse. The players must mediate before the Sealed Gate faction uses this as justification for war.

> [!warning]- GM Notes: Running the Oldborn Kingdoms
> The Oldborn are designed to challenge players' assumptions about civilization, magic, and the role of the divine. They are not primitives. They are not evil. They are a fully realized alternative to everything the players know.
>
> **Tone:** Proud, sophisticated, alien. The Oldborn should feel like a culture that has had millennia to refine itself without divine interference. Their cities are beautiful. Their laws are elegant. Their fear of the outlanders is justified.
>
> **Key Dynamic:** The players need the Oldborn's help (Primordial knowledge, path to the Loom's Edge, alternative magic). The Oldborn need the players' help (understanding the Second Silence, dealing with divine contamination, navigating the coming crisis). Neither side trusts the other. Both sides are right not to.
>
> **Campaign Role:** The Oldborn Kingdoms are the diplomatic and philosophical heart of the Unwritten Lands arc. Players who earn their trust gain access to Primordial magic, pre-Pattern history, and the road to the Loom's Edge. Players who alienate them lose all of this and gain an enemy they cannot afford.
>
> **The Real Secret:** The Covenant of Ash is not just a legal framework. It is a Primordial pact of civilizational scale, negotiated by the first Pact-Weavers with [[The Primordials#Ur-Ghael|Ur-Ghael]] itself. The cost was the Oldborn's collective memory of what came before, their pre-Covenant history is gone, genuinely erased, not forgotten but *untethered*. The Oldborn do not know what they were before the Covenant. They have chosen not to care. But the truth is buried in the Rootways, and it is not comforting.
