# The Veilshore

*Where two worlds collide, and neither understands the impact.*

---

## Overview

The Veilshore is the eastern coastline of [[The Unwritten Lands]], a narrow transitional zone where Primordial wildness begins to yield to recognizable geography. This is the gateway, the first landfall for ships from [[The Hollowed Reach]] that brave [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne's]] failing seal. It is a place of collision: between Oldborn and outlander, between Primordial magic and divine residue, between civilizations that share no common history and must now negotiate a shared future.

The Veilshore stretches for three hundred miles along black sand beaches, rocky coves, and steep coastal cliffs. The land here is more stable than [[The Primordia]], but only barely. The forests still grow too fast. The tides obey no lunar rhythm. Storms rise from clear skies. This is the edge of the Pattern's reach, and the edge is fraying.

In the past three years, since [[The Hollowed Reach]]'s first successful crossing, the Veilshore has transformed from empty coastline to contested frontier. Makeshift settlements cling to the shore. Trading posts spring up overnight. [[The Oldborn Kingdoms|Oldborn]] patrols watch from the treeline. And in the spaces between, smugglers, cultists, and opportunists carve out their own domains.

The Veilshore is a powder keg. One spark, one misunderstanding, one act of violence, and the fragile peace shatters.

## Geography

### The Black Sands

The majority of the coastline is covered in volcanic black sand, fine as silk and cold to the touch even under direct sunlight. The sand does not behave predictably. Footprints vanish within seconds. Objects buried in it sink or rise without reason. The Oldborn claim the sand is "listening," a Primordial echo of [[The Gyre]]'s awareness.

Camps built directly on the black sand report unsettling phenomena: tools misplaced, supplies vanishing, whispers at night in languages no one knows. Most settlements have moved to the rocky headlands, but the sand makes up the majority of landing sites, and ships have no choice but to beach there.

### The Tidemark Cliffs

Steep cliffs of dark gray stone rise 200 feet above the ocean, riddled with caves and natural arches. These cliffs bear strange markings, geometric patterns carved into the rock that predate [[The Twelve]]. The [[The Oldborn Kingdoms|Oldborn]] refuse to explain them. Scholars from [[The Star-Readers]] believe they are astronomical charts, but the constellations do not match any known sky.

The caves are used by smugglers, refugees, and those who prefer to avoid official contact zones. They are also home to things that were here long before mortals arrived, things that do not appreciate visitors.

### The Fernwald

Inland from the coast, a dense forest of massive ferns, some growing 50 feet tall, forms a natural barrier between the Veilshore and [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]]' territory. The Fernwald is not hostile, but it is *alive* in a way that unsettles outlanders. The trees shift position. Paths close and open. The forest decides who passes and who does not.

The Oldborn navigate the Fernwald through pacts with its Primordial essence. Outlanders who attempt to push through without permission rarely return.

### [[Tidecross]]

The largest and most established settlement on the Veilshore. Built around a natural harbor at the northern end of the coast, Tidecross is a chaotic mixing ground of [[The Hollowed Reach]] merchants, [[The Ashen Dominion]] missionaries, Oldborn observers, and independent explorers. It is part trading post, part diplomatic nightmare, part boomtown.

For full details, see [[Tidecross]].

## Inhabitants

### Outlanders

The collective term the Oldborn use for everyone from the known continent. Outlanders come in waves:

- **Merchants** from [[The Hollowed Reach]], seeking Primordial artifacts, rare materials, and anything they can sell for profit.
- **Missionaries** from [[The Ashen Dominion]], determined to bring the word of [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]] to a people who have never heard it and do not want to.
- **Scholars** from [[The Star-Readers]], hunting for excised fate-threads and Primordial knowledge.
- **Refugees** fleeing the Second Silence's collapse, hoping the Unwritten Lands will be safer than home. They are wrong, but they do not know that yet.
- **Cultists** from [[The Veil Unbound]], drawn by rumors that [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] influence is weaker here, or perhaps stronger. They are looking for something. They will not say what.

### The Oldborn Observers

The Oldborn presence on the Veilshore is officially limited to observation posts and diplomatic envoys. Unofficially, Oldborn scouts move through the Fernwald constantly, monitoring outlander activity, sabotaging expeditions that venture too deep inland, and occasionally abducting outlanders who ask the wrong questions.

The Oldborn are not hostile by nature, but they are profoundly alarmed. The outlanders carry divine taint in their blood, magical corruption in their tools, and a worldview that treats the land as a resource to be exploited. The Oldborn see this as an invasion, not in the military sense, but in the existential sense. These people will ruin everything they touch because they do not understand what they are touching.

The tension is rising. Incidents are increasing. Violence is inevitable.

### The Tidecallers

A small group of [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne's]] priests have made the crossing and established a shrine on the northern cliffs. They claim they were *called* here, that Yvenne whispered to them across the ocean and demanded they witness what is happening.

The Tidecallers are terrified. Yvenne's presence here is stronger than it should be, and her moods are more erratic. The ocean around the Veilshore churns with unnatural currents. Things rise from the deep that should not exist. The Tidecallers perform rites daily to keep Yvenne calm, but they are not confident it is working.

## Tensions

### Cultural Collision

The Oldborn and the outlanders do not speak the same language, literally or metaphorically. Trade negotiations collapse over misunderstandings. Religious gestures are interpreted as threats. The outlanders assume they are discovering a new land. The Oldborn know they are being invaded.

### The Land Itself

The Veilshore's instability is worsening. Storms that rise without warning. Tides that reverse direction mid-cycle. Forests that rearrange themselves overnight. Both groups blame the other. The outlanders say the Oldborn are sabotaging them. The Oldborn say the outlanders' divine corruption is poisoning the land.

Both are partly right. The Veilshore sits at the boundary between Primordial chaos and divine order, and neither system is stable. The collision is destabilizing both.

### Yvenne's Madness

The ocean is the only way in or out, and [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]] controls the ocean. Ships that displease her vanish. Captains report hearing her voice beneath the waves, singing in languages that predate words. The Tidecallers insist she is protecting the Veilshore. Others believe she is preparing to close the passage again, permanently, trapping everyone here.

## Hooks

1. **The Vanished Convoy.** A [[The Hollowed Reach|Concord of Tides]] supply convoy left [[Tidecross]] three days ago, heading south to establish a new outpost. They have not arrived. No wreckage. No survivors. No sign they ever existed. The Oldborn deny involvement. The Tidecallers report that Yvenne was calm that day. So where did twenty ships and 400 people go?

2. **The Fernwald Trial.** An [[The Ashen Dominion]] missionary entered the Fernwald without permission, carrying a relic of [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]]. The forest rejected him violently. He was found three days later, alive but changed, speaking in a language no one recognizes. The Oldborn demand he be handed over for judgment. The Dominion delegation refuses. [[Tidecross]] is on the brink of violence.

3. **The Black Sand Artifact.** A salvager dredging the Black Sands uncovered a crystalline structure unlike anything in the known world. It hums. It pulses. It shows visions of futures that have not happened. [[The Star-Readers]] want it for study. [[The Veil Unbound]] wants it for rituals. The Oldborn want it destroyed. The salvager wants to sell it to the highest bidder.

4. **Oldborn Kidnappings.** Outlanders are vanishing from the edges of settlements. They reappear days later with no memory of where they have been, speaking fluent Oldborn and carrying messages: *"Leave, or be taught to leave."* The Oldborn deny responsibility. The messages are getting more specific. The violence is escalating.

5. **Yvenne's Shrine.** The Tidecallers' shrine has begun bleeding seawater from its walls. The priests claim Yvenne is trying to manifest physically, that she is fighting [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] influence to protect the Veilshore. Others believe the shrine is a breach point, a crack in reality through which something is trying to push through. The players must investigate before whatever is happening reaches a breaking point.

## Settlements

- **[[Tidecross]]**, the largest settlement, a trading hub and diplomatic flashpoint.
- **Ashmark**, a small [[The Ashen Dominion]] missionary camp on the southern coast, isolated and increasingly paranoid.
- **The Wrack**, a smugglers' cove hidden among the Tidemark Cliffs, outside any law.

> [!warning]- GM Notes: The Veilshore as a Campaign Setting
> The Veilshore is designed to feel like a Western frontier town in a fantasy context, tense, lawless, beautiful, and dangerous. Use it for:
>
> **Diplomacy Challenges:** The cultural collision between Oldborn and outlanders creates rich roleplay opportunities. Players must navigate misunderstandings, negotiate peace, or choose sides.
>
> **Moral Ambiguity:** Neither side is clearly right. The Oldborn have legitimate grievances. The outlanders are desperate refugees. The "correct" choice is unclear.
>
> **Environmental Horror:** The instability of the Veilshore should be a constant low-level threat. Storms, shifting geography, and Yvenne's erratic moods create tension without requiring combat.
>
> **Gateway Function:** The Veilshore is the entry point to [[The Primordia]], [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]], and [[The Loom's Edge]]. Every expedition inland begins here. Every return ends here. It is the staging ground for the continent's mysteries.
