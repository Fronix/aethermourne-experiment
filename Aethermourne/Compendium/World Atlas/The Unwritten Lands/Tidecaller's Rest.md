# Tidecaller's Rest

> [!info]
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Region** | [[The Veilshore]], [[The Unwritten Lands]] |
> | **Population** | ~180 (mostly clergy and navigators) |
> | **Government** | Theocratic, led by the Tidecaller Conclave |
> | **Economy** | Navigation services, weather prophecy, tidal charting |
> | **Motto** | *"We listen so the sea may speak"* |
> | **Notable Feature** | The only place on the Veilshore where [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne's]] voice can be heard clearly |

## Overview

Tidecaller's Rest sits on a basalt headland forty miles south of [[Tidecross]], where the Veilshore's black sand gives way to ancient volcanic rock. The headland juts half a mile into the ocean, battered by waves that crash with deliberate, rhythmic force, not natural tides, but [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne's]] breathing.

This is a temple-town, purpose-built by the Tidecallers who crossed the ocean to monitor their goddess's failing seal. Every structure serves the work of listening. The central temple is a resonance chamber carved into the headland's stone, designed to amplify Yvenne's underwater vocalizations into sounds the human ear can parse. The residential buildings cluster behind windbreaks, modest and functional. There are no taverns, no markets, no frivolity. This is a place of vigil.

The Tidecallers came here because Yvenne called them. In dreams, in the ringing of tide bells, in the patterns of foam on storm-wracked water, she whispered: *come, witness, remember*. Thirty-seven priests made the crossing. Nine survived. They built the Rest in eleven days, working without sleep, driven by a compulsion none of them could articulate and none of them could resist.

Now they listen. Day and night, in rotating shifts, Tidecallers sit in the resonance chamber and record what Yvenne says. Most of it is incoherent, fragments of song, bursts of rage, long keening silences. But sometimes, between the madness, there are words. Coordinates. Warnings. Names of things that should not be named. The Tidecallers write everything down. They understand almost none of it.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Yvenne is not merely communicating. She is confessing. The fragments the Tidecallers record, when assembled correctly, tell the story of how [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] manipulated her into sealing the ocean. Yvenne sealed the Unwritten Lands not to protect or contain, but because Serith convinced her that something here would destroy what remained of the gods. Whether that was true or a lie, Yvenne no longer knows. The guilt is destroying her.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Resonance Chamber

The heart of the Rest, a natural cave system expanded and tuned by Tidecaller engineers into an acoustic instrument. Sound from the ocean floor travels through volcanic tubes in the basalt, filtered and amplified into the chamber where listeners sit in concentric circles. The experience is overwhelming. First-time visitors weep, vomit, or collapse. Experienced listeners develop a tolerance, though many report hearing Yvenne's voice in their dreams for weeks afterward.

The chamber operates in three-hour shifts. Longer exposure risks what the Tidecallers call "tide-lock," a fugue state where the listener's consciousness merges temporarily with the ocean. Two priests have been lost to permanent tide-lock. Their bodies sit in the chamber, breathing, blinking, but empty.

### The Tidal Observatories

Three stone towers along the headland's edge, each equipped with Tidecaller instruments for measuring wave height, tidal rhythm, and oceanic pressure. The data feeds into navigation charts that are sent to [[Tidecross]] weekly, the only reliable guide for ships attempting the crossing back to the known continent. Without the Rest's charts, the ocean passage would be suicide.

### The Vigil Quarters

Spartan dormitories where the Tidecallers live. Each room contains a cot, a writing desk, and a basin of seawater kept for ritual purposes. The walls are inscribed with Yvenne's liturgy, old hymns from the mainland adapted for the ocean's alien rhythms. The quarters are silent by tradition. Conversation happens in designated common areas. In the sleeping halls, only the sea speaks.

### The Driftwood Chapel

A small, open-air shrine built from timbers salvaged from ships that did not survive the crossing. The chapel serves as both a memorial for lost sailors and a gathering place for the few non-clergy who live at the Rest: navigators, cartographers, and the occasional scholar. Services are held at high tide, when Yvenne's presence is strongest.

## People & Culture

The Rest operates on Yvenne's rhythms, not the sun's. Shifts change with the tides. Meals are taken during calm periods. Sleep happens when the ocean permits. This creates a disorienting temporal dislocation for visitors; days blur together, and time loses its grip.

The Tidecallers are devoted, exhausted, and afraid. They came here to serve their goddess. What they found was a goddess unraveling. Yvenne's moods control the weather, the tides, and increasingly the mental state of anyone nearby. Good days bring clear skies and coherent transmissions. Bad days bring hurricanes, screaming from the chamber, and priests found standing at the cliff's edge with no memory of how they got there.

Despite the danger, the Tidecallers will not leave. Yvenne called them. They will answer until they cannot.

### Notable Residents

**[[High Tidecaller Jessamine Oren]]**, leader of the Conclave. Fifty years old, gaunt, sleepless, brilliant. Jessamine has spent more time in the Resonance Chamber than any living person. She has begun to understand fragments of Yvenne's transmissions, enough to know that the goddess is trying to tell them something critical. Not enough to know what.

**[[Tidecaller Bren]]**, the youngest priest at the Rest. Nineteen, earnest, and increasingly troubled. Bren hears Yvenne more clearly than anyone else, a gift that may become a curse. The other Tidecallers watch him carefully. His predecessor, who shared this sensitivity, walked into the ocean one night and did not return.

**[[Navigator Ossa Fell]]**, a secular cartographer from [[The Hollowed Reach]] who stays at the Rest to maintain the tidal charts. Practical, skeptical, and deeply uncomfortable with the religious atmosphere. Ossa does not believe Yvenne is communicating. She believes the ocean is producing natural acoustic phenomena that the Tidecallers are interpreting through faith. She is wrong, but her charts are flawless, so the Tidecallers tolerate her.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **Yvenne's Silence.** Three days ago, Yvenne stopped speaking. The Resonance Chamber produces nothing but static. The ocean is flat and still. The Tidecallers are terrified. When Yvenne goes silent, something catastrophic follows. Last time, it preceded a storm that sank four ships. Jessamine is sending for help.

2. **The Confession Fragments.** Jessamine has assembled enough of Yvenne's transmissions to suspect that [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] manipulated the ocean sealing. She does not know what to do with this information. If she shares it, it could destabilize every faction's understanding of why the Unwritten Lands were hidden. If she keeps it, the truth may die with her.

3. **Bren's Deterioration.** Tidecaller Bren has begun speaking in his sleep, in Yvenne's voice, in languages that predate [[The Twelve]]. His eyes have developed a faint blue luminescence. The Conclave is debating whether he is becoming a vessel for Yvenne's will or losing himself to tide-lock. Both possibilities are terrifying.

4. **The Navigation Monopoly.** The Rest's tidal charts are the only safe passage guide for the ocean crossing. [[Tidecross]] depends on them. If the Rest falls, the crossing becomes a death sentence and the Unwritten Lands are effectively sealed again. Three factions are pressuring Jessamine to share her methods. She refuses. The charts require Yvenne's cooperation, and Yvenne does not cooperate with strangers.

5. **Something in the Deep.** Ossa Fell's latest measurements show something massive moving beneath the headland, too deep to see, too large to be any known creature. It has been circling the Rest for a week. The Tidecallers believe it is Yvenne manifesting. Ossa believes it is something that followed the ships from the known continent. Neither explanation is comforting.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Tidecaller's Rest is the players' window into Yvenne's mental state and the truth behind the ocean seal. It functions best in Act II or III, when the players have enough context to understand the implications.
>
> **Key Secret.** The thing circling beneath the headland is neither Yvenne nor a creature. It is a manifestation of Serith's attention. Serith knows Yvenne is confessing. She is trying to silence the transmission before the truth reaches mortal ears. If Serith succeeds, the Tidecallers lose their connection to Yvenne, and with it, the only navigational aid that keeps the crossing viable.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the players protect the Rest and help Jessamine decode Yvenne's confession, they learn that Serith feared something in the Unwritten Lands, something connected to the excised threads in [[The Loom's Edge]]. If they fail, Serith silences Yvenne's voice here, and the crossing becomes impassable within months.
