# The Verdant Marches

> [!quote] Elder Rosk, Greensinger of Roothold
> "The land remembers her. Every root that breaks stone, every vine that pulls down a wall, that is Thyrea's heartbeat."

---

## Overview

The Verdant Marches are what happens when a goddess of nature dies and her corpse becomes the soil. [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]] fell in the west during the Theomachis, and her divine essence saturated the water table, threaded through the root systems, seeped into the stone and the clay and the bones of every living thing nearby. The result, two thousand years later, is a region of staggering and lethal beauty: forests where the canopy blocks the sky four hundred feet overhead, swamps whose waters rewrite flesh, mountains where the seasons change between breakfast and dusk, and an ecosystem that is not merely alive but semi-sentient, territorial, and utterly indifferent to the plans of mortals.

The people who survive here are not citizens of a nation. They are inhabitants of a living thing. A loose confederation of clans, shamans, hunters, and outcasts have built something that resembles a society, held together by shared survival, oral tradition, and the guidance of [[The Greensingers]], druids who commune with whatever fragment of Thyrea's consciousness still dreams beneath the roots. It is the antithesis of [[The Ashen Dominion]] in every way.

## Geography

### Thyrea's Cradle

The heart of the Marches, a vast primeval forest that grows from the body of a dead goddess. Three vertical layers define the Cradle:

- **The Floor**: Permanent twilight. Phosphorescent fungi, shallow streams glowing with dissolved divine essence, creatures adapted to darkness. The soil is warm, not sun-warm, body-warm.
- **The Mid-Canopy (The Green Sea)**: Two hundred feet up. Interlocking branches, moss platforms, and vine-bridges traversable for miles. Green-gold filtered light. This is where most life concentrates.
- **The Crown**: Four to five hundred feet up, open sky. An undulating sea of green broken by emergent titan-trees. Crown-birds circle in patterns the [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] say are Thyrea's thoughts.

The flora is aggressive. Vines reach for heat, flowers exude soporific pollen, root systems trip intruders who move too quickly. In the deepest heart dwell **Thyrea's Children**, massive plant-creatures that may be fragments of the goddess's consciousness given physical form.

### The Marrow Bogs

South of the Cradle, where Thyrea's divine marrow seeped into the water table. A labyrinth of channels, pools, and floating islands. Bioluminescent at night, pulsing green and gold and violet like breath. The beauty is a lure.

**Marrow-Water** rewrites living tissue on contact:
- **Incidental contact**: Heightened senses, accelerated healing. A pleasant warmth that makes you want to stay.
- **Moderate exposure**: Permanent physical changes. Bark-skin, green-gold irises, animal-sharp senses.
- **Heavy exposure**: Grotesque transformation. Limbs becoming root-like, minds going feral. The fully transformed are called the **Rootbound**, shambling things that were once people.

### The Bloom Peaks

Western mountains covered in vegetation that undergoes seasonal cycles compressed into days. Flowers at dawn, seeds at noon, frost at dusk. The atmosphere is thick with pollen and spores, mildly intoxicating, hallucinogenic with prolonged exposure. Some hallucinations are genuinely prophetic. Others are lethal. Rare alchemical ingredients grow here and nowhere else.

### The Scarwall

A living fortification of thorn-vines stretching hundreds of miles along the border with [[The Ashen Dominion]]. Not built, not planted, it emerged on its own. Notably asymmetric: hostile on the Dominion side (thorns extend, sap burns), gentle on the Marcher side (parts for recognized travelers, offers fruit). The Scarwall heals itself when damaged and is growing thicker.

## Society

Freedom above all. Survival is proof of worth. Respect for the wild. Distrust of rigid structure. The Marches reject kings, hierarchs, and any authority that claims power over a person's choices.

**Marchwardens** are chosen through tests of strength, wisdom, and the Rootspeaking, a spiritual rite where a [[The Greensingers|Greensinger]] communes with the land to determine whether the territory acknowledges the candidate. A candidate the land rejects cannot serve, regardless of other qualifications.

[[The Greensingers]] are druids who commune with the land, heal, mediate disputes, and defend the Marches by calling the forest itself to war. Their central theological debate: is the awareness they commune with truly Thyrea's consciousness, or simply divine residue that mimics awareness?

## Current Tensions

1. **[[Kaelith Thornborn]]'s Unification**: Four of twelve major clans have joined. Three lean her way. The rest are hostile or watching. [[Draven Ironbark]] leads the traditionalist opposition.
2. **Corruption in the Deep Cradle**: Creatures displaced outward, dark roots spreading. The [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] sense wrongness but cannot identify its source.
3. **Marrow Bogs Expanding**: New areas becoming saturated. Marrow-Water growing more potent, carrying a secondary despair effect that [[Vael Sundren]] cannot explain.
4. **Scarwall Agitating**: Growing thicker and more aggressive than at any point in living memory. The [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] believe it is responding to an invisible threat.
5. **Kaelith's Channel**: Her divine connection runs through the root-network, which is being infiltrated.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s shadow is reaching through the root system. Deep in the forest, corrupted growth appears: plants thriving in darkness, spiraling roots, fungal masses smelling of salt water and despair. This drives fauna outward and is the underlying cause of every major crisis. The corruption is approaching the root-segments that carry [[Kaelith Thornborn]]'s connection to Thyrea's awareness. If it reaches them, her visions could be subtly altered, turning Thyrea's warnings into Serith's manipulations. She would not know. The guidance would feel the same. Only the intent would be different.

## Factions

- **[[Marchwardens Council]]**: Twelve major clan leaders meeting at [[Roothold]]. Consensus governance, rarely agrees on anything that matters.
- **[[The Greensingers]]**: Spiritual heart of the Marches. Druids, healers, mediators, defenders.
- **Kaelith's Coalition**: The unification movement, centered at [[Thornhaven]].
- **Traditionalist Clans**: Led by [[Draven Ironbark]]. Freedom over coordination, decentralization over unity.
- **Mutagenists of [[Mirewatch]]**: Alchemists who study and trade Marrow-Water. Led by [[Vael Sundren]].
