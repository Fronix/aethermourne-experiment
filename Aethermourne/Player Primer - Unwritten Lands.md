# Player Primer: The Unwritten Lands

> *Before the gods, before the war, before the world was built on divine bones, something else was here. It is still here. And it does not care about your gods, your wars, or your bones.*

---

## What Are The Unwritten Lands?

Across the western sea, past the last charted waters, there is a continent that should not exist. The maps call it [[The Unwritten Lands]], though the people who live there call it home and find the name insulting.

This is the other half of the world, the half the gods never claimed. While [[The Twelve]] carved their domains into the known continent, the Unwritten Lands remained untouched by divine influence. No god fell here. No divine skeleton shaped the mountains. No crystallized blood veins the earth. Instead, the land is shaped by something older, something that predates the gods entirely: the **Primordials**, raw cosmic forces that were ancient when divinity was young.

For two thousand years, the known continent forgot this place existed. [[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas]], God of Fate, sealed the sea routes before the Godfall for reasons no one understands. Now those seals are weakening. Ships from [[The Hollowed Reach]] have reached the eastern shore. Trade routes are opening. And both continents are discovering that the other half of the world is stranger, more dangerous, and more necessary than anyone imagined.

Your characters are here now. Whether you came as explorers, traders, exiles, or accident victims, you are in a place where divine magic smells wrong, the ground sometimes forgets it is solid, and the locals have been negotiating with cosmic forces since before your civilization existed.

Good luck.

---

## The Four Sub-Regions

The Unwritten Lands are divided into zones of increasing strangeness, radiating inward from the coast toward the continent's incomprehensible interior.

---

### The Veilshore
**Eastern Coast, Where Two Worlds Collide**

The first thing outlanders see. [[The Veilshore]] is a contested coastal strip where the known continent's influence meets Primordial territory, and neither side wins cleanly. The beaches are made of black sand that whispers when you step on it. The tides don't follow the moon, they follow patterns no one from the known continent can predict. Ships anchor here, trade happens here, and cultural collision generates equal parts opportunity and violence.

**What You'd Know:**

- **[[Tidecross]]** is the largest outlander settlement, a chaotic trade hub built on black sand that occasionally swallows buildings. It has no walls, no formal government, and no shortage of people trying to get rich, get answers, or get killed. [[Captain Erys Vane]] runs the harbor with exhausted competence.
- The **Fernwald**, a dense forest of alien trees, separates the coast from the interior. Passing through it requires either an Oldborn guide or extraordinary luck. The trees do not attack, exactly, but the forest rearranges itself, and people who enter without guidance tend to walk in circles until they starve.
- **Outlanders** (that's you) are tolerated on the Veilshore but not welcomed. The Oldborn view newcomers from the known continent with suspicion bordering on contempt. You carry divine residue in your magic and your blood, and in a land shaped by Primordial forces, that makes you smell wrong.
- The [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]], followers of [[The Twelve#Aelindra|Aelindra]], have established a small presence here. They are studying the Primordial forces and trying to build diplomatic bridges. They are polite, organized, and quietly terrified by what they are finding.
- The **[[The Veil Unbound]]** has agents here, posing as scholars and traders. Most people do not know this. The Unbound believe that Primordial forces can be weaponized to accelerate the collapse of divine order on the known continent.

**Who Comes From Here:** Sailors who followed the new trade routes west. Merchants who smell profit in the unknown. Scholars chasing theories about pre-divine cosmology. Refugees from the known continent's troubles. Soldiers of fortune. Fools. Visionaries. If curiosity or desperation brought you across the sea, you landed on the Veilshore.

---

### The Primordia
**The Raw Heart, Where Reality Is a Suggestion**

Beyond the Fernwald, the world stops pretending to be normal. [[The Primordia]] is the continent's elemental core, a landscape where the Primordials' influence is strongest and physical law is more of a guideline than a rule. Mountains breathe. Rivers flow upward. Colors exist that your eyes cannot process. The ground sometimes liquefies, then resolidifies with you knee-deep in stone.

**What You'd Know:**

- The Primordia is not hostile. It is indifferent, in the way that a thunderstorm is indifferent. It does not want to kill you. It simply does not notice you, and the forces at work here can destroy you by accident.
- **[[Gyre's Maw]]** is the only permanent settlement in the Primordia, built around a massive [[The Gyre|Gyre]]-crystal called a **Primordial Anchor** that stabilizes the surrounding area. Inside the Anchor's field, physics behaves. Outside it, physics does what it wants.
- **Elemental Coalescences** are common, temporary manifestations of elemental force that form, drift, and dissolve. They are not creatures. They are weather. You do not fight weather. You shelter from it.
- The **Breathing Mountains** are a range that literally rises and falls in slow cycles, the peaks and valleys trading places over days. They are beautiful, terrifying, and the only route to the deep interior.
- Navigation requires an Oldborn guide. Outlanders who enter the Primordia alone have approximately the same survival rate as outlanders who jump off cliffs, and for similar reasons.

**Who Comes From Here:** Nobody comes from the Primordia. People survive it. The Oldborn who live near its edges are the hardiest, most pragmatic people you will ever meet, and they will tell you, with patient contempt, that you are not ready for what lies deeper.

---

### The Oldborn Kingdoms
**Central Civilization, Ancient and Proud**

The heart of Unwritten Lands civilization. [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]] are the homeland of the Oldborn, a people who have lived alongside Primordial forces for millennia, negotiating with cosmic entities the way you might negotiate with a landlord, carefully, respectfully, and with the understanding that the power imbalance is not in your favor.

**What You'd Know:**

- The Oldborn are not a separate ancestry. They are human, elven, dwarven, and everything else, just like the known continent. What makes them different is culture: they never had gods. They developed **Primordial pacts**, negotiated agreements with the raw forces that shape their land, instead of prayer and worship.
- **[[Keth'vaal]]** is the largest Oldborn city, a lakeside capital where three political factions argue about what to do with the outlanders. The **Sealed Gate** faction wants the borders closed. The **Open Hand** faction sees trade opportunity. The **Ash Reclaimers** want to reclaim sacred sites from Primordial instability. The debate is heated and ongoing.
- Oldborn magic is fundamentally different from what you know. Where your magic channels divine residue through ley lines, theirs negotiates directly with Primordial forces. A pact-holder asks the fire for warmth, and the fire asks something in return. The exchange must be balanced. When it isn't, the results are catastrophic.
- **The Structure-Broken** are pact-holders whose agreements have inverted, the Primordial force consuming them instead of cooperating. They are crystallizing, dissolving, or transforming into extensions of the forces they once commanded. This is happening more frequently. The Oldborn are frightened.
- The Oldborn view divine magic with instinctive revulsion. To them, it smells of something dead, which, technically, it is.

**Who Comes From Here:** If you are from the Unwritten Lands, you are probably Oldborn. Your magic comes from pacts, not prayer. Your history stretches back further than the Godfall. You know things about the world's foundations that outlanders find disturbing. And you have opinions about the newcomers from across the sea, none of them entirely positive.

---

### The Loom's Edge
**The Forbidden Interior, Where Time and Fate Unravel**

At the center of the Unwritten Lands, past the Primordia, past the Kingdoms, there is a place the Oldborn do not go. [[The Loom's Edge]] is the domain of temporal distortion, where [[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas]], God of Fate, buried something before the Godfall. What he buried, and why, is unknown. What is known is that time does not work properly here. People who enter age decades in minutes, or emerge younger than they went in, or come back speaking languages that will not be invented for centuries.

**What You'd Know:**

- The Loom's Edge is forbidden territory. The Oldborn Silence-Watchers guard its borders and turn away anyone who tries to enter. This is not authoritarianism. It is survival. The temporal distortion inside is lethal to anyone without extraordinary protection.
- Rumors persist of the **Loom of Fate** itself existing somewhere inside, a cosmic structure where the threads of all possible futures converge. Whether this is literal or metaphorical depends on who you ask.
- Things come out of the Loom's Edge occasionally. Artifacts, creatures, weather patterns that carry temporal contamination. The Silence-Watchers contain these incursions. They are overworked.
- No player character should expect to visit the Loom's Edge early in a campaign. It is endgame territory. But its influence bleeds outward, and you will feel its effects long before you see it.

**Who Comes From Here:** No one. Things come out of the Loom's Edge, but they are not people. Not anymore.

## The Primordials: What Everyone Knows

The known continent has dead gods. The Unwritten Lands has Primordials, forces that were old when the gods were born. They are not beings in any way you would recognize. They do not think, choose, or care. They are fundamental aspects of reality given overwhelming expression.

| Primordial | Nature | What It Does |
|---|---|---|
| **[[The Primordials#The Gyre\|The Gyre]]** | Raw potential, unformed possibility | The substrate beneath reality. Where all things exist as potential before becoming actual. The source of Primordial energy. |
| **[[The Primordials#Ur-Ghael\|Ur-Ghael]]** | Structure, pattern, the First Pattern | Imposes order on chaos. Not divine order, mathematical order. Crystallizes the unformed into the defined. Beautiful, relentless, and utterly indifferent to whether you wanted to be crystallized. |
| **[[The Primordials#Achren\|Achren]]** | Dissolution, entropy, unmaking | The opposite of Ur-Ghael. Dissolves the defined back into potential. Not destruction, transformation. Everything Achren touches becomes possible again, which means it stops being what it was. |
| **[[The Primordials#Thaelun\|Thaelun]]** | Multiplicity, simultaneous possibility | Exists in all states at once. The Breathing Mountains are Thaelun dreaming. Reality near Thaelun's influence flickers between versions of itself. |
| **[[The Primordials#Yith\|Yith]]** | Depth, pressure, the weight beneath | The force that pulls everything downward, inward, deeper. Gravity, but also the metaphysical weight of consequence. |

**The key difference:** Gods had personalities, agendas, and relationships with mortals. Primordials do not. Negotiating with a Primordial is like negotiating with fire: you can work with its nature, but you cannot change its mind, because it does not have one.

## Magic in the Unwritten Lands

### Primordial Pact Magic

The Oldborn's magical tradition is **pact-based negotiation** with Primordial forces. A practitioner does not command magical energy. They enter into an agreement with a specific aspect of the world, fire, stone, growth, dissolution, and the force provides power in exchange for something of equal value. The exchange must be balanced, always.

**What pacts look like:** A fire-pact holder might offer their body heat in exchange for the ability to shape flame. A stone-pact holder might offer flexibility, their joints stiffening permanently, in exchange for the ability to stabilize shifting terrain. The costs are real, physical, permanent, and proportional to the power gained.

**When pacts go wrong:** If the balance shifts, the force takes more than it gives. The pact-holder begins transforming into an expression of the force itself. This is happening to increasing numbers of Oldborn, and no one knows why.

### Divine Magic Here

Your divine magic works in the Unwritten Lands, but it feels wrong. The ley lines that carry [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]]'s shattered network barely reach this continent. Spells that draw on divine residue are weaker, unreliable, and occasionally attract unwanted attention from Primordial forces that find the scent of dead gods either offensive or fascinating.

**In practice:** Divine casters operate at a disadvantage in the Unwritten Lands. Spells may require higher DCs, produce unexpected side effects, or simply fail at inconvenient moments. Work with your GM on the specifics, but expect your magic to behave differently here.

## Building Your Character

### If You're From the Known Continent

You are an outlander. You crossed the sea for a reason, and that reason shapes everything about your experience here. Consider:

- **Why did you come?** Trade? Scholarship? Exile? Were you sent by a faction (Star-Readers, Lantern-Keepers, the Dominion)? Did you come voluntarily, or did circumstances leave you no choice?
- **How do you handle being the outsider?** The Oldborn find your magic repulsive and your ignorance dangerous. Your gods are dead, and the forces here do not answer prayer. How do you adapt?
- **What do you want to bring back?** Knowledge? Wealth? Power? Or are you not planning to go back at all?

### If You're Oldborn

You have lived your entire life in a world without gods, where the ground moves, reality shifts, and survival depends on negotiation with forces beyond mortal comprehension. The outlanders are newcomers, blundering into a world they do not understand. Consider:

- **What is your pact?** Every Oldborn of significance has negotiated with at least one Primordial force. What did you gain? What did it cost?
- **How do you feel about outlanders?** Contempt? Curiosity? Fear of what their divine contamination might do to the Primordial balance?
- **What faction do you support?** Sealed Gate, Open Hand, or Ash Reclaimers? The outlander question defines Oldborn politics right now.

### Tone

The Unwritten Lands are not the known continent with a different coat of paint. They are genuinely alien, a place where the rules you grew up with do not apply. Your character should feel that alienness, whether as threat, wonder, or opportunity. The Oldborn are not primitive. Their civilization is older than yours. They simply built it on a different foundation, one that is cracking for reasons neither side fully understands.

## What's Happening Now

**The pacts are failing.** Across the Oldborn Kingdoms, Primordial pact-holders are losing control of their agreements. The forces they negotiated with are taking more than they give. The Structure-Broken are multiplying. The Oldborn's entire way of life depends on pacts working, and they are breaking.

**Outlanders keep arriving.** The sea routes are open, and ships from the known continent arrive weekly. Tidecross is growing. The Oldborn cannot decide whether to welcome the newcomers or drive them back into the sea. Both options have consequences.

**The Primordia is expanding.** The unstable, reality-bending interior is pushing eastward. Areas that were stable for centuries are becoming dangerous. The Primordial Anchors that hold the chaos at bay are weakening. The Primordia does not care about borders.

**Something stirs at the Loom's Edge.** The Silence-Watchers report increased temporal distortion bleeding outward from the forbidden interior. Artifacts are appearing in places they should not be. Time is behaving strangely, even by Unwritten Lands standards.

**The Veil Unbound is here.** Agents of the shadow goddess [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] have infiltrated the Veilshore. They are buying Primordial materials, making contacts, and working toward a goal no one on this continent understands yet. Whatever they want, it involves the Primordials, and that should worry everyone.

---

*The gods never came here. Something older did. And now, for the first time in two thousand years, both halves of the world are looking at each other across the water, and neither side likes what it sees.*

*Your characters are standing in the gap. What they do next matters.*
