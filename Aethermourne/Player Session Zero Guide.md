# Session Zero Guide: Aethermourne

*Everything you need to run a Session Zero for a campaign in the world of dead gods.*

---

## Before You Begin

> [!info] What This Guide Covers
> This document helps GMs run a Session Zero for any Aethermourne campaign. It covers character creation, tone calibration, safety tools, party composition, and five ready-made character concepts. Hand the player-facing sections directly to your table. Keep the GM notes for yourself.

**Estimated Time:** 2-3 hours (character creation + discussion)
**Required Reading for Players:** [[Player Primer]] (share this before Session Zero)
**Required Reading for GM:** This guide + the relevant region atlas for your campaign's starting location

---

## Part 1: Tone and Safety

### The World's Tone

Aethermourne is **mythic dark fantasy**. Beautiful things are also haunted. Power always costs something. Moral simplicity is rare. The world is built on the corpses of dead gods, and everything, magic, politics, geography, carries that weight.

This is not grimdark. Hope exists. People build, love, and fight for things that matter. But the hope is earned, never given, and the cost of failure is real.

**Key tonal beats to establish with your table:**
- Violence has consequences. Combat is dangerous, not heroic spectacle.
- The gods are dead, diminished, or silent. Faith is complicated. There are no divine hotlines.
- Morality is contextual. The Dominion's order protects people *and* crushes them. The Marches' freedom is exhilarating *and* brutal.
- The world is worth saving. That is the point. Not because it is good, but because it is *theirs*.

### Content to Discuss

Aethermourne touches on themes that warrant a conversation before play begins. Establish comfort levels around:

- **Body horror:** Ashite influence changes thinking. Primordial Taint transforms flesh. Pact-Broken lose their humanity. The Consuming erodes identity.
- **Loss of agency:** Crown Wraiths compel obedience. Ashite subtly shifts personality. Some magical effects alter behavior.
- **Grief and loss:** The gods are dead. Entire civilizations are built on mourning. NPCs carry trauma.
- **Oppressive systems:** The Dominion's caste system, the Ashless, the Inquisitors. Systemic injustice is woven into the setting.
- **Existential dread:** Stars going dark. Reality fraying. The possibility that the world's foundations are failing.

> [!tip] Safety Tools
> Use whatever safety tools your table prefers (X-card, Lines & Veils, Script Change). Establish them now, before play begins. Aethermourne's themes are powerful because they are grounded. That grounding means they can hit harder than typical fantasy fare. Check in with your players.

## Part 2: Character Creation

### Ancestries

All standard ancestries exist in Aethermourne. Two thousand years of post-Godfall history have mixed populations thoroughly. No ancestry is locked to a region.

**Special considerations:**
- **Remnants** (Pale Wastes undead who retained their minds) are a playable option with GM approval. They are dead, they do not age, and the living are often afraid of them.
- **Gyre-Touched** (Unwritten Lands, if your campaign reaches there) are mortals transformed by Primordial exposure. Crystalline growths, impossible senses, bodies that do not fully obey natural law.
- Aethermourne does not have traditional "evil races." Every culture has its reasons, its virtues, and its failures.

### Magic Traditions

Your character's magical tradition shapes how their magic *feels*, not just what it does. Choose based on where your character trained:

| Tradition | Region | Style | Strength | Limitation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Calculism** | [[The Ashen Dominion]] | Math and geometry, Ashite focuses | Reliable, precise, teachable | Rigid, Ashite-dependent |
| **Primal Communion** | [[The Verdant Marches]] | Relationship with Thyrea's living awareness | Intuitive, powerful, adaptive | Demands vulnerability, land decides |
| **Tidal Sorcery** | [[The Hollowed Reach]] | Ocean rhythms, ebbs and flows | Flexible, responsive | Inconsistent, tide-dependent |
| **Vigil Craft** | [[The Pale Wastes]] | Cautious, practical, survival-first | Works under necromantic interference | Limited, always risks contamination |
| **Primordial Pacts** | [[The Unwritten Lands]] | Negotiation with foundational forces | No divine taint, stable foundation | Cannot command, only negotiate |

**Important:** All magic in Aethermourne flows from dead or diminished gods (except Primordial pacts). Spells are powered by divine decay. This does not change mechanics, but it should inform how your character *feels* about their power.

### Regional Backgrounds

Where your character comes from defines more than accent and wardrobe. It shapes worldview, assumptions, and blind spots.

| Region | Values | Blind Spots |
|---|---|---|
| **Ashen Dominion** | Order, duty, hierarchy, knowledge | Cannot see structure as oppression |
| **Verdant Marches** | Survival, community, adaptability | Dismissive of "civilized" solutions |
| **Hollowed Reach** | Trade, pragmatism, opportunity | Monetizes everything, underestimates the spiritual |
| **Pale Wastes** | Endurance, honesty, community | Suspicious of outsiders, fatalistic |
| **Unwritten Lands** | Self-determination, Primordial reverence | Hostile to anything divine-touched |

### Divine Relationship

Every character in Aethermourne has a relationship with the gods, even if that relationship is "I don't think about them." Discuss with your players:

- Do they worship a surviving god ([[The Twelve#Aelindra|Aelindra]], [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]])?
- Do they venerate a dead god's legacy ([[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]], [[The Twelve#Belara|Belara]], [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]])?
- Do they resent the gods for the Theomachis?
- Are they secular, treating the gods as historical figures rather than objects of devotion?
- Have they encountered divine remains personally (mined Ashite, heard Yvenne's voice, touched Morrhaelite)?

## Part 3: Party Composition

### What Works Well

Aethermourne campaigns benefit from parties that represent multiple regions and perspectives. The world's tensions are between cultures as much as between heroes and villains.

**Strong combinations:**
- **Dominion + Marches:** Order vs. freedom. These characters argue about everything and cover each other's weaknesses.
- **Reach + Wastes:** Pragmatism vs. principle. The merchant and the soldier see the world differently but share a stubborn refusal to quit.
- **Mixed regions + one Unwritten Lands character:** If your campaign involves the new continent, an Oldborn PC creates instant dramatic tension and a window into Primordial culture.

**Every party should have:**
- At least one character with strong social skills (diplomacy, investigation). Many Aethermourne conflicts cannot be solved with combat.
- At least one character with magical knowledge (Arcana, Religion, or equivalent). The world's mysteries require magical literacy to unravel.
- A reason to be together. Aethermourne rewards parties that have a shared purpose beyond "we met in a tavern."

### Campaign Style Guidance

| Campaign Style | Recommended Party Focus | Starting Region |
|---|---|---|
| **Political Intrigue** | Social skills, investigation, faction connections | [[The Ashen Dominion]] or [[The Hollowed Reach]] |
| **Exploration/Discovery** | Survival, arcana, adaptability | [[The Verdant Marches]] or [[The Unwritten Lands]] |
| **Horror/Survival** | Combat, endurance, community bonds | [[The Pale Wastes]] |
| **The Second Silence (main campaign)** | Mixed, all skills needed | [[The Hollowed Reach]] (Act I) |

## Part 4: Five Character Concepts

These are ready-made hooks, not complete characters. Take what works, discard the rest.

### 1. The Ashless Scholar

**Region:** [[The Ashen Dominion]]
**Concept:** Born casteless, educated themselves through stolen books and overheard lectures. Now they know things the Anointed would rather they didn't.
**Hook:** They found a reference to the *Ash Testimony*, a suppressed text about Solvaen's death that contradicts Church doctrine. They do not have the text. They know it exists.
**Tension:** Knowledge is their weapon and their vulnerability. The Dominion rewards knowledge in the right hands and punishes it in the wrong ones.

### 2. The Tide-Touched Navigator

**Region:** [[The Hollowed Reach]]
**Concept:** A Tidal Sorcerer whose magic changed during a Black Tide. Now they hear things in the water, not [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne's]] voice, something older, something that speaks in currents rather than words.
**Hook:** The voice is guiding them toward [[The Unwritten Lands]]. They do not know why. They cannot stop listening.
**Tension:** Their magic is more powerful than it should be, but it pulls them toward the deep, and the deep is not empty.

### 3. The Vigil Deserter

**Region:** [[The Pale Wastes]]
**Concept:** Served in the [[Major Factions#Ashen Vigil|Ashen Vigil]] for ten years. Left after the silence started, the dead stopped rising, and the veterans got nervous. Something is wrong in the north, and staying felt like waiting to die.
**Hook:** They carry a Vigil badge that still glows faintly with Morrhaelite resonance. It should not do that. Morrhaelite absorbs energy, it does not produce it. Something has changed about the material, or about the god it came from.
**Tension:** They left because they were afraid. They have not stopped being afraid. Running south did not help.

### 4. The Greensinger's Apprentice

**Region:** [[The Verdant Marches]]
**Concept:** Training under a [[Major Factions#Greensingers|Greensinger]] elder when the Rootmind surges started. The land's awareness is waking up, faster and more aggressively than anyone expected. Their mentor walked into the Thyrea's Heartwood and did not come back.
**Hook:** Before their mentor vanished, they said: "The Marches are not Thyrea's body. They are her *dream*. And she is having a nightmare."
**Tension:** Primal Communion requires trust in the land. The land is behaving erratically. Trust is getting harder.

### 5. The Oldborn Exile

**Region:** [[The Unwritten Lands]]
**Concept:** Exiled from [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]] for heresy: they believe the Oldborn should cooperate with the outlanders rather than expel them. Now they live on [[The Veilshore]], caught between two worlds that both reject them.
**Hook:** They carry a fragment of a Primordial Anchor, a shard small enough to fit in a palm, that stabilizes a 5-foot radius around them. It is the only thing keeping the Veilshore's instability from affecting them directly. It is also stolen, and the Silence-Watchers want it back.
**Tension:** They believe coexistence is possible. Every day on the Veilshore makes that belief harder to maintain.

## Part 5: What Players Should Know vs. What They'll Discover

### Tell Them Now

- The gods are dead, diminished, or silent. The world is built on their remains.
- Magic comes from divine decay. It works, but it is not clean.
- Four regions, each shaped by a different dead god's corpse.
- Stars are going dark. The seas are turning black. Something is wrong.
- Power always costs something. There are no free rides.

### Let Them Discover

- The nature of [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] imprisonment and her role in the crisis
- The existence and significance of [[The Unwritten Lands]]
- The [[The Primordials|Primordials]] and their relationship to the gods
- The truth about the [[Cosmology and Magic#The Loom of Fate|Loom of Fate]] and [[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas's]] silence
- The full scope of the Second Silence and how the crises interconnect
- The [[The Iron Descent]] as a real, accessible place (not just legend)
- The possibility that divine order was not the first or only way to structure reality

> [!warning]- GM Only: Calibrating Revelations
> The list above is a guide, not a rule. Some campaigns benefit from earlier reveals. If your party starts on the coast, they may encounter Yvenne's madness and the Unwritten Lands sooner. If they start in the Dominion, Serith's influence on the Church may surface first.
>
> The key principle: players should always feel like the world is larger than what they know. Aethermourne's mysteries are layered. Peel them slowly. Let the players sit with each revelation before introducing the next.
