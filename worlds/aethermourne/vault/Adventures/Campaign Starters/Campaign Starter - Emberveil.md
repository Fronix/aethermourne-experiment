# Campaign Starter: The Emberveil

*The forge burns. It has always burned. But listen closely, and you will hear it guttering.*

---

## Overview

A ready-to-run 3-session arc for a campaign beginning in [[Crucible]], capital of [[The Emberveil]]. The PCs arrive during a crisis the Forge Council is desperately suppressing: the Grand Forge, [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]]'s eternal fire, is going out. The city built on creation is learning what happens when the creator's light fails.

**Themes:** Legacy and succession, the cost of secrets, creation versus destruction
**Tone:** Forge-light and stone. The warmth of deep earth turning cold.
**Level:** 1-3

---

## Session 1: The Dimming

### Opening Monologue

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Crucible is carved into the mountain itself, a city of terraced forges and vaulted workshops descending in concentric rings toward the heart of the earth. The air is warm, thick with the smell of hot metal and coal-smoke, and the constant ring of hammers on anvils creates a rhythm that feels less like industry and more like a heartbeat.
>
> At the center, far below, the Grand Forge burns. [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]]'s fire, lit by the god himself before the Theomachis, sustained by the divine heat of his sacrifice. It has not gone out in two thousand years. Every piece of [[Cosmology and Magic#Forgeheart Steel|Forgeheart Steel]] is born in its light. Every dwarf in the city measures their life by its warmth.
>
> You descend through the Outer Ring, past the visiting merchants and traveling smiths, into the city proper. The forges here are busy, productive, the rhythm steady. But you notice something. The journeymen glance toward the center more often than they should. A master smith pauses mid-strike, cocks her head as if listening, then resumes with forced calm. A Mender passes, carrying diagnostic instruments toward the lower levels, moving too fast for routine maintenance.
>
> The forge burns. But something in its light has changed. The shadows are longer than they should be.

### Structure

**PC Focus:** All PCs equally. Establish the city's culture and the hidden crisis.
**Goal:** Ground the PCs in Crucible, introduce the Cooling crisis, create investment in the city's people.
**Threads Advanced:** Grand Forge dimming, Apprentice Jyn's trial, Dominion espionage.

### Scene 1: The Outer Ring

The PCs arrive seeking work, trade, or a specific commission. The Outer Ring is the public face of Crucible: merchant stalls, visiting delegations, and the Assessors who evaluate every piece of craft entering or leaving the city.

- **Atmosphere:** Pride and precision. Every surface is carved or decorated. Artisans argue about techniques the way soldiers argue about tactics. The Ember Guild maintains a prominent factor's office.
- **Information (free):** The Forge Council governs by merit, not blood. [[Brunhild Ironheart|Forge-Matriarch Brunhild Ironheart]] has led for forty years. She is 180 years old and sharp as the day she took the seat.
- **Information (DC 12 Persuasion, journeymen):** Something is wrong in the deep levels. Temperature drops where none should exist. Two deep-mining crews have been reassigned without explanation.
- **Information (DC 14 Streetwise, tavern talk):** [[Apprentice Jyn]], the controversial human apprentice at the Grand Forge, faces their guild examination in three days. Half the city wants them to succeed. Half wants them gone. Someone is making sure the examination will not be fair.

### Scene 2: The Archive

[[Corrin Ashveil|Factor Corrin Ashveil]], keeper of the Ember Archive, approaches the PCs in the Archive's reading room. He is nervous, scholarly, and carrying too many documents.

**Ashveil's Problem:**
- He has been studying historical temperature records from the Grand Forge. The data shows a pattern: three dimming events in the last year. Each lasted longer than the last. Each was followed by a temperature drop in the deep levels.
- The Forge-Matriarch has sealed the relevant records and forbidden further research.
- Ashveil believes the Grand Forge is dying. He needs someone outside the guild structure to help him access the sealed records and confirm his theory.
- He cannot pay much. He offers knowledge instead: access to the Archive's unrestricted collection, which contains pre-Theomachis forge-craft documentation.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Ashveil is right. The Grand Forge is cooling. Brunhild knows and has been suppressing the information to prevent panic. She is not wrong to fear the consequences (Crucible's economy, identity, and strategic importance all depend on the Forge), but her secrecy is making the problem worse. The sealed records contain references to a "rekindling" process that requires a sacrifice of devotion equal to Thalvor's original act.

### Scene 3: The Apprentice

[[Apprentice Jyn]] finds the PCs at their lodging. They are sixteen, desperately lonely, prodigiously talented, and terrified.

**Jyn's Situation:**
- They were born directly above a deep geothermal vent. They carry divine fire in their blood, literally: their skin is warm to the touch, their craft-work produces results that should require decades of experience.
- Their guild examination is in three days. A faction within the guild is sabotaging it. Jyn has found evidence that the practical examination materials have been tampered with: flawed metal that will fail spectacularly under forge conditions, ensuring a public humiliation.
- They need someone to help them prove the sabotage or acquire untampered materials before the examination.
- They do not ask for pity. They ask for fairness.

**Encounter: Saboteur's Workshop**

Investigating the tampered materials leads to a workshop in the Middle Ring where the saboteur has been working. The workshop is trapped (Dex DC 13 to avoid a forge-gas release, 2d6 fire damage in a 3 space area) and contains evidence implicating a senior guild member.

- 2 Guild Enforcers (hired muscle, not true believers): Lvl 2 | Armor M | HP 22 | Speed 6 | Str +2 | Dex +1 | Will +0 | hammer 1d6+2 bludgeoning
- They surrender if reduced to half HP. They were told it was "quality control."

**Resolution:** Finding the evidence of sabotage gives Jyn a chance at a fair examination. The senior guild member behind it (a traditionalist named Master Gronn) will deny everything. The Forge-Matriarch will have to adjudicate, which forces Jyn's case into the political arena.

## Session 2: Fire and Shadow

### Structure

**PC Focus:** Investigation PCs dig into the Cooling. Social PCs navigate the guild examination and Dominion delegation.
**Goal:** Reveal the Cooling's scope, introduce the external threat, force the PCs to choose what matters.
**Threads Advanced:** Sealed records, Dominion espionage, Deep Warren discovery.

### Scene 1: The Sealed Records

If the PCs help Ashveil access the sealed records (requiring either Brunhild's permission, a DC 16 stealth infiltration, or convincing a Council member to intervene), they find:

- Historical records showing two previous Cooling events in Crucible's history. Both preceded catastrophic forge failures that nearly destroyed the city.
- References to a "rekindling" process described in Thalvor-era texts. The process requires understanding the Deep Forge's architecture, which was mapped in the Last Workshop.
- A sealed report from [[Orin Deepstone|Delver-Captain Orin Deepstone]] documenting the discovery of the Last Workshop three months ago: a chamber where Thalvor's original tools still glow with divine heat. Three miners entered. One returned. The other two are inside, transformed, building something.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Brunhild has been sitting on the Last Workshop discovery because the returned miner spoke in Thalvor's voice, and Brunhild is afraid of what that means. A dead god speaking through a living person implies the god is not as dead as everyone believes, or that something else is speaking through the god's remains.

### Scene 2: The Dominion Delegation

A trade delegation from [[The Ashen Dominion]] is renegotiating Forgeheart Steel quotas. The delegation's leader is polite, professional, and not the threat. The threat is the trade attache, a [[The Cinders|Cinder]] agent using the delegation as cover.

**The Cinder's Mission:**
- Assess Crucible's defensive capabilities
- Identify leverage points for future pressure
- Determine whether the Grand Forge can be accessed or controlled externally

**Discovery (DC 13 Perception during a diplomatic dinner):** The attache excuses themselves and heads toward the deep-level access points. Following them reveals they are mapping the city's internal architecture, specifically the routes to the Grand Forge.

**Encounter: Confrontation**

If confronted, the Cinder agent fights to escape, not to kill.

- 1 Cinder Agent: Lvl 3 | Armor M | HP 30 | Speed 6 | Str +1 | Dex +3 | Will +2 | poisoned blade 1d6+3 piercing + Str DC 13 or 1d4 poison damage for 2 rounds
- **Smoke Bomb:** 1/encounter, creates 3 space heavily obscured area for 2 rounds.
- They carry coded documents referencing "assessing the Ember's viability for external channeling."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The coded documents reveal that the Dominion is aware of the Cooling and is exploring whether the Grand Forge's divine energy can be redirected for their own purposes, specifically as a weapon against [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]. The Dominion's theory is catastrophically wrong: redirecting the Forge's energy would not create a weapon. It would extinguish the fire permanently.

### Scene 3: Jyn's Examination

The guild examination is public. The entire city watches. Jyn must demonstrate mastery of three forge-craft disciplines. If the sabotage was exposed, the examination is fair and Jyn's talent speaks for itself. If not, the flawed materials fail spectacularly.

**Either way, something happens during the examination.**

The Grand Forge dims. For five heartbeats, the eternal fire at the city's center gutters and nearly goes out. Every forge in Crucible loses heat simultaneously. The metal Jyn is working cools mid-strike. The audience goes silent. Brunhild's face drains of color.

Then the fire returns. But it is not the same. The light is thinner. The heat is less.

Brunhild stands and announces that the examination is suspended due to "forge irregularities." Her voice does not waver. Her hands do.

## Session 3: The Last Workshop

### Structure

**PC Focus:** The whole party. Exploration and revelation.
**Goal:** Enter the Last Workshop, confront the truth of the Cooling, end on a cliffhanger.
**Threads Advanced:** Thalvor's legacy, the Second Silence's reach, campaign arc launched.

### Scene 1: Brunhild's Trust

The public dimming forces Brunhild's hand. She can no longer suppress the crisis. She summons the PCs (and Ashveil, and Orin Deepstone) to a private chamber.

**Brunhild's Admission:**
- The Grand Forge has dimmed three times this year. Each time longer. The fire is dying.
- Thalvor sacrificed himself to light the forge. His consciousness dissolved into the flame. The fire is not just energy. It is the last trace of a god's devotion.
- Something is draining it. Not from within. From without. The divine energy that sustains the forge is connected to the broader network that connects all divine remnants across the continent. That network, the Aetheric Web, is failing.
- The Last Workshop may contain answers. She needs a team to enter it, study what the transformed miners are building, and determine whether the forge can be saved.

### Scene 2: The Descent

Orin Deepstone leads the PCs into the deep levels. The temperature drops as they descend, which should be impossible (the deep levels should be warmer). The stone walls show ancient carvings: forge-spirits, tool-patterns, and a massive mural of Thalvor at work, his face serene, his hands building the world.

**The Last Workshop:**
A chamber the size of a cathedral, carved from living rock. Thalvor's original tools line the walls, each still glowing with divine heat. The air shimmers. At the center, two miners stand at an anvil, hands transformed into forge-tools, working in perfect, mechanical rhythm. They have been building something for three months without rest, food, or water.

- **The Object:** A complex geometric shape, part machine, part sculpture, part map. It pulses with residual divine energy. DC 14 Knowledge: it is a diagram of the Aetheric Web, rendered in three dimensions, showing the connections between all divine remnants on the continent. Several connections are dark. More are dimming.
- **The Miners:** They are not hostile. They are not conscious. Something is working through them. If addressed, they pause and speak in unison in a voice that is warm, vast, and sad: *"The network fails. The connections sever. What I built is being consumed. I cannot stop it. I can only show you where it is happening."*

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The voice is Thalvor's residual consciousness, the last ember of a god who sacrificed everything. He is not alive. He is a message, an echo of devotion powerful enough to animate two miners and build a warning. The Aetheric Web model shows the Second Silence in physical form: the network connecting divine remnants is being drained by something in the Abyssal Trench. The forge is not dying of natural causes. It is being murdered.

**Encounter: Spark Wraiths**

The Workshop's divine energy draws [[Spark Wraiths]], forge-fire that learned to want. Two drift in from the corridors, drawn to the PCs' creative problem-solving.

- 2 [[Spark Wraiths]]
- Lvl 4 | Armor None | HP 35 | Speed fly 6 | Str -2 | Dex +3 | Will +1 | engulf 2d6 fire
- **Creativity Drain:** Will DC 14 per minute of creative thought within 3 spaces.
- **Core Spark:** Armor H, 5 HP. Destroy to kill permanently.
- **Weakness:** Cold deals double. Mundane repetitive thought makes you invisible to them.

### Cliffhanger Ending

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> You stand in Thalvor's Last Workshop, looking at a three-dimensional map of divine connections spanning a continent. Half the nodes are dark. The rest are dimming.
>
> The miners' hands have stopped. The voice has gone silent. But the map remains, rotating slowly in the air, pulsing with fading light. Every dark node corresponds to a place you know: the Hollowed Reach, the Pale Wastes, the Verdant Marches. Every dimming node corresponds to a place that still has time.
>
> Brunhild stands at the chamber's entrance, her face reflecting the dying light. "The forge is not failing on its own," she says quietly. "Something is killing it. Something is killing all of them."
>
> She looks at the map. At the single point where all the dark lines converge: a location beneath the ocean, far to the east, at the bottom of the deepest trench in the world.
>
> "What is down there?"
>
> No one answers. The forge's light flickers once, recovers, and burns on. For now.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Where This Goes:** The Aetheric Web model is the most complete picture of the Second Silence's scope that any faction has produced. It shows, in physical form, that [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s prison is draining divine energy from every god's remains simultaneously. Crucible becomes strategically vital: the Workshop is the only place this model exists, and every faction will want access to it. Brunhild must decide whether to share the information or protect it. The PCs carry knowledge that connects the Emberveil to the continental crisis.
