# Campaign Starter: The Greymere Fenlands

*The water glows. The air hums. The goddess is in pieces and every piece is trying to think. Welcome to the place where magic bleeds.*

---

## Overview

A ready-to-run 3-session arc for a campaign beginning in [[Fenholm]], the largest settlement in [[The Greymere Fenlands]]. The PCs arrive during an escalating ley-surge crisis that is cracking the Lattice, destabilizing the [[The Confluence Council|Confluence Council]]'s readings, and drawing dangerous attention from factions who want to control, exploit, or destroy the fenlands' magic. Three branching paths in Session 2 converge on [[Sporeling]], where [[Brother Lumen]] is attempting something that could save the world or erase 800 people in the process.

**Themes:** The cost of knowledge, individuality vs. collective survival, broken things reaching toward wholeness
**Tone:** Luminous, strange, treacherous. A shattered goddess's mind leaks through the water and light. Everything glows, everything shifts, everything is beautiful and wrong.
**Level:** 1-3

---

## Hooks

> [!info] Hook 1: Hired Muscle
> [[Torben Greaves]] or the [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] hired the PCs to escort a cargo shipment down the [[Smuggler's Sluice]]. The pay is excellent because the route is sinking and bandits have been hitting runs weekly. They arrive at [[Fenholm]] during a ley-surge crisis and discover their employer's problems are considerably larger than cargo logistics.

> [!info] Hook 2: Fugitive's Refuge
> One or more PCs are fugitives, from the [[The Ashen Dominion|Dominion]], from debts, from war, from lives that fell apart. They've heard the fenlands don't ask questions. [[Fenholm]] is the first settlement that doesn't demand papers, caste markers, or explanations. They arrive seeking anonymity and find a community that needs them desperately.

> [!info] Hook 3: Scholarly Expedition
> A [[The Star-Readers|Star-Reader]] contact, or the PCs' own arcane curiosity, brings them to investigate anomalous magical readings in the Fenlands. The readings have been off the charts for months, far beyond normal surge patterns. They arrive during the worst surge in living memory and realize the anomaly is not a measurement error.

> [!info] Hook 4: Personal Connection
> A PC knows [[Brother Lumen]], a former colleague, an old teacher, a friend who once shared their curiosity about the divine. He stopped communicating three months ago. His chapter lead, [[Tessera Vane]], is worried. The PCs have come to find him, and what they find is worse than silence.

## Session 1: Arrival in Fenholm

### Opening Monologue

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The barge rounds a bend in the waterway and the world starts glowing. Not metaphorically. The water beneath the hull shimmers with veins of pale blue light, pulsing like something breathing beneath the surface. The air hums, a sound you feel in your teeth more than hear, and the reeds along the bank lean toward the water as if listening.
>
> Ahead, a mile of wooden platforms rises from the marsh on stilts as tall as houses. Bridges connect platforms to platforms, buildings perch on pilings, walkways zigzag between levels. The whole structure creaks and sways with the rhythm of the water beneath it. Lanterns filled with pale crystals line the railings, their light competing with the glow rising from below.
>
> This is Fenholm. The Stilts. The closest thing the Greymere Fenlands has to a capital, which means a town of six thousand souls balanced on wood and prayer above water that thinks.
>
> Today, the water is thinking loudly. As your barge approaches the docks, a pulse of light rolls through the channels beneath the Lattice, bright enough to cast shadows upward through the gaps between planks. The hull vibrates. A lantern on the dock explodes in a shower of crystal dust. Someone on the platform above shouts, and a dozen hands grab for railings as the entire structure shudders.
>
> Welcome to the Greymere Fenlands. The goddess is in pieces. The pieces are waking up. And the floor is made of wood.

### Structure

**PC Focus:** All PCs equally. Establish the party dynamic and ground them in the fenlands' strangeness.
**Goal:** Make the ley-surge crisis visceral and immediate. Introduce the four key NPCs. End with a choice.
**Threads Advanced:** Ley-surge escalation, political fragmentation, Brother Lumen's disappearance.

### Scene 1: The Surge

The PCs arrive during an active ley-surge. The Lattice is taking damage.

**What happens:**
- Ley-light flares beneath the platforms, bright enough to read by, pulsing in irregular rhythms
- A section of Lower Lattice cracks, three platforms dropping six inches as the pilings warp. Residents scramble to higher platforms with practiced urgency
- A Fencrafter on the Glow Market attempts to dampen the surge. Her spell misfires visibly: intended to calm the water, it instead accelerates the glow, and for three seconds every piece of wood in a fifty-foot radius sprouts pale green shoots before they wither and fall
- Ley-crystal lanterns along the dock overload and shatter in sequence, a chain reaction running east to west across the Upper Lattice

**Information (free):** Surges have always been part of fen life, but they're coming faster and hitting harder. The Lattice has survived for generations. Now it's failing. Three surges this week. The worst anyone can remember.

**DC 12 Arcana:** The surge is not random. It has directionality, like water being pulled toward a drain. Something to the south is drawing the ley-energy.

### Scene 2: The Fenmother's House

[[Maren Duskwater]] receives them at her stilt-house, identifiable by the blue lantern burning in its window. The interior is cluttered with maps, ledgers, and correspondence. She looks like she hasn't slept in days.

**Maren's Welcome:**
- Practical, calculating, immediately testing their usefulness. She doesn't waste time on pleasantries.
- If the PCs are hired escorts (Hook 1): "Your employer's cargo is the least of our problems. But if you can lift heavy things and follow instructions, I can pay you better than Greaves does."
- If fugitives (Hook 2): "I don't care where you're from or what you've done. Can you work?"
- If scholars (Hook 3): "Finally. Someone who might actually understand what's happening. Come in. I'll show you the readings."
- If looking for Lumen (Hook 4): "Brother Lumen. Yes. Sit down. This is going to take longer than you'd like."

**What she reveals:**
- Surges are intensifying beyond anything the [[The Confluence Council|Confluence Council]] has recorded. The Lattice's oldest supports are warping, ironwood pilings growing toward the ley-light beneath the water, pulling the settlement down.
- She has been using [[Cosmology and Magic#Ashite|Ashite]] to stabilize the worst surges, but her supply is running out and the smuggling pipeline from [[Ashcreek]] is under pressure.
- Brother Lumen went to [[Sporeling]] three months ago and stopped communicating. His Star-Reader chapter lead, [[Tessera Vane]], has been sending increasingly urgent messages.
- She needs external help because she cannot leave Fenholm during the crisis.

**DC 14 Insight:** Maren is holding something back about the Ashite. She chose her words carefully around it. (GM: her research revealed Ashite suppresses Fencraft. She hasn't told the Fencrafters.)

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Maren knows the Lattice may need to be abandoned within the year. She has told no one because there is nowhere to relocate. Her Ashite research offers a potential solution to the surge problem, but at the cost of suppressing Fencraft across any settlement it protects. She is calculating whether magical identity is worth more than structural survival, and she hates the math.

### Scene 3: The Confluence Dock

The Council Platform, the largest single platform on the Lattice. The PCs witness a Confluence Council emergency session: representatives from [[Fenholm]], [[Glimwater]], and [[The Drowning Bridge]] arguing about the surge crisis.

**What the PCs observe:**
- **Fenholm's position** (Maren's proxy, Jessa Thornhollow): Stabilize the Lattice first. Practical measures. Pool resources.
- **Glimwater's position** (via runner message): The [[Glimpool]] is expanding. If it reaches Glimwater's inner buildings, the settlement evacuates. They need the Council to prioritize containment.
- **The Drowning Bridge's position** (Torben Greaves's agent): The Sluice infrastructure is failing. Trade collapses without repairs. The economy dies before the magic kills anyone.
- No one agrees on what's causing the surges. No one agrees on priorities. The session ends in frustrated adjournment.

**DC 13 Perception:** [[Old Fenris]], seated at the edge of the platform, has said nothing during the entire session. He is staring south, toward the Deep Fen, with an expression that is not confusion but grief.

### Scene 4: Old Fenris

The ley-line prophet finds the PCs after the Council session. He does not introduce himself. He simply appears beside them on a walkway, an elderly man whose fingertips shimmer faintly with crystalline deposits, whose eyes are pale and unfocused but whose attention is absolute.

**What he says:**
- He asks strange questions: "What did you hear during the surge? Not the sound. The voice beneath it." If any PC has magical ability, he is intensely interested, leaning close, studying their eyes.
- "She's trying to speak. The Web is her voice and it's breaking."
- "The boy in the south thinks he can put her back together. He cannot. But he might teach her how to scream."
- Then he leaves. He does not say goodbye. He walks to the platform edge and steps onto a boat that wasn't there a moment ago.

**DC 15 Insight:** Fenris is not senile. He is translating something vast and inhuman into words too small to hold it. His lucidity comes and goes because what he hears comes and goes.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> "She" is [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]], the shattered Goddess of Magic. "The boy" is Brother Lumen. Old Fenris knows what Lumen is doing at Sporeling because the ley lines told him. He is paralyzed between horror at the cost and understanding that Lumen might be right about the problem, if catastrophically wrong about the solution. His deteriorating lucidity mirrors Nethys's fragmenting consciousness. He is becoming more her and less himself.

### Session 1 Ending

Maren asks the PCs to investigate one of three leads that might explain the intensifying surges. She cannot spare Fenholm personnel. The PCs choose their Session 2 destination:

- **Glimwater** (the magical heart, the expanding Glimpool, the prodigy who hears the goddess)
- **The Drowning Bridge** (the smuggling artery, the secrets beneath the water, the information broker who knows too much)
- **Ashcreek** (the political powder keg, the Dominion garrison, the border that's about to break)

## Session 2: The Branches

### Structure

**PC Focus:** Varies by path. Scholars and mages shine on Path A. Rogues and social characters on Path B. Fighters and diplomats on Path C.
**Goal:** Deepen the world through one of three lenses. All paths converge on the same revelation: something is happening in Sporeling, and Brother Lumen is at the center of it.
**Threads Advanced:** Branch-specific tensions, Lumen's silence, Nethys's fragments.

---

### Path A: Glimwater (Magic & Mystery)

**Scene 1: The Glimpool**

The PCs arrive at [[Glimwater]] to find the settlement in quiet crisis. The [[Glimpool]], half a mile across, its surface traced with luminous veins of raw arcane energy, has expanded thirty feet in every direction over the past year. Expansion is accelerating. Inner buildings are being evacuated.

Meet [[Wick]], the Fencraft prodigy. A teenager with an extraordinary sensitivity to ley-lines, training on the Surge Platforms at the pool's edge. They sense the PCs before seeing them.

**Information (free):** The Glimpool is the densest ley-line surfacing point on the continent. Fencrafters train here because nowhere else teaches you to ride magical chaos this intense. The pool is growing because the ley lines are overcompensating for weakness elsewhere in the [[Cosmology and Magic#The Aetheric Web|Aetheric Web]].

**DC 13 Arcana:** The [[Nethys Stone]], a crystalline outcrop at the pool's edge, has developed hairline fractures. The cracks widen during surges. Old Fenris once said if it breaks, something will be released.

**Scene 2: Wick's Gift**

Wick demonstrates something impossible. During a minor surge, they place their hand on the water and the glow dims. The surge calms. The hum quiets. For thirty seconds, the Glimpool is still.

Then the cost: Wick's eyes go white. They stagger. They forget their own name for a moment, staring at the PCs with an expression of blank, luminous confusion before recognition returns. They laugh it off. "That happens sometimes."

**DC 14 Insight:** It happens more than sometimes. Wick is losing pieces of themselves every time they touch the pool.

**Scene 3: The Pattern**

Wick shares a discovery: the surges aren't random. They've been mapping them, scratching patterns into the Surge Platform wood with a knife. The pattern is directional. Something to the south is pulling the ley-energy, sorting through it, selecting specific frequencies.

"It feels like someone sorting through broken glass," Wick says. "Carefully. Deliberately. Looking for pieces that fit together."

**Transition:** Wick mentions Brother Lumen visited Glimwater months ago, asking Elder Sarenne about fragment convergence, whether separate pieces of a shattered divine consciousness could be guided back toward coherence. He went to [[Sporeling]] and never came back.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Wick is a living junction point where multiple Nethys fragments communicate through a single consciousness. They are simultaneously the most valuable asset to anyone trying to repair the Aetheric Web and the most vulnerable point in the entire system. Brother Lumen does not yet know about Wick, but when he learns, he will want them in Sporeling. Wick channeling a reassembled Nethys is the only safe path, but "safe" is relative.

### Path B: The Drowning Bridge (Smuggling & Secrets)

**Scene 1: The Northern Arch**

The PCs arrive at [[The Drowning Bridge]], a colossal pre-Theomachis stone bridge half-submerged in the marsh, with a settlement built on and around its surviving arches. Meet [[Torben Greaves]] in his headquarters inside the Northern Arch.

Torben is affable, calculating, and more sentimental than he wants anyone to know. Good leather coat, mud under his fingernails, a thin scar from left ear to jaw. His smile doesn't always reach his eyes.

**What Torben reveals:**
- The Sluice is being hit from two directions: [[Sera Gloomhollow]]'s bandits raiding cargo runs, and ley-surge damage to underwater structures that guide barges through the safe channels.
- Trade is collapsing. If the Sluice closes, the fenlands lose their economic lifeline.
- He needs the PCs to escort a cargo run south. Standard pay, danger premium.

**DC 12 Insight:** Torben is testing the PCs. The cargo run is real, but he wants to see how they handle themselves before trusting them with anything important.

**Scene 2: Nettie's Kettle**

Visit [[Nettie Bridgeborn]] at her tea house, perched on a platform bolted to the bridge's eastern balustrade. She serves bog-herb tea and extracts as much as she gives.

Nettie mentions something stirring in the Bridge's sealed lower chambers. Vibrations during surges, synchronized with the ley-pulses. Stone grinding. Water moving in patterns. Once, a voice speaking an unrecognized language. "Don't open that door," she says. "Not yet. Not until you understand what's behind it."

**DC 14 Perception:** Nettie's hands tremble when she discusses the lower chambers. Whatever she found down there years ago changed her.

**Scene 3: The Sluice Run**

The cargo escort goes wrong. Midway down the Sluice, the barge enters a ley-surge zone. The water glows white-hot beneath the hull. Navigation markers are gone, dissolved by surge damage.

**Encounter: Sluice Ambush**
- Sera Gloomhollow's bandits (3 archers on a concealed platform, 2 boarders in a shallow-draft skiff) hit the barge during the surge confusion
- Lvl 1 | Armor: Light (leather) | HP 12 | Speed 6 | Dex +2 | 1d6 piercing (shortbow) or 1d6 slashing (machete)
- **Complication:** A ley-surge hits mid-combat. All spellcasting becomes unreliable: roll 1d6 per spell (1-2: fails; 3-4: altered effect; 5-6: double potency). The surge also illuminates everything beneath the water for three rounds, revealing submerged ruins below the Sluice.
- **Resolution:** The bandits retreat if two are downed. They are not suicidal, just desperate.

**Transition:** After the run, Torben or Nettie reveals that Brother Lumen has been ordering strange supplies through the Sluice, routed to Sporeling. Crystalline focusing arrays, copper wire, quantities of preserved fungal matter. Nobody has seen him in months, but the orders keep coming. Torben's barge captains say Sporeling feels different now. "The people there finish each other's sentences."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Drowning Bridge sits atop a pre-divine ley-line anchor from a civilization predating the Twelve. The crystalline structure in the sealed lower chambers pulses in sync with the surges and is reactivating. Nettie found it years ago and has been managing information flows to prevent anyone from looking too closely. A [[Veil Unbound]] cell has established itself on the Bridge specifically to access these chambers. Do NOT reveal this in the starter. Save it for later.

### Path C: Ashcreek (Dominion Conflict)

**Scene 1: The Garrison**

The PCs arrive at [[Ashcreek]], where the fenlands begin and the Dominion ends. Meet [[Captain Hale Ashmark]] at the grey-stone garrison. Fifty soldiers, orderly barracks, and a wardstone he has conveniently never calibrated to full sensitivity.

Ashmark is decent but under pressure. Orders from [[Ostivaar]] demand he extend tax collection deeper into the fens. He has been stalling with reports about "logistical challenges." His superiors are losing patience.

**What Ashmark reveals:**
- He has maybe two months before Ostivaar sends someone less sympathetic. Or sends orders he cannot refuse.
- The ley-surges are damaging Dominion infrastructure. The garrison's wardstone cracked during last week's surge. His soldiers are frightened of water that glows and air that hums.
- He is honest: "I am a soldier of the Dominion. I follow orders. But I am also not blind. If I push south now, people will die on both sides, and the surges will not care who holds a tax ledger."

**DC 13 Insight:** Ashmark has privately consulted Confluence Council Readers about ley-activity near Ashcreek without reporting this to his superiors. He is trying to understand what he's dealing with before acting.

**Scene 2: The Market Line**

The contested border strip between garrison and fen-town. Dominion-regulated stalls on the north side, unregulated fen stalls on the south. The Grey Creek runs between them, its water stained metallic by [[Cosmology and Magic#Ashite|Ashite]] runoff.

**What the PCs witness:**
- Tension between fen-folk and soldiers. A Dominion quartermaster demanding a fen herbalist submit to inventory inspection. The herbalist's quiet refusal. The soldiers behind the quartermaster reaching for weapons. [[Vedra Coalhand]], the civilian fen-boss, stepping between them with a smile that is not a smile.
- Soldier Pell Oakley, a young conscript, buying something from a fen-girl's stall. He sees the PCs watching and goes pale.

**DC 14 Perception:** Pell is not buying goods. The fen-girl, Lissa, is teaching him something. His fingers move in patterns that mirror her gestures. He is learning Fencraft.

**Scene 3: The Complication**

Choose one based on session tone:

- **The Cinders informant:** Pell's secret Fencraft lessons are about to be exposed. A Cinders operative embedded in the garrison has been watching him. If the PCs intervene, they make an enemy of the Cinders. If they don't, Pell is arrested and Lissa disappears into the fen.
- **Surge damage:** A ley-surge damages Dominion property, cracking the garrison wall and collapsing a watchtower. Hardliners in the garrison demand retaliation against the fenlands. Ashmark must choose between restraint and his soldiers' anger.

**Transition:** Ashmark admits the Dominion's real interest isn't taxes. It's Ashite. And Maren's research. His intelligence suggests she's found a way to use Ashite to stabilize ley-surges, which would make the fenlands genuinely independent, something the Dominion cannot tolerate. He's also heard rumors of something happening in Sporeling that has the Star-Readers worried. "Whatever your friend Lumen is doing down there, it has attracted attention from people who do not investigate things gently."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Elowen Greaves thread connects here. Torben's sister in [[Ostivaar]] leads the [[The Unbound Congregation|Unbound Congregation]], and her unopened letter in Torben's desk contains a plea for help against Cinders infiltration. Ashmark doesn't know about Elowen, but the same Cinders apparatus watching Pell is the one hunting her. This is a cross-region hook for later acts.

## Session 3: Into the Deep Fen

### Structure

**PC Focus:** The whole party. This session is a revelation.
**Goal:** Converge all paths on Sporeling. Reveal the deeper mystery. Present a moral choice that cannot be resolved. Open the campaign outward.
**Threads Advanced:** Brother Lumen's experiment, Nethys's fragments, the Second Silence's local effects.

### Scene 1: Deep Fen Travel

All paths converge on [[Sporeling]]. The journey south through the Deep Fen is dangerous, disorienting, and beautiful.

**Environmental Hazards (choose 2):**

- **Ley-Fog:** Luminescent mist rises from the water, beautiful and blinding. Navigation becomes impossible without a guide or magical sense. DC 13 Survival to maintain direction. On failure, the party loses 1d4 hours and emerges somewhere unexpected. During the fog, PCs hear whispers, fragments of sentences in a language that sounds like mathematics spoken aloud.
- **Surge Pocket:** The party crosses a zone where a ley-surge has frozen in place, energy looping in a standing wave. The water here flows uphill. Light bends wrong. A PC's spell misfires (or a non-magical item behaves strangely: a compass spins, a torch burns blue, a weapon hums). DC 14 Constitution save or suffer disorientation (disadvantage on attacks and checks for 10 minutes).
- **The Aware Marsh:** For a stretch of several hundred yards, the marsh responds to the PCs. Reeds lean toward them. Ripples follow their movement. A pool of water reflects not their faces but a vast eye, pale and luminous, that blinks and is gone. No mechanical effect, only the unsettling certainty that the landscape is watching.

> [!tip] GM Pacing
> The Deep Fen travel should feel like entering a living mind. The further south, the more the environment responds to the PCs' presence. The luminescence intensifies. The whispers become more frequent. Giant fungi tower overhead, their caps glowing with veins of pale light. The beauty is genuine and the wrongness is genuine and they are the same thing.

### Scene 2: Arrival at Sporeling

[[Sporeling]] emerges from the Deep Fen like a dream. A grove of massive fungi, caps spanning twenty feet, their undersides dripping with bioluminescent threads. Buildings grown from living fungal matter rather than built. The mycelial network is visible: pale threads connecting every structure, pulsing faintly with ley-light.

**What's wrong:**
- The residents are hospitable. Warm. Welcoming. They offer food, shelter, directions. Something is off.
- They finish each other's sentences. A woman begins a thought and a man across the grove completes it. A child laughs at a joke no one told aloud.
- They share knowledge they shouldn't have. A resident greets a PC by name before introductions. Another mentions a detail from the PCs' journey that no one could have communicated ahead.
- Their skin has a faint luminescence, visible in shadow. Their eyes reflect light that isn't there.

**DC 12 Insight:** The residents are not pretending. They genuinely do not notice the strangeness. This is normal to them. It has been normal for months.

**DC 14 Arcana:** The mycelial network is not just a root system. It is a biological extension of the Aetheric Web, grown from [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]]'s essence. The residents are connected to it. Information flows through the network like thought through a mind.

### Scene 3: Brother Lumen

[[Brother Lumen]] receives the PCs in his workshop, a converted fungal chamber threaded with copper wire, crystalline arrays, and instruments of his own design. He is brilliant, charismatic, and consumed. His eyes have the same luminescence as the residents, but brighter. His speech patterns are precise, rapid, infectious.

**What Lumen reveals:**
- The Aetheric Web is failing. Not slowly, not eventually, now. The [[Second Silence]] is not natural entropy. Something is actively siphoning the ley-line energy that keeps magic functional across the continent.
- Nethys built the Web. Nethys's fragments retain the knowledge to repair it. But the fragments are scattered, incoherent, degrading.
- He is reconnecting them. The mycelial network serves as a biological conduit, linking fragment to fragment through Sporeling's root system. It works. He has achieved partial coherence. The fragments are beginning to communicate.
- The cost is the residents. The network requires living minds as junction points. The residents volunteered, most of them. They are becoming less individual and more... connected. Shared thoughts. Shared memories. Shared identity. They are becoming less themselves and more her.
- He insists the cost is acceptable. "Eight hundred people, or every mage on the continent loses their magic within a decade. Every ward fails. Every healing spell stops working. Every enchanted bridge, every weather-barrier, every artifact that keeps the dead gods' influence contained, all of it fails. Eight hundred people, or the world."

**DC 15 Insight:** Lumen believes this completely. He is not lying. He is not evil. He may not be wrong about the problem. The question is whether his solution is the only one, or whether the cost makes it monstrous regardless.

### Scene 4: The Demonstration

Lumen demonstrates a fragment convergence. He activates the network. The mycelial threads blaze with light. The grove hums.

**What happens:**
- Every resident in Sporeling goes still simultaneously. Their eyes glow. Their lips move in unison, whispering in a language that sounds like the ley-fog whispers from the Deep Fen.
- The PCs experience a shared vision: a vast web of light spanning the continent, beautiful and complex, with dark patches spreading like rot. A woman's face, enormous and fractured, each piece trying to see, to speak, to warn. The vision carries a single overwhelming emotion: urgency.
- One resident, an elderly man called Elder Moss, does not come back fully. His eyes remain luminous. He sits down slowly and begins speaking in a voice that is not his own, reciting what sounds like arcane formulae. Then he stops. He smiles. He does not recognize his own name.

**Lumen's response:** "It happens. The network is imperfect. But Elder Moss was eighty-three. His mind was already fragile. The younger residents recover fully." A pause. "Usually."

### Scene 5: The Choice (Not Resolved)

The PCs now understand the stakes. They cannot fix this in Session 3. They can only decide what to do with what they know.

**The facts:**
- The Aetheric Web IS dying. Lumen is correct about the problem.
- Lumen's method works, partially, at devastating cost to individual identity.
- Nethys's fragments are genuinely reaching toward coherence. Lumen is helping something that wants to happen.
- The residents are changing. Some are losing themselves. The process is accelerating.
- Lumen will not stop voluntarily. He believes the alternative is worse.

**The question:** Who do the PCs tell? The answer shapes the campaign.

- **Maren Duskwater** (pragmatist, might support Lumen if it saves Fenholm, might demand control of the experiment)
- **The Star-Readers** (might shut Lumen down, losing the only active lead on saving the Web)
- **The Confluence Council** (would demand public deliberation, buying time but alerting every faction)
- **The Dominion** (would weaponize or destroy the experiment, and justify military intervention)
- **No one** (keep the secret, return to Sporeling later, try to find a better way)

The PCs carry a secret that every faction would kill for.

### Cliffhanger Ending

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The barge north feels different. The glow of the Deep Fen fades behind you, but not entirely. You can still feel it: a hum in the back of your skull, a warmth in your fingertips, the lingering echo of a voice too vast to understand speaking in a language made of light.
>
> Sporeling is behind you. Its people are still there, still smiling, still finishing each other's sentences, still glowing faintly in the dark. Brother Lumen is still there, copper wire and crystal arrays and the absolute certainty of a man who has calculated the acceptable losses and decided the math works.
>
> Elder Moss is still there, sitting in a fungal chamber, speaking in a voice that is not his own about things no mortal mind should contain. He smiled when you left. He did not know your names. He did not know his own.
>
> Ahead, Fenholm's lanterns flicker on the horizon, warm and ordinary and fragile. Maren Duskwater will want a report. Old Fenris will want the truth. The Confluence Council will want answers. None of them are ready for what you've seen.
>
> The water beneath the barge pulses once, bright and sudden, and in the light you see, just for a moment, the outline of something vast beneath the surface. A face. A web. A mind in pieces, reaching.
>
> Then the light fades, and you are left with the silence, and the knowledge, and the question of what you do with both.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **The Second Silence Connection:** The ley-surge intensification is partly [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s doing. She is actively siphoning ley-line energy to weaken the barriers holding her, destabilizing the Web to accelerate the Second Silence. The fenlands' surges are the Web overcompensating for the drain, like blood rushing to a wound. Within a year, surges will make the Pools uninhabitable. Within two, the overcompensation exhausts the local fragments entirely and reverses catastrophically, turning the most magically saturated zone on the continent into the largest dead zone.
>
> **Brother Lumen's Experiment:** Could work, but at catastrophic cost. He does not understand the full implications. Partial reconstitution of Nethys would reshape magic across the continent in unpredictable ways. Full reconstitution might restore the Web, but Nethys has been fragmented for centuries. What reassembles may not be the goddess who built the Web. It may be something else wearing her face.
>
> **Wick Is the Key:** The PCs don't know this yet. Wick is the only person who might channel a reassembled Nethys safely, a living junction point where multiple fragments already communicate through a single consciousness. If Lumen learns about Wick, he will want them in Sporeling. Protecting Wick while using their gift is a campaign-long tension.
>
> **Old Fenris Knows:** His deteriorating lucidity mirrors Nethys's fragmenting consciousness. He knows what Lumen is doing because the ley lines told him. He is paralyzed between horror at the cost and understanding that the problem is real. His silence is not ignorance. It is grief.
>
> **The Elowen Greaves Thread:** Torben's sister in the Dominion leads the Unbound Congregation. Her unopened letter is a cross-region hook connecting the fenlands to Ostivaar's political upheaval.
>
> **The Drowning Bridge's Sealed Chambers:** Contain pre-Theomachis technology from a civilization predating the Twelve. The crystalline structure within is a ley-line anchor that is reactivating in sync with the surges. Do NOT reveal this in the starter. Save it.
>
> **Continental Echoes:** The surges here mirror the Cooling in [[Emberveil]], the Trench expansion in [[The Hollowed Reach]], the fragment migration in [[Ironmarch]], the Cycle acceleration in [[The Thornwild]]. All are symptoms of the same disease: the Second Silence, accelerated by Serith's sabotage.
