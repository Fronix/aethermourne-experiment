# Campaign Starter: The Unwritten Lands

*You crossed an ocean no one was supposed to cross. The land on the other side was not waiting for you. It was watching.*

---

## Overview

A ready-to-run 3-session arc for a campaign beginning on [[The Veilshore]], the eastern coast of [[The Unwritten Lands]]. The PCs arrive on one of the first ships through [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]]'s weakened barrier, make landfall at [[Tidecross]], and are immediately caught between first-contact tensions with the [[The Oldborn Kingdoms|Oldborn]], supernatural threats from [[The Primordia|the interior]], and the discovery that someone has sabotaged their ship so they cannot leave.

**Themes:** Colonial contact, alien civilizations, the unknown
**Tone:** Frontier awe and existential dread. Everything is beautiful and nothing is safe.
**Level:** 1-3

---

## Session 1: Landfall

### Opening Monologue

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> Three weeks at sea. Three weeks of storms that rose from nothing and vanished without warning, of tides that ignored the moon, of nights where the water glowed with colors that have no names. The sailors stopped praying to [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]] on the seventh day. Not from faithlessness. From the growing certainty that she could hear them, and that what she felt about their passage was not approval.
>
> But the barrier held open. Barely. The ship shuddered through a wall of force that tasted like grief, and then the ocean changed. Calmer. Darker. The water here is black, not from depth but from composition, volcanic sand suspended in every wave, turning the sea into liquid obsidian.
>
> And then the coast. Black sand beaches stretching in both directions beneath cliffs of dark grey stone. A harbor, if you can call it that, wooden docks built around massive stone pilings that dwarf anything on the known continent. The pilings are ancient, carved with geometric patterns that predate [[The Twelve|the gods you know]]. Someone was here long before you.
>
> Someone is still here.
>
> Welcome to Tidecross. You are among the first outlanders to set foot on the Unwritten Lands. The land has noticed.

### Structure

**PC Focus:** All PCs equally. Establish wonder and unease.
**Goal:** Ground the PCs in Tidecross, introduce Oldborn tension, establish they cannot easily leave.
**Threads Advanced:** Yvenne's barrier, Oldborn-outlander tension, ship sabotage.

### Scene 1: The Docks

The PCs disembark into [[Tidecross]], a settlement of roughly 2,400 that feels like a pressure cooker. Three flags fly over the harbor: [[Concord of Tides]], [[The Ashen Dominion]], and a third, obsidian-black with a white spiral, that no one from the known continent recognizes.

**Key NPCs on the Docks:**
- [[Captain Erys Vane]], de facto harbormaster. Weathered, exhausted, sharp-tongued. She greets new arrivals with practiced efficiency and barely concealed anxiety. "Welcome to the edge of the world. Rules are simple: don't touch Oldborn property, don't enter the Fernwald, don't start fights you can't finish. Market closes at sunset. After dark, stay off the black sand."
- An [[The Oldborn Kingdoms|Oldborn]] observer stands at the edge of the docks, motionless. Tall, umber skin with geometric patterns visible beneath the surface, black eyes like polished obsidian. This is [[Vosh]], and they are watching.

- **Atmosphere:** Boomtown chaos. Merchants unloading cargo, missionaries preaching, smugglers negotiating in the Wrack tunnels beneath the cliffs. But beneath it: tension. Every outlander glances at the Oldborn observer. Every Oldborn structure (the massive docks, the carved pilings) is a reminder that this land belongs to someone else.
- **Information (free):** Tidecross was built around Oldborn docks that predate outlander arrival by millennia. The Oldborn tolerate the settlement but have not welcomed it. An observer named Vosh rotates through to watch the outlanders.
- **Information (DC 12 Streetwise):** The black sand is strange. Footprints vanish instantly. Tools left on it overnight go missing. People hear whispers. The market closes at sunset for a reason.

### Scene 2: The Crossroads Market

The heart of Tidecross, where all three factions grudgingly trade. Built on the black sand that gives the settlement its uneasy character.

- **Atmosphere:** Chaotic, multilingual, fascinating. Concord merchants hawking salted fish and iron tools. Dominion missionaries distributing pamphlets about [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]]. Oldborn traders (rare) offering resonance crystals and Fernwald herbs at prices that make outlanders blink.
- **NPC Introduction:** [[Salice]], smuggler queen of the Wrack, is browsing a stall with theatrical disinterest. If the PCs look interesting, she approaches. "New faces. Good. Old faces are getting boring and predictable."
- **Event:** Midway through the scene, the black sand *moves*. A shape presses upward from below, roughly humanoid, before subsiding. Nearby merchants scatter. Vane curses. Vosh watches without expression.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The black sand is a Primordial sensory apparatus. The Oldborn Docks beneath Tidecross are a listening post, and the sand is its surface layer. The shapes are the sand *analyzing* the concentration of divine residue from outlander presence. The Oldborn know this. They are waiting to see if the outlanders figure it out or respond with violence. Violence damages the docks and guarantees war.

### Scene 3: The Ship

That evening, the PCs' ship captain finds them. He is pale and furious.

**The Sabotage:**
- The ship's keel has been scored with acid in a pattern that weakens the hull. Not enough to sink it in harbor, but it will break apart in open water.
- The damage is below the waterline, discovered during routine inspection.
- The acid pattern is geometric, precise. Not random vandalism.
- The captain has no means to repair it in Tidecross. He needs materials that don't exist here, or time he doesn't have (Yvenne's barrier could close again at any moment).

**The PCs are stranded.** At least until the ship is repaired or another arrives.

**DC 14 Investigation (the acid pattern):** The geometry matches Oldborn design language. But DC 16 Insight: the execution is sloppy, imprecise. An Oldborn artisan would have done this perfectly. Someone *imitated* Oldborn work.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The sabotage was performed by a [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] agent aboard the ship, posing as a merchant. The agent, Jessalyn Crowe, wants to ensure outlanders remain on the Veilshore to increase divine residue concentration, which amplifies the micro-fractures in Yvenne's seal (and thus [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s ability to whisper through). The Oldborn-style acid pattern is meant to provoke conflict between outlanders and the Oldborn, serving the Veil Unbound's interests on both continents.

## Session 2: First Contact

### Structure

**PC Focus:** Social and investigative PCs lead. Combat PCs get spotlight in Scene 3.
**Goal:** Deepen Oldborn relations, investigate the sabotage, encounter the Primordia's influence.
**Threads Advanced:** Oldborn diplomacy, Veil Unbound presence, Primordial phenomena.

### Scene 1: Vosh Speaks

The morning after the sabotage, Vosh approaches the PCs. This is unusual. Vosh rarely initiates contact with outlanders.

**Vosh's Message:**
- Spoken slowly, precisely, in accented Common. "Your vessel was damaged. We did not do this. We would not need to. Your ocean goddess could close her hand and you would never leave."
- Vosh is offended by the implication (the acid pattern's Oldborn styling). This was designed to blame the Oldborn. Someone wants conflict.
- Vosh offers a limited exchange: answer one question from the PCs, in return for one answer from the PCs.

**Vosh's Question:** "Your gods are dead or dying. What do you intend to do about it?"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> This is a test. The Oldborn already know about the Theomachis, the Second Silence, the failing divine order. They are assessing whether outlanders understand their own situation. An honest answer ("we don't know" or "we're trying to find out") earns respect. A boastful or ignorant answer confirms the Sealed Gate faction's position that outlanders are too dangerous to engage.

**What Vosh Will Share (if asked about the sabotage):** "The acid is called *vael-thrin*. It is made from a mineral found in the Tidemark Cliffs. We use it for sculpture. An outlander could obtain it from the caves, or from your own smugglers." This points toward the Wrack.

### Scene 2: The Wrack

Following the acid trail leads to the Wrack, [[Salice]]'s smuggling network in the Tidemark Cliff caves.

**Salice's Deal:**
- She knows about *vael-thrin*. She sells it. She sold a quantity two weeks ago to a Concord merchant named Jessalyn Crowe who arrived on the PCs' ship.
- She'll share this for a price: the PCs owe her a favor. Unspecified. Redeemable later. "I'm a businesswoman. Information is worth exactly what it costs to get it."
- If pressed (DC 15 Persuasion): Salice adds that Crowe has been meeting with someone at the [[Tidecaller Mirenne|Tidecaller Shrine]] at odd hours. Not during services.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Salice genuinely doesn't know Crowe is Veil Unbound. She assumed Crowe wanted the acid for artistic purposes (a common outlander hobby). Salice's favor will be called in during a later session, likely the Smuggler's Dilemma quest from the quest board.

### Scene 3: The Fernwald Incursion

While the PCs investigate, something emerges from the [[The Veilshore#The Fernwald|Fernwald]].

**The Event:** A section of Fernwald at the settlement's edge *moves*. Fern fronds fifty feet tall bend toward Tidecross. The air fills with a green, electric smell. Animals panic. The black sand begins rippling in expanding circles.

**Encounter: Gyre-Wraith**

A single [[Gyre-Wraiths|Gyre-Wraith]] drifts out of the Fernwald, a shifting translucent shape that refuses to resolve. Humanoid, then angular, then liquid. Colors that have no names ripple across its surface. Looking directly at it causes eyes to water and thoughts to stutter.

- Lvl 5 | Armor: H (shifting form) | HP 30 | Speed 4 | Str +3 | Dex +3 | Will +5 | 1d10 dissolution (touch, target's equipment makes DC 13 save or warps)
- **Dissolution Aura:** Creatures within 2 spaces at start of turn: DC 12 Will save or take 1d4 psychic damage as body "forgets" its shape momentarily
- **Immune** to non-Primordially-attuned physical weapons and Aetheric (divine-based) magic
- **Vulnerable** to Primordial pact magic (the PCs won't have this yet)
- **Weakness:** Structured sound (music, rhythmic chanting, mathematical recitation) forces it to pause, unable to act for 1 round. DC 13 Performance or relevant skill.

**The Complication:** Conventional weapons pass through it. Divine magic amplifies it. The PCs cannot kill this thing with anything they have.

**Resolution Options:**
1. **Structured sound** drives it back temporarily (buys time)
2. **Vosh intervenes** if the PCs are clearly outmatched, speaking in Oldborn tongue, *negotiating* with the Wraith. It recedes into the Fernwald. Vosh collapses from the effort.
3. **Pact-Weaver arrival:** An Oldborn [[The Oldborn Kingdoms#Pact-Weavers|Pact-Weaver]] from [[Keth'vaal]] arrives (summoned by Vosh earlier) and dismisses the Wraith through a complex negotiation ritual that the PCs can observe but not yet understand.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Gyre-Wraith was drawn by the increased concentration of divine residue from the ship's arrival, exactly what the Veil Unbound agent wanted. More outlanders = more divine energy = more Wraith incursions = more pressure on the Oldborn to expel or destroy the settlement. The Veil Unbound benefits from chaos on both continents.

## Session 3: The Saboteur

### Structure

**PC Focus:** The whole party. Investigation culminates, alliances form.
**Goal:** Unmask the saboteur, confront the Veil Unbound's presence, establish campaign stakes.
**Threads Advanced:** Veil Unbound exposure, Yvenne's seal, Oldborn alliance potential.

### Scene 1: The Tidecaller Shrine

Following Salice's tip, the PCs investigate the Tidecaller Shrine on the northern cliffs where [[Tidecaller Mirenne]] maintains daily rites to [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]].

**The Shrine:** Walls weeping seawater. The air tastes of salt and something else, something that makes the back of the throat itch. Mirenne performs rites that are barely holding.

**Mirenne's Confession:**
- She has noticed someone visiting at night. She assumed it was a faithful outlander seeking private prayer.
- The shrine's condition is worsening. The seawater tastes of tears. She believes Yvenne is trying to communicate, but the message is garbled by pain or interference.
- She shows the PCs a section of wall where the seawater has etched a symbol: a spiral within a spiral. DC 13 Religion: this is not Yvenne's symbol. DC 15 Arcana: it is associated with [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], the imprisoned goddess.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The shrine is a breach point. Yvenne's weakening seal creates micro-fractures along coastlines where her influence is strong. The seawater is not Yvenne manifesting, it is the ocean becoming a conduit for Serith's whispers. Jessalyn Crowe has been performing amplification rituals here at night, widening the fracture. Serith's voice can now reach the Unwritten Lands, a terrifying escalation.

### Scene 2: Cornering Crowe

With evidence from the Wrack (acid purchase), the shrine (nighttime visits), and the ship (sabotage pattern), the PCs can confront Jessalyn Crowe.

**Finding Crowe:** She is lodged in the market district, posing as a textile merchant. DC 12 Investigation to locate her stall.

**Confrontation:**
- Crowe does not panic. She is calm, committed, and genuinely believes in what she is doing.
- If confronted with evidence: "You think I'm the villain? I'm trying to save everyone. The divine order is collapsing. The prison is breaking. When it does, Serith offers a new beginning. All I'm doing is ensuring the transition happens cleanly."
- She will not fight unless cornered. If cornered:

**Encounter: Jessalyn Crowe**
- Lvl 3 | Armor: None | HP 20 | Speed 6 | Str +0 | Dex +3 | Will +4 | 1d6 piercing (concealed blade) or 1d8 cold (Serith's whisper, 6 space range, DC 13 Will save or target hears Serith's voice and loses next action)
- **Serith's Gift:** Once per encounter, can cause all seawater within 6 spaces to surge violently (DC 14 Dex save or knocked prone and dragged 2 spaces toward nearest water source)
- **Escape Plan:** Smoke pellet + sprint toward cliffs. If she reaches the water, she Sleepwalks into the ocean and is gone.

**What Crowe Carries:**
- Coded correspondence with Veil Unbound cells on the known continent
- A vial of concentrated *vael-thrin* (enough to sabotage another ship)
- A small shard of dark stone, warm to the touch, that whispers when held (Serith's prison fragment, similar to the one found in the Hollowed Reach)

### Scene 3: The Aftermath

**Vane's Reaction:** Relief and fury. She suspected something but lacked proof. She asks the PCs to stay, formally, as "Tidecross's first intelligence asset that isn't a smuggler or a spy."

**Vosh's Reaction:** Measured respect. The PCs identified the true threat and did not blame the Oldborn. This matters. Vosh says: "You are not what we expected. This does not mean you are welcome. It means you are worth watching more carefully."

**Mirenne's Reaction:** Terrified. The Serith symbol on the shrine wall means the prison is failing faster than anyone realized. If Serith's whispers can reach the Unwritten Lands, nowhere is safe.

### Cliffhanger Ending

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The sun sets over Tidecross, painting the black sand in shades of copper and blood. The Fernwald's fifty-foot ferns sway in a wind that carries the smell of green lightning. The Oldborn docks hum with a frequency you can feel in your teeth.
>
> Your ship sits in the harbor, keel scored, unable to sail. Yvenne's barrier shimmers on the horizon, a wall of force between you and everything you knew. It flickers. Just for a moment. Then holds.
>
> In your possession: a shard of dark stone that whispers in a goddess's voice. Coded letters from a cult that spans two continents. And the growing certainty that you were not meant to come here by accident. Someone, something, wanted outlanders on the Unwritten Lands.
>
> Vosh stands at the edge of the docks, watching the horizon with those black obsidian eyes, and speaks without turning: "Your ocean goddess weakens. When she falls, the barrier falls. And then your continent and ours will no longer be separated by water. They will be separated by nothing."
>
> The black sand ripples beneath your feet. The land is listening. It has not yet decided what you are.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Where This Goes:** The PCs are now embedded in Tidecross with evidence of the Veil Unbound's multi-continental operation. The sabotaged ship forces them to engage with local politics rather than fleeing. The Gyre-Wraith encounter has shown them that conventional weapons and divine magic are insufficient here, they need to learn Primordial methods, which means engaging with the Oldborn. And the prison shard connects their story to the Hollowed Reach and the Ashen Dominion arcs. The Second Silence is not a distant threat. It is the water rising around their ankles.
>
> **Key Threads for Session 4+:**
> - Repairing the ship (requires Oldborn materials or knowledge)
> - The Veil Unbound's broader operation in Tidecross (Archive Heist quest)
> - Vosh's growing willingness to share Oldborn knowledge (conditional on PC behavior)
> - The Fernwald and what it teaches those who enter respectfully
> - The Tidecaller Shrine breach and Serith's expanding reach
