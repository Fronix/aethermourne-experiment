# Campaign Starter: The Verdant Marches

*The forest is alive. Not metaphorically. It has opinions about you.*

---

## Overview

A ready-to-run 3-session arc for a campaign beginning in [[Roothold]], heart of [[The Verdant Marches]]. The PCs arrive during a political crisis, the unification vote, and are drawn into the deeper truth: something is corrupting [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s root-network from below, and the jungle is beginning to turn.

**Themes:** Political fracture, ecological horror, the tension between unity and autonomy
**Tone:** Lush beauty hiding rot. A jungle that loves you and will eat you without contradiction.
**Level:** 1-3

---

## Session 1: Root and Branch

### Opening Monologue

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The Grandfather rises eight hundred feet above you, a tree so vast it has its own weather. Roothold clings to its roots and lower trunk in three chaotic tiers: farms in the Lower Roots, markets and homes in the Middle, and the Moot Hall in the Upper Roots, where the Marchwardens pretend to govern a land that has never been governed.
>
> You have climbed the Scarwall passage, or followed the river-roads, or descended from the canopy paths, and Roothold has opened itself to you the way the forest opens itself to everything: with indifference and generosity in equal measure. The air is warm, wet, and smells of growth so intense it borders on rot. Bioluminescent fungi pulse along the root-bridges like a heartbeat. Somewhere above, a Greensinger is singing, and the branches are listening.
>
> But the singing sounds strained tonight. And the fungi are pulsing in patterns you have never seen before: inward, toward the Grandfather's trunk, as if the tree is pulling its light close. Holding its breath.
>
> Something is wrong in the roots.

### Structure

**PC Focus:** All PCs equally. Establish the party dynamic and the world's living strangeness.
**Goal:** Ground the PCs in the Marches' political and ecological crisis, introduce the factions, create immediate stakes.
**Threads Advanced:** Unification crisis, shadow-root corruption, Tessara Wildbloom's disappearance.

### Scene 1: The Traderoot

The PCs arrive in Roothold's marketplace, a series of platforms suspended between root-arches, connected by vine-bridges. The market is tense. Clan banners hang everywhere, some torn, some defaced. A knot of people argue beneath a Marchwarden's standard.

- **Atmosphere:** Political anxiety wearing the face of commerce. Merchants selling fast, packing early. Children chasing glowing insects through the root-bridges. A Greensinger Seedling tending a wounded vine that is bleeding amber sap.
- **Information (free):** The Marchwardens are voting on unification in two weeks. [[Kaelith Thornborn]] says the Marches need a central authority to survive. [[Draven Ironbark]] says centralized power is a cage. The clans are splitting.
- **Information (DC 12 Persuasion, merchants):** The Greensingers are frightened. [[Tessara Wildbloom]], one of their Deep Roots, went into communion three weeks ago and has not come back. [[Elder Rosk]] is refusing to explain.
- **Information (DC 14 Streetwise, locals):** Strange things in the deep forest. Animals displaced, moving outward. Plants growing inward instead of toward light. A patrol found a clearing where every flower was blooming face-down, toward the earth.

### Scene 2: The Grove's Edge

The PCs' lodging (a root-hollow inn called The Amber Cup) is near the Greensinger Grove's boundary. That night, they notice something: the bioluminescent fungi along the Grove's edge are flickering in patterns that seem purposeful. Green-gold light, then dark, then a pulse of something that is not green-gold but grey-blue, the color of deep ocean.

A Greensinger Seedling named Fen approaches them, nervous, breaking protocol.

**Fen's Plea:**
- The Grove is sealed. Rosk has forbidden anyone from entering the inner circle.
- Tessara has been in communion for three weeks. Her body is present but her mind is gone.
- The soil around her body has darkened. The branches above her are curling inward, blackening at the edges. There is a faint smell of salt where no salt should be.
- Fen is terrified and wants outsiders to help because the Greensingers are too afraid to act.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Fen is genuine. His fear is real. Rosk sealed the Grove because he does not understand what is happening to Tessara and fears that interference could kill her or spread the corruption. He is also protecting the Grove's secrets. If the PCs approach Rosk directly, he will test them before trusting them.

### Scene 3: The Corrupted Glade

Whether led by Fen or investigating on their own, the PCs discover a small corrupted zone at the Grove's edge: a clearing where the forest has gone wrong. Roots spiral in impossible patterns. Fungi grow inward. The air smells of sap and salt water. A faint whisper threads through the undergrowth, not words but a feeling: *nothing grows forever. everything returns to the dark.*

**Encounter: Sorrow-Rooted (Nimble)**

A creature emerges from the corrupted undergrowth: something that was once a forest cat, now a horror of bark-flesh and flowering bone. It is in pain. It attacks anything that moves.

- 1 [[Thornwild Predators#Sorrow-Rooted|Sorrow-Rooted]]
- Lvl 4 | Armor M | HP 55 | Speed 6 | Str +3 | Dex +2 | Will +1 | vine-lash 1d8+3 bludgeoning + thorn-bite 1d6 piercing
- **Grief Aura:** Will DC 13 or disadvantage on attacks (3 space radius)
- **Weakness:** Fire prevents regeneration. Radiant deals double.

**Resolution:** Killing or calming the Sorrow-Rooted draws [[Elder Rosk]]'s attention. He appears at the clearing's edge, ancient, moss-covered, eyes sharp with fear and assessment. If the PCs handled the creature with respect (attempted to calm it, expressed grief for what it once was, or recognized the corruption rather than just hacking at it), Rosk speaks to them. If they were merely violent, he watches silently and leaves.

## Session 2: The Vote and the Shadow

### Structure

**PC Focus:** Social PCs shine in faction negotiations. Investigation PCs dig into the corruption.
**Goal:** Introduce the three swing-vote Marchwardens, deepen the corruption mystery, force the PCs to navigate politics.
**Threads Advanced:** Unification vote, Cinder interference, Marrow Bogs expansion.

### Scene 1: The Moot Hall

[[Kaelith Thornborn]] summons the PCs to the Upper Roots. The Moot Hall is a natural amphitheater formed by the Grandfather's root-crown, open to the sky. Twelve seats for twelve Marchwardens. The tension is physical.

**Kaelith's Request:**
- She has four confirmed votes. Needs seven. Three Marchwardens are undecided.
- She knows the PCs are outsiders, which makes them valuable: no clan loyalties, no agenda (she hopes).
- She wants them to visit the three swing votes and assess their concerns. Not persuade. Assess.
- She will not promise payment. She promises that what is happening in the deep forest will kill them all if the Marches cannot act together.

**The Three Swing Votes:**
1. **[[Senna Deepmoss]]** (Southern Cradle): Wants evidence, not prophecy. Will support unification if shown scientific proof the corruption is real and external.
2. **[[Brannoc Stillwater]]** (Stillwater): Wants trade preserved. Secretly selling Marrow-Water to Reach merchants without Greensinger approval.
3. **[[Torvin Ashwalker]]** (Ashwalker's Post): Wants the Scarwall border defended. Does not care about politics, only survival.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Draven Ironbark]] is being fed fabricated intelligence by [[The Cinders|Cinder]] agents about Kaelith's "true ambitions." A dead drop in the northern forest contains the evidence. If the PCs find it and expose it, Ironbark's opposition softens dramatically, but the Cinder agent will try to destroy the evidence rather than be captured.

### Scene 2: The Researcher

If the PCs visit Senna, she directs them to [[Vael Sundren]] at [[Mirewatch]], who has been studying the Marrow Bogs expansion. Vael can be found closer, at the Traderoot, purchasing supplies.

**Vael's Data:**
- The Bogs are expanding. New areas saturated where none existed before.
- Marrow-Water is growing more potent. New secondary effect: a quiet despair, a whisper that nothing matters.
- Two Green Draught subjects have begun sleepwalking toward the Bogs. One did not want to be stopped.
- She has data. She needs someone to help her collect samples from the newest expansion zone, where the concentration is too dangerous for her team alone.

**Information (DC 14 Knowledge):** The secondary despair effect is not natural. Marrow-Water has never carried emotional influence before. Something external is contaminating it.

### Scene 3: The Border

If the PCs visit Torvin at [[Ashwalker's Post]], they find the Scarwall growing thicker and more aggressive than anyone remembers. Three border patrols have been injured. A Dominion caravan was cocooned 20 feet up in thorns, passengers alive but trapped.

**Encounter: The Scarwall Probes**

While the PCs help free the caravan, they discover what the Scarwall is reacting to: a Cinder scout team probing the border, well-equipped, mapping Marches defenses.

- 3 Cinder Scouts: Lvl 2 | Armor M | HP 20 | Speed 6 | Str +1 | Dex +3 | Will +1 | short sword 1d6+3 piercing
- One carries a corrupted Ashite shard that smells of salt and despair (DC 13 Knowledge: this Ashite has been contaminated with something that is not Solvaen's).

**Resolution:** Capturing the scouts provides evidence of Dominion aggression. The corrupted Ashite shard connects the border threat to the corruption in the deep forest, the first tangible proof that these crises share a source.

## Session 3: The Root of It

### Structure

**PC Focus:** The whole party. This session is the crucible.
**Goal:** Escalate the corruption, force a choice between political action and direct intervention, end on a cliffhanger.
**Threads Advanced:** Tessara rescue, shadow-root exposure, unification vote climax.

### Scene 1: Rosk Speaks

Elder Rosk summons the PCs to the Grove. He has decided to trust them, or at least to use them. He is running out of options.

**Rosk's Truth:**
- Tessara went into deep communion to investigate the corruption and did not come back. Her consciousness is trapped in the root-network, held by something dark, something that whispers and waits.
- The corruption is spreading. It has reached the Grandfather's outermost roots. If it reaches the Greensinger Grove, Rosk's communion, the deepest in the world, becomes a vector for whatever is causing this.
- He cannot go in after her. His communion is too deep. If the dark thing catches him, it catches the Marches.
- He needs the PCs to enter a guided communion and find Tessara. He can send them in, but he cannot protect them once they are inside.

**The Risk:** Communion is a psychic experience. Combat is metaphorical. The enemy is not a monster but doubt, despair, and the seductive promise that surrender is peace.

### Scene 2: The Root-Walk

The PCs enter communion (Rosk's ritual, requiring physical contact with the Grandfather's root). The experience is visceral: they feel themselves spreading through the earth, awareness fragmenting through miles of root-network, sensing the forest's health like a physician sensing a pulse.

**The Journey:**
- **Layer 1 (Healthy Forest):** Green warmth. The forest knows them. Welcoming but vast.
- **Layer 2 (Transition):** The warmth fades. Roots here are grey and brittle. The forest's awareness is pulling inward, retreating.
- **Layer 3 (Corruption Zone):** Salt smell. Darkness threading through roots like black veins. A whisper: *nothing grows forever. everything returns to the dark. this is not cruelty. this is the way of things.*

**Finding Tessara:** She is at the heart of the corruption zone, her consciousness tangled in shadow-roots, slowly being read by the dark presence. She is still aware, still fighting, but losing coherence. She has critical intelligence: the corruption originates from beneath the earth, from the direction of the ocean, and it tastes of something that is not [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]].

**Encounter: The Shadow-Root**

The dark presence notices the PCs. It does not attack. It asks them a question, in a voice that sounds like their own: *"Why do you fight what is natural? Trees fall. Forests burn. Gods die. Why is your ending different?"*

If they answer with defiance, conviction, or genuine uncertainty (not bravado), they can pull Tessara free. The shadow-root lashes out:

- Shadow-Root Tendril: Will save DC 14 to resist psychic grapple. Failure: 2d6 psychic damage and one round of paralysis (overwhelmed by despair). Success: the tendril recoils. Three successful saves from different PCs frees Tessara.
- **If the PCs fail:** They are ejected from communion. Tessara remains trapped. Rosk's face is grey. The corruption advances.
- **If the PCs succeed:** Tessara is freed. She collapses, conscious but weak. Her first words: *"It comes from the ocean. It tastes of salt and silence. It is not Thyrea. It is something else. Something imprisoned."*

### Scene 3: The Vote

The Moot convenes. Twelve Marchwardens. The PCs' actions determine the vote:

- **If they brought Vael's evidence to Senna:** She votes for unification.
- **If they exposed the Cinder dead drop:** Ironbark's opposition fractures. Two of his supporters abstain.
- **If they freed the Dominion caravan and captured the scouts:** Torvin votes for unification with conditions (border protection guaranteed).
- **If they rescued Tessara:** Her testimony sways undecided Marchwardens. Rosk speaks publicly for the first time in decades.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The vote does not need to succeed for the campaign to continue. A fractured Marches is harder but not hopeless. What matters is that the PCs have seen the corruption, have Tessara's intelligence about the ocean origin, and understand that the Marches' political crisis and ecological crisis share a single cause. The thread now leads to the [[The Hollowed Reach|Hollowed Reach]] and the Abyssal Trench.

### Cliffhanger Ending

> [!quote] Read Aloud
> The vote is cast. The Marchwardens file out in silence, some relieved, some furious, some already planning their next move. The Grandfather's branches creak overhead, and for a moment, just a moment, the bioluminescent fungi pulse in a pattern you recognize: the same grey-blue light you saw at the Grove's edge. The color of deep ocean.
>
> Tessara sits in the healing grove, wrapped in blankets, staring at nothing. When you approach, she looks up with eyes that have seen too far.
>
> "It is patient," she says. "Whatever it is. It has been working for a very long time. The roots here are just one thread. There are others, in other places, other regions, all leading back to the same source. Beneath the water. Beneath everything."
>
> She pauses. "The Greensingers cannot fight this alone. The Marches cannot fight this alone. Whatever is down there, it is bigger than one dead goddess's roots."
>
> In the distance, the Scarwall shivers. Not from wind. From something moving beneath it, deep underground, heading south toward the coast.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Where This Goes:** The ocean-origin intelligence connects the Verdant Marches directly to the [[The Hollowed Reach|Hollowed Reach]] and Serith's prison in the Abyssal Trench. The shadow-roots are tendrils of Serith's influence, traveling through ocean floor, up coastal roots, into Thyrea's network. The PCs now have the first evidence that the Second Silence is not regional but continental, and that the corruption threatening the Marches originates from the same source as the Black Tides.
