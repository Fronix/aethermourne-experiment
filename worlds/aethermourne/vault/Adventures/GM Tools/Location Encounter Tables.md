# Location Encounter Tables

*Settlement-specific encounter tables for major cities and dungeons with active quest boards. Roll when the party explores, investigates, or spends downtime in these locations. These supplement the regional travel tables in [[Encounter Tables]].*

---

## Ostivaar (The Ashen Dominion)

*[[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]]'s ribcage looms over everything. The city runs on structure, hierarchy, and the slow erosion of both.*

| d8 | Encounter | Threat | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | A [[The Cinders\|Cinder]] agent tails the party through the Ashmarket, poorly disguised as a merchant. If confronted, they claim to be "ensuring public safety." A Perception DC 12 check reveals the Cinder sigil concealed beneath their collar. | Low | Social | Leads to [[The Cinders]] investigation threads |
| 2 | A crowd gathers in the Lower Ribs as a street preacher from the [[Unbound Congregation]] denounces the caste system. [[Order of the Ashen Flame]] soldiers are closing in. The party has moments to act before an arrest or a riot. | Moderate | Social | Connects to [[Elowen Greaves]]'s reform movement |
| 3 | The party passes through a district where every surface has been "perfected," dust arranging itself into geometric patterns, cracks sealing overnight, weeds pulling themselves from between cobblestones. Residents in this area speak in monotone and smile identically. Order Sickness is spreading. | Moderate | Environmental | Ties to Ostivaar Quest 4, Operation Quiet |
| 4 | An [[Ash Revenants\|Ash Revenant]] has manifested inside the Ashmarket, standing motionless before a jeweler's stall, reaching for a gold chain. Merchants are panicking. The Revenant's whispered phrase is a name: someone in the crowd. | Moderate | Combat/Mystery | Use [[Ash Revenants]] stat block, Ambition Trigger if jewels are touched |
| 5 | A foreman from the Spine mining crews stumbles into the party's path, wild-eyed, babbling about a chamber deep in the ribcage where the walls pulse with light and workers stand in circles, unresponsive. He collapses unconscious moments later. | Low | Mystery | Connects to the Ashite anomaly beneath the Spine |
| 6 | Graffiti appears overnight on the walls near the party's lodging: *"The order is a lie."* The paint is warm to the touch and resists removal. A [[The Cinders\|Cinder]] investigation team arrives within the hour, interrogating everyone nearby. | Low | Social | The graffiti appears by unknown means, a mystery even to the Unbound |
| 7 | A [[Hollow Ones\|Hollow One]] walks through the Upper Ribs at midday, ignored by locals who have learned not to see them. It stops before the party, studies them for an unsettling moment, then speaks a single sentence that references a PC's personal goal before walking away. | Moderate | Mystery | [[Hollow Ones]] stat block only if provoked |
| 8 | An Ashite crystal embedded in a public fountain cracks, releasing a pulse of structured energy. Everyone within 4 spaces makes a Will save DC 14 or acts with perfect, mechanical efficiency for 1 hour, unable to deviate from their current task. Guards begin "protecting" the fountain with lethal force. | High | Environmental | Order Sickness event, DC 14 Will to resist |

## Tidecross (The Unwritten Lands, Veilshore)

*A liminal town where the old world meets the new. The black sand shifts at night, and everyone watches everyone else.*

| d8 | Encounter | Threat | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | [[Captain Erys Vane]] intercepts the party at the docks, exhausted and terse. She needs outsiders to mediate a dispute between Oldborn dockworkers and Dominion missionaries. Both sides trust the party exactly as much as they distrust each other: barely. | Low | Social | Key NPC introduction, faction tension |
| 2 | The black sand on the beach moves at night. A party member on watch sees shapes pressing up from beneath the surface, humanoid outlines that sink back when observed. A Perception DC 14 check reveals the shapes are reaching toward the Archive House. | Moderate | Mystery | Primordial influence, connects to the Loom fragment |
| 3 | [[Prior Cael Drantine]], the Ashen missionary, approaches the party with earnest enthusiasm, offering "the comfort of Solvaen's order" and asking probing questions about Oldborn customs. He means well. He is also, by asking, endangering the Oldborn ceasefire. | Low | Social | Political tinderbox, well-intentioned threat |
| 4 | [[Salice]], the smuggler queen, sends a runner to invite the party to her ship for "a conversation about mutual interests." She knows things about the Veil Unbound's presence in Tidecross and will trade information for a favor. | Low | Social | Information broker, connects to Archive Heist thread |
| 5 | [[Vosh]], the Oldborn observer, is missing. An Oldborn delegation arrives at the harbor demanding answers. [[Captain Erys Vane]] has one week before the Oldborn interpret the disappearance as an act of war. She asks the party to investigate. | Moderate | Mystery | Connects to the Vanishing Oldborn quest thread |
| 6 | The ocean stops moving. For ten minutes, the harbor is perfectly still, no waves, no current, no tide. Fish float motionless at the surface. Then it resumes as if nothing happened. [[Tidecaller Mirenne]] is found weeping at [[The Twelve#Yvenne\|Yvenne]]'s shrine, whispering "She's waking up." | Moderate | Environmental | [[The Twelve#Yvenne\|Yvenne]] manifestation, escalating divine event |
| 7 | A [[The Veil Unbound\|Veil Unbound]] agent, posing as a traveling scholar, attempts to recruit a PC by offering "answers about the silence between the stars." They know too much about the party's recent activities. | Low | Social | Infiltration attempt, connects to Archive Heist |
| 8 | A section of the Oldborn docks, predating the Twelve, begins humming. Stone carvings that have been inert for millennia glow with amber light. [[Contract Wraiths]] manifest from the walls, targeting anyone who has broken an oath in the last year. Dex save DC 13 to avoid their initial manifestation burst. | High | Combat | [[Contract Wraiths]] stat block, Primordial awakening event |

## Crucible (The Emberveil)

*The Grand Forge burns. The dwarves craft. Something in the deep is cooling.*

| d6 | Encounter | Threat | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | [[Brunhild Ironheart\|Forge-Matriarch Brunhild Ironheart]] personally inspects a visiting party's weapons and armor, offering unsolicited critique. She is evaluating them as potential agents for a sensitive matter she cannot trust to her own people. | Low | Social | Connects to the Dimming Forge crisis |
| 2 | A [[Spark Wraiths\|Spark Wraith]] drifts through the Pattern Library while the party researches. It settles on the most analytical PC like a shawl of living embers, siphoning creative thought. The warmth feels comforting. The breakthrough they were close to begins fading. | Moderate | Combat/Mystery | [[Spark Wraiths]] stat block, Creativity Drain |
| 3 | [[Corrin Ashveil\|Factor Corrin Ashveil]] approaches the party in the Ember Archive, nervously offering access to sealed records in exchange for help retrieving a specific document from Brunhild's private vault. He has found patterns in the forge's cooling that terrify him. | Low | Social | Connects to Sealed Records access standoff |
| 4 | A geothermal vent erupts in the crafting quarter without warning. The blast reveals a previously sealed chamber containing Thalvor-era forge equipment still glowing with divine heat. Something is moving inside. [[Slag Crawlers]] emerge, drawn to the exposed divine material. | High | Combat/Environmental | Dex save DC 14 for initial vent burst (2d6 fire), [[Slag Crawlers]] stat block |
| 5 | [[Apprentice Jyn]], the controversial human first-generation apprentice, asks the party to witness her Trial at the sacred forge. Half the guild wants her to succeed. Half want her to fail publicly. The political tension in the chamber is thick enough to taste. | Moderate | Social | Guild politics, connects to succession question |
| 6 | The Grand Forge dims. For three heartbeats, the eternal fire at the city's heart gutters and nearly goes out. Every smith in the city stops working simultaneously. Brunhild's face goes white. She orders the incident sealed and threatens anyone who speaks of it. | High | Environmental | Dimming Forge crisis escalation, campaign-level event |

## Keth'vaal (The Unwritten Lands)

*The oldest city. The Great Anchor pulses. The Circle of Speakers cannot agree on anything except that the world is changing.*

| d6 | Encounter | Threat | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | [[Speaker Kaelthen]] of the Sealed Gate intercepts the party at the city gates, warning them that "outlanders who meddle in Oldborn affairs tend to vanish into the Rootways." This is not a threat. It is a statement of historical fact. | Low | Social | Isolationist faction, political tension |
| 2 | The Great Anchor pulses visibly, and for a moment every [[Contract Wraiths\|Contract Wraith]] in the city freezes. Then they resume patrol, but their routes have changed. They now orbit a specific building in the merchant quarter where [[Speaker Yavenne]] conducts her secret outlander correspondence. | Moderate | Mystery | Connects to Yavenne's Treason thread |
| 3 | A Rootway tunnel entrance in the lower city has been sealed for centuries. The seal is cracking. Travelers report sounds from the deep, grinding stone, something large moving. The [[Oldborn Council\|Circle of Speakers]] is debating whether to investigate or reinforce the seal. The party is asked for an outside perspective. | Moderate | Mystery | Rootway Incursion thread, connects to Gyre activity |
| 4 | [[Speaker Dravos]] of the Ash Reclaimers approaches the party openly, offering alliance. He wants to expand Oldborn influence and sees the party as useful tools. His offer is generous. His ambition is dangerous. | Low | Social | Militarist faction, political thread |
| 5 | A temporal distortion ripples through the Deep Library. For thirty seconds, the party sees the library as it was four thousand years ago: pristine, crowded with Oldborn scholars, the Great Anchor visible through the windows, newly built and blazing with power. Then it fades. A [[Gyre-Wraiths\|Gyre-Wraith]] manifests where the distortion was strongest. | High | Combat/Mystery | [[Gyre-Wraiths]] individual stat block, Great Anchor fracture event |
| 6 | An archivist approaches the party in whispers, offering access to the Deep Library's restricted collection in exchange for smuggling a specific text out of the city. The text contains pre-Theomachis maps of the Loom's Edge. The archivist is terrified and will not explain why. | Moderate | Social/Mystery | Connects to Library Heist and Loom's Edge lore |

| 2 | A Sleepwalker procession forms at midnight: seven citizens walking in lockstep toward the harbor, eyes closed, murmuring in a language no one recognizes. | 4 | A salt preacher in the Bilges draws a crowd with wild-eyed sermons about "the voice beneath the waves." The [[The Lantern-Keepers\|Lantern-Keepers]] are debating whether to arrest him. | Low | Social | Bilges Preacher thread, Yvenne stirring |

## Ironhaven (The Ironmarch)

*The Warspire looms. The Code of Iron holds. For now.*

| d8 | Encounter | Threat | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | [[Yrsa Bloodhand\|Iron Thane Yrsa Bloodhand]] addresses the party in the war-hall, measuring them with a fighter's eye. She offers work: reconnaissance on [[Mira the Red]]'s army positions. The pay is good. The implication is that refusal marks you as either useless or an enemy. | Low | Social | Main political thread, Warlord Challenge |
| 2 | A duel in the fighting pits escalates beyond the Code of Iron. One combatant, eyes burning with iron-orange light, refuses to yield and fights with inhuman ferocity. The Warspire's war-essence is influencing the pits. Castellan Drenn orders the party to subdue the fighter without killing them. | Moderate | Combat | War-essence contamination, Str save DC 13 to resist aggression aura within 2 spaces |
| 3 | [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]] of the [[The Ember Guild\|Ember Guild]] pulls the party aside in the smithing quarter. He is drafting evacuation plans for guild personnel. The Warspire's war-essence output has tripled in the last month. He believes something inside is waking up and wants the party to investigate the base. | Moderate | Social/Mystery | Warspire Escalation thread, Kaevroth fragment activity |
| 4 | An [[The Ambitious Dead\|Ambitious Dead]] walks through the market at midday, ignoring everyone, carrying a rusted sword toward the Warspire. Its eye sockets burn with iron-orange light. It has been walking for what appears to be decades, its armor fused to its body by rust. Guards do not interfere. Locals step aside by habit. | Low | Environmental/Mystery | [[The Ambitious Dead]] stat block only if obstructed |
| 5 | Forge-Mistress Kellan demands the party settle a dispute: an Ember Guild factor claims jurisdiction over a newly discovered vein of Ashite-laced iron beneath the smithing quarter. Kellan says the city's charter gives Ironhaven priority. Both sides are preparing for a fight that is not entirely legal. | Low | Social | Resource politics, Ember Guild vs. local smiths |
| 6 | [[Iron Mites]] swarm through the lower levels of the Warspire's base, drawn by a fresh crack in the structure. The swarm is growing, absorbing loose iron from the walls. If not contained, it will reach the war-essence core within hours. | Moderate | Combat | [[Iron Mites]] stat block, Convergence Response scaling |
| 7 | [[Mira the Red]]'s herald arrives at the city gates under flag of parley, delivering a formal challenge to Yrsa's rule. The terms are specific: single combat, the Warspire as the prize. The herald is also scouting the city's defenses. | Moderate | Social | Warlord Challenge escalation, espionage opportunity |
| 8 | The Warspire groans. The entire city feels it, a deep vibration through the ground that sets teeth on edge and makes iron objects hum. An [[Iron Elementals\|Iron Elemental]] tears itself free from the Warspire's base, 3 stories tall, burning with war-essence. It exists for 3-5 rounds before collapsing, but in that time it can level a city block. | High | Combat | [[Iron Elementals]] stat block, campaign-level event |

> [!tip] Using These Tables
> These location tables pair with the regional travel tables in [[Encounter Tables]]. Use regional tables for wilderness travel between settlements. Use these tables when the party is within city limits, exploring, investigating, or spending downtime.
>
> **Mixing tables:** If the party is near a city border, roll on both tables and use whichever result fits the moment better.
>
> **Escalation:** As the campaign progresses and [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s influence grows, shift encounter weights toward higher-numbered entries (which tend to be more dangerous and more connected to the main arc).
