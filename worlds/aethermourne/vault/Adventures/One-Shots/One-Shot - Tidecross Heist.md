# Tidecross Heist

*A one-shot adventure set in [[Tidecross]], [[The Veilshore]]*

---

## What You Need

> [!info] Adventure Overview
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Party Size** | 3-5 players |
> | **Recommended Level** | 3-5 (adaptable) |
> | **Tone** | Intrigue, heist, factional tension, ticking clock |
> | **Estimated Duration** | 3-4 hours |
> | **Setting** | [[Tidecross]], the chaotic trade hub on [[The Veilshore]] |
> | **Themes** | Competing loyalties, the cost of power, who controls dangerous knowledge |

**Summary:** A [[The Veil Unbound]] agent has smuggled a Primordial artifact into [[Tidecross]], a crystalline resonator stolen from the [[The Primordia|Primordia]] that amplifies raw [[The Gyre|Gyre]]-potential into usable form. If activated, it could destabilize the settlement's foundations, the black sand already listens, and a resonator would make it scream. Three factions want it. The players are hired to retrieve it before Tidecross tears itself apart.

**Key NPCs:** Captain Erys Vane (quest-giver), Moth (Veil Unbound agent), Salice (smuggler queen), Vosh (Oldborn observer), Prior Cael Drantine (Dominion missionary)

---

## The Artifact: The Choir Stone

> [!info] The Choir Stone
> A fist-sized crystal of deep violet that hums at frequencies below human hearing. Harvested from a Primordial Anchor in [[The Primordia]], it resonates with [[The Primordials#Ur-Ghael|Ur-Ghael's]] structuring force but has been modified, inverted, to amplify chaos rather than stability. In the wrong hands, it could turn Tidecross's unstable black sand into a weapon, a Primordial surge localized beneath the settlement.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Choir Stone is a prototype. [[The Veil Unbound]] has been experimenting with inverted Primordial technology, trying to weaponize the forces that the Oldborn negotiate with. This stone is a proof of concept. If it works in Tidecross, the Unbound will produce more. Their goal: accelerate the collapse of divine order by introducing Primordial chaos into areas still stabilized by the Aetheric Web.

## Scene 1: The Briefing

**Location:** [[Captain Erys Vane]]'s office, the Harbormaster's Tower
**Mood:** Urgent, exhausted, pragmatic

Captain Vane summons the players (or approaches them if they are new arrivals). She is blunt: something dangerous has been smuggled into Tidecross. Her informants say it is a Primordial artifact, unstable and weaponizable. She does not know exactly what it does, but the black sand has been behaving worse than usual, whispers louder, objects sinking faster, and three people vanished from the waterfront last night without a trace.

**What Vane knows:**
- The artifact arrived on the *Ashen Pilgrim*, a Dominion missionary supply ship, three days ago
- It was not in the ship's official manifest
- Her informant (a dockworker) saw a hooded figure unload a glowing crate at midnight
- The figure was met by someone matching the description of a known Veil Unbound operative: a person called **Moth**
- Moth has been in Tidecross for two weeks, posing as an independent scholar

**What Vane wants:**
- The artifact retrieved and brought to her
- Moth identified and detained
- Minimal disruption, Tidecross is already on the brink

**What Vane offers:**
- 500 gold equivalent in trade goods
- Priority dock access for the party's ship (if they have one)
- Her personal marker, a favor owed, redeemable across [[The Hollowed Reach]]

> [!tip] Roleplay Notes: Captain Vane
> Vane is a professional running out of professionalism. She has not slept in two days. She trusts the players because she has no one else, her own guards are spread thin, and she cannot involve the Oldborn without sparking a diplomatic incident. She is honest about the danger but understates her own desperation.
>
> **Sample line:** *"I am not asking you to be heroes. I am asking you to find a glowing rock before it turns this settlement into a crater. The pay is good. The alternative is swimming home."*

## Scene 2: The Investigation

**Location:** Various locations across Tidecross
**Mood:** Noir investigation, competing interests, paranoia

The players have roughly 12 hours before Moth activates the Choir Stone (the ticking clock). They can investigate in any order. Each location yields clues and complications.

### The Docks (Where the artifact arrived)

- **Clue:** Dockworker [[Hask]] confirms the midnight unloading. The crate was taken north, toward the Tidemark Cliffs. Persuasion DC 10 or 5 gold gets him talking.
- **Complication:** Two Oldborn observers are also investigating the docks. They noticed the anomalous cargo. If the players are spotted by them, [[Vosh]] becomes aware of the situation and begins their own hunt for the artifact.

### The Crossroads Market (Where Moth shops)

- **Clue:** A crystal vendor named **Pellam** sold Moth specialized containment vials two days ago, the kind used to handle unstable Primordial materials. Pellam describes Moth: average height, gray cloak, distinctive moth-wing tattoo on the left hand, soft voice.
- **Clue:** Investigation DC 14 at Pellam's stall reveals a receipt for a second purchase: resonance tuning forks. These are used to attune Primordial artifacts to specific frequencies. Moth is not just storing the stone, they are calibrating it.

### The Ashen Chapel (Where the artifact was hidden in transit)

- **Clue:** [[Prior Cael Drantine]] is horrified to learn his supply ship was used for smuggling. He cooperates fully. He identifies the crew member who allowed the contraband: **Deacon Surl**, who has been missing since landfall.
- **Complication:** Drantine insists on accompanying the players to "rectify this affront to Solvaen's mission." He is sincere but loud, conspicuous, and will inadvertently alert Moth if brought along. Persuasion DC 16 to convince him to stay behind.

### The Wrack (The smugglers' cove)

- **Clue:** [[Salice]] knows Moth. She sold them a safe house in the Tidemark Cliffs, a cave with a reinforced door. She will share the location for a price: 200 gold, or a favor (Salice wants the Choir Stone destroyed, not delivered to Vane, she fears what any faction would do with it).
- **Complication:** Salice warns the players that the safe house is trapped. Moth is paranoid and competent. The cave has alchemical wards and a secondary exit.

### Vosh's Observation Post (Optional)

If the players seek out [[Vosh]], the Oldborn observer, they find a cold, precise individual who already suspects the artifact's nature. Vosh offers a deal: they will provide the exact location of Moth's hideout (they have been tracking the Unbound agent for days) in exchange for the artifact being surrendered to the Oldborn rather than Vane.

- **Vosh's argument:** The artifact is made from stolen Primordial material. It belongs to the Oldborn. Giving it to outlanders ensures it will be studied, replicated, and weaponized.
- **Vosh's leverage:** They know where Moth is. Without Vosh, the players must find the cave on their own (adds 2-3 hours to the clock).

## Scene 3: The Heist

**Location:** Moth's safe house, a cave in the Tidemark Cliffs
**Mood:** Tense infiltration, traps, confrontation

The cave is accessible via a narrow path along the cliff face (Athletics DC 12 to navigate safely, DC 8 with rope). The entrance is a reinforced wooden door set into the rock, locked (Thieves' Tools DC 16, or Strength DC 20 to force).

### Cave Layout (Described)

**Entry tunnel.** 30 feet long, 5 feet wide. Trapped: a tripwire at the 15-foot mark triggers an alchemical smoke bomb (fills the tunnel, heavily obscures for 3 rounds, Str save DC 13 or coughing imposes rolling twice and taking lower on attacks).

**Main chamber.** 20x30 feet, carved smooth. Contains:
- A workbench with resonance tuning forks, notes in cipher (Intelligence DC 15 to decode: activation instructions for the Choir Stone)
- A bedroll and supplies for one person
- A locked chest (Thieves' Tools DC 14) containing Moth's Veil Unbound credentials and a coded message from their handler

**The Choir Stone** sits on the workbench in a containment circle drawn in salt and ground crystal. Moving it without disrupting the circle requires Arcana DC 14. Disrupting the circle causes a localized Primordial pulse: everyone in the chamber makes Str save DC 14 or takes 3d6 force damage and is knocked prone.

**Secondary exit.** A narrow chimney leading to the cliff top. Requires Athletics DC 14 to climb.

### Moth

**Moth** is present unless the players arrive during the first 4 hours of the investigation (in which case Moth is in town and the players can take the stone without confrontation, but Moth returns and hunts them).

**Moth's stats:** Use a spy/assassin stat block appropriate to party level. Add: resistance to force damage (Primordial exposure), and one use of *misty step* (reflavored as a Gyre-flicker, briefly becoming unstructured).

**Moth's behavior:** Moth does not fight to the death. They are a professional. If outnumbered, they negotiate:
- "The stone is worthless to you. Give it to Vane and she locks it in a box. Give it to the Oldborn and they bury it. Give it to me and it *changes things*."
- If pressed: "The divine order is dying. You know this. The Unbound offer an alternative. Not chaos, *liberation*. The Primordials were here first. They will be here after. Choose the winning side."
- If combat goes badly (half HP), Moth flees via the chimney exit, leaving the stone behind.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Moth's notes reveal that this operation is a test run. The Veil Unbound has three more Choir Stones being prepared at an unknown location. Even if the players succeed here, the threat is not eliminated, only delayed.

## Scene 4: The Delivery

**Location:** Tidecross, various
**Mood:** Moral complexity, factional pressure, consequences

The players have the Choir Stone. Now they must decide who gets it. Each option has consequences.

### Option A: Deliver to Captain Vane

Vane locks it in the Archive House vault alongside [[The Star-Readers]]' other acquisitions. She pays the players as promised. The immediate crisis is resolved.

**Consequence:** The Star-Readers study the stone. Within months, they understand inverted Primordial technology. The knowledge spreads. The genie does not go back in the bottle.

### Option B: Surrender to the Oldborn (Vosh)

Vosh takes the stone with cold satisfaction. The Oldborn delegation's hostility toward outlanders decreases slightly, a rare diplomatic victory. Vosh provides the players with a token of Oldborn trust (useful for future interactions with the Kingdoms).

**Consequence:** The Oldborn destroy the stone and begin hunting for the remaining three. They become more aggressive in policing outlander activities, which increases tensions in Tidecross.

### Option C: Destroy the Stone (Salice's request)

Salice provides instructions: submerge it in seawater and strike it with iron. The stone shatters. No one gets it.

**Consequence:** Salice becomes a reliable ally. Vane is furious (lost intelligence value). The Oldborn are relieved. The Veil Unbound accelerates their timeline, they know their operation was compromised.

### Option D: Keep It

The players decide to hold onto the Choir Stone. Every faction is angry. The players now possess a weapon of mass destabilization. The stone hums in their pack, and the black sand of Tidecross whispers their names as they leave.

**Consequence:** The players become targets. Every faction wants the stone. It also begins affecting the carrier: vivid dreams of geometric patterns, a creeping desire to *use* it, just once, just to see what happens.

> [!warning]- GM Notes
> **Heist structure:** This adventure rewards planning. Give the players time in Scene 2 to gather information and make alliances. The more they learn, the easier Scene 3 becomes. If they rush, the heist is harder and Moth is more prepared.
>
> **Factional balance:** No faction is "right." Vane will study the stone. The Oldborn will militarize over it. Salice's destruction removes intelligence value. There is no clean answer. Let the players argue.
>
> **Connecting to campaign:** The Veil Unbound's inverted Primordial technology is a new threat vector. If the players recover Moth's notes, they learn about the three remaining Choir Stones, a hook for future sessions. The Unbound's willingness to weaponize Primordial forces mirrors Serith's strategy of accelerating divine collapse.
