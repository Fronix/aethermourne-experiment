# Quest Board: Cyclethorn

*Between the pulse and the silence, someone always needs help. The pay is strange and the dangers are stranger.*

---

## 1. The Narrowing Watch

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Kael Duskborne]], Ashen Warden of the Thornguard |
> | **Objective** | Map the Threshold's new boundary markers along Cyclethorn's perimeter and report what you find |
> | **Difficulty** | Low |
> | **Reward** | 80 gold, Thornguard patrol rations for a week, Kael's trust |
> | **Complication** | The boundary has shifted further than anyone expected. The amber forming at the new edge contains things that should not be there. |

**Hook:** "Kael pins a hand-drawn map to the Shift Market's notice board. Half the boundary markers he placed last month are now deep in either the Bloom or the Wilt. He needs fresh eyes to walk the perimeter, plant new markers, and tell him honestly how bad it is. He already knows. He needs someone else to confirm it."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The boundary survey reveals two things. First, the Threshold has narrowed by another quarter-mile since Kael's last mapping, far faster than [[Yara Thornbloom]]'s models predicted. Second, the amber crystallizing at the new boundary edge contains inclusions that defy explanation: preserved organisms from no known species, fragments of text in a pre-Theomachis script, and in one piece, what appears to be a face frozen mid-expression. The [[Threshold Weavers]] are spinning faster and more aggressively near these crystallization points, as if compelled. Returning this information to Yara triggers her private concern about the "third pulse" she has been detecting, though she will not share that secret unless the PCs earn significant trust. This quest is the entry point to the Threshold's deeper mysteries and connects directly to the Under the Stone hook in [[The Threshold (Thornwild)]].

## 2. Harvest Run to Bone Garden

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Garret Boneharvest]], chief trader of Bone Garden |
> | **Objective** | Escort a cycle-material shipment from Cyclethorn to [[Bone Garden]] through Wilt-edge territory |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 200 gold, a vial of stabilized Thornwild amber (minor healing properties), Boneharvest trading credit |
> | **Complication** | The route passes through territory where [[Cycle-Stalkers]] have been ranging further from the Bonewood. And something is wrong in Bone Garden when you arrive. |

**Hook:** "Garret is in the Shift Market, sweating through his cheerful mask. His last two shipments arrived short, porters spooked by the Wilt-edge going strange. He needs capable escorts who will not bolt when the trees start moving. The pay is good because the route is bad, and it is getting worse."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The escort is the easy part. Two or three [[Cycle-Stalkers]] harry the caravan in the Wilt-edge transition zone, probing and retreating, testing the defenders the way the packs always do. The real problem waits at [[Bone Garden]]. The bone-trees surrounding the settlement are showing new growth that is neither flower nor leaf: dark-veined fungal structures reaching upward from the bark, pulsing faintly in time with the Cycle's death-phase. Master Coris at the Crucible has been studying samples and believes the fungal growth is a symptom of the Cycle's acceleration pushing Morrhael's influence into substrate that was previously balanced. She needs fresh specimens from the grove's outer edge, where the fungal growth is newest, and the collection is dangerous because the affected trees react to proximity. This connects to Bone Garden's "grove is changing" tension and foreshadows the deeper instability spreading from the Bonewood.

## 3. The Lost Pilgrims

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Revenant Moss]] (via a Remnant courier in Cyclethorn's Stray Quarter) |
> | **Objective** | Find a group of [[Remnants Court|Remnant]] pilgrims who left [[Ashenmere]] bound for [[Ashbloom]] and never arrived |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | No gold. Revenant Moss's gratitude, Ashbloom hospitality, and a Remnant-carved bone token that grants passage through Wilt territory without attracting Death Tide risen |
> | **Complication** | The pilgrims are not dead. They are caught in a transition loop, cycling between death and renewal in a clearing where the Wilt's rhythm has gone recursive. Pulling them out means understanding the Cycle well enough to break it. |

**Hook:** "The Remnant courier stands in the Stray Quarter with the patience of someone who has all the time in the world and none of the warmth to enjoy it. Six pilgrims left Ashenmere three weeks ago. They should have reached Ashbloom in ten days. Moss sent the courier to ask for help, not from the Cyclekeepers, who have their own problems, but from anyone willing to walk into the Wilt and look for people the living world has already forgotten."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The pilgrims are trapped in a transition loop two days into the Wilt, in a clearing where the Cycle's death-phase and life-phase are repeating the same twelve-minute interval without progressing. The Remnants cycle through: cold and still (death-phase), then a gasp of warmth and sensation (life-phase), then cold again. They are aware during the life-phase but cannot move beyond the clearing's edge. One pilgrim, an older Remnant named Savin, has begun speaking during the life-phase in fragments that sound like the [[Bone Choir]]'s harmonic, words that are not his own. Breaking the loop requires disrupting the Cycle's rhythm in the clearing, loud sound, channeled energy against the current phase, or physically dragging the Remnants out during the life-phase's peak. Savin may resist, the fragments he is speaking feel important to him, like a message he needs to finish delivering. What he is channeling is a piece of [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s shattered death-song, and if transcribed before the loop is broken, it connects to the Bone Choir's sentence from [[The Wilt]]'s hooks. This quest is emotionally heavy. The Remnants' gratitude is genuine and painful.

## 4. Fennara's Request

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Root-Speaker Fennara]], Rootpyre |
> | **Objective** | Enter the [[The Bonewood|Bonewood]], find [[Seedling Vera]], and bring her back to [[Rootpyre]] |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | 350 gold, a Rootpyre-grown thornwood staff (focus for primal magic), Fennara's offer to teach the basics of dual-channeling |
> | **Complication** | The Bonewood resists entry. Vera may not want to return. And the Heartroot in Rootpyre is destabilizing while Fennara waits. |

**Hook:** "A druid arrives in Cyclethorn with mismatched eyes, one green, one grey, and a message from Fennara. Her best student walked into the Bonewood three days ago. Fennara cannot leave Rootpyre because the Heartroot is cycling faster than ever and her circle is the only thing keeping the settlement's living structures from crushing everyone inside. She is asking for help. Fennara does not ask for help."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Seedling Vera]] reached the Bonewood's deep interior and found [[The Bloom-Wraith]]. She is not trapped. She is listening. The Bloom-Wraith's fragmentary communication has latched onto Vera's dual-channeling attunement, and she is receiving more coherent messages than anyone has before, but the process is consuming her. When found, Vera is sitting cross-legged in a transition-loop clearing, eyes open, one iris flickering between green and grey so fast it appears white. She is whispering in two overlapping voices. Pulling her out is possible (DC 14 Athletics to physically move her, DC 16 Primal Communion or equivalent to break the attunement gently). If pulled out violently, Vera loses the message but survives. If pulled out gently, she retains fragments: a warning about a "third voice in the Cycle" and the phrase "the shadow drinks from both roots." This is the first coherent reference to [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s destabilization of the Thornwild. Vera will be shaken but will insist she needs to go back. Fennara will forbid it. The tension between them becomes a recurring thread.

## 5. The Ember Guild's Interest

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | Alchemist Drue, former [[The Star-Readers|Star-Reader]] apprentice, Cyclethorn |
> | **Objective** | Investigate who has been stealing cycle-material samples from the Star-Reader expedition and determine where they are going |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | 300 gold from the Star-Reader expedition fund, Drue's alchemical services at cost, Yara Thornbloom's direct attention |
> | **Complication** | The thief is embedded in the expedition. Exposing them makes an enemy of [[The Ember Guild]]. Letting them continue compromises Cyclethorn's independence. |

**Hook:** "Drue corners the PCs near the Shift Market, speaking quickly, glancing over her shoulder. Inventory discrepancies. Three batches of rare transition-phase amber, gone. Sample logs altered. Someone in the Star-Reader expedition is skimming, and the materials are leaving the Thornwild on the southern trade road. Drue suspects the Guild. She cannot prove it without help, and she cannot go to Yara without proof because Yara will shut down the entire expedition, and Drue still believes the research matters."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The infiltrator is a junior researcher named Factor Hallen, placed by [[The Ember Guild]] to assess the commercial viability of Thornwild amber at scale. Hallen is not malicious, they genuinely believe the Guild's involvement would benefit the Thornwild through trade infrastructure, but they are naive about what the Guild actually wants: exclusive contracts, controlled harvesting, and eventually a permanent Guild factor in Cyclethorn with authority over amber exports. The stolen samples are being sent to an Ember Guild assessor in the [[The Verdant Marches|Verdant Marches]] border region. Confronting Hallen presents a choice. Exposing them to Yara earns the Cyclekeepers' trust and results in the Star-Reader expedition being placed under strict Cyclekeeper oversight, preserving Cyclethorn's sovereignty but straining the Star-Reader relationship. Negotiating with Hallen (DC 15 Persuasion) can produce a compromise: the samples stop, Hallen reports back to the Guild that the amber is unstable and commercially unviable, and the Guild loses interest, for now. Letting Hallen continue means the Guild arrives within months with contracts and leverage. There is no clean solution.

## 6. The Bloom-Wraith's Whisper

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Yara Thornbloom]], Senior Cyclekeeper |
> | **Objective** | Reach [[The Bloom-Wraith]] in the heart of the [[The Bonewood|Bonewood]], hear its message, and return alive with what you learn |
> | **Difficulty** | Very High |
> | **Reward** | A piece of Thornwild amber containing a preserved fragment of the Cycle's original rhythm (campaign artifact), permanent standing with the Cyclekeepers, and the truth about what is happening to the Thornwild |
> | **Complication** | The Bonewood has turned Yara back nine times. No one who has reached the Bloom-Wraith has returned unchanged. The message may be worse than the silence. |

**Hook:** "Yara does not post this on the board. She finds the PCs after they have proven themselves, after the boundary survey, or the Bonewood rescue, or the investigation. She meets them at the Perch, alone, at dusk, when the Threshold is at its narrowest and the hum of both the Bloom and the Wilt presses against Cyclethorn's walls. She says: 'Something in the Bonewood is trying to speak. I have tried to listen nine times. Nine times the forest pushed me back. I do not think it is pushing you back. I think it is waiting for someone who is not me.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> This is the Thornwild's campaign-level quest. [[The Bloom-Wraith]] carries fused shards of both [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s divine consciousness. It knows what [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] is doing to the Cycle, threading her influence through both divine legacies to destabilize the equilibrium that keeps the Thornwild from collapsing into either a Bloom Zone or a Death Zone. The Bloom-Wraith's message, if received coherently, reveals: the Cycle is not breaking naturally, it is being broken deliberately. A "third voice" is speaking through both the growth-pulse and the death-pull, turning allies into opponents. The Thornwild is a test case for what Serith intends for the entire continent, a world where divine legacies war with each other while mortals are ground between them.
>
> Reaching the Bloom-Wraith requires surviving the Bonewood's full gauntlet: rapid Cycle shifts, [[Cycle-Stalkers|Cycle-Stalker]] packs, transition loops, and the disorienting beauty-horror of a landscape that dies and is reborn every few hours. Slowing the Cycle around the Bloom-Wraith long enough for coherent communication requires dual-channeling knowledge (from Rootpyre), Cycle-reading skill (from the Cyclekeepers), or brute spiritual endurance (DC 18 Will save, repeated). The Bloom-Wraith does not attack. It does not flee. It waits, shifting between vine-beauty and bone-horror, speaking in two overlapping voices that resolve into clarity only in the moments of transition between phases. What it says changes the PCs' understanding of the Thornwild and connects directly to the Second Silence's larger arc. This quest should not be available until the PCs have completed at least two other Cyclethorn quests and have earned Yara's trust.
