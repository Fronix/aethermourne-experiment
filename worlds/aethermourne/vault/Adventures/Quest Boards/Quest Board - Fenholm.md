# Quest Board: Fenholm

*The water glows. The platforms creak. Everyone here is running from something, and the fen remembers all of it.*

---

## 1. The Lattice Is Failing

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Maren Duskwater]], the Fenmother |
> | **Objective** | Investigate why the oldest ironwood supports of the Lattice are warping and determine if the damage is structural, magical, or both |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 200 gold, Fenmother's trust, permanent lodging on the Lattice |
> | **Complication** | The warping accelerates at night. Something in the ley-light intensification is physically changing the wood. Repairs made during the day undo themselves by dawn. |

**Hook:** "Maren meets the party on the Council Platform, one hand resting on a support beam that visibly bows under her touch. 'This beam has held for two hundred years. Last month it started bending. Last week it started *humming*. The Lattice holds six thousand people above water that will kill them if they fall in. I need to know why my city is unmaking itself before it drops someone's grandmother into a ley-pool.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The ironwood supports are warping because the ley-lines beneath Fenholm are intensifying, overcompensating for the [[Aetheric Web]]'s continental weakening. The wood, which grew in ley-saturated soil and retains trace magical properties, is responding to the increased energy by *growing again*, not upward but inward, curling toward the brightest ley-concentrations beneath the water. The Lattice is not failing. It is being pulled down. [[Old Fenris]] suspects this but cannot confirm it without descending into the water, which would kill him in his current state (his body is already crystallizing from ley-exposure). The solution requires either dampening the ley-output beneath Fenholm (which [[Maren Duskwater]] can do with her Ashite stabilizer, but this would suppress Fencraft ability in the settlement) or redirecting the ley-flow away from the Lattice foundations (which requires a skilled Fencrafter, and [[Wick]] is the only one talented enough to attempt it). Either option has costs.

## 2. The Silent Researcher

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Old Fenris]], Confluence Elder |
> | **Objective** | Travel to [[Sporeling]] and determine why [[Brother Lumen]] has stopped responding to messages |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | 300 gold, Confluence Council intelligence on ley-line patterns, Old Fenris's personal guidance on Fencraft |
> | **Complication** | The route to Sporeling passes through the Deep Fen, where [[Sera Gloomhollow]]'s Hollowguard patrol. And Sporeling itself may no longer be entirely safe. |

**Hook:** "Old Fenris's eyes glow faintly in the dim light of his consultation room. He does not blink anymore. 'Brother Lumen has been silent for three weeks. That man sends me messages complaining about mushroom varieties. Three weeks of nothing means something has changed. Either he has succeeded in what he is attempting, or he has failed. I need to know which, because both outcomes frighten me equally.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Brother Lumen]] has connected a fourth [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]] fragment through [[Sporeling]]'s mycelial network. The connection is holding, but the effect on Sporeling's population is alarming: residents are experiencing shared dreams of Nethys's death (a woman shattering into light, stars darkening), losing the boundaries between their own thoughts and their neighbors', and in three cases, speaking in a voice that is not their own. Lumen believes this is progress. He is preparing a fifth connection that he believes will create a stable partial reconstitution of Nethys's consciousness. Old Fenris's fear is justified: if the fifth connection succeeds, it could repair a section of the Aetheric Web or it could destroy Sporeling's 800 residents by channeling more divine consciousness than mortal minds can contain. Lumen will not stop willingly. He believes this is the only way to prevent the Second Silence from collapsing magic entirely.

## 3. The Sluice War

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Torben Greaves]], master smuggler of [[The Drowning Bridge]] |
> | **Objective** | Stop [[Sera Gloomhollow]]'s Hollowguard from raiding Sluice caravans long enough for Torben to complete a critical shipment |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 250 gold, free passage on the Smuggler's Sluice, access to Torben's information network |
> | **Complication** | Sera's cause is just, even if her methods are not. And Torben's "critical shipment" is not what he claims. |

**Hook:** "Torben leans against his desk in the back room of the Bridge's largest warehouse, arms crossed. 'Sera Gloomhollow has hit three of my convoys in two months. She calls it liberation. I call it piracy with better speeches. I have a shipment coming through in four days that cannot be delayed, cannot be rerouted, and cannot be lost. I need it escorted. I will pay well and ask no questions about your methods, if you extend me the same courtesy.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Torben's critical shipment is not trade goods. It is an unopened letter from his sister [[Elowen Greaves]], the leader of the [[Unbound Congregation]] in [[Ostivaar]], smuggled out through a chain of intermediaries. The letter warns that [[The Cinders]] have infiltrated the Congregation and asks Torben for help extracting key members before a mass arrest. Torben does not know the letter's contents (it is sealed), but the intermediary who arranged transport was murdered two days ago, meaning someone does not want this message delivered. Meanwhile, [[Sera Gloomhollow]]'s raids are genuinely hurting fenland communities that depend on Sluice trade. Sera knows this and it is destroying her. She is exhausted, ley-exposed, and hearing whispers from [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]] fragments that she cannot interpret. If the party brokers peace between Sera and Torben, both become powerful allies. If they side with one against the other, the loser becomes a permanent enemy in a region where enemies know every waterway.

## 4. The Dominion Tax Envoy

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Maren Duskwater]], the Fenmother |
> | **Objective** | Assist Maren in stalling, negotiating with, or discrediting an [[The Ashen Dominion|Ashen Dominion]] tax envoy who has arrived in Fenholm demanding tribute and formal submission |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 200 gold, political standing in the Fenlands, Maren's research notes on Ashite stabilization |
> | **Complication** | The envoy has a valid legal claim under the Dominion's interpretation of territorial law. And [[Captain Hale Ashmark]] at [[Ashcreek]] has orders to enforce collection by military expedition if the envoy fails. |

**Hook:** "Maren's jaw is tight. 'The Dominion has sent a tax envoy. Polite. Professional. Carrying documents that say the Fenlands are a Dominion protectorate and always have been. The documents are forged, but they are *well* forged, and the envoy knows we cannot prove it without accessing Dominion archives, which we cannot enter. I have been stalling for a week. The envoy's patience is genuine but finite. When it expires, Captain Ashmark marches south with soldiers.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The tax envoy, Prior Tessen, is genuinely conflicted. He believes the Dominion's claim is legitimate and that Fenholm would benefit from Dominion infrastructure and order. He is not wrong that the Fenlands are vulnerable and isolated. But his superiors do not care about infrastructure; they want the Fenlands' ley-crystal resources and a staging ground for influence over the Smuggler's Sluice trade route. [[Captain Hale Ashmark]] at Ashcreek has been ordered to prepare a military expedition south if Prior Tessen's diplomatic approach fails, but Ashmark is stalling too, quietly. He has grown sympathetic to the fenfolk and knows a military occupation of the Lattice would be a logistical nightmare. The Cinders have placed an informant, Soldier Pell Oakley, in Ashmark's garrison. Pell is secretly learning Fencraft from a local girl and is deeply conflicted about his mission. Exposing Pell gives Ashmark leverage to resist his orders. Turning Ashmark openly would cost him his career and family's standing in the Dominion, but he is closer to that choice than anyone realizes.

## 5. The Prodigy's Problem

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Wick]], Fencraft prodigy |
> | **Objective** | Protect Wick from three competing factions attempting to recruit, kidnap, or control them |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | 300 gold (from the Confluence Council), Wick's friendship and Fencraft assistance, critical intelligence about Nethys's fragmented state |
> | **Complication** | Wick is sixteen, frightened, and hearing voices from dead goddess fragments that are converging through their mind. Protecting them means understanding what they are becoming. |

**Hook:** "Wick finds the party at the Glow Market, soaked and shaking. They speak in a rush: 'Three people tried to take me this week. A Star-Reader with a contract I did not sign. A Dominion scholar who said I was "evidence." And someone I could not see who whispered from the water that I should come with them to a place that does not exist on any map. I need help. Not later. Now.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Wick]] is not simply talented. Multiple [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]] fragments are converging through their mind simultaneously, drawn by Wick's natural attunement to the ley-lines. The [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]] want Wick studied (their chapter lead, Tessera Vane, has dispatched an operative). The Dominion's Calculists want Wick contained (a living ley-convergence is theologically threatening). [[Brother Lumen]] wants Wick brought to [[Sporeling]] as a catalyst for his fifth Nethys fragment connection, which could either repair a section of the Aetheric Web or kill everyone in Sporeling. The whisperer from the water is a Nethys fragment speaking directly, the first coherent communication from the shattered goddess in centuries. It is asking for help, not offering danger, but Wick cannot tell the difference. If Wick's convergence is allowed to develop naturally (with protection), they may become the first mortal capable of consciously interfacing with the Aetheric Web. If forced or rushed, the convergence will burn Wick out, scattering the fragments and accelerating the Second Silence in the Fenlands.

## 6. The Drowning Bridge Below

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Nettie Bridgeborn]], tea house owner and information broker |
> | **Objective** | Explore the sealed chambers beneath [[The Drowning Bridge]] and determine what is making sounds in rooms that have been underwater for centuries |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | 350 gold (from Nettie's personal savings), first pick of any artifacts recovered, Nettie's complete intelligence file on Sluice operations |
> | **Complication** | The chambers are flooded, sealed, and pre-date [[The Twelve]]. Whatever built them was not divine, not mortal, and not Primordial. |

**Hook:** "Nettie pours tea with hands that do not shake, though they should. 'I was born on the Bridge. Lived here sixty-seven years. I know every creak, every groan, every way the water sounds when the tide shifts. Three nights ago I heard something new. Grinding stone. Moving water where water should be still. And a voice, in a language I have never heard, from chambers that have been sealed since before the gods died. I am too old to go down there. You are not.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Drowning Bridge predates the Theomachis, possibly predates [[The Twelve]] entirely. The sealed chambers beneath the waterline contain a crystalline structure that [[Nettie Bridgeborn]] discovered years ago but never reported. The structure is now activating, its pulse accelerating in sync with the ley-surges that are intensifying across the Fenlands. The chambers were built by a pre-divine civilization (possibly during the age when the Gyre Primordials shaped the world before the gods emerged). The crystalline structure is a ley-line anchor, a physical node in what would later become the [[Aetheric Web]] when [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]] built her magical infrastructure on top of it. The anchor is activating because Nethys's fragments are failing, and the older, deeper system is attempting to compensate. If the party reaches the anchor, they discover something that reshapes their understanding of magic in Aethermourne: the Aetheric Web was not Nethys's invention. She built on a foundation that was already there. And that foundation is still functional, still running, still waiting for someone to use it. The [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] has established a cell on the Bridge specifically to access these chambers. They know what is down there. They are moving agents and materials into position. The party is in a race they do not know they are running.
