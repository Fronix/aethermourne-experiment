# Quest Board: Greyhold

*The dead outnumber the living. The living do not yield.*

---

## 1. The Bone-Carved Message

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Bjorn Ashken]], Holdwarden of Greyhold |
> | **Objective** | Investigate a bone-carved warning recovered from the Hollowdeep periphery and determine its origin |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | 400 gold, Holdwarden's trust, seat at the next Holdwardens' Council |
> | **Complication** | The message was carved by a dead woman. Or a woman who should be dead. Either answer changes everything. |

**Hook:** "Bjorn meets the party in the Vigil Fortress, alone. He holds a fragment of divine bone no larger than his palm, scratched with crude lettering: *Merren Vask lives. The quiet has teeth.* Merren Vask was one of fifty-four scouts sent into the Hollowdeep eighteen months ago. None returned. Bjorn has told only one other person about this message. He needs someone to find out if it is genuine."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The message is genuine. [[Merren Vask]] died in the Hollowdeep and rose as a Remnant, retaining her intelligence and mission focus. She has spent eighteen months hiding in the crater periphery, observing the organized undead formations, mapping patrol routes, and scratching intelligence into her own armor. She cannot escape because the rim is patrolled by coordinated undead sentries. Reaching her requires navigating the crater's outer ring, which is crawling with bone-wraiths and organized patrols unlike anything the Vigil has encountered. Rescuing Vask delivers critical military intelligence: the undead are drilling in formations, building structures, and operating under a command structure radiating from [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s skull. This is the first concrete proof that the Hollowdeep silence is preparation, not dormancy.

## 2. The Whispering Storm

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Thessa Ironveil]], Commander of the [[The Ashen Vigil|Ashen Vigil]] |
> | **Objective** | Intercept and study the next bone-storm before it reaches Greyhold, and determine why recent storms carry whispers that do not belong to Morrhael |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 250 gold, Vigil field equipment (Morrhaelite lenses, bone-ward talismans), access to the Watcher's Archive at Lastlight |
> | **Complication** | The storm must be studied in the open Wastes, where exposure to bone-dust causes death-sight and tissue necrosis. Time in the storm is measured in minutes, not hours. |

**Hook:** "Thessa's briefing is precise and rapid. Bone-storms have increased forty percent this year. That alone is troubling. But the last three storms carried something new: whispers. Not wind-noise, not the ambient hum of Morrhael's remains, but words. Fragmented, repeating, in a voice that the Ash-Speakers cannot identify. Thessa needs a team willing to ride into the next storm with recording equipment and survive long enough to capture a sample."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The whispers are [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s voice, projected through Morrhael's broken death-domain. Serith has learned to piggyback on the necromantic energy radiating from the Marrow Fields, using bone-storms as a broadcast medium. The whispers are not directed at the living. They are commands to the dead, coordinating the Hollowdeep's military buildup from the Abyssal Trench. Capturing and translating the whispers reveals fragmented tactical orders: formation designations, timing references, and one phrase repeated like a heartbeat: *"When the flame dims."* This refers to the Eternal Flame at [[The Pyre]]. Serith is waiting for an opportunity to corrupt or extinguish it.

## 3. Frostmere's Silence

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | Holdwarden Bjorn Ashken, via the Holdwardens' Council |
> | **Objective** | Travel to [[Frostmere]], a fishing hold on the Bitter Sea that has gone silent, and determine why no messages have arrived in six weeks |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 200 gold, salvage rights to anything recovered, Bjorn's public endorsement |
> | **Complication** | The overland route to Frostmere passes through the Marrow Fields' northern edge. The coastal route is faster but exposed to the Bitter Sea's unpredictable storms. Neither is safe. |

**Hook:** "Bjorn spreads a map across the table in the Hall of Names. His finger rests on a dot at the coast's edge. 'Frostmere. Eight hundred people. Last message six weeks ago, routine supply request. Nothing since. I have sent two riders. Neither returned. I cannot send Vigil, I need every fighter on the walls. I need someone expendable.' He looks up. 'That came out wrong. I need someone capable who is not already committed to dying somewhere else.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Frostmere is not destroyed. It is occupied. The settlement's population is alive but under the control of a Remnant who calls herself the Tidewatcher, a centuries-old undead who retained her intelligence but lost her empathy over the long years. She has sealed Frostmere and is using the living population as bait, waiting for the Vigil to send a rescue force she can ambush and convert. She serves no faction; she is acting on her own deteriorating judgment, believing that converting the living into Remnants is mercy, sparing them the suffering of a death that no longer works properly. The situation is a moral crisis for [[Revenant Yael]] and the Remnants' Court: the Tidewatcher is proof that Remnant consciousness can erode into something monstrous, undermining Yael's argument that Remnants are people deserving rights.

## 4. The Pyre Ground Disturbance

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | Fire Watch Captain Orin Ashward |
> | **Objective** | Investigate why the Pyre Ground's flames briefly turned black during last night's cremation ceremony and ensure the fire is not compromised |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 150 gold, Fire Watch gratitude, a Morrhaelite bone-ward (minor protective artifact) |
> | **Complication** | The Pyre Ground is Greyhold's most sacred site. Any suggestion that the fire is failing will cause panic. The investigation must be discreet. |

**Hook:** "Captain Ashward catches the party at dawn, voice low. During last night's burning, the flames turned black for eleven seconds. The body on the pyre sat up. It did not rise, it was not a Remnant event, but the corpse's mouth opened and a voice emerged that was not the dead man's. It spoke a name no one recognized. Then the flames returned to normal and the body was consumed. Ashward has told no one. He needs answers before the next burning at sundown."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The black flame is [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] testing her ability to project through Morrhael's death-domain. The Eternal Flame at [[The Pyre]] draws residual divine energy from Morrhael's remains; Serith has learned to touch this connection. The Greyhold Pyre Ground draws from the same source on a smaller scale. The name the corpse spoke was "Edda Voss," one of the fifty-four lost Vigil scouts, now raised and serving as Serith's eyes on the Hollowdeep rim. Serith was not sending a message to the living. She was calling roll. Investigating the Pyre Ground's divine-bone foundation reveals trace contamination: a hair-thin thread of shadow-energy woven into the ward structure, identical to the shadow-roots found in [[The Verdant Marches]]. Serith's reach extends further than anyone realized.

## 5. The Remnant Accords

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Revenant Yael]], leader of the [[Remnants Court|Remnants' Court]] |
> | **Objective** | Escort Yael to Greyhold for a formal appeal to extend the Remnant Accords, and protect her from those who would see the appeal fail |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | Remnants' Court alliance, Yael's intelligence network (Remnants can sense undead at considerable range), passage through Ashenmere |
> | **Complication** | At least two Hold delegations have vowed to destroy Yael on sight if she enters Greyhold. Bjorn supports the appeal but cannot guarantee her safety without appearing to take sides. |

**Hook:** "Yael meets the party at the Ashenmere gate, standing perfectly still in maintained Vigil armor. She does not breathe. She does not blink unless she remembers to. 'I need to reach Greyhold alive. Not alive. Present. Functional. You understand the difficulty.' A pause, then something that might be dark humor: 'I have been dead for longer than most of your soldiers have been alive. I can wait. But the Hollowdeep cannot, and neither can the people who will die because the Holds refuse to use every weapon available to them.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Yael's appeal is genuine and strategically sound: Remnants can sense undead movement at range, making them invaluable early-warning systems against a Hollowdeep offensive. The opposition comes from Holdwarden Torvin of [[Bonecairn]], who lost his family to a Remnant that deteriorated without warning, and from an Ashen Dominion priest traveling with the southern trade caravan who considers all undead abominations. The priest, Brother Aldren, carries sealed orders from [[Hierarch Valdren III]] to assess whether the Pale Wastes' tolerance of Remnants constitutes heresy. If Yael's appeal succeeds, it strengthens the Wastes' defenses but draws Dominion scrutiny. If it fails, the Remnants withdraw cooperation at the worst possible time. Bjorn will support Yael privately but needs the party to make the public case.

## 6. The Lost Fifty-Four

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Thessa Ironveil]], Commander of the Ashen Vigil |
> | **Objective** | Join Thessa's expedition into the Hollowdeep to determine the fate of the fifty-four lost scouts and assess the undead threat |
> | **Difficulty** | Very High |
> | **Reward** | 500 gold, Vigil commendation, intelligence that will shape the defense of the entire Pale Wastes |
> | **Complication** | Thessa has seen a figure on the crater rim using Vigil field-signs. It may be a survivor signaling for rescue. It may be bait. |

**Hook:** "Thessa stands at Lastlight's ridge, looking down into the Hollowdeep. The crater is thirty miles across. Tens of thousands of dead fill it. She has watched them for months through Morrhaelite lenses: drilling, building, organizing. She turns to the party. 'I sent fifty-four scouts into that crater. Fifty-four. I will not send more. I will go myself. What I need is someone to come with me who understands that we may not come back, and who considers that an acceptable price for knowing what is down there.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> This is the Pale Wastes' endgame expedition. The Hollowdeep contains an organized undead army numbering in the tens of thousands, coordinated by Serith's influence channeled through Morrhael's skull. The figure on the rim is Scout-Captain Edda Voss, one of the fifty-four, now raised and serving as Serith's observer. She retains enough memory to use Vigil field-signs but is no longer autonomous. Deeper in the crater, the party will find evidence of [[Merren Vask]]'s survival (if they have not already rescued her in Quest 1). At the crater's core, Morrhael's skull radiates a rhythmic pulse that is not necromantic entropy but a signal, a countdown. Buried near the skull is [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s black lantern, the divine artifact that could theoretically restore the death-passage and depopulate the Wastes of mindless undead overnight. Serith knows its location and has arranged the army specifically to guard it. The expedition should be one of the campaign's most harrowing experiences: descending into the largest concentration of undead in Aethermourne, surrounded by an army that is not a horde but a military force with tactics, formations, and a purpose the party does not yet understand.
