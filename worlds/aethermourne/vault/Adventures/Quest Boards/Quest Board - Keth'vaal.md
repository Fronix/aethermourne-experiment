# Quest Board: Keth'vaal

*The oldest city in the world does not post quest boards. It issues invitations, challenges, and tests.*

---

## 1. The Speaker's Challenge

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Speaker Yavenne]], Open Hand faction |
> | **Objective** | Prove the party's worth to the Circle of Speakers by completing a ritual trial in the Rootways |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | Official diplomatic standing in the Oldborn Kingdoms, limited Deep Library access, Speaker Yavenne's open support |
> | **Complication** | The trial is designed for Oldborn. Outlanders carrying divine residue face additional obstacles the trial was not designed to account for. |

**Hook:** "Yavenne meets the party in the Ash Market's outlander section, speaking quickly. She has secured them an opportunity: the Circle will grant diplomatic standing to any outlander who completes the Rootway Trial, a navigation challenge through the underground network. No outlander has ever attempted it. The Sealed Gate faction expects them to fail publicly."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Rootway Trial requires navigating from Keth'vaal to a checkpoint and back using only Primordial-pact markers for guidance. The markers react to divine residue by going dark, leaving the party blind in sections. Yavenne has secretly arranged for a sympathetic Pact-Weaver to leave alternative markers, but the Sealed Gate's [[Speaker Kaelthen]] has discovered this and removed them. The trial becomes genuinely dangerous. Completing it despite Kaelthen's sabotage earns even his grudging respect.

## 2. The Deep Library Request

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Archivist Vel]], keeper of the Deep Library |
> | **Objective** | Retrieve a text from the Library's restricted seventh level, a book that has been "moving" between shelves on its own |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | One hour of unrestricted research access in the restricted collection, Vel's personal guidance on any topic |
> | **Complication** | The book is moving because a Primordial entity has nested in the seventh level. Vel cannot enter, their perfect-recall pact makes them too "visible" to the entity. |

**Hook:** "Vel approaches the party without preamble, speaking in a voice that resonates at uncomfortable frequencies. A text is misbehaving. They need someone whose mind is not pact-bound to enter the seventh level and retrieve it. They offer knowledge in exchange. From Vel, this is an extraordinary offer."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The entity on the seventh level is a fragment of [[The Primordials#Yith|Yith]], the Dreaming Deep, that has seeped upward through the Library's foundation. It is not hostile, it is dreaming, and its dreams are reshuffling the Library's contents. The book Vel wants is a pre-Covenant text that survived the memory erasure, hidden in the Library by an unknown archivist centuries ago. It describes the entity the Oldborn worshipped before the Covenant. Vel suspects this but will not say so directly.

## 3. The Anchor Diagnosis

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | Senior Pact-Weaver Council |
> | **Objective** | Determine why the Great Anchor is developing micro-fractures using knowledge the Oldborn do not possess (divine magic theory) |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | 500 gold equivalent in Primordial-crafted goods, Pact-Weaver Council favor, basic Primordial negotiation training for one party member |
> | **Complication** | The diagnosis requires the party to touch the Great Anchor, which is sacred ground. This is politically explosive. |

**Hook:** "The Pact-Weaver Council convenes an unprecedented meeting with the outlander party. The Great Anchor is fracturing. Oldborn diagnostics find nothing. The fractures do not respond to Primordial analysis. The Council's theory: the cause is not Primordial in origin. It is *divine*. They need someone who understands divine energy to examine the Anchor."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The fractures are caused by temporal resonance from [[The Loom's Edge]]. The awakening fate-threads are emitting a frequency that Primordial structures cannot process, it passes through them like radiation through glass, causing structural damage. The party's divine-magic knowledge allows them to identify the resonance pattern, but the solution requires both Primordial and divine understanding working together, a collaboration that has never been attempted. This is the first concrete evidence that the Loom's Edge threatens the entire continent, not just the forbidden interior.

## 4. The Unwritten Speaker

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | An unnamed woman who appears at the party's lodging |
> | **Objective** | Help a former Speaker who has been "unwritten" (erased from all Oldborn records and memory) prove her innocence |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | Critical information about the Rootways' deepest levels, political leverage within the Circle |
> | **Complication** | The party are the only people who remember her (outlanders are not bound by the Covenant's erasure). Every Oldborn they ask will deny she ever existed. |

**Hook:** "A woman appears at the party's door at midnight. She has no name, not one she has forgotten but one that has been taken. She says she was a Speaker. She says she was unwritten for discovering something in the Rootways. She says the party is the only people in the world who can see her."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The unwritten Speaker discovered the entrance to a chamber in the deep Rootways that contains the Covenant of Ash's original terms, including the clause describing what the Oldborn paid: their collective memory of their pre-Covenant worship. She brought this to the Circle. The Circle panicked and unwritten her to suppress the discovery. [[Speaker Kaelthen]] ordered the unwriting. [[Speaker Yavenne]] does not know it happened. Restoring the Speaker requires proving the unwriting was politically motivated rather than justified, which means confronting the Circle with evidence of its own corruption.

## 5. The Pact-Weaver's Bargain

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | An elderly Pact-Weaver named Tessohn |
> | **Objective** | One party member learns the basics of Primordial negotiation by completing a live pact with a minor elemental force |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | Basic Primordial negotiation ability (necessary for Loom's Edge survival), understanding of Oldborn magic |
> | **Complication** | Primordial pacts always cost something personal. The cost is unknown until the pact is struck. |

**Hook:** "Tessohn finds the party at the Ash Market and speaks without greeting: 'You will need what I can teach if you intend to go where I believe you are going. The cost is real. It is always real. But the alternative is going into the Edge without protection, and that is not a cost. That is death.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Tessohn is one of [[Speaker Yavenne]]'s allies, instructed to prepare the outlanders for the Loom's Edge expedition. The pact-training is legitimate and necessary, without basic anchoring and negotiation skills, the Loom's temporal distortions are unnavigable. The cost of the training pact will be something meaningful to the PC: a cherished memory, the ability to taste a specific flavor, the capacity to feel a particular emotion. Minor, but real. This is how Oldborn magic works. Nothing is free.

## 6. The Diplomatic Banquet

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Speaker Yavenne]], Open Hand faction |
> | **Objective** | Attend a formal banquet where all three Oldborn factions are present, and prevent [[Speaker Dravos]] from provoking a diplomatic incident with the outlander delegation |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | Deepened relationships with all three factions, intelligence about each faction's true positions, potential alliance |
> | **Complication** | Dravos has planted an agent among the outlander delegation who will "accidentally" insult Oldborn customs, giving Dravos justification to demand the outlanders' expulsion. |

**Hook:** "Yavenne's invitation arrives on crystalline paper: the party is invited to the Circle's diplomatic banquet as honored guests. She includes a private note: 'Dravos is planning something. I cannot stop it from inside. Watch the outlander delegation. Not everyone in it is what they seem.'"

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The banquet is a political minefield. Oldborn custom requires guests to offer a truth about themselves before eating (a remnant of pre-Covenant pact culture). The party must decide what truths to share. Dravos's agent is a mercenary hired from [[Tidecross]], briefed to spill wine on a sacred object. If the party identifies the agent before the act, they can expose Dravos's manipulation. If they cannot, they must manage the fallout: Kaelthen will use the incident to push for isolation, and Yavenne's position weakens dramatically.
