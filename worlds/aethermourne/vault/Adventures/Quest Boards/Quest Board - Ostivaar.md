# Quest Board: Ostivaar

*Within the bones of a dead god, order is law. But law is not justice.*

---

## 1. The Hollow Chamber

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Foreman Edda Haelstrom]], Spine mining overseer |
> | **Objective** | Investigate a newly discovered chamber inside Solvaen's Spine where miners have become unresponsive |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 200 gold, Church sponsorship token, access to restricted Spine levels |
> | **Complication** | The chamber contains spherical Ashite crystals that project visions of Solvaen's final moments. The Church wants this suppressed. |

**Hook:** "Six miners walked into the chamber. Six miners sat down. None of them have moved in three days. They are alive, breathing, eyes open. They will not speak. They will not eat. Haelstrom needs answers before the Church sends its own investigators."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The spherical crystals are memory-stones, fragments of Solvaen's dying consciousness. The miners are experiencing Solvaen's last thoughts on loop: his realization that the Theomachis was inevitable, his acceptance, and his final prayer, not to his followers, but to [[The Twelve#Orenthas\|Orenthas]], asking whether this was always the plan. The Church will destroy these crystals if they learn what they contain.

## 2. The Unbound Congregation's Plea

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Elowen Greaves]], leader of the Unbound Congregation |
> | **Objective** | Smuggle a group of Hewn-caste refugees out of the Lower Ribs before the Cinders arrest them |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 100 gold (all Greaves can afford), Unbound Congregation trust, information about Cinder operations |
> | **Complication** | One of the refugees is a former Cinder informant who turned. [[Delric Mourne]] wants them back, alive. |

**Hook:** "Greaves meets the party in a back-alley shrine, hands shaking. Twelve families marked for arrest. Their crime: attending an unsanctioned prayer meeting. The Cinders will come at dawn. She has a route through the Ashmarket sewers, but she needs protection."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The former Cinder informant, Petra Venn, has seen [[Delric Mourne]]'s secret shrine to [[The Twelve#Serith\|Serith]]. She is the only living witness to the head of the Cinders worshipping the imprisoned goddess. Getting her testimony to the right people could bring down the Cinders. Mourne knows this. He has deployed three Cinder squads to find her.

## 3. The Graffiti Campaign

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Commander Ashara Voss]], Order of the Ashen Flame |
> | **Objective** | Identify the source of *"The order is a lie"* graffiti appearing in impossible locations across Ostivaar |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 250 gold, military escort privileges, Voss's confidence |
> | **Complication** | The graffiti artist is a former Cinder agent who discovered Mourne's Serith worship. Voss does not know the Cinders are compromised. |

**Hook:** "The phrase keeps appearing: on the Hierarch's gate, on the Cathedral steps, inside sealed rooms. No one has been caught. Voss suspects magical assistance. She wants the vandal found, but she also wants to know what 'the order is a lie' means. She senses it is more than paint."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The graffiti artist, Cassia Lorn, is a former Cinder who discovered Mourne's shrine during a routine security sweep. Unable to report it through Cinder channels (Mourne controls them all), she chose public disruption. Each graffiti location is a clue: together they spell coordinates to the shrine's location. The party can follow the trail to the shrine, blow open the Cinder conspiracy, or be co-opted by one side or the other.

## 4. The Ashmarket Poisoning

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Torren Hearthforge]], Guildmaster |
> | **Objective** | Determine why Ashite tools sold in the Ashmarket are causing Order Sickness in their users |
> | **Difficulty** | Low |
> | **Reward** | 150 gold, a custom-forged Ashite item, Hearthforge's trust |
> | **Complication** | The tools are not defective. They are sabotaged, tainted with trace amounts of a substance that amplifies Ashite's order-enforcement to dangerous levels. |

**Hook:** "Hearthforge's reputation is being destroyed. Three craftsmen using his tools have developed Order Sickness, their thoughts rigidifying, their creativity dying. Hearthforge swears his work is clean. He needs the party to find where the contamination is entering the supply chain."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The sabotage traces to a Cinder operation. Mourne is deliberately amplifying Order Sickness in the working class to create a more docile population. The tainted substance is derived from concentrated Ashite combined with a reagent stolen from the Church's restricted stores. Exposing this threatens both the Cinders and elements of the Church who approved the requisition without asking questions.

## 5. The Hierarch's Whisper

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Prior Gault Ashworth]], mid-ranking Church official |
> | **Objective** | Deliver a sealed message from Prior Ashworth to [[Hierarch Valdren III]] without Cinder interception |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | 400 gold, Church endorsement, private audience with the Hierarch |
> | **Complication** | The Hierarch is effectively imprisoned in his own throne room. Access requires bypassing Cinder checkpoints. Ashworth's message contains evidence of Mourne's corruption. |

**Hook:** "Ashworth pulls the party aside after a public sermon, eyes haunted. He has documentation proving Cinder misconduct, enough to turn the Hierarch against Mourne. But Mourne controls access to the throne. Every official channel is compromised. He needs someone the Cinders do not know."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Ashworth's evidence is real but incomplete, it proves financial corruption, not Serith worship. If delivered to Valdren, it forces a confrontation between the Hierarch and Mourne, but Mourne will escalate: revealing that Valdren himself signed off on several questionable Cinder operations. The result is a political crisis that can either topple the Cinders or entrench them, depending on the party's follow-up actions.

## 6. The Lower Ribs Uprising

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Captain Daren Kolst]], City Watch |
> | **Objective** | De-escalate rising tensions in the Lower Ribs before a full uprising erupts |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 175 gold, safe passage through all Ostivaar districts, Kolst's network of street informants |
> | **Complication** | The catalyst is an unjust execution. Both sides have legitimate grievances. The Cinders are deliberately stoking the conflict to justify martial law. |

**Hook:** "Kolst meets the party at a checkpoint, voice tight. A Hewn-caste worker was publicly executed yesterday for 'sedition,' the real charge was refusing to inform for the Cinders. The Lower Ribs are mobilizing. If violence breaks out, the Cinders will use it as justification to crack down. Hard."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Elowen Greaves]] is trying to keep the protest nonviolent, but she is losing control. A Cinder provocateur is embedded in the crowd, armed with an incendiary device designed to make the uprising turn bloody. If the party identifies the provocateur before the device is used, they can prove Cinder manipulation and shift public opinion. If they fail, the Lower Ribs burn, and Mourne gets his martial law.
