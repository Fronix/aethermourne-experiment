# Quest Board: Riftmarket

*Reality is negotiable here. So is everything else.*

---

## 1. The Vanishing Dust Shipment

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Oren Blackfinger]], Master Dust-Trader |
> | **Objective** | Track a missing shipment of Vorrhyn's Dust and Empyrean Shards that disappeared between Edgewatch and the Dust Exchange |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 350 gold in trade credit at the Dust Exchange, plus a personal favor from Riftmarket's most connected merchant |
> | **Complication** | The escort guides arrived Changed and cannot remember the last three hours of their journey. Something in the Shimmer Zone took the shipment, and it left the guides' memories rearranged. |

**Hook:** "Oren catches the party near the Dust Exchange, his permanently stained black hands gesturing urgently. A shipment of stabilized Vorrhyn's Dust, two sealed crates and a lockbox of Empyrean Shards, never arrived. The guides stumbled into Riftmarket three hours late, Changed: one now sees colors as sounds, the other speaks backward when frightened. Neither remembers anything between the Shimmer Zone waypoint and the Anchor Stone. Oren has noticed unusual buying patterns for Empyrean Shards lately. This feels connected."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The shipment was intercepted by [[The Veil Unbound]] agents operating out of [[Shimmer]]. They are stockpiling Empyrean Shards for a ritual at the Maelstrom's edge, one designed to widen the wound in reality enough for [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s influence to reach the [[Cosmology and Magic#The Loom of Fate|Loom of Fate]]. The guides were Changed deliberately, a side effect of the Unbound's use of inverted Primordial resonance to mask their theft. Tracking the shipment leads through the Shimmer Zone to a hidden cache near the Threshold border, guarded by a Veil agent posing as a Shimmer-glass buyer. The cache contains not just this shipment but three previous ones, enough Empyrean Shards to alarm anyone who understands what they could be used for.

## 2. The Face in the Glass

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Prior Echoes]], Prior of the Echoists |
> | **Objective** | Investigate a piece of Shimmer-glass sealed in the Still House that has developed independent movement and begun whispering |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | 500 gold, Echoist contacts across the Shattered Edge, insight into the Maelstrom's nature |
> | **Complication** | The face in the glass is not a reflection. It is looking back from the other side of the Maelstrom, and it has noticed the party. |

**Hook:** "Prior Echoes seeks the party at the Guide Hall, expression troubled beneath shifting scalp-patterns. A master glassworker in [[Shimmer]] captured something in a piece of Shimmer-glass, a face that looks back and moves independently. The piece was sealed in the Still House, Shimmer's containment facility for dangerous distortion artifacts. Now it is whispering. Prior Echoes does not know what it is saying, but two Echoists who listened too long have begun sleepwalking toward the Threshold border. He needs someone to examine the glass, determine what is on the other side, and stop it from pulling anyone else toward the Maelstrom."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The face belongs to something on the other side of the Maelstrom that has noticed the thinning barrier. It is not hostile in the way a creature is hostile. It is curious, and its attention is itself dangerous, intensifying the Shimmer Zone's distortions and pulling susceptible minds toward the Threshold. The two sleepwalking Echoists are being drawn by the entity's curiosity, not malice. The glass can be neutralized by severing its resonance connection (Arcana DC 16 and a stabilized Anchor shard) or destroyed outright (which releases a burst of Shimmer-zone distortion, DC 15 Str save or temporarily Changed for 1d4 hours). The deeper question: if something on the Maelstrom's other side is reaching through, what happens when the barrier thins further? This connects to the Veil Unbound's efforts to widen the wound.

## 3. The Probability Merchant

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Tyrell Vane]], Senior Edgewalker |
> | **Objective** | Find and stop a con artist in the Wander-Lanes who is selling "guaranteed futures" to desperate merchants, then determine how they are actually reading probability threads |
> | **Difficulty** | Moderate |
> | **Reward** | 300 gold, priority standing with [[The Accord of Echoes]], Edgewalker recommendation letter (valuable across the Shattered Edge) |
> | **Complication** | The con artist's predictions are accurate. Every single one. No non-Edgewalker should be able to read probability threads, and the method they are using has side effects they do not understand. |

**Hook:** "Tyrell Vane summons the party to the Anchor Stone. His luminescent eyes, a remnant of his own Changing twenty years ago, track something invisible as he speaks. A vendor in the Wander-Lanes is selling fortunes, probability readings sold as certainties. Three merchants followed the vendor's advice, and all three profited enormously. Now a fourth merchant followed advice that led to a deal with an Oldborn delegation, and the deal is souring in ways that could damage Riftmarket's trade agreements. Tyrell cannot intervene personally, an Edgewalker investigating a civilian fortune-teller would cause panic. He needs outsiders."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The "con artist" is a Foretouched Changed, someone who was exposed to temporal bleed from [[The Loom's Edge]] during a recent surge and now perceives fragments of probability threads without training or control. They are not fraudulent, they genuinely see futures, but they cannot distinguish between probable futures and merely possible ones. Their readings are accurate roughly 80% of the time, which is enough to build a reputation and dangerous enough to cause catastrophe. The failed fourth prediction was not wrong, it was a probability thread that collapsed when a Veil Unbound agent deliberately interfered with the Oldborn deal. Finding the Foretouched reveals two things: Loom's Edge bleed is creating Changed with unprecedented abilities, and someone is manipulating probability threads for political ends. Tyrell will want the Foretouched brought to him for training and protection, not punishment.

## 4. The Mirror's Offer

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[The Mirror]], information broker (approaches the party, not the other way around) |
> | **Objective** | Retrieve a sealed case from a Dominion "containment initiative" delegation anchored in Riftmarket's harbor, without being traced |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | Information: The Mirror will answer one question about anyone or anything on the Shattered Edge, truthfully and completely |
> | **Complication** | The sealed case contains ward-specifications for a barrier the Dominion plans to build around the entire Shattered Edge. If the barrier is built, the Edge is cut off from the known continent. If the specifications are leaked, the Dominion sends soldiers instead of diplomats. |

**Hook:** "The party is approached by someone whose features are difficult to remember. The Mirror never uses the same face twice. They speak precisely and without warmth: 'The Dominion delegation in the harbor is not here to help. They are here to contain. I need to know what they are planning. You need to know it too, whether you realize it yet or not. Bring me the sealed case from the delegation leader's cabin. I will tell you something worth more than gold in return.' The Mirror offers no further explanation and no assurance of safety. The offer stands for twenty-four hours."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The sealed case contains the Dominion's full containment initiative plans: a ward-wall encircling the Shattered Edge, powered by Ashite columns, that would seal the Maelstrom's influence inside a barrier. The Dominion frames this as protection. In practice, it would imprison everyone living on the Edge inside a shrinking zone of distortion. [[Kessa Thornwall]] at [[Edgewatch]] has been refusing the Dominion's overtures, but the delegation plans to bypass her and negotiate directly with settlement representatives. The Mirror wants the plans because they are building leverage against the Dominion, but their motives are complicated: they were originally from the Dominion, Changed and discarded. This is personal. Retrieving the case requires infiltrating the delegation's ship (DC 14 Stealth through harbor patrol, DC 16 Thieves' Tools on the cabin lock, or creative alternatives). The delegation leader, Assessor Hale, is competent and suspicious. Getting caught creates a diplomatic incident that [[Tyrell Vane]] must resolve.

## 5. The Ward Collapse

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Lysara Shimmerborn]], Head Researcher at [[The Last Certainty]] (via urgent message to Riftmarket) |
> | **Objective** | Escort a replacement ward-crystal from the Dust Exchange to The Last Certainty before the outer stabilization ring fails completely |
> | **Difficulty** | Very High |
> | **Reward** | 600 gold, access to The Last Certainty's archives (invaluable for any scholar or mage), a stabilized Anchor shard |
> | **Complication** | The route passes through the Shimmer Zone, which is intensifying. The ward-crystal itself attracts Maelstrom distortion. And someone inside The Last Certainty is actively sabotaging the wards. |

**Hook:** "A message arrives at the Guide Hall, carried by an Edgewalker courier who looks like she has not slept in days. [[Lysara Shimmerborn]]'s outer ring wards failed for seventeen minutes last week. During the failure, nearby books rewrote themselves in an alien language. The wards were restored, but the outer ring is degrading faster than repairs can hold. She needs a specific ward-crystal from the Dust Exchange, and she needs it delivered through increasingly unstable territory by people who can fight if the Shimmer Zone decides to swallow the road."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The ward degradation is not natural. A junior researcher named Aldis is a [[The Veil Unbound]] mole, deliberately weakening the wards from inside by introducing micro-fractures in the stabilization matrices. [[Lysara Shimmerborn]] suspects something, she perceives Aldis behaving differently in one of the three timelines she simultaneously tracks, but she cannot identify the source without proof. The escort mission is dangerous (2-3 Shimmer Zone distortion encounters, the crystal attracting a minor Maelstrom surge) but the real challenge is delivering the crystal only to watch the wards degrade again. Investigating the internal sabotage leads to Aldis, whose Veil Unbound handler operates from Shimmer. Exposing the mole protects The Last Certainty, but Aldis has dead-drop protocols, and their handler will know the operation is compromised within hours.

## 6. The Woman from the Maelstrom

> [!info] Quest Details
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Quest Giver** | [[Kessa Thornwall]], Warden of [[Edgewatch]] (message relayed through Riftmarket Edgewalker post) |
> | **Objective** | Interview and protect a woman who claims to have walked out of the Maelstrom's center, and determine whether her story is true |
> | **Difficulty** | High |
> | **Reward** | 500 gold, Edgewatch garrison support for one future mission, information that could reshape understanding of the Maelstrom |
> | **Complication** | The woman is carrying a wooden cup that cannot be Changed by any Maelstrom distortion. Three factions already want her. The Veil Unbound wants her silenced. |

**Hook:** "Kessa Thornwall's message is terse: 'A woman walked out of the Threshold yesterday carrying a cup. A wooden cup. Unchanged. Nothing survives the Threshold unchanged. I need people I can trust who are not on my garrison roster, which means people nobody is watching. Come to Edgewatch. Tell no one.' The messenger adds, quietly, that two strangers have already been asking questions about the woman in Riftmarket's Crossroads Market."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The woman genuinely walked out of the Maelstrom's center, having touched the [[Cosmology and Magic#The Loom of Fate|Loom's Edge]]. She was stabilized by proximity to the Loom, the opposite of the normal Changing effect. The wooden cup is proof, an object that passed through the Maelstrom's heart without distortion, which should be impossible. She carries fragmented knowledge from the Loom's Edge: partial visions of probability threads, awareness of excised patterns [[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas]] buried there, and the sense that something is pulling the Maelstrom open from the other side. The Veil Unbound wants her because she knows things about the Maelstrom's interior that could advance or compromise their plans. The Accord of Echoes wants her testimony. The Echoists want her as proof that the Maelstrom remembers [[The Twelve#Vorrhyn|Vorrhyn]]. Protecting her means choosing which faction to trust, and whether to share her knowledge or keep it hidden. The two strangers in the market are Veil Unbound scouts. If they locate the woman before the party reaches Edgewatch, they will attempt extraction, not assassination, she is too valuable to kill.

> [!warning]- GM Notes
> **Factional web:** Every quest on this board connects to the Veil Unbound's operations on the Shattered Edge. Quests 1, 5, and 6 directly involve Unbound agents. Quests 2 and 3 reveal consequences of the Maelstrom's widening, which the Unbound is accelerating. Quest 4 introduces the Dominion's containment response, which is both a threat to Edge independence and a legitimate (if brutal) response to the Unbound's activities. Let the players discover these connections organically.
>
> **Escalation:** These quests are designed to be run in roughly ascending order of stakes, but they work in any sequence. Quest 6 functions as the capstone, introducing the Maelstrom woman whose knowledge reframes everything the players have learned.
>
> **Connecting to campaign:** The Shattered Edge is where [[The Twelve#Vorrhyn|Vorrhyn]]'s destruction and [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s ambitions intersect. The Maelstrom is both scar and doorway. The Veil Unbound wants to widen it. The Dominion wants to wall it off. The Accord of Echoes wants to understand it. The players' choices here determine which approach gains ground, with consequences that ripple across both the Shattered Edge and the known continent.
