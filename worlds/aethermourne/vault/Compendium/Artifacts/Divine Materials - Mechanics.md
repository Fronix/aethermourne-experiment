# Divine Materials: Mechanical Reference

> [!info]
> This document provides gameplay mechanics for the divine materials described in [[Cosmology and Magic]]. Each entry covers: what the material does, how to acquire it, what it costs to use, and what happens with prolonged exposure.

---

## General Rules

### Exposure and Consequence

All divine materials carry risk. A single use is dramatic but manageable. Repeated use is transformative. Track exposure with a simple tier system:

| Tier | Exposure Level | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | No contact | No effect |
| 1 | Single use or brief contact | Minor sensory effect, fades in hours |
| 2 | Multiple uses or prolonged contact | Persistent side effect, fades in days |
| 3 | Regular use over weeks | Permanent minor alteration, does not fade |
| 4 | Heavy use over months | Major transformation, reversible only through extraordinary means |
| 5 | Sustained dependency | Terminal condition or fundamental identity change |

Each use of a divine material raises exposure by one tier. Exposure decreases by one tier per month of complete abstinence. Tier 3+ effects are permanent regardless of abstinence.

### Crafting General Rules

Crafting with divine materials requires three things:
1. **Skill.** Only trained artisans can work divine materials. Untrained attempts waste the material and risk immediate Tier 2 exposure.
2. **Forge conditions.** Most divine materials require specific conditions: geothermal heat for forgeheart steel, living wood for Thyrea's Amber, salt water for Belara's Tears.
3. **Time.** Divine material crafting cannot be rushed. Minimum crafting time is one week for minor items, one month for significant items, three months for campaign-grade artifacts.

## Material Reference

### Ashite (Solvaen's Crystallized Blood)

> [!info]
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Source God** | [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]], God of Order and Law |
> | **Appearance** | Pale white crystal, faintly luminous, geometrically perfect |
> | **Primary Region** | [[The Ashen Dominion]] (abundant) |
> | **Rarity** | Common in the Dominion, expensive elsewhere |

**Mechanical Effect:** Amplifies structured magic. A calculist using Ashite as a focus gains perfect precision, spells execute exactly as designed with no deviation. Ashite can also enforce order on chaotic environments: placed in a room, it suppresses wild magic, calms emotional outbursts, and straightens crooked objects.

**Acquisition:** Mined from veins in Solvaen's spine (the geological formation beneath the Dominion). The [[Archonate|Archonate]] controls mining rights. Black market Ashite exists but is heavily policed.

**Cost of Use:**
- Tier 1: Thoughts become unusually organized for hours. Mild discomfort with disorder.
- Tier 2: Emotional range narrows. Creativity becomes difficult. Strong preference for routine.
- Tier 3: **Order Sickness onset.** Obsessive pattern-seeking. Cannot tolerate ambiguity. Social relationships strain.
- Tier 4: Catatonic response to genuine disorder. Cannot function outside structured environments.
- Tier 5: The mind crystallizes. The user becomes a living extension of Solvaen's order, aware but unable to deviate from rigid behavioral patterns.

**Crafting:** Ashite can be ground into focusing powder (amplifies a single spell), cut into lens-stones (permanent focus for calculist equipment), or woven into architectural keystones (structural wards that enforce physical order on a building). The [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] produces the finest Ashite-inlaid instruments, though they find the material philosophically distasteful.

### Thyrea's Amber (Hardened Divine Sap)

> [!info]
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Source God** | [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]], Goddess of Nature and Growth |
> | **Appearance** | Golden-green resin, warm to the touch, smells of living forest |
> | **Primary Region** | [[The Verdant Marches]] (moderate supply) |
> | **Rarity** | Uncommon, harvested carefully by [[The Greensingers]] |

**Mechanical Effect:** Enhances growth, healing, and primal magic. Applied to a wound, it accelerates natural healing dramatically. Applied to soil, it produces explosive plant growth. Used as a primal magic focus, it deepens communion with the land's awareness.

**Acquisition:** Harvested from the oldest trees in the Verdant Marches, where Thyrea's sap still rises through root systems. The Greensingers regulate harvesting to prevent over-extraction. Sustainable supply, but demand far exceeds it.

**Cost of Use:**
- Tier 1: Heightened awareness of living things. Mild euphoria in natural settings.
- Tier 2: Skin develops a faint green tinge. Appetite shifts toward raw, uncooked food.
- Tier 3: **Root-Mind onset.** Increasing difficulty distinguishing self from environment. Plants respond to emotions.
- Tier 4: Physical transformation begins. Bark-like patches on skin. Roots grow from feet if standing still too long.
- Tier 5: Consciousness merges with the ecosystem. The body roots into the ground. The person becomes part of the forest's awareness, technically alive, functionally gone.

**Crafting:** Thyrea's Amber can be shaped into healing salves (single-use, powerful), living wood grafts (permanently enhances a wooden weapon or tool with self-repair), or communion stones (Greensinger focus items that deepen land-connection). Cannot be worked with metal tools, only shaped by living hands or bone implements.

### Morrhaelite (Crystallized Divine Bone)

> [!info]
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Source God** | [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]], God of Death and the Afterlife |
> | **Appearance** | Dense black crystal with faint gray veins, cold to the touch |
> | **Primary Region** | [[The Pale Wastes]] (abundant) |
> | **Rarity** | Common in the Wastes, feared elsewhere |

**Mechanical Effect:** Anti-undead properties. Severs necromantic bonds, disrupts undead animation, creates wards that repel or contain the dead. A blade edged with Morrhaelite destroys undead on contact rather than merely damaging them. Wards inscribed in Morrhaelite prevent the dead from crossing.

**Acquisition:** Mined from Morrhael's skull in the Pale Wastes. The [[The Ashen Vigil|Ashen Vigil]] controls primary mining operations. Independent prospectors ("Bone-Callers") operate illegally in the outer deposits.

**Cost of Use:**
- Tier 1: Mild emotional numbness. Reduced fear of death. Brief sensation of cold.
- Tier 2: Persistent low-grade depression. Wounds stop bleeding quickly (reduced inflammatory response).
- Tier 3: **Death-Touched onset.** Body temperature drops permanently. Wounds do not bleed at all. Comfort in the presence of the dead.
- Tier 4: Aging stops. Natural healing ceases. The user exists in a state between life and death, functionally immortal but unable to recover from injuries without magical aid.
- Tier 5: The heartbeat stops. The user becomes partially undead, conscious, aware, but no longer fully alive. Morrhael's broken afterlife begins to pull at their soul.

**Crafting:** Morrhaelite is worked by the Ashen Vigil's Wardsmiths into weapons (undead-destroying edges), ward-stones (area denial against necromantic creatures), and containment vessels (trap and hold individual undead or souls). Requires cold-forging, heat destroys its properties.

### Kaevroth's Iron (Shattered Divine Body)

> [!info]
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Source God** | [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]], God of War and Conquest |
> | **Appearance** | Dark red-black iron, warm, faintly vibrating, smells of blood |
> | **Primary Region** | Scattered across battlefields, deepest deposits in [[The Emberveil]] |
> | **Rarity** | Rare, fragments found in deep mines and ancient battlefields |

**Mechanical Effect:** Amplifies aggression, ambition, and combat ability. A weapon incorporating Kaevroth's Iron hits harder, moves faster, and makes its wielder fearless. Armor made with it makes the wearer resistant to pain and fear. In both cases, the user becomes increasingly violent and unable to disengage from conflict.

**Acquisition:** Found in deep mining operations (especially in the Emberveil) and at sites of major Theomachis-era battles. The [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] maintains a Fragment Registry tracking known deposits. Unregistered fragments are illegal in Emberveil territory.

**Cost of Use:**
- Tier 1: Heightened confidence. Reduced hesitation. Mild irritability.
- Tier 2: Aggressive responses to minor provocations. Difficulty backing down from confrontation. Vivid combat dreams.
- Tier 3: **War-Fury onset.** Persistent paranoia. Everyone is a potential threat. Sleep is interrupted by visions of [[The Iron Descent]].
- Tier 4: Inability to feel peace. Every social interaction becomes adversarial. Physical changes: skin takes on a metallic sheen, body temperature rises.
- Tier 5: The user becomes an avatar of war, a relentless combat machine incapable of any emotion except rage and the grim satisfaction of victory. Kaevroth's domain claims them.

**Crafting:** Kaevroth's Iron must be forged at extreme temperatures (the Grand Forge in Crucible is one of few facilities capable). It can be alloyed with conventional steel for weapons and armor, or shaped into war-totems that amplify the combat ability of nearby allies (and their aggression). Only master smiths of the Ember Guild attempt it, and even they take precautions: forge-sessions are limited to four hours, and the smith is monitored for behavioral changes afterward.

### Forgeheart Steel (Thalvor's Ongoing Sacrifice)

> [!info]
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Source God** | [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]], God of Fire and Craft |
> | **Appearance** | Silver-gold metal with an amber inner glow, warm to the touch |
> | **Primary Region** | [[The Emberveil]], deep forge-chambers only |
> | **Rarity** | Rare, tightly controlled by the [[The Forge Council|Forge Council]] |

**Mechanical Effect:** The finest crafting material known. Forgeheart steel takes and holds enchantment better than any other metal. Blades forged from it never dull. Armor never dents. Tools never break. It carries a residual warmth, Thalvor's fire, that makes it effective against cold-based threats and comforting to hold in darkness.

**Acquisition:** Found only in the deepest mine shafts of the Emberveil, near the forge-chambers where Thalvor's heat is strongest. The Forge Council controls all extraction. Export is limited and extremely expensive. The Ember Guild's inner circle knows the approximate locations of the deepest accessible chambers and guards this knowledge.

**Cost of Use:** Forgeheart steel is unique among divine materials in that it carries **minimal personal risk**. Thalvor's sacrifice was one of pure generosity, his fire sustains without demanding. Prolonged contact produces only warmth, comfort, and an increasing appreciation for well-made things. At extreme exposure (Tier 4+), users may develop a compulsive need to create, to build, to make, unable to rest while they perceive imperfection. This is inconvenient but rarely dangerous.

**Crafting:** Can only be worked in the Grand Forge or similar geothermal forges. Conventional furnaces cannot reach the necessary temperatures. Forging forgeheart steel is considered a sacred act by the Ember Guild, performed with ritual precision and communal participation. A single forgeheart blade takes three master smiths working in rotation for one month.

### Belara's Tears (Crystallized Divine Grief)

> [!info]
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Source God** | [[The Twelve#Belara|Belara]], Goddess of Love and Mercy |
> | **Appearance** | Clear, teardrop-shaped crystals that feel warm and produce a faint, sorrowful hum |
> | **Primary Region** | The Weeping Isles |
> | **Rarity** | Uncommon, harvested from the shores of the Isles |

**Mechanical Effect:** Miraculous healing. Belara's Tears can cure diseases, mend old wounds, restore lost senses, and reverse magical corruption. A single Tear, dissolved in water and consumed, heals any non-fatal condition. Applied directly to a wound, it seals and regenerates tissue instantly. Applied to a cursed or corrupted object, it cleanses the corruption.

**Acquisition:** Found on the shores of the Weeping Isles, where Belara died shielding mortals from the Theomachis. The Tears wash ashore in irregular waves, more frequently during storms. No faction controls the Isles, but several maintain collection outposts. Harvesting is considered sacred, and violence on the Isles is taboo across all cultures.

**Cost of Use:**
- Tier 1: Bone-deep melancholy lasting hours. A sense of loss that has no specific object. The world seems beautiful and unbearably sad.
- Tier 2: Persistent empathy that borders on telepathy. Feeling others' pain as your own. Difficulty in combat, every wound you inflict hurts.
- Tier 3: **Mercy Sickness.** Inability to cause harm, even in self-defense. Compulsive healing behavior. The user cannot watch suffering without acting.
- Tier 4: The user absorbs the pain and grief of everyone nearby. Physical wounds appear on their body that mirror injuries sustained by others within range. They become a living conduit for Belara's sacrifice.
- Tier 5: The user dissolves into pure compassion, a gentle, warm presence that heals everything it touches but has no identity, no will, no self. They become an echo of Belara's final act, forever giving, forever grieving, forever gone.

**Crafting:** Belara's Tears cannot be forged, cut, or shaped by tools. They can only be dissolved (for healing draughts), set in living wood (for healing staves), or woven into cloth by hand (for mercy-cloaks that absorb damage meant for nearby allies). Crafting with Belara's Tears requires the artisan to weep genuine tears during the process. Forced or performative emotion produces no result.

### Empyrean Shards (Fragments of the Collapsed Heaven)

> [!info]
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Source God** | All of [[The Twelve]] (the collapsed Empyrean) |
> | **Appearance** | Iridescent fragments, no two alike, humming with unstable energy |
> | **Primary Region** | [[The Hollowed Reach]] (coastal deposits), scattered impact sites |
> | **Rarity** | Rare, unpredictable |

**Mechanical Effect:** Wildcard. Empyrean Shards retain traces of the Empyrean's collapsed divinity, but no single god's signature dominates. Effects are unpredictable: a Shard may grant a vision, warp local reality, produce a burst of healing, trigger a temporal anomaly, or do nothing at all. No two uses produce the same result.

**Acquisition:** Found at coastal impact sites in the Hollowed Reach and occasionally at inland locations where pieces of the Empyrean fell during the Theomachis. Cannot be reliably sought, only stumbled upon.

**Cost of Use:**
- Tier 1: Sensory displacement. Colors sound wrong. Sounds have texture. Lasts minutes.
- Tier 2: Brief spatial distortion. The user occupies slightly more or less space than they should.
- Tier 3: **Reality fraying.** Occasional involuntary glimpses of the Empyrean as it was, beautiful, whole, and gone.
- Tier 4: The user's body begins to reflect the Empyrean's shattered state. Parts of their form flicker, become transparent, or briefly vanish.
- Tier 5: The user becomes a living shard, a fragment of collapsed heaven walking around in mortal shape, beautiful and terrifying and no longer entirely present in any single moment.

**Crafting:** Empyrean Shards resist conventional crafting. They can be set into items as unstable power sources (the item gains a random beneficial effect each dawn but a random detrimental effect each dusk), or ground into iridescent dust that, when scattered, reveals hidden magical auras. The Star-Readers are the foremost experts in Shard manipulation, though even they admit they are guessing.

### Marrow-Water (Thyrea's Divine Marrow, Liquid)

> [!info]
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Source God** | [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]], Goddess of Nature and Growth |
> | **Appearance** | Viscous, golden-green liquid that glows faintly, smells of deep earth |
> | **Primary Region** | The Marrow Bogs, [[The Verdant Marches]] |
> | **Rarity** | Uncommon, growing more potent and more available (which is alarming) |

**Mechanical Effect:** Mutagenic transformation. Ingestion or immersion causes physical change aligned with the natural world: enhanced senses, increased strength, bestial features, accelerated healing. The transformation is permanent and cannot be fully controlled. The Mutagenists of Mirewatch have developed the **Green Draught**, a diluted form that provides more predictable (but still risky) enhancements.

**Acquisition:** Collected from the Marrow Bogs by the Mutagenists of Mirewatch. Supply is increasing as the Bogs expand, which alarms the Greensingers, who believe expansion indicates Thyrea's distress rather than generosity.

**Cost of Use:**
- Tier 1: Minor physical enhancement (sharpened senses, toughened skin). Mild disorientation.
- Tier 2: Visible mutation. Animal features emerge: claws, fur patches, altered eyes. Personality shifts toward instinctual behavior.
- Tier 3: **Chimeric transformation.** The body is no longer entirely human. Multiple animal features merge. The mind oscillates between rational thought and animal instinct.
- Tier 4: Loss of humanity. The body becomes a chimeric hybrid, powerful but unrecognizable. The mind is more animal than mortal. Speech becomes difficult. Relationships with non-transformed individuals collapse.
- Tier 5: Complete reversion to bestial form. The person is gone. What remains is a creature of the Marches, beautiful, powerful, and without memory of what it was.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Marrow-Water's increasing potency and its new secondary effect (despair) are caused by [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s influence bleeding through the deep root network into Thyrea's divine essence. The Green Draught is unknowingly distributing Serith's influence, making every enhanced Rootborn a potential vector. [[Vael Sundren]] suspects something is wrong but cannot identify the cause without outside help.

**Crafting:** Marrow-Water is used in alchemy, not forging. It can be distilled into the Green Draught (controlled mutation), concentrated into Rootblood (emergency healing, high mutation risk), or infused into living wood to create weapons that grow, adapt, and repair themselves.

### Vorrhyn's Dust (Maelstrom Residue)

> [!info]
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Source God** | [[The Twelve#Vorrhyn|Vorrhyn]], God of Chaos and Change |
> | **Appearance** | Shimmering, iridescent powder that shifts color constantly, weightless |
> | **Primary Region** | Near the Maelstrom, [[The Hollowed Reach]] |
> | **Rarity** | Very rare, extremely dangerous to collect |

**Mechanical Effect:** Chaotic catalyst. Added to any magical process, it amplifies unpredictability. Potions become more powerful but with random side effects. Enchantments gain additional properties that the enchanter did not intend. Spells cast with Vorrhyn's Dust as a component produce their intended effect *plus* something else entirely.

**Acquisition:** Scraped from surfaces near the Maelstrom's edge, where Vorrhyn's destruction site bleeds chaotic energy into the physical world. Collection is life-threatening. The Maelstrom's proximity warps body, mind, and reality.

**Cost of Use:**
- Tier 1: Brief euphoric instability. The world seems more vivid, more possible, more fluid.
- Tier 2: Minor reality warping in the user's vicinity. Objects shift position. Colors change. Gravity hiccups.
- Tier 3: **Existential dissolution.** The user's sense of self becomes fluid. They are less certain of who they are, what they want, where they end and the world begins.
- Tier 4: Physical instability. The user's body begins to change without warning, shifting between possible forms, aging and de-aging, growing and shrinking.
- Tier 5: The user becomes a localized Maelstrom, a point of pure chaos that warps everything in their vicinity. They may still be conscious, but they have no control over what their body or their environment does.

**Crafting:** Vorrhyn's Dust cannot be crafted into stable items. It is used as an additive: a pinch in a forge produces weapons with unpredictable enchantments, a trace in an alchemical mixture creates potions with amplified but random effects. The [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]] use it in controlled experimental settings to study chaos-magic interactions. No responsible artisan uses it without extensive precautions.

## Crafting Quick Reference

| Material | Crafting Method | Difficulty | Best Products |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ashite | Ground, cut, or inlaid | Moderate | Focusing powder, lens-stones, architectural keystones |
| Thyrea's Amber | Shaped by hand or bone tools | Moderate | Healing salves, living wood grafts, communion stones |
| Morrhaelite | Cold-forged | Hard | Anti-undead weapons, ward-stones, containment vessels |
| Kaevroth's Iron | High-temperature forging | Very Hard | Weapons, armor, war-totems |
| Forgeheart Steel | Geothermal forging only | Extreme | Weapons, armor, tools (all superior quality) |
| Belara's Tears | Dissolved, set, or hand-woven | Hard (emotional) | Healing draughts, mercy-cloaks, healing staves |
| Empyrean Shards | Set or ground | Unpredictable | Unstable power sources, detection dust |
| Marrow-Water | Alchemical distillation | Moderate | Green Draught, Rootblood, living wood infusion |
| Vorrhyn's Dust | Additive only | Dangerous | Random enchantments, experimental catalysts |

> [!warning]- GM Notes
> **Economy of divine materials.** These materials are not loot. They are the bones of gods. Treat acquisition as significant, use as consequential, and dependency as a character arc. A PC who starts using Kaevroth's Iron should feel the aggression creeping in. A PC healed by Belara's Tears should carry the melancholy. The tier system exists to make these costs visible and trackable without being punitive at low levels.
>
> **Cross-material interactions.** Combining divine materials from opposing gods produces unpredictable results. Ashite (order) + Vorrhyn's Dust (chaos) = catastrophic instability. Morrhaelite (death) + Thyrea's Amber (life) = violent rejection. Forgeheart Steel + Kaevroth's Iron = a weapon of extraordinary power that drives its wielder to conquest. These interactions are campaign moments, not routine crafting.
>
> **Primordial Pact Magic interaction.** Divine materials from the known continent destabilize Primordial pacts. A PC carrying Ashite into an Oldborn city will cause Anchors to flicker. This is not a punishment, it is a plot driver. The material conflict between divine-residue magic and Primordial-pact magic is central to the Unwritten Lands arc.
