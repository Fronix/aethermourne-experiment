# The Shattered Mirror

> [!info]
> | | |

> | **Associated God** | [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], Goddess of Shadow and Secrets |

---

## Description

In its intact form, the Mirror was a full-length looking glass of black obsidian so perfectly polished it reflected not appearance but truth. Anyone who looked into it saw not their face but their deepest secret, the thing they most wanted to hide, rendered in perfect, unflinching clarity. [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] used it to see through every shadow in the world, reading the hidden hearts of gods and mortals alike.

When the surviving gods imprisoned Serith, they shattered the Mirror to prevent her from using it. The seven fragments scattered across the continent, each retaining a fraction of the original's power. Each fragment is roughly the size of a hand, irregularly shaped, and cold to the touch. The surface still shows reflections, but they are fragmented, partial, disturbing.

## History

Before the Theomachis, the Mirror hung in Serith's sanctum in the Empyrean. It was the most feared object among the gods, because Serith knew everything, every betrayal, every doubt, every secret alliance, and the Mirror was how she knew. When she was imprisoned, the gods broke the Mirror specifically to blind her. They could not destroy it (divine artifacts do not break easily, even when other gods do the breaking), so shattering it was the next best option. The fragments were supposed to be scattered beyond recovery. They were not scattered far enough.

## Properties

### Single Fragment

**Shadow Sight.** Looking into a fragment reveals one hidden truth about a person, place, or object the viewer is focusing on. The truth is always accurate and always unwelcome.

**Shadow Walk.** A fragment can be used to step into and travel through shadows, emerging from any shadow within a mile. The passage is cold, disorienting, and leaves the user feeling watched.

**The Veil's Whisper.** Prolonged possession of a fragment opens a one-way channel. Serith can see through the fragment, observing whoever holds it and their surroundings. The holder does not know this is happening.

### If Reassembled (Theoretical)

Full omniscience through shadow. Serith regains her ability to see through every shadow in the world. The Mirror becomes a key to her prison's locks, because the prison's seals were designed by gods who had secrets, and the Mirror knows every one.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The fragments appear throughout the campaign. The [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] is actively collecting them. One fragment should appear in Act I (recovered from a Veil Unbound cell). Others surface in Acts II through IV. The party must decide whether to collect and guard the fragments, destroy them (is this even possible?), or use them despite the cost.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Veil Unbound has three of the seven fragments. The Cinders (unknowingly serving Serith through [[NPCRegistry#Thane Delric Mourne|Delric Mourne]]) have one. The [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]] have one in their archive, not knowing what it is. One is in the [[The Hollowed Reach#The Abyssal Trench|Abyssal Trench]]. The seventh is lost, possibly in [[The Iron Descent]].
>
> **The Deeper Truth.** The Mirror was not merely shattered by the gods. Serith broke it herself as part of her imprisonment, a calculated sacrifice. She knew the fragments would be scattered, found, and used. Every mortal who uses a fragment opens a window for her. The shattering was not her defeat. It was her strategy.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If all seven fragments are reunited (by the Veil Unbound or the PCs), the Mirror reforms. If Serith's agents hold it, she can see everything and accelerate her escape. If the PCs hold it, they possess the most dangerous intelligence tool in the world, and the knowledge that Serith is watching through it every moment.
