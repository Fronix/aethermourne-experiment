## Description

A Bloom-Lure does not look like a creature. It looks like the most beautiful clearing in the forest, a sunlit grove carpeted with flowers in impossible colors, the air thick with a scent that is equal parts honeysuckle and warm skin. The flowers are real. The beauty is real. The thing beneath the flowers that is digesting the last person who stopped to admire them is also real.

Bloom-Lures are sessile predators, root-networks that span a clearing of roughly thirty to fifty feet across, buried beneath a surface layer of flowering growth. The visible flowers are lure-organs: each petal exudes a hallucinogenic pollen that induces euphoria, suggestibility, and a profound reluctance to leave. Victims wander deeper into the clearing, drawn by the intensifying scent, until they reach the center where the root-maw waits. The maw is a pit of interlocking root tendrils that drags prey underground and digests it over days, cycling the nutrients back into the flowering canopy above.

During the death-phase, the flowers wilt and the pollen dissipates, revealing the clearing for what it is: a ring of churned soil littered with bone fragments and scraps of clothing. [[Root-Speaker Fennara]]'s dual-channelers can sense the death-phase lurking beneath the life-phase's beauty, which makes them partially immune to the pollen's effects. This is one of the reasons [[Rootpyre]] has survived in the Bloom while outsiders frequently do not.

## Behavior & Tactics

Bloom-Lures are ambush predators that rely entirely on their pollen for offense. They do not chase, they do not move, they do not need to. The pollen does the work. Victims under its influence walk voluntarily into the root-maw, smiling. The only active defense a Bloom-Lure deploys is vine-tendrils that lash out if prey attempts to flee after partial exposure, grasping at ankles and wrists to prevent escape while the pollen continues its work.

The pollen's range extends roughly twenty feet beyond the visible flower border. Approaching a Bloom-Lure from upwind reduces exposure. Covering the nose and mouth helps but does not eliminate risk, as the pollen absorbs through skin on prolonged contact. The most reliable defense is awareness: knowing what a Bloom-Lure looks like before entering one. [[The Cyclekeepers]] teach a simple test: throw a stone into a suspiciously beautiful clearing. If the flowers lean toward the impact, leave.

Bloom-Lures reproduce during Bloom Surges, when [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s growth-essence peaks and the forest erupts with accelerated growth. New Lures can establish a clearing in hours during a Surge. Paths that were safe yesterday become lethal today. This is the primary reason the Thornguard re-scouts trails after every Surge.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Bloom-Lures are why the Thornwild's missing-person rate is so high and so poorly understood. Victims disappear into beautiful clearings that look different every death-phase. By the time anyone finds the bones, the flowers have regrown and the clearing looks inviting again. [[Cyclethorn]]'s records show thirty-seven people unaccounted for in the last two years. [[Yara Thornbloom]] suspects Bloom-Lures are responsible for most of them.
>
> **Fennara's Secret.** [[Root-Speaker Fennara]] has not just learned to resist Bloom-Lures. She has learned to communicate with them. The root-networks respond to dual-channeled energy in ways that suggest rudimentary awareness, not intelligence, but something like preference. Fennara can guide a Bloom-Lure's growth away from paths and toward areas she designates. She has not told the Cyclekeepers because she knows it would confirm every accusation of heresy the [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] have leveled at her. She is, functionally, farming predators.
>
> **Campaign Hook.** A Star-Reader researcher went missing in the Bloom three days ago. The last entry in her journal (found on the trail) describes "the most extraordinary garden, I must document it more closely." The party can track her to a Bloom-Lure clearing. She is still alive inside the root-maw, being digested slowly. Extraction is possible but requires entering the pollen zone.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Bloom-Lure (root-maw) | 4 | H | 40 | 0 | +4 | -2 | — | vine-lash 1d8+3 + Grapple |
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Hallucinogenic Pollen.** Any creature starting its turn within 4 spaces of the flower border makes a DC 14 Will save. Failure: charmed (moves toward the center on its turn, cannot willingly attack the Lure). Success: immune for 1 round. Covering airways grants advantage on the save. Dual-channelers (Rootpyre druids) are immune.
> - **Vine Grapple.** On hit, target is grappled (escape DC 15 Strength). Grappled targets are dragged 2 spaces toward the root-maw at the start of the Lure's turn.
> - **Root-Maw Engulf.** A creature dragged to the center is engulfed. Engulfed creatures take 2d6 acid damage at the start of each turn and are blinded and restrained. Escape requires DC 16 Strength check from inside, or allies dealing 15+ damage to the root-maw in a single round.
> - **Death-Phase Vulnerability.** During the death-phase (or if a character channels necrotic energy into the root system), the pollen ceases for 1d4 rounds and the Lure's HP is halved.
>
> **Weakness:** Fire damage to the flower border destroys pollen production for the encounter. Necrotic damage forces the death-phase early. Cold damage slows vine-lash attacks (once per round instead of twice).
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 3-5 players of level 3-5

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The trail opens into a clearing that stops the party in their tracks. Sunlight filters through a canopy of flowering vines onto a carpet of blossoms in shades of violet, gold, and deep crimson. The air is warm and sweet. A butterfly lands on the fighter's gauntlet. Somewhere ahead, a voice is humming, or maybe it is the wind through the petals. The ranger notices the flowers are leaning, ever so slightly, toward the party. The cleric notices the bones half-buried in the soil near the center, threaded through with roots. The wizard notices nothing. The wizard is already walking forward, smiling.
>
> **Complication:** The missing Star-Reader is inside the root-maw (5 rounds until lethal digestion). Burning the Lure would kill her too. The party must enter the pollen zone to extract her, and at least one of them will fail the Will save.
