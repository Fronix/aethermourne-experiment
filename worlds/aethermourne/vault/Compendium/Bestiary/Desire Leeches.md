# Desire Leeches

## Description

Desire Leeches are almost invisible. They are small, translucent parasites no larger than a thumb, their bodies a faintly shimmering membrane wrapped around a hollow core. In the dim light of the Iron Descent, they are nearly impossible to see, registering only as a brief distortion in the air, like heat shimmer over hot metal. They drift in clouds through the deeper levels of the Descent, drawn to concentrations of desire the way insects are drawn to warmth.

They do not attach to flesh. They attach to want. A Desire Leech anchors itself to a specific ambition, a goal, a longing, a need, and begins to feed. The host does not feel the attachment. There is no sting, no pressure, no physical sensation at all. What the host feels is subtler and worse: the slow drainage of motivation. The goal that burned in their chest begins to feel distant. The longing that drove them forward becomes abstract, academic, something they remember caring about but can no longer feel. Over hours or days, the feeding deepens. Purpose becomes indifference. Drive becomes apathy. The host does not become sad, exactly. They become empty, hollowed out, a person-shaped container with nothing inside that pushes.

## Behavior & Tactics

Desire Leeches are not aggressive. They have no combat capability, no physical attack, no defense beyond being nearly impossible to see. They are dangerous because they are subtle and because their effect compounds. A single Leech drains one ambition slowly enough that the host might not notice for days. A cloud of Leeches can strip a person of all motivation in hours, leaving them sitting on the iron ground, staring at nothing, unable to remember why they came here or why they should care about leaving.

Detection requires knowing what to look for. A Leech-infested person does not act sick or distressed. They act reasonable. They start questioning whether their quest is worth the effort. They suggest turning back, not from fear but from a calm, logical assessment that nothing here matters enough to risk their lives. They become persuasive advocates for surrender, because a mind stripped of desire sees ambition as foolishness.

Removal is straightforward once the Leeches are identified: intense heat drives them off, as does any strong burst of emotion directed at the host. A passionate speech, a genuine expression of need or anger or love, disrupts the Leech's feeding and forces it to detach. The host's desires return slowly afterward, like blood flowing back into a numbed limb.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Desire Leeches are a roleplay encounter disguised as a monster. The real challenge is recognizing that a party member has been compromised when their behavior changes are subtle and logical-sounding. Have the affected player argue against the party's goals using perfectly reasonable arguments. The other players must figure out that their companion is being drained, not just having second thoughts.
>
> **The Iron Market Connection.** Desire Leeches are common in the [[Level 3 - The Iron Market|Iron Market]] because the Market runs on desire. The merchants there consider Leeches a cost of doing business. Some deliberately cultivate them as a negotiation tool, releasing Leeches near a client to drain their resolve before presenting a contract.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party rests in the Iron Market. The next morning, one PC (chosen by the GM based on who has the strongest current motivation) begins acting slightly different. They are calm. Agreeable. They suggest the party take things slowly, maybe reconsider whether they need to go deeper. Their arguments are sound. Over the next few hours, their conviction continues to fade. If the party does not intervene, the PC eventually sits down and stops moving, watching the Market's commerce with mild, detached interest, unable to remember what they came here to do.
>
> **Resolution:** Another PC must make a genuine emotional appeal, not a mechanical check, but an in-character moment of connection. "Remember why we came here. Remember what we're fighting for." The Leeches detach in a visible shimmer, and the drained PC gasps as their motivation floods back, raw and overwhelming.

## Stat Block

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Desire Leech | 0 | None | 1 (individual) | fly 2 | -4 | +2 | +3 | none (psychic parasite) |
>
> **Detection:** Perception DC 18 to spot a single Leech, DC 14 for a cloud.
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Ambition Drain (passive):** A Leech attached to a creature drains one specific motivation over 1d4 hours. No save, the attachment is imperceptible. After the drain completes, the host loses all emotional investment in that goal.
> - **Cloud Drain:** A cloud of Leeches (10+) drains all motivation in 1d4 x 10 minutes. The host becomes completely apathetic.
> - **Reasonable Surrender:** A Leech-drained creature argues persuasively against continuing the mission. Insight DC 16 to recognize this as Leech influence.
> - **Detection Difficulty:** Medicine DC 14 or Arcana DC 14 to diagnose Leech attachment once symptoms appear.
>
> **Weakness:** Intense heat drives them off immediately. A genuine burst of emotion directed at the host forces detachment, no check required. Cold iron disrupts their feeding.
>
> **Encounter Note:** Roleplay encounter, not combat. Any level.
