# Fog Singers

*You don't hear them at first. You hear the sea. Then you realize the sea is humming a melody, and the melody has words, and the words are your name.*

## Description

Fog Singers are not creatures. They are not visible, not tangible, not anything that can be pointed at or pinned down. They are a phenomenon: a melody that rises from the perpetual coastal fog on certain nights, weaving through the mist like a current through water. The melody harmonizes with [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]]'s tidal rhythms, rising and falling with the waves, but it is pitched in a key that does not exist in any musical tradition, a tone that sits between notes, that the ear reaches for and cannot quite grasp.

The effect is cumulative. On the first night, the melody is barely distinguishable from the sound of wind and surf. By the third night, it has words, or almost-words, syllables that feel meaningful without resolving into language. By the fifth night, the listener hears their own name woven into the melody, spoken with the tenderness of someone who has been waiting a very long time. By the seventh night, the listener walks toward the water and does not understand why anyone would want to stop them.

Coastal communities have learned to recognize the signs. When the fog thickens and the night is still and someone in the household starts humming a song they cannot name, the family stuffs cloth in the sleeper's ears and ties their wrist to the bedpost. It is not dignified. It works.

## Behavior & Tactics

Fog Singers do not attack, pursue, or respond to aggression. They simply sing. The melody permeates the coastal fog and cannot be blocked by walls, only by physical barriers against sound: stuffed ears, sealed rooms, counter-noise. The singing is strongest between midnight and dawn, during high tide, and during the nights preceding a Black Tide.

The compulsion builds over consecutive nights of exposure. Each night spent within hearing range of a Fog Singer requires a Wisdom save, and each failed save makes the next night's save harder. The victim does not feel compelled. They feel drawn, the way one feels drawn to a warm fire on a cold night. The desire to walk toward the water feels natural, reasonable, even pleasant. Victims who are prevented from going report frustration, not fear, irritation at being kept from something beautiful.

Fog Singers cannot be fought because there is nothing to fight. They can be dispersed by loud, sustained noise, ship's bells, war-horns, communal singing, anything that fills the air with sound that is not theirs. [[The Tidemother's Bell]] dispels them instantly within its range. They reform within hours, drawn back together by the fog's saturated divine resonance.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The melody is a lullaby. Specifically, it is the lullaby [[The Twelve#Yvenne|Yvenne]] sang to calm the sea before the Theomachis, a divine song of peace and stillness that soothed storms and gentled currents. [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] has stolen it and twisted it, keeping the peace but redirecting it, peace as surrender rather than peace as safety. The corruption is subtle enough that the melody still carries Yvenne's genuine compassion, which is what makes it so effective.
>
> **Recognition.** Anyone who recognizes the original melody becomes immune. This requires a DC 18 History check, or is automatic for [[Concord of Tides|Concord of Tides]] members who were raised in traditional seafaring families where Yvenne's lullaby is still sung to children. This creates an interesting dynamic: the oldest, most traditional families in the Reach are naturally resistant to Serith's most insidious weapon, while newcomers and outsiders are vulnerable.
>
> **Connection to Sleepwalkers.** Fog Singers are the entry point of Serith's influence. They create the initial susceptibility that, over weeks, deepens into the full [[Sleepwalkers|Sleepwalker]] phenomenon. A person who resists the Fog Singers never becomes a Sleepwalker. A person who listens, even once, becomes slightly more vulnerable to Serith's direct psychic contact. The Lantern-Keepers have not made this connection yet.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is staying at a coastal inn in [[Saltmere]]. The innkeeper bolts the shutters at sunset without explanation. At midnight, a PC on watch hears it: a melody in the fog, beautiful, aching, intimately familiar despite being unlike anything they have heard before. It sounds like the sea is singing, and the song knows their name. A Wisdom save (DC 13, first exposure) determines whether they resist the urge to open the shutters and walk outside.
>
> **Complication:** The innkeeper's daughter did not get her ears stuffed in time. She is standing at the front door, hand on the latch, humming along. This is her fourth night. Her save DC is 17. If the party does not intervene, she walks into the fog and toward the sea.

---

> [!example] Mechanics Reference (Nimble)
> Fog Singers have no stat block. They are an environmental hazard.
>
> **Cumulative Exposure:**
> - Night 1: Will save DC 13. Failure: mild fascination, restless sleep.
> - Night 2: Will save DC 14. Failure: humming the melody unconsciously.
> - Night 3: Will save DC 15. Failure: hearing words in the melody, strong urge to go outside.
> - Night 4+: Will save DC 16+1 per additional night. Failure: walking toward the water unless physically restrained.
>
> **Dispersal:** Loud sustained noise (war-horn, ship's bell, group singing) disperses the Fog Singer for the remainder of the night. [[The Tidemother's Bell]] disperses all Fog Singers within hearing range permanently (they do not reform for 1d4 weeks).
>
> **Immunity:** Recognizing the original lullaby (DC 18 History, automatic for traditional Concord families). Stuffed ears (disadvantage on Perception but immune to the melody). Being deafened (immune).
>
> **Reset:** One full week away from the coast resets cumulative exposure to zero.
