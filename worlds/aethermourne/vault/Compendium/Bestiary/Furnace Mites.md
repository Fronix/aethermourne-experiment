## Description

Thumbnail-sized arthropods with matte-black carapaces and legs tipped with mineral-boring mandibles, Furnace Mites are the Emberveil's least glamorous residents and its most persistent problem. They feed on geothermal energy, boring into warm stone and siphoning thermal output through specialized organs in their abdomens that glow faintly orange when full. A single mite is harmless, a minor irritant that a miner flicks off their boot. A swarm of thousands is a disaster.

A mature infestation honecombs the stone around a thermal vent, riddling it with thumbnail-width tunnels until the rock crumbles under its own weight. The structural damage outlasts the infestation itself: even after the mites are cleared, the compromised stone remains fragile, requiring expensive shoring and reinforcement. Miners in [[Hearthdeep]] and the Deep Warrens have a saying: "Mites eat the heat, then the floor eats you."

## Behavior & Tactics

Furnace Mites swarm toward the strongest nearby heat source and bore in. Their life cycle is simple: feed, breed, disperse. A queen establishes in warm stone, lays hundreds of eggs that hatch within days, and the colony expands outward in concentric rings, draining thermal energy as it goes. A healthy vent can sustain a colony for months before the mites exhaust it and disperse to find new sources. Miners treat infestations like mine-fires: seal the affected tunnel, cut off air flow, and wait for the colony to starve. The process takes weeks and costs production time that [[The Forge Council]] measures in lost ingots.

The Cooling has changed everything. As geothermal sources weaken, mite swarms are migrating upward, abandoning depleted deep-level veins for inhabited tunnels, surface buildings, and anywhere warmth persists. [[Cinderfall]]'s therapeutic hot springs are infested, the mites boring into the pool walls and draining the healing heat that miners depend on. [[Hearthdeep]]'s middle levels are riddled with structurally compromised stone where colonies have moved through. Forges, hearths, and even sleeping bodies now attract swarms that would once have stayed deep underground.

They are not causing the Cooling. But they are accelerating it locally, draining what heat remains from sources that can no longer replenish as fast as the mites consume.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The mites' migration patterns, combined with [[Vent Wyrms|Vent Wyrm]] displacement and [[Cinder Sprites|Cinder Sprite]] die-off, form a three-species ecological warning system. Anyone studying all three patterns simultaneously would realize the Cooling is not uniform but follows specific underground channels, tracing the paths of [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]]'s residual energy as it drains toward the Deep Warrens and the compensatory mechanism beneath [[Hearthdeep]].
>
> [[Brunhild Ironheart]] has the data for all three species but has not connected them. A naturalist, a [[The Star-Readers|Star-Reader]], or a particularly observant PC could assemble the picture and produce the first accurate map of the Cooling's progression, information that would be invaluable to [[The Forge Council]] and extremely dangerous in the hands of [[The Cinders]] or [[The Ashen Dominion]].

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Environmental Hazard
> | Name | Trigger | Effect |
> |---|---|---|
> | Mite Swarm (active) | Proximity to warmth (forge, torch, body heat) | Swarm moves toward heat source at Speed 4. Contact: 1d4 fire damage/round (boring heat-drain). Extinguishing heat sources redirects the swarm. |
> | Compromised Stone | Weight or impact on infested stone | DC 14 Perception to notice honeycomb damage before stepping on it. Failure: floor collapses, 2d6 falling damage, Dex save DC 13 to catch the edge. |
> | Dormant Colony | Disturbing sealed tunnel | Sealed mite colonies awaken when warmth returns. Opening a sealed tunnel with a torch triggers immediate swarming. |
>
> **Countermeasures:** Cold damage disperses a swarm instantly (mites go dormant below freezing). Smoke drives them from tunnels. Removing all heat sources causes the swarm to scatter and seek elsewhere. The [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] sells ceramic mite-traps baited with forge-coals, effective for small colonies but overwhelmed by large infestations.

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The tunnel ahead is warm, warmer than it should be at this depth. The walls glisten with a faint orange shimmer that moves. A closer look reveals thousands of thumbnail-sized shapes clinging to the stone, their abdomens pulsing with stolen heat. The floor beneath them is pockmarked with tiny boreholes. A dwarven warning sign, half-consumed by the colony, reads: "SEALED, DO NOT ENTER." Someone has pried the seal open recently. Fresh bootprints lead deeper, toward the sound of crumbling stone.
>
> **Complication:** A junior mining crew broke the seal chasing a rumored forgeheart vein. They are now trapped beyond the colony, their torches attracting the swarm. The floor between the party and the miners is compromised, one wrong step from collapse. Cold-based solutions will save the miners but kill the colony, and the thermal vent it feeds on is one of [[Hearthdeep]]'s last healthy ones on Level Six.
