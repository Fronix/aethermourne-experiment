# Memory Eaters

## Description

Memory Eaters drift through the Marrow Fields at night, translucent bell-shaped bodies trailing luminescent tendrils that display fragments of consumed memories: a woman laughing in sunlight, a child's first steps, the view from a mountain peak that no living person remembers climbing. They are beautiful in the way that grief is beautiful, all soft light and stolen moments playing across their surfaces like lantern-shows projected on silk. Their bodies pulse with a slow, cold radiance, and they move with the unhurried grace of deep-sea creatures, drifting on currents of necromantic energy that flow through the Marrow Fields like invisible rivers.

They are what emerged to fill a gap in [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s broken ecology. The God of Death maintained the transition of souls, which carried memories into the afterlife as part of the passage. With the domain shattered, those memories have no destination. They crystallize in [[Cosmology and Magic#Divine Materials|Morrhaelite]] deposits, dense with the accumulated experiences of the dead, and the Memory Eaters evolved, or manifested, to feed on them. They are scavengers, recycling what should have passed on, and their proliferation near Morrhaelite veins is the clearest sign of how much memory has accumulated without release.

They are drawn to the living because living minds produce fresh memories, warm and immediate and richer than the stale crystallized residue in the stones. A Memory Eater approaching a campfire is not hostile. It is hungry in the way that a starving animal is hungry: desperate, focused, and incapable of understanding why the food resists.

## Behavior & Tactics

A Memory Eater feeds by touch. Its tendrils, on contact with a living creature, do not cause physical damage. Instead, they drain memories, specific, identifiable recollections that the victim feels vanishing in real time. The sensation is not pain. It is absence: a hole where something used to be, a name on the tip of the tongue that will never arrive, a face that blurs and empties and is gone. Victims describe it as grief without cause, mourning something they can no longer identify.

Mechanically, each successful tendril-hit removes a specific memory. The player chooses which memory, or the GM rolls: a skill proficiency (forgot the training), a language (forgot the words), a personal relationship (forgot a specific NPC and all associated history). The stolen memory plays across the Memory Eater's body in vivid detail, visible to everyone except the victim, who can no longer recognize what they are seeing.

The combat puzzle is preservation. A Memory Eater destroyed carefully, with precise, targeted strikes that kill without rupturing the body, releases its consumed memories as luminescent motes that drift back to their owners, restoring what was taken. But area damage, fire, or explosive force shreds the delicate membrane that holds the memories, scattering them into the necromantic currents of the Marrow Fields. Those memories are gone forever. The more violently you fight, the more you lose.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Memory Eaters are drawn to Morrhaelite deposits. Their presence in an area is a reliable indicator of rich crystal veins below the surface, which makes them inadvertently useful to [[The Ashen Vigil]]'s mining operations. Some Vigil commanders have begun using Memory Eater sightings as prospecting tools, a pragmatic decision that the Ash-Speakers find theologically disturbing.
>
> **Campaign connection:** A Memory Eater that has fed extensively near [[The Pale Wastes#Hollowdeep|Hollowdeep]] carries memories of the dead who fell there, soldiers, travelers, scouts who ventured too close. Those memories contain intelligence about Hollowdeep's interior: troop movements, fortification layouts, the army's composition. Capturing a Hollowdeep-fed Memory Eater intact and extracting its memories is a high-risk intelligence operation that [[Bjorn Ashken]] would pay dearly for.
>
> **Emotional encounter:** The most devastating version of this creature is one that is already carrying the memories of someone the PCs knew. It drifts toward them displaying a dead friend's final moments, their laughter, their fears, their face, and then it reaches for the PCs with tendrils that want to add to its collection.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is camped in the Marrow Fields when soft light appears in the darkness. A Memory Eater drifts toward the fire, tendrils trailing, its body displaying stolen fragments: an old man teaching a child to fish, a soldier saluting at dawn, a woman singing in a language no one in the party speaks. It is beautiful. It is also reaching for the nearest PC. Combat is possible, but every area-effect spell risks destroying memories that might belong to someone, somewhere, who is still alive and missing a piece of themselves.
>
> **Complication:** A second Memory Eater appears from the opposite direction, this one displaying the face of an NPC the party knows, a contact, a friend, someone who should be alive. The memories it carries suggest the NPC is dead. Is the creature showing the truth, or feeding on memories it stole from a living mind?

>
> **Resistances/Immunities:** Immune to poison, cold, and psychic. Resistant to necrotic. Vulnerable to radiant.
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Memory Drain (tendril attack).** Target makes Will save DC 14. Failure: loses one specific memory (player chooses or GM rolls: 1-proficiency, 2-language, 3-NPC relationship, 4-personal history). The stolen memory plays across the Memory Eater's body, visible to all except the victim.
> - **Luminescent Display.** The Memory Eater's body continuously displays fragments of consumed memories. Creatures watching make Will save DC 12 or are fascinated for 1 round (recognize a face, hear a familiar voice, see something they cannot look away from).
> - **Fragile Membrane.** If the Memory Eater takes 10+ damage from a single area-effect source (fireball, thunder, etc.), all stored memories are destroyed permanently. Precise attacks (single-target, melee or ranged) preserve stored memories.
> - **Memory Release (on death by precise damage).** Stored memories emerge as luminescent motes, drifting back to their owners within 1 mile. Memories of the dead or those beyond range crystallize into small Morrhaelite fragments (1d4 fragments, each containing a single recoverable memory).
>
> **Bloodied (half HP):** The Memory Eater becomes desperate. Memory Drain save DC increases to 16. It targets the creature with the most memories (highest Int or Wis modifier) preferentially.
>
> **Weakness:** Radiant damage deals double and causes the creature to release one stored memory per hit (returning it to the owner). Bright light (daylight spell or equivalent) repels Memory Eaters, forcing them to remain at least 6 spaces from the light source. They cannot feed on creatures who are sleeping or unconscious (the memories must be actively held to be drained).
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 3-4 players of level 2-4
