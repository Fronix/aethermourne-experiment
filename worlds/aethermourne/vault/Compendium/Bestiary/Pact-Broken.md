# Pact-Broken

> [!info]
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | **Region** | [[The Unwritten Lands]], all inhabited areas |
> | **Origin** | Oldborn pact-holders whose Primordial bond exceeded their capacity |
> | **Threat Level** | Serious threat to legendary danger (varies by pact type) |
> | **Associated God** | None, Primordial-origin |

## Description

Every Oldborn child learns the story of the first Pact-Broken before they learn to read. It is the only cautionary tale their culture needs.

A Pact-Broken is what remains when a Primordial pact consumes the person who holds it. The negotiation fails. The cost exceeds the caster's capacity. And the Primordial force, which never cared about the caster in the first place, simply keeps flowing, filling the space where a person used to be with raw, unmediated expression of its nature. The caster does not die. Death would be kinder. They *become* their pact, a living monument to a bargain that went wrong.

They are still in there. That is the worst part. Behind the crystalline shell of a Structure-Broken, behind the dissolving cloud of a Dissolution-Broken, there is a mind, fragmented and screaming, that remembers being a person. The Oldborn do not kill Pact-Broken if they can avoid it. They contain them, ward them, and grieve for them. Some families visit their Pact-Broken for generations, speaking to the thing their ancestor became, hoping something still listens.

Something does. It just cannot answer.

## The Five Forms

Each [[The Primordials|Gyre Primordial]] produces a distinct type of Pact-Broken. No two are identical, the specific manifestation depends on what the caster was, what they wanted, and how their pact failed, but they share common characteristics within each type.

### Structure-Broken ([[Ur-Ghael]])

A living statue of crystalline matter that grows outward from the caster's body, replacing flesh with geometric perfection. They do not move. They do not speak. They *impose*. Everything within their influence snaps to rigid order: water freezes into perfect hexagonal lattices, soil compresses into stone, living creatures' muscles lock, bones straighten, joints fuse. Animals caught in the field become statues of flesh. People caught in the field become statues of people, still alive, unable to move, arranged in geometric patterns that satisfy a logic no mortal mind can follow.

Structure-Broken grow slowly, their crystalline shell extending a few inches per year. The oldest known Structure-Broken, contained in a warded vault beneath the Oldborn city of Kaethyn, has been growing for six centuries. Its influence field now extends three hundred meters. The vault's wards are checked daily.

### Dissolution-Broken ([[Vorthaan]])

A walking absence. The caster's body has lost all coherent form, becoming a drifting cloud of matter in constant decay, flesh becoming fluid, fluid becoming vapor, vapor becoming nothing. New matter is drawn in to replace what dissolves, pulled from the environment in a slow, relentless consumption. Stone crumbles. Wood rots. Metal corrodes. Living things age decades in minutes, their cells forgetting how to replicate, their bodies surrendering structure as the Dissolution-Broken passes.

Dissolution-Broken move. That is what makes them more dangerous than their Structure counterparts. They drift with the wind, following no pattern, consuming everything in their path. They cannot be contained by physical barriers, any wall they touch erodes. The Oldborn use Achren pact-holders to create boundary-wards that redirect rather than block, channeling the Dissolution-Broken into uninhabited regions where they can consume stone and air without harming anyone.

They weep. The sound is unmistakable, a thin, wet keening from somewhere inside the dissolving cloud. It is the only evidence that the person inside still exists.

### Multiplicity-Broken ([[Thaelun]])

The most disorienting of the Pact-Broken. The caster exists in multiple states simultaneously, visible as a stuttering, overlapping collection of possible selves. They are young and old, sitting and standing, speaking and silent, alive and dead, all at once. They flicker between versions of themselves so rapidly that observers experience nausea, temporal vertigo, and brief episodes of their own multiplicity, seeing their own possible futures and pasts bleeding into the present.

Multiplicity-Broken are not physically dangerous. They do not dissolve matter or impose order. Their threat is cognitive. Prolonged exposure causes the observer's sense of linear time to fracture. Cause and effect decouple. Memories from futures that have not occurred intrude. Decisions become impossible because every option and its consequences are perceived simultaneously. The Oldborn call this "being skeinned," and it can take weeks to recover.

The Multiplicity-Broken themselves experience every possible version of their life at once. They are dying and being born. They are here and elsewhere. They remember everything that could have happened and cannot distinguish any of it from what did. Somewhere in that infinite overlap, a singular person is trying to find themselves. They have been trying for a very long time.

### Boundary-Broken ([[Achren]])

The caster has become a living edge, a boundary made flesh. They exist at the exact threshold between things: between light and shadow, between solid and liquid, between here and there. They are visible only in peripheral vision, occupying the space between observation and recognition. Looking directly at a Boundary-Broken reveals nothing. Looking away reveals a silhouette, sharp as a blade, standing exactly where your attention was not.

Their presence *divides*. Relationships fracture in their proximity. Companions turn on each other, not from hostility, but from an overwhelming sense of separateness, a sudden, visceral awareness that the person beside you is fundamentally *other*, unknowable, unreachable. Groups splinter. Bonds dissolve. Even the land around a Boundary-Broken develops fissures, the ground cracking along lines of force that radiate outward from the living edge.

Boundary-Broken are the loneliest things in existence. They have become the principle of separation itself, unable to touch, unable to connect, unable to be perceived by anyone who is trying to see them. The Oldborn do not contain them. They cannot be found reliably enough to contain. Instead, communities that sense a Boundary-Broken's presence perform bonding rituals, strengthening connections between people and place to resist the dividing influence.

### Dream-Broken ([[Yith]])

The caster has fallen into [[Yith]]'s perpetual reverie and dragged the waking world in with them. They appear to be sleeping, curled in whatever position they occupied when the pact consumed them, but the air around them shimmers with visions. Dreams bleed outward from their body in concentric rings: at the edge, faint impressions, colors and sounds that do not belong. Closer in, full hallucinations, places and people that exist only in the Dream-Broken's mind, rendered with perfect sensory fidelity. At the center, where the dreamer lies, reality and dream are indistinguishable, and anyone who reaches the sleeper may not be able to find their way back to the waking world.

Dream-Broken attract the desperate. Their dream-field grants visions, answers to questions, glimpses of truth hidden from waking minds. Pilgrims come seeking wisdom. Some receive it. Some are drawn deeper into the dream than they intended and join the sleeper's reverie, adding their own dreams to the expanding field. The oldest Dream-Broken, a woman called Yithavel who broke her pact four centuries ago, has a dream-field that extends a full mile. Seventeen pilgrims wander within it, sleeping with open eyes, their bodies sustained by the dream while their minds dissolve into Yith's infinite reverie.

The Silence-Watchers maintain a watch on every known Dream-Broken. Not to contain them, containment is unnecessary, they do not move, but to warn travelers and to study the visions. Some of the most important insights about [[The Gyre]]'s nature have come from interpreting the dream-fields of the Broken.

## Behavior & Tactics

Pact-Broken do not have tactics. They do not choose to act. They are expressions of force, not agents of will. Engaging a Pact-Broken in combat is like fighting a storm or arguing with erosion. The force does not care. It simply *is*.

The appropriate response depends on the type:

- **Structure-Broken:** Do not approach. Maintain distance beyond the influence field. If the field is expanding toward a populated area, an Achren pact-holder can create a boundary-ward to deflect it. Ur-Ghael pact-holders should NOT attempt to counter a Structure-Broken, they risk triggering their own pact-failure through resonance.
- **Dissolution-Broken:** Redirect, do not block. Achren boundary-wards guide them away from settlements. If direct engagement is unavoidable, Ur-Ghael pact-holders can temporarily impose structure on the dissolving cloud, buying time, not resolving the problem.
- **Multiplicity-Broken:** Limit exposure time. No one should remain in a Multiplicity-Broken's presence for more than a few minutes. Close your eyes. Focus on a single, concrete memory. Do not look at your own hands, you may see too many of them.
- **Boundary-Broken:** Cannot be reliably found or engaged. Focus on mitigating the effects: strengthen bonds, repair relationships, mend the fissures they leave behind.
- **Dream-Broken:** Do not enter the inner ring of the dream-field unless prepared to remain there. Yith pact-holders can navigate the dream-field safely, but even they risk being drawn deeper than intended. Communication with the dreamer is possible from the middle ring but requires mental discipline.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Pact-Broken as moral argument:** The existence of Pact-Broken is the strongest evidence that Primordial pact magic, for all its advantages, carries costs that Aetheric magic does not. No calculist has ever become a living statue of mathematical perfection. No tidal sorcerer has ever dissolved into the ocean's will. The risks of Aetheric magic (Order Sickness, Root-Mind, Tide-Touched, Death-Touched) are severe, but they are *conditions*, treatable, manageable, survivable. Pact failure is not a condition. It is a transformation. This complicates the Oldborn's critique of Aetheric magic as "tainted," their alternative has its own horrors, and those horrors are arguably worse.
>
> **Campaign function:** Pact-Broken serve three purposes. First, they are environmental hazards, interesting terrain and encounter complications. Second, they are emotional anchors, each one was a person with a name and a family, and the Oldborn remember them. Third, they are thematic mirrors for the campaign's central question: what is the cost of power, and who pays it? The known continent built its power on dead gods. The Oldborn built theirs on living negotiation. Both systems extract a price. Neither system is clean.
>
> **Can they be saved?** Deliberately left open. The Oldborn believe Pact-Broken are permanent. But the Oldborn have never encountered Aetheric magic, divine materials, or Nethys's fragments. It is possible, though not certain, that combining Primordial and Aetheric approaches could stabilize a Pact-Broken, force the Primordial energy back into negotiable terms, and recover the person inside. This is an excellent late-campaign quest hook, especially if the Pact-Broken in question is someone the PCs care about.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter: The Crystalline Grandmother
> The party is traveling through [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]] with an Oldborn guide. The guide leads them down a detour, avoiding a valley. When asked why, the guide explains quietly: their grandmother is in that valley. She was an Ur-Ghael pact-holder who reinforced the city walls for forty years. Three years ago, the cost caught up with her. She crystallized in her garden, mid-step, reaching for a flower she would never pick.
>
> The valley is beautiful. Every blade of grass stands at perfect attention. Every stone is geometrically flawless. Every insect is frozen mid-flight, encased in crystal so fine it is nearly invisible. At the center, a woman of dark stone and glass, arm outstretched, fingers reaching, expression serene, radiates a field of absolute order.
>
> If the PCs approach, they feel their muscles tighten, their joints stiffen, their thoughts sharpen into rigid lines. If they reach the inner field, they must resist or begin to crystallize themselves.
>
> **Stakes:** The guide will not enter the valley. They have not visited in two years because last time their left hand crystallized for a week. But they will ask, quietly, if the PCs know of any magic that might bring her back. They have heard that the outlanders' magic works differently. They have heard that there are people on the known continent who can mend broken things.
>
> The answer, honestly, is that no one knows. But the question is the point.

The five Pact-Broken variants function as environmental hazards as much as combat encounters. Each reflects the nature of its associated Primordial.

> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Influence Field:** All creatures within 12 spaces make Str save DC 15 at start of each turn. Failure: movement halved (joints stiffen). Two consecutive failures: restrained (partial crystallization). Three consecutive failures: paralyzed (full crystallization, reversible only by leaving within 1 minute, permanent after 10 minutes).
> - **Growing Radius:** Field expands 1 space per year. Ancient Structure-Broken have fields measured in hundreds of meters.
> - **Structural Perfection:** Within the field, all terrain becomes difficult terrain. Water freezes. Fire extinguishes. Sound dampens.
>
> **Weakness:** Achren boundary-wards deflect expansion (DC 16 pact check). Cannot penetrate Primordial Anchor zones. Ur-Ghael pact-holders must NOT engage (resonance risk).
