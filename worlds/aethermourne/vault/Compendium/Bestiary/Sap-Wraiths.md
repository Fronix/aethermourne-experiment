## Description

Sap-Wraiths are what the Cycle produces when it hesitates. In rare moments during the transition between life-phase and death-phase, when both divine essences are precisely balanced, the energy does not resolve into either state. It coalesces. The result is a translucent, vaguely humanoid shape, roughly five feet tall, filled with flowing sap that shifts color with the dominant phase: warm green-gold during the life-phase, ashen grey during the death-phase. They have no faces, no features, no anatomy. They are shapes that the Cycle forgot to finish.

In the wild, Sap-Wraiths are erratic and unpredictable. They drift through the Bloom and the Threshold, flickering between phases, their sap-bodies rippling as if disturbed by wind that is not there. When startled, they lash out with tendrils of half-solidified sap that burns like acid in one phase and numbs like frostbite in the other. They are not malicious. They are confused, incomplete things responding to stimuli they cannot process.

Near [[Rootpyre]], they are something else entirely. [[Root-Speaker Fennara]]'s dual-channeling practice produces a stable field of balanced Thyrea-Morrhael energy, and Sap-Wraiths are drawn to it the way a cold animal is drawn to a fire. Within Fennara's influence, their flickering stabilizes. Their movements become deliberate. They develop what can only be called preferences, lingering near specific people, avoiding others, reacting to voices with subtle shifts in their sap-flow. Fennara's circle has named several of them. The [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] would burn Rootpyre to the ground if they knew.

## Behavior & Tactics

Wild Sap-Wraiths are encounter hazards, environmental features of the Thornwild that are best avoided rather than fought. They cluster near transition points (the same areas where [[Threshold Weavers]] build webs) and react to disruption with defensive lashing. Their attacks are inconsistent, sometimes acid, sometimes cold, sometimes both in rapid alternation, and their phase-shifting makes them difficult to target with magic that relies on a consistent creature type. A spell that targets undead works during the death-phase but not the life-phase. A spell that targets living creatures works during the life-phase but not the death-phase. The Wraith shifts between the two every few seconds.

Stabilized Sap-Wraiths near Rootpyre do not attack unless Rootpyre itself is threatened. They function as an inadvertent early warning system: when danger approaches, the Wraiths' sap-flow accelerates and their color shifts to deep amber, a stress response that Fennara's apprentices have learned to read. During the Greensinger confrontation last year, a stabilized Wraith positioned itself between Fennara and an aggressive Greensinger emissary and did not move until the emissary backed down. Fennara insists it was coincidence. No one believes her.

Killing a Sap-Wraith disperses it into a puddle of inert amber-sap that hardens within minutes into low-grade [[Cosmology and Magic#Thornwild Amber|Thornwild amber]]. This has made them targets for unscrupulous harvesters, which is another reason Fennara keeps her stabilized Wraiths close and her settlement's borders well-patrolled.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Sap-Wraiths are the strongest evidence in the Thornwild that [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s divine legacies can coexist peacefully. The [[The Bloom-Wraith]] is the apex example (a permanent, conscious fusion), but Sap-Wraiths demonstrate that the principle scales. If Fennara's dual-channeling technique could be taught widely, it might be possible to stabilize the entire Cycle. The Cyclekeepers suspect this. The Greensingers fear it, because it would validate a heresy that undermines their theological monopoly on Thyrea's legacy.
>
> **The Acceleration Problem.** As the Cycle speeds up, wild Sap-Wraiths are forming more frequently (transition moments happen more often) but lasting shorter durations (the phases resolve faster). The result is a growing population of increasingly unstable Wraiths, flickering in and out of existence, lashing out more violently, harder to avoid. Fennara's stabilized Wraiths are holding, but she is channeling more energy to maintain them. She is exhausting herself, and she has not told anyone.
>
> **Campaign Hook.** A faction within the [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] wants to capture a stabilized Sap-Wraith and study it, not to understand coexistence, but to find a way to destroy the Morrhael component and "purify" it. If they succeed, it would prove their theology but destroy the only evidence that balance is possible. Fennara will fight this with everything she has. The PCs may be asked to choose sides.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Sap-Wraith (wild) | 2 | L | 16 | 4 (drift) | +0 | +3 | +1 | sap-lash 1d6 acid/cold (alternating) |
>
> **Resistances/Immunities:** Resistant to non-magical physical damage. Immune to poison.
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Phase Flicker.** At the start of each round, roll d6. Even: life-phase (counts as living, vulnerable to necrotic, immune to effects targeting undead). Odd: death-phase (counts as undead, vulnerable to radiant, immune to effects targeting living). This changes which spells and abilities work against it.
> - **Sap-Lash.** Melee attack, 2-space reach. Deals 1d6 acid damage (life-phase) or 1d6 cold damage (death-phase).
> - **Amber Burst (death).** When reduced to 0 HP, the Wraith explodes in a 2-space burst of hardening sap. Creatures in range make DC 12 Dexterity save or are coated in amber-sap (restrained, escape DC 13 Strength). The sap hardens into low-grade Thornwild amber worth 10gp after 10 minutes.
> - **Stabilization Response.** If a character with dual-channeling ability (or who casts both a life-domain and death-domain spell in the same encounter) is present, the Wraith ceases hostility and drifts toward that character.
>
> **Weakness:** Consistent damage of a single type (3+ instances of the same damage type in a row) destabilizes the Wraith, dealing +1d6 bonus damage on the third hit. Thunder damage forces immediate phase-shift.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 2-3 players of level 1-3 (per 2-3 wild Wraiths)

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The path to [[Rootpyre]] passes through a grove where the light keeps changing, golden warmth one moment, grey pallor the next, flickering like a candle in wind. Three translucent shapes drift between the trees, their bodies filled with flowing sap that pulses green, then grey, then green again. One of them turns toward the party. It has no face, but the sap-flow shifts, and for a moment, the shape of it looks almost like a person reaching out a hand. Then it flickers grey and the hand becomes a tendril and the tendril whips forward.
>
> **Complication:** One of Fennara's apprentices is nearby, searching for a stabilized Wraith that wandered from Rootpyre. If the party kills wild Wraiths in her presence, she will be furious, not because these specific Wraiths matter, but because "every one of them could become what ours are, if anyone bothered to try." She offers to stabilize the remaining Wraiths if the party protects her while she channels.
