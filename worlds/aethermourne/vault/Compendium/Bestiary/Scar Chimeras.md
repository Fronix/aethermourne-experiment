## Description

Where [[The Twelve#Solvaen|Solvaen]]'s order meets [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s growth, the land itself cannot decide what it is. Neither can the creatures born there. Scar Chimeras are animals reshaped by two divine influences fighting for dominance in a single body. Deer with calcified antlers sprouting living flowers. Insects with ceramic exoskeletons and soft, pulsing organic interiors. Wolves whose front halves are white stone, rigid and geometric, while their rear halves are vine-woven muscle, flexible and alive.

They are unsettling to observe. The line where order meets growth is visible on their bodies, a jagged seam where pale mineral gives way to vivid green tissue, and the seam *moves*, shifting slowly as one influence gains ground over the other. A chimera that was mostly stone last season may be mostly vine this season, or the reverse. They are living maps of the divine border war playing out beneath the soil.

## Behavior & Tactics

Stable chimeras, those in which the two influences have reached equilibrium, are passive. They graze, nest, and behave like the animals they once were, if somewhat stiffly on the calcified side. They avoid contact with travelers and pose no threat.

Unstable chimeras are violently erratic. Their bodies lurch between calcification and wild growth, shedding stone flakes and sprouting tissue in rapid alternation. They attack anything nearby, not from aggression but from pain, two forces tearing them apart from within. Their combat behavior oscillates: roll a d6 each round. On 1-3, order phase, the chimera is slow, hits hard, and moves in predictable geometric patterns. On 4-6, growth phase, the chimera is fast, unpredictable, and regenerates 1d6 HP at the start of its turn. The transition between phases is visible, a ripple of change washing across the body, giving observant combatants a moment to adjust.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The chimeras are a bellwether. When Solvaen's influence dominates the Scar, more chimeras calcify and become passive. When Thyrea's influence surges (especially if [[Kaelith Thornborn]] finds [[The Verdant Crown]]), the chimeras grow wild and dangerous, and the unstable population spikes. Monitoring chimera behavior tells you which dead god is "winning" the border war, and by extension, how the campaign's divine power balance is shifting.
>
> **Practical Intelligence.** The [[Thornwall]] garrison tracks chimera populations as an early warning system. A sudden increase in unstable chimeras means the Scar is shifting, and shifting means the Scarwall may surge. PCs who learn to read chimera behavior gain a tactical advantage and earn [[Thornwall]]'s respect.
>
> **Campaign Connection.** In Act III, if Serith's corruption reaches Thyrea's root systems, the growth-side of the chimeras begins expressing Serith's shadow rather than Thyrea's vitality. Chimeras with dark, thorned tissue and eyes that glow faintly violet are the first visible sign that Serith has penetrated the Marches' divine ecosystem.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is traveling along [[The Ashen Dominion#The Scar|the Scar]] toward [[Thornwall]] when they encounter a herd of chimeric deer grazing in a clearing where grey ash meets luminous moss. Most are stable, moving with the stiff grace of creatures half-made of stone. But one, larger than the rest, is shuddering. Its antlers alternate between white crystal and writhing green vine in visible pulses. Mineral flakes shed from its flanks as living tissue pushes through. It raises its head. One eye is crystal. One eye is alive. It charges.
>
> **Complication:** A [[Thornwall]] ranger is tracking this herd, documenting chimera ratios for the garrison's border report. She will not appreciate the party killing her research subjects, but she needs help subduing the unstable one before it panics the herd into the Scarwall's vicinity.
