## Description

When a masterwork weapon melts back into slag on the Cooling Yards, something of its intended purpose survives. That residual intent, compressed and distorted by the dissolution process, pools in the rivers of molten iron and takes shape. Slag Crawlers are what emerges: low-slung, eel-like creatures of semi-molten metal that flow through the liquid iron channels of the [[Level 4 - The Forge Eternal|Forge Eternal]] like predators through dark water. Their bodies shift constantly, never fully solid, never fully liquid, a state between that allows them to move through metal as easily as air.

Their surfaces ripple with heat distortion, and the air around them smells of burning oil and superheated copper. They range from arm-length juveniles to full-grown specimens longer than a cart, their bodies thickening with consumed metal over time. The strangest thing about them is their output. Slag Crawlers consume metal, weapons, armor, constructs, raw ore, and produce objects. Not functional objects, not weapons or tools, but sculptures, spirals, lattices, and abstract forms of extraordinary beauty and zero utility. They leave these creations in their wake like a trail of discarded art, cooling on the iron ground in shapes that make the eye linger and the mind search for meaning that is not there.

## Behavior & Tactics

Slag Crawlers are drawn to worked metal. Raw ore interests them only when nothing better is available. A well-crafted sword is irresistible. A suit of enchanted armor is a feast. They emerge from the molten rivers with startling speed, lunging at metal-bearing targets with mouths that dissolve iron on contact. Their bite does not cut; it liquefies. Metal touched by a Slag Crawler's maw loses its structural integrity, sagging and flowing like warm wax before being drawn into the creature's body.

They are not interested in flesh. A Slag Crawler will ignore an unarmed, unarmored person entirely. But a party carrying weapons and wearing metal armor is a moving buffet, and Crawlers will pursue them relentlessly, stripping away equipment piece by piece. The tactical nightmare is fighting a creature that eats your weapons mid-swing. A sword thrust into a Crawler's body does not wound it; the Crawler absorbs the blade and grows slightly larger.

Non-metallic weapons, stone, bone, wood, and magic are effective against them. Fire drives them into a frenzy rather than repelling them. Cold slows their metabolism, causing their semi-liquid bodies to seize and harden temporarily. A frozen Slag Crawler is vulnerable but not dead; it will thaw and resume hunting within minutes unless shattered while solid.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Slag Crawlers are equipment-destroyers, and players hate losing gear. Use them sparingly and telegraph their presence so players can prepare. The rivers of molten iron should ripple before a Crawler emerges. The smell of burning copper should precede an attack. Give the party a chance to stash their metal equipment before engaging.
>
> **The Beautiful Trash.** The objects Slag Crawlers produce are genuinely beautiful and occasionally valuable. Collectors in the Iron Market pay well for Crawler-art, considering it the purest expression of creation without intent. Some of these objects, rarely, turn out to have unexpected properties, accidental enchantments produced by the combination of consumed magical items.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The party is crossing a narrow bridge over a river of molten iron in the Forge Eternal. Halfway across, the surface of the river bulges upward. A Slag Crawler, twelve feet long and glowing cherry-red, rises from the molten flow and fixes on the party's metal equipment. It lunges for the most heavily armored PC. The bridge is narrow enough that dodging means risking the river. Behind the party, a second, smaller Crawler emerges, cutting off retreat. Between their feet, intricate metal sculptures begin forming from drops of slag the Crawlers shed, beautiful, useless, and in the way.
>
> **Resolution:** The party can fight with non-metal weapons, use cold-based attacks to freeze the Crawlers temporarily, or sacrifice a piece of metal equipment as a distraction, tossing it into the river and running while the Crawlers feed.

> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Metal Dissolution.** On hit against a metal-armored or metal-wielding target, the equipment makes a DC 13 durability save or loses 1 step of effectiveness (armor degrades from H to M to None; weapons lose 1 damage die step). At None/0, the item is destroyed and absorbed.
> - **Metal Absorption.** Absorbing a metal item restores 1d8 HP and increases size. After absorbing 3+ items, the Crawler's bite damage increases to 2d8.
> - **Semi-Liquid Form.** Can flow through gaps as narrow as 1 inch. Immune to bludgeoning damage. Slashing and piercing damage from metal weapons is halved (non-metallic weapons deal full damage).
> - **Heat Aura.** Creatures within 1 space take 1d4 fire damage at the start of their turn.
> **Bloodied:** The Crawler's semi-molten form destabilizes, shedding drops of liquid iron. Heat Aura range extends to 2 spaces and damage increases to 1d6.
> **Weakness:** Cold damage freezes the Crawler solid for 1 round (armor becomes None, all attacks deal full damage, can be shattered if reduced to 0 HP while frozen). Non-metallic weapons deal full damage. Completely ignores unarmed, unarmored creatures.
> **Recommended Party Size:** 3-4 players of level 3-5 (per 2-3 Crawlers)
