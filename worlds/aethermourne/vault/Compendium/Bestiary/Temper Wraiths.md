# Temper Wraiths

> [!info]
> | | |
> |---|---|

> | **Origin** | Forge-magic intent that manifested when the crafting process failed |
> | **Threat Level** | Serious threat |
> | **Associated God** | [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]], God of Fire and Craft |

## Description

Forge-magic is intent made material, the crafter's purpose woven into metal through hammer and heat. When the process succeeds, the intent binds to the finished object and stays there. When the process fails, the intent has nowhere to go.

Temper Wraiths are what happens next.

They are translucent, ember-colored, and shaped like the object they were meant to become. A failed blade produces a slashing phantom, a jagged outline of orange light that moves with cutting purpose. A failed shield produces a blocking presence, a broad luminous shape that shoves creatures away from whatever surface it has claimed. A failed set of tongs manifests as a grasping, pinching thing that seizes limbs and tools alike. Each wraith embodies a single function, the function its creator intended, and pursues that function without rest, reason, or restraint.

They haunt the workshops and roads near [[Crucible]], drifting through walls and floors with the absent persistence of smoke. They are not intelligent. They do not communicate. They are purpose without a vessel, and they will attempt to fulfill that purpose on anything they encounter. A blade-wraith cuts. A shield-wraith blocks. A chain-wraith binds. They cannot help it. It is all they are.

## Behavior & Tactics

Temper Wraiths manifest within hours of a forge-magic failure, coalescing from the residual heat of the ruined work. They linger near the site of their creation for days before drifting, drawn by the sound and heat of active forges, seeking the completion they were denied. They are most active at night, when the ambient glow of [[Crucible]]'s forges makes them harder to distinguish from normal firelight until they strike.

Combat with a Temper Wraith is shaped entirely by its type. Blade-wraiths are aggressive, darting in with slashing passes that leave burns rather than cuts, the wound cauterized before it bleeds. Shield-wraiths are defensive nightmares, positioning themselves in doorways and corridors and shoving back anything that tries to pass. Tool-wraiths are unpredictable, grasping, twisting, hammering, whatever their intended function dictates.

The true resolution is not destruction but completion. A DC 16 Crafting check allows a smith to "finish" the wraith's work, shaping raw metal with the same intent that created the wraith and giving the purpose a home. The wraith flows into the finished object and goes still, its glow settling into the metal as a permanent, faint warmth. Objects finished this way carry a minor enchantment reflecting the wraith's intent, a blade that holds its edge unnaturally well, a shield that deflects slightly more than physics allows.

Before the Cooling, forge-magic failures were rare and Temper Wraiths rarer still, perhaps one or two a year across the entire region. Now they appear weekly. Master smiths are terrified, not of the wraiths themselves, but of what their increasing numbers mean. The craft is failing. [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]]'s fire, the foundation of everything the Emberveil builds, is no longer reliable enough to sustain the magic that defines their culture. The Emberveil's identity is literally haunting them.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Temper Wraiths are not random failures. The compensatory mechanism beneath [[Hearthdeep]], the deep forge straining to maintain thermal output, is drawing so much of [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor]]'s residual creative essence downward that surface forge-magic is being starved. The wraiths are proof that the forge's self-preservation is cannibalizing the culture it sustains.
>
> [[Corrin Ashveil]]'s historical records in the Athenaeum show a similar wraith-surge occurred briefly after the Theomachis, the last time Thalvor's fire underwent a major transition. That surge lasted months before stabilizing. This one is accelerating.
>
> **[[Apprentice Jyn]] Connection.** Jyn can finish Temper Wraiths instinctively, the same way they finished [[The Quenched]]. Their success rate is unsettling: every wraith they have encountered has been resolved in minutes, the intent flowing into raw metal as if it had been waiting for them. If Jyn is the future of forge-magic, the wraiths may be the proof.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Temper Wraith (blade) | 4 | L | 32 | fly 6 | +0 | +3 | +2 | slash 1d8+3 fire |
> | Temper Wraith (shield) | 4 | H | 40 | fly 4 | +3 | +0 | +2 | shove 1d6+3 force, pushes 2 spaces |
> | Temper Wraith (tool) | 4 | M | 35 | fly 5 | +2 | +2 | +2 | grapple + 1d6 fire/round |
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Incorporeal.** Can move through solid objects. Takes half damage from non-magical physical attacks.
> - **Single Purpose.** Each wraith can only perform its intended function. Blade-wraiths only slash. Shield-wraiths only block and shove. Tool-wraiths only grapple.
> - **Forge-Drawn.** Moves toward the nearest active forge or source of forge-magic. Ignores creatures not between it and the heat source unless attacked.
> - **Finish.** DC 16 Crafting check (5 minutes, requires smithing tools and raw metal). On success, the wraith is absorbed into the finished object, which gains a minor enchantment. On failure, the wraith lashes out (automatic hit, double damage).
>
> **Bloodied:** The wraith's glow intensifies and it becomes erratic, attacking the nearest creature regardless of type. Its single-purpose restriction breaks: blade-wraiths grapple, shield-wraiths slam, tool-wraiths slash.
>
> **Weakness:** Cold damage solidifies the wraith for 1 round (stunned, loses incorporeal). Forge-magic effects deal double damage.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 2-4 players of level 3-5 (per 1-2 wraiths)

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The workshop is dark. The forge has been cold for three days, ever since the master smith's latest commission, a ceremonial blade for [[The Forge Council]], cracked during the final tempering. She will not speak of what went wrong. She will only say the metal "refused."
>
> Something moves in the workshop. A shape of ember-light, long and thin and edged, drifts between the anvils. It passes through a hanging rack of tools without disturbing them. It reaches the cold forge and hovers, pulsing, as if waiting for the fire to return. Then it turns. The blade-shape orients on the nearest metal object, the fighter's sword, and glides forward with the quiet, purposeful motion of a thing that knows exactly what it was made to do.
>
> **Complication:** Two more wraiths haunt the street outside, a shield-wraith blocking the main road to the Grand Forge and a tool-wraith that has seized the door of a neighboring workshop and will not release it. [[Apprentice Jyn]] is inside the blocked workshop, trying to talk the tool-wraith into letting go. They are close to succeeding. They could use help, or they could use everyone to stop interfering.
