## Description

Vent Wyrms are serpentine predators that ride the thermal updrafts spiraling around the [[The Emberveil#The Caldera Ring|Caldera Ring]]. Ten to fifteen feet long, their bodies are sheathed in heat-blackened scales that shimmer like cooling obsidian, and their internal temperatures run hot enough to warp untempered metal on contact. They have no wings. Instead, broad lateral fins of heat-resistant membrane catch rising columns of superheated air, allowing them to glide for hours between the peaks of [[Mount Thalvor]], the Anvil, the Bellows, and the Tongs. From below, they look like ribbons of dark smoke curling through the volcanic haze.

They nest in active volcanic vents, laying clutches of three to five eggs in superheated gas pockets where no scavenger can reach them. The eggs incubate for months, absorbing thermal energy until the hatchlings emerge already hot enough to burn. Solitary ambush hunters, Vent Wyrms strike from above, dropping onto prey, coiling tight, and cooking it alive through sheer body heat. Livestock, mountain goats, and the occasional unwary miner on an exposed ridge are all prey.

## Behavior & Tactics

A Vent Wyrm hunts by reading thermals. It spirals high above its territory, sensing temperature differentials through heat-pits along its jaw, then folds its fins and drops. The strike is near-silent, just a rush of displaced air and a sudden, crushing heat. Once coiled around prey, the wyrm's body temperature spikes, searing flesh through armor and clothing in seconds. Victims who survive the initial constriction rarely survive the burns.

They are territorial and solitary, each wyrm claiming a thermal column and the hunting grounds beneath it. Disputes between wyrms are settled by aerial displays, two serpents spiraling around the same updraft, the one that can climb higher claiming the territory. These displays are spectacular and visible for miles, which is how the [[The Emberveil#The Ashfall Slopes|Ashfall Slopes]] villagers know when a new wyrm has moved in.

The Cooling is collapsing their high-altitude thermals. As the updrafts weaken, Vent Wyrms are being pushed lower into the [[The Emberveil#The Warm Valleys|Warm Valleys]] and Ashfall Slopes, where they attack livestock, threaten villages, and occasionally nest in forge-chimneys, drawn by the heat of active forges. Ashfall Slope villagers are demanding [[The Forge Council]] act. The Council is paralyzed because killing Vent Wyrms in their volcanic nests risks destabilizing already fragile vents, and the displaced wyrms are nesting in locations where a violent death-thrash could collapse a chimney onto a working forge.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Vent Wyrm displacement maps the Cooling's progression with startling accuracy. Tracking where wyrms abandon territory reveals which thermal columns are failing, making them an unintentional early warning system. [[Brunhild Ironheart]] has quietly tasked several rangers with logging wyrm movements, though she has not explained why.
>
> The Deep Sound, the compensatory hammer-strikes from beneath [[Hearthdeep]], agitates them. Increased wyrm aggression correlates directly with stronger hammer-strike cycles, as the vibrations travel up through the stone and disrupt their heat-sensing. During particularly intense episodes, every wyrm on the Caldera Ring goes into a frenzy simultaneously, a spectacle that terrifies the Ashfall Slopes and confirms to anyone paying attention that the deep earth and the surface ecology are connected in ways the [[The Ember Guild|Ember Guild]] does not fully understand.

## Encounter Notes

> [!example] Stat Block (Nimble)
> | Name | Lvl | Armor | HP | Speed | Str | Dex | Will | Attacks |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
> | Vent Wyrm | 5 | M | 48 | fly 7 (thermal only), ground 3 | +3 | +3 | -1 | constrict 1d10+3 bludgeoning + Sear |
>
> **Special Abilities:**
> - **Sear.** A constricted target takes 1d6 fire damage at the start of each of its turns. Metal armor heated: after 2 rounds, armor AC drops by 1 (cumulative, repaired by a smith).
> - **Thermal Strike.** When diving from at least 6 spaces above, the wyrm's first attack deals an additional 2d6 damage and the target must make a Str save DC 14 or be knocked prone and grappled.
> - **Heat Shimmer.** While airborne near a thermal source, attacks against the wyrm have disadvantage beyond 6 spaces.
>
> **Bloodied:** Internal temperature spikes. Sear damage increases to 2d6. The wyrm's scales crack, venting heat: creatures within 1 space take 1d4 fire damage at the start of the wyrm's turn.
>
> **Weakness:** Cold damage grounds a flying wyrm for 1 round (thermal fins seize). Without thermals, the wyrm cannot fly and fights at disadvantage on Dex checks.
>
> **Recommended Party Size:** 3-5 players of level 4-6 (single wyrm)

> [!example] Sample Encounter
> The path along the Ashfall Slopes narrows between two ridges, and the air above shimmers with volcanic haze. A farmer's cart sits abandoned on the trail, its ox missing. Drag marks lead to a blackened patch of stone where something heavy and very hot rested recently. The stone is still warm. Above, circling the nearest peak in lazy spirals, a dark ribbon of scale and membrane rides an updraft. It has not noticed the party. The ox's remains are wedged in a crevice thirty feet up the ridge, still steaming.
>
> **Complication:** A second wyrm has claimed the forge-chimney of a smithy in the village below. The smith refuses to abandon his workshop. The wyrm has laid eggs in the flue. Killing it risks collapsing the chimney onto the forge, and the eggs will hatch in days.
