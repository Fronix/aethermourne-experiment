# Factions of Aethermourne

> *"Every throne rests on alliances, and every alliance rests on lies."*
> [[Torren Hearthforge|Guildmaster Torren Hearthforge]], declining to take sides (again)

This document indexes the twelve major factions operating across Aethermourne and maps their relationships. For full details on any faction, follow the links to individual faction files.

> [!info]
> **GM Reference:** Each faction's individual file includes goals, structure, strengths, weaknesses, and hooks for player interaction. The Faction Relationship Matrix below provides a quick-reference grid for inter-faction dynamics.

### Reading the Matrix, Key Relationships

**Dominant Alliances:**
- [[Order of the Ashen Flame]] and [[The Cinders]] appear to be close allies. In truth, the Cinders are exploiting this relationship to manipulate the Order and the Hierarch.
- [[The Greensingers]] and the [[Marchwardens Council]] are deeply intertwined, the Greensingers serve as spiritual advisors and the Council relies on their communion for strategic intelligence.
- [[The Lantern-Keepers]] and [[The Star-Readers]] share information freely. Ashvane trusts the Star-Readers' analysis; the Star-Readers value the Lantern-Keepers' field observations.

**Hidden Axis of Serith:**
- [[The Cinders]] and [[The Veil Unbound]] are secretly allied through their shared service to Serith. Their leaders (Mourne and the Whisperer) communicate through intermediaries and coordinate strategy. Neither fully trusts the other, but both serve the same goddess.
- Both factions are independently targeting [[The Ember Guild]]'s Kaevroth fragment registry.
- Both factions are working to undermine the institutions in their respective regions, the Cinders hollowing out the Dominion, the Veil Unbound sabotaging the Reach.

**Notable Tensions:**
- [[Order of the Ashen Flame]] views the [[Marchwardens Council]] as a growing threat. Commander Voss's preemptive war plan would shatter any possibility of alliance.
- [[Concord of Tides]] and [[The Lantern-Keepers]] should be natural allies but are locked in a funding dispute. The Concord sees the Lantern-Keepers as a cost center; the Lantern-Keepers see the Concord as negligently endangering the coast.
- [[The Ashen Vigil]] and [[Remnants Court|the Remnants' Court]] coexist uneasily. Many Vigil members respect the Remnants; many others consider them monsters wearing the faces of the dead. This internal debate mirrors the broader question of what it means to be alive.
- [[The Star-Readers]] are viewed with suspicion by [[Order of the Ashen Flame]] and [[The Cinders]] because their pursuit of truth threatens the Dominion's carefully maintained narratives.

**Cross-Regional Bridges:**
- [[The Ember Guild]] and [[The Star-Readers]] are the only factions with genuine continent-wide reach and cross-regional neutrality. Both serve as bridges between regional powers that would otherwise have no communication channels.
- [[The Ashen Vigil]] receives nominal support from [[Order of the Ashen Flame]] (supply shipments) and friendly relations from [[The Star-Readers]] (information exchange), but is largely isolated.
