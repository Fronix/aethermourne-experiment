# The Iron Companies

|||
|---|---|
| **Region** | [[The Ironmarch]] (operating continent-wide) |
| **Type** | Mercenary Guild Confederation |
| **Leader** | No single leader. Governed collectively through [[The Quartermasters Circle]] |
| **Alignment** | Professional neutral, they fight for whoever pays |
| **Founded** | ~300 AS, formalized ~600 AS |

## Description

The Ironmarch cannot grow enough food, cannot maintain stable governments, and cannot stop fighting. What it can do is produce the finest soldiers on the continent. The Iron Companies are the result of two thousand years of war-saturated natural selection: professional mercenary guilds whose members are born into conflict, trained by conflict, and exported to fight other people's conflicts for coin. They are the Ironmarch's primary export, after [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] itself, and they are the reason the rest of Aethermourne takes the region seriously despite its lack of central government.

A "company" is a self-contained military unit, typically between fifty and five hundred fighters, organized around a captain, a charter, and a set of contracts. Companies operate independently but are bound by a shared code of conduct enforced through [[The Quartermasters Circle]], the neutral body that brokers contracts and arbitrates disputes from [[The Red Garrison]]. The code is simple: honor your contracts, respect neutral ground, settle blood debts through sanctioned channels, and never fight for free. Beyond that, each company sets its own rules.

## Major Companies

- **The Silent Company**, led by [[Harsk the Quiet]]. Three hundred fighters, the most feared company in the Ironmarch. Known for discipline, silence on the march, and devastating shock assaults. Currently between contracts.
- **The Red Dust**, formerly independent, now absorbed into [[Mira the Red|Mira the Red's]] personal army. Its absorption is technically a violation of company law, as companies are not supposed to become personal retinues.
- **The Ashwalkers**, a two-hundred-strong company specializing in escort and caravan protection. Reliable, professional, and boring, which is exactly what their clients want.
- **The Rust Wolves**, a mid-sized company recently quarantined in [[The Red Garrison]] after their leadership was contaminated by a [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] fragment in the Shardfall.
- **The Dusk Shields**, a company of one hundred that has made a permanent contract with [[Duskfield]], serving as the town's garrison in exchange for food, shelter, and a life outside the fragment-fields.

## The Mercenary Code

The Code is unwritten, passed orally between captains, and enforced by [[The Quartermasters Circle]]. Its core tenets:

1. **The contract is sacred.** A signed contract is an oath. Breaking it means dissolution of the company and branding of its members.
2. **Neutral ground is inviolable.** [[The Red Garrison]] and any site designated by the [[The Quartermasters Circle|Quartermasters]] may not be attacked, occupied, or used for military staging.
3. **Blood debts are personal.** Vendettas between individuals do not extend to their companies. Debts are settled in sanctioned duels, not campaigns.
4. **Payment before deployment.** Half up front, half on completion. No credit. No promises. No IOUs.
5. **The dead are released.** A fallen mercenary's obligations end with their death. Their company may not be held liable for uncompleted contracts due to casualties.

## Strengths

- **Quality.** Ironmarch mercenaries are hardened by the most hostile environment on the continent. Their baseline competence exceeds most standing armies.
- **Availability.** The Companies provide military force to anyone with coin, making them indispensable to factions that lack standing armies or need deniable operations.
- **The Code.** The mercenary code provides stability and predictability in a profession defined by violence. Clients know what they are buying.

## Weaknesses

- **[[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] contamination.** Long-term exposure to war-essence makes every mercenary a ticking clock. Aggressive behavior, paranoia, and eventually madness are occupational hazards.
- **No unified command.** The Companies cannot be mobilized as a single force. Each operates independently, and coordinating multiple companies requires expensive negotiation.
- **Loyalty is contractual.** A company fights for whoever pays. This makes them unreliable as long-term allies and vulnerable to being outbid.

## Hooks for Player Interaction

- **Hiring muscle.** PCs can hire Iron Companies for specific missions. Quality varies. Prices are non-negotiable.
- **Joining up.** PCs with martial backgrounds could take temporary contracts with a company, gaining access to the Ironmarch's military network.
- **The Rust Wolves crisis.** The quarantined company is a ticking bomb. PCs could help contain or cure their contamination, earning the Companies' gratitude and the [[The Quartermasters Circle|Quartermasters']] trust.
- **Mira's recruitment drive.** [[Mira the Red]] is pulling companies out of the neutral pool. Stopping or slowing this drain is a priority for [[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal|Voss Irenthal]] and [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Yrsa]].

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Companies' neutrality is a fiction that works only as long as the balance of power holds. If [[Mira the Red]] offers enough, or if [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] war-essence intensifies enough, the Code will fracture. Companies will choose sides, and the structured violence that keeps the Ironmarch functional will become unstructured slaughter. [[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal|Voss Irenthal]] knows this. He is preparing contingency plans, including a potential alliance with [[The Ashen Dominion]] that would make him a traitor to Ironmarch independence but might save the institution he has spent his life building.
