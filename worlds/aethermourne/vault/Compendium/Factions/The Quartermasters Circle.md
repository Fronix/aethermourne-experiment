# The Quartermasters Circle

|||
|---|---|
| **Region** | [[The Ironmarch]], based in [[The Red Garrison]] |
| **Type** | Neutral arbitration body |
| **Leader** | [[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal]] |
| **Alignment** | Institutional neutral, committed to the Mercenary Code and the Garrison's independence |
| **Founded** | ~600 AS, alongside the formalization of [[The Iron Companies]] |

## Description

The Quartermasters Circle is the governing body of [[The Red Garrison]] and the institutional backbone of the Ironmarch's mercenary economy. It is not a government, not a guild, and not a military command. It is an arbitration service backed by centuries of precedent and the collective agreement of every [[The Iron Companies|Iron Company]] that the alternative, anarchy, is worse. The Circle brokers contracts, mediates disputes, enforces the Mercenary Code, and maintains the neutrality of [[The Red Garrison]] with the quiet, implacable authority of an institution that has outlasted every warlord, every thane, and every conqueror the Ironmarch has produced.

The Circle's authority rests on two foundations: legitimacy and necessity. Every company captain has sworn to respect its rulings, and every captain knows that without the Circle, the contract market collapses, the Code becomes unenforceable, and the Ironmarch's violence ceases to be productive. The Circle does not have an army. It does not need one. It has something more powerful: it has everyone's self-interest on its side.

## Structure

The Circle consists of seven Quartermasters, each responsible for a domain:

1. **The Head Quartermaster** ([[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal|Voss Irenthal]]), presiding authority and final arbiter of disputed rulings
2. **The Contract Quartermaster**, oversees all active contracts, ensures terms are honored, and mediates renegotiations
3. **The Arbitration Quartermaster**, hears disputes between companies, between companies and clients, and between captains
4. **The Records Quartermaster**, maintains the Garrison's archives, the institutional memory of every contract, ruling, and precedent
5. **The Marshals Quartermaster**, commands the Garrison's small enforcement staff, responsible for maintaining peace within the walls
6. **The Trade Quartermaster**, manages the Garrison's finances, supply chains, and commercial relationships with outside factions
7. **The Intelligence Quartermaster**, gathers information on external threats, client reliability, and company conduct, the Circle's eyes beyond the walls

Quartermasters are elected by company captains for ten-year terms. The Head Quartermaster is elected by the other six Quartermasters. Removal requires a two-thirds vote of registered captains, a threshold that has been reached exactly twice in the Circle's history.

## Goals

- **Maintain the Garrison's neutrality.** This is the Circle's existential purpose. Without neutral ground, the contract market dies.
- **Enforce the Mercenary Code.** The Code is the Ironmarch's only law. The Circle enforces it because no one else will.
- **Preserve institutional memory.** The archives contain centuries of precedent. The Circle guards them because history, in the Ironmarch, is the only argument that reliably wins.
- **Adapt to survive.** The Circle has endured because it changes with the times while preserving its core function. Rigidity kills institutions faster than war.

## Strengths

- **Legitimacy.** Every company recognizes the Circle's authority. This is cultural, not legal, and that makes it stronger.
- **Indispensability.** Without the Circle, the contract market has no mediator, the Code has no enforcer, and the Garrison has no government.
- **Institutional memory.** The archives give the Circle historical depth that no warlord can match. Precedent is power.

## Weaknesses

- **No military force.** The Circle's enforcement capacity is limited to the Garrison's small marshals corps. If a major company defects, the Circle cannot compel obedience through force.
- **Legitimacy is fragile.** If enough captains stop recognizing the Circle's authority, it ceases to function. This has nearly happened during periods of especially intense [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] contamination.
- **Neutrality as vulnerability.** The Circle cannot take sides, even when one side is clearly right. This makes it powerless against existential threats that require partisanship.

## Hooks for Player Interaction

- **Arbitration services.** PCs with disputes, legal questions, or contract needs can petition the Circle. The process is formal, fair, and slow.
- **Intelligence sharing.** The Circle's intelligence arm knows who is hiring whom, who is moving where, and who is about to start a war. This information is available, for a price, or a favor.
- **Defending the Garrison.** If the neutrality is threatened, the Circle will seek outside help, discreetly. PCs who protect the Garrison earn the gratitude of every company that depends on it.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Circle is the Ironmarch's immune system, and it is failing. [[Mira the Red|Mira's]] recruitment drive is pulling companies out of the neutral pool faster than the Circle can replace them. [[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal|Voss Irenthal's]] network of informants inside [[Mira the Red|Mira's]] camp reports that she intends to either co-opt the Garrison or destroy it, she has not decided which. If the PCs do not intervene, the Garrison will fall within six months, either to direct assault or to the slow hemorrhage of companies defecting to [[Mira the Red|Mira's]] cause. Voss's contingency plan, smuggling the archives to [[The Ashen Dominion]] and reestablishing the Circle in exile, is desperate, undignified, and the only option he has left.
