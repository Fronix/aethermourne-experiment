## Appearance

A dwarven woman in her early one hundred and twenties, Ashara Cinderbloom has the weathered warmth of someone who spends her days between steam and stone. Her copper-red hair is kept in a loose braid that frequently escapes its binding during work. Her hands are her most distinctive feature, broad and steady, the skin permanently flushed from years of forge-magic healing, warm to the touch even in cold weather. Her patients describe the sensation of her hands on their skin as like being held by heated metal, firm and almost uncomfortably warm, but deeply soothing. She wears the undyed linen of a Mender, stained at the sleeves with the mineral residue of the hot springs, and carries a leather satchel of herbs and salves that she is never without.

## Personality

Ashara is gentle where most Emberveil dwarves are blunt, patient where they are direct, and emotionally perceptive in a culture that values practical assessment over feeling. This makes her both beloved and slightly alien to her own people. She listens before she speaks, asks questions before she diagnoses, and treats the whole person rather than the symptom. Beneath this warmth is a core of quiet ferocity, she will fight any bureaucracy, any tradition, any authority that stands between her and a patient's well-being. She is also honest enough to admit when she cannot help, and brave enough to say so to the patient's face.

## Backstory

Ashara was born in [[Crucible]] and apprenticed to the forge, following the standard dwarven path. She was competent but unhappy, finding the work technically satisfying but spiritually empty. At thirty, she witnessed a mine collapse that killed eight and injured twenty, and spent three days helping the Menders treat the wounded. She found her calling in the precise application of forge-magic to living tissue, the same principles of shaping and intention applied to flesh instead of steel.

She trained under the previous Head Mender of Cinderfall for forty years, then succeeded her when the old healer finally admitted her own hands had grown too cold. Ashara has run the Mending Pools for thirty years, building [[Cinderfall]]'s reputation as the finest healing centre in the Emberveil. She has treated patients from every region, including Dominion soldiers who crossed the border wounded and desperate. She healed them without asking questions, which earned her a reprimand from [[The Forge Council]] and the undying loyalty of her staff.

Two years ago, she began noticing that her treatments were taking longer. Patients who should have recovered in days lingered for a week. The springs' water, analysed with her forge-magic sensitivity, felt thinner, as though something essential in its composition was fading. She has been documenting the decline meticulously, and her correspondence with [[Corrin Ashveil]] in [[Crucible]] has begun to form a picture neither of them expected.

## Relationships

- [[Corrin Ashveil|Factor Corrin Ashveil]], Scholarly correspondence that has grown into urgent collaboration. He provides historical context; she provides current empirical data. Between them, they are assembling the most complete picture of the Cooling that exists.
- [[Brunhild Ironheart|Forge-Matriarch Brunhild Ironheart]], Ashara respects the Matriarch but is frustrated by her secrecy. She has written to Ironheart twice requesting a meeting to share her findings and received polite non-responses.
- **Pilgrim-Warden Dask**, The old dwarf at the Smoke Temple trusts Ashara more than anyone. His reports about the mountain's growing silence reach her first, before the Spring Council.
- [[Apprentice Jyn]], She has treated Jyn for heat-burns sustained during forge training and was struck by the intensity of their forge-magic aura. She quietly wonders whether a human with that much forge-sensitivity might perceive things about [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] fire that dwarves cannot.

## What They Want

Ashara wants to understand why the springs are failing and to find a way to stop it. She wants to protect Cinderfall and its patients. At a deeper level, she wants someone in authority to listen to her data and act on it, because she is increasingly certain that the Cooling is not a local phenomenon but something fundamentally wrong with the source of [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] fire, and the longer the Emberveil's leaders delay acknowledging it, the fewer options they will have.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Emberveil's diagnostic voice. She has the clearest empirical evidence of the Cooling's effects on surface systems and is the most likely NPC to explain the problem to the PCs in terms they can understand and act on. She is also a potential healer-ally for parties operating in the Emberveil.
>
> **Key Secret.** Her forge-magic sensitivity has shown her something she has not yet shared even with [[Corrin Ashveil|Corrin]]: the divine energy in the springs is not just weakening. It is being pulled downward, as though something deep below is drawing it away. She does not know what this means, but it suggests the Cooling is not passive decay but active drainage.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs connect her with [[Brunhild Ironheart|the Forge-Matriarch]] and [[Orin Deepstone|Deepstone]], the resulting collaboration produces a clear diagnosis of the Cooling and actionable intelligence about the Deep Forge. If ignored, she eventually publishes her findings independently, causing a public panic that the Forge Council is unprepared to manage.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks with a healer's cadence, calm and measured, with warmth that feels medicinal rather than social. Touches people's hands when she talks to them, reading their physical state through contact. Smells faintly of mineral steam and dried herbs. When distressed, she works, mixing salves, checking patients, moving constantly.
>
> *Sample dialogue:* "The water doesn't lie. For two thousand years, it has carried his warmth to us. Now it carries less. Whatever you believe about gods and sacrifice, the numbers are the numbers."
