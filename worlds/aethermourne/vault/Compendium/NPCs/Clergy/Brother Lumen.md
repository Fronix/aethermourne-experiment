## Appearance

A thin, intense man in his late fifties with pale skin, deep-set grey eyes ringed by dark circles, and unkempt sandy hair going white at the temples. He wears a Star-Reader's blue robe, faded and stained with fungal spores, that he refuses to surrender despite his expulsion. His hands are perpetually ink-stained, and his fingernails are rimmed with the luminescent residue of Sporeling's mushrooms. He moves with the distracted energy of someone whose mind is always three steps ahead of his body. A faint mycelial glow has begun spreading along his forearms where the fungal network's influence is strongest.

## Personality

Brilliant, obsessive, and dangerously certain. Lumen possesses the intellectual intensity of a true scholar combined with the moral flexibility of a man who has decided that his work matters more than its consequences. He is not malicious. He genuinely believes that reassembling [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]] will save the Aetheric Web and, by extension, all magic in Aethermourne. He simply cannot see, or will not see, that his experiment might destroy [[Sporeling]]'s population in the process. When confronted with the ethical implications of his work, he becomes defensive, dismissive, and coldly logical.

## Backstory

Lumen was a Lens-Bearer, the Star-Readers' field research rank, stationed at the chapter house in [[Fenholm]]. His area of study was the nature of [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]]'s fragmentation: specifically, whether the goddess's shattered consciousness retained enough coherence to be communicated with. The official Star-Reader position was that Nethys was irretrievably broken, her fragments inert arcane residue. Lumen disagreed.

His research led him to the fenlands because the ley-line concentration here offered the best chance of detecting conscious activity in Nethys's remnants. He found what he was looking for. The fragments weren't inert. They were thinking, trying to think, flickering in and out of awareness like a candle in wind. He published a paper arguing that Nethys could be partially reconstituted if enough fragments were gathered and connected through a suitable medium. The Star-Readers' leadership rejected the paper as dangerous speculation. When Lumen persisted, they expelled him.

He didn't leave the fenlands. He went deeper.

Three months ago, he arrived in [[Sporeling]] and recognized the fungal network for what it was: Nethys's nervous system, rebuilt in organic form. A natural conduit for fragment reassembly. He began working immediately, modifying the network's growth patterns, amplifying its reach, and channelling fragments through it. The work is progressing. The network is expanding, the fragments are converging, and Nethys's whispers through the mycelium are becoming words. Lumen considers this the most important scientific work in two millennia. He has not considered what happens to the eight hundred people connected to the network when a goddess's consciousness floods through it.

## Relationships

- **[[The Star-Readers]]** , he was expelled but still considers himself a member. He believes they will welcome him back when his work succeeds. They will not. Arch-Reader Tessera Vane considers him a cautionary tale.
- **[[Wick]]** , he met the prodigy once in [[Glimwater]] and recognized their potential immediately. He needs a living conduit for the final stage of his experiment: someone who can channel multiple fragments simultaneously. Wick is the only candidate he's identified. He hasn't told them this.
- **[[Old Fenris]]** , the elder suspects what Lumen is doing and hasn't stopped him. Lumen interprets this as tacit approval. It is actually horrified paralysis.
- **The Sporeling community** , they tolerate his presence because he pays for his lodging in Star-Reader knowledge and helps maintain the grounding wards in the Shard Bogs. They don't understand his research. He hasn't tried to explain it.
- **Tessera Vane** , the Star-Reader chapter lead in [[Fenholm]], sent him to Sporeling as a Lens-Bearer mission before his expulsion was formalized. She doesn't know his current work has moved far beyond observation.

## What They Want

To reassemble Nethys. He believes this is the only way to repair the Aetheric Web before the Second Silence destroys all magic. He is willing to sacrifice anything to achieve it, including things that aren't his to sacrifice.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Antagonist-by-ignorance. Lumen is not a villain; he is a well-intentioned extremist whose work could cause a catastrophe. He is the NPC who forces the PCs to grapple with a genuinely difficult question: is reassembling a shattered goddess the right response to the Second Silence, even if the cost is horrific?
>
> **Key Secret.** Lumen's experiment is further along than anyone realizes. He has successfully connected three major Nethys fragments through the mycelial network and is preparing to attempt a fourth. Each connection has accelerated the network's growth and deepened its hold on Sporeling's residents. The fourth fragment, he believes, will trigger a cascade that draws in fragments from across the fenlands. If it works, a partial Nethys will awaken in the network. If it fails, the energy release will kill everyone connected to the mycelium, Sporeling's entire population, and possibly Lumen himself.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** Lumen begins off-screen, a mystery the PCs investigate. When they find him, they must decide: support his work (accepting the risks), stop it (accepting that the Aetheric Web may collapse without intervention), or find a middle path (perhaps involving [[Wick]] as a safer conduit). There is no obviously right answer.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks rapidly when excited, which is most of the time. Uses academic jargon without checking whether his audience understands it. Genuinely passionate, leaning forward, eyes bright. But when challenged ethically, he becomes cold and precise, deploying logic like a weapon. He does not ask about the Sporeling residents by name. This is not an accident.
>
> *"Do you understand what she was? Not just a goddess of magic, the architect of the Web itself. Every spell cast in two thousand years runs on infrastructure she built. And it's failing. The Web is failing. I can fix it. I just need, more time. And the courage to do what must be done."*
