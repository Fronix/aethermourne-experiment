# Garret Boneharvest

## Appearance

Garret is a stocky, barrel-chested man in his late forties with the permanent squint of someone who has spent too many years watching things transition between states that the human eye was not designed to track. His face is broad, weathered, and often smiling in a way that reaches his eyes. His hands are covered in the pale, faintly luminescent scars common to all cycle-material harvesters, a tracery of old chemical burns and transition-energy contact marks that glow slightly in dim light.

He dresses practically: leather apron over wool, heavy gloves hanging from his belt, boots reinforced with deadwood soles. Around his neck he wears a vial of raw Thornwild amber on a leather cord, warm on one side, cold on the other, which he fidgets with when thinking. His hair is brown going grey, kept short because long hair catches on things in the bone-groves. He smells of alchemical preservatives and the particular sweet-mineral scent of cycle-materials, a smell his neighbors have stopped noticing and visitors never forget.

## Personality

Practical, cheerful, and honest in a way that people find either refreshing or alarming. Garret came to the Thornwild for profit and stayed because the place makes sense to him in a way the rest of the world does not. He likes clear transactions. The Thornwild's economy is the clearest there is: life becomes death becomes life, and in the turning, value is created. No pretense. No politics. Just the Cycle and what it produces.

He is a gifted negotiator who refuses to cheat. Not out of morality, though he is not immoral, but because the Thornwild punishes dishonesty directly. A harvester who cuts corners gets cycle-burn. A trader who sells unstable materials loses customers to chemistry before they lose them to reputation. Garret finds this elegant. In a world of courtly politics and factional scheming, he has found a place where competence is the only currency that doesn't devalue.

He is generous with his time, fair with his prices, and absolutely relentless when someone threatens his town.

## Backstory

Born in the [[The Ashen Dominion|Ashen Dominion]], the son of a middling merchant family in a middling city. Garret was competent at trade, bored by it, and quietly furious at the layers of corruption, favors, and factional politics that determined success in the Dominion's markets. He wanted to trade in something real, and when rumors reached him of a wilderness in the northwest where unique alchemical materials could be harvested from the boundary between life and death, he packed a cart and left.

He arrived at the Thornwild expecting a frontier outpost. He found a half-dozen desperate people camped in a grove of bone-white trees, collecting transition-materials with their bare hands and dying of cycle-burn at a rate that should have emptied the camp in a season. Garret took one look at the operation, sat down, and redesigned it. Proper collection equipment fashioned from resistant materials. Timing schedules based on the Cycle's phases. Stabilization techniques borrowed from Dominion alchemy and adapted for materials that existed in two states simultaneously.

Bone Garden grew from that camp. Garret built it with the same practical optimism he brings to everything: identify the problem, build the solution, adjust when the solution stops working. He has been adjusting for fifteen years, and the town's prosperity, modest but real, is his monument. He does not think of it that way. He thinks of it as work.

## Relationships

- **[[Yara Thornbloom]]**: Professional respect. Garret depends on Cyclekeeper warnings for his harvesting schedules and pays for the service with a percentage of trade revenue. He thinks Yara works too hard and sleeps too little, and he sends food to the Perch when he remembers.
- **Master Coris**: Business partner and friend. The head alchemist at the Crucible translates Garret's raw materials into trade goods. They argue about pricing, agree about quality, and share a flask of Dominion whiskey on rest days.
- **[[The Ember Guild]]**: Wary interest. Ember Guild traders have approached Garret with offers that would make him wealthy. He has declined so far because the strings are visible, but the offers keep improving, and Bone Garden's economy is fragile.
- **[[Kael Duskborne]]**: Gratitude. The Thornguard has saved Bone Garden's harvesters more than once. Garret pays the militia in supplies, information, and the occasional bottle of something strong.

## What They Want

Garret wants stable trade routes connecting Bone Garden to the wider world. He wants cycle-materials recognized as legitimate trade goods by the Dominion's markets. He wants his town to prosper.

What he needs, and would laugh if you said it aloud, is to matter. Garret left the Dominion because he could not make a difference there. In the Thornwild, he built something from nothing, and the fear that it could vanish, that the Cycle's acceleration could destroy Bone Garden's economy or the Thornwild's isolation could end in exploitation rather than trade, keeps him up at night more than he admits.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Economic contact and information broker for Thornwild arcs. Garret knows everyone who trades in the Thornwild and hears everything. He is also the first to notice when outside interests, particularly [[The Ember Guild]], begin interfering.
>
> **Key Secret.** An Ember Guild agent has already infiltrated the Star-Reader expedition at Cyclethorn and is sending reports on Thornwild amber production back to the Guild. Garret does not know this yet, but he has noticed unusual questions from one of the researchers and is suspicious. If the Guild succeeds in commercializing Thornwild amber, they will strip-mine the Threshold and destroy the Cycle's balance to maximize production.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs help Garret establish independent trade routes, Bone Garden becomes a legitimate economic power, providing the Thornwild with resources and the continent with unique materials. If the Ember Guild takes control, Garret loses everything, and the Thornwild's exploitation becomes a secondary crisis layered on top of the Cycle's destabilization.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Warm, direct, uses trade metaphors for everything. Fidgets with the amber vial when thinking. Speaks with the casual confidence of a man who knows his business. Gets quiet when angry rather than loud. Voice: mid-register, slightly rough, with faint Dominion accent. Sample line: "Everything dies. Everything comes back. At least here, they're honest about it. Try getting that kind of transparency from a Dominion merchant house."
