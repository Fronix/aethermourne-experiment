## Appearance

A gnomish woman of indeterminate age, somewhere between sixty and a hundred and sixty, which for a gnome could mean almost anything. Polara Facet is small even by gnomish standards, barely three feet tall, with sharp features, silver-white hair cut precisely at the jawline, and eyes of pale grey that seem to evaluate everything they rest on. Her fingers are long, thin, and extraordinarily steady, the hands of a surgeon applied to stone and metal. She wears a jeweler's loupe on a chain around her neck and magnifying lenses on a brass headband that she pushes up onto her forehead when speaking to people, as though the world at normal magnification is slightly disappointing. Her clothing is expensive, understated, and immaculate.

## Personality

Polara is brilliant, exacting, and has a tolerance for incompetence that could be measured with her own precision instruments and found to be zero. She does not suffer fools, she does not suffer the moderately talented, and she barely suffers the genuinely skilled. Her standards are impossible and non-negotiable. Those who meet them earn her respect, which she expresses through marginally less withering commentary. Beneath the acerbic exterior is a mind of genuine wonder, a person who chose gem-cutting because she finds the internal geometry of crystals more beautiful than anything else in the world. She is also deeply worried, and deeply alone with that worry, because the things she has seen in her Empyrean Shard experiments have shaken a woman who believed she was beyond being shaken.

## Backstory

Polara came to [[Anvilrest]] at twenty from a gnomish community in [[The Verdant Marches]], drawn by the Conclave's reputation as the finest precision-craft institution in Aethermourne. She was talented, driven, and socially unbearable. Her masters alternated between wanting to promote her and wanting to expel her. Her masterwork, a focusing crystal of such geometric perfection that it briefly focused ambient magical energy into visible light without any external input, settled the question. She was the youngest Master in the Conclave's history and has spent the intervening decades ensuring no one forgets it.

She became Master of the Conclave fifteen years ago after her predecessor retired, citing "the desire to enjoy my remaining years in peace, preferably out of earshot." Under Polara's leadership, the Conclave has produced its finest work in generations, and the waiting list for Anvilrest-certified instruments stretches years into the future.

Her experiments with Empyrean Shards began as an academic exercise, applying gem-cutting techniques to divine material to see what would happen. What happened exceeded her expectations and her comfort. The cut facets seem to function as lenses for something she does not understand, showing glimpses of events that have not yet occurred. She has seen the Grand Forge going dark. She has seen stars winking out one by one. She has seen a figure of shadow reaching through cracked stone.

## Relationships

- [[Brunhild Ironheart|Forge-Matriarch Brunhild Ironheart]], A relationship of mutual, slightly prickly respect. Polara finds the Forge-Matriarch's governance style heavy-handed; Brunhild finds Polara's autonomy inconvenient. They agree on the things that matter and argue about everything else.
- [[Torren Hearthforge|Guildmaster Torren Hearthforge]], He is one of the few people whose standards Polara considers comparable to her own. She would never say so to his face. He would probably nod once and return to his work.
- **Assessor Thimble Gask**, Her protégé and the only person Polara trusts to grade work in her absence. Thimble is the sole person who has seen the Empyrean Shard experiments and the visions they produce.
- [[The Star-Readers|The Star-Readers]], She has professional relationships with several Star-Readers who use her focusing crystals. Their recent agitation about the Star Fade resonates uncomfortably with what she has seen in the Shards.

## What They Want

Polara wants to understand what the Empyrean Shards are showing her. She wants confirmation that the visions are prophecy rather than madness, and she wants someone capable of interpreting them, because her skill is in cutting and seeing, not in understanding what the cuts reveal. Beneath everything, she wants to be wrong about what she has seen, the dying forge, the dark stars, the shadow in the stone.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Information source and potential crafting ally. Her Empyrean Shard visions provide fragmentary but genuine glimpses of the campaign's endgame. If the PCs help her refine her technique, the Shards could become a powerful divination tool, providing cryptic but actionable intelligence about the Second Silence.
>
> **Key Secret.** The figure of shadow she saw reaching through cracked stone is [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], though Polara does not know this. The vision shows Serith's hand emerging from her prison, reaching toward something that burns, the Deep Forge. If a Shard-vision can be combined with [[Corrin Ashveil|Corrin Ashveil's]] historical analysis, the resulting picture would be the most complete intelligence anyone has on Serith's plan.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs engage with her experiments, she becomes a valuable source of fragmentary prophecy and an exceptional crafter of magical instruments. If left alone, she becomes increasingly isolated and paranoid, eventually destroying the Shards rather than facing what they show her.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in clipped, precise sentences. Evaluates people's hands first, faces second. Frequently adjusts her lenses. Delivers devastating assessments of mediocre work with the same tone she uses to order lunch. When genuinely unsettled, she becomes quieter rather than louder, her silence more alarming than her criticism.
>
> *Sample dialogue:* "I cut the Shard to sixty-four facets. Standard brilliant cut, adapted for non-crystalline divine substrate. I expected refraction anomalies. I did not expect to see the end of the world. The cut was flawless, so the problem is not my technique."
