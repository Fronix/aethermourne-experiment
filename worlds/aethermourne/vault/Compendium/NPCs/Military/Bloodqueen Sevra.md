## Appearance

Sevra moves like a weapon deciding where to land. She is tall, lean, and precise in a way that has moved past athleticism into something mechanical. Her body is a catalogue of combat, scarred densely enough that the marks overlap and form patterns, iron-grey whorls that trace the history of ten thousand kills across her arms, torso, and face. Her hair is cropped to stubble, iron-colored, and her eyes have gone the flat grey of a spent blade. She wears minimal armor, strips of black iron bound at the joints, because she learned centuries ago that mobility matters more than protection when you cannot permanently die. The crown she earned, a circlet of fused weapon fragments from her most significant kills, sits on her brow like something that grew there.

## Personality

Cold, controlled, and utterly certain. Sevra has moved beyond rage, beyond ambition, beyond the hunger that drives most Crucible combatants. What remains is a clinical mastery of violence so refined that it has become aesthetic. She fights the way a master calligrapher writes, with economy, intention, and an awareness of form that transcends function. She is not cruel. Cruelty requires emotional engagement. She is efficient.

Beneath the ice is an absence that Sevra has learned to mistake for peace. She was mortal once. She had a life, a name, a history, connections to people and places that meant something. All of it is gone, worn away by the Proving Grounds' erosion cycle over what might be millennia. She does not miss what she has lost because she cannot remember having it. She is, in the deepest sense, complete, and the completeness is a cage she cannot see the bars of.

## Backstory

Sevra arrived in the Proving Grounds carrying a name that the domain took at the threshold, as it takes everyone's. She earned a new name in her first arena match and has not lost since. Ten thousand kills. The number is verified by [[The Registrar]], annotated in the Record Hall's oldest active ledger, spanning entries that stretch back further than most souls on Level 2 have existed. Her current name, "Bloodqueen," is not a name she chose. It accrued, assembled from the titles and epithets of the warriors she defeated, compressing into a single word that carries the weight of every victory.

She maintains her own barracks, a section of the Crucible that has been claimed through the simple physics of reputation. Other combatants gravitate to her not out of loyalty but out of survival instinct. Being near Sevra means being on the winning side. Her followers are not an army. They are a constituency, bound by the practical calculus that the Bloodqueen's patronage is the safest position in a place defined by violence.

The name she carries allegedly predates the Theomachis. This claim appears in the Registrar's records but cannot be verified because the oldest entries are written in a language that only the Registrar can read. If true, it means the name was first earned when [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] still lived, when combat in the Proving Grounds had divine purpose. Sevra carries a relic of the domain's original function without knowing what it means.

## Relationships

- [[Warlord Keth]], the only entity in the Crucible that Sevra respects without qualification. They have never fought. Both understand that the fight would end the Crucible's power structure regardless of outcome, and neither is willing to destroy the only order that exists.
- [[The Registrar]], who maintains Sevra's record with a meticulousness that borders on reverence. Sevra has no opinion about the Registrar, which the Registrar finds statistically unusual.
- [[Mira Ashborn]], a mortal soldier whose persistence interests Sevra. She has offered mentorship, genuinely, because Mira's refusal to be eroded reminds Sevra of something she cannot quite recall.
- [[Pit-Master Grenn]], who manages Sevra's arena appearances with the care of a curator arranging a gallery exhibition. Sevra fights rarely now. Each appearance is an event.
- [[The Peacemaker]], the only entity Sevra has never fought. Not because she cannot, but because she tried once, raised her weapon, and found that she could not bring herself to strike. She has never mentioned this to anyone.

## What They Want

Sevra wants a fight that matters. Ten thousand kills and none of them have changed anything. The arena resets, the names circulate, the cycle continues. She is the Proving Grounds' greatest success and its most elegant proof of futility. What she cannot articulate, because the memory is too eroded to surface, is that she once fought for reasons beyond fighting, and the ghost of that purpose haunts her like a word on the tip of the tongue.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Sevra is a major obstacle or powerful ally depending on how the party approaches her. Her challenge to take a PC's name is a pivotal encounter. Defeating her earns access to the Descent Stair without Keth's permission and establishes the party's reputation across the Crucible.
>
> **Key Secret.** The name Sevra carries does predate the Theomachis. It was given by [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] himself to his finest warrior as a mark of divine favor. The name carries a fragment of Kaevroth's war-essence, which is the true source of Sevra's unbroken streak. She is not just skilled. She is partially divine, a mortal frame running on the last fumes of a dead god's approval.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If a PC defeats Sevra in arena combat, the name transfers, and with it the fragment of war-essence. The PC becomes marked by Kaevroth's power. If the party instead helps Sevra remember her mortal life, the name's hold on her breaks, and she becomes something the Proving Grounds has never seen: a warrior who chooses to stop fighting.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Economy of language. Never says three words when one will do. Stands perfectly still when not in motion. Does not blink at a natural rate. Tilts her head slightly when assessing a potential opponent, like a blade being angled for the cut. Sample line: *"Ten thousand. Each one thought they were different. Stance tells me you think so too. Prove it."*
