## Appearance

A massive man, six and a half feet tall, with the build of someone who has carried heavy armor for decades. His skin is weathered and dark, his head shaved, and his face would be unremarkable except for his eyes: they are iron-grey, fully, with no distinction between iris and pupil, and they do not blink at a normal rate. He wears battered plate armor painted matte black, carries a greatsword of [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] that most men could not lift one-handed, and moves with the unnerving silence that gives his company its name. He has not spoken a word in seven years. He communicates through gestures, written notes, and a system of hand signals his lieutenants have learned to read fluently. His presence fills a room the way cold fills a cellar: gradually, completely, and without permission.

## Personality

Before his silence, Harsk was reportedly a loud, profane, gregarious man who told terrible jokes and laughed at his own stories. That man is gone. What remains is watchful, deliberate, and impossible to read. He is not hostile, not aggressive, not unkind. He is simply absent in some fundamental way, as if part of him left when the words did and what stayed behind is running on discipline alone. His company follows him with near-religious devotion, not because he inspires them but because they have seen what happens when he draws his sword, and the silence becomes something else entirely, focused, purposeful, and terrifying.

## Backstory

Harsk was a career mercenary, one of the best, a captain at twenty-five and a legend by thirty. He led the Silent Company (then called the Iron Wolves) through a dozen successful campaigns across the continent. Seven years ago, during a contract in the Shardfall, he touched a [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] fragment bare-handed. Accounts differ on why. Some say it was a dare. Some say the fragment called to him. Some say he tripped and fell against it. What is consistent is what happened next: he stood motionless for three hours, eyes wide, mouth open in a silent scream, and when he finally moved again, he did not speak. He has never explained why. The company renamed itself in his honor, or perhaps in recognition of what he became.

What no one knows is what he saw during those three hours. Harsk experienced the Theomachis from the inside, [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] last moments, the god consuming fragments of his fallen siblings, growing vast and terrible, and then the combined weight of the surviving gods bearing down on him, shattering him, scattering him across the continent in a rain of iron and fury. Harsk lived through a god's death. He felt the rage, the hunger, the betrayal, and the final, terrible realization that the war had no winners. He stopped speaking because no words in any language could contain what he knows, and he is afraid that if he opens his mouth, what comes out will not be words at all.

## Relationships

- [[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal]], one of the few people Harsk trusts. Their relationship is transactional, contracts, obligations, and the quiet respect of professionals, but there is warmth beneath it that neither acknowledges.
- [[Mira the Red]], who has offered Harsk command of her entire army. He has not responded. His lieutenants believe he is considering it. They are wrong. He is watching her the way one watches a fire spreading toward a building.
- [[Yrsa Bloodhand]], who respects Harsk as a peer and fears what he might do if the fragment's influence deepens. She has instructed her agents to keep him under observation.
- **Lieutenant Sera**, his second-in-command, a sharp-featured woman who interprets his gestures for outsiders and manages the Silent Company's day-to-day operations. She is fiercely protective of him and increasingly worried.

## What They Want

On the surface, Harsk continues to function as a mercenary captain, taking contracts, leading campaigns, fulfilling obligations. But his deeper drive is something he cannot articulate even to himself: he is searching for a way to put down what he picked up. The fragment's memory is inside him, a divine recording of war and death that loops endlessly. He wants silence, true silence, the kind he had before the fragment filled his head with a dead god's final screams. He does not know if such silence exists.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Wild card in Acts II-III. Harsk is the most dangerous individual combatant in the Ironmarch, and the Silent Company is the most effective fighting force. Where he commits his sword tips the balance of power.
>
> **Key Secret.** Harsk is carrying a fragment of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] consciousness in his mind. Not the full god, not anything close, but a shard of divine memory that replays the Theomachis in an endless loop. This makes him immune to further [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] contamination (he is already saturated) but also means he is, in a sense, a living [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] fragment. The other fragments are faintly aware of him. When he enters a room with [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]], the metal resonates.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs help him find a way to release or contain the divine memory, Harsk becomes a powerful ally with unique insight into [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] nature and the fragment migration. If they do not, the memory will eventually overwhelm him. He will speak, and what comes out will be [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] voice, a war-cry that drives everyone who hears it into berserker frenzy. The Silent Company becomes a weapon of mass destruction.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Never speaks. Communicates through gesture, expression, and written notes (short, direct phrases, no flourishes). His silence is not peaceful, it has weight, like the silence before an avalanche. When he draws his sword, all ambient noise seems to deaden. Describe his physical presence constantly: the way floorboards creak under his weight, the way people unconsciously step back.
> *Written on a scrap of parchment, passed to a PC: "The iron remembers. I remember. Do not touch what I touched."*
