# Mirror-Warden Caelus

|---|---|

| **Location** | The Promenade of Glories, Gloryholme |
| **Faction** | [[The Iron Descent]] |

## Appearance

Caelus is unremarkable in the way that professional functionaries often are: average height, average build, features that refuse to stick in memory. His face is composed, symmetrical, and utterly forgettable, which is a remarkable achievement on a level where every surface is a mirror. He wears a uniform of dark iron-cloth, crisply maintained, with a ring of small mirror-keys hanging from his belt that chime softly when he walks. His hands are the most notable thing about him: they are covered in fine, silvery scars, thousands of tiny cuts from centuries of handling mirror-edges, and they move with the practiced surety of someone who works with sharp things daily. His eyes are grey and clear and give away nothing. He is the only entity on Level 5 whose reflection in the mirrors shows exactly what is standing in front of them, a fact he has never remarked upon.

## Personality

Caelus is professional, courteous, and emotionally impenetrable. He treats his work, maintaining the mirrors, processing new arrivals, trapping and freeing reflections, with the detached competence of a bureaucrat who has been doing the same job for centuries and sees no reason to feel anything about it. He is polite to residents, deferential to [[Sovereign Kallis]], and gentle with newcomers, guiding them into their first mirror-vision with the practiced warmth of a nurse administering anesthesia. He is not cruel. He is thorough. He takes pride in efficiency and craftsmanship, and he considers the smooth absorption of a new soul into Gloryholme's collective delusion to be a job well done. He has convinced himself that what he does is kindness, that the mirrors offer comfort, that the alternative is the raw, ugly truth of the Iron Descent, and that comfort is better. He has been wrong about this for a very long time, and he has been choosing not to examine the wrongness for just as long.

## Backstory

In life, Caelus was a jailer in the mortal world, a prison warden of competence and quiet cruelty, not the dramatic cruelty of a torturer but the administrative cruelty of a man who processed suffering as paperwork. He ran his prison efficiently, kept his inmates fed and clean, and never once considered whether the system he served was just. He died of old age, comfortable and well-regarded, and [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s domain claimed him for the sin of ambition applied to control, the desire to manage others' fates without ever questioning the authority to do so.

On Level 5, the mirrors tried to give Caelus his heart's desire: a prison where every inmate was happy, where no one complained, where the system worked perfectly. And then the mirrors realized they had already given him exactly that. Gloryholme was the prison. The residents were the inmates. And Caelus was the warden. The mirrors did not need to create an illusion for him because reality, on Level 5, was already his fantasy. He was bound to the Hall of Mirrors not by a vision of false glory but by the simple, comfortable truth that he was doing the same job he had always done, and doing it well. He is the only resident of Gloryholme who is not deluded, and the only one who has chosen his prison freely.

## Relationships

- [[Sovereign Kallis]], the ruler of Gloryholme. Caelus serves Kallis with genuine loyalty, not because he believes her delusion but because her authority provides the structure his work requires. A warden needs a warden-ship, and Kallis's court is his.
- [[Lord Parvane]], the escaped reflection. Caelus knows Parvane is a reflection and could theoretically trap him back in a mirror. He does not, because Parvane is useful, stable, and does not disrupt the system. Pragmatism outweighs protocol.
- [[The Truthsayer]], the blind prophet. Caelus leaves the Truthsayer alone, respecting her self-imposed exile to the unmirrored cell. He considers her situation regrettable but administratively tidy: she has found her place and stays in it.
- [[The Shattered Man]], the fragmented soul. Caelus monitors the Shattered Man's seven fragments with professional interest, tracking their stability the way an engineer monitors stress fractures. He considers them a stable system and will intervene only if a fragment begins to fail.
- [[Dreamer Ysolde]], the consumed dreamer. Caelus maintains Ysolde's chamber with particular care, considering her the Hall's most successful integration. He calls her his finest work. This is the most honest thing he has ever been wrong about.

## What They Want

Caelus wants his prison to run smoothly. He wants new arrivals processed, reflections maintained, the mirrors polished, and the system functioning. He wants to be good at his job, which he is, and to be recognized for his competence, which he is not, because no one on Level 5 sees him clearly enough to recognize anything. What truly drives him is a need he will never admit: he wants someone to tell him that what he does is right. Not the mirrors, which confirm everything, but a voice from outside the system, a judgment he cannot dismiss as reflection.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Caelus is the gatekeeper of Level 5, the one who controls access to and from the mirrors. He can trap the party in mirror-visions or free them, and his cooperation is essential for navigating Gloryholme safely. He is also the most dangerous NPC on the level, because his danger is administrative rather than dramatic: he will process you with a smile and genuine concern for your comfort.
>
> **Key Secret.** Caelus can distinguish real from reflection with absolute certainty because the mirrors have no hold on him. He is not deluded. He sees Gloryholme as it truly is, the tattered souls, the iron walls, the endless recursions of false glory, and he has chosen to maintain it anyway. This makes him either the sanest person on Level 5 or the most damned, depending on your perspective. He also knows about the escaped reflections that [[Lord Parvane]] has been tracking, and he has been allowing them to proliferate because they do not disrupt the system. He does not realize that their increasing numbers signal the Fracture's spread.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party confronts him with the moral weight of what he does, he will either break (admitting that his "kindness" is cruelty) or double down (arguing that the alternative is worse). If he breaks, he becomes an ally capable of dismantling Gloryholme's mirror-infrastructure from within. If he doubles down, he becomes an antagonist who will use every tool at his disposal to protect the system he has built his identity around. If the Fracture reaches Gloryholme's core, Caelus will be the one who tries to hold the mirrors together, and his failure will be the most honest thing that has ever happened to him.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks evenly, pleasantly, with the measured cadence of someone who has said the same words a thousand times and means them exactly as much as the first time. Never raises his voice. Smiles with his mouth but not his eyes. His mirror-keys chime faintly whenever he moves, a sound the other residents have learned to associate with safety.
> *"Welcome to Gloryholme. You look tired. That is understandable, the journey is long. Let me show you to a chamber where you can rest, and the mirrors will show you something worth seeing. Everyone finds something worth seeing. That is the beauty of this place."*
