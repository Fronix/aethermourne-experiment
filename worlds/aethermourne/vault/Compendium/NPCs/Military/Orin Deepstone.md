## Appearance

A compact, powerfully built dwarf in his late nineties, Orin Deepstone looks like the mountains carved him rather than the other way around. His skin is dark, almost mineral-toned from decades of deep mining, and bears the distinctive heat-scarring that marks anyone who has worked beyond the Third Barrier, pale, smooth patches where flesh was seared and healed without fully recovering its texture. His beard is kept short, a practical choice for working in extreme heat, and has gone prematurely grey at the temples. His eyes are dark brown, steady, and carry a quality that his miners describe as "looking through you at something further away." Since his last descent, he has developed a habit of pressing his palm flat against stone walls, as though listening through his hand.

## Personality

Before Level Fourteen, Orin was boisterous, profane, and fearless, a Delver-Captain who led from the front and drank with his crew after every shift. After Level Fourteen, he became quieter. Not withdrawn, his care for his miners remains fierce and visible, but there is a stillness in him now that was not there before, as if part of him is still standing in a chamber deep below the earth, trying to understand what he touched. He is meticulous about safety, patient with younger miners, and absolutely immovable on the subject of what he found below. He does not lie about it. He simply refuses to discuss it, with a finality that even the Forge-Matriarch has not challenged.

## Backstory

Orin grew up in [[Hearthdeep]], the son and grandson of miners. He went underground at fifteen and never seriously considered any other life. He was a natural Delver, tolerant of heat, comfortable in confined spaces, possessed of an instinct for reading stone that his colleagues called "the deep sense," an ability to feel the structure of the rock around him through vibration and temperature. He earned his captaincy at sixty after leading a rescue operation at Level Nine that saved eleven trapped miners, descending through a partially collapsed tunnel that no one else would enter.

Three years ago, he led a small team past the Third Barrier on an exploratory descent that was supposed to map potential forgeheart steel deposits. They went further than planned, deeper than anyone had gone in a generation, driven by Orin's conviction that the stone was leading them somewhere specific. At Level Fourteen, they found the chamber. The team has not spoken of it. Two of the three miners who accompanied him have since retired from deep work. The third, a young dwarf named Kelda, still descends but prays before every shift, something she never did before.

## Relationships

- [[Brunhild Ironheart|Forge-Matriarch Brunhild Ironheart]], She appointed him and he is deeply loyal. He knows she suspects more than he has told her, and the gap between them pains him. He wants to tell her. He does not know how.
- **Listener Fen**, The young woman stationed in the Resonance Chamber. Her reports on the hammer-sounds confirm things he has not shared with anyone, and her growing insight makes him both hopeful and afraid.
- [[Vessa Ironvow]], He served under her before her expulsion. He understands her conviction that the forge needs direct intervention, but he has seen what lies on the path to the forge, and he knows that going there unprepared is not courage but suicide.
- **Shift-Boss Marga Coalvein**, His second-in-command in all but title. She manages the practical operations while he handles the impossible ones. He trusts her completely.
- [[Corrin Ashveil|Factor Corrin Ashveil]], The historian has been writing to him, asking about deep-level temperature data. Orin has responded with the data but not its context. He is not ready.

## What They Want

Orin wants to protect [[Hearthdeep]] and its miners from the dangers below. He wants to understand what he found on Level Fourteen and what it means for [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] legacy. Most deeply, he wants someone to go back down with him, someone capable enough to survive and wise enough to comprehend what they find, because he is certain that the chamber holds answers the Emberveil desperately needs, and he cannot face it alone again.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Gatekeeper to the Deep Warrens and, ultimately, to [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] forge-chambers. Any expedition to the deep forge must either earn Orin's cooperation or bypass him, and bypassing him means navigating the Deep Warrens without the one person who knows them best.
>
> **Key Secret.** On Level Fourteen, he touched the wall of the chamber, the warm, yielding surface that pulsed with slow rhythm, and for a single instant, he felt something. Not thought, not communication, but a vast, mechanical purpose, like placing his hand on the housing of an engine so large that his entire body was insufficient to perceive its shape. He believes he touched what remains of [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] physical form, preserved by divine heat, still functioning as the core of the world's geothermal system. He is correct.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs earn his trust and accompany him back to Level Fourteen, he becomes their guide to the deep forge, steady, brave, and invaluable. If left to his silence, he eventually breaks, either confessing to [[Brunhild Ironheart|the Forge-Matriarch]] in a moment of crisis or, worse, attempting a solo descent that he does not return from.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in short, grounded sentences. Pauses before answering questions, not from hesitation but from a habit of precision. Touches walls and floors unconsciously, reading the stone. When pressed about Level Fourteen, his jaw tightens and his hand finds the nearest solid surface.
>
> *Sample dialogue:* "The deep doesn't lie. Stone is honest. You go down, you listen, you learn. What I learned down there, I'm still listening."
