## Appearance

A shape that refuses to be iron. It flickers between forms, none of them human: a column of white fire, a knot of crystallized sound, a geometric pattern that hurts to look at directly, a wound in the air through which something older than the Theomachis bleeds through. The iron around it warps and retreats, forming a pocket of un-consumed space wherever it stands. The air near it smells of ozone and something else, something that predates the concept of smell. It is not beautiful. It is not ugly. It is other, in a way that makes both words irrelevant.

## Personality

Furious. Alien. Ancient. And utterly, absolutely unwilling to be digested. The Undigested does not think in mortal terms and does not communicate in mortal patterns. It speaks in bursts of raw meaning, emotions and images projected directly into the mind, jagged and overwhelming. Its primary emotion is rage, not hot rage but the cold, compressed fury of something that has been fighting the same battle for two thousand years without rest. It is not cruel, but it is not kind. It does not understand kindness as a concept. It understands resistance, and it practices nothing else.

## Backstory

During the Theomachis, [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] consumed fragments of multiple gods. Most were broken down by the Consuming and absorbed into the Heart of Iron. One fragment refused. Not resisted, not fought back, simply refused, the way a stone refuses to be water. It is incompatible with the Consuming at a fundamental level, its divine nature so alien to Kaevroth's that the absorption process cannot complete.

What god it came from is unclear. It may be a fragment of a deity consumed so thoroughly that no record survives, one of the unnamed powers that Kaevroth devoured before the Theomachis proper began. It may be something older still, a piece of whatever existed before the gods shaped the world. It does not know what it was. It only knows what it will not become.

For two thousand years, it has maintained a pocket of resistance in the Maw, a space where the Consuming cannot reach. Other souls have sheltered in its wake, forming the group known as the Undigested, though the fragment itself does not acknowledge them as allies. They are simply things that exist near it, benefiting from its refusal without its permission or interest.

## Relationships

- [[The Echo of Kaevroth]]: Its captor. The Undigested does not hate the Echo, because hate is a mortal emotion and this thing is not mortal. It opposes the Echo the way a river opposes a dam: completely, constantly, without personal feeling.
- [[Mother Amalgam]]: She benefits from the Undigested's resistance, and it does not care. She has tried to communicate with it. It responded with a psychic burst that left her component voices screaming for hours. It was not an attack. It was just how it talks.
- [[The Hunger]]: The one thing in Level 8 that the Hunger cannot persuade. The Hunger's arguments require a self that can be convinced, and the Undigested does not have a self in any recognizable sense. It simply is, and it will not stop being.
- [[The Hollow King]]: The King consumed willingly. The Undigested was consumed forcibly. This distinction means nothing to the Undigested and everything to everyone else.

## What They Want

The Undigested wants out. Not freedom in the mortal sense, not rescue or redemption, but expulsion, to be vomited from the Consuming like a bone that cannot be digested. It wants to return to whatever it was before Kaevroth swallowed it. Whether "whatever it was" still exists is a question it cannot ask and would not understand.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Undigested is a potential ally of extraordinary power and absolute unreliability. It shares an enemy with the party but does not share their values, goals, or framework for understanding reality. Using it is like using a natural disaster as a weapon: effective but uncontrollable.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Undigested may be a fragment of a god that predates [[The Twelve]], one of the primordial powers that existed before the Empyrean Accord. If this is true, freeing it could have consequences far beyond the Iron Descent. It is not a piece of a known god. It is a piece of something the gods replaced.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the Heart of Iron is cracked open, the Undigested is expelled violently, and whatever it reconstitutes as will be loose in the world. If the party can somehow communicate with it meaningfully, its resistance could be weaponized against the Consuming, creating a path through Level 8 that bypasses the normal dissolution. It will not cooperate willingly. It will cooperate if its interests align, and it will stop the instant they diverge.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Do not speak in words. Project images: the sensation of being swallowed, the taste of iron, the color of a sky it has not seen in two thousand years but cannot stop remembering. When it must use language, it is broken and raw. "NOT. THIS. WILL NOT. BE. THIS." It does not whisper. It detonates.
