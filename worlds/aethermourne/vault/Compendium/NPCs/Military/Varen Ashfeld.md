# Flame Captain Varen Ashfeld

## Appearance

A broad-shouldered man in his early forties with iron-grey hair cropped to stubble, a square jaw, and pale blue eyes that assess every room as a battlefield. His skin bears the faint grey tinge of prolonged [[Cosmology and Magic#Ashite|Ashite]] exposure, and his hands are scarred from decades of blade-drill and combat. He wears the Ashguard's heavy plate armor even in Council chambers, the breastplate marked with overlapping burn-circles from hundreds of Flame Blessings. When he moves, the armor rings like a bell. He smells of forge-smoke, weapon oil, and the faint mineral tang of Ashite dust. His voice is parade-ground loud even when he is not trying to be heard.

## Personality

First impression: disciplined, confident, intimidating. Ashfeld is a soldier's soldier, respected by his troops for leading from the front and demanding no more from them than he demands from himself. He speaks in short, decisive sentences, treats problems as tactical puzzles, and has no patience for political equivocation. Beneath the command presence is a man who genuinely believes the Dominion is under existential threat and that hesitation will doom them all. He is not cruel, he is not power-hungry, but he is absolutely certain that the [[Marchwardens Council|Verdant Marches]] must be conquered before they unite into a rival power. This certainty makes him dangerous.

## Backstory

Varen rose through the [[Order of the Ashen Flame|Order's]] ranks on merit and ruthlessness, starting as an Ember at seventeen and earning his Flame Captain rank at thirty-two, the youngest in a generation. His first command was a border patrol that intercepted a Marcher raiding party deep in Dominion territory. He killed six raiders personally and captured the rest for interrogation. The [[Hierarch Valdren III|Hierarch]] personally commended him.

He took command of the Ashguard seven years ago, tasked with transforming the Dominion's elite heavy infantry from a ceremonial garrison force into an offensive weapon. Under his leadership, the Ashguard has doubled in size, drills daily in siege tactics and forced marches, and studies Marcher guerrilla patterns obsessively. Ashfeld believes defensive posture is a slow death. He has presented [[Ashara Voss|Commander Voss]] with three variants of an invasion plan, each more aggressive than the last.

His personal conviction was shaped by a single event: ten years ago, he led a rescue mission into the Scarwall to recover an Order patrol trapped by living vines. He found them alive but barely recognizable, skin turning to bark, roots growing through their legs, eyes green-gold and distant. The Marcher scouts who found them first had left them to transform rather than grant a clean death. Ashfeld executed all six to spare them. He has never forgiven the Marches for what he calls "Thyrea's cruelty," and he will not rest while the Scarwall stands.

## Relationships

- **[[Ashara Voss]]**, his Commander. Ashfeld reveres Voss's tactical brilliance and shares her belief that preemptive action is necessary. He is her most loyal captain and the one most likely to act on her orders without question.
- **[[Sera Ironholt]]**, his rival. Ironholt's advocacy for diplomacy infuriates him. He considers her northern focus a distraction from the real threat, and their clashes in War Council meetings have become legendary.
- **[[Aldren Voss]]**, a disappointment. Ashfeld considers Flame Captain Voss's sympathy for the [[Unbound Congregation]] a betrayal of the Order's principles and has recommended his reassignment to a remote garrison.
- **[[Kaelith Thornborn]]**, the enemy. Ashfeld studies every report on Thornborn's movements, sees the unification movement as confirmation of his worst fears, and has marked Thornborn as the primary strategic target in his invasion plans.
- **[[Delric Mourne]]**, cautious ally. Ashfeld trusts [[The Cinders|the Cinders]] to provide intelligence on Marcher movements but dislikes Mourne's preference for subtlety over direct action.

## What They Want

Ashfeld wants to launch a preemptive strike against the Verdant Marches before [[Kaelith Thornborn|Thornborn]] unifies the clans, crush the Marcher threat permanently, and secure the Dominion's western border. His deeper need is vindication for the six soldiers he executed in the Scarwall, proof that their deaths were not in vain, that the Marches are as dangerous as he believes, and that only total military dominance can prevent another patrol from suffering their fate. If the Order does not act and the Marches unite, Ashfeld will never forgive himself for failing to push harder.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Primary military antagonist for the Verdant Marches storyline in Acts II-III. Ashfeld is the face of Dominion aggression, the officer most likely to escalate border tensions into open war. He is competent, principled, and utterly wrong about the strategic necessity of his position.
>
> **Key Secret.** Ashfeld's invasion plans are being quietly encouraged by [[Delric Mourne|Mourne]] and [[The Cinders]], who want the Dominion and Marches fighting each other to distract from [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] operations. Ashfeld does not know he is being manipulated. If he discovers the Cinders' betrayal, his loyalty will shift violently.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** Begins as an obstacle PCs must navigate or neutralize. If convinced the Marches are not the primary threat (evidence of Serith's influence, Cinders' betrayal, or a greater danger from the north), he can become a powerful ally. If not, he will lead the Ashguard into a disastrous invasion that weakens both regions and serves Serith's goals.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Parade-ground voice, even indoors. Speaks in tactical terms, every conversation framed as a problem to solve. Interrupts flowery speech with direct questions. Stands at parade rest when not actively moving. Sample line: *"You can negotiate with the Marches if you want. I'll be training the men who will march through Roothold when your negotiations fail."*
