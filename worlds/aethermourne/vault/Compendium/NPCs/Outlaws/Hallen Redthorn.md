# Hallen Redthorn

## Appearance

A thin man in his late twenties with nervous energy, pale skin unusual for a Marcher, and red-brown hair worn in the traditional clan braids. His clothes are too fine for his station, Dominion-style wool and linen that mark him as someone trying to appear more important than he is. His hands are ink-stained from constant note-taking, and he carries a leather satchel stuffed with reports, maps, and correspondence that he guards obsessively. He smells of parchment, cheap wine, and anxiety. His eyes dart constantly, tracking who is speaking to whom, who is leaving which meeting, who is angry and who is satisfied. He moves like a man who knows he does not belong and is desperate to prove his value.

## Personality

First impression: eager, obsequious, slightly untrustworthy. Hallen is the kind of person who ingratiates himself through usefulness, offering intelligence, running errands, delivering messages others cannot be bothered with. He speaks quickly, often interrupting himself to add details he thinks are important. Beneath the eagerness is a man profoundly insecure about his clan's minor status and desperate to matter, to be taken seriously, to prove that a Redthorn can shape events as much as a Thornborn or an Ironbark. This desperation makes him the perfect asset for someone who knows how to exploit it.

## Backstory

Hallen is the youngest son of the Redthorn sept's elder, a minor clan with poor land and poorer prospects. He grew up in the shadow of the major clans, attending Moot sessions where his family had no vote and no voice. When he was twenty-three, he was selected as the Redthorn observer to the Council, a ceremonial role that gave him access to the Moot-Hall but no power.

Two years ago, a Dominion merchant approached him in a [[Roothold]] tavern, introduced himself as a concerned citizen of both regions, and offered to pay for accurate intelligence on Council proceedings. The money was good, the requests were reasonable, and Hallen convinced himself he was serving peace by helping the Dominion understand the Marches better. Over time, the requests shifted, the merchant wanted predictions of how Marchwardens would vote, details on private meetings at the Lakehouse, intelligence on factional disputes. Hallen provided it all.

What he does not know: the merchant is a [[The Cinders|Cinder]] agent operating under [[Delric Mourne|Mourne's]] direction, and the intelligence Hallen receives in return, fabricated reports of Ironbark's secret Dominion negotiations, forged letters suggesting Thornborn is planning to seize power by force, is carefully designed to deepen the Council's divisions and push both factions toward conflict.

## Relationships

- **[[Kaelith Thornborn]]**, target. Hallen has passed intelligence to Thornborn's coalition "warning" them of Ironbark's secret Dominion contacts. Thornborn's inner circle considers Hallen a useful source but does not fully trust him.
- **[[Draven Ironbark]]**, target. Hallen has passed intelligence to Ironbark's opposition "warning" them that Thornborn plans to declare herself High Warden regardless of the Council's vote. Ironbark considers Hallen an annoying but occasionally useful informant.
- **[[Seren Ashwood]]**, growing problem. The Moot-Chair has noticed inconsistencies in Hallen's reports and has begun asking pointed questions about his sources. Hallen is increasingly nervous around her.
- **[[Delric Mourne]]**, unknown handler. Hallen has never met Mourne and does not know his merchant contact answers to the Cinders. He believes he is working for a Dominion peace faction.
- **[[Brannoc Stillwater]]**, occasional contact. Hallen sometimes delivers messages for Stillwater in exchange for access to Lakehouse meetings. Stillwater considers him harmless.

## What They Want

Hallen wants to be important, to be the person who prevents a war through his careful intelligence work, to prove that a minor clan observer can shape history as much as any Marchwarden. His deeper need is simpler: he wants his family to be proud of him, to matter to someone, to escape the crushing insignificance of being a Redthorn in a world run by Thornborns and Ironbarks. He does not realize that he is being used to start the war he thinks he is preventing.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Unwitting [[The Cinders|Cinder]] operative spreading disinformation within the [[Marchwardens Council]]. Exposing him is a key step in revealing [[Delric Mourne|Mourne's]] broader manipulation of Marcher politics.
>
> **Key Secret.** All of Hallen's "intelligence" on Ironbark and Thornborn comes from his Cinder handler. The fabricated reports are designed to confirm each faction's worst fears about the other. Hallen genuinely believes he is serving peace and has no idea he is deepening the rift. If confronted with proof of the forgeries, he will be devastated and potentially turned against his handlers.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If PCs investigate Council intelligence leaks, Hallen is the obvious weak link. Confronting him with evidence of the forgeries forces a choice: continue serving his handlers out of fear, or cooperate with the PCs and expose the Cinder operation. If he turns, he becomes a source on Cinder methods and possibly a conduit for feeding false intelligence back to Mourne.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks quickly, interrupts himself, over-explains. Nervous laughter. Fidgets with his satchel, adjusts his too-fine clothes. Drops names to establish credibility ("I was just speaking with Marchwarden Ironbark..."). Sample line: *"I have it on good authority, very good authority, that Ironbark met with a Dominion envoy last month. No, I can't reveal my source, but the intelligence is solid, I've cross-referenced it with, well, I can't say, but it's solid."*
