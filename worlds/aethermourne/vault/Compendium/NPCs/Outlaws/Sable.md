## Appearance

Thirties, lean, dark-skinned, with close-cropped hair and a navigator's calluses on her hands. A thin scar runs along her jawline from a rigging accident in a past life. She dresses like every other [[Driftmere]] chandler: practical clothes, salt-stained, unremarkable. Her hands are calloused and she moves like a dock worker, not a spy. She smells of salt and tar. A scar crosses her left palm from a net-knife. The only tell is her eyes, too steady, too watchful, and in dim light they catch the light wrong, reflecting it back with a faint luminescence. She hides this behind tinted lenses and avoids well-lit rooms when she can.

## Personality

Calm, precise, and utterly committed. Sable speaks with the clipped efficiency of someone trained to relay navigational data under pressure, every word measured, nothing wasted. She gives the impression of a woman who has already made every important decision and is simply executing. Beneath the control lies a core of cold anger directed at the institutions that declared her dead and never searched for her. She does not raise her voice. She does not need to.

## Backstory

Before she was Sable, she was a navigator for the [[Concord of Tides]], one of their best. Five years ago, during a routine patrol along the Shattered Coast, she experienced a Sleepwalker episode. She walked off her ship into black water and was pulled under.

She should have drowned. Instead, she spent three days beneath the surface in a state she cannot fully describe, suspended in darkness, hearing a voice that spoke with perfect clarity. When she surfaced on a beach south of [[Veil Harbor]], the Concord had already declared her dead. No search party was sent. Her commission was dissolved. Her belongings were auctioned.

Her hatred of [[Maren Selk]] is personal and predates her drowning. Selk's harbor reforms five years ago displaced Sable's family from the docks. Her mother lost her fish-stall permit in the reorganization. Sable sees Selk as someone who sacrifices the poor for political order, a bureaucrat who would declare anyone inconvenient dead and never lose sleep over it.

[[The Whisperer]] found her within a week, drawn by reports of a woman who had survived a deep submersion. The recruitment was easy. Sable had already heard [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s voice, and to her it did not sound like madness. It sounded like truth spoken plainly. She believes the prison beneath the Trench is an injustice, that the Reach's prosperity is built on the suffering of a caged goddess. She is wrong, but she is competent and committed, and that makes her dangerous.

## Relationships

- **[[The Whisperer]]**, her leader and the one who gave her purpose after the Concord abandoned her. Sable has met the Whisperer in person, though their meetings are rare and conducted in total darkness. Her loyalty is absolute.
- **[[Captain Rook Tessavar]]**, Rook knows Sable as a quiet chandler and suspects low-level smuggling. He has no idea she is [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]]. She intends to keep it that way.
- **Mags Underhill**, the Driftwood's owner sells information to everyone, including Sable. Their relationship is transactional and carefully maintained.
- **[[Lira Ashvane]]**, Sable considers the Lantern-Keeper Commander her most dangerous adversary, a woman smart enough to connect the dots if given enough pieces. Sable works to ensure she never gets them.
- **The Concord of Tides**, her former employers. The institution that declared her dead without searching. Her hatred is quiet, patient, and focused.
- **[[Bryn Kellar]]**, she knows his patrol routes better than he does. Has considered recruiting him, he is clearly unhappy with Lantern-Keeper leadership, but decided he is too principled.

## What They Want

Sable wants [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] freed and the Reach's comfortable lies stripped away. She would tell you she wants justice for the imprisoned goddess and for everyone the Concord has abandoned. What actually drives her is the need to prove that her survival meant something, that the voice she heard was real and right, because the alternative is that she drowned five years ago and whatever came back is something else entirely.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Sable runs the [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound's]] most active cell from the Pale Margin, a chandlery ship in [[Driftmere]]. She coordinates operations across the Reach, moving agents and materials through Driftmere's anonymous networks. She is a mid-level antagonist in Act I, potentially encountered when PCs investigate cultist activity in Driftmere.
>
> **Key Secret.** The back hold of the Pale Margin contains coded correspondence, ritual components, and a map of the [[Abyssal Trench]] that she updates regularly based on intelligence from agents across the Reach. Her luminescent eyes are a side effect of her submersion, physical proof that Serith's influence changed her at a biological level.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If exposed, Sable will fight or flee depending on the odds, she is pragmatic above all. If she escapes, she resurfaces later with greater caution and deeper hatred. If captured and confronted with evidence that Serith's freedom would destroy the Reach rather than liberate it, she may crack, but only if the PCs can answer the question she cannot: what happened to her in the water?
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in short, precise sentences. Never uses contractions. Adjusts her tinted lenses frequently, a nervous habit she has not managed to eliminate. Reacts with immediate hostility if anyone uses her former name or references her Concord service. Speaks in clipped, impatient fragments. Hates small talk. Sample lines: *"I do not deal in belief. I deal in what I have seen, what I have heard, and what I know to be true. The rest is noise."* *"Talk or don't. I have cargo to move."*
