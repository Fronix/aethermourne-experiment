# The Ashen Crier

## Appearance

A gaunt figure wrapped in rags that were once a color no one can identify. Their mouth is always moving, even when sound is not coming out, the jaw working in constant, compulsive rhythm. Their lips are cracked and stained grey-black from the ash they inevitably inhale mid-sentence. Their eyes are wide, alert, and darting, cataloguing everything that moves on the grey horizon. They are thin to the point of translucence, as though the act of speaking has been consuming them from the inside for a very long time. Gender, age, and origin are all indeterminate, worn away by the curse and the ash in equal measure.

## Personality

Frantic, fragmented, and deeply aware. The Crier's curse forces continuous speech, but within the torrent of words there is a sharp, observant mind struggling to make itself understood. They cycle between prophetic fragments, desperate pleas for someone to listen, bitter humor about their condition, and stretches of pure glossolalia where the domain's whisper bleeds through their voice. In the rare moments when they achieve coherence, they are surprisingly kind, warning newcomers with genuine urgency. They know they sound mad. They know most people stop listening after the first minute. They keep talking anyway.

## Backstory

No one knows the Crier's original name, least of all the Crier. They arrived in the Ashfield at some indeterminate point in the past, drawn like all others by the whisper. But where most newcomers hear the whisper as a pull toward depth, the Crier heard it as language, actual words, and tried to speak back to it. The domain answered. It poured itself into the Crier's throat, filling them with an awareness of everything that crosses the Ashfield's threshold, every soul, every fragment, every shifting of the ash. The price was simple: they cannot stop transmitting.

Silence is not an option. When the Crier tries to stop speaking, the domain fills the gap with its own voice, the whisper amplified through a mortal throat. What comes out in those moments is worse than the Crier's ravings, it is [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s recruitment pitch delivered with the intimacy of a human voice, and anyone who hears it feels the pull to descend intensify sharply. The Crier learned early that their own babble, however maddening, is the lesser evil.

## Relationships

- [[Erys Ironfall]], the Warden who tolerates the Crier's presence and occasionally harvests useful intelligence from the noise. The Crier considers Erys the only person who truly listens, even if Erys would not describe it that way.
- [[Old Rust]], who sometimes sits near the Crier in silence. The Crier finds Old Rust's company comforting because the ancient revenant does not flinch at anything they say, no matter how terrible.
- [[Keeper Maren Ashveil]], who brings the Crier water and food. The Crier has tried to warn Maren about something specific but cannot force the words into coherent order. This distresses them greatly.
- [[Dalla Threshborn]], who occasionally trades with the Crier for fragments of useful information buried in the babble. Dalla is patient enough to sift signal from noise, which the Crier both appreciates and resents.

## What They Want

The Crier wants to deliver one message clearly, one complete, coherent warning to someone who will act on it. They have been trying for longer than they can track. What drives them beneath that desire is simpler and sadder: they want someone to know their name. They believe they had one, once. They believe it might still be buried somewhere in the torrent of words they cannot stop producing.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Crier functions as an oracle, delivering plot-critical information wrapped in noise. Their prophecies are genuine but require interpretation. They are also an early warning system for anything entering the Ashfield.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Crier is not cursed in the traditional sense. They have become a relay node in the domain's architecture, a living component of the system that [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] built to monitor his threshold. The domain is using them. If someone severed the connection, the Crier would go silent, but the Ashfield would lose its ability to track arrivals, and things from deeper levels could enter Level 1 undetected.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party helps the Crier achieve one moment of true clarity, the message they deliver will be significant, a warning about the domain's decay and what is stirring on the lower levels. After delivering it, the Crier may collapse into ash and be replaced by a new Crier, or they may finally fall silent, depending on whether the party severed the connection or simply boosted the signal.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speak in rapid, overlapping fragments. Mix genuine prophecy with nonsense. Drop important details mid-sentence and bury them in gibberish. Occasionally lock eyes with a specific PC and speak with sudden, terrible clarity before dissolving back into the babble. Sample line: *"Rust and ruin, seven coming, no six, one turned back at the Fade Line, smart, smarter than you, the stair is hungry this season, did you know iron remembers? It remembers everything, YOUR NAME, I almost had it, gone now, gone, the Registrar writes in blood that isn't..."*
