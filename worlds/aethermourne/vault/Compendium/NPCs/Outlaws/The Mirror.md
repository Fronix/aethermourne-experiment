# The Mirror

|||
|---|---|
| **Title** | None (known only as "The Mirror") |
| **Location** | [[Riftmarket]], [[The Shattered Edge]] (nominally; moves frequently) |
| **Faction** | Independent |
| **Status** | Alive |

---

## Appearance

Unknown. The Mirror's Changing gave them the ability to temporarily copy another person's appearance, voice, and mannerisms with unsettling precision. They use this ability constantly, cycling through identities the way other people change clothes. PCs who encounter The Mirror will meet them wearing someone else's face, and they may not realize it.

When between identities, witnesses report a figure of indeterminate height and build with features that seem to blur at the edges, as if reality cannot quite decide what they look like. Their skin has a faint, reflective quality, catching light like polished glass. Whether this is their "true" form or simply another iteration is unclear, possibly even to The Mirror.

---

## Personality

The Mirror is careful, curious, and deeply lonely. Their ability makes them an exceptional spy, thief, and information broker, but it has also erased the boundary between self and role. They have worn so many faces that the question of which one is "real" has become genuinely unanswerable. This does not trouble them as much as it probably should, which is itself troubling.

They are professional in their dealings, reliable in their information, and surprisingly ethical for someone in their line of work. The Mirror does not sell false intelligence, does not betray clients to other clients, and does not copy the faces of people who have asked them not to. These are their rules, the scaffolding that holds a dissolving identity together.

They are fascinated by people who are certain of who they are. They study conviction the way a scholar studies a foreign text, with admiration and the suspicion that they are missing something fundamental.

---

## Backstory

No one knows The Mirror's original name, gender, or appearance, including, possibly, The Mirror. They emerged from a Maelstrom surge approximately ten years ago with the ability to copy other people's physical forms. The first years were chaotic: identities adopted and discarded, petty crime, confusion, a period of apparent crisis during which they disappeared from the Edge entirely.

They returned to [[Riftmarket]] five years ago, composed and purposeful, and established themselves as an information broker of remarkable capability. Their method is simple. They copy the appearance of someone with access, walk into the place that person would be, learn what there is to learn, and walk out. It works because on the Shattered Edge, where people's appearances can change overnight, identity verification is already unreliable.

Riftmarket tolerates The Mirror because their intelligence is accurate and their services are available to anyone who can pay. Fear them, yes. But also need them.

## Relationships

- **[[Tyrell Vane]]**: Vane knows about The Mirror and has used their services, discreetly, to gather information about the [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]]'s activities. The Mirror finds Vane unsettling because Vane's probability-sight means he always knows which face is real.
- **[[Oren Blackfinger]]**: A client relationship. The Mirror has done several jobs for Oren, investigating competitors and verifying supplier claims. Oren pays well and does not ask unnecessary questions.
- **[[The Veil Unbound]]**: The Mirror has been approached by Veil agents and has taken their money for small jobs, gathering information about Edgewatch patrol schedules and the Certainty's ward rotations. They do not know the Veil's larger purpose and would not care, except that the most recent requests have felt different, more urgent, more specific, as if building toward something.
- **The Changed community**: The Mirror is respected among the Changed as proof that even the most dramatic transformation can be survived and turned to advantage. Some of the Changed seek them out for reassurance.

---

## What They Want

- **To remember.** Somewhere beneath the layers of copied faces is an original person. The Mirror would like to know who that person was, even if they can never go back.
- **To be needed.** Their business is their anchor. Without it, they would drift into the blur of shifting identities and never come out.
- **To understand the Veil's interest.** Their survival instincts, honed by a decade of espionage, are telling them that their recent clients are planning something significant. The Mirror wants to know what, not out of altruism, but because being caught in the blast radius of someone else's plan is bad for business.

> [!warning]- GM Notes
> **Campaign Role.** The Mirror can serve as information source, ally of convenience, or unexpected complication. They know things about the [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]]'s movements in [[Riftmarket]] that they will share for a price, or for a favor. They can also provide PCs with a unique perspective on identity, transformation, and what it means to be Changed.
>
> **The Original Face.** The Mirror's original identity is genuinely lost, not suppressed or hidden but *erased* by the Changing. However, the Maelstrom did not destroy it completely. At the Maelstrom's edge, where probability is densest, The Mirror has reported catching glimpses of a face they do not recognize but feel they should know. This is their original form, preserved in the probability threads like an echo. Reaching it would require a journey into the Threshold that The Mirror is too afraid to attempt alone.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** The Mirror adopts whatever persona suits the moment, matching their mannerisms to whichever face they're wearing. The tell is their voice: no matter whose appearance they copy, their speech patterns carry a subtle, distinctive cadence, a habit of pausing before proper nouns, as if briefly uncertain which name belongs to which identity. PCs with high perception may notice this consistency across encounters with apparently different people.
