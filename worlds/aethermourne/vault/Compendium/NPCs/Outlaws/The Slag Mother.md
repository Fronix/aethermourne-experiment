# The Slag Mother

## Appearance

The Slag Mother is barely recognizable as human. Her body is crusted with layers of metallic waste, cooled slag fused to her skin in overlapping plates that give her the appearance of something geological, a formation rather than a person. Her face is visible only as a dark gap in the slag-crust, two faintly glowing eyes and a mouth that opens with a wet, crackling sound, like cooling metal splitting. She moves with a low, shuffling gait, her slag-shell scraping against the tunnel walls and leaving silver-black streaks behind her. She is smaller than she looks, the slag adding bulk to a frame that was once slight. She smells of molten metal and something older, something that might be stone or might be bone. When she speaks, her voice is a hiss pushed through a throat lined with iron filings, sibilant and raw.

## Personality

The Slag Mother is shrewd, patient, and utterly without sentiment. She chose the waste channels over the forges because she understood, earlier than anyone else, that the Forge Eternal punishes hope more than it punishes misery. In the waste, there are no quotas, no dissolving masterworks, no cycle of creation and loss. There is only the slag, and the slag does not pretend to be anything other than refuse. She trades information and secrets the way other souls trade labor, hoarding knowledge as currency and spending it only when the return is worth the cost. She is not cruel, but she is unsparing. She will tell you the truth, and she will not soften it, and she will charge you for it afterward.

## Backstory

The Slag Mother was one of the first mortal souls trapped in the Forge Eternal after [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s death, condemned for the sin of ambition without craft, a warlord who spent armies like currency, building empires from the bodies of soldiers she considered expendable. Kaevroth claimed her soul for what he saw as the highest form of waste: ambition applied without skill.

In the Forge Eternal, she was assigned to the smelters and lasted three centuries before she broke, not from the labor but from the dissolution. Watching her work dissolve was too close to watching her conquests crumble in life, the same cycle of achievement and loss that had defined her mortal existence. She fled to the waste channels beneath the Foundry, where the slag pools and cools and is forgotten, and there she found something unexpected: the waste remembered. The slag carried traces of every design ever forged on Level 4, dissolved but not destroyed, and over centuries, the Slag Mother learned to read those traces. She has assembled a fragmentary archive of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s original forge-designs from the residue in the waste, including blueprints that the Pattern Library has lost to corruption.

## Relationships

- [[Forge-Warden Irontooth]], the level's overseer. He fears her, and she knows it. She possesses knowledge of the original forge-designs that predates his authority, and her refusal to submit to his rule is a constant reminder that his power has limits. Their enmity is old and carefully maintained by both sides.
- [[Smeltress Kora]], the master smith. Kora is the Slag Mother's most reliable trading partner, exchanging enduring metalwork for information. The Slag Mother respects Kora's craft and, in her way, is protective of her, warning her when Irontooth's attention grows too possessive.
- [[Apprentice Nil]], the Pattern Library cataloguer. The Slag Mother watches Nil with a wariness that borders on reverence. She has read things in the slag that suggest Nil is not a mortal soul, and the implications frighten even her.
- [[Bellows-Keeper Tharn]], the keeper of the forges' breath. Tharn once visited the waste channels and told the Slag Mother that the slag "sings in a different key than the forges." She has been thinking about that sentence for forty years.

## What They Want

The Slag Mother wants the original blueprint for [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s masterwork, the weapon he was building when he decided to break the Empyrean Accord and start the Theomachis. She has assembled fragments from the slag waste, but the complete design eludes her. She believes it is the key to understanding why the forges cannot stop, and perhaps, the key to making them stop. What drives her beneath that intellectual hunger is a need for control. She lost everything in life because she commanded without understanding. She will not make that mistake again.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Slag Mother is Level 4's information broker and the party's best source for deep lore about [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s pre-Theomachis ambitions. She knows things about the god's final weapon that no other soul in the Iron Descent possesses, but she will not share them for free.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Slag Mother has found things in the waste that should not exist: fragments of designs that are not Kaevroth's. They bear the signature of another god's forge-work, subtle but unmistakable to someone who has spent centuries reading slag-residue. [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] has been seeding the waste channels with her own designs, using the Forge Eternal's dissolution cycle to smuggle blueprints into the domain's substrate. The Slag Mother does not know whose designs they are, only that they are alien and purposeful, and that they are slowly reshaping the architecture of the waste channels themselves.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party brings her real iron from the mortal world, iron forged with love (Kora's work would qualify), she will share her reconstructed blueprints and reveal the alien designs in the slag. If they betray her to Irontooth, she will seal the waste channels and take her knowledge into the depths, where no one will find her. If she completes the masterwork blueprint, she will attempt to forge it herself, an act that could restart the forges with purpose or tear Level 4 apart.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in a low, continuous hiss, barely above a whisper. Never raises her voice. Words come out in clusters separated by wet, metallic clicking sounds as her slag-crust shifts. She watches whoever she is speaking to with unblinking intensity, reading them the way she reads the slag.
> *"You want the god's last design. Everyone does. But no one asks the right question. The question is not what he was building. The question is what he was building it to kill. Bring me iron that was loved, and I will show you the answer."*
