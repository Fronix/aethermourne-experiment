# Warden Senna Deepmoss

## Appearance

Tall and lean, in her forties, with dark skin and calm grey eyes that give nothing away. Her hair is worn in a single long braid wrapped with luminescent Bog-moss that glows faintly green at night, marking her as a border-clan leader who lives close to the [[The Verdant Marches#The Marrow Bogs|Marrow Bogs]]. She moves with deliberate economy, the way someone does when they are always assessing a situation. Her hands are calloused from fieldwork, not ceremony, and she wears layered leathers stitched with dried seedpods that rattle softly when she walks.

## Personality

Careful, measured, analytical. Senna watches the unification debate with the patience of someone who knows the right choice depends on information she does not yet have. She respects both [[Kaelith Thornborn]] and [[Draven Ironbark]] but trusts neither completely. She makes decisions slowly and changes them rarely. Where other Marchwardens posture and speechify at the Moot, Senna asks questions, quiet ones that cut to the heart of whatever argument is being made. She has no patience for prophecy, divine mandates, or appeals to tradition. She wants evidence.

## Backstory

Senna inherited the Deepmoss wardenship from her mother, Olia Deepmoss, who died of Bog-sickness after decades of exposure to the southern edge of the [[The Verdant Marches#The Marrow Bogs|Marrow Bogs]]. Watching her mother's slow transformation, bark creeping up her arms, thoughts drifting into something green and distant, taught Senna two lessons: the land gives freely, and the land takes without asking. She swore her wardenship would be guided by observation, not faith.

Clan Deepmoss holds the Southern Cradle, the transitional territory where the great forest thins toward the Bogs. It is a difficult stretch to govern. The soil is rich but unpredictable, saturated with diluted Marrow-Water that makes crops grow strangely and livestock occasionally sprout fungal growths. Her people are hardy, pragmatic, and accustomed to watching the treeline for signs that the Bogs have crept closer overnight. In recent years, they have crept closer more often.

That expansion is what drove Senna to quietly commission [[Vael Sundren]] to investigate the cause. She did not go through the Council. She did not consult the [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]]. She went to a scientist, because scientists give you data, not dreams. The investigation is ongoing, and its results may determine the fate of the entire unification debate.

## Relationships

- [[Kaelith Thornborn]], respected as a leader, but Senna distrusts anyone who claims divine guidance. Respect without allegiance.
- [[Draven Ironbark]], shares his suspicion of concentrated power, but considers his traditionalism dangerously rigid in the face of real threats.
- [[Vael Sundren]], her quietly commissioned investigator. Senna trusts Vael's methods and protects her research from political interference.
- [[Essa Deepmoss]], a young relative and Reader of the [[The Confluence Council]], ley-sensitive since birth. Senna watches her progress with guarded pride.
- [[Brannoc Stillwater]], neighboring Marchwarden at [[Stillwater]]. The two share a border and a cautious mutual respect, trading information about Bog movements.

## What They Want

Senna wants proof. Not rhetoric, not emotion, not divine marks. She wants verifiable evidence that the Bog expansion has an external cause, and a plan to address it that does not require surrendering clan autonomy to a single leader. If the evidence supports unification, she will commit fully. If it does not, she will block it, because binding the clans together on faith alone is how you build a cage and call it a nation.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Senna is the "convince me" NPC, the swing vote whose decision could tip the entire [[Marchwardens Council]] toward unification or deadlock. She appears primarily in Act I and Act II of [[The Second Silence]].
>
> **Key Secret.** Senna already suspects the Bog expansion is not natural. Her scouts have reported dark roots spreading through the southern soil, spiraling patterns that do not match any known growth. She has not shared this with the Council because unconfirmed suspicion is not evidence, and because she fears what the Greensingers would do with the information before the science is complete.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If [[Vael Sundren]]'s investigation confirms an external cause (likely [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s corruption reaching through the root system), Senna sides with [[Kaelith Thornborn]] and becomes a decisive, unshakable ally. If the PCs bring her evidence directly, framed as data rather than prophecy, she moves faster. If no evidence comes, she remains neutral until the crisis forces her hand, by which point it may be too late to unify peacefully.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in measured, unhurried sentences. Never raises her voice. Asks more questions than she answers. When she does commit to a position, her tone does not change, but her words become absolute. Fidgets with the moss-wrapped end of her braid when thinking. Sample line: *"You've told me what the land whispered to you. Now tell me what you saw with your own eyes."*
