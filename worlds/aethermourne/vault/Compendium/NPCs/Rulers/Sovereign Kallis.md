## Appearance

Kallis is tall, commanding, and radiantly beautiful in a way that shifts depending on who is looking. Her features rearrange subtly to reflect whatever the viewer considers regal: sharper jaw for those who respect severity, warmer eyes for those who value compassion, broader shoulders for those who admire strength. The one constant is her crown, a circlet of mirror-glass that catches light from angles that should not exist, throwing prismatic shards across the walls whenever she moves. She wears robes of polished iron-silk that flow like liquid mercury, and her voice carries the absolute, unquestioned weight of someone who has never been contradicted, not because dissent is forbidden but because the mirrors ensure she never hears it. She is magnificent. She is also, in the strictest sense, not entirely a person anymore.

## Personality

Kallis is gracious, generous, and utterly delusional. She rules with genuine warmth and real competence, managing the affairs of Gloryholme with the skill of a seasoned monarch, issuing decrees, settling disputes, hosting feasts, planning civic improvements. The fact that none of it is real does not diminish her effort. She is the Hall of Mirrors' most perfect creation: ambition so pure and so total that it generated its own reality, a sovereignty built from nothing but the absolute conviction that she deserves to rule. She is kind to her "subjects," solicitous of their needs, and terrifyingly effective at folding newcomers into her court. She does not manipulate. She simply radiates such complete certainty that the mirrors around her make it true.

## Backstory

In life, Kallis was a mortal general in [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s service, one of the god's most capable and devoted commanders. She led armies with brilliance and sacrificed everything for conquest: family, health, mercy, all of it burned on the altar of ambition. When she died in the Theomachis, fighting for a god who was already falling, Kaevroth personally placed her soul on Level 5 as a reward. She was the last soul he settled before his death, and he intended her stay to be temporary, a brief paradise before she resumed her climb toward his throne.

But Kaevroth died, and the mirrors locked. Kallis's temporary reward became permanent. Over six centuries, the Hall of Mirrors amplified her ambition until it became self-sustaining, a closed loop of desire and fulfillment that no longer required external validation. She forgot she had ever served anyone. She forgot there was anything above Level 5. She forgot her own death. In her mind, she has always been Sovereign, and Gloryholme has always been her domain. The mirrors confirm this at every glance, and she has no reason to look away from the mirrors.

## Relationships

- [[Mirror-Warden Caelus]], her warden and enforcer. Kallis considers Caelus her most trusted advisor and captain of her guard. She does not realize that Caelus serves the mirrors themselves, not her. Their relationship is a masterwork of mutual utility: Kallis provides the authority, Caelus provides the mechanism.
- [[Lord Parvane]], the escaped reflection. Kallis considers Parvane her court ambassador, a charming noble who handles diplomatic matters. She does not know he is a reflection, and he has never told her, finding her company more agreeable than truth.
- [[The Truthsayer]], the blind prophet. Kallis considers the Truthsayer a harmless eccentric, a court curiosity who speaks in riddles. She visits the Truthsayer's cell occasionally, bringing food and gentle words, genuinely believing she is caring for a troubled subject. The Truthsayer finds these visits almost unbearable.
- [[Dreamer Ysolde]], the consumed dreamer. Kallis checks on Ysolde regularly, treating her as a beloved citizen who has earned a peaceful retirement. Kallis does not see the horror of Ysolde's situation because the mirrors will not show it to her.

## What They Want

Kallis wants her reign to continue. She wants her subjects happy, her court orderly, her domain prosperous. These are the desires of a good ruler, and in any other context, they would be admirable. What she truly needs, buried beneath six centuries of reinforced delusion, is to remember who she was before the mirrors: a soldier, a servant, a woman who fought for something larger than herself. That woman carried a message from [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]], and she forgot she was carrying it.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Kallis is the primary authority on Level 5 and the party's first point of contact in Gloryholme. She can be a generous host or a terrifying obstacle depending on whether the party plays along with her court or challenges it.
>
> **Key Secret.** Kallis carries a fragment of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s final orders, a command embedded in her soul by the god himself before the Theomachis. The command has never been delivered because Kallis forgot she was carrying it. If her delusion is broken carefully, with precision rather than force, the command can be recovered. It contains Kaevroth's contingency plan for the Iron Descent in the event of his death: instructions for shutting down the domain safely, level by level, releasing the souls and decommissioning the machinery. It is the kill-switch for the entire plane.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party breaks her delusion gently, Kallis will experience devastating grief followed by fierce, clear-eyed purpose. She will remember who she was, deliver Kaevroth's final orders, and become a powerful ally in navigating the Iron Descent. If the delusion is shattered violently (by destroying mirrors or confronting her publicly), she will fragment, her identity splitting like the Shattered Man's, and the command will be lost among the pieces. If the party simply plays along, she will be a gracious host who inadvertently traps them deeper in the mirrors' influence.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks with warm authority, every word measured and musical. Uses the royal "we" naturally. Maintains eye contact with absolute comfort. Her composure never cracks, not because she is hiding anything but because the mirrors ensure she has nothing to hide from.
> *"We welcome you to Gloryholme, travelers. You have come a long way, and we would see you rested and honored. Tell us of your journey, that we may celebrate your triumphs as our own."*
