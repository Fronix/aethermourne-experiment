# Speaker Dravos

## Appearance

Dravos is a man in his early fifties, built like a siege engine: broad-shouldered, thick-armed, and utterly unyielding. His skin is dark umber, marked with dense Pact-Weaver patterns that cover both arms, his chest, and half his face. Unlike the delicate silver tracings of most Pact-Weavers, his patterns are iron-gray, almost black, and they do not pulse. They are static, fixed, as if burned into his flesh rather than grown.

He bonded with [[The Primordials#Ur-Ghael|Ur-Ghael]], the Primordial of structure, and it shows. His movements are precise, mechanical, every gesture calculated for maximum efficiency. He does not fidget. He does not hesitate. When he speaks, his voice is deep, resonant, and utterly certain. He believes every word he says, and that belief is infectious.

He wears military-style robes in charcoal and deep red, cut for function rather than ceremony. He carries a Primordial resonator forged into a staff, a tool, a weapon, a symbol. He is the only Speaker who brings arms into the Circle's chamber. [[Speaker Kaelthen]] has objected three times. Dravos ignores her. The staff stays.

---

## Personality

Dravos is a visionary, a term that means both prophet and fanatic depending on who you ask. He believes the outlanders' arrival is not a threat but an *opportunity*, the chance for the Oldborn to finally step out of isolation and claim their rightful place as the inheritors of a post-divine world. The known continent is collapsing. When it falls, someone will fill the void. It should be the Oldborn.

He is charismatic, dangerously so. He does not argue. He *persuades*. He frames his expansionism as moral necessity: the outlanders are suffering, their divine infrastructure is failing, and the Oldborn have a responsibility to share their superior magical framework. He calls it liberation. [[Speaker Kaelthen]] calls it conquest. Both are correct.

Dravos is not stupid. He knows his rhetoric is inflammatory. He uses it anyway because it works. He has built the **Ash Reclaimers** from a fringe faction to one-third of the Circle's Speakers in five years through sheer force of personality and strategic alliance-building. He votes with [[Speaker Yavenne]]'s Open Hand on engagement policy, not because he shares her ideals, but because engagement serves his goal: access to the known continent.

Beneath the charisma is ambition, cold and absolute. Dravos does not believe in the Oldborn's moral superiority because it is true. He believes in it because it is useful. Power requires justification. Philosophy provides that justification. He is not a monster, he genuinely believes Oldborn magic is superior, that expansion would benefit everyone, but his belief is secondary to his will. If the philosophy stopped serving his ambition, he would find another.

## Backstory

Dravos was born in [[Draveth]], a mining city on the edge of [[The Primordia]], where the Oldborn extract Primordial materials from the Shimmer's boundary. Life in Draveth is hard, dangerous, and unforgiving. You learn to take what you need or you do not survive. Dravos learned early.

He joined the Oldborn military at eighteen, served for twenty years, and rose to the rank of **Boundary Commander**, responsible for defending Oldborn settlements from Primordial incursions. He was brilliant at it. He understood Primordial forces better than most Pact-Weavers because he did not revere them. He saw them as resources, dangerous but exploitable. Under his command, Draveth's defensive perimeter expanded by 40%. He was a hero.

Then the outlanders arrived. Dravos saw immediately what [[Speaker Kaelthen]] saw: a threat. But where Kaelthen saw contamination, Dravos saw weakness. The outlanders built their civilization on dead gods. Their magic was failing. Their infrastructure was collapsing. They were desperate, vulnerable, and ripe for influence.

Dravos left the military, entered politics, and founded the **Ash Reclaimers** on a single argument: the Oldborn could either hide from the collapsing world or inherit it. He built his faction from military veterans, ambitious Pact-Weavers, and pragmatists who agreed that survival required expansion. He became a Speaker at forty-eight through ruthless coalition-building and strategic compromises with [[Speaker Yavenne]], whose naivety he finds useful.

He is now the Circle's loudest voice for engagement, not for humanitarian reasons, but because engagement is the first step toward expansion. And expansion is inevitable.

## Relationships

- **[[Speaker Kaelthen]]** (Sealed Gate Leader), Mutual contempt. Kaelthen views Dravos as a would-be conqueror dressed in philosophical robes. Dravos views Kaelthen as a coward hiding behind threat assessment. They agree on almost nothing. Both know the other is dangerous. Neither can outmaneuver the other politically. The stalemate frustrates Dravos. He is patient, but his patience has limits.

- **[[Speaker Yavenne]]** (Open Hand Leader), Exploitative alliance. Dravos and Yavenne vote together on engagement policy. Yavenne thinks they are building cooperation. Dravos is building infrastructure for conquest. He finds her idealism charming and deeply naive. He will use her political capital until it runs out, then discard her. She suspects this. She allies with him anyway because the alternative is worse.

- **The Ash Reclaimers Faction**, Fanatical loyalty. Dravos's faction is composed of military veterans, ambitious Pact-Weavers, and pragmatists who believe expansion is necessary. They follow him not because he is kind or just, but because he is *right*. The known continent is collapsing. The Oldborn can inherit it or be consumed by it. Dravos offers a third option: conquest framed as salvation.

- **[[Captain Erys Vane]]** (Tidecross Harbormaster), Strategic interest. Dravos has been watching [[Tidecross]] closely. Erys is competent, pragmatic, and overextended. She is the perfect point of leverage. If Dravos can convince Erys that the Concord has abandoned her, that the Oldborn offer stability the outlanders cannot provide, she will become his advocate on the known continent. He has sent three covert messages offering support. She has not responded. He interprets silence as consideration.

- **The Draveth Mining Guilds**, Financial backers. Dravos's home city funds his political career. The guilds see expansion as access to new Primordial resources, new markets, new wealth. They do not care about philosophy. They care about profit. Dravos gives them both.

---

## What They Want

**Surface Goal:** Establish Oldborn presence on the known continent. Share Primordial magic with the outlanders (on Oldborn terms). Position the Oldborn as saviors of a collapsing civilization.

**Deeper Need:** Dravos wants to be remembered. He wants to be the Speaker who led the Oldborn out of isolation, who inherited a dying world and remade it in the Oldborn image. He wants statues, histories, a legacy that outlives him. He believes he is destined for greatness. He will sacrifice anything to achieve it.

---

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role:** Dravos is the "ally" who makes the players deeply uncomfortable. He offers genuine help: access to Oldborn resources, political support, military aid. The cost is implicit: he is building leverage. Everything he gives the players is a future obligation, and when he calls it in, the price will be steep.
>
> **Key Secret:** Dravos is planning a coup. Not against the Circle, against the known continent. He has been cultivating contacts in [[The Hollowed Reach]]'s merchant houses, offering trade deals, information, and Primordial artifacts. He is building a network of outlanders who owe him favors. When the Second Silence reaches critical mass, when the divine infrastructure collapses completely, Dravos will offer "aid": Oldborn Pact-Weavers to stabilize the collapse, Primordial Anchors to replace failing ley lines, and a new magical framework to replace the Aetheric Web. The cost will be Oldborn oversight, Oldborn governance, Oldborn control. It will not be conquest. It will be a rescue. The result is the same.
>
> **Arc Trajectory:** If the players ally with Dravos, he provides extraordinary resources but slowly co-opts their goals. He reframes their victories as evidence of Oldborn superiority. He uses their successes to justify expansion. If the players reject him, he shifts tactics, offering aid to their enemies, destabilizing [[Tidecross]], and accelerating the known continent's collapse to create the crisis he needs.
>
> **If the PCs Do Nothing:** Dravos executes his plan unopposed. He consolidates power in the Circle, outmaneuvers [[Speaker Yavenne]], and launches the "Reclamation," a coordinated Oldborn intervention in the known continent framed as humanitarian aid. [[The Hollowed Reach]] accepts. [[The Ashen Dominion]] resists. Civil war erupts. The Oldborn inherit the ashes. Dravos becomes the architect of a new age. History remembers him as savior or conqueror depending on who writes it.
>
> **Roleplay Notes:** Dravos speaks with absolute confidence, no hedging, no doubt. He makes bold statements and waits for you to disprove them. He is charming, persuasive, and utterly certain of his righteousness. His tells: gripping his Primordial staff when challenged (a subtle threat), stepping closer when persuading (invading personal space to dominate), and pausing before answering difficult questions (calculating his response). Sample line: *"You fear we seek conquest. I understand. But ask yourself: when your ley lines fail, when your Ashite reserves run dry, when your divine infrastructure collapses entirely, who will you turn to? We offer stability. We offer knowledge. We offer survival. The only question is whether you will accept it as a gift or demand it as tribute after everything else has failed."*
