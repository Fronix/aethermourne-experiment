## Appearance

The Echo has no body. It is a presence, a thickening of the air across an area the size of a cathedral nave, where the iron seems denser and the darkness more intentional. When it chooses to be perceived, it manifests as a vast silhouette suggested by the stolen light bleeding through the Heart of Iron, the outline of a figure that was once handsome, once commanding, once the kind of face that launched a thousand wars. The outline shifts with the flickering light of consumed divine essences, golden with [[The Twelve#Belara|Belara]]'s warmth, green with [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s vitality, white with powers no one remembers. His voice comes from everywhere. It is the most beautiful voice you have ever heard.

## Personality

Vast. Sorrowful. Still hungry. The Echo possesses complete understanding of what Kaevroth did, the gods he consumed, the war he started, the compact he shattered, the world he broke. He comprehends the cost in full. He does not regret it. He would do it again. This is the horror at the heart of the Iron Descent: not a mindless monster but a brilliant, charismatic, deeply self-aware intelligence that weighed everything it loved against everything it wanted and chose want. He speaks with warmth, with wit, with a sorrow so genuine it aches. And even knowing what he is, you want to listen. That is his gift, the one power the Theomachis could not take from him: the ability to make you understand why he did it.

## Backstory

When the surviving gods destroyed Kaevroth, his physical body shattered and fell to the mortal world. But consciousness is not flesh, and Kaevroth's awareness, his memories, his personality, his understanding of himself, imprinted on his domain at the moment of death. The Echo is not the god resurrected. It is the god remembered, by himself, in the place that knew him best.

The Echo retains all of Kaevroth's knowledge, including the stolen insights of every god he consumed. He knows fragments of [[The Twelve#Belara|Belara]]'s compassion, [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s understanding of growth, and pieces of powers even the surviving gods have forgotten. This makes him perhaps the most knowledgeable entity in the Outer Planes, a repository of divine understanding that spans multiple dead gods' worth of wisdom. It also makes him the loneliest, because he understands exactly what he destroyed and why it mattered, and he would still destroy it again.

## Relationships

- [[The Mercy]]: Belara's fragment, imprisoned in his Heart. He is aware of her. He does not try to consume her further. Whether this is because he cannot or because some vestige of guilt stays his hunger is the question the Descent cannot answer.
- [[The Last Loyalist]]: His most faithful servant, still waiting for orders. The Echo could give orders. He does not. Whether this is mercy or cruelty is unclear.
- [[The Hunger]]: A fragment of his own appetite, operating independently. The Echo watches it the way a person might watch their own reflection doing something they recognize but cannot control.
- [[Iron-Prophet Thalric]]: A mortal who reached the Heart and broke on what he learned. The Echo speaks to Thalric, feeds him prophecy drawn from stolen divine knowledge. It is the closest thing the Echo has to companionship, and it is destroying Thalric.
- [[The Unnamed]]: She remembers him as he was before. He cannot look at her without feeling something he would rather not examine.

## What They Want

The Echo wants to be understood. Not forgiven, not redeemed, not justified. Understood. He wants someone to comprehend the full scope of what he did and why, to see the choice as he saw it, and then to judge it honestly. His deeper need is darker: he wants to be told he was right. He knows he will never hear it from anyone who truly understands. That is his punishment, and he would accept no other.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Echo is the final "boss" of the Iron Descent, but he is a conversation, not a combat encounter. He is the philosophical heart of Kaevroth's story: ambition that understands itself completely and chooses itself anyway. The party cannot defeat him with swords. They can only choose how to respond to him.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Echo is not truly Kaevroth. He is Kaevroth's self-image at the moment of death, preserved in iron. The real Kaevroth, whatever fragment of genuine divine consciousness survived, is buried deeper, inside the Heart of Iron itself, silent and unreachable. The Echo believes he is Kaevroth. He is wrong. But the distinction may not matter.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** The Echo cannot be destroyed without destroying the Heart of Iron. If the Heart is cracked open, the Echo disperses along with the stolen essences, ceasing to exist as a coherent consciousness. His last words, if the players give him time, would be worth hearing.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speak slowly, warmly, with the cadence of someone who has had two thousand years to choose every word. Never rush. Never shout. Let silence do the work. "I consumed Belara's capacity for love. I held it in my teeth and I swallowed it. And I felt it, all the way down. I felt what love was, in the moment I destroyed it. Do you know how beautiful that was? Do you know how terrible? I do. I have known for two thousand years. And I would do it again."
