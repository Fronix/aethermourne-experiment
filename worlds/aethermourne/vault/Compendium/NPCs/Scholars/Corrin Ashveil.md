## Appearance

A lean, bookish dwarf of one hundred and ten, Corrin Ashveil looks more like a scholar than a smith, which in the Emberveil is a social handicap. His beard is neatly trimmed and ink-stained at the corners where he chews the end of his pen. His eyes are pale blue, slightly magnified by the reading spectacles he wears perpetually. His hands are soft by Emberveil standards, callused from pen-work rather than hammer-work, though he maintains his forge credentials with a minimum-required annual proving. He dresses in the Ember Guild's formal dark leather but has worn through the elbows with desk-work. He smells of parchment, lamp oil, and the faintly metallic tang of the Ember Archive.

## Personality

Corrin is methodical, thorough, and possessed of the particular intensity of a researcher who has found a pattern that no one else can see. He is not socially awkward so much as socially impatient, tolerant of conversation only when it advances his understanding. He respects data above opinion, evidence above tradition, and documented fact above comfortable belief. This makes him valuable and unpopular in roughly equal measure. When he is certain of something, he is immovable. When he is uncertain, he is honest about it, a trait that earns him more trust than his manner would suggest.

## Backstory

Corrin was born to a family of forge-engineers in [[Crucible]], expected to follow the standard path of apprenticeship, journeyman work, and eventual specialization. He was a competent but uninspired metalworker who discovered his true gift during a mandatory rotation in the Ember Archive, where apprentices study the historical techniques of the craft. While his peers found the archives tedious, Corrin found them revelatory. Every record was a story, every logbook a map of what the Emberveil had been and what it might become.

He petitioned for a permanent Archive position at thirty, a request so unusual it had to be approved by the Guildmaster personally. [[Torren Hearthforge|Hearthforge]] granted it, reportedly saying, "The Guild needs someone who remembers what we've already tried, so we stop trying it again." Corrin has maintained and expanded the Ember Archive for eighty years, building it from a dusty record-room into a proper historical institution.

Six months ago, while cataloguing deep-mining logs from the last century, he noticed a pattern: periods of reduced forgeheart steel yield correlating with broader disruptions, political upheavals, magical anomalies, unexplained phenomena across the continent. He cross-referenced with historical accounts from other regions and found that [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] fire has fluctuated before, always preceding major crises. The current fluctuation fits the pattern exactly. He has written to [[Ashara Cinderbloom|Mender Cinderbloom]] for surface-level data and to [[Orin Deepstone|Delver-Captain Deepstone]] for deep-level readings. The picture assembling itself in his notebooks terrifies him.

## Relationships

- [[Brunhild Ironheart|Forge-Matriarch Brunhild Ironheart]], He has requested access to the sealed records and been refused. He understands her caution but believes she is wrong, that the historical data in those records could provide critical context for the current crisis. The standoff is professional but increasingly tense.
- [[Ashara Cinderbloom|Mender Ashara Cinderbloom]], His most productive correspondent. Her empirical data fills gaps in his historical analysis. Together, they are closer to a complete understanding of the Cooling than anyone in the Emberveil.
- [[Orin Deepstone|Delver-Captain Orin Deepstone]], Orin has provided temperature data but withheld context. Corrin suspects the Delver-Captain knows more than he is sharing and is patient enough to wait, but not indefinitely.
- [[Torren Hearthforge|Guildmaster Torren Hearthforge]], His original patron. Corrin's loyalty to the Guild and its records is absolute, but he is increasingly certain that the Guild's secrecy around the Deep Forge is a liability rather than a protection.

## What They Want

Corrin wants access to the sealed records. He wants to complete his analysis of the Cooling and present it to someone with the authority and will to act on it. Deeper than that, he wants the truth to matter, wants the carefully documented history of [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] fire to be used for what it was always meant for: understanding the present through the patterns of the past.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Emberveil's analytical voice. Where [[Ashara Cinderbloom|Cinderbloom]] provides empirical data and [[Orin Deepstone|Deepstone]] provides direct experience, Corrin provides historical context and pattern analysis. Together, the three of them represent a complete intelligence picture of the Cooling, but they need someone to connect them and convince them to share fully.
>
> **Key Secret.** His cross-referencing has revealed something he has not yet fully processed: every previous fluctuation in [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] fire preceded not just a crisis, but specifically a crisis involving one of the imprisoned or weakened gods. The last major fluctuation, four hundred years ago, coincided with the earliest recorded activity of [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] influence seeping from her prison. He has not made the connection to the current campaign arc, but the data is there, waiting for someone to ask the right question.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs engage with his research, he becomes the intellectual backbone of the Emberveil's response to the Second Silence, providing historical precedent and analytical framework for action. If ignored, he eventually presents his findings to the Forge Council unilaterally, forcing a public reckoning that [[Brunhild Ironheart|the Forge-Matriarch]] is not ready for.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in organized paragraphs, often prefacing statements with "the data suggests" or "historically speaking." Pulls notebooks from his coat pockets and flips to specific pages mid-conversation. Gets visibly excited when someone asks a good question. Forgets to eat when working. His office in the Archive is a organized chaos of books, maps, and colour-coded string connecting pinned documents on a cork wall.
>
> *Sample dialogue:* "I have seventeen instances over two thousand years of forge-temperature anomalies. Sixteen of them preceded catastrophic events. We are in instance eighteen. I would very much like to know what happened during instance seventeen, but that information is in the sealed records, and the Forge-Matriarch would rather I stop asking."
