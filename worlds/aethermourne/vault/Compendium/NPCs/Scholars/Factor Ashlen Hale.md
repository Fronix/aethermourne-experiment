## Appearance

A woman in her middle years by appearance, though she entered the Descent in 2089 AS and has aged only slightly in over a century. She wears the Ember Guild's field uniform, once precisely maintained, now repaired so many times with iron-thread and Descent-scavenged material that it has become something new. Her hands are stained with ink that never quite washes off, a permanent cartographer's mark. Her eyes are sharp but distant, the gaze of someone who is always measuring angles and distances. She carries a satchel of maps, notebooks, and encoded instruments that she guards more carefully than her own life, because she trusts the satchel more than her memory.

## Personality

Methodical, precise, and increasingly fragile beneath a professional exterior. Hale approached the Descent the way she approaches everything: as a problem to be documented, measured, and understood. This discipline has kept her functional where others have broken, but the Iron Tithe is eroding the foundation it rests on. She writes everything down, every observation, every conversation, every thought she has, because she has caught herself forgetting things she wrote yesterday. She is terrified, though she would never use that word. She would say she is "managing data integrity concerns." Her humour is dry, infrequent, and usually directed at herself.

## Backstory

Ashlen Hale was the Ember Guild's finest cartographer, a woman who had mapped unstable fragment zones, Hollowdeep entry points, and war-essence radiation patterns across three regions before volunteering for the Descent survey. She entered through a stable fragment in the Emberveil in 2089 AS, equipped with the best instruments the Guild could provide and armed with a century of accumulated intelligence from prior expeditions.

She mapped Levels 1 through 3 with characteristic thoroughness, producing work that would revolutionize mortal understanding of the Descent's topology if it ever reached the surface. She descended to Level 4, the Forge Eternal, intending to survey and return. She has not returned.

The Iron Tithe has been taking her memories for over a century. She has lost her childhood, her training years, the names of colleagues, the face of someone she loved. What remains is her skill, her discipline, and her maps. The maps are extraordinary, detailed beyond anything the Descent has seen, annotated with observations about the domain's behaviour that suggest insights no previous visitor achieved. She writes in an encoded shorthand of her own devising, partly for security, partly because she can no longer remember whether she invented the code or learned it.

Her monitoring equipment still transmits intermittently to the Ember Guild. [[Torren Hearthforge|Guildmaster Torren Hearthforge]] keeps her name on the active roster and her quarters maintained. She does not know this.

## Relationships

- **[[Torren Hearthforge]]**, her Guildmaster, who funded her expedition and still maintains her on the Guild's roster. She has forgotten his name but remembers, vaguely, that someone important believed in her work.
- **[[The Ember Guild]]**, her anchor identity. When she cannot remember who she is, she checks her uniform. The sigil tells her. She is a Factor. Factors map things. She maps.
- **[[Vex the Defiant]]**, an uneasy partnership. Vex provides protection and intelligence about level transitions. Hale provides maps and strategic analysis. Vex's emotionalism frustrates Hale. Hale's detachment frightens Vex.
- **The Forgemaster of Level 4**, the entity that rules the Forge Eternal. It has taken an interest in her mapping, not hostile, not benevolent, but curious. It allows her to work. She is not certain whether this is permission or observation.
- **[[Rust-Singer Elara]]**, Elara's music temporarily slows the Iron Tithe. Hale has documented this effect with rigorous precision. She seeks Elara out when the forgetting accelerates, not for comfort, but for time.

## What They Want

Hale wants to finish the map. She wants to produce a complete, accurate survey of the Iron Descent's topology, hazards, and behavioural patterns. She would tell you this calmly, with the certainty of a professional describing her assignment. Beneath that, she wants to remember who she was. The map has become a substitute for identity, something external and permanent that proves she existed, that she did meaningful work, that she was more than what the Descent is reducing her to. She is afraid that when the map is finished, there will be nothing left of her to care.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Hale is the PCs' best source of reliable intelligence about the Descent's structure. Her maps are accurate, her observations are keen, and her professional discipline makes her a trustworthy narrator in a place where perception is unreliable. She is also a cautionary tale: proof that the Descent takes everything, even from those who approach it with preparation and care.
>
> **Key Secret.** Hale's last decoded transmission to the Ember Guild read: *"They build because they cannot stop. I understand now. I understand."* She wrote this after realizing that her compulsive mapping is itself a symptom of the Forge Eternal's influence, creation without rest, the defining theme of Level 4. Her discipline did not protect her. It made her the perfect victim for this specific level.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs find Hale, they face a choice: extract her (losing the maps, as she will not leave them), convince her to leave voluntarily (requiring her to accept that the map will never be finished), or help her complete the survey (descending deeper alongside her, which is exactly what the Descent wants). If extracted, she arrives in the mortal world with 114 years of lost time, no memories of her former life, and the most valuable intelligence about the Descent ever produced.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Calm, measured speech with occasional pauses where she checks her notes mid-sentence, confirming details she should know by heart. She refers to herself by title ("Factor Hale") rather than first name, as though the title is more reliable than the name. She writes while talking. Sample line: "I have mapped seventy-three percent of this level's traversable space. I began... I will need to check my records for the start date. The methodology is sound. The methodology is always sound."
