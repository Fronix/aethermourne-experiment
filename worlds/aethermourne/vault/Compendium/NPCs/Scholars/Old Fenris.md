## Appearance

A gaunt, ancient man who looks older than his seventy-eight years. His skin has a faintly translucent quality, as if lit from within, and in low light you can see the tracery of his veins glowing with pale blue ley-light. His eyes are permanently luminous, a soft phosphorescent white that makes it impossible to tell where he's looking. His hair is long, thin, and white, and his hands tremble constantly except when he's reading the ley lines, at which point they become perfectly still. He wears layers of patched fenland clothing and carries a walking staff carved with symbols he won't explain.

## Personality

Half-mad, half-prophetic, and entirely aware that the distinction has become meaningless. Fenris speaks in a mixture of precise technical observation and cryptic poetry, often in the same sentence. He is gentle, patient with those who struggle to understand him, and given to long silences during which he appears to be listening to something no one else can hear. He is also terrified, has been for years, and hides it behind the eccentricity that people expect from him.

## Backstory

Fenris arrived in [[The Greymere Fenlands]] sixty years ago as a young man with a talent for sensing magical currents. He was running from something, he no longer remembers what, just a pressing need to be somewhere that no authority could reach him. The fenlands took him in, as they take everyone in, and the ley lines took him in a different way.

He was one of the first Fencrafters, though the term didn't exist when he started. He learned by doing, by standing in the surge-fields and letting the Aetheric Web wash through him, by listening to the water's hum until it resolved into something like language. Over decades, the exposure changed him. His body absorbed ley-energy the way a sponge absorbs water, permanently saturating his tissues with arcane charge. His skin began to glow. His eyes lost their colour. His dreams became someone else's memories.

He founded the [[The Confluence Council|Confluence Council]] thirty years ago, teaching others to read the ley lines' fluctuations so that settlements could prepare for surges. The Council grew around his expertise, but he has never sought to lead it. He reads the lines. He reports what he finds. What people do with the information is their business.

In recent years, the readings have changed. The ley lines are not just surging, they are carrying information, fragmented, degraded, but unmistakable. Fenris hears [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]]'s voice more clearly than any living mortal, and what she is saying frightens him: she is trying to warn them about something, but she can't remember what it is.

## Relationships

- **[[Maren Duskwater]]** , his closest friend and the person he trusts most. She is the only one who knows the full extent of what he hears. He advises her on everything, and she is one of the few people who can translate his cryptic warnings into actionable plans.
- **[[Wick]]** , he recognizes in them the same receptivity he had as a young man, but stronger, more focused, and more dangerous. He watches over them from a distance, guiding without explaining. He has not told Wick what the voices are because he believes they need to discover it themselves, or they won't believe it.
- **[[Brother Lumen]]** , Fenris met the Star-Reader before he left for [[Sporeling]] and found him brilliant, obsessive, and reckless. Fenris knows what Lumen is trying to do with the fungal network, and he is quietly terrified. He has not stopped him because he is not sure Lumen is wrong, only that the risks are catastrophic.
- **The other Council members** , they respect him as the founder and the most experienced reader, but most of them think he's losing his grip. They are not entirely wrong. The line between what Fenris hears from the ley lines and what he imagines has been blurring for years.

## What They Want

To understand Nethys's warning before it's too late. And, selfishly, to die before the ley-light in his body finishes replacing whatever used to be human about him.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Prophet, oracle, and tragic figure. Fenris provides cryptic but vital information about the Aetheric Web, Nethys's fragments, and the Second Silence. He is the NPC who understands the cosmic stakes better than anyone in the region, but his ability to communicate clearly is deteriorating.
>
> **Key Secret.** Fenris is dying. The ley-energy that saturates his body is slowly replacing his organic tissue with crystallized arcane matter. He is becoming, in effect, a living ley-crystal. He estimates he has one to two years before the process completes. He has told no one. He does not know whether the end result will be death, transformation into something inhuman, or a permanent connection to Nethys's fragmented consciousness. Any of these outcomes terrifies him.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** Fenris begins as the wise eccentric. As the Second Silence accelerates, his readings become more urgent and his transformation more visible. The PCs can seek his counsel, but must accept that his answers are increasingly tangled with Nethys's fragmented thoughts. His final arc is a choice: let the transformation complete (potentially gaining clarity at the cost of his humanity) or sever the connection (saving himself but losing the one channel capable of translating Nethys's warning).
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks slowly, pauses mid-sentence to listen to things that aren't there, then finishes with startling precision. Occasionally answers questions that haven't been asked yet, or responds to conversations that happened hours ago. His lucid moments are sharp and devastating. His hands glow brighter when he's agitated.
>
> *"The Web is screaming, child. Not in pain. In recognition. Something is coming that it remembers from before, and it is trying to tell us what happened last time. The trouble is, it can only speak in the language of a dead goddess, and she can only remember in pieces."*
