# Surveyor Verity Ashgrove

## Appearance

Early thirties, thin and precise in her movements, with ink-stained fingers and the faintly grey complexion of someone who spends too much time near raw [[Cosmology and Magic#Ashite|Ashite]]. Her dark hair is cut short for practicality, and she wears the dove-grey robes of a Keeper-caste scholar with a surveyor's leather apron over them, pockets full of chalk, calipers, and folded computation sheets. A brass Ashite focus hangs from her neck on a chain, the lens slightly cracked from overuse. She smells of chalk dust and cold stone.

## Personality

Methodical to a fault, which in the Dominion is considered a virtue. Verity thinks in numbers and spatial relationships, mapping the world as a series of geometric problems to be solved. She is quiet in groups, preferring to observe and calculate before speaking, but when she speaks it is with the uncomfortable precision of someone who has checked her figures three times and knows she is correct. She does not enjoy being correct. The numbers she is finding terrify her, and terror in a Calculist manifests as compulsive rechecking, sleepless nights, and a growing inability to make the figures say what her superiors want them to say.

## Backstory

Born in [[Ashveil]] to a family of minor Church administrators, Verity showed aptitude for [[Cosmology and Magic#Calculism|Calculism]] early and was elevated to Keeper caste at sixteen, a modest honor that her parents treated as the culmination of three generations of obedient service. She trained at the Athenaeum under Archscholar Verity Dunne (no relation, a coincidence that has caused her nothing but awkwardness) and specialized in geological survey, the mathematical mapping of Ashite vein networks beneath the Spine of Order.

Her work was routine for years. She mapped veins, calculated extraction rates, filed reports that confirmed what the Church expected: the Ashite would last generations. Then she was assigned to the deep shafts beneath [[Hearthstone]] and her numbers stopped cooperating. The veins were emptying faster than extraction could account for. Something was draining Ashite from below, or from within, at a rate her models could not explain. She filed her findings. The report was returned with a note suggesting she recalibrate her instruments. She recalibrated. The numbers got worse. She filed again. The report was "lost."

She has filed seven reports in the past two years. None have been acknowledged. She has begun keeping copies hidden in a sealed case beneath her field tent, written in a personal cipher that would take another Calculist weeks to decode.

## Relationships

- [[Edda Haelstrom]], the mine foreman at Hearthstone. Edda has noticed the same discrepancies from the production side. They have not compared notes yet, but Verity watches the foreman with the desperate recognition of someone who has found the only other honest person in the room
- Archscholar Verity Dunne, her former teacher at [[Ashveil]]. The Archscholar would understand her findings but is deeply embedded in Church hierarchy and would be obligated to report her
- [[The Cinders]], invisible adversaries. Verity does not know the Cinders exist as an organization, but she has noticed that certain mining redirections coincide with her reports being suppressed, a pattern she has documented without understanding its source
- Her survey team, three junior Calculists who trust her methodology but are increasingly nervous about the political implications of their work

## What They Want

Verity wants someone to read her reports and act on them. She is a scholar, not a revolutionary. She does not want to overthrow the Church or expose a conspiracy. She wants an authority figure to look at her numbers, confirm they are correct, and do something about the fact that the Dominion's foundation is being hollowed out. Her deeper need is permission to be afraid. The Dominion's culture punishes uncertainty, and Verity has been carrying mathematical certainty about a catastrophe while pretending she is merely "conducting further analysis."

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Verity's data, combined with [[Edda Haelstrom]]'s production records, forms the documentary case against [[Delric Mourne]]'s sabotage of Ashite extraction. She appears in Act II as a potential ally the PCs can recruit. Her evidence is the analytical complement to Edda's practical observations.
>
> **Key Secret.** Her seventh report, the one she never filed, contains a projection: at current depletion rates, the Spine's deep veins will be functionally exhausted within eight years, not the "generations" the Church promises. The shallow veins will follow within fifteen. She has not shown this projection to anyone.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs gain her trust, she shares the hidden reports and becomes a critical intelligence asset inside the Church's administrative structure. If ignored, she will eventually be discovered by the Cinders and reassigned to a remote posting where her findings cannot reach anyone who matters. Her survey team will be scattered.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in measured, precise sentences. Pauses before answering questions, visibly calculating. Fidgets with her cracked Ashite focus when anxious. Avoids eye contact when lying or omitting information, which she does badly. Her voice drops to a near-whisper when discussing her findings, as if the numbers themselves might be overheard.
> *"The instruments are calibrated. The methodology is sound. The numbers are what they are. I cannot make them be something else."*
