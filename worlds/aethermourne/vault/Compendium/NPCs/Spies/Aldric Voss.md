# Aldric Voss

## Appearance

A well-dressed man in his early forties with receding brown hair, pale eyes the color of shallow water, and the kind of forgettable face that makes him excellent at intelligence work. He dresses in Reach merchant-formal, dark wool and silver buttons, clothes expensive enough to signal status without drawing attention. His hands are ink-stained from reports, and he carries a leather portfolio stamped with the Concord's tide-mark seal. He smells of salt air, parchment, and the faint metallic tang of [[Cosmology and Magic#Serithine|Serithine]] corruption, though only someone attuned to divine resonance would notice. His voice is measured and bureaucratic, the tone of a man who has learned to sound helpful while saying nothing of value.

## Personality

First impression: competent, professional, unremarkable. Aldric is the kind of functionary who makes intelligence agencies work, organized, detail-oriented, loyal to his superiors, and utterly forgettable in a crowd. He speaks in measured tones, deflects difficult questions with procedural language, and has a gift for making people feel heard while steering them away from sensitive topics. Beneath the bureaucratic mask is a man who made a single terrible choice and has spent years justifying it. He is a true believer in [[The Veil Unbound|the Veil Unbound's]] cause, convinced that [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] freedom will usher in a new age of enlightenment, not the apocalypse his colleagues fear.

## Backstory

Aldric grew up in [[Tidewall]], the son of a customs inspector, and followed his father into the Concord's administrative ranks. He was recruited into the intelligence service at twenty-eight, rose through competence and discretion, and became senior intelligence officer under [[Cordell Raith|Tidekeeper Cordell Raith]] six years ago.

Three years ago, a [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] recruiter approached him with a question: what if the gods' deaths were not a tragedy but a liberation? What if [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]], imprisoned and vilified, was the only deity who truly understood human freedom? The argument was compelling, the evidence persuasive, and Aldric was tired of serving oligarchs who treated the Reach's people as resources to extract wealth from. He joined the cult willingly, convinced he was choosing enlightenment over ignorance.

Since then, he has fed carefully curated intelligence to Raith, suppressing reports of [[The Veil Unbound|Veil Unbound]] activity, exaggerating threats from rival factions, and subtly steering Concord policy away from actions that would disrupt the cult's operations. Raith, already a fatalist who believes [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] release is inevitable, accepts Aldric's reports without scrutiny and uses them to justify his own inaction. Aldric tells himself he is serving a higher truth. He does not yet understand that he is serving an imprisoned god's desperation.

## Relationships

- **[[Cordell Raith]]**, his superior and unwitting tool. Aldric respects Raith's intelligence but sees his fatalism as weakness. He feeds Raith intelligence designed to deepen that fatalism and paralyze the Concord's response to the cult.
- **[[Maren Selk]]**, growing threat. The First Tidekeeper's instincts are sharper than Raith's, and Aldric knows she suspects something is wrong with the intelligence she receives. He has been working to isolate her from direct access to raw reports.
- **[[Orenna Deepwell]]**, fellow compromised Tidekeeper. Aldric and Orenna coordinate through intermediaries but have never met directly. He knows she is [[The Whisperer]] but does not know her true identity.
- **[[The Veil Unbound]]**, his true allegiance. He reports directly to a cult handler he knows only as "the Advocate," a masked figure who meets him in Tidewall's dockside warehouses.
- **[[Sable]]**, occasional contact. Aldric has funneled intelligence to the cult's [[Driftmere]] operations through Sable's network, though he does not know her personally.

## What They Want

Aldric wants to serve [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] liberation, to be part of the transformation he believes will free humanity from the tyranny of dead gods and corrupt oligarchs. His deeper need is simpler and more tragic: he wants to believe his betrayal of the Concord, his lies to colleagues who trust him, his complicity in the cult's manipulations, serves a higher purpose. If [[The Veil Unbound|the Veil Unbound's]] promises are false, then he has spent three years destroying everything he once stood for in service of nothing. He cannot face that possibility, so he doubles down on the lie.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Internal Concord mole for [[The Veil Unbound]]. Exposing him could provide PCs with leverage over Raith or evidence to convince Selk of the cult's infiltration. Turning him (showing him proof that Serith's release means apocalypse, not liberation) could flip him into a double agent, but it would shatter him personally.
>
> **Key Secret.** All intelligence suppression within the Concord traces back to Aldric. He has been the primary filter between field reports and Tidekeeper decision-making for three years. If PCs gain access to raw intelligence reports and compare them to what reached the Tidekeepers, the pattern of omissions will point directly to him. He has kept meticulous personal records, a habit from his bureaucratic training, and those records would condemn him if discovered.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the cult's plans advance unchallenged, Aldric remains invisible, the perfect mole. If PCs investigate Concord intelligence leaks, he is vulnerable to exposure through pattern analysis or [[Maren Selk|Selk's]] growing suspicions. If confronted with proof that [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith's]] freedom means the Veil's collapse and mass death, he faces a choice: accept that he has been a tool of apocalypse and cooperate, or reject the evidence and flee to the cult. Either outcome breaks him.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in careful, procedural language. Deflects with "I'll need to review the files," "That's outside my jurisdiction," or "I'll pass that along to the Tidekeeper." Never volunteers information, always responds. Rubs the Concord seal on his portfolio when lying, a tell only the observant notice. Sample line: *"I appreciate your concern, but our intelligence on that matter is... inconclusive. I'd recommend consulting with Tidekeeper Raith's office directly. I can schedule a hearing for you, perhaps next month?"*
