## Appearance

Tharn is a stooped, narrow figure who seems to have been compressed by centuries of crouching in the bellows chambers. His spine curves sharply, forcing him to look up at anyone he addresses, and his arms are disproportionately long from decades of reaching into the pumping mechanisms. He is covered in soot so thoroughly that his original complexion is a matter of speculation, his skin a uniform matte black broken only by the whites of his eyes and the pink of his gums when he smiles, which is rarely. His fingers are long and sensitive, calloused at the tips like a musician's, and they never stop moving, tapping, adjusting, calibrating something invisible. He breathes in shallow, ragged wheezes, his lungs scarred by centuries of superheated air, and he speaks in the same rhythm, words pushed out between gasps like bellows themselves.

## Personality

Tharn is obsessive, single-minded, and possessed of a serenity that unnerves everyone around him. He has been at the bellows so long that fear burned away centuries ago, replaced by a calm, absolute conviction that the answer is in the airflow. He is not mad, or at least not in the way the Forge Eternal usually drives its workers mad. He is simply consumed by a problem he believes is solvable, and the fact that he has been solving it for over a thousand years has not shaken his faith. He is kind to those who visit him, in the distracted way of someone whose real attention is always elsewhere, always on the bellows, always on the breath of the forges.

## Backstory

In life, Tharn was a bellows-operator in a mortal foundry, a man of no particular ambition who loved the simple mechanics of airflow and combustion. He died of lung disease, his body ruined by the same work he adored, and [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s domain claimed him for the sin of contentment without ambition, a life spent serving a forge without ever aspiring to master it. In the Forge Eternal, he was assigned to the Bellows, and he has never left.

Over centuries, Tharn discovered something the other workers missed: the forges are not random in their output. The quality, shape, and durability of each piece correlates to the airflow feeding the flames. When the bellows breathe in a certain rhythm, the weapons last minutes longer. When the pressure hits a specific threshold, the armor briefly fits bodies that actually exist. Tharn has been mapping these correlations, adjusting the bellows by fractions of a degree, chasing the precise calibration that will make the forges produce something permanent. He is closer than anyone suspects. What he has not told anyone is what the forges produce when the airflow is perfect: not weapons at all, but music, a resonant harmonic that opens sealed passages and makes the workers stop and listen, and for a moment, forget to weep.

## Relationships

- [[Forge-Warden Irontooth]], the level's overseer. Irontooth tolerates Tharn's endless adjustments because they keep the bellows running smoothly. Tharn tolerates Irontooth's authority because the Forge-Warden leaves him alone, which is all Tharn has ever wanted from power.
- [[Apprentice Nil]], the Pattern Library cataloguer. Nil once asked Tharn what the forges "recognize" about her. He told her the forges breathe differently when she is near, as if adjusting for a heat source that is not fire. He has regretted the honesty ever since, not because it was wrong but because the truth frightened her.
- [[Smeltress Kora]], the master smith. Tharn has noticed that Kora's best work coincides with his best calibrations. He suspects they are connected, that her enduring creations and his harmonic airflow are two symptoms of the same phenomenon. He has not shared this theory because sharing it would mean admitting he does not fully understand his own work.
- [[The Overseer]], the awakening war-construct. Tharn is one of the few workers who is not afraid of the Overseer. He treats it as a fellow technician and appreciates the calibration weights it leaves him. He suspects the Overseer understands the bellows better than he does but lacks the language to say so.

## What They Want

Tharn wants to hear the forges sing. He has heard fragments, seconds of harmony when the calibration approaches perfection, and those fragments are enough to sustain him through centuries of adjustment. What truly drives him is a conviction that the forges are trying to say something, that [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s machinery contains a message that can only be heard when the breath is right, and Tharn intends to be the one who finally listens.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Tharn is the party's best source of practical intelligence about how the Forge Eternal actually works. He understands the machinery at an intuitive level that even Irontooth cannot match, and his calibration data could be the key to controlling, repairing, or shutting down the forges.
>
> **Key Secret.** Tharn knows what the forges are really making. When the calibration approaches perfection, the output is not weapons or armor but something else entirely: iron seeds, tiny, dense spheres of compressed potential that contain the blueprint for a living forge, a self-replicating production system that could build war-constructs anywhere, on any plane, without requiring a divine domain to power it. [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] was building the means to take his war-production mobile. Tharn found the first iron seed decades ago and has been hiding it in the bellows mechanism, studying it, trying to understand what it wants to grow into.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party earns his trust, he will share the iron seed and his calibration data. If they help him achieve perfect calibration, the forges will sing, sealed passages will open, and the party will gain access to parts of Level 4 that have been closed since the Theomachis. If [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] learns about the iron seeds, she will prioritize seizing the Forge Eternal above all other objectives in the Iron Descent.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks in wheezes, with pauses for breath in the middle of words. Taps surfaces compulsively, always testing resonance. His eyes go distant when he hears anything rhythmic, as if comparing it to the bellows' song. Surprisingly warm when he focuses on someone long enough to see them.
> *"The forges... are not broken. They are... waiting. For the right... breath. I have been breathing... for them... for a very long time. I am... almost... there."*
