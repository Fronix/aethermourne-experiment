# Dreamer Ysolde

## Appearance

Ysolde sits in a chamber of warm, golden light, surrounded by mirror-constructs that form the shapes of a family: a husband, two children, a dog, a hearth. She is smiling. She is always smiling. Her face has been frozen in an expression of gentle contentment for so long that the muscles have locked, the smile permanently etched into features that were once mobile and expressive. She is middle-aged, soft-bodied, with laugh lines that suggest a woman who found joy easily and often. Her hair is auburn, shot through with grey, and it falls around her shoulders in the exact style someone once told her was beautiful. Her eyes are open but see nothing that is actually in front of her. She wears a simple dress of iron-cloth that the mirrors have disguised as homespun wool, and her hands rest in her lap, occasionally making the motions of stirring a pot or mending a garment. She is warm to the touch. The light in her chamber is the warmest thing on Level 5.

## Personality

Ysolde is, by any measurable standard, happy. She radiates a contentment so genuine that it affects the air around her, warming the cold iron of the Hall of Mirrors into something that almost feels like home. She responds to visitors with maternal warmth, offering seats in chairs that do not exist, cups of tea brewed from nothing, smiles that have not changed in centuries. She calls everyone by names that are not theirs, names that belonged to people she loved, and her conviction is so complete that visitors sometimes respond to them. She is not aware that she is in the Iron Descent. She is not aware that she is dead. She is in her kitchen, with her family, on a warm afternoon, and she has been in that afternoon for a very long time.

## Backstory

In life, Ysolde was a farmer's wife in the mortal world, a woman of no particular ambition whose only desire was the life she already had: a home, a family, warmth, and enough. She died of a fever, quickly and without drama, and [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s domain claimed her for the sin of contentment, the same category that claimed [[Bellows-Keeper Tharn]]: a life lived without aspiration, without the hunger that Kaevroth demanded of all souls.

On Level 5, the mirrors gave Ysolde exactly what she wanted: her family, her home, her warm afternoon. Unlike the ambitious souls who receive thrones and conquests, Ysolde's desire was small and specific and complete. The mirrors had nothing to escalate, no grander vision to offer, no higher glory to tempt her with. So they simply gave her what she asked for, perfectly and permanently. She fused with the vision within the first year. The mirror-constructs of her family became as real to her as breathing, and the boundary between Ysolde and her dream dissolved entirely. She is now more a feature of Level 5 than a resident of it, a permanent installation of happiness in a place built for ambition.

The other residents of Gloryholme avoid her chamber. Even souls deep in their own delusions sense that something about Ysolde's situation is different, worse, more final. The mirrors gave every other soul a fantasy they could theoretically wake from. They gave Ysolde a fantasy she would never want to wake from, and that distinction makes her imprisonment absolute.

## Relationships

- [[Sovereign Kallis]], the ruler of Gloryholme. Kallis visits Ysolde regularly, treating her as a beloved citizen enjoying a peaceful retirement. Kallis cannot see the horror because the mirrors will not show it to her. In Kallis's perception, Ysolde is simply a happy woman who has earned her rest.
- [[The Truthsayer]], the blind prophet. The Truthsayer perceives Ysolde's true situation with devastating clarity and considers her the most disturbing thing on Level 5. She has described Ysolde as "a wound the domain is proud of."
- [[Lord Parvane]], the escaped reflection. Parvane sometimes stands outside Ysolde's chamber and watches, not entering, not speaking. He finds her situation clarifying: she is proof that happiness can be a more perfect prison than any cell.
- [[Mirror-Warden Caelus]], the hall's jailer. Caelus maintains Ysolde's chamber with particular care, ensuring the mirror-constructs remain stable. He considers her his finest work, which is the most honest thing he has ever been wrong about.

## What They Want

Ysolde wants nothing. She has everything. Her family is here, her home is warm, the afternoon sun slants through windows that do not exist, and she is stirring a pot of stew that smells like love. This is the horror: she has no desire that could be used as a lever to wake her. The only thing that could reach her is something the mirrors cannot simulate, something real, something imperfect, something that smells like actual earth and actual sweat rather than the iron-bright perfection of the Hall of Mirrors. A memory that the mirrors did not give her.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Ysolde is not a quest-giver or an ally. She is a moral challenge. The party must decide whether to wake her, knowing that waking her means destroying the only happiness she has ever known and replacing it with the knowledge that her family is gone, she is dead, and she is trapped in a dead god's domain. There is no good answer, and the party's choice reveals their values.
>
> **Key Secret.** Ysolde's dream is not entirely a mirror-construct. One element is real: the dog. A minor spirit, a fragment of animal loyalty that somehow found its way into the Hall of Mirrors, was drawn to Ysolde's warmth, and became part of her vision. The dog is the only genuine thing in her chamber, and it is the only thing the mirrors did not create. If the party can identify the real element, they can use it as an anchor to pull Ysolde out of the dream without shattering her entirely, though the process will still be agonizing.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party wakes her gently (using the dog as an anchor), Ysolde will emerge devastated but whole, carrying the memory of centuries of false happiness. She will be fragile, grief-stricken, and surprisingly resilient, because she survived the mortal world once before and can do it again. If they wake her violently (by smashing her mirrors), she will shatter, her identity unable to survive the sudden transition. If they leave her, she will remain in her dream forever, and the party will carry the knowledge of what they chose not to do.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks softly, warmly, always mid-conversation with someone who is not there. Offers food, asks after children, inquires about the weather. Calls PCs by the wrong names with absolute certainty. The warmth in her voice is real. The love is real. Everything else is not.
> *"Oh, you must be cold, come in, come in. There is stew on. Marten, get our guest a blanket, the good one, the wool one your grandmother made. Sit, sit. Tell me about your day."*
