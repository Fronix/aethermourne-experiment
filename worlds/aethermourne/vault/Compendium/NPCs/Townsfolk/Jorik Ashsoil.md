# Jorik Ashsoil

## Appearance

Late forties, weathered and lean, with the grey-tinged skin common to lifelong Ashfield farmers. His hands are rough and permanently stained the color of old pewter from decades of working the grey soil. He wears patched homespun dyed in the dull green-grey that passes for color in the Ashfields, and heavy boots caked with pale dust. His face is deeply lined, particularly around the eyes, from squinting across flat country under an overcast sky. He carries a notched tally-stick at his belt and a small leather journal that he consults with the reverence other men reserve for scripture.

## Personality

Stubborn, practical, and quietly furious. Jorik does not have the vocabulary of a scholar or the confidence of a leader, but he has the farmer's gift for noticing when things are wrong. He watches the soil, the crops, the Finger's glow, and the patterns of the seasons with an attention that most people reserve for prayer. When he speaks, it is in short declarative sentences delivered with the certainty of someone who has counted the same thing forty times and gotten the same answer. He distrusts anyone who tells him the numbers are wrong without looking at the numbers first.

## Backstory

Jorik's family has farmed the Ashfields around [[Greyfield]] for five generations, always within sight of the village's thirty-foot Finger. His grandfather told him that in the old days, the Finger glowed bright enough to read by at midnight. His father told him the glow was dimmer but steady. Jorik has watched it fade in his own lifetime, from a reliable pulse at dawn and dusk to a flicker that sometimes does not come at all.

Three years ago, he began measuring the Finger's output with his own crude methods: chalk marks on the Finger's base tracking the glow-line, crop yield tallies compared season over season, a rain gauge he built to prove the Finger's weather-stabilization was weakening. His journal is full of these measurements, scratched in a farmer's shorthand that no Calculist would recognize as data but which tells a clear story. The Finger is dying. The crops are thinning. The ash storms are hitting harder.

He petitioned Elder Seren Holloway to send for a Church surveyor. Holloway sent the petition to [[Ostivaar]] eight months ago. No response. Jorik has begun to suspect the silence is not bureaucratic delay but deliberate, and that suspicion is slowly transforming his frustration into something more dangerous.

## Relationships

- Elder Seren Holloway, the village leader of [[Greyfield]]. Jorik respects her but is losing patience with her faith that the Church will respond
- Young Jorin, the village boy with wild magic. Jorik has noticed that Jorin's uncontrolled magical surges have increased since the Finger began dimming, and he wonders if the two are connected
- The farming families of Greyfield, who look to Jorik as the one person willing to say aloud what they all suspect: the Church is not coming to help
- [[Verity Ashgrove]], unknown to Jorik. If the PCs connect the two, a Calculist surveyor who can read his data would give his crude measurements the scientific framework they need

## What They Want

Jorik wants someone with authority to look at his journal and confirm what he already knows: the Finger is being drained, not cycling. He would settle for a Church surveyor who takes his measurements seriously. His deeper need is simpler and more desperate: he wants to know that his children will be able to farm this land. If the Finger dies, Greyfield dies, and five generations of Ashsoil roots in this soil mean nothing.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Jorik is the ground-level witness to [[Delric Mourne]]'s agricultural sabotage. His crude measurements, while not scientifically rigorous, independently corroborate [[Verity Ashgrove]]'s Calculist data and [[Edda Haelstrom]]'s mining discrepancies. Three witnesses, three methods, one conclusion. He is the human cost of the conspiracy, the face the PCs put on the numbers.
>
> **Key Secret.** Last month, Jorik followed the Finger's root-line underground through a collapsed irrigation channel and found something he does not understand: a thin vein of [[Cosmology and Magic#Ashite|Ashite]] running from the Finger's base into the earth, visibly darker than it should be, as if the energy were flowing the wrong direction. He has not told anyone because he does not have the words for what he saw.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the PCs listen to him and investigate, Jorik becomes a loyal local ally with deep knowledge of the Ashfield terrain. If ignored, Greyfield's Finger will fail within the year, the village will petition for relocation, and Jorik will refuse to leave. He will be found dead in his fields the following spring, still tending crops that no longer grow.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speaks slowly, choosing words carefully, not because he is slow but because he does not waste them. Pulls out his journal to reference specific numbers when making a point. Looks at the ground when he talks, not from shame but because the ground is what he knows. Smells of grey soil and woodsmoke.
> *"I count what I count. The Finger was brighter last year. It was brighter the year before that. You don't need a Calculist to know which way a number goes."*
