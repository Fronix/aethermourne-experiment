# Old Rust

## Appearance

Old Rust looks like a sculpture left out in iron rain for centuries. His body is humanoid but only in the loosest sense, a frame of compressed ash and corroded metal that has fused into something between flesh and mineral. His skin is the exact color of the Ashfield, a grey so deep it absorbs what little light exists, and it has the texture of hammered plate left to oxidize. His face retains the suggestion of features, eye sockets that glow with a faint ember-light, a jaw that moves with a grinding sound like millstones. He sits near the base of the Warden's Nail in the same position every day, wrapped in a cloak of layered ash that has solidified around his shoulders like geological strata.

## Personality

Quiet, patient, and observational in a way that feels geological rather than personal. Old Rust does not hurry. He does not react to urgency. He speaks in sentences that take a long time to arrive and a longer time to finish, each word produced with a grinding effort that suggests the act of communication costs him something physical. Despite this, he is not hostile or dismissive. He is simply old in a way that makes mortal timescales feel irrelevant. He has a dry, iron-flavored humor that surfaces rarely and without warning, and a gentleness toward newcomers that contradicts his intimidating appearance.

## Backstory

Old Rust was here when the Ashfield was still warm. He does not remember arriving because he may not have arrived at all. He may have formed, condensing from the ash and iron debris in the aftermath of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s death, a revenant given sentience by the concentrated war-essence that saturated the domain during the Theomachis. Or he may have been a mortal soul who arrived so long ago that the distinction between "person who became ash" and "ash that became a person" no longer applies.

What he remembers, in fragments that surface like iron ore from stone, is the domain before it cooled. He remembers fire, purpose, the sound of Kaevroth's voice giving commands that shook the foundations of every level. He remembers the silence that followed, the awful, ringing emptiness when the god's presence withdrew. He watched the fires go out. He watched the grey creep in. He watched the first lost souls arrive and try to leave and fail. He has watched this cycle repeat for longer than most civilizations have existed.

## Relationships

- [[Erys Ironfall]], the Warden who governs Rustgate. Old Rust respects Erys as the only soul in the Ashfield stubborn enough to build something. He has never told Erys everything he knows, because some knowledge makes surviving here harder.
- [[The Ashen Crier]], whose company Old Rust tolerates. He sits near the Crier sometimes, unflinching through the babble, because he recognizes in the Crier's curse something familiar, the domain using a mortal as infrastructure.
- [[Dalla Threshborn]], who visits him when her optimism cracks. Old Rust does not offer comfort. He offers presence, which Dalla has learned is the Ashfield's only honest currency.
- [[Keeper Maren Ashveil]], whose shrine he has never entered. He watches it from a distance with something that might be reverence, might be grief.

## What They Want

Old Rust wants to remember what he was before the grey. He sits near the Warden's Nail because he believes, in a way he cannot articulate, that the tower contains something from the time before, a memory, a fragment, an echo of the domain when it still had fire. What drives him deeper is simpler: he does not want to dissolve. Every ash revenant eventually loses coherence and drifts apart. Old Rust has held himself together through will alone for longer than should be possible, and the effort is growing harder.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Old Rust is the Ashfield's living memory. He can provide exposition about the domain's history, Kaevroth's nature, and the Theomachis-era Iron Descent that no other NPC on Level 1 possesses. He is also a source of the map mentioned in the Ashfield's encounter hooks.
>
> **Key Secret.** Old Rust is not a revenant in the normal sense. He is a splinter of the domain's consciousness, a fragment of the Ashfield's awareness that gained individuality. He is to Level 1 what Warlord Keth is to Level 2, an immune response, a maintenance function that developed personhood. Unlike Keth, Old Rust suspects the truth. He has felt the domain's pull, the urge to dissolve back into the collective ash, and he recognizes it not as entropy but as reabsorption.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party gives Old Rust something with genuine color from the mortal world, it triggers a cascade of memories that nearly overwhelms him. He remembers the Ashfield in fire, Kaevroth's voice, and critically, the location of a passage to the lower levels that bypasses the Descent Gate. If the domain continues to decay, Old Rust will eventually lose his fight against dissolution, becoming the most significant loss Rustgate has ever suffered.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Speak slowly, with grinding pauses between phrases. Each word sounds like it is being forged in the moment of speaking. Never use contractions. Refer to time in geological terms ("seasons of ash," "the iron age before this one"). Sample line: *"I have. Sat here. Through the dissolution of. More souls than your world has. Named. They all. Burned brightly. And then. They were ash. You will be. Different, you think. They all. Thought that."*
