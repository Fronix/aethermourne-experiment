# The Shattered Man

## Appearance

The Shattered Man is seven people, or one person in seven pieces. Each fragment looks like a variation of the same man: middle-aged, lean, with sharp features and dark hair going grey at the temples. But each version is subtly different, shaped by the single trait it carries. The Brave one stands taller, shoulders back, jaw set. The Clever one is thinner, eyes constantly moving. The Kind one has softer features, hands open. The Cruel one has the same face hardened into angles, mouth a thin line. The Afraid one is hunched, eyes wide. The Ambitious one carries himself like a king. The Nothing one is the hardest to look at: the same face, emptied, a sketch that was never finished. They all wear identical iron-cloth, and they all move through the Gallery of Triumphs in overlapping orbits, sometimes clustered together, sometimes scattered to different wings, arguing in fragments of sentences that only make sense when you hear all seven sides.

## Personality

There is no single personality, only seven partial ones, and they interact with the world and each other in constantly shifting configurations. The Brave one speaks first in dangerous situations. The Clever one negotiates. The Kind one apologizes for the others. The Cruel one says what the others will not. The Afraid one warns of consequences. The Ambitious one pushes for action. The Nothing one says nothing, stares, and occasionally weeps without knowing why. When they cooperate, they can be surprisingly effective, seven perspectives on every problem, seven instincts checking and balancing each other. When they argue, they become incoherent, seven voices speaking over each other in a chorus of fractured identity. They refer to themselves in the plural and have accepted, with varying degrees of grief, that they will never be singular again.

## Backstory

In life, the Shattered Man was a mortal historian named Aldren Voss, a scholar who devoted himself to recording the truth of the Theomachis. He traveled battlefields, interviewed survivors, catalogued atrocities with unflinching precision. His ambition was simple: to ensure that what happened was remembered accurately, without embellishment or softening. He died old, surrounded by his work, and [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s domain claimed him for the sin of ambition applied to preservation rather than conquest, a historian's sin, the desire to make truth permanent.

On Level 5, the mirrors tried to give Aldren his heart's desire: a world where his histories were celebrated, his accuracy honored, his life's work recognized as definitive. But Aldren knew the mirrors were lying. He had spent a lifetime recognizing historical fabrication, and the mirrors' constructed glory was the crudest fabrication he had ever seen. He resisted. He fought. He tried to document the truth of the Hall of Mirrors the way he had documented the Theomachis.

The mirrors responded by shattering him. If he would not accept a single glorious lie, they gave him seven partial truths, splitting his soul along the lines of his strongest traits and distributing each into a separate reflection. The fragments stepped out of seven different mirrors simultaneously, and the original was gone, distributed among them like a book torn into chapters. No single fragment has enough of Aldren to be him, and together they are too many to be anyone.

## Relationships

- [[The Truthsayer]], the blind prophet. The Truthsayer can see all seven fragments simultaneously and knows which one holds the core of the original Aldren. She has not told them, because the revelation would destabilize the fragment that holds the others together.
- [[Sovereign Kallis]], the ruler of Gloryholme. Kallis appointed the Shattered Man as curator of the Gallery of Triumphs, a role he accepted because it let him study the lies the mirrors create. The Ambitious fragment considers it an honor. The Clever fragment considers it a research position. The Kind fragment considers it penance.
- [[Lord Parvane]], the escaped reflection. Parvane observes the Shattered Man with sympathy and horror, seeing in him a more extreme version of his own fragmented existence. They occasionally share drinks, which requires seven cups.
- [[Mirror-Warden Caelus]], the hall's jailer. Caelus considers the Shattered Man a stable system: seven fragments in equilibrium, none dominant, none dissolving. He monitors them the way an engineer monitors a load-bearing structure.

## What They Want

Each fragment wants something different, and these desires cannot be reconciled. The Brave one wants to fight the mirrors. The Clever one wants to understand them. The Kind one wants to forgive them. The Cruel one wants to shatter them the way they shattered him. The Afraid one wants to hide. The Ambitious one wants to reassemble, to become Aldren again, whole and singular. The Nothing one wants to stop existing. What the original Aldren would have wanted, if he could still want as one person, was simpler: to finish his work. To complete the history he was writing. To record the truth of the Hall of Mirrors so that someone, someday, would know what happened here.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** The Shattered Man is both a puzzle and a moral dilemma. The party must decide whether to try to reassemble him (difficult, potentially impossible, and guaranteed to destroy at least one fragment), help his fragments cooperate more effectively, or accept his condition and work with the seven as they are.
>
> **Key Secret.** The Nothing fragment is the key. It appears empty, purposeless, the leftover after the six defined traits were distributed. But Nothing is not emptiness. It is the capacity for change, the undefined potential that allows a person to grow, to surprise themselves, to become someone they were not. The mirrors could not categorize it, so they filed it as nothing. If the party recognizes what the Nothing fragment truly is, they can use it as the seed for reassembly, letting the undefined potential draw the other six back into a coherent whole. But the reassembled Aldren will not be the same man who was shattered. He will be something new.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the party reassembles him using the Nothing fragment, Aldren Voss is reborn as someone changed, a historian who has experienced the dissolution of self and can now document the Iron Descent with a perspective no mortal has ever possessed. If they help the fragments cooperate without reassembly, the Seven become a functional collective, less than whole but more than broken. If they fail or do not try, the fragments will eventually drift apart, each one fading until the Gallery of Triumphs has no curator and Aldren Voss is truly lost.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Voice the fragments distinctly: Brave is firm and steady, Clever is quick and dry, Kind is soft and hesitant, Cruel is clipped and cold, Afraid is whispery and rushed, Ambitious is loud and certain, Nothing is almost inaudible, barely a breath. They interrupt each other constantly. They finish each other's sentences, sometimes correctly, sometimes not.
> *Brave: "We can help you." Clever: "For a price." Kind: "That sounds mercenary, I'm sorry." Cruel: "It IS mercenary. Everything here is." Afraid: "What if they don't pay?" Ambitious: "Then we take what we're owed." Nothing: "..."*
