# Expeditions into the Descent

> [!quote]
> *"Everyone who enters comes back less. The question is whether what remains is worth the cost."*
> , Arch-Reader Thessaly Vane, final recorded statement

---

## Overview

The Iron Descent is not a place mortals are meant to visit. It is a collapsed divine domain, sealed within the [[The Shattered Empyrean|Shattered Empyrean]], accessible only through [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s scattered iron fragments. Every fragment is a potential doorway. Most remain closed. Some open unpredictably. A few have been forced open deliberately.

What follows is the most complete record of mortal expeditions into the Descent, compiled from [[The Ember Guild]]'s fragment registry, [[The Star-Readers]]' theoretical archives, and the [[The Ashen Vigil|Ashen Vigil]]'s sealed reports. Many details are contested. Some are classified. All are incomplete.

---

## 1. The Accidental Entry (~200 AS)

The first known mortal contact with the Descent was not an expedition at all. A miner working a deep shaft near the [[The Ashen Dominion#The Spine of Order|Spine of Order]] cracked through a layer of compressed stone and struck something that was not stone: a buried Kaevroth fragment, warm to the touch and humming with a frequency that made his teeth ache.

He fell through.

The accounts vary on how long he was gone. His mining crew said three days. The miner, whose name survives in no reliable record, said it felt like months. He returned twenty years older, his hair white, his hands shaking, unable to stop talking about *iron cities* and *towers that grew from the ground like teeth* and *a sky the color of old blood*.

He was dismissed as a madman. The shaft was sealed. The miner died within the year, of no identifiable cause, though witnesses reported that he spent his final weeks pacing in circles and muttering about *the price of wanting*.

No one connected his experience to Kaevroth for another six centuries.

## 2. The Vigil's First Descent (847 AS)

Commander **Aldric Solhane** of the [[The Ashen Vigil|Ashen Vigil]] led forty soldiers into the Hollowdeep, the vast cavern network beneath [[The Pale Wastes]], to investigate reports of organized undead activity. The dead in the Wastes are typically feral or confused, remnants of [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s broken domain stumbling through the border between life and death. Organized undead, undead acting with purpose and coordination, suggested an external influence.

Solhane's company found the influence. Deep in the Hollowdeep, where Morrhael's domain and Kaevroth's fragments overlap, they discovered what they called the **Bone Gate**: a doorway framed in fused iron and calcified remains, pulsing with a rhythm like a slow heartbeat. The organized undead were not being commanded. They were being *drawn*, pulled toward the gate by the ambient ambition radiating from the Descent, given purpose by a domain that feeds on wanting.

Solhane ordered his company through.

Twelve soldiers returned. Solhane was not among them. The survivors' report was classified by the Vigil's commanding council and has never been released to the public. What is known, pieced together from the personal journals of three survivors, is fragmentary:

- They emerged on Level 6, the Conquest, bypassing the upper levels entirely through the Bone Gate.
- The level was populated by beings that wore military uniforms from no mortal nation.
- Solhane attempted to negotiate passage. The negotiation succeeded. The price was not recorded.
- Twenty-eight soldiers did not return. Their current status is unknown.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Solhane is still alive, or what passes for alive in the Descent. He accepted a command position on Level 6 in exchange for his company's passage home, not realizing the domain's terms were absolute. He has been fighting an endless territorial war on the Conquest for over 1,300 years, unable to stop because stopping would mean admitting defeat, and the Descent will not permit that.

## 3. The Scholar's Folly (1023 AS)

**Prior Essek**, a theologian of the [[Order of the Ashen Flame]], became convinced that Kaevroth's fragments were not merely dangerous artifacts but communication devices, that the god's essence, scattered across the continent, could be contacted and reasoned with. He spent eleven years studying fragment behavior, documenting resonance patterns, and developing a theory of *divine sympathy* that would allow a mortal mind to interface with Kaevroth's residual consciousness.

He activated a fragment in a controlled setting beneath the Dominion's theological archives in [[Ostivaar]]. He entered Level 1.

He never returned.

His journal was found at the entry point, open to its final page. The last entry reads:

> *"The Ashfield is not hostile. It is welcoming. That is far worse. I can see a city on the horizon. It looks like Ostivaar if Ostivaar were honest about what it"*

The sentence ends there. The remaining pages are blank except for a single word, written in a hand that does not match Essek's, on the very last page: **DEEPER**.

The theological archives sealed the fragment and classified Essek's research. His findings on fragment resonance patterns were later rediscovered independently by [[The Ember Guild]].

## 4. The Star-Reader Expedition (1203 AS)

The most scientifically rigorous expedition into the Descent was organized by [[The Star-Readers]], who had developed theoretical models of post-Theomachis pocket planes and wanted empirical confirmation. **Arch-Reader Thessaly Vane** led a team of seven through a fragment that the Star-Readers had been monitoring for decades, one embedded in a cliff face on the northern coast whose resonance patterns were predictable enough to allow a controlled entry.

Vane's team reached Level 3, the Iron Market. They spent what they estimated as nine days inside, though only four days passed in the mortal world. Vane kept meticulous notes, and her account remains the most detailed description of the Descent's structure, economy, and inhabitants that any mortal has produced.

One survivor returned: Vane herself.

She emerged speaking in two voices. Her own, precise and scholarly, reporting observations and measurements. And something else's, a deeper register that commented on her observations with corrections, clarifications, and occasional amusement. Vane appeared aware of the second voice but unable or unwilling to silence it.

Over the following three months, Vane transcribed everything she had witnessed into the Star-Readers' archives. The account describes the Iron Market in extraordinary detail: its tiered bazaars, its currency of memory, its merchants who buy and sell mortal desires as physical commodities. She documented the [[Laws of the Descent|Iron Tithe]], the gravity of ambition, and the time dilation with a rigor that has not been matched since.

Thessaly Vane died ninety-three days after her return. The cause was recorded as "essential depletion," a Star-Reader euphemism for a soul that has been partially consumed. The second voice did not die with her. Her colleagues report that her personal effects, her journals, her instruments, her reading chair, occasionally speak in that deeper register when no one is looking directly at them.

## 5. The Cinder Probe (1847 AS)

[[The Cinders]], the Dominion's secret police, took a characteristically pragmatic approach to the Descent. They did not send scholars or soldiers. They sent twenty condemned prisoners through a fragment, fitted with monitoring amulets designed by Ember Guild artificers and promised pardons they would never receive.

Three returned.

All three were altered. Their eyes had developed a metallic sheen that caught the light at wrong angles. Their emotional responses were flattened, as though certain feelings had been surgically removed. They spoke of the Descent with a familiarity that suggested weeks of experience, though they had been gone for only six hours.

Two of the three proved valuable. Their time in the Descent had stripped away fear, hesitation, and most forms of empathy, qualities that the Cinders found professionally useful. They became operatives of uncommon effectiveness, willing to do whatever was asked without the internal resistance that limits most agents.

The third became something else. Exposure to the Descent had not stripped her emotions. It had replaced them with a single, overwhelming conviction: that the Iron Court's will was the only order worth serving. She became the most fanatical convert the Whisperer, Level 7's intelligence network, had ever produced, a mortal agent of the Descent operating openly in the mortal world. The Cinders did not realize this for seven years. By then, she had compromised three intelligence operations and recruited two other agents to the Whisperer's cause.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Cinders still have the two "useful" operatives on their roster. [[Delric Mourne|Thane Delric Mourne]] considers them reliable assets. He does not know that all three returnees are compromised, that the Descent's influence is subtler than simple conversion. The two "useful" operatives are not agents of the Iron Court, but their emotional dulling makes them perfect vectors for fragment influence. They carry trace amounts of the Descent's essence in their bodies, and every person they interact with is exposed to a faint, nearly undetectable aura of ambient ambition.

## 6. The Ember Guild Mapping Expedition (2089 AS)

**Factor Ashlen Hale**, a senior cartographer of [[The Ember Guild]], volunteered for what the Guild termed a "comprehensive survey mission." Armed with Thessaly Vane's century-old accounts, updated Star-Reader models of pocket-plane topology, and the best fragment-monitoring equipment the Guild could produce, Hale entered a stable fragment in the Emberveil with the stated intention of mapping Levels 1 through 4.

She has not returned.

Hale's monitoring equipment still transmits intermittently. Her life signs register as present but fluctuating, spiking during what the Guild's analysts interpret as moments of intense activity and dropping to near-nothing during what might be sleep, or might be something else. The signal's location, as best the Guild can triangulate, places her somewhere around Level 4, [[Level 4 - The Forge Eternal|the Forge Eternal]].

She has been inside for one hundred and fourteen years.

[[Torren Hearthforge|Guildmaster Torren Hearthforge]] maintains Hale's name on the Guild's active roster. Her equipment budget is still funded. Her quarters in the Guild's Ostivaar chapterhouse are kept as she left them. Whether this is loyalty, denial, or a political statement about the Guild's commitment to its people is debated internally.

The monitoring equipment has, on three occasions, transmitted something other than life signs: brief bursts of encoded data that the Guild's cryptographers have partially decoded. The fragments suggest Hale has mapped extensive portions of Level 4 and has made contact with the Forge Eternal's inhabitants. One decoded segment appears to read: *"They build because they cannot stop. I understand now. I understand."*

## 7. The Current Crisis (2200+ AS)

The Second Silence is destabilizing the divine mechanisms that have contained the Descent for over two millennia. Fragment resonance events, once rare enough to be catalogued individually, are now occurring weekly across the continent. The boundary between the Descent and the mortal world is thinning.

People are falling in.

Most are accidental entries: miners, travelers, soldiers camped too close to buried fragments that have begun to activate spontaneously. Some emerge within hours, shaken and aged. Some never return. A few come back changed, speaking of iron cities and endless grey plains with a familiarity that suggests they were expected.

The Iron Court, the political machinery of Level 7, has noticed the uptick. Intercepted fragment transmissions suggest the Court is adjusting its operations to account for increased mortal traffic, establishing processing protocols for new arrivals, and expanding the Ashfield's capacity to receive and sort incoming souls.

The Descent is not merely becoming more accessible. It is preparing for guests.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Quest Hooks from the Expeditions**
>
> - **The Solhane Recovery:** The Ashen Vigil has recently unsealed Solhane's file. A young officer has been asking questions. If the PCs learn that Solhane is still alive on Level 6, retrieving him could provide invaluable intelligence about the Descent's military structure, but extracting someone who has been fighting for 1,300 years presents unique challenges.
> - **Vane's Voice:** Thessaly Vane's speaking artifacts are held in a Star-Reader archive. The second voice has become more active recently, offering unsolicited commentary to anyone who handles Vane's effects. It claims to have information about Level 3's economy that could be exploited, for a price.
> - **Hale's Signal:** The Ember Guild will pay handsomely for anyone willing to enter the Descent and locate Factor Hale. Her mapping data could be invaluable. The Guild does not mention that her last decoded transmission suggests she may no longer want to leave.
> - **The Cinder Moles:** If the PCs discover that the Cinders' Descent operatives are compromised, exposing them could cripple Cinder operations in a specific region, but it would also alert the Iron Court that mortals are aware of its infiltration methods.
> - **The Accidental Arrivals:** With fragment activations increasing, the PCs may encounter someone who has just fallen through and returned. These recent returnees are the freshest sources of intelligence about the Descent's current state, but they are also the most vulnerable to ongoing influence.
