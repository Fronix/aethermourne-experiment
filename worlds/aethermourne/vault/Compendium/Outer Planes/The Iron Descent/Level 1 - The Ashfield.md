## Description

The Ashfield is where the mortal world bleeds into [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s domain, and the transition is gentle enough to be dangerous. Endless grey plains stretch in every direction, covered in fine iron ash that rises in slow, drifting curtains with each step. The ground crunches underfoot like frozen snow, but warmer, faintly warm, as though something vast exhaled beneath the surface not long ago. On the horizon, rusted towers stand in jagged silhouette, leaning at angles that suggest they were once much taller.

The air tastes of metal. Not the sharp tang of fresh-forged steel, but the dull, oxidized flavor of something ancient and corroded. It coats the tongue, dries the throat, and after a few hours becomes so familiar that you stop noticing. That is the first surrender. The sky is a flat, featureless grey, neither overcast nor clear, simply absent of color. There is light, but it has no visible source, no warmth, no direction. Shadows fall inconsistently, as though the Ashfield cannot decide what time it is.

Everything here is grey. The ash, the sky, the distant towers, the ground beneath, all rendered in shades of iron and dust. The only color that exists in the Ashfield is what visitors bring with them. And it fades. Clothing dulls within hours. Hair loses its luster. Eyes go pale. The process is slow enough to rationalize, fast enough to notice if you're watching for it. Most newcomers are not watching. They are listening to the whisper.

It is not a voice, exactly. More a feeling that kindles in the chest, a restless certainty that something better lies deeper. The Ashfield does not threaten. It does not punish. It simply makes staying feel like settling, and going deeper feel like ambition. That is [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s first and most elegant trap: the promise that you deserve more than this grey, empty place, and that more is waiting just one level down.

## The City: Rustgate

Rustgate is a sprawl of corroded iron structures huddled around the largest intact tower on the Ashfield, a listing spire called the Warden's Nail. The settlement has no walls, no gates despite its name, only a perimeter marked by iron stakes driven into the ash at irregular intervals. The stakes hum faintly when the ash-winds blow, a low drone that residents call "the lullaby." It is the closest thing Rustgate has to a heartbeat.

The city functions as a waystation, refugee camp, and trap in equal measure. Souls arrive here through cracks in the planar membrane, pulled by fragments of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s influence in the mortal world, by battlefields, by moments of consuming ambition, by proximity to iron fragments at the hour of death. Some arrive alive, having found passage through ritual or accident. All of them share the same conviction upon arrival: this is temporary. They will find what they came for and leave. The Ashfield has heard this promise from every soul who has ever set foot on its grey plains. The ash is made of the ones who stayed.

### Notable Residents

- **Warden Erys Ironfall**, the closest thing Rustgate has to a leader. He maintains order not through authority but through the simple fact that he has been here longer than anyone and still remembers his own name. He assigns work, settles disputes, and quietly prevents newcomers from descending before they understand what that means.
- **The Ashen Crier**, a figure who stands at the edge of Rustgate and shouts warnings into the grey. No one knows their original name. They speak in fragments of prophecy, most of it nonsense, some of it devastatingly accurate.
- **Dalla Threshborn**, a mortal who arrived as a scholar and became the Ashfield's most resourceful smuggler. She insists she can leave whenever she chooses. She has been saying this for what she believes is three weeks. It has been much longer.
- **Old Rust**, the oldest resident anyone can identify. He sits near the Warden's Nail and trades stories for food. His skin has taken on the color of the ash.
- **Keeper Maren Ashveil**, who maintains a small shrine to no god in particular, a place of quiet in a place that is already too quiet.
- **Scavenger Polk**, who ranges the outer Ashfield collecting salvage from the rusted towers and trading it in Rustgate. He claims to know paths that lead back to the mortal world. He has never taken one.

## Notable Locations

**The Warden's Nail**, the central tower of Rustgate. Fifty feet of rusted iron, leaning twelve degrees south and held upright by faith and structural stubbornness. Erys Ironfall governs from its upper chamber. The tower predates any living memory, and its lower levels descend into darkness that no lantern has fully mapped.

**The Fade Line**, an unmarked boundary roughly three miles from Rustgate where the grey intensifies. Beyond it, color drains faster, the whisper grows louder, and the distant towers seem closer. Experienced Ashfield residents know not to cross it without purpose. Newcomers cross it without realizing it exists.

**The Shard Garden**, a field of iron fragments jutting from the ash like broken teeth. Each shard hums with faint war-essence, remnants of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s shattered body that were too small to fall as far as the mortal world. Proximity breeds irritability, then aggression, then a focused, burning certainty that you have enemies.

**The Descent Gate**, a massive iron archway half-buried in ash at the Ashfield's lowest point. Beyond it, the path leads down to [[Level 2 - The Proving Grounds|The Proving Grounds]]. The gate is always open. No one guards it. No one needs to. The people who reach it have already decided.

## Native Creatures

**Ash Revenants**, humanoid shapes formed from compressed ash that rise from the plains when disturbed. They are not hostile by nature, merely echoes of souls who dissolved into the Ashfield long ago. They mimic the movements of the living, following travelers at a distance, pantomiming conversations. They become dangerous only when ignored for too long, as though neglect offends whatever remains of them.

**Fragment Hounds**, low, canine shapes that hunt the Shard Garden and surrounding plains. Their bodies are laced with iron splinters, and their bite carries war-essence that inflames aggression in the wounded. They hunt in silence, which is worse.

**Threshold Crawlers**, pale, many-limbed things that inhabit the spaces between the mortal world and the Ashfield. They cluster near planar thin points and feed on the disorientation of new arrivals. More scavenger than predator, but scavengers with very little restraint.

**Ash Typhons**, rare and massive, storms of animated ash that sweep the open plains. They are not creatures in any biological sense, but they hunt with purpose, drawn to strong emotion, particularly ambition. A Typhon that catches a traveler strips color, memory, and eventually identity, adding another layer to the ash.

## History

The Ashfield was once the outermost reach of [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s divine domain, the threshold where he welcomed challengers, supplicants, and those who sought his favor. In the age before the Theomachis, this level blazed with iron fire, a crucible that tested the worthy before they could descend to petition the war-god. It was a place of honor.

After Kaevroth's death and the shattering of his body, the Ashfield cooled. The fires went out. The iron oxidized. The grey crept in. But the domain did not die, it continued, running on momentum and residual divine will like a heart that keeps beating after the body has gone cold. The whisper that draws souls deeper is not Kaevroth's voice. It is the shape his ambition left in the architecture of this place, an echo so perfect it might as well be the original.

## Encounters & Hooks

1. **The Newcomer's Bargain.** A recently arrived soul, still vivid with color, begs the party to help them find the exit. They claim to have been pulled here against their will. This is true. What they do not mention is that the iron fragment that pulled them is embedded in their chest, and removing it may kill them.
2. **Old Rust's Map.** Old Rust claims to possess a map of the lower levels, drawn by a soul who descended and returned. He will trade it for something with color, anything from the mortal world that still holds its hue. The map is genuine. The route it describes is no longer safe.
3. **The Shard Garden Dispute.** Two factions in Rustgate are escalating toward violence over control of the Shard Garden. Both sides are certain the other started it. Neither is wrong, the shards are inflaming them both. The party can mediate, pick a side, or destroy the shards, each option with consequences.
4. **Dalla's Research.** Dalla Threshborn has discovered something in the lower chambers of the Warden's Nail and needs an escort. What she has found is a intact fragment of Kaevroth's memory, a moment of the god's life preserved in iron. Experiencing it is transformative and not entirely safe.
5. **The Ashen Crier's Warning.** The Crier's ravings suddenly become coherent: they describe the party by name, predict an event that has not yet occurred, and then collapse into ash. A new Crier appears at the edge of Rustgate within hours, already screaming.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Connection.** The Ashfield serves as the primary entry point for any mortal expedition into The Iron Descent. If the [[Ember Guild]] discovers the location of the Heart of Iron beneath the Ashen Dominion's capital, they may seek passage through the Descent to understand Kaevroth's domain and how his fragments connect to the surviving architecture of his plane.
>
> **The Whisper's Source.** The whisper that draws souls deeper is not random ambient magic. It is a standing command, the last order Kaevroth gave before the Theomachis: "Send me soldiers." The domain is still obeying. Every soul that descends past the Ashfield feeds the deeper levels with raw material, ambition and will, that the domain uses to maintain itself. Rustgate exists because Erys Ironfall figured this out and has been quietly discouraging descent ever since, but she cannot stop the willing.
>
> **Planar Thin Points.** The Ashfield has three known thin points connecting to the mortal world, one in [[The Hollowed Reach]], one in [[The Ashen Dominion]], and one whose mortal-side location is unknown. The Reach thin point is near a Kaevroth fragment. The Dominion thin point is directly above the Heart of Iron. This is not coincidence.
