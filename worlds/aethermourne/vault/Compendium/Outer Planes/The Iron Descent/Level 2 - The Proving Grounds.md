## Description

The sound reaches you before anything else. Metal on metal, screaming, the wet percussion of impact, the grinding roar of formations colliding, all of it layered into a wall of noise that comes from every direction at once. The Proving Grounds are vast, a continental expanse of iron-hard terrain stained in colors that shift between rust and something wetter. Armies fight here. They have always been fighting here. They will not stop.

The sky is the color of a bruise, deep violet and sickly yellow swirling in slow rotation above battlefields that stretch beyond sight. There is no sun, but the bruised light is bright enough to fight by, which is the point. The ground is flat, featureless iron plate scarred with trenches, craters, and the compressed remains of ten thousand engagements. Nothing grows. Nothing rests. The air smells of ozone and copper and the particular staleness of exertion that never ends.

The wars here have no cause, no objective, no resolution. Armies form from the souls that descend past [[Level 1 - The Ashfield|The Ashfield]], drawn by the whisper's promise that glory waits below. They arrive and are given weapons, armor, a banner, an enemy. They fight. They fall. They reform, wounds closing, memories blurring, and they fight again. The cycle has no rhythm, no pause, no season. It is war as weather, war as geology, war as the fundamental state of existence on this level.

What makes the Proving Grounds truly terrible is not the violence but the erosion. Each cycle of death and reformation strips something away. Identity dissolves. Purpose simplifies. After enough cycles, a soul no longer remembers why it fights, only that it must. The oldest combatants on the Grounds are little more than silhouettes, iron-grey shapes that swing weapons with mechanical precision and no awareness at all. [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] did not design this as punishment. He designed it as refinement, a forge that burns away everything except the will to struggle. The fact that it destroys what it claims to perfect is the central lie of ambition without wisdom.

## The City: The Crucible of Names

The Crucible sits at the center of the Proving Grounds, a massive complex of arenas, barracks, healing houses, and record halls built from black iron and the compressed remains of broken weapons. It is the only place on this level where combat is forbidden, enforced not by law but by the architecture itself. Weapons drawn within the Crucible's walls become too heavy to lift. Fists raised in anger lock in place. The city enforces peace through the simple mechanical fact that violence does not function here.

You arrive at the Crucible nameless. This is literal. Whatever name you carried from the Ashfield or the mortal world dissolves at the gate, pulled from your memory like a tooth. You are given a number. Your name is earned, taken from the defeated in sanctioned arena combat. The name you win becomes yours until someone takes it from you. The strongest warriors in the Crucible carry names that have passed through dozens of holders, names heavy with accumulated reputation that precedes their current owner.

The city's social structure is brutally simple. Named souls outrank numbered souls. Souls with older names outrank those with newer ones. Warlord Keth, who has held the same name for longer than anyone can verify, sits at the apex. The system is self-reinforcing: the named have access to better equipment, better quarters, better intelligence about the battlefields outside. The nameless serve, train, and wait for their chance in the arena.

### Notable Residents

- **Warlord Keth**, ruler of the Crucible, a figure of enormous physical presence whose iron-grey skin has taken on the texture of hammered plate. Keth does not remember arriving. Keth does not remember not being here. Keth's name may be the oldest in the Crucible, or Keth may be the Crucible's original architect. Even Keth does not know.
- **The Registrar**, a gaunt, precise soul who maintains the Record Hall, an archive of every name, every transfer of ownership, every arena result since the Crucible's founding. The Registrar has no name and wants none. They consider themselves above the system, a position that irritates Keth and fascinates everyone else.
- **Sergeant Mira Ashborn**, a mortal soldier who fell through a thin point during a battle in [[The Ashen Dominion]] and has been fighting her way through the Proving Grounds ever since. She still has color in her eyes. She still remembers her name, the one from before, which she guards like a weapon.
- **Bloodqueen Sevra**, who holds the arena's longest active winning streak and controls a name so old it allegedly predates the Theomachis. She fights with a calm precision that suggests she has moved beyond rage into something colder and more efficient.
- **The Peacemaker**, a soul who patrols the Crucible's interior and resolves disputes through conversation, an almost miraculous skill in a city built on combat. The Peacemaker's methods work because they understand that most conflicts here are not about what they appear to be about.
- **Pit-Master Grenn**, who manages the arena schedules, matches combatants, and ensures fights are, if not fair, at least entertaining. Grenn takes a craftsman's pride in spectacle and considers a boring fight a personal failure.

## Notable Locations

**The Arena of Echoes**, the Crucible's primary combat venue, a tiered iron amphitheater that seats thousands. The arena's floor is stained with something that is not blood but behaves identically. Every blow struck in the Arena produces an echo that is slightly louder than the original sound, a cumulative effect that makes prolonged fights deafening. The longest matches in the Arena's history have ended not through injury but through the combatants' inability to hear, think, or maintain coordination under the sonic assault.

**The Bone Relay**, a chain of signal towers stretching across the Proving Grounds, built from fused weapons and maintained by nameless souls who consider the work a form of penance. The towers relay information about troop movements, battlefield conditions, and the locations of newly formed armies. The information is always accurate. It is never useful in time.

**The Trench of Still Voices**, a deep gash in the iron plate where the oldest, most eroded souls eventually drift. They no longer fight, no longer speak, no longer move with purpose. They stand in the trench like monuments, iron-grey and silent, their weapons fused to their hands. Visiting the Trench is considered bad luck. Some warriors go anyway, looking for someone they used to know.

**The Descent Stair**, a narrow iron staircase at the Proving Grounds' lowest point, leading down to [[Level 3 - The Iron Market|The Iron Market]]. Unlike the Ashfield's open gate, the Stair is guarded by two of Keth's most decorated warriors. Passage requires either Keth's permission or defeating both guards. Most who descend have already lost enough of themselves that the Market's prices seem reasonable.

## Native Creatures

**Iron Wraiths**, the final state of souls who have cycled through too many deaths on the Proving Grounds. They are translucent, metallic, and fight with the accumulated muscle memory of a thousand engagements. They do not think, do not strategize, do not feel. They simply execute combat with flawless, empty precision. They cannot be reasoned with because there is nothing left to reason with.

**Battle Echoes**, temporal distortions that replay fragments of past engagements. They appear as ghostly armies clashing in formations that no longer exist, using tactics from wars fought centuries ago. Walking through a Battle Echo subjects the traveler to the sensory experience of the original combat, pain, fear, adrenaline, the whole catalogue. Prolonged exposure can overwrite a traveler's own memories with those of the echoed soldiers.

**Gore-Golems**, massive constructs that form spontaneously from the accumulated detritus of the battlefields, broken weapons, shattered armor, and the compressed essence of fallen combatants fused into lumbering shapes. They are drawn to large-scale engagements, wading into battles and swinging limbs made of a thousand swords. They are not intelligent. They are the battlefield itself, reaching up and joining in.

## History

The Proving Grounds were [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s personal training ground, the level where he forged his divine armies before the Theomachis. In those days, combat here had meaning, structure, and an end state. Warriors who proved themselves ascended to Kaevroth's inner circle. The arena system, the name-taking, the hierarchies of merit, all of it served a purpose. Kaevroth watched every fight personally, and his attention gave the violence dignity.

When Kaevroth fell, the purpose drained out but the machinery kept running. The armies still form. The arenas still fill. Names still change hands. But there is no god watching, no promotion to earn, no inner circle to join. The Proving Grounds became a closed loop, violence generating violence generating violence, a war machine with no war to fight and no capacity to stop. Warlord Keth, who may remember the old purpose or may simply have invented a memory of it, maintains the Crucible as a last preserve of structure in a level that has otherwise surrendered to entropy dressed as combat.

## Encounters & Hooks

1. **The Name Thief.** A numbered soul in the Crucible claims their name was stolen outside the arena, a violation of the Crucible's only law. The Registrar confirms the name transfer was never recorded. Someone is taking names by force within the city's walls, which should be impossible. The architecture's enforcement is failing, and no one knows why.
2. **Mira's Escape.** Sergeant Mira Ashborn approaches the party with a proposition: she knows a route back to the mortal world, but it runs through the deepest part of the battlefield, past three active engagements and the Trench of Still Voices. She needs combat-capable allies. What she does not mention is that she has been tracked by an Iron Wraith that wears her commanding officer's face.
3. **The Undefeated.** Bloodqueen Sevra challenges a party member to arena combat for their name. Refusing is social death in the Crucible. Accepting means fighting the Proving Grounds' most accomplished warrior. Winning earns the party access to the Descent Stair without Keth's permission. Losing means giving up your name, and everything that comes with it.
4. **The Still Voice.** A figure in the Trench of Still Voices moves. It should not be able to. It turns toward the party and speaks a single word, a name that one of the PCs recognizes. The name belongs to someone who died in the mortal world decades ago. The figure then resumes its stillness, but its weapon hand has shifted to point deeper into the Trench.
5. **Keth's Commission.** Warlord Keth summons the party and offers free passage to the lower levels in exchange for a task: destroy a Gore-Golem that has been growing on the eastern battlefield for months and is now large enough to threaten the Crucible itself. The mission is straightforward. The complication is that the Gore-Golem contains, at its core, the fused remains of someone Keth once knew, and Keth wants confirmation of what it has become.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Connection.** The Proving Grounds are where [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]]'s war-essence is most concentrated. If the Heart of Iron beneath the Ashen Dominion were ever activated or destroyed, the Proving Grounds would be the first level to respond, either surging with renewed purpose or collapsing entirely as the last thread of divine will snaps. The [[Ember Guild]] theorizes that the Proving Grounds may function as a kind of resonance chamber for Kaevroth's fragments in the mortal world, amplifying their effect on mortal conflicts.
>
> **Keth's Secret.** Warlord Keth is not a soul who arrived and earned dominance. Keth is a construct, the Proving Grounds' immune response to the loss of its god. The domain created Keth to maintain the combat cycle, to keep the machinery running in Kaevroth's absence. Keth does not know this. Keth genuinely believes they are a warrior who rose through merit. If they were to discover the truth, the psychological collapse could destabilize the entire level, and whatever form the Proving Grounds takes without Keth's stewardship would be far worse.
>
> **The Architecture's Failure.** The Crucible's violence-suppression is weakening. This is not a local malfunction but a symptom of the Iron Descent's slow decay without its god. As the domain's self-sustaining mechanisms break down level by level, the structures that maintained order will fail. The Name Thief encounter is an early warning. If the party reports it to Keth, Keth will be more disturbed than they should be, because Keth has noticed other failures and has been keeping them quiet.
