## Description

The hammering never stops. It echoes through rivers of molten iron that carve the landscape like waterways, pooling in vast basins of liquid metal that glow white-hot and cast everything in shifting amber light. The Forge Eternal is a continent-sized workshop, a level of [[The Iron Descent]] given over entirely to the act of making. Massive anvils the size of castles stand at every junction, their surfaces pitted with the memory of ten thousand years of relentless work. The air is thick with soot, with sparks, with the acrid sweetness of superheated iron, and beneath it all, the rhythm. Strike, strike, strike. The forges breathe.

This was where [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] built his war-constructs, the iron soldiers and siege engines that once poured from the divine forges to fuel conquests across mortal and immortal battlefields alike. Every great weapon of the Theomachis was conceived here, drawn in molten light on floors of beaten copper, then hammered into terrible reality. The forges were the engine of Kaevroth's ambition made physical, creation as an act of war.

But Kaevroth is dead, and the forges did not stop. They cannot stop. The machinery grinds on without design or directive, producing endlessly. Weapons emerge from the smelters in impossible shapes, swords with three blades that cut nothing, armor that fits no body that has ever existed, siege engines that collapse under their own weight. Everything the forges produce is beautiful. Everything is masterwork. And none of it has purpose. The weapons are sharp but strike nothing. The armor gleams but protects no one. Within hours of completion, each piece dissolves back into slag, and the cycle begins again.

The cruelty of the Forge Eternal is not heat or labor. It is the knowledge that everything you make is meaningless, and the inability to stop making it. The souls trapped here feel the compulsion like a heartbeat: build, build, build. They weep at their anvils and their hands keep moving.

## The Foundry

The Foundry is less a city than a vast industrial complex stretched across a plateau of cooled slag, surrounded on all sides by rivers of molten iron. It is organized into districts by function: smelting, forging, assembly, finishing, and dissolution, where the day's useless output is melted back down for tomorrow's useless output. Chimneys taller than cathedral spires belch smoke that never disperses, hanging in a permanent orange haze overhead.

The workers are damned souls, condemned to the Forge Eternal for the sin of creation without conviction. Artists who made only what would sell. Generals who built armies they never believed in. Craftsmen who worked without love. They labor at their stations in shifts that never end, because there are no shifts. There is only work. They produce masterwork items of stunning quality, swords that sing when drawn, armor chased with filigree so fine it seems alive, and every piece dissolves into rust and slag within hours of completion. The workers watch their creations crumble, and then they begin again.

Forge-Warden Irontooth rules from the Assembly Hall at the Foundry's center, a creature of fused iron and stolen ambition who was once one of Kaevroth's war-construct prototypes. He enforces quotas that cannot be met for products that cannot last, and punishes failure with reassignment to the Slag Pits, where workers must forge with their bare hands in pools of half-molten metal.

## Notable Locations

**The Assembly Hall** , the Foundry's seat of power, where Forge-Warden Irontooth holds court atop a throne of fused, failed weapons. The hall is lined with display cases showing the finest works ever produced on this level, each one slowly dissolving as visitors watch. Irontooth considers the decay beautiful. He calls it "the god's review."

**The Slag Pits** , punishment detail and raw material source. Vast open pits where molten slag bubbles and pops, tended by the most wretched souls on the level. Workers here shape metal with their hands, their flesh regrowing just fast enough to be burned away again. The Slag Mother, an ancient soul who has worked the pits since the Theomachis, knows secrets about Kaevroth's original forge-designs that Irontooth would kill to possess.

**The Pattern Library** , a massive archive of blueprints etched into iron plates. These are the original designs for Kaevroth's war-constructs, siege engines, and divine weapons. Most are corrupted, their lines wandering into impossible geometries, but a skilled artificer might salvage something genuine. The library is tended by Apprentice Nil, who has been "almost finished" cataloguing it for three thousand years.

**The Cooling Yards** , where freshly forged items are laid out in long rows to cool before dissolution. For a few hours, this is the most beautiful place in the Iron Descent: thousands of gleaming weapons and armor pieces catching the forge-light, a museum of perfection that will be slag by morning. Souls sometimes come here just to look, to remember what completion felt like.

**The Bellows** , the mechanical lungs of the Forge Eternal, a network of enormous pumping chambers that force air into the forges. Bellows-Keeper Tharn maintains them with obsessive precision, convinced that if the airflow is calibrated perfectly, the forges will finally produce something that lasts. He has been adjusting the bellows for centuries. He is always almost there.

## Native Creatures

**The Forgeborn** , failed war-constructs that achieved a mockery of life. They wander the Forge Eternal in aimless patrols, following orders that were never given, guarding posts that don't exist. Some are humanoid. Others are siege-engine hybrids, battering rams with legs, catapults that drag themselves across the slag fields. They attack anything that disrupts the forges' rhythm.

**Slag Crawlers** , creatures born from the dissolution of failed creations. When a masterwork weapon melts back into slag, something of its intended purpose survives, a predatory instinct compressed into molten metal. Slag Crawlers are fast, low-slung things that move through the rivers of liquid iron like eels, erupting to drag victims beneath the surface.

**Spark Wraiths** , the ghosts of ambitions that were hammered into weapons but never used. They manifest as humanoid shapes made of drifting sparks and ember-light, beautiful and translucent. They whisper promises of purpose to anyone who listens, offering to "complete" a mortal by fusing them with a forge and giving them eternal, meaningful work. Those who accept become part of the machinery.

**Iron Mites** , tiny, insectoid constructs that swarm through the Foundry's infrastructure. Individually harmless, they consume metal and replicate, and a swarm can strip a suit of armor to nothing in minutes. The workers consider them vermin. The Overseer considers them quality control.

## History

The Forge Eternal was the fourth domain Kaevroth claimed when he carved the Iron Descent from raw planar matter. He designed it as the production floor of his ambitions, the place where thought became weapon and desire became army. During the height of his power, the forges produced war-constructs by the thousand, each one a perfect instrument of conquest. The level hummed with purpose, every hammer-stroke a note in a symphony of divine industry.

When Kaevroth fell in the Theomachis, the forges did not receive the order to stop. No order came. The machinery, imbued with divine compulsion, continued. The souls bound to the forges continued. The only thing that changed was the absence of design. The forges still produce with the same divine precision, but without Kaevroth's will to direct them, the output is beautiful, masterful, and utterly without function. The Forge Eternal is a factory whose owner never came home, and whose workers cannot leave.

## Encounters & Hooks

1. **The Unbreaking Blade** , a weapon in the Cooling Yards has not dissolved. It has been there for three days, gleaming and intact, and the workers are terrified. Irontooth wants it brought to him. The Slag Mother wants it destroyed. Apprentice Nil wants to study it. The blade may be a sign that Kaevroth's will is reasserting itself, or something worse.

2. **The Quota** , Forge-Warden Irontooth has declared a production competition. The workshop that produces the finest weapon wins a rest period, something unheard of in the Forge Eternal. The catch: Irontooth defines "finest" differently each round, and no one has ever won.

3. **The Slag Mother's Secret** , the ancient soul in the Slag Pits claims she knows the original blueprint for Kaevroth's masterwork, the weapon he was building when he decided to break the Empyrean Accord. She will share it, but only with someone who brings her a piece of iron from the mortal world. Real iron. Iron that was made with love.

4. **The Bellows Sing** , Bellows-Keeper Tharn has finally calibrated the airflow, and the forges are producing something new: not weapons, but music. The hammering has taken on a melody. Workers are stopping to listen. Irontooth is furious, because stopped workers mean missed quotas, but the music is doing something to the level's architecture, opening passages that have been sealed since Kaevroth's death.

5. **Smeltress Kora's Bargain** , a soul who remembers being a mortal artificer. She can forge something real, something that will last, but she needs materials from outside the Forge Eternal. She offers her knowledge of the Iron Descent's deeper levels in exchange for raw materials smuggled from the mortal plane.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Forge Eternal is the key to understanding Kaevroth's war-constructs, which are relevant to the mortal world because his iron fragments scattered across Aethermourne still carry forge-resonance. A sufficiently skilled artificer could use the Pattern Library's blueprints to build constructs powered by Kaevroth fragments, or, more dangerously, to *unmake* the fragments by reversing the forge process.
>
> The Unbreaking Blade is not a sign of Kaevroth's return. It is a sign of [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s interference. She has threaded a sliver of her will into the forges, testing whether she can repurpose Kaevroth's production machinery for her own designs. If she succeeds, the Forge Eternal becomes a weapon factory under her control.
>
> The Overseer is not a person. It is a fragment of Kaevroth's consciousness, embedded in the level's infrastructure like a reflex. It does not think. It does not plan. It simply enforces production. But it is growing more coherent, responding to stimuli with increasing complexity. If it fully awakens, it could restore purpose to the forges, or it could remember that it is a dead god's rage and bring the entire level to war.
