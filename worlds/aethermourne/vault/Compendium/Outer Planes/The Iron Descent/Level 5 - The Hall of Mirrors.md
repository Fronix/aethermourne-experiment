## Description

The Hall of Mirrors is the most beautiful level of [[The Iron Descent]], and the most insidious. Its corridors are built from polished iron so perfectly reflective that the walls seem to disappear entirely, replaced by infinite recursions of yourself at your finest. Every surface is a mirror, but none of them show what is. They show what you *want*. You, victorious. You, beloved. You, powerful beyond reckoning. The reflections move a half-second ahead of reality, showing you not what you are doing but what you are about to do, and it is always magnificent.

The architecture is impossible. Corridors branch and reconnect according to the desires of whoever walks them. A soul seeking glory finds a throne room. A soul seeking love finds a bedchamber filled with the faces of everyone who ever mattered to them. A soul seeking knowledge finds a library containing every answer they ever needed. All of it is iron and light, constructed from reflections layered so densely they become solid. Nothing here is real in any structural sense, but the illusions have weight, texture, warmth. They feel more real than reality, and that is precisely the trap.

[[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] designed this level as a reward for his most ambitious followers, a paradise where every soul could rule, where every desire was fulfilled, where you were always the hero of your own story. It was supposed to be temporary, a respite before the soul resumed its climb toward Kaevroth's throne. But without the god's will to impose time limits, the mirrors locked. Souls entered and never left. They forgot they were in a mirror. They forgot what "real" meant.

The longer you remain in the Hall of Mirrors, the harder it becomes to distinguish reflection from reality. After days, you begin to trust the mirrors more than your own senses. After weeks, you cannot remember what you looked like before. After years, there is no before. There is only glory.

## Gloryholme

Gloryholme is a city of ten thousand palaces, and every resident believes they are its sovereign. The architecture reflects this: each building is a seat of power, a throne room, a grand hall, a war-planning chamber, all of it rendered in polished iron and mirror-light. The streets are wide and clean because every resident perceives them as the royal processional leading to *their* palace. The city is, by the standards of the Iron Descent, genuinely pleasant. There is no suffering here, or at least none that anyone recognizes as suffering.

The residents are unfailingly courteous, because each one believes the others are their loyal subjects performing their roles with admirable dedication. Lord Parvane greets the baker as "my faithful provisioner." Dreamer Ysolde addresses the gate guard as "my stalwart champion." Neither the baker nor the guard hear these titles. They hear whatever confirms their own delusions. The city functions because its collective madness is harmonious, every soul playing the role the others need them to play, none of them aware they are doing so.

Sovereign Kallis rules from the Palace of Reflections at the city's center, a structure that appears different to everyone who looks at it. To Kallis, it is the grandest palace ever conceived. To visitors, it appears as whatever seat of power they most respect or fear. Kallis has ruled for six centuries and has never once questioned their authority, because the mirrors have never given them reason to.

## Notable Locations

**The Palace of Reflections** , Sovereign Kallis's seat of power, a structure built from mirrors layered so densely it seems to be made of frozen light. Every room contains a version of history where Kallis made the right choices, won the crucial battles, earned the love of the people. The deeper you go, the grander the delusions become. At the palace's heart is the Mirror Throne, a chair that shows whoever sits in it the version of themselves they most want to be.

**The Gallery of Triumphs** , a vast museum curated by the Shattered Man, displaying the greatest victories of every soul in Gloryholme. The exhibits are mirror-constructs, three-dimensional scenes played out in reflected light, showing each resident's finest hour. None of the victories actually happened. The Shattered Man knows this, which is why he is shattered.

**The Truthsayer's Cell** , a small, unmirrored room deep beneath Gloryholme, the only space on the entire level where the walls are raw, unpolished iron. The Truthsayer lives here voluntarily, having gouged out their own eyes rather than see the mirrors' lies. They are the only soul on this level who perceives reality as it is, and they are considered dangerously insane by Gloryholme's residents.

**The Promenade of Glories** , the main thoroughfare of Gloryholme, where residents parade in their imagined finery. To outside eyes, they wear the same tattered iron-cloth as every damned soul. To their own eyes, they are resplendent. The Promenade is also where new arrivals are "welcomed," meaning gently absorbed into the collective delusion. Mirror-Warden Caelus oversees this process with genuine kindness and absolute ruthlessness.

**The Fracture** , a crack in the level's mirror-surface that appeared shortly after Kaevroth's death. Through it, you can see the raw iron substrate of the Iron Descent, ugly and real. Gloryholme's residents avoid it instinctively. Those who stare into the Fracture too long begin to remember what they actually look like, a process that is as painful as it sounds.

## Native Creatures

**Mirror Shades** , reflections that have separated from their owners and developed independent existence. They look exactly like the soul they split from, but they are the *idealized* version, stronger, more beautiful, more confident. They follow their original soul at a distance, mimicking their movements with a slight delay. If a Mirror Shade catches its original, it replaces them, and the original soul becomes the reflection, trapped in the mirrors, watching their better self live their life.

**Vanity Swarms** , clouds of tiny mirror-fragments that drift through the Hall of Mirrors like glittering fog. Each fragment shows whoever looks into it a single, perfect memory: their greatest triumph, their most beautiful moment, their most powerful self. The swarms are drawn to doubt. When a soul begins to question the mirrors, the Vanity Swarm descends, smothering them in a thousand perfect reflections until the doubt is gone.

**Spark Wraiths** , the same creatures found on other levels of [[The Iron Descent]], but here they are subtler. Instead of offering purpose through labor, they offer *validation*. They appear as admirers, as cheering crowds, as the faces of loved ones expressing pride. They feed on the emotional energy of self-deception, growing stronger as the soul they attend sinks deeper into delusion.

## History

The Hall of Mirrors was Kaevroth's concession to vanity, both his own and his followers'. He built it during the height of his power, when the Iron Descent was not merely a punishment realm but a proving ground for ambitious souls. The fifth level was designed as a paradise for those who had earned it, a place where ambition was rewarded with the experience of total success. The mirrors were calibrated by Kaevroth himself, each one tuned to the individual soul it reflected, showing them not generic glory but their *specific* glory, the exact future they hungered for.

The system required Kaevroth's active maintenance. Without him, the mirrors defaulted to their base function: reflecting desire without limit or discernment. The nuance disappeared. The carefully calibrated visions became crude wish-fulfillment, and the mechanism that was supposed to release souls after their reward period locked permanently open. Gloryholme's residents have been living in their perfect reflections for over two thousand years. Some of them have been happy for every single moment of that time, and the horror of that happiness is the level's true punishment.

## Encounters & Hooks

1. **The Shattered Man's Request** , the curator of the Gallery of Triumphs is the only resident who knows the exhibits are lies. He was once a mortal historian who valued truth above all else, and the mirrors could not break him. But he is cracking. He asks the party to destroy the Gallery before he loses the will to care, but doing so would shatter the delusions of every soul whose "triumph" is displayed there.

2. **The Truthsayer's Warning** , the eyeless prophet beneath Gloryholme claims that the mirrors are not merely showing illusions, they are *recording*. Every soul's deepest desire, catalogued and stored in the mirror-substrate. Someone is reading those records. Someone who is not Kaevroth.

3. **Mirror-Warden Caelus's Offer** , the warden who processes new arrivals offers the party a deal: one hour in the mirrors, supervised, to see their hearts' desire. No permanent effects, he promises. But Caelus is not lying out of malice. He genuinely believes the mirrors are harmless. He has been wrong about this for a very long time.

4. **Sovereign Kallis's Court** , the ruler of Gloryholme invites the party to a grand feast in their honor. The feast is real, the food sustaining, the hospitality genuine. But attending means playing a role in Kallis's delusion, and every moment spent performing reinforces the mirrors' hold. Refusing Kallis's hospitality is treated as treason, and the Sovereign's guards are very real.

5. **Dreamer Ysolde's Doubt** , one of Gloryholme's residents has begun to dream of a life she does not recognize, a mortal life, ugly and small and real. She is terrified. She wants the dreams to stop. If the party helps her remember, she could become an ally, but remembering will break her, and she will never be happy again.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Truthsayer is correct. The mirrors are recording, and [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] is reading. She has been using the Hall of Mirrors as an intelligence-gathering tool, cataloguing the desires of every soul that passes through. This data is extraordinarily valuable: a comprehensive map of mortal ambition, organized by type, intensity, and vulnerability. Serith uses it to identify which living mortals are most susceptible to manipulation.
>
> The Fracture is not natural decay. It was created by Morrhael's death-energy bleeding upward from Level 6, where his domain connects to the Iron Descent via the Bone Gate. The Fracture is slowly spreading, and where it goes, the mirrors fail. If it reaches Gloryholme's core, every delusion on the level will shatter simultaneously, releasing thousands of souls into the sudden, devastating awareness of what they truly are. The psychic shockwave would ripple through the Iron Descent.
>
> Sovereign Kallis was, in life, a mortal general who served Kaevroth faithfully and was rewarded with rule over Gloryholme. They were the last soul Kaevroth personally placed on this level before the Theomachis. Deep beneath their delusions, Kallis still carries a fragment of Kaevroth's final orders, a command that has never been delivered because Kallis forgot they were carrying it. If their delusion is broken carefully, the command could be recovered.
