## Description

The Conquest is a graveyard of empires. It stretches in every direction as a landscape of ruins, toppled columns, shattered walls, throne rooms open to a sky that is not a sky but the iron ceiling of the level above. Every great conquest [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth]] ever inspired is here, miniaturized and crumbling, a diorama of ambition's aftermath. You walk through the wreckage of a thousand kingdoms, and the scale betrays you constantly. One moment you are a giant striding over model cities, crushing tiny towers beneath your heel. The next, you are ant-sized, standing in a throne room so vast that the ceiling is weather.

The ruins are not static. They cycle. A kingdom rises from rubble over the course of hours, its walls rebuilding, its banners unfurling, its population of echo-souls returning to their posts. Then it peaks, holds for a single, perfect moment of imperial glory, and collapses again. The cycle takes different lengths for different kingdoms: some rise and fall in a day, others in a week. But every one of them falls. The Conquest permits no permanent victory.

The air here tastes of dust and iron filings, and there is a sound beneath everything, not hammering, like the Forge Eternal, but marching. The distant, rhythmic percussion of armies on the move, boots on stone, hooves on dirt, war-drums in the fog. The armies never arrive. They are always approaching. The Conquest exists in the moment before the battle that will decide everything, forever suspended in the breath between readiness and ruin.

This is also where the Iron Descent bleeds into [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s broken domain. In the level's deepest reaches, a fracture in the floor called the Bone Gate opens into a passage where war-energy and death-energy mingle, creating a border zone that belongs to neither god. The dead soldiers of the Conquest do not merely reset when their kingdoms fall. Some of them *linger*, caught between Kaevroth's cycle of ambition and Morrhael's shattered passage, unable to rise again and unable to rest.

## The Thousand Thrones

The Thousand Thrones is not one city but a thousand palaces pressed together, each one a perfect recreation of a real seat of power from mortal history. The palace of the first Ashen Hierarch stands beside the war-hall of a forgotten barbarian king. A merchant-republic's senate chamber shares a wall with the tower of a sorcerer-emperor whose name has been lost for fifteen centuries. Every palace is immaculate, maintained by echo-souls who repeat the daily routines of courts that no longer exist.

The throne rooms are occupied. In each one sits an echo of the ruler who once held power there, performing the rituals of governance for an empire of ghosts. They sign decrees that no one reads. They hold audiences with petitioners who dissolved centuries ago. They declare wars against kingdoms in adjacent palaces, and the wars play out in miniature on the fields between, tiny armies clashing and dying and resetting while the rulers watch from their windows and believe they are winning.

The Last General rules the Thousand Thrones from no fixed palace. They move between them, occupying whichever throne is currently at the peak of its cycle, presiding over the single perfect moment before the fall. The Last General is the only soul on this level who understands the cycle, who knows that every empire falls, and who chooses to rule anyway. They consider this wisdom. It may be the deepest form of the level's madness.

## Notable Locations

**The Bone Gate** , a jagged fracture in the level's iron floor, roughly a hundred paces across, glowing with a sickly light that shifts between Kaevroth's amber forge-glow and the pale, cold luminescence of [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s death-domain. The passage descends into a border zone where the two gods' residual energies clash and mingle. Undead soldiers from the Conquest patrol the edges, caught in a state that is neither Kaevroth's cycling resurrection nor Morrhael's broken rest. The Bone Gate is guarded by Herald Unbroken, who has been ordered to let no one pass and has been following that order since before the Theomachis ended.

**The Field of Banners** , an open plain between the palace-clusters where the miniature wars are fought. Thousands of tiny banners flutter in wind that does not exist, each one representing a real army from mortal history. The battles play out in accelerated time, entire campaigns compressed into hours. Crown-Keeper Ash tends the field, collecting the banners of fallen kingdoms and cataloguing them in a vault beneath the plain. The vault contains the complete military history of the mortal world, rendered in silk and iron thread.

**The Cartographer's Tower** , a structure that stands apart from the palaces, tall and narrow, occupied by the Cartographer, a soul who has been mapping the Conquest since the level's creation. Their maps are works of art and madness, showing every kingdom at every stage of its cycle simultaneously. The tower's walls are papered floor to ceiling with overlapping charts, and the Cartographer speaks only in geographical coordinates. They are the only soul who knows the Conquest's full layout, including passages to the Bone Gate that the Last General does not control.

**The Coronation Steps** , a grand staircase at the Thousand Thrones' center where new rulers are crowned. The steps are worn smooth by two thousand years of use, because every echo-ruler is crowned again each time their kingdom resets. The ceremony is always the same: the crown descends, the crowd cheers, the ruler ascends. Queen Vassera has been crowned eleven thousand times. She weeps each time, from what she believes is joy.

**The Deposed's Hollow** , a pit beneath the palaces where the echoes of overthrown rulers gather between cycles. When a kingdom falls, its ruler does not vanish, they descend to the Hollow and wait, stripped of title and purpose, until the cycle raises them again. The Deposed, the collective name for these waiting souls, are the only residents of the Conquest who experience the gap between reigns. Some have gone mad in the dark. Others have achieved a terrible clarity.

## Native Creatures

**Crown Wraiths** , the ghosts of ambitions that were never realized. They manifest as spectral figures wearing crowns that shift and blur, cycling through every royal symbol in the Conquest's history. Crown Wraiths are drawn to anyone who desires power, and they offer it freely, whispering strategies, revealing weaknesses, promising victory. Following their counsel always leads to conquest. It also always leads to ruin.

**Banner-Haunts** , sentient war-banners that drift through the Conquest on winds that blow from no direction. Each one carries the identity of a fallen army, and when a Banner-Haunt wraps around a living soul, that soul is consumed by the memories of the army's final battle. They feel every wound, every death, every moment of the defeat that ended the campaign. The experience lasts seconds but feels like years.

**Iron Wraiths** , the most dangerous creatures on this level, born from the intersection of Kaevroth's war-energy and Morrhael's death-energy at the Bone Gate. They are soldiers who died in wars Kaevroth inspired, raised by Morrhael's broken domain, and trapped in a state of perpetual combat-readiness. They do not cycle like the Conquest's echo-armies. They are permanent, furious, and strategic. They fight with the skill they had in life, enhanced by divine rage.

**The Ambitious Dead** , souls caught in the Bone Gate's border zone who have absorbed both war-energy and death-energy in equal measure. They are neither fully undead nor fully cycling. They retain their memories, their tactical minds, and their hunger for conquest, but they cannot be permanently destroyed on this level. They reform at the Bone Gate within hours. Some have organized into warbands that raid the Thousand Thrones, conquering palaces for the brief satisfaction of victory before the cycle resets and strips their gains away.

## History

The Conquest was Kaevroth's trophy room, built to celebrate every war he had ever inspired and every empire his influence had raised. Each miniature kingdom was crafted with meticulous care, populated by echo-souls drawn from the actual participants in those historical conflicts. Kaevroth walked this level when he wanted to remember what his ambition had achieved, and the scale-shifts were deliberate, a god surveying his works from above, then descending to walk among them and feel the texture of conquest from within.

The Bone Gate predates the Theomachis. Kaevroth and Morrhael's domains always bordered each other, because war and death are inseparable. But the connection was once controlled, a diplomatic passage between two gods who respected each other's functions. When both gods fell, the passage ruptured. Now it is an open wound between two collapsing divine realms, and the energies that bleed through it are creating something neither god intended.

## Encounters & Hooks

1. **The Last General's Challenge** , the ruler of the Thousand Thrones offers the party a wager. Choose any kingdom in the Conquest and lead it through a full cycle. If they can prevent its fall, the Last General will grant them passage to the Bone Gate. No one has ever prevented a fall. The Last General does not expect the party to succeed, but they are curious whether mortals fight differently when they know they cannot win.

2. **Queen Vassera's Memory** , after her eleven-thousandth coronation, something has changed. Vassera remembers. Not everything, but enough. She remembers that she was real once, that her kingdom was real, that it fell for reasons she can now articulate. She wants to speak to someone from the mortal world, to learn whether her kingdom still exists in any form. It does not.

3. **The Bone Gate Expedition** , Herald Unbroken is weakening. The order they follow is two thousand years old, and the will behind it is dead. The Ambitious Dead are massing for a push through the Gate, and if they breach it, war-energy and death-energy will flood in both directions, destabilizing both the Iron Descent and whatever remains of Morrhael's domain beyond.

4. **Crown-Keeper Ash's Archive** , the keeper of the Field of Banners has discovered something troubling in the vault: a banner that does not belong to any historical army. It is new. It is still being woven. Someone in the mortal world is raising an army that Kaevroth's residual influence has already claimed. Ash wants the party to identify the banner's mortal origin before the army marches.

5. **The Deposed's Plea** , the overthrown rulers in the Hollow have achieved consensus for the first time in centuries. They want out. Not release from the Iron Descent, they have accepted their damnation, but release from the *cycle*. They would rather exist permanently in the Hollow, aware and powerless, than endure the endless repetition of crowning and overthrow. They need someone to break the mechanism that resets their kingdoms.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Bone Gate is the most strategically important location in the Iron Descent for the campaign's purposes. If Morrhael's Lantern (hidden in the Hollowdeep in the mortal world) is ever relit, the death-energy flowing through the Bone Gate will reverse direction, pulling war-energy from the Conquest into Morrhael's domain. This would effectively weaponize the passage of souls, creating an army of war-dead under the control of whoever holds the Lantern.
>
> [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] knows this. It is one of the reasons she has ensured the Lantern remains buried. The Bone Gate is too valuable as a choke point between war and death for her to allow either side to gain dominance.
>
> Crown-Keeper Ash's new banner belongs to the Ashen Dominion's current military expansion. The Hierarch's armies are being influenced by the Heart of Iron buried beneath the capital, and that influence has resonated with Kaevroth's trophy room, spawning a new kingdom-cycle in the Conquest. If the Hierarch's campaign succeeds, the cycle will play out in the Conquest as well, rise and fall in miniature, and the echo-Hierarch that forms will carry a fragment of the real Hierarch's strategic knowledge, accessible to anyone who finds it.
>
> The Last General was Kaevroth's mortal champion in the final battle of the Theomachis, the last soldier to fight under Kaevroth's banner before the god fell. They carry a splinter of Kaevroth's iron body embedded in their chest, the only fragment of the god that exists within the Iron Descent itself. This fragment still pulses with ambition, and it is the mechanism by which the Last General maintains authority over the cycling kingdoms. If removed, the Conquest's cycles would desynchronize, and every kingdom would rise and fall at random, creating permanent chaos on this level.
