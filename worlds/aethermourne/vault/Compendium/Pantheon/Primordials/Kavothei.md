# Kavothei, The Collector

> [!info]
> **Nature:** Accumulation, an entity that gathers things that drift out of creation through micro-fractures in the Boundary
> **Manifestation:** The absence of things that should exist, the gap where a memory used to be, the silence where a name was spoken
> **Cross-Cultural Names:** "The Hoarder Beyond" (Star-Reader marginal notation), "What Takes the Lost" (Hollowed Reach fishing superstition), "The Keeper of Forgotten Things" (Greymere folk tales)

**What It Is**

Kavothei is acquisitive, meticulous, and utterly alien in its priorities. It does not distinguish between a forgotten song and a dead civilization. Between a child's lost toy and the original words of a divine prophecy. Everything that drifts out of creation through the microscopic fractures that inevitably form in any boundary, no matter how well-constructed, is of equal interest to Kavothei. It collects the way the ocean collects rain: passively, comprehensively, without discrimination.

It has been collecting since the Boundary was established. Two thousand years of lost things.

**What It Has**

Everything creation has discarded, forgotten, or lost. The original words of [[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas]]'s prophecies, unfiltered by mortal interpretation and transcription error. The architectural plans for the Empyrean, the divine realm that collapsed when the gods died. The true name of every soul lost in [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s broken domain, names that the souls themselves can no longer remember. The memory of what [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]] was like before she shattered, her voice, her laugh, the way she perceived the universe as a single shimmering web of energy.

Also: a child's toy lost in a river 1,400 years ago. A merchant's ledger burned in a fire. A love letter never sent. The name of a dog that died before anyone thought to write it down. Kavothei does not discriminate. Everything that drifts out of creation is collected, catalogued in an alien system, and preserved with equal care.

**Campaign Significance**

The knowledge needed to solve the Second Silence may already exist in Kavothei's collection. Lost by creation, preserved by an alien collector beyond the stars. The means to reassemble Nethys. The key to relighting Morrhael's Lantern. The unfiltered truth of Orenthas's intentions. The original design of the Empyrean Accord. The plans for the Boundary's construction. All of it, drifted out through micro-fractures over two millennia, gathered and kept by something that has no concept of significance, only of completeness.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> If someone could contact Kavothei, they could recover literally anything that has been lost. The temptation is immense. The cost of such contact is unknown and probably terrible, not because Kavothei is malicious, but because communication across the Boundary is itself dangerous, and because Kavothei's alien nature means any "exchange" would operate according to rules mortals cannot predict.
>
> The most likely point of contact would be the Abyssal Trench, where the Boundary is thinnest. [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] is almost certainly aware of Kavothei. She has been imprisoned at the Boundary's edge for two millennia. She may have already established some form of contact. If so, she may have access to knowledge that no one inside creation possesses, knowledge recovered from creation's own discarded memories.
>
> This reframes her as even more dangerous. Serith does not merely know secrets. She knows the *lost* secrets, the ones creation itself has forgotten.
