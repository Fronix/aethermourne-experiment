## Description

The Bitter Sea is the frozen ocean at the northern edge of the known world, stretching beyond the horizon into territory no expedition has mapped and returned from. The ice is old, decades-thick in places, layered in strata of white and grey-blue that record winters the way tree rings record years. In summer, the surface softens to a treacherous crust over meltwater channels that can swallow a person in seconds. In winter, it locks solid enough to support buildings, and the fishing Holds extend their operations miles onto the ice, cutting holes and lowering nets weighted with divine bone fragments into the black water below.

The fish are pale and translucent, cold-water species driven into the bone-weighted nets by an attraction no naturalist has adequately explained. They taste of salt and something faintly metallic, like blood or iron, and a good day's haul feeds a Hold for a week. The fishing is essential. Without the Bitter Sea's harvest, the northern Holds would starve. This fact is what keeps people on the ice despite what moves beneath it.

Dark shapes pass under the ice. They are too large to be fish, some casting shadows twenty or thirty feet across. They move too purposefully to be currents, changing direction, pausing, circling back. They are aware of the people above them. The locals do not discuss the shapes. This is not superstition or denial. It is a negotiated silence, an understanding that acknowledging the shapes means acknowledging a question no one in the Wastes wants answered. The shapes have been here as long as anyone can remember. They have never broken through. The ice holds. That has been enough.

The cold on the Bitter Sea is not natural. It carries the same necromantic chill as the tundra but deeper, wetter, more intimate. It does not just freeze exposed skin. It numbs the way bone-pull numbs, a creeping peace that starts in the extremities and works inward, a cold that makes you feel warm if you stop fighting it. Ice-fishers work in pairs for the same reason bone-harvesters do: one works, one watches for the moment their partner stops shivering, because stopping means the cold has reached something deeper than muscle.

[[Frostmere]], the northernmost fishing Hold, housed roughly eight hundred people on the sea's edge. It has recently gone silent. No messengers. No trade caravans. The bone-cairn route to Frostmere still glows, but no one has traveled it in weeks. The last communication from Holdwarden [[Nara Frostmere]] mentioned the shapes being "more active than she had ever seen." Nothing since.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Nara Frostmere]]'s final note, recovered by a Vigil patrol that reached the outskirts of the Hold but did not enter, reads: *"The shapes are closer. They press against the underside of the ice and the ice bends but does not break. I can see faces. They are not human faces. They are patient. I think they have been patient for a very long time."*
>
> The shapes beneath the Bitter Sea are deliberately left undefined. They are not explained. They are not Serith's. They are not Morrhael's. They are something else, something that predates the Theomachis, possibly connected to [[The Fathom]], possibly not. Their narrative function is isolation and dread. They are the unknown at the edge of the map, the reason the Pale Wastes feel like the end of the world. Do not resolve this mystery unless the campaign demands it. The horror is stronger while it remains unnamed.
>
> What lies north across the ice is similarly undefined. All expeditions have failed to return. This is campaign-reserve territory, available for future development but not to be filled prematurely.
>
> Frostmere's population was drawn to the [[The Marrow Fields|Marrow Fields]] by a weaponized bone-storm (detailed in the Marrow Fields entry). The Hold itself is empty but intact, a preserved snapshot of a community that walked away from everything mid-sentence.
>
> **Campaign Role.** The Bitter Sea is the Pale Wastes' frontier of the unknown. It establishes that the world has edges the characters cannot reach and threats they cannot yet comprehend. Frostmere's silence is an investigative hook that connects to the Marrow Fields and, through the weaponized bone-storm, to Serith's larger operation. The shapes are a long-term mystery seed.

## Encounters & Hazards

**The Ice.** The Bitter Sea's surface is treacherous. In summer, meltwater channels hidden beneath a thin crust require DC 13 Perception to detect before stepping on them. Failure: the character breaks through into waist-deep freezing water (DC 14 Constitution or suffer 1d6 cold damage and hypothermia, requiring warmth within 1 hour or escalating damage). In winter, the ice is stable but pressure cracks open without warning, DC 12 Dexterity to avoid a sudden fissure.

**Necromantic Cold.** Exposure on the Bitter Sea is more dangerous than on the tundra. Characters without cold-weather gear make DC 12 Constitution saves every hour. Failure: 1d4 cold damage and creeping numbness (disadvantage on Dexterity checks). After two failed saves, the character feels warm, a false comfort that is the necromantic chill working deeper. A character who feels warm on the Bitter Sea is dying. DC 14 Medicine to recognize the symptom in another person.

**The Shapes.** Dark forms pass beneath the ice during fishing or travel. They are visible as shadows, twenty to thirty feet across, moving with purpose. They do not attack. They do not break through. They circle, pause, and move on. Their presence imposes no mechanical effect, but DC 15 Will to avoid unease that disrupts concentration (disadvantage on skill checks for 10 minutes after a close pass). If a character strikes the ice above a shape, it stops. It does not flee. It waits. Then it moves closer to the surface, close enough to make the ice creak. Then it moves on. Do not escalate this further.

**Frostmere Investigation.** The Hold is intact. Fires in hearths are cold but the wood is recent. Meals sit on tables, uneaten, frozen solid. Doors are open. Beds are made. There are no bodies, no blood, no signs of violence. Eight hundred people's worth of personal belongings remain in place, coats on hooks, tools on workbenches, children's toys on floors. The only sound is the ice beneath the Hold, groaning in patterns that almost sound like speech. DC 16 Perception to distinguish the groaning from wind. DC 18 to recognize repetition in the pattern. The shapes beneath Frostmere are more active than anywhere else on the sea.

## Hooks

1. **The Silent Hold.** [[Frostmere]] has not sent a messenger or trade caravan in six weeks. [[Greyhold]]'s Holdwarden has dispatched a Vigil patrol, but the patrol returned after reaching the Hold's outskirts and refused to enter. They reported the Hold standing, empty, and silent, with sounds coming from beneath the ice that they described as "wrong." A full investigation team is needed, people willing to enter the Hold, search for survivors or evidence, and determine what happened to eight hundred people.

2. **Closer to the Surface.** Ice-fishers working the southern sea have reported the shapes moving closer to the underside of the ice than ever recorded. At one site, a fisher saw a shadow press upward against the ice hard enough to bow the surface without breaking it. The observation post at [[Frostmere]] is gone with the Hold's population, leaving the southern fishing camps without early warning. Someone needs to establish a new watch and, if possible, determine why the shapes are rising.

3. **The Net's Catch.** A fishing crew hauling nets near the sea's southern edge pulled up something that is not a fish. It is not bone. It is not stone. It is not anything anyone in the crew can name. It is cold, smooth, roughly the size of a human head, and it hums at a frequency that makes teeth ache. The crew brought it to [[Ember's Rest]] and has not slept well since. Neither has anyone who has touched it.
