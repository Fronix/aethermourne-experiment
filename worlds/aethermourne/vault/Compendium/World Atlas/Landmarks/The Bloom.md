## Description

The Bloom is where [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s growth-essence saturates the land beyond all restraint. The southwestern reaches of [[The Thornwild]] are a cathedral of green that rebuilds itself by the hour, canopy soaring to impossible heights at dawn, then choking down to suffocating density by dusk as new growth competes for every inch of light. Vines grow fast enough to watch. Flowers open, pollinate, wilt, and seed in the span of an afternoon. The air is thick with pollen, sweet and heavy, carrying the scent of a garden that never learned the word "enough."

Beneath the beauty is [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s underlying pulse. The deadwood and mulch that carpets the forest floor is not decay in the natural sense but the death-phase of the Cycle doing its work, breaking down what the Bloom built so it can be built again. The rhythm is relentless. At the Bloom's heart stands the Heartroot, a massive central tree that completes a full life-death-rebirth cycle every six hours, its bark shifting from verdant green to ash-grey to bone-white and back while sap runs in colors no living tree should produce.

[[Rootpyre]], a community of roughly 600 Marcher exiles and heretical druids, has carved out a settlement in the Bloom's western edge. [[Root-Speaker Fennara]] leads a circle that practices dual-channeling, drawing on both Thyrea's growth and Morrhael's decay in a synthesis that [[The Greensingers]] have declared anathema. Her stabilized [[Sap-Wraiths]] patrol the settlement's perimeter, developing preferences and protective instincts that wild Wraiths lack entirely.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] considers Fennara's dual-channeling a direct threat. Harmonized Thyrea-Morrhael energy resists Serith's manipulation in ways that neither force does alone. The stabilized [[Sap-Wraiths]] near Rootpyre are the strongest evidence in the Thornwild that the two divine legacies *can* coexist, not merely collide. Fennara's practice is not invention but rediscovery, an echo of pre-Theomachis tradition when Thyrea and Morrhael were collaborators. If the PCs understand dual-channeling, it provides critical insight into stabilizing the Cycle regionwide.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Bloom Surges** are the primary environmental threat. When the Cycle's life-phase peaks, growth spirals beyond the Bloom's already aggressive baseline. Roots crack stone. Pollen clouds thicken until visibility drops to arm's length and breathing becomes difficult (Constitution save DC 13, increasing by 1 per round of exposure; failure induces hallucinations and euphoria). Vines reach for body heat, wrapping around limbs and gear with surprising strength. Healing magic cast during a peak Bloom amplifies uncontrollably, potentially over-healing wounds into painful growths of excess tissue (a cure spell might heal the wound and sprout a ridge of bark along the scar).

**[[Bloom-Lures]]** are sessile predators whose root-networks span 30 to 50 foot clearings of beautiful flowering growth. Their lure-organs exhale hallucinogenic pollen (Will save DC 14 to resist; failure draws the victim deeper into the clearing). The root-maw at the center drags prey underground and digests over days. During the death-phase, the clearing reveals itself: churned soil, bone fragments, scraps of clothing. Thirty-seven people have gone missing in Bloom-Lure clearings over the past two years.

**[[Sap-Wraiths]]** form during transition moments. Wild Wraiths are erratic and dangerous, lashing with acid or frost tendrils depending on which phase of the Cycle is ascendant. Near [[Rootpyre]], Fennara's stabilized Wraiths are a striking contrast: calm, purposeful, capable of recognizing individuals and responding to simple instructions. The difference is the most visible proof that the Cycle's forces can be guided rather than merely endured.

Aggressive plant growth is a constant passive hazard. Gear left on the ground for an hour may be woven through with roots. Camp must be cleared regularly. Vines probe tent seams overnight. Nothing in the Bloom stays still, and nothing that stays still remains unclaimed.

## Hooks

1. **Surge at Rootpyre.** A Bloom Surge is building, stronger than any in recent memory. [[Root-Speaker Fennara]] needs help channeling it before it crushes the settlement, but her methods require participants to open themselves to dual-channeling. The [[Cyclekeepers]] in [[Cyclethorn]] are reluctant to assist with heretical practice, even to save lives.
2. **The Beautiful Clearing.** A new [[Bloom-Lures|Bloom-Lure]] has established itself on the trail between [[Rootpyre]] and [[Cyclethorn]], claiming three travelers in two weeks. Fennara believes she can communicate with it and redirect its growth. [[Yara Thornbloom]] wants it destroyed outright. Both approaches carry consequences, and the choice may define the party's relationship with both leaders.
3. **The Greensinger Delegation.** [[The Greensingers]] have sent representatives to the Bloom's edge, demanding Fennara cease her dual-channeling practice and submit to judgment. Tensions between Rootpyre and the delegation are rising daily. The party may be asked to mediate, escort, or choose a side.
