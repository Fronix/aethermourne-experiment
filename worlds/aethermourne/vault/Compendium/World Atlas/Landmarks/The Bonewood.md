## Description

The Bonewood is where the Cycle stops pretending to be natural. In [[The Thornwild]]'s deep interior, [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s growth and [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s decay collide at such intensity that trees sprout, reach full height, wither, die, rise as skeletal husks, collapse into mulch, and sprout again within hours. The air smells of flowers and rot simultaneously, the two scents layered so precisely that neither overwhelms the other. The ground shifts between fertile black soil and necrotic grey dust as the Cycle turns underfoot. Nothing here is stable. Nothing here is still.

No permanent settlement exists in the Bonewood. Even the [[Cyclekeepers]] enter only when necessary, and never alone. The interior resists habitation not through hostility but through indifference: a camp set at dawn may be threaded with roots by midmorning, buried under deadwood by afternoon, and sprouting new growth through the wreckage by dusk. Harvesters drawn too deep sometimes become caught in transition loops, trapped in pockets where the Cycle repeats the same few minutes endlessly, alive and dying and alive again until someone pulls them out. They can be extracted if found quickly. After a few days, they stop wanting to leave.

[[The Bloom-Wraith]] resides somewhere in the Bonewood's heart, a being permanently suspended in the transition state between life and death. It shifts constantly: vine-threaded beauty during the life-phase, exposed bone and wasted flesh during the death-phase, and something luminous and indescribable during the moments between. It speaks in fragments, two overlapping voices, one warm and growing, one cold and fading, delivering messages that sound urgent but arrive incomplete. The Bonewood has turned [[Yara Thornbloom]] back nine times when she attempted to reach it. [[Kael Duskborne]] believes the Bloom-Wraith is asking for help. His [[Thornguard]] patrols the Bonewood's edges but does not enter.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[The Bloom-Wraith]] carries fused shards of both Thyrea and Morrhael's divine consciousness. It knows what [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] is doing to the Cycle and has been trying to warn anyone who can hear, but its communication is fractured by the transition state that defines its existence. Three origin theories circulate among the [[Cyclekeepers]]: the Wanderer (a mortal trapped and transformed), the Convergence (a natural phenomenon of extreme Cycle energy), and the Fragment (a piece of both dead gods fused during the Theomachis). The Fragment theory is closest to the truth.
>
> Slowing the Cycle around the Bloom-Wraith long enough for coherent communication would yield a campaign-altering warning about the Second Silence. Reaching it is a campaign milestone, not a casual expedition. [[Seedling Vera]] is planning an unauthorized solo expedition, convinced she can succeed where Yara could not.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Rapid Cycle** is the baseline hazard. Trees grow through unattended gear. Deadwood spontaneously combusts as decay-energy concentrates in dry husks, only for new growth to smother the flames within minutes. The ground beneath a resting traveler may shift from solid soil to crumbling ash to tangled roots in the span of a short rest. All five of the Thornwild's known creature types, [[Sap-Wraiths]], [[Threshold Weavers]], [[Bloom-Lures]], [[Bone Choir]], and [[Cycle-Stalkers]], appear here in concentrated, accelerated forms.

**[[Cycle-Stalkers]]** are the most persistent combat threat. Packs in the Bonewood are larger and faster-cycling than elsewhere, rising from death within minutes and immediately adapting. The mega-packs of sixteen reported near the Bonewood border are the ones that fled outward. What remains inside is worse.

**Transition Loops** trap the unwary in pockets of repeating time-Cycle. A traveler steps into a clearing and finds themselves living the same few minutes of growth-death-growth on repeat. The loop is perceptible from outside (the same bird takes flight, the same leaf falls, the same vine reaches) but invisible from within. Breaking someone free requires disrupting the loop's rhythm, loud noise, sudden violence, or channeling energy against the Cycle's current phase.

**[[The Bloom-Wraith]]** is an observation and interaction encounter, not combat. It does not attack. It does not flee. It continues its endless transition and speaks to those who come close enough to hear. Its fragmentary messages carry weight and urgency but arrive without context, puzzle pieces from a being that can see the whole picture but cannot hold a thought long enough to describe it.

## Hooks

1. **Yara's Request.** [[Yara Thornbloom]] needs someone to reach [[The Bloom-Wraith]] and attempt communication. She has been turned back nine times and suspects the Bonewood is specifically resisting her, not all visitors. The party may have a better chance, but the journey through accelerating Cycle territory is dangerous and the Bloom-Wraith's messages may raise more questions than they answer.
2. **The Missing Seedling.** [[Seedling Vera]] has entered the Bonewood alone, convinced she can succeed where Yara could not. She has been gone for two days. The [[Cyclekeepers]] need her retrieved before she becomes trapped in a transition loop, or worse, reaches the Bloom-Wraith unprepared for what it has to say.
3. **The Exodus.** A mega-pack of [[Cycle-Stalkers]], sixteen or more, has been driven out of the Bonewood's interior and is moving toward [[Cyclethorn]]. Something deeper in the Bonewood pushed them out. [[Kael Duskborne]] needs help turning or dispersing the pack before it reaches the settlement, but the real question is: what scared a predator that treats death as a tactic?
