## Description

The Covenant Spire rises from a volcanic caldera three hours' walk south of [[Keth'vaal]], a needle of black obsidian 300 feet tall, perfectly smooth, untouched by weather or time. It is the oldest structure in the [[The Oldborn Kingdoms]], older than the cities, older than the Rootways, older than any living memory. This is where the first Pact-Weavers struck the Covenant of Ash with [[The Primordials#Ur-Ghael|Ur-Ghael]], the Primordial of Pattern, and in doing so, founded Oldborn civilization.

The Spire is not carved. It is not built. It is *grown*, extruded from the volcanic stone by Ur-Ghael's influence at the moment the Covenant was sealed. Its surface is covered in microscopic patterns, visible only under magnification, that describe the terms of the original pact in a language that predates all mortal writing systems. Pact-Weavers study these patterns for decades without exhausting their content. The patterns shift, slowly, rearranging themselves as the Covenant's terms are tested and fulfilled. The Spire is not a monument. It is a living contract.

The caldera around the Spire is barren, black glass underfoot reflecting the sky so perfectly that standing on the caldera floor feels like standing on a mirror. No vegetation grows here. No animals approach. The air is warm, still, and carries a faint taste of copper, the mineral signature of Ur-Ghael's presence. The silence is absolute, not the oppressive silence of absence, but the focused silence of something paying attention.

The Oldborn consider the Covenant Spire the most sacred place in their civilization. Access is restricted to Pact-Weavers, Speakers, and those granted permission by the Circle. Outlanders have never been permitted within the caldera. The Oldborn believe that the Spire's presence anchors the entire Covenant, that if the Spire were destroyed, every Primordial pact in the Kingdoms would unravel simultaneously. Whether this is theology or fact is unclear. No one has tested it.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Covenant Spire is more than a monument to a pact. It is the pact itself, the physical manifestation of the agreement between the first Pact-Weavers and Ur-Ghael. The Spire contains, encoded in its shifting patterns, the *terms* of the Oldborn's civilizational bargain: what they gained (Primordial cooperation, Anchor stabilization, the framework for their magic) and what they paid (their collective memory of pre-Covenant existence).
>
> What was erased is the most dangerous secret in the Oldborn Kingdoms. Before the Covenant, the Oldborn worshipped something in [[The Gyre]], not the five named Primordials, but something else, something that demanded devotion in exchange for power. The Covenant was an act of liberation, severing that relationship and replacing worship with negotiation. The entity the Oldborn once served is still in the Gyre. It still remembers them. And as the Second Silence weakens all constraints across Aethermourne, its influence is reaching upward through the substrate, pressing against the Covenant's protections.
>
> The shifting patterns on the Spire's surface are moving faster than they have in centuries. The Pact-Weavers interpret this as stress, the Covenant responding to pressure from the entity the Oldborn tried to forget. If the Spire cracks, the Covenant breaks, and the Oldborn's forgotten god comes calling.
>
> **Campaign relevance:** The Covenant Spire's secret connects to the campaign's central theme: the relationship between mortals and the forces they depend on. The Twelve imposed their Pattern on the known continent. The Oldborn negotiated theirs. Both systems are failing. The question the PCs face is whether any mortal civilization can exist without *something* holding the chaos at bay, and what the cost of that protection always is.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Ur-Ghael's Regard.** Anyone who enters the caldera feels watched. Not threatened, not hostile, simply *observed* by an awareness so vast and fundamental that the sensation is overwhelming. Mortals with sensitivity to Primordial energy (Pact-Weavers, Gyre-Touched, or PCs who have been exposed to the Primordia) feel this more intensely. At its strongest, the Regard manifests as a sensation of being *understood completely*, every thought, every intention, every hidden motive laid bare. This is not telepathy. It is pattern recognition at a cosmic scale.

**Covenant Resonance.** Touching the Spire triggers a vision of the Covenant's formation, experienced from the Primordial side. The viewer does not see the first Pact-Weavers. They see what Ur-Ghael saw: small, fragile creatures offering to trade their history for a future, and a vast, patient intelligence deciding that the bargain was acceptable. The vision is profoundly alien and deeply humbling. It also reveals, to those who know what to look for, the shape of what was erased.

**The Mirror Floor.** The caldera's obsidian floor reflects more than the sky. At certain angles, it shows the caldera as it was before the Covenant: a thriving settlement, filled with people worshipping at a structure that is not the Spire, offering something to something that is not Ur-Ghael. The reflection fades quickly. The Pact-Weavers claim it is a natural phenomenon. They are lying.

## Hooks

1. **The Cracking Pattern.** During a renewal ceremony at the Spire, one of the microscopic patterns on the surface becomes visible to the naked eye, enlarged, glowing, insistent. It describes a Covenant term that has been violated, but the text is in the pre-mortal language of the original pact, and no living Pact-Weaver can fully translate it. The PCs, with their outsider perspective and potential divine knowledge, may be able to decipher what has gone wrong.

2. **The Forbidden Reflection.** A young Pact-Weaver saw the caldera's mirror-floor reflection clearly and is now asking dangerous questions about what the Oldborn worshipped before the Covenant. They approach the PCs because outlanders are not bound by Oldborn taboos. They want to know what was erased. The knowledge could save the Kingdoms or tear them apart.

3. **The Entity Stirs.** The Spire's patterns are shifting faster than ever recorded. Pact-Weavers across the Kingdoms report nightmares of being watched by something vast and hungry. A tremor runs through every Primordial Anchor simultaneously. Something beneath the Covenant is waking, and it remembers its worshippers. The PCs must decide: reinforce the Covenant (preserving the Oldborn's amnesia) or break it (restoring their memory, at the cost of exposing them to the entity's renewed influence).
