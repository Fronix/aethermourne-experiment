## Description

Thirty miles across and deep enough to swallow weather, the Hollowdeep is where [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s skull struck the earth during the Theomachis. The crater's rim rises like a wall of frozen dirt and shattered stone, and beyond it the land drops into a bowl of absolute wrongness. The air inside is colder than the surrounding tundra by twenty degrees, not the honest cold of wind and ice but the necromantic chill of a god's broken death-domain radiating outward from the skull at the bottom. Sound dies as you descend. Wind first, then birdsong, then your own footsteps. By the inner rim, the only thing you hear is your heartbeat, and you become very aware of how fragile that sound is.

The skull itself is visible from [[Lastlight]], eight miles to the southwest, through long-range telescopes. It sits at the crater's lowest point, half-buried in frozen earth, black as a void and roughly two miles across. The eye sockets emit a light that is not light, an anti-luminescence that makes the darkness around it darker, that draws the eye and holds it. The Watchers at Lastlight enforce a thirty-second observation limit. Longer exposure causes fixation, a bone-pull variant that targets the mind rather than the body. Three Watchers have been lost to it over the decades, found standing at their telescopes, unresponsive, staring south with expressions of terrible peace.

Between the rim and the skull, tens of thousands of undead move in disciplined formation. Skeletal infantry drills in ranks. Spectral entities patrol in overlapping circuits. [[Cairn Knights]] maintain perimeter squads of six to twelve, armed with [[Cosmology and Magic#Morrhaelite|Morrhaelite]] blades and shields of fused skulls. The dead have built fortifications from crystallized bone and frozen earth: walls thirty feet high, watchtowers, a gate facing south large enough to march an army through. Supply lines move resources the dead do not need along routes that suggest logistics planning by a living mind.

The Hollowdeep has been silent for twenty years. No eruptions. No hordes spilling out. The previous pattern, periodic surges of mindless undead pouring over the rim, stopped abruptly two decades ago and was replaced by this. Organization. Purpose. Patience. [[The Ashen Vigil]] has lost fifty-four scouts sent to gather intelligence. None have returned. The silence is not peace. It is preparation.

A shadow figure has been observed near the skull by Lastlight's most experienced Watchers, a silhouette that gives orders the dead obey. This is Serith's projected influence, not a physical presence but a command-relay through the death-domain's infrastructure.
>
> Scout-Captain [[Edda Voss]], ostensibly posted to the crater rim as a Vigil observer, is Serith's agent. She has been feeding intelligence on Hold defenses back through the death-domain for three years. Her reports are carved into bone fragments and left at dead-drop points where [[Cairn Knights]] collect them.
>
> [[Merren Vask]], a Vigil scout, survived eighteen months at the Hollowdeep's periphery before being extracted in a state of severe psychological damage. His journal, currently held at [[Greyhold]], contains the most detailed intelligence on the army's composition and structure. He describes the bone gate, the drilling formations, and a sound he heard once near the skull: a voice speaking a language he did not recognize, giving orders that every dead thing in the crater obeyed simultaneously.
>
> [[The Black Lantern]], one of the campaign's key artifacts, is buried near the skull. Serith knows it is there. The army is, in part, a guard force for it.
>
> The army's target is the midwinter Great Burning ceremony at [[The Pyre]], when every Holdwarden in the Wastes gathers in one place. A decapitation strike against the Wastes' entire leadership structure.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Necromantic Saturation.** Approaching the Hollowdeep imposes escalating bone-pull effects. At the rim (8+ miles): DC 11 Will save every hour or suffer fatigue and emotional numbness. Inner slopes (2-8 miles): DC 13 every 30 minutes or suffer grey depression, disadvantage on Charisma checks, and a growing conviction that nothing matters. Crater floor (under 2 miles): DC 15 every 10 minutes or succumb to peaceful acceptance, sitting down in the frost and waiting for death with a faint smile. Each failed save makes the next DC 2 higher. Removal from the area resets the progression over 1d4 hours.

**[[Cairn Knights]] Patrols.** Disciplined squads of six to twelve patrol the perimeter in overlapping routes. They deploy shield walls, execute flanking maneuvers, and retreat-and-regroup when losing ground. Breaking formation (killing the squad leader, creating loud chaos, isolating individuals) is more effective than raw damage. A separated Cairn Knight becomes confused and docile, touching its rank badge and whispering numbers.

**[[Memory Eaters]] at the Rim.** Memory Eaters drift along the crater's edge at night, drawn by the concentrated necromantic energy. Some carry fragments of the fifty-four lost scouts' memories, replaying final moments, last words, the face of whoever they were thinking of when they fell. A Memory Eater carrying scout memories is both a threat and a source of tactical intelligence about the army's interior.

**The Silence.** Inside the crater, ambient sound is progressively swallowed. At the rim, wind dies. On the inner slopes, conversation drops to whisper. Near the floor, the only audible sound is the listener's heartbeat. This is not magical silence, spells still function, but it is deeply unsettling. Perception checks relying on hearing suffer cumulative -2 penalties per zone descended.

**Lone Knight.** Not every encounter is combat. PCs may find a single [[Cairn Knights|Cairn Knight]] separated from its squad, sitting in the snow, touching the rank scratches on its armor. It does not attack. It looks up when approached and says, in a voice like wind through hollow stone, a name that might be its own. It does not remember anything else.

## Hooks

1. **The Ironveil Expedition.** Commander [[Thessa Ironveil]] is assembling a major expedition to penetrate the Hollowdeep's perimeter, recover the fifty-four lost scouts (or confirm their deaths), and gather intelligence on the army's strength. She needs experienced fighters who can resist bone-pull. The expedition is the Vigil's most ambitious operation in a generation, and its failure would break morale across the Wastes.

2. **The Scout's Last Memory.** A [[Memory Eaters|Memory Eater]] has been spotted near [[Lastlight]] carrying what appears to be a coherent memory, not fragments but a sustained sequence. Vigil analysts believe it belongs to one of the lost scouts and may contain tactical intelligence about the army's command structure. Capturing a Memory Eater's cargo without destroying the memory requires precision and restraint.

3. **Orders in a Dead Language.** A patrol near [[Bonecairn]] recovered a bone fragment from a destroyed [[Cairn Knights|Cairn Knight]]. Carved into it are orders written in a script no one in the Wastes recognizes, certainly not a language any dead soldier would know. The fragment has been sent to [[Greyhold]] for analysis, but scholars there are baffled. A [[The Star-Readers|Star-Reader]] or someone with knowledge of pre-Theomachis languages might identify it.
