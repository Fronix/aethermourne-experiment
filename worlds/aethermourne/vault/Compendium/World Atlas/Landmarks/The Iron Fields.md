## Description

The Iron Fields are the Ironmarch's interior heartland, a rolling expanse of rust-red earth stretching from the foothills near [[Duskfield]] to the dense monolith territory of the Shardfall. This is where [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] fragments are densest, where the soil is so saturated with divine iron that it stains boots, skin, and teeth within days of arrival. Shards protrude from the ground like broken teeth, ranging from fist-sized splinters to jagged pillars taller than a man, their surfaces dark with oxidation and faintly warm to the touch. The earth between them is hard-packed and barren, cracked in geometric patterns that follow the subsurface iron deposits. Nothing grows tall here. The grasses are low, wiry, and the color of dried blood.

The hum is what visitors remember. A subsonic vibration at the edge of hearing, felt more than heard, like distant drums or a heartbeat not your own. It is constant, rising and falling with the density of fragments nearby, loud enough in the deep Fields to rattle loose teeth and make sleep difficult. The Ironmarch's people call it the Drumbeat. Newcomers find it maddening. Long-term residents stop noticing it the way coastal folk stop hearing waves, which is precisely when it begins to work on them. Tempers sharpen. Suspicions crystallize. Arguments escalate into violence with a speed that would alarm anyone who had not already been ground down by months of ambient war-essence.

### The Drumheart

At the geographic center of the Iron Fields, the hum peaks. The earth here vibrates visibly, a fine tremor that sends ripples across standing water and makes metal objects buzz against any surface they rest on. The fragments in this area are arranged in a rough spiral, their tips angled inward as if drawn toward a central point. There is nothing visible at that point, just a shallow depression of rust-red earth, but animals refuse to enter it and compasses spin uselessly within fifty yards.

This is where [[Mira the Red]] held her first gathering. She stood in the depression and spoke to three hundred fighters, and every one of them heard her voice clearly despite the hum, as if the vibration carried her words instead of drowning them. Her army has since moved to a more defensible position on the Fields' eastern edge, but the Drumheart remains a site of pilgrimage for her followers. They leave offerings of iron there, shards driven point-first into the earth, and the offerings disappear into the soil within days, pulled downward by something that wants to be fed.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Iron Fields are not the destination. They are the staging ground. [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] fragments are migrating westward through the Fields, through the [[The Contested Corridor|Contested Corridor]], and toward the Heart of Iron buried beneath [[Ostivaar]] in [[The Ashen Dominion]]. The Drumheart is the point where the collective will of the scattered fragments is strongest, a resonance node where the dead god's hunger to reassemble achieves something like coherence.
>
> [[Mira the Red]] heard [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] voice here because the Drumheart amplified it. She is not special in kind, only in degree. Anyone with sufficient exposure to the fragments could hear the same voice, the same command to gather, to march, to become whole. Mira's army is massing on the eastern Fields, thousands of fighters drawn by a charisma that is equal parts human talent and divine compulsion. If she marches, the fragments in the soil will follow, accelerating their drift to match her pace. She will be a lodestone pulling a continent's worth of divine iron behind her.
>
> [[Factor Gellen Ashworth]] of [[The Ember Guild]] at [[Wrathspire]] has mapped the migration pattern and calculated that the fragments' convergence rate has tripled in the past year. He has shared this data with [[Yrsa Bloodhand]], who understood its implications immediately. She has not shared it further.

## Encounters & Hazards

**War-Essence Exposure.** The ambient contamination in the Iron Fields is severe enough to affect visitors within hours. Initial symptoms are irritability, heightened alertness, and difficulty sleeping. After three days, paranoia sets in. After a week, violent impulses become difficult to resist. The contamination is not magical in the traditional sense, it cannot be dispelled, only endured or counteracted with opposing divine essences.

> [!example] Iron Fields Exposure (Nimble)
> After 8 hours on the Fields: Will DC 12 or gain the Irritable condition (disadvantage on social checks). After 3 days: Will DC 14 or gain the Paranoid condition (must succeed on Will DC 13 to accept help, trust information, or sleep near others). After 7 days: Will DC 16 each day or act on violent impulses toward the nearest source of frustration. DCs increase by 1 for each piece of [[Cosmology and Magic#Kaevroth's Iron|Kaevroth's Iron]] equipment carried.

**[[Iron Locusts]]** swarm across the Fields in rust-colored clouds, following the fragment migration and feeding on exposed divine iron. Swarms are most frequent in the deep Fields near the Drumheart. **[[Wrath-Stalkers]]** hunt the margins, packs of war-essence predators that target anyone radiating aggression, which, on the Iron Fields, is nearly everyone.

**Mira's Outriders.** [[Mira the Red]]'s forces patrol the eastern Fields. They are not hostile to travelers by default, but they are watchful, suspicious, and operating under the influence of the same war-essence that saturates everything here. Misunderstandings escalate quickly. Mira's scouts carry iron-grey pendants that mark them as hers, and they expect deference.

## Hooks

1. **The Drumheart Investigation.** [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] wants the Drumheart examined by someone who is not already contaminated. She suspects it is a convergence node for the fragment migration and wants to know if it can be disrupted. She cannot send her own forces without alerting [[Mira the Red]], whose followers treat the site as sacred ground.
2. **Duskfield's Buffer.** [[Duskfield]]'s calm zone, the three-mile radius of weak contamination that makes it the Ironmarch's only farmland, is shrinking. The boundary has contracted by a quarter-mile in the past year, and the outermost fields are producing stunted, bitter crops. Mayor [[Alder Brightfield]] believes the fragment migration is pulling iron through the calm zone's edges. He needs someone to confirm this before the harvest fails completely.
3. **The Scout Who Came Back.** One of [[Quartermaster Voss Irenthal]]'s informants inside Mira's camp has gone silent. The last message described a ritual at the Drumheart, Mira standing in the depression with her arms raised, fragments in the surrounding soil rising inches out of the earth and orienting toward her like compass needles. The informant's silence could mean discovery, death, or conversion. Voss needs to know which.
