## Description

The Shard Bogs begin where [[Sporeling]]'s luminescent grove ends and the Deep Fen turns hostile. Past the last fungal stalks, the ground softens into sucking black mud broken by shallow pools of water that smells metallic and sweet, like breathing lightning. The mud is treacherous, thigh-deep in places, bottomless in others, and the standing water carries a faint iridescent sheen that shifts through colours no natural mineral produces. Patches of ground glow from beneath, pale and angular, where [[Cosmology and Magic#Divine Materials|Empyrean Shards]] are working their way to the surface.

The Shards are fragments of the Empyrean, the divine realm that collapsed when the gods died and shattered during the [[Timeline|Silence]]. They look like angular crystals of impossible colours, warm to the touch, humming faintly with residual divinity. Some are small enough to pocket. Others jut from the mud like standing stones, their facets catching ley-light and throwing it in directions that don't match the angles. The air around a surfacing Shard tastes of ozone and copper, and prolonged proximity induces visions: fleeting impressions of golden architecture, soaring vaults, a sky that was not a sky but a ceiling of living light. The Empyrean as it was before the fall.

Shard-hunters from [[Sporeling]] have worked these bogs for generations, harvesting the crystals that are the settlement's primary source of outside income. The work is brutal. The terrain shifts daily as the bog digests old paths and opens new sinkholes. Bog gas collects in pockets that ignite when magic is performed nearby. The fauna of the Deep Fen, drawn to Shard energy, congregates here in numbers found nowhere else in the fenlands. A shard-hunter works in pairs at minimum, carries a ten-foot pole for testing ground, and never stays past dark.

The Shards are surfacing faster. In the past year, the rate has roughly doubled. New clusters are appearing closer to [[Sporeling]]'s perimeter. The shard-hunters consider this a windfall. The [[The Confluence Council|Confluence Council]] considers it a warning.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Shard Bogs represent a physical boundary failure between the material plane and the Empyrean's remnants. The [[Cosmology and Magic#Ley Lines and the Aetheric Web|Aetheric Web]] normally maintains this boundary as part of its infrastructure, but the Second Silence is weakening it. As the Web destabilizes, the floor between planes thins, and the Empyrean's wreckage pushes through like debris surfacing in a receding tide.
>
> During strong ley surges, shard-hunters have reported seeing golden structures beneath the mud: arches, pillars, the suggestion of walls and corridors, like a city buried just below the bog's surface. This is the collapsed Empyrean itself, its geography overlapping with the material plane where the boundary is thinnest. The structures are not physically present yet. If the boundary continues to erode, they will be.
>
> [[Brother Lumen]] has begun using specific Shards as anchoring points for his fragment reassembly work in [[Sporeling]]. Each Shard retains a faint resonance signature that can be tuned to a particular [[The Twelve#Nethys|Nethys]] fragment, functioning as a beacon to draw scattered consciousness toward the mycelial network. He has sent requests through intermediaries for particular Shard types, offering Star-Reader knowledge in trade. The shard-hunters fill his orders without understanding why he wants crystals of specific colours and harmonic frequencies.
>
> The Bogs' expansion toward Sporeling is not coincidental. The mycelial network's growth, accelerated by Lumen's work, is pulling Shard energy toward it, and the Shards are responding by surfacing along the path of least resistance. Lumen is inadvertently drawing the Empyrean's wreckage toward the settlement.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Terrain.** The Shard Bogs are physically punishing before anything supernatural happens. Sucking mud, unstable ground, and hidden sinkholes make every step a calculation. Visibility is poor: mist clings to the surface, thickened by Shard luminescence into a glowing haze that obscures distance and direction.

> [!example] Shard Bog Traversal (Nimble)
> Movement: half speed through the Bogs. Every 10 minutes of travel: Athletics DC 12 or become mired (one action to free yourself, DC 14 if carrying heavy gear). Bog gas pockets: Perception DC 13 to spot the telltale shimmer. If a spell is cast within 10 feet of a gas pocket, it detonates (2d6 fire damage, 15-foot radius). Navigation: Survival DC 14 per hour or become lost. Shard-hunters from [[Sporeling]] grant advantage on navigation checks.

**Shard Radiation.** Proximity to a surfacing Shard cluster induces visions of the intact Empyrean: soaring golden halls, a sky of living light, the sensation of being welcomed home. The visions are beautiful and addictive.

> [!example] Shard Exposure (Nimble)
> Within 30 feet of a Shard cluster for more than 10 minutes: Will DC 13 or become entranced (fascinated, unwilling to move away, lasts 1d10 minutes). After one hour of continuous exposure: Will DC 15 or gain Shard-sight, the Empyrean overlays the material plane in the character's vision. Shard-sight grants advantage on detecting hidden Shards and magical auras but disadvantage on Perception checks involving material-plane threats. The condition fades 1d4 hours after leaving the Bogs. Repeated exposure (3+ visits) makes the condition persistent: Will DC 16 each morning or Shard-sight returns for the day.

**Deep Fen Fauna.** [[Spell-Scarred Lurkers]] congregate around Shard clusters, drawn by the arcane energy. [[Echo Pools]] form in the standing water near large Shards, reflecting scenes from other times or places. [[The Fenlight]] occasionally drifts through the Bogs, following pathways that trace the ley lines beneath the mud.

**Ley Pocket Surges.** Surfacing Shards create unstable pockets where the Aetheric Web's energy concentrates unpredictably. Spellcasting within 20 feet of a Shard triggers a ley surge (use the Travel Rules surge table, DC increased by 2 in the Bogs).

## Hooks

1. **The Missing Shard-Hunter.** [[Sporeling]]'s Fen Holloway has not returned from the Shard Bogs in four days. The mycelial network is agitated, pulsing in patterns the elders cannot interpret. Holloway found something: a Shard cluster surrounding a partially surfaced Empyrean structure, an arch of golden stone rising from the black mud. He has been exposed too long. He is alive but altered, seeing the Empyrean overlaid on reality, unable to distinguish between planes, unable to find his way back to ground that looks like ground. Rescuing him means entering the deepest Bogs, finding the structure, and pulling him free before the exposure changes the rescuers too.
2. **The Golden Arch.** What Fen Holloway found is real: the upper curve of an Empyrean gateway, surfacing as the boundary thins. It is the first physical structure from the collapsed divine realm to breach the material plane. [[The Star-Readers]], [[The Ember Guild]], and [[The Veil Unbound]] all want access. [[Brother Lumen]] wants it kept secret. The arch hums at a frequency that makes nearby Shards vibrate in sympathy, and during ley surges, the space beneath it briefly opens onto a golden corridor that leads somewhere that no longer exists.
3. **Sporeling's Perimeter.** New Shard clusters are surfacing within a quarter-mile of [[Sporeling]]'s grove. The shard-hunters are profiting, but the Mycelial Circle is alarmed: the Shards' energy is interacting with the fungal network in ways they do not understand. If the Bogs continue expanding, Sporeling's territory will become indistinguishable from the hazard zone within months. The community must decide whether to harvest the windfall or evacuate, and [[Brother Lumen]]'s reassembly work is accelerating the problem.
