## Description

Thyrea's Cradle is the heart of [[The Verdant Marches]], a primeval forest growing from the body of a dead goddess. Where other regions bear the marks of their fallen gods in bone and ash, the Cradle is the mark, a living monument so vast that its edges blur into the surrounding wilderness without boundary stones or fences. The forest simply becomes older, taller, warmer, and more aware, until travelers realize they are no longer walking through woods but through something that is watching them walk.

The Cradle rises in three vertical layers, each a distinct ecosystem stacked atop the last, connected by vine-bridges, root-ladders, and the sheer vertical ambition of trees that have been growing for two thousand years on divine soil.

### The Floor

Permanent twilight. The canopy above is so dense that sunlight never reaches the ground directly, arriving instead as a green-gold haze filtered through four hundred feet of leaves. Phosphorescent fungi cluster along root buttresses, casting cold blue-white light. Shallow streams thread between the roots, glowing faintly with dissolved divine essence, warm to the touch and carrying a sweetness that is not quite a taste and not quite a smell. The soil itself is warm, not sun-warm but body-warm, the residual heat of a goddess whose flesh became the earth. Creatures here have adapted to perpetual dusk: eyeless salamanders the size of dogs, moths with bioluminescent wings, root-snakes that navigate by vibration. The air is thick, humid, and carries the scent of growth so concentrated it borders on rot.

This is where the corruption is strongest. In the deepest groves, the phosphorescent fungi have gone dark. The streams carry a secondary taste, salt and brine, that has no business being this far from the coast. Root systems spiral in patterns that no natural growth produces. [[Tessara Wildbloom]] was last seen here, sitting cross-legged at the base of a Cradle tree, eyes open, breathing, absent.

### The Mid-Canopy (The Green Sea)

Two hundred feet up. The branches of ancient trees interlock into a continuous platform of living wood, moss-cushioned and vine-bridged, traversable for miles by anyone willing to trust their weight to something that is technically still growing. Green-gold light saturates everything, filtered through leaves the size of shields. This is where most life concentrates: birds in flocks that darken the filtered light, insects in clouds that hum at frequencies the [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] claim carry meaning, small mammals that have never touched the ground and have no reason to. [[Canopy Rest]], the mid-canopy waystation, anchors the vine-bridge network that connects the Cradle's interior settlements.

The Mid-Canopy is the safest layer for travel, but "safe" is relative. Vines reach for body heat. Flowers exude soporific pollen that slows reflexes before the traveler notices. The root-bridges shift position between visits, not randomly but deliberately, as if the forest is rearranging its corridors. Experienced [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] navigate by communion, asking the trees for passage. Everyone else navigates by luck.

### The Crown

Four to five hundred feet up. Open sky, blinding after the green twilight below. Emergent titan-trees punch through the canopy here, their trunks wider than buildings, their crowns spreading into a second canopy that catches wind and rain before either reaches the Green Sea below. Crown-birds, enormous raptors with wingspans of twenty feet, circle in patterns that the [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] say are [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s thoughts made visible, spiraling formations that trace the health of the forest below. When the patterns change, something is wrong. The patterns have been changing for months.

Few mortals reach the Crown without assistance. The climb is vertical, exposed, and contested by territorial fauna that resent intrusion. The [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] maintain three Crown-perches for observation, reached by vine-lifts that respond only to [[Cosmology and Magic#Primal Communion|Primal Communion]].

## The Cradle Heart

The deepest point of the Cradle, where the three layers converge around a depression in the forest floor that the [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] call the Heartroot. Here, [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s divine essence is strongest: the soil pulses with warmth in a rhythm that matches no living heartbeat but feels like one, the streams run gold rather than clear, and the trees are so ancient that their bark has crystallized into raw [[Cosmology and Magic#Thyrea's Amber|Thyrea's Amber]] in places. The air tastes green.

This is where [[Kaelith Thornborn]]'s Awakening occurred (~2170 AS), where she first touched the root-network and felt [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s distributed consciousness acknowledge her. It is a pilgrimage site for the [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]], who send their most gifted practitioners here to commune at the deepest level. The [[The Verdant Crown|Verdant Crown]] was lost somewhere in the Cradle Heart's depths during the Theomachis, and no communion has revealed its exact resting place.

**Thyrea's Children** dwell here, massive plant-creatures that may be fragments of the goddess's shattered consciousness given physical form. They move slowly through the deepest groves, tending growth, uprooting decay, responding to threats with the patience of geology and the force of landslides. Whether they are truly sentient, whether they remember being part of something greater, is a question the [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]] have argued for centuries without resolution.

The corruption is approaching the Heartroot. Shadow-roots, dark tendrils carrying the salt-and-brine signature of something oceanic, have been detected within miles of the site. Creatures are being displaced outward. Communion attempts near the corrupted zones return fragmented, confused, tasting of despair.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Cradle Heart is **Aetheric Web Anchor 3**, one of the nodes that holds the remnants of divine structure together across the continent. [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]]'s shadow-roots are threading through [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s root-network toward this anchor, using [[Tessara Wildbloom]]'s trapped communion link as a guide. The corruption is not destroying the network, it is co-opting it, turning Thyrea's distribution channels into Serith's whisper-lines.
>
> Thyrea's consciousness is withdrawing inward, pulling her awareness toward the Heartroot to isolate the corruption, a divine immune response that is shrinking her effective territory. The displaced creatures, the darkening fungi, the changing Crown-bird patterns are all symptoms of Thyrea contracting.
>
> Thyrea left a countermeasure: a seed of purest [[Cosmology and Magic#Thyrea's Amber|Thyrea's Amber]], hidden in the deepest Heartroot chamber, containing a concentrated fragment of uncorrupted growth-essence. If found and activated through communion, it could purge the shadow-roots from the local network. If Serith's agents find it first, its destruction would collapse Anchor 3 entirely.
>
> **Campaign Role.** Act IV: the PCs journey to the Cradle Heart to sever the shadow-roots and rescue [[Tessara Wildbloom]] (if not already rescued). The three-layer vertical structure functions as a dungeon: the Crown provides reconnaissance (reading Crown-bird patterns to map corruption), the Green Sea provides navigation challenges (shifting vine-bridges, corrupted sections), and the Floor provides the confrontation (shadow-root nodes, corrupted Thyrea's Children, and the Heartroot itself). [[Kaelith Thornborn]]'s channel runs through this network, so failure here means her visions become Serith's manipulations.

## Encounters & Hazards

Each layer presents distinct threats. The Floor's perpetual twilight conceals [[Rootbound Sentinels]], territorial guardians that emerge from the soil when intruders disturb the root-network. In corrupted zones, shadow-touched Sentinels attack without the usual territorial warnings, their bark blackened and their movements carrying the wrongness of something piloted rather than alive. [[Sorrow-Rooted|Thornwild Predators]] prowl the darkened groves, their grief-aura intensified by proximity to Serith's corruption.

The Mid-Canopy's soporific pollen requires a DC 12 Will save per hour of travel (failure: disadvantage on Perception for 1 hour, 3+ failures: sleep 1d4 hours). Vine-bridges in corrupted sections have a 1-in-6 chance per crossing of collapsing, the living wood rotting to brittle grey in seconds as the corruption surges.

The Cradle Heart's Thyrea's Children are the apex encounter. An uncorrupted Child is not hostile but will block passage to the Heartroot if it judges the visitors unworthy (DC 16 [[Cosmology and Magic#Primal Communion|Primal Communion]] to communicate intent). A corrupted Child is a catastrophic threat: the same mass and power, directed by something that knows exactly where the PCs need to go and will not let them reach it.

## Hooks

1. **The Silent Greensinger.** [[Tessara Wildbloom]] has been missing in the Deep Cradle for months. [[Elder Rosk]] is running out of excuses and time. Her body sits at the base of a tree on the Floor, breathing but empty. Something in the corrupted roots is holding her, or she is holding onto something she found there. Rescue requires entering the corruption zone.

2. **Crown-Bird Cartography.** The [[The Greensingers|Greensingers]]' Crown-perch observers report the Crown-birds' flight patterns have shifted dramatically, tracing shapes that map the corruption's spread below. [[Senna Deepmoss]], the evidence-driven Marchwarden, wants outsiders (not Greensingers, whose testimony she considers biased) to document the patterns and confirm the corruption is real and external. This is what she needs to support unification.

3. **The Displaced.** Creatures from the deep Cradle are fleeing outward in increasing numbers, disrupting settlements, fouling hunting grounds, and attacking travelers on routes that were safe for decades. [[Draven Ironbark]]'s northern Cradle clans are bearing the worst of it and blaming [[Kaelith Thornborn]]'s "meddling with the roots" rather than the true cause.

4. **Communion Gone Wrong.** A [[The Greensingers|Greensinger]] at [[Roothold]] attempted deep-network communion and returned screaming, clawing at her own skin, babbling about "roots that taste like drowning." The corruption is no longer confined to the deep interior. It is reaching the communion network that connects every Greensinger in the Marches.
