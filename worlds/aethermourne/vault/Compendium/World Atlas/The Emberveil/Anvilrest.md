## Overview

Where [[Crucible]] is heat and hammers and industrial grandeur, Anvilrest is light and lenses and meticulous silence. This small enclave, nestled in a sheltered valley between the Tongs and the Quench on the western arc of the [[The Emberveil#The Caldera Ring|Caldera Ring]], a full day's travel from [[Crucible]] and accessible only through a single narrow pass, is the Emberveil's centre of fine craft, gem-cutting, precision metalwork, jewelry, and the optical instruments that [[Cosmology and Magic#Calculism|Calculists]] across the continent depend on. The settlement is quiet by Emberveil standards, the work here requiring steady hands and focused attention rather than brute force and furnace heat.

The town is built from pale volcanic stone, its buildings low and wide with large windows designed to capture the maximum natural light. Workshops are arranged around central courtyards where gem-cutters work in the open air, their tables angled to catch the morning sun. The streets are clean, the public spaces immaculate. Anvilrest takes pride in its appearance the way a jeweler takes pride in a setting, every detail considered, nothing wasted. The locals joke that Crucible builds the world and Anvilrest makes it beautiful, and the joke carries an edge of genuine rivalry.

The Jewelers' Conclave governs Anvilrest with an autonomy that sometimes irritates [[The Forge Council]]. The Conclave argues, with some justification, that fine craft requires different governance than heavy industry, and that their contribution to the Emberveil's economy, measured in value per piece rather than volume, earns them the right to self-manage.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Polara Facet|Gemwright Facet]] has been experimenting with Empyrean Shards, fragments of [[The Twelve#Orenthas|Orenthas's]] divine material, using her gem-cutting techniques. The results are extraordinary and deeply unsettling. When she cuts an Empyrean Shard with the same precision she applies to gemstones, the facets seem to reflect things that are not in the room, glimpses of other places, other times, patterns that suggest the future. She has seen the Grand Forge going dark. She has seen the stars going out. She has not told anyone, because she does not know if she is seeing prophecy or going mad.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Crystal Courts

Anvilrest's central workspace, a series of open-air courtyards paved with polished volcanic glass that reflects and amplifies natural light. Gem-cutters and fine metalworkers occupy assigned stations here, their work visible to passersby, both a point of pride and a form of quality control. The courts are silent except for the precise sounds of craft, the tap of a setting hammer, the whisper of a polishing wheel, the crystalline ping of a gemstone being checked for flaws.

### The Conclave Hall

Headquarters of the Jewelers' Conclave, a modest building with an immodest interior. The walls are inlaid with examples of the Conclave's finest work spanning centuries, a living museum of the craft's evolution. The vaults below contain the Conclave's reserves, finished gems and precision instruments held against market fluctuations. Security is exceptional.

### The Lens Works

A specialized workshop producing the focusing crystals and optical instruments used by [[Cosmology and Magic#Calculism|Calculists]] and [[The Star-Readers|Star-Readers]] across the continent. [[Polara Facet|Gemwright Facet's]] crystals are considered the finest available, and orders from [[The Ashen Dominion#Ashveil|Ashveil's]] Athenaeum alone keep the Lens Works profitable year-round. The workshop is restricted, the techniques used here are closely guarded trade secrets.

### The Quiet Forge

Anvilrest's forge, smaller and cooler than Crucible's, purpose-built for the delicate metalwork that fine jewelry and precision instruments require. The forge is geothermally heated but carefully regulated, the temperature controlled to within fractions of a degree. The smiths here are not the muscular hammer-wielders of Crucible but patient, precise artisans who work with tools the size of needles.

## People & Culture

Anvilrest is the most gnomish settlement in the Emberveil, with gnomes comprising roughly a third of the population, drawn by the town's emphasis on precision and detail. The culture values patience, exactitude, and quiet mastery over spectacle. Where Crucible celebrates Provings with festival atmosphere, Anvilrest's guild examinations are hushed affairs where the quality of the work speaks for itself.

Wealth is visible but understated. Anvilrest's craftspeople are among the best-compensated in Aethermourne, and their homes reflect it, comfortable, well-appointed, decorated with examples of their own work. The social hierarchy is based entirely on skill, a master gem-cutter commands more respect than a competent one, regardless of commercial success.

### Notable Residents

- [[Polara Facet|Gemwright Polara Facet]], Master of the Jewelers' Conclave. Gnomish, brilliant, impatient with mediocrity. Her experiments with Empyrean Shards are taking her into uncharted territory.
- **Silversmith Aldric Hammerquiet**, A dwarven jeweler of exceptional skill and profound deafness, whose work in acoustic instruments (bells, chimes, tuning forks) is paradoxically the finest in the region. He tests his instruments by touch, feeling the vibrations through his fingertips.
- **Assessor Thimble Gask**, A gnomish bureaucrat responsible for grading and certifying all gems and instruments that leave Anvilrest. Her standards are legendary. Three master gem-cutters have wept in her office.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The Empyrean Experiments.** [[Polara Facet|Gemwright Facet's]] work with Empyrean Shards is producing results that defy conventional understanding. She needs someone to verify what she is seeing in the cut facets, someone she trusts enough to share the visions of catastrophe they seem to reveal.
2. **The Dominion Contract.** The Athenaeum in [[The Ashen Dominion#Ashveil|Ashveil]] has placed an unusually large order for focusing crystals. The Conclave is suspicious of the order's size and urgency, what do the Calculists need that many crystals for?
3. **The Forgery Ring.** Someone is producing convincing counterfeit guild-marks on inferior metalwork and selling it as Anvilrest-certified. The counterfeits are good enough to fool casual buyers but not expert inspection. The Conclave suspects the operation is based in [[Smokewall]], where enforcement is looser.
4. **The Quiet Forge Cooling.** Even Anvilrest's carefully regulated forge is showing temperature instability. The smiths are compensating with artificial heating, but the forge-magic that depends on geothermal energy is becoming less reliable. Precision work that once came naturally now requires conscious effort.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The large Calculist order from the Athenaeum is legitimate, not a Cinder plot. The Star-Readers are trying to build instruments sensitive enough to detect whatever is causing the Star Fade, and they need the finest focusing crystals available. However, the order's size has attracted [[The Cinders]]' attention, and Cinder agents may try to intercept the shipment or suborn the delivery, not to prevent the research, but to ensure the Cinders see the results first.
