## Overview

Cinderfall is a town of steam and sorrow and slow healing. Built on the lower slopes of Mount Thalvor, the Emberveil's largest active volcano, the settlement sprawls around a cluster of geothermal pools whose mineral-rich waters have drawn the sick, the wounded, and the grieving for centuries. The springs range from pleasantly warm to scalding, their temperatures shifting with the deep earth's moods. The water smells of iron and sulphur, and it leaves the skin flushed and tingling. The locals say it draws pain out through the pores.

The town itself is terraced into the mountainside, white stone buildings with red-clay roofs arranged around the pools in a pattern that channels steam through the streets like a slow, warm river. On cold mornings, the entire town vanishes into its own mist, voices and footsteps emerging from white nothing. The effect is dreamlike, and visitors often describe Cinderfall as a place that exists slightly apart from the rest of the world.

Mount Thalvor looms above, its summit perpetually wreathed in smoke. The volcano has been unusually quiet lately, no tremors, no minor venting, none of the small eruptions that the locals consider normal and healthy. To anyone else, a quiet volcano would be reassuring. To Cinderfall, it is ominous. A volcano that grumbles is a volcano that breathes. Silence means something is building, or something is dying.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Mount Thalvor's quieting is directly linked to the Cooling. The volcano's activity is driven by [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] geothermal energy, and as that energy weakens, the volcanic systems are losing pressure. This is a slow process, years, not months, but [[Ashara Cinderbloom|Mender Cinderbloom]] has noticed the springs' healing properties weakening in parallel. She is the closest anyone in the Emberveil has come to diagnosing the problem correctly: the divine energy in the water is thinning.
>
> If Mount Thalvor erupts, it will not be from volcanic pressure but from the forge's compensatory mechanism overreacting. The deep forge's attempts to maintain output could send a pulse of energy through the volcanic system that was designed for steady flow, not surges. The eruption would be devastating but brief, and it would leave the forge even weaker afterward.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Mending Pools

The heart of Cinderfall, a crescent of seventeen geothermal pools ranging from tepid to near-boiling. Each pool has different mineral composition and therapeutic properties, one for joint pain, one for burns, one for respiratory ailments, one, the warmest, said to ease grief itself. The pools are tended by Menders, healers who combine forge-magic techniques with herbalism and hydrotherapy. Treatment is not free, but no one is turned away for inability to pay. The Menders keep a ledger of debts, and most are eventually honoured.

### The Mourning Pool

The largest and most famous spring, set apart from the others in a natural stone amphitheatre where the mountainside curves inward. The water here is blood-warm and almost perfectly still. It is where people come to grieve. There are no Menders here, no treatments, no conversation. Visitors enter the water in silence and leave when they are ready. Some stay for hours. Some come back every year. The Mourning Pool is considered sacred ground, and any disturbance of its peace is the gravest social offence in Cinderfall.

### The Ash Gardens

Terraced gardens on the slopes above the pools, where volcanic soil and geothermal warmth produce medicinal plants that grow nowhere else. The gardens are tended by a dedicated guild of herbalists who supply the Menders with their pharmacopoeia. The plants here bloom year-round, vivid colours against the dark volcanic earth, and the gardens are considered the most beautiful place in the Emberveil, life thriving on the skin of something that could destroy it.

### The Smoke Temple

Not a temple in the traditional sense, but a natural cave near the summit trail where volcanic gases vent in patterns that shift with the mountain's internal pressure. Pilgrims come here to "hear Thalvor breathe," sitting in the warm cave and listening to the hiss and rumble of the vents. Some claim to receive visions. The Menders consider this practice medically irresponsible but culturally important, and they station a healer at the cave entrance during peak pilgrimage season.

## People & Culture

Cinderfall is the most cosmopolitan settlement in the Emberveil, drawing visitors from every region. The permanent population is mixed, roughly half dwarven and half human, with a notable gnomish community that runs many of the town's hospitality establishments. The culture is gentler than Crucible's industrial intensity, shaped by the constant presence of people in pain and the work of easing it.

The Menders are Cinderfall's most respected citizens, healers who understand that forge-magic's principles of shaping and intention can be applied to flesh as well as metal. Their hands are said to feel like warm metal against the skin, a side effect of channelling geothermal energy through forge-magic techniques. The profession is demanding, combining physical skill, magical aptitude, and emotional resilience in equal measure.

### Notable Residents

- [[Ashara Cinderbloom|Mender Ashara Cinderbloom]], Head Mender and chair of the Spring Council. Her patients' recovery rates are declining, and she is desperate to understand why before the springs' reputation collapses.
- **Pilgrim-Warden Dask**, An elderly dwarf who manages access to the Smoke Temple. He has sat in the cave every morning for forty years and claims the mountain's voice has been growing quieter. He whispers this to no one but the Menders.
- **Innkeeper Lissel Ashbrook**, A human woman who runs the Warm Stone Inn, Cinderfall's largest hostelry. She hears everything the visitors say and shares selectively with the Spring Council. A valuable source of intelligence about regional attitudes.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The Weakening Springs.** The healing properties of the Mending Pools are declining. [[Ashara Cinderbloom|Mender Cinderbloom]] has documented a fifteen percent reduction in efficacy over the past two years. Patients who would have recovered in a week now take ten days. The Menders are compensating with herbal supplements and longer treatments, but the trend is clear. If it becomes public knowledge, Cinderfall's economy collapses.
2. **The Silent Mountain.** Mount Thalvor has not vented in three months, the longest quiet period in recorded history. The town is divided between those who find this reassuring and those who find it terrifying. Pilgrim-Warden Dask has begun advising people to prepare evacuation routes, which has not made him popular with the inn-keepers.
3. **The Grief Merchant.** A visiting merchant from [[The Hollowed Reach]] has been offering a "concentrated essence" of the Mourning Pool's water, claiming it can ease grief anywhere. The product is fraudulent, just mineral water in expensive bottles, but the Menders are furious at the commodification of their most sacred site. The merchant has friends in the [[Concord of Tides]] and is not easily removed.
4. **The Ash Garden Blight.** Several medicinal plants in the upper gardens have begun to wilt, their roots cooling as the geothermal warmth beneath the soil weakens. The herbalists are trying to compensate with forge-warmed stones and artificial heating, but the plants seem to respond to [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] heat specifically. Artificial warmth is not enough.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[Ashara Cinderbloom|Mender Cinderbloom's]] research has led her closer to the truth than she realizes. She has been corresponding with [[Corrin Ashveil]] in [[Crucible]], and between them, they are assembling a picture of the Cooling that neither could see alone. If the PCs connect these two, the resulting analysis could provide the first coherent understanding of what is happening to [[The Twelve#Thalvor|Thalvor's]] fire, and the first real data about the Second Silence's physical effects.
