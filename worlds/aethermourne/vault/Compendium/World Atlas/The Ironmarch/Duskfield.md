## Overview

Duskfield is an anomaly. Situated in the central-western [[The Ironmarch#The Iron Fields|Iron Fields]], roughly halfway between [[Ironhaven]] to the southwest and [[The Red Garrison]] to the north, the town occupies a broad, flat stretch of plain where the rust-red soil unexpectedly gives way to dark, fertile earth. In a region where the soil is saturated with divine war-essence, where crops grow stunted and livestock turn vicious, Duskfield sits in a pocket of calm, a three-mile radius where [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] influence fades to a murmur. The grain grows tall here. The cattle are docile. The people do not fight over nothing. It is, by the Ironmarch's standards, paradise, which means everyone wants to control it and no one can afford to destroy it.

The town itself is simple. Low timber-and-stone buildings, granaries built like fortresses, fields stretching to the edges of the calm zone where the red soil begins and the crops wither. A wooden palisade surrounds the settlement, reinforced with iron salvaged from the surrounding countryside. It would not stop a serious assault. Everyone knows this. The town's real defense is its indispensability: destroy Duskfield's harvest and the Ironmarch starves. Even the most ambitious warlord needs to eat.

[[Alder Brightfield]], the town's mayor, has declared Duskfield neutral territory. He supplies grain to all sides, charges fair prices, and refuses military alliances. It is a strategy built on the knife-edge between diplomacy and desperation, and [[Alder Brightfield|Brightfield]] knows it will eventually fail. Someone will decide that controlling the food is worth more than buying it. He just hopes that day comes after his lifetime.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The calm zone is not natural. Beneath Duskfield lies a fragment of [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]], Goddess of Nature, a piece of divine bone that fell during the Theomachis and was buried by centuries of soil. Thyrea's nature-essence dampens [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] war-essence in a localized radius. No one in Duskfield knows this. [[The Ember Guild]] does not know this. If the fragment were excavated or destroyed, the calm zone would collapse and Duskfield would become as hostile as the rest of the Ironmarch within weeks.
>
> [[Alder Brightfield]] is more cunning than he appears. He has quietly negotiated mutual-defense pacts with three smaller settlements, stockpiled weapons in hidden caches, and bribed scouts from both [[Yrsa Bloodhand|Yrsa's]] and [[Mira the Red|Mira's]] forces for advance warning of any attack. He is not a warrior, but he is a survivor, and survivors in the Ironmarch learn to plan for the worst.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Granary Fort

Duskfield's granaries are its most important structures, built with stone foundations, iron-banded walls, and guarded day and night. Three massive buildings capable of storing enough grain to feed the entire Ironmarch for two months. The Granary Fort is the most heavily defended part of town, more fortified than the mayor's hall, because everyone understands what matters here.

### The Green Belt

The three-mile radius of calm farmland surrounding the settlement. Wheat, barley, root vegetables, and modest orchards thrive within its borders. At the edges, where [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] influence reasserts itself, the crops transition rapidly from healthy to blighted, a visible boundary that locals call the Redline. Stepping across the Redline feels like walking from a warm room into a cold wind, except the cold is anger.

### The Market Square

A modest open square where Duskfield conducts its grain trade. Buyers from across the Ironmarch come here, and the square is one of the few places where representatives of rival warlords stand in the same space without fighting. The peace holds because of the grain. Merchants who cause trouble are banned from purchase, and a banned merchant returns to their warlord empty-handed.

### The Quiet Chapel

A small shrine to no particular god, though some whisper it was originally dedicated to [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]. The chapel is a place of rest, rarely used for formal worship, more often visited by travelers who want to sit somewhere that does not hum with war-essence. The building is old, older than the town itself, and its foundation stones do not match any local quarry.

## People & Culture

Duskfield's people are farmers, not fighters, and they are proud of this distinction. In a region that worships strength and ambition, Duskfield values patience, cooperation, and the quiet competence of people who make things grow. This does not make them weak. It makes them stubborn in a different way, the stubbornness of roots rather than steel.

The town is deeply communal. Harvests are shared, labor is rotated, and the council makes decisions by consensus. Newcomers are welcomed cautiously, screened for signs of heavy contamination and given time to "calm down" before being integrated. The process takes weeks, sometimes months, as the absence of war-essence allows deeply ingrained aggression to surface and dissipate.

### Notable Residents

- [[Alder Brightfield]], mayor. A farmer who became a diplomat by necessity. He wants Duskfield to survive, and he will do whatever it takes to ensure that, including things that keep him awake at night.
- **Healer Maren**, the town's physician and midwife. One of the few trained healers in the Ironmarch, she came to Duskfield because it was the only place she could practice without her patients attacking her.
- **Tam Redsoil**, the town's head farmer and Brightfield's closest advisor. A weathered woman who can predict the harvest to within a bushel and has never been wrong.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. [[Mira the Red|Mira the Red's]] forces have been spotted near the Redline. They have not crossed it, but they are not leaving either. [[Alder Brightfield]] has sent messages to [[Yrsa Bloodhand]] requesting support and received no reply.
2. A well near the Quiet Chapel has begun producing water that tastes faintly sweet and floral, utterly unlike anything else in the Ironmarch. Roots in the well shaft have been growing at unnatural speed. Something beneath Duskfield is waking up.
3. A group of refugees from a destroyed settlement has arrived at Duskfield's gates. They are aggressive, contaminated, and desperate. Turning them away might mean their deaths. Taking them in risks the town's fragile peace.
4. The Granary Fort's stores have been depleted faster than expected. Either the harvest was smaller than reported, or someone is stealing grain. [[Alder Brightfield|Brightfield]] suspects the latter and trusts no one enough to investigate openly.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]] fragment beneath Duskfield is responding to the same forces that are activating [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] fragments. As the war-essence intensifies, the nature-essence is pushing back harder, which is why the well water is changing and the roots are growing. If the PCs investigate the Quiet Chapel's foundations, they can find a sealed passage leading to a natural cavern containing a piece of [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea's]] divine bone the size of a wagon. The bone radiates calm, growth, and renewal, the opposite of everything the Ironmarch represents.
>
> This fragment is enormously valuable. [[The Verdant Marches]] would go to war to retrieve it. [[The Ember Guild]] would pay a fortune to study it. And if [[Mira the Red]] learns about it, she will try to destroy it, because a pocket of peace in the Ironmarch is an obstacle to the total war that [[The Twelve#Kaevroth|Kaevroth's]] will demands.
