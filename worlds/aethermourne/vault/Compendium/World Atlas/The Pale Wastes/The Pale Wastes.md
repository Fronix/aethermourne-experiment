# The Pale Wastes

> [!quote]
> "The dead don't hate you. That's the part they never understand in the south. The dead don't feel anything at all."
> , Commander [[Thessa Ironveil]], address to newly sworn Vigil recruits

---

## Overview

The Pale Wastes are the frozen northern reaches of Aethermourne, a vast ash-colored tundra where [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]], God of Death, fell from the sky during the Theomachis and did not die properly. The mechanism of death shattered. Things still die here, they die often, badly, and young, but the part that comes after, the staying-dead part, broke. The dead outnumber the living by a margin no one likes to calculate. The cold goes deeper than weather, a necromantic chill radiating from divine bones buried in the frozen earth.

## Geography

### The Ashborn Tundra

Vast frozen plains covering hundreds of miles, rendered in shades of grey and bone-white. Hardy lichens and frost-grass cling to the ground. Bone-cairn route markers guide travelers between Holds, each cairn glowing faintly in the dark, within sight of the next under clear conditions. Frost-elk, massive domesticated beasts standing seven feet at the shoulder, serve as mounts and draft animals. They can sense undead at a distance.

**Bone-Storms** are the tundra's most feared hazard. Winds from the [[#The Marrow Fields|Marrow Fields]] carry divine bone-dust saturated with necromantic energy. Effects include **death-sight** (temporary perception of spirits), **tissue necrosis** (blackened extremities, autonomous twitching in severe cases), and **spell contamination** (healing may drain, necromancy amplifies wildly). Bone-storms have increased roughly 40% in frequency, and recent storms carry whispers that do not belong to [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]].

### The Marrow Fields

Scattered sites where Morrhael's black divine bones protrude from the frozen earth, ranging from finger-length fragments to ribs the size of city walls. Standing near the bones causes progressive emotional erosion: exhaustion, then grey depression, then a peaceful acceptance that is the trap. People stop wanting to leave, sit in the frost, and wait for the cold to finish them. The [[The Ashen Vigil|Vigil]] calls this **bone-pull**, one of the leading causes of death in the Wastes.

**Morrhaelite**, a black glass-like crystal growing along fracture lines in the divine bone, is harder than steel, holds an edge indefinitely, and resonates with necromantic energy. The [[The Ashen Vigil|Vigil's]] signature black-glass blades are forged from it. Bone-harvesting teams work in threes: one cuts, one carries, one watches for bone-pull and undead. Dangerous, lucrative, grim.

### The Hollowdeep

A thirty-mile crater where [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael's]] skull struck the earth. The most dangerous location in Aethermourne outside the Abyssal Trench. Epicenter of necromantic contamination. Tens of thousands of organized undead dwell within: skeletal formations, spectral entities, bone-wraiths, and worse things near the skull that the Watchers at [[Lastlight]] refuse to describe.

The Hollowdeep has been **silent for twenty years**. No eruptions, no hordes spilling out. The Watchers have observed undead forming ranks, drilling in military formations, and constructing structures within the crater. This silence is preparation, not peace.

### The Bitter Sea

The frozen ocean to the north. Ice-fishing provides essential food, pale translucent fish driven into nets weighted with divine bone. Shapes move under the ice, dark forms too large to be fish, too purposeful to be currents. The locals do not discuss them. Expeditions north across the ice have never returned.

## Society & Culture

**Survival first.** Everything else is luxury. A Hold that prioritizes beauty over firewood has beautiful frozen corpses.

**The dead must be honored and feared.** They deserve names spoken aloud and ashes kept. They also deserve fire, immediately, before the thing wearing their face stands up and kills someone.

**Strength through endurance.** The Wastes reward stubbornness the way other cultures admire wit.

**Community is everything.** A person alone in the Wastes is dead. This is not metaphor. Hold-folk live in close quarters, share meals, share labor, share warmth.

**Pragmatism over purity.** If [[Remnants Court|Remnants]] can fight, they fight alongside the living. If necromantic bone can ward against the dead, it is harvested and used.

### Funerary Practice

Bodies are cremated within four hours on Pyre Grounds dusted with divine bone-ash. At **The Naming**, every person present speaks the deceased's name aloud, a single syllable of defiance against the quiet. Ash is kept in sealed urns by the family. Failure to burn the dead is not negligence. It is betrayal.

### The Remnants

Intelligent undead who retained awareness after rising. Controversial. [[Ashenmere]] is their settlement, founded 300 years ago under the Remnant Accords. Some Holds accept them as allies. Others destroy them on sight. The debate is the Wastes' deepest fault line.

## Current Tensions

1. **The Silent Hollowdeep.** Twenty years without eruption. Organized formations observed. Something is building an army.
2. **Remnant integration.** [[Bjorn Ashken]] advocates cooperation. Many Holds resist. [[Revenant Yael]] demands recognition.
3. **Deepening isolation.** [[The Ashen Dominion]] is reducing the [[Pale Sentry]] garrison. No outside help is coming.
4. **Bone-storms increasing.** Forty percent more necromantic particulate. Storms now carry whispers.
5. **Lost scouts.** Fifty-four [[The Ashen Vigil|Vigil]] veterans vanished in the Hollowdeep. [[Thessa Ironveil]] is planning a major expedition.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> [[The Twelve#Serith|Serith]] is directing the Hollowdeep's undead through [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael's]] shattered death-domain. She has spent fifty years learning to command the dead, and the last twenty building military formations. The army will march south through the Wastes, past [[Pale Sentry]], and into the heartland of Aethermourne. The lost scouts were captured, their bodies added to the army, their maps of Hold defenses taken. The silence is not peace. It is a weapon being loaded.
