## Overview

Ashbloom should not work. A community of living humans and intelligent undead, deep in the Wilt where [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s death-pull saturates everything, surviving on subsistence and stubbornness. It should have collapsed years ago. Instead it has endured for over a century, and it has become something that exists nowhere else in Aethermourne: a place where the living and the dead share a table, share labor, share grief, and, during the Cycle's life-phase, share something that might be hope.

When [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s growth-pulse sweeps through the Wilt, it carries warmth. For the living, this is pleasant, a few hours of green and bloom in an otherwise skeletal landscape. For [[Remnants Court|Remnants]], it is shattering. The Cycle's life-phase briefly restores what death took: warmth in cold flesh, hunger in an empty stomach, the sensation of breathing in lungs that no longer need air. It lasts minutes, sometimes an hour. Then it passes, and the cold returns, and the Remnants endure until the next turn.

They come back anyway. From [[Ashenmere]], from the Holds, from wherever the undead who remember being alive have heard the rumor. Ashbloom. The place where the dead can feel alive. Even if it is only for a moment. Even if the moment makes the silence afterward worse.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> The Cycle's restoration effect on [[Remnants Court|Remnants]] is not random. Ashbloom sits on a convergence point where [[The Twelve#Thyrea|Thyrea]]'s deepest root-tendrils reach into the Wilt, creating a pulse of life-energy strong enough to temporarily override [[The Twelve#Morrhael|Morrhael]]'s death-hold on Remnant bodies. This convergence is fragile. If the Cycle tips further toward death-dominance, the restoration effect will cease, and Ashbloom's Remnant population will lose the only thing that keeps them sane.
>
> [[Revenant Moss]] knows this. They have felt the restoration weakening over the past year and have not told anyone. They are afraid that if word gets out, the community will fracture, the living leaving, the Remnants despairing.

## Layout & Landmarks

**The Ashfield**, a broad clearing of grey soil and bone-white grass where the community gathers during the Cycle's life-phase. When the warmth comes, Remnants sit in the Ashfield with their eyes closed, feeling the sun on skin that can feel it again. Living residents sit with them. No one speaks. It is the most sacred space in Ashbloom, not because anyone declared it so, but because everyone understands.

**The Quiet Houses**, Ashbloom's residential quarter. Simple structures of preserved deadwood and stone, built low and sturdy against the Wilt's bone-cold winds. Living and Remnant residents live side by side, though the Remnants' homes are unheated, they don't need warmth, and the fuel is better spent on the living. Each Quiet House has a marker by the door: green for living, grey for Remnant, so visitors know which customs to observe inside.

**The Welcome Fire**, a permanent bonfire at the settlement's edge, maintained by the living residents as a beacon for incoming Remnant pilgrims. The fire serves a practical purpose, Remnant travelers navigating the Wilt can see it for miles, and a symbolic one. Fire is the traditional enemy of undead in the Pale Wastes. In Ashbloom, fire means safety. The inversion is deliberate.

**Moss's Hall**, a meeting house where [[Revenant Moss]] holds community councils and receives pilgrims. The hall is decorated with pressed flowers from the Cycle's life-phases, carefully preserved between sheets of bark, each one a record of a moment when the dead felt alive.

## People & Culture

Ashbloom's culture is built on radical empathy. The living residents chose to share their home with the dead, not out of obligation or ideology, but because they watched the Remnants weep when the warmth came, and they could not walk away from that. The Remnants chose to stay, not because the restoration is reliable or sufficient, but because Ashbloom is the only place that does not ask them to pretend they are not grieving for what they lost.

Daily life is quiet, practical, and gentle in ways that surprise visitors. The living cook for themselves and offer food to Remnants during the life-phase, when they can taste it. Remnants handle the heavy labor during the death-phase, when their undead strength is at its peak and the living need rest. Both groups maintain the settlement, tend the Ashfield, and care for each other with the matter-of-fact tenderness of people who have stopped being afraid of death because they live next to it.

Funerary practices in Ashbloom are unique. When a living resident dies, the community waits one full Cycle before cremation, giving the deceased a chance to rise as a Remnant. Some do. Some don't. Either way, the community grieves and then continues.

### Notable Residents

- **[[Revenant Moss]]**, community leader and voice of the Thornwild's Remnant population. They have been here forty years and have become the settlement's moral center. They want Ashbloom to endure and the restoration to continue, but they fear both are failing.
- **Healer Sif**, a living woman who came to Ashbloom as a young medic and never left. She specializes in treating the living residents' ailments and has developed techniques for easing the physical distress Remnants experience when the restoration fades. She wants to understand the restoration well enough to extend it.
- **Pilgrim Thane**, a recently arrived Remnant from [[Ashenmere]] who experienced their first restoration a week ago and has not stopped talking about it since. They want to bring more Remnants here. They are not yet aware of the complications.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The restoration is weakening.** Life-phase warmth in Ashbloom has been shorter and less intense for the past year. [[Revenant Moss]] suspects the Cycle's acceleration is responsible. If the restoration fails entirely, Ashbloom's Remnant population will face despair on a community-wide scale.
2. **Pilgrim surge.** Word has reached [[Ashenmere]], and [[Remnants Court|Remnants]] are arriving in increasing numbers. Ashbloom cannot support them all. The joint council is debating limits on pilgrims, a decision that feels like closing a door on the desperate.
3. **Vigil patrol.** An [[The Ashen Vigil|Ashen Vigil]] patrol has been sighted in the Thornwild's northeast, investigating reports of "an undead settlement." Their intent is unclear. Some Ashbloom residents remember why they left the Wastes and are preparing to fight.
4. **A Remnant is not returning.** During the last death-phase, one Remnant, a pilgrim named Saen, did not revert. They remain warm, breathing, apparently alive. It has been three days. No one knows if it will last. Everyone is watching.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> Saen's apparent restoration is permanent, but it is not what it appears. The Cycle has not restored Saen to life. It has locked them in a permanent transition state, similar to [[The Bloom-Wraith]] but on a smaller scale. Saen feels alive, but their body is neither living nor dead, and it will begin to deteriorate in ways that neither medicine nor necromancy can address. This is a preview of what happens when the Cycle's balance breaks on a human scale, and it is a tragedy wearing the mask of a miracle.
