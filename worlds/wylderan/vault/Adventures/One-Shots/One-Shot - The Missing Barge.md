# One-Shot: The Missing Barge

*An investigation into a vanished grain barge leads to the discovery that the gods are still dying, and the living are building their economies on crumbling divine bones.*

---

## What You Need

**Players:** 4-6 characters, levels 1-3

**Setting:** [[Millford]] (investigation hub), the Millrun River (travel), [[The Pull Site]] (climax location)

**Tone:** Investigation with social pressure and mounting supernatural dread. The world is fragile. The answers are worse than the questions. No solution is clean.

**Key NPCs:**
- [[Halford Quick]] (dock master, knows the river)
- [[Dace Torrn]] (barge captain, hires PCs or provides intel)
- [[Lira Fenn]] (Saltmere trader investigating from the other end)
- [[Miren Hallis]] (widow of missing barge captain, desperate for closure)
- [[Brenna Cray]] (miller, notices ford deepening, suspects something wrong)

**Maps/Handouts:**
- Millford settlement map (docks, mills, ford)
- Millrun River route sketch (Millford to delta, travel distance)
- Pull Site hazard diagram (convergence channels, wreck location)
- Barge manifest (cargo from [[Ashcrag]], stamped by [[Garrick Thane]])

**Music Suggestions:**
- Investigation: Low, ambient river sounds, tension strings
- Travel: Flowing water, distant drums, building unease
- Climax: Deep bass pulse (heartbeat), distorted grinding stone

## Overview

Three weeks ago, a grain barge left [[Millford]]'s docks bound for [[Saltmere]]'s markets. The barge carried highland grain worth 200 gold, milled at [[Garrick Thane]]'s operation and financed by lowland creditors. The crew: three experienced river men, including captain Hallis, a veteran of the route. The journey should have taken two days.

The barge never arrived.

The captain's widow, [[Miren Hallis]], has been demanding answers from [[Millford]]'s village council, but [[Halford Quick]] (the dock master) has no explanation. Pirates? Sinking? Desertion? The river is unpredictable, barges are lost, but three weeks without wreckage washing up is unusual. Rumors circulate among the dockworkers: "the pull," a strange autumn current that drags barges off course. Older barge captains avoid the delta during low water, but no one will say why.

The PCs are hired to investigate. Follow the barge route downstream, ask questions in [[Saltmere]], find evidence. If the barge sank, recover cargo or bodies for closure. If it was attacked, identify the threat. Simple work. Except the river is changing, and what they discover at the delta will reveal that the dead gods are not inert. They are still dying. And the trade route that feeds two regions is built on bones that are dissolving into silt.

---

## Background

Seventy-five years ago, [[The Passage Keeper]], god of rivers and crossings, died during the Theomachis. The god's body did not petrify like [[The Twelve#The Stone Warden|The Stone Warden]]. It fragmented, breaking into pieces that scattered along the Millrun River. The skull rests at the highland confluence. The ribcage stabilizes the ford at Millford. The heart, the largest fragment, lies beneath the delta marshes at [[The Pull Site]].

The heart did not stop beating when the god died. It pulses, trying to pump divine essence back through the fragmented body, sending out a command to "return, flow back, come together." This divine will manifests as a supernatural downstream current that appears during autumn low water, when the river's natural flow weakens. The pull is strengthening. What was once a rare phenomenon is now a deadly threat. Three barges have been lost in the past year.

The [[Saltmere Merchant Assembly]] knows about the pull. They have chosen to suppress the information, fearing that public knowledge will halt grain shipments and trigger economic collapse. Older barge captains know to avoid the delta at low tide, but younger captains are not warned. The Assembly believes the disappearances are an acceptable cost to maintain trade.

The missing barge was caught in the pull, dragged into the convergence channel, and capsized. All three crew drowned. The wreck rests at the bottom, 20 feet down, held in place by the same current that killed them.

## Adventure Synopsis

**Act I: Millford Investigation (Hub)**
PCs arrive in [[Millford]], meet the widow or are hired by [[Dace Torrn]]. They interview NPCs, gather clues about the barge's route and "the pull" rumors, and discover economic pressure to keep the investigation quiet. They establish [[Millford]] as a base and learn the ford is sacred geography (divine bones stabilizing the crossing).

**Act II: River Travel (Escalation)**
PCs follow the barge route downstream, either by hiring a boat or traveling the riverbank. They encounter environmental hazards tied to divine decay, witness the pull's effects (reversed currents, shadows beneath the water), and meet [[Lira Fenn]] investigating from the Saltmere side. The supernatural threat escalates as they approach the delta.

**Act III: The Pull Site (Climax)**
PCs reach the convergence point where the pull is strongest. They navigate the hazard, discover the missing barge wreck, and potentially dive to recover bodies or cargo. The climax reveals the divine heart beneath the delta, pulsing and reassembling. PCs must choose: report the truth publicly (trigger economic panic), suppress findings for payment (complicit in cover-up), or investigate deeper (commit to campaign arc).

## Hooks

Choose one or combine multiple entry points:

> [!info] Hook 1: The Widow's Plea
> [[Miren Hallis]], widow of the missing barge captain, approaches the PCs at [[Millford]]'s docks. Her husband has been missing for three weeks, and the village council has done nothing. She offers 50 gold (her life savings) to find out what happened. She does not expect him to be alive. She wants closure, a body to bury, or confirmation that he died honorably. She suspects foul play, lowland pirates or rival traders sabotaging highland cargo.

> [!info] Hook 2: Dace Torrn's Contract
> [[Dace Torrn]], a veteran barge captain and river trader, hires the PCs directly. He needs to know if the delta is safe. Three barges lost in a year is not normal. If there is a hazard, navigational or supernatural, he wants it mapped so he can adjust his route. He offers 100 gold plus a share of any salvaged cargo. Dace suspects "the pull" is real but needs proof to convince other captains to change their autumn schedules.

> [!info] Hook 3: Council Directive
> [[Halford Quick]], Millford's dock master, approaches the PCs on behalf of the village council. Officially, the council wants closure for the widow. Unofficially, [[Garrick Thane]] (the dominant miller) wants to know if his cargo was stolen or lost. If stolen, he will pursue compensation. If lost to hazard, he will adjust future shipments. The council offers modest payment (30 gold) but expects discretion.

> [!info] Hook 4: Lira Fenn's Warning
> [[Lira Fenn]], a Saltmere barge captain investigating from the delta side, intercepts the PCs at [[Millford]]. She knows the barge is at the Pull Site and warns them that diving there is deadly. She offers to guide them if they share findings, but she suspects the [[Saltmere Merchant Assembly]] is covering up the hazard. She wants proof to force the Assembly to act. Lira provides the most direct path to the truth but also brings factional tension.

## Act I: Millford Investigation

### Overview

The PCs arrive in [[Millford]], a working river town of 600 people where three water mills dominate the economy and the ford crossing is the town's reason for existing. The investigation begins at the docks, where [[Halford Quick]] manages barge traffic and [[Dace Torrn]] operates as the most respected captain on the route. The widow, [[Miren Hallis]], lives in a small cottage near the Upper Docks, within sight of where her husband's barge last launched.

The key to Act I is establishing Millford as a place of economic fragility and factional tension. The millers take a fifth of every bushel. The farmers resent them. The barge captains navigate between highland creditors and lowland markets. Everyone is in debt to someone. The missing barge is not just a tragedy, it is a financial loss that ripples through multiple factions.

### Scene 1: The Docks

**Setting:** Millford's Upper Docks, rough-built wooden platforms with mooring posts and covered storage sheds. Barges are tied up waiting to cross the ford or launch downstream. The smell of river water and grain dust. Dockworkers hauling cargo. [[Halford Quick]]'s small office overlooks the activity.

**What Happens:**
The PCs meet [[Halford Quick]] and/or [[Dace Torrn]], depending on which hook brought them here. Halford is defensive, reluctant to admit he does not know what happened. The dock master's job is to keep barges safe, and losing one reflects poorly on him. He provides basic facts: the barge left three weeks ago, crewed by Hallis (captain) and two workers, carrying 200 gold worth of milled grain from [[Garrick Thane]]'s operation, bound for [[Saltmere]]'s markets. No wreckage has washed up. No word from Saltmere.

**Skill Checks:**
- **DC 10 Insight:** Halford knows more than he is saying. He has heard rumors about "the pull" but dismisses them as superstition.
- **DC 12 Persuasion:** Halford admits older barge captains avoid the delta during autumn low water, but he does not know why. "Ask Dace. He knows the river better than I do."

**NPC: [[Halford Quick]]**
Mid-40s, weather-worn dock master. Practical, defensive, overworked. He wants the barge found so the widow stops harassing the council, but he fears what the truth might reveal. If the delta is unsafe, trade collapses, and Millford starves.

**NPC: [[Dace Torrn]]**
Early 50s, experienced barge captain. Gruff but fair. He has heard the pull rumors and believes them. He warns the PCs: "The delta changes in autumn. Current goes the wrong way. Shadows under the water. Grinding sounds. I avoid it. Smart captains do the same. Hallis was smart. Something caught him."

**Complication:**
If the PCs ask too many questions publicly, a dockworker warns them: "[[Garrick Thane]] won't like you poking around. That cargo was his. He has debts to lowland creditors. If it was stolen, he needs proof. If it was lost, he needs silence."

### Scene 2: The Widow

**Setting:** Miren Hallis's cottage near the Upper Docks, a small timber structure with a single room, a cold hearth, and a barge captain's tools hanging on the wall. [[Miren Hallis]] is in her late 30s, exhausted, grieving, and angry.

**What Happens:**
Miren provides personal context. Her husband was a veteran of the route, cautious and skilled. He would not have taken risks. She suspects foul play: lowland pirates, rival traders, or Highland Merchant Cartel sabotage (her husband owed debts to [[Tallow Breck]], and she believes the Cartel wanted the cargo seized). She gives the PCs the barge manifest, showing the cargo was stamped by [[Garrick Thane]] and financed by lowland creditors.

**Skill Checks:**
- **DC 12 Empathy:** Miren is desperate for closure, not vengeance. She will accept any truth, even if it means her husband drowned in an accident, as long as she knows.
- **DC 14 Insight:** Miren suspects the village council is suppressing information because [[Garrick Thane]] does not want the investigation to reveal economic problems with the trade route.

**Roleplay Note:**
Miren is exhausted from grief. She does not shout or plead. She asks, quietly, "Just tell me what happened. I need to know he did not suffer. Or if he did, I need to know it was not my fault for letting him take that run."

### Scene 3: The Miller's Pressure

**Setting:** [[Brenna Cray]]'s mill (Kess Mill), upstream on the northern edge of town. A sturdy stone structure with a single large grinding wheel. Brenna is the finest miller in town, respected but economically weaker than [[Garrick Thane]].

**What Happens:**
If the PCs ask around about the ford or the river's behavior, they are directed to Brenna. She has noticed the ford is changing: the water is deeper than it was five years ago, the cobbles are sinking, and the current is less predictable. She does not know why, but she suspects the river itself is unstable. "My grandfather built this mill sixty years ago. He told me the ford was sacred, blessed by the river god. I thought it was a story. Now I wonder."

**Skill Checks:**
- **DC 10 Nature or History:** The ford's cobbled bed is not mortar-laid. The stones are too smooth, too uniform. They look like bone fragments, partially mineralized.
- **DC 12 Religion or Lore:** The Passage Keeper, the river god, died during the Theomachis. The ford could be built on divine remains, which would explain its stability and why it is now failing.

**Complication:**
If the PCs reveal they are investigating the missing barge, Brenna warns them: "[[Garrick Thane]] controls the council. If your investigation threatens his trade contracts, he will make your life difficult. Be careful who you trust."

### Scene 4: Gathering Clues

The PCs compile evidence before heading downstream:

**Key Clues:**
1. **Barge route:** Millford to Saltmere, 25-30 miles downstream, 2-day journey at normal speed.
2. **The pull:** Older captains avoid the delta during autumn low water. Rumors describe reversed currents, shadows beneath the water, and grinding sounds.
3. **Economic stakes:** The cargo was financed by lowland creditors. If it was lost, highland producers (including [[Garrick Thane]]) face debt default. If publicized, trade stops.
4. **Ford instability:** The sacred crossing is sinking, suggesting divine decay.
5. **Assembly silence:** [[Saltmere Merchant Assembly]] has not sent word about the missing barge, which is unusual. Standard protocol is to notify Millford if a barge fails to arrive.

**Transition to Act II:**
The PCs decide to follow the barge route downstream, either by hiring a boat (50 gold from [[Dace Torrn]] or [[Lira Fenn]]) or traveling the riverbank on foot (slower, 3 days instead of 1-2). [[Dace Torrn]] warns them: "If you go by boat, launch at high tide. Avoid the delta at low water. If you feel the current pulling wrong, turn back immediately."

## Act II: River Travel

### Overview

The journey downstream from Millford to the delta is 25-30 miles, taking 1-2 days by boat or 3 days on foot. The river widens and slows as the terrain flattens. The banks become lower, more prone to flooding. Oxbow lakes and abandoned channels scar the floodplain, evidence of the river's unstable course. As the PCs approach the delta, the pull's effects become visible: reversed currents, shadows beneath the water, and the grinding pulse of the divine heart.

Act II establishes escalation. The investigation shifts from social pressure (Millford factions) to supernatural dread (the river itself is wrong). The PCs encounter environmental hazards, witness the pull's phenomena, and meet [[Lira Fenn]] investigating from the other direction.

### Scene 1: Downriver Passage

**Setting:** The Lower Millrun, between Millford and the delta. The river is wide, 100-150 feet across, with slow current and muddy banks. Marsh willows and reeds grow along the edges. Oxbow lakes glimmer in the distance. The water is murky, cold, and smells faintly of silt and decay.

**What Happens:**
The PCs travel downstream, navigating the river. If by boat, they make steady progress but must watch for sandbars and shallow channels. If on foot, they follow the riverbank trail, passing abandoned ferry crossings and small farmsteads. The journey is uneventful at first, but signs of the pull begin to appear:

- **Reversed Current (first warning):** At one point, the PCs notice the current flowing the wrong direction, upstream toward Millford instead of downstream toward the bay. The effect lasts 10-15 minutes, then reverses back to normal. Local fishermen on the bank ignore it, as if this is routine.

- **Shadows Beneath the Surface:** Large, slow-moving shapes are visible beneath the water when the light is right. They do not surface. They do not behave like living creatures. A DC 12 Perception check reveals: "The shadows are circular, turning in place, converging on something downstream."

- **Grinding Sound (distant):** A deep, rhythmic grinding noise rises from the river ahead, audible over wind and wave. It sounds like stone scraping against stone, like millstones turning. The sound pulses, faint but regular. DC 10 Awareness: "The sound is coming from the delta."

**Skill Checks:**
- **DC 12 Navigation:** The PCs avoid sandbars and shallow channels, making good time.
- **DC 14 Nature:** The river's behavior is unnatural. Currents do not reverse on their own. Something is pulling the water upstream.

### Scene 2: Meeting Lira Fenn

**Setting:** A small ferry crossing 10 miles north of the delta, where [[Lira Fenn]]'s barge *Tidemark* is moored. Lira is repairing rigging damage from a recent encounter with the pull.

**What Happens:**
Lira spots the PCs and hails them. She is direct: "You're heading to the delta, aren't you? Looking for the missing barge? I know where it is. At the bottom of the convergence channel. The pull dragged it down three weeks ago. I saw the mast sticking up at low tide."

Lira offers to guide the PCs to the Pull Site, but she wants something in return: proof that the [[Saltmere Merchant Assembly]] is covering up the hazard. "The Assembly knows barges are vanishing. They are hiding it to keep trade running. If you can prove it, I will force them to act. Otherwise, more people die."

**NPC: [[Lira Fenn]]**
Early 30s, skilled barge captain from Saltmere. Blunt, pragmatic, angry at the Assembly's cover-up. She respects competence and hates dishonesty. She will guide the PCs if they agree to share findings, but she will not risk her life for free.

**Complication:**
Lira warns the PCs that diving at the Pull Site is deadly. "The water is thick near the convergence. Hard to swim. The current pulls you down. I have seen divers go under and not come back up. If you want the cargo, you need rope, weights, and luck. If you want bodies, same. If you just want proof the barge is there, we can see the mast at low tide without diving."

**Transition:**
Lira agrees to guide the PCs to the Pull Site, but only at low tide when the mast is visible. "High tide masks the hazard, but we cannot see the wreck. Low tide exposes it, but the pull is stronger. Your choice."

### Scene 3: Delta Approach

**Setting:** The delta marshes, 2 miles upstream from [[Saltmere]]'s docks. The river splits into three channels: Westrun, Centerflow, and Saltcurrent. The convergence point is ahead, where the channels meet before widening into the bay. The water is deeper here, 15-20 feet, and the currents create eddies and cross-flows.

**What Happens:**
As the PCs approach the convergence, the pull's effects intensify:

- **Reversed Current (strong):** The water flows upstream against the tide, pulling vessels toward the convergence point. Steering requires a DC 12 skill check (Seamanship, Navigation). On a failure, the boat is pulled 50 feet closer to the center.

- **Grinding Sound (loud):** The pulse is audible now, clear and rhythmic. Thud. Pause. Thud. Like a massive heartbeat. DC 10 Awareness: "The sound is organic, not mechanical. Something living is down there."

- **Water Density:** Near the convergence, the water becomes heavier, thicker. Oars drag. Poles sink too deep. DC 12 Athletics to maintain rowing speed.

**Skill Checks:**
- **DC 14 Navigation:** Avoid being pulled into the center. Three consecutive failures mean the boat is caught and cannot escape without jettisoned cargo or external rescue.
- **DC 12 Willpower Save:** PCs near the water hear whispers in the current (*return, flow back, come together*). On a failure, they feel compelled to move toward the convergence, drawn by the divine will.

**Complication:**
If the PCs are caught in the pull, they must choose: jettison cargo to lighten the load (lose salvage value), cut the boat loose and swim for shore (DC 14 Athletics, drowning risk), or call for rescue (Lira tows them out if present, but it takes time and exposes her boat to the same risk).

## Act III: The Pull Site

### Overview

The climax occurs at [[The Pull Site]], where the three delta channels converge before widening into the bay. The missing barge wreck is here, 20 feet below the surface, held in place by the supernatural current. Beneath it, deeper still, the Passage Keeper's heart rests on the riverbed, the size of a house, pulsing and sending out divine will to reassemble the fragmented corpse.

Act III is choice-driven. The PCs can:
- **Recover evidence** (bodies, cargo, barge manifest) to prove what happened.
- **Investigate the divine cause** (dive deeper, discover the heart, understand the threat).
- **Decide what to report** (public truth, suppressed findings, or commit to deeper investigation).

The tone is supernatural dread balanced with moral pressure. The river is dying. Trade depends on ignoring the danger. The truth will harm people. Silence will kill more.

### Scene 1: The Wreck

**Setting:** The convergence point at low tide. The broken mast of the missing barge protrudes 2 feet above the waterline, angled and splintered. The water around it is dark, murky, and unnaturally still despite the surrounding current.

**What Happens:**
The PCs locate the wreck. At low tide, the mast is visible for 2-3 hours, confirming the barge is here. To recover cargo or bodies, they must dive 20 feet in water affected by the pull's density and reversed current.

**Diving Challenges:**
- **DC 14 Athletics:** Swim down to the wreck against the current and water density. On a failure, the PC is pulled deeper toward the heart (additional DC 16 save to avoid being dragged to 40 feet depth).
- **Drowning Risk:** PCs can hold their breath for 1 minute (10 rounds). After that, DC 12 Constitution save each round or begin drowning.
- **Cargo Recovery:** Grain sacks are rotted, but a few are salvageable (20 gold value if returned to [[Garrick Thane]]). The barge manifest, sealed in a waterproof pouch, is intact.
- **Body Recovery:** Two crew bodies are trapped inside the hull. Recovering them requires DC 12 Strength to break planks and DC 14 Athletics to haul them to the surface. [[Miren Hallis]] and the families will be grateful (50 gold reward, reputation increase in Millford).

**Complication:**
If the PCs spend more than 3 rounds underwater near the wreck, they hear the heartbeat clearly and see flashes of other locations along the river (the ford at Millford, the confluence at Ashcrag). DC 12 Willpower save or become disoriented (disadvantage on next action).

### Scene 2: The Heart

**Setting:** 40 feet below the surface, at the deepest part of the convergence channel. The Passage Keeper's heart rests on the riverbed, partially buried in silt, covered in barnacles and river weeds. It is the size of a house, oblong and pulsing. The pulse is visible: the surface ripples, the silt shifts, and divine light glows faintly through cracks in the mineralized tissue.

**What Happens:**
If the PCs dive deeper (intentionally or by being pulled), they discover the heart. This is the campaign's thesis moment: the dead gods are not inert. They are still dying. Their remains are active, dangerous, and reshaping the world.

**Discovery:**
- **DC 10 Religion or Lore:** This is the Passage Keeper's heart, a fragment of the river god's corpse. The heart did not stop beating when the god died.
- **DC 12 Arcana or Nature:** The heart is trying to pump divine essence back through the fragmented body, sending out a command to "return, flow back, come together." This is what creates the pull.
- **DC 14 Insight:** The heart's pulse rate is accelerating. What was once an hourly beat is now every 40 minutes. If this continues, the pull will become permanent, and the river will collapse into a one-way current.

**Divine Manifestation:**
PCs who approach the heart directly (within 10 feet) experience:
- **Whispers (clear):** The voices are overwhelming. *Return. Come together. Flow back. Why am I broken? Where are the rest of me?*
- **Visions (intense):** The PCs see the river as the god saw it: a single connected flow from source to sea, unbroken and sacred. Then the vision shatters, fragmenting into disconnected pieces. The god is trying to understand why it is broken.
- **Compulsion (DC 16 Willpower Save):** On a failure, the PC feels an overwhelming urge to touch the heart, to help it reconnect. Touching the heart does not heal the god, it burns the PC (2d6 divine damage) and brands them with a fragment of divine will (permanent effect: the PC always knows which direction the nearest body of water is, but hears faint whispers when near rivers).

**Complication:**
If the PCs attempt to damage or remove the heart, the pull intensifies. The current becomes a whirlpool, DC 18 Athletics to escape. The grinding sound becomes a scream, and the water thrashes. The river fights back.

### Scene 3: The Choice

**Setting:** The PCs surface, return to shore, and must decide what to do with their findings.

**Option 1: Report Publicly**
The PCs return to [[Millford]] or [[Saltmere]] and publicize their findings: the pull is real, caused by a divine heart, and worsening. Barge traffic halts immediately. [[Saltmere]] faces grain shortages within weeks. [[Millford]]'s economy collapses as lowland creditors panic and call in debts. The [[Saltmere Merchant Assembly]] blames the PCs for triggering the crisis. [[Garrick Thane]] and [[Forge Council]] demand compensation for lost trade. The PCs are heroes to some (they prevented more deaths) and villains to others (they destroyed livelihoods).

**Campaign Hook:** Factions demand the PCs fix what they revealed. "You told us the river is dying. Now tell us how to stop it."

**Option 2: Suppress Findings**
The PCs agree to keep quiet in exchange for payment (200 gold from the [[Saltmere Merchant Assembly]] or [[Garrick Thane]]). The widow gets closure (bodies returned), the cargo is written off as lost, and the pull remains a secret. Trade continues. Barges keep vanishing. The PCs are complicit in the cover-up. Months later, more families mourn, and the PCs must live with the knowledge that their silence killed people.

**Campaign Hook:** The PCs are approached by a survivor or family member of a later wreck. "You knew. You could have stopped this."

**Option 3: Investigate Deeper**
The PCs commit to solving the divine decay problem. They report the findings but frame it as a solvable threat, not an unsolvable crisis. "The pull is caused by a dying god. We need scholars, mages, priests, anyone who understands divine remains. We can fix this." This delays the economic collapse and buys time, but it commits the PCs to a campaign-length arc: understanding the Theomachis, navigating faction politics, and deciding whether to heal, destroy, or contain the god's remains.

**Campaign Hook:** The PCs become the region's experts on divine decay, pulled into conflicts over other god-corpses (the Stone Warden's coldness, other fragments across Wylderan).

## GM Notes

> [!warning]- Running the Adventure

**Pacing:** This one-shot is designed for 3-4 hours. If time is limited, compress Act II (skip environmental encounters, meet Lira earlier). If time is abundant, expand Act I with additional Millford NPCs or faction politics.

**Tone Balance:** The investigation should feel grounded (economic stakes, real people suffering) until Act III, when the supernatural horror escalates. Do not reveal the heart immediately. Let dread build.

**The Choice Matters:** Do not force the PCs into a specific ending. All three options (report, suppress, investigate) are valid and lead to different campaign directions. Respect player agency.

**Divine Horror:** The heart is not evil. It is broken. The god does not want to hurt people. It is trying to heal and does not understand that its reassembly will kill thousands. This is tragedy, not villainy.

**Factions React:** If the PCs publicize the truth, factions will blame them and demand solutions. If they suppress, factions will later discover they were complicit. If they commit to investigation, factions will try to control or sabotage their efforts. Every choice has consequences.
