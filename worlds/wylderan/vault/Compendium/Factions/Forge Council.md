## Overview

The Forge Council is the ruling body of [[Ashcrag]], a guild-dominated governance structure that controls the highland economy through market fees, barge loading priorities, and timber licensing. Founded approximately 22 years ago by guild masters and landowners as a defensive alliance against lowland creditor expansion, the council was restructured 20 years ago by [[Myra Cadros]] after a catastrophic winter crisis. What began as a decentralized consensus-based body became a centralized instrument of guild power under Myra's leadership. She made the two master smith seats permanent, restricted landowner eligibility to guild-affiliated farmers, and consolidated decision-making authority in the council head position, which she has held for 20 consecutive years.

The council governs [[Ashcrag]] and the surrounding valley watershed, controlling grain exports, forge operations, and timber production. It operates independently of Crown authority, sending only a ceremonial representative once per year to renew nominal fealty. In practice, the council answers to no one beyond its own five members. This independence is both its strength and its fragility. The council has maintained stability and resisted lowland economic control for two decades, but it is now under siege from three directions: the [[Highland Workers Coalition]] organizing tenant farmers and miners to demand representation, the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] expanding its economic grip from [[Ironhollow]], and lowland creditors tightening their hold on highland debts through the [[Saltmere]] Merchant Assembly.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Characterwriter assessment:** "A fortress built on sand."
>
> The council looks stable from the outside, five members, monthly meetings, majority votes, but it is fracturing from internal contradictions. [[Myra Cadros]] controls the vote but is losing her grip on the economy (charcoal shortage, grain reserves depleted, farmer organizing). [[Aldric Harth]] is compromised by lowland debt and secretly smuggling grain to avoid default. [[Hal Brennan]] quietly undermines Myra while voting with her publicly. The coalition knows Myra is monitoring but not when she will act. If the PCs intervene, the council's fate hinges on which internal faction they empower.

## Leadership

**[[Myra Cadros]]** (Council Head, Master Smith, Cadros Ironworks): Early 50s, soot-stained hands and sharp mind, 20 years in power. Restructured the council after the harsh winter crisis, centralized authority, made guild seats permanent. Believes guild-run governance is the only structure keeping [[Ashcrag]] from descending into chaos. Wants to expand forge trade, reclaim the [[Ironhollow]] market from cartel tools, and break lowland debt leverage. Pragmatic and ruthless, prepared to use economic pressure (barge denial, timber license revocation) to crush the [[Highland Workers Coalition]] if it does not collapse on its own.

**[[Hal Brennan]]** (Master Smith, Brennan's Forge): Early 60s, grizzled traditionalist who resents Myra's centralization but lacks the charisma to challenge her directly. Runs Brennan's Forge, specializing in rougher tools (nails, hinges, wagon fittings) after losing precision contracts to Myra's expansion. Votes with Myra publicly to maintain guild solidarity but undermines her privately (slow-walking contracts, spreading rumors, possibly funding [[Orrin Pike]]'s organizing anonymously). Wants to restore the guild to its earlier decentralized structure but will not act openly until he sees which way the wind blows.

**[[Aldric Harth]]** (Landowner Representative, Grain Lord): Mid-40s, well-fed and well-dressed, largest landowner in [[The Stone Heights]], controls a third of [[Ashcrag]]'s grain exports. Sits on the council as the farmer representative but is seen by most farmers as too close to the guild. Owes significant money to [[Corwin Marsh]] in [[Saltmere]], debt due in two months. Secretly smuggling grain outside council-approved channels to make partial payments. Torn between council loyalty and desperation to save his estate. Wants the council to crush the [[Highland Workers Coalition]] immediately before farmer strikes destroy his harvest, but Myra refuses to act.

**Jorin Kelm** (Landowner Representative, Myra Loyalist): Mid-50s, owns 200 acres east of town, contracts exclusively with Cadros Ironworks for tools. Conservative and risk-averse, sits on the council to protect his forge relationship. Votes with Myra on everything. His loyalty gives Myra the three-vote majority she needs (herself, Brennan, Jorin) to control all standard council votes.

**Marna Veld** (Landowner Representative, Swing Vote): Late 40s, owns 150 acres south of town near the timber yards. Sells grain through [[Grett Voss]]'s tally house but also maintains informal deals with river traders. Neutral and pragmatic, votes based on what benefits her farm. Her land neighbors the timber yards where [[Orrin Pike]] operates, and if the coalition boycotts the spring grain market, she loses buyers. She is exploring whether to negotiate with Pike or side with Myra, making her the potential swing vote if the council fractures.

## Structure & Governance

The Forge Council is a 5-member body: 2 master smiths (permanent seats) and 3 landowner representatives (elected, 3-year staggered terms). This structure was designed by Myra after the harsh winter crisis to ensure guild dominance. The two master smith seats are held by the owners of [[Ashcrag]]'s two largest forges, Cadros Ironworks and Brennan's Forge, and do not rotate. The three landowner seats are elected by landowners who own 50+ acres and are guild-affiliated (defined as those who contract with the forges or sell through council-approved traders). This eligibility restriction excludes independent farmers and all tenant farmers, [[Orrin Pike]]'s central grievance.

**Elections:** Landowner representatives serve 3-year terms with staggered elections (one seat up for election each year, rotating so that two election years have one seat and one election year has two seats). Representatives can be re-elected unlimited times. [[Aldric Harth]] has served for 9 years across three consecutive terms.

**Voting:** Standard decisions require a majority vote (3 of 5 members). Structural changes (altering council composition, expelling a member, levying new taxes or fees) require unanimous votes. Myra, as council head, holds a tiebreaker in the event of a 2-2 vote, though this is a legacy rule from the original decentralized structure and has never been used under the current 5-member system.

**Council head:** Originally rotated among members annually. Myra has held the position for 20 years, re-confirmed by vote each year. In practice, she controls the vote because she commands a 3-vote majority (herself, Brennan through guild solidarity, and Jorin through loyalty).

**Meetings:** The council meets monthly on the first tenday of each month at the Stone Hearth tavern's second-floor meeting hall. Quorum requires 4 of 5 members present. Meetings are not public, but outcomes (ordinances, fee changes, barge schedules) are posted at the market ground afterward.

## Territory & Jurisdiction

The Forge Council governs [[Ashcrag]] (population ~350) and claims authority over "all settlements and holdings within the Ashcrag valley watershed," defined as the drainage basins of the three streams that form the Millrun River: Cold Run, Ashwater, and Piney Flow. This includes outlying farms within 5-10 miles of town, timber operations south of the settlement, and charcoal kilns feeding the forge economy.

**Boundaries:**
- **Northern:** The ridgeline separating the Ashcrag valley from [[Ironhollow]]. The council has no claim to Ironhollow, which lies beyond the ridge and has been a [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] company town for ~10 years.
- **Southern:** The Confluence, where the three highland streams converge to form the Millrun River. This is the last council-controlled point before the river flows downstream to [[Millford]].
- **Eastern/Western:** Undefined, roughly where the forested hills become too remote for regular trade with [[Ashcrag]].

**Ironhollow:** The council does not claim jurisdiction over [[Ironhollow]], which is 8 miles north on the far side of the ridgeline and was never part of the council's founding territory. However, [[Myra Cadros]] frames cartel control of Ironhollow as "highland iron serving lowland profits" and uses this rhetoric to rally support for reclaiming the Ironhollow tool market. This is propaganda, not policy. The council has no legal claim and no mechanism to challenge cartel control directly.

**Enforcement:** The council has no standing militia, no jail, and limited enforcement capacity. It relies on economic leverage (market fee collection, barge loading denial, timber license revocation) and social pressure (expulsion from the market, blacklisting from guild contracts) to enforce compliance. Farmers who bypass the council-controlled market by selling directly to smugglers or lowland traders are violating council ordinances, but the council can only punish them if they are caught and still need council services (barge access, tool purchases).

## Resources & Assets

**Market fees (10% of all transactions):** The council's primary revenue source, collected by [[Grett Voss]] at the tally house. Estimated at 300-400 gold per harvest season from grain sales, plus 100-150 gold per season from timber, tools, and miscellaneous transactions. Revenue funds council operations, road maintenance to [[Millford]], emergency grain reserves, and Grett Voss's salary as market factor.

**Barge loading priority:** The council sets the schedule for which farmers load grain onto barges bound for [[Millford]] and the lowlands. Farmers who pay fees on time receive priority. Late payers or non-compliant farmers are pushed to the back of the queue, meaning their grain sits longer and risks spoilage. This is the council's most effective weapon: it can financially cripple a farmer by delaying barge access during peak trade season (spring and autumn).

**Grain price influence:** The council does not set prices directly, market forces apply, but it manipulates supply by controlling barge loading schedules. This allows the council to create artificial scarcity or gluts, stabilizing prices during shortages (preventing panic) or punishing farmers who challenge council authority (flooding the market to drive prices down).

**Timber licensing:** The council does not own the forests, but it licenses timber operations. Families like [[Orrin Pike]]'s charcoal burners operate under council-approved contracts. The council can revoke licenses for "unsafe practices" or "overextraction," vague definitions enforced politically. Revoking a timber license destroys a family's livelihood, making this another powerful lever of control.

**Emergency grain reserve:** Established by Myra 20 years ago after the harsh winter crisis. Stored in a council-controlled warehouse near the market ground, the reserve holds approximately 50 tons of grain, enough to feed [[Ashcrag]] for 2-3 months in a crisis. Myra has been quietly drawing from this reserve to stabilize prices during the current grain shortage caused by [[Corwin Marsh]]'s debt pressure and reduced highland exports. Farmers do not know the reserve is being depleted. If the shortage continues, the reserve will be empty before winter.

**What the council does NOT control:** Forge output (Myra and Brennan own their forges privately), land ownership (landowners like Aldric hold private estates), or charcoal supply (timber families control production, and the council can only pressure them via licensing).

## Goals & Motivations

**Maintain highland independence from lowland influence.** The council was founded to resist [[Saltmere]]'s Merchant Assembly expansion and lowland creditors buying up highland debts. Myra frames every council decision through this lens: does it strengthen highland self-sufficiency or hand leverage to the lowlands? She sees [[Corwin Marsh]]'s debt pressure on [[Aldric Harth]], the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]]'s control of [[Ironhollow]], and even the Crown's nominal authority as threats to highland autonomy.

**Preserve guild control over the economy.** Myra believes guild-run governance ensures stability and prosperity. The guild knows what is best for the town, in her view, because guild masters understand trade, production, and long-term planning. Tenant farmers and laborers are "represented" through landowner representatives like Aldric, so there is no need for direct seats on the council. Giving [[Orrin Pike]] a council seat would destabilize the structure that has kept [[Ashcrag]] functional for 20 years.

**Expand forge trade and reclaim the Ironhollow market.** Myra wants to secure long-term contracts with lowland merchants in [[Millford]] and [[Saltmere]] for highland tool exports, positioning [[Ashcrag]] as the essential hub of the highland economy. She also wants to reclaim the [[Ironhollow]] ore market, which the cartel captured ~10 years ago by importing cheaper lowland tools. Myra sees this as highland resources feeding lowland profits and wants to undercut cartel prices through Cadros Ironworks.

**Maintain the emergency grain reserve.** Myra is haunted by the harsh winter 20 years ago when families starved because the council could not coordinate a response. The emergency grain reserve is her insurance against another crisis. The fact that she is secretly depleting it to stabilize current prices terrifies her, but she sees no alternative. If the shortage continues and farmers discover the reserve is empty, her credibility will shatter.

## Opposition

**[[Highland Workers Coalition]]:** Nascent labor organizing network between tenant farmers in [[Ashcrag]] (led by [[Orrin Pike]]) and contract miners in [[Ironhollow]] (led by [[Senna Kade]]). Farmers strike against the Forge Council, demanding representation, land reform, and reduced market fees. Myra knows about the organizing and is monitoring it, waiting for the coalition to collapse from internal tensions. She is prepared to use economic pressure (barge loading denial, timber license revocation, blacklisting from forge contracts) if the coalition escalates to a market boycott or sabotage. The council's weakness: it does not know about the Ironhollow-Ashcrag coordination between Senna and Orrin, so it underestimates the coalition's reach.

**[[Highland Merchant Cartel]]:** Economic rival controlling [[Ironhollow]] (~10 years) and financing lowland trade through factors like [[Tallow Breck]]. The cartel displaced [[Ashcrag]]'s tool exports to [[Ironhollow]] by importing cheaper lowland tools, and it holds leverage over highland traders through debt. Myra sees the cartel as highland resources serving lowland profits and wants to reclaim the [[Ironhollow]] market through economic competition (undercutting cartel prices, offering better contracts). The council and the cartel are not openly hostile, they compete for influence, but if the council allies with the [[Highland Workers Coalition]] against the cartel, or if the cartel offers to finance the council in exchange for control, the rivalry could explode into open conflict.

**[[Saltmere]] Merchant Assembly:** Lowland creditors led by [[Corwin Marsh]], who holds [[Aldric Harth]]'s debt (due in 2 months). If Aldric defaults, Marsh seizes the Harth Estate and controls a third of highland grain exports, giving the Assembly leverage over the entire river trade network. Myra sees this as the existential threat: if the Assembly controls highland grain, the council loses economic independence and becomes a puppet of lowland interests. She wants to prevent Aldric's default but does not know he is smuggling grain to make partial payments, undermining council authority.

**Crown Authority (potential):** The council governs independently of the Crown, sending only a ceremonial representative once per year. The Crown has not taxed, enforced, or governed the highlands in living memory because the region is too remote and poor to prioritize. However, if the [[Saltmere]] Merchant Assembly seizes highland grain and petitions the Crown to legitimize the claim, the Crown might reassert authority to protect lowland merchant interests. Myra fears this: she frames the council as "highland self-governance resisting Crown interference," but she knows that independence is contingent on the Crown continuing to ignore the highlands.

## Internal Tensions

**Myra vs. Aldric:** Guild interest vs. landowner desperation. Myra wants to maintain council stability and resist lowland debt leverage. Aldric wants to maximize grain exports to pay off [[Corwin Marsh]] before his estate is seized in two months. Aldric is secretly smuggling grain outside council-approved channels, bypassing [[Grett Voss]]'s tally house and the 10% market fee. If Myra discovers this, she will expel Aldric from the council and possibly seize his council seat. If Marsh discovers it, he will call the debt immediately. Aldric is trapped between two forces, and the council is one betrayal away from losing its landowner majority.

**Guild vs. Landowners (structural):** The council has 2 guild master seats (permanent) and 3 landowner seats (elected), creating an inherent structural tension. Myra controls the vote now (herself, Brennan, Jorin = 3), but if landowners unite behind a common interest (resisting guild fees, demanding price concessions, supporting farmer representation), they could outvote the guild. Myra's centralization after the harsh winter was designed to prevent this by restricting landowner eligibility to guild-affiliated farmers, but the tension remains latent. If [[Orrin Pike]]'s coalition fractures the landowner bloc, or if Marna Veld flips to support farmer demands, the guild loses its majority.

**Myra vs. Brennan:** Quiet rivalry masked by guild solidarity. Brennan votes with Myra publicly on every issue to maintain the appearance of guild unity, but he undermines her privately. He slow-walks forge contracts that would benefit Cadros Ironworks, spreads rumors that Myra is hoarding charcoal for her own forge, and possibly funds [[Orrin Pike]]'s organizing anonymously to destabilize Myra's control. Brennan wants to restore the guild to its earlier decentralized structure, where master smiths shared power equally, not serve under Myra's permanent council head position. If the [[Highland Workers Coalition]] forces a crisis, Brennan might break with Myra publicly, but only if he believes he can win.

**Coalition response divide:** Myra wants to wait for the coalition to collapse, preparing economic pressure but avoiding arrests that would make [[Orrin Pike]] a martyr. Aldric wants an immediate crackdown (arrests, blacklisting, forge contract denials) because any delay risks farmer strikes that would destroy his harvest and force his default. Brennan is waiting to see which way the wind blows, voting with Myra for now but prepared to flip if the coalition gains strength. The council has not formally voted on a response because Myra is keeping it off the agenda until she decides her strategy. This indecision is itself a vulnerability: the coalition knows the council is watching but not when it will act.

## Counter-Insurgency Stance

Myra knows about the [[Highland Workers Coalition]]'s organizing. [[Grett Voss]] monitors market activity and reports farmer petitions, meeting attendance, and coalition sympathizers. The council also benefits from an informant network: Thom Harrow, a tenant farmer in [[Ashcrag]], reports to [[Tallow Breck]] (cartel factor), who shares intelligence with the council when it serves cartel interests. However, the council does not know about the cross-settlement coordination between [[Orrin Pike]] (farmers) and [[Senna Kade]] (miners in [[Ironhollow]]). Myra believes the coalition is a local farmer complaint, not a synchronized highland-wide labor movement.

**Current strategy:** Monitoring phase. Myra is waiting for the coalition to collapse from internal tensions (goal misalignment between debt relief and land reform, class friction between miners and farmers, geographic distance between [[Ironhollow]] and [[Ashcrag]]). She is prepared to escalate to economic pressure if the coalition does not dissolve: deny barge loading access to coalition members, revoke timber licenses for [[Orrin Pike]]'s family, blacklist coalition sympathizers from forge contracts. She has not decided whether to arrest Orrin yet because it would make him a martyr and inflame farmer resentment.

**Trigger points:** If the coalition declares a strike (boycotting the spring grain market), sabotages council assets (burning grain reserves, destroying barge platforms), or commits violence against council members or guild masters, Myra will move to immediate crackdown. This would include arrests, expulsion from the market, and possibly petitioning [[Tallow Breck]]'s cartel for enforcement support (mercenaries, debt foreclosures on coalition members). Aldric is pushing for this response now, but Myra refuses because she does not want to provoke a violent confrontation unless absolutely necessary.

**Weakness:** The council's counter-insurgency relies on economic leverage, which only works if coalition members still need council services (barge access, market sales, tool purchases). If the coalition finds alternative trade routes (smuggling grain, bypassing the market, importing tools from the cartel), the council's leverage evaporates. Myra does not yet realize this vulnerability.

## Crown Relationship

The Forge Council governs [[Ashcrag]] independently of Crown authority. Once per year, the council sends a representative (usually Jorin Kelm, the least important member) to the regional Crown seat to renew fealty in a ceremonial ritual. The representative swears loyalty, presents a token gift (usually tools from the forges), and returns. The Crown does not send tax collectors, officials, soldiers, or judges to [[Ashcrag]]. In living memory, the Crown has not governed, taxed, or enforced laws in the highlands. This de facto independence emerged after the Theomachis, when the Crown's reach collapsed and highland regions like [[Ashcrag]] were too remote and poor to be worth administering. The Crown focused on rebuilding core territories (lowlands, coastal cities), and highland settlements were left to govern themselves. Over time, neglect became independence.

**Why the Crown tolerates this:** [[Ashcrag]] is small (~350 people), remote, and economically marginal from the Crown's perspective. The highlands produce grain and tools, but the [[Saltmere]] Merchant Assembly controls the trade network, so the Crown taxes lowland merchants instead of highland producers. The Crown benefits from highland-lowland trade without having to administer the highlands directly. If [[Ashcrag]] becomes a problem (rebellion, banditry, trade disruption), the Crown could intervene, but it has not been worth the effort.

**Fragility:** If [[Corwin Marsh]] seizes [[Aldric Harth]]'s estate and petitions the Crown to legitimize the claim (arguing that highland debt defaults threaten lowland merchant stability), the Crown might reassert authority to protect Assembly interests. Myra fears this scenario: lowland creditors using Crown legitimacy to annex highland resources. She frames the council's independence as "highland self-governance resisting Crown interference," noble and traditional, but she knows that independence is fragile and contingent on the Crown continuing to ignore the highlands.

## Campaign Role

The Forge Council is the primary political power structure in [[The Stone Heights]], controlling [[Ashcrag]]'s economy and serving as the face of entrenched guild authority. PCs investigating highland trade, debt crises, or class tensions will encounter the council as an obstacle, an ally, or a quest hub depending on their approach.

**Obstacle to labor reform:** The council refuses [[Highland Workers Coalition]] demands for farmer representation, land reform, and reduced market fees. If PCs side with [[Orrin Pike]] and the coalition, the council will see them as threats and use economic pressure (barge denial, market access revocation, blacklisting) to oppose them. If PCs escalate to sabotage or violence, the council will move to arrests and crackdown.

**Potential ally against the cartel:** The council and the [[Highland Workers Coalition]] share a common enemy: the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]]. If PCs approach [[Myra Cadros]] with a strategy to break cartel control over [[Ironhollow]] or reclaim the highland tool market, she may negotiate. However, she will not compromise guild dominance or give the coalition representation on the council. Any alliance is conditional and fragile.

**Political quest hub:** The council is the center of [[Ashcrag]]'s political intrigue. PCs can influence council votes, exploit internal faction rivalries ([[Myra Cadros]] vs. [[Hal Brennan]], Myra vs. [[Aldric Harth]]), mediate between the council and the coalition, or manipulate council decisions for their own ends. Council meetings, held monthly at the Stone Hearth, are opportunities for negotiation, eavesdropping, bribery, or sabotage.

**Power shift scenario:** If [[Aldric Harth]] defaults to [[Corwin Marsh]] in two months, the [[Saltmere]] Merchant Assembly seizes the Harth Estate and controls a third of highland grain exports. This shifts the balance of power from the council to the Assembly and threatens highland independence. If PCs prevent the default (negotiating with Marsh, finding alternative income for Aldric, or exposing Marsh's leverage), they preserve council stability. If they allow or accelerate the default, they weaken the council and empower lowland creditors, reshaping the campaign's political landscape.
