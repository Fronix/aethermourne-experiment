# Highland Merchant Cartel

---

## Overview

The Highland Merchant Cartel controls the flow of capital through the northern river trade network. They do not own barges, mills, or cargo, they own the money that makes all of those things move. Independent traders like [[Dace Torrn]] borrow from the Cartel to finance cargo loads, repair barges, or cover losses. Highland farmers borrow to expand their land or survive bad harvests. Lowland merchants borrow to secure priority on grain shipments. The Cartel does not produce wealth, it extracts it, one interest payment at a time.

The Cartel is patient. They will extend deadlines, restructure loans, and accept partial payments, because a debtor who stays in business can be bled for years. But they are not forgiving. When a debtor cannot pay, the Cartel takes everything: the barge, the cargo, the mill, the land. They have been doing this for three generations, and their reach now extends from the highland farms to the lowland docks.

---

## Structure & Leadership

The Cartel is governed by a council of five **Factors**, each controlling a different economic sector: grain trade, barge transport, forge and tool production, land loans, and foreign goods. The Factors meet quarterly to set interest rates, negotiate territory, and decide which debtors to foreclose on.

The current **Master Factor** is [TBD - Writer1's highland NPC], who chairs the council and casts the tiebreaking vote. The Master Factor is not the wealthiest or the oldest, they are the one the others trust to keep the Cartel stable. Stability is profit.

Beneath the Factors are dozens of agents, clerks, and enforcers who handle day-to-day operations: tracking debts, collecting payments, repossessing collateral. Most traders never meet a Factor. They deal with agents who speak softly, carry ledgers, and remember every missed payment.

---

## Economic Power Base

The Cartel's power rests on three pillars:

1. **Capital concentration.** The Factors control more liquid wealth than any individual merchant, miller, or landowner. When someone needs money quickly, only the Cartel can provide it.

2. **Information.** The Cartel knows who owes whom, who's late on payments, who's overleveraged. This knowledge lets them predict failures and position themselves to profit from collapse.

3. **Patience.** Most merchants think in seasons. The Cartel thinks in decades. They can wait out a bad year, a failed harvest, a lost barge, because they have reserves and multiple revenue streams. Their debtors do not.

The Cartel does not compete in markets. They finance the competitors and take a cut regardless of who wins.

## Relationships & Tensions

**[[Saltmere]] Merchant Assembly:** Complicated. The Cartel finances some Assembly members and competes with others. [[Corwin Marsh]] has borrowed from the Cartel in the past but has since paid off his debts and become a rival creditor in his own right. The Assembly sees the Cartel as a necessary evil, the Cartel sees the Assembly as a potential market to capture.

**Forge Guilds:** Cooperative rivalry. The forge guilds produce tools and metal goods; the Cartel finances their expansion and raw material purchases. The relationship is stable as long as the guilds stay profitable. If the guilds consolidate power and try to bypass Cartel financing, the Factors will move to break them.

**Independent Traders:** The Cartel's bread and butter. Traders like [[Dace Torrn]] and [[Lira Fenn]] depend on Cartel loans to operate, which gives the Cartel leverage over who gets priority cargo, favorable routes, and protection from competition. The Cartel does not want traders to fail, they want traders to stay perpetually in debt.

**Highland Landowners:** The Cartel's long-term project. By extending land loans to farmers and minor lords, the Cartel is slowly acquiring foreclosed estates. In another generation, the Factors may control more highland land than the nobility. This terrifies the traditional landholding class, but they are too fragmented to resist effectively.

## Current Tensions

- **Dace Torrn's debt:** [[Dace Torrn]] owes the Cartel for cargo lost two years ago. He's been making payments, but he's overdue. The Cartel is deciding whether to foreclose on the *Kingfisher* or extend his deadline in exchange for smuggling work.
- **Corwin Marsh's expansion:** [[Corwin Marsh]] in [[Saltmere]] is building his own lending network, directly competing with Cartel influence in the lowlands. The Factors are watching closely. If Marsh becomes a true rival, they will move to crush him.
- **Forge guild consolidation:** Rumors of forge masters forming a unified guild to negotiate better loan terms. If this happens, the Cartel will lose leverage over tool production. The Factors are considering strategic foreclosures to break the coalition before it forms.
