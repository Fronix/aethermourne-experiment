## Overview

The Highland Workers Coalition is not yet a faction. It is a network of contract miners from [[Ironhollow]] and tenant farmers from [[Ashcrag]] organizing in secret to challenge the economic stranglehold of the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] and the Forge Council. It has no charter, no public identity, no formal structure. It exists in whispered conversations in boarding houses, late-night meetings at the timber yards, and messages carried along the Cold Run between settlements.

Two years ago, [[Senna Kade]], a contract miner organizing resistance in [[Ironhollow]], met [[Orrin Pike]], a tenant farmer doing the same in [[Ashcrag]]. They recognized a shared enemy and began coordinating. What started as information-sharing has grown into something more dangerous: a plan for a dual-target coordinated strike that could reshape the highland economy or collapse into bloodshed.

The coalition is fragile. Its dual leadership is separated by eight miles of rough terrain. Its members are divided by geography, class, and conflicting goals. It is infiltrated by informants. It has no external support, no resources beyond charcoal and desperation. And yet, it represents the first labor organizing in Wylderan's history. If it succeeds, it changes everything. If it fails, the cartel crushes it, and organizing dies for a generation.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Characterwriter assessment:** "Powder keg with damp fuse."
>
> The coalition could become a real faction with PC help, external recognition (other settlements joining), or a shared manifesto that unifies their goals. It could also collapse from mass arrests, informant betrayal, or cartel concessions that split the miners from the farmers. The PCs will likely determine which trajectory it takes.

## Leadership

**[[Senna Kade]]** (Ironhollow, contract miners): Early 30s, wiry and scarred, commands respect in the pits. Organizes the miners, coordinates strike readiness, maintains cell structure. Wants debt relief, safety standards, and an end to the rigged scales. Knows the coalition is fragile and moves cautiously.

**[[Orrin Pike]]** (Ashcrag, tenant farmers): Early 30s, lean and work-worn, runs a charcoal kiln south of town. Organizes the farmers, coordinates fuel leverage, demands council representation. Wants land reform and breaking up estates like [[Aldric Harth]]'s. More willing to take risks than Senna, which creates tension.

The dual leadership is both the coalition's strength and its weakness. Senna and Orrin trust each other, but they lead different groups with different goals. If one is arrested or killed, the other cannot hold both factions together.

> [!info] Design Note
> Senna Kade and Orrin Pike do not have formal Highland Workers Coalition faction affiliations in the NPCRegistry. This is intentional: they began organizing independently before the coalition existed, and their primary identity remains as independent labor organizers. The lieutenants (Torvin, Dessa, Halden, Mira) joined the coalition as an organization, so they carry the faction affiliation.

## Structure

The coalition aspires to a cell structure but has not fully achieved it. Most members know only their local organizers. Communication between settlements flows through two message runners: [[Kael Torrn]] (river worker, travels between settlements) and [[Lira Venn]] (charcoal hauler, independent contractor). Both are vulnerable to interrogation or pressure.

**Ironhollow lieutenants:**
- [[Torvin Marks]] (veteran miner, fatalistic but loyal)
- [[Dessa Kord]] (boarding house cook, strategic, Senna's brain trust)

**Ashcrag lieutenants:**
- [[Halden Cray]] (tenant farmer, inspiring but reckless)
- [[Mira Voss]] (grain sorter, covert information source)

**Committed members:** 12 miners in [[Ironhollow]], 15 farmers in [[Ashcrag]]. Another 18 miners and 25 farmers are wavering, sympathetic but not yet willing to strike.

**Informants:** The coalition knows it is infiltrated. Rennic Vale in [[Ironhollow]] reports to [[Caden Wroth]]. Thom Harrow in [[Ashcrag]] reports to [[Tallow Breck]]. [[Dessa Kord]] suspects Rennic but cannot prove it.

## Goals

The coalition's members do not fully agree on what they want, which is one of its deepest vulnerabilities.

**Miners (Ironhollow) want:**
- Debt relief (clear accumulated debts, fair wages instead of credit)
- Safety standards (rescue protocols, equipment maintenance)
- Fair scales (end [[Halven Mir]]'s rigged weighing)

**Farmers (Ashcrag) want:**
- Land reform (break up large estates, redistribute to tenant farmers)
- Forge Council representation (voice for farmers, not just guild and landowners)
- Fair market fees (reduce [[Grett Voss]]'s ten percent cut)

Both groups want dignity and survival. But if the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] offers concessions to the miners, or the Forge Council offers concessions to the farmers, the coalition splits.

## Resources & Strategy

**Charcoal leverage:** [[Orrin Pike]]'s family controls charcoal supply to [[Ironhollow]]. Without charcoal, [[Caden Wroth]] cannot test ore. Without tested ore, [[Myra Cadros]]'s forges receive bad batches. Orrin is already withholding shipments quietly, sabotaging the cartel's supply chain.

**Explosives:** Contract miners in [[Ironhollow]] have access to explosives purchased on credit from [[Marna Tess]]'s company store. This is not a resource the coalition wants to use, but it exists.

**Seasonal workers:** Farmers from [[Ashcrag]] work [[Ironhollow]]'s pits during planting gaps. They move between settlements regularly, making them natural couriers. The cartel expects this movement, so it does not raise suspicion.

**Winter isolation:** When the roads freeze, cartel oversight weakens. [[Tallow Breck]] cannot reach [[Ironhollow]] from downstream. The coalition plans to front-load organizing in autumn, use winter to entrench, and strike in early spring when the roads open and trade resumes.

**Meeting site:** [[Halfway Burn Site]], an abandoned charcoal burn site halfway along Cold Run. Neutral ground, far from both settlements, used for cross-settlement planning meetings.

## Opposition

**[[Highland Merchant Cartel]]:** Represented by [[Caden Wroth]] (Mining Factor), [[Tallow Breck]] (financier), [[Halven Mir]] (weighmaster), and [[Marna Tess]] (company store). The cartel controls ore extraction, debt terms, and supply prices. They have informants, foreclosure leverage, and a willingness to use violence. They are patient. They do not need to crush the coalition immediately. They can wait for it to collapse on its own.

**Forge Council:** Led by [[Myra Cadros]] (master smith, guild head) and including [[Aldric Harth]] (largest landowner). The council controls grain prices, market fees, and barge loading priority. They see farmer demands for representation as a threat to guild-run stability. [[Myra Cadros]] knows about the organizing but has not yet decided how to respond.

**Informants:** Rennic Vale and Thom Harrow feed information to the cartel. The coalition does not know how much the cartel knows.

## Symbolism

Coalition members mark themselves with a small charcoal smudge on the left wrist, easily washed off if needed. It is a quiet signal, visible only to those who know to look for it. The charcoal represents both fuel and labor: the thing that makes the forges burn and the thing the workers control.

## Internal Tensions

**Goal misalignment:** Miners want debt relief. Farmers want land reform. These are not the same fight. If one group gets what it wants, the other may feel abandoned.

**Class friction:** Some tenant farmers see miners as worse off and therefore unreliable allies. Some miners see farmers as having more freedom and therefore not truly oppressed. Both are wrong, but the resentment is real.

**Geographic distance:** Eight miles is manageable, but it is still a barrier. Senna and Orrin cannot meet frequently. Coordination relies on message runners who could be caught, bribed, or killed.

**Leadership fragility:** If Senna is arrested, [[Dessa Kord]] takes over (strategic but not charismatic). If Orrin is arrested, [[Halden Cray]] takes over (inspiring but reckless). Neither succession is stable.

**Trust issues:** The coalition knows it is infiltrated. Members are afraid to recruit openly. Fear isolates them further.

---

## Trajectories

**The coalition could become real if:**
- PCs provide external support (protection, resources, legitimacy)
- Other settlements join (expanding beyond Ironhollow and Ashcrag)
- A shared manifesto unifies miner and farmer goals
- The strike succeeds and forces concessions from both power structures

**The coalition could collapse if:**
- Mass arrests (leadership decapitated)
- Informant betrayal exposes key members
- Cartel offers targeted concessions that split the coalition
- Internal conflict between miners and farmers becomes unresolvable
- Strike fails and the cartel retaliates with blacklisting, foreclosures, or violence
