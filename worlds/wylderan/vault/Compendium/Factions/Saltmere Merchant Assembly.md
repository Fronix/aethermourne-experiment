## Overview

The Saltmere Merchant Assembly is the ruling body of [[Saltmere]], a guild-dominated governance structure that controls the lowland economy through grain price-setting, salt production quotas, and barge licensing. Founded approximately 40 years ago by the four original merchant families (Marsh, Greave, Vorren, Salter) as a coordinated response to coastal competition from larger ports, the Assembly was expanded 10 years ago to include the Brack fishing fleet, consolidating control over all major economic sectors in the delta. What began as a defensive alliance of salt traders has evolved into an entrenched oligarchy that holds the power of life and death over every resident of Saltmere through its control of grain imports.

The Assembly governs [[Saltmere]] and the surrounding salt marshlands, controlling grain distribution, salt production quotas, barge contracts, and maritime trade licensing. It operates nominally under Crown authority but in practice answers only to its five member houses. The Assembly sends tax revenue to the Crown quarterly and maintains a ceremonial Crown representative (a minor official with no real power), but day-to-day governance belongs entirely to the merchants. This economic independence is built on a foundation of sand: Saltmere cannot feed itself, and the Assembly's power depends entirely on maintaining highland grain imports.

The Assembly's strength is coordination. Five families who could compete and destroy each other instead meet weekly, share information, and enforce collective decisions. This prevents price wars, stabilizes trade, and presents a unified front to highland suppliers and coastal competitors. But the same coordination creates fragility. Every decision requires consensus or Speaker tiebreaker, and the five houses have competing interests that grow sharper every year. [[Corwin Marsh]] is building a debt empire that threatens to subsume the other houses. The Greave family is expanding into highland acquisitions, creating internal rivalry. [[Halden Brack]]'s fishing fleet is the poorest house and increasingly irrelevant as grain trade dominates the economy. The Assembly holds together through shared interest, but one major crisis, a grain shortage, a debt cascade, or a missing barge that will not stay buried, could fracture it permanently.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Lorekeeper assessment:** "An oligarchy built on hunger."
>
> The Assembly looks stable from the outside, five houses, weekly meetings, Speaker mediation, but it is one bad harvest away from collapse. Saltmere has no farms. The marshland cannot grow food. If highland grain stops flowing, the city starves in months. [[Corwin Marsh]] knows this and is leveraging highland debt to gain direct control of the mills. The other houses see what he is doing but cannot stop him without destabilizing the entire trade network. The missing barge is the first crack in the foundation. If the Assembly cannot explain it, traders will lose confidence, shipments will slow, and the grain dependency will become a death spiral.

## Leadership

**[[Corwin Marsh]]** (Marsh House, Grain & Debt Leverage): Mid-50s, soft-spoken and ruthless, controls most highland grain importation and holds significant debt on traders upriver, including [[Garrick Thane]] in [[Millford]] and [[Aldric Harth]] in [[Ashcrag]]. Sits on the Assembly as the Marsh family representative but quietly controls most votes through favors and obligations. Wants to consolidate control of the entire highland grain supply by calling debts and seizing upstream mills. Patient, strategic, and willing to wait years for the right moment. The most powerful merchant in Saltmere and the most dangerous.

**Ossian Greave** (Greave House, Trade Goods & Highland Expansion): Late 30s, aggressive and public, handles barge contracts and trade goods. Currently in [[Millford]] collecting debt from [[Kellan Thane]]. Represents the Greave family's push to expand into highland acquisitions, seizing assets from defaulting traders. Less subtle than [[Corwin Marsh]], more willing to use public threats and intimidation. The Greave strategy is faster and riskier than Marsh's slow consolidation. If Ossian succeeds in seizing Southgrain Mill, it gives Greave a highland foothold and threatens Marsh's grain monopoly. If he fails, Greave loses face and Marsh solidifies dominance.

**[[Marda Vorren]]** (Assembly Speaker, Vorren House, Coastal Trade): Late 50s, sharp-eyed and pragmatic, built the Vorren house from nothing through coastal cloth and seafood trade. Elected Speaker 10 years ago and re-elected every term since. Neutral in merchant disputes, which makes her valuable as a tiebreaker. Wants to expand Saltmere's influence further down the coast by building a charter port, but grain supply problems are holding her back. Respects [[Corwin Marsh]] but does not defer to him. The political anchor of the Assembly, holding the fragile coalition together through fair mediation and ruthless neutrality.

**Kellen Salter** (Salter House, Salt Production): Early 60s, weathered and conservative, owns the largest cluster of salt evaporation ponds west of Saltmere. Represents the original merchant families who built the town on salt trade before grain dependency reshaped the economy. Resents the Assembly's shift toward grain importation, which has made the Salter house less relevant every year. Votes conservatively, opposes expansion, and wants to return to a salt-focused economy. Rarely speaks at Assembly meetings but holds enough economic weight (salt exports fund a third of the town's revenue) that his vote cannot be ignored.

**[[Halden Brack]]** (Brack House, Fishing Fleet): Early 40s, weathered and blunt, owns Saltmere's fishing fleet and represents the poorest of the five merchant houses. Added to the Assembly 10 years ago to consolidate control over maritime labor. Respected for his honesty and work ethic, dismissed for his lack of wealth and political sophistication. Pushing the Assembly to investigate the missing barge publicly while the other houses want to bury it. Increasingly isolated but too stubborn to stop. The moral voice of the Assembly, ignored by the others until he forces them to listen.

## Structure & Governance

The Saltmere Merchant Assembly is a 5-house body: each of the five major merchant families (Marsh, Greave, Vorren, Salter, Brack) holds one permanent seat. Membership is hereditary, passed down through family leadership. When a house head dies or retires, the family selects the next representative internally. There are no elections, no term limits, and no mechanism for removing a house from the Assembly except voluntary withdrawal or economic collapse that destroys the family's trade base.

**The Speaker:** Elected by majority vote of the five houses, serves indefinitely until voted out or voluntarily steps down. [[Marda Vorren]] has held the position for 10 years. The Speaker chairs weekly meetings, casts tiebreaker votes (3-2 deadlocks), and represents the Assembly in negotiations with Crown officials, highland traders, and coastal merchants. The Speaker has no unilateral authority, all decisions require Assembly vote, but the position grants enormous political leverage through agenda-setting, mediation, and external representation.

**Voting:** Standard decisions (grain price adjustments, barge fee changes, salt quotas) require a simple majority (3 of 5 houses). Structural changes (expanding Assembly membership, levying new taxes, negotiating Crown charters) require unanimous votes. In practice, [[Corwin Marsh]] controls most votes through debt and favors, but he operates through persuasion and coalition-building rather than open dominance. [[Marda Vorren]]'s neutrality as Speaker makes her the swing vote when Marsh cannot secure a majority.

**Meetings:** The Assembly meets weekly on tenday mornings at the Merchant Assembly Hall, a timber structure built on pilings over Centerflow channel. Meetings are open to the public, but only Assembly members may speak or vote. Outcomes (price changes, contract awards, barge schedules) are posted publicly after each meeting. Quorum requires 4 of 5 houses present.

**Enforcement:** The Assembly has no standing militia, no jail, and limited direct enforcement capacity. It relies on economic leverage (denying barge contracts, revoking market access, blacklisting from salt quotas) and social pressure (public censure, expulsion from merchant networks) to enforce compliance. Traders who bypass the Assembly by smuggling grain or evading fees are punished through market exclusion, which in a city dependent on coordinated trade means economic death.

## Territory & Jurisdiction

The Saltmere Merchant Assembly governs [[Saltmere]] (population ~3,200) and claims authority over "all trade conducted through the Millrun River delta and associated salt production lands," defined as the delta channels, the bay approaches, and the salt evaporation ponds extending west and south of the city. This includes maritime trade licensing, barge contract enforcement, and salt production quotas.

**Boundaries:**
- **Northern:** The delta begins where the Millrun River splits into three channels, approximately 3 miles south of [[Millford]]. The Assembly claims no authority over Millford itself but controls all barge traffic passing through the delta.
- **Southern:** The bay approaches where the delta meets open ocean. The Assembly licenses coastal fishing and river-to-ocean trade but has no jurisdiction over seagoing vessels once they leave the bay.
- **Eastern/Western:** Undefined, roughly where the salt marshes transition to dryland farming (east) or open coastline (west).

**Millford relationship:** The Assembly does not govern [[Millford]], which operates under its own village council dominated by millers like [[Garrick Thane]]. However, the Assembly controls barge licensing for vessels entering the delta, giving it effective veto power over Millford's downstream trade. This creates an unstable power dynamic: Millford controls grain milling, the Assembly controls grain distribution. Both sides need each other, and both resent the dependency.

**Enforcement:** The Assembly enforces barge contracts through harbormaster inspections (managed by [[Venna Cade]]), market access denial, and trade blacklisting. It has no legal authority to arrest traders or seize vessels, but it can revoke barge licenses, effectively ending a captain's career.

## Resources & Assets

**Barge licensing fees:** The Assembly's primary revenue source. Every barge entering or leaving the delta pays a licensing fee based on cargo tonnage. Estimated at 600-800 gold per season from grain barges alone, plus 200-300 gold from coastal fishing and trade goods. Revenue funds Assembly operations, dockyard maintenance, harbormaster salaries, and Crown tax payments.

**Grain price-setting:** The Assembly does not grow grain, but it sets the buy price for grain imported from the highlands and the sell price for grain distributed to Saltmere residents and coastal buyers. This creates a profit margin (typically 15-20%) that the Assembly collects as a "stabilization fee." The Assembly justifies this as necessary to buffer price fluctuations and maintain emergency grain reserves. In practice, it is a tax on hunger: residents pay inflated prices because they have no alternative.

**Salt production quotas:** The Assembly sets annual salt production quotas for each pond operation, preventing overproduction that would crash prices. Quota allocations favor the Salter and Marsh families, who own the largest ponds. Smaller pond operators (independent families, contract laborers) must apply for quota increases through Assembly approval, which is rarely granted.

**Emergency grain reserve:** Established 30 years ago after a barge sinking caused a two-week grain shortage. Stored in Assembly-controlled warehouses near Centerflow docks, the reserve holds approximately 200 tons of grain, enough to feed Saltmere for 4-6 weeks in a crisis. The reserve is managed by the Speaker and audited annually. [[Marda Vorren]] has not drawn from the reserve during her tenure, but the missing barge has her reconsidering whether to tap it if highland shipments slow.

**Market access control:** The Assembly licenses all market stalls in the three market districts (Westrun, Centerflow, Saltcurrent). Traders who operate without Assembly approval are expelled. This gives the Assembly veto power over who can sell goods in Saltmere, a powerful tool for punishing merchants who challenge Assembly authority.

**What the Assembly does NOT control:** Salt production itself (pond operators own their land and equipment), fishing boats (the Brack fleet and independent fishers operate privately), or upstream grain milling (the highland mills like those in [[Millford]] and [[Ashcrag]] are beyond Assembly jurisdiction).

## Goals & Motivations

**Maintain grain supply stability at all costs.** The Assembly's overriding goal is to ensure uninterrupted highland grain imports. Saltmere cannot feed itself, and the city's survival depends on weekly barge shipments from [[Millford]] and beyond. Every Assembly decision, grain pricing, barge licensing, debt leverage, is evaluated through this lens: does it secure the grain supply or threaten it? [[Corwin Marsh]]'s debt strategy is designed to control upstream mills directly, guaranteeing grain flow. [[Marda Vorren]]'s coastal expansion plans are designed to diversify food sources. The missing barge terrifies the Assembly because it threatens trader confidence in the route.

**Prevent internal price wars and merchant competition.** The Assembly was founded to stop the merchant families from destroying each other through cutthroat competition. Before the Assembly, salt traders undercut each other, grain importers hoarded supplies to drive up prices, and barge captains played merchants against each other for favorable contracts. The Assembly ended this by enforcing collective price-setting, contract mediation, and quota coordination. Maintaining this coordination requires constant negotiation, compromise, and Speaker mediation. If the five houses fracture, Saltmere's economy collapses into chaos.

**Expand Saltmere's influence along the coast.** [[Marda Vorren]]'s long-term vision is to transform Saltmere from a river delta port into a chartered coastal trade hub, competing with larger ports downriver. This requires Crown approval, significant capital investment, and unanimous Assembly support. [[Corwin Marsh]] supports the idea because it would expand his grain export routes. [[Halden Brack]] opposes it because it would shift resources away from the river trade that sustains his fleet. Kellen Salter opposes it because coastal expansion would dilute the salt trade's importance. The proposal is stalled, waiting for the right political moment.

**Control highland debt without triggering rebellion.** [[Corwin Marsh]]'s strategy is to leverage highland debts (owed by traders like [[Garrick Thane]] and landowners like [[Aldric Harth]]) to gain direct control of upstream mills. This would make Marsh the dominant grain supplier, but it risks provoking highland resistance. If Marsh seizes too many assets too quickly, highland traders might organize a trade boycott, cutting off grain entirely. Marsh is patient, calling debts selectively and maintaining the illusion that the highlands remain independent.

## Opposition

**[[Forge Council]]:** The highland ruling body governing [[Ashcrag]]. The Forge Council resents lowland debt leverage and sees the Assembly as an economic colonizer extracting highland wealth. [[Myra Cadros]], the Council head, frames the conflict as "highland independence vs. lowland creditors." The Assembly sees the Forge Council as a fragile coalition of guild masters clinging to an outdated economic model. The two factions are not openly hostile, they need each other for trade, but if [[Corwin Marsh]] seizes [[Aldric Harth]]'s estate or the [[Highland Workers Coalition]] organizes a grain boycott, the relationship could collapse into open economic warfare.

**[[Highland Merchant Cartel]]:** Economic rival controlling [[Ironhollow]] and financing lowland trade through factors like [[Tallow Breck]]. The Cartel competes with the Assembly for highland grain contracts and debt leverage. [[Corwin Marsh]] has borrowed from the Cartel in the past but has since paid off his debts and become a rival creditor. The Cartel sees Marsh's debt empire as a threat and is watching closely. If Marsh becomes too dominant, the Cartel will move to crush him through economic pressure (undercutting grain prices, offering better terms to highland traders, or financing rival Assembly houses).

**Labor organizing (potential):** Saltmere has no organized labor movement yet, but the conditions are building. Pond workers, dock crews, and fishing laborers work brutal hours for low wages while the merchant families accumulate wealth. If grain prices rise due to shortages, labor unrest could ignite. The Assembly has no counter-insurgency plan because it has never faced organized resistance. Its enforcement tools (market access denial, barge license revocation) only work on traders and merchants, not on laborers who have nothing to lose.

**Crown authority (contingent):** The Assembly governs with Crown approval but minimal Crown oversight. The Crown taxes Assembly trade revenue but does not interfere in governance. However, if the Assembly's internal conflicts (debt seizures, grain shortages, or trade disruptions) threaten broader regional stability, the Crown could reassert direct authority. [[Marda Vorren]] fears this scenario: the Crown appointing a governor to replace the Assembly, ending merchant self-governance.

## Internal Tensions

**Corwin Marsh's quiet consolidation vs. the other houses.** [[Corwin Marsh]] is building a debt empire that threatens to subsume the other merchant families. He holds leverage on highland traders, coastal buyers, and even some Assembly members. The other houses see what he is doing, Greave is trying to compete through highland acquisitions, Vorren is trying to diversify through coastal expansion, Salter is retreating into salt conservatism, Brack is too poor to compete, but no one has found a way to stop him. Marsh operates through persuasion and favors, never forcing votes, always building consensus. This makes him impossible to oppose directly without fracturing the Assembly.

**Greave ambition vs. Marsh dominance.** The Greave family's highland expansion (Ossian's debt collection in [[Millford]]) is a direct challenge to Marsh's grain monopoly. If Ossian seizes Southgrain Mill, Greave gains a foothold in the highlands, threatening Marsh's supply control. Marsh is watching Ossian closely. If Ossian succeeds, Marsh will move to undermine Greave through economic pressure (undercutting barge contracts, denying market access). If Ossian fails, Marsh will consolidate further. Either way, the Marsh-Greave rivalry is the Assembly's most volatile internal tension.

**Grain dependency vs. coastal expansion.** [[Marda Vorren]] wants to build a coastal port to diversify Saltmere's economy away from highland grain dependency. [[Corwin Marsh]] supports this because it expands his export routes. [[Halden Brack]] opposes it because it shifts resources away from river trade. Kellen Salter opposes it because it dilutes salt's importance. The proposal requires unanimous Assembly approval, which it does not have. Vorren is waiting for the right moment, a crisis that proves the need for diversification or a concession that wins Brack and Salter over, but the missing barge has made everyone too cautious to consider long-term investments.

**The missing barge cover-up.** Three weeks ago, a grain barge from [[Millford]] disappeared in the delta. The Assembly voted to suppress the news to avoid panic, but [[Halden Brack]] dissented and has been pushing for a public investigation. The other houses want it buried: [[Corwin Marsh]] fears it will spook highland traders, [[Marda Vorren]] fears it will destabilize grain prices, Ossian Greave fears it will interrupt his Millford collection trip. Brack is isolated but relentless. If he goes public or the PCs force the issue, the cover-up becomes a scandal that fractures the Assembly's credibility.

**Speaker neutrality under pressure.** [[Marda Vorren]]'s power as Speaker depends on her reputation for neutrality. She mediates disputes, casts tiebreaker votes, and represents the Assembly externally without favoring any house. But the missing barge is testing this neutrality. Brack wants an investigation, the other houses want silence, and Vorren is trapped between transparency and stability. If she sides with Brack, she loses Marsh and Greave's support and risks her Speaker position. If she sides with the cover-up, she loses her moral authority and Brack's loyalty. Either choice fractures her neutrality.

## Campaign Role

The Saltmere Merchant Assembly is the primary political power structure in [[The Saltfen]], controlling grain distribution, salt production, and maritime trade. PCs investigating trade disruptions, grain shortages, or the missing barge will encounter the Assembly as an obstacle, an ally, or a quest hub depending on their approach.

**Obstacle to transparency:** The Assembly is covering up the missing barge to avoid panic. If PCs investigate the disappearance, the Assembly will see them as a threat and use economic pressure (denying market access, revoking barge contracts, pressuring local contacts) to discourage their inquiry. [[Corwin Marsh]] will offer bribes or favors to redirect them. [[Marda Vorren]] will ask them politely to stop. Only [[Halden Brack]] will support their investigation openly.

**Potential ally against the Cartel:** The Assembly and the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] are economic rivals. If PCs approach the Assembly with intelligence about Cartel operations, evidence of Cartel manipulation, or a strategy to break Cartel influence, they may negotiate an alliance. However, any cooperation is transactional and temporary. The Assembly will abandon the PCs if cooperation threatens grain supply stability.

**Political quest hub:** The Assembly is the center of Saltmere's political intrigue. PCs can influence Assembly votes, exploit internal faction rivalries (Marsh vs. Greave, Brack vs. the cover-up coalition), mediate between the Assembly and highland traders, or manipulate Assembly decisions for their own ends. Weekly Assembly meetings are opportunities for negotiation, eavesdropping, bribery, or exposure.

**Power shift scenario:** If the missing barge investigation reveals "the pull" as a supernatural threat, the Assembly faces a crisis: continue grain imports through a dangerous route or find an alternative (coastal imports, rationing, emergency reserves). How the Assembly responds, transparency vs. cover-up, collective action vs. internal fracture, reshapes Saltmere's political landscape and determines whether the merchant coalition survives.
