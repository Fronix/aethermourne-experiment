# Aldric Harth

## Appearance

Aldric is a well-fed man in his mid-forties with a round face and soft hands that have not worked a field in years. He wears fine wool clothing, dark greens and browns that stand out in a town of homespun and leather, and a silver chain across his vest that marks him as a man of means. His dark hair is receding, and he keeps it oiled back. His face is starting to show the lines of chronic worry, especially around his eyes and mouth. He moves with the careful dignity of someone who knows they are being watched and judged. He smells like lavender soap and anxiety.

## Personality

Aldric is ambitious, desperate, and trapped. He presents himself as confident and commanding, the voice of the farming interest on the Forge Council, but beneath that facade, he is terrified. He knows he is one bad harvest away from losing everything. He makes decisions based on fear, not strategy, which leads him to pursue contradictory goals: trying to please the council while negotiating with their enemies, trying to maximize grain exports while hoarding reserves, trying to maintain his dignity while scrambling for coin. He is not cruel, but he is selfish. When forced to choose between his own survival and someone else's, he will choose himself every time and hate himself for it later.

## Backstory

Aldric inherited the Harth Estate from his father twelve years ago. The estate was profitable then, a sprawling operation with tenant farmers, grain storage, and reliable contracts with lowland merchants. Aldric wanted more. He borrowed heavily from [[Corwin Marsh]] in [[Saltmere]] to expand, buying out three neighboring farms and consolidating them into the estate. For five years, it worked. The estate produced a third of Ashcrag's grain, and Aldric became the wealthiest man in the highlands. Then the harvests started to falter. A late frost, a dry summer, a blight that spoiled half the stored grain. The debt payments kept coming, but the profits did not. Marsh has been patient, but now he is calling the debt. Aldric has two months to pay, or Marsh seizes the estate.

The formative event: Eight years ago, Aldric outbid a rival farmer, Gregor Thane, for a lucrative lowland grain contract. Gregor, unable to pay his own debts without that contract, lost his farm. His family left the highlands, and Gregor died two years later, broken and poor. Aldric felt no guilt at the time, it was business. Now, facing the same fate, he remembers Gregor's face and wonders if he is being punished.

## Relationships

- [[Corwin Marsh]] — Aldric's creditor and greatest threat. Aldric owes Marsh more money than the estate is worth. He has been trying to negotiate an extension, offering future harvests, exclusive contracts, anything to delay the seizure. Marsh has refused. Aldric hates him, fears him, and depends on him in equal measure.
- [[Myra Cadros]] — Council head. Aldric sits on the Forge Council as the farmer representative, but Myra dominates every meeting. He defers to her publicly because he needs her support, but he resents her for dismissing his warnings about lowland debt pressure. He suspects she would not intervene if Marsh seized his estate.
- [[Orrin Pike]] — Labor organizer. Pike has been rallying tenant farmers to demand council representation, and some of those farmers work Aldric's land. Aldric sees Pike as a threat to the social order that keeps the estate profitable, but he also knows Pike's grievances are valid. He has refused to support Pike's petitions because it would anger Myra, but privately, he wonders if Pike's coalition could help resist Marsh's influence.
- [[Grett Voss]] — Market factor. Aldric sells his grain through Voss's tally house, and Voss knows exactly how much Aldric is moving, and how much he is hoarding. Voss has not shared that information with the council yet, but Aldric knows they could. He treats Voss with wary respect and excessive courtesy, hoping to keep them neutral.
- [[Tallow Breck]] — Cartel factor. Aldric has considered borrowing from Breck's cartel to pay off Marsh, swapping one creditor for another. Breck has made it clear he would lend, but at rates Aldric cannot afford. Aldric is terrified that if he defaults to the cartel, they will be even more ruthless than Marsh.

## What They Want

Aldric wants to save the Harth Estate. He wants to pay off Marsh, restore his family's reputation, and secure his position as the wealthiest grain lord in the highlands. He wants respect, stability, and the assurance that his children will inherit what he built.

What he needs is to accept that the estate, as it exists, cannot be saved, and that holding onto it is destroying him. He needs to let go, negotiate a smaller holding, share the land with tenant farmers, or find a way to break Marsh's leverage by allying with people he has dismissed. He needs humility, but he does not know how to find it without losing his identity.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Aldric is the hinge between highland and lowland power. If he defaults, [[Corwin Marsh]] gains control of the highland grain supply, which shifts the entire river trade network's balance of power. PCs investigating the missing barge, the grain shortage, or Corwin's expansion will encounter Aldric as a desperate man willing to make dangerous deals. He is a potential ally, enemy, or tragic casualty depending on PC choices.
>
> **Key Secret.** Aldric has been secretly negotiating with smugglers to sell grain outside official channels, bypassing [[Grett Voss]]'s tally house and the council's market fees. He is using the coin to make partial payments to Marsh, buying time. If the council discovers this, they will expel him and possibly seize his seat. If Marsh discovers it, he will call the debt immediately.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Aldric will default in two months, Marsh will seize the estate, and the highlands will fall under lowland economic control. This is a campaign-scale power shift. If the PCs help Aldric (negotiating with Marsh, finding alternative income, or breaking Marsh's leverage), they can prevent the seizure but make an enemy of Corwin Marsh. If they expose Aldric's smuggling, they will destroy him but potentially weaken Marsh's plans.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Aldric speaks carefully, choosing words like a man walking through a minefield. He smiles too much, laughs too loud at jokes, and agrees too quickly when pressed. When cornered, his voice tightens and he stammers slightly. He avoids eye contact when lying, which he does often. He is polite to everyone, even people he despises, because he cannot afford to make more enemies. Sample line: "The Harth Estate has fed this town for three generations. I will not be the one to lose it. I'll do whatever it takes, pay any price, make any deal. Just... tell me there's a way out of this."
