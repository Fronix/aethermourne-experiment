# Brenna Cray

## Appearance

Brenna is a wiry woman in her early thirties with dark hair she keeps tied back in a severe braid. Her hands are scarred from years of hauling stone and timber, rebuilding the mill her family lost. She wears men's work clothes, rough linen and leather, because she can't afford to ruin good fabric and won't dress like someone who doesn't work with her hands. Her face is weathered from long days in the sun, and there's a permanent crease between her eyebrows from squinting and scowling. She smells like river water and sweat.

## Personality

Brenna is driven, stubborn, and exhausted. She has no patience for excuses or small talk. When someone wastes her time, her jaw tightens and she cuts them off mid-sentence. She's blunt to the point of rudeness, not because she enjoys it, but because she doesn't have the energy to soften her words. Beneath the hard exterior, she's fiercely loyal to the few people she trusts. She believes the world is unfair and the only way to survive is to outwork everyone else. She's deeply, privately afraid that she's going to fail, that all the sacrifice will amount to nothing, and that [[Garrick Thane]] was right to count her out.

## Backstory

Brenna was nineteen when the spring flood destroyed her family's mill. Her father, Aldric Cray, had been the upstream miller for twenty years. The flood broke him, not just financially but spiritually. He died two years later, worn down by debt and shame. Brenna's two older brothers left Millford to find work in the lowlands, but Brenna stayed. She borrowed money from everyone who would lend, sold everything the family owned, and rebuilt the mill herself. It took five years. She learned carpentry, stonework, and waterwheel engineering through trial, error, and the grudging advice of a retired millwright who pitied her.

The formative event: When [[Garrick Thane]] bought the wreckage of her family's mill for a pittance, Brenna swore she'd rebuild it and take back what was stolen. Garrick offered her work at one of his mills once. She told him she'd see him drowned in the millrace first. That refusal cost her, but it also defined her.

## Relationships

- [[Garrick Thane]] — Her enemy. She blames him for profiting off her family's ruin. Every success she has is measured against him. She undercuts his rates when she can afford to, even if it means operating at a loss. It's personal, and it's consuming her.
- [[Osmund Vey]] — The senior farmer was friends with her father. Osmund helped her with the first loans and sends farmers to her mill when he can, though most still go to Garrick. She's grateful but resents needing his charity.
- [[Dace Torrn]] — River trader. Dace hauls grain for both millers, which Brenna hates. She's tried to secure an exclusive arrangement, but Dace won't commit. She suspects Dace prefers Garrick because Garrick pays better.
- [[Halford Quick]] — Dock master. Brenna pays her mooring fees on time but can't afford Halford's "considerations" like Garrick does. She's aware she gets slower service and worse berths. It grinds at her.

## What They Want

Brenna wants vindication. She wants to prove that rebuilding the mill was worth it, that her father's legacy isn't dead, that [[Garrick Thane]] was wrong to dismiss her. She wants to crush Garrick economically, to see him beg.

What she needs is to stop letting Garrick define her. Her entire life is a reaction to him. She needs to find something worth building for its own sake, not as revenge. She needs rest.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Brenna is the underdog, the scrappy fighter against an entrenched power. PCs looking for allies against economic injustice will find her compelling. She's also a potential point of failure: if she pushes too hard against Garrick, she'll break. She can be a patron, a quest-giver, or a tragic figure depending on how the PCs engage.
>
> **Key Secret.** Brenna's mill is one bad season away from collapse. She's been operating at a loss to undercut Garrick, and her creditors are getting nervous. If the harvest fails or the river floods again, she's done. She knows this and lies awake every night calculating margins.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Brenna will burn out or make a desperate move (sabotage Garrick's mill, take on impossible debt, flee). If the PCs help stabilize her, she could become a powerful ally and a voice for the common folk against concentrated power. If they side with Garrick, she'll see them as enemies.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Brenna speaks quickly, clipped sentences, like she's always in the middle of ten tasks. She doesn't make eye contact for long, always glancing at the mill, the river, the wheel. When she's truly angry, her voice drops and gets quieter, not louder. Sample line: "You want to talk rates? Fine. I'll beat Garrick's price by two copper per sack. I don't care if it bleeds me dry. He doesn't get to win."
