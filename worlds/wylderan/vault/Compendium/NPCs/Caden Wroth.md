# Caden Wroth

## Appearance

Caden is a broad-shouldered man in his mid-40s with the build of someone who worked the mines before he learned to profit from them. His hands are thick and scarred, his face weathered and hard. His eyes are pale gray, the kind that look through you while calculating what you are worth. He wears a heavy canvas coat reinforced with leather at the shoulders and elbows, practical for the cold but better quality than any miner could afford. He carries two things everywhere: a leather-bound ledger tucked under one arm, and a weighted oak club hanging from his belt. The ledger tracks every debt, every quota, every ounce of ore. The club enforces it. He moves with deliberate weight, never hurrying, because he does not need to. He owns the time of everyone around him.

## Personality

Caden is ruthless, methodical, and utterly unsentimental about his work. He does not see miners as people, he sees them as assets that produce ore until they break. He is not cruel for pleasure, he is cruel as policy, the way you are cruel to a horse that refuses to pull. When he speaks, it is in short, declarative sentences: you owe this much, you will produce this much, this is what happens if you do not. He does not threaten, he informs. Beneath this, he is deeply insecure. He clawed his way out of the mines by being harder and meaner than everyone else, and he is terrified of falling back. Every act of control, every display of power, is a defense against the fear that someone will take it from him the way he took it from others.

## Backstory

Caden was born in [[Millford]], the son of a barge worker who drowned when Caden was eight. His mother moved them to [[Ashcrag]] and worked as a laundress until she died of fever when Caden was fourteen. He went to work in the mines north of town, contract labor that paid almost nothing and left him in perpetual debt. He spent ten years in the pits, watching men break their backs for scraps while the Highland Merchant Cartel got rich. He hated the miners around him for being weak, and he hated the cartel for being strong, and he decided he would rather be the cartel.

At twenty-four, he informed on a group of miners planning to steal ore and sell it outside cartel channels. The cartel rewarded him by clearing his debt and hiring him as an enforcer. He spent the next decade breaking strikes, collecting debts, and making examples of miners who challenged the system. Fifteen years ago, the cartel promoted him to Mining Factor and sent him to [[Ironhollow]] to take full control of the settlement. He has been there ever since, turning a chaotic collection of independent prospectors into a disciplined extraction operation that funnels ore, and profit, to [[Tallow Breck]] and the cartel.

The formative event: Twenty years ago, Caden was part of a work crew that tried to organize a slowdown to protest unpaid wages. The cartel sent enforcers, arrested the leaders, and foreclosed on their families. Caden's best friend, a miner named Renn, was beaten in front of the crew as an example. Caden stood with the other miners and did nothing. Renn never worked again. That day, Caden decided he would never be the one on his knees. He would be the one holding the club.

## Relationships

- [[Tallow Breck]] — Cartel factor and direct superior. Caden reports to Breck quarterly, delivering ledgers of ore production, debt collection, and labor stability. Breck trusts Caden to keep [[Ironhollow]] profitable and under control. Caden respects Breck's cold efficiency and fears disappointing him, because failure means being replaced, and replacement means going back to the mines.
- [[Senna Kade]] — Labor organizer and enemy. Caden knows Senna is organizing miners to strike, and he sees her as a direct threat to his authority. He has tried intimidation, threats, and sabotage to break her coalition, but she refuses to bend. He is preparing to make an example of her, violently if necessary, to crush the organizing effort before it spreads.
- [[Jorin Velk]] — Independent prospector and target. Jorin works a claim outside cartel control and has discovered a rich vein. Caden wants that claim, both for the ore and to eliminate the example of independence. He has been pressuring Jorin to sell, fabricating claim disputes, and preparing to seize the land by force. Jorin's refusal to yield enrages him.
- [[Halven Mir]] — Weighmaster and subordinate. Caden relies on Halven to rig the scales and undercount ore deliveries, ensuring miners stay in debt and the cartel maximizes profit. Halven is loyal because Caden protects him from miner retribution. Caden does not trust him, but he needs him.
- [[Marna Tess]] — Company store operator and ally. Marna tracks miner debt through the store, and Caden uses her ledgers to identify which miners are falling behind and need pressure. They work in parallel, economic extraction and physical enforcement. Caden respects her ruthlessness and her lack of sentimentality. She is the only person in [[Ironhollow]] he considers a peer.

## What They Want

Caden wants [[Ironhollow]] to run at maximum efficiency, ore production high, labor costs low, no disruptions. He wants to crush [[Senna Kade]]'s organizing before it spreads, seize [[Jorin Velk]]'s claim, and deliver another profitable year to [[Tallow Breck]]. He wants the cartel to recognize his value, promote him, give him more settlements to control. He wants power, always more power, because power is the only thing that keeps him safe.

What he needs is to confront the fact that he has become the thing he hated. He tells himself he is stronger and smarter than the miners he controls, but deep down he knows he is just luckier, and that luck could run out. He needs to let go of the fear that drives him, but he does not know how, and he is not sure he could survive without it.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Caden is the face of cartel oppression in [[Ironhollow]] and the primary antagonist for labor organizing storylines. PCs investigating highland labor exploitation, helping [[Senna Kade]] organize a strike, or defending [[Jorin Velk]]'s claim will clash with Caden directly. He is not a villain who can be reasoned with, he is a system enforcer who will use violence to maintain control. If the PCs defeat or remove him, the cartel will send a replacement, but labor organizing will have a brief window to succeed.
>
> **Key Secret.** Caden has been skimming ore from cartel shipments and selling it through [[Grett Voss]] in [[Ashcrag]], pocketing the difference. He justifies this as payment for his work, but if [[Tallow Breck]] discovers the theft, Caden will be ruined or killed. This makes him vulnerable to blackmail and desperate to avoid external scrutiny.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Caden will crush [[Senna Kade]]'s strike, seize [[Jorin Velk]]'s claim, and tighten cartel control over [[Ironhollow]]. If the PCs help the strikers or expose his theft, he will escalate to violence, bringing in cartel enforcers and turning [[Ironhollow]] into a battleground. If he is defeated, he will either flee (and become a recurring antagonist seeking revenge) or be killed (and become a martyr for the cartel's narrative that resistance leads to chaos).
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Caden speaks in short, flat sentences, never raising his voice. He refers to his ledger constantly, flipping to specific pages and citing exact figures. He makes eye contact when delivering ultimatums but looks away when lying. He never apologizes, never explains, never justifies. When threatened, he does not bluster, he calculates. Sample line: "You owe four hundred twenty-three silver. You have mined two hundred sixty silver worth of ore this month. That leaves one hundred sixty-three outstanding. You will deliver that amount by winter close, or the cartel takes your contract. That is not a negotiation. That is the ledger."
