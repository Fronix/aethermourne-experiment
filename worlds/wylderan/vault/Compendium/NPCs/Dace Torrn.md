# Dace Torrn

## Appearance

Dace is a lean man in his late thirties with sun-darkened skin and ropy muscle from years hauling lines and steering barges. His dark hair is streaked with early gray and hangs to his shoulders, usually tied back with a strip of leather. He wears a weathered oilskin coat and tall boots caked with river mud. His hands are covered in old scars, and he's missing the tip of his left pinky from a rope accident. He moves with the easy balance of someone who lives on water, and he smells like tar, wet wood, and tobacco.

## Personality

Dace is affable, adaptable, and entirely untrustworthy in the best possible way. He's charming when it suits him, blunt when charm won't work, and always calculating the angles. He treats everyone like a potential customer or useful contact. He's a gossip and an information broker, trading news as freely as goods. People like him because he's entertaining and useful, but no one truly trusts him. Dace doesn't mind. Trust is expensive and not particularly profitable. He's loyal to the river first, profit second, and people a distant third.

Beneath the pragmatism, Dace is deeply superstitious about the river. He drops offerings into the current before every dangerous run, copper coins or twists of tobacco, and whispers the old prayer: "Flow steady, return safe." He wears a carved wooden fish token on a cord around his wrist, a Keeper symbol his grandmother gave him. He won't cross the ford without touching the water first. When he's alone on the *Kingfisher* at night, he talks to the barge like it can hear the god listening through the current.

## Backstory

Dace grew up on the river, the son of a barge family that worked the trade routes between the highlands and lowlands for generations. He inherited his father's barge, the *Kingfisher*, ten years ago, along with the routes, contacts, and debts. Dace learned early that the river has moods, and the people along it have needs. He built his reputation on being reliable (mostly), discreet (when paid), and willing to carry things other traders wouldn't touch.

The formative event: Seven years ago, Dace was caught smuggling untaxed wool past [[Lyman Corvish]], the toll collector. Corvish could have had him arrested, fined, or barred from the river. Instead, Corvish let him go in exchange for Dace becoming an informant, reporting on who's moving what and avoiding tolls. Dace agreed, then immediately started feeding Corvish useless or misleading information while continuing to smuggle. It's become a game. Corvish knows Dace is playing him, Dace knows Corvish knows, and they both pretend otherwise. It's exhausting and profitable.

## Relationships

- [[Garrick Thane]] — Garrick is Dace's best customer. Dace hauls grain for Garrick and occasionally smuggles it past the toll collector for a cut of the savings. He likes Garrick's no-nonsense efficiency but finds him joyless.
- [[Brenna Cray]] — Dace also hauls for Brenna, though less often because she pays less and doesn't have as much grain to move. He feels bad for her but won't give her a discount. He's offered to smuggle for her too, but she's too proud (or too broke) to take the risk.
- [[Lyman Corvish]] — The toll collector. Dace has a complicated, adversarial relationship with Corvish. They're locked in a mutual con, each trying to outwit the other. Dace finds it more entertaining than Corvish does.
- [[Halford Quick]] — Dock master. Dace pays Halford's mooring fees and occasionally slips him extra coin to look the other way when Dace unloads cargo that wasn't on the manifest. They have an understanding.

## What They Want

Dace wants freedom and the means to maintain it. He wants the *Kingfisher* to keep running, the river to stay navigable, and enough profit to avoid being tied down to any one patron or place. He wants to stay ahead of Corvish, ahead of his debts, and ahead of any trouble that might catch up with him.

What he needs is to choose a side. His neutrality is profitable, but it's also isolating. He has contacts, not friends. He's forty and still living on a barge with no family, no roots, no legacy. He doesn't want to admit that bothers him.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Dace is the mobile connector, the source of news and rumors from beyond Millford. He's a natural quest-giver for jobs involving transport, smuggling, or information. He's also a barometer for regional tensions: if Dace starts avoiding certain routes or raising his rates, something's wrong upriver or downriver.
>
> **Key Secret.** Dace is deeper in debt than anyone realizes. He owes money to a highland merchant cartel for cargo lost in a storm two years ago. They've been patient, but they're not forgiving. If they call in the debt, Dace will lose the *Kingfisher*. He's been smuggling more to pay them off, which increases his risk.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Dace will keep juggling debts and risks until one catches up with him. A storm, a raid, or Corvish finally making an arrest could break him. If the PCs ally with him, they might help clear his debts (or make them worse). If he's forced to choose between profit and loyalty, he'll agonize over it and probably choose wrong.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Dace is conversational and loose, always leaning against something or gesturing with a pipe. He laughs easily but never at himself. When he's nervous, he fidgets with a leather cord around his wrist. Sample line: "Toll collector's asking questions again. Not about you, not yet. But he's sniffing around. I can make that problem go away, for a small consideration. Or you could take your chances. Your coin, your risk."
