# Dessa Kord

## Appearance

Dessa is a sharp-faced woman in her mid-twenties with quick, intelligent eyes that miss nothing. She has short dark hair, practical and easy to manage, and her hands are scarred from kitchen work, burns from stoves and cuts from knives. She wears a plain canvas dress with a leather apron, both stained with grease and flour, and she moves with brisk efficiency, no wasted motion. She smells like woodsmoke, stew, and the faint tang of onions. Her expression is neutral by default, a mask she wears to blend into the background, but when she speaks, her voice is sharp, precise, and cutting. She is the kind of person who stands at the edge of a room and sees everything.

## Personality

Dessa is sharp, strategic, and utterly pragmatic. She is not a miner, does not work the pits, does not face the same dangers as the people she is organizing, and she knows this makes some of them distrust her. But she feeds them, hears their complaints, tracks their fears, and keeps a mental ledger of who is committed, who is wavering, and who is a potential informant. She is [[Senna Kade]]'s brain trust, the person who tells Senna when a plan is foolish, when trust is misplaced, and when to pull back. Beneath this, she is lonely. She has no friends in [[Ironhollow]], only informants and assets, and the line between the two has blurred. She tells herself this is necessary, that sentiment is weakness, but the truth is she has forgotten how to trust anyone.

## Backstory

Dessa was born in [[Saltmere]], the daughter of a dock worker who died when she was eight. Her mother remarried, to a man who drank and gambled away every coin the family had. Dessa ran away at fourteen, hopping a barge north and ending up in [[Ashcrag]]. She worked tavern kitchens for a year, then moved to [[Ironhollow]] when she heard the boarding houses paid better. She has been cooking for the miners for six years, feeding sixty men a day, listening to their complaints, their fears, their plans.

Three years ago, [[Senna Kade]] came to her and asked if she would help organize the miners. Dessa said no, she was not political, she just cooked. Senna came back a month later and said she did not need Dessa to organize, she needed her to listen. Dessa listened. She realized she already knew more about the miners than Senna did, knew who was desperate, who was angry, who was too scared to act. She started reporting to Senna weekly. Eighteen months ago, Senna asked her to formally join the coalition as a lieutenant. Dessa said yes, not because she believed in the cause, but because organizing was more interesting than cooking, and she was tired of being invisible.

The formative event: Two years ago, a miner named Goran came to Dessa and asked if she would help him write a letter to his family in [[Millford]]. He could not write, and he was ashamed to ask anyone else. Dessa wrote the letter. Three days later, Goran died in a roof collapse. Dessa found out he had been behind on his debt and [[Caden Wroth]] had assigned him to the most dangerous section of the pits as punishment. She realized in that moment that the system was not broken by accident, it was designed to crush people, and that ignoring it made her complicit. She has not ignored it since.

## Relationships

- [[Senna Kade]] — Leader and closest thing Dessa has to a friend. Dessa respects Senna's courage and hates that Senna trusts people too easily. She is the person who tells Senna hard truths: that the coalition is fragile, that [[Rennic Vale]] is probably an informant, that half the committed miners will fold if the cartel threatens their families. Senna values Dessa's strategic mind but sometimes resents her cynicism, because it reminds Senna of how precarious everything is.
- [[Torvin Marks]] — Colleague. Dessa appreciates Torvin's experience and his refusal to lie about the coalition's chances. They have a quiet partnership: Dessa identifies problems, Torvin executes solutions, and neither of them pretends they believe the coalition will win. Dessa knows Torvin is dying, she has seen the blood on his rag, but she has not confronted him because she needs him functional.
- [[Rennic Vale]] — Suspected informant. Dessa is 90% certain Rennic is reporting to [[Caden Wroth]]. He joined the coalition six months ago, asks too many questions, volunteers for everything, and never complains. She has been feeding him false information to test him: false meeting locations, fake message schedules. So far, cartel patrols have shown up at two of the fake locations. She has not told [[Senna Kade]] yet because exposing an informant without proof could fracture the coalition worse than leaving him in place. She is waiting for absolute confirmation.
- [[Kael Torrn]] — Asset. Dessa debriefs Kael every time he delivers messages from [[Orrin Pike]], asking about routes, surveillance, and cartel activity. She finds him naive and too trusting, but she values his loyalty. She has warned him multiple times that he is carrying dangerous information and will be killed if caught, but he does not seem to understand what that means. She expects him to die within a year.
- [[Caden Wroth]] — Enemy. Dessa sees Caden as the face of the system she is fighting, a man who climbed out of the pits by betraying everyone below him. She has never spoken to him directly, and she prefers it that way. If the coalition strikes, she expects Caden will target her specifically, because he knows she is the one feeding Senna intelligence.

## What They Want

Dessa wants the [[Highland Workers Coalition]] to succeed, not because she believes in abstract ideals like justice or freedom, but because she is tired of watching people like Goran die for a system that treats them as disposable. She wants [[Senna Kade]] to survive the strike, [[Caden Wroth]] to lose his position, and the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] to learn that exploiting workers has consequences. She wants to prove that organizing is not futile.

What she needs is to reconnect with the people she is organizing, not as assets but as humans. She has spent three years treating miners as pieces on a board, tracking their commitments and fears like data points, and she has forgotten that they are people with lives and families and hopes. She needs to trust someone, to let her guard down, to admit that she is not just strategic, she is scared. But trust requires vulnerability, and vulnerability is the one thing she cannot afford.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Dessa is the coalition's intelligence network, the person who tracks threats, identifies weaknesses, and advises [[Senna Kade]] on tactics. PCs investigating the coalition, helping it, or hunting it will encounter Dessa as a formidable obstacle. She will lie to protect Senna, feed false information to test loyalty, and sacrifice individuals to protect the coalition. If the PCs earn her trust (difficult, requires proving they are competent and discreet), she becomes a valuable ally with deep knowledge of coalition operations. If they betray her, she will dismantle them methodically.
>
> **Key Secret.** Dessa has been testing [[Rennic Vale]] by feeding him false information, and she has confirmed he is an informant. She has not told [[Senna Kade]] because she wants to use Rennic to feed disinformation to [[Caden Wroth]], turning the cartel's surveillance against them. But this strategy is dangerous, if Rennic realizes he is being manipulated, he will tell Caden, and the coalition loses its counterintelligence advantage. Dessa is playing a game with lives, and if she miscalculates, people die.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If [[Senna Kade]] is arrested or killed, Dessa becomes the acting leader of the [[Ironhollow]] cell. She is strategic and competent, but she lacks Senna's charisma, and the miners do not trust her the way they trust Senna. The coalition could hold together under her leadership, but only if she learns to inspire, not just organize. If the coalition collapses, Dessa will disappear, burning records and destroying evidence before the cartel can connect her to the organizing. If the PCs expose her manipulation of [[Rennic Vale]], she loses Senna's trust and the coalition fractures.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Dessa speaks in short, precise sentences, always to the point. She asks pointed questions and waits for answers, watching how people respond. She does not make small talk, does not explain herself, does not waste time on sentiment. When she is planning, she talks through options aloud, listing pros and cons in a flat, analytical tone. Sample line: "You want to know if the miners will strike? Twelve will. Eighteen won't. The twelve are enough to stop ore production, but not enough to hold the line if the cartel brings in scab labor. If you want to win, you need external pressure. A buyers' boycott, a sympathetic merchant house, something that makes the cartel negotiate instead of crush. Without that, you are asking twelve people to die for nothing. Is that what you want?"
