# Grett Voss

|---|---|
| **Title** | Market Factor, Information Broker |
| **Location** | [[Ashcrag]], [[The Stone Heights]] |
| **Faction** | Forge Council (employed) |

## Appearance

Grett is a lean person in their late thirties with sharp, assessing eyes that miss nothing. They dress in practical but well-maintained clothing, dark wool and leather, with a leather satchel always at their side containing ledgers, ink, and tally sticks. Their hands are ink-stained and move quickly, whether recording figures, counting coin, or gesturing while speaking. They keep their dark hair tied back in a tight braid, and their face is difficult to read, neutral by default, revealing emotion only when they choose. They move efficiently through the market ground, weaving between wagons and farmers like water around stones. They smell like parchment and lamp oil.

## Personality

Grett is neutral, observant, and ruthlessly practical. They do not take sides in disputes, which makes them valuable to everyone and trusted by no one. They listen more than they speak, and when they speak, it is with precision, no wasted words, no unnecessary detail. They do not lie, but they choose what truths to share and when. Beneath this professional neutrality, they are deeply curious. They collect information not just for profit but because they want to understand how the highland economy works, who has leverage over whom, what makes people act against their own interests. They see patterns where others see chaos, and they use that knowledge carefully, because knowledge is the only power they have.

## Backstory

Grett was not born in Ashcrag. They grew up in [[Millford]], the child of a ferryman who drowned when they were twelve. They learned to read and write by clerking for a grain merchant, tracking shipments and keeping ledgers. At twenty, they moved to Ashcrag, hired by the Forge Council to run the market tally house after the previous factor died of fever. They have held the position for fifteen years, longer than any of their predecessors. They are not a council member, they are an employee, but they attend every meeting and provide the financial reports that shape council decisions. They have no land, no forge, no family in Ashcrag, which makes them uniquely unattached. They owe their position to competence, not birth or connection.

The formative event: Ten years ago, [[Aldric Harth]] tried to bribe Grett to misrecord a grain sale, allowing Harth to avoid part of the market fee. Grett refused and reported the attempt to the council. Harth was fined. Since then, Harth has treated Grett with wary respect, and Grett has watched Harth's sales closely. That moment taught Grett that neutrality has value only if it is genuine, and that being trusted by both sides requires disappointing both sides equally.

## Relationships

- [[Myra Cadros]] — Council head. Grett respects Myra's competence and provides her with accurate financial reports, even when the numbers are bad. Myra trusts Grett to run the market without interference. When Grett pushes for early supply stockpiling, Myra listens but does not act, which frustrates Grett, but they do not press. They know Myra sees them as a useful tool, not a peer.
- [[Aldric Harth]] — Grain lord. Grett knows Harth is smuggling grain outside the official market. They have not reported it yet because they want to understand why and who he is selling to. Grett sees Harth as a man drowning slowly, and they are deciding whether to throw him a rope or let him sink. They do not like him, but they do not want to see him destroyed without understanding the consequences.
- [[Orrin Pike]] — Labor organizer. Grett has spoken with Pike several times, listening to his grievances and recording his petition signatures. Grett is sympathetic to Pike's cause, tenant farmers are being squeezed, but they do not support him publicly because it would compromise their neutrality. Grett has quietly advised Pike on how to structure his petitions to make them harder for the council to dismiss.
- [[Tallow Breck]] — Cartel factor. Grett and Breck have a professional relationship built on mutual recognition. They both traffic in information and debts. Breck knows Grett tracks every barge contract, and Grett knows Breck tracks every trader's solvency. They share information when it serves both their interests, and they avoid each other when it does not. Grett does not trust Breck, but they respect his skill.
- [[Dace Torrn]] — River trader. Grett has processed Dace's barge contracts for years and knows he owes money to Breck's cartel. Grett has warned Dace, obliquely, that his debt is becoming council gossip. Dace took the warning seriously, which earned Grett's quiet approval.

## What They Want

Grett wants to keep the Ashcrag market functioning. They want smooth transactions, timely payments, predictable trade flows. They want the farmer-guild conflict to be resolved without violence, because violence disrupts commerce. They want the winter road closure to be managed proactively, not reactively, because panic leads to hoarding, which collapses markets.

What they need is to choose a side. Neutrality has served them well, but the conflicts brewing in Ashcrag, Pike's farmer coalition, Harth's debt crisis, the charcoal shortage, are too large to remain neutral through. Grett will have to decide what they value more: stability, fairness, or survival. They do not know yet.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Grett is the information hub. PCs investigating highland trade, debt networks, or grain smuggling will need to talk to Grett. They are not an obstacle, but they are cautious. They will share information if the PCs prove they are serious, competent, or offer useful information in return. Grett can become a valuable ally if the PCs earn their trust, but betraying them will make them an enemy who knows exactly where the PCs are vulnerable.
>
> **Key Secret.** Grett knows [[Aldric Harth]] is smuggling grain and has tracked three buyers: a lowland merchant bypassing [[Corwin Marsh]]'s network, a smuggler working the southern road, and [[Tallow Breck]]'s cartel. Grett has not decided whether to report this to the council, use it as leverage, or warn Harth that others are noticing. This knowledge makes Grett dangerous to multiple factions.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Grett will continue collecting information and waiting for the right moment to act, which may never come. If the PCs ally with them, Grett will share what they know and help navigate Ashcrag's political tensions. If the PCs threaten the market's stability, Grett will work against them quietly, using information to undermine their efforts. If forced to choose sides, Grett will side with whoever offers the best chance of keeping the market intact.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Grett speaks in short, precise sentences. They ask clarifying questions before answering, stalling to decide what to share. They do not volunteer information, they trade it. When they trust someone, they lean in slightly and lower their voice. When they distrust someone, they maintain perfect politeness and reveal nothing. Sample line: "I keep the tallies. I know who sells, who buys, who's late, who's early, who's lying about their numbers. That's my job. What you do with that information, if you had it, is yours. But information is a trade, not a gift. What are you offering?"
