# Hal Brennan

## Appearance

Hal is a wiry man in his early sixties with iron-gray hair tied back in a leather cord and a face weathered from decades of forge work. His hands are calloused and scarred, knuckles swollen from arthritis but still steady enough to work hot metal. He wears a leather apron stained with soot and rust, patched at the shoulders, and homespun wool beneath it. His beard is thick and untrimmed, streaked with ash, and his eyes are pale blue, watchful and calculating. He moves deliberately, economically, every gesture measured. He smells like charcoal smoke, hot iron, and old sweat, the scent of a man who has spent his entire life in the same building doing the same work. When he speaks, his voice is low and rough, worn down like a stone in a river.

## Personality

Hal is cautious, resentful, and deeply committed to tradition. He believes the guild was stronger when master smiths shared power equally, before [[Myra Cadros]] centralized control and made herself permanent council head. He resents her expansion, her dominance, and her refusal to acknowledge that she has changed the guild into something it was never meant to be. But he lacks the charisma, the political skill, and the raw ambition to challenge her directly. So he undermines her quietly, passively, in ways that can be plausibly denied. He slow-walks contracts that would benefit Cadros Ironworks, spreads rumors that Myra is hoarding charcoal, votes with her publicly while sabotaging her privately. He is waiting for someone else to break her, so he can step into the void without having to risk everything himself. Beneath this, he is tired. He has spent forty years at the anvil, twenty of them watching Myra take what should have been shared, and he is running out of time to see the guild restored.

## Backstory

Hal was born in [[Ashcrag]], the son of Oren Brennan, who founded Brennan's Forge fifty years ago. Oren was a master smith who specialized in precision work, hinges that never squeaked, nails that never bent, wagon fittings that held for decades. Hal learned the trade at his father's side, starting at the bellows at age eight and moving to the anvil by fifteen. When Oren died thirty years ago, Hal took over the forge and continued his father's tradition. For ten years, Brennan's Forge was the second-largest operation in [[Ashcrag]], competing with Old Cadros's workshop for lowland contracts. Then Myra Cadros took over her family's forge.

Twenty years ago, after the harsh winter crisis, Myra restructured the [[Forge Council]], made guild seats permanent, and centralized power under the council head position. Hal voted for it because the guild needed stability, but he did not expect Myra to hold the position for twenty consecutive years. Over the next decade, Myra expanded Cadros Ironworks, hired more apprentices, negotiated exclusive contracts with [[Millford]] merchants, and undercut Brennan's prices on precision work. Hal lost contracts one by one, forced to shift his forge into rougher tools, nails, hinges, wagon fittings, the work his father built his reputation on but which pays less and commands no prestige. Myra took the lucrative contracts and left Hal the scraps.

The formative event: Fifteen years ago, Hal bid on a tool contract with a lowland merchant consortium. He offered better prices and faster delivery than Myra, but she lobbied the [[Forge Council]] to impose a new quality certification requirement for lowland exports. The requirement favored larger forges with multiple apprentices, which Hal could not meet. Myra won the contract. Hal realized in that moment that Myra was using council authority to protect her own forge at the guild's expense. He has resented her ever since, but he has never challenged her openly because she controls the votes.

## Relationships

- [[Myra Cadros]] — Rival and enemy. Hal and Myra compete for contracts, apprentices, and council influence, but it is not an equal rivalry. Myra dominates him economically, politically, and socially. Hal votes with her publicly to maintain guild solidarity (the guild must present a united front against landowner and farmer interests), but he undermines her privately. He slow-walks contracts that would help her forge, spreads rumors that she is rationing charcoal for Cadros Ironworks while letting smaller smithies struggle, and waits for her to overreach. He knows he cannot beat her directly, so he is waiting for her to destroy herself.
- [[Orrin Pike]] — Potential ally, secretly funded. Hal has been quietly funneling small amounts of coin to Orrin's organizing effort through intermediaries, anonymous donations left at meeting sites or passed through sympathetic charcoal haulers. Hal sees Orrin's [[Highland Workers Coalition]] as a tool to destabilize Myra's control. If the coalition forces a crisis, Myra will either compromise (weakening her authority) or crack down (making her look tyrannical). Either way, the guild's centralized structure fractures, and Hal can push for decentralization in the aftermath. Hal does not care about farmer representation, he cares about breaking Myra. Orrin does not know who his anonymous benefactor is.
- [[Aldric Harth]] — Fellow council member, occasional ally. Hal votes with Aldric when it serves his interests, forming a temporary coalition of landowner and guild rival against Myra's dominance. But Hal does not trust Aldric. He knows Aldric is compromised by lowland debt and sees him as weak and desperate. If Aldric defaults and loses his estate, Hal will not defend him.
- Jorin Kelm (council member) — Myra's loyalist. Hal despises Jorin for blindly voting with Myra on every issue. He sees Jorin as a man who sold his independence for forge tool contracts. Hal treats him with thinly veiled contempt.
- Apprentices and workers — Brennan's Forge employs four apprentices and two journeyman smiths. Hal treats them fairly, pays on time, and teaches the craft properly. He is a better teacher than Myra, more patient, less demanding, and his apprentices are loyal to him. But Myra hires more of them, so loyalty does not translate to power.

## What They Want

Hal wants the [[Forge Council]] to return to its original decentralized structure, where master smiths shared power equally and the council head position rotated annually. He wants Myra to lose her grip on the council, either through political collapse, economic failure, or external pressure from the [[Highland Workers Coalition]]. He wants Brennan's Forge to reclaim the precision contracts his father built the business on, to be recognized again as the premier smithy in [[Ashcrag]] instead of Myra's second-rate competitor. He wants his name to mean something before he dies.

What he needs is to recognize that funding Orrin Pike's coalition is playing with fire. If the coalition succeeds and Myra falls, the guild structure collapses, and Hal will not be able to control what replaces it. If the coalition fails and his funding is exposed, Myra will expel him from the council and destroy his reputation. He is gambling everything on a strategy he does not fully control, and he is too proud and too tired to admit it might be a mistake.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Hal is the council's swing vote and its potential fracture point. If PCs help the [[Highland Workers Coalition]], Hal may break with Myra publicly and vote to negotiate with [[Orrin Pike]], giving the coalition legitimacy. If PCs side with Myra and expose Hal's funding of the coalition, Myra will expel him from the council, destroying the guild's appearance of unity. If PCs approach Hal directly and offer him a path to restore the guild without empowering the coalition, he will listen, because he is desperate for an alternative.
>
> **Key Secret.** Hal has been funding [[Orrin Pike]]'s organizing for eighteen months, approximately 50 gold total, funneled through intermediaries and charcoal haulers. The money is coming from forge profits, hidden in the ledgers as material costs. If [[Myra Cadros]] audits Brennan's Forge or if one of the intermediaries is caught and interrogated, Hal is exposed. If [[Grett Voss]] (market factor, Myra's ally) investigates coin flows, the trail leads back to Hal. Exposure means expulsion from the council, loss of guild standing, and possible criminal charges (funding sedition).
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the coalition declares a strike, Hal will abstain from the council vote on crackdown, breaking guild solidarity for the first time. If Myra discovers his funding, she will move to expel him, and the council will fracture between Myra's loyalists and those who resent her centralization. If the coalition collapses, Hal will lose his leverage and remain trapped under Myra's dominance for the rest of his career. If the PCs help him navigate a middle path (restoring decentralization without empowering the coalition), he becomes a valuable ally with council influence and guild legitimacy.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Hal speaks slowly, choosing words carefully, pausing between sentences as if weighing every phrase. He does not interrupt, does not raise his voice, and does not make grand gestures. When he disagrees, he says "I see it differently" and lets silence do the work. When he is angry, his jaw tightens and he taps his fingers on the anvil, one slow tap per breath. He makes eye contact but does not hold it long. He is polite to everyone, even Myra, because open hostility is weakness. Sample line: "Myra's done well for the guild. No one can say otherwise. But the guild was stronger when we shared the load. One smith, no matter how capable, cannot carry it all. Eventually, the weight breaks you. Or you break the guild trying."
