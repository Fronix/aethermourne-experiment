# Halden Brack

## Appearance

Halden is a weathered man in his early forties with sun-darkened skin, calloused hands, and the permanent squint of someone who has spent decades on the water. His dark hair is shot through with premature gray and tied back in a short tail. He wears practical fishing clothes even to Assembly meetings: oilskin jacket, canvas trousers, boots crusted with salt. He smells like fish, brine, and tar. He moves with the rolling gait of a sailor, and his hands are never still, always mending a net, tying a knot, or working a piece of cord. He has a scar across his left forearm from a knife that slipped while gutting fish.

## Personality

Halden is blunt, stubborn, and fiercely loyal to his people. He does not have the education or the polish of the other Assembly merchants, and he does not pretend otherwise. He says what he means, does what he promises, and expects others to do the same. When someone lies to him, he does not get angry, he gets cold. He has no tolerance for merchants who play political games while fishermen and barge captains risk their lives on the water. Beneath this, he is deeply insecure. He knows the Brack family is the poorest of the five merchant houses. He knows the others see him as a charity case. He resents it, but he needs the Assembly seat to protect his fleet, so he swallows his pride and shows up.

## Backstory

Halden was born to the Brack fishing family, which has worked Saltmere's waters for three generations. The Bracks have always been small-time: a handful of boats, enough to feed the family and sell surplus at market. Halden's father died in a storm when Halden was twenty, and Halden took over the fleet. He worked hard, borrowed carefully, and built the fleet up to eight boats. Ten years ago, the Merchant Assembly expanded from four houses to five, offering the Bracks a seat to represent the fishing trade. Halden accepted, thinking it would give him a voice. Instead, it has been ten years of being outvoted, dismissed, and patronized by merchants who trade in grain and salt but have never hauled a net.

The formative event: Five years ago, a merchant named Tallow tried to buy out the Brack fleet for a fraction of its value, claiming Halden was too poor to maintain the boats. Halden refused. Tallow spread rumors that Halden was insolvent, trying to drive him to sell. [[Marda Vorren]] intervened, publicly supporting Halden's fleet and voting to extend him favorable mooring fees. Halden has never forgotten that Marda saved him. He trusts her, even when he disagrees with her.

## Relationships

- [[Marda Vorren]] — Halden respects Marda deeply. She is the only Assembly member who treats him like an equal. When she votes against him, he accepts it because he knows she has a reason. He wishes she would push harder for the fishing trade, but he understands she has to balance the interests of the whole town. If she asked him for a favor, he would say yes without hesitation.
- [[Corwin Marsh]] — Halden has heard Corwin's name but has never met him. Corwin is too powerful, too wealthy, too far above the Brack fleet to notice them. Halden resents the merchant houses that borrow from Corwin, because he knows it makes them dependent. He has never borrowed from Corwin and never will.
- [[Venna Cade]] — The harbormaster. Halden has worked with Venna for years. She runs the docks fairly, and he respects that. When Jorin's barge went missing, Halden went to Venna demanding an investigation. She filed a report but has not pushed hard enough for his liking. He suspects she is hiding something, but he does not know what.
- [[Lira Fenn]] — Halden knows Lira by reputation: a barge captain who runs independent, not tied to any merchant house. He respects that. He has offered her mooring space and favorable rates when she is in Saltmere, because he believes independent traders strengthen the port. Lira has been asking questions about the missing barge, and Halden has been helping her quietly.

## What They Want

Halden wants the Merchant Assembly to take the missing barge seriously. He wants an investigation, not just a report. He wants justice for Jorin Hallis and safety for every captain who works the Millrun River. He wants the Assembly to stop treating this as a cost of doing business and start treating it as a threat.

What he needs is to stop fighting alone. He has been the lone voice pushing for the investigation, and it is exhausting. He needs allies, but he does not know how to build them. He is too proud to ask for help and too stubborn to give up.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Halden is the moral anchor of the missing barge mystery. He is the one who will not let it go, the one who keeps pushing the Assembly to act. If the PCs investigate the disappearance, Halden will be one of their first and best allies. He will offer them resources (his boats, his contacts, his knowledge of the river), and he will expect them to follow through. If they succeed, he will be fiercely loyal. If they abandon the investigation, he will see them as just another set of outsiders who do not care about river people.
>
> **Key Secret.** Halden has been quietly funding his own investigation into the missing barge. He has hired a few trusted fishermen to search the riverbanks for debris, and they have found something: a piece of the barge's hull with scorch marks, washed up two miles downriver from Millford. Halden suspects foul play, possibly a fire set deliberately. He has not shared this with the Assembly yet because he does not trust them to act. He is waiting for someone he can trust, the PCs or [[Lira Fenn]], to present the evidence to.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Halden will continue investigating on his own, eventually discovering enough evidence to force the Assembly to act, but it will take months and the trail will be cold. If the PCs ally with him, he will share his findings immediately and provide significant support (boats, crew, local knowledge). If they dismiss him or side with the Assembly's inaction, he will lose faith in outsiders and become isolated, eventually abandoning the investigation in despair.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Halden speaks in short, direct sentences. He does not waste words or soften bad news. When he is frustrated, he clenches his jaw and looks away. When he trusts someone, he makes eye contact and nods once. He does not smile often, but when he does, it transforms his face. Sample line: "Jorin Hallis was a good man. Careful. Knew the river. Barges don't just vanish. Someone knows what happened. Assembly won't push for answers, so I am. You want to help, fine. You want to get in my way, don't."
