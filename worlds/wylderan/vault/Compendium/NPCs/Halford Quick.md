## Appearance

Halford is a compact, barrel-chested man in his mid-fifties with thinning gray hair and a neatly trimmed beard. He dresses better than most in Millford, in wool vests and linen shirts that are clean but not extravagant. His boots are always polished, a point of pride. He walks with a slight limp from an old injury and leans on a carved walking stick, which he uses as much for emphasis as support. His eyes are pale blue and constantly assessing, weighing the value of every person and transaction.

## Personality

Halford is polite, efficient, and entirely mercenary. He treats everyone with professional courtesy, but his loyalty is always for sale. He doesn't apologize for this. The docks are his domain, and control of access to the river is power. He keeps meticulous records, remembers every slight and every favor, and never forgets who owes him. He's not cruel, but he's calculating. He'll help you if you pay, ignore you if you don't, and ruin you if you cross him. He enjoys the small satisfactions of his power: assigning the worst berths to people who've annoyed him, making important traders wait while he "checks the records."

## Backstory

Halford wasn't born in Millford. He came twenty years ago as a barge worker, young and ambitious. When the old dock master, a drunk named Harrow, died in a brawl, Halford stepped in and quietly made himself indispensable. He organized the chaos of the docks, imposed mooring fees, and negotiated with traders and millers. The town council realized they needed him more than they liked him and made the arrangement official. Halford used the position to build a network of small favors, paid "considerations," and controlled access.

The formative event: Fifteen years ago, a merchant tried to bypass Halford's fees by mooring downstream and hauling goods overland. Halford quietly spread word that the merchant was smuggling stolen goods. The merchant's reputation was ruined, and he left Millford broke. The message was clear: cross Halford, pay the price. No one's tried since.

When Halford took the dock master role, he inherited an informal duty that Harrow had neglected: maintaining the small shrine to the Passage Keeper near the Upper Docks. Halford keeps it clean, replaces offerings left by older traders, and leads the annual Ford Blessing festival each spring, though attendance has dwindled over the years. He does this not from faith but from tradition, and because his father was a ferryman who taught him that disrespecting the river brings bad luck. Privately, Halford worries the town's neglect of the old ways is why the floods are worsening and the ford is deepening.

## Relationships

- [[Garrick Thane]] — Garrick pays Halford a yearly "consideration" to prioritize his barges. Halford likes Garrick's straightforwardness: Garrick doesn't haggle or complain, he just pays. It's a comfortable arrangement.
- [[Brenna Cray]] — Brenna can't afford Halford's considerations, so her barges get assigned the worst berths and slower service. Halford's not doing it out of spite, he's just being pragmatic, but Brenna hates him for it. Halford finds her resentment tedious.
- [[Dace Torrn]] — Dace pays his fees and occasionally pays extra to look the other way. Halford appreciates Dace's professionalism. They're not friends, but they have a functional working relationship built on mutual benefit.
- [[Lyman Corvish]] — Corvish, the toll collector, often stops at the docks to inspect cargo. Halford cooperates fully with Corvish's inspections, because angering the crown's representative is bad business. But Halford also tips off traders when Corvish is coming, for a price.

## What They Want

Halford wants stability and control. He wants the docks to keep running smoothly, the fees to keep rolling in, and his position to remain secure. He wants to retire in a few years with enough saved to live comfortably in the lowlands, away from the grinding work of the docks.

What he needs is to realize that his reputation is becoming toxic. People resent him, and resentment builds. If the town ever turns against him, his carefully built power will crumble. He needs to invest in goodwill, not just leverage, but he doesn't know how.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Halford is the gatekeeper. If PCs need river access, information about trade, or a way to avoid [[Lyman Corvish]], they'll deal with Halford. He's not an antagonist, but he's not a friend. He's transactional. If the PCs pay or offer something valuable (information, favors), he'll help. If they try to bypass or threaten him, he'll become an obstacle.
>
> **Key Secret.** Halford has been skimming from the town council's share of the mooring fees for years. It's not a fortune, but it's not nothing. If the council audits the books, he's finished. He's paranoid about this and keeps two sets of ledgers: one real, one for show.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Halford will continue accumulating small fortunes and small resentments until something breaks. A bad harvest, a trade disruption, or a direct challenge from a trader or the council could expose him. If the PCs befriend him, they might convince him to ease up. If they antagonize him, he'll make life difficult in a hundred small, petty ways.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Halford speaks in clipped, formal sentences, always polite but never warm. He taps his walking stick for emphasis when making a point. When he's annoyed, he smiles slightly, which is more unnerving than anger. Sample line: "Of course I can accommodate your barge, friend. The standard fee is eight copper, plus two for overnight mooring. Or we could discuss a more... flexible arrangement. Entirely up to you."
