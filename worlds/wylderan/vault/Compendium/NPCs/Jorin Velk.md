## Appearance

Jorin is a grizzled man in his mid-50s with a face like weathered stone, all hard angles and deep lines. His beard is gray and unkempt, his hair tied back with a leather cord. His hands are thick and scarred from decades of mining, the knuckles swollen from arthritis and old injuries. He wears patched canvas work clothes stained with ore dust and sweat, and a wide-brimmed leather hat to keep the sun and rain off. He carries a prospector's hammer on his belt, worn smooth from use, and a coil of rope over one shoulder. He smells like sweat, iron, and the sharp mineral scent of freshly broken rock. He walks with a slight limp, favoring his left leg from an old rockfall injury, but he moves with stubborn determination, like a man who has walked a hard road and refuses to stop.

## Personality

Jorin is stubborn, independent, and deeply distrustful of authority. He does not believe in guilds, councils, or cartels, he believes in the work you do with your own hands and the claim you stake with your own sweat. He is taciturn, speaking in short sentences or grunts, and he does not waste words on people he does not respect. When he does speak, it is blunt and to the point, no pleasantries, no diplomacy. He values competence, honesty, and self-reliance, and he has no patience for people who complain without acting. Beneath this, he is lonely. He has spent thirty years working alone, trusting no one, and it has hollowed him out. He tells himself he prefers solitude, but the truth is he is afraid of being betrayed again, so he pushes everyone away before they can disappoint him.

## Backstory

Jorin was born in [[Millford]], the son of a failed merchant who lost everything to bad debts. He watched his father beg the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] for extensions, watched him sell the family home and tools, watched him die bitter and broken. Jorin swore he would never owe anyone anything. At eighteen, he left [[Millford]] and went north to the mines, working contract labor for a cartel operation near [[Ashcrag]]. He spent ten years in the pits, saving every coin, trusting no one, and learning the trade. At twenty-eight, he had enough money to buy a prospecting claim outside cartel territory and strike out on his own.

For the last twenty-five years, Jorin has worked the eastern ridgeline above [[Ironhollow]], staking small claims, extracting ore by hand, and hauling it to [[Ashcrag]] to sell through [[Grett Voss]]. He has avoided the cartel system entirely, refusing their contracts, their equipment loans, their company store. He lives in a rough shack on his claim, eats what he hunts or buys in [[Ashcrag]], and trusts no one. Three months ago, he broke into a new vein and discovered the richest iron ore deposit anyone has seen in years. He has kept it quiet, but word spreads, and now [[Caden Wroth]] is circling like a vulture, looking for an excuse to seize the claim.

The formative event: Fifteen years ago, Jorin partnered with another prospector, a man named Hallis, to work a claim together. They split costs, split profits, and Jorin thought he had found someone he could trust. Six months in, Hallis sold Jorin's half of the claim to the cartel behind his back, pocketing the money and leaving Jorin with nothing. Jorin confronted him. Hallis laughed and said it was just business. Jorin broke his jaw. He has worked alone ever since, and he will never trust a partner again.

## Relationships

- [[Grett Voss]] — Buyer and ally. Jorin hauls his ore to [[Ashcrag]] and sells it through Grett, bypassing the cartel entirely. Grett takes a cut but pays fair prices and asks no questions. Jorin trusts Grett more than anyone else, which is to say, not much, but enough to keep doing business. Grett has warned Jorin that [[Caden Wroth]] is looking for a way to seize his claim, and Jorin is preparing for a fight.
- [[Caden Wroth]] — Enemy. Caden wants Jorin's claim, both for the rich vein and to eliminate the example of independence. He has tried intimidation, fabricated claim disputes, and threats. Jorin has refused every offer and ignored every threat. He knows Caden will eventually move to seize the claim by force, and he is preparing to defend it, violently if necessary. He keeps a crossbow in his shack and sleeps with a hammer under his pillow.
- [[Senna Kade]] — Reluctant connection. Senna has approached Jorin multiple times, asking him to support the miners' organizing efforts. Jorin refuses, saying he stays independent by keeping his head down and not causing trouble. Senna sees him as selfish, and Jorin sees her as naive. He respects her courage but thinks organizing miners is a waste of time because the cartel will crush them. He has told her he will not join the strike, but if she needs to hide after it fails, she can use his shack.
- [[Halven Mir]] — Obstacle. Halven refuses to weigh Jorin's ore at the cartel scale house, forcing him to haul it eight miles to [[Ashcrag]]. Jorin sees Halven as a petty bureaucrat enforcing cartel control, and Halven sees Jorin as a disruption to the system. They do not speak. When their paths cross, Halven looks away, and Jorin spits.
- [[Marna Tess]] — Avoided. Jorin refuses to buy from the company store, hauling supplies from [[Ashcrag]] instead. Marna sees this as an insult to the system, and Jorin sees Marna as a collaborator. They do not interact unless forced, and when they do, the conversations are hostile and brief.

## What They Want

Jorin wants to work his claim in peace, extract the ore, sell it on his terms, and owe nothing to anyone. He wants [[Caden Wroth]] and the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] to leave him alone. He wants to prove that independence is possible, that you do not need to bend to the system to survive. He wants to die on his claim, working until his body gives out, and be buried on the land he staked with his own hands.

What he needs is to let someone in. He has spent thirty years trusting no one and pushing everyone away, and it has made him bitter and isolated. He needs to recognize that independence without connection is just loneliness, and that fighting alone makes you vulnerable. He needs to trust [[Senna Kade]] or [[Grett Voss]] or someone, but trust is the one thing he cannot give.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Jorin is the independent operator resisting cartel monopoly, a symbol of what resistance looks like without collective action. PCs investigating cartel operations, defending miners from exploitation, or helping [[Senna Kade]] organize will encounter Jorin as a potential ally or a cautionary tale. If the PCs help him defend his claim, they make an enemy of the cartel. If they do not, Jorin will be driven off or killed, and the cartel tightens its grip. Jorin's arc is a question: can independence survive in a system built to crush it?
>
> **Key Secret.** Jorin has discovered not just iron ore, but traces of meteoric iron in his vein, metal that can be forged into exceptional tools and weapons. [[Myra Cadros]] would pay a fortune for it, and the cartel would kill to control it. Jorin knows the ore is valuable but does not know why. If the PCs investigate and recognize meteoric iron, they could broker a deal between Jorin and Myra that bypasses the cartel entirely, or they could sell the information to [[Caden Wroth]] and doom Jorin.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, [[Caden Wroth]] will fabricate a claim dispute, send enforcers to seize Jorin's land, and Jorin will fight back. He will kill one or two enforcers before being overpowered, arrested, or killed. His claim will be absorbed into cartel operations. If the PCs help him (legal defense, witnesses, or muscle), he could retain his claim and become a recurring ally. If they broker a deal with [[Myra Cadros]], Jorin could bypass the cartel entirely, weakening their monopoly. If they betray him, he will die cursing them as another system that crushes the little man.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Jorin speaks in short, blunt sentences, often just grunts or single words. He does not make eye contact unless sizing someone up for a fight. He spits when annoyed. He does not explain himself or justify his choices. When he does speak at length, it is about mining, rock, and claims, the only things he trusts. Sample line: "You want my claim? Come take it. Bring shovels. You will need them to bury your men. I have worked this rock for twenty-five years. No cartel, no council, no guild. Just me and the stone. It stays that way. Get off my land."
