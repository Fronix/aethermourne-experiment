## Appearance

Kellan is in his early thirties, younger than his brother [[Garrick Thane]] by more than a decade. He has the same dark hair and broad shoulders, but where Garrick stands tall, Kellan slouches. He wears work clothes that are perpetually stained with grain dust and river mud, and he rarely bothers to clean them. His face is often flushed from drinking, and his eyes are bloodshot more often than not. His hands shake slightly in the mornings, a sign of too many nights at The Crossing House. He smells like sweat, cheap ale, and the faint sourness of the mill.

## Personality

Kellan is bitter, self-destructive, and deeply aware of his own failures. He knows he's a disappointment to his brother, to the family name, and to himself. He resents [[Garrick Thane]] for making success look easy and for handing him Southgrain Mill like a test he was never going to pass. At the same time, he resents himself for not being strong enough to walk away. He drinks to numb the shame and works just enough to avoid total collapse. He's not mean or cruel, just hollowed out. When he's sober, he's quiet and evasive. When he's drunk, he talks too much, revealing things he shouldn't.

## Backstory

Kellan grew up in his older brother's shadow. Garrick was the capable one, the one their father groomed to inherit the mill. Kellan was an afterthought, the spare son. He tried to find his own path, worked as a barge hand for a few years, considered leaving Millford entirely. But when Garrick bought Southgrain Mill and offered to let Kellan run it, Kellan saw it as a chance to prove himself. He moved into the mill house, took over operations, and immediately struggled. The mill's equipment was old, the flour quality poor, and Kellan didn't have the skills or discipline to fix it. Garrick expected him to figure it out. Kellan started drinking instead.

The formative event: Three years ago, Kellan borrowed money from a lowland trader named Ossian Greave to make repairs to the mill. He gambled that better equipment would improve profits enough to pay back the loan. The repairs helped, but Kellan couldn't stay sober long enough to manage the operation properly. The profits never came. Now Ossian's in Millford demanding payment, and Kellan has nothing. He's asked Garrick for help, and Garrick refused. Kellan doesn't know if he's relieved or devastated.

## Relationships

- [[Garrick Thane]] — Brother. Kellan loves Garrick and hates him in equal measure. He wants Garrick's approval desperately but also wants to prove he doesn't need it. He resents Garrick for setting him up to fail, even though rationally he knows Garrick gave him a real chance. He avoids Garrick when possible.
- [[Brenna Cray]] — Brenna once offered Kellan a job at Kess Mill, half joking. Kellan laughed it off, but he's thought about it more than once. He respects her for standing up to Garrick and wonders what it would be like to work for someone who doesn't expect perfection.
- [[Halford Quick]] — Halford sees Kellan at The Crossing House regularly and has listened to him ramble while drunk. Halford doesn't judge, but he also doesn't intervene. Kellan appreciates the lack of judgment, even if it means Halford has seen him at his worst.

## What They Want

Kellan wants to escape. He wants to be free of Southgrain Mill, free of his brother's expectations, free of the debt, free of Millford. He wants to disappear and start over somewhere no one knows his name. He also wants to matter, to prove he's not a failure, to make Garrick proud. These wants are mutually exclusive, and he knows it.

What he needs is to stop running and make a choice. Either commit to fixing the mill and his life, or walk away cleanly. Right now, he's trapped in the middle, failing slowly while everyone watches. He needs to face Ossian Greave, face Garrick, and decide who he wants to be.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Kellan is the tragic figure, the failure everyone talks about. He's a source of local gossip and a potential quest hook through his debt to Ossian Greave. If the PCs help him (pay the debt, mediate with Garrick, help him leave town), he could become an ally or at least stop being a liability. If they exploit his desperation, he'll do stupid things that make their problems worse.
>
> **Key Secret.** Kellan has been skimming grain from Southgrain Mill's inventory and selling it quietly to pay down his debt. Not enough to be obvious, but enough that if Garrick audited the books, Kellan would be caught. He's terrified of this, but he doesn't know what else to do. Ossian Greave knows about the skimming and is using it as leverage.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Kellan will lose Southgrain Mill to Ossian Greave and probably flee Millford in disgrace. If the PCs intervene (clear his debt, confront Garrick, or help him sabotage the mill's sale), he has a chance to rebuild. If they push him too hard, he might do something reckless, like burn down Garrick's mill or turn informant for someone looking to hurt the Thanes.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Kellan speaks quietly, avoiding eye contact. He mumbles and trails off mid-sentence. When he's drunk, he gets louder and more animated but also more reckless, saying things he regrets immediately. He fidgets constantly, picking at his nails or rubbing his face. Sample line: "Garrick thinks I'm stealing from him. Maybe I am. Maybe that's all I'm good for. He wanted me to run this place? Fine. I'm running it into the ground. At least that's something I can do right."
