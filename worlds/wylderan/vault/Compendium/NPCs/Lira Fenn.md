# Lira Fenn

## Appearance

Lira is a compact woman in her early thirties with short-cropped dark hair, sun-bronzed skin, and forearms roped with muscle from years hauling lines and steering a heavy barge. She dresses in practical river clothes: leather vest, canvas trousers, tall boots, and a wide-brimmed hat to keep the sun off. She wears a brass compass on a leather cord around her neck, a gift from her father. Her hands are scarred from rope burns and splinters, and she moves with the economical grace of someone who has spent half her life on a barge deck. She smells like river water, tar, and woodsmoke.

## Personality

Lira is independent, competitive, and fiercely self-reliant. She trusts her own judgment above anyone else's and will argue her point until she wins or is proven wrong with evidence. She does not back down, does not compromise when she believes she is right, and does not ask for help unless she has exhausted every other option. This makes her an excellent barge captain and a difficult ally. Beneath this, she is lonely. She has spent years building a reputation as someone who does not need anyone, and now she has exactly what she wanted: no partners, no crew, no one to rely on. She tells herself this is freedom. She is starting to suspect it is isolation.

## Backstory

Lira grew up on the river. Her father, Wren Fenn, was a barge captain who ran trade routes between Millford and Saltmere for thirty years. Lira learned the river from him: every bend, every sandbar, every current. When he retired five years ago, he sold her the barge, the *Tidemark*, for half its value and moved inland to a quiet village. Lira took over the routes and ran them solo, hiring temporary crew for heavy cargo but mostly working alone. She has built a reputation as a reliable, fast captain who does not cut corners. She is profitable, but barely. Running a barge solo is expensive and exhausting.

The formative event: Three years ago, Lira and [[Dace Torrn]] were both bidding on a lucrative grain contract from a Millford miller. Lira's bid was lower and her schedule faster. Dace spread a rumor that Lira cut her ropes too thin and overloaded her barge, implying she was reckless. She lost the contract. She has hated Dace ever since. She knows he is a smuggler, and she resents that he gets away with it while she follows every rule and struggles to break even. Their rivalry is personal for her, professional for him, which makes it worse.

## Relationships

- [[Dace Torrn]] — Rival. Lira sees Dace as everything wrong with river trade: he smuggles, he bribes, he plays games, and he still gets contracts she loses. She would never work with him unless forced, and even then, she would watch him constantly. She fantasizes about catching him in a smuggling run and reporting him to the river wardens, but she has never had proof.
- [[Halden Brack]] — Halden has been kind to Lira, offering her favorable mooring rates in Saltmere and recommending her to merchants who need fast transport. She respects him for running an honest operation and for treating independent traders fairly. When Jorin Hallis went missing, Halden asked Lira to help investigate. She agreed, because she liked Jorin and because she owes Halden.
- [[Venna Cade]] — The harbormaster. Lira respects Venna's competence and pays her fees on time. Venna respects Lira's professionalism and does not ask questions about her business. When Jorin went missing, Lira went to Venna for information. Venna was evasive, which made Lira suspicious. She thinks Venna knows more than she is saying, and she is determined to find out what.
- [[Jorin Hallis]] (missing, presumed dead) — Jorin was a mentor to Lira. He was friends with her father and taught Lira advanced navigation techniques when she first started running the *Tidemark*. She respected him deeply. When he disappeared, she took it personally. She has been investigating on her own, asking questions at every dock, checking every barge for debris, following every lead. She will not stop until she finds answers.

## What They Want

Lira wants to find out what happened to Jorin. She wants justice, if someone hurt him, and closure, if it was an accident. She wants to prove that she is capable, that she does not need help, that she can solve this on her own.

What she needs is to let people in. She has been running solo for so long that she has forgotten how to work with others. She needs to trust someone, to build a crew, to stop carrying everything alone. She needs to admit that investigating a missing barge is bigger than one person can handle.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Lira is the mobile investigator. She has been asking questions at every dock between Millford and Saltmere, and she has pieced together more of the timeline than anyone else. If the PCs investigate the missing barge, they will encounter Lira, and she will be a valuable source of information, but also a potential rival. She is protective of her investigation and will not share everything unless the PCs prove they are serious and capable. If they help her, she will become a loyal ally. If they compete with her, she will resent them.
>
> **Key Secret.** Lira has discovered that Jorin's barge was delayed leaving Millford by a full day, but she does not know why. She has been trying to track down the cargo manifest to see if there was additional freight loaded, but she has hit a wall: the records in Millford are incomplete, and [[Venna Cade]] in Saltmere has been stonewalling her. Lira suspects Venna is hiding something, and she is getting desperate enough to consider breaking into Venna's office to find the manifest. She has not done it yet, but she is close.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Lira will continue investigating alone, eventually burning out or getting into trouble (breaking into Venna's office, confronting the wrong person, putting herself in danger). If the PCs ally with her, she will share her findings and become a key partner in solving the mystery. If they dismiss her or compete with her, she will see them as obstacles and may sabotage their investigation to protect her own. If they help her find closure about Jorin, she will let go of some of her independence and start building real connections again.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Lira speaks quickly and with certainty, even when she is unsure. She interrupts when she thinks she has a better answer. When she is wrong, she acknowledges it bluntly and moves on without dwelling. She does not apologize for taking up space. When she trusts someone, she stops interrupting and starts listening. She rarely laughs, but when she does, it is loud and genuine. Sample line: "Jorin didn't just disappear. I've run that route a hundred times. There's no sandbar, no snag, no rapids that could take down an experienced captain. Someone did something. I'm going to find out who, and I'm going to make sure they answer for it. You want to help? Keep up."
