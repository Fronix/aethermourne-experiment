# Lira Venn

## Appearance

Lira is a lean woman in her mid-thirties with the weathered face and scarred hands of someone who has hauled charcoal through the highlands for years. Her dark hair is streaked with gray and tied back in a tight braid, and her eyes are hard, cold, the eyes of someone who has lost everything and expects to lose more. She wears patched canvas work clothes, always stained with charcoal dust, and a wide leather belt with hooks for rope and tools. She smells like woodsmoke, sweat, and the sharp mineral tang of charcoal. She walks with a limp, favoring her right leg from an old injury, and she moves slowly, deliberately, as if every step costs her something.

## Personality

Lira is bitter, cautious, and utterly pragmatic. She does not believe the [[Highland Workers Coalition]] will succeed, she has seen too many people crushed by the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] and the [[Forge Council]] to believe resistance is possible. But she wants the system to burn, because it killed her husband and left her with nothing, and if [[Senna Kade]] and [[Orrin Pike]] can light the fire, she will carry the kindling. She will not join openly, will not sign petitions, will not stand on the front lines, but she will carry messages, because facilitating someone else's fight is safer than fighting herself. Beneath this, she is deeply lonely. She has spent five years working alone, trusting no one, and it has hollowed her out. She tells herself she prefers solitude, but the truth is she is afraid of caring about anyone again, because everyone she cares about dies.

## Backstory

Lira was born in [[Ashcrag]], the daughter of a tenant farmer who worked land owned by [[Aldric Harth]]'s family. She married young, to a miner named Jorik who worked the pits outside [[Ironhollow]]. They spent ten years hauling charcoal together, working the route between the timber yards south of [[Ashcrag]] and the forges in town, barely scraping by. Jorik was careful, methodical, and safe. Five years ago, he was working a contract in a new pit when a roof collapse buried him and three other miners. The [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] paid for the ore that was lost in the collapse but did not pay for the burials. Lira dug the grave herself.

She inherited Jorik's charcoal hauling contracts, because the cartel does not care who pulls the wagon as long as the charcoal arrives on time. She has spent the last five years working the route alone, sleeping in her wagon, eating what she can afford, and hating everything. Eighteen months ago, [[Senna Kade]] approached her at the [[Halfway Burn Site]] and asked if she would carry messages between [[Ironhollow]] and [[Ashcrag]]. Lira refused to join the coalition, refused to sign anything, refused to commit, but she said she would carry messages if no one asked her why. Senna agreed. Lira has been running messages ever since, hidden in charcoal sacks and supply crates.

The formative event: Six months after Jorik died, Lira went to [[Caden Wroth]] and asked if the cartel would pay death benefits for miners killed in collapses. Caden told her the contract was for ore extraction, not life insurance, and that if miners wanted safety, they should work harder and dig more carefully. Lira left without another word, and she has never spoken to Caden again. That was the moment she decided that if someone ever tried to burn the cartel to the ground, she would help them.

## Relationships

- [[Senna Kade]] — Ally by circumstance. Lira respects Senna's determination but thinks she is naive, that the coalition will fail and everyone involved will die. She carries messages for Senna because she wants the cartel to suffer, not because she believes the coalition will win. Senna values Lira's access to cartel supply routes but worries that Lira's bitterness will turn to betrayal if the coalition looks like it is losing.
- [[Orrin Pike]] — Distant ally. Lira has met Orrin a handful of times at the [[Halfway Burn Site]], and she sees him as well-meaning but reckless. She thinks organizing farmers is even more futile than organizing miners, because farmers have more to lose and less to gain. She carries messages for him because Senna asked her to, not because she trusts him.
- [[Kael Torrn]] — Fellow runner. Lira sees Kael as dangerously naive, a kid who does not understand what he is risking. She has warned him multiple times to be more careful, to stop trusting people so easily, to assume everyone is watching. He thinks she is too cynical. She thinks he will be dead within a year.
- [[Caden Wroth]] — Enemy. Lira hates Caden with a cold, quiet fury. He is the face of the system that killed her husband and told her it was her fault. She fantasizes about watching him burn, but she is too pragmatic to act on it. If the coalition strikes and Caden is targeted, she will not intervene. If he falls, she will not mourn.
- Jorik Venn (deceased, husband) — The reason Lira is still alive and the reason she is hollow. She carries a small charcoal carving he made for her, a bird in flight, and keeps it in her pocket. When she is alone, she holds it and talks to him, telling him about the coalition, about the messages she carries, about the system that killed him. She does not believe in an afterlife, but talking to him makes the loneliness bearable.

## What They Want

Lira wants the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] to suffer. She wants [[Caden Wroth]] and [[Tallow Breck]] and every cartel factor who treats people like disposable tools to lose everything they have built. She wants the [[Forge Council]] to collapse, the forges to go cold, and the highland economy to grind to a halt, because if the system burns, maybe people will remember that it was built on bodies like Jorik's. She does not care if she survives to see it.

What she needs is to let someone in. She has spent five years alone, trusting no one, and it has made her bitter and reckless. She needs to recognize that helping the coalition is not a suicide mission, it is a choice, and that she still has agency. She needs to trust [[Senna Kade]] or [[Kael Torrn]] or someone, but trust is the one thing she cannot give. She needs to decide whether she wants revenge or redemption, because she cannot have both.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Lira is the coalition's reluctant lifeline, a message runner who refuses to commit but cannot walk away. PCs investigating the coalition, helping it, or hunting it will encounter Lira as a wildcardm someone who could help or hinder depending on circumstances. If the PCs earn her trust (difficult, requires proving they care about workers not causes), she becomes a valuable ally with access to cartel supply routes. If they pressure her or threaten her, she disappears and the coalition loses a critical courier.
>
> **Key Secret.** Lira has been skimming charcoal from her cartel shipments for two years, selling it on the side through [[Grett Voss]] and hiding the coin in her wagon. She justifies it as compensation for Jorik's death, but if the cartel discovers the theft, they will seize her contracts and leave her with nothing. This makes her vulnerable to blackmail and desperate to avoid scrutiny.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Lira will continue carrying messages until the coalition strikes or collapses. If the cartel pressures her (threatens her contracts, accuses her of theft), she will fold and stop helping the coalition, possibly becoming an informant to save herself. If the PCs help her secure her position (clearing her debt, protecting her from scrutiny, or giving her a reason to hope), she could formally join the coalition and become a committed member. If the coalition collapses, she will vanish into the highlands and never speak of it again.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Lira speaks in short, flat sentences, no wasted words, no emotion. She does not make eye contact unless sizing someone up for danger. She spits when annoyed. When she talks about Jorik, her voice softens, just for a moment, before hardening again. She does not explain herself, does not justify her choices, does not ask for help. Sample line: "You want me to carry a message? Fine. You want me to join your coalition, sign your petitions, stand in your picket line? No. I haul charcoal. I keep my head down. I survive. That is all I owe anyone. The message will arrive. Do not ask me why I am doing this. I do not owe you that either."
