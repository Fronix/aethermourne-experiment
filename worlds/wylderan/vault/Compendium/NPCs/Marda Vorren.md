## Appearance

Marda is a sharp-eyed woman in her late fifties with silver-streaked dark hair pulled back in a practical knot. She dresses in well-tailored but understated clothing: dark wool dresses with minimal decoration, a leather satchel always at her side containing ledgers and correspondence. Her hands are ink-stained from years of keeping her own books. She stands straight-backed despite decades of long hours, and her gaze is direct, assessing. She wears no jewelry except a simple iron ring on her left hand, a reminder of her origins. She smells like parchment and lamp oil.

## Personality

Marda is pragmatic, fair-minded, and ruthlessly neutral. As Assembly Speaker, she has made a career of being the tiebreaker, the one who listens to all sides and then makes the decision that serves Saltmere's long-term interests, not individual merchants. This has made her respected but not loved. She does not play favorites, does not take bribes, and does not tolerate merchants who put personal profit above the town's stability. Beneath this, she is ambitious. She built the Vorren trade house from nothing, and she wants Saltmere to expand, to grow beyond river trade into coastal shipping. She sees the future in salt and seafood, not just grain. She is patient, strategic, and willing to wait years for the right opportunity.

## Backstory

Marda was born in Saltmere to a family of dock laborers. No wealth, no connections, no prospects. At sixteen, she started working as a clerk for a merchant named Galway, keeping his ledgers and managing his correspondence. She learned the trade by watching, listening, and asking questions. When Galway died without heirs, he left her a small sum in his will, not enough to retire but enough to start. She used it to buy a partnership in a failing salt trade route, turned it profitable, and built the Vorren merchant house over thirty years. She was elected to the Merchant Assembly fifteen years ago and became Speaker ten years ago when the previous Speaker retired. She has been re-elected every term since.

The formative event: Twenty years ago, Marda watched a merchant named Cray lose his mill to debt after refusing help from [[Corwin Marsh]]. Cray's pride destroyed him. Marda learned that pride was a luxury she could not afford. When Corwin offered her capital to expand Vorren trade, she accepted his terms without hesitation. She owes him nothing now, she paid off the debt years ago, but she remembers. She knows Corwin could have crushed her the way he crushed Cray. She respects his patience and his ruthlessness in equal measure.

## Relationships

- [[Corwin Marsh]] — Marda and Corwin have a professional, mutual respect. She borrowed from him once, paid him back, and has maintained a courteous distance since. She knows he is the most powerful merchant in the Riverlands and treats him accordingly: with respect, but not deference. She will not oppose him unless he threatens Saltmere's stability.
- [[Ossian Greave]] — Ossian is a minor merchant in Marda's sphere, barely worth her attention. She views him as reckless and short-sighted. If he continues to make a spectacle of himself in Millford, she will have the Assembly formally censure him. She has no patience for merchants who damage Saltmere's reputation.
- [[Halden Brack]] — Halden is the poorest of the five Assembly merchant houses, and Marda has voted in his favor more than once to keep him afloat. She respects his honesty and his work ethic, but she is frustrated that he refuses to diversify. He clings to the fishing trade when Saltmere's future is in coastal expansion. She wants him to succeed, but she will not wait forever.
- [[Venna Cade]] — The harbormaster. Marda and Venna have a professional, efficient relationship. Marda trusts Venna to run the docks competently and does not micromanage. Venna appreciates this. When the missing barge disappeared, Marda asked Venna for a report and accepted her assessment without question. She does not know Venna is hiding information.

## What They Want

Marda wants to see Saltmere expand beyond river trade. She wants the town to build a coastal port, to compete with larger markets downriver, to become a hub for salt, seafood, and ocean goods. She wants the Vorren house to be remembered as the one that made Saltmere a real power.

What she needs is to let someone else take over. She has been Speaker for ten years, and the job is exhausting. She needs to trust the next generation to carry the work forward, but she does not believe anyone on the Assembly is ready. She is trapped by her own competence.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Marda is the political anchor of Saltmere. If the PCs come to town investigating trade, the missing barge, or grain shortages, they will eventually need to deal with the Merchant Assembly, and that means dealing with Marda. She is not an antagonist, but she is cautious. She will not support anything that destabilizes Saltmere's economy or reputation. If the PCs can prove their investigation serves Saltmere's interests, she will become a powerful ally. If they threaten the town's stability, she will become an obstacle.
>
> **Key Secret.** Marda has been quietly lobbying the crown for a charter to build a coastal port. She has not told the Assembly yet because she does not have the votes. [[Corwin Marsh]] supports the idea because it would expand his grain export routes. [[Halden Brack]] opposes it because it would shift resources away from the river trade that sustains him. Marda is waiting for the right moment to bring the proposal to a vote, but the missing barge has everyone on edge. She needs stability before she can push for expansion.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Marda will continue to consolidate power and push for coastal expansion, eventually winning the charter and reshaping Saltmere's economy. If the PCs destabilize the town (expose corruption, cause merchant houses to collapse, or disrupt trade), she will prioritize stability over expansion and become more conservative. If they help her solve the missing barge mystery and restore confidence, she will move forward with the port charter and reward them with trade access and political support.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Marda speaks in measured, deliberate sentences. She does not interrupt and expects the same courtesy. She takes notes during conversations, writing down names and details. When she makes a decision, she states it clearly and does not revisit it unless new information emerges. She rarely smiles, but when she does, it is genuine. Sample line: "Saltmere's prosperity is built on trust. Trust between merchants, trust between captains, trust between this Assembly and the people who depend on us. If we cannot find Jorin Hallis's barge, we lose that trust. So we will find it. Agreed?"
