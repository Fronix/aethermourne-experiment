# Marna Tess

## Appearance

Marna is a sharp-faced woman in her late 40s with iron-gray hair pulled back in a severe bun and eyes like flint, hard, cold, and unforgiving. She wears practical canvas dresses in dark colors, always spotless despite the ore dust that coats everything in [[Ironhollow]]. Her hands are ink-stained from keeping ledgers, her nails trimmed short, and she wears a leather apron with deep pockets filled with tally sticks, coins, and keys. She moves with brisk efficiency, no wasted motion, and her expression is set in a permanent scowl of disapproval. She smells like lamp oil, dried goods, and tobacco, which she smokes from a short clay pipe when the store is closed. Her voice is sharp and clipped, every word a transaction, every sentence a judgment.

## Personality

Marna is ruthless, unsympathetic, and utterly pragmatic. She does not see miners as people, she sees them as accounts: debts owed, credit extended, balances due. When a miner begs for lower prices or more credit, she does not argue or explain, she simply states the terms and waits for compliance. She believes the world is divided into those who produce value and those who consume it, and miners, in her view, produce barely enough to justify their existence. She has no patience for complaints, excuses, or sentiment. The company store is not charity, it is business, and if miners cannot afford to eat, they should work harder or die faster. Beneath this, she is deeply bitter. She has spent twenty years extracting profit from desperate people, and it has corroded her soul, but she tells herself she is doing her job, and that sentiment is weakness.

## Backstory

Marna was born in [[Ashcrag]], the daughter of a failed grain farmer who lost his land to debt. Her father drank himself to death when she was twelve, and her mother worked as a laundress until she died of exhaustion when Marna was sixteen. Marna was left with nothing, no family, no land, no prospects. She went to work for a cartel warehouse in [[Ashcrag]], sorting grain shipments and tracking inventory. She was good at it, meticulous and efficient, and the cartel noticed. At twenty-four, she was offered a position running the company store in [[Ironhollow]]. She accepted because the pay was better than anything she could get in [[Ashcrag]], and because no one in [[Ironhollow]] knew her past.

For the last twenty-three years, Marna has run the store, selling food, tools, and supplies to miners at inflated prices on credit. She tracks every debt in her ledger, and she has never forgiven a balance, not once. She tells herself she is providing a service, that without the store, miners would starve, but the truth is she is extracting profit from people who have no other options. She has watched miners work themselves to death trying to clear debts that only grow. She has watched families beg for credit when they have nothing left. She feels nothing. The ledger is all that matters.

The formative event: Eighteen years ago, a miner named Torven, a man in his thirties with a wife and two young children, came to Marna and begged for credit to buy food. He was behind on his debt, and Marna had cut him off. He explained that his children were hungry, that he just needed a few days to catch up on his quota. Marna refused, citing company policy. Torven's youngest child, a girl of five, died of starvation two weeks later. When Marna heard, she felt a flicker of guilt, quickly suppressed. She told herself it was not her fault, that the system was not her responsibility, that she was just doing her job. She has told herself that lie every day since.

## Relationships

- [[Caden Wroth]] — Employer and ally. Marna works for Caden, tracking miner debt through the store while Caden enforces quotas and collects on balances. They compare ledgers regularly to ensure no miner escapes the system. Marna respects Caden's efficiency and his willingness to use force when necessary. They are the two faces of cartel control: economic and physical. Marna does not like Caden, she finds him crude, but she trusts him to protect her from miner retribution.
- [[Halven Mir]] — Colleague. Halven tracks ore production while Marna tracks consumption. They compare ledgers to ensure miners stay trapped in debt. Marna appreciates Halven's precision and his lack of sentimentality. They understand each other: the ledger is the only truth, and the people behind the numbers are irrelevant. They have a professional respect but no personal connection.
- [[Senna Kade]] — Threat. Marna knows Senna is organizing miners to strike, and she sees it as a threat to the system that employs her. If miners stop working, the store becomes worthless, and Marna loses her position. She has reported Senna's activities to Caden and suggested that the cartel make an example of her before the strike can materialize. Marna does not hate Senna, she sees her as a problem that needs to be removed, like a rat in the storeroom.
- [[Jorin Velk]] — Avoided. Jorin refuses to buy from the company store, hauling supplies from [[Ashcrag]] instead. Marna sees this as an insult and a disruption to the system. Every purchase Jorin makes elsewhere is profit the cartel loses. She has lobbied Caden to seize Jorin's claim, not for the ore, but to force him back into the company system where he belongs.
- [[Tallow Breck]] — Distant superior. Marna reports to Breck quarterly, sending ledgers of store sales, credit extended, and debt accumulated. Breck trusts her to maximize profit and minimize waste. Marna values this trust and works to exceed his expectations, because the cartel is the only stability she has, and losing her position would mean losing everything.

## What They Want

Marna wants the company store to run efficiently, miners to buy on credit, debts to accumulate, and profits to flow to the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]]. She wants no disruptions, no strikes, no challenges to the system. She wants [[Senna Kade]]'s organizing crushed, [[Jorin Velk]] forced back into the company system, and every miner in [[Ironhollow]] trapped in debt for life. She wants to keep her position, her security, and the order that gives her life meaning.

What she needs is to confront the fact that she is complicit in a system that destroys lives, and that her efficiency is cruelty. She tells herself she is just doing her job, that the cartel would replace her if she did not, but the truth is she chooses to do this work, and she could choose to stop. She needs to acknowledge her guilt, but guilt would mean confronting twenty years of suffering she enabled, and she is too afraid to face that.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Marna is the economic face of cartel oppression, the company store as weapon. PCs investigating miner debt, cartel operations, or [[Senna Kade]]'s organizing will encounter Marna as an obstacle. She is not a fighter, she is an administrator who uses credit and debt to enforce control. If the PCs confront her, she will cite company policy. If they threaten her, she will call [[Caden Wroth]]. If they try to bribe her, she will consider it, but only if the price is high enough to offset the risk.
>
> **Key Secret.** Marna has been embezzling from the company store for ten years, skimming coin from daily sales and hiding it in a lockbox buried beneath her quarters. She justifies it as compensation for her loyalty and hard work, but if [[Caden Wroth]] or [[Tallow Breck]] discover the theft, she will be ruined or killed. This makes her vulnerable to blackmail and desperate to avoid external scrutiny.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Marna will continue running the store, tracking debts, and enabling cartel exploitation. If [[Senna Kade]]'s strike succeeds and the system collapses, Marna will lose her position and her security, and she will spiral into desperation, possibly turning on the cartel to save herself. If the PCs expose her embezzlement, she could become an informant, providing evidence of cartel operations in exchange for protection. If the PCs simply destroy her, she will die cursing them and insisting she was just doing her job.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Marna speaks in short, transactional sentences, always stating prices and terms. She does not engage in small talk, does not offer sympathy, does not explain herself. When challenged, she cites company policy and waits for compliance. She never apologizes, never negotiates, never shows weakness. Sample line: "Flour is eight silver per sack. Lamp oil is three silver per bottle. Explosives are twelve silver per case. All purchases on credit. Your current balance is two hundred forty-seven silver. If you cannot pay, you do not buy. Next customer."
