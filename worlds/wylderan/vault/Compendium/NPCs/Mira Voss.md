# Mira Voss

## Appearance

Mira is a slight woman in her early forties with the weathered face and thin frame of someone who has worked long hours for little pay. Her dark hair is streaked with gray and tied back in a simple bun, and her eyes are sharp, watchful, always scanning. She wears plain homespun clothing, practical and nondescript, the kind of outfit that helps her blend into markets and grain warehouses without notice. She smells like wheat dust, sweat, and the faint scent of ink from the ledgers she sorts. She moves quietly, deliberately, as if every step is calculated to avoid attention. She is the kind of person who stands in the corner of a room and no one remembers she was there.

## Personality

Mira is quiet, cautious, and utterly invisible. She works as a grain sorter at the [[Ashcrag]] market, reporting to [[Grett Voss]], and she uses her position to gather intelligence for [[Orrin Pike]]. She hears council meetings through walls, reads ledgers when no one is watching, and tracks which farmers are being pressured, which merchants are under investigation, and which council members are making deals. She is the coalition's eyes, and she knows that if she is exposed, the coalition loses its information advantage and she loses everything. Beneath this, she is terrified. She has spent two years balancing a knife edge, pretending to be a loyal council employee while feeding intelligence to the coalition, and the strain is wearing her down. She does not trust anyone, not even [[Orrin Pike]], because trust requires vulnerability and vulnerability means death.

## Backstory

Mira was born in [[Ashcrag]], the daughter of a potter who died when she was sixteen. She married young, to a tenant farmer named Corlen who worked land owned by [[Aldric Harth]]. They had no children, which Mira considered a blessing because children meant more mouths to feed on a tenant's income. Corlen died eight years ago, crushed by a wagon when a grain shipment broke loose during loading. The [[Forge Council]] investigated, ruled it an accident, and paid no compensation. Mira was left with nothing: no land, no savings, no family.

She went to work sorting grain at the market, employed by [[Grett Voss]]. She spent six years keeping her head down, sorting shipments, recording weights, and saying nothing. Three years ago, [[Orrin Pike]] approached her at the market and asked if she would help organize tenant farmers. Mira refused, said she was not political, that she just wanted to survive. Orrin came back a month later and asked if she would just listen, report what she heard in the market and at council meetings. Mira said yes, not because she believed in the coalition, but because the council let her husband die and called it an accident, and she wanted them to suffer for it.

She has been feeding intelligence to [[Orrin Pike]] for two years, quietly, carefully, never writing anything down, never speaking about it outside their meetings at the [[Halfway Burn Site]]. She has kept her cover intact, and [[Grett Voss]] trusts her because she is competent, quiet, and invisible. But the strain of living two lives is breaking her, and she knows that if [[Halden Cray]] says her name in a tavern or if [[Myra Cadros]] audits the market ledgers, she will be exposed.

The formative event: Two years ago, Mira overheard [[Myra Cadros]] discussing a foreclosure plan with [[Aldric Harth]] in the market office. They were planning to seize land from three tenant farmers who were behind on rent, consolidate it under Harth's estate, and sell it to the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]]. Mira reported this to [[Orrin Pike]], and he warned the farmers, who paid their rent early by borrowing from neighbors. The foreclosure was canceled. Mira realized in that moment that information was power, and that she could hurt the council by simply listening. She has been listening ever since.

## Relationships

- [[Orrin Pike]] — Leader and the person she reports to. Mira respects Orrin's determination but thinks he trusts people too easily. She feeds him intelligence at their monthly meetings at the [[Halfway Burn Site]], and she has saved the coalition from exposure multiple times by warning him when the council is planning crackdowns. Orrin values her as the coalition's most critical asset, but he does not fully understand how dangerous her position is, because he has never had to maintain a cover identity.
- [[Grett Voss]] — Employer and unwitting cover. Mira works for Grett, sorting grain and recording market transactions. Grett trusts her because she is competent and quiet, and has no idea she is feeding information to [[Orrin Pike]]. Mira is terrified that Grett will eventually realize she has been reading ledgers she should not have access to, but so far, Grett has not questioned her. Mira does not know if Grett suspects and is letting her continue, or if Grett is genuinely oblivious.
- [[Halden Cray]] — Colleague and liability. Mira is terrified of Halden. He is loud, reckless, and says too much in taverns. She has asked [[Orrin Pike]] to keep Halden away from her, because if Halden mentions her name in the wrong place, her cover is blown. Halden does not understand why she avoids him, and thinks she is just shy.
- [[Myra Cadros]] — Enemy and surveillance target. Mira has never spoken to Myra directly, but she monitors her movements, reads her correspondence when it passes through the market office, and reports her plans to [[Orrin Pike]]. Myra does not know Mira exists, which is exactly how Mira wants it. If Myra discovers Mira is a coalition informant, Mira will be arrested, and the coalition will lose its intelligence network.
- Corlen Voss (deceased, husband) — The reason Mira is fighting. The council let him die and called it an accident, and she has never forgiven them. She carries a small copper ring he made for her, worn on a cord around her neck, hidden under her shirt. When she is scared, she touches it and reminds herself why she is doing this.

## What They Want

Mira wants the [[Forge Council]] to suffer. She wants [[Myra Cadros]] and [[Aldric Harth]] and every council member who prioritizes profit over people's lives to lose their positions, their wealth, and their power. She wants [[Orrin Pike]]'s coalition to win, not because she believes in abstract ideals like justice, but because the council let her husband die and she wants revenge. She wants to survive long enough to see it.

What she needs is to trust someone. She has spent two years living a double life, pretending to be a loyal council employee while feeding intelligence to the coalition, and the isolation is destroying her. She needs to tell someone the truth, to admit how scared she is, to ask for help. But asking for help means exposing vulnerability, and vulnerability means exposure, and exposure means death. She is trapped by her own caution.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Mira is the coalition's intelligence network, the source of critical information about council plans, foreclosures, and crackdowns. PCs investigating the coalition, helping it, or hunting it will encounter Mira as a ghost, someone referenced but rarely seen. If the PCs discover her identity and protect her, she becomes an invaluable ally with access to council secrets. If they expose her, intentionally or accidentally, she is arrested, and the coalition loses its eyes. If they pressure her to take risks, she will refuse, because survival is her first priority.
>
> **Key Secret.** Mira has been copying council ledgers for two years, smuggling them out of the market office page by page, and hiding them in a lockbox buried under her cabin floor. The ledgers document foreclosures, price manipulations, and backroom deals between [[Myra Cadros]], [[Aldric Harth]], and [[Tallow Breck]]. If exposed, the ledgers could destroy the council's legitimacy. But if the council discovers them, Mira will be arrested for theft and espionage, and the coalition will be exposed as having insider intelligence.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If Mira is exposed, she will be arrested by the [[Forge Council]], interrogated, and forced to give up coalition names or face imprisonment. Under pressure, she will break and reveal [[Orrin Pike]], [[Halden Cray]], and the entire [[Ashcrag]] cell. If the PCs help her maintain her cover (providing false trails, distracting council audits, or extracting her before exposure), she survives and continues feeding intelligence. If the coalition collapses, Mira will destroy the ledgers, burn her notes, and disappear, leaving [[Ashcrag]] forever.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Mira speaks softly, in short sentences, and only when necessary. She does not make eye contact unless checking if someone is watching her. She pauses frequently, listening to the environment, tracking who is nearby. When she delivers intelligence, she states facts with no emotion, no commentary, no opinion. When she is scared, she touches the copper ring under her shirt. Sample line: "The council is planning a foreclosure sweep next month. Three farms, all tenants behind on rent. [[Aldric Harth]] is buying the consolidated land. [[Myra Cadros]] signed off two days ago. You have three weeks to warn them, get them current on payments, or find another way to block it. I cannot help beyond this. If I am caught reading those ledgers, I am dead. Do not ask me for more."
