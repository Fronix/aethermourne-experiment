## Appearance

Miren is a lean woman in her late thirties with dark hair streaked with early gray and eyes that have not stopped looking for someone who is not coming back. She wears mourning clothes, a black dress and shawl, not because she has accepted Jorin's death but because the community expects it. Her hands are rough from work, and she moves with a determined, restless energy. She has not slept well in three weeks, and it shows: dark circles under her eyes, a tightness around her mouth. She smells like lavender and grief.

## Personality

Miren is relentless, desperate, and angry. She refuses to accept that Jorin is gone without answers. She does not grieve quietly. She demands answers from anyone who might have them: the harbormaster, the river wardens, other barge captains, merchants who knew Jorin. She is not subtle or polite. She interrupts conversations, shows up uninvited, follows leads that go nowhere. People are starting to avoid her, which makes her angrier. Beneath this, she is terrified. If Jorin is dead, she is left with two young children, no income, and debts she cannot pay. She cannot afford to mourn. She needs answers, and she needs them now.

## Backstory

Miren grew up in Saltmere, the daughter of a seamstress. She married Jorin Hallis fifteen years ago, when he was a young barge captain building his trade. They built a life together: two children, a small house near the docks, steady income from Jorin's regular runs up and down the Millrun River. It was not wealth, but it was stable. Jorin was careful, reliable, professional. He knew the river better than anyone. Three weeks ago, he left on a routine run to Millford, carrying trade goods upstream and planning to bring grain back down. He never returned. No one has seen his barge, his cargo, or him.

The formative event: Two weeks ago, Miren went to the river wardens and demanded they search for Jorin. They told her that barges go missing sometimes, that the river is dangerous, that Jorin probably hit a sandbar or a snag and sank. They said they would keep an eye out. She realized they were not going to help. She realized no one was going to help unless she made them. She has been investigating on her own ever since, piecing together Jorin's last known movements, talking to everyone who saw him leave Millford, asking questions that make people uncomfortable.

## Relationships

- [[Jorin Hallis]] (missing, presumed dead) — Her husband. Miren loved him, trusted him, built her life around him. She cannot accept that he is gone without knowing why. If she stops searching, she has to face the grief, and she cannot do that yet.
- [[Venna Cade]] — The harbormaster. Miren goes to Venna's office every few days demanding updates. Venna has been patient, but Miren can tell she is hiding something. Miren does not know what, but she is determined to find out. She respects Venna's competence but resents her lack of answers.
- [[Dace Torrn]] — Miren knows Dace by reputation: a river trader who knew Jorin. She has tracked Dace down once, asking if he heard anything about Jorin's disappearance. Dace was evasive, which made her suspicious. She plans to find him again and press harder. She does not trust him, but she thinks he knows more than he is saying.
- [[Corwin Marsh]] — Miren has heard Corwin's name in connection with grain trade and insurance. She suspects that if Jorin was carrying insured cargo, Corwin might be involved. She has not approached him yet because she does not know how to get past his gatekeepers, but she is gathering information. She sees him as either a potential source of answers or a potential enemy.

## What They Want

Miren wants to find out what happened to Jorin. She wants his body, if he is dead, so she can bury him. She wants justice, if someone killed him. She wants closure, even if it destroys her.

What she needs is to let other people help her. She is burning through every relationship she has, alienating allies, exhausting herself. She needs to trust someone, but she does not know who.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Miren is the emotional anchor of the missing barge mystery. She is the one who will not let it go, the one who reminds everyone that a real person is missing. If the PCs investigate the disappearance, they will encounter her, and she will be a relentless source of information and pressure. She is not a quest-giver, she is a fellow investigator who will follow the PCs, demand updates, and push them to keep going when they might otherwise give up.
>
> **Key Secret.** Miren has found evidence that Jorin's barge was delayed leaving Millford by a day. She does not know why yet, but she has a scrap of paper, a revised shipping manifest with a note in Jorin's handwriting: "Delayed one day, additional cargo from Tallow." She does not know who Tallow is, but she is trying to find out. If the PCs help her trace this lead, it will connect to Venna Cade's bribe and Tallow's involvement.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Miren will investigate until she burns out, alienates everyone, and eventually gives up in despair. If the PCs help her, she will be fiercely loyal and grateful, offering her home as a safe house, her knowledge of the docks as information, and her determination as an asset. If they discover that Jorin is dead and give her closure, she will mourn but survive. If they abandon her or lie to her, she will become an enemy, spreading word that they are not to be trusted.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Miren speaks quickly, urgently, as if every moment wasted is a moment Jorin slips further away. She makes intense eye contact and leans in close when she talks. She does not take no for an answer. When she is frustrated, she clenches her fists and her voice shakes. Sample line: "You don't understand. Jorin knew that river. He knew every current, every sandbar, every crossing. He wouldn't just disappear. Someone knows what happened. Someone saw something. I need you to help me find them, because no one else will, and I can't do this alone."
