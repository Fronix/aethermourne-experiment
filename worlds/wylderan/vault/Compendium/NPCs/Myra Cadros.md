# Myra Cadros

## Appearance

Myra is a broad-shouldered woman in her early fifties with iron-gray hair cropped short for practical reasons, long hair is a liability around forge fires. Her hands are permanently stained with soot and crisscrossed with burn scars from decades of working hot metal. She wears a leather apron over homespun wool, practical clothing that can withstand sparks and heat. Her face is lined from years of squinting into forge light, and her forearms are roped with muscle from hammer work. She moves with deliberate efficiency, every gesture purposeful, no wasted motion. She smells like charcoal smoke, hot iron, and the sharp tang of quenching water.

## Personality

Myra is pragmatic, decisive, and convinced she knows what's best for Ashcrag. She does not suffer fools, waste time on sentiment, or tolerate inefficiency. When someone brings her a problem, she listens once, makes a decision, and expects it to be followed. She is fair in her own estimation, she pays her workers on time, honors contracts, and expects the same from others, but she has no patience for complaints she sees as shortsighted or self-serving. Beneath this, she is deeply insecure about the fragility of Ashcrag's economy. She knows the town depends on the forge trade, and she knows one bad season, one winter that runs too long, one creditor calling debts at the wrong time, could collapse everything. She responds to that fear by tightening control, not loosening it.

## Backstory

Myra was born in Ashcrag, the daughter of a journeyman smith who worked in her grandfather's forge. She learned the trade young, starting with bellows work at age ten and moving to the anvil by fifteen. Her father died when she was twenty, crushed by a falling beam during a roof repair. Her grandfather, Old Cadros, trained her for another decade before handing her the forge. She took over Cadros Ironworks thirty years ago and has expanded it from a single workshop to the largest forge in the highlands, employing twelve smiths and controlling most of the tool contracts with [[Millford]] and the lowlands.

The formative event: Twenty years ago, a harsh winter closed the southern road for five months. Ashcrag ran out of imported salt, and half the town's grain stores spoiled. Families starved. The Forge Council, newly formed and weak, could not coordinate a response. Myra took control, organizing rationing, negotiating emergency barge runs when the ice broke, and restructuring the council to prevent another disaster. She has led the council ever since. That winter taught her that survival requires central control, and she has never let go of that lesson.

## Relationships

- [[Aldric Harth]] — Fellow council member, grain lord. Myra respects his land holdings but sees him as weak, too indebted to lowland creditors to make independent decisions. She tolerates him because the council needs farmer representation, but she does not trust him to put Ashcrag's interests above his own debts.
- [[Orrin Pike]] — Labor organizer, charcoal producer. Myra sees Pike as a troublemaker stirring up tenant farmers with unrealistic demands. She believes giving Pike a council seat would destabilize the guild structure that keeps Ashcrag functional. She has refused his petitions repeatedly and is preparing to break his coalition if he pushes harder.
- [[Grett Voss]] — Market factor. Myra relies on Voss to keep the grain market running smoothly and collect the fees that fund council operations. She trusts Voss's neutrality and competence. When Voss pushes for early stockpiling, Myra listens but balks at the cost, a decision she may regret.
- [[Tallow Breck]] — Cartel factor. Myra has borrowed from Breck's cartel in the past to expand forge operations. She repaid on time and maintains a professional relationship. She sees the cartel as a necessary evil, they provide capital the highlands cannot generate alone, but she does not like their growing influence over highland trade.
- Hal Brennan (rival smith) — Runs Brennan's Forge, the other master workshop in Ashcrag. Myra and Brennan compete constantly for contracts, apprentices, and council influence. They are civil in public, but Myra would be quietly pleased if Brennan's forge failed.

## What They Want

Myra wants to expand Cadros Ironworks and secure long-term contracts with lowland merchants, cementing Ashcrag's position as the highland's primary tool supplier. She wants the Forge Council to remain stable and guild-dominated, because she believes that structure is the only thing keeping Ashcrag from descending into chaos.

What she needs is to let go of control and trust others to share the burden. She is burning out, running the forge, leading the council, and fighting every challenge to her authority. She needs allies, not subordinates, but she does not know how to build them without feeling like she is losing control.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Myra is the face of entrenched power in the highlands. She is not a villain, she genuinely believes her decisions protect Ashcrag, but her refusal to adapt or share power is creating the conditions for conflict. PCs investigating highland trade, Dace's debts, or [[Corwin Marsh]]'s expansion will encounter her as either an obstacle or an ally, depending on how they approach her.
>
> **Key Secret.** Myra knows the charcoal shortage is worse than she has admitted. The timber families cannot sustain current forge output, and she has been rationing fuel to keep Cadros Ironworks running while letting smaller smithies struggle. If this becomes public, it will shatter her credibility and validate Orrin Pike's accusations that the council prioritizes guild profit over community survival.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Myra will tighten control until something breaks, likely Orrin Pike's farmer coalition erupting into violence or the forge fuel shortage forcing production shutdowns. If the PCs help her find a compromise (giving Pike representation, negotiating better charcoal terms, or diversifying Ashcrag's economy), she could become a powerful ally. If they side with Pike or expose her secrets, she will see them as threats and use council authority to oppose them.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Myra speaks in short, declarative sentences. She does not explain her reasoning unless pressed, and when she does, it is with the tone of someone explaining the obvious to a child. She makes direct eye contact and does not break it first. When frustrated, she crosses her arms and exhales through her nose. She respects competence and despises incompetence. Sample line: "The forge feeds this town. The council keeps the forge running. Pike wants to dismantle that because his kiln work is hard. Everyone's work is hard. That's not a reason to break what works."
