# Orrin Pike

## Appearance

Orrin is a lean man in his early thirties with the wiry build of someone who has worked hard their entire life and never had enough to eat. His hands are scarred from charcoal burns, permanent black stains under the nails, and his face is weathered from long days in the timber yards. He wears rough homespun clothing, patched and re-patched, and a leather apron that smells of smoke and pitch. His dark hair is tied back with a strip of old cloth, and his eyes are sharp, watchful, always assessing. He moves with the careful economy of someone who has learned to conserve energy because every day is exhausting. He smells like woodsmoke, sweat, and charcoal dust.

## Personality

Orrin is angry, determined, and exhausted. He has spent his entire life watching the Forge Council take more and more from people who have less and less, and he is done accepting it. He is not a natural speaker, he stumbles over words, repeats himself, gets flustered when challenged, but his passion is undeniable. When he talks about the council's abuses, his voice shakes with fury. When he talks about the farmers and laborers he represents, his voice softens with genuine care. He is relentless, not because he is confident, but because he does not know how to stop. Beneath this, he is terrified. He knows organizing makes him a target, that [[Myra Cadros]] could crush him if she chose, that he is one bad harvest away from losing everything. But he also knows that if he does not fight, no one else will.

## Backstory

Orrin was born in [[Ashcrag]], the son of a tenant farmer who worked land owned by [[Aldric Harth]]'s father. His family was never wealthy, they paid rent to the landowner, sold their grain through the council's market, and bought tools from the guild forges. Every transaction took a cut, and by the end of each year, there was barely enough to survive. Orrin's father died when Orrin was sixteen, crushed by a falling tree while working a timber contract to pay off debt. Orrin took over the family's charcoal kiln, working the timber yards south of town. He married young, had two children, and spent the next decade struggling to stay afloat.

The formative event: Five years ago, a harsh winter closed the southern road early, and [[Myra Cadros]] tripled the price of charcoal to keep the forges running. Orrin's family, and every other charcoal burner, was forced to sell at the higher price just to afford food. The forges made record profits that year. Orrin's wife, Mara, became sick with fever that winter and died because they could not afford the healer's fee. Orrin buried her and swore that he would never let the council take from his family again without a fight. He started organizing tenant farmers the next spring.

## Relationships

- [[Myra Cadros]] — Council head and enemy. Orrin sees Myra as the embodiment of everything wrong with the highland economy: she prioritizes guild profits over people's survival, refuses to share power, and dismisses grievances as laziness. Myra sees Orrin as a troublemaker undermining the structure that keeps Ashcrag stable. They are on a collision course, and both know it.
- [[Aldric Harth]] — Landowner and council member. Orrin works land that Harth owns and pays rent to Harth's estate. He sees Harth as a hypocrite who claims to represent farmers while living in luxury and siding with the guild on every vote. Orrin has tried to recruit Harth's tenant farmers into the coalition, which Harth sees as a direct threat.
- [[Grett Voss]] — Market factor. Orrin respects Voss's neutrality and has sought their advice on how to structure his petitions. Voss has helped him quietly, suggesting language that makes the petitions harder to dismiss, but Voss will not publicly support him. Orrin understands this but wishes Voss would take a stand.
- [[Tallow Breck]] — Cartel factor. Orrin has no direct dealings with Breck, but he sees the cartel as another parasite bleeding the highlands dry. He knows several farmers who lost their land to cartel foreclosures, and he views Breck as no different from Myra, another person who profits from others' suffering.
- Mara Pike (deceased, wife) — Orrin's wife, who died five years ago during a harsh winter. Her death is the wound that never healed, the reason he organizes. He keeps a small wooden carving she made, a bird in flight, and carries it in his pocket. When he is discouraged, he holds it and reminds himself why he started this fight.

## What They Want

Orrin wants a seat on the Forge Council for tenant farmers and laborers. He wants the council to stop prioritizing guild profits over people's survival. He wants land reform that breaks up estates like Harth's and gives tenant farmers a chance to own the land they work. He wants his children to grow up in a town where hard work is rewarded, not exploited.

What he needs is to recognize that he cannot win this fight alone, and that organizing is not the same as leading. He has gathered forty farmers behind him, but he does not know how to turn that coalition into lasting change. He needs allies with power, strategy, or resources, but he does not trust anyone with power. He needs to learn when to compromise and when to push, but compromise feels like betrayal.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Orrin is the voice of the dispossessed, the people the highland economy depends on but does not value. PCs investigating the highland trade network, debt crises, or class tensions will encounter Orrin as an ally, a quest-giver, or a complication. If the PCs side with the Forge Council, Orrin will see them as enemies. If they side with Orrin, they make powerful enemies in [[Myra Cadros]] and the guild. If they try to mediate, they will need to navigate deep distrust on both sides.
>
> **Key Secret.** Orrin's farmer coalition is more fragile than he admits. Of the forty farmers who signed his petition, only fifteen are committed enough to boycott the spring grain market. The rest will fold if the council pressures them, threatens eviction, or offers concessions. Orrin knows this and is terrified that when the moment comes, he will be standing alone. He is bluffing, hoping the council does not call it.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Orrin will push for a council meeting, the council will refuse his petition, and his coalition will fracture. Some farmers will radicalize and turn to violence (sabotaging forges, burning grain stores). Others will capitulate and return to the system. Orrin will be left isolated, possibly arrested or exiled. If the PCs help him (negotiating with the council, strengthening his coalition, or providing resources), he could win concessions or even a council seat, reshaping Ashcrag's power structure. If they betray him or expose his coalition's weakness, he will be destroyed, and tenant farmers will lose their only advocate.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Orrin speaks quickly when passionate, his words tumbling over each other. He gestures with his hands, leaving soot smudges on whatever he touches. When he is tired, his voice goes flat and quiet. He makes eye contact when he is angry, looks away when he is scared. He does not trust easily, and when someone offers help, he asks why, because in his experience, help always comes with a price. Sample line: "You want to know what it's like? Every year, we work the land, cut the timber, burn the charcoal. Every year, the guild takes more, the rents go up, the market fees go up, the tool prices go up. And every year, we have less. My wife died because I couldn't afford a healer. How many more people have to die before the council cares? Tell me that."
