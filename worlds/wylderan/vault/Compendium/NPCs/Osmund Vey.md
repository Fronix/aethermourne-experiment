# Osmund Vey

## Appearance

Osmund is a weathered man in his early sixties, broad-shouldered and stooped from decades of fieldwork. His face is deeply lined, tanned like leather, with a thick gray beard he keeps short. His hands are gnarled and scarred, the knuckles swollen with old injuries. He wears patched wool and linen, sturdy but threadbare, and heavy boots tied with cord. He moves slowly, with the careful deliberation of someone whose body aches constantly. His voice is gravelly but steady, the kind of voice that carries across a field or commands attention in a room.

## Personality

Osmund is patient, pragmatic, and deeply tired. He's been the voice of the farming community for so long that he's forgotten what it's like to just tend his own land without carrying everyone else's grievances. He's the mediator, the negotiator, the one who talks people down from rash decisions. He believes in community, in working together to survive, but he's also a realist. He knows [[Garrick Thane]] has them over a barrel, and he knows complaining won't change that. He picks his battles carefully and hates that he has to. Beneath the calm, there's a simmering anger at the unfairness of it all, but he's too disciplined to let it show.

## Backstory

Osmund has farmed the same plot of land his entire life, land his father and grandfather worked before him. He married young, had five children, buried two. His wife, Maren, died three years ago from a fever. His remaining children work the land with him, though his eldest son, Tomas, has been talking about leaving for the lowlands. Osmund dreads that conversation.

The formative event: Twenty years ago, when the farmers tried to organize and refuse Garrick Thane's rates, it was chaos. Some farmers held out, others broke ranks, and in the end, the harvest rotted while they bickered. Osmund was the one who convinced them to accept Garrick's terms, arguing that survival mattered more than pride. He's been the farmers' negotiator ever since, but he's never forgotten the look in their eyes when he told them to give in. Some saw him as a traitor. He still wonders if they were right.

## Relationships

- [[Garrick Thane]] — Osmund negotiates with Garrick on behalf of the farmers. It's a grinding, thankless task. Garrick respects Osmund's practicality and Osmund respects Garrick's efficiency, but there's no warmth between them. Osmund knows Garrick holds all the power, and it galls him.
- [[Brenna Cray]] — Osmund was friends with Brenna's father, Aldric. He helped Brenna rebuild her mill with loans and advice, and he tries to send farmers to her when he can. He sees her as the daughter he wishes he had: fierce, determined, unbowed. But he worries she's going to destroy herself fighting Garrick.
- [[Lyman Corvish]] — Osmund has no love for the toll collector, who represents the distant authority that takes taxes and gives nothing back. Corvish treats the farmers with barely concealed contempt, and Osmund returns the feeling.
- [[Dace Torrn]] — Osmund doesn't trust Dace, who he sees as a profiteer and a smuggler. But Osmund's also hired Dace to transport grain when Garrick's barges were full, so he's pragmatic about it.

## What They Want

Osmund wants the farmers to survive and thrive. He wants his son to stay, to work the land, to keep the family rooted here. He wants fair rates from the millers and fewer taxes from the crown. He wants rest, though he'd never admit it.

What he needs is to let go of the burden of being everyone's spokesman. He's been carrying the community's weight for so long that it's breaking him. He needs to trust the younger generation to step up, even if they make different choices than he would.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Osmund is the moral center of Millford's farming community. If PCs investigate social tensions, grain shortages, or conflicts between the farmers and millers, Osmund is the first person they should meet. He's a source of context, history, and grounded perspective. He's also a potential ally if the PCs want to help the farmers organize or resist exploitation.
>
> **Key Secret.** Osmund is dying. He has a wasting illness, probably a cancer, and he knows it. He hasn't told anyone, not even his children. He's trying to ensure the community is stable before he goes, which is why he's been working so hard to keep the peace with Garrick. He doesn't want to die knowing the farmers will fall apart without him.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Osmund will keep negotiating and mediating until his health fails. His death will leave a power vacuum: either Tomas steps up, or the farmers fracture, or someone like Brenna becomes their voice. If the PCs help stabilize the community or find a way to reduce the farmers' dependence on Garrick, Osmund can die knowing he succeeded. If they make things worse, his final months will be consumed by guilt.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Osmund speaks slowly, choosing his words with care. He pauses often, like he's weighing whether something is worth saying. When he's frustrated, he rubs the back of his neck and sighs. He rarely raises his voice, but when he does, people listen. Sample line: "You want to know why we don't fight back? Because we tried. Twenty years ago. The harvest rotted, and we starved. Pride doesn't fill bellies. So we bend, because bending means we live to see spring. That's all."
