# Ossian Greave

## Appearance

Ossian is a wiry man in his early forties with a receding hairline and a patchy beard that never quite fills in. He dresses like someone trying to look more prosperous than he is: a leather coat that is well-maintained but starting to crack at the elbows, boots that are polished but worn thin at the soles. He has nervous energy, always shifting his weight, tapping his fingers, checking over his shoulder. His eyes are pale and bloodshot, and he smells like cheap ale and old sweat. When he smiles, it does not reach his eyes.

## Personality

Ossian is desperate, aggressive, and deeply insecure. He puffs himself up with loud threats and public confrontations because he knows he has no real power. He is the kind of man who shouts because no one listens when he speaks quietly. He resents everyone more successful than him, which is almost everyone. He tells himself he is owed respect, that the world has cheated him, that if people would just pay what they owe, he would be fine. Beneath the bluster, he is terrified. He owes money to people scarier than him, and if he does not collect from [[Kellan Thane]], he will be the one facing consequences. He does not sleep well.

## Backstory

Ossian grew up in Saltmere, the son of a failed trader who drank himself to death. Ossian inherited debts, not wealth. He clawed his way up through small trades, lending at predatory rates to desperate farmers and small merchants. He made enough to survive but never enough to thrive. He operates in the shadow of men like [[Corwin Marsh]], who barely acknowledge his existence. Three years ago, he lent money to Kellan Thane to repair Southgrain Mill, seeing it as his chance to break into the mill trade. Kellan defaulted. Ossian has been bleeding money ever since, paying interest to his own creditors while Kellan makes excuses.

The formative event: Six months ago, Ossian's largest creditor, a highland cartel factor named Tallow Breck, told him that if he did not collect from Kellan within the year, Tallow would take Ossian's boat and trading license. Ossian came to Millford to collect, but Kellan has nothing. Ossian discovered that Kellan has been skimming grain to make partial payments, which means Ossian now has leverage: he can threaten to expose Kellan to [[Garrick Thane]] unless Kellan pays in full. Ossian is using this leverage as hard as he can, but he does not know what to do if it fails.

## Relationships

- [[Kellan Thane]] — Ossian sees Kellan as the reason for all his problems. He loaned Kellan money in good faith and got nothing but excuses. Ossian hates Kellan but also needs him. If Kellan cannot pay, Ossian is ruined. He has been making increasingly public threats at The Crossing House, hoping to shame Kellan into finding money. It is not working.
- [[Garrick Thane]] — Ossian approached Garrick once, asking him to cover Kellan's debt. Garrick refused. Ossian resents Garrick for having the power to solve the problem and choosing not to. He fantasizes about ruining Garrick but knows he cannot touch him.
- [[Corwin Marsh]] — Ossian operates in Corwin's sphere but far below him. Corwin views Ossian as a useful tool for collecting debts that are too small for Corwin's attention. Ossian resents being dismissed but knows Corwin could destroy him. He avoids Corwin when possible.
- [[Halford Quick]] — Dock master in Millford. Ossian has been staying at The Crossing House and pestering Halford for information about Kellan's comings and goings. Halford finds him exhausting but tolerates him because Ossian pays his tab.

## What They Want

Ossian wants to collect the debt and get out of Millford. He wants to return to Saltmere, pay off Tallow, and never have to deal with the Thanes again. He wants to feel secure.

What he needs is to stop chasing money he will never see and cut his losses. He has wasted six months on Kellan when he could have been building other deals. He needs to let go, but his pride and his fear will not let him.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Ossian is the pressure point. He is currently in Millford making a scene, which means everyone in town knows about Kellan's debt. If the PCs investigate the Thane family or the mill economy, they will encounter Ossian. He is not a villain, just a desperate man making bad decisions. He could become an ally if the PCs help him escape his own debts, or he could become a tool if someone with more power (Corwin, Garrick, or the PCs) decides to use him.
>
> **Key Secret.** Ossian's creditor, Tallow, is losing patience. If Ossian does not return with payment in the next two weeks, Tallow will send enforcers. Ossian knows this and is considering desperate measures: forging a debt transfer to make it look like Garrick owes him instead, or even sabotaging Southgrain Mill to force a payout. He has not decided yet, but he is running out of time.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Ossian will either succeed in seizing Southgrain Mill (ruining Kellan and destabilizing Garrick's plans) or give up and flee, leaving his own debts unpaid. If the PCs intervene (pay off Kellan's debt, negotiate with Tallow, or expose Ossian's leverage over Kellan), they can resolve the situation, but Ossian will not be grateful. He will resent them for making him look weak.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Ossian talks too much and too fast, interrupting himself mid-sentence. He gestures wildly when he is agitated, which is most of the time. When he is trying to be threatening, he leans in too close and his voice drops to a hiss. It comes off as pathetic more than intimidating. Sample line: "You think I'm joking? You think I won't do it? I'll tell Garrick everything. I'll have the council seize the mill. I've got rights, legal rights, and if Kellan thinks he can, can just, just ignore me, he's got another thing coming. You tell him that. You tell him Greave's not going away."
