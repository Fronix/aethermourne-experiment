# Senna Kade

|||
|---|---|
| **Title** | Contract Miner, Labor Organizer |
| **Location** | [[Ironhollow]], [[The Stone Heights]] |
| **Faction** | None (organizing resistance) |
| **Status** | alive |

## Appearance

Senna is a wiry woman in her early 30s with the scarred hands and lean frame of someone who has worked the mines since childhood. Her face is weathered beyond her years, lined from squinting in ore dust and cold wind, and a jagged scar runs from her left temple to her jaw, a souvenir from a rock fall three years ago. Her dark hair is cut short and practical, tied back with a strip of cloth. She wears patched canvas work clothes and a leather apron stained with ore dust and sweat. Her eyes are sharp and tired, always watching, always calculating. Her voice is her most striking feature, low and powerful, carrying across the pits and barracks without shouting. When she speaks, people listen, because she sounds like someone who has nothing left to lose and everything to fight for.

## Personality

Senna is angry, determined, and exhausted. She has spent fifteen years watching the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] bleed miners dry, and she is done accepting it. She is not a natural speaker or a strategist, she stumbles over words when nervous, forgets names, repeats herself when passionate, but her conviction is undeniable. When she talks about the cartel's abuses, her voice shakes with fury. When she talks about the miners she is trying to organize, her voice softens with genuine care. She is relentless because she does not know how to stop, and because stopping means her father's death meant nothing. Beneath this, she is terrified. She knows organizing makes her a target, that [[Caden Wroth]] could destroy her at any moment, that the strike might fail and leave everyone worse off. But she also knows that if she does not fight, no one else will.

## Backstory

Senna was born in [[Ashcrag]], the daughter of a charcoal burner named Corvan Kade. Her mother died in childbirth, and Senna grew up in her father's timber camp, learning the trade. Corvan was a careful, quiet man who worked hard and saved his coin. When Senna was sixteen, he borrowed money from the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] to expand his operation, buying a second kiln and hiring workers. A year later, a harsh winter collapsed demand for charcoal, and he could not make payments. The cartel restructured the loan, adding interest and penalties. He spent the next three years working to clear the debt, but it only grew. When Senna was nineteen, Corvan died of lung disease, coughing blood in their shack while the cartel's debt collectors waited outside.

Senna inherited the debt. The cartel offered her a choice: work it off in the mines at [[Ironhollow]], or they would seize everything her father owned and leave her with nothing. She took the contract. That was twelve years ago. She has been mining ever since, working brutal hours in the pits, watching miners die from cave-ins and exhaustion, watching the cartel take everything while giving nothing. Five years ago, she started talking to other miners, quietly, carefully, about what it would take to make the cartel listen. Three years ago, she met [[Orrin Pike]] during a supply run to [[Ashcrag]] and realized tenant farmers and contract miners were fighting the same enemy. Last year, they began planning a coordinated strike.

The formative event: Seven years ago, Senna watched a miner named Tovan, a man in his fifties with a family, collapse from exhaustion in the middle of a shift. He begged [[Caden Wroth]] for a day of rest. Caden refused, citing quota obligations, and told Tovan to get back to work or forfeit his contract. Tovan tried. He made it ten steps before his heart gave out. He died in the pit. The cartel did not pay for a burial. Senna and three other miners dug the grave themselves on their rest day. That was the moment she decided the system had to break.

## Relationships

- [[Orrin Pike]] — Ally and co-conspirator. Senna met Orrin during a supply run to [[Ashcrag]] and immediately recognized a kindred spirit, someone fighting the same fight in a different place. They have been coordinating secretly for two years, planning a joint strike between tenant farmers and contract miners that would cripple both the Forge Council and the cartel. Senna trusts Orrin's passion but worries his coalition is fragile and that when the moment comes, he will be standing alone. She has promised him the miners will strike if the farmers strike, but she is not sure she can deliver.
- [[Caden Wroth]] — Enemy. Caden is the face of everything Senna hates: a man who came from the mines, betrayed his own, and now grinds miners into the stone to enrich himself and the cartel. He has threatened her, sabotaged her organizing efforts, and made it clear that if she pushes too hard, he will make an example of her. Senna is not afraid of him, but she is afraid of what he represents, the system's willingness to use violence to maintain control. She knows that when the strike happens, Caden will come for her first.
- [[Jorin Velk]] — Reluctant ally. Jorin works outside the cartel system, which makes him a symbol of resistance, but he refuses to join Senna's organizing. He says he stays independent by keeping his head down and not causing trouble. Senna respects his stubbornness but sees him as selfish, a man who could help the miners but chooses not to. If Jorin loses his claim to Caden, Senna will see it as proof that independence without solidarity is meaningless.
- [[Marna Tess]] — Obstacle. Marna runs the company store and tracks every miner's debt. Senna sees her as a collaborator, someone who profits from miner suffering and could choose to stop but does not. Marna sees Senna as a troublemaker threatening the stability that keeps everyone fed. They do not speak unless necessary, and when they do, the conversations are tense and hostile.
- Corvan Kade (deceased, father) — The reason Senna fights. Her father was a good man destroyed by a system that treated him as disposable. She carries a small iron chisel he made for her when she was a child, kept in her pocket. When she is discouraged, she holds it and reminds herself why she started this fight. She wants the cartel to pay for what they did to him, but more than that, she wants to make sure no one else loses a parent the way she did.

## What They Want

Senna wants the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] to release contract miners from debt servitude and negotiate fair wages, safe working conditions, and the right to organize. She wants the strike to succeed, the coalition with [[Orrin Pike]] to hold, and the highland economy to reshape itself around justice instead of extraction. She wants her father's death to mean something.

What she needs is to recognize that she cannot win this fight alone, and that organizing is not the same as leading. She has gathered thirty miners behind her, but half of them will fold if [[Caden Wroth]] threatens their families or offers concessions. She needs allies with power, strategy, or resources, but she does not trust anyone with power. She needs to learn when to compromise and when to push, but compromise feels like betrayal.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Senna is the voice of labor resistance in [[Ironhollow]] and the anchor for any storyline involving highland worker organizing. PCs investigating cartel exploitation, helping [[Orrin Pike]]'s coalition, or defending miners from abuse will meet Senna as an ally, quest-giver, or moral compass. If the PCs help her, they make enemies of the cartel. If they betray her, she will see them as another tool of oppression. If they try to mediate, they will need to navigate deep distrust on both sides.
>
> **Key Secret.** Senna's coalition is more fragile than she admits. Of the thirty miners who support the strike, only twelve are committed enough to walk out if [[Caden Wroth]] retaliates. The rest will fold if threatened or offered debt relief. Senna knows this and is terrified that when the strike begins, she will be standing with a handful of miners while the rest go back to work. She is bluffing, hoping the cartel does not call it.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Senna will push for a strike, the cartel will crush it with violence, and her coalition will fracture. Some miners will radicalize and turn to sabotage, others will capitulate and return to the system, and Senna will be arrested, exiled, or killed. If the PCs help her (strengthening her coalition, negotiating with the cartel, or providing external pressure), the strike could succeed, reshaping highland labor dynamics and weakening cartel control. If they betray her or expose her coalition's weakness, she will be destroyed, and contract miners will lose their only advocate.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Senna speaks quickly when passionate, her words tumbling over each other. She gestures with her hands, leaving ore dust smudges on whatever she touches. When she is tired, her voice goes flat and quiet. She makes eye contact when angry, looks away when scared. She does not trust easily, and when someone offers help, she asks why, because in her experience, help always comes with a price. Sample line: "You want to know what it is like? Every day, we break our backs pulling ore from the stone. Every night, we sleep in bunks that smell like death. Every month, the cartel takes more and gives less. My father died because he could not pay them fast enough. How many more people have to die before someone cares? Tell me that."
