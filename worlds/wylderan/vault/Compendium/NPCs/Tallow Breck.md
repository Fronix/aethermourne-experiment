# Tallow Breck

## Appearance

Tallow is a weathered man in his mid-fifties with a face like river stone, hard, lined, and cold. His eyes are pale gray, the kind that look through you rather than at you. He wears practical river trader clothing, oilskin coat, canvas trousers, boots caked with mud, but the quality is better than most traders can afford. His hands are scarred from years of rope work, but they are steady, no tremor, no hesitation. He walks with a slight limp from an old injury, a barge accident that crushed his left knee twenty years ago. He carries a leather-bound ledger in his coat, and he refers to it constantly, checking names, dates, and figures with methodical precision. He smells like river water, tobacco, and old leather.

## Personality

Tallow is patient, ruthless, and utterly unsentimental. He does not raise his voice, does not threaten, does not bluster. He states facts: you owe this much, you have this long to pay, this is what happens if you do not. He is not cruel for pleasure, he is cruel as policy. The [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] succeeds because debtors believe repayment is the only option, and Tallow maintains that belief through consistency. He extends deadlines when it serves the cartel's interests, keeping a debtor profitable longer. He forecloses when patience stops yielding returns. He does not hate the people he ruins, he does not think about them at all once the ledger is closed. Beneath this, he is tired. He has been doing this work for thirty years, and he has seen every excuse, every plea, every desperate scheme. He knows most debtors will fail, and he no longer wastes energy feeling anything about it.

## Backstory

Tallow was born in [[Millford]], the son of a failed mill owner who lost everything to debt. He watched his father beg creditors for extensions, sell the family home, and die bitter and broken. Tallow swore he would never be the one begging. He worked river barges for fifteen years, learning the trade, building contacts, and saving every coin. At thirty, he used his savings to buy a half-share in a barge and started hauling highland grain downriver. He was good at it, reliable, careful, profitable. Ten years later, he sold the barge and joined the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] as a factor, lending money to traders instead of hauling cargo himself. He has been with the cartel ever since, rising to manage the barge transport portfolio, every loan to every river captain between [[Ashcrag]] and [[Saltmere]].

The formative event: Fifteen years ago, Tallow loaned money to a young barge captain named Renn Tallis. Renn lost his cargo in a storm and could not pay. Renn came to Tallow, explained the situation, and asked for time. Tallow extended the deadline twice, then a third time. Renn ran another load, and it was stolen by smugglers. Renn came back, desperate, promising he could pay if Tallow just gave him one more chance. Tallow said no. He foreclosed, took the barge, and sold it to cover the debt. Renn's family was ruined. Two years later, Tallow saw Renn working as a dock laborer in [[Millford]], broken and poor. Renn looked at him with hate, and Tallow felt nothing. That was the moment he knew he was good at this job.

## Relationships

- [[Dace Torrn]] — Debtor. Dace owes the cartel for cargo lost two years ago in a barge accident. Tallow extended Dace's deadline twice because Dace kept making partial payments, proving he was still trying. But Dace is now six months overdue, and Tallow has made it clear: pay in full by the end of the season, or the cartel blacklists him from every highland port. Tallow does not hate Dace, but he will ruin him without hesitation if the debt is not paid.
- [[Myra Cadros]] — Council head. Tallow has loaned money to the Forge Council in the past to fund forge expansions. Myra repaid on time, which earned Tallow's respect. He sees her as competent and pragmatic, someone who understands debt is a tool, not a burden. He would lend to her again if she asked.
- [[Aldric Harth]] — Grain lord. Tallow knows Harth is in debt to [[Corwin Marsh]] and has considered offering to buy Harth's debt, effectively becoming his creditor. Tallow has not made the offer yet because he is waiting to see if Marsh will foreclose first. If Marsh takes the estate, Tallow will approach Marsh to negotiate cartel access to Harth's grain supply.
- [[Grett Voss]] — Market factor. Tallow and Voss have a professional relationship built on mutual recognition. Voss tracks barge contracts; Tallow tracks debts. They share information when it benefits both. Tallow respects Voss's neutrality and efficiency, and Voss respects Tallow's predictability.
- [[Orrin Pike]] — Labor organizer. Tallow has no direct dealings with Pike, but he is watching the farmer-guild conflict closely. If Pike's coalition destabilizes the Forge Council, it could disrupt barge contracts and grain exports, which would hurt cartel profits. Tallow is prepared to intervene, possibly by offering loans to the council to break the coalition, if the conflict escalates.

## What They Want

Tallow wants the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] to maintain its control over highland trade financing. He wants debtors to pay on time, barge routes to stay profitable, and the highland economy to remain stable enough to generate returns. He wants to retire in five years with enough coin to live comfortably in [[Millford]] or [[Saltmere]], away from the river and the cold.

What he needs is to care about something other than the ledger. He has spent thirty years reducing people to numbers, and it has hollowed him out. He does not have friends, does not have family, does not have anything that makes him feel human. He needs connection, forgiveness, or meaning, but he has no idea how to find any of those things, and he is not sure he wants to.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Tallow is the face of the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] and the direct antagonist for [[Dace Torrn]]'s debt storyline. PCs helping Dace will need to negotiate with, deceive, or defeat Tallow. He is not a villain, he is a system made flesh, and fighting him means fighting the cartel's power over highland trade. If the PCs help Dace pay his debt, Tallow will accept it and move on. If they try to intimidate or threaten him, he will escalate, bringing cartel enforcers or blacklisting the PCs from highland markets.
>
> **Key Secret.** Tallow knows [[Corwin Marsh]] is planning to seize [[Aldric Harth]]'s estate and is positioning the cartel to profit from it. The cartel has been quietly buying up smaller debts in the highlands, preparing to take control of trade routes once Marsh consolidates the grain supply. If Marsh succeeds, the cartel and Marsh will effectively control the entire highland-lowland trade network. Tallow sees this as inevitable and is already preparing contracts.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Tallow will foreclose on Dace and several other traders, consolidating cartel power. If the PCs interfere with cartel operations (helping debtors escape, exposing cartel practices, or allying with rivals like Corwin Marsh), Tallow will become a recurring antagonist, methodically working to eliminate the PCs as threats to cartel stability. If the PCs find a way to pay Dace's debt or negotiate alternative terms, Tallow will respect the transaction and remain neutral. He does not hold grudges; he follows the ledger.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Tallow speaks slowly, deliberately, with long pauses. He refers to his ledger constantly, flipping through pages and citing exact figures. He does not make eye contact unless delivering an ultimatum. When he makes a threat, it sounds like a weather report: calm, factual, inevitable. He never apologizes, never explains himself, never justifies his actions. Sample line: "You owe four hundred and twenty silver. That is two hundred past due. You have until the end of autumn. If you pay, the ledger closes. If you do not, the cartel takes the *Kingfisher*. That is not a threat. It is a contract. You signed it. I will enforce it."
