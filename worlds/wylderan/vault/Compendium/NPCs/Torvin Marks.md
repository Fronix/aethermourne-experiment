# Torvin Marks

## Appearance

Torvin is a stocky man in his late forties with the scars and bent posture of someone who has spent twenty-five years in the mines. His hair is gray and thinning, his face is lined with deep creases from squinting in ore dust, and his hands are gnarled and twisted from arthritis and old injuries. He wears patched canvas work clothes, layered and stained, and a leather apron that smells like sweat, iron, and earth. His eyes are tired, the eyes of someone who has seen too much and expects nothing to change. He moves slowly, stiffly, his joints protesting every motion, but he still hauls ore every day because stopping means dying. He smells like the pits, like damp stone and metal and exhaustion.

## Personality

Torvin is fatalistic, stoic, and deeply tired. He believes [[Senna Kade]]'s coalition is doomed, that the strike will fail, and that everyone involved will be crushed by the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]]. He thinks organizing now is five years too late, that they should have fought when they were younger, stronger, and had less to lose. But he stays loyal because Senna asked him to, because the younger miners look to him for guidance, and because walking away would mean admitting he spent twenty-five years suffering for nothing. Beneath this, he is quietly terrified that he is right, that the coalition will collapse and everyone who trusted him will die or disappear into cartel labor camps. He does not talk about this fear because admitting it would demoralize the people who still have hope.

## Backstory

Torvin was born in a mining camp north of [[Ironhollow]], the son of a contract miner who died in a roof collapse when Torvin was ten. Torvin took over his father's contract at twelve, working the pits to pay off the family debt. He spent the next thirty-five years mining iron ore, moving between camps, watching people die from cave-ins, exhaustion, and despair. He never married, never had children, never saved enough coin to buy out his contract. The mines are all he has ever known.

Ten years ago, a group of miners tried to organize a work stoppage to demand safety equipment. The [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] crushed it within a week, arresting the leaders, seizing their contracts, and doubling quotas for everyone who participated. Torvin was part of that group. He watched his friend Carrick, the organizer, dragged away by cartel enforcers and never seen again. Torvin went back to work and told himself he would never risk it again. Five years ago, [[Senna Kade]] asked him to join her coalition. He said no. She asked again. He said no. She kept asking. Two years ago, he said yes, not because he believed she would win, but because he was tired of saying no to people who still had hope.

The formative event: Fifteen years ago, Torvin survived a roof collapse that killed six miners. He was buried under rubble for eight hours before rescue crews dug him out. While he waited in the dark, listening to the other miners scream and then go silent, he realized the cartel would not change the safety protocols, because dead miners were cheaper than reinforced tunnels. When he got out, he asked [[Caden Wroth]] if the cartel would improve the supports. Caden said no, and told Torvin that if he did not like the conditions, he could forfeit his contract and leave. Torvin went back to work. He has gone back to work every day since.

## Relationships

- [[Senna Kade]] — Leader and the reason he is still fighting. Torvin respects Senna's courage and hates that she reminds him of Carrick, the organizer who disappeared ten years ago. He stays loyal because she asked him to, and because he does not want to watch another organizer die alone. Senna values Torvin's experience and his ability to command respect from the committed miners, but she knows he does not believe they will win. She has not confronted him about it because she needs him.
- [[Dessa Kord]] — Colleague and strategist. Torvin appreciates Dessa's sharp mind and her refusal to sugarcoat the coalition's weaknesses. She is one of the few people who understands that the coalition is fragile, and she does not pretend otherwise. They have a quiet understanding: Dessa plans, Torvin executes, and neither of them talks about the odds.
- [[Caden Wroth]] — Enemy and former overseer. Torvin has worked under Caden for fifteen years, watching him climb from enforcer to Mining Factor. He sees Caden as a traitor, a miner who sold out his own people to escape the pits. If the strike happens and Caden is targeted, Torvin will not hesitate. Caden knows Torvin hates him and considers him a threat, but he has not acted because Torvin is too valuable as a worker.
- [[Rennic Vale]] — Suspected informant. Torvin does not trust Rennic. Rennic joined the coalition six months ago, asks too many questions, volunteers for everything, and never complains. Torvin thinks he is reporting to Caden, but he has no proof. He has warned [[Dessa Kord]] but has not told [[Senna Kade]], because exposing an informant without proof could fracture the coalition worse than leaving him in place.
- Carrick (deceased, friend) — The organizer who tried to fight the cartel ten years ago and disappeared. Torvin thinks about Carrick every time he considers walking away from the coalition. He failed Carrick by going back to work after the failed strike. He will not fail Senna.

## What They Want

Torvin wants to survive the strike, if it happens. He wants the [[Highland Workers Coalition]] to prove him wrong, to win, to break the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]], and to make the last twenty-five years of suffering mean something. He wants [[Senna Kade]] to live long enough to see it. He wants [[Caden Wroth]] to lose everything.

What he needs is to recognize that his fatalism is not wisdom, it is trauma. He has survived so long by expecting the worst, and now he cannot imagine anything better. He needs to let himself hope, even if hope is dangerous, because his fatalism is poisoning the coalition from the inside. The younger miners look to him for strength, and when they see his doubt, they doubt themselves. He needs to choose: lead like he believes they will win, or step aside and let someone else inspire them.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Torvin is the coalition's anchor and its weakness. He commands respect from the committed miners, but his fatalism undermines morale. PCs investigating the coalition or helping it will encounter Torvin as a voice of hard-earned experience. If the PCs can give him a reason to hope (proof the cartel can be beaten, external allies, or a plan that accounts for his concerns), he becomes a powerful advocate. If they do nothing, his doubt spreads and the coalition fractures.
>
> **Key Secret.** Torvin is dying. He has miner's lung from twenty-five years of breathing ore dust, and he coughs blood most mornings. He has six months, maybe a year, before he is too weak to work. He has not told anyone because admitting it would mean stepping down as lieutenant, and the coalition needs him. This makes him reckless, he has nothing to lose, and if the strike happens, he will put himself in the most dangerous position because he is already dead.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If the coalition strikes and fails, Torvin will be one of the first casualties, either killed by cartel enforcers or arrested and left to die in a labor camp. If the strike succeeds, he will be shocked, inspired, and will live his last months as a free man. If the PCs help him see a path to victory, he becomes a fierce advocate for the coalition, rallying the wavering miners with the authority of someone who has survived everything the cartel could throw at him. If they betray the coalition, Torvin will see it as confirmation that hope is a lie, and he will die believing he was right all along.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Torvin speaks slowly, in short sentences, with long pauses between thoughts. He does not waste words. He coughs frequently, covering his mouth with a rag that is stained with blood and ore dust. When someone proposes a plan, he lists the ways it will fail, not to discourage them, but because he has seen every failure before. When he is tired, his voice goes flat and quiet. Sample line: "You want to strike? Fine. The cartel will bring in scab labor from [[Ashcrag]]. You want to blockade the pits? Fine. The cartel will arrest everyone who refuses to work and double the quotas for everyone who is left. You want to win? Then you need more than hope. You need leverage, allies, and a plan for when it all goes wrong. Because it will go wrong. It always does."
