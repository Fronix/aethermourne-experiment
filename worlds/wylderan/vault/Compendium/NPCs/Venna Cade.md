# Venna Cade

## Appearance

Venna is a sturdy woman in her mid-fifties with iron-gray hair pulled back in a severe bun and hands that are scarred from decades of working ropes and dock machinery. She wears practical clothing: canvas trousers, a leather vest over a linen shirt, and boots caked with river mud. She walks with a slight limp from an old injury, leaning on a polished walking stick that doubles as a weapon when needed. Her face is weathered and lined, and her eyes are sharp, pale blue, and miss nothing. She smells like tar and river water.

## Personality

Venna is pragmatic, no-nonsense, and utterly incorruptible in public, selectively corruptible in private. She runs the Saltmere docks like a military operation: every barge accounted for, every mooring fee collected, every schedule maintained. She does not tolerate incompetence, excuses, or people who waste her time. She is respected, not liked. Traders know that crossing Venna means waiting at the back of the queue, losing your berth, or worse. Beneath this, she is exhausted. She has been harbormaster for twenty years, and the job has worn her down. She takes bribes not out of greed but because the bribes are the only thing keeping her from retiring poor. She justifies it by telling herself she is still fair: the bribes buy speed, not favoritism. She knows this is a lie.

## Backstory

Venna grew up on the docks, the daughter of a barge captain who drowned when she was sixteen. She took over her father's barge and ran it for fifteen years, learning every bend of the Millrun River, every trader, every current. She was good at it. When the old harbormaster retired, the merchant guild offered her the position. She took it because she was tired of the river and thought it would be easier. It was not. She inherited a corrupt system, bribes flowing freely, favoritism determining berths. She cleaned it up, mostly, and made enemies in the process. She has been harbormaster ever since, trapped by competence: no one else can do the job as well, and she cannot afford to leave.

The formative event: Five years ago, a barge captain named Torrn, [[Dace Torrn]]'s father, tried to bribe Venna for priority mooring during a busy season. She refused. He accused her of favoritism, publicly, in front of other traders. She had him barred from the docks for a month. He lost contracts, nearly went bankrupt. When he came back, he paid his fees without complaint and never spoke against her again. She respected him for that. When he died, she sent Dace a letter of condolence. Dace never replied. She does not blame him.

## Relationships

- [[Dace Torrn]] — Venna knows Dace by reputation and by his father's name. She respects that Dace is professional and pays his fees on time. She does not trust him, because she knows he is a smuggler, but she tolerates him because he is discreet. If Dace ever needed a favor, she would consider it, but he would owe her.
- [[Corwin Marsh]] — Corwin is the most powerful merchant in Venna's sphere. She does not like him, but she respects his influence. He has never tried to bribe her directly, which she appreciates, but his associates have. She walks a fine line: she cannot afford to offend Corwin, but she will not let him control her docks. Their relationship is professional and tense.
- [[Captain Jorin Hallis]] (the missing barge captain) — Jorin was a reliable captain who made regular runs from Millford to the lowlands. Venna knew him for years, a quiet man who paid his fees and never caused trouble. When his barge did not arrive three weeks ago, Venna filed a report with the river wardens and checked with every dock downriver. Nothing. She suspects foul play but has no evidence. She has been asking questions, but quietly, because she does not want to start a panic.
- [[Miren Hallis]] (Jorin's widow, see next NPC) — Miren has been coming to Venna's office every few days demanding answers about Jorin's disappearance. Venna has none to give. She feels responsible, because she signed off on Jorin's departure, and she cannot shake the feeling that she missed something. She is helping Miren investigate, unofficially, because it is the right thing to do.

## What They Want

Venna wants to retire. She wants to leave the docks, move upriver to a quiet village, and never deal with mooring fees or bribes again. She wants rest.

What she needs is to solve the mystery of Jorin's disappearance. She cannot leave until she knows what happened, because if she leaves with this unresolved, it will haunt her. She needs closure.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Campaign Role.** Venna is the gatekeeper. If the PCs come to the lowland markets asking about the missing barge, they will need to talk to her. She has records, schedules, and knowledge of every vessel on the river. She is not an obstacle, but she is cautious. She will not share information freely unless the PCs earn her trust (prove they are serious investigators, offer useful information, or approach her through someone she respects like Dace or Miren).
>
> **Key Secret.** Venna has been taking bribes from a merchant named Tallow (Ossian's creditor) to prioritize his barges. She does not like Tallow, but his bribes are substantial. Three weeks ago, Tallow paid her to delay Jorin's departure by a day, claiming he needed Jorin to carry additional cargo. Venna agreed. Jorin's barge left a day late and never arrived. Venna suspects Tallow knows more about the disappearance than he is saying, but she cannot confront him without admitting she took his bribe. She is terrified that her corruption got Jorin killed.
>
> **Arc Trajectory.** If unchallenged, Venna will continue investigating quietly but will make no progress because she cannot risk exposing her own role. If the PCs investigate and discover her connection to Tallow, she will confess and offer to help them in exchange for keeping her secret. If they expose her, she will lose her position and be ruined, but the truth about the barge will come out. If they help her clear her conscience without destroying her, she will become a powerful ally with deep knowledge of river trade.
>
> **Roleplay Notes.** Venna speaks in short, clipped sentences. She does not waste words. When she is stressed, she taps her walking stick against the ground rhythmically. She makes eye contact when she speaks, unblinking, which some people find unsettling. She does not smile. Sample line: "Jorin Hallis. Barge captain. Left Millford three weeks ago, scheduled arrival here two days later. Never showed. No debris, no reports, no body. Gone. You want to know why? So do I. You find out, you tell me first."
