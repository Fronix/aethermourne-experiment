## Description

The Halfway Burn Site sits in a shallow clearing along Cold Run, four miles downstream from [[Ironhollow]] and four miles upstream from [[Ashcrag]]. It is an abandoned charcoal production site, once one of several operated by timber families in the hills south of [[Ironhollow]]. The forest here has been cut back to stumps and thin saplings, the soil blackened by decades of charcoal ash. At the center of the clearing stands the stone foundation of a kiln, cracked and overgrown with moss. A rusted iron grate lies half-buried in the dirt, and the remnants of a timber shed lean against the hillside, roof collapsed and walls rotting.

The site was abandoned fifteen years ago when the forest was logged out and charcoal production moved closer to [[Ashcrag]]'s timber yards. Most people have forgotten it exists. The road between the two settlements bypasses it to the west, and the path along Cold Run is rough and rarely traveled except by those who know it. In winter, the river freezes solid, and the clearing becomes a windswept pocket of silence, accessible only on foot across the ice.

It is this isolation that makes it valuable. The [[Highland Workers Coalition]] uses the Halfway Burn Site as neutral meeting ground for cross-settlement coordination. It is far enough from both [[Ironhollow]] and [[Ashcrag]] that cartel patrols and council eyes do not reach. It is close enough to the water route that travel is manageable. And it is forgotten enough that no one questions why someone might be walking this stretch of Cold Run, if they are seen at all.

## History

The Halfway Burn Site was established forty years ago by a family of charcoal burners who worked the forests between [[Ironhollow]] and [[Ashcrag]]. They built a stone kiln and a timber shed, cut the surrounding pines, and produced charcoal for the forges downstream. For twenty-five years, the site operated steadily, supplying fuel to [[Myra Cadros]]'s ironworks and Brennan's Forge. But the forest was finite, and the burn sites crept further from the clearing as nearby trees were exhausted.

Fifteen years ago, the family moved their operation south to [[Ashcrag]]'s timber yards, where fresh forest still stood. The kiln was left to the weather. The shed collapsed. The clearing grew quiet. Hunters and trappers sometimes shelter in the ruins during winter storms, but otherwise, the site is empty.

Two years ago, when [[Senna Kade]] and [[Orrin Pike]] first met to discuss coordinating their organizing efforts, they needed a place to talk that was not controlled by the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] or the Forge Council. [[Orrin Pike]], whose family worked charcoal, remembered the abandoned site. They met there in autumn, under the rusted grate, and agreed to build a coalition. Since then, the Halfway Burn Site has become the coalition's coordination hub, used for planning meetings before winter isolates the settlements.

## Current Use

The [[Highland Workers Coalition]] uses the Halfway Burn Site for cross-settlement planning meetings. [[Senna Kade]] and [[Orrin Pike]] meet here when they need to coordinate strategy face-to-face, usually in autumn before the roads freeze. Message runners [[Kael Torrn]] and [[Lira Venn]] use the site as a dead-drop location, leaving messages hidden under the rusted grate for the other settlement's organizers to retrieve.

The coalition does not meet here in winter. The frozen river makes travel easier, but it also makes tracks visible, and anyone crossing the ice can be spotted from the hillsides. Instead, they front-load their planning in autumn, meet at the Halfway Burn Site to finalize strike timing and demands, and then retreat to their separate settlements to organize through the winter isolation.

The site is vulnerable. If the [[Highland Merchant Cartel]] discovers its use, they could set a trap. If informants like Rennic Vale or Thom Harrow report the location, [[Caden Wroth]] or [[Tallow Breck]] could send patrols. The coalition knows this. They vary their meeting times, approach from different directions, and never gather more than four people at once.

## Geography

The Halfway Burn Site is accessible via a rough trail that parallels Cold Run. The trail is overgrown and rocky, passable on foot but not by cart or horse. From [[Ironhollow]], it is a four-mile walk downstream, roughly two hours at a steady pace. From [[Ashcrag]], it is a four-mile walk upstream, also two hours. The clearing is hidden from the main road by a ridge to the west, so travelers on the road between settlements do not see it unless they know to look.

In winter, the trail is impassable under snow, but Cold Run freezes solid enough to walk on. The frozen river becomes the route, cutting travel time to 90 minutes from either settlement. However, tracks on ice are visible, and the open river offers no cover. Coalition members must weigh speed against exposure.

The site is surrounded by thin forest, mostly saplings and scrub. Visibility is good in all directions, which makes it difficult to approach unseen but also difficult for ambushers to hide. A lookout posted on the hillside above can see anyone approaching along the river or the trail.

## Encounters & Hooks

1. **Coalition meeting in progress.** The PCs are traveling between [[Ironhollow]] and [[Ashcrag]] along Cold Run and stumble upon a meeting. [[Senna Kade]], [[Orrin Pike]], and two lieutenants ([[Torvin Marks]] and [[Halden Cray]]) are gathered at the ruined kiln, discussing strike timing. The PCs must decide whether to reveal themselves, listen and leave, or intervene. The coalition will be desperate to know if the PCs can be trusted.

2. **Cartel patrol.** [[Caden Wroth]] has received word from an informant that coalition members are using a site along Cold Run. He sends a patrol to investigate. The PCs arrive at the Halfway Burn Site and find evidence of recent meetings (charcoal smudges, boot prints, a scrap of paper with a message). Shortly after, the cartel patrol arrives. If the PCs are seen here, the cartel assumes they are coalition members or sympathizers.

3. **Message courier.** [[Kael Torrn]] or [[Lira Venn]] asks the PCs to carry a message to the Halfway Burn Site and leave it under the rusted grate. The message contains strike timing details. If the PCs agree, they risk being seen by informants. If they refuse, the coalition loses critical coordination.

4. **Winter ambush.** The PCs are crossing the frozen Cold Run in winter and see smoke rising from the Halfway Burn Site. Investigating, they find [[Halden Cray]] and two farmers ambushed by cartel enforcers sent by [[Tallow Breck]]. The farmers are outmatched and desperate. If the PCs intervene, they make an enemy of the cartel. If they do not, the farmers are captured or killed, and the coalition loses key members.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Key infrastructure for coalition coordination.** If the cartel discovers this site and sets a trap, it could decapitate the coalition leadership. If the PCs help protect the site (posting lookouts, clearing alternate routes, providing warning of patrols), they significantly increase the coalition's chances of success.
>
> Conversely, if the PCs are hired by [[Caden Wroth]] or [[Tallow Breck]] to spy on the site, they gain leverage with the cartel but betray the coalition. This is a moral test and a faction choice.
