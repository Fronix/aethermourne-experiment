# The Pull Site

> [!info]
> | | |
> |---|---|

> | **Status** | Active and worsening |

## Description

The Pull Site is where the Millrun River's delta channels, Westrun, Centerflow, and Saltcurrent, converge before widening into the bay. In normal conditions, this is navigable water, a broad meeting of currents where barge captains make final course corrections before reaching [[Saltmere]]'s docks two miles downstream. The water is deeper here than the surrounding marshes, 15-20 feet at low tide, and the channels create eddies and cross-currents that require careful steering but are not dangerous.

Except during autumn low water, when the pull appears.

The water moves wrong. Currents flow against the tide, pulling vessels upstream when they should drift toward the bay. Shadows move beneath the surface, too large and too slow to be fish or debris. The sound of grinding stone echoes from below, rhythmic and deep, like something vast turning in its sleep. Barges that drift too close to the convergence point are dragged off course, pulled into the deep channel regardless of how hard the crew rows or poles. The water becomes heavier, harder to move through, as if it has thickened into something more than liquid.

The Pull Site is not marked. No buoys warn of the hazard. [[Saltmere Merchant Assembly]] knows about the pull but has not publicized it, fearing economic panic. Older barge captains avoid the convergence point during autumn, steering wide toward the eastern shallows. Younger captains, unaware of the danger, assume the older generation is superstitious. Three barges have been lost here in the past year. One crew drowned. The other two vanished entirely, barges and all.

The missing barge from [[Millford]], the one that disappeared three weeks ago, is here. At the bottom. Caught in the pull.

## The Pull Phenomenon

The pull is a supernatural downstream current that appears unpredictably during autumn low water (September-November), when the Millrun's natural flow weakens and tidal influence reaches further upstream.

**Physical Manifestations:**

- **Reversed Current:** Water flows upstream against the tide, pulling vessels toward the convergence point rather than letting them drift toward the bay. The current is not fast, perhaps 2-3 knots, but it is relentless. Rowing against it exhausts crews within minutes.

- **Shadows Beneath the Surface:** Large, slow-moving shapes are visible beneath the water when the light is right. They do not surface. They do not behave like living creatures. They move in circles, converging on the deepest part of the channel. Barge captains who have seen them describe "darkness the size of a house, turning over and over."

- **Grinding Sound:** A deep, rhythmic grinding noise rises from the water, audible above wind and wave. It sounds like stone scraping against stone, like millstones turning without grain. The sound pulses, once per hour when the pull is weak, every 40 minutes when it is strong. The pulse rate is increasing.

- **Water Density:** Near the convergence point, the water becomes heavier, thicker, harder to move through. Oars drag. Poles sink too deep. Swimming is nearly impossible. Divers report feeling as if the water is pulling them down, resisting upward movement.

**When It Appears:**

The pull manifests during autumn low water, when river flow is weakest and tidal cycles dominate the delta. It does not appear every day. Some weeks, the convergence is safe. Other weeks, the pull activates daily, strongest at low tide when the water level drops and exposes the deeper channels.

Experienced barge captains time their runs to avoid autumn low tide entirely, launching at high water when the bay's flood tide pushes upstream and masks the pull. Inexperienced captains who launch at low tide often do not realize they are in danger until they are already being dragged off course.

## Encounters & Hazards

**Environmental Dangers:**

- **Navigation Skill Checks:** Steering clear of the pull requires a DC 12 skill check (Seamanship, Navigation, or similar). On a failure, the barge is pulled 50 feet closer to the convergence point. Three consecutive failures mean the vessel is caught and cannot escape without external assistance (rescue tow, jettisoned cargo to lighten load, cutting the barge loose and swimming for shore).

- **Drowning Risk:** Crew who fall overboard near the convergence point are pulled under. The water's unnatural density makes swimming difficult (DC 14 to stay afloat, DC 16 to swim to shore). Bodies of drowned sailors are not recovered. They sink to the bottom and remain there.

- **Barge Damage:** Prolonged exposure to the pull strains hulls. Planks crack, seams leak, and barges begin taking on water. A barge caught in the pull for more than 10 minutes will sink unless the crew bails constantly.

**Divine Manifestations:**

- **The Heartbeat:** At the deepest point of the convergence, the grinding sound becomes a pulse. Thud. Pause. Thud. Like a massive heartbeat. PCs who hear it clearly (DC 10 Awareness check) realize the sound is organic, not mechanical. Something living is down there.

- **Whispers in the Current:** PCs in or near the water may hear voices, faint and distorted, sounding like water flowing over stones. The voices do not speak words, but they convey intent: *return, come together, flow back*. PCs who fail a DC 12 Willpower save feel compelled to move toward the convergence, drawn by the divine will.

- **Visions of Fragments:** Divers or PCs who fall into the water may see flashes of other locations along the Millrun, the ford at [[Millford]], the confluence at [[Ashcrag]], as if the river is trying to connect its scattered pieces. These visions are disorienting (DC 14 save or become stunned for one round).

## The Missing Barge

The grain barge that left [[Millford]] three weeks ago is here, at the bottom of the convergence channel, 20 feet below the surface.

**Evidence:**

- **Wreckage Visible at Low Tide:** When the tide drops during autumn low water, the barge's broken mast can be seen protruding above the waterline for a few hours. This is how the [[Saltmere Merchant Assembly]] knows the barge is here, but they have not attempted recovery because diving near the pull is deadly.

- **Cargo Spilled:** Grain sacks litter the riverbed around the wreck. Most have rotted or been eaten by fish, but a few are still identifiable, marked with [[Ashcrag]] branding and [[Garrick Thane]]'s mill stamp.

- **Crew Bodies:** Two of the three crew members are trapped inside the hull, drowned. The third body is missing, likely dragged deeper by the pull. If PCs recover the bodies, the families in [[Millford]] can bury them, providing closure and earning the PCs gratitude (and possibly information from [[Dace Torrn]] or [[Halford Quick]]).

**What Caused the Wreck:**

The barge launched from Millford during early autumn, when the pull was active but not yet widely known. The crew was experienced but unfamiliar with the delta's hazards. They were caught in the pull, dragged toward the convergence, and capsized when the current overturned the barge. All three crew drowned. The barge sank and has remained at the bottom ever since, held in place by the same current that dragged it down.

## Hooks

1. **Missing Barge Investigation:** The barge captain's family in [[Millford]] hires the PCs to search the delta and confirm what happened. [[Dace Torrn]] or [[Halford Quick]] can point the PCs toward the convergence point based on rumors from other barge captains. The investigation leads to the Pull Site, where PCs must navigate the hazard and recover evidence (or bodies).

2. **Assembly Cover-Up:** [[Saltmere Merchant Assembly]] has been covering up barge disappearances to prevent economic panic. If the PCs publicize the pull's danger, grain shipments halt, [[Saltmere]] faces food shortages, and the Assembly retaliates. If the PCs agree to keep quiet, the Assembly offers payment but barges keep vanishing. Moral dilemma: protect traders or protect the economy?

3. **The Pull is Worsening:** Older barge captains report the pull is activating more frequently and lasting longer. What was once a rare autumn hazard is now a near-constant threat. If the trend continues, the delta will become impassable within a year, cutting off highland-lowland trade entirely. The PCs are hired to investigate the cause and find a solution (which leads to discovering the divine heart).

4. **Salvage Contract:** A merchant offers the PCs a contract to salvage cargo from the missing barge. The reward is significant (the grain is valuable, and the barge itself can be sold for timber). But diving near the pull is dangerous, and the Assembly will not help. The PCs must navigate the hazard, recover the cargo, and escape without being dragged under.

5. **Divine Discovery:** If the PCs dive or use magic to investigate the convergence's depths, they discover the Passage Keeper's heart resting on the riverbed. The heart is the size of a house, partially buried in silt, covered in barnacles and river weeds. And it is beating. This revelation connects to the larger campaign arc: the dead gods are not inert, their remains are active and dangerous.

> [!warning]- GM Only
>
> **The True Cause:**
> The pull is created by the Passage Keeper's heart, the largest fragment of the god's corpse, resting 20 feet below the convergence point. The heart did not stop beating when the god died. It pulses, trying to pump divine essence back through the fragmented body, sending out a command to "return, flow back, come together." This divine will manifests as the supernatural downstream current.
>
> The heart's pulse rate is accelerating. What was once an hourly beat is now every 40 minutes. As the pulse quickens, the pull strengthens and appears more frequently. Eventually, the heart will pulse constantly, creating a permanent one-way current that makes upstream navigation impossible.
>
> **What Happens If the Pull Continues:**
> If unchecked, the pull will collapse highland-lowland trade. Barges will no longer be able to reach [[Saltmere]]. The city will face grain shortages. Highland producers will lose their primary market. Economic war between [[Saltmere Merchant Assembly]], [[Highland Merchant Cartel]], and [[Forge Council]] will erupt as each faction blames the others.
>
> **Potential Solutions (All Have Costs):**
> - **Destroy the heart:** Stop the pull permanently, but destabilize the entire river. The Millrun's course is held partially stable by the god's remains. Remove the heart, and the river may shift unpredictably, flooding settlements or drying up channels.
> - **Sever the connection:** Use magic or divine intervention to cut the heart off from the other fragments (skull at [[Ashcrag]], ribcage at [[Millford]]). This stops the reassembly will but may trap the god's consciousness in eternal fragmentation, a fate worse than death.
> - **Complete the reassembly:** Intentionally bring the fragments together, allowing the god to "heal." This sounds compassionate, but what emerges will not be the Passage Keeper. It will be a broken, incomplete, insane echo of a god, a thing that wants to be whole but cannot remember how. The river will become a living nightmare.
>
> **Campaign Escalation:**
> This is the first major divine decay threat the PCs encounter. How they handle it sets precedent for future god-corpse crises. Do they prioritize mortal safety or divine dignity? Do they treat the remains as resources to exploit or sacred sites to protect? Do they trust factions who want to "fix" the problem, or do they recognize that all solutions carry risk?
>
> The Pull Site is not just a hazard. It is the campaign's thesis: *the dead are still dying, and the living must choose how to survive the decay.*
