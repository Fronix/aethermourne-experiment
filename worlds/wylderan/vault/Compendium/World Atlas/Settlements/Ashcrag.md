## Overview

Ashcrag sits in a wide valley where three cold streams converge to form the Millrun River. The town is built on a gentle slope above the waterline, stone foundations rising from bedrock that shows through the thin highland soil. The air smells of charcoal smoke and forge heat, cut with the green scent of pine forests that blanket the surrounding hills. In winter, the smell disappears under snow, and the town becomes an island of smoke plumes rising into a white sky.

The town exists because of two things: fertile highland valleys that produce short-season grain, and the iron ore deposits in the hills to the north. Farmers bring their wheat and rye to the central market, where river traders buy it by the wagonload and load it onto barges bound for [[Millford]] and the lowlands beyond. The forges, two master workshops and a half-dozen smaller smithies, turn imported iron and local charcoal into tools, nails, hinges, and occasionally weapons. The river trade flows both ways: grain and tools go downstream, salt and cloth come back up. Ashcrag is the knot that ties the highland economy together.

It's a harder place than [[Millford]]. The growing season is shorter, the winters longer and colder. The roads south freeze solid from first snow to spring thaw, cutting the town off from downriver markets for three months every year. People here know how to stretch food, repair tools until they can't be repaired anymore, and wait out the cold. There's pride in that endurance, and suspicion of lowlanders who don't understand it.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **The Forge Council vs. the farming families.** The council is dominated by [[Myra Cadros]], master smith and guild head, and her allies among the merchant class. Farmers resent the council's control over grain prices and barge loading priority. [[Orrin Pike]], a vocal tenant farmer, has been organizing farmers to demand representation. This tension could explode into open conflict if pushed.
>
> **Debt pressure from downriver.** [[Aldric Harth]], the wealthiest grain lord in Ashcrag, owes significant money to [[Corwin Marsh]] in [[Saltmere]]. If Marsh calls the debt, Harth loses his land, and half the highland grain supply disappears. Marsh knows this and is using the leverage to control highland exports.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Confluence

The point where the three highland streams, Cold Run, Ashwater, and Piney Flow, meet and become the Millrun River. The banks are reinforced with stone pilings and wooden platforms, creating a natural barge-building yard. During spring and autumn, when the water is high enough for barges to float downstream, this area is chaotic with activity: traders shouting bids, farmers loading sacks of grain, barge captains inspecting hulls. During winter, the platforms stand empty, ice choking the streams.

A small stone shrine sits on the south bank of the confluence, dedicated to water and stone spirits. Older residents leave offerings here before the first barge launch of spring, a tradition the Forge Council tolerates but doesn't encourage.

### The Forge Quarter

Two streets of workshops, storage sheds, and charcoal piles climbing the slope on the east side of town. The two master forges, **Cadros Ironworks** (run by [[Myra Cadros]]) and **Brennan's Forge** (run by her rival, an older smith named Hal Brennan), dominate the quarter. Both operate massive bellows-driven furnaces and employ a dozen workers each. The sound of hammering echoes across town from dawn until dusk, six days a week.

Cadros Ironworks specializes in precision tools: plowshares, saws, chisels. Brennan's Forge makes rougher work: nails, hinges, wagon fittings. The guild sets prices for both, which keeps them from outright price wars but doesn't stop the constant competition for contracts and apprentices.

### The Market Ground

A broad dirt square in the center of town where farmers bring grain wagons every tenday during harvest season. [[Grett Voss]], the market factor, keeps a tally house at the square's north edge, recording sales and collecting the market fee (ten percent of every transaction, paid to the Forge Council). River traders negotiate prices here, farmers argue about quality, and barge captains arrange transport.

The market ground is also the town's social center. The **Stone Hearth**, a two-story timber tavern, sits on the square's west side. It's the largest building in town aside from the forges, with a common room that can hold eighty people and a second-floor meeting hall where the Forge Council gathers monthly.

### The Timber Yards

South of the confluence, near the treeline, several families operate timber operations. They cut pine and birch from the surrounding hills, process it into charcoal for the forges, and sell the rest as building lumber to lowland traders. The work is brutal and dangerous, and most timber families are poor, living in rough cabins on the forest edge. [[Orrin Pike]] and his extended family work a charcoal kiln here, and Orrin uses his position to organize tenant farmers and laborers against the council.

### The Harth Estate

The largest farm in the region, a sprawling compound of grain storage buildings, workers' cottages, and a stone manor house on the hillside north of town. [[Aldric Harth]] owns this land and controls roughly a third of the grain sold through Ashcrag's market. His family has been here for three generations, and he sits on the Forge Council as the representative of the farming interest, though most farmers see him as too close to the guild.

## People & Culture

Ashcrag is a town of farmers, smiths, and traders, all bound together by the seasonal rhythm of the highland economy. Spring and autumn are frantic: planting, harvesting, barge-loading, tool orders. Winter is slow, the town cut off by frozen roads, people hunkered in by their fires waiting for thaw. Summer is steady work: tending crops, running the forges, cutting timber.

People here value self-sufficiency, hard work, and skepticism of outsiders. Lowlanders are soft, coastal folk are slick, and anyone who doesn't understand a hard winter doesn't understand much. There's a deep cultural divide between the landowning farmers and guild smiths, who have influence and comfort, and the tenant farmers and laborers, who scrape by on wages and debt. The divide sharpens every year as lowland creditors like [[Corwin Marsh]] tighten their grip on highland debt.

Religious practice is minimal and practical. Most families acknowledge the old spirits of stone and water, leaving small offerings before planting or before dangerous river runs. There's no formal temple, no priests. The Forge Council discourages superstition, calling it unproductive.

### Notable Residents

**[[Myra Cadros]]**, master smith and head of the Forge Council. Early 50s, broad-shouldered, with soot-stained hands and a sharp mind for contracts. Runs Cadros Ironworks and controls most of the tool trade with [[Millford]]. Believes the council's guild-run model keeps Ashcrag prosperous and stable. Wants to expand forge operations and sees tenant farmer complaints as shortsighted.

**[[Aldric Harth]]**, grain lord and largest landowner in the Stone Heights. Mid-40s, well-fed, wearing fine wool that stands out in a town of homespun. Owns the Harth Estate and sits on the Forge Council. Owes significant money to [[Corwin Marsh]] in [[Saltmere]] and is desperately trying to increase grain exports to pay it down before Marsh calls the debt. Torn between his council position and his creditors.

**[[Grett Voss]]**, market factor. Late 30s, sharp-eyed and efficient. Runs the tally house and collects market fees on behalf of the council. Neutral in most disputes, which makes them valuable as an information broker. Knows every sale, every debt, every barge contract that flows through Ashcrag. Wants to keep the market running smoothly and avoid the farmer-guild conflict that's brewing.

**[[Tallow Breck]]**, river trader and cartel factor. Mid-50s, weathered face and cold eyes. Represents a lowland trade cartel that finances barge operations and loans money to river traders, including [[Dace Torrn]]. Stays in Ashcrag during trade season, arranging contracts and collecting debts. Has a reputation for patience, until the debt comes due, and then he's ruthless.

**[[Orrin Pike]]**, tenant farmer and labor organizer. Early 30s, lean and work-worn, hands scarred from charcoal burns. Runs a charcoal kiln south of town and has been organizing tenant farmers and timber workers to demand council representation. Believes the guild is strangling the highland economy by prioritizing forge profits over farmer survival. Wants a voice on the Forge Council and land reform that breaks up estates like Harth's.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The Forge Council vs. the farmers.** [[Orrin Pike]] has gathered signatures from forty tenant farmers demanding a seat on the Forge Council. The council, led by [[Myra Cadros]], has refused, claiming farmers already have representation through [[Aldric Harth]]. Pike's supporters are threatening to boycott the spring grain market, which would collapse the highland trade network. The next council meeting could turn violent.

2. **Aldric's debt crisis.** [[Aldric Harth]] owes [[Corwin Marsh]] a sum he cannot pay unless this year's harvest is exceptional. Marsh has sent word that he's calling the debt in two months. If Harth defaults, Marsh seizes the Harth Estate, and the highland grain supply falls under lowland control. Harth is considering desperate measures: selling to smugglers, cutting deals with rival creditors, or sabotaging his competitors to corner the market.

3. **The winter road closure.** Last winter, the southern road to [[Millford]] was impassable for four months instead of the usual three. Traders are worried this winter will be worse. If the road closes early, Ashcrag will be cut off with a season's worth of unsold grain and no way to buy imported salt or cloth. [[Grett Voss]] is pushing the council to stockpile supplies and negotiate early barge contracts, but [[Myra Cadros]] doesn't want to pay the premium.

4. **Tallow's collection run.** [[Tallow Breck]] is in town to collect debts from multiple river traders, including [[Dace Torrn]]. Dace owes money from a failed barge run two seasons ago. Tallow has made it clear: pay up, or the cartel blacklists Dace from every highland port. If Dace can't trade upriver, his livelihood collapses. Dace is scrambling to raise the coin, and rumors say he's considering smuggling or theft.

5. **The forge fuel shortage.** The timber families south of town are struggling to meet charcoal demand from the two master forges. [[Myra Cadros]] is pressuring them to increase output, but the forests are thinning, and cutting deeper into the hills is dangerous and expensive. [[Orrin Pike]]'s family runs one of the kilns and is refusing to expand production without higher pay. If the forges run out of charcoal, tool production stops, and the highland economy stalls.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Hook 1 (Farmer uprising)** could escalate into: council overthrow, violent protest, PC mediation, or a split that fractures the highland economy and hands leverage to lowland creditors.
>
> **Hook 2 (Aldric's debt)** connects directly to [[Corwin Marsh]]'s plans in [[Saltmere]]. If Marsh seizes the Harth Estate, he controls highland grain exports, which gives him dominance over the entire river trade network. This is a campaign-scale power shift.
>
> **Hook 4 (Tallow's collection)** ties into [[Dace Torrn]]'s existing storyline. Tallow is the creditor mentioned in Dace's background. If the PCs help Dace, they make an enemy of the cartel. If they don't, Dace is ruined.
