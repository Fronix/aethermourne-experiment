## Overview

Ironhollow sits in a narrow valley where Cold Run begins its descent toward [[Ashcrag]], eight miles north through hills that grow rockier and more barren with every mile. The town is a collection of rough timber buildings clustered around a staging yard where ore carts are loaded for the journey south. The air smells of ore dust, sweat, and smoke from the smelting fires that test each batch before shipping. In winter, the smell is sharper, cut with the cold wind that howls down from the exposed peaks to the north. The ground is stained red-brown from generations of ore spillage, and slag heaps rise like burial mounds on the hillsides above the town.

This is not a place anyone chooses to live. It is a place people end up when debt, desperation, or winter cold drives them north from [[Ashcrag]]. The Highland Merchant Cartel owns everything: the mines, the company store, the boarding houses, the ore itself. Miners work under contract, bound by debt they can never quite clear. The cartel's Mining Factor sets the terms, the prices, the quotas. There is no council, no guild, no voice for the workers. You mine, you eat. You stop mining, you starve or freeze. The only law is the cartel's ledger.

The mines themselves are scattered across the hillsides within a half-day's walk: shallow pit mines where surface iron ore is pried from exposed veins in the rock. It is brutal, dangerous work. Cave-ins, rock falls, equipment failures, exhaustion. The cartel does not pay for safety measures. Dead miners are replaced by new debtors. The ore must flow south to [[Ashcrag]], where [[Myra Cadros]] and the Forge Council turn it into tools and wealth. The miners see none of that wealth. They see debt, cold, and the inside of the pits.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **The cartel's stranglehold.** The Highland Merchant Cartel uses Ironhollow to control the entire highland economy. If they cut off ore supply, the forges in [[Ashcrag]] go dark, tools stop flowing to [[Millford]] and [[Saltmere]], and the river trade collapses. [[Myra Cadros]] knows this and is quietly plotting to wrest control of the mines from the cartel, but she lacks the leverage. The cartel, through [[Tallow Breck]] and the Mining Factor, is prepared to use violence to maintain their monopoly.
>
> **Labor organizing.** [[Senna Kade]], the contract miner leader, is in secret contact with [[Orrin Pike]] in [[Ashcrag]]. They are building a cross-settlement alliance between tenant farmers and contract miners, planning a coordinated strike to force both the Forge Council and the cartel to negotiate. If this succeeds, it could reshape highland power dynamics. If it fails, the organizers will be blacklisted, imprisoned, or worse.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Ore Yard

The central staging area where ore is sorted, weighed, and loaded onto carts for the journey to [[Ashcrag]]. The yard is dominated by a massive timber-frame scale house where the cartel's weighmaster records every load in the company ledger. Miners queue here with handcarts and wheelbarrows, waiting for their ore to be assessed and credited against their debt. The weighmaster, an old man named Halven Mir, is meticulous and feared. A single miscalculation, he claims, and the whole system collapses. In practice, the scales are rigged to favor the cartel, and everyone knows it, but no one dares challenge him.

During peak season, the ore yard is chaos: shouting, cursing, the clatter of iron on iron, and the creak of overloaded carts. In winter, when the road to [[Ashcrag]] is impassable, the yard becomes a massive stockpile, ore heaped under tarps, waiting for spring thaw. Miners are paid when ore ships, not when it is mined. Winter means months of accumulating debt with no income.

### The Company Store

A two-story timber building on the west side of the valley, run by [[Marna Tess]], the cartel's supply factor. The store sells everything miners need: tools, food, clothing, lamp oil, explosives. Prices are triple what they would be in [[Ashcrag]], and everything is purchased on credit against future wages. The cycle is simple: work the mines, buy supplies on credit, mine more ore to pay off the credit, need more supplies, accumulate more debt. Marna keeps immaculate records and has no sympathy for complaints. She tells miners that if they do not like the prices, they are free to walk back to [[Ashcrag]] and buy there. No one does. The walk is eight miles through rough terrain, and time away from the mines means falling further behind on quotas.

### The Boarding Houses

Three long, low barracks buildings near the ore yard, housing the permanent miners and seasonal workers. Each building sleeps forty in cramped bunks, two tiers high, with a shared hearth at each end. The air inside is thick with smoke, sweat, and the smell of unwashed bodies. Privacy does not exist. Theft is common. Fights are common. The cartel charges rent, deducted from wages, and provides minimal heating fuel. Miners supplement with scrap wood scavenged from the hillsides, but the forests near Ironhollow are already stripped bare.

[[Senna Kade]] sleeps in the north barracks and uses it as an organizing base. Late at night, after the exhausted miners collapse into their bunks, she gathers a core group in whispered conversation, planning the strike that might free them or destroy them.

### The Pits

Scattered across the hills north and east of the valley, the pit mines are shallow excavations where iron ore is exposed in surface veins. Each pit is a chaos of broken rock, hand tools, and improvised shoring. The cartel provides minimal equipment: hammers, chisels, pry bars, wheelbarrows. Explosives are available from the company store, on credit, but many miners cannot afford them. Most ore is extracted by hand, brutal and slow. Cave-ins are frequent. The cartel does not investigate. Bodies are buried in unmarked graves on the hillside, and new workers are assigned to the pit the next day.

[[Jorin Velk]], the independent prospector, works a claim on the eastern ridgeline, refusing to join the company system. He sells his ore directly to [[Grett Voss]] in [[Ashcrag]], bypassing the cartel. The cartel tolerates him for now, but [[Caden Wroth]], the Mining Factor, is looking for an excuse to seize his claim.

## People & Culture

Ironhollow has no culture, only survival. Miners work dawn to dusk, six days a week. The seventh day is for rest, which usually means sleeping, repairing tools, or drinking rotgut whiskey bought on credit from the company store. There is no tavern, no gathering place, no shared traditions. People here do not stay long enough to build community. They work until their debt is cleared, or until they die, or until they give up and flee south, abandoning everything.

The hierarchy is simple: the Mining Factor at the top, the company store operator and weighmaster just below, and everyone else at the bottom. Contract miners resent independent prospectors, who resent seasonal workers, who are resented by everyone for undercutting wages. Trust is rare. Cooperation is rarer. The only unifying force is shared hatred of the cartel, and even that is not enough to overcome the grinding isolation and despair.

### Notable Residents

**[[Caden Wroth]]**, Mining Factor for the Highland Merchant Cartel. Mid-40s, broad-shouldered and cold-eyed, carries a ledger and a club. Enforces quotas, collects debts, and ensures the ore flows to [[Ashcrag]]. Loyal to the cartel and willing to use violence to maintain control.

**[[Senna Kade]]**, contract miner and labor organizer. Early 30s, wiry and scarred, with a voice that carries across the pits. Working off a debt inherited from her father, but refuses to accept the system. Secretly organizing a strike with [[Orrin Pike]] in [[Ashcrag]].

**[[Halven Mir]]**, weighmaster and cartel functionary. Late 60s, gray-haired and meticulous, operates the scale house and records every transaction in the company ledger. Claims neutrality but profits from rigged scales.

**[[Jorin Velk]]**, independent prospector. Mid-50s, grizzled and stubborn, works a claim on the eastern ridgeline. Sells ore to [[Grett Voss]] instead of the cartel. Harassed, threatened, but refuses to yield. His claim is rich, and [[Caden Wroth]] wants it.

**[[Marna Tess]]**, company store operator. Late 40s, sharp-tongued and unsympathetic, runs the store and tracks every debt. Represents the cartel's economic stranglehold. Despised by miners but untouchable.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The strike conspiracy.** [[Senna Kade]] is organizing contract miners to walk out and refuse to work until the cartel reduces debt terms and improves safety. She has been in contact with [[Orrin Pike]] in [[Ashcrag]], planning a coordinated action between miners and tenant farmers. If the PCs arrive in Ironhollow, Senna will approach them, desperate for outside support or a way to get word to allies downriver. If the strike happens and fails, the cartel will crush the organizers. If it succeeds, the highland economy will be reshaped.

2. **Jorin's claim.** [[Jorin Velk]] has discovered a rich vein on his eastern claim, the best ore anyone has seen in years. [[Caden Wroth]] has been pressuring him to sell to the cartel. Jorin refuses. The cartel is preparing to fabricate a claim dispute and seize the land by force. Jorin needs witnesses, legal help, or muscle to protect his claim. If the PCs intervene, they make an enemy of the cartel. If they do not, Jorin will be driven off or killed, and the cartel tightens its grip.

3. **Winter stockpile crisis.** The road to [[Ashcrag]] closes in two weeks, and the ore yard is already overflowing. [[Myra Cadros]] has sent word that she cannot take any more shipments until spring, which means miners will not be paid for months. [[Caden Wroth]] is offering "emergency loans" at brutal interest rates to keep miners fed through winter. Miners who accept will be trapped in debt for years. Miners who refuse will starve. [[Senna Kade]] is pushing for a work stoppage now, before winter, to force the cartel to negotiate wages instead of debt.

4. **Cave-in at the north pit.** Six miners are trapped in a collapsed pit mine. [[Caden Wroth]] refuses to authorize a rescue, claiming it is too dangerous and the miners are not worth the cost. Survivors and families are begging for help. If the PCs organize a rescue, they save lives but defy the cartel openly. If they do not, the miners die, and resentment against the cartel deepens.

5. **The charcoal connection.** Ironhollow needs charcoal to smelt test samples of ore before shipping. They buy it from [[Orrin Pike]]'s family in [[Ashcrag]]. But Orrin has been withholding shipments, quietly sabotaging the cartel by choking their fuel supply. [[Caden Wroth]] suspects sabotage but cannot prove it. If Orrin is caught, the cartel will destroy him. If the charcoal shortage continues, ore testing stops, and bad batches get shipped to [[Myra Cadros]], damaging her forges and her trust in the cartel.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Hook 1 (strike)** is the centerpiece of the Ironhollow arc. It connects to [[Orrin Pike]]'s organizing in [[Ashcrag]] and threatens the entire highland power structure. If the PCs help the strike succeed, they shift the balance of power away from the Highland Merchant Cartel and toward the workers. This has ripple effects across all settlements.
>
> **Hook 2 (Jorin's claim)** is a standalone moral test: do the PCs defend an independent operator against a powerful cartel, even when it makes them enemies? It also seeds the resource depletion thread: surface ore is running out, and control of the richest remaining claims will determine who controls the future of the highland economy.
>
> **Hook 3 (winter crisis)** connects directly to the seasonal isolation established in [[Ashcrag]]. Winter is not just inconvenient, it is a weapon the cartel uses to trap miners in debt.
>
> **Hook 5 (charcoal sabotage)** ties Ironhollow to the charcoal shortage thread in [[Ashcrag]] and shows how Orrin Pike is already fighting a guerrilla economic war against the cartel. If the PCs investigate the charcoal shortage in [[Ashcrag]], this hook pulls them north to Ironhollow.
