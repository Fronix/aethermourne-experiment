## Overview

Saltmere sprawls across the Millrun River delta where fresh water meets the salt marsh and ocean beyond. The river splits into three main channels before emptying into a shallow bay, and the city has grown up along all three, connected by wooden bridges and causeways built on pilings driven deep into the marsh mud. The air smells of brine and fish, drying salt and rotting seaweed. The sky is always wide here, the land too flat to hold it back.

The city exists because of salt and because of the river. The marshlands around Saltmere are useless for farming, the soil too brackish and the land too waterlogged, but perfect for salt evaporation ponds that stretch for miles in every direction. Salt is wealth here, and the merchant families who control the ponds control the city. But salt alone doesn't feed a population, and so Saltmere has become dependent on the highland grain trade flowing down the Millrun. Grain barges arrive weekly from upriver, processed at mills like those in [[Millford]], and the city's merchant assembly redistributes both grain and salt across the wider coastal trade network.

It's a working port. The docks are larger and busier than Millford's, handling river barges, coastal fishing boats, and the occasional seagoing merchant vessel. The Merchant Assembly meets in a timber hall built over the water on the central island. Three sprawling market districts, one on each main channel, hum with commerce. Cloth merchants from the coast, tool smiths from inland forges, fish traders, barge captains, salt guild enforcers, and laborers hauling sacks across slick wooden walkways. Saltmere is louder, dirtier, and more prosperous than Millford. It's also more fragile.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **The missing barge is the first sign.** Three weeks ago, a grain barge from Millford disappeared in the delta. It never reached Saltmere's docks. The Merchant Assembly is quietly investigating, but they're covering up the loss to avoid panic. Grain supplies are already tighter than usual, and another disappearance could spark hoarding or riots.
>
> **"The pull" is real.** Near the river mouth, where the three channels converge before meeting the bay, there's a zone of unpredictable current. Locals call it "the pull." Most seasons it's harmless, a tricky navigation point. But during autumn low water, something changes. The current becomes stronger, more chaotic, and barges that drift too close have vanished. Older barge captains avoid the area entirely during autumn. Younger ones think it's superstition. The truth is supernatural, tied to something beneath the delta, but that's undefined for now.

## Layout & Landmarks

### The Three Channels

The Millrun splits into three channels before reaching the bay. From west to east: **Westrun**, **Centerflow**, and **Saltcurrent**. Each channel has its own docks, market district, and character.

**Westrun** is the widest and slowest channel, bordered by the largest salt evaporation ponds. The Westrun Market handles bulk salt trade and barge cargo. The docks here are built for river barges, not seagoing vessels. The Salt Guildhall, a squat two-story building with a red tile roof, dominates the waterfront. The guild enforces production quotas, taxes pond labor, and settles disputes between salt families.

**Centerflow** is the main channel, deepest and most navigable. The Merchant Assembly Hall sits on pilings at the junction of Centerflow and the main east-west causeway. This is the political and commercial heart of Saltmere. The Centerflow Market is the largest, handling imported goods from both upriver and the coast: tools, cloth, pottery, preserved fish, and grain. The docks here accommodate river barges and coastal traders.

**Saltcurrent** is the easternmost channel, narrowest and fastest, bordered by fishing shanties and smaller salt operations. The Saltcurrent Market specializes in fresh fish, dried seaweed, and marsh-scavenged goods. This is the poorest district. Many of the families here work seasonal labor in the ponds or crew fishing boats. The docks are rough-built and less maintained.

### The Merchant Assembly Hall

A two-story timber structure built on thick pilings over Centerflow. The Assembly meets weekly to set grain prices, negotiate barge contracts, and manage the city's relationships with highland traders and coastal merchants. The hall is open to the public, but seats on the Assembly are reserved for representatives of the five major merchant houses: the Marsh family (salt and grain), the Greave family (trade goods and barge contracts), the Vorren family (cloth and coastal imports), the Salter family (salt production), and the Brack family (fishing fleet).

The current Assembly Speaker is **Marda Vorren**, a sharp-eyed woman in her fifties who built her family's coastal trade network from nothing. She's the tiebreaker when the merchant families deadlock.

### The River Mouth and "The Pull"

Where the three channels converge, about a mile south of the city proper, the river spreads wide and shallow before meeting the bay. Sandbars shift with the tides. The current is tricky even in calm seasons. During autumn low water, the zone becomes dangerous. Barge captains report strange eddies, water flowing in directions it shouldn't, and a sense of being pulled off course no matter how they steer. Locals avoid the area during autumn. Some say it's tidal interference. Others whisper about something beneath the water, something old and hungry.

### The Salt Ponds

Miles of shallow evaporation ponds stretch west and south of the city, separated by narrow causeways and dikes. Workers rake salt crystals from the dried beds, load them into barrows, and haul them to the processing sheds near Westrun. The work is brutal, hot in summer, cold in winter, always under the wide sky. Most pond workers are contract laborers who owe debts to the salt families. The ponds are beautiful in a stark way, flat white expanses reflecting the sky, but they smell of brine and decay.

## People & Culture

Saltmere is a city of merchants and laborers, divided cleanly between those who profit from trade and those who work for wages. The merchant families live in multi-story timber houses on the better-maintained causeways, with private docks and household guards. The laborers, pond workers, dock crews, and fishers, live in crowded shanties on the edges of the channels, places that flood during storm surges.

People here respect wealth and contracts. A deal made in front of witnesses is binding. Breaking a contract is a faster way to ruin than theft. The Merchant Assembly enforces this through trade bans, anyone who breaks an agreement can be barred from the markets, which means starvation in a city that doesn't grow its own food.

There's little room for sentiment or idealism. Saltmere runs on trade, and trade requires trust, or at least predictability. Outsiders are welcome if they have goods to sell or coin to spend. Religious practice is minimal, though there's a small shrine to river and tide spirits at the river mouth, maintained by the fishing families who leave offerings before heading into the bay.

### Notable Residents

**[[Corwin Marsh]]**, head of the Marsh family, one of the five merchant houses. Mid-50s, soft-spoken but ruthless. Controls much of the grain importation from the highlands and holds significant debt leverage over traders upriver, including [[Garrick Thane]] in [[Millford]]. Sits on the Merchant Assembly and quietly shapes most votes through favors and obligations.

**[[Ossian Greave]]**, member of the Greave family, currently in [[Millford]] collecting debts from [[Kellan Thane]]. Late 30s, aggressive and public about his business. The Greave family handles barge contracts and trade goods. Ossian's reputation for collecting debts has made him valuable to the family, but his lack of subtlety has made him enemies.

**Marda Vorren**, Assembly Speaker and head of the Vorren family. Late 50s, sharp-eyed, built her family's coastal cloth trade from scratch. Neutral in most merchant disputes, which makes her valuable as a tiebreaker. Wants to expand Saltmere's influence further down the coast, but grain supply problems are holding her back.

**[[Halden Brack]]**, head of the fishing fleet and Brack family representative. Early 40s, weathered and blunt. The Brack family is the poorest of the five merchant houses, but they control the fishing boats and employ half the laborers in Saltcurrent district. Halden has been pushing the Assembly to investigate the missing barge, but the others want to keep it quiet.

**[[Lira Fenn]]**, barge captain and river trader. Early 30s, Dace Torrn's main rival on the highland-lowland trade route. Runs the barge *Tidemark* and specializes in fast deliveries. Knows the river better than most and has been asking questions about the missing barge. She thinks "the pull" is real and wants the Assembly to hire someone to investigate the river mouth.

**[[Venna Cade]]**, Harbormaster of Saltmere. Mid-50s, sturdy woman with iron-gray hair and a limp from an old injury. Runs the docks with military precision, respected but not liked. Has been harbormaster for twenty years and wants to retire, but cannot leave until she solves the mystery of the missing barge. She knew Captain Jorin Hallis, whose vessel disappeared three weeks ago, and has been quietly investigating his fate while dealing with pressure from his widow.

**[[Miren Hallis]]**, widow of Captain Jorin Hallis, the missing barge captain. Late 30s, dark hair streaked with early gray, wearing mourning clothes. Relentless and desperate, refusing to accept her husband's disappearance without answers. Has been demanding information from [[Venna Cade]], the river wardens, and anyone else who might know something. Left with two young children and mounting debts, she cannot afford to grieve, she needs answers. Some in Saltmere are starting to avoid her, but she will not stop searching.

## Tensions & Hooks

1. **The missing barge.** A grain barge from [[Millford]] disappeared three weeks ago. It was scheduled to arrive in Saltmere but never did. The barge captain's family in Millford is demanding answers. The Merchant Assembly is quietly investigating but keeping the disappearance secret to avoid panic. Grain supplies are already tight, and if word spreads that barges are vanishing, highland traders might stop sending shipments. [[Halden Brack]] is pushing for transparency. [[Corwin Marsh]] wants it buried. [[Lira Fenn]] is conducting her own investigation.

2. **Grain shortage fears.** Saltmere is entirely dependent on highland grain imports. The salt ponds can't grow food, and the marshland is useless for farming. If the highland trade stops, the city starves. The missing barge has made the Merchant Assembly nervous. Some families are quietly hoarding grain, driving up prices. If this continues, the laborer districts will riot. The Assembly is considering sending a delegation upriver to [[Millford]] to negotiate guaranteed shipments, but that would reveal their desperation.

3. **"The pull" at the river mouth.** Barge captains and fishers whisper about a dangerous current near the river mouth during autumn low water. Most avoid the area entirely. [[Lira Fenn]] believes the missing barge was caught in it. [[Halden Brack]]'s fishing crews have reported strange sightings, water moving against the tide, shadows beneath the surface, sounds like grinding stone. The Assembly dismisses it as superstition, but the older families know the stories. Something is down there, and it's waking up.

4. **Corwin Marsh's quiet takeover.** [[Corwin Marsh]] holds debts on half the traders in the highlands, including [[Garrick Thane]] in [[Millford]]. If the grain shortage continues, Marsh could call in those debts and take direct control of the mills. This would make him the most powerful figure in Saltmere by far, controlling both supply and distribution. The other merchant families see this coming and are looking for ways to break his leverage, but Marsh is careful and patient.

5. **Ossian's collection trip.** [[Ossian Greave]] is currently in [[Millford]] trying to collect a debt from [[Kellan Thane]]. If he succeeds and seizes Southgrain Mill, it would give the Greave family a foothold in the highlands, disrupting the balance of power in Saltmere. [[Corwin Marsh]] is watching this closely. If Ossian succeeds, Marsh's monopoly on highland grain is threatened. If Ossian fails, the Greave family loses face.

> [!warning]- GM Only
> **Hook 1 (Missing barge)** is the entry point for the river mystery thread. The barge is at the bottom of the delta near the river mouth, caught in "the pull." Investigation will reveal wreckage, bodies, and signs of something unnatural. This connects to Hook 3.
>
> **Hook 3 ("The pull")** is the supernatural core. The pull is a manifestation of something ancient beneath the delta, undefined for now but tied to the world's emerging supernatural framework. This is a slow-burn threat that could escalate into a regional crisis if not addressed.
>
> **Hook 4 (Corwin's takeover)** connects back to Millford and [[Garrick Thane]]. If Garrick's debt is called, it collapses the highland grain supply, which cascades into Saltmere's economy. This is a political/economic hook with high stakes.
